### Post by Stochastic on 2009-01-27
Two bumps within two hours of the original post is excessive.  This section of the forums doesn't turn over that fast, so there's no need to bump on the same day as the last posting.

As for your troubles, Tascam is notoriously bad for not letting Linux devs know anything about their products.  I have (in a drawer somewhere) a Tascam US-122 and to get that working in Linux it needs the windows firmware driver wrapped by the USX2Y loader.  This may be the same case with your card, I wouldn't be surprised if that works, but I found very little info on your card when I went looking through google so you'll be poking around in the dark with that.

Two possible ideas are 1) phone up Tascam and request support (try to be polite, and suggest that the alsa developers would be happy to help them with this) 2) if they give you the cold shoulder, then politely let them know you'll be returning the soundcard in favour of a supported card such as the Focusrite Saffire cards, or the [=perfect"]other ffado supported cards]("http://www.ffado.org/?q=devicesupport%2Flist&filter0=&filter1=&op2=OR&filter2[) then go return that card and support a Linux friendly company.
