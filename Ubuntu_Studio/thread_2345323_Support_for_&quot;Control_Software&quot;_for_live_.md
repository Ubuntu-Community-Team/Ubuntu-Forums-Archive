### Post by jimmypants on 2016-12-02
Hello,
I am a newbie to Ubuntu Studio...
My main area of work is **Live Events**, where I use control/edit software to set up or edit show files for digital audio mix consoles, often weeks in advance of a show.  Live events encompass everything from live sound reinforcement at a concert to live broadcast from a studio.

However, I may use **multi-track digital audio recording out of a live digital audio mixer** as a tool to perform "virtual soundchecks" by recording a band during their warmup and then playing things back to myself after they leave on break until the show, so that I can take my sweet time to set their levels and signal processing parameters.

[B]Question #1:
What do our Developers and Users think about the possibility of having Ubuntu Studio embrace not merely "studio" technology, but expanding to include "event & studio" technology?[/B]

Event technology could possibly cover recording, broadcasting/streaming and live control/mixing in the following disciplines:

[LIST]
[*]Audio 
[*]Video 
[*]Lighting 
[*]Robotics (potentially very useful in Broadway theater) 
[*]Miscellaneous Controls 
[/LIST]
 
One major thing would be to encourage equipment manufacturers to create Linux versions of their control softwares.  (If Linux is stable and powerful enough for web-servers, then what about control softwares for live events??)
Going out on a limb here...  Maybe the distro could even be renamed "Ubuntu ES", where "ES" stands for "Event & Studio".  ...or maybe would it be better to make a fork?

[B]Question #2:
Is there any way that we could get some popular (and free of cost!) control softwares included in future releases of Ubuntu Studio, or at least included in the Ubuntu Studio "Software" repository program?[/B]

Event technology manufacturers are beginning to recognize Linux as having excellent capabilities for critical situations, such as live events.

 For example, "X32-Edit" **control software** is a very popular software, made for the ubiquitous and economical Behringer X32 digital audio mixer.  The X32 is so popular that they have a Linux version of X32-Edit.  This thing even has an app for Raspberry Pi:[INDENT][http://www.music-group.com/Categories/Behringer/Mixers/Digital-Mixers/X32/p/P0ASF/downloads](http://www.music-group.com/Categories/Behringer/Mixers/Digital-Mixers/X32/p/P0ASF/downloads)
[/INDENT]

...and are there any open-source softwares that compare to Ableton? (Particularly Ableton Live?)   I refuse to go Mac.  [https://www.ableton.com](https://www.ableton.com)
