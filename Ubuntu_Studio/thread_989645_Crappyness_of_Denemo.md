### Post by paulmerchant on 2008-11-22
Denemo is a little buggy, but it's what I use. It plays sound, but first you have to make sure timidity is installed on your system. You won't have very much control over the playback, however. The only help documentation I could find is on the Denemo website: [http://www.denemo.org/]("http://www.denemo.org/").

Denemo isn't perfect, but the project is moving forward well and so far I've enjoyed using it.

### Post by Stochastic on 2008-11-22
A few other options are:

-Coding directly into lilypond language manually (probably a little advanced for a middle schooler - particularly if you have troubles with a pencil & paper)

-MuseScore (probably your best bet)
-NtEd
-NoteEdit
-Rosegarden (probably overkill that might not fit on your Nokia)

There are other options too, but those are your best.  Denemo should play once you install/configure timidity (the other apps may also require timidity) but it has some bugs (though it's a project that's progressing quickly - the Intrepid repo version is already a couple releases behind).
