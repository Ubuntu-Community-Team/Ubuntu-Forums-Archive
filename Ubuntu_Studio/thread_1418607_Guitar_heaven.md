### Post by sgx on 2010-02-28
With the release of Amplitube 3, guitarists who really like it still sometimes mention LePou amp/cab sims as their preferred choice. 

[http://lepouplugins.blogspot.com/](http://lepouplugins.blogspot.com/)

I tested some of these using fst, and routing them to Rakarrack.
I used a preamped electric guitar clean signal, and also the zynaddsubfx
clean guitar patch. I am far from an expert on 'tone', but I really
enjoyed many hours with LePou LeXTAC and HyBrit, giving some punch to Rakarrack, as well as by themselves, and I admit to feeding some hardware BBKing, BRaitt, PFloyd patches, great fun.

To use LePou plugins without Rakarrack, a cab-sim is needed, LeCab is
quite nice, and at the same site. Use qjackctl to route LexTAC to LeCab,
and LeCab to your output.

I should mention Amplitube 3 installed fine, and worked both as a standalone and plugin, with zynaddsubfx and guitars sounding great.
CPU usage seems to be about 10% to 30% more than a LePou/Rakarrack setup,
(to be expected considering whats under the hood, and displayed on screen) and from a guy who can't identify 'tone', both sounded great. The
new gui, 720 categorized presets, and large group of modeled gear make Amplitude easier to use than previous versions. I think IK Multimedia always having Mac versions helps their plugins work in well in linux Wine installations. So a serious guitarist using linux may want to at least
try the 10 day unrestricted demo at [www.ikmultimedia.com](www.ikmultimedia.com)

I like the simple skeletal, scalable, color-changing gui of Rakarrack, as it is easy to use for quickly building a good sound, with few limits as to complexity.
The links at the LePou site to Nick Crow and others, also contain amp-sims
considered 'the best', by some guitarists, so check those out.

Hope you all 'be jammin :)
