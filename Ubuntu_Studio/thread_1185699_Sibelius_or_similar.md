### Post by Stochastic on 2009-06-12
One of the first things I should recommend is the search function in these forums.  This is a recurring question and has been asked here already: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1154258](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1154258) among other places.

To actually answer your question, I prefer to use Lilypond with the Frescobaldi editor.  This does take a while to get used to, but I find myself able to do things in this that I could only dream of in my Sibelius or Finale days.  It also results in some of the most gorgeous scores I've seen come out of a home computer.  Expect to have the manual open the entire time you write your first score in Lilypond.

Lilypond is code (like HTML) that turns into music scores, and it may be too difficult for some people, but there are GUI notation programs for Linux.  Denemo, NoteEdit, MuseScore, and Rosegarden are just a handful off the top of my head.

Sibelius is really nice software however, and I've heard some people have had success running it in WINE.

Hope that helps.
