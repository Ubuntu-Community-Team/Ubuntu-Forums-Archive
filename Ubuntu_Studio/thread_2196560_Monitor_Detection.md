### Post by newholm1 on 2013-12-30
Hi there. I recently took apart my computer to clean and dust the insides, and since I reconstructed it, the operating system will not detect my monitor (1600x900). The option to do so is not even there. Instead it is displaying at 1024x768, and text looks fuzzy and uneven, and ripples when I move text boxes around on the screen. I have tried using xrandr to force the resolution, but it does not solve the text display issue. I am using 12.10 on a Gigabyte GA-H55M-USB3 motherboard. Any suggestions would be appreciated!

---

### Post by jjmarc on 2013-12-30
Some monitors have the ability to display lower resolutions than their native resolution. If your monitor has an "Auto-Adjust" button, try pressing that. Also check the link: [http://askubuntu.com/questions/19954/how-to-set-the-monitor-to-its-native-resolution-which-is-not-listed-in-the-resol](http://askubuntu.com/questions/19954/how-to-set-the-monitor-to-its-native-resolution-which-is-not-listed-in-the-resol)

Thanks - on a hunch I checked the cable, and realised I had bent a couple of pins when I reinserted it into the back of the computer. Bent them back into shape, problem solved. Should have checked that first I suppose. Oh well, you live and learn.
