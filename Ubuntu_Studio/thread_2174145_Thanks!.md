### Post by matleb2 on 2013-09-13
Good morning all,
 

 I'd like to thank the thousand of people helping and working on open systems for the great work and for keeping it up.

 

 Not knowing LINUX at all, I tried a few times in the past different disrtros but gave up quite quikly at that time because of non supported devices.
 I recently tried Windows 7 and 8 that did not convinced me at all as I had to load a virtual machine in order to be able to use my good old HP scanner. HP does not support their (old?!!?) materials...
 

 So, I came back to LINUX and choose what seemed the most appropriate distribution to me: UBUNTU STUDIO.
 

 Every devices including my FIREWIRE PRESONUS FIREBOX worked perfectly (I must say better than ever) almost out of the box! (exept for the mixer which is not a big deal).
 I mean, without configuring anything, I had QjackCtl working and Pulseaudio connected automatically in order to playback all sounds from any software. This is what I call a likeable start.
 

 Thinking about it, I've always been attracted by MS alternative solutions for all the good reasons one can find but had to admit for a basic user that LINUX systems were not as advanced and "basic user friendly" than MS.
 

 From two weeks, I have a recent, up to date, 64 bit, user friendly LINUX system that works better including softwares than MS Windows 7 and 8 for my Personnal use.
 

 Considering billions of devices from CPU to webcam that are increasing exponentially daily, I can't picture the amount of work that what just occured for me represents.
 

 I figure that Open source systems are likely to expand quickly for users like me.
 

 Thanks again and keep on!
