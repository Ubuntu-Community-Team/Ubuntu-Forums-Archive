### Post by Pablo_F on 2010-09-27
LOL, that short answer was because the long one is that you should understand some basic concepts of audio in ubuntu. Sooner or later, you will have to decide about the sound server that you will use for your audio card or cards. Pulseaudio (default, good for general purpose), jack (non trivial setting, oriented to pro audio) or pure alsa (more basic).

The three of them take advantage of the same driver; an alsa module. However, they manipulate the audio in different ways and need different apps to configure the server and route the in/outs. 

Also, they serve to different apps or, at least, you need to configure the audio for a given app for pulseaudio, jack or alsa. Not all apps have the three options. 

The choice depends on your needs which you didn't tell us, so I gave you the short answer! Yes, it does.
