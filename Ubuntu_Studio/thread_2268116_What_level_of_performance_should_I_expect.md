### Post by marseille2 on 2015-03-09
Francis, I have two computers running UbuntuStudio, and I can't deal with the old one for music audio. Granted... I ragged it out pretty bad, and rarely turned it off over a 4-year time period. But for some comparison (since we don't know your PC's specs) ... my old computer is a custom-built dual-core AMD with about 2.9Ghz of CPU power, 8gigs of RAM, and an old motherboard from Gigabyte (with the dinosaur AMD Radeon 2100 video module). And although I can use it for recording, I just can't get what I need from it. For instance, if I do realtime audio, the oldie can't really handle anything outside 44.1k / 16-bit with 1024 frames/period (to deal with latency). 



If I lower the frames/period to try and reduce that evil delay between audio gear and pc ... I get dropouts  ("XRUNS") where nothing is recorded at all. So then I'm stuck with latency issues that become noticeable when I'm monitoring audio with 1024 frames/period. Still... if I was stuck on Windows with the oldie, the latency would probably be atrocious (that's one of the big reasons why I quit Windows in the first place). And compared to my new custom build, Ardour doesn't open up anywhere near as fast. I've gone from seconds to milliseconds!:D



That said, the oldie still works like a charm with midi.... so I have no problems when I'm messing around on the keyboard or with drum samples, using as many tracks as I need. It's also reliable for recording speech, since it doesn't need the kind of power I'm looking for when recording music.
