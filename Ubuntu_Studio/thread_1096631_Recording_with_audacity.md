### Post by kayosiii on 2009-03-15
how different does it sound to the guitar plugged in and amplified??? Where is the pickup located on the instrument (I am assuming it is piezo)... 

Have a look at your mixer settings see if setting the microphone boost... test the signal with a headset microphone (you can get these really cheap) and see if that is giving the same low frequency boost.

You can apply EQ in Audacity. Typically the sound below 400hz is attenuated (lowered) for most recordings of Acoustic guitars. You can then amplify the signal to compensate...

Ardour is a better animal for this as you can add plugins and modify them in realtime without effecting your source recorded files. 

Beyond that there is only so much that you will be able to do with onboard sound and a piezo based pickup...

### Post by rayj on 2009-03-16
If, as the above post insinuates, you are recording direct with a piezo pickup (on an acoustic guitar, right?), then good luck. In that case, using some compression will help a little...but it will still be a bit dirty.

I would suggest getting/borrowing a mixer/mic preamp and putting a microphone on it (if it is acoustic). There are some reasonably priced firewire/USB audio interfaces that will get you away from the inevitable noise and crap an internal soundcard has. If you have no budget/friends, you might be able to use a transformer (the Radio Shack XLR-mic level to 1/4"line-level adapters...8 or 9 bucks)or a direct box...I've managed that in a pinch.

Audacity isn't your issue here...hardware is. Use a condenser mic, and try it in the bathroom for a nice bright sound.
