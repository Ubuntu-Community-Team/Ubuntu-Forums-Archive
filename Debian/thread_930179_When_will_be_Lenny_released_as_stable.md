### Post by Cracauer on 2008-12-29
I think you need to give this laptop a serious round of hardware testing, mprime, superpi, memtest86, all of those.

As I said in my longer post, crashes would be kernel related, and that's really not the issue here. People like me usually run a conservative distribution (such as Debian-stable) but slam in brand new kernels all the time.

What matters to us is how many userland programs get shot in the head by new libc's or how much mess some huge dependency chain such as an updated GNOME causes.
