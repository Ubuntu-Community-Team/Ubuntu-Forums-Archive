### Post by tommcd on 2008-09-13
In my experience packages in Debian-testing are more or less as up to date as Ubuntu. Right after a new version of Ubuntu is released many packages in Ubuntu will be newer than those in Debian-testing; but Debian-testing tends to catch up, and may even get ahead of, the packages in Ubuntu.

Just don't mix repositories. For example, don't mix stable, testing, and unstable repos in your sources.list unless you really know what you are doing. If you stick with just testing repos you should be fine, especially now, since Lenny is about to go from testing to stable.
