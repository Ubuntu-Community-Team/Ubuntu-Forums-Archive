### Post by UniverseA7X on 2008-06-28
Why? 

For one. It's FAST. It boots within 30 seconds on my old Dell Latitude with 128 MB RAM and a 450Mhz Pentium III. 

It's stable. Rock Solid. No bugs. It just works. And when I want it to.

And an important point for me..

It's not barebones and stripped, and it's not bloated. It gives you what you need, no foofoo included. This is a no nonsense OS. 

After venturing into unstable software that's in development (KDE 4.1, I decided to check out the nightly builds.) I decided that I value stability over bleeding edge. I want to power on my computer, and know it's going to work just like the day before. That's why I'm going to stay with Hardy till the end of it's support. By then, it'll be tested and true, and will be trustworthy and stable. 

That said. I may try Sid on a virtual Machine, but I'm not too sure about a computer I'd use everyday. 

And my last reason I like Debian so much... Is that the logo for it is so cool looking! Might sound like a lame reason, but between this and all of the above, I'd say I have room to argue. :)

### Post by Mizzou_Engineer on 2008-06-30
Debian is a nice distro and it is what I run on my machines. I like it as it is possibly one of the most flexible distributions as they give you a wide range of install methods (CD, DVD, USB stick/SD, NFS, PXE + netinst, mini-CD + netinst, floppy + netinst, etc.) and the installer gives you a wide range of options as to install type and categories. They also do not muck around much with "customizing" packages, which can make installing not-in-repo packages pretty tricky at times. I also like how the testing branch gets continual updates yet is stable enough for daily use. I am not particularly a fan for the "wait for the next relase in X months if you want the new version of Y program" approach many binary distros use and only one of my computers is really fast enough to make installing and continually updating source-based distros Gentoo painless enough. Yes, Gentoo can run very nicely on an old computer but it has to finish compiling first :D

As far as performance goes, Debian is on the faster end of average but not that different from the various other Debian-based distributions and most non-RPM-based distributions in general. It runs pretty nicely on my junker of a 6-year-old laptop that is my MythTV frontend (2.2 GHz P4-M, 256 MB RAM, 4200 rpm HDD)- that machine is about as snappy as my girlfriend's nice 6-month-old 2.0 GHz Mobile Sempron 64 laptop with 4 GB RAM and a fast 7200 rpm HDD running Vista Basic 32-bit. I don't know if that is more of a good job on Debian's part for making an effecient, effective OS or a knock on MS for making an OS bloated enough to require 1.5 GB RAM before you can try to even run an application without swapping like mad.
