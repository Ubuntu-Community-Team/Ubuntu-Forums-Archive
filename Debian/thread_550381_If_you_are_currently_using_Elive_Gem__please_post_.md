### Post by Caffeine_Junky on 2007-09-24
Hey RAV..

I installed "Elive Gem 1.0" a few hours ago, so at this point in time I have not delved to deeply into it (ie: personal configuring etc..).. 

** 
_A little background info_:
My setup is a "stand alone" desktop PC: GIGABYTE i-DNA nVidia GEFORCE [6100] series onboard gfx/surroundSound Mobo. / AMD64 / 1GB ram (shared) / 17" CRT monitor / ADSL + SpeedStream 42oo modem/router. 3 HDD's ~160 GB For Media storage | 120GB for Ubuntu/debian/Elive OS's | 80GB with my original XP install on it.

I have not been using Linux for very long (as my forum Join date reflects). My flavor of choice at this point in my Linux experience is debian based distro's.

The two that I have been working with are:
  * Ubuntu 7.04
  * debian etch
I enjoy Ubuntu because it was/is easy to install and get up and running for my basic needs (listen to music surf the net  ..play with Gimp etc..). So when I wanted to try another distro, debian was a logical choice for me, considering that the learning-curve in linux is challenging enough without trying to comprehend the use of two differently structured systems. By this I refer to package management / DE configuration between Gnome 'n' KDE etc  ( ie: openSUSE. Ubuntu).

My interest in computers only started two years ago when I decided to buy a cheep PC(I have since upgraded to the above spec's + tower) and checkout this thing called the "World Wide Web", ...and let me tell you, ...the PC bug bit me hard, and now my PC/internet  is my major passion in life. 
Yeah I am a little obsessed with it :lolflag:  

..but I think it is a good obsession, and very enjoyable/rewarding one in terms of knowledge and understanding of other people and cultures. (heh.. I have the whole world in my lounge-room).

I started out on Win98SE and from there moved onto XP. It was on XP that I gained most of my current computer skills (ie:  partitioning, installing, good security/surfing habbits, problem solving skills, ..Google is my favorite tool!). I have done so many formats/installs of Windows and Linux on my HDD's,\( in the interests of learning how to).  
It is a wonder they are still in working order.. :)
**

OK, enough rambling, or, well, maybe just a little more... 

.._DE's / Wm's I have tried_:  Gnome, KDE, Fluxbox, Enlightenment-(16 ~ 17) on Ubuntu & debian-etch.
***
ELIVE Gem 1.0
 OK, now here I am sitting infront of Elive Gem 1.0 listening to my 5000+ mp3 collection which I have loaded into XMMS. 
..XMMS is my music player of choice, and handles my library/shuffle well, so that is cool.

_The Desktop/theme_:    I am currently using the "Lucax theme" (night theme, black 'n' blue).  I like the way that E17 animates the desktop background, (ie: "twinkling stars").  
I have been looking for a feature like this in a desktop-environment since i first started using PC's, ...very nice.

I will have to play around with the D-Top and see if I can set it up the way I like it, ..and that seems to be easy to do with the Right-click/edit feature.

hmmm ..well so far I am very impressed with this distro, I understand that E17 is still in it's Alpha? stage, ..so I will just wait and see how stable it runs over long periods of use and configuration.

I have found debian etch to be very stable in my dealings with it, so I don't anticipate any problems on that front (correct me if I am wrong about elive having debian etch as it's base ..I am sure I read that on the elive site).

Anyhoo, I am going to have to spend some time getting right into the   inner workings and functionality of elive 1.0, before I can comment any further on it.  But at this point in time I will say it is a good thing!
 ...let the testing 'n' tweaking begin!! :p

cheers.

### Post by hoboken on 2008-01-31
I've got everything working correctly except my keyboard layout (spanish). Wireless is working through a crude hack, there doesn't seem to be any knetworkmanager style app installed... wifi radar is just an apt-get away, though, so no real problems there.

Issues...

1. The rather annoying forced donation. I have direct links to alternate mirrors, so if you can't get one of those annoying codes then PM me.

2. How in the name of hell are you supposed to manage your windows?? If you must have an OSX-like dock, then include osx-like functionality.. Namely, give me some way to control my active windows besides alt-tabbing or crudely draggimg my active window to see what's underneath. This really disappointed me... There is a space where your minimized windows go, but it only shows windows after you minimize them. And there is no option to show ALL windows. Which brings me nicely to..

3. Customization. This is nowhere near highly customizable: I can't even remove the battery and temp gauges, or add/remove icons from the dock! Wha?

In short, I am pretty disappointed with Elive. I need to check out OpenGEU next to see if that handles enlightenment with a bit more common sense in the department of practicality, and if not I'm hopping over to Dreamlinux. I miss XFCE already.
