### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2008-06-07
Hi, I've started up a new group and I just wanted to invite the former Ubuntu users who are current Debian users to take a look and consider joining.

It's a moderated group, and I'm not sure exactly what that means, but it could be I have to invite you to join. If that's the case please pm me. It could just mean that I have control over messages posted to the group page. I suppose I'll find out if anyone tries to join. 

I've written a short reason for the creation of the group on the group page. I've also slightly modified a tux icon you're free to use if you like. The key point is that you're a Debian user who wants to either just help Ubuntu users, or help Ubuntu users become Debian users.

If you decide to join, you don't really have to do anything different. Just stick a little link in your signature to the group page to let Ubuntu users know that you're a Debian user who thinks Ubuntu is a great Distro, and there are Debian users out there willing to help.

Or just sign up and share what you think. Just don't let it degrade into a us versus them mentality, whatever side you think us, is. There's another Debian social group already. And I want no truck with them.

The page is in my signature.
