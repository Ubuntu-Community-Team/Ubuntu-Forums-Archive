### Post by PatrickMay16 on 2007-05-12
In recent times I've been upset with the bloat that is building up in Ubuntu. Ubuntu 5.04, 5.10, and 6.06 have been OK, but since 6.10, it's been really pretty bloated; a lot more resources are used than the older releases. 
As I'm sure you all know, Debian Etch was released just last month. So I decided to give it a try, to see if it would be any better than Ubuntu in terms of resource usage.
I installed Debian Etch on my spare computer (180MB RAM, 733MHz celeron, 10GB disk), accepting the default options for a desktop system. A previous attempt with a beta of Feisty proved unusable. 
However, Debian gave me a fairly usable system, even on this limited hardware.

On my laptop Edgy uses about 134MB RAM after booting and logging in (and that's after I tweaked things; before that it used close to 200MB!!). Debian just uses about 80MB. And over all, things seem faster and more stable than with Edgy.
And I was pleasantly suprised to find that mp3 support is installed by default. 

I think over time, I may be migrating my laptop and desktop computers from ubuntu to debian. Though with my desktop, which has ubuntu 6.06 installed, I'll wait until the support for 6.06 is dropped or the system gets messed and needs a reinstall; because I installed that system in September of 2005, and updated to new versions of ubuntu without reinstalling. So I'm saying, since it isn't broken, I won't fix it until it is.

But now, enough about me and my experiences. What do you guys think of Debian Etch? And what have your experiences been like?
