### Post by deadflowr on 2017-02-28
You can try checking the throttle settings.
Ubuntu sets a menu bar in the top panel that hides until you hover over it (by default)
So see if hovering over the panel shows the menu, and if it does open options then click on frameskipping and then click on throtting.
You should be able to reset it to a lower level.

Hope that does something.

**Edit:**

I see there are multiple frontends for vba.
One is visualboyadvance-gtk
The other is vbaexpress

vbaexpress seems to be the only one available in the Ubuntu Software.
(visualboyadvance-gtk is installable through the command line apt-get, or synaptic package manager -if installed)

So looking at the vbaexpress one, that adds a cfg (configuration file) to your home folder called VisualBoyAdvance.cfg.
It has a setting for frameskippng and throtting that you can edit to your liking.

Hope that adds more.
