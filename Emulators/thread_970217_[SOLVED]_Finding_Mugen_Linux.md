### Post by itfische on 2009-07-18
That file works fine for me.  Perhaps you got a corrupted version?

To extract it, I simply did:

```

tar jxvf LinuxMugen.tar.bz2.bz2

```in the directory I wanted to extract it in. The 'j' is the 'bz2' filter. The 'x' means extract. The 'v' is verbose. The 'f' says you're giving it a file.
