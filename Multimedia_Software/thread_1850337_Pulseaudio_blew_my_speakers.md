### Post by mauvebic on 2011-09-26
I had a nice pair of JBL speakers that blew because of the popping and crackling generated by pulseaudio. At first i though the speakers were simply due to be replaced, so i did. I bought CA 2.1 speakers, alas the popping and crackling was still there and almost blew my new speakers. After tinkering back and forth in .conf files (changing udev, lfe, sample sizes settings, etc.) to no avail i finally tried reverting to pure alsa, the problems are gone, for now, but come next release i will have to remind myself to remove pulse, and i already remove too much stuff to remember everything.

[soundcard hda intel ALC883]

So, how do i keep the next release from blowing my speakers?

### Post by aeiah on 2011-09-27
your soundcard shouldnt be able to push enough power out of its line-out port to blow a set of speakers. do the crackle and pops occur with other sound devices? pulseaudio might just be exposing soundcard problems that alsa doesnt.

its probably worth buying one of these cheap soundcards just to check - they're very cheap and work in linux out of the box. beware though, they do default to 100% volume :p

[http://www.ebay.co.uk/itm/USB-EXTERNAL-3D-5-1-CHANNEL-AUDIO-PC-SOUND-CARD-ADAPTER-/220806006085?pt=UK_Computing_ComputerComponents_SoundCards&hash=item3369104545](http://www.ebay.co.uk/itm/USB-EXTERNAL-3D-5-1-CHANNEL-AUDIO-PC-SOUND-CARD-ADAPTER-/220806006085?pt=UK_Computing_ComputerComponents_SoundCards&hash=item3369104545)


but for what its worth, i still use alsa. havent seen the point in pulseaudio for my needs really.
