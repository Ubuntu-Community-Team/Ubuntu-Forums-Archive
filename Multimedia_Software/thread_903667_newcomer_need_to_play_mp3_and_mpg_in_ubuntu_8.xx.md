### Post by ajgreeny on 2008-08-28
Sorry, but with no internet connection you can't download mp3s and play them, you will need to go about things in a different way.  You will have to download various packages somehow to get the codecs and other packages needed to play mp3s, though if you use ogg format for your audio, rather than mp3, they will play straight away in Ubuntu, being open source.

Do you have any mp3s already you want to play or are you trying to download music files as well?  Let us have more info and more help will come, I'm sure, though you will have to accept, I think that without an internet connection Ubuntu is much more difficult to manage, and playing multimedia may be very difficult.

If you can get hold of some way to download the iso file for Linux Mint you will have more luck as it comes with all multimedia codecs already installed and will play mp3 and other audio and video file types "out of the box", so may be a better way to go.  It is based on Ubuntu and in many ways is very similar.
