### Post by Kasyan666 on 2012-08-10
Operating Systems: **Ubuntu 12.04** Desktop amd64 (liquorix), **Windows 7** Ultimate x86-64.
Problem: **unstable work of webcam**.

On **Windows**, camera is working properly, but only after installing vendor's software (despite statements of supporting Plug-n-Play). Native software for UNIX on the official site is absent.

In **Ubuntu**, when reconnecting camera, in the **/dev** directory appears **v4l** folder. When starts **Cheese**, **Guvcview** or any other program works with a webcam, that catalog sometimes is disappearing, while in these programs it expressed in absences pictures from the camera (black rectangle). But if the **/dev/v4l** directory missing, but camera is physically connected, **Cheese** "silently" shows a black rectangle. However if physically disconnect the camera, it says something about camera is not found. In **Google Hangouts** (via **Google Chrome** 21 Stable) camera is triggered only once out of ten.

Some time after rebooting **Ubuntu** camera triggered more often. In case of failure needs close application, reconnect the camera and restart application. Two successful launches in a row does not happen. Built-in camera microphone is working correctly and stable under both OS.
