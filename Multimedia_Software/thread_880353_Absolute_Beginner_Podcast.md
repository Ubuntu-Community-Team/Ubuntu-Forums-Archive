Several years ago I published a critically acclaimed audiobook called "Exploring Music & Sound on Your PC."  Frankly, it was a bit ahead of the curve, as MP3s etc, had not caught on.  It also explained MIDI and MOD music.

Now I've taken the part about audio recording and updated it. It's a series of FREE podcasts at my site acpress dot com. No catches and it is divided into segments. There's examples of audio rates, good vs bad mics, mixing sfx and dealing with legal issues of copyrighted music.

You'll also hear the only classical music ever played on a Timex-Sinclair 1000!!!

Again, free and you can place it elsewhere if you feel it will help new users.  BTW I've been in broadcasting for more than 25 years and digital audio for more then ten.  My first 500 MB (!) hard drive that could handle audio was USD$1000! My, how times have changed.
