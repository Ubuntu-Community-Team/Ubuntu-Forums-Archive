Yeah, that is impossible. If both your tv and computer have HDMI, that is the best way to do it since HDMI transmits high-def video and audio in the same cable.

If your TV has HDMI but your computer does not, then the next best thing would be a VGA to HDMI adapter. (like this one [http://www.overstock.com/Electronics/VGA-to-HDMI-Black-6-foot-M-M-Cable-Set-of-2/4668501/product.html?cid=202290&kid=9553000357392&track=pspla&kw={keyword}&adtype=pla]("http://www.overstock.com/Electronics/VGA-to-HDMI-Black-6-foot-M-M-Cable-Set-of-2/4668501/product.html?cid=202290&kid=9553000357392&track=pspla&kw={keyword}&adtype=pla") ) It will give you the best possible picture quality besides pure HDMI, but the VGA  output on your computer does not output audio so you will want to save your headphone to RCA cable for that.

If your TV also does not have an HDMI input, the next best thing after that would be a VGA  to component video adapter (like this, [http://www.amazon.com/VGA-RCA-Component-RGB-Cable/dp/B000FM3EQ0]("http://www.amazon.com/VGA-RCA-Component-RGB-Cable/dp/B000FM3EQ0") )

But if your TV only has RCA inputs, then I would get a VGA to RCA/s-video adapter like this ( [http://www.monoprice.com/products/product.asp?c_id=101&cp_id=10114&cs_id=1011407&p_id=4724&seq=1&format=2]("http://www.monoprice.com/products/product.asp?c_id=101&cp_id=10114&cs_id=1011407&p_id=4724&seq=1&format=2") ) But rca/s-video has the worst picture quality out of these options if your output resolution is over 640x480. So only use this option if you really have to. And if you do, I would just think about getting a new TV instead. lol

So, use the best adapter that will work for you, then your headphone to rca cable to hook up the sound from your computer up to your tv or stereo.

If you have any other questions, let me know the exact model of your tv and computer and I'll help you pick the best combination to use if you need it.
