### Post by FakeOutdoorsman on 2011-01-12
High Definition usually refers to the resolution of the video. The most common frame sizes are 1280x720 and 1920x1080, but a video doesn't have to be those exact sizes to be considered HD. Re-encoding a standard definition video to high definition is not generally recommended. Doing this won't magically make it look good either. Instead, you can use your player to upscale the video if you want it to play larger than it's native resolution.

libx264 is a video encoder, but it isn't required to encode a video to HD. You can encode a video with just about any encoder as long as it, or something else along the process, is able to resize or scale the image.

Why do you want "HD" video?  What are you trying to do?
