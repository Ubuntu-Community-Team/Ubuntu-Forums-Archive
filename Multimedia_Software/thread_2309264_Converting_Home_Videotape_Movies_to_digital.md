### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-01-09
How do you put a video tape into the computer? :D

You need a device which goes between the VCR and the computer to connect the VCR's audio/video outputs to; [a PCI video capture card]("https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yp-wp4Hx16Y") which fits into a slot on the motherboard (which were 'high end' but seem to be rarer now as VHS is obsolete) and gives you inputs/outputs at the back of your computer, or a [USB dongle which has a bunch of input/outputs you can use]("http://www.ebay.com.au/bhp/usb-video-converter?rmvSB=true").

Probably the easiest and cheapest way out is a USB video capture device. Keep in mind you need the hardware to handle this, particularly editing, but sounds like you do a bit of AV so you are aware of that fact. ;)

Hope that helps.

### Post by FakeOutdoorsman on 2016-01-09
I'm currently digitizing about 250 VHS tapes. One thing to look out for is mold. You can see it on the tape itself, and the stuff I've seen is white. You should avoid those or do those last because any following tapes can get infected by it.

Make sure to clean the heads in the VCR first before you start. There are several videos on YouTube that have decent instructions.

Avoid a VCR that displays when it is tracking, otherwise it will become part of the captured video. I'm using a Mitsubishi HS-U448 that does not have the annoying tracking OSD, and a Toshiba that does. However, the Toshiba can play the few SVHS tapes that show up.
