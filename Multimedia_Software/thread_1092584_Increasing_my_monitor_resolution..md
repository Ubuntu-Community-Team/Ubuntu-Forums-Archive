### Post by saxofoner on 2009-03-10
Your monitor has a resolution limit that will apply to any graphics card and any OS.  *Usually* you can tell this by looking at the highest option.  Look in System > Preferences > Screen Resolution and poke around with those settings.  

If you are in fact looking to upgrade to a more compatible graphics card, any Nvidia card will do.  Nvidia (until very recently) has named all of their cards like this:
NVidia Xxxx, i.e. Nvidia Geforce 5200, or 6800, or 6600, or 8600.  The second digit gives you relative quality whereas the first number tells you what series it is.  So any 8xxx series card is better than any 5xxx series card, but a 6800 might be better than a 7600.  That might be too much information :)  but if you're not into any serious graphics stuff, an old 6xxx series card will be fine.  You can get a 6600 pretty cheaply these days, probably under $50 if you look.
