### Post by syntaxerror74 on 2014-05-17
This was a tough task, which I just solved myself by coincidence. Perhaps it will help others, too.
I'm on Lubuntu Trusty.

At first, I was wondering why my audio output would never respond to my changes in volume control slider (attached on panel), until I learned that the volume changes would only be audible in my *onboard* audio (which I didn't want to turn off in BIOS for the time being since I would have to reboot the machine again).
But how can I control the volume of my *hardware* audio card?

Well, it's dead simple: add a SECOND volume slider to the panel!!
And optionally, remove the first one, letting the originally second one take the place of the first one.

There might be a simpler way to assign a certain device to these volume control applets, but at least in the applet settings I couldn't find any way to do so.
