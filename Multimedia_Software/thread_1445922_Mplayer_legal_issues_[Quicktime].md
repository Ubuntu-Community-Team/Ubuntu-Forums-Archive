### Post by skullmunky on 2010-04-07
You could also try contacting the Electronic Frontier Foundation.  They may be able to give you some advice, and possibly better advice than your University's legal department since not everyone's equally familiar with the nuances of the patent issue here.  Like they say over on Groklaw, (who also might be able to give some advice), IANAL, but I'm sure that you would not be the only university using the codecs you're asking about.  The first issue I imagine has to do with the potential balance of liability between the distributor of the code and the end-user, the second with whether there is precedent relating to this use.  I'm not actually aware of a case where Apple or other codec patent holders have sued linux users or businesses for using another implementation of a codec - meaning that even if there is potential liability it's untested.  In other patent cases, the ones I can think of tend to be patent holder vs. someone manufacturing and selling and a device or program, rather than patent holder vs. end user.  This is different from the scorched-earth legal policy of the MPAA and RIAA, which is simply to criminalize everyone on the planet ;).
