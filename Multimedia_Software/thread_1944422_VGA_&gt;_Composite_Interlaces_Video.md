### Post by wyliecoyoteuk on 2012-03-21
It does say on the info for the converter "supports interlace formats" which is the default for most composite devices.
For £10, that is probably all you will get.
These units are usually for output to an older CRT device.
I think that what you need is a scandoubler or "flicker fixer".
These devices have more complex electronics and cost considerably more.
The screen may only support interlaced input on composite.
