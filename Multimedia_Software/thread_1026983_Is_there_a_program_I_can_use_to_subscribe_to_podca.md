### Post by Starbuck13 on 2009-01-01
Try a program called "gPodder". It's listed in the regular Ubuntu repositories, but I'd recommend the newer version from the gPodder website here:

[http://www.gpodder.org/downloads.html](http://www.gpodder.org/downloads.html)

Very nice little program, and if you go under the "Subscriptions" menu, you'll find the option to "Search for Podcasts" which will give you access to an ok number of different podcasts. The list of podcasts you'll be looking at comes from an included .opml file---it's a kind of specially formatted list of available podcasts. gPodder does let you go find your own .opml file out there on the internet and import it, thus giving you a bigger, better selection of podcasts. 

This link:

[http://www.digitalpodcast.com/opml.php](http://www.digitalpodcast.com/opml.php) 

kind of explains .opml files, and you should be able to just google "opml directory" or "opml file" and get something decent. Once you find it, just go to that "Subscriptions" menu again and choose "Import a OPML File", it's pretty easy.

Otherwise, I find it's best to just go to an online Podcast directory and browse there. Just find the podcst you want, and copy the url, then go to the "Subscriptions" menu and choose "Subscribe to new podcast url". Here's a few of the directories I like:

[http://www.podcastdirectory.com/](http://www.podcastdirectory.com/)
[http://www.learnoutloud.com/Home](http://www.learnoutloud.com/Home)
[http://www.npr.org/rss/podcast/podcast_directory.php](http://www.npr.org/rss/podcast/podcast_directory.php)

And as always, don't forget to use your friendly neighborhood Google to find individual podcasts you might be interested in. Hope this helps!
