### Post by cotcot on 2012-03-20
You need an editor able to deal with audio and video on separated tracks.

If you do not have experience with video editors I suggest to use kdenlive (easier to learn). If you imoort the video audio and video will be combined in 1 track. You can split it as explained [[COLOR="Red"]here[/COLOR]]("http://www.linuceum.com/Desktop/KDEnliveAudio.php"). Then you can cut and paste video and audio tracks separately. Cut and paste the audio track of the english version on a new track. Same for the good quality video and render them (after deleting the original tracks).

If you are more confortable with editors you could use Blender VSE. Blender imports video an audio on separate tracks.

There are other editors as well but I limit my advise to those where I have myself experience with doing comparable jobs.
