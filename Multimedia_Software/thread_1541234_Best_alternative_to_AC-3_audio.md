### Post by watupgroupie on 2010-07-28
I recently bought a WD HD TV Media Player and I'm trying to use it with my movie collection. The video section of it is all fine, but I'm having issues with the audio. In order to use 6 ch AC-3 audio it requires the use of a digital audio cable to a receiver. Since this isn't always an option when I'm away from home I want it to work without it. The only way this is achievable is with stereo sound. 

I'm wondering what the best alternative would be. I was thinking AAC but it's annoying to handle. I'd also like to shy away from the use of AviDemux because it tags files and I don't want them tagged and the window never properly shows when using a higher res video. Suggestions?

### Post by watupgroupie on 2010-07-29
Fixed my problem. I decided to just go ahead and use AAC. I finally fixed my issues I was having with it earlier. I would take the AC-3 audio and run it through MPlayer to convert it to wav, and then use the nero encoder. But MPlayer didn't like any of my higher bitrate AC-3 files so I didn't know what to do. 

I looked into FFMpeg and found that it's much better at converting. After finding the guide available on the forums for installing all of the restricted extras I was good to go. Works perfectly and has less steps now too :)
