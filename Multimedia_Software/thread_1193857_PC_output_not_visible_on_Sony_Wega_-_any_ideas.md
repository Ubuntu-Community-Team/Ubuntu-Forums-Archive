### Post by Clochard on 2009-06-22
I have a [Sony Wega]("http://esupport.sony.com/CA/perl/model-documents.pl?mdl=KV34XBR800&LOC=3") TV that has a DVI-D input option.  I've hooked up my DVR to it using a DVI-D to DVI-D cable.  I've ensured that the TV is set to display that input.  However when I try to view anything on that input, it does not show anything.

On the PC (running Jaunty) I've installed the 180 NVidia drivers and used nvidia-settings to confirm that the TV is being detected.  In fact the nvidia-settings automatically detected that the TV was a Sony, and that its native resolution was 1920 by 1080.

So obviously the TV is sending some info to the PC.  However it is not displaying the input.

The manual states the following regarding that input:

> DVI-HDTV - Can accommodate a copy-protected digital connection (HDCP*) to other devices (such as digital set-top boxes) that have compatible interfaces. The DVI-HDTV input terminal is compliant with the EIA-861 standard and is not intended for use with personal computers. See the instruction manual that came with your equipment for details about connecting and using it with the TV.


I'm not really sure what the EIA-861 standard is, but a google search showed me a few other people asking the same question and not getting any answers other than "It works for me" and "It doesn't work for me".

Well it obviously "kinda works for me" as the OS can see the TV brand attached and its native resolution.  If there anyone with any ideas of how to troubleshoot this, or even try to fix this?
