### Post by poikiloid on 2009-09-24
a program i use (Anki) uses mplayer as its slave audio player in the Windows/Debian version (not sure what Mac version uses). with anki, i listen to very short audio files in quick succession.

this leads to a terribly annoying experience, because mplayer (and totem, but not rhythmbox) produces an audio artifact at (or near) the end, and usually the beginnings, of each audio file. When I play the files using rhythmbox, the artifact is not heard, and when I play the files on a Mac, it is not heard. So, it is not part of the files, but is added by the player software. And it's not a problem with anki cause it's heard when played outside of anki.

You might describe the artifact as a blip, a beat, a skip, etc. And it becomes very distracting when listening to many files one after the other.
