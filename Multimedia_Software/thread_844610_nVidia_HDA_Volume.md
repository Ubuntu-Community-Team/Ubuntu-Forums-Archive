Hi all,

An experiment for anyone with a (Linux/Windows) dual boot system:-

1.  Play some music on your Linux machine that has a nVidia HDA soundcard.  Set the volume to max on PC and adjust speaker volume until comfortable.

2.  Reboot into Windows and play music.  Make sure you have toilet roll to hand as you may c**p yourself!!!

Why is the volume SOOO much louder on Windows than on Linux? 

The codec is Realtek ALC883 and the chip is a MCP61 apparently. I've read many posts about systems that do not work with this chipset, but mine does - although quietly.  I've tried a few 'model=' options to the module but without much success as there isn't one specific to my motherboard (abit NF-M2S) and have put all mixer sliders to max.

This has been with a 32 and 64 bit version of Ubuntu (and I even tried Gentoo a while ago!!).  Seems to me to be a kernel developers problem and not Ubuntu specific, but I have to ask somewhere!

I'm using the PC as my TV at the moment and with all volume turned full it's usable as long as I'm a couple of metres away, but no further.

Has anyone had any success in getting reasonable volume from this chipset?
