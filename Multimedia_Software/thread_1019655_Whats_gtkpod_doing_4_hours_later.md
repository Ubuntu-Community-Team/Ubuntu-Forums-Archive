### Post by PDA1 on 2011-12-03
For me GtkPod is a love & hate situation.  I mostly hate it.  I've spend countless days just trying to get it installed and usually have to fall back on the old .99 version.

The gtkpod help list isn't really that at all.  

Face it folks- if GtkPod was that great of a program it would work on all computers upon installation....which should be easy.  But, it's not.  No amount of talk or pointing fingers at the one installing the program can excuse a poorly designed program that doesn't make it simple to install.  Only arrogance upon the programmers part frustrates and hinders the development of a good program for public use and one that's very much needed for iPod use in Linux. 

Most iPod users are chained to that wretched iTunes and resultingly- Window$.  My iPod has around 4,500 songs and videos on it so I need a program that'll handle it.

The soul that invents a program that can easily import and export music and videos off of an iPod will be a millionaire in a year.  A vertical display- left side of the screen is Local Music files.  The right side of the screen is the iPod playlists.  Drop and drag the stuff around that you need moved or removed from your iPod.

GtkPod is very useful in moving music and video files around on an iPod- it does it very well.  But lets see you try and copy the music or videos FROM you iPod TO you hard drive!  And a program that doesn't put Playlists in alphabetic order is just bad.

I have found that Guayadeque is an excellent program for iPod management.  It is very good and the support is very helpful and quick.  Why anyone would use Banshee is beyond me.
