### Post by Theredbaron1834 on 2011-07-26
iTunes is a both a music player, a media store, a ipod synce, ect.

Now, I don't have an ipod/phone, but I do believe that it works with banshee, since it depends on libgpod4, and that comes with modern Ubuntu. And Rythmbox is the old one, and ipods at least work with it too. And while I liked rythmbox, I really love banshee. I was using it before Ubuntu changed.
Amarok, I know, works for iphones with firmware 1.x. If you have a 2.x firmware phone, you need to jailbrake it, and then exit a file on it, as apple really WANTS you to only use iTunes.

So, as long as they don't use an iphone, and keep it updated, there are plenty of choices. Among those are the allready mentioned Banshee, Rythmnbox, and Amarok, as well as things like LXplayer, Audacious, Exaile, Listen, Gnome Music Player, Sonata, and DeaDBeeF. There are even some nice ones that you use a term for, of which my favourite is called Cmus. Most of the gui ones can sync mp3 players, at least non ipods ones, don't know about that. Cmus is just a music player, but a great low cpu one. So good for a crappy comp, or as a background music player when doing cpu extensive stuff.

O, and since songbird is opnesource, people are porting it aka [Nightingale.]("http://getnightingale.com/")


O, and I just checked banshee with both a hacked 4.3.3 (my brothers :) ), and a straight 4.2.1 (my sisters :) ) and they both worked to copy music to it. So I guess that banshee works fine with them, and I bet most others do too, since they all use libgpod to transfer music back and forth.
