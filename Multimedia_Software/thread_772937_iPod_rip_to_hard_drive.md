### Post by kostkon on 2008-04-28
> **marimbaday said:**
> I've used Listen and Banshee, not exactly an exhaustive list, for certain.  I'm just wanting to play it safe, because it's my roommate's iPod and she'd rather I not break it any time soon.
Then you must be extremely careful, indeed! 

You could try to use [*gtkpod*]("http://www.gtkpod.org/") or [*Floola*]("http://www.floola.com/"). The first you will find it in the repos and the latter you can download it from its site or [*getdeb.net*]("http://getdeb.net/").

I can easily transfer files to the hard disk with *Floola*, which is the application I use for my *iPod* management.

The only risk, although small, that exists is the possibility of something going wrong when one of these programs will try to import the *iTunes* library of the *iPod*.

It would be better for you to avoid adding or removing files from the *iPod*, i.e. modifying the library, in order to avoid anything going wrong when your friend will try afterwards to access the *iPod* with *iTunes*.

Although, I believe the best and safest option would be to ask your friend to copy the files to his/her PC and then to some other medium (DVDs, flash stick) that will then give to you.

EDIT: by the way, what model and generation is the *iPod*?
