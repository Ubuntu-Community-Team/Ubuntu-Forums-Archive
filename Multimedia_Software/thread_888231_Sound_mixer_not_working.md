### Post by jslick on 2008-08-13
The volume dial probably adjusts a different channel than you're master volume.  Maybe your volume dial adjusts PCM, PCM Front, or Front instead of Master volume.

Yes, you need to turn on the tone switch to use the bass/treble controls.  Although I would advise against this if you can help it.  Turning up the bass affects ALL six speakers.  Bass wears down speakers faster.  Keep your tone controls at default and just adjust your subwoofer control.  Your mixer might have an LFE channel (Low frequency effects) that boosts bass - use this.  My 5.1 control has knobs for sub and master volume.

If you hear crackling your mixer is pre-amping the bass too much and is probably distorting the sound.  Use the controls on your physical control if you have them.
