### Post by steev182 on 2010-10-04
From reading the thread on Guayadeque, there aren't many plans for video support, which is a bit of a shame.

But that in mind, I don't see anything that is able to match iTunes for it's video library management.

What I would like is something that can manage my TV Shows, Movies, Video Podcasts across my devices.

I wouldn't worry too much about it being a player, VLC is awesome for that, but I would like it to record playcounts across the devices.

Banshee was ok, but the last time I used that, there was no difference between Movies and TV Shows still. I would like to know on my TV what I haven't watched yet, or what is new. Then once it has been watched, a universal playcount has been updated, perhaps even recording the point it has stopped so it can be continued from that point on another device later.

This may be beyond the scope of some things, and maybe the current devices that can connect to uPNP servers (PS3/xbox 360) wouldn't be able to update playcounts or even read them.

Apple does do some things well, maybe Boxee or XBMC could find a way of porting a player to Android - I don't know. All I know is I would love something that can handle media for several devices.
