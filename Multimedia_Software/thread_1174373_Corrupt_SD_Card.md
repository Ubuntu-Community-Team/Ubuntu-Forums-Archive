### Post by 73ckn797 on 2009-05-30
Do you have another SD card to make sure the camera has not malfunctioned?

I would think that if the computer cannot see the card then it may be permanently failed. I use a digital camera daily (100+ photos a day) and recall only once that a similar issue developed.

I know that you do not want to lose any photos but see if a reformat of the card can be performed by the camera. If not then it is time to purchase another SD card.

_**Unrelated:**_ I have from time to time not been able to copy files from the SD card due to some unknown cause making the data read only upon plugging into my computer. My situation has me immediately downloading the images to my laptop but I save the images in a zipped file back to the SD card to later move to my desktop. That is where the read only situation come up.

I have had one SD card become unusable due to the amount of use I put them through.

Some where I have read that flash sticks do corrupt after a while. Something to do with being written to so frequently. Reminds me of how the old 3.5 floppy disks would corrupt after a while of a lot of use.

My problems have been that I have used/worn out 5 digital cameras in 14 years.
