### Post by laceration on 2009-10-21
All the hype over internet tv is absurd imho. The satellite stuff I've seen looks as credible as a Nigerian banking deal. There is a lot of bs out there.  There are only 2 things that are even close to good and have any kind of selection.  Hulu and bitorrent.

I laugh though that people miss the obvious that over the air and unencrypted cable are the best source for TV.  The only downside is that they are not new and shiny like the internet.  With a pci card or usb device you can stream it right into your computer.  I record high definition on my hard drive and watch it on my big monitor using a remote control.  I've got a sweet setup with OSTV.

### Post by laceration on 2009-10-21
The thing is with Satellite and cable is that they totally lock up their content.  There is no way you will ever get their stuff on your computer or without their locked down hardware.  I find it quite ironic that you have these people in open source linux with mythtv using ir blasters with cable boxes.  I think Microsoft looks open and free next to Cable/Satellite.

The internet does not do TV well, except for maybe Hulu, but it is lower quality and they make you wait an extra week for shows.  The best thing you can do is get the Over the Air or unencrypted cable.  That is basically free open source TV and it never stops with buffering...buffering... messages!  CSPAN is unencrypted cable and is a great place for non-dumbed down viewpoints.
