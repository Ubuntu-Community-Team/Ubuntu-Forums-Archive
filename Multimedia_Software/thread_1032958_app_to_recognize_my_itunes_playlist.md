### Post by Shwefty on 2009-01-06
I use Banshee.  As I'm running a Gnome desktop, I don't want all of the dependencies that come with the KDE based Amarok (which is also popular).

Banshee recognizes .m4a's (can't play .m4p's though...nothing can except an authenticated iTunes [within the realm of legality]).  All you have to do in Banshee is select to Import Media from a folder, navigate the folder once in which all your iTunes music is in (even on your Windows partition), click on that folder, hit open, let the magic happen.  It'll import all of your songs/tags(metadata) and even download album cover art.
