### Post by lovinglinux on 2010-03-22
I don't know if it is a solution for you, since you might need to distribute the video by other means, but YouTube has a video annotation feature that works great and exactly the way you want. Additionally, it allows you to change the annotations any time you want, since they are not hardcoded into the video.

For example, see [this demo video](http://lovinglinux.megabyet.net/?p=441) on my blog.
