### Post by SeijiSensei on 2010-12-20
My daughter has a Sansa that she manages with Amarok. They're very Linux-compatible and even support uncommon formats like FLAC and ogg.  For use in a gym, you should investigate the [Sansa Clip]("http://www.sandisk.com/products/sansa-music-and-video-players/sandisk-sansa-clip-mp3-players.aspx").  It has an 8GB model.  Here are [some reviews](http://www.amazon.com/Sandisk-Sansa-Clip-Player-Black/product-reviews/B001JIDEXW/ref=dp_top_cm_cr_acr_txt?ie=UTF8&showViewpoints=1).

You can also just connect devices like these with a USB cable, and they'll appear as a mass storage device.

### Post by wkulecz on 2010-12-20
For the gym I have a 4GB "no name" player from Fry's that is basically a large USB stick with an earphone jack and an AAA battery.  It mounts like any other USB memory stick and I just copy the song directories there via drag and drop and it just plays through them.  I just want music, no futzing around with playlits or navigation.  I think I gave like $20 for it last year.  I'm sure they have 8GB ones now.

Just stay away form the "Centon" brand -- I had two before I got my money back and they all had silent data errors (file transfer completed error free, but the file is corrupt!) even when  copying the files using Windows!
