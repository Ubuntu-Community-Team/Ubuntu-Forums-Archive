### Post by AaronP_ on 2009-04-07
Something in your desktop environment is changing the resolution as you're logging in, overriding the 1280x1024 default you configured.  This is typically a problem with GNOME's screen resolution tool.

### Post by dbtruitt on 2009-04-07
thx AaronP_!!! This was just the clue I needed:

And Ringi, your reply to that, "Yes check if you have same resolution in Screen and Graphic and Nvidia settings.", just what I needed.

While the NVIDIA X Server Settings was updating the xorg.conf correctly, the GNOME desktop was setting it back, all I had to do was go to:

System -> Preferences -> Screen Resolution

And set it higher.  This was the very first place I went to increase resolution, but at that time the max resolution it allowed was 1024x768.  Then, after nvidia-settings allowed me to bump that max up to 1280x1024, this is now available to choose in "Screen Resolution" :)  But I had gotten so wrapped up in NVIDIA settings, I forgot about the GNOME desktop setting...too many places :)
