### Post by jason0x21 on 2011-01-09
I've got a fresh install of 10.10 with the mythbuntu and mediabuntu repos added in.  When I first tried out 10.10, I noticed that I was no longer able to monitor a recording I was making (in audacity).  That was fine, I could just look at the sound levels in the meter in the "Sound Preferences" dialog to make sure things were working right.

This morning, the levels were really low in meter in Sound Preferences, and in audacity.  I worked with the "Input Level" slider, but it would only appear to "peak out" and distort at a very low level.  The Sound Preferences meter would get "pinned" at the third "light" or so and never move.

Then I adjusted the "Output Volume" slider at the top of the "Sound Preferences" dialog.  Suddenly the Input Level went up!  Fiddling around with Input Level and Output Volume got me to a place where the the input was reasonably high without distorting.

But, needless to say, this seems really really not the way it should work. I do have a handful of audio devices, but I'm using the basic motherboard line in here.  I've attached the alsa info output, just in case.
