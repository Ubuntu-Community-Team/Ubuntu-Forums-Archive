### Post by Donalb on 2009-03-31
Somewhere yesterday while surfing around I saw an article "5 reasons why I love Songbird".
So today I was running Songbird for the first time in about a week (I installed it about a month ago).
And this rejoinder to the above came to me, just from a few hours use.

8 Reasons for NOT Using Songbird;

1:Web integration; Utterly unnecessary and adds bloat. Why fight the Fox? Who wakes and thinks "you know what Linux needs? A music player with built-in browser". Wouldn't ONE, just one, fully featured music player and library manager for Linux be better, instead of all the half-arsed solutions we have? (Yes I've tried: Rhythmbox, Banshee, Exaile, gmusicbrowser, Songbird, Audacity, Amarok and whatever else is available that I can't remember right now).

2: Addons which are really unstable but still "recommended".
Examples: Firetray (can take up multiple screens, author doesn't know how to fix it) to give applet controls. 
A buggy Now Playing applet needed instead of letting you know what your queue is. And an add-on that deleted the data before it got pulled. 

3: Missing basic expected functionality. Check out the Songbird (crappy) forums to see users clamouring for what they consider basic functionality being told otherwise by developers.
E.g. a Now Playing queue is considered "extra" despite hundreds (majority) of users saying otherwise.
or a mini-player. 

4:Instability. Crashes. Still crashes. I installed at ver 1.0 and immediately uninstalled. Reinstalled at 1.1 Better but still  regular file/playing errors.  

5:Very poor metadata and tag handling. Can't tag->filename and vice versa. Can't (properly) flat-edit tracks (i.e right click song name, try to edit and the whole name disappears. 
Starting Songbird 1.1 with a very mature (used for years) library I still had to go back and re-edit 100's of tags. Tags I had fully fixed a long time ago. And I'm not talking about stuff like rating that's handled differently. Artists/Titles/Albums. Basic stuff.

6: Play engine not knowing what it's doing. Hard to explain. Say you're dragging files from library to Now Playing (add-on) and changing order..seems to confuse Songbird so that regularly Songbird is playing one song, but displaying different artist/track data.

7: The Songbird Forums, which suck big time in layout. Esp. for people used to the standard of other OSS forums and the Gold Standard of Ubuntu Forums. Navigation is awful. Dispay is poor and skewed to white space instead of information.

8: I'm with the many Linux users who are frustrated by the lack of a really good music manager. I know some are running Media Monkey (THE Standard) in Wine but it didn't work for me. Songbird isn't it either.

Donal
