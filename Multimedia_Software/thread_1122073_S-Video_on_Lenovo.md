### Post by hampton49 on 2009-04-20
It happened to me and I got it solved with a staple!

Get a pressed staple from a stapler and connect the two top pins of one side of the S-Video cable with it, getting its curved sides to go around them (it takes several tries to get it right ans the staple musn't be too large). Then have the staple rest on the bottom of the connector and plug it into your TV or other device. The colors should be back.

I haven't been using this for a long time so there's a chance it's not the top pins that need to be connected but the bottom ones. I just recall they were the furthest ones apart from each other.

This can sound as a pretty cheap workaround but it worked for me when I needed it so hopefully it will help.
