### Post by cbanakis on 2012-07-02
I assume the speakers on the monitors connect using a standard 3.5mm (headphone) plug?

If they are USB, then I have no idea.

If they are 3.5mm, you can find all that you need at radio shack.

There you can get adapters to make the single stereo output on your sound card, into 2 mono outputs (left and right)

Then you can reverse the process by having the mono output go to a mono to stereo adapter, then into the monitor.

There is probably a way to do the center channel too, but you might need a lot of adapters :)

There may be a software solution too, but I doubt you could get it setup exactly how you want it.

### Post by cbanakis on 2012-07-02
Get 1 of these...
[http://www.bestbuy.com/site/eForCity+-+1/8+3.5mm+Mini+Plug+to+2+RCA+Stereo+Audio+Cable+-+6Ft+-+Black/4595818.p?id=1218492483208&skuId=4595818&st=rca%20cable&cp=3&lp=10]("http://www.bestbuy.com/site/eForCity+-+1/8+3.5mm+Mini+Plug+to+2+RCA+Stereo+Audio+Cable+-+6Ft+-+Black/4595818.p?id=1218492483208&skuId=4595818&st=rca%20cable&cp=3&lp=10")
(plug in into your sound card)

Then get 2 of these...
[http://www.bestbuy.com/site/Dynex%26%23153%3B+-+RCA+Y+Audio+Adapter/9784802.p?id=1218174133750&skuId=9784802&st=rca%20splitter&cp=1&lp=10](http://www.bestbuy.com/site/Dynex%26%23153%3B+-+RCA+Y+Audio+Adapter/9784802.p?id=1218174133750&skuId=9784802&st=rca%20splitter&cp=1&lp=10)
(use them to convert your 2 plugs into 4, so there are 2 left's and 2 right's)

Then 2 of these...
[http://www.bestbuy.com/site/Hosa+Technology+-+3.5mm+TRS-to-Dual+RCAF+Cable/8559945.p?id=1208562112770&skuId=8559945](http://www.bestbuy.com/site/Hosa+Technology+-+3.5mm+TRS-to-Dual+RCAF+Cable/8559945.p?id=1208562112770&skuId=8559945)
(Use them to convert your 2 left rca's into a stereo 3.5mm jack, and plug it into your left monitor)
repeat for right monitor.

This is assuming your monitors have a 3.5mm input jack.

You can obviously mix and match to come up with something you like more.

But you should get the concept from the above.

If you want to, you can get the center channel setup working too, but you would need more adapters.

I do not think all this can be accomplished with software, or at lease not plausibly.

But I am pretty confident that my mess of adapters will do the trick.
