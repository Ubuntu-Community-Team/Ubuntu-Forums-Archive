### Post by ReplicateThis on 2010-09-14
I'm a relatively new user and still learning my way around, but I believe upon installation Ubuntu has open-source drivers that give limited functionality to your graphics card, including some rudimentary 3D acceleration.  The proprietary drivers are there for folks who require more than the basic functionality of the open-source drivers, or who prefer to use the card manufacturers' own drivers for whatever reason.  I believe ultimately, though, that the choice is yours.  You just need to figure out which gives you the better bang for your buck on your particular system...

As to removing the card, most motherboards have some kind of integrated graphics that would probably work, but you may loose the eye-candy (like Compiz and fancy themes) that the 3D card gives you.
