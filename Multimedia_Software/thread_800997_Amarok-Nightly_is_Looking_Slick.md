### Post by VanHammersly on 2008-05-20
I have been very curious about the progression of Amarok 2.0  Installing beta software (or alpha in this case) is one of my guilty pleasures.  When the nightly build of Amarok was announced, I downloaded and installed it right away.  

Amarok is one of those programs I embrace, get curious about other media programs, try something new for a little while (Rhythmbox when it gets a facelift, Banshee, and Listen), and then come always back to Amarok.  With so many options for media applications, its nice to have choices.  One of the best qualities of the state of Linux right now is the many application choices available to individuals to meet their needs (email, multimedia, web browsers, word processing, etc.).  I say this as a criminal defense attorney in the U.S. who uses Ubuntu to run my practice.

I am curious about what others think about where Amarok is headed.  Personally, I am very impressed with the new Amarok and really like the direction it is heading.  I think the layout is much cleaner, more intuitive and visually appealling.  I like right hand playlist section and the small thumbnail with album art.  It's a nice little touch that really spruces up the playlist.  It feels snappier to me than the current Amarok and integrates well with my desktop (I am using a modified gnome desktop powered by Hardy).

Having said this, I have had my share of problems (stalls on loading the library and the program simply not starting).  Of course, it is alpha and this is to be expected.
