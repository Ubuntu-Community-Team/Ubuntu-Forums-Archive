### Post by hazelnut on 2010-03-17
The original dvd will always look better, but you don't want to store 6 to 8 gig files on your hard drive.  

I've been using ffmpeg to encode to h264 in mp4 container with -crf 20 or 21.  I get files around the size of 800MiB to 1.2GiB depending on the length of the movie.  I also encode at the original video resolution of 720x480/1.777 aspect or 720x352/2.35 aspect.  My videos at that rate factor (crf 20-21) look VERY close to the original dvd and look really nice on my 46 inch Sony Bravo.  They're not HD, but they do look nice.
