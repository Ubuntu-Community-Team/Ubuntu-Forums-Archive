> **xzero1 said:**
> Sounds great. Will we be able to tweak the mplayer command line?

No. It won't open the standalone player, it will replace the flash player with a mplayer plugin on the web page, after it loads. So it will play embedded as it should. Take a look at the [screenshot ](http://picasaweb.google.com/lh/photo/Jd4lAzbQbqq1QOlt9YwvXv9pzu9gv55MCOY_qJ5NDAA?feat=embedwebsite).

> **xzero1 said:**
> Will flash need to be installed?

For now, yes. But you can block it with NoScript, so when my extension kicks in you just need to allow the script and enjoy the low CPU video streaming.

No. I have plans to port some of my extensions to Chrome, but that will take time and I'm not sure will be even possible.
