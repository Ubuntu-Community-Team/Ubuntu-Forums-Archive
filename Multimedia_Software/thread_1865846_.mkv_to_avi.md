### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2011-10-20
You should post the complete ffmpeg cmd & std output up to where it starts into the progress/status..

mkv & avi (& mpeg4) are containers.
avi is deprecated & does not support modern video compression/codecs.
avi does not support out-of-order frames or B frames or variable bitrate.
avi leads to AV sync & seeking problems.

I suspect your mkv file contains video stream compressed with some type of mpeg4 codec (DivX, Xvid ASP-VC1, H246AVC)'
(mpeg4 can be a container type & AV codec types)

It is possible ffpmeg makes the decision to not vcopy into a AVI but remuxes/transcodes to no B frame/in-order/fixed rate.

It would make sense to determine the exact capability of the target playback device as avi container does not.
