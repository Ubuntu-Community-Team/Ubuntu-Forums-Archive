### Post by chris1379 on 2013-12-31
I've seen lots of discussion on this but I'd like to include some specifics of how I use ACDSee that make it so hard to replace. I have many pictures from several drives and even some on network shares. I don't want to put them all in one place. I use Windows Explorer to find them and then click on one photo. This opens ACDSee. From there I can click page up/down to view other pictures, ESC to exit, or Enter to open the thumbnail viewer. Right now I am in the process of finding duplicates and organizing some folders. With ACDSee, I can compare 2 folders (including subfolders) or search duplicates within a folder. I'll probably do some batch renaming too. I rarely do any editing in the program as there are better programs for that. I might do a quick crop to post something on Facebook but that's about it. I just tried digiKam but it tried to move everything into my home folder and completely filled my Ubuntu hard drive.
