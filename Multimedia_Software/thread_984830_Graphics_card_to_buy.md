### Post by Kubunteando on 2009-01-16
The differences are huge.
Those bits refer to the memory bandwidth. So 256 bits means at least 4 times more memory bandwidth which makes a huge difference when executing 3D applications or Games. It can be extrapolated (not directly) to around 4 times more FPS in Games.

I bought a 4550 because I wanted low consumption since I plan to leave the computer on long hours. But on the other hand I can play Games at mid resolution and not all of them. I can play Quake Wars at mid resolutions but I cannot play Crysis.

If you want gaming the 4850 is a good option. If you want low consumption the 4550 is the best option.

Also you can consider the 4650 or 4670 which are 128 bits and are between the 4550 and the 4850.
