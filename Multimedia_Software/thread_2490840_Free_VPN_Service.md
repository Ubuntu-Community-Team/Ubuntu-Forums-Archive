### Post by rudratrivedi on 2023-09-20
They do have a paid service, which is how they're able to subsidise the Free version (which has access to only 3 countries and is usually high on load). I used the free version for a while, and started paying for Premium about a  ago.
Works great, has native WireGuard support and supports p2p and port forwarding for those Linux ISO downloads too.
Also allows you to download config files so you're not locked into their client.

As for the company itself, founded by CERN scientists, Swiss based. You can read their privacy policy [here]("https://protonvpn.com/privacy-policy") and decide for yourself if you trust their claims.
