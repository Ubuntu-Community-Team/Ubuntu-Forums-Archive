### Post by Sven6210 on 2009-10-20
Hello, I am facing a comparable problem. Actually the history of my problem:

1. Ubuntu 9.04 in the beginning
In July I installed Ubuntu 9.04 and I had trouble to get the microphone to work at all. After spending many hours I found the right settings. Actually I had to update ALSA, change the ALSA configuration file and some other file.

After all these changes everything worked very well and I had excellent quality using Skype and Ekiga. I travelled a lot in September and had no communication problems either from one continent to another.

2. Ubuntu 9.04 after an update
After I installed some automatically suggested updates in Ubuntu my microphone stopped again working. I redid all the changes I did before but somehow I did not get the old quality back. Especially Ekiga gave me trouble but also Skype was not good enough to be used for telephone calls

3. Ubuntu 9.10 Beta
On 11th October I gave Ubuntu 9.10 a try. I only needed to make one update in the ALSA configuration file to get a good quality of the microphone. Then I installed Skype and Ekiga.

Skype: works O.K., I can make phone and Skype calls however my voice gets interrupted sometimes. It is like using a mobile with bad reception but at least it is usable.

Ekiga: does not work, the quality is too bad and my voice gets interrupted too often, not good enough for a decent conversation


However it is not a microphone problem any more, at least the sound recorder of Ubuntu records my voice very clearly and without problem. I do not really know where to look further. If anybody has a good idea I am very open for it.
