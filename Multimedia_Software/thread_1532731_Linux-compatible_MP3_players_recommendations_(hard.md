### Post by jimhenry on 2010-07-16
My Sony Walkman NWZ-E436F seems to be on its last legs, its battery being erratic about recharging the last couple of times.  My initial inclination is to buy another instance of the same model, since it's worked pretty well for me, but I thought I'd fish for recommendations here first.  What other digital music players support Linux well (i.e., automatically mount as a USB flash drive when plugged in without special setup and don't require proprietary software to update some database or index) and have at least as good a feature set as the Walkman?  Specifically, I'm looking for:


[LIST]
[*]at least 4GB memory
[*]can fast-forward and rewind at two different speeds
[*]has a good-sized display that shows track title, artist, etc. in about 10 or 12-pt type with good contrast
[*]can do a shuffle all that's a true shuffle, not "pick random next song which could easily be one of the last few songs played, or even the very song just played"
[*]can rewind from the beginning of one track into the last seconds of the previous track (useful when listening to audiobooks)
[/LIST]
Other features that would be nice to have:


[LIST]
[*]supporting formats other than mp3, esp. oga and wma (the latter of which is the only format in which many public library audiobooks are available)
[*]being able to bookmark a place in a podcast or audiobook chapter, go listen to music for a while, and come back to the same place in the podcast or book
[*]being able to do a "shuffle all" on a specific branch of the directory hierarchy (so I could e.g. put music albums into one hierarchy and audiobooks and podcasts into another hierarchy and not have random audiobook chapters and podcasts come up when shuffling music)
[*]FM radio, esp. with recording capability (the newer Walkman models support this, but I'm not sure it's worth the extra money)
[/LIST]
As for battery life and general durability, I bought this Walkman used from someone who'd owned it for a year, and it worked perfectly well for me for about seven more months; I've needed to recharge it about every five or six days, when listening to it about 2-4 hours a day on average.  Even now, it appears that it's going to continue working as a flash drive for a while after its battery no longer holds a charge well enough to play music.
