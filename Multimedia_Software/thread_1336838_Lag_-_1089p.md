### Post by VeeDubb on 2009-11-25
The problem is a complicated one.

In windows, video decoding is handled by your video card (duh, right?)

But in linux, this is not the default.

AFAIK, there is no way to make flash offload to the video card, which sucks because watching hulu in hi-res just sucks.

However, if you install Gnome MPlayer (not just regular MPlayer) you will see that you can go into the video preferences and one of the Video output options if "vdpau"

If you switch your video output to vdpau, your video card (assuming you have a "real" video card and not some sucky on-board intel chipset, will help decode the mp4, and then you'll be lag free.

You can also compile vlc with vdpau support, but the last time I checked (which, I admit, was a while ago) Totem (the default Ubuntu video player) and VLC were both lacking vdpau support.

### Post by gradinaruvasile on 2009-11-25
> **sombrancelha said:**
> My video card is a NVIDIA GeForce Go 6150.

I installed gnome-mplayer, but when I changed the video output to vdpau, it stoped playing the the video and played only the audio.

No joy with that one. VDPAU is supported from the 8 series upwards. But you can try this:

Press alt+f2, type gstreamer-properties
Go to the video tab, select Xwindow (X11/...) plugin, set  the Device to nv17 (or whatever number you have) video texture. This way you will have accelerated output. 

Not as good as VDPAU though. The good thing about VDPAU that it helps with decoding (transferring it to the GPU), not only 
painting the pixels on the screen.

Select xv as output device in xine/mplayer/vlc. Also i found mplayer from the command line to be the fastest player ever(syntax is: mplayer -ao alsa (or pulse) -vo xv (or vdpau,opengl, etc) moviename). Also you might try opengl/opengl2 output, but in my experience is not as stable as xv and not faster either if you have the output set to the nvXX texture.


Also, as general rule: no compiz (desktop effects) while playing movies.
