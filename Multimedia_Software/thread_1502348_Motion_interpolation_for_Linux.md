### Post by darkxman on 2010-06-05
After seeing an article for motion interpolation on Lifehacker ([http://lifehacker.com/5551295/enable-motion-interpolation-for-movies-on-your-pc](http://lifehacker.com/5551295/enable-motion-interpolation-for-movies-on-your-pc)) and the original article on Tested ([http://www.tested.com/news/how-to-enable-motion-interpolation-on-your-movie-files/329/](http://www.tested.com/news/how-to-enable-motion-interpolation-on-your-movie-files/329/)), I became really interested in seeing this effect of creating extra frames in video for myself, but the process in the article seemed to be way too much hassle. 

[IMG]http://i48.tinypic.com/2nkkh7d.jpg[/IMG]

After reading through the comments on both articles, people pointed out that certain video players had this capability built in. The wiki on motion interpolation ([http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_interpolation](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_interpolation)) revealed that WinDVD, CrystalPlayer, and PowerDVD all had this ability. I gave CrystalPlayer a try. It looked cool, but produced a number of errors and corruption in the videos. CrystalPlayer also crashed a lot, and couldn't even play some videos. I also gave PowerDVD a try. It look much better than CrystalPlayer, and it didn't produce any errors. The only negative with PowerDVD was that the video didn't look hardware accelerated. It looked like there was no anti-aliasing on the video. It looked very jagged and rough looking, but the motion was very smooth.

It took a while for my eyes to get used to the effect, but now I hate watching the average TV show without it. It just adds a little pop to the viewing experience. :) Is there any way to produce this effect with a Linux video player. I want to go back to my Ubuntu partition, but I am being held hostage in Vista for now.
