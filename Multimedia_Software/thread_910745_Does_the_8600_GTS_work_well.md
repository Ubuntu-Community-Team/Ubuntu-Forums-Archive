### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-09-04
what ATI card do you have?
IMO newer ati cards properly installed with the latest drivers will give you much better video playback than nVidia cards is that's what's important to you.

I have an 8600 gt, not gts (and gts is supposed to be a step up, right?) and you can see some benchmarks from phoronix test suite here:
[http://global.phoronix-test-suite.com/?k=profile&u=redice-31745-17767-28213](http://global.phoronix-test-suite.com/?k=profile&u=redice-31745-17767-28213)

I was gone while most of them ran, but for gaming it's not half bad. @ 1680x1050 is got 80+ FPS for most of the games tested.
Video playback isn't really benchmarked, and keep in mind that no linux card currently supports accelerated decode for anything but mpg2, so there is often a cpu decode bottleneck. The best playback I've ever gotten on linux has been from ati cards, particularly taking image scaling (ie 720x480 --> 1680xX). The nvidia is just okay.

I wouldn't bother with the 8600GTS at all, it's pretty much just an 8600GT with higher clocks.  Better off getting a 9600GT for about the same price, and it has double the number of shaders/fillrate. ### Post by shazbut on 2008-09-04
Whoa there, check the link I posted before.  The 8600GT and 9600GT are poles apart.
8600GT = 32/8/16/128bit (shaders/ROPs/textures/memory bus)
9600GT = 64/16/32/256bit (so roughly double the 8600GT)

Closest 9 series to an 8600GT is the 9500GT.  Stupid marketers messing up naming conventions.
Not as bad as the 8800GS/9600GSO thing though...
