Hi, I saw your post earlier but noticed you had already considered using a wireless stereo adapter, maybe of the type that can connect a personal MP3 player to a pair of remote speakers. I expect the price new will vary, but I have seen ones at just under 30 GB pounds that ought to be suitable. Looks like they have a 3.5 mm jack to plug into the headphone socket of the MP3 player at the transmitter end.

If a new unit works out at more than you wanted to spend, I expect there would be plenty of second hand ones out there that you could adapt to your needs, in which case I doubt that you could save very much more by any sort of DIY approach.

As for Ubuntu, I am very new to it within the last couple of weeks, but I understand the need to have driver support for any hardware that connects to the system. You drew a diagram suggesting in effect a point to point wireless link to replace the lead that you are currently using, and this general approach has the advantage of avoiding any such issues.

But I like you would be interested to hear of any other solutions that would be better :p. 

Whatever you eventually decide, I hope it works out well for you.
