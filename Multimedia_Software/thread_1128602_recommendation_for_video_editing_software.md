### Post by cotcot on 2009-04-18
Blender will be closest to what you want. (3D) Check cinelerra as well. Both are high level (keyframes; ipo; multitrack; scale; rotate; ...)
However do not expect that you can make effects with 2 clicks and no understanding of what under the hood. Take enough time to check them. Both have good manuals and forums.
