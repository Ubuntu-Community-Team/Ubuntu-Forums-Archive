### Post by GenePayne on 2009-04-08
I don't know about the other 2 cameras, but the Nikon Coolpix L18 will record as long as its memory card has space. It also records with sound. It supports SDHC cards, so you should be able to insert a big card like a 16GB and record for much longer than 10 min. For recording video you should get a fast data transfer SD card (class 6).

As per your question about "working with Linux":
I have purchased a few digital cameras, and I have never used the proprietary software that comes with them, even back when I used windows. If you have an external SD card reader, you can just remove the SD card from the camera and plug it in. Then browse through the picture / video files and copy them to your hard drive. This type of use will work just fine in Ubuntu. You might even be able to connect a USB cable directly between your computer and your camera, and be able to browse the files without needing a separate card reader.
