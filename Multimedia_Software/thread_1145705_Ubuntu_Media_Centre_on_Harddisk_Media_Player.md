I'm in the USA so I'm not to sure what an "iSmart" hard disk is actually. One opion is XBMC. Which I use on my XP box along with my Ubuntu laptop. XBMC will play pretty much any video format out of the box. I have had no probelms with videos playing from a hard disk or network. Only issues with online streams. You just add the directory where your drive is and *BAM* all your media is seen.

The second option is Boxee. This is a fork from XBMC. Uses XBMC "engine," if you will, to play the video, music, etc. While it incoperates the "social networking." Your online friends will recommend things to watch and such. I haven't used it so much because it seems to have a "lack of intrest" for the linux port. That's just my opinion.

Third option is Linux Media Center Edition (LMCE). I had nothing but problems with this. It is a resource hog. Make's it's own directories for media. It was VERY confusing to me. There was little to no help from the forums. If I did recieve help, people were rude and just down right nasty. PLUS only certain hardware is supported. It was a hassle. I then moved my computer to WinXP and now running XBMC on it.

I hope this helps you.
