### Post by rhardie on 2012-01-26
I don't monitor this forum but about once a year now, but I thought the Linux community might be interested to see Linux support from a 3rd party commercial software provider.

Corel recently purchased Bibble Pro, jazzed it up a bit and re-packaged it as AfterShoot Pro.  I've used it for a couple of years now for it's fast speed and other functionality.

The Linux Penguin is on the front of the box right along with the MS and the MAC logos.

[http://www.corel.com/corel/product/index.jsp?pid=prod4670071&cid=catalog20038&segid=6000006#tab4](http://www.corel.com/corel/product/index.jsp?pid=prod4670071&cid=catalog20038&segid=6000006#tab4)

Some people choose ONLY open source and others will use what ever they like (I'm the latter).  I personally use Firefox, Thunderbird, OpenLibre, etc., but when it comes to certain specialized applications, I use what helps me get the job done the fastest and easiest regardless of the price or license.

In this case, Aftershot Pro(originally created using QT by a former gaming programmer who understood speed)runs incredibly fast on AMD multi-core processors.  Faster than anything around and it's darn easy to use.

See you next 12 months from now.
