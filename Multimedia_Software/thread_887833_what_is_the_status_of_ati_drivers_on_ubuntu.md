### Post by pparks1 on 2008-08-12
ATI Cards are far better supported now than they were 2-3 years ago.   I used to be stuck with ATI cards at work and would have to find an older machine with an NVIDIA card in it to get any decent performance.

However, with Hardy (8.04), I've been able to run on multiple Dell's with ATI Radeon X600, X1300/1500 and HD2400's all right out of the box by enabling the restricted drivers.   And I've had no problems enabling compiz-fusion features after that.

I certainly hope you plan to dual boot and play some serious Windows games with those 2 video cards that you have chosen as both are powerhouse cards that really won't be utilized anywhere near their potential in Linux.

From what I have read, ATI has the power/price advantage right now with the 4850,4870 and 4870X2 cards.
