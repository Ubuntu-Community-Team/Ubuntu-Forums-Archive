### Post by Phil Usher on 2011-01-06
I am chasing a problem which manifests only after the system has been running a while.  The GUI terminates and presents a dialog with “Ubuntu is running in Low Graphics Mode” and only the console option is of any use.  A reboot of the system corrects the problem until the next failure.   



  Device:  MSI 6676-001BUS Intel Atom D510 (1.66GHz, dual-core) Intel NM10 Intel GMA 3150
See [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856167041](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856167041) for more product details.


  The failure of the GUI is particularly disappointing as without it remote desktop access (VNC) isn’t available; a primary function for this machine.  The computer also serves as an occasional workstation, part-time server and often as my media player (Pandora).
  

My Linux knowledge is minimal and my efforts to research this problem haven’t produced anything which matches my circumstances.   Most reports of this problem are from people who have completely lost their GUI through some form up upgrade mishap.   This isn’t my problem.


  I suspect my problem might be somehow related to screen saver or power saving but can’t offer any solid diagnostic proof of this.


  Any suggestions welcome.

### Post by Phil Usher on 2011-01-11
Update: I disabled sleep:  

System/Preferences/Power Management

[LIST]
[*]Put Computer to sleep when inactive for:  [COLOR=Blue]Never[/COLOR]
[*]Put display to sleep when inactive for:  [COLOR=Blue]Never[/COLOR]
[/LIST]
  and I have not had the "Ubuntu Running in Low Graphics mode" since.

I simply turn off the monitor when not in use.

This is not an ideal work around but I prefer this over certain failure at uncertain times.
