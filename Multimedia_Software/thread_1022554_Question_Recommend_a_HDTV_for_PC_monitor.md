### Post by frito11 on 2008-12-27
Well i can tell you that they say not to connect via HDMI/DVI most likely because they will usually only work with standard TV resolutions that are not the same as PC resolutions 

720 x 480 (480i/p) 
1280 x 720 (720p)
1920 x 1080 (1080i/p) 

it wont hurt to try plugging it in to see if it works, and if it doesnt its because your TV wants one of the above signals in the TV form so simply setting your PC to output that resolution may be enough but you might have to do more, i'm not sure, also what video card/drivers are you using?

My experience with this sort of thing is limited to getting my old Philips CRT rear projection HDTV working with my PC with a 1080i signal via VGA (analog signal, not digital) I was able to get it working in windows XP using a program that enabled odd resolutions in the Nvidia drivers

and yes the TV has a 15pin VGA connection but does not support anything but HDTV signals though it 

as for picture quality, it should be better if you can get your PC to output to the displays native resolution as when an LCD is not running at its native res it has to convert and scale the signal to its native res. and depending on the displays hardware may look anywhere from bad to half way decent, PC LCD's do this same thing
