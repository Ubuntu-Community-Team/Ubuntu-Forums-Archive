### Post by Skripka on 2008-11-08
There are several possible problems:

1)  The game itself, and whatever settings preferences it has....It may not even be using your card, as it did not detect it on install (theory)
2)  Wine may be factoring in, with the game collude to confuse the game as to what hardware you have.
3)  The ATi drivers themselves, which are anything but good in Linux (though AMD/ATi have finally gotten around to making reliable and very capable windows drivers, their Linux drivers are incredibly primitive in comparison).  DirectX support on ATi cards in linux is often limited to only DirectX 7 or 8 (as in the Half-Life 2 run of games, comes to mind) -due to their primitive drivers.
4)  The interaction of all the above.


Lots of possible culprits....most simple to play with game settings, first-see if you can get the game to redetect your hardware, if the game does not list your adapter, odds are it is throwing everything through the CPU........next down the list of troubleshooting (NO double-entendre intended) any settings regarding DirectX, also settings in the ATi driver itself, regarding Ansiotropic filtering/Antialiasing/performance etc etc.

Have you checked the Winehq page for any hints on getting better game performance?
