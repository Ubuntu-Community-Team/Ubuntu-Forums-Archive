### Post by treyes on 2009-08-17
Hi! Well, I just realized what was going on, apparently in Jaunty the LFE (subwoofer) remixing is disabled by default. I followed this guide [http://cviorel.easyblog.ro/2009/04/30/no-lfe-low-frequency-effects-response-in-ubuntu-904-jaunty-jackalope/](http://cviorel.easyblog.ro/2009/04/30/no-lfe-low-frequency-effects-response-in-ubuntu-904-jaunty-jackalope/)
and now it's working alright!

In simple word you have to edit your daemon.conf 
[COLOR=#000000]**sudo gedit /**[/COLOR]etc[COLOR=#000000]**/**[/COLOR]pulse[COLOR=#000000]**/**[/COLOR]daemon.confand change the line "; disable-lfe-remixing = yes" and set it to NO, like this:
disable-lfe-remixing = noI think it's necessary to delete the ";" symbol in the beginning as well.

That would do it!
