I'm on cable myself (and my tuners are a couple of years old, so probably not as good as newer ones), so I won't give hw advice. linuxtv.org is a good place to go though, you'll get new drivers there if the ones in Ubuntu do not support your card yet and the wiki has quite a lot of info on supported hardware: [http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Main_Page](http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Main_Page)

The basic choice you have to make is do you want to decode the MPEGs on hardware or using your CPU: Full-featured cards (with hw decoders) are more expensive and budget cards are cheaper: [http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hardware_or_Software_Decoder%3F](http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hardware_or_Software_Decoder%3F) . I use budget cards and I've been quite satisfied -- but then again, I have a dedicated media box.

EDIT: looks like the current trend is budget cards. I'd go that route anyway.

### Post by beartard on 2008-04-28
Actually, it's an ATSC card, as the US is dropping analog at the beginning of next year.  Most ATSC drivers for the same chipset are the same as DVB ones.  To the OP, I would recommend Hauppauge brands.  The usually have similar models for ATSC/NTSC and DVB, depending on where you are.  A good majority of their cards are supported just fine in Linux...and they usually tell you on their site which are compatible.  If you plan on getting one with a remote control, be sure it's one made for windows media centre.  This usually provides an external, USB IR transceiver, rather than one that plugs into the tv card itself.  The USB models are generally more compatible.

Check linuxtv.org for a better compatibility list, as it's maintained in Europe.

I recently bought a card by them.  Analog (NTSC) isn't supported, but Digital (ATSC) works just fine.  You may have to download the drivers from linuxtv, if Ubuntu isn't as up-to-date as we'd like.

Another thing...Kaffeine supports DVB, though nobody seems to broadcast how it's done.  If you need a hand on that end of the deal once your card is installed an working, PM me and I'll try to help.
