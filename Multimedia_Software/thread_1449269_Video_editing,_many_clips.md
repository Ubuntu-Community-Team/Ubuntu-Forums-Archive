### Post by MSich on 2010-04-07
I'm just looking for some suggestions.  I'm producing videos of my son who is being recruited as a college pitcher.  So, I'm taping all of his pitches and having to sort them all out into a highlight reel type production for college coaches.
 
Right now I'm working with Kino.  I'm using Kino's capture to create my files on the computer.  The problem is a pitch only lasts about 3 seconds, 6 if something happens in the play that you want to capture.  So I have a lot of garbage to edit out.
 
Here's what I've tried so far:
1. Pausing and unpausing the camera.  Very distracting and annoying.
2. Turning on and off the capture.  Which is also annoying as it stops the playback each time and I have to continue to hit play.
3. Capture the entire video.  Then go back in and split the video at the beginning and end of the pitch.  Then delete every other clip, as that is the garbage in between.  Finally going back through the clips and deleting the pitches we don't want.
 
In the end there will be a lot of clips to go through (thousands), but only about 20 that make the cut.
 
Additionally, I'll have new clips from new games I'll want to get in there and I don't know if I just add all the clips to a giant project, or sort them out by game and them bring only the short versions together for final selection.
 
I've only tired Kino and OpenShot.  OpenShot looks cool, but I had real problems cutting all the clips.  If someone has another idea, it would really save some time instead of me trying a bunch of different software.
 
Any thoughts?  Thanks!

### Post by Alan James on 2010-04-08
I'm a professional video editor who uses Windows 7, OS X and Linux for my production needs. I use Linux for servers and rendering visual effects but have recently been trying programs like Kino. I had not been impressed what Linux had to offer for video editing but I then I tried KDENLIVE, which is showing a lot of promise. I highly recommend it even though it wont be able to compete with Apple and Adobe's proprietary NLEs for a number of years. I think its much better then Kino at doing simple tasks like you are talking about.
