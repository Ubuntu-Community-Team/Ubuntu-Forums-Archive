### Post by midnightramen on 2013-04-18
not sure if related to your issue, but I am suspecting that the recent xorg server updates may be related to this. For some reason, I think my .Xauthority file got corrupted in my home directory yesterday, and then today, I had to reinstall the Nvidia driver after another xserver update today.

In any case, you may want to try "dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg" which should reconfigure your xserver. You may find some additional clues to what may be going on under /var/log/Xorg.0.log as well.

** EDIT: it is actually the .Xauthority, not .Xsession
