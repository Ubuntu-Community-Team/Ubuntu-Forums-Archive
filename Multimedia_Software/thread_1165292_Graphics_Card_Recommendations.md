### Post by Legace on 2009-05-21
Let me clear a few points for you..

First of all, it you are going to upgrade a laptop graphics card, PCI-E cards will NOT go in. You'll need a card with a special port.

If you have a desktop, boot Windows and google and download a program called PC Wizard. With it, you should be able to get your motherboard model number with which you can Google and find your answer.

And last, when you see the name GeForce (nVidia) or Radeon (ATi/AMD) in a graphics card, it means that it has a GeForce/Radeon processor.
So, a graphics card called ABC GeForce 260, it means that the card is made by "ABC", but the core, or graphics processor, is made by nVidia :)
