[FONT="Arial"][/FONT] Greetings, everybody out there,

Thousands of apps on offer in Ubuntu Software centre – but *not a single one* to capture VHS from a VCR (or DVD from a DVD player, come to that). So, if I wish to transfer VHS  tape to external hard disk *straight away*, Windows XP plus an expensive VHS capturing device (Honestech's VHS to DVD 5.0 Deluxe, in this case) seems the only way to go.

This, however, turns out to be a slow, time-consuming and – more often than not – frustrating process; and besides – in my experience, anyway – it works only in fits and starts. No problem copying anything between, say, twenty minutes and an hour in this fashion; but a feature of ninety minutes or more will take a hell of a lot of patience.   

I'd very much like Ubuntu to be the sole platform for digitizing VHS tapes. Yet, as far as I'm concerned, Ubuntu seems not without drawbacks either, in this respect. Before even *thinking* of editing, VHS needs to be transferred to DVD first; and with more than a hundred tapes in stock, I'm in for quite some job ahead.

Recently, I came across VHS capturing hardware (**EasyCap**) that might be working for Linux Ubuntu – if, to my understanding, not without a bit of tinkering. I also found a blog ([http://easycap.blogspot.com/](http://easycap.blogspot.com/)) in which you'll find extensively explained how best to go about here.

My question now is this: I'd like to hear about your experiences with EasyCap. Do you think it worthwhile?

As to specs: the situation is pretty fluid at the moment. Currently, I'm running two Dell desktops, of which the main unit is a dual booth (Ubuntu 10.04 LTS/Windows XP). This is likely to change, in the foreseeable future, to Ubuntu 10.04 LTS/Dream Studio 11.10. On the other one, Windows XP or Windows 7 will shortly be installed (choice not yet made), for some applications Ubuntu doesn't support.
