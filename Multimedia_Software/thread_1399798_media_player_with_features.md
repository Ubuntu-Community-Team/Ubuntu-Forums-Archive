### Post by lidex on 2010-02-06
I don't think Banshee does video, does it? If you want something that does both, your best bet is to look at the video players that support audio (vlc, xine, mplayer, totem, etc.) but i doubt you'll find one with a user interface resembling wmp. If you're like me, multimedia in ubuntu is an ongoing process. My go-to apps are Exaile for audio and Xine for video although I have many others installed.

Have a look here:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MultimediaApplications]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MultimediaApplications")

Apps:
[http://www.gnomefiles.org/]("http://www.gnomefiles.org/")

One thing I haven't looked at is Kaffeine. it may be more to your liking if you don't mind installing the kde libraries:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Kaffeine]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Kaffeine")
