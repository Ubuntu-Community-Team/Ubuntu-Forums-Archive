### Post by TBerk on 2010-05-10
OK, I'm starting this thread based on having lost a few things along the way from 9.10 to 10.04 Lucid Lynx, namely I can now run **Quicktime** videos on my system again but not with sound.

Also I've lost the ability to play *some* video files formatted as '**.avi**'.

And just to pile on, I'd like to be able to play "**.mkv**" while we're at it.


Here are some interesting & informative links to start with:


**Medibuntu** 
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu)

> Medibuntu (Multimedia, Entertainment & Distractions In Ubuntu) is a repository of packages that cannot be included into the Ubuntu distribution for legal reasons (copyright, license, patent, etc). 


**Movies, DVDs and videos**
[https://help.ubuntu.com/8.04/musicvideophotos/C/video.html#video-badformats](https://help.ubuntu.com/8.04/musicvideophotos/C/video.html#video-badformats)

**Library for reading and writing Quicktime files** (x11 utilities)
[http://packages.ubuntu.com/lucid/quicktime-x11utils](http://packages.ubuntu.com/lucid/quicktime-x11utils)


**Restricted Formats**
[https://help.ubuntu.com/8.04/musicvideophotos/C/video.html#video-badformats](https://help.ubuntu.com/8.04/musicvideophotos/C/video.html#video-badformats)

> Ubuntu strives to make all software that meets the licensing terms in the Ubuntu License Policy available. However patent and copyright restrictions complicate free operating systems distributing software to support proprietary formats.

Ubuntu's commitment to only include completely free software by default means that proprietary media formats are not configured 'out of the box'.
