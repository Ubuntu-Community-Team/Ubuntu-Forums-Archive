### Post by pseudonym on 2008-12-22
I'm assuming you're talking about Flash video eg youtube? How fast is your CPU? If your machine is old Flash can be really hard on it, just check out the system requirements - [http://www.adobe.com/products/flashplayer/systemreqs/](http://www.adobe.com/products/flashplayer/systemreqs/) .

With Flash on Linux, less use, if any, is made of your video card. This means that your CPU will be doing most or all of the work. Add to that the overhead of viewing it from within a web browser and an old machine can really suffer.

My secondary PC is a 1GHz Pentium 3 with a 64MB video card and Flash costs around 80-90% CPU on that, often getting choppy if it's a long video. I counteract it on that machine by downloading the .flv files separately and viewing them with mplayer.

If you have a fast computer and the above doesn't apply, then check to see if you're running the latest version of Flash (version 10). Update it if you aren't.
