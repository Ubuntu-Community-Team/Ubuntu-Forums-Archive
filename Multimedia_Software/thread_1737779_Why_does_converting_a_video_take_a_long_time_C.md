### Post by FakeOutdoorsman on 2011-04-24
> **TheNerdAL said:**
> I'm converting a movie that is .mkv to .avi and it's taking a long time. I'm using a command.

Encoding time can vary depending on (this isn't an exhaustive list):
[list]
[*] duration and complexity of the input
[*] the encoder you are using and the settings you choose (not all encoders are equal)
[*] your hardware
[*] bottlenecks to the encoding process
[*] decoding and muxing time
[*] the length and density of your neckbeard (neckbeards decrease encoding time, but also decrease social interactivity)
[/list]

> **TheNerdAL said:**
> It's for my tv. It plays videos via USB, but not .mkv files.
MKV is a container format. Like a box that can hold various media formats. MKV is very flexible and therefore almost anything can be thrown into it. One option is to simply copy and paste the video and audio steams from the MKV into another container format (assuming the container is compatible) that the TV supports. This is *much* faster than re-encoding. Do you have a link to the specifications of this TV or any information on what it can handle?

> **TheNerdAL said:**
> Anyways, is there another alternative to make it go faster?
Show your encoding command. It could possibly be improved for speed (depending on whatever formats the TV claims to support).
