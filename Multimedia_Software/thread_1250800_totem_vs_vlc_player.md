### Post by Cybie257 on 2009-08-27
VLC, in my opinion, is about the best 'all-around' media player based on the fact that it tends to play just about everything and has some cool controls (IE: Ability to adjust video to audio timing for poor recorded movies). In my practice, I don't ever remove a working player of any kind due to the fact that every now and then, something just likes the other player better and having the ability to try other players when you encounter issues works to an advantage. 

As for ALL media... That's all preference. I prefer to use Rhythmbox for MP3's and VLC for just about anything else. 

Now, I'm not saying that VLC is the BEST player, but for functionality, it has rarely failed me in the many ways I've used it, even for converting from one format to another, a feature not so known by many that it can do.

-Cybie
