### Post by gradinaruvasile on 2009-12-27
In Windows every app has its own update manager, and its a mess - every app has a tray icon that annoys with its messages and eats resources. 
In Ubuntu the updates are handled by update manager (apt), thus you have one unified update system that updates every app in your computer.
The Windows apps have their own separate updating system because of the lack of a unified system Ubuntu (and Linux in general) have.
