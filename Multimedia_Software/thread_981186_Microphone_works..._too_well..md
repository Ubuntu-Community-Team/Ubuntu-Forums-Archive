### Post by riromero on 2008-11-13
I've recently made Skype work with my Audigy 2 ZS PCI sound card on Ubuntu 8.04. The microphone works for Skype calls, sound recordings, everything. Problem is, the microphone also sends all sounds out over the digital coax output connected to my speakers. All ambient sounds are amplified and broadcast over the speakers all the time. When I play music, fan noise, papers rustling on my desktop, anything whatsoever gets sent to the speakers. Now I can mute the microphone with the sound manager all the time and unmute it when using Skype but this is cumbersome when a call is incoming. How can I disable microphone input to the speakers permanently while keeping the input to programs active?

---

### Post by markbuntu on 2008-11-13
You should be able to just turn off the mic in the playback section of your sound mixer to get it out of your speakers. It is only the capture/recording settings that skype is interested in for your mic.
