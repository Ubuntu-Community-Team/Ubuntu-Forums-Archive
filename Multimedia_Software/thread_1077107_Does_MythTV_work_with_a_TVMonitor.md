### Post by Chronon on 2009-07-10
A TV/monitor is generally classified as an output device.  It uses its own internal tuner or displays a signal decoded elsewhere.  Even if it has a video output jack on the back of it (for some reason) this would only give a way to output the video signal to another monitor.  You would need some kind of specialized card to take the video signal in and allow processing by your PC.  Ask yourself, "How is it possible to get the data decoded by your TV into your PC's RAM?"

### Post by issih on 2009-07-10
if you want telly whilst using the pc you need something that gets the video signal into the computer. unless the monitor is specifically designed to connect to the computer by usb to share the digital tuner (not a bad idea really, but I certainly don't know of any that do it) then its not really going to work.

The absolute best you could hope is that the monitor provides a video out (of some format) that always outputs the tv tuners signal regardless of whether the display is showing the monitor feed or the telly. If you fed that output into a a video card's input (assuming your card has an input) then you could get the tv feed into the machine that way.

I have never seen a monitor with a tv out that works this way though.

All in all, I'd say just buy a usb tv tuner, that will work.

hope that helps
