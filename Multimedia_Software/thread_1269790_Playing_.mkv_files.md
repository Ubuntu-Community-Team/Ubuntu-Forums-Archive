### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-09-18
The problem is probably not the mkv container, but the resolution of the video. Since mkv are popular for storing HD content, I bet your video has at least 720p. That's the real issue, since such resolutions can drain a lot of CPU cycles. 

I have similar problems in Linux and Windows, because my processor is not powerful enough to play such videos.

You could use smplayer, since it allows you to easily tweak the performance settings, but I don't know which would be the best. You have to play with the settings a little bit to find which gives you better results.

Another option would be to re-encode the video, reducing it's resolution. But this is a drastic measure. I'm sure someone will come with a better solution.
