### Post by Linoobius on 2009-03-30
Zaphod,

So I guess it all comes down to what you want to do with your system. For basic computing tasks like surfing the web, composing email and openoffice documents, and playing the occasional video file, I'd say you're probably just fine as is. On the other hand, if you're planning on decoding high definition H.264 video files or playing 3d video games or enabling all of the compiz desktop effects then there's probably merit to looking into an upgrade. Historically I've been an Nvidia fan as I've had a few bad experiences with ATI cards though I will throw in that one of the best cards I ever had was an old ATI all-in-wonder. Recently though I purchased an Nvidia based card for an HTPC build only to discover that the audio has to be piggybacked onto the Nvidia card from another source which the base machine I started with didn't have. The ATI boards, by contrast, have the audio chip built into their HDMI capable boards making them a better choice if you don't have an S/PDIF header readily available on your motherboard. That was a $50 lesson in doing my research before hitting the submit order button on newegg. Anyhow, I hope this help or reinforces what you already knew, if you want more specific recommendations on particular boards, just post back here and I'm sure there's no shortage of opinions on what the best card to go with would be.

Best of luck,
Linoobius

### Post by Therion on 2009-03-30
Just some things to think about...

Onboard video (also known as an "integrated graphics chipset ") relies heavily, if not entirely, on the CPU for video processing.  When you have a discrete video solution (meaning a graphics card) those precious CPU cycles are freed up to do other things, like rendering your web page loads or spinning your desktop cube.  

Secondly, integrated graphics chipsets frequently "borrow" system RAM to work their magic.  Since a graphics card will have it's own memory installed your OS will have more RAM available to do things like... Render your page loads and spin your desktop cube.
