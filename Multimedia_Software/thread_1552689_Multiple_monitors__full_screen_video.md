### Post by chewyfood on 2010-08-14
Asus EeePC 1000HE -> Intel Atom N280, 1GB RAM, 10.1" screen, Intel graphics 945GM, 160GB hard drive. 
Ubuntu 10.04 Netbook Edition
Software in question: Movie Player and VLC

I had such difficulty watching a video on my TV.  I am not sure if this is an Ubuntu issue (it sure seems like it, since I had no problems watching the same videos using Windows 7) or a hardware utilization issue...

So Ubuntu recognized my tv no problem (Samsung LED 55") as a Samsung 40" in the Monitors utility.  And it was able to do multiple monitor output or single output on the netbook screen or tv screen, but video output would not work **unless** I was viewing ONLY one screen, either the tv or the netbook screen.  The second I enabled a second screen the video went black (in either windowed or full-screen modes).  Side note: audio worked fine regardless.  I figured it was an issue with Movie Player so I downloaded VLC and it had the same behavior.

The more annoying thing was that I would turn off the netbook screen so I could have only the TV screen on (to get video output) and the video would work, **but when I maximized the video** (VLC or Movie Player) it would re-enable the netbook screen and it would be all messed up.  The netbook startup panels would be on the tv screen, but the Ubuntu bar (at the top of the screen) would appear on the netbook (???).

I finally had to iron down my maximization process so I could get video output:
1) Maximize the video on my tv with 2 monitors turned on
2) Alt-tab to the monitors window, turn off the netbook screen
3) Alt-tab back to the video and enjoy the video.

Any ideas? Solutions?
