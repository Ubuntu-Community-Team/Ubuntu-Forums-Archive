### Post by JCoster on 2009-05-20
brendanpiater,
I have the FW31ZJ - in fact I'm on it at the moment - and have had mixed experiences with the ATI card.  
The games I've tried on it (under Windows seem slow; CoD4, etc) but will happily run older games at full HD resolution where available.  I'm not into WoW so can't give any advice on that.
I've done HD-video editing under Vista and can thoroughly recommend the laptop for that- brilliant screen and a pretty good processor/graphics combination.
As for running Ubuntu on it, 8.10 runs perfect without any modifications.  However, when I upgraded to 9.04 I had problems with the ATI driver where it hung for a couple of seconds whenever trying to maximise a window.  However, a fix came in the form of an xorg.conf tweak (if i remember rightly). It may be worth noting tha tI'm running proprietary drivers.
I'm still battling with fullscreen video on Jaunty and tearing; but i haven't tried the newly released Catalyst 9.5 but I plan on installing it sometime this week and seeing if that fixes the maximising problem by default and hopefully also video playback.
Hope this is of use to you,
JC

Edit: here is the link for the tweak to fix the maximise problem with proprietary drivers: [https://edge.launchpad.net/~ubuntu-x-swat/+archive/xserver-no-backfill]("https://edge.launchpad.net/~ubuntu-x-swat/+archive/xserver-no-backfill")
