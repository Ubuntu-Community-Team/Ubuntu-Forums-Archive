### Post by irie on 2010-10-05
I installed Ubuntu 10.10 RC x64 to "correct" the issues with the kernel upgrade hosing my video.  I'm running an Nvidia 8800 GTS, needless to say, there were others with the issue but no clear way of correcting the issue.
The fresh install went smooth.  Everything was working great.  I installed Mythbuntu 10.10 RC also without a problem.  I set Myth up but I was having problems getting the volume loud enough and using 5.1 with my X-FI card. I never fully resolved that issue.  
I then started Rhythmbox only to find out that I no longer had sound.  In the Sound Preferences,in the output all I saw was "dummy stereo".  My sound card wasn't an option anymore.  I tried figuring out what the problem was to no avail.  I ended up rebooting and started Rhythmbox.  Guess what, I had sound, but there was a "high, tinny sound" playing in the "background" of every audio file.  I played a video and the background "noise" was still there.  
I went into the Myth front end to check the audio setting for Myth. The volume level is still to low for my choice, but I didn't change anything.  I started up Rhythmbox again and the "background noise" is gone.  
I don't know if it'll come back or not, but I wanted to let everyone (especially developers) know what is going on.  Maybe the issue will be fixed with the final.
