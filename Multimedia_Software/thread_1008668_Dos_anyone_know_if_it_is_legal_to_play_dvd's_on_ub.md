### Post by markbuntu on 2008-12-12
If you have the dvd you can play it on anything you want by any means you can conjure up. The reason dvds are encrypted is to prevent unauthorized distribution. Since you are not distributing the dvd you are doing nothing illegal if you decrypt it or otherwise bypass the drm protection for your own use. You can even copy it for your own use.

Ownership of viewing rights are inherent in posession of the dvd. Neither the Digital Millenium Copyright Act or any other law, at least in the US and EU preclude you, as posessor of the dvd from using any means available to view a dvd or require that you must pay for the decoder for your own personal viewing.

You do not need to buy any software. The reason Canonical must sell these codecs is because they are distributing them. end user licenses are free.
