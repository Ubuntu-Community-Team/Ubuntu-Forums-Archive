### Post by mattharris4 on 2015-04-13
I would take Bucky Ball's advice, it won't take that long to check.  However, if you haven't messed with Pulse Audio I would suspect a failing headphone jack.  I haven't seen that with a computer but I usually plug in my computer speakers and only mess with it once or twice a month.  However, I have had bad headphone jacks with Walkmans before where I did plug and unplug headphones almost daily and what you describe is what it sounds like.  You can do one of two things.  The first is to have a computer tech replace the headphone jack.  If this is for a laptop that is expensive and obnoxious as it involves modification of the motherboard (it would likely cost at least $200 due to the skilled labor involved).  A second is to purchase a $20 external USB sound card (do a Google search, they are sold by several internet retailers) and use that.  The external card will come with headphone and microphone jacks along with (usually) a volume control.  It may take trial and error to find one that functions in a Linux environment so purchase from a retailer that will allow returns.  If this is a desktop just replace the sound card with a separate internal card, the old equipment would likely remain in place but you would switch to the new card and plug in your headphones or speakers into that.  Don't buy a Nvidia card (or Nvidia anything if you use Linux), they usually won't work with Linux.  An external card is an option here as well.
