### Post by cotcot on 2009-03-15
There is nothing suspicious in your specs up till now. 

How much ram memory do you have ? I had the same problem because of the ram memory. I had 1 x 1 GB ; when I add 3 x GB (to get 4 GB in total) the problems as you describe started. Any combination of more than 1 GB gave the same problem. So I went back to 1 GB. Then I got 1 x 2 GB from another make; added it to the 1 GB I had and no problem. Obviously the motherboard could not live with more than 1 memory strip of the make I had (Apacer). 2 of these 1 GB memory strips however are working fine in another computer now.

Another reason in the past was the nvidia driver. Going back to an older version of the driver solved the problem. It was a documented bug. But this story was already 2 years ago.

Maybe you just do not have enough memory. 1 GB or more should work fine. You could also check if your swap is functioning (with Top or System Monitor).
