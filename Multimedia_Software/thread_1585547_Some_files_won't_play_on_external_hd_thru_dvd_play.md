### Post by SeijiSensei on 2010-09-30
Go find your player's manual and look at the assortment of codecs and containers it supports.  Are you trying to watch H.264-encoded material in the Matroska container (.mkv), for instance?  Many commercial players don't support Matroska because it wasn't invented by other commercial entities the way DivX and AVI/WMV were.

Most commercial players support DivX or XviD in the AVI container and a variety of Windows Media formats.  Some of them might also support the mp4 container which is a favorite of Sony.
