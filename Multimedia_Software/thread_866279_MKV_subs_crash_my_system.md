### Post by shirilover on 2008-07-22
Have you tried using another player, i.e, MPlayer, to see if that alleviates the problem.
One thing to keep in mind with soft-subs, is that they need to be rendered onto the video stream which requires some CPU cycles.
Also, with respect to the mkv file, what is the video codec used and the resoltion of the video? High resolution H.264 video requires significant processing power to play without dropping frames and tearing.
