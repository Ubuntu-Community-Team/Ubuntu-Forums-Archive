### Post by evilsoup on 2013-05-27
If you're talking about a video file, rather than actually putting what is on your laptop monitor on the TV, there are a few options. Most new TVs have USB ports, so you could put the video on a memory stick.

If it's a smart TV connected to your LAN, you could set up a DLNA server on your laptop (I would recommend [ps3mediaserver](https://launchpad.net/~happy-neko/+archive/ps3mediaserver) for its simplicity). I do this to stream from my netbook to my PS3.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2013-05-27
I think you'd have better success with a VGA to component video cable like [this one](http://www.amazon.com/HDE%C2%AE-VGA-RCA-Component-Cable/dp/B0096TTOHG).  Both VGA and component video use analog signals avoiding any need to convert from analog to digital as in the case of HDMI.

You'll need a separate cable for the audio of course.
