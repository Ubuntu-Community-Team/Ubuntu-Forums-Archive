### Post by lidex on 2011-06-10
My first guess would be your model selection in alsa-base.conf
Go ahead and remove that and reboot. The line would read:
```
options snd-hda-intel model=alc883-6stack-dig
```

Question: You state this is an Asus P5L-MX, yet your alsa-info shows P5K-VM?

### Post by Atrixium on 2011-06-10
You hit the nail on the head my friend, I just figured it out moments before you posted, although there is still the issue of all the channels being backwards :???:

I had to add the following to alsa-base.conf:
 	Code:
 	options snd-hda-intel model=alc883-6stack-dig 
*And* include the .asoundrc here ([http://forums.opensuse.org/english/get-technical-help-here/hardware/407603-snd-hda-intel-alsa-5-1-audio.html](http://forums.opensuse.org/english/get-technical-help-here/hardware/407603-snd-hda-intel-alsa-5-1-audio.html)) as asound.conf in /etc.

Instead of rebooting I just ran sudo alsa force-reload.

If I just used the alsa-base.conf I would still get front audio from all channels, it wasn't until I added that asound.conf that it started (mostly) working. I'm not really sure what that file does entirely, but it does seem to do the trick!

So now I finally have 5.1 out but all the channels are reversed, ie left is right and right is left. It's not really a big deal I'll just reverse the cables.
