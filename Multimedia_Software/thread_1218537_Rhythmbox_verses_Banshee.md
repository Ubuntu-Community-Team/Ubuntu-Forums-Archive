### Post by divinityofnumber on 2009-07-20
I have had problems/annoyances with both, but I prefer Banshee over RhythmBox.

Banshee is decent at finding and applying most cover art to you albums. Also, it is very easy to edit the track information in your music library. 

Problems I have had:

1. The album cover art looks great in CoverFlow on the iPod, but under the Album menu the art is slanted to the side. Not a huge deal, but worth a mention. 
2. There is no way to "Skip when shuffling." I have a lot of audiobooks, so when I put my iPod on shuffle, every 3rd song is some random track from a book. iTunes lets you select a track or tracks and choose "skip when shuffling", which is nice for people who have a lot of non-music stuff, books, lectures, etc, on their Ipods. 
3. If Banshee does not find the cover art for your album, it can be hard to add your own. It seems almost random. Some albums, I find a nice .jpg, rename it to cover.jpg, put it in the albums folder, go into Banshee, play one of the tracks from the album, and the art pops right up and it applies it nicely. Sometimes though, it just decides that it will not apply art to something no matter how hard you try. I have tried different sized .jpg's, naming it cover.jpg, front.jpg, etc. 
4. As of yesterday Banshee would not let me load anything else onto my iPod. It would say that it did it, and the files would show up in Banshee as being in my iPod, but when I disconnected, there was nothing there. So I restored it on a Windows machine in iTunes to factory condition, and now am in the process of reloading all of my stuff onto it. Take that for what it is worth, could be some obscure problem that is specific to my system, who knows. 

As far as playing music on your computer, Banshee has always worked fine for me.

### Post by martinbaselier on 2009-07-21
To just play music, banshee is nice. Featurewise songbird, is a lot more interesting. I really like the wikipedia/last.fm integration, which shows soms basic info about the artist. It does however use far too much cpu-power and memory. About the bugs: I use it quite a lot and it works flawlessly for me. 

VLC is also great. It's highly configurable and natively plays almost all formats. The search function in the media library is bit slow though. I do however mainly use it for watching movies. If you ever encounter a moviie, where the sound is out of sync, use vlc and synchronize it. The same is true for subtitles.

I never used rhytmbox, so I can't tell. My first impression was negative, so I removed it.
