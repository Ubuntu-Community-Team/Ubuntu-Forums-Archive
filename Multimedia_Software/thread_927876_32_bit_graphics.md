Sorry if this question was asked already, but I didn't find any results on it.

I was wondering why Linux doesn't use videos cards to their maximum potential.  What I mean is, the graphics settings are always at most 24 bit.  I am aware both 24 and 32 bit have the same number of colors BUT 32 bit would open new doors to graphical features for Linux, and probably performance increases.

For those of you who don't know, the only difference between 24 and 32 bit is 32 has alpha settings.  This enables transparency.

With 32 bit video, developers can make true transparency to their programs and applets.  I noticed that programs like docks (such as wbar or cairo dock) or gnome-panel just take a screenshot of what is below them and set it to the background.  it looks like you have a clear background but once something slides under it, the effect is ruined.  This is also slow when the programs starts.
