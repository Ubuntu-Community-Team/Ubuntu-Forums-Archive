### Post by ctdahle on 2010-02-12
I have to wonder the same thing. I switched to Asunder a couple of weeks ago and initially it was quite fast... 3-5 minutes to rip an average audio CD at highest quality. I had figured that by ripping 5-10 cd's per night, and doing it in the background while I worked on other tasks, I would have the whole collection safely stored away on the new hard drive in less than a month.

But now it is taking much longer...I have a new Dire Straits cd in the drive right now and I started the rip at 6:00, it is now 6:30 and the rip is less than 10% complete. There is still more than 400 GB free on a 500GB drive, so the machine should not be struggling to find room for the files.

Also, when I installed Asunder, I set the program to eject the disk when the rip was complete. For the first half dozen cds, it did so, but it no longer does, and I have double checked the settings.

I find this behavior to be quite bizarre.

Not that I'm interested in switching back to windows, but several years ago I ripped my entire cd collection...then less than 250 disks...to my previous computer in just two afternoons. But at the present pace, it is going to take me a year, and I am liable to quit out of frustration long before I get it done. I have already started to pick and choose rather than just copy every disk in alphabetical order from my archive boxes.

Things like this are what keeps linux from growing beyond a small core user base. I don't mind tweaking and trying to figure out better ways to do things, but I cannot convince friends to migrate to linux when little glitches like this keep cropping up. While I have audio and video applications working pretty reliably at this point, both are a bit kludgey. For me, this is acceptable...but it's hard to convince others.
