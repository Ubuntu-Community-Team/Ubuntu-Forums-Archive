### Post by Ghost|BTFH on 2009-11-30
For recording, just use sound recorder - you have several options on how to save it.

For editing said sound file, might I suggest Audacity which has the ability to remove background noise through a sampling?

I've used it for when my wife was going to college classes and recording the classes on an mp3 player - piss poor quality, but within 15 minutes I'd have it sounding like a professional recording.

As for video - I would have no idea, but I do know there are programs out there for motion detection - they have programs like that for security, I wouldn't see an issue with adopting them for this purpose.
