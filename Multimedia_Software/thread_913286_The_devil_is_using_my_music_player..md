### Post by I_eat_candy on 2008-09-07
Yesterday I was listening to my Chrono Trigger OST as I was sewing. The program I was using to play my CD was, "Rhythmbox" its version number 0.11.5. This program has a little feature unbeknownst to me, (until I played my FFVII OST) where it displays cover art. If you are unawares of what the cover art for the Chrono Trigger OST looks like, here you go: [http://downloads.khinsider.com/album_images/856-bonwvvrbwi.jpg]("http://downloads.khinsider.com/album_images/856-bonwvvrbwi.jpg")

I was listening to, "Delightful Spekkio" and it was great and suddenly the cover art image displays on the music program that I referred to above. Low and behold it was the scene of nightmares. Mind you now, I had paid my satan bill this month, so I do not understand why I received this cryptic image letter. Does Rythmbox search online for cover art? Perhaps it searches the outer reaches of space; some sort of deal with the aliens? Is this image, perhaps, the next step in human evolution?

If someone were to enlighten me, I would greatly appreciate it.

Thank you and be careful of the wolves.

### Post by benerivo on 2008-09-07
The album cover that was shown to you was Original Sound Version by Discordance Axis (see [here]("http://www.last.fm/music/Discordance+Axis/Original+Sound+Version")), who play in an avante garde audio-shock style. It happened to you because the album art plugin of rhythmbox was unable to find a match for the music you were playing, and thought that this was the closest match.

### Post by ad_267 on 2008-09-07
I've had problems with Rhythmbox sometimes getting the wrong cover art. What I do is do a google image search for the name of the album, download the correct image to the desktop, then drag the image onto the cover art in rhythmbox and it will be replaced. Then you can delete the image off the desktop.
