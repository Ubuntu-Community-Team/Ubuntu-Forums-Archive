### Post by gordintoronto on 2010-03-08
Trying to further compress compressed files won't gain much.

Archiving usually refers to taking files offline, such as making a "holding" folder, moving a set of files in which will fill a DVD, burn to two DVDs which will be stored in different locations, delete the content of the holding folder, repeat.  Yes, it's gruesomely slow. But it works.

### Post by gordintoronto on 2010-03-08
I think the usual provisos for long-term storage apply.  Put a bunch of DVDs into a sealed bag and store them in a dry, cool, dark place which is also away from electrical flows. (A corner of the basement, but not on the floor.)  I have read 20-year-old diskettes, and they are a lot more fragile than optical media.

I also have hundreds of gigabytes of videos I have not backed up since I downloaded them.

The big archive killer is technology changes. I recently pitched hundreds of 5 1/4" diskettes, since I do not have a functioning computer which can read them.
