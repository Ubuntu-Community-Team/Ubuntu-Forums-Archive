### Post by hd_sheena on 2008-12-30
In case anyone else happens upon here and would like to know, here's what worked:
Apparently M4A and MP3 formats are proprietary. I didn't even know EXE was proprietary, so it doesn't surprise me that I didn't know these two were. Rather than hunt around for some sort of solution to playing them on linux (like Wine did for my Exe files), I just used SoundConverter to turn them into Ogg files. Problem solved, they play fine now, and I kept the originals so I can send to friends or put them on my Mp3 player. I definately will be looking for a Ogg player now, though :)
Thanks to the IRC chat for this helpful solution!
