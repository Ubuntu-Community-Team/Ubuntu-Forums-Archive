### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-07-15
There is a version of Nero for linux.  Audacity has a lot of filters.  You need to experiment to see what works for you.  *Sox* has an earwax filter:

> earwax Makes audio easier to listen to on headphones.  Adds `cues' to 44.1kHz stereo (i.e. audio CD format) audio so  that  when  listened  to  on
              headphones  the stereo image is moved from inside your head (standard for headphones) to outside and in front of the listener (standard for
              speakers).  See [http://www.geocities.com/beinges](http://www.geocities.com/beinges) for a full explanation.

### Post by Dennis N on 2014-07-15
You need a plug-in called "pseudo-stereo". It can be downloaded here:

[http://wiki.audacityteam.org/wiki/Nyquist_Effect_Plug-ins#Pseudo-Stereo](http://wiki.audacityteam.org/wiki/Nyquist_Effect_Plug-ins#Pseudo-Stereo)

Installation:

[http://audacity.sourceforge.net/download/plugins](http://audacity.sourceforge.net/download/plugins)
