### Post by Bablefish on 2008-09-29
ATI drivers can be a BIG problem, they were for me. This laptop of mine has an ati card. I have given up trying to install the drivers epecially after my video drivers crashed and I got no display. I was lucky to get the whole thing working again without reinstalling my OS. But I did loose what 3D I had. I know I will eventually have to reinstall my OS.

Call this for what it is a WARNING!!! If you have anything on that computer of yours back it up BEFORE attempting to install the drivers. If they crash and you have no better than a 50/50 chance of it doing so. Keep you install disc handy, and it would not hurt too look for a hard drive erasing program. When you attempt to do this.

### Post by markbuntu on 2008-09-29
First, you need to know which card you actually have. The driver available from the repos does not support many newer cards like the 31xx and 48xx series. Read the release notes to make sure your card is supported first. 

Then use this guide, it has never failed me:

[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Hardy_Installation_Guide](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Hardy_Installation_Guide)
