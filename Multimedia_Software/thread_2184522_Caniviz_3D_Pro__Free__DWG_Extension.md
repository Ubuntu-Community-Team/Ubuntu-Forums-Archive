### Post by akimb0 on 2013-10-29
Dear all,

i just wanted to share my experiences with Caniviz 3D Pro.
Caniviz is a 3D CAD app which can handle also DWG (AutoCAD) with some extra packages. 

There is not only the Pro but also a Free and a Light version available in the Ubuntu Store.

Here what the developers say:

> **CaniVIZ 3D viewer**


 

 The CaniVIZ 3D viewer is  the ideal tool to share CAD (Computer Aided Design) 3D productions.  CaniVIZ is accessible to everybody with a simple user interface and it  is able to render huge 3D models on all type of devices. In addition,  with the free _[workshop]("http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-3d-workshop-en.html")_, you can immediately share your 3D file on the Web.
 

 Information exchanges are facilitated by its ultra-compact VIZ file format and allow to work on mobile devices. In addition, this proprietary and unpublished format protects the 3D data.
 

 Our product exists in three versions: 
 
[LIST]
[*]CaniVIZ 3D Pro has all the functionnalities to create annotations, animations, read CAD files, etc. 
[*]CaniVIZ 3D Free allows to handle 3D models, to read the annotations and to play the animations, it is ideal for your customers, 
[*]CaniVIZ 3D Light has more options, such as the improved 3D rendering and has more functionnalities to edit the models. 
[/LIST]
 

 CaniVIZ works on Windows  XP, Vista, 7 and 8, Mac OS X Leopard, Snow Leopard, Lion, Mountain Lion,  Linux Ubuntu 12.10 and other Linux such as RedHat, Debian or Slackware.
 

 The softwares are available in Chinese, English, French, German, Italian, Japanese and Spanish and the User Guide in _[English]("http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/help-en.html")_ and _[French]("http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/help-fr.html")_.
 

 This _[comparative]("http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-3d-viewers-comparator-en.html")_ shows the differences between the CaniVIZ versions.
 

 There is no demonstration versions of the CaniVIZ softwares. However, they can be tested online with _[CaniVIZ Web]("http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-3d-web-fr.html")_ or in the _[CaniVIZ Workshop]("http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/explorer_fr.html?params=UjdRago4VDRUNQ5zC21aaQ")_.



Website: [http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-cad-3d-viewer-en.html](http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-cad-3d-viewer-en.html)

Caniviz 3D Free: can be purchased in your Ubuntu Store or at [http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html](http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html)

Caniviz 3D Pro: can be purchased in your Ubuntu Store or at [http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html](http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html)

Caniviz DXF/DWG importer: can be purchased in your Ubuntu Store or at [http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html](http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html)

Caniviz Step / IGES importer: can be purchased in your Ubuntu Store or at [http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html](http://www.caniviz.com/caniviz/caniviz-online-store-en.html)

I already own the Pro Version and the IDWG and IGES extension. Works flawlessy with our AutoCAD DWG files here.

It is, with its native DWG 3D model support, the only real 3D CAD app which can handle Autocad files on linux except ARES Commander Edition which is much more expensive.

On top of that the Support is really awesome, they reply to you within a few minutes to handle your problem if you have one :-)

Give it a try...
