### Post by Paddy Landau on 2012-08-19
Apple does not support Linux at all (even though OS X is much closer to Linux than it is to Windows).

You may be able to get iTunes to work under Wine or PlayOnLinux, but do not rely on it — it may break with a later update.

Rather use dual-boot, and boot into Mac or Windows when you need iTunes. Or, you can load Windows + iTunes into a virtual machine and access your iPhone from there.

You can connect you iPhone to Ubuntu via Rythmbox, but I haven't tried it; and, of course, it's not iTunes, so you could expect trouble when you do connect it to iTunes (as iTunes has a nasty habit of wiping everything from your iPhone, iPod or iPad without asking).
