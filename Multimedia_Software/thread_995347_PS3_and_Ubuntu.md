### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-11-28
Yellow Dog is a PowerPC-specific distribution, and it's the official distribution of the Playstation 3.

Ubuntu, on the other hand, doesn't have an officially-supported PowerPC version. The community maintains a port.

Regular Ubuntu won't run too well on the PS3 due to its meagre amount of memory (256MiB), so you'd be better advised to try Xubuntu.

Many people don't realise before installing: Due to the different CPU architecture, there is no Skype, no Wine, no 3D accelerated graphics, no Flash Player, no Google Earth or Picasa, no Virtualbox, no proprietary applications, no Blu-ray support, and very few Deb packages apart from what's in the Ubuntu repositories. In short, it will not be as rich an experience as running Linux on an x86 processor with lots of RAM.
