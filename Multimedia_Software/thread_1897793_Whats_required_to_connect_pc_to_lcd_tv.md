### Post by Haventfoundme on 2011-12-19
Hi sofasurfer,
If you have a fairly new computer you should have a [VGA port]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VGA_connector") on your desktop computer. Now if you have a laptop this may be a different story but you can still accomplish the same thing with a TV tuner adapter. I've noticed many TV's are coming with VGA ports on them, but they call them "PC Connector" or "PC VGA Connection". 
If the TV you want to buy only comes with HDMI(I would double check this), then you can still use it with your desktop but you will need to either invest in a graphics card; setup a VGA to DVI to HDMI wiring setup ["Amazon VGA to HDMI cable"]("http://www.amazon.com/VGA-HDMI-Cable-6-feet/dp/B002TUC64W")(no guarantee that it will work), or just look into another TV. I bought a Vizio three years ago and it came with a VGA connector. Good luck and I hope this was useful information

### Post by aeiah on 2011-12-20
wow. lot of bad advice in this thread.

regarding LED HDTVs - yes, this is newer, but LEDs are just used as the backlighting technology and say nothing about the input signals the television can accept.

as for the actual crux of the issue:

you need to see what outputs your computer has. the three that are most likely are VGA,DVI and HDMI. [this example has all three]("http://www.orpheuscomputing.com/images5/Diamond-2400PRO512PE-connectors.jpg").

HDMI is just DVI+audio - so you can get cable with dvi on one end and hdmi on the other and simply plug it into any hdtv that has hdmi in. VGA is an analog signal, and whilst vga-hdmi converters do exist, it would be much easier to purchase a television that has VGA-in.

### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2011-12-20
HDMI is not just DVI with audio added..

The common HDMI is physically 19 pin with electrical digital single link.
DVI-D is digital dual link.

DVI-I is an output with DVI-D (digital dual link) & VGA. This is why a simple adapter is possible for DVI-D --> HDMI & DVI-I --> VGA.

Not all DVI outputs are DVI-I, some are only DVI-D.
A close inspection of the pattern of pins is required.

The DVI standard does not have an extension for audio BUT it is supported by the newer video cards because it is the "2nd" HDMI jack..

The VGA input on TVs is not normally capable of native resolution HD because of lack of HDCP.
A digital connection will allow YUV colour & native resolution direct pixel mapping & no overscan nonsense.

HDA audio over HDMI started with PCIex GT220/430 (forget the GT210).

### Post by Haventfoundme on 2011-12-20
Phew, glad I didn't make that list. Oh and I have to add some things that was kinda of mentioned by everyone. The quality of the output will be based on cable you use, the TV, and the computers graphics card(onboard or dedicated). HDMI connection on the motherboard or even better from a graphics card to a HDMI supported LED HDTV = Very good quality. VGA (on board or graphics card) + "PC Connector" on the LED HDTV = Good quality but you won't get the TV's full potential. DVI + LED HDTV = it can be just as good as HDMI the only downside is that you will need a separate cable for audio(VGA setup will require audio cable too). 
Now let's say you use a VGA to HDMI cable to connect to your TV, all I can say is that it won't be as good as a straight HDMI cable from your PC graphics card to the TV. 
This should get me on the list of bad advice now. Good luck man and enjoy.
