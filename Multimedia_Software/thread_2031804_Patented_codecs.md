### Post by Erik1984 on 2012-07-22
Honestly I don't know if that's possible nor desirable to do automatically. You could probably use ffmpeg (or graphical frontend winff) to batch convert, for example, your mp3s to ogg but then you are converting an already compressed format to another compressed format which means quality loss.

For your own video projects you are in control. You can let OpenShot or KdenLive encode to Theora (.ogv) or another free format. If you want to upload your video on youtube then encode to Webm. However there is no way to prevent youtube from serving your video in a patended format.
