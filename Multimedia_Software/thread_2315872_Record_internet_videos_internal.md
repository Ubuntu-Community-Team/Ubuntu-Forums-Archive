**Considerations on xvidcap**

For me, xvidcap seems to be mainly a 32 bit application. But binaries in 64 exist also. It depends from imagemagick as well as from pulseaudio. it uses it because pulseaudio (no problem for ubuntu: only Lubuntu does not use it, but it can probably be added on it) is able to select in and output of sound. This way, you can record the internal sound instead of the sound coming to your standard sound output, generally the speaker of your PC. Using this way, your video capture get a maximal quality. You can define yourself pushing / drawing on only one diagonal the rectangular cutout to record after moving it on the right place. the video quality is perfect. using the internal way with pulseaudio, the sound quality has also to be perfect.

Thereby, xvidcap is probably the most modern and effective recording system at home for videos and has no limit as the cutout can cover the browser...
