### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-04-25
It's highly unlikely you'll ever see a service like this unless it uses a DRM solution like Microsoft Silverlight or an online server-based solution as we've seen for some games.  I also doubt that any DRM solutions for movies will likely ever be compatible with Linux.  Netflix could have written a Linux-compatible player as a binary blob.  The fact that they haven't suggests either the Linux market is too small to bother with, or that the movie studios don't believe that they can control their products adequately on Linux.
