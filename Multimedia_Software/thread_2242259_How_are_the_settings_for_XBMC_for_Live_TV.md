### Post by Jacques van Gorp on 2014-08-31
31-8-2014 XBMC Live TV, how must be the settings?

Startup XBMC (under Ubuntu14.04) -> System -> Live TV -> TV Settings -> General, and after clicking <Enable> a message appeared:
"No PVR add-ons could be found you need a tuner, backend software, and an add-on for the backend to be bootable to use PVR, please visit [http://xbmc.org/pvr](http://xbmc.org/pvr) to learn more."
After clicking <OK> and after restart XBMC PVR-Add-ons were missing to Install!

After much searching, by Googe, I found out that:
"Windows come with PVR add-ons already included."
"Linux users will need to install PVR-ons using the instructions for Their specific PVR backend".
So I did install XBMC, a second time, but now under Windows-7 and yes there came a long list of PVR Clients.
Through the intercession of a Youtube movie from "Vdubt25", I chose for <PVR IPTV Simple Client>.
Then it turned out that there was a need for a "M3U Playlist URL" and so (Brandan's tutorial) resolved it by his list used on: [http://xty.me/vdubt25.MSUPlay.ListURL](http://xty.me/vdubt25.MSUPlay.ListURL). It resulted in an extra item within the xbmc main screen, namely: "LIVE TV" between “PICTURE” and “VIDEO”,  so I was again a step further. However, a list of channels lacked, which he so beautifully in his YouTube video displayed. Another tutorial was clearer by indicating the PlayList was locating on C:\ users\ .. and so we have to conclude that the list of channels must be installed in advance! To check I went to search his website (//xty.me), with the rsult that it  did not exists anymore: probably the reason that, in my case, nothing appeared.

The "PVR IPTV Simple Client" (under Windows7) was palmed as "XBMC Allows you to watch and record TV without the need of a tuner". Likely my goal was to get rid of DVB-C (coax) by to changing to IPTV, that means applying the "network internet signal" from an ADSL modem and lateron originated from a fiberglass connection. The latter is not yet available in my village. However, in another location it is and so I was able to watch TV from an IPAD by the applycation of a specific APP (named ITV from provider KPN). The WiFi signal from the fiber optic digital connection (router) did the job! So I thought it should also be possible to feed. XBMC from the Internet instead of  a DVB-C tuner. 
Of course DVB-S is another matter and interesting possibility to play TV free?

So my question is: how the settings of XBMC LIVE TV must  be and how to find a PLAYLIST for pre-installing?
It remains an interesting application, so the second question is how are we able to run Live TV on RaspberryPi under Linux, e.g. Raspbmc?
