### Post by speartip on 2017-04-15
That's probably because Rhythmbox is ripping individual files, & when you copy them to a flash drive they're listed in Alphabetical order. Hence all the Track 1s, 2s etc..
The best way to rip music to your computer is by using a dedicated program like Asunder.
This will rip each album into its own folder. Then copy the folders to your flash drive instead of the individual files.
Your car stereo should then read each folder separately, and the tracks be in order.

### Post by DuckHook on 2017-04-16
> **Ed W. said:**
> &#8230;I did use Rythmbox to copy the files to the hard drive and it works on the CPU but not in the car.  I started copying them over with Asunder as you suggested and found some of the discs I bought say "takes advantage of ConnecteD technology" and Asunder can't copy them.  Rythmbox copied them but in the wrong order.Rhythmbox *will* rip from CD to HDD and place every album in its own folder. However, whether it subsequently segregates copies from HDD to USB/SDCard into separate albums depends on how you copy. If you do so through playlists, then nothing is segregated. All songs go over all jumbled into one directory. Your car playback software will likely then play back according to song title. Since the default behaviour of Rhythmbox is to prepend each song with its track number, your car will play every #1 track first followed every #2 track, etc.

A quick workaround is simply to change the track numbers on your USB/SDCard. A more "elegant" solution is to define playlists.

Every car model uses different playback software, but most allow you to define playlists. Please read your car documentation for how to do this.
