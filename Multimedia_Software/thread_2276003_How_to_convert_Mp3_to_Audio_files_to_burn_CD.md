### Post by ajgreeny on 2015-04-29
xfburn can do that with no conversion to anything else and you don't need to convert files from mp3 if you have the correct decoders and encoders on your system; you should be able to simply use xfburn CD burner (they all work in this way as far as I'm aware) and choose to burn an "Audio CD" or similar, depending on your burning application, then add the mp3 files you want to burn to audio CD and that should work fine.

I have done it many times without a problem with just about every audio filetype I have on my hard disk; only .aac files cause me a problem with a gstreamer error appearing, probably because I don't have the right decoder or something but I have not bothered to find out why, as I have so few aac files that it is not worth the time expenditure.
