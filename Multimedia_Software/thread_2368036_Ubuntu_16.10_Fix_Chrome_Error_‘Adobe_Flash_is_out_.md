### Post by PaulW2U on 2017-08-06
Ubuntu 16.10 is no longer supported. See [https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-announce/2017-July/000223.html](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-announce/2017-July/000223.html)

As such it will no longer receive any updates. Out of date software will remain out of date. You should now upgrade to a supported release, currently just 17.04.

Hopefully this will fix your issue but in any case you will continue to receive other important security updates and bug fixes until Ubuntu 17.04 reaches its own "End of Life" in January 2018.

### Post by Autodave on 2017-08-07
To avoid having to update the release every 6 months, you could also stay with just the long term service releases (LTS). These are issued every 2 years. The current one is 16.04 (16 being the year of 2016 and 04 being the month of April). These LTSs are supported for 5 years. The next LTS will be 18.04. Unless you need or want the bleeding edge, the LTSs are way more user friendly in that you don't have to upgrade every 6 months.
