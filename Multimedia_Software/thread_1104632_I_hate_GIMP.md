### Post by Jonadab on 2009-03-27
> **UbuntuNerd said:**
> Im a long time photoshop user, I try to use Gimp and I just couldn't do it not because I don't like or I think is bad but because I don't have the time to spend learning it like I did photoshop

I think that hits the nail on the head, right there.  BTW, my experience was similar, but coming from the other direction.  Having used Gimp for a couple of years, I was placed in a situation where I needed to work with some files that a former coworker had created before leaving.  She was a Mac user, so she of course had Photoshop and Pagemaker.  (This was before InDesign existed.  Why she didn't have Illustrator, I don't know.)  Anyway, long story short, it was one of the most frustrating experiences I've ever had with a computer.  I ended up just recreating everything from scratch in Gimp and OOo, it was so much faster than banging my head against the wall trying to get the Adobe software that I didn't understand to do what I wanted.

I mean, you make a couple of little changes to an image in Photoshop, and you save it, and then you try to do a Save As to export a .png or whatever.  Easy, right?  I mean, in Gimp, I could just do Save As, make the extension what I want, and presto.  But in Photoshop?  The only format it would let me save in is .psd.  I don't want a .psd.  I need to put this on a website.  I need a .png or at least a .jpg.  Nope, those are greyed out.  Any *reason*?  None given, they're just greyed out.  No error message, nothing, you just can't do that.  Sure, I eventually figured it out:  you have to manually merge all the layers first, because the software's too lame to make that part of the save process for formats that need it.  It's a small thing, sure.  Most of your work with the image would not be bothered by this little niggle, and you only have to bang your head against the wall for two hours *once*, and then you know.  So, no big deal.

But it wasn't just that one thing.  I kept running into things like that, over and over and over.  After two days, it felt *good* to throw out all her old files and start creating them new from scratch in more familiar software.

I suppose people who know Photoshop will have a similar experience moving to Gimp.  Because, the two programs are indisputably *different* in the way they do things.

Personally, I don't understand the complaint about drawing rectangles.  In the first place, it's not something you do very often in a photo editor.  In a vector drawing program, sure, but in a photo editor?  WHY?  Nothing's a rectangle in a photograph, nothing.  Ever.  It's not a feature I can imagine ever wanting, unless you're trying to use Gimp to draw flowcharts or floor plans or something like that, in which case, you really REALLY want a vector drawing program instead, really, no fooling, honest to goodness, take my word for it, you want a drawing program, not a photo editor, for that kind of work.  Illustrator, not Photoshop, or Inkscape, not Gimp.

In the second place, even if you *do* for some completely unfathomable reason want to put a rectangle in a photograph, filling and/or stroking a rectangular selection is not only a very easy way to to it, but also very flexible, since you can stroke not just with any brush you want, but also with any tool.  Aren't you at least as likely to want to erase or blur a rectangle as to brush or pencil one in?
