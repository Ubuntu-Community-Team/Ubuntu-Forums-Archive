### Post by tgeer43 on 2009-09-23
It does indeed sound like an interference problem. In fact, I'm fairly certain that it is.
Since that network adapter is essentially obsolete (D-Link stopped supporting it as of Dec. 31, 2008) and new network adapters are so incredibly cheap nowadays, I'd suggest replacing it. Since your laptop doesn't seem to have very good shielding, I'd opt for a USB adapter rather than a cardbus adapter. This will put your wireless adapter outside of the computer case and if the problem persists, you can run it from a short USB cable to further distance it from the machine.
