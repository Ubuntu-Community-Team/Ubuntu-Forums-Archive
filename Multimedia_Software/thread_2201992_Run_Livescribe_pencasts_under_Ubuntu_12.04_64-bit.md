### Post by cigtoxdoc on 2014-01-26
I am moving towards a 100% Ubuntu environment for my home and business PCs.  A big obstacle has been dealing with PDF files that only run correctly under Adobe Reader XI.  Livescribe pencast files are used by my Son's AP chemistry teacher to give step-by-step instructions on how to solve problems that the students will see on the AP chemistry exam.  Not only do you need Adobe Reader XI to run these pencasts, but you also need Adobe Flash Player, but not the Linux version.  Both of those programs will run under WINE 1.6.  I have tried this on three different PCs running Ubuntu 12.04 64-bit.  Part of what you need to know can be found at [www.winehq.org](www.winehq.org) under the listings for both programs.  You have to run (from the terminal) winetricks -mspatcha first.

I downloaded and installed the version of Adobe XI for win SP3.  I downloaded and installed the latest version of Adobe Flash Player that had XP (along with Win 7/8) in the description.  The Adobe Reader XI has to be installed with the Protected Mode DISABLED.

i tried to post this at the winehq web site, but I got an e-mail back that said essentially that my IT pedigree was not good enough to post to that site.

BTW, Livescribe files will not run correctly under Studio 8 Professional or the version of Adobe Reader for Linux.
