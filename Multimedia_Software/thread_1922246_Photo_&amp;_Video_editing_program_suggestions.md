### Post by bcarlowise on 2012-02-09
Darktable is a nice opensource workflow software but it is not designed as an editing solution. The former Bibble application is now Corel Aftershot Pro. It is a workflow and editing app much like Photoshop and may be your best bet. I use and like it alot but it requires you to do things differently than Photoshop so you will need to give yourself time to get used to using it. It is quite powerful and full-featured. The cost is comparable to Photoshop Elements instead of the full Photoshop CS series so in my mind it is a great alternative to CS.
As for video, I use Openshot for my video editing. I'm not a full fledged videographer so my needs are really simple...editing and converting home videos. The one flaw with Openshot is that the video rotation does not actually rotate the entire video....it only rotates the video within the context of landscape orientation therefore, any video shot in portrait mode (e.g. I film my grandkids in portrait mode) ends up being "squashed" to fit landscape orientation. For any video I have to rotate I use AviDemux or do it via command line using mencoder.
Hope this helps.
