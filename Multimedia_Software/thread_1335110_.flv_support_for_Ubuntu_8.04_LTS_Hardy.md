### Post by mc4man on 2009-11-23
> ...VLC player or Totem Movie player...

Those players for the most part depend on libavcodec for decoding which in hardy was severely outdated when hardy was released and has since been left far in the dust.

Without doing some work to bring hardy up to date the best you'll do is enable medibuntu and update to their slightly better ffmpeg libs.

What would work well is to follow  this guide and build a current mplayer, pair it up with a current smplayer gui and you should be able to play back most anything ( on a 32 bit ubuntu also install w32codecs for extra decoding capabilities

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=558538](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=558538)
