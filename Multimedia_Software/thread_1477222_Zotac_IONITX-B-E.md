There are a few people using the IONITX-B-E board with Ubuntu and XBMC on it; I'm configuring one now for that.  I'm not streaming BD rips or HD media, though, just standard def MKV files and music.  There are some people at the [XBMC forums]("http://forum.xbmc.org/showthread.php?t=72982&highlight=zotac+ionitx-b-e") who are streaming HD, and they say the Ubuntu support for the Nvidia graphics works well.  As I understand it, flash support would be an issue in a web browser or for your IPTV content, but not for media files you have ripped and stored on a NAS.  The -B-E board has a single core Atom N230 processor, and some people are saying you'll have better luck with the dual core, Atom 330 version of the board.

I'll know more once I get my hard drive and install a full Ubuntu desktop and XBMC and try to play content served by [Playon]("http://www.playon.tv/index.php"). I have XMBC running from a 4GB usb thumb drive right now. Its nearly silent.  I have the Apex MI-008 case, using the stock power supply, and its very quiet; you can also opt for a pico-PSU external power supply brick that would eliminate the power supply in the case and that fan.  The dual core Atom boards look like they have a processor fan.

Playon does have a Hulu application and it works well with lightweight media streamers like my WDTV-Live.  I'll have to try it on my new HTPC when the harddrive comes and I get it configured.
