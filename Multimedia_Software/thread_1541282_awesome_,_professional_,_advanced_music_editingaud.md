### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-07-29
For pro audio you will need to look into setting up the jackd sound server, which was designed just for this. As mentioned, Ardour is a great multitrack recorder, as is qtractor which supports midi.
Pro audio with jack works a little differently than it does with other OSes, you will have lots of different apps running all doing one thing as opposed to an all-in-one DAW like LMMS or Pro-tools.
Jack gives you complete control over where you send your audio which means a lot more freedom and possibilities when mixing and recording.
For real time fx I would suggest using calf plugins or jack rack.

64studio is OK, but it is quite out of date by now so I would sooner recommend Ubuntu Studio which you can upgrade to via synaptic (search for ubuntu-studio-audio).
