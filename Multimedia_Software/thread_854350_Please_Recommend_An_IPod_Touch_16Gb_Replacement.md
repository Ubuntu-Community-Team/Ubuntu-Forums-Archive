### Post by Naegling23 on 2008-07-11
[www.rockbox.org](www.rockbox.org)

go buy any old player that is compatible with it (you can ebay them for very cheap usually), and toss rockbox on it (very easy, in fact, they even have a tool to do it for you).

Video, pictures, games, etc.  The interface is not as clean as your ipod, and the video player works, but you need to convert everything to a very specific format (winFF does this on linux very easily).  But im a big fan, im running it on an iriver H10, without rockbox, I would have ditched the player a couple of years ago, but rockbox makes it good enough to stick with.  In fact, when my player dies, I will likely just buy an older ipod or something and rockbox that too.
