Apparently, Ubuntu now handles well the OpenDisc CD's, contrary to what happened in the [past]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=537155")
When I inserted one of these things in my drive (Interpol/Antics),
- Nautilus sees it as an "Audio CD", lists Track1.wav, Track2.wav, etc.
- Rhythmbox and Sound Juicer recognize the disc and play and can extract the music!

Have you had similar experiences with other discs crippled with this "enhancement"?

### Post by Tetenterre on 2008-12-30
Hmmm. May be something to do with my hardware, but I have to "trick" my drive into reading Opendisc CDs, otherwise it just reports "no disc in drive". The method I discovered (by accident) is to use Brasero to start to copy an ordinary CD, then to put in the Opendisc CD and it will read it as an audio CD and also allow the data part to be mounted as a distinct drive.

Ripping still takes an age; simplest way I have discovered is to copy the .WAV files to my HDD, then use Sound Converter to change them to MP3. Even so, the CD-drive still keeps chuntering away and pretends to be still copying long after all the .WAVs have been copied to the HDD!
