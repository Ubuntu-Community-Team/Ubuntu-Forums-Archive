### Post by Rob Sayer on 2014-11-23
Your use of vlc in terminal may be wrong.  See this for an example:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2136395](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2136395)

I don't actually have vlc installed on the netbook I'm typing this on.  But I'd try them with another video player first.  SMplayer is a GUI front end for mplayer is the one I almost always use.  BTW it's a myth that vlc is the "play any video" app.  It's good at that but smplayer is better.

Or if you want a lighter mplayer GUI, Gnome mplayer is good too.

The reason I suggest trying another player is that many of these videos people dl are encoded by people who don't know what they're doing.  They have their windows systems messed up with crappy quality 3rd party codec packs or they don't know how to use the advanced features of their encoders ... which are admittedly complex.

In other words you can't always blame video problems on vlc or the underlying OS.  Try something else.
