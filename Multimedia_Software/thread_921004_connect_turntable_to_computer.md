### Post by Whiffle on 2008-09-15
Unless its one of those goofy USB turntables, you're limited to your line-in input, which doesn't require any programs to play, I think it just has to be unmuted.  Just about any sound card ever made has a line-in.

Additionally, in order to hook up a turntable you'll probably need a phono preamp as the outputs from the needle of a turntable isn't very powerful and will be very very quiet on a regular input.  [http://www.phonopreamps.com/faq.html#need](http://www.phonopreamps.com/faq.html#need)  Some googling around should find one, they're very simple devices.

### Post by snowpine on 2008-09-16
Very helpful link Hal!

In a nutshell, a phono preamp does two things:

1) Boosts the signal to line level
2) Applies the RIAA equalization curve

The EQ is necessary because vinyl is mastered with a severe bass cut. Otherwise, the needle would jump right out of the groove with the bass drum. This is why when you put your ear right up to the spinning record, it sounds so tinny. 

I believe you can apply the RIAA EQ as an Audacity plugin. However, if you want to listen in real time, it's probably more convenient to have a real phono preamp. I have a USB phono preamp that was less than $100, however, I haven't tested it in Linux yet. :)
