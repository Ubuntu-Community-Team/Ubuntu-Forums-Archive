### Post by inferiorwang on 2008-04-28
Ubuntu is working wonderfully on my desktop with the exception of one minor annoyance.  My desktop has a built in speaker that normally plays sounds when nothing is plugged into the sound card.  However, with Hardy, it never turns off.  I can connect my computer to my stereo and that works, but it still leaves the internal speaker playing so that whenever I turn my stereo down, I can still hear the low quality sound coming from inside the computer.  How do I go about correcting this?

---

### Post by bmac on 2008-04-28
You can use your volume applet:

Right click on volume applet and select "open volume control" if your PC speaker isn't in the volume control add it by:
In volume control
Select "edit" / "preferences"
left click on PC speaker

This will add the PC speaker to the volume control. Then you can either control the volume or at the bottom on the PC speaker column left click on the speaker icon and it will turn the PC speaker off...

Hope this helps...
