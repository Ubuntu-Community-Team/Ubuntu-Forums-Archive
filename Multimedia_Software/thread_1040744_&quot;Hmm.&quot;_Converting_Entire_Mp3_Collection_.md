It depends on what you are starting with when converting from Mp3 to Ogg they are both lossy compression formats, so if you convert a low bitrate Mp3 to Ogg, your are  still going to end up with a low bitrate Ogg.

### Post by logos34 on 2009-01-16
if you can at all avoid it, don't convert.  Like cariboo said, with lossy-2-lossy conversion you'll just get degraded sound, or no improvement at best.

just keep what mp3 or aac/m4a you already have as they are...ubuntu can handle the former with restricted codec pkgs, and has several players with apple plugins to handle the latter. But as to the comparision between the two, ogg is better at the lower bitrates (especially aotuvb5 encoder)--IMO at least.  I use ogg exclusively now, but I see no point in converting my existing library of mp3 tunes (most of them high-quality vbr-new V 2 or better).  Most codecs are transparent above ~180K or so threshold, so I doubt I'd notice the diff even if I were to convert them all.

good luck
