### Post by jackharvest on 2009-10-30
Alright, once again, I've solved my own problem. Heh. Who knew the Ubuntu Forums were so **** helpful??! :D  It gives you a new buzz to the mind as to what you should try next, that's all. 

Anyway, if some silly soul who is still using Zen V Plus rolls around here, I have instructions:

First off: Restart your freaking machine. Make sure your Zen isn't plugged in.
2nd: Open up Rhythmbox (this is what I used, might work for others).
3rd: Make sure the plugin for MTP devices is checkmarked in the plugins list within Rhythmbox.
4th: Close Rythmbox and open it again if you had to enable the plugin.
5th: Once re-opened, NOW plug in your Zen device. It should show up on the left, ready for drag and drop goodness from your music/queue.

NOTE: I *did* perform one extra step that I ... don't think was necessary, but might have been. I opened up Rhythmbox on another machine running Jaunty (9.04) and performed the above steps. This was so I could drag at least ONE song onto the sucker. I am not sure... but I think Karmic has a harder time finding the Zen when it's empty -- it's almost like a lost cause to find it or something. heh.

Anyway, hope that helps somebody.
