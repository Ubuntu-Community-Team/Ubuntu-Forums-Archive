Hi,

I need to solve a problem with my desktop environment.

In my job I use 
[LIST=1]
[*]A Mac laptop (standard cell phone headset jack)
[*]An Ubuntu box (standard cell phone headset jack, and also the digital optical output)
[*]A Gentoo box
[*]A cell phone.
[*]Sometimes an extra device (usually a computer) comes into play.
[/LIST]

Each of these things is a stereo output and a mono input.

I want to be able to use a single stereo headset plugged into a single device, and using a mixer I want to control:
[LIST=1]
[*]Mix each stereo output into the headset while keeping each device's sound distinct from the others.
[*]Select which single device gets the microphone.
[*](optional) mix the microphone to all devices with volume level.
[*]I'm a little hard of hearing, so amplification is a definite benefit.
[*]High quality signal is a must.  I need to wear this every day, all day.
[/LIST]

I need to find a hardware mixer that can easily manage this.  Audio mixers I see online have 1/4" mono jacks or rca jacks, I'd like to avoid this if possible.  My preference is:
[LIST=1]
[*]5x 3.5mm standard headSET jacks (with microphone) to computers and phones.
[*]1x or 2x 3.5mm standard headSET jack(s) to headsets.  If 2x then a separate volume control for each headset.
[*]It would be interesting to have bluetooth capability and USB headset capability, but this is not necessary.  I think bluetooth audio sucks.
[/LIST]

This mixer could be a separate piece of hardware or it could be a sound card with good support for Linux, provided there's a mixer app that can handle my requirements.

If there's a sound card with all this then obviously I can do without one of the 3.5mm headset jack inputs.

Notes:
[LIST=1]
[*]A gaming mixer is NOT what I want.
[*]I do not want to taint audio of each system with input from other systems.
[*]I often have two different meetings on two different computers, where one of them I only need to be there if they call on me.  I definitely don't want them to cross over.
[LIST=1]
[*]For example, I'll be on a webex and need to take a skype call.
[*]I would rather remove the possibility of accidental crossover at the mixer than need to worry about muting the webex.
[/LIST]

[*]I also often have music playing, and I don't want that to bleed into the meetings.
[/LIST]

Does anyone have a recommendation, either as a sound card or as a separate device?

Thanks.
