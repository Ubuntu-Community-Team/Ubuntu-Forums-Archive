### Post by mikeserv on 2009-02-22
I've been interested in something like this as well...  I've actually thought about buying an iPod Touch or an iPhone because you can put VNSea (a VNC client) on them and simply control your computer from anywhere you're connected to the Wifi network...

Anyway, here's a video that talks about it: [http://www.sutree.com/howto.aspx?from=rss&s=17556](http://www.sutree.com/howto.aspx?from=rss&s=17556)

The truth is, that video probably sucks.  But if you get the VNSea client on one of those things then all you need is to enable Desktop Sharing in the Ubuntu system settings and go from there.  You plug your HTPC's ip into the client and you've got a pretty kick *** remote!

-Edit: there's always the bluetooth thing too.  A lotta people use Wiimotes or PS3 controllers.  They might work through a wall - they're RF.  And of course you can always get an rf remote, I guess.  I still wanna do the little handheld touch screen vnc client thing though...

### Post by warfacegod on 2009-02-22
About nine years ago, I bought a universal remote from Radioshack that would broadcast it's signal in Infrared and Radio for about $75 US. I bought it to play jokes on my brother in law. As it turns out, it was very useful to me later on. Anyway, it came with a Radio receiver that then sent out an IR signal to whatever electronic device was programmed into it. If the batteries in it were reasonably fresh, it would work from essentially anywhere in the house as well as from about 45-55 Ft. away from my house when I took it outside. Maybe this isn't quite what you were looking for but it might be a decent place to start.
