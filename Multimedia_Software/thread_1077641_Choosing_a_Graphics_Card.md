### Post by mikeah on 2009-02-25
I always have initial problems with my NVIDIA cards, mostly because the turkeys won't release the code as Open Source.  The ATI card works well but try to find one that has decent memory.  

The reason that I can't tell you what to get is because I don't know your architecture.  Does your machine have a PCI-E slot or one of the older type VESA technologies?  Even if you just have a spare PCI slot there are new cards available. 

If you do go NVIDIA, the very first action should be to load the proprietary driver.  Once you get the card working, then load the other stuff. ### Post by practic on 2009-03-03
In my experience, Nvidia works better than ATI when you combine Compiz with other programs such as Elisa, TVTime, or Google Earth.  I used to get flashing and screen redraw problems with those on Radeon cards, so I stick to Nvidia now (although I have not tried the latest ATI drivers).

I can't speak for games, as I'm not a gamer, but as far as Compiz goes, I'm continually amazed at what it runs on.  I've run it on GEForce2 MX400 cards (32mb ram) without difficulty...I recently put Compiz on an older Compaq Evo 610 laptop with a Pentium 4 and Radeon 7000 (X100) graphics, which is only supported with the open source "radeon" driver, and it worked beautifully!

I've used GEForce FX 5200 and 5700 cards with good success.  I would go for not less than 128mb though, if I had the choice.
