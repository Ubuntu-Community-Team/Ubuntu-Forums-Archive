### Post by thisisjay on 2010-12-10
On my bedroom system I would classify the sound(for music) as livable, but far from optimal. I compared the quality of W7 laptop running Foobar 2000 with ASIO connected to a Benchmark DAC1 via USB .vs Movie Player(which beat out every other media player I tried including the almighty VLC in terms of overall sound quality) in Ubuntu connected to the same DAC. 

The bedroom system consists of an old 70's amp connected to small Klipsch speakers.

The overall differences on the bedroom system were small, but apparent.

I booted the same laptop into the Live CD and played files through the DAC on my living room system that consists of a 60WPC KT88 based tube amplifier and Klipsch Cornwall speakers.

This time the differences between Ubuntu and W7 were much larger. The sound in Ubuntu lacked weight and detail in contrast to the W7 system with Foobar 2000. 

There is no way that the sound quality could be taken seriously in Ubuntu.
