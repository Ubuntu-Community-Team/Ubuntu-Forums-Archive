### Post by thelionroars1337 on 2013-12-13
You just need a converter for whatever port is available on your TV that you wish to use - eg. a VGA to HDMI converter. Look for a fairly cheap one, digital cables are often sold for many times their real value by retailers because people don't know any better (you don't need gold plating etc.)

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2013-12-13
> **thelionroars1337 said:**
> You just need a converter for whatever port is available on your TV that you wish to use - eg. a VGA to HDMI converter. Look for a fairly cheap one, digital cables are often sold for many times their real value by retailers because people don't know any better (you don't need gold plating etc.)

I disagree. You don't want to cheap out on a VGA to DVI/HDMI adapter because it's not a simple adapter. It will need to have an analog -> digital converter (ADC), and you don't want a low quality one.

As for the cable, it depends on what kind of adapter you get. If you get an adapter that's meant to reside near the display (example: [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16812150115](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16812150115) ), then you'll actually be using an analog cable.
