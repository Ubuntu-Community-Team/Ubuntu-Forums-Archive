### Post by seanlano on 2009-03-12
I have some recorded DVB files on my computer, edited with ProjectX and in .ts format. I can play them well with VLC, but it looks horrible without deinterlacing, which requires a few extra clicks to enable than is necessary. 

Is it possible to force VLC to use Linear Deinterlacing by default, instead of none?

---

### Post by mc4man on 2009-03-12
Shouldn't be a problem.
I'm on my little laptop w/ vista atm so the setting's menu may be a little different. (even so it be close to this...
 
Try opening vlc -> tools -> preferences and click on the 'all' radio button.
Expand video and highlight "filters", you should see a box to enable the deinterlacing filter.
Then expand 'filters' and click on 'deinterlace' and there you can set the mode.
Remember to click "save"
