### Post by nateman7097 on 2010-03-08
Sometimes you download or receive video that you cannot play because your processor cannot handle the load. In other cases you have ultra high quality videoos that take up too much harddrive space.

Either case this is how to REDUCE the quality of your videos. Also, if you are not pleased with the results this will not modify the original video file.

Warning this will take a while if it is a long video.

1.) Download Avidemux video processor. You can do this using the Ubuntu Software Application.

2.) Open the video you want to change. 

3.) On the left side select the first tab under video and change it from copy to FLV1. (I'm sure other video types work, but this one works for me for sure.)

4.) Select configure under video.

5.) Lower the target Bitrate. Ex if originally 1500kb/s try 750kb/s.

6.) Save the project under a new name.

Now you're done.
