### Post by kwaanens on 2009-06-08
Regardless of your personal experience with some from the XBMC team, XBMC is a great piece of multimedia software. I've been a happy user for quite some time.

(Note that the XBMC IRC channels are really ugly chatrooms. Some guys there have their own particular way of saying things, reminding more of 4chan than anything seen on Ubuntuforums.)

Your personal need to have a 64 bit system does not reflect the needs of the average user. In time, when having more RAM is more common on these kinds of computers, 64 bit support will probably be very good.

(When I used 64 bit, I saw a lot of reasons to go back to 32 bit, all of which was software support.)
