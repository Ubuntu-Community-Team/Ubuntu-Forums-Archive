### Post by peakshysteria on 2008-11-21
I'm sorry, I must apologize for my post above. You cannot use the usual **4 Installing the restricted drivers manually**. The howto clearly states;
> Please note that for the current version 8.11 of the catalyst drivers, this method of installing with deb packages is broken in intrepid. Use the automated installation method instead.* 

Meaning you have to go for: [Installing the restricted drivers "the Ubuntu way" ]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Intrepid_Installation_Guide#Installing_the_restricted_drivers_.22the_Ubuntu_way.22")
This worked perfectly for me after an upgrade from 8.0.4.1.

I got the black screen at boot. Choose *Low graphics mode* when prompted after second boot.

```
fglrxinfo
```gave me unsurprisingly MESA instead of ATI. Enabled the driver in the restricted drivers manager. After a reboot I once again had to choose *Low graphics mode*. Strangely enough each time I choose Low graphics mode I got to a desktop with perfect resolution (1280x1024). fglrxinfo still gave me MESA. Anyways, I followed the *Ubuntu Intrepid Installation Guide* [Installing the restricted drivers "the Ubuntu way" ]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Intrepid_Installation_Guide#Installing_the_restricted_drivers_.22the_Ubuntu_way.22"). And when prompted to check the xorg file I adjust the resolution there at the same time. Putting my 1280x1024 resolution as the first line (just cut and paste it). Then end the howto and reboot. And now everything is hunky dory. Running smooth as ever. Not sure why but I'm running the 8.10 version of the driver. 8.11 is better for your card I think.

Hope this helps.
