### Post by CatKiller on 2011-07-14
> **derekeverett said:**
> On just one track we sometimes need to zip and un-zip it several times before the final mix. 

ZIP and similar data compression methods are lossless. You get out exactly what you put in. If you find that you aren't actually making the files much smaller you could experiment with FLAC (an audio format, but also lossless).

You won't get any generational degradation with lossless compression, just don't do it with a lossy method like MP3 or Ogg.

Depending on what you're actually doing (composing as opposed to mixing, say) you might find that working in MIDI is sufficient. Those files are tiny - they're essentially text files. It's a completely different way of working, though, so it might not be what you're after.
