### Post by perspectoff on 2010-10-24
You should install either ubuntu-restricted-extras or kubuntu-restricted-extras.

That installs a version of Flash (and Java) automatically, as well as many multimedia codecs and modules.

Hulu is great. You don't need to sign up to use it.

Megavideo has lots of stuff, much of it redistributed illegally. I'm sure no one at Ubuntu Forums advocates accessing illegal content.

Actual TV content (using a TV video adapter, for example) can be viewed through Miro, MythTV, or even Kaffeine. But that takes a little effort to set up and, of course, you need a TV adapter connected to cable or an antenna, or something.

If you are using Firefox, there are many, many add-ons that enable downloading of online video content. Just browse the Firefox add-ons.

Again, Tudou also redistributes a lot of content illegally, and again, I'm sure no one at Ubuntu Forums advocates downloading content illegally.
