### Post by the-nobody-machine on 2013-09-05
I'm running Ubuntu 12.04 64bit, using an nVidia graphics card and dual monitors.

  At the login screen, the resolution is fine, however when I log in the resolution drops to 1024x768 on both monitors. I then have to open the nvidia x server settings and reset the resolution to 1680x1050 for each display.  I have tried the "Save to X Configuration File" button in the program, but it just doesn't seem to stick. Upon restarting and logging back in, the resolution has defaulted again.   

I recently moved my home partition, and found that during a brief period of having a defaulted home folder (due to a typo in my fstab), my resolution problem had vanished. When I re-established my home partition and got it working properly again, the problem returned. I made a new user to test my theory, and sure enough a fresh user has no problems with monitor resolution at all.  A quick scout through the hidden folders of my home directory turn up some likely suspects- there's a .compiz-1 and .nv folder, could one of these be over-riding my saved settings and defaulting the screen resolution?
