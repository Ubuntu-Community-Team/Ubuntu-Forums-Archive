### Post by monkeybrain2012 on 2012-10-22
Provided that internet connection is good, I have not  had problems viewing 720p in Ubuntu even with flash (on my 3.5 year old laptop 3G of ram, Pentium dual core cpu with a cheap Nvidia card, not  state of the art machine even when new) even though it seems a bit  slow. But then flash is not very smooth even on more powerful Windows machines so I try to avoid flash as much as possible.  I prefer to use Smplayer for 1080p smooth playback. For viewing in Firefox or Chrome I use ViewTube using mplayer or Totem as backend. [http://userscripts.org/scripts/show/87011](http://userscripts.org/scripts/show/87011)   With 12.04 even nouveau driver works great for 1080p on my laptop, with Nvidia's driver there is the added bonus of hardware acceleration.

Unfortunately gecko-mediaplayer doesn't seem to work in 12.10, it works on Firefox but not chrome in 12.04. [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2073487](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2073487)


P.S. VLC used not to be able to play 1080p (local files) without glitches, but it works as well as smplayer in 12.04 (again just using the nouveau driver)
