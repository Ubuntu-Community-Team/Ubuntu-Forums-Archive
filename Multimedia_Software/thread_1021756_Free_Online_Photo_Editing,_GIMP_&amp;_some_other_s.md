### Post by YMS_1975 on 2008-12-25
During my ongoing transition (over a year now) to Ubuntu, I'm still learning new things; in particular, how I should look at software, and how it relates to the whole Linux experience.

Previously I'd use PhotoShop Elements to edit my photos. I started doing some research online because I was trying to look up how to run PSE using Wine. But the more I did my research, the more I came across one universal question which sprouted it's ugly (because it's a reality I have to deal with now) head : **If you want your pc to run Windows app's, then why not keep Windows?**

A valid question. Unfortunately it means I have to look at alternatives now, because I honestly don't see the point in running Windows software when there are alternatives.

So you research Ubuntu + photo editing, and of course....**GIMP** comes up. Seems like a decent enough program (based on the reviews I've read). I tried running it but it's a bit overwhelming at first. Maybe it's the fact that I'm more accustomed to PSE or maybe it's the whole look and feel of the program. Whatever it is...I'm learning it bit by bit (very slow process for me though).

I also came across another option : "**Free Online Photo Editing**".
Go ahead, Google it. Hits like "picnik.com", "flauntr.com", "fotoflexer.com" & a bunch of others will come up. They all claim you can upload your pictures and edit them online, thereby bypassing the need to download software on your PC.

I tried both picnik.com & flauntr.com. Both of them failed miserably.  :(
I'm not sure if it's a browser issue, or if it's just not Ubuntu/Linux friendly (despite their claims on the website).

Picnik.com consistently crashed my browser (the screen would just go dark dark grey) until I did a forced exit. And flauntr.com refused to see my JPEG images for whatever reason when I'd attempt to upload my pictures to their site.

I've used both Opera 9.63 & FireFox 3.0.5, I have Java and Flash installed, so I'm not sure what the problem is.

Has anybody else tried these free online photo editing websites? I'm trying to figure out if it's a software issue or if it's a website flaw.

Lil' help?
