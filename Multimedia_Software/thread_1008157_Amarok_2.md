### Post by vgrisham on 2008-12-11
What is bizarre to me is that they actually dropped some of the major features that differentiated them from Rhythmbox, Banshee and Exaile. 

Amarok (hereafter known to me as Amarok Classic) gives the user a lot of control over their portable music devices. Specifically, a user can manage exactly where they want their playlists and podcasts sync-ed to, and you could mount and unmount such devices. That tab of the preferences menu is simply gone now in New Amarok (remember New Coke?). What's more, they've totally dropped support for Linux friendly mass storage mp3 players such as the iAudioF2. Instead, they've turned their attention to supporting MTP restricted players and iPods. 

In fairness, they're apparently planning on eventually bringing back mass storgage support. It's just weird that they moved forward without this very Linux friendly feature. In the meantime, I'm sticking with 1.4. Hopefully Exaile, Rhythmbox or Banshee will step in and make their mass storage managment more sophisticated.
