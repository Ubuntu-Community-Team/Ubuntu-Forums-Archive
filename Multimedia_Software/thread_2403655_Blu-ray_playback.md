### Post by franny01 on 2018-10-14
Hi all, I'm dual booting with Win10 and I'm a newbie to Linux as such. However I have been dabbling with getting my Blu-ray movies to play. I had playback in Mint 19 using the Vlc by following the online instructions etc, however I have Ubuntu installed now and kept getting the error message in Vlc this time, so no Blu-ray playback. After pondering about it, I thought I would try Wine. I installed Wine then installed PlayOnLinux, launched it and created a virtual folder for installing my Blu-ray software used in Windows (purchased when I bought re-writer years ago) Installed and registerd online as normal. It just put the same icon on the PlayOnLinux folder as in Windows. So I clicked on it and the app launched as expected. Put a movie in and it played just as though it was in Windows or on the TV Bd Player. All the Menus etc where there to select/setup disc and it played faultlessly.Even subtitles selected. So I will just leave Vlc now for DVD playback and use the Wine app for Blu-ray. Also want to inform that my Hauppauge Xbox Solo Wintv works perfectly using Kaffeine, just follow the installation from Hauppauge support page for Linux.
              I know some will say using a purchased software licence goes against the spirit of the Gnu freeware etc, but what the hell, I have a right to play my movie collection on whatever and by any means possable. After all, I bought it, I own it and I'll use it. :P
