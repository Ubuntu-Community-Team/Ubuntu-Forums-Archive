### Post by LepeKaname on 2009-05-29
did you checked if your audio card is supported by ALSA? 
According to your computer model, you have a: Analog Devices AD1885 AC97 Sound card...
ALSA AD1885 setup: [http://alsa.opensrc.org/index.php/Ad1885](http://alsa.opensrc.org/index.php/Ad1885)

According to ubuntu, your system is well supported: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesDesktopsDell](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesDesktopsDell)

Try this:

1. sudo killall pulseaudio
2. sudo alsa force-reload
3. go to System>Preferences>Sound and change everything to ALSA

In alsamixer press "TAB" and see if the important channels are not in mute.

If still no sound, check this post:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1046137](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1046137)

In which they include an "alsaconf" script which will detect and install your card.

Also, I remember that in Medibuntu ([www.medibuntu.org](www.medibuntu.org)) there are other alternatives to fix your sound problem. 

If ALSA do not work for you, you may try to use OSS as well..

### Post by ryecomp on 2009-05-30
try this 

(1) sudo gedit /etc/modprobe.d/alsa-base.conf and add following lines at the end 
options snd-pcsp index=-2
alias snd-card-0 snd-hda-intel
alias sound-slot-0 snd-hda-intel
options snd-hda-intel enable_msi=1
(2) set all sound to ALSA
(3) reboot
