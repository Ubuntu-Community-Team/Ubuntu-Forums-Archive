### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-03-29
Rhythmbox is just a player like any other of the thousands you can choose from. It "owns" no files whatsoever. There's no such thing as "Rhythmbox music files"...
Keep that in mind and:

- Your music is a bunch of audio files (probably MP3 but can be others as well) stored somewhere in your drive, most likely in the "Music" folder inside /home/<yourusername>.
- Rhythmbox shouldn't be used for that anyway.

Again, you "music" is just files. Files that can be copied just like any other. Use ANY graphical file manager for that or even command line but NEVER EVER use a music player for that purpose.

### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-03-29
If you copied all the files from that folder -and- you don't have music scattered in other folders or even other drives THEN, by copying all of them back to a new Music folder, the player will pick them up exactly as before. Whatever is missing is due to the fact that you *might* not have backed up everything.

The default for Rhytmbox is to watch the default system/user folder for Music but you can change it to other folder or just add more folder to watch for. That you must confirm in the original installation, the one you're copying the media from.
