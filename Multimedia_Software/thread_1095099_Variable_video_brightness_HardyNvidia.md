### Post by varangian on 2009-03-13
Ever since I switched to Ubuntu as my working OS a couple of years back I've noticed that I was always going into the settings panel in VLC to tweak the brightness, something I rarely needed to do under XP. Recently I got a bit more scientific about it, playing the same video on different occasions under Ubuntu and XP. It seems that whilst on some occasions under Ubuntu a given video plays fine out of the box on others, for no obvious reason, the playback is much darker than usual and I have to up the brightness to around the 65% mark instead of the the 50% VLC defaults to.

The problem does not seem to be VLC as I also tried Movie Player etc. and when VLC plays dark so do they. A quick search revealed others noting problems with brightness with Nvidia hardware though in somewhat different contexts. I should add that although video playback goes dark the desktop stays perfectly constant and its brightness is on par with its XP equivalent. Also I'm talking about a desktop system with an 8800 GTS card using the restricted video driver.

And re being a desktop system I saw a reference on another thread to a brightness applet so I promptly added it to my panel, found it didn't do anything (if I'd read a bit further down the thread I'd have seen it's for laptops only) and removed it. This proved to be quite an efficient way of terminally crashing the desktop and probably the OS as I couldn't get an restart other than by a hard reset. Thought I'd pass that on as a warning to others.

Anyway if anyone has experienced similar issues and knows how to prevent this happening (I was wondering if certain screensavers might be altering settings as this would explain the random occurrences) I'd be interested in learning more.
