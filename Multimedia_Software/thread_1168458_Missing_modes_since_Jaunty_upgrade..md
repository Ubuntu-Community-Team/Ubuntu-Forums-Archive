### Post by Drokles on 2009-05-24
No problem :).

It looks sort of messy though. What I think you need to do is first create a modeline for your choice of resolution/frequency. First go here:
[http://bohne-lang.de/spec/linux/modeline/](http://bohne-lang.de/spec/linux/modeline/)

Enter your desired resolution and frequency. The output will be a modeline That you're supposed to add into the monitor section. Now, modelines describe what settings your monitor is capable of using. If you don't have any specified your system will guess on your behalf. It took me a while to figure out, I also started out only being able to use 1024x768.

Now, you should see something like "1280x1024" at the beginning of the modeline. This is the name of the modeline, and under the section screen under modes you must specify the modelines you're going to use with your screen settings by typing in their names. Since the modes you added under your current screen section don't exist as modes available to your monitor it doesn't work ;).

I'll post my xorg.conf so you have something to guide you by.

```
Section "Monitor"
    Identifier     "MyMonitor"
    HorizSync        30 - 81
    VertRefresh    55 - 80
    DisplaySize    390 393
    VendorName     "SHG Computers"
    ModelName      "SCT17-8BS"
    Modeline       "1280x1024" 151.83  1280 1360 1544 1888  1024 1024 1027 1072 -HSync -VSync
EndSection

Section "Device"
    Identifier     "GeForce 7900 GT/GTO"
    Driver         "nvidia"
EndSection

Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Default Screen"
    Device         "GeForce 7900 GT/GTO"
    Monitor        "MyMonitor"
    DefaultDepth    24
    Option         "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"
    SubSection     "Display"
        Depth       24
        Modes      "1280x1024"
    EndSubSection
EndSection

```Some of the stuff I have in there isn't neccesary, and I'm not even sure that it makes it a whole lot better. But just make sure you edit the parts I talked about.
I'm guessing that the HorisZynch and VertRefresh lines you've added under the monitor sections are something specified by the producer of your monitor. If so then ignore the suggestion the modeline generator gives you.
Also, delete all those subsections you have under screen. You only really need one, and you only need to specify your depth of choice once.

I hope it works out for you this time.
Good luck!
~ Drokles

[Edit]I think you may want to know that there's a 185 BETA driver that you might want to try. It works well for me at least. But I think you should try to make this work first.[/Edit]

[Edit]But whatever you do, make sure to create a backup first

```
 $ Sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.cong /etc/X11/xorg.conf_backup
```[/Edit]

### Post by Drokles on 2009-05-24
You have to delete those modes that you don't use I think. If you your screen section was reduced to something like:

```
Section "Screen"
Identifier "Default Screen"
Device "Configured Video Device"
Monitor "Generic Monitor"
DefaultDepth 24
SubSection "Display"
Depth 24
Modes "1280x768"
EndsubSection
EndSection
```

I'm not too sure what the numbers in modeline mean. I think the first is your pixel dot clock frequency thingy in MHz. The next is your horizontal resolution and your vertical resolution should be there somewhere as well. hsync and vsync specify how you want your screen polarized. I'm not sure why but I suppose polarized light goes easier on the eyes?
