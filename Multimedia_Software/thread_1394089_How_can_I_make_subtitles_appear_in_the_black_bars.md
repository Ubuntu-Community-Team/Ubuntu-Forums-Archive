### Post by mc4man on 2010-01-30
You should say what your source material is.

The more problematic would be dvd video, though vlc can move dvd subs. (at least 1.0.4/5 and 1.1 that I have here, maybe the 0.9.9a in jaunty. (or 1.0.2 from a ppa

It's a bit unwieldy, you may need to restart vlc after making the adjustments 
(tools -> preferences -> subtitles -> force subtitle... -> save

Also the number of pixels to move may be more than you'd imagine to get into the black, though don't go to far. ( figure 24 - 50

You may need to adjust per film to some degree and in some films, if it opens with credits the subs may be at the top to start. Those unfortunately would also be moved down farther into the picture.

( for many of my foriegn films I found it better to rip, use a .VOB subtitle editor and adjust the subs as needed, sometimes change color, fix forced subs, ect.  and then burn a play disk.

In that case though I'd use DVDSubEdit which works remarkably well ( though only in win. last I checked.
