### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-09-06
> **saskatchewan said:**
> I have been trying to hook up my old EEE 1000 to my TV but have not been having any luck.  I bought a cable that runs from the VGA to the "S" port on my flat screen but I can get neither video or audio. I am running Ubuntu 10.10.

You won't get any audio over the video cable.  You'll need to use a cable to connect from the earphone jack on the computer to the audio side of the TV.  Typically you'd use a cable that connects with a mini-phone-plug on one end and "RCA" plugs on the other like [this](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=9SIA0AY01S5485).

The video is a more difficult beast to tackle.  First, does the TV only have an S-Video connection but no VGA port?  Obviously if it has a VGA connector you should be using that.

Next, check the netbook for an Fn-key combination that switches between the internal display and the external output.  A quick Google search suggests that Fn+F8 does the trick.

If you decide to purchase a streaming device like the WDTV box you linked to, I'd recommend skimming [this forum]("http://www.avsforum.com/avs-vb/forumdisplay.php?f=39") at AVSForum for some informed reviews.  That particular item seems to be the subject of [this thread](http://www.avsforum.com/avs-vb/showthread.php?t=1283694).

Check your TV manual. Recent TVs have a PC input consisting in a VGA for video, and a headphone connector for audio (sometimes RCA instead).

As said before, using VGA will give you much better quality than S-Video.
