### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-07-18
Get a mobo that's known to be decently linux compatible (I think these days most solid midrange boards, particularly from companies like asus are good.)
The main things to watch for from my experience are what RAID controller and Audio chips they use, as these seem to be the 2 main areas that things are sometimes a bit iffy. 

If you plan on getting 4+ gigs of RAM be sure to install 64 bit.

Right now something to watch is that we're on the DDR2/DDR3 junction. Some mobos will support DDR2 up to speeds of 1066 or so, while high end ddr3 systems are at something like 1333 or so. It's your call whether solid memory in a decent quantity is better than paying through the roof for speedy RAM. In any case, pay attention to the memory voltages your mobo supports and buy accordingly. These days it seems like there's a lot of divergence in rated voltages for the same memory speeds.

So far as CPU, anything's good. If you want quad core, people seem to agree that intel is a better performer, but I don't think it's clear cut at every price point. 

I'd make sure you have at least 4 SATA II ports on the mobo. 

With internal card reader that use USB headers, I say go for a cheaper and more generic one. IMO, the fancier they are, the less likely they'll be relatively generic linux hardware.


If you plan on putting wireless cards or a DVR card, that takes more research.  For DVR, might try this: [http://www.pchdtv.com/](http://www.pchdtv.com/) or this:
[http://www.silicondust.com/wiki/products/hdhomerun](http://www.silicondust.com/wiki/products/hdhomerun)
depending on what you want.


Finally, I'm a huge fan of modular power supplies. Not necessary, but I love em.

I'd say you should be doing the following offhand:
Quad core CPU, Intel quad, 1066 - 1333 ghz FSB,
3-6 gigs RAM, maybe 1066 DDR2 or overclocked DDR2 800.
4+ Internal SATA II connectors
2 SATA II 7200 RPM  Seagate drives, 320 gigs to 1 terabyte each
SATA DVD/CD reader/writer
Internal USB multi card reader
At least 6 USB ports
maybe a firewire port or 2


anyway, just some ideas. (Based on the notion that this won't be a particularly budget system)
