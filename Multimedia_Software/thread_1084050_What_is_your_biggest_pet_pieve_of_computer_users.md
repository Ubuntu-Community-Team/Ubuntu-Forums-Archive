### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2009-03-01
I would have to say my biggest pet pieve of most computer users, and this applies to ALL operating systems, is by far and away lack of organization.  There are HUNDREDS of applications available to help keep files organized in an orderly fashion.  F-Spot is great for organizing photos, and almost every single media player out there gives you tons of options to tag and organize all of your music and videos.
My biggest gripe centers specifically around music and iPods (and other MP3 players).  A few years ago MusicMatch Jukebox was my weapon of choice for tagging, converting, naming organizing and listening to my music.  It was beautifully simple.  It would automatically look up track tags for songs, convert them into a common bitrate and file type, and automatically organize them into a very commonly accepted ~/music/Artist/Album/track # - Track Title.file type format.  I personally would always tag my songs and rename them in my personal format, (# - song - album - artist) ie:
"/My Music/Avenged Sevenfold/04 - Bat Country - City Of Evil - Avenged Sevenfold"
I did so because it's the ONLY format that makes sense for albums that have multiple artists on them, like for example a lot of electronic music I listen to.  I can go to the store and purchase a mix CD by Armin Van Buuren, but it will have 13+ tracks by several different artists.
I've been using Rhythmbox primarily since I switched to using Ubuntu as my only operating system and I love the fact that it allows you to sync multiple iPods with it and you can get music off of your friend's iPod without any problems.  But when I rip a few hundred songs from someone else's iPod, they're invariably scattered about with f(*&ed uo tags and no common bitrate, naming scheme or organization.
Why don't any of the music players available now have the ability to look up track tags for you?  I've tried using Amarok but every time I try to use "music brainz" it always gives me an error, regardless of the file format I'm using, be it mp3, ogg, AAC, m4a, etc.
looking up all those tracks online is tedious and time consuming.  It would be nice to have an automated way of doing it.

What is your biggest pet pieve?
