### Post by dangreaves on 2009-12-30
Hey,

I have recently converted my 65GB music collection from the .m4a format (iTunes on Windows) to the much more open .mp3 format for use with Ubuntu. I have discovered that iTunes doesn't store album art or certain tags in the correct format so much of my information has been lost.

I'm currently in the process of going through my entire collection using EasyTAG to add the missing tags and album art back in again using the correct ID3 format. However, I have noticed that there is a lack of "Album Artist" field in the ID3 tags. This means that if I have an album with multiple artists, it will show up on my iPod as multiple albums by those different artists for example:

Good Girl Gone Bad by Rihanna
Good Girl Gone Bad by Rihanna feat. Someone Else

Does anybody know a way of overcoming this issue?

Dan

### Post by kat_ams on 2010-11-23
I think we were all just baffled at why someone would covert MP4 (M4A, MPEG-4 all the same thing) files, which are newer, higher quality than mp3 and owned by Frauenhofer to MP3 files which are older yet still owned by Frauenhofer.

During the conversion process loosing quality of the sound of your files.

Instead of converting them to OGG Vorbis or FLAC. Which are free and open-source and has matching if not better quality than MPEG-4 (M4A).

To answer your question though: in the ID3 tag format you can '(ab)use' the TPE2 tag as your extra artists.
