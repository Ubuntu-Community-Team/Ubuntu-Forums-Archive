### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-03-09
It depends what you want to do when you talk about "managing pictures".

I use a number of different applications but most often now it is gthumb that I go to; it is much improved recently, and as I always manage the photos/pictures I have in separate sub-folders under the ~/Pictures main folder, it works well for me.  It has a pretty good photo editing option which is good for most actions that I ever need to do, though I'm not sure if it can handle raw images; I don't have any so can not even try that out.

Of the other apps mentioned here, digikam would be my favourite, even if running gnome, as it is most flexible in use, and has a very wide range of options and plugins (kipi plugins) which allow it to do almost anything. On the downside, with my older machine it is a bit too resource hungry.
