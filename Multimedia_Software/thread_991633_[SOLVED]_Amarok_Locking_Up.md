### Post by TheZergcorp on 2008-11-24
So, last year after switching over to Ubuntu I was using Rhythmbox to begin with since it came with the distro and supported the hotkeys on my laptop. Shortly thereafter, however, I switched to Amarok, for while it didn't have hotkey support, it did support far more of my music library's formats than Rhythmbox did. I enjoy the interface and feel it's a good fit for me overall, but sometimes quite frequently it will lock up for seemingly no reason.

Occasionally I'll swear it has something to do with trying to play a file while some other media with sound is playing, i.e. a youtube video or something playing on movie player. Other times, the only program running currently is amarok, and nothing is happening to suggest I should worry.

The gist of it is that I'll pause a file for an extended period of time, or I'll have started amarok up and waited for it to load my collection before clicking on an item. Once I select a track/unpause one, the analyzer field at the bottom will start displaying jumps as if something is beginning to play, even though no audio is being put out. After about half a second, the bars will halt for a second and then jerk up and down constantly about a millimeter, as if stuck looping the same few milliseconds of audio. 

It's as if a CD is skipping, but instead of popping amarok out of my computer and  computer and cleaning it carefully, amarok then stops responding within half a minute or so, and unless I force quit and use the system monitor end all the amarok-associated processes, I can't get it to start back up again. Occasionally, even clearing out all the amarok-associated entries isn't enough, and I'll have to reboot the system.

The only thing I can think of is that my entire library is on my 500GB external hard drive, and perhaps the fact that it's a separate mounted drive is throwing the program for a loop. Any suggestions?
