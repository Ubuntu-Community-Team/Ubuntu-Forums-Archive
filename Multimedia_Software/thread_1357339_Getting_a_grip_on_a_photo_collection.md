Don't worry, this isn't another thread about choosing a photo manager.


I have this personal picture collection. It goes back at least 10 years, weights 40GB Crosses 3 platforms: Mac (9/X), Windows, Linix. It has seen many photo managers, iPhoto (1 to 3), Pacasa (1-2), F-Spot, DigiKam and others I've forgotten. This collection has photos taken with my digital cameras, and the usual assortment of photos/wallpapers/jokes/etc that get downloaded or automatically put in your Pictures-type folder. Don't forget the AVIs that some managers took of a camera and buried in there. 


Enough is enough, I want to get a handle on this beast. My collection's file structure is reflected in it's crazy history; named folders from Picasa, full dates from DigiKam, Y/M/D with thumbs/originals from iPhoto etc etc. I want to automate sorting out the pictures, to a point where I can sort cutting out the garbage. If I could automate putting photos in to a YEAR/MONTH/DAY/ directories based on the metadata. Then move the other photos for manual sorting. Alternatively into DigiKams YEAR-MM-DD style. Either way, something has to change. 

I have searched the interwebs and can't really find something to do this. I am sure it could be done with some shell scripting with some CLI photo tools, but well, I have alot to learn. Somewhere I bet someone has had this problem before and solved it.
