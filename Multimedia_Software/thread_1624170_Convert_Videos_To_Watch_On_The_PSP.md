### Post by nl4m on 2010-11-17
I've seen a lot of complicating walkthroughs that do not work. Here, I have written a guide you help you convert video so that they can be viewed on your PSP. Since I own a PSP I have tested this out and it works for me... 

To convert videos so that they can be played on the PSP:

1. Click "Applications", "Ubuntu Software Center", in the search bar type in "Avidemux", and download the one called "Avidemux (GTK+)".

2. To launch the application, click "Applications", "Sound and Video", and click on "Avidemux". 

3. Click on the "Open" button and search for the video you want to convert. You will get a pop up saying "H.264 detected..." click on "Cancel", DO NOT click on "Safe Mode".

4. On the menu bar on top click on "Auto" and choose just "PSP", DO NOT "PSP (H.264)". On the "Target Type: " choose "iPod (320*240), and click on "Ok".

5. Under the "Video" option choose "MPEG-4 ASP (Xvid)"

6. Under the "Audio", the choose "AAC (Faac)" should be chosen if not choose it, and click on "Filters".  Under the option "Mixer" choose "Stereo", and make sure that "Resampling (Hz): " is set to "24000". And click "Ok".

7. Click the button "Save" and name the file anything you want with the extension ".MP4", for example "MyFirstVideo.MP4", (< "mp" are to be typed in capital letters). and click on "Save". 

8. Wait until the conversion is over, pop the Memory Stick into your computer and move the video file to the folder of the Memory Stick called "VIDEO". Close that window, right click the Memory stick icon, and click on "Safely Remove...", and pop the Memory stick into your PSP.
