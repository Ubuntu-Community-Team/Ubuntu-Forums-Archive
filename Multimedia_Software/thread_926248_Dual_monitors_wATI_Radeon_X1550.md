### Post by whoobuntu_owl on 2008-10-04
I got it to work; here's what I did:

```

sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
# accept all defaults

```

This disabled my proprietary drivers, so I had to re-enable them in System->Administration->Proprietary drivers.

Then, I followed the **old version** of the instructions here (all credit goes to Ziox):
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1773544](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=1773544)

In particular, I ignored the aticonfig stuff (which has never worked for me despite trying countless times), and just pasted the following into the "Device" section of my clean xorg.conf:
```

Option "DesktopSetup"  "horizontal" #Enable Big Desktop
Option "Mode2"         "1280x1024" #Resolution for second monitor
Option "DesktopSetup" "LCD,LCD" #the types of monitors that is connected LVDS = LCD, CRT, AUTO
Option "EnablePrivateBackZ" "yes" #Enable 3d support
Option "HSync2" "65" #This sets the horizontal sync for the secondary display. 
Option "VRefresh2" "60" #This sets the refresh rate of the secondary display.

```

And then I restarted X, and voila, it works. If you haven't tried this since updating to hardy (or maybe even if you have), it's worth a shot.

I hope this helps!
