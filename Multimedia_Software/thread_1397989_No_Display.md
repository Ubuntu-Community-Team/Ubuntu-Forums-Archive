### Post by efflandt on 2010-02-04
Unless you have integrated video, you might try removing and reseating the card just in case the contacts got a little corroded or something.  Or try a different cable.

I have had my current HP desktop PC since 2004, and occasionally my monitor quit working and after unplugging/replugging everything it seemed to work.  But one time that did not work so I tried a different video card and still nothing.  Then I suspected that maybe that thick stiff Monster DVI cable was stressing out its connectors and not making good contact, because if I switched to VGA it worked.  The inexpensive DVI cable that came with an up converting DVD player worked fine.

I just got a 32" LG 1080p HDTV to use as a monitor, and that works great with DVI to HDMI cable.
