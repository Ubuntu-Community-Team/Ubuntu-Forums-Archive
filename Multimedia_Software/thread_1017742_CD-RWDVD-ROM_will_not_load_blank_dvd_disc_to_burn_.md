### Post by bellmount on 2009-01-03
If it's indeed a CD-RW/DVD-ROM you can only write your movies to CD. (Sorry.)
ROM = Read Only Memory
RW = Read & Write
So in order to burn your movies to a DVD, you need a DVD RW drive and here's where the different formats your friend told you about come in: + and -,.
Your blank disk should be DVD-R or DVD+R according to what your DVD recorder's capable of processing (see manual or visit manufacturer's site for exact specifications). Some DVD drives/recorders/players write only DVD+R, others only DVD-R, some both (then you'd see + and - on top of each other which I can't type here), some (mostly older ones) read only either + or -, most read both. 
All the various formats and combinations of such can be pretty confusing, so I won't go into HD and bluray now, that's all explained by e.g. WIKI... ;-)

Just check your drive's properties again, maybe it's a DVD-RW after all (or DVD+RW or +-RW) in which case the good news is: it should allow you to write DVDs. But the bad news is: your problem of not even being able to see the DVD in drive is a much more complicated one. 

Have a nice day,
bellmount
