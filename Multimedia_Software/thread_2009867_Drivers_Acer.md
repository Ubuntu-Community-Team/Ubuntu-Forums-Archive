### Post by Kreaninw on 2012-06-25
I'm using Acer. The quality of the cam I found in Cheese and Crystal Eye Webcam app are no difference. They both have similar settings. I have not yet test my web cam on Facebook or Skype. But as long as Cheese works just fine, it means no driver problem here.

For the sound, the quality when enabled Dolby enhancement on Windows compared to the sound on Ubuntu through Rythmbox was vastly difference, in my opinion Rythmbox dumb chuck. However, Audacious is there for you, a hardcore listener. Audacious has many equalizers and DSP to choose from, downside is that no cover view or any library relate features, just a half-baked library plug-in available though. Another music player I found it has a good out put is BeatBox. This one comes with stylish interface, but in the time I tested it, it has a bug that doesn't show album cover in sound icon.

I recommend you install a working Windows too. Currently, I can't find a way to enable my touchpad back in Ubuntu if I disabled it on Windows. I have tried to fix it for days to found out the easiest(and may be costly) fix ever. I'm using Aspire 4745G.
