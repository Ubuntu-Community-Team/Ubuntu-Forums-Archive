Hi mc4man,

That certainly fills in a few blanks :-). The supported codecs / containers look fairly inclusive:

>     * Containers: AVI, WMV, MKV, *_MP3 **[COLOR="Red"](??)[/COLOR]**_*, FLV (Flash Video), MP4, M4V, QuickTime
    * Video codecs: Xvid, DivX, Windows Media, MPEG 1/2, H264
    * Audio codecs: MPEG 1/2/3, Windows Media, AAC 

Simply a matter of trial and error to see which runs best on this gadget. But video transcoding is not really for the faint-hearted and I worry a little S3loth when you say:

> Don't need a bunch of fancy settings like choosing bit rate and stuff like that.

There are truly *a lot* of fancy settings and a lot of overnight encoding sessions involved in video conversion.
