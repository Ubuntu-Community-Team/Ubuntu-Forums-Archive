This is my annual search for a media player that do 2 things that I haven't been able to find so far. Both are available in windows media player. There have been some advances in the past year, such as a limited variable speed capability in smplayer, but nothing seems to match the elegance of windows mp when playing archived internet radio broadcasts. Here are the capabilities I REALLY want to find:

1. Move to any point of the broadcast. VLC's slider will let me advance about 25 minutes of a 4 hour broadcast ONCE, but if I try a second time the app crashes. Additionally, it won't move to any point forward or backward - it's more like a tab.  SMplayer has the controls to advance +- 1 minute and 10 minute intervals, but it doesn't seem to work out of the box.

2. Playback at different speeds. WMP does this while keeping the tempo/pitch constant. Again, smplayer can play back faster or slower but it doesn't really seem to work (it plays faster for a bit but hangs so the total playback time ends up being the same). VLC has a tab to play faster but it goes silent when invoked.

On archived broadcasts I can download, I currently load them in audacity and compress them with the tempo feature (which I had to compile myself since the latest beta of audacity no longer includes the tempo feature). If I can't download the archived broadcast, I seem to be out of luck.

Someday these features will be available in linux; is today the day?
