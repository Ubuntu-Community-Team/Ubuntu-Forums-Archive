### Post by ajgreeny on 2014-07-18
I have had this problem myself but generally now find that audacity seems to get the timing correct.

I often use mp3splt to divide mp3 files into separate tracks in a lossless manner, rather than audacity itself which has to decode then re-encode when splitting files, thereby losing some data.  I tried using totem, mplayer, vlc, rhythmbox, and probably others, but the timings did very quite a lot over a long audio file meaning the splits I made were not in the right places.

Audacity seems to get it correct and you can zoom into the timings shown to get to 0.01 of a second.
