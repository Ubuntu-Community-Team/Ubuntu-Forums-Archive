Good morning, JDL.  

Just because you are using XFCE does not mean that you are confined to using XFBurn.  You can use any burning program you wish with XFCE, including Brasero or K3B.  

The only places where you may have occasional difficulty is the copy/paste function or drag and drop.  The X11 standard does not specify how "drag and drop" or "copy and paste" works, so each desktop XFCE, Gnome or KDE has their own way.  Sometimes, copying from a Gnome program to paste into a KDE program may not work as expected.  Beyond that relatively minor inconvenience, the programs should work flawlessly. 

You can even decorate them so that they "fit in" with XFCE.  For example, you can use qtconfig to tell KDE apps to use GTK to decorate themselves, making them look like XFCE instead of KDE.  

I personally prefer k3b, in XFCE, even though it is a KDE program.  It feels closer to Roxio and other similar programs.
