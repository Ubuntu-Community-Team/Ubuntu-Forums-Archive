### Post by angry_johnnie on 2010-04-27
if it is an old nvidia graphics adapter, you might want to try the old 96 driver instead of the newer ones.  

other than that, just disable compositing while watching videos.

i don't know whether to call it a bug, or just what the graphics adapter is capable of doing.

i've gotten used to disabling/enabling compositing.

i have an icon in my upper panel that points to a custom script to do this.  it's still annoying every now and then, but it's a one click solution. ;)

also, lucid lynx's default driver did a wonderful job at streaming video.  not having the actual nvidia driver means no special effects, but at least there's no more tearing.
