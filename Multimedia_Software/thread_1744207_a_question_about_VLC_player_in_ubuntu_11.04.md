### Post by tcamdg on 2011-06-08
Install CompizConfig Settings Manager, then go to that utility under applications, and edit the settings in Ubuntu Unity Plugin.
Default is "Dodge Windows," which is what you're experiencing.

Here are some detailed instructions:

[http://www.wmlcloud.com/linux/how-to-customize-unity-panel-features-resize-unity-icons/]("http://www.wmlcloud.com/linux/how-to-customize-unity-panel-features-resize-unity-icons/")

### Post by beew on 2011-06-08
Yeah, I was about to post a thread and then I found the solution. Just click at the middle of the VLC screen (while VLC in full screen mode) and the Unity bar and the top panel will disappear.


P.S. Nothing to do with VLC not being default, I don't have this problem with Umplayer or gnome-mplayer, those are not default either.
