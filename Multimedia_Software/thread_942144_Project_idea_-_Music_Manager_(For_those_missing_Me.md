### Post by ne4ve on 2008-10-08
Hi everyone. I'm new around here as well as to Ubuntu. At least to the level required to be considered a user. I've dabbled over the last year or two but now my main machine (A spangly new laptop) is a Ubuntu PC! Horray!

So to the topic of a decent Music Manager. I know, I know there are tons out there in Linux land and I've tried a good few of them over the last few weeks perhaps not to the extent I should but still judging by the comments about the lack of a MediaMonkey type music manager for Linux I think maybe it's worth a little discussion.

So far I'm using Muine. It's a nice little player that focuses on albums (with Album Art, nice!) and I can easily scroll through my 300 albums or search for something in particular. It's how I tend to listen to my music so it's perfect from that perspective. However, it's not, nor is it meant to be, a manager for ripping and tagging CDs and that's just for starters.

As I've tried Amarok, Exaile, Rhythmbox, Songbird and a number of others I'd rather not discuss the relative merits of those. Instead I like to concentrate on what you would like to see in a music player/manager. Perhaps referring to the various features of particular players would be useful but I would especially like to hear from MediaMonkey users. What features would make you use a Linux music manager over MediaMonkey. What are the killer features you can't live without. 

For example. I absolutely need to have good quality CD ripping, tagging lookup and album art that places it in the MP3 and in a file called folder.jpg which is pretty standard but you'd be surprised at the rubbish that iTunes and WMP stick on your hard drive. Another idea I had after using the simple Muine player was to split the playing and management into separate apps or have a library module and separate front-ends depending on the way you like to work with your collection. Just an idea. I've not really thought it through yet. Plugins also seem to be the way to go these days so that's something else I need to think about.

So let me hear it. You never know, I'm looking for a project to get into on Linux, this could be it. Also recommendations for libraries, APIs, GUIs, languages, etc to help me kick things off would be good. I'll probably be targeting gnome for this but will use design patterns to ensure it can hopefully be ported in the future.

Just to give you a little background on me. I've mainly been doing web application development (server-side and front-end) and the odd Windows desktop app for the last eight-years commercially and a lot longer before that. I've used C, C++, Java, C#, x86 assembler, Ruby, and even old ASP and VB.

So just to recap what are the killer features you need to start using a music player/manager. The idea here is that I take the top requests (obviously including my own!) and try to knock something together and get it out there for people to try as quickly as possible then build on it. It's just an idea at the moment no certainty of me developing it but it's been at the back of my mind for sometime even while using MediaMonkey as even it has it's problems.

Wow that was a long post. I must be serious!
