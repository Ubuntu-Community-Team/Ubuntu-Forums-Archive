### Post by riksaga on 2011-03-31
Thought I would let everyone know the Acer Aspire TimelineX w/ 13.3" display, 2ghzcpu, 4gigram, intelHD128mb+sharedmem, can do 2 external displays (if you turn off laptop screen) 

HDMI and VGA ports are too close to use simultaneously with standard cords.
 
1. hack saw 1 finger screw off a vga cord (takes 2 minutes) 
2. attach vga monitor (I have a dell 19" monitor 4:3)
3. plug hdmi/dvi monitor (I have a samsung 32" lcdTV 16:9)
4. Open display manager (gnome control panel or kde system settings)
5. un-check mirror screens
6. turn on 2 of the 3 screens, set resolution and abrakadabra multi-monitor

I had hoped for 3 screens, 1 laptop + 2 monitors, but the intelHD is probably too weak.

**Note** after downloading a brrip movie I simply unplug everything and re-attach HDMI to a 52" LG plasma TV (Plasma picture is far superior to my vga or lcd monitors - brightness, color, crispness etc).  I click on configure display and I switch on the fly.

In my 10+ years with linux this Acer w/ intelHD + ubuntu has been hands down the best hassle free setup.
