### Post by eye208 on 2008-08-12
You don't need any 3rd party software (Medibuntu etc.) to fix this. Ubuntu comes with all the components you need.

First enable the "multiverse" repository in System > Administration > Software Sources. Then, if you encounter a video that doesn't play in Firefox, right-click the video frame on the page and choose "Open in movie player" from the pop-up menu. This will open the video stream in Totem, Ubuntu's default media player.

Here's the interesting part: Whenever you open an unknown video or audio format in Totem, it will automatically tell you which plugins need to be installed to play the format. These plugins usually reside in the "multiverse" repository, which is why you should have enabled it in advance. After downloading the plugins, close Totem and reload the page in Firefox. The video should now play fine.
