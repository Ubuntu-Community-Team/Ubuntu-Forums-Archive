### Post by Rhubarb on 2009-08-29
This is because DeVeDe transcodes your source videos into MPEG2 at usually much larger bitrates.

The reason why it transcodes to MPEG2 is so that the resultant DVD can be played in any DVD player.  - Rather than just a data disc containing the source videos in their given formats that would almost certainly be incompatible with DVD players.

Please note that DeVeDe does give you the option of adjusting the video / audio bit rates so you can trade quality for file size.

Hope this is of some help to you.
- Rhubarb
