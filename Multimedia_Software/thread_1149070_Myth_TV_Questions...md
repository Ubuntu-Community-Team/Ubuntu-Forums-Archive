### Post by HappyFeet on 2009-05-04
> **wbee said:**
> 

I have skimmed but not studied(yet) the Myth TV documentation and wiki page.That would be a great idea. However, I will help answer your questions.



> **wbee said:**
> --Those times I want to watch live TV,can I do so using Myth without recording to the hard drive?Yes. It will only record when you specifically tell it to. There is a "Watch Live TV" (without recording) option.

> **wbee said:**
> --When I do record something to watch later,once watched,is erasing it easy,so I don't overload my hard drive?Yes, it is easy. You can change the default record location to a more erase friendly location such as Home. Otherwise you will need to be root to erase. But either way, no big deal.

> **wbee said:**
> --I have dual layer DVD. Can I record to a disk instead of a hard drive? I don't believe that can be done, and you will have to do any DVD authoring after it is recorded.

> **wbee said:**
> --I have the plug in infrared sensor for the TV tuner card. Does Myth have good capacity with most universal remotes?If your TV tuner card is working well in linux to begin with, chances are your remote will be handled just fine. There are also custom LIRC files that people have made for the few remotes that are not MythTV friendly.
