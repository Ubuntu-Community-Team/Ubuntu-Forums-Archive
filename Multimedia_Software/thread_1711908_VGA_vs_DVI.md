Depends on your monitor and your video card.

VGA can handle higher resolutions and refresh rates than a single link DVI. Dual link DVI can handle at least the same resolutions and frame rates as VGA.

If your monitor is a fixed resolution display like an LCD, get a DVI cable now. Otherwise you suffer the iffy attempts of your monitor to synchronize itself with the video coming from your VGA cable. This leads to sometimes blurry or jittery pictures.

Personally I use dual link DVI to connect to my monitor to get 2560x1600 resolution.

Not all monitors or video cards support dual link DVI

### Post by tgalati4 on 2011-03-22
You can find cheap DVI cables, no reason to spend $30.  DVI has a few different flavors.  One flavor simply allows pass through of analog VGA signals:  RGBHV--so no improvement of picture quality.  If you have an LCD monitor that only has a DVI connector, then you will either need a VGA-to-DVI connector or find a video card with a DVI output.  DVI single and dual mode depends on the resolution of the monitor.  I know that my powerbook will drive a 23" CineDisplay to full resolution using single DVI.  It will also drive my 24" Samsung TV/Monitor to full resolution (1920x1080) with single DVI.  Bigger monitors or higher resolution monitors will require dual-DVI (more pins in the connector) to reach full resolution.

So, without knowing the full resolution of your display, or what your computer is, it's difficult to make a recommendation.
