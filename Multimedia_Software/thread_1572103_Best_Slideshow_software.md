Hello Linux community,

What would you say is the best slideshow software out there for Linux?  I've used Imagination and I'm using Videoporama now as I type, but they both have their quirks and don't seem exceptionally well-polished.  I'm looking for something that:

1. makes it really easy to do Ken Burns effects (Videoporama is pretty good with it, I don't think Imagination supports it at all...oh wait, I think it's just ridiculously slow now that I think about it), 

2. add songs (Imagination is better, since Videoporama only lets you add one, so you'd have to combine multiple songs into a single track before inserting it), 

3. get a quick preview of a short section to work out timing with events in songs, 

4. export to a couple of different formats.  ogg is what I'm currently using

5. let me edit the pictures as I'm making the slideshow (Imagination is pretty good about that, I can't even get Videoporama to rotate a sideways picture).

6.  give me an accurate estimate of the length of the picture portion and the song portion of the slideshow.  Imagination's estimates are way off, and I can't comment on Videoporama yet.

7.  have some different transitions, or at least fade in and out.

Anyway, what do you like to use and why?
