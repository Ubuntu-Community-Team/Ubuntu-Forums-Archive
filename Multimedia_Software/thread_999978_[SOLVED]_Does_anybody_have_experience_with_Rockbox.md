### Post by semitone36 on 2008-12-04
So I tried it out and Rockbox works great!  It doesnt really sync with Songbird automatically but putting songs on it is actually a lot easier IMO than the standard iTunes way.  

For anyone who tries out rockbox but doesnt understand how to put music on it just plug your iPod into your comp, open up your iPod's directory, and creat a new folder in it called Music.  Every time you want to add music to your player, just drag and drop songs into this folder.  Its best if you make a system of subfolders within the Music folder in the style of /Music/ArtistName/AlbumName/Song.  After you have draged and dropped your songs into the directory, unplug your device, start up Rockbox, and then under /settings/generalSettings/directory set "Auto Update" to "yes" and also select "initialize now". Then press Menu/Select to reboot and your database will automatically add any new songs/artists/albums you have added to the Music folder.

### Post by semitone36 on 2008-12-10
Haha well I actually find rockbox to be a ton easier to navigate than the iPods firmware.  Rockbox's directory is nearly identicle to the iPods firmware except you dont have to keep hitting Menu like a back button.  If you press Menu on Rockbox, it puts you immediately back to the main menu and also remember what directory you were just in so that you wont have to re-navigate through the entire directory to get back to your playlist.  It also has a ton of cool little plugins like an Ocilloscope, a bunch of screensavers, and some fun games/apps. I would definately try it out.

Plus whats also great is that you dont have to erase your iPod firmware AND Rockbox will detect any songs that are on the iPod firmware and port them over.  And if you want to use your iPod firmware for whatever reason (I use it in the car with my iTrip) your songs can still be played on the iPod side.

In fact, the only hard part about rockbox is finding an iPod that is compatible with it.  After that its smooth sailing
