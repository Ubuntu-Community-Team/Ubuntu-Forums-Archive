### Post by QIII on 2011-12-27
Unity is the default Desktop Environment for Ubuntu now.  That is the one with the purplish background and the group of icons running down the left side of the screen.  That is what I assume you are seeing when you log in.

(I use the Kubuntu spin and not the generic Ubuntu, so i'm going from memory here...)

When you first boot up, you get to the screen where you log in.  On the screen where you enter your user name, in the lower left corner, there should be a button to allow you to choose the "type" of session.  One of them should be GNOME.

Try logging in to a GNOME (GNOME 3, GNOME shell or whatever it is called now) session and see if the sound works.

Log out (don't shut down) and go back to the login screen.

Next time you log in, go to the same button at the lower left and select Unity again (it may just say "Ubuntu" or something like that) or you will automatically go to another GNOME session.

I know there are posts here about sound not working for some in Unity when it does in GNOME.

I just want to check to see if that is the case for you and then I can give you a link to one of those threads.
