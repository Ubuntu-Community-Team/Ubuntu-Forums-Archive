### Post by vincentertainment on 2010-03-28
I'm a newbie to F-Stop, but from what I understand its a clone of Photoshop Lightroom. For lightroom users with significant photo collections, (especially photographers who shoot in raw mode) it is recommended to have 3 separate terabyte hard drives. 1 internal drive for hi-res copies and metadata edits, 1 external drive for archiving original unedited raw images, and 1 external drive for backups. 
Not having these drives is why I haven't gotten too deep into lightroom yet. For most people having three hard drives dedicated to one program is a bit much. However, the benefits are worth it for someone who wants a great system for archiving, editing, and managing their collection of photos. 
Right now, many of my pics are on a hard drive that crashed and I wish I'd properly backed everything up a year ago.
