### Post by Murdoc_of_puts on 2012-04-06
I have a similar set up, using my laptop to output video through HDMI even using VLC.  The best thing I've found to do is keep the mirror screen, and close the laptop.  This works if you have your laptop set to "do nothing/blank screen" when it's closed.  I've had no end of trouble trying to set up a profile so that when the HDMI is plugged in, it switches to dual output, or that the HDMI is the primary output.  If you just use the mirror screen, and close the Laptop, the HDMI driver should not allow the HDMI output to go to the blank screen.  This will allow you to do all of your functions without switching back to another video setting and messing things up.  I know it's not an exact fix to your problem, but it does allow for an ease of use workaround.  Hope this helps you somewhat.

### Post by Murdoc_of_puts on 2012-04-07
USb keyboard, or I picked up a wireless mouse for about $10 of of [www.newegg.com](www.newegg.com) , really makes a world of difference.  For example, you can just double click the screen and it's full screen. 

To set up your laptop to not do anything when your laptop closes, go to the preferences > screensaver
you should see a little button to the right called power management, click that > on ac power >never go to sleep > when laptop is closed >blank screen
do something similar for the battery power if you use your laptop unplugged. Your video output should be automatically detected when you plug in the cord. And you should get a/v out on your tv.  That's about the best that I have for you now.  Let me know if you need anything else.
