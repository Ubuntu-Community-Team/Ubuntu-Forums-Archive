### Post by benerivo on 2009-08-09
XviD should be fine. It will be the settings you use when you convert it to XviD that causes the large file size. You can get get it much smaller if you lower the resolution, bitrate, and audio bitrate. Check what resolution the device has. I think many devices use qvga, which is 320*240. The MP4 format is commonly used for small displays.

How this is done depends on what program you use, such as avidemux, ffmpeg, mencoder, etc..
