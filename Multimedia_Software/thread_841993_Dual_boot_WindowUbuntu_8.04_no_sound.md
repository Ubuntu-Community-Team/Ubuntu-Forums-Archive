### Post by graymadder on 2008-06-26
First off I am a total noob when it comes to Ubuntu/Linux. I decided that Windows sucks and Linux is the way to go. So I downloaded Ubuntu and dual booted my pc. No problems everything works great. So I decide to dual boot my laptop. 

Here is where I had a two problems. Wireless which I was able to resolve and the second problem was sound. I searched the forums for no sound problems and I found that sound seems to be an issue. I tried many solutions and none worked. I ended up re-installing ubuntu many times because I noobed it up and felt that a fresh install is always the place to start. 

It appeared as everything was working right. The mixer would display that both channels were working. The volume controls both physical and software all appeared to work just no sound from the speakers or headphones. 

Well I was about to give up when I decided that I better boot into XP and verify that sound still works in XP and verify that the onboard sound still funtions. Booted up to Windows, which after booting into Ubuntu for the last week XP is DEAD slow. I went to test my sound and noticed that I had muted my sound in the sys tray in XP. I unmuted it and verified I had sound. Logged off and restarted.

I booted into Ubuntu and I have sound. I think to myself how can muting sound in XP carry over into another OS? I used the volume control in XP, not the physical volume slider on the laptop, to adjust the sound. I tested this by booting back into Windows and muting it and no sound in Ubuntu. I wiped out the install of Ubuntu and did a fresh install. Sound at first boot. 

Sorry for the long post but I thought others might benefit from this information. And maybe an explantion might come my way as to why this would be. I also didn't know exactly where to post this as well. 

If anyone wants the steps that I used to get the wireless working I can post it. I found the steps here but I don't know who posted it so I can't give proper credit.

Specs... Toshiba U205-5057
