### Post by dmp1ce on 2011-07-19
I had a similar problem which I just solved for myself.  [This article]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=83973") told me how to solve it, but it took me awhile to understand it.

This is what I had to do:

[LIST=1]
[*]Knowing the horizontal and vertical resolution along with the refresh frequency I ran the command cvt.
```
cvt 1280 1040 60
```
This will give you the Modeline you need to put in the **Section "Monitor"** block.
```
# 1280x1040 59.95 Hz (CVT) hsync: 64.69 kHz; pclk: 110.75 MHz
Modeline "1280x1040_60.00"  110.75  1280 1368 1496 1712  1040 1043 1053 1079 -hsync +vsync
```
[*]The link I sent you then tells you to list the modes in the **Section "Screen"** block.  I did this as they said but my Modes line was deleted by nvidia-settings and I think the setting didn't matter.  Instead, I think what you want to do is edit the Option "metamodes" line.  Mine looks something like this:
```
Option "metamodes" "CRT-0: 1280x1024_60"
```
[*]You will also want to change your monitors HorizSync and VertRefresh ranges found in the **Section "Monitor"** block.  In fact, doing this may be enough to show you all of the resolutions that you are looking for.
[*]After you have made all of your changes to the /etc/X11/xorg.conf file.  Save it and restart X.  I logged out to restart X.  Don't forget to make a backup of xorg.conf if you want one!  X may not boot up if you have made a mistake somewhere.
[/LIST]

Hopefully now you will be able to go into nvidia-settings and change your configuration as you would normally.  Good Luck!
