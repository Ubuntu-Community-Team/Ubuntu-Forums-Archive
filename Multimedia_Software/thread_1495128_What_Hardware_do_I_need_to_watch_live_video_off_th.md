### Post by jerenept on 2010-05-27
you can increase internet speed, and try not to stream the video during peak times (around 4pm-8pm). There is not much hardware issue with internet streaming. I have an AMD Sempron @ 2.35GHz and 1GB RAM and Youtube works just fine (no hulu in my country :( ). 4GB-8GB is overkill for a computer used mainly for watching Hulu Videos! 
BTW, there are some great free media center apps for ubuntu (moovida,Mythtv,boxee,etc. You might want to check them out.)

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-27
'Buffering' is normally nothing to do with your hardware, adn more to do with bandwidth, either at your end or at the source. 

1GB should be more than enough for watching online videos. I agree 100% with jerenept, 4-8GB is overkill for watching online videos. 

If your getting a new computer, 2GB will be more than good. 4GB is nice, but its better to spend the money you would have spend on getting 2GB extra on other stuff. 

BTW, video cards can help. ATI wont help as much as nVidia at the moment. Also, anything more than a 8600GT/9500GT/GT220 nVidia card is overkill as well ;)

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2010-06-13
cascade9 is saying that the cards he listed are relatively modest in terms of price and 3D power, but are great for HD video acceleration. A more powerful/expensive card is not going to help you unless you need more 3D performance (for more demanding games or other 3D tasks).

The GT220 is the ideal card for video playback and light gaming: [http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=nvidia_gt_220&num=9](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=nvidia_gt_220&num=9)
