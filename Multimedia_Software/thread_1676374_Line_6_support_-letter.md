### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2011-01-27
> I plan to mail, email and send via their feedback system. (would you consider this rude?)
It is not rude, but sending the same information more than once would be seen as spamming and then you would likely be ignored. Send something and wait for a response, if you actually get one then try to develop a correspondence with someone at the company.

The letter is good but I would make a few additions. Include links to these that show the demand for support, hard numbers are convincing even if they are small:
[http://www.tanzband-scream.at/line6/poll.php5?results](http://www.tanzband-scream.at/line6/poll.php5?results)
[http://line6.com/community/thread/4031](http://line6.com/community/thread/4031)

As the links above show, someone is already making a Linux driver for Line6 products. Your best bet should be to encourage Line6 to support this person (and any others) by opening the source code (Line6 will likely never open the code, but it is good practice to aim high). Also refer to M-Audio which is a rival company that has supported Linux with native drivers.

You could also say that Ubuntu has roughly 1 million users worldwide (according to Canonical). In order to show the audio production potential on Linux, show links to [KXStudio]("http://kxstudio.sourceforge.net/"), Ubuntu Studio, [AVLinux]("http://www.bandshed.net/AVLinux.html") and [puredyne]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puredyne").

The last 2 paragraphs I would leave out, Chrome OS is not Unix based and it is not about to take over major market share from anyone. Plus a business will not care about feeling special, they care about profit and market share.

Good luck, be sure to post any response you get back here.
