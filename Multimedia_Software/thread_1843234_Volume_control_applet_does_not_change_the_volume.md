### Post by kiki_h on 2011-09-13
Ubuntu 10.04 LTS recognizes the volume dial on my Logitech Media Elite Keyboard, which is nice. When turning it, there is a nice semi transparent pop-up indicating a change of volume. This is connected to the slider of the gnome-volume-control-applet: it moves up and down nicely.

But there is an issue: in spite of the visual indication the volume I hear is not changed at all. (There is sound, music and skype work fine - just on a fixed volume.)

Workaround: I installed pavucontrol. This allows me to change the actual volume. But it does not move the slider of the volume control applet. That does not matter much, but it is a little annoying to have to open pavucontrol as an extra application just to be able to change the volume when there is an applet in the panel and a dial on the keyboard.

(I am not sure if it is a gnome or multimedia issue, please move if this is not appropriate here.)
