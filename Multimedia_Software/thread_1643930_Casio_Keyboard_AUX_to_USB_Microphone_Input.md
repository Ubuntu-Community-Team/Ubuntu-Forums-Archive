### Post by Jose Catre-Vandis on 2010-12-12
OK, I have a similar keyboard CTK 611. If you are trying to output sound from the "Headphone" socket (what you are calling the aux connector) you will need to use a 1/4 " jack reducer to 3.5mm and connect to your PC at Line In, then record / listen to the sound as analog. The keyboard doesn't output an electrical/digital signal from the Heaphone output. This is what you use the midi ports for. You will have to fiddle about with settings on the keyboard, your midi interface and LMMS to get the right keybaord patch going to get the right sounds through midi. An AUX-USB cable is probably expecting to serve up an electrical/digital signal.

Also check through the trouble shooting section of the manual, especially page 54
