### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-04-23
Superior Drummer and EZDrummer by ToonTrack aren't made for Linux for a start. The other probably isn't either. I think you should perhaps have a look at what's available open source and perhaps delve into Ubuntu Studio for this. That comes with what you'll need pre-installed. I've never used Ubuntu for what you're doing although I do have UStudio installed on the desktop AV machine and have played around a bit. I dual-boot with Windows for all high-end audio stuff, but mostly nowadays for Sibelius. The furthest I go in Ubuntu is Audacity which I also use in Windows. 

Good luck and happy hunting. 

PS: Hydrogen is the drum app I was thinking of (default in UStudio). You might also want to have a look here:

[http://www.dogpile.com/info.dogpl.t5.1/search/web?fcoid=417&fcop=topnav&fpid=2&q=drum+loops+ubuntu&ql=&timestamp=1366696278531](http://www.dogpile.com/info.dogpl.t5.1/search/web?fcoid=417&fcop=topnav&fpid=2&q=drum+loops+ubuntu&ql=&timestamp=1366696278531)

You may also want to become familiar with Jack, the audio plugin. That can be your friend when things get complex.
