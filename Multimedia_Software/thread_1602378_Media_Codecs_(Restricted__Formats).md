### Post by P4man on 2010-10-21
Indeed, it depends on what country you live in, and almost everywhere outside the US you should be fine. Even in the US there is legal uncertainty about what is what isnt a patent infringement (for instance MP3 codecs). The chance it will ever be a legal problem for you as a person is  non existent as long as dont start distributing and selling illegal copies;  but if its a matter of principle for you, consider buying a codec pack:
[http://shop.canonical.com/product_info.php?products_id=244](http://shop.canonical.com/product_info.php?products_id=244)

Note that doesnt include quicktime and some others, but at least you can view your windows media files with a clear conscience that you didnt deprive poor MS from their royalties.
