### Post by markbuntu on 2008-11-20
That sounds more like a slow connection or a cpu issue than a problem with the players. I have a single core cpu machine that has big troubles streaming 1080p but anything less works fine, 1080p also plays fine offline. Check your connection speed and also your cpu to see if it is maxing out trying to download and play at the same time.

Another thing you can try is turning off your desktop effects, they use a lot of cpu cycles when a player is windowed.

I don't know if you resolved your problem since I was going over some old posts.

Did you try blacklisting ipv6?

Add to /etc/modprobe.d/blacklist:

```

# disable ipv6
blacklist ipv6

```

I get stuttering as well even with the blacklist.  The two right most numbers in the mplayer output are CPU% (0 3%) for a dual-core machine.  Definitely not CPU-bound.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2009-06-26
That appears to be the issue.  I have 7 Mbps ATT DSL which is 1 up and 6 down.  So simple math shows that 9.6 Mbps blows that limit.  Talking to an ATT tech who was installing fiber, he said that fiber is only going to the neighborhood junctions.  It's still copper to the house.  He said the limit with copper will always be ~10 Mbps.  ATT's Uverse Optical service will provide 2 simultaneous HD streams.  (These are rather severely compressed from what I've seen.)  So that seems to correspond with the poor HD feeds and audio syncing problems with Uverse and also steaming HD movie trailers--especially at 1080p.

You can still download them and watch them offline.  Not as convenient, but until ATT pulls fiber to the house, that is what we can expect.
