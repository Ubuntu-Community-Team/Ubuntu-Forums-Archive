### Post by eye208 on 2008-09-11
Make sure you are using suitable codecs in Avidemux. MP4 is a container for MPEG formats; it is mostly used for H.264 video and AAC audio. This will at least make the files playable on your computer. The walkman may impose additional restrictions in terms of bitrate, usage of B-frames etc.

If you simply copy audio and video it won't re-encode with the required codec. I guess the right option is x264, which will encode in h.264 compatible format.

You could also try [WinFF]("http://www.winff.org"). It is simpler than Avidemux for this kind of conversions. For instance, if you select mp4 as output it will only allow you to choose between h.264 (16:9) or h.264 (4:3).
