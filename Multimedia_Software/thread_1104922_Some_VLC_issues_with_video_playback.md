### Post by humphreybc on 2009-03-24
Hi there I am having some problems with VLC video playback. It's quite annoying because often I am watching a TV show or movie with my friends on my laptop and when Ubuntu screws up it doesn't give a great reputation to them...

These are the issues I am having:

1) When watching certain .avi files, every so often (about 5-10mins) the picture will freeze for around 10sec and the sound will continue, then the picture will return to normal after being garbled for a few frames.

2) I still get noticeable horizontal video tearing in videos (eg. concerts) that have very fast camera angle changes and lighting changes.

3) When using my HDMI output cable, the audio will cut out for around 5secs every 5 or 10 minutes.

4) Sometimes when I press 'F' to enter fullscreen mode, it will display a blank screen. It takes a few presses of 'F' to finally get it to display a picture.

5) Fast-forwarding can often screw up the image, freeze it, or not display the image at all.


Note: Yes, I have tried turning off Compiz, enabling X11 output, using a couple of different players, trying different drivers and messing around with the settings in VLC (but there are just too many to do trial and error with). None of what I have done so far has made ANY difference - the videos still play with Compiz turned on or off, X11 or default output, fullscreen or windowed and I still get the same problems.

Please bear in mind that the videos are still watchable, these are just minor problems that become very annoying after a while... I think it could be a codec problem but I am not sure. I am using the proprietary 9.2 Catalyst drivers.

VLC version 0.9.9-rc2 Grishenko

Thanks!
