### Post by dartmusic on 2009-05-07
I haven't seen much about Listen for the past couple of years, and to be honest, I tried it a couple of years ago and found it to be lacking in features that were important to me...mostly smooth handling of large collections.

With the current state of Amarok 2 and the impending demise of the Amarok 1.x branch, I have been on the lookout for a player that could do the following:

- handle large collections (I have nearly 50k tracks)
- handle cover art appropriately (Songbird is dodgy, Banshee is abysmal)
- be solid (no crashes or disappearing windows, etc., at least as much as any program)
- able to edit metadata swiftly, with lookups (CDDB, MusicBrainz, etc.)
- handle iPods appropriately (transfer artwork correctly, transcode when necessary - I have a lot of music in FLAC format)
- handle transferring music to a USB mass storage device AND playlist transfer/creation (unfortunately Listen doesn't do this, but then neither do any other program I've tried)
- watch your music library for changes (additions, deletions, etc.)
- fun stuff like Wikipedia/last.fm/similar tracks, favorites, etc. (while not necessary by any means, these are things that I've grown accustomed to in Amarok and would LIKE to find in a new player...anything to enhance the music listening experience)

I was quite surprised to find that there was recent development on Listen, so I loaded it today to find that it actually does quite a lot of what I'm looking for in a music player and management system.  

It's unfortunate that Amarok has become something completely different than what I and (obviously) many others have grown to love.  Rhythmbox is more than sufficient, but as noted above, I like a little bit of the "candy" with my player.  And while I like the fun stuff, I do not want it at the sacrifice of core features (stability, transcoding, artwork handling, music player transferring).  Songbird, while started with the best of intentions, seems to be caught up in a cycle of ignoring the core features at the expense of adding things like CD ripping (most OSes have this built in, right?  We have Grip...), and video playing.  Banshee is a downright nightmare; cover art functionality is sadly broken and doesn't look to be fixed any time soon, they advertise G1 integration, though that functionality is broken, and this list could go on.

I'm curious to find out if anyone has tried the new Listen, or has any information about their roadmap, etc.  In my (granted only 4 hours of) testing, Listen definitely seems promising.  Check it out:  [http://www.listen-project.org/](http://www.listen-project.org/)

Thanks.
