### Post by Flash858 on 2010-05-30
The Zune will not, and I repeat WILL NOT work natively in Ubuntu. It will not work in Rhythmbox, nor Amarok, nor Songbird (which is dropping Linux anyway), and while it will mount, you cannot do anything with it. You cannot run the Zune software in Wine either, so don't bother.

You have 2 options: Either do what was stated above, and dual boot Windows (what I do usually), or set up XP in a Virtual Box, and install Zune there (what I sometimes do). The advantage of the former is that it runs faster and smoother, and keeping track of your music is easier. The advantage of the latter is that you never have to switch out of Ubuntu, but as you will drop to 1.1 USB speed, and have to keep your library either on an external drive (not a bad thing at all, in fact, it has advantages) or duplicated on the Virtual Drive, it takes longer. If you watch TV shows on your Zune recorded from the Windows Media Center, conversion takes so ridiculously long, that I have never even been able to get through one. Basically, I keep it in a virtual box to sync a couple of (linux) Podcasts before going to the gym, but I do all of my music, TV shows, Videos, Audio Books, etc. in Windows.

I am surprised that this has not been worked out yet for Linux actually, as we have mono (so it should run in wine?), and it has been several years since this has been an issue, but suffice to say that though I love my Zune, and will continue to use the Zune Marketplace. However when mine dies, I will likely go with a different player that works in Linux natively, and switch completely to the Ubuntu One Music Store (by that time it should be fairly robust).
