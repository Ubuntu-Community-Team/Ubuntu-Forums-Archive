### Post by CosmicFlux on 2009-06-06
Hey,

I just thought I'd share this information for anyone who wants to use mainstream legal music download services (i.e. NOT Amarok) in Linux. 

The latest version of Napster has the ability to run from a web browser, independently of OS which means that you can now enjoy one of the great benefits of Windoze users. It's still not ideal as you can't actually play tracks from your HDD, only stream them but it's a step in the right direction and hopefully Napster and other large software manufacturers will start taking Linux more seriously.

One of my main reasons for having to put up with Windows Vista was the fact that I am a Napster-To-Go subscriber and have thousands of Mp3's that I couldn't use within Ubuntu because Napster didn't have a Linux client available. This latest release solves that problem, finally!

This is definitely a great development for Linux as it creates a real chance for users to switch completely over without having to run a dual boot/hybrid system just to deal with compatibility issues.


Cosmic
