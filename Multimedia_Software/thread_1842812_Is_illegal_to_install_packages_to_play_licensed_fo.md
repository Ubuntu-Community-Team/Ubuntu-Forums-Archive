### Post by Enigmapond on 2011-09-12
No..it is not illegal. Remembering that Ubuntu is a free and open source OS, these packages are affected by certain patents and license restrictions in some countries. With that in mind, Ubuntu is not able to include them in the default installation however they can be got after it's installed and used. The sources however are protected. That's all this means.

### Post by Lars Noodén on 2011-09-12
> **Enigmapond said:**
>  The sources however are protected. 

That's confusing copyright (distribution monopoly) with patents (usage monopoly) a little bit.  With patents, it does not matter where the software comes from, only what it does.  

The classic example was the patent problem with the GIF format.  

Users are on their own in regards to MP3 usage.  In some countries that means that, while not illegal per se, the patent owners can demand payment at a future date.  If or how they do that is anybody's guess.
