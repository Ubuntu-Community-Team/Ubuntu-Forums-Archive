### Post by EtherBunny on 2009-04-22
Thanks for the replies.

I'm kind of torn between Banshee and Exaile.

Banshee has good support for album art and it integrates with Gnome nicely, but it behaves really strangely at times. For example, when I open files, it doesn't add them to a playlist. It adds them to a "file-system queue" which can't be custom-sorted. Essentially, I have to open a bunch of files, then go to Banshee and move them all into a playlist, then sort the playlist as I want it, then hit play. On the other hand, the integration with Jaunty's new notification system looks amazing.

Exaile is missing some features. It doesn't support embedded album art and the global keyboard shortcuts plugin doesn't seem to work, but at least it gets the concept of a playlist. It's written in python, so I might try to fix these issues with a plugin or a patch.

Edit: Using Rhythmbox now. The only thing it's missing is some keyboard shortcuts to show/hide the player. It also works with Jaunty's notification system, though the album art doesn't show there. Hopefully this will be fixed though. Mark Shuttleworth [confirmed the bug]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/notify-osd/+bug/332356").
Edit(again): Rhythmbox has the same ridiculous non-custom-sortable playlist behavior as Banshee. Arrgh.. who makes these design decisions?

I'm still open to suggestions for new players, especially basic ones that don't put too much focus on having a music library. The music on my computer is constantly being added and deleted which library-based media players tend not to like.
