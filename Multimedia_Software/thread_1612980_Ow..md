### Post by efflandt on 2010-11-03
Did you originally install nvidia drivers from the repositories (Synaptic or Hardware Drivers) or some other way?

I would first try moving xorg.conf to a different filename.  You do not need it for Intel (unless you need to configure something special later).  However, glx may not work properly for 3D until you remove the nvidia drivers.  

But if you can boot to X and assuming you used Ubuntu drivers, then you could use Synaptic to remove them.  Look for anything to remove that mentions **nvidia-current** or anything **nvidia** that mentions 185, 180, 173, or 96.

You can leave the default xserver things related to nouveau or nv in case you later boot the drive on a system with nvidia.

### Post by lykeion on 2010-11-04
You shouldn't need to enable restricted driver for your intel chipset: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedDrivers#Intel](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedDrivers#Intel)
Install **xserver-xorg-video-intel** package, it provides the driver for the Intel i8xx and i9xx family of chipsets, including i810, i815, i830, i845, i855, i865, i915, i945 and i965 series chips.

Maybe someone with hands-on experience with intel video drivers can jump in here to give you more info.

### Post by lykeion on 2010-11-04
As mentioned earlier I think it's very hard to troubleshoot video problems for a driver I've no experience with. Here are some general tips regarding video drivers:[LIST]
[*]Read [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Reporting](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Reporting)
[*]Browse [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X) and see if there is some relevant information for you problem
[*]Update to latest Ubuntu version
[*]Maybe update to bleeding edge driver with PPA: [https://launchpad.net/~xorg-edgers/+archive/ppa](https://launchpad.net/~xorg-edgers/+archive/ppa)
[*]Try various options in `/etc/X11/xorg.conf` (see `man intel`)
[/LIST]
