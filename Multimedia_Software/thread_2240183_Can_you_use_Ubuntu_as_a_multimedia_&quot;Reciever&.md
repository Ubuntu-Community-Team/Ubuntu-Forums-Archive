### Post by TheFu on 2014-08-18
Either use VLC (server mode) or setup DLNA on all the boxes so you can "send-to".  The real issue is that this works for local media, not so much for internet-based media.

Also - the "any pc in the office" can include all sorts of weird things, so it cannot be guaranteed to work - not all of them support all the necessary codecs.

The easiest way I know to do this is with Plex Server for local media and using DLNA clients+renderers for viewing.  Lots of things are DLNA clients today - smartTVs, Bluray players, WD TV Live, chromecasts, roku, anything with android, or a PC running XBMC or other, similar software.

You have a bunch of research to do - something that sounds simple really isn't.  If you don't want to do the research and have lots of money - pay apple for their stuff and be done (er mostly).
