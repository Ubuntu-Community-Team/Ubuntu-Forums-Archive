### Post by jbruced on 2009-06-03
I'm feelin' your pain.

I fought this over a year ago, followed every guide, made changes I didn't understand, read config docs, tore my hair out etc.. 

Finally someone said use nvidia-settings.

So here's kinda what I did, and what I suggest, 

reboot, choose recovery mode, choose fix video problem(let it automatically write your xorg.conf), then do normal boot

find and install nvidia-settings through synaptic(guess you already got it so skip this)

look at hardware drivers under 
system > admin, and make sure nvidia driver is enabled and in use.

Open a terminal and type

sudo nvidia-settings


poke around the gui, click and drag screens, set twinview etc..
choose apply
choose save settings


reboot right after doing that, don't test yet! (it wouldn't work correctly till a reboot for me) 


After that, works fantastic for me. I let nvidia-settings do the settings, nvidia should know better than anyone else.


GL
Bruce

P.S. Let us know if that worked.
