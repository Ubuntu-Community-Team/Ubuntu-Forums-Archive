### Post by goodstuff9 on 2020-07-30
Ubuntu 18.04.4, and gnome shell.  

Until recently, for my Ubuntu desktop computer all audio played out of a sound output device called "Line-Out - Built-in Audio".  I have never had any issues with that.  

When I work from home, I remotely connect my Ubuntu desktop computer to my employer's Windows 10 computer (xfreerdp).  While working this way I will often have music playing through the Line-Out - Built-in Audio sound output device of my Ubuntu desktop computer to my desktop speakers.  No problem.  

Recently my employer asked that I use an employer-provided phone number.  They gave me software to install (Avaya one-X) that "routes" calls to and from my work phone number through my employer Windows 10 computer.  I also have a Plantronics Savi W740 telephone headset plugged into my Ubuntu desktop computer.  

Now, I see an additional, new sound output device on my Ubuntu desktop computer:  Analog Output - Plantronics Savi 7xx-M.  

Here is the problem:  When working on my computer with music playing through the Line-Out - Built-in Audio sound output device to my desktop speakers, if I receive a work call on my work phone number, when I put on the phone headset, I hear nothing.  What I have to do is first turn off the music, and then go into settings on my Ubuntu computer, go to sound, and in the output tab manually change the sound output device from Line-Out - Built-in Audio, to Analog Output - Plantronics Savi 7xx-M.  Then I can hear the call - and only the call - through the Plantronics headset.  

Is there no fancy pants software that will automagically do all that for me - i.e., stop the music and change the sound output device on the Ubuntu computer - when the Ubuntu computer detects that I'm using the Plantronics headset for a phone call?
