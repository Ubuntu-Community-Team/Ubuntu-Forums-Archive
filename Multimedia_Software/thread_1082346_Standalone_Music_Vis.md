Hi all,

i was wondering if there were any standalone music visualisation programs for Ubuntu?  (there are a couple for Windows but none of them seem to hook into the audio data, and work independent from it, in other words they work even when no audio is playing and don't use beat detection etc.)

The reason i'm asking is that it's often the case that you might like a particular audio visualisation in one music program, but you don't like the program itself and would prefer to use another one to play music.

With a standalone music visualisation you could just choose the audio output which is playing your music (ALSA, OSS etc) and the standalone audio visualisation would use that audio output to control its graphical display (hopefully with beat detection etc).  That way your music program and your visualisations are kept separate (like the design and content of websites are kept separate with Style Sheets).  And seeing how Linux is open source, you could begin to write your own visualisations (preferably in a GUI) and share them with everyone else.

I'm pretty useless at the kind of coding necessary to create a music vis program myself, but if there isn't a standalone program which already does that, it might be a nice project for one of you uber-linux-coders out there.
