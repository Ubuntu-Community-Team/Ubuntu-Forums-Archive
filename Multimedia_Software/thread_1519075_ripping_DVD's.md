### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-06-27
**k9copy** is good, or if you can afford the hard drive space, just "rip" the dvd into an iso file and use something like smplayer to play it back.
Sometimes DVDs can be quiet, so I use the normalise audio feature in smplayer.

To rip to an iso, just right click on the dvd on your desktop ans select copy disc, then follow tghe prompts to save to the hard disk.

### Post by JohnAStebbins on 2010-06-28
> **GlazedWicker said:**
> I can't wait for a stable version of Handbrake that works with lucid...

Then don't.  Historically, the snapshots are just as 'stable' as the release.  

The real difference is a support issue.  After a 'stable' release, we gain experience with what that releases bugs are and are able to quickly help people when they come to the handbrake forums.  Whereas with the snapshot, we haven't built up this experience and sometimes have no quick answer.

Note that the quick answer we very often give to users that have problems with the release is, "Use the snapshot."
