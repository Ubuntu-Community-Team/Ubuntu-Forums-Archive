### Post by ghostborg on 2009-05-16
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats")

Depending on your distro you need to install the Ubuntu restricted extras.
For Ubuntu:

sudo apt-get install ubuntu-restricted-extras


Sound Juicer and it is integrated into Rhythmbox, I believe.
Menu Applications Under Sound and Video its called CD Audio Extractor, under preferences you need to change the output format to MP3 once your codecs are installed.

This worked for me.
Sound Converter is another useful tool, slow though.
I also installed the codecs from 
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu")

Checkout Category5.tv
They have a linux script called perfectbuntu .
Under their Menu Resources then Linux Scripts.
[http://www.category5.tv/content/blogcategory/15/77/]("http://www.category5.tv/content/blogcategory/15/77/")

Hope this helps and good luck to ya!
