### Post by ankspo71 on 2009-12-13
Hi,

When I was using Ubuntu 9.04, I had similar problems. I searched around and found out that it was a problem with Fspot. I don't recall the error number I got though, but maybe this will help.

See **"2) Camera doesn’t mount when connected and switched on in Ubuntu 9.04(Jaunty)"**
[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/some-of-known-ubuntu-904jaunty-jackalope-bugs-with-workarounds.html](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/some-of-known-ubuntu-904jaunty-jackalope-bugs-with-workarounds.html)

You can also exit fspot, then locate your camera contents/drive in the places menu, or look inside the /media/ folder for your camera. 

It's a bit of a hassle but it can be done.  I haven't used my camera (an easyshare too  btw) long enough to look for alternatives to fspot. I don't think I have used my camera in Karmic yet either.

I hope this helps,
James
