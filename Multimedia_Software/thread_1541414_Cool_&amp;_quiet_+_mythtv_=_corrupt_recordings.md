### Post by kamolt on 2010-07-29
Not so Long ago I gave an AMD (A8N32-SLI) based PC a new life as a MythTv back-end, it serves three front-ends. In the beginning all worked very well,  the SD recordings as well as the HD recordings were perfect. Gradually the picture was less perfect and more and more artifacts and jittery appeared. I thought it was a reception problem and repositioned my dish but to no avail.
Finally after a lot of desperate attempts I disabled the cool & quiet option in the bios and the picture was perfect once again. In the mythtv wiki I read that cool & quiet could influence the front-end but I was unaware that it could cause bad recordings. After all the backend only records the signal it receives, it does not recode. The kernel upgrades were probably the reason why the backend worked OK in the beginning and deteriorate later. I hope this post will be of help for others
