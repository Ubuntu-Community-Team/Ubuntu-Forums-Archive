### Post by Rossi9x87 on 2009-11-30
Those months ago, I ended up purchasing the SRM and I have to say that it is just spectacular.  It works cleanly on 64-bit, no install, minimal configuration issues and it delivers a punch. In short, it does exactly what I wanted it to do.  

I also got it to take an optical line out and pumped that into my home stereo system, its overkill, but that opens up a whole new box of neighbor-annoying fun...

### Post by Rossi9x87 on 2009-12-02
Likely true, and there are no linux software packages as you surmised.  At least with the SRM, you lose the additional software equalizer ability and some of the optional effects (so you don't get all of the bang for your buck) but running it into your home theater like what I have done lets the receiver do all of the decoding, and with a good media player you still get epic sound.  And vice-versa, tossing a mic or midi into the S/PDIF lets you do some quality recording with minimal issues.  

Not too sure what the Amigo is capable of and what its limitations are, but I am satisfied with the SRM.
