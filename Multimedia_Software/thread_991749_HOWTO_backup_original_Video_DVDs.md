Hi there!

Since I've been working the whole sunday on backing up a original video dvd that I legally own (perfectly legal in Austria where I reside) under Ubuntu, I wanted to share what I found out.

-) First, use the command "dvdbackup -M" to copy the whole dvd to your hard-disk. before that, switch to a directory where you want to temporarly save the disc. you probably need 9gb for that.

-) Second, use k9copy. Not on the original disc, but on the copy of the dvd that resides on your hard-disk. To do that, you have to use the button thats beside the textbox that shows the name of your dvd-drive. the buttons name (tooltip) is "Open a folder" After that, you can select the titles, languages and subtitles that you want to keep on your copy (drop what you dont need, otherwise the quality can get quite bad) and then use the dvd copy button to either make a iso file that can be burnt, or directly burn to dvd.

Thats it. that worked for me. Directly using k9copy on the dvd produces fatal errors.
