### Post by needhelpplease on 2009-06-08
Every time I install a new release, I notice there are big changes in the sound system, and yet, one thing remains the same always:

Sound doesn't work.

I have to spend an hour or two reading forum posts, messing around with settings, and whatever else, to get a sound out of the computer.

Oh and then when it works, DVD playback is so quiet that it's unusable, something which has been a problem on laptops for YEARS now, so once I finally get it working, I have to play DVDs using:

mplayer -softvol -softvol-max 5000

And then after that's all working I have to spend another hour (or three or four) figuring out how to get audio to work properly in Skype.

I'm not a novice Linux user, not at all.  I've been using Linux on the desktop for the past decade and I'm a professional software developer, mainly in the Linux platform.

If sound is this problematic for me, and it happens this way EVERY SINGLE TIME A NEW RELEASE COMES OUT, obviously, there is something badly wrong with the way sound is working within Linux.

Computers have had sound support as a standard feature for at least the past 10 years, probably the past 15 years.

Basic, simple, sound is ultimately not that complicated: it's just some signals to the sound controller.

This is probably the single ugliest failing within the attempt to make Linux into a desktop operating system.  I say that as a confirmed, long-term, 100% Linux desktop user, as someone who has never even owned a Windows XP or Vista PC (including no dual-boot), no Mac, only Linux.
