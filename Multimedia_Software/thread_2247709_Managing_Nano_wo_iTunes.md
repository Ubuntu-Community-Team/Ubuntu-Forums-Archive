### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-10-09
I've used [floola]("http://www.floola.com/home/") to move music around.  You must use the 32-bit version on 32-bit machines, and 64-bit on 64-bit machines.  There are Windows and Mac versions.  So conceivably you could have 4 copies of floola on your Nano--all using the same music database.  If you use multiple copies of floola, give them different names!

### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-10-10
Horrors!  Sorry to get your expectations up.  That is why it was given to you.  Not useful outside the Apple Garden.

A quick google search shows that there is nothing available to connect an Apple Nano 7g to linux.  I did find that [mediamonkey]("http://www.mediamonkey.com/news/") works on Android and the 7g.  So if you have an Android phone, you could move music (with difficulty) from linux to Android and then use Mediamonkey to dump it to the Nano 7g.
