### Post by snakeman21 on 2009-09-26
Most mp3 players will work... Do not get a Zune, they currently do not work under Linux.  Every other model I have tried works just fine.  Linux is very iPod friendly (more so than windows or mac, IMO).  My suggestion is to shop around for one online.  When you find a model that you like, visit this forum and do a search for it to see if it will work or not.  Or you could go to google and type the brand and model you're looking at, followed by the word "Linux" and see what pops up.  Good luck!

### Post by Rifester on 2009-09-26
After switching to Ubuntu I was so happy with the OS that I finally decided to digitize all of my cd music.  After much reading I ordered a Sansa Fuze (which I am very happy with).  You can insert a micro sd card for more storage.   I also ordered one for my wife and the company accidentally sent her a Sansa Clip.   Which I think I like better.   It is very small and will not display cover art, but functions well and has great sound.  Both work well with Ubuntu.  Just be sure to switch them to MSC mode prior to moving files to the player.    The only negative I can think of compared to Ipod is there are many cool accessories with the Ipod.   My MP3 player spends much of it's time playing music through my home stereo.   It would be nice to be able to control it by remote.   Not gonna happen....  But I don't mind.  I am glad to be free of MS and Apple.
