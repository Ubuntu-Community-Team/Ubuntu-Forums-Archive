[FONT="Georgia"]Hey all, I'm just wondering if Ubuntu will play better hi-def HDMI video than Windows 7? I just purchased a new Acer Aspire 1551-3876 and attached it to my 46" TV. It plays well when I play on the unit's small screen, but not on the larger display.
Anyway, I was just curious if Ubuntu would provide better playback because it is less of a resource hog, and whether I should give it a whirl?[/FONT]

### Post by teejay17 on 2011-07-25
Well I went and answered my own question: Ubuntu (in this case 11.04 with Unity installed) does indeed play HDMI better than Windows 7. This machine I am running is a good test because it is a very limited piece of hardware; the processor is an AMD 1.4ghz dual core with built in Radeon graphics. 
When I attempted to play using Windows 7, let's just say the experience was frustrating and choppy. With Ubuntu it didn't solve the deficiencies with the hardware, but it did play video through to my TV smoothly, and that is a great start.
