MarianeMarine, I am rather new to ubuntu, but I could not get k9 copy to work either. It did shrink down a commercial dvd, but when I burned them and tried to play them they were buggy. I have had good luck with dvd95 and acidrip; dvd95 shrinks commercial dvd's down to burnable size and acidrip makes avi files from dvds. BTW, if you are using Nautilus in drag and drop mode to burn dvd's it is best not to. It's better to burn from an ISO file. Most dvd shrink programs will make an iso file. From there, cancel the window that pops up when you insert a blank dvd and then is double click on the iso file. Nautilus will give you a window to burn the iso file, which will extract the DVD files and burn them. If you get any burn errors, you can also slow down the burn rate.
