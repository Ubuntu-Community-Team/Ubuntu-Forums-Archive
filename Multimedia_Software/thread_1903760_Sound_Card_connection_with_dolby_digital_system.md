### Post by weesol on 2012-03-06
Hi Kingrat

The problem you are having is due to the headphone port on your computer is a stereo output (ie L+R), a simple fix may be found as an audio setting ie stereo bypass (its not 5.1 or 7.1 but it will make equal sound out of all speakers.  for proper Surround Sound look at the back of your computer to see if you have an optical port,  if you do you may be able to connect the surround sound system (SSS) only if SSS supports optical in.

in regards to your second issue ie the sound quality, that is down to using the microphone port on your SSS.

by the sounds of it your DVD SSS may be the type which has a Microphone port only for Karaoke, and so will not have audio quality (cant be certain without make and model number).
have a look at the back of the DVD if theres an audio in port that will have much better sound quality, the audio in ports may have either 6 or 8 ports dependent on 5.1 or 7.1 which should connect to the back of your pc via 6 3.5 jacks (if you are able to use fiber optic you will not need this)
