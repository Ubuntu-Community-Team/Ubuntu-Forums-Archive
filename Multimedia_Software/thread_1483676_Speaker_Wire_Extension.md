### Post by GregBrannon on 2010-05-14
A pair of wires of approximately the same gauge or larger should be fine.  I've seen people use paired power cord wires like those found on smaller lamps.  Compare the gauge of the headphone cord you are thinking of using, and if it's noticeably smaller than the existing wires, try to find something else.  You won't notice any difference in sound quality adding 10 feet of wire, unless you do something wrong.

Speaker wiring is pretty simple; a plus and a minus.  Standard speaker wire usually has one of the wires marked with a colored stripe on the insulation or even a different colored wire so that you can hook them up correctly when the connections are made directly to the bare wire rather than with a plug.  The speakers will work even hooked up incorrectly, but the sound may be affected.  It's very hard to tell.

There's usually no "power" to the speakers unless you have powered speakers.  In that case, there should be a separate power cord that plugs into a wall outlet.
