### Post by cariboo on 2009-01-05
I would suggest that you clean out all the fans in your computer, using a can of compressed air, be sure to hold the fans from spinning while blowing them out.

Note: do not use a shop compressor unless it has some sort of air dryer installed.

Jim

### Post by baudday on 2009-01-05
Thank you, I cleaned it out. I hadn't looked at it in a long while, and a lot of dust had collected, and that was causing the temperature to rise, and the fan having to work extra hard. I always wondered why my computer was so loud, but never really checked into it. This seems to have solved the problem.
