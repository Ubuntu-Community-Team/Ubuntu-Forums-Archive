### Post by rossmoore on 2009-05-21
I have a Sansa Fuze, which has 8gb of on-board memory and takes SD cards. The music database allows you to browse by Artist, Album or whatever and unifies the files from both on-board memory and the SD card.
Sound quality is, IMO, pretty good - especially for the price.
Downsides:
a) you have to manage music transfers from your computer to your player and the SD card separately.
b) the screen scratches pretty easily, especially in comparison to the much more expensive Cowon and iPod devices with anti-scratch screens.

Otherwise great battery life, nice clear screen, and supports ogg, mp3, wma and flac (but not m4a).

### Post by raffraffraff on 2009-05-23
Nokia N95
It's not an MP3 player, but it's got an excellent remote mic with audio controls and a 3.5" socket for earphones. It supports WMA, MP3 and M4a. It integrates the music player and phone functions perfectly too - if I'm cycling and the phone rings, it tells me the name of the caller (in a Stephen Hawkins way) and fades the music out for the call. When I hang up, it fades the music back in. But the killer functions are really based on the fact that it's a teeny tiny computer that you can write software for. Example: with Mobbler, I can scrobble to Last FM or play streams from it.  I can transfer music several ways - WiFi, Bluetooth, USB cable or by simply removing the 8GB card and plugging it into my laptop. If you were to offer me the 'best MP3 player in the world' I wouldn't take it.

Creative Zen X-Fi
I bought this for my wife. It got rave reviews. It's supposed to be able to play AAC files (which makes up about 50% of my music). But does it actually support AAC files? Maybe some weird Creative Labs type of AAC, but not any of mine. Believe me, I've tried everything (MP4Box, iTunes, AtomicParsley) to mod the files, but they simply won't play when I put them on. The sound quality is good, but it has a tendency to 'hang' sometimes. It also turns itself on and starts playing automatically when you plug it into the USB. Really stupid.

### Post by c_wilson on 2009-05-23
i have to second (or is it third) the sansa recomendations. I bought a fuze a month ago and it's been great. To my ears the sound quality is great and over at head-fi.org more accomplished ears seem to agree. It supports ogg and wma, also supports flac (yes it works!) if you're really interested in great sound. The seamless microSD card integration means that as memory prices continue to plummet and capacities expand, you get a bigger player on the cheap. I use it in drag and drop mode with Jaunty 64bit and it works flawlessly... also seemed to work fine with Rhythmbox.

EDIT: it doesn't come with a wall charger but i got a wall and car charger together for about $5 on amazon that work fine.
