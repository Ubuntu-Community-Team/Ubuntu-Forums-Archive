### Post by Saidear on 2010-07-30
I installed ALSA to do something with bluetooth (iirc, apparently bluetooth headsets worked better or some such with ALSA) since PulseAudio at the time wasn't doing the trick.  Problem is, I don't know how to remove ALSA now that PulseAudio seems to work instead.  

Is there a way to go about it, and clean up my Sound menu at the same time, without completely gimping my ability to watch DVDs and movies?

---

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-07-30
You need ALSA installed to use pulse, and ALSA is installed by default on Ubuntu. Pulse is a sound server that works on top of ALSA, so you don't need to remove anything.
If you want to clean up your menu there is an app in the preferences menu for that.
