### Post by internalkernel on 2009-07-20
We usually use what's called a ground lift... There is a very long winded explanation that has to do with the way the electric currents in a typical building or structure are grounded... all electrical circuits are connected to the same ground. All that excess electricity being grounded down the same line cause a feedback hum in microphones. 

Basically... if you remove the ground from the computer, you won't hear this sound. They cost about .49$ at Home Depot, those little orange plugs that turn a three pronged plug into a two. That's a ground lift. 

Now, whether you want to remove the ground from your laptop is entirely up to you... obviously, it goes without saying a surge would probably fry your system. (I sometimes do work as an A/V tech, we setup lots of sound systems connected to computers.)

Most houses are wired for 10 or 15 amps, which should be sufficient.
