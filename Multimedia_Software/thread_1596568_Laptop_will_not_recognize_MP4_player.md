### Post by Scott. on 2010-10-14
I have a cheap 32GB MP4 player I bought on Ebay.  I was made in China.  I paid $20.00 for it.  Under WinXP my laptop recognized it as a flash disk.  I used Win Media Player 11 to Sync up my downloads.  WMP 11 did NOT have to be running in order for the OS to recongnize the player.  I could treat it just like a jump drive.

Ubuntu 9.1 (64bit) won't recognize it.  I don't use ver. 10.04 or 10.10 due to problems with video, wireless and other problems.  9.1 works like a dream on ALMOST everything.  This issue is a big one for me.  I'm tired of dual booting.  Any ideas would be appreciated.

Thanks - Scott

### Post by Scott. on 2010-10-15
Fixed It!  I wiped the MP4 player clean with a FAT32 format.  Then I reSynced the a file sync program I use called AllWaySync.  It gives a choice on how to handle the target disk.  It defaults to Windows Folder.  I changed it to Removable Disk.  Not only was it recognized, Ubuntu knew it was an MP4 audio player.  Another fortunate accident.  (Haven't looked for AllWaySync for Linux yet.
