### Post by InspiredIndividual on 2009-04-03
I suppose you're using the 32-bit version of Ubuntu 8.10 and you're trying to access Flash objects on websites (e.g. Youtube videos)? As far as I know, it's perfectly normal Flash doesn't show up in Applications > Internet. What happens when you try to view a Flash video, say a Youtube video using Ubuntu's standard browser Firefox? I think that if you are unable to watch the video, there should be some kind of dialog to help you install the necessary files. If you're still having issues, the Comprehensive Multimedia & Video Howto on this forum may be useful: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=766683](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=766683) . It helped me a lot when I had issues with Flash after switching to Ubuntu.

If you want to watch Flash video's offline (say, you want to watch video.flv), the default media player in Ubuntu 8.10 (Totem) should be able to play them. Applications > Sound & Video > Movie Player. I get the feeling I'm not correctly understanding your question, though, so please elaborate if my reply hasn't solved your problems.
