### Post by PlainShane on 2008-09-15
Let me begin this by saying that I am just your average Ubuntu user - can build a PC, have had the internet since AOL blew up, began to grow tired of XP after 7 years, did not like Vista and decided to try Ubuntu after throwing away old perceptions of Linux.

Would you consider the ability to sort your library or playlist by artist, genre, title, album, etc... a "feature"? Would you consider the ability for an audio player to display embedded album artwork a "feature"? No, those are requirements carrying such a strong expectation that their existence does not burst into conscious thought. I am a die hard Media Monkey user on the windows side. Understanding that this open source and free environment I decided to install will have some faults is certainly whole. There was no expectation for a Media Monkey replacement when I switched over to Ubuntu but come on... those are standard features.

I have used Rhythm Box, Banshee, and Songbird. The first can sort my music correctly by artist but loves to go out on the web and search for album art when there is higher quality & **correct** images already embedded within the mp3 files. I have not found out a way to bypass this and force it to read what it should. Banshee reads embedded art maybe 25% of the time and is completely incapable of sorting/filtering files. Below is a screenshot of this in action. Songbird is just not my cup of tea. I apologize for sounding whiny but I find these absences absurd and borderline comical for one, am incapable of understanding why all windows audio players can do this but not linux ones, and am dumbfounded at the responses I got at the Banshee support forums that should really be considered high handed slaps in the face.

Most of the music I download is correctly tagged due to the high requirements put upon the source by its parent source. Otherwise, Media Monkey has an auto-tag feature that will retrieve information and I can choose what to apply and whether or not to write id3 v1, v2, or both. There is confidence in this statement; my music is correctly tagged.

Am I the only one that sees this or is there a solution that someone can humiliate me with? Please.
