### Post by jpkotta on 2008-09-05
You have an AGP slot. [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agp](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agp)

You can get more information about the PCI (and AGP) slots with the lspci command.

In the past, nVidia cards had better drivers.  ATI is catching up, though.  I would probably still go nVidia if I bought a card today.

### Post by doas777 on 2008-09-13
well, to the poster that found the AGP card did not fit, you may have different speeds of card and slot. AGP came in 1x,2x, 4x, and 8x flavors. most slots still around today are 4x/8x (will seat a 4x or an 8x card). older 1/2x or 2/4x slots are less common. 

also, most AGP slots turn the card upside down, so that the GPU is facing to the floor rather than the powersupply.


in terms of maker, a year ago i would have said buy nvidia, but nowadays it seems ATI has better linux support at the moment. I've never been able to get TV outs to work with an ATI card, but they were always older ones, like 9250's and i was using frglx. 

anyway good luck,
