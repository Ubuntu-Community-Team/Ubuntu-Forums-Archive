### Post by rossmoore on 2010-04-19
Here's my understanding of the situation:
You will be able to pay just about any music format on any platform (windows, linux, mac) provided it isn't locked down with DRM.
MP3 is the lowest common denominator of music formats - it's reasonable quality, and most importantly every portable music player will be able to play it.

If your music is another format - ogg, flac, aac/m4a, wma - there will be a bit of software or a portable music player that won't be able to play it. That means you'll have to transcode from one format to another, and (in most cases) lose audio quality along the way and take up time for your CPU to do the transcoding.

The exception to this is flac. Flac is lossless, so you lose nothing transcoding to whatever format your portable music player wants. The downside is that the files are big.

Lastly, ogg and wma both give better audio quality for a given file size / bit rate than mp3.

I personally have 90% of my music in mp3 due to historical reasons and that's how you can buy it from 7digital / Ubuntu in Lucid, and the quality is fine provided the bit rate is high enough. Not brilliant, but fine. The rest is stuff I've ripped from CD recently, which I now store in Flac because my music player (Nexus One) plays it.
