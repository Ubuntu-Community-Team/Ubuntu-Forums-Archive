### Post by Seq on 2009-01-16
> **kyroha said:**
> I think the easiest way would be to pick up a TV tuner card. The ones that seem to work best with Linux are from Hauppauge. I have done what you are trying to do with my PS2 and it worked fine.

I tried this a number of years ago and cannot recommend it. Audio and video is synced, but there is typically about a 0.5 second delay (the cards usually encode the video and audio stream to MPEG, then your video player then decodes and plays it).

0.5 second delay does not typically matter since audio and video are synced up, unless you are actually needing interactivity -- which a game system does. I found it very, very annoying.

That said, I had previously used a component->vga adapter and had connected directly to my monitor on the unused VGA port. It worked acceptably well.

If the main issue is proximity to the router, I think it would be more economical to buy a longer ethernet cable for the 360. The Wii only has a wireless card by default...

### Post by Soo on 2009-01-17
The latency is too high to actually run it through your video card. What you want is an adapter from (component/composite) to (DVI/VGA). Otherwise you'll be playing with like a 1-second delay from button press to what happens on screen.
