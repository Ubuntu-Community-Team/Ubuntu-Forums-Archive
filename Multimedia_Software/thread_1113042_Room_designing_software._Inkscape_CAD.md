### Post by Linoobius on 2009-04-01
Why not go all out and do it in 3D using Blender, Qcad will work good for 2D applications, it's takes a bit to get used to it especially if you're used to AutoCAD or Microstation but it's not too bad. Those are probably your best two options in the Linux environment. I don't have experience with Inkscape but have used Xara for vector graphics and if you just want quick and dirty without much learning curve, vector graphics might be the right choice.
