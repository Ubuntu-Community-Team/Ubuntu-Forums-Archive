### Post by wintercorn on 2010-02-28
Short answer: No, there seems to be absolutely no possibility in the linux world to watch movies with a secondary subtitle track displayed. I've been searching for a way to get this done for years and found nothing.

The only workaround I know of is to re-encode the video, hardcode at least one subtitle track and let the player display the other one on top. However, calling this a pain would be an understatement. You can expect to spend days searching through forums and playing with avidemux/dvd:rip etc configs to figure out how to get what you want. Graphic-based subtitles that are common with non-Latin scripts make things even worse.

I ended up installing Windows again just for watching movies because I badly need this feature (friends with different native tongues, language learning) and it's a standard feature in many windows players.
