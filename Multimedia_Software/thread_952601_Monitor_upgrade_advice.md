### Post by kingtiger01 on 2008-10-19
Setting up a monitor in Ubuntu is easy as pie, and no you don't need a "32 inch" monitor to achieve 1600x + resolution. most modern LCD monitors in the 17" to 22" Variety offer resolutions around that.

The problem is you most likely have a TFT LCD monitor in that laptop. there pinned, and usually VERY difficult to Put a new one in(unless its from another laptop. then its just modding the existing pins, if it applies.)

So.. if you're serious, and think you can do it.. You should make a check list..

1. Find a TFT LCD monitor that supports you're range, and is the same size as the existing monitor's frame.(other wise you will be needing to make you're own hinges and frame. not as easy as it sounds. unless you do it often)

2. Finding as much as you can about you're Current monitor and this monitor, as you will need to know what each pin does (G A B B A R G B G - most older Analog*DSUB BASED* dell's pin out)

3. Buy the tools to do it RIGHT, you should NEVER, i mean NEVER, Mess around with monitors without Grounding you're self some how, and ALWAYS, use the Right Screwdriver for the job. If it feels like it don't Fit DON'T TRY!
