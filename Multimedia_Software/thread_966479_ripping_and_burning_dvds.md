### Post by VeeDubb on 2008-11-09
DVD:RIP 'looks' very complicated, but is actually about as simple as it gets.

Step 1. Put a DVD in the drive

Step 2. Launch DVDrip

Step 3. Fill out all the information and 'do' everything that each tab let's you do, one tab at a time.  Don't move on to the next tab until you've done everything on the tab you're working on.

Step 4. The last tab is the ugliest of all.  My advice is not to adjust anything unless you know what you're doing.  The only thing I recommend you adjust is the quality/size setting in the lower left quarter of the tab.  Adjust them to whatever you're trying to do.  If you go with a manual BPP setting instead of setting a size, I find that 55 makes something about halfway in between VHS and DVD, and is VERY watchable.

That's really all it is.  I had somebody suggest the same thing to me, and it took me all of 3 or 4 tries to get it right.



Now, if all you want to do is make a copy of a DVD, install and use DVD95.  Copying a DVD is all it's good for, but it's VERY easy to use.
