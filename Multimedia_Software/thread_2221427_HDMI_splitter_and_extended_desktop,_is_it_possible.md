### Post by TheFu on 2014-05-02
No. You need two distinct outputs from the video card.
There are USB-based video cards for non-gaming uses, which might be an option?  Of course, verify the support is built-into the Linux kernel and NOT some drivers that must be downloaded from a vendor website.  I've been burned on that with RAID HBAs and never plan to do that again for ANY hardware.  If the Linux kernel doesn't support the hardware, then I don't want it.

### Post by dannyboy79 on 2014-05-02
a splitter would only split your signal on to two displays, which would be a mirror. this is only good for if you want whatever is on your 1st screen to show up on your second screen for presentations and stuff like that. you would not be able to extend your desktop.

like the previous poster suggested, if it's only for non-gaming stuff than a usb video card may work. like this one, [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16815153012](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16815153012)
