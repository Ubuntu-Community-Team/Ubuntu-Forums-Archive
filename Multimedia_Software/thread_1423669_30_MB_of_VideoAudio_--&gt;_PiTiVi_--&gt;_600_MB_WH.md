### Post by xtnsgo on 2010-03-07
Revolutionary101's questions make a lot of sense and are certainly worth exploring.  

Just a suggestion, though:  have you tried Kdenlive? Very flexible, easy to use, and its stabiity issues are greatly exaggerated.  

With prudent saving, it far outdoes PiTiVi, Kino, Openshot, and LiVes capability-wise.  It looks and acts a lot like Sony ACiD, only with video in addition to audio.  Multiple video/audio tracks (my current project has 4/4, respectively with about 50 elements--video clips, audio clips, stills, and titles), drag and drop functionality, a deep undo history, a host of audio/video effects and transitions, etc...   And with the right libraries installed, great container/format support as well (it will input anything VLC can play, so far--with a ton of output options as well).  

Memory footprint is surprisingly small as well--i can work comfortably (with a little lag, but nothing serious) with my Core dinosaur and 2Gb of memory.  

Just a thought.

Best of luck!
