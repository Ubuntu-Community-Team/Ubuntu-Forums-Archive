### Post by Neobuntu on 2010-10-03
If anyone finds this, just install DigiKam. It will also show the NEF (thumbnail from it), and edit it, by first automatically developing the (adjustable as 16bit) Raw; before it goes to the same (DigiKam) editor, that you would use for JPEG.

Note: Nautilus (Gnome file browser) just shows the NEF thumbnails, now-a-days (you may just need to load some non-free codecs, it's easy). I also set ufraw; for developing (viewing larger) NEF files; from a click in Nautilus. Set your "Open with" through PREFERENCES; to make them stick! Make sure you go through preferences first, or it will not stick (till that's fixed). I use it; just to see a NEF, larger view, and an alternate Raw developer, if/when I happen to be browsing with Nautilus. Better to just bring up DigiKam (In my experience), however. Browse, and or edit, with DigiKam. Including 16-bit Raw conversion; with adjustments that makes Raw relevant.

The is no right or wrong with JPEG vs. Raw. There are pros and cons, to each. It depends a lot on the camera and Raw software (and computer speed), too. You do NOT have to do one or the other.  

DigiKam offers me no trouble; being run in Ubuntu's (10.04) Gnome.

Miscellaneous: ufraw can send to Gimp; with a click. You can also install the ufraw, gimp plug-in. Then, if you click on a NEF with Nautilus, and it is set to open with Gimp, it will go through ufraw. I use DigiKam instead, and send a compressed PNG (zero loss, little bitty living space) to Gimp, for rare pixel manipulation (removing spots, repainting stuff, using layers etc...). 

I only use PNG for the transition(if needed), and ALWAYS save the keepers, UNCHANGED, original files (Raw or JPEG), and save some version (or two or three) of the finished product (I don't do "catalogs" and I delete the (big) temp work png; when I'm finished. I always have the original); depending on the desired outcome (Print/Web/distribution). Always adjusting the balance, of any noise (some NR in the initial Raw conversion; but The final NR on the resized picture); with a sharpness/NR matched (slight "grain" and no color noise), to the already resized product, only.
