### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2010-06-13
Xorg.0.log is the log of the most recent X session. My idea was to boot in non-recovery mode so the error occurs, then boot using a livecd so you can mount your root filesystem and post the file that way.

I guess you could also boot normally to incur the error, then boot into recovery mode and the log you want will be Xorg.0.log.old. You could copy it to a usb stick (or you could even use a text-based web browser if you know how).

### Post by cwinter on 2010-06-13
Thanks for your help.

I found a fix to this problem.

There was neither xorg.conf file in /etc/X11/  so I created one from one of the other xorg.conf. bak files and then edited the HorizSync and Vert Refresh lines so that they fell within the limits of my monitor.
