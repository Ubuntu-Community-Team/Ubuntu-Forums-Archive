### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-08-18
A modern netbook should be OK for light audio work, for multitrack projects you might want to look into using a lightweight DE like openbox or iceWM. I have a 10 year old laptop with similar specs that can handle a few more things than I thought.  
I'd also say that something like qTractor is a better solution for multitracking, Audacity is more of a sound editor.

### Post by snowpine on 2010-08-18
I use Audacity on my Asus EEE 900HA netbook running Fedora to record band rehearsals in mono using the built-in microphone. It works flawlessly for this purpose! :)

### Post by AutoStatic on 2010-08-18
I'm with cchhrriiss121212. Audacity is a pretty decent wave editor but not such a good multitracking program. Qtractor would be a better choice, runs flawlessly on my [cheapo netbook]("http://www.gateway.com/product_spec.php?product_recid=529668268").
