### Post by TheFu on 2016-02-05
HD is hard and requires a ton of "up" bandwidth. Assuming you have that, you can create a google hangout to do it. There's a checkbox that will add a youtube link, so that anonymous people (non-google people) can watch too.  This will put a load on the CPU of the machine showing the film, so it needs enough power to do both.  If you want a more do-it-yourself thing, Justin.tv/livestream and other similar services have lots of examples of devices that get connected to the hdmi out from any content creation device and will stream to their services.  These physical devices don't require a computer during the streaming, but they will not break HDCP either.  Let me see if I can find a link. Looks like $400-$600.
[https://livestream.com/broadcaster](https://livestream.com/broadcaster)

I have an $80 device that captures 1080p video, but only stereo audio and stores that to USB storage. No networking, so no live broadcasting. BTW, compressing HiDef video in "real-time" requires huge CPUs, so most of these devices perform minimal compression and just suck bandwidth - about 8+G/hr. Lots of upstream bandwidth needed for the real-time, cheap, devices.
