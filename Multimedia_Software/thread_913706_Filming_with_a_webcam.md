### Post by Herman on 2008-09-08
I don't know why you need a webcam either. If your tutorial is about things you're doing in your computer, then GTK recordMyDesktop will probably be all you'll need. Maybe you are making a tutorial about something completely outside the realm of computers though?

There's a program called '**cheese**' which works for my wife's webcam in her eeepc.
We can record video with it, but they seem to be silent videos, at least the ones I made are. I'm not sure about that, I haven't had time yet to make the effort to see if it's possible to add sound easily. (Maybe I should try reading the 'help' menu, I might learn something there).
We can also use the webcam to take photos with 'cheese'.

We also tried 'camorama', but that didn't support our type of webcam, so I don't know what that's like.

I also like 'avidemux', it's very handy for watching videos, because it's possible to advance or reverse film one frame at a time or copy a series of frames to a directory as photos. That's very handy for those of us who are learning a sport or a martial art and want to see exactly what movements are on the film frame by frame. Avidemux is very good for video editing. Avidemux has some audio tools, so you might be able to use that to merge a recorded sound file into the video even if cheese doesn't record sound.
