### Post by kahlil88 on 2008-07-21
Check out the [Hymn Project]("http://hymn-project.org/") - there's several programs that remove DRM from AAC files. [Amarok]("http://amarok.kde.org") is great if you're using KDE. Otherwise, check out [Exaile]("http://www.exaile.org/") and [Banshee]("http://www.banshee-project.org/"). iTunes runs very poorly on Wine, and it's not as good as the above mentioned programs (especially with library management).

### Post by cozmicharlie on 2008-07-21
The problem is not that they are in aac format - Ubuntu can play aac files.  The problem is when you buy music from itunes you are not purchasing the music you are buying a license to play the music and that license is very restrictive.  As kahlil88 wrote you must remove the drm but also note when you do this you are breaking your license.  My recommendation for the future if you want to play music on different computers, burn cd's move music to an mp3 player (and why shouldn't you be able to do any of these if you buy the music?) then don't buy from itunes or any other store that is selling a restricted license.  

Good luck
