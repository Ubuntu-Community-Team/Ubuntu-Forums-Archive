### Post by nuclearj on 2008-10-23
So I decided I wanted to put music videos on my PS3, and I accomplished this task, with flying colors in three hours.  And the solution is sooooo easy and thanks to Ubuntu and WinFF and who can forget Google? This is such an awesome reason to use Ubuntu.  On any other system, who knows?  I just know that I did not have to deal with a lot of bull-oney. 

Here's my solution,

1) Go to YouTube and go to the page of the video you want.

2) At the bottom of the video is the time line bar, watch the feint red line until it reaches the end of the video (i think that's the buffer or something)

3) Once the buffer reaches the end of the time line go to your /tmp folder

4) Look for file name that looks like 'FlashFfu4jL', cut and paste it to whatever folder you save the types of files in. (rename it something memorable)

5) Now you have, a .flv (flashvideo) file and you need to convert it to whatever format you need (in this case .mp4)

6) I used WinFF because of my special needs; 1. converting a .flv 2. I wanted to do it for free and 3. WinFF is a GUI front for ffmpeg which is a very powerful piece of software indeed!
Installing it and getting it to work the way I wanted to took sometime to figure out but these are the settings I used:

**[Convert to ... 'PSP'  'XviD for PSP (4:3)']** Let me know if you need help installing or configuring.

7) I put the converted videos onto my PSP and then connected the PSP to the PS3 where I transfered the files to.

8) Now you have a rocking video juke box in your home for parties and such!


So there you have it!  Sooo simple and easy (once you get WinFF going)  And that's why I heart Ubuntu.
