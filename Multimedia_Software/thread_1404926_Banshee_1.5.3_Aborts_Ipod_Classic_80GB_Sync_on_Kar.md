### Post by cdeobald on 2010-02-12
OS:  Karmic, 9.10 32-bit
Banshee Version: 1.5.3
Ipod:  Classic 80GB

Problem: When attempting to sync my music library with My Ipod classic 80GB, Banshee aborts the sync and reports "Track duration is zero".  When it does this, it does not update the database properly, and I end up with many GB of orphaned music files categorized as "other" where I can do nothing to clean them up.  To make matters worse, the pathnames of the "offending" files are often too large to be displayed within the error message window, and the the window is not re-sizable, which makes deleting the files almost impossible.

I have over 65GB of music I'm trying to sync, and the only thing I can do to clear out the orphaned files is to reset the Ipod and start the sync over.

---

### Post by cdeobald on 2010-02-13
Part of the problem is solved.  If I hover over one of the offending file names without selecting it, a pop-up window will reveal the entire path name. At least now I can delete the file(s).
