### Post by deanjm1963 on 2010-11-22
It's not that they're bad, it just depends on what you want your processor/gpu to do. I personally have an i5 680 and use the intel gpu, as I only ever browse, watch videos, etc, the linux support is great. I get all the eye-candy, compiz, great plymouth support, no problems with HDMI etc, and if you're a casual gamer the intel gpu would be more than adequate. If you're after hard-core gaming with all the "flash bang whistles and smoke", I'd go for something with a dedicated graphics card. Again, think of what you'd really be using your machine for.
