### Post by PeterDB on 2009-06-06
Hi,

After much search in the Ubuntu forums and the VLC forums and only finding old archived (and too time consuming) solutions.

I needed to have one instance of VLC, so when I watch movies I don't get multiple VLC showing. I just need one and I want movies to reload in the same.

Unfortunately, the found solutions were too much, I just needed something simple.

Check here:

[http://www.debrass.net/2009/06/06/ubuntu-linux-vlc-one-instance/](http://www.debrass.net/2009/06/06/ubuntu-linux-vlc-one-instance/)

or

1. Open VLC (Applications > Sound & Video > VLC Media Player)
2. Go to “Tools”, then “Preferences”
3. Under the “Instances” section, you should see the “Allow only one instance” check-box option.
4. Check it and click “Save”.
5. Now close VLC for the changes to take effect.

/Peter
