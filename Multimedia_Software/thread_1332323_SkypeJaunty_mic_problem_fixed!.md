### Post by jauntybluefish on 2009-11-20
Hi,
I have started two or three threads asking for help to get my mic working.
I had no luck in finding the solution I needed and a few days ago was about ready to zap Ubuntu and return to the dark side.

I was running the older version of Skype 2.00.27. 
I had already tried to configure the beta version 2.1 with pulseaudio as per a gazillion threads here, but gave up and went back to 2.00.27 where I had at least known it work on the odd occasion.
I had removed all pulseaudio files from the system and installed gnome alsa mixer.

Having realised that every time I started up skype my mic was muted I started asking for help and unfortunately did not get anything that helped. In fact the single reply I did get related to Karmic on the provided link so I ignored it.

Now the good news!
With my finger hovering above the nuke button, I hestitated and in that moment I decided I had nothing to lose by re-installing Skype 2.1 beta before consigning Ubuntu to the scrapheap.
Now the strange thing is that I did nothing after that except change the Skype option in Sound devices, to Device default in all three boxes and the bloody thing actually worked.
It worked clearer and better than ever before and I have not booted windows for 5 days now.

Now I would love to know why that worked when all else failed, but at the end of the day, I have a fully functioning system that is a joy to use. (apart from the orbicam that never worked under XP either).


Ok ...I'm off to learn a little CLI.
