### Post by edd07 on 2009-12-23
Same reason as for the KDE 3 -> KDE 4 switch, I guess: abandoning older paradigms and build a next-generation media player (at least that was the goal). Besides, with KDE 4 already happening, it was an opportunity to embrace Plasma, Phonon and Solid, the new technologies it brought with it.

What version are you running? If it's an older one, I suggest upgrading, even one release makes a lot of difference. If you're running 2.2, you could even try the 2.3 beta, in my experience Amarok betas are pretty stable.

Hope this helps.
