### Post by RedRat on 2009-02-09
It may be that your Oppo firmware may need to be updated. I have both a Toshiba and a Philips, the Philips being older, and in some cases it does not play ***_some_*** Divx files, I have chalked that up to an outdated firmware codec. I am not familiar with that brand, but you might want to check and see if they have an online update for the firmware. There are other web sites that provide alternate firmware for various machines, but one must be careful using these flashes. You might end up with a useless player. It does sound as if your player is a bit newer so it should have the right codecs. Be aware that Divx has now released its new Codec, version 7, that permits HiDef. I don't know if the Codecs are available for Linux yet but I would doubt that your player could handle version 7 since it was released just in January 2009.

With regard to burning software, I you might want to try Gnomebaker, I found it to be a bit more reliable than Brasero, although Brasero kind of works. Maybe the latest update for Brasero is tad better.
