### Post by seventyeights on 2008-05-09
I use a dual head agp card now, but I used to use an agp and a pci card together. At the time, I was able to play video or set the menu/taskbar on the primary (agp) card only. I also could not stretch my city landscape wallpaper across both monitors (instead, I had two instances of the same wallpaper). I don't know how well the two-card method works these days, but that's what I experienced previously.

If you have the extra card, give it a try, but keep in mind that the dual head will work alot more smoothly.

### Post by seventyeights on 2008-05-16
Sometimes there is a setting in the bios to set pci or agp as the primary graphics adapter. In your case though, you may not be able to use the onboard  adapter with a pci graphics adapter present. I have a similar problem with my mb where I can't use the onboard sound if a pci sound card is present (but I can have 2 pci sound cards)

Poke around in your bios. I think you are headed in the right direction if you can get both screens to show at power-up.
