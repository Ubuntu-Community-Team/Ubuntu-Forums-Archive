### Post by tamman2000 on 2008-07-09
It seems as though the upgrade to 8.04 broke amarok.  I first tried searching here, and I didn't find any posts from people who seemed to be having the same problem.  I did find what I was looking for here ([http://www.nybergh.net/notes/2008/05/16/amaroks-sound-output-broken-by-default-in-ubuntu-804/](http://www.nybergh.net/notes/2008/05/16/amaroks-sound-output-broken-by-default-in-ubuntu-804/)) and I thought it should be somewhere on this forum...

My symptoms were that amarok would not play any audio, and the progress through the track (as judged by the slider at the bottom right) would stop after 1 or 2 seconds, any subsequent attempt to play a song would freeze amarok.

The gist is that amarok is not always set up to play through ALSA even though that might be what your system is listening on (in my case it seems that the amarok auto-detect feature was not working).

### Post by stats on 2008-07-09
do you have the medibuntu repo in your sources by any chance.
I had a similar problem with the medibuntu version of amarok.

If you do, goto system->administration->software sources->third party and remove it

then,
```
sudo apt-get remove amarok
sudo apt-get clean
sudo apt-get update
sudo apt-get install amarok
```

### Post by c0t1 on 2008-07-30
I had the EXACT same problem.

Here's what fixed it for me (big thanks to Simon for the advice):

* Change the output plugin to "Alsa".

In amarok, go to:

Settings -> Configure Amarok -> Engine -> Output Plugin

and change it to "Alsa".

Worked for me.  I hope it works for you.
