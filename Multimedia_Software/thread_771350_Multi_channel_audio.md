### Post by skollywhop on 2008-05-22
I had a similar problem, go to system > preferences > sound. Then in the devices tab, change everything to the pulseaudio sound server. It worked for me, 
p.s. you don't want to uninstall pulse audio, its is a part of the OS, so I wouldn't advise it. But if you do need to uninstall it for any reason, system > administration > synaptic package manager. Then search "pulseaudio" and mark to unistall.(This WILL UNISTALL ubuntu desktop and its dependancies.)
