### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-04-16
Welcome to the forums. 

I use MeTV in Ubuntu which writes a channels.list for you. It does an autoscan (providing your TV card is up and running, o course).

I bought a dual-splitter for the antenna and one spilt goes to the PVR (in your case, the digital receiver) and the other to my USB TV dongle. I'm presuming yours is a TV card in a desktop by the sounds of it. You could try this rather than daisy chaining them.

I found I really needed a clear signal before I could get anything to work well.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-04-16
At the moment you have an antenna cable from an external antenna plugged in where? What I'm saying is put a splitter on the antenna before it hits anything else. Send one cable from the splitter directly into your TV card, the other to where it normally goes, no change. That way you know you have a solid signal and there is no problem with the daisy chain. When our PVR was off I had problems until I sent a direct line to the TV card.

You could test this out by plugging the antenna cable directly into the TV card, if it's possible.
