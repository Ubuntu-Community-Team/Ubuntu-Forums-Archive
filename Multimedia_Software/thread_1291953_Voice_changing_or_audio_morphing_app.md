Yes, this is a very old thread and I have yet to find a program that does 'live' voice changing without any kind of latency.

I have since used a hardware device (Boss Pitch Shifter) but the issue I had with that is that the voice changing was fairly dramatic and while it 'sounded good', it made my voice less legible and I have had numerous requests to repeat what I said.  So I stopped using it within the first hour of starting to use it.  Being a unit that cost less than $200, it had plenty of hiss when it operated too.  I wasn't happy with it.

It seems that you would need a really good (and expensive) device to pull something like this off.  I know that Evantide was a very famous make for such devices, but they are extremely expensive (in the 1000's).  Needless to say, that is out of my budget.

I think what I am going to do this year is just use my regular voice and try to add a bit of reverb to it.  In this regard it doesn't have to be real-time, and latency shouldn't be an issue.  Plus I can dial in the amount of reverb so I can be sure to keep my voice understandable.
