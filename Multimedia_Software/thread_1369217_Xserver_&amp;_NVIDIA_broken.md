### Post by Seq on 2009-12-31
You probably do not want to be running 'startx', especially if you have X running already. After you log out, you can switch to a console (CTRL+ALT+F1), log in, and run "sudo service gdm restart". If you stop instead of restart, you could play around with startx.

That should restart X.

You could post your xorg.conf. For reference, here is mine:
```
Section "Screen"
    Identifier  "Default Screen"
    DefaultDepth    24
EndSection

Section "Module"
    Load    "glx"
EndSection

Section "Device"
    Identifier  "Default Device"
    Driver  "nvidia"
    Option  "NoLogo"                    "True"
EndSection
```

### Post by Seq on 2009-12-31
> **lasleym said:**
> So, the command "sudo service gdm restart" does what exactly... restarts services?

'service' is a utility for controlling SysV init scripts in /etc/init.d. gdm is the service that gives you your graphical login prompt, and in the process starts X.

Restarting that service also has the effect of restarting X

The only thing I can think of is the nvidia kernel module isn't loaded before X starts. It works for me here, but I am using a newer driver that doesn't support the geforce2 series anymore. Perhaps it does something that yours does not.

Could you try the following?
```

```

This tells your system to always load the nvidia module at boot. Try that and let me know if it persists after a reboot.
