### Post by prshah on 2012-05-04
> **Odense said:**
> I have a HTC Desire S phone and have accumulated too many double and more instances of some contacts. I would like to get the whole contact list into a spreadsheet to clean it up and then after deleting all the contacts on the phone putting it back for the spreadsheet.


If this is an Android phone, you can open [www.google.com/contacts](www.google.com/contacts) on your laptop, and login with your primary gmail account (as setup on phone).

From there, you can export the entire contact list to a CSV (comma seperated values) file, which can be easily imported into a spreadsheet.

From there, you can clean it up to your satisfaction, save it as a CSV, and then import it into Google Contacts. Your saved file and the original CSV file should have the same "structure", eg dont change column order or format.

Your phone should automatically sync with the list in Google Contacts.

This is only a suggestion, I have not performed this myself.
