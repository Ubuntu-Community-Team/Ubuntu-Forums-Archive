Why ?:
Why did you upgrade the kernel ?
Why not use the package management system & get a ubuntu tested kernel ?
That link is just to some "nobody" showing off. It is just pointless rubbish.

Same applies to the nvidia driver 195 (old) or did this come from Ubuntu repos ?

H/W:
Without serious internet searching..
I would say your laptop has Optimus graphics because you have (2) VGA adapters in the error file.

Your current problem:
You have tried to install the nvidia driver & now it is being loaded for the intel GPU.
This is never going to work..

Optimus is **_not_** supported by nVidia in Linux.
You have to use deBumblebee or vgaswitcharoo to control/toggle between intel & nvidia GPUs.

Optimus types:
There are (2) forms of it, some have framebuffer mux controllable from BIOS.
So you can disable Optimus in BIOS **only **some h/w.

Solution:
You need to get at that xorg.conf file..edit the nvidia keyword to intel.
Or just delete it completely..
Boot from install/Live CD DVD..

You will have to understand the Linux filesystem & mount points..
