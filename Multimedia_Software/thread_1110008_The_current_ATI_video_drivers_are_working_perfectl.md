### Post by TomB19 on 2009-03-29
I've always been an nVidia guy and nVidia products have always worked well for me on Linux.


Recently decided to switch to AMD/ATI on my main desktop for political reasons.  First, I like AMD.  Second, I believe nVidia will be out of business or purchased by Intel in the next three years.

I like Intel too.  In fact, I like Intel a lot.  I'm not anti-Intel in any way.

Anyway, I got a crazy deal on an AMD quad core and motherboard ($185 for a 9850/AMD790GX/SB750 setup).  8 GB of RAM later and I'm a happy AMD64 Kubuntu 8.10 user.

I purchased the system knowing others have complained about ATI driver bugs.  I was prepared to put up with some glitches.  After all, I had learned to not be annoyed by check boxes not displaying properly without refreshing on my nVidia system.

When I first installed Kubuntu, in January of this year, there were a couple of glitches but it was OK.  With the latest drivers, I am not aware of a single bug.  It works perfectly.

Thank you, ATI.  :)
