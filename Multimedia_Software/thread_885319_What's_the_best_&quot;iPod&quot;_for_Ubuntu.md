### Post by AusIV4 on 2008-08-10
I find iPods to be a pretty good bet. They have pretty solid support under Linux, and a huge wealth of accessories.

My mother recently purchased a Sony mp3 player, and while it was less expensive than a similarly spec'd iPod, finding accessories for it has been a disaster.

She wants it to play through her car radio. Her radio has direct support for iPods (she could control it through her steering wheel), but there's no way to plug in her mp3 player.

She bought an FM transmitter that works with generic mp3 players, but it requires being plugged in to the car's power supply, so she can't charge her mp3 player in the car and listen to it at the same time - and that's assuming we could find a car charger for this mp3 player. The quality on the FM transmitter is pretty shakey as well, but she just wants it for audio books, so that's not too big an issue.

All of the accessories I saw were for iPods or Zunes. It's my understanding that Zunes have pretty pitiful support under Linux, so I'd steer clear. If you're going to be wanting to do anything other than listen to it through headphones, I'd recommend an iPod.

As far as which iPod is the best, it depends on what you want to do. If you have a lot of stuff to keep on it, the iPod classic offers the most space. If you are going to watch videos on it frequently, the iPod touch offers the largest screen (among other features). If you're looking for low cost, the iPod Nano does pretty well. The shuffle is quite cheap, but they don't have as many accessories.
