### Post by tuxpenguin on 2009-01-05
From what I understand, card speed is more important when you are trying to run 3D applications [games]. If you're just trying to use the desktop, especially with two large monitors, VRAM is more important. Also, just because it's a higher model number doesn't mean it's faster per se. Check the clock speeds to be sure.

Personally, I haven't had any experience with these cards in Ubuntu, so I can't advise you on their compatibility...maybe someone else could.

I would pick the one with more VRAM. Since you're running two monitors, you'll want the extra memory...and the cards aren't that different in power anyway. But that's just my opinion...I'm no expert. ;)
