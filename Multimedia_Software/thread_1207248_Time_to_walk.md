### Post by Dipper on 2009-07-08
I have been patient and there has been no hint of any improvement to the support for ATI Radeon cards.  I blame ATI in the first place for dropping support for older video cards.  But I blame Ubuntu even more for using a version of xserver that does not accomodate them in Jaunty and future releases.  Upgrading hardware on a desktop is one thing, but a laptop is quite another.  I am not willing to go out and spend another $1200 and find myself in the same predicament three years from now.

Shame on Ubuntu for not thinking this through.  Shame on Ubuntu for leaving "legacy" ATI users out in the cold.  The open-source drivers are crap.  2D barely works and 3D doesn't work at all, at least not for my purposes.  It's time to show my extreme displeasure with Ubuntu by voting with my feet. Maybe I will come back when Ubuntu gets its act together.

My question is what distribution uses KDE 4.2, is user-friendly (which excludes Debian), and the xserver that supports discontinued ATI cards?
