### Post by RoosterHam on 2013-03-06
> **ciscoboy said:**
> My goal with this program was to use it to use CB frequencies to talk to people with CBs.

I'm a little confused about how you are trying to use fldigi. CB officially (in all western countries that I am familiar with regarding RF regulations) doesn't usually involve digital communications, certainly not in the modes that fldigi is designed for. There are also the 'freebander' operators, who actively communicate outside of their country's regulations for frequency and power output, but in most cases use the same code-of-conduct that Amateur operators are bound to (except for licensing and frequency). Freebanders don't tend to use digital modes at all. Freebanders that use digital modes are either very advanced experimenters that really would benefit from becoming licensed amateurs, or more likely a CBer/Freebander using digital communications modes are probably into something a bit shadowy.

Fldigi, as you said, is an Amateur Radio application. Within each Amateur band there is a segment set aside for digital modes. CW/morse code, rtty, packet radio and so forth. This is the sort of arena fldigi is meant for, and really where it's use is limited to. Of coarse you can also view the faxes of weather maps sent to ships and so forth. 

I've used fldigi with a laptop also. All you need is a microphone plugged into your laptop, and some sort of speaker in or attached to your receiver/transceiver. Point fldigi to your sound cards mic, put the mic next to your radios speaker, then tune to a digital signal. It helps incredibly to know what each digital signal sounds like and might look like on the band scope, otherwise you have to test each mode in fldigi, and usually each option for each mode till you find which mode and options decodes the signal to ineligible text.

[http://www.kb9ukd.com/digital/](http://www.kb9ukd.com/digital/)
[http://www.k3dcw.net/index.php?p=1_4_Digital-Mode-Samples](http://www.k3dcw.net/index.php?p=1_4_Digital-Mode-Samples)
[http://hfradio.org.uk/html/digital_modes.html](http://hfradio.org.uk/html/digital_modes.html)
[http://www.arrl.org/digital-data-modes](http://www.arrl.org/digital-data-modes)

When it comes to transmitting with fldigi, well that really should be left to licensed amateurs (non-professional electricians in reallity) who can do so without blowing up their radios and computers.

Hope this helps.

### Post by d_in_Conduct on 2013-03-13
+1 to what RoosterHam wrote.

Most importantly, fldigi is not a stand-alone program.  You need a proper radio to receive digital signals for fldigi to decode and to send digital signals that fldigi generates.  If you had a proper radio you wouldn't need fldigi.  You could just pick up the mic and talk to CBers.
