### Post by tbraun on 2008-12-09
I used to use xmms as my trusty, simple audio player, but ever since 8.04 this is not in the repos anymore. Audacity is the suggested alternative now. I think many people have noticed that Audiacious has very high CPU usage, though. Just playing an ordinary song it can take betwen 12% to 14% of the CPU. Not nice.

Fortunately, I found [this here]("http://www.geekzone.co.nz/foobar/6042"), which descibes a nice alternative: xfmedia

Xfmedia is the media player for the xfce desktop. Since it's written with GTK+, it actually integrates quite nicely in a Gnome desktop. It's also in the repos by default. And finally, it can read old xmms-generated playlists, which is good.

How much CPU does it use? Between 3% to 4%. Much better!

So much for Audacious, then. Since all I need is a simple, ligh-weight audio player, xfmedia is what I was looking for.

Just thought I share that with those of you who are still looking for a more light-weight alternative to Audacious.
