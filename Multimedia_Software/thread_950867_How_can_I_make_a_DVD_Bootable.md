### Post by Innova on 2008-10-17
A friend wanted me to reformat his laptop.  He was given a recovery DVD with it that was about 6GB of data burned on a DVD+R DL.  This DVD was bootable, but it had read errors during startup, so the boot wouldn't complete.

I thought that I might have better luck reading the data from Ubuntu, so I just copied the files from the DVD to my hard disk.  I then thought that I would be able to copy them back to the DVD, and hopefully any read errors would have been corrected.  When I did this the second DVD was not bootable.

Is there some switch that I have to toggle to make the DVD bootable?

---

### Post by Therion on 2008-10-17
You need to burn the new DVD as a bootable .iso file.  It's not a "data" DVD that you want.  

Try doing a "Copy DVD" operation instead of copying the files from the factory DVD to your hard drive and burning a new DVD.  This way, most likely, the formatting of the original will be kept when burning the new DVD.
