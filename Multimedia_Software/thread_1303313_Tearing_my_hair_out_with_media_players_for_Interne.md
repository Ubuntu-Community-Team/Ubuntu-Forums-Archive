### Post by Allen3 on 2009-10-28
One of the things I miss about not having my *Windows* OS is media players! I used *Winamp* and it did everything! I could play all the music from my library and tune in Internet Radio from *Shoutcast* lists. It then would let me **bookmark** my favorite ones. I started with *Kubuntu 8.04* and have upgraded all the way to *9.04* and prefer the *Gnome* interface to the *KDE4*. I started running the *BMPx* player first as the stock *Rythmbox* player would bring up *Shoutcast* lists but in _very limited_ genres. The *BMPx* player was _extremely unstable_ in the *Gnome* interface! I tried others and found that only *Amarok* would work somewhat similar but I could not make it pick up *Shoutcast* lists whether I ran it in *KDE4* or the *Gnome* interface! I ended up bringing up the *Shoutcast* website, selecting my genre and then right clicking the **Tune In** button and then selecting **Properties**. Then I had to cut/paste into my own personal playlist. God is that inconvenient! It took me **two days** to get all my station's in! Is there any media player that works like *Winamp* available for the *Gnome* interface? *Amarok* seems to run funny too, particularly in *Gnome*. So far my experience is that all the media players for *Ubuntu* are pure junk! Does anyone have any recommendations on a media player that allows convenient access to *Shoutcast* lists similar to *Winamp?* Also, the interface for *Amarok* is flat out ugly, I wish I could get a player with a nice looking interface, (*Audacious* did not cut it at all so don't recommend it). Anyone got any ideas on this?
