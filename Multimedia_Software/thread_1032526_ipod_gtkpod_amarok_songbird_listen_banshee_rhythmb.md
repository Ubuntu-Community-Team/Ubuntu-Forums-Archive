### Post by greyfox1 on 2009-02-09
I have a 30GB iPod Video (2nd-generation of the 5th generation if that makes sense) that I use with Ubuntu Intrepid.  My music collection is larger than the capacity of my iPod, so I can't simply sync everything-- what I do is sync specific playlists.  This, coupled with the fact that I view album art support a must, creates a specific need that not every program can satisfy.

I've tried out most of the popular players and IMO, the only one that really does everything properly is AmaroK 1.4. Here's why the others don't live up:

**Songbird**: Can effortlessly sync my playlists, is (AFAIK) the only program that can "restore" your iPod to factory defaults like iTunes can, BUT has no support for artwork (yet).  This is a deal-breaker for me.  Another strike against Songbird in my book is the fact that it ignores use Gnome's window manager and uses its own.

**GTKPod**: I have to admit I hate this one.  The things you have to do to get stuff on the iPod are just silly.  The interface is not intuitive.  Also there seemed to be no easy (possible?) way to just sync the playlists I want-- I can transfer a whole playlist there, but when I update the playlist there is no quick way to sync the changes to the iPod.  It does support artwork, however.

**Banshee**: Supports artwork. Does a pretty good job overall but again, no real "sync" feature.  Like with GTKPod, I'm stuck having to drag and drop things I want and can't just sync changes to my playlists.  This one is a bummer because Banshee is my preferred app for library management & playback on my desktop.

**Rythmbox**: Basically the same as Banshee with respect to syncing.  Didn't bother to check if it will put the artwork on my iPod.

...which brings us to...

**AmaroK**: I'm not a big fan of KDE apps in Gnome due to the differences look and feel (especially ugly KDE 3.5 apps) but AmaroK 1.4 just does everything I want, the way I expect.  It supports selective syncing (e.g. my playlists, even when I update them) and puts the artwork on the iPod. AmaroK 2 doesn't have device support yet, so I'm sticking with 1.4 for this sole reason.

### Post by shayguitarra on 2009-02-10
I was using Rockbox for a few months, dragging and dropping. And it worked ok, album art, replay gain, relatively easy to scrobble ipod plays etc. But this became increasingly onerous and as I put more and more tracks on it took longer and longer for the database to update. So I started looking for alternatives. Also, as I have most of my collection in flac space was becoming an issue.

I have a 5th gen 80GB video ipod, and am using kubuntu 8.10.

I originally tried Amarok when I first started using ubuntu but had problems getting it to convert my flac files. 

So this time the first thing I tried was gtkpod. And while it seemed to work ok at first I realised that it wasn't updating the ipod properly (ignoring tracks after a certain point etc.) and I was also disappointed that I couldn't simply sync between pc and ipod. I updated to the newest version but that turned out to be a bad idea as it wouldn't convert files anymore or update certain tags.

I tried floola but to be honest that was worse, it kept crashing, the interface was inefficient and again I couldn't get it to convert files.

None of this is to demean the hard work done by the developers of these programs. It is quite possible that there were problems with dependencies on my installation and so on. But as an end user they weren't doing what I wanted.

So I tried Amarok again (with five months experience of ubuntu under my belt) and I must say it's working fine so far. I installed transkode and it has handled large scale conversions of my flac files with no problem. It updates my Amarok database with ipod plays. I even use it to transfer video files although there is no tag support. The only slight problems are Amarok's transcoding instructions that mean I have to transfer ipod compatible and incompatible files separately. And I've noticed that Amarok doesn't transmit my ipod plays to last.fm when I have a large number of them. But in that case I just use the last.fm client.

The only annoying thing is that development on Amarok 1.4.10 is finished. And Amarok 2, to be blunt, is dreadful. I know it's a new version and not an update of Amarok 1.x but shouldn't it at least start out with the same functionality?
