### Post by Eoghanalbar on 2009-05-23
Just threw Jaunty on an old HP Pavilion desktop, making it a dual boot with Windows 98. 

Microphone and speakers/headphones work fine in Windows 98, but weren't in Jaunty.

After a bit of fiddling, I've found the the audio line out DOES work in Jaunty if I plug it into a second jack on the back of the computer...

-Two different cards on the back of the computer

1st card:
-has line out,line in, and microphone jacks (also s video and yellow RCA jack)
-line out and mic jacks works in Windows 98, but not in Jaunty. So better sound and can record in Windows 98
EDIT -Does not plug directly into the mother board. Has one line going to 2nd card, and another one going I don't know where (somewhere up behind the CD, floppy, and hard drives)

2nd card:
-has a line out speaker jack, a couple phone line jacks (is it a modem?) and an [I don't know the right term but kinda like a monitor plug-in]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D-subminiature") thing that's labeled with what I THINK is a stylized picture of a joystick?
-Line out jack is the only one that works in Jaunty. Worse sound, no recording.
EDIT -Plugs directly into the motherboard. Three lines: to CD drive, DVD drive, and 1st card.

Before discovering how the guts were all hooked up, I figured I'd just take out mysterious 2nd card. I did so, and of course there was no sound at all. Put 2nd card back in.

NOW the microphone works, the sound quality seems better... and moving the mouse makes a weird electric buzzing noise over the headphones when the mic is plugged in!

Man, computers are WEIRD.
