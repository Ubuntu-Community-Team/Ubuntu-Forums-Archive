### Post by DuckHook on 2017-01-07
It could simply be a bad burn. The file headers may be readable from the DVD (so it shows up on your file manager) whereas the actual file data itself may be corrupted.

[LIST=1]
[*]
[*]Try burning another one.
[*]How old is the DVD drive?
[*]How old is the Media?
[*]Is the DVD readable on another machine?
[*]Are other older media readable from this drive? Especially original pressed media (*not* burned)?
[/LIST]
The reason for all of the above questions is: the lasers in DVD drives deteriorate with time. This gives rise to erratic behaviour that's not even always the same. Sometimes, it's bad burns. At other times, it's bad reads/rips. Sometimes, one machine will read the media whereas another will not. Also, since the dyes in DVD media are organic, they also deteriorate.
