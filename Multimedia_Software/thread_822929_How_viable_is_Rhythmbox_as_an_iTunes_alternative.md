### Post by chuyler1 on 2008-06-11
I had all sorts of problems with Rhythmbox.

1) It crashes randomly
2) When you rip CDs to mp3 format it uses m4a instead, and my iPod couldn't read the files.
3) I couldn't get the swing of adding and removing files from my iPod.  It rarely seemed to work as expected.

Anyway, now I'm using Amarok.  It has gapless playback and easy management of your iPod.  The only issue I have found so far is that if you add a playlist to your iPod that already exists instead of syncing it, you'll end up with the songs twice in the list.  I also haven't figured out the correct command to eject the iPod on disconnect but the overall experience is far superior to Rhythmbox in every other regard.  I haven't tried Songbird yet but maybe I'll give it a trial run to see if it is any easier to use than Amarok.

### Post by ufugu on 2008-06-16
There are a lot of programs that work great for music management (Songbird, Amarok, Rhythmbox etc etc), but they each have their limitations with the iPod.  At least, none have been perfect for me.

I would heartily recommend using the program of your choice for library management and playback, and then using [Floola]("http://www.floola.com") to manage your iPod.

Floola lives directly on your iPod (is not installed on your computer), comes in Mac/Windows/Linux flavors, lets you add/remove music and or video, create playlists, playback music directly from the iPod, manage podcasts, etc.

It used to be kind of flaky but in the last year or so it's come a long way.  I use it regularly on all three platforms and it sure is nice to not have my iPod tied to any one OS or music player.
