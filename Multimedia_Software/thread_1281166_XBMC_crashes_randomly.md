### Post by why register? on 2009-12-22
I'm not usually moved to post here but when it comes to XBMC yes, I am a fanboy. I think XBMC *on the XBOX* is one of the best pieces of software I've used since dBase II or Turbo Pascal. I'd have to admit though that the Linux version is not so good. For me the biggest problem is the "bunny in the headlights" indecision whether to support a mouse/keyboard or to assume that users will be using a lowest common denominator remote control. (E.g. if you have a one hour .mpg3 file how do you scroll through it?) The problem is not that the coders are hobbyists - they're clearly capable of wonderful things. The problem is that without the constraints of the XBOX hardware it's hard for an OSS project to make the tough decisions required to give a coherent and polished look and feel. Design by committee will only get you so far.

Similarly, Ubuntu used to stand out from other distros in that it had a benevolent dictator with the will (& clout) to knock heads together and get silly problems resolved. Recently not so much. (E.g. the ALSA/PulseAudio trail of tears.)
