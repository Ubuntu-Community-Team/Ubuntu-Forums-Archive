### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-06-18
If it's just text or rendered HTML, most browsers like Firefox will let you highlight the text with your mouse, right-click on the highlighted area, choose Copy, then Paste the contents into another application.

If it's a PDF file, it depends on whether the author allows copying.  I use Okular on KDE, and I can enable the Selection tool, highlight the text I want to save, and again copy it to the clipboard with Copy.

If it's in a graphic like PNG or JPEG, then there's no easy way to copy text.  You could save the graphic and use an editor like GIMP to select the text area and save it, but you'll only have a graphic, not the underlying text.

If we're talking about something like an eBook, it may or may not be possible depending on whether the document is protected by some form or digital rights management.
