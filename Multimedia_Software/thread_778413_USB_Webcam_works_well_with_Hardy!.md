### Post by ingrid_ozikem on 2008-05-02
I noticed a lot of anguished posts about webcams and Ubuntu on this forum and on other websites found through Google.  

I recently installed Ubuntu Hardy Heron (new user, fresh install on windows dual boot machine) and my Microsoft VX 3000 webcam (or is it 6000?) works right away. The image is a bit grainy in programs like Camorama & Mplayer but Skype for Linux gives a great image. The microphone built into the webcam is also recognized.

I found this other webpage that might be helpful to people looking to fix their problematic webcam/microphones.. 

[http://www.buberel.org/serendipity/index.php?/archives/260-Using-a-USB-Webcam-on-Ubuntu-Linux-to-produce-good-quality-video-captures-that-can-be-uploaded-to-YouTube-directly.html](http://www.buberel.org/serendipity/index.php?/archives/260-Using-a-USB-Webcam-on-Ubuntu-Linux-to-produce-good-quality-video-captures-that-can-be-uploaded-to-YouTube-directly.html)

My experience was almost identical to his. Chin up everyone!
