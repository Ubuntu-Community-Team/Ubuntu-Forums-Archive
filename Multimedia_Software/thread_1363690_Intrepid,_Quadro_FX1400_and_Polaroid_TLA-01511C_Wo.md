You didn't say what resolution you are aiming for?

A cursory googling for your LCD TV:

[http://www.walmart.com/catalog/product.do?product_id=5645637](http://www.walmart.com/catalog/product.do?product_id=5645637)

2. Amazon says the TV has resolution of 1440x900
[http://www.amazon.com/Polaroid-15-2e4-22-LCD-Television-TLA-2d01511C/dp/B000PLZINI](http://www.amazon.com/Polaroid-15-2e4-22-LCD-Television-TLA-2d01511C/dp/B000PLZINI)

Aside:
I believed sometimes a TV's manual is not completely honest (or maybe it's confusing).  I have a no brand LCD TV.  The manual claimed max resolution of 1280x1024 is supported, but the technical spec of the same TV quote 1366 x768 (which I believed is the actual LCD panel capability).

For best picture, we should always try to match 1-to-1, or as close as possible to the LCD panel physical dimension.

However, when setting the resolution to 1366x768, I saw some artifacts from mismatched pixel counts (the edges of white texts are not crisp white boundary).  Instead, I got the best results by setting the resolution to 1280x768.
