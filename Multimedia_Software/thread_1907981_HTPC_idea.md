### Post by MidnightbluRose on 2012-01-12
Hello all,

Without going too in dept.  I have a lot of media, and I use Ubuntu to watch it all.  I have them all on spare IDE drives I had around, and used old IDE external drive parts to use them.  Well the two externals I had different wires rip, and don't really work.

So I started trying to think of ways to watch my media off the IDE drives.  Some of them included:

- Building a desktop from spare parts
- Buying some sort of IDE dock/enclosure
- Buying like a 1TB drive, building a temp system and transferring stuff over to that.  Then using a dock to watch.

I found some flaws in both.  The last two was money; which made the first one look more appealing.  So I started looking for ways to to make the first option work.  Backup and restores etc.  Then It hit me.  How am I going to watch this?  I liked laying on my bed watching the stuff on my laptop, and I don't have a spare monitor large enough to make this work.  

So I though maybe an HTPC?  

I've heard of them, but never really did a lot of looking.  I'm thinking of building a PC (spare parts) and then just having it stream to my laptop.  That way I can have my casual viewing, still use my spare IDE drives, and not have to worry about swapping and having all these drives around.

Is this doable?  I'm thinking about just having the machine in a room, and turning it on when I need it, and control everything from my laptop.  I want my laptop to be the only thing it communicates with.  So, I don't want it seen by other computers on the network.

### Post by vandorjw on 2012-01-13
Its very possible. I am not sure about other people not being able to 'see' it, but you can certainly restrict their access to it.

In your possition what I would do, is get an old computer, put your hdd in it, set up Samba, and configure IpTables to drop all conections except from your own IP.

Best of luck.

If you have any specific questions, just post.
