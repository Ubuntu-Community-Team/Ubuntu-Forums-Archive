### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-01-27
More detail, please. What type of files are the footage? How do you mean you can't figure how to export the video back to the desktop. From where? Please explain more explicitly exactly what you are trying/wanting to do. 

You question is in two parts, so for the second part: Kino, Openshot are straightforward and fairly basic vid editors. I use Blender for video editing (give yourself some time to get your head around that) and Handbrake to compress them to whatever format. Blender is an extremely powerful and flexible NLE but nothing like you've ever used I'd think. Steep learning curve unless you're already au fait. Experience vid editing with a pro level NLE helps. Worth the effort if you have the time. 

Kino, from memory, only wants DV camera files and will try to change whatever you open there to DV. All the apps mentioned are in the repositories (free), but the Openshot from the Openshot repo worked for me whereas the on in the Ubuntu repos didn't. 

Moving on with editing, there is also Cinelerra (don't know how that going) and have heard of [Lightworks]("https://www.lwks.com/") but not looked any further. 

If what you're saying is you have footage that you want to compress to another file type, then Handbrake it is.

When looking for Ubuntu alternative apps, you can get good results with a search for what you want to do and add Ubuntu. For instance; 'video editing ubuntu'. You'll find plenty. 

Hope that's a start. ;)
