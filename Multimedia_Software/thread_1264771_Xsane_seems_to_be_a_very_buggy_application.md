### Post by bdalzell on 2009-12-04
I have not figured out how to get the scan window to not be maximized but the problem with not being able to do anything with it is because the requestor that is asking you what to do with the window has taken up residence behind the maximized window. Why programmers do not make these window control requestors have the attribute "always on top" I do not know.

Cntrl Alt D will minimize all the windows on the screen. Then you can go along the bottom panel and find the representation for the window close requestor and bring it alone up.

When the Xsane scan window is open right after the scan. If you move your mouse to the top of the screen, the menus for that window will appear and you can save the scan.
