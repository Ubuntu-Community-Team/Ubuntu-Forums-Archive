### Post by logos34 on 2009-05-21
> **Miljet said:**
> While cleaning out a closet, I ran across a box of old stereo cassette tapes. Can they be converted to digital and eventually burned to CD? Or is the result not worth the effort?

sure you can convert them! Just need a y-cable [edit](RCA plugs to 1/8" stereo ) to connect the tape player to the PC (line-in jack)...Use Audacity to capture sound card output as .wav file and export to lossy format if desired...Unless they're rare/out of print stuff, probably not worth it because of quality (tape hiss, etc), even after you clean them up with filters...IMO, that is--others may disagree.  I threw out my small collection of tapes because I've either listened to them enough (life is short, time to move on to new music), or I've found CD replacements at the public library, which I've ripped.

### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2009-05-21
A few years ago I converted a lot of material for a university from cassettes to FLAC. The tape player will determine the quality of the output more than anything else. A $30 clock, radio, tape player will sound like its worth $30. If you're serious about it look for a good tape player or maybe borrow one from a buddy.

Agreed that unless they are not available in another medium, the effort and results might not really pay off. Any tapes that I had laying around I eventually replaced with used CD from local music stores for less than $100 (cheaper than a good tape player!). Cheers!
