### Post by cariboo on 2008-11-26
Your webcam is more then likely configured, so all you need is a program to view the output. Cheese and Camorama are two programs for viewing web cam output.

My Logitech Quickcam Messanger works with Ekiga as well.

All the programs mentioned above are available in the repositories and can be installed using Add/Remove Programs, Synaptic package Manager and of course the command line,

### Post by forkandles on 2008-11-26
Jeni,
If your laptop is anything like the Dell Vostro 1710 then you should be fine with your webcam. 
[http://blog.ptpbs.com/?p=37](http://blog.ptpbs.com/?p=37)
I am assuming that you are using 8.10.
You probably wish to contact friends and family so I suggest you download and install Skype via Synaptic Package Manager, then set up your own Skype account. You can partially test the video and sound under Options and Test Call.
Things may get more complicated with PulseAudio and sound settings.
For further information look here.
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=990057&highlight=webcam](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=990057&highlight=webcam)
