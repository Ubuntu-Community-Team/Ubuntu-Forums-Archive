### Post by Ghost|BTFH on 2010-09-23
Fixing mp3 tags can be a pain in the rear ports.

I personally like to use easytag for all my mp3/flac/ogg needs.  It's got a slight learning curve, but once you figure out that you can actually have it scan all the files and retag it by how the mp3 is named, it becomes a godsend.

For example, my music collection is broken down into artists alphabetically, and each file has a setup as follows:

Artist - Song Title.flac

In easytag, you can tell it, "Hey, the area before the first - is the artist, the area after it is the song title..." and then tell it to set that for all songs, it will scan them, change the tag artist and title for you, then all you have to do is hit save.

It can even handle more detailed stuff like:

Track number - artist - album - song title.flac

and once you find the right setup (which you can customize to suit your needs) you can have your entire collection retagged in a matter of minutes.

Hope that helps!

Cheers,
Ghost|BTFH

P.S.: I also like using GPRename for having to rename a large group of files...like for example, you rip a CD and get:

01-song title.mp3
02-song title.mp3
03-song title.mp3
etc

and you actually want:

artist - song title.mp3

GPRename can do that.  It's an OCD audio collector's best tool. ;)
