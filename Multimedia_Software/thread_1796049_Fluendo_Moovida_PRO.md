### Post by BBQdave on 2011-09-15
Just an update: I am using my old eMac as a file server and to manage my music collection through iTunes 8.  I am using my dell 1100 inspiron laptop (with debian 6) as my main work horse, and I added the Fluendo mp3 codec pack for mp3 playback on my dell.  My portable music player is a Sansa clip plus.

The Fluendo mp3 codec pack is a free download.  And this allows you to see if it works on your system.

For me, this works well, as the eMac also functions as a secondary workstation.  I like having two work stations (in case one machine fails).  And it is the best of both worlds, with iTunes for music and *Linux for everything else!*

My machines are old, so down the road when hardware fails, most likely I will give Fluendo Moovida Pro a try for mp3 encoding on new Linux hardware.  Hopefully continuing on with my mp3 music library.

For those advocating *Ogg Vorbis*, I tried encoding a large sample of my CD collection,  and it was inconsistent with encoding.  An example: a CD two disc album, the first disc would encode first few tracks than the remaining tracks were the same song with different names labeled; on the second disc, it encoded perfectly, all tracks with correct names.  The discs I sampled, I had successfully encoded through iTunes (mp3).  And for me the mp3 file transfers well among my different devices (desktop, laptop, portable music player).:)

I've always encoded to mp3 using LAME. I don't know if these moovida codecs have any advantage.
