### Post by piponline on 2008-06-24
Hello all.
Weird subject, I know. I'll try to shred some light into it.

I use Kino for home video editing (movies of my kids, mostly) and I really miss the so much called for feature of automatic titles with the timestamp of each movie part.

So I do it by hand. After an entire tape is captured I go through the movie and I insert a titler effect (with the timestamp of each movie part) wherever I detect significant differences in the timestamps. I’ll do it if movies were shot in different days or in different parts of the day, I won’t do it if movies were shot some minutes apart.

I create these effects with 5 seconds duration so that when I’m playing the movie the timestamp title appears for 5 seconds and then disappears, until the next timestamp title comes along again for 5 seconds and so on.

Now, I had already noticed from previous recordings that after rendering and creating the DVD, the movie kind of stumbles when it finishes playing the kino altered part of the movie, I mean, the 5 seconds part that has a title, and proceeds to next movie part. (I have Kino exporting the movie into DVD format, a file per scene, and then I produce the DVD with DVDStyler)

I noticed this when playing the files on the computer but mainly when playing the DVD in the standalone DVD player in my living room.

This never bothered me much but now, with my recent production, I’m getting a different behaviour: instead of stumbling the standalone DVD player completely stalls at exactly those points.

When I run the .vob files in Ubuntu sometimes it goes smoothly, sometimes it stumbles, but it doesn’t stall. Tonight I’ll try to run the DVD media in Ubuntu although I am almost certain that the results will be the same. I mean, this is not a problem with the burn.

It seems as if there is something not quite right with the .vob creation...?

Someone?

Thank you!
