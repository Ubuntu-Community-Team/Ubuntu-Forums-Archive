### Post by cubeist on 2009-03-14
try
```
glxinfo | grep direct
```

This just outputs information about your video configuration, what you are looking for is *Yes* for Direct Rendering. If it says yes then your videos should play at full speed, but if direct rendering is not on, then it would make sense to have slow videos as the GPU is not actually doing the processing while compiz is running, rather the CPU is carrying the load...

### Post by cubeist on 2009-03-15
Check out post #5 in this thread for options you can add to your xorg.conf file that may fix the fullscreen problem:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=849422](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=849422)

I have tried all of the options, and in the end the default ati setup works the best for me, but YMMV.

Also, when using these options
Option "TexturedVideo" "on"
Option "VideoOverlay" "off"
Option "OpenGLOverlay" "off"

At all times you may only have one of those options as "on", if you turn on, for example, textured video and video overlay, your X session will crash and you will loose video (at least for me).
