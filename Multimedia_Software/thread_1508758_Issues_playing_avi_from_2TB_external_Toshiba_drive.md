### Post by sossanator on 2010-06-13
I used to store all my avi files (Tv Shows and Movies) on a 500 Gb ide drive. When i put it in my checked luggage the baggage handlers threw it to hard and i guess corrupting the HD. I then purchased a 2tb external Toshiba drive to recover files before the 500 completely crashed. I used the freezer method i read about on [www.lifehacker.com](www.lifehacker.com) to recover my files from the failing 500 Gb drive. I used rsync. But now when i try to play avi files from the 2Tb the shows are "jumpy." Is this due to corrupted files from the recovery process or is there a deeper issue.

I am running the avi files in vlc. Using 10.04 64 bit. My 2Tb is encrypted with AES with TrueCrypt, and my laptop is encrypted using the ubuntu encryption. HP Pavilion dv2000 is the computer.

Any ideas?

### Post by sossanator on 2010-10-07
So i did more research a while back and never got around to updating the post. I found out that the drive automatically spins down after a determined amount of time and when it spins up thats when the problem arises. The files were fine but when it reads them after the spin up thats when the issues start. It would be nice if there was a way to disable this spin down feature.
