### Post by dabl on 2010-04-29
After backing up your current /etc/X11/xorg.conf file, in the "Screen" stanza, add this line after your "option - metamodes ..." line:

```
    Option         "AddARGBGLXVisuals" "True"
```

At the end of the /etc/X11/xorg.conf file, append this little stanza:

```
Section "Extensions"
    Option         "Composite" "Enable"
EndSection
```

After restarting X (or rebooting), open a terminal window and try glxgears

If compositing is working, you'll see the gears.  If not ... then it's not fixed yet.

FYI, newer Nvidia drivers will work on your GF 8400, and the 195- series might do a better job.

Hope this helps.

Interesting.  There are actually no errors in the Xorg.0.log.  However, look at the (WW) (warning) lines, regarding the monitor. It's telling you that it's not able to "ping" the monitor and get a valid EDID from it.

A semi-wild guess would be that it is using bad refresh rates because it doesn't know what your monitor requires.  You might try (after learning the horiz. and vert. refresh specs for your monitor) setting a "modeline" in your xorg.conf file, to force it to use an appropriate mode.
