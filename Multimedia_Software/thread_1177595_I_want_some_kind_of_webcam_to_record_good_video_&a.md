### Post by chinoy on 2009-09-10
If its for Video then you can forget about anything USB which pretty much knocks out all your sub 100$ cams.

I have a USB 2Mp Integrated web cam on a high speed USB2 Bus.
And it cant cope with video in any resolution faster than 800x600 without droping frames.
I have tried in Vista / XP / Win7 and now Ubuntu. 
Picture quality in Ubuntu is the worst.

So if your budget is tight first figure out what is the min resolution your willing to live with.

If resolution is critical then the handy cam with a connection to your firewire port is the way to go all the best with trying to set it up in Ubuntu though.

Im trying to get my Handycam to do the same thing except my handy cam only has a composite out. So I purchased a ExpressCard PCI composite in which would allow me to take this composite signal in but things never work out as planed.
