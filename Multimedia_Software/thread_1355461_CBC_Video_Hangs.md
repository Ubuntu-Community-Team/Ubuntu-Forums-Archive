### Post by superdan006 on 2009-12-15
This thread is meant to complement the Launchpad.net bug report ([https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/firefox-3.5/+bug/455852](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/firefox-3.5/+bug/455852)) filed by George Farris on 2009-10-19.

Some Canadian Ubuntu and Linux users may have noticed that the CBC Video website has not worked in recent months. It appears that the site upgraded its video players and media content can no longer be shown in Linux. Many, who took note of this bug, decided to alert the technical staff at the CBC with hopes that it would be repaired.

One user, PaulNorth, received this response from an employee with CBC Audience Relations:
> Dear Paul,

We have checked with our technical staff, and unfortunately, as you point out, the new Flash player we are installing does not work on Linux systems. However it does work on almost all other systems, including Windows and Mac. The number of systems that could not see the video in our new player is likely around the 1% or less range.

We are not in a position to develop any workaround that would prevent revenue from being generated when users watch video on cbc.ca. Funding for CBC from the government of Canada has not been growing, and the funds from Parliament (less than $40 per Canadian taxpayer per year) only cover part of the Corporations broadcasting operations. I'm sorry you did not get an earlier response from CBC.

Parker Bishop
Communication Assistant
CBC Audience Relations

Such an excuse is upsetting for many who pride themselves on the Linux experience (Myself included). And while this won't be the last time Linux users face issues with compatibility, it shows a very sad side of the CBC not just to Canadians but others who enjoy public broadcasting in their country. 

Luckily, user Tarek Loubani saved the day with a wonderful "hack" which works around the issue. Anyone having problems viewing the CBC video website should visit Tarek's [http://tarek.org/cbc](http://tarek.org/cbc) and follow his instructions for viewing videos.

Those of you who feel the need to express your feelings to the CBC should visit [http://www.cbc.ca/contact/](http://www.cbc.ca/contact/) and file a complaint. Their phone number is listed 1-866-306-4636 and is Toll-Free (Only in Canada, I believe) for you to call, as well.

Thanks for your time,
-Dan
