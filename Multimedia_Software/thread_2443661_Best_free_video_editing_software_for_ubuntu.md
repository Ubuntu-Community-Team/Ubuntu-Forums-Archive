### Post by ajgreeny on 2020-05-18
I have used only Openshot and have to admit that it is very easy to use and even the defaults of, for example, fading between separate clips joined together works very well.

From what I have read about the other applications, they all appear to work similarly in practice, so try them out and see which you prefer; it may depend on exactly how complicated your requirements are.
Don't forget you need a reasonably powerful machine to use any of these and even then an edit can take a longish time depending on the input file sizes and filetypes and the output type you wish create.

People making videos have different needs than someone just trying to remove specific parts.  if you don't want to transcode the entire video, which always lowers quality, then you don't want any of the major editors.
