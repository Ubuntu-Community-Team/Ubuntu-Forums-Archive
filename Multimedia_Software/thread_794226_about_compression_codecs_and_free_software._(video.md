### Post by disturbedite on 2008-05-14
kino is excellent for importing video from digital video cameras.  after importing, i'd recommend using avidemux, it is like a much better version of virtualdub for windows, if you're familiar with VD.

if you want a free video codec, i would recommend xvid, but its development is dead.  but you could use [OGM]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OGM"). but it is not a format/codec, but a container.  (i.e. avi & mov are media containers).  OGM supports many different audio & video formats.

another alernative that may have even better cross platform support is mkv.  (matroska).  it is similar to OGM, it also supports many audio & video formats.

for my own uses, i use h264 & ac3.  for free formats i just use theora+ogg in the OGM container.
