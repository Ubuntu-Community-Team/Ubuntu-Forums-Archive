### Post by phedup on 2015-03-08
Hi,
I assume you're talking about outboard DVD players, right?  Dvd's that play on a computer system have a different file extension than DVD's that play on outboard DVD players.  If you want to get them to play on the DVD player, you should pick .wav or .cda as a file extension when ripping and saving them, and they ought to play.  That's been my experience, though it's been quite some time ago.
Good luck.
