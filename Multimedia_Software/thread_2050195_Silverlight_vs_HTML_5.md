### Post by evilsoup on 2012-08-30
Content streaming services will switch to HTML5 only if someone develops reliable DRM... at which point it wouldn't be 'pure' HTML, and it's a toss-up whether that DRM would be compatible with Linux. There is no compelling reason for them to move away from Silverlight until Microsoft stops supporting it, and even then it'll probably be a few years, unless someone finds a devastating exploit.

A government website with no need for DRM has no reason to use Silverlight, though, and if they are then that is terrible.
