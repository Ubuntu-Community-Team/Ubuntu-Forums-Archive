### Post by markbuntu on 2008-07-02
Any audio/video you own or someone gives you or makes avilable to you who is legally entititled to do so is legally playable in gstreamer or any other player no matter what codec it is inscribed with. This is the fair use doctrine as defined by the US Supreme Court. You buy it, you can use it however you want for your own personal enjoyment. Public broadcasts are also freely useable for your own personal entertainment. 

You cross the line only when you do any of three things, distribute, broadcast, or otherwise publicly disseminate copyrighted material without the permission of the copyright holder. 

Other countries have different views on this. That is partly why most of the codec decoders are not available through the normal Ubuntu repositories.

As far as what formats are playable, I can't say, a better question might be which ones are not.

Countries, I don't know. Why don't you talk to an international IP lawyer.

There is no violation when these codecs are used for playback for personal use, they are freely available for that. 

Business use is an entirely different matter and for that it does not matter which player you use or which operating system, you need the license/permission of the patent/copyright holder to use this stuff.
