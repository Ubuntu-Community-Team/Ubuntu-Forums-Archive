### Post by mtbsoft on 2009-02-23
I received the following message in my ATI Catalyst Update RSS feed today...

> Catalyst updates will no longer be available as an RSS feed We are excited to announce the availability of exclusive updates on Twitter!!

...this coming just three weeks after the previous feed change...

> Catalyst releases will be announced on a unified RSS feed with Catalyst releases for Microsoft Windows operating systems. Please visit the Catalyst driver download web site to subscribe to the new RSS feed.

Now I don't know about you but I, for one, have no intention of exposing myself to the inanities of Twitter for the sole purpose of getting driver update notifications.  

Ignoring the fact that RSS feeds are ideal for this sort of product update and the fact that Twitter (and the like) are all blocked in nearly every location that I work at, what is the point of this change?  Have they never heard of the old adage "if it ain't broke, don't fix it"?

So if you are an ATI customer and are as annoyed as I am about this latest change, please go to the ATI website are send them some feedback - maybe, just maybe, they might come back to the real world and keep the RSS feed too.

Wow, power of the people rules.  Looked at the twit(ter) thread about this and it seems the manager in charge of this idea was unaware that lots of people use the RSS feed and don't want to lose it.  Seems like he's going to reinstate it.

Not a big victory but a victory nonetheless.
