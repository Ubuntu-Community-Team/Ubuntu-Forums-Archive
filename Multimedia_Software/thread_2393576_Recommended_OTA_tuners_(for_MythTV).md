### Post by TheFu on 2018-06-05
I have HDHR4-US and HDHR3-US tuners. Highly recommend them.  I do not use MythTV, but the HDHR4 is DLNA compatible so it works with pretty much any OS.  I watch live TV through it on a raspberry-pi v2 with the mpeg2 license and Kodi. Took next to nothing to get working. OTA broadcasts are ATSC where I live in mpeg2 1080i ts format.  
MeTV and VLC also work and recording a show can be accomplished using **wget** worst case.  I have a little script that does it with wget.

I've never gotten the HDHR3 to work with Linux, but didn't try very hard.  

Of course, where I live, these tuners will all need to be replaced when ATSCv3 becomes the broadcast standard and breaks all current tuners like they did switching to digital ATSC or digital cable. I expect every 15-20 yrs or so, similar mandated changes will happen.
