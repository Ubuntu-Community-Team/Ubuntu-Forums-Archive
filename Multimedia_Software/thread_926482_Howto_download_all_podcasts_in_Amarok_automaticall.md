### Post by jmjohn on 2008-10-08
Hey all,

Thanks for your comments.  I figured it out, mostly.

If you want Amarok to automatically download a podcast, you have to first subscribe as described here: [http://www.newlinuxuser.com/howto-use-amarok-to-listen-to-podcasts/](http://www.newlinuxuser.com/howto-use-amarok-to-listen-to-podcasts/) .  Next, right click the podcast and tell it to "automatically download media".  

Finally, this is the odd thing, you have to right click the most recent podcast under your subscribed podcasts and click "Download Media".  Now, when you right-click podcasts (under playlists) and hit "refresh all" it actually downloads them to disk.

Also by right-clicking podcasts and selecting "Scan Interval" you can tell it to check for podcasts ever x hours.  I haven't really tested that to see if it works, but it appears not to.

And google IS my buddy...  ...but this quirk wasn't documented anywhere I could find, and I couldn't get it working for a few days.
