### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2011-01-18
IMO.
That PC spec is only okay as a power wasting backend server.
AGP graphics & pentium toaster processor not even a good boat anchor.

Consider separate backend & frontend boxes.

Modern CPUs & mobos are much more efficient & capable.
The video card is > 10 years old. 
The best solution for MPEG-4/10 H264 is PCIe vdpau purevideo feature set C GPU.

A good projector is a lot of money for a limited brightness & lifetime...
Big plasmas & LCDs are very cheap.

Blue-ray is not working "out-of-the-box" for encrypted commercial disks.
MythTV 0.24 can play blue-ray disk file system if not encrypted.

Study doom9 website etc before wasting money on blue-ray optical drive.

### Post by perrti-y02 on 2011-01-18
I would say that if you ever intend to play HD movies you will need a much better machine than that. I have a 2.66GHz core 2 duo which when playing full HD gives a processor usage of around 70%.

I would also advice against getting a Blu-Ray drive for some time. It is possible to get it to work but, for the most part, it is more hassle than it is worth. You would be better off buying the cheapest Blu-Ray player that you can.

Also, if you intend to stream over the network, I would personally not use wireless. From my limited experience streaming over wireless can be a little bit choppy from time to time. I still haven't worked out why, so it could just be my network, but a wired network (if possible) would probably be better.
