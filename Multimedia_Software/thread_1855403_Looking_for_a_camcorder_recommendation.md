### Post by WasMeHere on 2011-10-06
It depends how you are going to use it.

If you want something cheap, that makes reasonably good movies, you can buy a regular camera (for photos), that can record video in 1280x720 format. The simpler ones make avi files with low compression, and the max file size 4 GB of the FAT filesystem may be a severe limitation for each video clip. The video files are easy to upload to your computer via USB, and they can be edited and converted (compressed) later on.

If you want to record on tape there several classic camcorders and some of them are quite cheap. Usually you connect to your computer via firewire, which is a little tricky in linux, but I have done it with *Kino*. The quality of the recording can be good and the length of recording is limited by the battery or tape length, often approx 1 hour.

More advanced video cameras can produce full high definition video, 1920x1080 progressive or interlaced, but they are not cheap yet. These cameras often make AVCHD format MTS files with high compression, that are easy to upload via USB. The length of recording is limited by the battery or flash card size, often approx 1 hour for maximum quality and longer for reduced quality (interlaced video).
