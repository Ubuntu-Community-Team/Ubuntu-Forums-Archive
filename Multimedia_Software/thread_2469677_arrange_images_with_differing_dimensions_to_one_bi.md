### Post by TheFu on 2021-12-06
Montage is the term.  I think imagemagick does that. Yep. [https://imagemagick.org/script/montage.php](https://imagemagick.org/script/montage.php)
I've seen better results.  

There is a guy who makes impressive imagemagick scripts. [http://www.fmwconcepts.com/imagemagick/index.php](http://www.fmwconcepts.com/imagemagick/index.php)  Those take complex things and make them easy.

[https://legacy.imagemagick.org/Usage/montage/](https://legacy.imagemagick.org/Usage/montage/) has some examples.

There are probably easier tools. Hopefully, someone will post something about them.

### Post by Dennis N on 2021-12-12
I wouldn't rely on some automatic arranger. I have done these a number of times by importing the images onto a background layer in Inkscape, dragging them where I want, and sometimes resizing an image if needed. With Inkscape, you can also add text and graphics to the montage as desired.
