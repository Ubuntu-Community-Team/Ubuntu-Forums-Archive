### Post by oldfred on 2013-02-24
It is my understanding that there is only one Intel driver and it is the open source one. That is from Intel. But is regularly being updated.
You might try the i915.i915_enable_rc6=1 setting.
I would test all settings with rebooting and at grub menu use e and change quiet splash to a new setting.

       Some other settings:
[http://ubuntu-tutorials.com/2010/05/06/ubuntu-10-04-lucid-blank-screen-at-startup-workaround/](http://ubuntu-tutorials.com/2010/05/06/ubuntu-10-04-lucid-blank-screen-at-startup-workaround/)


[LIST]
[*]Older Intel video card: i915.modeset=1 or i915.modeset=0 newer:  i915.i915_enable_rc6=1
[*]nVidia: nomodeset
[*]Generic: xforcevesa or nouveau.modeset=0
[*]Radeon: radeon.modeset=0
[/LIST]
     Some systems need other settings.
       [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1817374](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1817374)
acpi_osi=linux pci=noacpi
    
And someone posted this:
       For Intel graphics running slow:
sudo apt-get install mesa-utils
glxinfo | grep OpenGL | head -n3
