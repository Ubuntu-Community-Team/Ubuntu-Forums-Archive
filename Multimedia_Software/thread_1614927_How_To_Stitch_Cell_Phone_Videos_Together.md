### Post by Tootler on 2011-01-01
If you are comfortable with kino, then click on "File>Insert After" or the corresponding icon on the toolbar (looks like a scrap of 35mm film) and navigate to your movie clips, select the desired one and click "Open". Kino will import your file converting it to DV format as it does so. I have imported both .MOV and .AVI clips OK this way.
 
Repeat with your other movie clips. You can then edit them as normal and export to your desired format - presumably .DV if you are wanting to create a DVD. Creating DVD's is something I am not familiar with as I usually export to MPEG and upload to You Tube or copy them to a USB stick.
