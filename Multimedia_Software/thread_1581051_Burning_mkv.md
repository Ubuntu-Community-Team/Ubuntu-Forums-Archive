### Post by endotherm on 2010-09-24
in the long run, your best bet if you want to do this regularly, is to set up a home theartre PC (just a PC with a TV-out or HDMI hooked to your TV). that way you don't have to convert it at all. 

I used to use a nero product to rerender avi's to DVD, but it was hit and miss, as not all players knew both -R and +R media, and some couldn't deal with burned media at all. it was a real pain, since the encoding could take 14-18 hours per disk, required a disk, and was a horribly inefficient use of space. 

just my two bits
