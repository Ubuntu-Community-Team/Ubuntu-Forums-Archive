### Post by evets25 on 2008-07-16
Listen is pretty good as well, though its development seems to be less active of late. :/ Still, it's a excellent gtk-based music player that's commonly overlooked. Also, if you want to go uber-nerdy and lightweight, take a look MPD - Music Player Daemon. MPD uses a client server model, which means MPD runs as a service in the background, which you connect to with any one of several dozen different clients (like sonata). The client is your gui to control it, and MPD does the actual work of playing the music and whatnot. 

enjoy.

### Post by TeeLicht on 2008-07-17
I'd recommend [gmusicbrowser]("http://squentin.free.fr/gmusicbrowser/gmusicbrowser.html") as you don't have to install the gstreamer backend for it as gmusicbrowser can use the MPlayer backend as well. So you can use GMPlayer instead of Totem to avoid pulling in loads of Gnome-Dependencies. It's also very configurable and quite low on resources.
