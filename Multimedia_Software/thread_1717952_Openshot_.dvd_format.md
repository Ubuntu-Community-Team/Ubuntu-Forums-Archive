### Post by PZJM on 2011-04-01
Well, I guess I'll answer my own question after days of research.  Openshot seems to be the only video editing software that performs well for me.  I've tested KDEnLive, Pitivi, and a few others but the main thing I noticed was the Openshot was able to graphically keep the video up-to-date while viewing.  KDEnLive had some serious lag issues and occasionally crash.  The same went with Pitivi.  The only bummer part about Openshot is the output and burning capability.  Also, good luck trying to register for their forum and their webmaster seems to be non-responsive.  Anyway, I needed to download Devede to convert the Openshot output format into something more useful.  Then, I used k3b to burn it to disc.  This seems like a long winded process but it work.  Next, I will be testing DVDStyler in hopes of making a more professional home movie.
