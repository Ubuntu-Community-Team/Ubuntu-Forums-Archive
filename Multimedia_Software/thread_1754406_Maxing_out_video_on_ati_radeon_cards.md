### Post by Duncan Williams on 2011-05-10
After installing a new agp graphics card on my pc a few weeks back > ATI HD3650 with 512mb ram. (agp)

I had quiet a few problems re-installing ubuntu and getting the most out of my graphics card.

I learnt a few things along the way though.

> Use proprietory ati drivers (should be available in `additional Hardware' or synaptic).

> Do not play videos etc, from `windows' partition (copy files to ubuntu partition) as they will play up.

> Use ati catalyst control centre to adjust your 3d settings to get hd video.
(adjust while video playing in minimised mode)  (try them all on maximum/quality) 

> make sure everything related and associated is up to date (inc, update manager - additional hardware - latest versions)

> test video card in windows first (if you have dual boot)

> Double check all your bios settings especially re/ agp/sata - dram - pci - usb - S.M.A.R.T Hard Drive (off).
