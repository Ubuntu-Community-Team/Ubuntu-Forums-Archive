Right now I guess I'll stick with Rubyripper as its reliable even though it takes twice as long.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-08
I always use rubyripper to rip on linux systems. I still think that EAC is slightly better- it doesnt seem to take as long to rip, even though I always use 'test and copy' which like rubyripper rips the tracks twice and compares them. EAC also seems to deal with damaged discs better than rubyripper. 

I'd prepared to spend longer ripping, as I've got a dual-core CPU and because I always rip to .flac, then put my discs into storage. Better to do it once, and then never have to worry about it again IMO.
