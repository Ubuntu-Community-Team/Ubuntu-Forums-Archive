### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-02-28
Powered hubs can sometimes bleed power back into the host computer and that can cause issues.  I suspect that you need a 5VDC 2.0 Amp supply for your beagle bone black.

[http://beagleboard.org/peripheral](http://beagleboard.org/peripheral)  (1.2 amp is the minimum recommended).

A quick google search shows 2-amp power supplies seems to be the ticket.  Try some different USB hubs.  Also running a hard drive and anything else on a hub is generally not advised because it can cause corruption of the drive as well as power drops since the hard drive uses so much power.

If you want to learn of the problems with USB on embedded boards check out this video:  [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rzKI0ASB_Js](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rzKI0ASB_Js) (start at 6 hours 24 minutes).

### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-02-28
Paper specs and reality are two different things.  I had the same issue with a RaspberryPi.  Too many devices plugged in and you will have problems.  I now have several 2A power supplies.  They are large because they perform a lot of filtering.  The power supply that you use to charge a cell phone and the power supply you use to run a computer are different.  AC ripple, high frequency noise, voltage sag, etc are all worse with phone power supply.  Check them with an oscope if you have one.  A decent power supply with be the size of a small laptop brick.  A cell phone power supply is the size of travel shampoo--and works just as well.

Many small linux boards will run with a phone power supply, but that's about it.  Hook up even a mouse and you start having issues.

Good luck with your experiments.
