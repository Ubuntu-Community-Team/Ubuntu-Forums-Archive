### Post by VeeDubb on 2008-11-09
out of curiosity, how is your external drive formatted?

If it's NTFS, it would be badly fragmented at this point, and in DESPERATE need of a defrag, and I have no idea how to accomplish that from ubuntu.

That would certainly explain the slow-down.

What you would need to do in that case, is first find a way to defrag the drive, and then you would want to consider a way to back the data up somewhere else, reformat to something that isn't plagued by fragmentation, and move the data back.

If it's ext3, fragmentation isn't the issue, and i have no clue.
