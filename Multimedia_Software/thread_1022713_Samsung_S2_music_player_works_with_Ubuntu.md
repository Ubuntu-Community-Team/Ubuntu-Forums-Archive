### Post by ahood on 2008-12-27
Hello,

I just want to report that my wife and I gave our son a [**[COLOR="Sienna"]Samsung YP-S2 music player[/COLOR]**]("http://www.samsung.com/us/consumer/detail/detail.do?group=audiovideo&type=mp3players&subtype=flashmemory&model_cd=YP-S2ZB/XAA") for Christmas and it works well with Ubuntu. 

Because this is my son's first portable music playing device, we wanted to give my son an inexpensive unit, compatible with Ubuntu and plays OGG. 

The S2 fits the bill ([**[COLOR="Sienna"]~$30[/COLOR]**]("http://www.google.com/products?hl=en&client=firefox-a&rls=com.ubuntu:en-US:unofficial&q=samsung+s2+pebble&um=1&ie=UTF-8&sa=X&oi=product_result_group&resnum=1&ct=title")) very nicely. It is UMS, which means that Ubuntu recognizes it by simply connecting it with the USB dongle that came with the device. Adding music is simply a matter of drag-n-drop via the Ubuntu File Manager. The S2 plays our ogg encoded music, but also will play mp3 coded music too.

It doesn't have many thrills for features, but it does what it is meant to do (play music) in a small package.

The only negative is the proprietary USB dongle, but I can see Samsung did this to save space.
