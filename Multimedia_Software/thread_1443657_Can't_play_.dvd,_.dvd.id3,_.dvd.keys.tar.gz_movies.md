### Post by hitman_dreams on 2010-03-31
Hi all, I was hoping someone in here would have some insight that will help me. I setup Linuxmce a while back to use as a media computer and I put a whole bunch of my DVD library on it. I want to move all of those movies over to my Ubuntu 9.10 laptop, but they are in a weird format that I don't know what to do with. Each movie was put into three separate files, .dvd, .dvd.id3, and .dvd.keys.tar.gz(this has two folders with files inside that look like this inside: e1:5c:47:64:f2, and each file is 16bytes, assuming this is memory?). Individually none of these work, and I can't seem to get them to work together in Ubuntu. Instead of re-ripping all of my DVDs to Ubuntu, which would take weeks, is there a way to get these movies to play? Thanks.

---

### Post by hitman_dreams on 2010-04-01
Well, I finally figured it out. Just right click on the .dvd file and open with another application, VLC Player for example, and it plays. I believe you must have the .dvd.id3 and .dvd.keys.tar.gz for it to work though. Hope this helps someone else.
