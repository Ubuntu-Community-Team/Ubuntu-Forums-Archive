### Post by brometheus on 2011-05-15
I've recently switched over to linux from windows vista- and for the most part I'm really loving my Ubuntu 11.04, more-so now than ever as most of the kinks I've encountered are ironed out. This desktop is mostly used for gaming and the web, I don't ask too much :) but vista was a pain in my rear from the onset, and I hoped to improve the systems performance by ridding myself of it (32-bit vista's main drawbacks for me 4gig memory cap, insanely slow load times, instability)
Vista did, however, get me accustomed to running fullscreen flash content on my second monitor while gaming fullscreen on the primary --all with SLI enabled. 
Natty is having none of same problems vista did (thankfully!) but it's come with its own inherit flaws...

So on to the issue- 

Lack of support for TwinView with SLI enabled- I've got 2 nvidia 9800gt video cards installed using nVidia's proprietary 270.41.06 driver, along with 2 monitors. The best I've been able to do in Natty is Twinview without SLI, leaving one card useless. This mode actually runs really well and without conflicts, games run at a reasonable framerate, VLC and mPlayer can go fullscreen on monitor 2 (flash won't). But still, video card number two, sits there chillin, hangin out, laughing at the other computer parts who are actually doing work. I want to get him back into the action!
I've tried switching to Xinerama, but it conflicts with compiz (so i have to replace with metacity, not a huge issue), it also gives me flash issues and, most importantly, ruins the framerate in games (even with SLI enabled in Xinerama mode).
I'm not sure if seperate X screens support SLI,  or if it would help performance- but I'd prefer to not lose the single desktop feel.

I'm definitely now a Ubuntu fan, and the experience was great, but really the goal behind this ordeal was to improve system performance over vista, and without utilizing both cards all Natty's gains are more or less evened out. So I need another option.

Someone tell me I've missed something and SLI totally works for Twinview, I'm just crazy, or maybe I'm just using the wrong distro... anyway, any thoughts or ideas or whatever you've got for me, I'm willing to try almost anything, even returning to MS if I have too :(
