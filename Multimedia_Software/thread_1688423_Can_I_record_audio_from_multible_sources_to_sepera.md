### Post by surfmonkee on 2011-02-15
a quick and dirty solution would be to record each track of tape in to audacity to a separate mono track

line up the three tracks and mix as required, then export to stereo or mono and keep the project for later edits.

warm the tape machine up for 20 minutes with tape playing to get the capstan soft and smooth warm motor so you dont get speed variations from the tape.

you do not need a multitrack card.

once the tracks are recorded save each as a mono wav and go to ardour to do amazing mix and edits too. i save hours of multi track tape this way with good results

connect one track output from the tascam to the line in on yr pc, in audacity preferences select one track mono to record

hit record, play the first track in, stop.
mute that track
hit record and play in the second track, a new track will automatically appear in audacity each time u hit record.

repeat

unmute the tracks, cut the silence off each track front and use the slide tool (left and right arrow tool in the toolbar) to slide the tracks in to time. zoom in for accuracy

done, export as mp3,wav,ogg , save the project for further use,

have fun
