### Post by yogo on 2009-04-02
This is not a spam thread.

It can be quite an ordeal to get a webcam working in Ubuntu. I went through an ordeal that took me several days to finally get my Creative webcam to work. It would be mind boggling to explain what lengths I went to.

I ordered a webcam from Ebay from Hong Kong and got it for 6 bucks with free shipping. This thing is great. It is driverless and installed in seconds on my Laptop with XP Pro and then I took it and connected it to my desktop with Ubuntu and it immeadiately worked. 

There are several driverless webcams on Ebay so I am going to pick up a few for other family members..

The camera is much better than my Creative and was not the problem to get it going in 8.10

Just thought I would post this as it may same someone hours of frustration trying to get theirs going.

### Post by cariboo on 2009-04-02
There is no such thing as driverless hardware. The drivers for your webcam are being installed automagically either on bootup or when you plug your device in. It seems the far eastern manufacturers use comodity chipsets that are supported in the kernel, whereas will all know how good Creative's support of Linux is. Many of their products don't work well in Windows, so why should we expect them to work any better with Linx.
