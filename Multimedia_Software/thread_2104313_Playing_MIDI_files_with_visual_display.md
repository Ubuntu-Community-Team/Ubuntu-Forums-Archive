### Post by Adam_GUI on 2013-01-14
For the record,
The Baldwin Fun Machine Organ and rhytm machine [_more here_]("http://m.matrixsynth.com/2009/12/baldwin-fun-machine-organ-and-rythm.html") were "fun" ways to go about enjoying sheet music.  As time went by, the name stuck with other brands and some models would often teach you how to play pre-programed songs by lighting up the keys in order.  It's a visual reference of what keys to press when.  [_Like this YouTube Video Explaining Fur Elise_]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M6i8atZ5EFo&list=PL8E89A1326C482365").

As opposed to the Sheet Music Staff notation that you'd get in a common beginner's manual.  Like, say [_Mel Bay's You Can Teach Yourself Piano_]("http://www.amazon.com/Mel-Bays-Teach-Yourself-Piano/dp/0786641037/ref=sr_1_2?s=books&ie=UTF8&qid=1358148326&sr=1-2&keywords=Mel+Bay+Piano") Book.
