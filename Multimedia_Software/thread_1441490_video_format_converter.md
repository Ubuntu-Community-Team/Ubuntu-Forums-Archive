### Post by PaiSand on 2010-03-29
Or just mount the ISO image as a drive and watch it like if it was another drive.
If I remember well you can do it from Nautilus, or at least that worked for me one time. When you try to open it or doing right click you have the option to mount it as a drive, and then you can browse it like with any other disk drive.

If that simple solution don't work do a search on the forum to see if you find the answer.
