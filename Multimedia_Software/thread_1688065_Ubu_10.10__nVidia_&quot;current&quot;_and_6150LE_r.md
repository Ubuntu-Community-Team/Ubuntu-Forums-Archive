### Post by BicyclerBoy on 2011-02-15
The EDID probe/read from monitor failed.
This seems to be a common problem with either 10.10 or kernel or driver..

With no valid EDID, the driver makes very safe video mode choices.
To fix this now, you need to add an option to your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file.
Screen section:

    Option "UseEDID" "FALSE"
The driver will make a mode pool of all std internal video modes.
You can choose the correct resolution from nvidia-settings prgm & then save it back into the xorg.conf (fix it permanently).

You may need to set some values in the monitors section for HorizSync & VertRefresh.
Not sure what the defaults (not tabled) ranges are..
HorizSync 27.0 - 60.0
    VertRefresh 49.0 - 75.0

In addition:
You should comment out the modules section in the xorg.conf file; AFAIK with nvidia driver, all required modules are auto-loaded in the correct sequence.
