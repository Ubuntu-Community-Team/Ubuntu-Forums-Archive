### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-02-14
It's obviously not communicating with your Sony camera in ptp mode, but is in USB mass storage.  Why not use that?  It can still be used with f-spot, if you want to for importing photos, I think, even if you use a card reader.  My camera does take ages to show anything in ptp mode, whilst USB is almost instant, so perhaps you just need to wait longer in ptp mode.

I must admit I just drag and drop photos with a card reader, though my camera (Panasonic DMC-FZ38) works with both USB and ptp modes.  I put photos into the correct sub-folder in my Pictures folder in home.  I have never put everything into one huge folder with tags alone to sort them; it seems too dangerous to me as I am not sure all tags are application interchangeable, and I would much rather be able to find photos using a file manager if needed, rather than the "overkill" of something like f-spot.

In fact I mostly use gthumb for the simplest photo activities, as it has such a comparatively low overhead of resources; it is also getting much better as it matures.
