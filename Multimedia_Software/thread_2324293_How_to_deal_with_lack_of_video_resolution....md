### Post by JimHiTek on 2016-05-12
**Background: I've been using Win10** for several months on a newer ASUS mobo (Quad core) tower computer. Because of the particularities of the hardware, I've had to utilize DVI to output to HDMI on my Vizio Smart TV. The DVI-HDMI conversion is easily accomplished with a simple and readily available cable as the smart TV only accepts HDMI. When I want to watch a NFL video on the smart TV, it's not that complicated, I just turn on the TV, set it for HDMI, and up pops my desktop. Navigate to the game, and bingo, there it is, fills but doesn't overfill the screen. Using the TV remote, I have 4 choices of screen 'Mode': Normal, Wide, Panoramic, Zoom. The resolution is set by the source material, and for just the desktop screen, 1080p is displayed. Which also tends to be typically output via HDMI for the videos I watch. HD Netflix movies display higher resolutions. Some older youtube vids display 720p. That all works and it's easy to manipulate.

**Now to my problem: I'm satisfied** with the capabilities of the Win10 OS as far as the video goes, but naturally, I want to move to Ubuntu and have tried several times over the months to solve this video resolution issue. Which is this, when I use Ubuntu 14.04 *with the exact same hardware, *changing nothing except installing and booting from the Ubuntu HDD, I cannot get the resolution control I have with Win10. I get two choices, Normal & Zoom, both of which are unsatisfactory for most of the videos I try to watch on the TV. I have tried adjusting any and every setting I find in the display setup of Ubuntu to no avail. I've tried adjusting the resolution manually at the TV but that is not satisfactory either.
[B]
My question: [/B]Is there an app that will give me more control of video output from Ubuntu?? Is there some hidden setting I'm suppose to use?

What graphics card are you using and what driver? If using AMD or Nvidia graphics cards you'll get the most control over the hardware with the proprietary drivers which are not installed by default.
