### Post by highgeere on 2009-02-17
You probably just need to check your TV's manual for the necessary resolution and refresh rate. That said, I also had trouble using my TV as my primary display, but it works perfectly as a secondary display via d-sub, hdmi, or dvi to hdmi. I think the reason is that when you are adding a second display you allow ubuntu to recognize the display and automatically configure the output settings accordingly. When you try to connect your TV to the same output that you previously had a monitor connected to, the interface is still configured per the requirements of the previous monitor. This would be especially troublesome if the monitor was a CRT.
