There are a couple things to look at here...First, have you changed media? The type of DVD's that you use? Are you using DVD+R or DVD-R? Have you changed since using it before?

Players are very finicky and sometimes if you don't do that one thing more correctly they will freak out...ok....so the test I would give you is to go get some reputable DVD media. If your burner only does one type, then stick with that. If your burner can do +/- get a small pack of both. 

I have also heard that you can run into a bad batch of media that will give all kinds of errors. You can get some with pitted foil causing the laser to lose focus to having some with tainted substrate...ie, bad lamination. This again can cause focus issues and problems. 

Back to our tests. So...get the two...or a different manufacturer of DVD media and burn something on both discs at what ever rate you normally use...test results on players.

If this produces the same problems, repeat the tests using a slower speed. I used not the slowest speed but in between on mine...you may want to try the lowest speed. It will produce more accurate results...

Now...let's examine some things about k9Copy. How close to the 4.7Gb cut off are you getting? If you are getting 4.6 and some change you may be overdoing it and thus creating a coaster for some decks and an ok ride for others...check this when your iso is made. I just made a back up last night and I full compressed everything else on the disc and pulled as much compression off of the main move is K9 would let me. 

Other things that will boost your quality in the process, look for added games that I consider, useless to the movie...do you need the previews??? No??? Remove them by unchecking them. Each time, watch the bar at the bottom to move to the greener side of things. I have also found the the windows DVD shrink works with Wine and you can use that...but I have found you have to trick it to mount it and then put your DVD in to make it shrink what you need...Remove any thing you don't need period..such as any extra audio tracks... I can't speak french or spanish well so, they were removed. I removed a two channel audio track for the hearing impaired because even though I am hard of hearing, I can hear still...just stuff like that that might make a difference to your burned disc.

So, try those tips out and see what you get...the other thing to do is to make the iso but don't burn using K9. I don't. I make the iso and then burn with Brasero.
