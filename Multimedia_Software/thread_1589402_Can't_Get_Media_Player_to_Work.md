### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-10-06
Hello
You should try installing a package called ubuntu-restricted-extras, which will allow you to play the majority of audio files in existence.

Regarding audio CDs: you don't "install" these to your PC, you "rip" them. Ripping means you take the raw audio from the CD and compress and encode it to a smaller size.
I would recommend you install a program designed for ripping CDs, such as sound juicer. It is simple to use and lets you decide what quality you want. I'd recommend using mp3 format at a bitrate of 256kbps. It will also download the correct tracklisting and numbering for you.

It is hard to say what is going on with Rhythmbox, tracks should play in the order they appear in. If they are playing/appearing in the wrong order then you can click on the track number heading to sort them in sequence (I take it your music has track numbers).

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-10-07
If you want to move files from your sd card to your PC you will need to tell your computer to do so. Open up your file browser, which is Places>Home on the main taskbar. On the left you should see your SD card listed so open it up, then select all your music, copy it (edit>copy), and paste it (edit>paste) to your music folder in home.
