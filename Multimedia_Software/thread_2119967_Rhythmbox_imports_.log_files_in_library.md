### Post by uc50_ic4more on 2013-03-02
You mean these files are getting imported into your playlist and are being displayed alongside actual audio??? I have had many log files and playlist files, etc. reported as importing errors in Rhythmbox but never anything non-audio "successfully" imported into my library!

I guess the first two things I'd try are:

1) removing the files from your library
If you have set Rhythmbox to auto-import files from your Music directory ("watch for new files" it may be called in the preferences dialogue) these files may well end up right back in your library.

2) remove the offending files from the directory(ies) from which Rhythmbox forms it's library
All of my music is in ~/Music and I have set Rhythmbox to "watch" that directory for new material. Whenever I get something new, I simply drag and drop it into ~/Music and by the time I look for it, Rhythmbox has imported it. I ensure that ~/Music is free of files that are unnecessary: I prune album cover .jpg's, playlist files and .txt files before placing music in ~/Music.
