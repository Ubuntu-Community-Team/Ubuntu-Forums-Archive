### Post by Sethalos on 2009-05-15
I am incredibly impressed with the new SongBird jukebox application. I have been using Amorok for a few years now and I have always found it buggy and very slow for my large music library (550 Gb).

I tried the new Songbird last week and was completely blown away by the layout, the extensions, the ease of use, and the features. When the heck did this all happen, the last time I used Songbird it wasn't all that great of a music app.

So if you haven't given this app a looksee in awhile, I highly recommend it.

A few of the addons I really like are the: LastFM Images, mAshtape, LyricMaster, MediaFlow, and The Exorcist (which saved me a crap load of time and effort).

So, just sharing my views here, but this is by far the best music app IMHO for Linux users. 

My only con about this app is lack of a burning option for music cd's, mp3 cd/dvd's..etc.  

I still use MediaMonkey on my Windows Platform because of this, but if they can add a burning option, I would switch in a heartbeat, for now when I am just going to listen to music, SongBird is it.

Seth
