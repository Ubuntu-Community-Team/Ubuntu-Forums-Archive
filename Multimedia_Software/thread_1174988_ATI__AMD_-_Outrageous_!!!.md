### Post by QuinnHarris on 2009-05-31
I just switched from a NVIDIA 8600GTS to an ATI X1650 because the NVIDIA performance in KDE 4.2 sucked. It was a known issue it got a little better with later drivers but never very good.  So I can't say NVIDIA is all that great.  If you don't need great 3D performance your best bet is an ATI card in the X1xxx (r5xx) or earlier that are well supported by the open source drivers.  Hopefully the newer ATI/AMD cards will be well supported by the open source driver within the year.  Intel would work but I understand 9.04 has some issues with Intel that are expected to be fixed in 9.10.

### Post by Melcar on 2009-05-31
For r5xx chips and older you got the open source drivers, which provide 2D/3D acceleration and decent performance/functionality (by years end it should be even better).  For newer cards (r6xx/r7xx) you got the proprietary driver.  I really don't see the big deal.  This is far better than what you get from Intel or even nvidia.
