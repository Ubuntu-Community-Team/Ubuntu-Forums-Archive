### Post by joseph1975 on 2010-03-01
First of all, let me say that I love Kubuntu, especially 4.3, and it is my first choice for a distro.

I have a Hauppauge 1250 tv tuner in my computer (works out of the box), and installed in my desktop computer.  It is a AMD 2850e processor, 2gb ram, and Nvidia integrated graphics card.  I have used the Nvidia driver in Restricted Hardware, as well as use the PPA Nvidia repository for the updated version (195).  The live tv in Kubuntu 9.10 (using Kaffeine) is very choppy and unwatchable (particularly the HD signals).  Thinking that I had underpowered hardware, I decided to give Opensuse 11.2 a whirl.  I installed the Nvidia repository for Opensuse, started up Kaffeine, and to my amazement, the live tv worked like a charm.  Even the HD channels came in crystal clear with no choppiness at all.

Anyone playing around with the Alphas of Kubuntu 10.04 have better luck than 9.10?  I've installed 4 OS's over the past couple of days, and am a little tired of switching for a little while.  I'm really hoping Kubuntu 10.04 comes through, as I really like their setup of KDE better.
