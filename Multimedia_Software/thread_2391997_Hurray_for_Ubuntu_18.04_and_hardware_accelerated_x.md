### Post by ltburch2000 on 2018-05-15
I have really struggled with this, getting a high bitrate 4K h.265 stream decoded in earlier versions of Ubuntu, looked like it was going to take a machine with either stand alone nVidia card and possibly a 8+ thread CPU, even a 7th gen 4 thread i7 came no where close.  However 18.04 now supports the Intel 7th gen h.265 hardware decoding and I can run smoothly even with high bit rate 4K video with a i5 with Intel Iris 640 @ about 50% CPU.  I had really thought the X Box One X would be up to the job but strangely MS still does not have h.265 built into either their media player or VLC (though I think Netflix uses h.265 somehow).  So happy to finally have hardware support, gone are the days when you can just do the codec in pure software, 4K is just too many pixels and x.265 just too compute intensive for a software only solution.
