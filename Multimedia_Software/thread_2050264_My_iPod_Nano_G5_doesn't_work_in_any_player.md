### Post by clockworkangel on 2012-08-30
Hi everyone,

I am running a very recent (two weeks ago) install of Ubuntu 12.04.  All packages are up to date.

I tried plugging in my iPod Nano G5 with Banshee running, and Banshee froze.  I had to force quit to kill it.  The same thing happened when I plugged in my iPod and then launched Banshee after.

So I switched to Rhythmbox.  All seemed well at first.  It saw my iPod, as well as its contents.  But when I tried to sync anything, it claimed to be synching, but nothing actually synced.  In fact, on one of my sync attempts, it messed up the iPod so badly that it wouldn't play any of its playlists or individual songs brought up from the Music menu.

I rebooted into Windows, and had iTunes reset the iPod.  With a blank slate, I rebooted into Ubuntu, and tried again with Rhythmbox.  This time, it said my iPod was uninitialized, and asked if I wanted to initialize it.  It had no models to select from, but I tried typing in a name for the device and hit OK.  It froze.  Force quit.  This happened repeatedly.

I switched to Amarok.  It, too, saw my iPod as uninitialized.  At least it gave me a list of models to select.  Unfortunately, it gave me an error message box--it failed to initialize.

Finally, I tried Clementine.  It saw what iPod model I had right away.  It showed it as having no contents. But it refused to sync.

I finally gave up and rebooted into Windows to sync with my iTunes library.

I read all these success stories on how the iPod Nano G5 is working.  How is it that any of you Nano G5 owners are getting it to work in Linux?  Please!  I must know the secret!

Thanks in advance!

### Post by spaceshipguy on 2012-09-29
> **melinda said:**
> Apparently when the ipod updated to the iOS 5 -- ALL of them wont sync in Ubuntu. 

Sad but true. Apple changed the codes on their database to make the devices harder to talk to. The codes haven't been cracked yet.

The solutions include running iTunes on a virtual windows machine, or downloading OPlayer Lite (free from app store) and dragging music to the documents folder it provides in Nautilus.
