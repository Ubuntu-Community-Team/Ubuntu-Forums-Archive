### Post by patchin_house on 2009-11-27
A couple of years ago, because I didn't want another Apple iPod I opted to buy a Hacha PC20 (because of its FLAC support). I later realized that I wouldn't be able to "synch" this player to any computer the same way my old iPod Mini could to my Mac. But my Kubuntu 8.04 system (an old Sony Vaio laptop) can "open" the Hacha like any other flash device, and so transferring files is a piece of cake.

Because I still also use a Mac, I *do* have to clean out the "hidden" files after the transfer is done, because they are never hidden on my Hacha. 

You might want to poke around a few other sites for MP4 players that will play nice with penguins; House of DAP <http://www.houseofdap.com/> and MP4 Nation <http://www.mp4nation.net/> immediately come to mind. The latter also sells SmartQ's Q7 WiFi touchscreen units, two of which use a customized Ubuntu as their OS. Yummy. A penguin in your pocket. (All it needed was OGG support.)

You can also check Chinavasion <http://www.chinavasion.com/> if you're not adverse to off-brand electronics.

Just don't give up hope.
