### Post by ufugu on 2009-05-05
The is NOT a "what is your favorite player" post or a complaint.  It's general question/concern about the state of audio players/music collection managers.

A couple of years ago there was a flood of players coming out. There were new players with fresh ideas like Listen, Exaile, BMPx, Banshee, Songbird and more along with the biggies like Amarok and Rhythmbox.  It was really  exciting!

Now things seem really quiet. 

Rhythmbox [may be]("http://d0od.blogspot.com/2009/03/apps-rhythmbox development-to-cease.html") at a dead end.  Amarok is rebuilding but lost a lot of features. Songbird seemed to peak around 0.8 and has grown more buggy instead of less (at least for some of us).  Banshee is still growing but many have issues with Mono (regardless of merit of that argument) and it can't even browse by genre yet.

The Listen, Exaile and Audacious sites hint at new versions but are short on information or recent updates.

Lesser known projects like Quod Libet and gmusicbrowser seem to be left to sit.  Even taggers like the great EasyTag and Audio Tag Tool seem to be dormant projects last updated a couple years ago.

It's not like any of these reached a state of perfection and never needed an update.  All were on there way to it but never *quite* got there...

I know open source development is a challenge and many of these are built small teams or individuals doing work for free in their spare time, and I understand that projects come and go.  Still, it seems these are dark days for audio players on Linux.

What I want to know is:

1) Does anybody have information on **new** projects that are moving forward?

2) Any exciting news on the future of any of the old projects?

I'm looking forward to, well, looking forward! :guitar:

### Post by bobince on 2009-05-07
Maybe we don't need Yet Another specialist Audio Player.

Personally I despise the iTunes model of having everything built into a monolithic application. I don't want to fill my screen with multiple panels of track databases and taggers and album art and shops and rippers and burners.

Just give me a small, lightweight playhead and an optional playlist and I'm happy. I can organise my music quite well enough in the filesystem using the normal file manager tools without having to learn a quirky new interface to do the same thing. I can run a ripper or burner or download site in a separate program that does that one thing well instead of trying to do it all, and doing it badly.

Totem is already nearly there; its main fault is in forcing you to keep the space-wasting video viewer pane open all the time (I'm not interested in visualisations/album art/the Totem logo). VLC is not bad, but some interface features (particularly the playlist) are clunky. XMMS is at least small, but its skinned interface is ugly as hell (native UI widgets please).

Yeah, I know, I'm the only one. Oh well.
