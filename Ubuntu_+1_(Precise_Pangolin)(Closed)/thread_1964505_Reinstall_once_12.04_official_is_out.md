### Post by arpanaut on 2012-04-24
If you are not having any noticeable  problems then, I would say go with the flow. continue to Update-Upgrade.

If you feel the need for that "real clean" feeling and your modifications are not difficult to reproduce then, go for the pristine install.

I have had installations hat have lasted 2-3 upgrades w/out problems, and I have fresh installs that have given me problems to no end...

Generally I have found out that Linux systems are repairable if you have the time and are wiling to give the effort to do thae repairs.  It's a judgment call whether it is worth it or not.

A basic teueism...  If it ain"t broke, don't fix it.
