### Post by AnrDaemon on 2012-02-22
It really depends on your usage pattern and your intentions.
If you want to help with testing, I would suggest doing both clear reainstall of every released image and upgrade from any available installation to the today's repository. Logging and reporting any inconveniences you may come across.
If you just want to make sure you are not going to hit a brick wall, do as you feel more comfortable to deal with OS upgrades. Some prefer reinstall, some tend to upgrade... It's your call.

### Post by bobcollard on 2012-02-24
In general terms there are not set rules for determining when you should upgrade.  I have three main partitions on my HD, One is my daily use System, and I never touch it other than to update daily.  Then there is one I use for a change of pace, a different desktop manager, kernel and in general everything is experimental, changing applications as often as I change my socks.  Then there is the final one with this working Alpha which I will continue to update daily until something fails.  Then I will download a fresh Daily build and start over again.  When it is finished, if I decide this is better than what I have in everyday work, I will replace that and move on.  Currently I am using Debian Stable SalineOS as everyday, SolusOS (Also Debian Stable) with more upgrades and Xubuntu 12.04 for this alpha.  Since you can have four systems on one HD, you have a variety of choices.  Hope this clarifies it for you.
