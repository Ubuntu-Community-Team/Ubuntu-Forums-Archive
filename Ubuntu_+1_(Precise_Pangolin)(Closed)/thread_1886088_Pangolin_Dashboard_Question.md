### Post by rg4w on 2011-12-05
> **cariboo907 said:**
> I'd be very surprised if moving the Launcher to the bottom becomes official, it doesn't meet any of the design goals.
Sometimes design goals conflict, and when they do it becomes a good argument for an optional setting.

Apple puts their Dock on the bottom on the screen, and Unity on the side.  For all the reasons discussed before, on this decision Canonical is right and Apple is wrong.  It's not a good use of space on the majority of modern screens, since they're mostly 16:9 ratio and we spend a lot of time surfing predominantly-vertical web pages and reading email and other vertically-oriented documents.

But - 

Not everyone has a widescreen display, and not everyone wants the Launcher only on the left side.

The one thing Apple did better than Canonical on this was to provide an option to let the user decide what's best for themselves.

Having Unity's Launcher on the left side is a good default choice.  But making it cumbersome or impossible for the user to make other choices would be a mistake.

I've read the arguments against having "too many settings", but what really constitutes "too many"?

Right now the number of settings available to the user is the smallest of nearly any OS I've worked with for more than 20 years.

I see no harm in cautiously adding just a few more; to blindly require some arbitrary notion of "the fewest settings possible" risks being recognized as reductio ad absurdum.

Do we need the complexity of Compiz Config?  Not for new users, and rightly it's available in the Software Center for those who want that level of control.

But giving the user a choice in the placement of something as central and important as the Launcher is hard to argue against convincingly.
