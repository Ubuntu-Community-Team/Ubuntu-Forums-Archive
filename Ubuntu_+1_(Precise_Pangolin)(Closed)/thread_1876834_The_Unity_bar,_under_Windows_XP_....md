### Post by ErkDemon on 2011-11-06
... kinda. 

WinXP has a choice of "XP" or "Classic" taskbar modes (because MS didn't want to alienate existing users by forcing them to use the newlook system, because that would have been silly, ahem ... ). 

Anyway, mine happens to be set to "Classic". 

Right-click on the taskbar, and a menu pops up. Uncheck the "Lock the Taskbar" checkbox, and you can drag the taskbar to any of the four screen edges. Let's drag it to the left, Unity-style. 

Now, as well as the Windows Button at the top, and the notification panel at the bottom, the rest of the strip is in two sections, one for your favourite launcher icons, and one for all the programs that you have currently running. There's a divider between them which is slidable, so you can choose how much space is devoted to each. If there are too many launcher icons to fit, there's a little double-chevron symbol that opens up a flyout panel with the extra program-launcher icons. 

It still doesn't //quite// look like Unity, so let's right-click on the teeny handle under the Windows button, and there's a list of options including "View / * Large Icons". Click that, and you now have a left-hand launcher strip with large icons that looks and functions awfully like Unity's ... only better.

Double-click the weeny handle and it cycles between various display modes.  XP lets you drag programs onto the taskbar, and right-click to set keyboard shortcuts for each launcher (which I think Unity probably also does), but unlike Unity, XP also lets you drag the edge of the panel to widen or narrow it, and the launcher icons auto-tile into the available space, so if you want two columns of launch icons instead of one ... or three, or five columns... it's easy. 

So ... the XP launcher seems to do everything that Unity does, but it has more user-friendly configuration features that Ubuntu probably still won't have on its Unity launcher by 12.04 .  The XP launcher even lets you add comments and browse different icons from within each program's exe file, or from other locations on your harddrive if you don't happen to like the default graphics. You can even make up your own.

And where Unity makes a great play about how it optimises screen space on 16:9 screens by moving the launcher stuff into a fat column on the left, doing this under XP actually frees up even //more// vertical screen space than with Ubuntu, because XP's notification panel is part of the resulting fat column, which means that there's no longer any strip along the top or bottom of the screen. Ubuntu can't do this trick, because the new, "space-saving" global menu needs a persistent horizontal strip along the top of the screen, even when no menu is present, and that's also where the Ubuntu "notifications" stuff lives.    

As a result, if you're using a small widescreen netbook, and youre really short of vertical space, and you use a space-optimised browser like Chrome, then once you've dragged the XP launchbar to the left, XP is arguably more screen-optimised than Ubuntu. The "space-saving" global menu uses more space in this situation than than XP's "no menu at all". 

To summarise: The Unity bar isn't a radical new forward-looking interface, its equivalent was already built into WinXP. It just wasn't very popular, even though the XP version was more sophisticated and user-friendly than the current Ubuntu one. It was there all along, people just weren't bothered enough to actually use it.
