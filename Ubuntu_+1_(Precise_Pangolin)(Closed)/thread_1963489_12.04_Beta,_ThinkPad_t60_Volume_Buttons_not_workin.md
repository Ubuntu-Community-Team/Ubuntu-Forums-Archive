Hi Everyone!

The subject line pretty much sums it up.

I'm doing some (late) testing of the 12.04 beta. My 'thinkvantage' button seems to work, that is, it creates an 'event' so I can map it to useful functions. My mute and volume keys do not work.

In 11.10, these keys worked out of the box, but I had to apply a simple workaround to enable the on screen display. This time if I try to configure them in the keyboard shortcuts settings, they do not seem to create any event whatsoever.

I've been googling the problem but no dice yet. As other t60 users begin to adopt 12.04, perhaps they can post their solution here as I haven't found one yet and I'm just not sure how to troubleshoot this issue. 

I'm aware of the Thinkwiki, but honestly now don't have the hours of time it takes to troubleshoot these issues anymore.

[edit]Looks like the solution on Thinkwiki will probably work:

[http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_get_special_keys_to_work](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_get_special_keys_to_work) and [http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Installing_Fedora_13_on_a_ThinkPad_T60](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Installing_Fedora_13_on_a_ThinkPad_T60)

I'll update in a couple of days once I get 12.04 installed again.

**Last edit, I found the solution. It's actually the same solution as what worked for 11.10:**

Add the following line to /etc/rc.local

```

cp /sys/devices/platform/thinkpad_acpi/hotkey_all_mask  /sys/devices/platform/thinkpad_acpi/hotkey_mask
```

I also had to go to sound settings, and on the 'output' tab, select 'Analog output,' or I had no sound at all. Seems to be working now, with on screen display.
