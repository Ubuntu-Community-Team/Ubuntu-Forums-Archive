### Post by kaldor on 2011-11-21
Progress/Change is a surprisingly frightening thing :confused:

I'll clear this up for future readers...

Ubuntu 12.04 still uses GNOME. Ubuntu is, and always will be, a GNOME distro. Unity is a shell atop GNOME 3 as is the official **GNOME Shell**. Unity *is* GNOME. 

When people refer to "GNOME" they are usually referring to GNOME 2.x which Ubuntu used from 4.10 to 11.04 (called Classic in 11.04). The GNOME Project, not Canonical, are behind the changes in UI. GNOME 2.x is dead, and GNOME 3 will just keep going like it is. If people give it a chance and stop trying to use it like their old UI I think they'll be pleasantly surprised.

GNOME is more than just a two-panel desktop environment.
