### Post by balloons on 2012-02-10
I have been meaning to write up some more detail on opportunities to get involved with testing ubuntu. Everyone running precise and hanging out in this forum is a rock star for helping shape good experiences for everyone running stable. Thank you! Your helping fix the issues before everyone gets to have to deal with them :-)

With that in mind, I wrote [this post]("http://www.theorangenotebook.com/2012/02/community-qa-opportunities-in-ubuntu.html") on my blog, which I am linking you out to now to avoid making this post be pages long :-) In it I discuss some ways to get more involved and some of the work other teams inside of the ubuntu community are doing and how to join them if interested. I plan to highlight opportunities for doing QA work as they come up, starting with [this post on updating our application testcases]("http://www.theorangenotebook.com/2012/02/opportunity-writing-application-test.html").

Right now we are attempting to update our manual test cases for application testing. You may have seen or remember the testcases wiki. That is what we're trying to update in anticipation of moving to a new tool, as well as getting some awesome testing in during the beta release time period for precise. You can help do this! Update the testcases for your favorite app and help get the wiki updated. Then when the time comes, you can help run through your testcases as well.

While I will be posting new posts in an on-going fashion on my blog, I will also try and post calls for testing and opportunities here (without spamming everyone too much :-) ). So keep your eyes on the lookout and jump in. We've got lots of interesting things going on this cycle, and I'll help make you aware of them as they happen.

As always, feel free to reach out to me with any questions, comments or concerns you may have. I am happy to shine a light on the work everyone is doing here, and help integrate that work into the greater QA ubuntu community. Happy testing and keep rocking.

### Post by effenberg0x0 on 2012-02-22
I think it was suggested some time ago (can't remember if OO or earlier) to have Ubuntu automatically ask some questions to the user (is sound working, is your VGA OK, etc) and get && posts specs of users hardware to a database. Not only it would create some good statistics and guidance to development, but Ubuntu could check, during install, what would be the appropriate ("reported to be working at a large percentage of users") drivers for that hardware and take actions based on that. Bac then we had discussed that, technically, it would mean prompting the user a little (the questions), getting PCI IDs and then insert it into a database, so it is not hard. 

So I'm *guessing* all of that evolved to the Checkbox utility, but I'm not sure how they keep this database, how they plan to use it, who has access to it, etc.

For online helpers, as well as hired support personnel, it would be  amazing to have such a feature available: No more asking users for stuff they  don't know how to answer. For testers it would be amazing. We could focus on whats reportedly broken. Push developers of those particular things, etc. 

But I'm unaware of any policy regarding access to this database now.

My suggestion: A bash script, get & parse hardware specs, pipe to editmoin binary, auto post to wiki.ubuntu.com.

Regards,
Effenberg
