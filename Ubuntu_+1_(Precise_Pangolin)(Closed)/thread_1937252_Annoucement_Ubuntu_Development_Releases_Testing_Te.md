Actually let me just reinforce something in the U+1 Team Membership / Ubuntu Membership realm, which I predict might be confusing for some.  

Sadly right now U+1 can't really offer any form of distinction to U+1 Team members. Member Ranks are exclusive to Ubuntu Members. No team can directly provide Ubuntu Memberships, only the Community Council does. The CC has delegated membership approval to some Team Councils, when these teams have proven to be organized (this is the case for the Forum Council, i.e. Ubuntu Membership via UbuntuForums Contribution). But, basically, our team is too new: We do not have a council, we haven't accomplish anything **yet** and so we can't prove serious management to the CC and request to have our own governance body. 

The other form of distinction in this forum are different colored handles, but these are given to admins and moderators as described [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4561527&postcount=11"). It's for the staff. No deal there too.

So, at best, our team members will receive a big "thank you" from the community if they work well, and that's it. They might receive a signed picture of Clippy as well - this is not really defined right now. 

However, they can[COLOR=Red]_[ apply for Ubuntu membership]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=404")_[/COLOR] directly, of course, if they feel like they have provided _[COLOR=Red][sustained and significant contributions]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Membership")[/COLOR]_ (and many of Ubuntu+1 regulars already are Ubuntu Members anyway).

Code:

sudo aptitude update
sudo aptitude full-upgrade

### Post by effenberg0x0 on 2012-03-29
> **ventrical said:**
> What I would like is some help from some others to go through the old databases and gather  some stuff in a format like this:
[]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1858991")

It's not too complicated. There are many ways to do it: convert everything to XML, strip html tags with common bash commands, etc. I'd go with Bash cause I'm more comfortable with it, but most people would probably do it with Python and an adequate lib to handle html.

Using shell scripting one would have wget to the OO thread list at [http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?s=&daysprune=&f=403](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?s=&daysprune=&f=403) and extract (with grep, sed, sed, etc) the ID of each thread in OO. Then wget each of these threads to a tmp file, and use a tool like html2text or simply remove all html tags, and append the results to a text format file.

Silly example (with that thread you mentioned):
```
wget -q http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1858991 -O - | html2text -nobs -ascii -o -
```

The problem is: There are 1945 threads in OO archive. 
- I'm not sure but UF admins will probably not like the idea of you running a spider like this. Maybe one should ask for permission or, at least, code it to sleep something like 30 seconds or more between accesses. But with 1945 threads times 30 seconds you have 972.5 minutes or 16 hours!!
- You'll end up with a HUGE txt file, and reading through it all will be a very tough and time consuming job.

Ideally, you could install MySQL and pipe the results of the previous command to proper columns in a MySQL table. Then you could search with keywords / match. It would be very fast.

But then, despite the speed gain of working with a local database, it wouldn't be different than using the already existing search structure of UF :) UF search would be actually more comfortable (visual/GUI) than typing queries.

So, it's totally doable, I'm just not really sure how to create something usable/viable with it.

Effenberg

Just an addition to what Cariboo907 explained above: U+1 was created with the goal of empowering an already successful and accomplished group of testers that had been hanging around here since forever and decided to join the team. It was also designed to accommodate new users that want to learn. What the team proposes to all its members is defined in its Wiki. U+1 is now recruiting members on IRC, because it's not tied to Ubuntu+1 exclusively. Unlike so many other teams we know, which failed due to power games and lack of interest by managers and members, U+1 was created over a clearly defined, solid and transparent set of values, processes, governance and goals that enforce longevity and democratic management. One of the interesting aspects of such structure is that *any* member can apply for a leadership position. Also, all members are encouraged to contribute with ideas and we will commit to turning good ideas into real projects. All that is explained in details at [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/U+1](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/U+1).

Instead of taking the traditional route of simply subscribing to an existing team and absorbing a bunch of rules and processes and having its members somehow digest that, U+1 is keeping its independence and working to create solid partnerships with other Ubuntu structures, teams, groups, in a way that it benefits its members and Ubuntu. That is the way the team was created by me, that's what was presented to Ubuntu governance boards, it's the way I'll fight to keep it.

However, it is important to notice that UbuntuForums, The Ubuntu+1 sub-forum and The U+1 Team are different things. UbuntuForums is a forum. Ubuntu+1 is not a team, it's a sub-forum. And not all of this sub-forum users are U+1 members. The U+1 Team has no role in the management of Ubuntu+1, which is a resource of UbuntuForums, managed by the Forum Council and its rules. And UbuntuForums also have no role in the management of the U+1 Team. No one should feel forced to join the U+1 Team in order to happily use the Ubuntu+1 sub-forum. And U+1 Team members do not have to exclusively hang and contribute at The Ubuntu+1 sub-forum: They can do whatever they want, go to IRC, any community if any. All of these entities (UF, Ubuntu+1, U+1Team) share some strong relation, obviously, which is evolving into a powerful and healthy partnership. 

So, if some of the regulars here have decided not to join, I completely respect that and this is absolutely fine, of course. However, I once again ask you all to read our docs in the referred Wiki, with an open mind, and consider joining. Not only we need more ideas, more opinions, but we could use the extra help. We're working *really hard* to create new and interesting activities, as Cariboo907 mentioned.
