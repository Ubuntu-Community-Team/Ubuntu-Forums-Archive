### Post by ranch hand on 2012-04-25
Well folks, it has been real and it has been fun.  It hasn't been real fun.

I will be missing many of the folks here that I have come to know and admire.  Unfortunately there are few of you left.  Been missing the ones that left before too.

You have taught me an awful lot in the last few years and I really appreciate that.

I really hope this new setup works out for all of you, including the new testers.

For the new testers, remember these things.
1>we were all new at sometime
2>learn the value of the CLI and synaptic
3>learn to chroot
4>avoid Update Mangler until the last couple of weeks of a testing cycle

For you energetic folks setting up this new arrangement for testing.

IRC is chaotic and hard to follow by folks that are short on time.  You have a forum, use it for communication.  It is good for more than "sound bites" and has delineated threads.

Groups and "perks" associated with them are not what a good tester is after.  What they are after is respect and support and freedom of thought.

I hope that everything goes smoothly and that you turn out excellent releases of Ubuntu.

As for me I have other things to do and am going to be deleting my Xubuntu 12.04 partitions on Friday.  This will make my box, once more, Ubuntu free.
