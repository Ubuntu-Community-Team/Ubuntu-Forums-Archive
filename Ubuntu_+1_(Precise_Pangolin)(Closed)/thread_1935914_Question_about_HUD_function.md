### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-03-05
Do not think Desktop PC. Think tablet PC with touch screen and hand gesture recognition as well as on-screen keyboard but no mouse.

Now, we begin to see where we are going with things like the Launcher, Dash, global menu and Head Up Display. Yes? Well I can.

Drop down menus work fine with a mouse and even somewhat with keyboard shorts cuts. But imagine the usual PC desktop on a tablet PC without a mouse.

To use technical language the HUD is not intrusive. It is there but if you do not use it then it does not interfere  with you or your way of working.

I do not see the HUD as being a useful replacement for the mouse. But on a tablet PC without a mouse where it has learnt what menus I frequently use in each application and have them ready instead of scrolling down a menu list, then I can see its value.

Think of an Android+Ubuntu phone where you carry the docking attachment and one of those rolled up flexible keyboards but no mouse. Then the HUD will be useful.

Regards.
