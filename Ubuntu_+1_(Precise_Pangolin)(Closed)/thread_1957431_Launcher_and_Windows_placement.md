### Post by Mathor on 2012-04-12
That is why they removed hiding the dash by default, because a lot of people (like myself) ran into that problem. Imho Unity became much more usable for me when I let that place remain reserved, and that's how it's designed. Sure, it is a waste of space, but if you want that space to remain reserved anyway, what is the point of that space at all, for one little tiny strip of your wallpaper? Try going to default non autohide and I think you'd be surprised how much better it is.

Mark Shuttleworth posted about the change a couple months ago moving the window dodge off of default for that exact reason.
[https://lists.launchpad.net/unity-design/msg07852.html](https://lists.launchpad.net/unity-design/msg07852.html)
