I can only see this be accomplished by including (older) and (more obsolete) PCs to remain in the fold.  Some developers may take a contrary view because  the concept base behind their apps does not include more legacy desktops but rather Newer  e-Books, Androids and tablet PCs. If I can run GNOME classic with the Precise kernel on a 550MHZ Celeron with 512MB of SDRAM as reasonably as I can run XP then it is a given that , for the most part that obsoleted PC, is part of the Ubuntu fold.

  From a technical point of view one may balk at this, because, of course, they have bigger fish to fry but, a working OS is a working OS not matter how old the machine is. It is a high expectation to put on devs to have all these bases covered. Maybe even a mission impossible. In the long run we can still learn from older systems. Bugs seem to behave differently, have different properties so to speak and so therefor can lend to be a helping hand to the developers.

  So, in brief, I think it has to do with care. Percentage wise most users of tablet,PCs  don't care what OS is on their pack. Just as long as it works. But on a greater scale and from an economic viewpoint, people do care if they can bring old desktops and laptops back to life. We may never know the exact number of legacy PC being utilized by Precise  but by having a helpful and open concept during testing, then numbers may start comming in.
