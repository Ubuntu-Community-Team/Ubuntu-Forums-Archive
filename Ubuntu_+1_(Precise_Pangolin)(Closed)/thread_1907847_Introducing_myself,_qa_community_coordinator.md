### Post by balloons on 2012-01-12
Hello everyone. I just wanted to introduce myself for those in this specific subforum willing to live on the bleeding edge and test things to make the end user experience better. Thank you to all of you. I am the new QA community coordinator jono [blogged]("http://www.jonobacon.org/2011/12/12/nicholas-skaggs-joining-the-canonical-community-team/") about last month. I am on the ground now and excited to see the work you are doing here on the forums. I wanted to let everyone know they are free to reach out to me via email or on IRC with any questions/concerns they have or simply an introduction. I'd love to hear your ideas and help you accomplish them. Swing by on IRC and say hello.

While I'm here I would also like to take the opportunity to inform everyone of ways they can get more involved if they wish. Currently the QA Team wiki page is being overhauled, but you can already find out about the new work we are doing. Check out the [main page]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/QATeam"), or the list of [tasks for the precise cycle]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/QATeam/TasksPrecise"). Manual testing is a very important piece of what will help make this release rock solid. Anyone who wants to get more formally involved in this way I would encourage to post to the ubuntu-qa mailing list, hop onto the #ubuntu-testing IRC channel or shoot me an email. Here's to making precise the best ubuntu release yet.

My contact details can be found on my launchpad page:

[http://launchpad.net/~nskaggs](http://launchpad.net/~nskaggs)

### Post by balloons on 2012-01-16
kevpan815, I don't set the release schedules, the release team does -- you can read about there work or get more involved here:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ReleaseTeam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ReleaseTeam)

However, they do change things up for LTS's -- this is pulled from there wiki:

> LTS Schedule
To support our goal of ensuring stability, we plan to make a small number of changes to the release schedule:

Reduced Alpha Stage: Because we will have substantially less new code, we can reduce the number of Alpha releases, and extend the Beta stage to allow for more system testing.

Two Beta Releases: We generally get more bugs filed in the Beta stage because of the increase in user base. In order to address more of these issues, we will provide an additional Beta release.
