### Post by Temposs on 2009-05-20
Directing this toward System76:

I will be buying a laptop within 6 months, and I have not seen a system that fits exactly what I have in mind. Essentially, what I'd like is a system like the Darter Ultra, on the smaller side, light weight, but importantly, having some pretty powerful hardware options available. 

But, what I don't want is the optical drive. The optical drive takes up a good amount of hardware real estate and weighs a significant amount.

Optical drives, I think, are falling by the wayside in terms of usefulness for portable systems:

1) With more ubiquitous high speed internet access and sites allowing high quality streaming/downloadable video and audio, there isn't much need for facilitating the playing of CDs and DVDs on the go.

2) As well, vast hard drives allowing storage of everything you need on your machine. Where data needs to be made portable, it isn't CD/DVD media used, but USB flash drives, which are becoming the de facto standard for portable data storage.

Everyone's got these small underpowered netbook machines without optical drives now, like your Starling, and Apple's got its expensive MacBook Air(but comes with OSX). The Dell Mini 12 is about the design I'd like, but it's still in the netbook class, and doesn't have high performance hardware.

I think it's time to see optical drives phased out of laptops. I hope that System76 can be a leader to this end too! :-)
