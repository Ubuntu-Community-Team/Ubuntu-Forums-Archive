### Post by trinitydan on 2011-01-22
Four-five years is a decent life expectancy for a laptop IMO, but then I use my computer extensively, like for several hours a day.  The main killer of laptops in general I have seen is heat.  Keep your laptop elevated.  The tiny area that is left open under laptops is not enough to provide adequate cooling or ventilation.  The sleek streamlined cases manufacturers and consumers seem to prefer are not easy to keep cool. Install conky so you can easily monitor CPU temps.  You might even want to invest in one of those cooling mats for laptops.  When your laptop is a couple years old, you should take the fan out and clean it.  I waited until my laptop was severely overheating to do thus and now it seems that my chips are permanently damaged.  When I did finally take out my fan it was fully pugged with a thick wad of dust and lint right by the heat sink fins.  :(  Taking apart laptops is pretty intensive (or rather getting them back together can be) so if you are not experienced in computer repairs you might want to have a professional help with this.  

I don't know any suppliers that will offer a four year warranty on computer parts.

### Post by themrfreeze on 2011-01-24
> **trinitydan said:**
> Four-five years is a decent life expectancy for a laptop IMO, but then I use my computer extensively, like for several hours a day.  The main killer of laptops in general I have seen is heat.  Keep your laptop elevated.  

I support a large number of laptops from various manufacturers, and Trinitdan's comment is right on the money..keeping a laptop cool helps it to last longer.  Keep the laptop elevated while on a hard surface...you can use a laptop stand, or even just something stuck under the back edge to prop it up.  Also, NEVER place on a soft surface (like a blanket), which can block cooling vents on the bottom of the machine.

Also, laptops suck in dust, the dust builds up in the cooling fans and exhaust vents, and the machine starts to overheat.  Some laptops are far worse than others in regards to this, but they all need to be cleaned out now and again.  

If you're not adventurous enough to open your laptop and blow the dust out of the fan(s) with compressed air, run a vacuum over the intake and exhaust vents every once in a while.

### Post by Ranko Kohime on 2011-05-07
On the topic of batteries: while batteries will naturally lose performance over time and with use, the number one killer is over-charging.  You'd think with charging controllers this would not be an issue, but not so.  :P

This isn't System 76 specific, I've experienced the same on EVERYTHING with a Li-on battery in it: cell phones, iPod's, and other laptop computers.

I've got a 12 year old IBM Thinkpad with a 6 year old battery which still holds a charge greater than it's design capacity: this laptop has spent most of the last 6 years in a toolbag waiting for those rare occasions it gets used with Autotap.  Of course, after a month in the bag the battery completely forgets that it was charged up, but after a charge it will run wireless for over 4 hours.

If you use a laptop for extended periods while plugged in, remove the battery after it has reached full charge.
