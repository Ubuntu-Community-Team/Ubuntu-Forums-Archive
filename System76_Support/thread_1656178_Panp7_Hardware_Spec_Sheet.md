### Post by isantop on 2010-12-30
It looks like those drives are 12.5 mm thick, and they will not fit into the Pangolin Drive Bay. 

As long as you select the hard drive upgrade or SSD at purchase time, your experience should be the same as anyone else -- just with a more storage space. SSD's provide extreme performance benefits in addition to high shock resistance, so if those are features that are important to you, I'd recommend one.

We don't have a super detailed spec sheet available; the best we have is what's listed on our website. I have an older PanP7, and I could send you an lsmod/lspci/dmidecode if you'd like.

### Post by ArielSonique on 2011-05-30
The Pangolin Panp7 from System76 is the same as CLEVO Model: W765CUH. From the Clevo website the Model series is: W76xCUH series.

You can download a DETAILED manual and specifications (spec sheet) sheet in five languages (155 pages long) for the Pangolin panp7 or the Clevo W765CUH from the CLEVO page here: [http://www.clevo.com.tw/en/e-services/download.asp](http://www.clevo.com.tw/en/e-services/download.asp)

That page also has the windows drivers for the notebook. Alas, no linux drivers. Most of the drivers are inbuilt in ubuntu, but not in other flavors such as Crunchbang and other debian-based distros. For example, I am running CrunchBang Statler on my PanP7 and still don't have wireless connectivity because there is no pre-compiled driver for the wireless card. Compiling it from scratch requires some expertise and extra files that I don't have thus far.

Hope this helps future googlers.
