### Post by dodo3773 on 2012-08-14
> **Carborundum said:**
> Could you elaborate a little bit on what what feature is/does, for the benefit of the non-initiated (e.g. me)?

Sure thing. It basically works like this: When you leave your laptop plugged in you can set a threshold for the battery charge. So you can tell it to charge until it hits 85% and discharge until it hits 30% etc.. etc... So, that way if you leave your machine plugged in a lot it won't stay at 100% and wear your battery down over time. I have pretty much just excepted that it is the way it is for GNU/Linux until I learned about tp_smapi (thinkpad laptop kernel modules) that allows you to control this. Here is a quote from thinkpad people: "Batteries, especially of the modern Li-Ion type, wear out quicker when they hold a large charge or are subject to higher temperatures". It is true too. I have had a few batteries go bad from leaving them at 100% all the time.
