### Post by paullangdon678-1 on 2014-05-21
Hi,
Just a post to relate my experiences with Bonobo Extreme (Bonx 6). Overall I am pretty happy with this laptop - performance is very good and it has generally met my expectations.
But I am not sure I would buy another despite System 76's advantages.
You should note that I am a UK customer which contributes to my issues.
The dark side:
The Bonobo Extreme has had a screen/graphics problem. This has not affected my use of the laptop hugely but is disappointing. Currently I have a pixel problem which I can resolve by gently pressing a rubber against the affected area of the laptop screen.
The keypad is almost useless - this has been well documented. It is inconsistent and very hard to use - I turn it off and use a mouse.
System 76 support is above average but I have had a few issues - sometimes possibly to do with small language/interpretaion between US and UK.
I have just been receiving support for fitting an additional drive to the Bonobo. I looked up the model on the system 76 site and used a link within a document for the Bonx 6 as I needed to refresh my memory on what my options were. The link took me to information on the current Bonobo model but I did not notice that. I then could not locate the mSata hard drives referred to and sought System 76 support. Support suggested that the mSata drives were under the keyboard and provided a link to a document showing me how to remove keyboard. Unfortunately one of the five screws retaining the keyboard was impossible to remove as the screw had no purchase (overtightened/poor quality). After helpful advice I then drilled the screw out.
Some of you may be ahead of me now -there are no mSAta drives on the bonx6 - I only have just the two hard drives. Support thought I had a bonx 7.
I concede this was partly my error but I am mildly frustrated that support did not recognise my models configuration correctly. I did state that the model was a bonx 6 and obvioulsy the system 76 configuration information states that.
In summary I probably would not buy again:
Quality issues with laptop esp the disapointment of the superb glossy screen being marred by pixel/graphics issue.
Mouse can only be described as terrible.
US based vendor making warranty work too difficult and financially - I thought the Bonobo would be a better quality build than it seems to be.
Support issues (support better than industry average though in my experience). But slightly disappointed, particularly with the drive issue.
Lack of (comprenhensive) manual/instructions/documentation for laptop. 

But laptop performs well and gaming is good. I just do not know how much to value the System 76 Linux support. I would probably buy from UK vendor Novatech who while not 'Linux' will supply without windows O/S installed. and have verg good levels of service.

Just my view form England.
