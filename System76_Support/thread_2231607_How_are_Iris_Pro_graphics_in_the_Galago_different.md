### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-06-26
All three are just chipsets, whether or not they're onboard bears little relevance for the matter in discussion. Calling "video card" to an onboard/integrated Intel chip isn't the end of civilization as you know it. Unlike what you think it isn't confusing. Confusing for many users would be having to describe all the different little bits about one or the other. Instead, and as system requirements go you're informed you must have one of the three of those mentioned or higher.

It is. So what? Provided it's powerful enough who cares if it is integrated or on a add-on card? Just compare your current generation integrated Intel graphics (Iris Pro) with any nVidia or ATI based card from 5 years ago... Defense rests :lolflag:

Obs.: There are a few differences, the only relevant one being the additional dedicated RAM you get from an add-on card while the integrated chips have yet no other way then reserving part of the system's RAM for video only. This might be significant for older systems but it really doesn't matter in newer ones where you have plenty of memory to spare.
