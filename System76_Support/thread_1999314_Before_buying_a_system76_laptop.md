### Post by brdmn on 2012-06-08
> **monfu said:**
> 
How is the ventilation?  Does the trackpad get too hot, or the computer overheat?


You'll probably get a better response if you post the configuration you are considering.  But I can tell you that I have the lemu4 with the i5 processor option, 8GB RAM and the 64GB SSD.  The trackpad does get a little warm under lower loads--yes, a little paradoxical, but probably it's because the fan runs slower at low load (which of course is nice and quiet!)  At the same temps, my Thinkpad T410 is much more comfortable, but of course, it is a more expensive machine.

Unfortunately, there is no software solution for manipulating the fan (even if Clevo had any, I bet it's for MS-Windows only), so my solution was to buy one of those Belkin "lapdesks" with a small USB powered fan.  That drops the temps by about 7 to 8 degrees C, which makes the trackpad cooler to the touch.

I'll add a last point: comfort is a subjective issue, and I may be more sensitive to warm trackpads than others.  However, in terms of overall ventilation/safety, I am not too concerned.  I expect the laptop will be just fine and the build quality feels pretty solid.

### Post by brdmn on 2012-06-08
> **monfu said:**
> Lemur ultra is what I am most likely to buy, it is the size of my current computer.

Then my comments are likely to be applicable, since I have a Lemur Ultra (lemu4).  The build is solid, especially compared to many Dell brand laptops I've had.  There's not much flex in the base or the screen.  No small parts that could break off--the lid just flips open, it has two hinges that feel robust.  I think this machine can take reasonable day-to-day wear.

On the graphics card, HD Graphics 3000 (if you have the i5 processor) supports DirectX 10.1, while HD Graphics 4000 (if you have the i7 processor) supports DirectX 11.  This info is from the Intel website:  [http://www.intel.com/support/graphics/intel915g/sb/CS-011807.htm](http://www.intel.com/support/graphics/intel915g/sb/CS-011807.htm)

Also, in either case, there should be backwards compatibility with Pixel Shader 2.0.
[]("http://www.intel.com/support/graphics/intel915g/sb/CS-011807.htm")

### Post by re1d on 2012-06-09
I have a Pangolin P7 - so this may not directly apply. 

_Heat_: Mine never has gotten hot (right now the temp reads 49C) and ventilation seems adequate. The highest temp I've seen was 65C.

I will say: it has vents on the bottom, which can be blocked when using it on a bed or something. I'm aware of that, and avoid blocking them. Maybe that could cause a problem if it was done inadvertently. But operating temperature has never been a worry with my machine.

_Durability_: I've had mine for 2 1/2 years with no problems. I use it constantly, it's my primary work machine. Nothing has loosened up, come off, worn off, or broken. I do travel with a good bag and try to take care of it, since I need it to work. But I haven't babied it, either.

_Other problems_: None - mine came with 10.04 LTS and I've kept it, as it's worked fine and I've seen no need to change. I briefly considered going to 12.04, but then decided to make the change next year when support for my LTS runs out. Why fix what ain't broken? I need this thing to work, and it works great.

I did have a configuration problem back in the beginning, and Tom helped me fix it. It's great to have friendly, knowledgeable, motivated support - instead of what you get with Dell, HP & others. Been there, done that, and certainly don't want the T-shirt! System76 support is stellar, I don't think you'll find better.

Hope this helps! I think you'd be making a good move to get one.

Reid
