### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-12-10
Sounds like a battery or power issue.  Did you get a new machine or a refurb as a replacement?  I wonder if your battery pack is bad and you got a new machine with your original battery pack.  Sometimes batteries can't deliver the required current and cause a low-voltage condition (say 10 VDC instead of 12 VDC).  The power conditioner in the laptop senses this low voltage and the BIOS causes a safety shutdown.  Without this "feature", it's possible to pull a lot of current from the battery causing a fire.

Will your laptop boot and run without the battery--just plugged into AC?  Some will boot, others won't.  If it runs on AC only (with no battery installed), try running it for several hours and check for stability.

It could be a bad batch of batteries.
