### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-04-24
One chip leaves room for upgrading, however two chips will offer slightly better performance because you're accessing two busses instead of one, doubilng the potential throughput. If you think there's a high probability you'll upgrade later, get one stick. If you think the chance of wanting to upgrade is pretty slim, you'll get better performance (and price) from two sticks.
