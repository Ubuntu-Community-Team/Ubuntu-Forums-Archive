### Post by oakhilltop on 2012-08-02
I received my Lemur yesterday, so have not had a whole lot of time to use it. Mine has an i5 processor, Intel ssd and 8G of ram.

It was packaged very well and in good condition. There is one tiny dot next to the touchpad which seems to be intentional in the plastic. I will have to look closer at it. I was a little nervous when cutting some of the tape that held the protective wraps on, but it all went well.

I turned it on, it booted fast and soon asked if I wanted to connect to the wireless. This was the one hiccup I had with it. I think it defaulted to not connecting at this time, but I figured I may as well get it over with. Entering the passphrase was strange, and it ended up aborting and re-starting the configuration process. I can't repeat this, so the description of what happened is vague. The second time, I selected to connect to the wireless later, and it went smoothly.

The Lemur is fast, quiet and cool (in more ways than one). I like its physical appearance, weight and feel. Then again, I'm coming from a 7 year old, 10 pound HP clunker.

Physical things I don't like:
- The SD card sticks out!! Very bad for a laptop whose portability is a main feature. I want to have a SD card permanently inserted. I will have to be careful with it.

- The headphone and microphone jacks are not color coded, so it is hard to see which one to plug the headphones into. It will be second nature after awhile, but why not color code them green and pink like other laptops?

- I'm not thrilled with the placement of the speakers on the bottom. They get muted when using it on your lap. Not a big deal, since I will use headphones or external speakers most of the time.

I'm a long time linux user and have used fvwm, fvwm2, icewm, windowmaker, various versions of gnome and kde and am probably forgetting a few others. I didn't mind any of them and never got into the gnome vs. kde type of discussions. I had heard that Unity was awful, but approached it with an open mind .... for a very short time. OMG! What a good way to send newbies back to windows! IMHO, it is a very poor choice for System76 to have as their default. What is worse, I couldn't just install cinnamon from the software manager or apt-get. I had to go through the process of adding a repository, updating apt-get and installing. Mint provides a much nicer user experience. I now have cinnamon setup to my liking. I will have to do the same for the guest account in case someone else wants to use it. I wouldn't want to hear the "this is linux? how do you use it?" from a newbie.

I connected the Lemur to an external monitor using a hdmi to dvi cable. Wow, the display looks much nicer than the old vga connection from my old laptop. Same resolution.

I tried connecting the hdmi to a TV, without success and haven't had enough time to debug it. I did get the wallpaper on the TV, but the youtube video I was playing in a window was not on the TV. I went into the Display area of the settings and tried a higher resolution for the TV, but that didn't work either. I think it messed up the laptop's display. Again, I was kinda rushed and didn't really take enough time with this. Any tips on how to show a movie at 1080p on a TV would be appreciated.

That's about all I have done with it so far. Everything else seems to work well and overall it is a nice laptop. I still have the protective cover on the display, just in case I decide not to keep it. The SD card sticking out bugs me, and if I can't get the hdmi to TV working, I may return it. But I'm pretty sure the hdmi will work with a little more investigation. For all I know, the problem could be on the TV side of things.

### Post by isantop on 2012-08-17
> **brdmn said:**
> Glossy.  At least, it appears that way to me.  Don't expect to use it under bright light.  Good viewing angle under normal light though.

Actually, glossy screens typically do slightly better under bright light than matte because the finish lets the light bounce through the screen and reflect away, whereas matte finishes will diffuse the light over the entire screen and wash out the image.

Matte does have an advantage when you have two areas contrasting brightness, such as an office (with lights on the ceiling and a dark floor) because it will smoothly fade the glare, rather than reflecting it.
