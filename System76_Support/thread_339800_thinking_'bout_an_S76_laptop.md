### Post by emale07 on 2007-01-16
Are you trying to decide whether or not to buy a laptop from System 76?  You should, and here's why:

I purchased a Gazelle Performance a little over three months ago.  I am a pastor and do much research online, I write sermons, etc.  I also play online games, download video and audio.  I'd say I do a bit more "computing" than the average bear (at least the bears I know).  Suffice it to say - (I think) I run this little puppy pretty hard.

A bit after I got the machine I was getting lags.  I'm smart enough to know that nothing's perfect and that there's usually a pretty easy fix for most stuff so I emailed Carl for some help.

The man spent almost an hour on the phone with me trying to help me get things figured out!  He sent me some drivers and new configs and the solutions worked for a bit.

I got into my Christmas rush season and things started getting a little funky again.  I knew my problem was rare - I mean, they wouldn't have built the success they've had so far if they were selling crap.  When I was running Mac, the tech guys would often ("often" - isn't that sad?) tell me "Yeah, when this has happened before..."  Carl said "I've never seen anything like this before."

So, I wrote Carl and said "I've gotta have this thing working, and none of the previous fixes work this time."  No sweat.  No argument, no shaming the customer ("it's an ID-10t problem") - just "let us make this right for you."

Boom! - I've gotten the machine back now and have been running it hard again.  Smooth sailing.  no problems.  life is good (so good I'm trying to convince my wife to switch over).

Linux takes work, we all know that.  But between the top notch components and the over the top, above and beyond customer service, these guys are champs.  Great product, great price, great customer service.

Buy with confidence.  You will not regret it.

### Post by jml on 2007-03-06
Mateo, its just a matter of degree, and is somewhat arbitrary.  The Darter which is marketed as an ultraportable has a 13.3 inch wide screen display, weighs 4.3 pounds, 4.5 hour battery life and costs start at $999.00.

The Gazelle has a 14 inch wide screen display, weighs 5.5 pounds, three hour battery life,  and costs start at $699.00.

So you are paying a premium for the smaller size.  If you conpare equivalent set ups, the Darter will run you several hundred dollars more just for the smaller size and lighter weight.  Since I carry mine to work every day, and I take in on the road for meetings and vacations, saving that extra pound of weight, and getting an extra hour of run time on battery, is worth it to me.  However, if you don't intend to carry the laptop around a lot and use it primarily in one place with occaisional ventures away from your desk, the bigger laptop would make more sense.  Costs less, bigger screen.  I've pasted the System 76 web page advertising thier laptops here.
