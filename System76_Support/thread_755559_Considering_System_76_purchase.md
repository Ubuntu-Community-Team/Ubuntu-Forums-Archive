### Post by greenstar on 2008-04-15
My $0.02 worth:

I always prefer discrete graphics (Nvidia, ATI) to on-board or shared-memory graphics. It's my opinion that the entire 
computing experience is better with the video tasks offloaded to a gpu and vram rather than eating system cpu cycles and hogging system ram.

That being said, laptops bring economics into play much more so than do desktops, where a decent graphics controller can be added after purchase for a few dollars. If that's not much of a concern, get the one with better graphics.

### Post by SniperSlap on 2008-04-16
I would stay away from any ATI video offerings as they are typically underpowered compared to nvidia.  In turn, you may pay a bit more for nv, but you'll notice a world of a difference.

Nvidia still also has the best most predictable drivers for linux as well.  ATI drivers seem to have issues with performing within expected specs and can exhibit all kinds of strange behaviour.
I've had ati laptops & desktops where they wouldn't output full screen 3d to an lcd properly - but were fine on CRTs.

An intel video chip is great, just don't expect good 3d and also, don't expect it to behave right.

There's no reason to go anything but NV unless power is a concern - then go intel.  But getting ATI is just a waste and they tend to market their cards very deceptively.

I know tons of people who thought they were getting onboard video but ended up with shared memory because the wording was juuuuuust-right.
