These excerpts are from Wikipedia:

```
Consumer products are expected to become available in 2010.[51] Commercial
controllers are expected to enter into volume production no later than the
first quarter of 2010.[52] NEC is aiming to produce its first USB 3.0
controller in June 2009, initially priced at US$15.00. Monthly production of
NEC Electronics' µPD720200 host controller is expected to reach approximately
1,000,000 units in September 2009.[53] On September 24, 2009 Freecom announced
a USB 3.0 external hard drive.[54]

On October 27, 2009 Gigabyte announced 7 new P55 chipsets motherboards that
included onboard USB 3.0, SATA Rev. 3 (6Gb/s) and triple power to all USB
ports.[55] On January 8th, 2010 Gigabyte was also the first manufacturer to
release the world's first USB 3.0-certified motherboard, the GA-P55A-UD3.[56]
To ensure compatibility between motherboards and peripherals, all USB-certified
devices must be approved by the USB Implementers Forum.

Intel will not support USB 3.0 until 2011[63], which will slow down mainstream
adoption. These delays may be due to problems in the CMOS manufacturing
process,[64] a focus to advance the Nehalem platform [65] or a tactic by Intel
to boost its upcoming Light Peak interface.[66] Current AMD roadmaps indicate
that the new southbridges released in the beginning of 2010 will not support
USB 3.0[64]. Market researcher In-Stat predicts a relevant market share of USB
3.0 not until 2011.

```
