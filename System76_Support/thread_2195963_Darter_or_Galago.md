### Post by pcroque on 2013-12-28
Don't have the Darter, but just got the Galago. Bottom and top appear to be thin metal (but I wouldn't swear by it).

Keyboard is excellent. No missed keystrokes. Very little flex. Oh...and the layout of the keys is good also (Ctrl key is in the lower left corner...which wasn't the case on the Thinkpad I just returned).

The screen is the real star though. I love the 1920x1080. Can't really see the pixels...just a pleasant 'graininess' that gives the whites a papery look. And the colours are vibrant and well defined, without being over saturated. No washed-out look.

So far, I'm very pleased.

### Post by isantop on 2013-12-31
I haven't received any support tickets about Darter keybaords that weren't simple defective keyboards. The Galago keyboard is slightly better, but they're both pretty good.

The Darter is made with polycarbonate. The Galago use magnesium alloy for the bottom panel and the top of the lid; the palmrest and screen bezel are polycarbonate (this allows extremely high rigidity, without making the system heavy).
