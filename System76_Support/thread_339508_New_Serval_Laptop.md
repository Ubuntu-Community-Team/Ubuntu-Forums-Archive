### Post by crichell on 2007-01-17
paranoid - many (if not most) of our customers are first time Linux users that are simply tired of Windows and looking for something different.

> given an only moderate level of competence, how often can I expect to screw things up of my own accord?

Less so than an typical Windows users.  Linux has legendary stability and by design it's inherently more secure.  Spyware, viruses, popups, and other annoying problems that hose tons of Windows machines daily simply don't exist in Linux.

> Do updates have the potential to break previously working setups?

Ubuntu has had one breakage that caused some trouble; however, we posted here as soon as we noticed it and urged customers to hold off on updates until it's fixed.  The Ubuntu devs also gained significantly from the error so its recurrence is unlikely.

> Can I always expect to have an up-to-date and perfectly-working machine up to the limit of the hardware I'm buying?

Yes - and we support all future Ubuntu releases on our products.

Joe - you're right we don't do Dual Boot systems.  We provide a [How To]("http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Windows_-_Add_MS_Windows_to_Your_System76_Machine") if customers want to add Windows down the line.
