### Post by Ubun2to on 2012-08-23
Are you having trouble with it? I don't think that should happen, unless you enable hardware acceleration (which is known to cause problems with Youtube videos going too fast or slow), but even that would have a hard time making the fan get loud, even if you were watching 1080p.
If you get an SSD, you should rarely hear the fan (unless you a serious power user that loves to compile kernels and high intensity processes like that).
If you get an HDD, then this would be more likely to happen naturally (HDDs get warmer than SSDs because they have moving parts-moving parts generate heat the same way rubbing your hands together does), not necessarily because you are streaming 1080p content.
