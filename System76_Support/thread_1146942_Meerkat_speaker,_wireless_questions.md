### Post by tonyyarusso on 2009-05-03
Just got the Meerkat nettop.  Two quick questions while I'm getting things set up:

1) The package came with a tiny speaker in one of those little ziploc bags.  I assume this is supposed to be the internal one (don't see one in now), for my console bell if nothing else.  How/where does this attach, if at all on this model?  I don't see any likely-looking pinouts on the mobo.  (The connector is made to go on four pins all in a line, with only the two outermost spots connected to the two wires of the speaker.)

2) I also got the wireless option, which shipped as the Linksys WUSB54GC USB adapter.  According to the Ubuntu Wiki it's fully supported, but I haven't yet figured out just how it works.  Any pointers on that?  (I've tried in both 8.10 and an upgraded-from-RC 9.04 so far, with it showing an entry in 'lsusb', but nothing in 'ifconfig -a'.)  Feeling a bit dumb on this one...

Overall it looks like a pretty nice system.  For a future revision I would like to see DVI out and an integraded SDHC card reader, and possibly building in the wireless option with a mini PCIe card (like on the MSI Wind PC).  I'd imagine you could use the second USB connector on the board to run a cable to the front panel if you can fit one in physically.  Also, the CPU fan was substantially louder than I expected (not awful, but more than is probably necessary for this kind of machine), so checking out other options for that part may be worthwhile.  Liking the gigabit ethernet, overall build quality, slim DVD drive, and actually will be making use of the serial port (I know, I'm a minority there).  The Atom 330 seems pretty slick so far too - definitely glad you got that in here.
