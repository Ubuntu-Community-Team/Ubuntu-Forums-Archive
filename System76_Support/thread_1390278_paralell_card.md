Hi...  IIRC the 4100 has a built in JetDirect card, which if so I'd recommend just networking it to your router and making it a network printer.  The drivers for these old HP printers are fairly standard, so you'd just have to set it up as a network printer with the IP and pick the driver with no hassle with finding a USB to Parallel cable or some legacy parallel port card.

I have an older HP Laserjet 5N which is almost identical to the 4100, and I've used it for years over the network... since Red Hat 7.x if not before that.  Plus it would be accessible to any computer on your network.

Just a suggestion...
