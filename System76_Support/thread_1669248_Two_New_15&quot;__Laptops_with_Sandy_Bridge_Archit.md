### Post by jml on 2011-01-20
mbidewel, I can't comment on the two laptops specifically, but I have three system 76 laptops.  A first generation Starling, a first and third generation Darter.  I have Debian running on both Darters (a distro, not known for its support of cutting edge hardware,) and everything I need works fine. I would recommend that if you are offered a choice of wireless cards, go for the Intel ones rather than any generic card offered.  Intel cards have better Linux support.

maybe nouveau is obsolete in the future..Nouveau is the most promising hardware effort in the FOSS area imo.  There are many reasons for it to be successful, most notably the awesome  hardware functionality being accessible in an open way with things like  Gallium3d and LLVM, which would put GNU/Linux out of competition compared to the blob-only support of MS and Apple.

The PS3 is also a  possibile target of Nouveau in the future and a very interesting piece  of hardware esp. when FOSS gaming arrives (which is already promising  atm.). I guess that the vendors, esp. NVidia when exploring the embedded  area with ARM, will jump on the bandwaggon of open drivers rather  sooner than later, since important pieces for KMS are located in the  GPL'd kernel now anyway. They might still ship a propr. Gallium3d blob,  but this would already be a progress and a next step to open drivers.
