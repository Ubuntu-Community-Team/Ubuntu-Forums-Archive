### Post by TheOtherShoe on 2007-03-20
I've been doing a lot of research on the subject of where laptops come from lately, so I will share what I have learned.

None of the big brand name computer manufactures - e.g. Dell, HP, Apple - actually manufacture their own laptops. Instead they commission them from original design manufacturers, also known as ODMs. There are a handful of ODMs that together manufacture the vast majority of the world's laptops; these include Quanta, Asus, and Compal. [This article]("http://www.powernotebooks.com/articles/index.php?action=fullnews&id=17") has some more information about how all of this works.

The big name guys usually commission laptop models for their own exclusive use - which is why HP laptops look nothing like Apple laptops, even though they are made by the same ODM in many cases. But the ODMs also produce lines of laptops that anybody can buy and rebrand: these are called "whitebooks". Whitebooks are also often called "barebones" laptops because they are sold without certain components, such as processors, RAM,  and hard drives. They are kind of like empty desktop cases, except that they usually come with a screen and a video card and a few other things that are built by the ODM specifically for that laptop model.

Small-scale brands, like System76, buy whitebooks from various ODMs, buy the missing components from other sources, install the components in the whitebooks, and sell the finished product under their own brands. Basically every component of the laptop that you can customize when you buy it is one that was not built in by the ODM, but that was bought separately and was installed by the reseller. The reason that certain components can't be customized, for example why different screen resolutions aren't always available, is that these components have to be made by the ODM.

The different System76 models are based on whitebooks from different ODMs. As other posters have pointed out, these include Quanta, Asus, Compal, and possibly others.

It's possible that System76 outsources the assembly of the laptops with their final components to another company. I don't know if any of the ODMs offer that sort of full-service deal - my guess is that assembly is done either by System76 or by a third party that none of us has ever heard of.
