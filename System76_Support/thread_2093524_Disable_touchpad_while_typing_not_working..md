### Post by kingbanditjing on 2012-12-11
Hi all, I have a new System76 Gazelle running Ubuntu 12.10. Accidental input on the touchpad is out of control and has become a serious issue. The usual clicking in a different spot while typing problem occurs. Sometimes scarier things occur however like inadvertently attaching a document to an email while composing.

In System Settings > Mouse & Touchpad > Touchpad > Disable touchpad while typing is checked. Additionally, ps shows syndaemon running:

syndaemon -i 2.0 -K -R -t

However it is still easy to reproduce touchpad tapping while actively typing. Suggestions?  I'm happy to mess around in the CLI if needed. 

Note: This is a repost of [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2092044](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2092044) I didn't realize there was a System76 forum at that time.

### Post by Guayaba on 2012-12-12
I have a Bonobo Extreme with 12.10.  I use Fn+F1 to disable the touchpad.  Be careful not to do Fn+1 accidentally--it will set your fan to warp speed!
