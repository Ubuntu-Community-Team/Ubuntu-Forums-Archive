### Post by osx424242 on 2011-08-31
> **BBQdave said:**
> I'm curious, either I am really out of touch, or missing the whole tablet thing.  It seems like it's lack of practical function would keep people away... but folks are cobbling it up:confused:

Tablets are actually more practical than laptops/desktops, for some situations.  It may make more sense to think of tablets as *content consumption* devices rather than *content creation* devices.  For reading news (e.g. RSS feeds or PDFs of magazines), watching movies, or viewing pictures they have a decent screen size (especially compared to a smartphone) but are a lot easier to hold at different viewing angles than a laptop.

If you use your computer primarily for creating content (blogging, coding, sending emails, etc.) then no, a tablet wouldn't make a lot of sense.  I certainly wouldn't want a tablet at work!  But at home, where I want to watch some videos or read some web pages, and want more flexibility than my laptop can provide (I'm not going to hold my laptop up in the air while lying on my back on the couch, or rotate it 90 degrees so I can read a PDF of a comic book without panning on every page), a tablet is ideal.

And no, tablets aren't right for everyone, but then again neither is a smartphone, netbook, laptop, or desktop.
