### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-12-11
That question comes up here from time to time. Here are the answers:

1. Several people have requested AMD, and we're always looking at profitable ways to fulfill customer requests. We've sold AMD before and will probably sell it again at some point.

2. AMD generally is Open Source friendly. So is Intel; they've helped us through a number of jams. However, AMD is not very easy to navigate for small businesses. Intel provides FAR better channel support than AMD. And when you're selling and supporting many thousands of computers, that becomes VERY important.

> I thought it is one OEM that can't be bought by Microsoft... but it got bought by Intel instead.

At least as it pertains to System76, that's probably not an applicable perspective. Intel doesn't *buy* companies. But they do provide quality products and services that are necessary for a growing, fast-paced business.

Don't get me wrong, I'm sure that we will have an AMD offering or two in the future. But we don't currently take the hardline "AMD or bust" side of the argument.
