### Post by jml on 2011-11-09
Before you do that try this.  Turn off your computer.  Remove the battery and unplug it.  Hit the power button to make sure that your computer is truly powered down.  Wait a few minutes, 5 - 10 should be more than enough.  Put the battery back, plug it in and hit the power button.  If that does not work, you may want to send an e-mail to System 76 support.  They may have a suggestion that may save you from doing a reinstall.  Good luck.

### Post by zappabark on 2011-11-09
Okay, so I got impatient. I did a full reinstall as per System76 directions. I had to burn a new ISO, etc. Kind of a pain, but whatever.

After that, the System76 driver would not install due to somewhat mysterious dependency problems. Turns out all the dependencies were in the Python IDE, so once that was installed, the System76 driver installed and ran.

An email from System76 (they responded within an hour) was apologetic. Apparently there was an issue with an installation script that was not compatible with Ubuntu 11.10. They were helpful and responsive. Had I waited instead of doing a reinstall, I'm confident their fix would have worked.

I've had cleaner experiences with new laptops, obviously, but all is well now. Fortunately, I'm a long time Linux user, so this wasn't too bad.
