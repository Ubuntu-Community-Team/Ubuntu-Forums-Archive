That is EXACTLY how my gazv2 failed, although I've only had one of the hinges fail.  The other is enough to hold it up, but it's much flimsier than before.

I'm not sure if I can describe why/where it failed underneath that v-shaped plastic cover, but here goes.  The actual hinge rotates on a cylindrical bearing, pretty much exactly like the hinge on a door.  The only difference is that it's shaped like an L, with the top part being a much thicker & sturdier piece of metal, attached to the lid frame.  The lower part of the "L" is the cylindrical hinge, and it's only about 1 quarter of an inch long, and less than an eighth of an inch wide.  To me, it seems like that part is much too small and has way too little leverage to absorb ANY lateral forces one might typically encounter in normal laptop use.  I'm not talking about smacking the thing on the side or dropping it.  You reach across the table for your beverage, and OOPS!  There goes another 30% of the serviceable life of your laptop hinge.

I'm really glad to hear that the new ones are much improved in this area.  The only thing I've ever had to complain about my gazv2 is that it's a little flimsy.:(
