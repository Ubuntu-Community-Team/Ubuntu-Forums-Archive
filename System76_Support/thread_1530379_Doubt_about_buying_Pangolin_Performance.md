### Post by bill516 on 2010-07-13
I'll let the System 76 folks respond to your technical questions, let me address the substantive concern you have as a grad student.

Everything turns on your application software.  No matter how good the machine/operating system might be if necessary application software will not run you are in a bad situation.

It seems to me there is an easy way to test this.  Download a 10.4 LiveCD and install it on your present laptop.  Then systematically test the software you must run.  If Ubuntu passes that test you are good to go.

PS:  There a distro known as "Scientific Linux."  You might want to investigate it.  They have a forum and the people on it may know your software.

Assuming Linux will handle your needs I certainly would endorse System 76.  My PanP4 is a daily workhorse and it has been a good purchase for me.  Just note that battery life is not long, 90 - 120 minutes.

### Post by 3Miro on 2010-10-05
I am not the original poster, but I was in my last year as a grad student when I got my Pangolin (it was also an older model). I now have my Ph.D. in Math and it is partially due to the laptop. I am not sure about geophysical science, but in computational Math Linux is the way to go. There is a reason why supercomputers and servers run on Linux, Windows cannot handle such a workload. Mac on the other hand is like commercial Linux that comes with a ton of restrictions.

Right now, my wife is using the laptop for her degree and she is couple of months short of graduation. She majors in education and she did all her work on the laptop.

Basically, unless you play heavy windows games or you need some special windows only software, Linux is the way to go. It is different from Windows, however, it is in no way "harder".
