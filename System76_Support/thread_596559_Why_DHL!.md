### Post by jjdarling on 2007-10-29
So I'm really excited about receiving my Gazelle that I ordered almost a month ago.

However, my computer is now sitting in a warehouse in Franklin, IL, and DHL seems absolutely incompetent when it comes to getting it from this sleepy midwestern town out to my suburb of Chicago. They were supposed to deliver it on the 24th (Thanks system76 for the free 2nd day shipping!), but either the driver can't read street signs, door #s, or signed notes on doors telling them where to leave the package. They also fail to accurately update their tracking system, and there seems to be no communication between their (unhelpful, unfriendly) customer service agents and the couriers.

Now I'm the type of person who doesn't usually have bad luck with shipping, but I don't think it's a coincidence that I've only ever run into trouble when dealing with DHL. 

System76 seems like a hard-working company, and their customer service is one of their greatest assets. They're just not doing themselves any favors when they do business with companies like DHL. 

Anyway, that's just my 2 cents. Did anybody else have shipping problems in receiving their computers?

### Post by imhavoc on 2007-10-29
I hate to agree with this, but I have to agree with this.

I happily ordered my machine from System76, then I was seriously tempted to cancel the order when I saw that it would be shipped via DHL.

It wasn't easy, but I held my breath for the days it was in transit. Sweat and anxiety were my world while I waited to see if DHL would drop the ball as expected, or surprise me by delivering an undamaged package on time. I was thrilled when it arrived -- just the fact that it actually arrived.

When you set your expectations low enough, surprise is around every corner!

I have to say, you'd do your customers the service of lowering their blood pressure if you'd select UPS or even FedEx's Ground Service for delivery. When I was in a business which relied on delivery of products to my customers, I never selected AirBorne/DHL after the first three or four experiences with them.
