### Post by jml on 2007-09-25
This may border on heresy, but in my quest to find interesting Linux alternatives for my first generation Darter, (the white one,) I rediscoverd Kanotix.  Now before you stop reading, and before the mod's delete this post, let me explain my reasoning.  I use Ubuntu, I like Ubuntu and I have a great deal of respect for what Ubuntu has accomplished in a very short time.  That being said, Linux is about freedom and choice.  And, while this is an Ubuntu Forum, it is also a System 76 sub-forum and as a system 76 customer and a satisfied Darter user, I felt an obligation to give back to this community a bit of my experiences with this distro.  So, here goes.

Kanotix is now based on Debian stable (currently Etch) with some applications added from the backports repository and some custom drivers for newer hardware.  The problem I faced with my Darter was that the wireless card is an Intel Pro/Wireless 3945 ABG Mini-PCI Express card. Linux support is rather spotty.  Ubuntu does support this with custon/proprietary drivers. Until recently, not many other distros did.  Enter Kanotix 2007 RC6.  In brief, it recognized all of my Darter's hardware; the wireless card, 3D-graphics, the memory card reader, the works.  I now have an alternative distro for my Darter if I want one.  I am not suggesting that Kanotix is better than Ubuntu, its simply different.  I wanted the other Darter owners to know.

Joe
