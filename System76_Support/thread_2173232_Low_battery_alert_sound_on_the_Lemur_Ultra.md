### Post by timbo3 on 2013-09-29
This issue is not solved. However, while searching for answers I found someone who had a way to get around the problem. The system sound beep is disabled when your soundsettings is set to mute. So if you have muted the sound, the beep will not go off when battery is critically low. It is a temporary solution  but better than none. I've been in contact with System76 but they don't know what is wrong. I think I will email them again.

Don't forget to post here if you find a better solution to this.

### Post by timbo3 on 2013-09-29
That is exactelly the case. There is a setting in BIOS but it is not working even if it says that 'system alarm beep' is off. 

One more thing. This solution I just posted is only working if you have your sound setting to 'play sound through' Speakers. I just had my laptop plugged into the TV with HDMI with the sound going through the HDMI. When I unplugged it I muted the volum in the upper right corner of Ubuntu. This however did not mute the system alarm beep when the battery went low because the system still thought I was connected via HDMI. I needed to click the "play sound through Speakers" once again and then mute it. :)
