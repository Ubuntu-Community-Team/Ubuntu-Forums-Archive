### Post by TheFu on 2014-03-22
I cannot say anything about your specific needs.

A few weeks ago, I was looking for a $250 netbook and visited the System76 website only to be disappointed. Next went to Dell and found a model that seemed good for $280, but they don't make them anymore and forced a touch screen for $350.

Ended up buying an Acer C720 for $211 (inc tax).  Loaded Ubuntu 13.10 and I'm mostly happy.  Ordered a $100 128G SSD upgrade for the box.  Size, weight, battery and CPU were my main conciderations.  The Celerion 2955U is an amazing CPU for power management AND it has VT-x support.  I would have happily paid $50 more to get a standard keyboard. Happily.  Today alone, I've hit the **fracking power button** about 15 times - it is located where DEL is on every other laptop I've ever used.  The C720 Chromebook designers should killed, slowly for that.

Oh - and the 2G RAM is a minor issue. Acer's 4G model has been sold out for months. If someone sold a 4G-8G model, I would have been all over that!  Battery life is more important than raw CPU performance for many people.

When my Core i5 laptop dies in a few years, I'll look at System76 for a replacement first. They don't have to be the lowest priced offer, just within $100.

### Post by tlroche on 2014-03-23
> **DaimyoKirby said:**
> [Are] System76 laptops are overpriced for what you get[?]

That question is rather vague: several factors need better defined in order to make a reasonably-sound comparison.

[LIST=1]
[*]Does "what you get" == hardware + pre-installed, working linux? If so, I suspect (though ICBW) the answer is "no." If not, you can *definitely* find windows boxes out there that are cheaper for same *hardware* performance.
[*]Does "what you get" include service/support? My experience with System76 support to date has been quite positive. I suspect it's esp valuable to the linux newbie.
[*]What's your time worth? If ~= 0, then you can absolutely buy cheaper/hotter hardware and fiddle away. Otherwise, esp for linux newbies, the System76 price delta (which is, IIUC, > 0) is probably well worth your time.
[/LIST]
