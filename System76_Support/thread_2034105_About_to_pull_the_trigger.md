### Post by ufugu on 2012-07-30
OK, review as promised: I played with my Gazp7 all weekend. Got the 8GB DDR3 and 128GB Crucial SSD options. Tried a few different distros and had a blast (I settled on sticking with my current favorite, Voyager, based Xubuntu).

The Gazelle is an AWESOME machine.  Pull the trigger!

I have never had a hi-res display on a laptop before.  Didn't think it was a big deal as a feature.  I was wrong, it makes a HUGE difference in comfort and usabilty of the laptop.

Everything is fast. Almost instant. I almost kept Unity on there--turns out Unity is much less annoying running on a super fast machine with a hi-res display.  :lolflag:

Tried a couple things that I've had trouble with in the past:

* Plugged in my USB mic and fired up Empathy for a Google video chat with a friend using an Eee.  Everything went smoothly.

* Booted up from my external KXStudio drive and had no issues using it with my external USB audio interface--it went so well I may get the drive caddy and use the Gazelle as my primary audio workstation as well.

The heat on the palm rest (as discussed a few times in this forum) is no different than any laptop I've used before, maybe lower than average.  And it's almost silent.  I've been able to get the fan going when I really push the machine, but I'm pleasantly surprised by how quiet it sounds and how relatively cool it stays.  The loudest noise it makes is the clunk of the CD drive during startup (a sound we all know well!).

I see the Gazelle as a desktop replacement. It's not really something I would use as a daily portable.  It's heavy and bulky. It barely, barely squeezes into my soft case which fits other 15.6" laptops easily.  Haven't thoroughly tested the battery life yet, but the indicator starts running down from ~3 hours.  Honestly, I don't really care about that--I favor a tablet over a laptop for travel anyway.

I have some thoughts on the overall experience which are not as glowing as my impressions of the hardware, but they do not reflect at all on the performance of the machine AT ALL or the great value I think it is. (I'll post an email to System76 with those thoughts, but they are minor and don't reflect negatively on the company at all).  

Bottom line: If you are thinking of the Gazp7 for a laptop that you'll use primarily at home, get it! You won't find a better deal.

### Post by Oliphant on 2012-08-08
Got it late Monday, was out all day.  Tuesday I didn't have much time to mess around but from what I saw so far so good.  Today I really was able to mess around a lot.  

The display is very nice.  I went with the glossy and I like the real estate it provides me.  

Videos looks great on this display.  The unit itself came with a plastic case which resembles brushed aluminum.  Nice touch, I like it.  It feels hefty enough where I don't have to act like its make of glass.  Even being the larger of the S76 units it is lighter than my last laptop.  Not by much but it is so I really don't notice much of a problem.  I was most concerned about the size of the power pack that was described but once I got it, yet again something the same size as the old laptop.  

I like the flush touch-pad and the overall look of the machine.  I'm impressed with its speed and so far I'm very happy.  I did experience this lockup upon resume but one once.  

Battery life, eh its a laptop so 2:30 - 3 is fine for me.

The heat issue near palm rest wasn't bad.  Reference my last laptop it was even hotter so this is nice.  

The fan is nice and quiet and hasn't really had to kick on high gear yet.  The Disc drive, sounds no different than any other machine I have to use.  I'm very happy with this unit, it feels durable, Ubuntu has run very smooth compared to other machines I've put it on.  

I had it at work today and since I do have some software that just won't run under Wine I had to put Win7 under VBox.  Everything went smooth with that, installed fast and runs great for the little bit I need.  

I didn't really get the opportunity to mess with video / audio yet but I will do it this weekend.  I don't expect to be disappointed.  

_Overall_
I'm extremely happy with System 76!  If anyone from S76, right from the start I called your Sales, (was a few weeks ago) I don't remember his name I THINK it was Ian but not sure.  Anyway, aces of a guy. Answered all my questions thoroughly and each and every person I had contact with after that has been wonderful.  It's so nice dealing with a company that takes pride in their work and customer service, so Thank you \\:D/
