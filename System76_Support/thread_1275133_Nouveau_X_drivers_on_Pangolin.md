### Post by Lee_Machine on 2009-09-25
I am running Fedora 11, and I was using the Nouveau drivers, they work fine for things like 3D acceleration in Compiz. But there is still not an easy to use GUI like the NVIDIA Display Settings. 

Also the NVIDIA drivers are more stable, have more features, and just work. 

Now as a HUGE fan of open source I prefer it, but I also think NVIDIA has supported Linux very well...at least compared to ATI. 
 
This is just one of those things that it's proprietary, but its free and it works.

Like many of the audio, and video codecs you use. 

This is on a PanP4n, with Fedora 11. The Nouveau drivers are used by default....not sure it they are enabled during a live session so you can try it out.
