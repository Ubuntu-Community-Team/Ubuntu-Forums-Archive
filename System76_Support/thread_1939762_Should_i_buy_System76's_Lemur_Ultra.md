I purchased the Lemur Ultra recently, and I am quite happy.  I had an issue with the stock wireless card (there is a recent thread in here somewhere that discusses it), but I've been told that now the Lemur only comes with the wireless card that I am now using that fixed the issue I was having.

The battery life is not as good as the battery life I have with my small netbook that has only an SSD with no fan, but I wouldn't say it sucks.  Also, in the upcoming versions of the Linux kernel are supposed to be some bug fixes and enhancements that really improve battery life.

I upgraded the RAM to 8 GB, but that's the only thing I did beyond stock configuration.  I just configured my exact machine again, and it would be under $800.

Other than the battery life, I'm not sure what other issues you've heard in the mixed reviews you've read so I can't address them.  I will say that I didn't even boot in to Ubuntu when I got it - I immediately wiped the drive and installed Xubuntu with the drives partitioned to my liking, so I can't tell you if Unity bogs the machine down or in some way mucks things up.

Anyway, I'm happy with it, and I'm specifically not unhappy with the battery life.

### Post by rk8102 on 2012-03-26
I was considering a System76 laptop as well, but there are two things that aren't sitting well with me at the moment.

The first is the off-brand OEM - take that as you will.

The second is this... I was looking at their website, and I was wondering about a return policy in case I didn't like it or whatnot. Sure enough, they have a 30-day guarantee.  Great! Then, I got thinking about what do they do with systems that people return (because they didn't like the glossy screen or whatever other thing), maybe they sell them at a discount which is what everyone else does. Sure Apple's refurb discount is tiny, but hey...

So I inquired about such returned systems; they replied that they don't sell them, and instead they salvage the parts for the repair department.

This makes no sense to me. Maybe a desktop where you can pull out the motherboard, etc. But a laptop?? Who in their right mind would take apart a perfectly good laptop and use it as parts? That just doesn't make any financial sense at all, there would always be plenty of parts that you never use, a total waste of money.

And, in the guarantee they state they'll charge $40-100 for anything returned with scratches.

My suspicious mind tells me that what they are actually doing is replacing any part on a return that is scratched or doesn't look "new" and selling these resulting systems as new. But of course they would not be new, they would be refurbished.

They sort of deny this, taking a more "well you're just going to believe what you want" stance instead of an outright denial.

But it seems too dodgy for me, just my 2 cents, I'm looking elsewhere.
