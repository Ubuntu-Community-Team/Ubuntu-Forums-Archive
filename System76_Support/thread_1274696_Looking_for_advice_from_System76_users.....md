### Post by marshmallow1304 on 2009-09-25
The S76 driver is simply a deb package.

You would upgrade on your own, in whichever way you desire, then re-add the S76 repository if you did a clean install (which would have wiped your user-defined software sources), download the driver, install, and reboot.  Easy.

Just make sure they've got Karmic supported before you do.  I would assume the start of support would coincide with the official release, but I've had my S76 machine for less than 6 months, so I don't know for sure.

### Post by gila_monster on 2009-09-25
> **marshmallow1304 said:**
> Just make sure they've got Karmic supported before you do.  I would assume the start of support would coincide with the official release, but I've had my S76 machine for less than 6 months, so I don't know for sure.

Marshmallow is correct, Thomas said yesterday (I think) that not only does the driver not support Karmic right now, but they haven't even started testing it on Karmic. So it will be a while. Generally, official S76 driver support of a *buntu release coincides with the official release from Canonical. It certainly won't be AFTER that. I normally just wait for the normal dist upgrade to show up in synaptic, and the S76 updates with it.

It *is* possible to modify the driver to recognize Karmic on your own. I don't recommend this by and large, as a) it's not for everyone (need to be fairly confident in some coding ability), and b) as mentioned, you don't know if it will work at all anyway.
