### Post by Bluebearbevis on 2010-12-28
Happy New Year Everyone,

I thought it was high time I wrote a review of my panp6 and of System76 in general after a Baker's dozen months of ownership.  If I wax poetic, please bear with me.

I won't get into the technical jargon too much, sufficient to say "Panny" has 2 cores, 2 threads, 2 Gigs Ram, and 250 Gigs hard drive with nVidia 512 Megs graphics card.  It's not an I7, nor does it have a solid state drive, however is it "fast" and can it handle all I ask?  Yes on both counts.  Fast being a relative term, I'm not an "extreme" on line gamer, it's like lightning to thunder compared to my old Pentium4 desktop.  I use Gimp and Inkscape simultaneously, so for kicks I opened Google Earth and the System Monitor in the other work stations, and never were the core needles buried in the red, so to speak, nor did I ever use more than 1 Gig of my 2 in Ram.

Now about System76.  If for no other reason than the following, and there are plenty more, my next computer will come from System76.  Support, accessibility, personal concern, support!  Living in a semi-unique situation, I live about 4 miles down the road from the System76 address on the website, my first experience with them was when I went to the "store" to buy my laptop.  Ha Ha.  Although Carl did not have one to sell me, he was more than happy to let me fiddle/fart around with one at the shop.  I ordered my own shortly thereafter.  Tom was there immediately when I encountered a graphics issue after a fresh install, twice!  Their new guy, Ian, is cut from the same cloth.  When I had to re-image "Panny" after a coup attempt by Fedora and the cd/dvd device no longer worked correctly, Ian walked me through the troubleshooting process with patience and didn't make me feel like too much of an idiot when it turned out to be only a dirty lens.

What else can I say, except, when Ian needs an assistant as we know he soon will, can I have the job?

Thanks Everyone for listening,

Richard A Bevis

### Post by NathanZal on 2011-01-05
Hey, 12 month review is a great idea. I got my P6 in November, 2008. It's worked flawlessly from day one. My machine is like Bluebear's only with 6Gig memory, P8700 Dual Core, and a 500G disc. To give you a small idea of what I run on this thing, and therefore what "Performance" really means to me, here's a typical setup:

- Windows 7 on Virtual Box downloading some stuff from Usenet onto a couple of network drives I have mounted.
- Windows XP on another Virtual Box running Photoshop editing some layout or other. Other times, Dreamweaver.
- Running Compiz
- Playing a .mkv HD movie from another network drive
- Running Chrome with half a dozen tabs open
- Editing a document with Openoffice.org.
- Never mind all the XTerm windows open; I never count those.

All this as smooth as can be, except when some CPU hog like quickpar takes everything momentarily. Then it doesn't matter how little I'm doing, everything waits.

The bottom line for me is the this little machine appears to be able to take everything I can throw at it. I can't quite record realtime in Windows XP running Cubase, but that's asking a lot of a VM; too much, probably. On the other hand, maybe my next machine will do it...

I've never regretted this purchase for one minute. Besides, I LOVE it when people say, "wow, what is that, is it a Mac or something?" and I show them The Cube & spin it around a little. They always freak out.

All the best,

Nathan
