### Post by thomasaaron on 2007-06-23
Not really.  We don't do testing with all possible peripherals, nor could we. 

Our goal isn't really to ensure our computers work with specific peripherals, but to make sure our hardware is properly configured with Ubuntu. Often if a peripheral doesn't work, it is because Ubuntu doesn't support it yet (although I'm sure there are certain System 76-specific incompatibilities that pop up from time to time.) And sometimes it is just a matter of someone who really wants to use that peripheral putting the necessary time and effort into making it work.

Usually Googling "Ubuntu <manufacturer / model #>" is a pretty good indicator of potential problems in installing/configuring various pieces of peripherals.

HOWEVER, perhaps you guys are onto a nifty idea.  It might be nice to have a user-driven database that collects data on peripherals System 76 customers have found to work (or not work) with specific System 76 models. :idea:  What do you guys think?
