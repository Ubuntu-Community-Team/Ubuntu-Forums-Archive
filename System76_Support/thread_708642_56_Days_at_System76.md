### Post by crichell on 2008-02-26
On my blog last night I posted the happenings at System76 over the last 56 days.  It's been a tremendous start to the year so I thought I'd share it here too.
[B]
Ubuntu 64 bit Pre-Installed and Supported[/B]

Almost since inception System76 has shipped laptops and desktops that support 64 bit operating systems. We continuously tested 64 bit Ubuntu until finally, with the 7.10 release, we felt confident that the operating system was ready for our main stream (and enthusiast) customers. Now our customers can take advantage of increased performance and ever increasing amounts of affordable memory — 4 GB of laptop memory is only $160 from System76.
[B]
Adobe Flash Pre-Installed[/B]

Along with 64 bit Ubuntu, System76 licensed and started shipping Adobe Flash pre-installed. This was a tough one for me. I support and prefer open source alternatives whenever possible and if there is a choice I’d rather leave it to our customer to decide. In this case a temporary Ubuntu bug caused confusion when customers tried to install Adobe Flash and with the critical nature of sites like YouTube we decided to act.
[B]
New Server Configurations[/B]

Most online server configuration tools include an unnecessary number of options - particularly when it comes to hard drives and storage. When I think of a server I think of space not hard drives. We adjusted our configurations accordingly. Instead of choosing each hard drive, the RAID controller, and the RAID level we created options based on your desired storage capacity and the type of redundancy you require.  Check out our server storage options and compare them to other companies -- I think it makes much more sense.
[B]
New System76 Desktops Released[/B]

In January we quietly released a Koala Mini platform refresh and three new desktops. The Ratel Value and Wild Dog feature 80 Plus certified power supplies. The 80 plus program is a forum of energy companies, industry manufacturers, and consumers aiming to bring greater energy efficient to desktops and servers.  Pics of the new desktop line are at [www.system76.com](www.system76.com)

Koala Mini: The stylish Koala Mini now supports 4 GB of memory, 250 GB hard drives, and Core 2 Duo processors all in a tiny desktop form factor.

Ratel Value: Our new, extremely solid, Ratel Value starts at $349.00. This isn’t a watered down desktop experience either. There’s plenty of power for desktop effects, Open Office, Evolution, Firefox, and the full desktop you’ve come to expect.

Sable Performance: The slimline Sable Performance desktop supports up to 8 GB of memory, 750 GB hard drives, and Intel Core 2 Duo processors.

Wild Dog Performance: The Wild Dog represents top of the line desktop performance. nVidia graphics, Quad Core CPU’s, enterprise hard drives, and an excellent design.
[B]
That's all... for now[/B]

Carl
