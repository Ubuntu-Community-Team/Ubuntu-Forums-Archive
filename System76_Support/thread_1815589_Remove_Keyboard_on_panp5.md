### Post by isantop on 2011-08-01
Actually, you don't need to remove any screws to remove the keyboard. There are five tabs along the top edge of the keyboard, near the screen. To remove the keyboard, simply slide the tabs up towards the screen, and the keyboard will pop out.

---

### Post by Iteria on 2011-08-01
Are you sure? I've tried sliding the keyboard both up and down to no avail.

---

### Post by isantop on 2011-08-02
Actually, the tabs are attached to the chassis, and the keyboard is stationary. They're spring loaded, so you'll want to use a small, flat screwdriver to push them up, and I find it easiest to start towards the middle of the computer and work towards the outside.
