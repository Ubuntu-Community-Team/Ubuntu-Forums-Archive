### Post by Laurence_Montrose on 2014-05-21
This is a common brick size for high-powered laptops. The Bonobo brick provides roughly 4x the wattage of the AC adapter for my little machine, which is a standard 90W size. The battery for the Bonobo is rated for 90W, so it might work to find a smaller one, but I have an iGO that supposedly came with universal adapters and none of the will fit, so that might be the hard part.

One the other hand, the bigger one won't die an early death from being overstressed.

### Post by Laurence_Montrose on 2014-05-23
I went to your link, found the one you were pointed to, found the manufacturer's specs and some better pictures. It looks like it's in the exact same case, has the exact same cable, and the connector has the same configuration. The only difference seems to be a slighly lower output power, 220W vs. 320W. It might weigh a little less, but it would be no less inconvenient to pack around. It should last about 20 years on average. If that were a consolation. 

I guess the reason this doesn't seem like a big deal to me is that I intentionally got the Bonobo Extreme because I wanted a big screen, a powerful CPU, and graphics that didn't give me a headache. The "laptop" is truly a laptop, in fact, you could snuggle up to a friend and the thing would sit on both your laps. Since the laptop is so big, it's not going to be convenient to lug around, in the first place. I bought a spare battery, which is a quarter the size of the AC adapter and a third the weight, as a mitigator.
