### Post by myrkat on 2014-09-17
I have a **Kudu Professional** (Clevo W670SZQ) from System76 and it arrived fine, and worked great right out of the box.  Being a constant nerd, I promptly wiped out the system and put Kubuntu 14.04 on it... then Windows 8.1... then wiped it all out again, added another SSD (I ordered mine with just the mSata Samsung EVO card @120GB, so I added a "normal" 120GB EVO SSD in the laptop drive bay - which took all of 3 minutes tops, take that Apple!).  Anyway, I've put a few flavors of distro's on it, currently it has some Windows (think I went back to 8.1, but didn't activate because M$ is a bull$hit company like that... really, I'm tempted to get a copy of the OS instead of the stack of licensed products I have from them...)

Not sure if your sound analysis will be depending on the laptop's speakers, if so, Clevo laptops notoriously have the worst sound!  I mean, it's terrible.  Get some headphones and you'll be fine, however.  I've only used the DVD drive to load my winblows intstalls, as real operating systems in the twenty first century are available digitally for USB's.

The laptop DOES do UEFI, if you need secure booting, etc.  I forget if ubuntu that came pre-installed was installed using UEFI, sorry.

Was a great value, the company did a followup phone call after my order, then again after (or around) delivery.  I had a few technical questions prior to the order, and they were responded to promptly.

As for SIZE and POWER (battery):  The thing IS a 17" laptop, so it's slightly cumbersome to actually wield it in the field... unless you're set up on a desk-like surface or something.  This ain't no MacBook Air!
The power-saving is OK, but most of the power will be to the screen, and if you're outdoors, you'll likely want the highest brightness, thus your battery life might only be 2 hours or so.  I usually have mine plugged in.  Indoors, the screen is REALLY bright, I often have to put it down to 1/2 the setting via the Fn key combo.  You can also turn off the backlight to the keyboard, not sure how much that will save, but there it is.  I also turn off the radios (especially bluetooth, since I don't use it).

I think the Gazelle would be easier to carry around.  The weight for the Kudu is not light, but it is also not nearly as heavy as other 17 inch "full size" laptops.  For portability, I use my MacBook Air (stupid Apple and their breaking NFS in Mavericks).  The thing I love about this Kudu (and the Gazelle has it too!) is the Numeric Keypad!  I really don't realize how much I miss a full keyboard until I'm out on my MacBook and wishing I could have a keypad... :(
