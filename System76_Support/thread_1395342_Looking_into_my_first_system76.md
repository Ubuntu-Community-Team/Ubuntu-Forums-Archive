### Post by jml on 2010-01-31
I have to agree with steveneddy.  I have purchased three laptops from System 76 (first and third generation Darter, and first generation Starling.)  I have been very happy with all three of them. The Starling initially had some wireless networking issues, but this company worked very hard to get the hardware to run correctly.  (For the record, the problem was with a Linux driver and not with the hardware.)

Tom, (a System 76 employee and moderator of this sub-forum,) can answer you question about the optical drive's power supply.

As to which laptop is best, That will depend on how you plan to use it. Given the applications that you list, either one would be able to do the job well with a stock configuration.  Depending on how much music you want to store on your laptop, you may want to upgrade to a larger hard drive.  One key factor is if you plan to use your laptop on battery power a lot.  In that case, the two laptops differ.  The Lemur gets about 4 hours of battery life.  The Pangolin and the Serval get about 1.5 - 2 hours of battery life. 

Which ever laptop you get, I am sure that you will be happy with it.  If you have specific sales question, go to their web site and contact them via e-mail.  They tend to answer quite rapidly and seem to be happy to help.

### Post by Georgesl on 2010-02-04
I've had two System76 computers, a Starling and a Darter.  I, too, was working on the theory that it would be a good idea to buy from a company that specialized in Ubuntu computers because their equipment would be tested and fully compatible with the OS.

The Starling had wireless issues as was earlier discussed.  I decided that I didn't want to keep an unusable computer for the uncertain amount of time before a fix was ready so I returned the Starling and bought a Darter.

The Darter worked OK, but had serious issues with power management.  It couldn't detect when it was running on battery, ran the CPU and fan at high speed continuously (and noisily), and went through its battery in under 1-1/2 hours.  More research into the specific problem revealed that this had been a known issue for several months with no solution in sight.  That computer went back too.  There was no problem getting a refund as it was still within the return time limit, but I was still out the shipping and the hassle.

Support is provided on these forums, but the only thing I was advised to do was "Reinstall the System76 drivers." and "Wait for Karmic."  Not terribly satisfying.

I'm forced to the conclusion that buying from System76 is no guarantee that the hardware will be compatible with Ubuntu.  My advice is to search these forums carefully for the model of computer of interest and pay particular attention to the problems that customers are having before clicking "Buy."
