System76 is a small company that specializes in a relatively small market (Ubuntu-shipped PCs).  The cost goes up because they can't purchase in bulk like Dell/HP/Toshiba/etc can.  Also, they provide VERY good customer service (through this forum, telephone and email) that is local to the United States.  Most companies cut costs by shipping their support overseas and increase paid costs for support from a "local" tech.

If you purchase a computer from System76, you'll get great support, a great PC (desktop or laptop), and the knowledge that you didn't purchase a PC that has Windows pre-installed on it that counts toward Microsoft's sales figures.  If you're going to install Ubuntu on the computer, you might as well purchase something that has Ubuntu on it, right?

Anyway, I'm not affiliated with System76 in any way, other than being a very happy customer (I have a laptop and a netbook from them).  I love both of the machines, and I think they are great quality for what I paid.

### Post by jml on 2010-12-14
Just to add my comments.  System 76 does not sell a low end laptop.  If you spec out the base configuration on any of their computers, the hardware that they choose is usually better than the low end hardware you get from the major manufacturers.  Add to that, that they don't have the buying power of a Dell or HP, and you can see why it costs a bit more for a System 76 computer. 

 But if you value aftermarket support, then System 76 wins hands down.  I have owned low end Dell and HP computers in the past.  They worked OK, but when I had a technical question, trying to talk to a human being was very difficult.  if I could not find an answer on the web site, I was SOL.  And that was with question about the Windows OS.  I got virtually no support when I started running Linux on those computers.  When I wanted to play with Windows 7 (I know, I'm a glutton for punishment,) I found an unadvertised special from a major retail chain for a laptop running W7 for $250.)  I bought it, and luckily it works fine, but the policy of the store where I bought it stated if I had any question or problems, I was not to contact the store, but I was directed to the manufacturer.  Not exactly superior customer service, but for the price I paid not unexpected.

Two examples of the support I have gotten from System 76.  I wanted to install Debian on two of my System 76 laptops.  They do not officially support this distro, but they still responded to my questions and helped me get Debian installed on my first and third generation Darter.  I also had a question on their third generation Starling.  Tom replied to my e-mail over a weekend!  I am willing to pay a premium for this kind of support.  Again, just my opinion.
