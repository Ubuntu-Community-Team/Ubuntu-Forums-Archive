### Post by XxsydenxX on 2009-10-05
Hey guys, im just here to  throw out some ideas which would make system 76 better known to the "Average user". Recently in my neighborhood, I made a survey out of personal interest. And it was first of all if the survey taker knew of linux mainly ubuntu, and if they would use linux for its benefits in which it offers.

Long story short here, a majority of these average users were a bit more educated on technology then i thought, and amazingly what they wanted more so then, support with windows API, was OS to Hardware reliability,sophistication, and manufacturer support. Now alot of them seem to dislike the the clutter of there desktop mid towers. After i explained to some of them about the benefits of using Linux, they were certainly shocked. They loved the fact that it was free, That it was stable and less prone to viruses. And that there were alternatives to things they used on windows. But it still left the issue of them wanting a new experience with there desktop computers, less wires...Less components, and the security to know they were supported if anything went wrong.

That got me to thinking, maybe system 76 should invest in making a ALL IN ONE pc, which has components inside known to work well not only with each other but with linux itself. You know buyers first impressions of a product come from the way it looks.....if you see less clutter and less to worry about, these users whom want simplicity will really be drawn to it.

So I decided this week, spite my hurt knee, to go around with another little survey of my own. This time, I shooped a Macintosh Imac, in two colors replacing the apple logo with an  system76[SIZE="1"]tm[/SIZE] logo. One of them was glossy orange and the other a black I took photos of the existing desktops as well. Alot of these people recognized me, I explained that this was a continuation off of my last survey. And i asked if any other family members were in the home, so they could sign the chart as well, it was a well made chart...with pictures ans computer specs which i explained to them and base pricing. And im going to give you the results of that chart now. The question asked was. "Please review the following desktop computers on the list and tally the one you would most likely purchase."

[B][U]System76 AIO Black/Orange: 58

Meerkat Nettop: 23

Meerkat ion Nettop: 27

Ratel Value: 9

Wild Dog Performance: 0 [ I have no idea why nobody wanted it o.o;]

Leopard extreme: 12[/U][/B]

Now note that i made fantastical base specs for the AIO computers.

22inch LCD Display  1650x1080 Resolution
Intel Pentium Dual Core e5300 2.60 ghz processor 
2 gigabytes ddr2 ram 800mhz
Built in speakers
Intel x1300 Graphics Media Accelerator 
CD-rw/DVD-rw drive
Ubuntu 9.04 (Jaunty Jackalope)

Priced around $779.00

When I asked why people chose the AIO PC's over the ones with "monster performance" the mass of them said Its more convenient. I recall one man saying that it was more convenient AND as soon as he would buy the performance one it would be outdated...which is true to say the least.


I'll let those results speak for themselves o.o...I hope you enjoyed reading ^.^

### Post by japhyr on 2009-10-08
It seems that when people buy Windows computers they might work well at first, but they get bloated over time and start crashing at some point.  Then these folks have their work cut out for them.  Linux users seem to do some work up front setting up their system, but then end up with a fairly stable, reliable system.  It seems like a 50/50 question, do you want to do some work on your own up front, with the help of a lot of people, or deal with bloat and viruses, and proprietary upgrade issues at some point in the future?  This idea that Windows-based computers just work out of the box is getting harder to accept.

I am teaching a class of 12 high school students to rebuild old computers.  We accepted about 30 computers from the public that were destined for landfills, and students have been learning to install ubuntu on them.  They love it, and none of them had ever seen linux before.  They will probably never go back to Windows, not because we've bashed Microsoft but because they've seen how much control they can have over their computers.
