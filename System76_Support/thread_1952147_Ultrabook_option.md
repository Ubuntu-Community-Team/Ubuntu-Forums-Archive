### Post by SemiExpert on 2012-04-04
Consider for a moment that Intel has provided $300 million in support to Windows PC OEMs in an attempt to jumpstart the ultrabook concept.  Consider for a moment that most Ultrabooks either have a metal case, or at least try to immitate a metal case, which is more expensive to tool up for than plastic.  I don't think the odds are very great that there will be very many choices for third party, rebranded whitebooks in the Ultrabook form factor.  For all I know, pre-loaded Windows might be a prerequisite for using the Ultrabook nameplate.
