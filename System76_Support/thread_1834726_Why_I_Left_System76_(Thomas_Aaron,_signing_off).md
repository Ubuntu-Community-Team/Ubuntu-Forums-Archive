### Post by thomasaaron on 2011-08-28
Hello, friends.

I have received many emails, Facebook messages and Google+ messages asking me why I left System76 and wishing me well. So, I thought it appropriate to say thank you and address everyone's questions here ... where many of my past interactions with you have been held.

Before I left System76, the owner and I were musing about how many of you "founding customers" I knew by name. Some of you occasionally would call just to chat with me. (What other computer company does *that* happen in?!) The friendships I made with System76 customers were always, and still are, the crown jewel of my accomplishments there.

I worked for System76 for a little less than six years, and I have left with no complaints. I left to follow two dreams: one that has been deeply ingrained in me since childhood, and which made the early years at System76 so fulfilling for me (entrepreneurship), and another that has been a big part of my life for the last two years (dogs). In a nutshell, I achieved a dog training certification and decided to run with it. System76's owner, Carl Richell, is as avid an entrepreneur as anyone I've ever worked with/for, and he saw the end of our road approaching and supported me in my efforts to establish my own business ([http://www.fetchmasters.com](http://www.fetchmasters.com)). For this, and all of the friendship and knowledge he has given me over the years, I am eternally grateful.

Having known and experienced the inner workings of System76 for so long, and now having departed and being completely free to say whatever I want to whomever I want, I would like to give you the no-holds-barred truth about System76. Are you ready? OK, here goes ...

The owner of System76 is extremely dedicated to customer service. He doesn't sit around on his laurels and watch the money roll in. He is always assisting the tech support and customer service departments in finding ways to make sure customers are treated well.

While there have been a small handful of customers over the years who may not have felt that way, I can tell you from experience that any failures we had in that aspect of the business were thoroughly mourned. And much effort was put into patching any support shortcomings we found. While it's true you can't please everybody all of the time, System76 probably comes as close to disproving that rule as any company can.

I truly believe that System76 (more, the people that work there, from the tech support team, to customer service, right up the chain to the owner himself) ... care. Find another computer company with that attitude if you can.

System76 runs as lean as possible, and everyone that works there carries a lot of responsibility and a heavy workload. Be nice to them. They are human, and they know that you are too. There hearts are in the right place, and they are diligently working to run an honest business.

As for me, I learned a lot working there that I'm bringing to my own business ... from running all my stuff on Ubuntu to top-notch customer care. I'll be on the forums from time to time (although I know how to fix most of my own problems :lolflag: ). But please feel free to email me (trainer@fetchmasters.com), friend me on Facebook ([http://facebook.com/tbaaron](http://facebook.com/tbaaron)) or even pitch in and help me gain more followers on my business FB page ([http://facebook.com/fetchmasters](http://facebook.com/fetchmasters)).

God bless, and I look forward to a continued relationship with all of you who have enriched my life for the last six years. And thank you for letting me know how I have enriched yours. It means a lot to me and confirms that the last six years of hard work were worth it.

Kindest regards,
Thomas Aaron
