### Post by Phantom784 on 2007-12-03
The Gazelle looks like a rip-off when compared with the Pangolin.  They both cost the same.  However, the Gazelle has a smaller screen than the Pangolin.  Also, it only lets you upgrade the graphics card to 8400 versus Pangolin's 8600.  However, both graphics card upgrades cost $199.  I couldn't find any other differences besides this, please point out if there's something I've missed.

The Serval also looks like a rip-off.  Once you upgrade Pangolin's video card to the one that Serval comes with, the Serval is $199 more than the Pangolin.  At this point, the two laptops are the same, except for the fact that the Serval has a higher screen resolution (albeit with the same screen size, so the only effect is that everything appears smaller but you can cram more stuff on the screen) and an S-Video port.  Since the Serval is listed as coming with a built-in webcam and the other's aren't, this seams like another advantage to the Serval, but in reality it is not.  Upgrading the Intel to the Nvidia on the other two also give you the webcam, but still at a lesser price.

Other than the graphics card options, all three laptops have the same available upgrades at the same prices.  Based on this, the best laptop to get is the Pangolin, because you can get a bigger screen and better graphics card for the same price.  Even if the Nvidia card doesn't matter to you, it still gives you a better screen.  The only reason I could see to buy the Gazelle is if you want the smaller physical size for some reason. The only reason I see to shell out for the Serval is if the extra screen resolution or the S-Video port (and I don't know when I'd ever use that).

The Darter Ultra, which I haven't mentioned yet, looks like a rip-off too.  It gives you the webcam without requiring the Nvidia upgrade, and includes Bluetooth rather than provide it as an upgrade.  However, it has the smallest screen of the bunch.  By taking the Pangolin, we can build an even better system for less money! Uping Pangolin's graphics to Nvidia gives us a better graphics card than the Darter, as well as giving us the webcam.  The Bluetooth only adds and extra $39, bringing the Pangolin's total up to $937.  This is less than Darter's starting price of $995, and with a bigger screen and better graphics card!!!

Quick version: The Pangolin is the way to go :)
