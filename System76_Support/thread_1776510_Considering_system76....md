### Post by Flyers2391 on 2011-06-07
> Anyways, what is system76 support like?

Anything I need/want answered in a timely manner I email.  If it was very important I would call.  If it is just generic and not urgent in any way, I post here.  Though, recently it does seem forum support has dissipated a bit (maybe it's a busy time of year?).  

> How mod-friendly is the company (for instance, can I replace my own RAM and hard disk if I choose to and NOT void the warranty)?

RAM, HD, ODD, battery: all user-replaceable.  Check for compatibility if you want to upgrade but pretty much if it isn't soldered and is a common hardware replacement, you should be good.  You can always ask if you aren't sure.  

> Is there any possibility of getting a different OS installed besides the current ubuntu (I want LTS, not current)?

They stick with the most recent release because they use such recent hardware ...  this is for compatibility.  

> How generic is the hardware (can I install any other OS and have it work out of the gate)?

Others have installed many linux flavors but it all depends on the exact model to determine how much or how little trouble you will experience.  It seems the closer to ubuntu the release the better your chances of perfect/near perfect hardware support (ie: Mint, Kubuntu).  

> What sort of things can I expect to receive with the laptop (CD's, "little touches")?

They are "green" so you'll get very little with the packaging.  Most documentation is online.  You do, however, get an ubuntu logo covering the Windows logo button on laptop keyboards.  

> How is the build quality of the laptops - is it junky like a lot of HP lappys, or super sturdy like a thinkpad?

Not much can really compare to a good thinkpad & its keyboard but they are close.

I personally try to avoid posting my opinion here, as I don't really qualify as a good source for that type of thing. I did see your post in the other thread, and I didn't feel could answer in an unbiased way. I do apologize if it looks as if we don't care about the sales questions, but our customers are much better sources of information regarding that.

As for factual information, however, I can provide that. Flyers2391 is spot on with all of his points. I should clarify a few things, just in case:

> How mod-friendly is the company (for instance, can I replace my own RAM and hard disk if I choose to and NOT void the warranty)? 

In addition to what he said, this won't void the warranty at all. For obvious reasons, if a part you replaced fails, then it's not covered, but if anything that came with the laptop fails, regardless of what other parts are installed, we will cover it for you, including shipping both ways.

> Is there any possibility of getting a different OS installed besides the current ubuntu (I want LTS, not current)? 

Some of our systems aren't compatible with earlier versions of Ubuntu. Our new Pangolin, for instance, isn't compatible with versions prior to 11.04, and the Gazelle, Serval and Bonobo require 10.10 or higher, and work best with 11.04.
