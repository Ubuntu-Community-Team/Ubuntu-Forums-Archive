### Post by jml on 2007-04-18
Well, until the moderator moves your post, let me share my limited experience.  First, System 76 is a sub-forum under the 3rd party sub-forum header.  System 76 is a company that specializes in selling computers, both desktop and laptop with Ubuntu preinstalled.  The value that they add is that they make sure everything works out of the box, (3d accelerated video, wireless netwoking, sound, memory card readers, etc.)  They also offer very good aftermarket support for their products.  I am the proud owner of a Darter.

Now to your other question.  I've installed Linux on several different brands of laptops.  I have had very good luck with ThinkPads, (those sold by IBM, have no experience with Lenovo.) HP laptops sold through their small business web site.  What I like about HP is the fact that you can order a laptop without Windows if you want.  If you get one with Windows, HP tends not to load it up with a lot of useless software/trialware, etc, so there is less stuff to delete.  If wireless networking is important to you, try to avoid any laptop that has the Broadcon wireless chipset.  Its a very common wireless card, and since they do not opensource their drivers, its very hard for Linux developers to write good drivers for them.  It took me nearly a year to get my Broadcom card working with Linux the way I wanted.  Hope this is helpful.

Joe

### Post by jml on 2007-04-19
System 76 has been asked about the AMD issue on this forum before.  They respond that they regulary look into AMD, and when suitable hardware comes along, they may include it in the product line.  I might be wrong, but I think that at least one of their desktop computers uses an AMD processor.  

As far as graphics capabilities are concerned, two of their laptop lines, (Gazelle and Serval) offer dedicated (Invidia,) graphics cards.  I have a Darter loaded with max RAM and the integrated Intel graphics works well for me. One last point and then I will close.  If you read the various threads on the System 76 sub-forum you will hear many comments about their great customer support.  Let me give you a concrete example.
