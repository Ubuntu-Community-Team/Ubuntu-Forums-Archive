### Post by maandverband on 2008-08-01
I hate to see Microsoft-logo's everywhere. You go to a shop and you see PC's and laptops with Windows case-badges, hardware with the Windows-logo on the box and there's no way to buy a keyboard without that Windows-logo. I don't use Windows, so I don't want a Windows-logo on my keyboard.

It's really nice to see System76 selling PC's, laptops and servers with Ubuntu. I like those case-badges with the Ubuntu-logo. Really a great job! I teared the "Designed for Windows XP"-badge off my laptop and replaced it with the "Powered by Ubuntu"-badge. Way better.

Next task: Get rid off that Windows-logo on the keyboards. Why do the laptops from System76 have a Windows-key? Why can I only choose from Logitech keyboard (which have Windows-keys) when ordering a desktop?

It'd be nice if System76 could make a Ubuntu-keyboard or a Tux-keyboard. A Tux-keyboard would be even better, because then people who run an other distribution can also order the keyboard.

The number of Linux-users keeps growing and growing, so I think there's a market for these keyboard. It's also more proffessional when there isn't a Windows-key on a laptop bought at System76 (or any other manufacturer who sells Linux-systems).
