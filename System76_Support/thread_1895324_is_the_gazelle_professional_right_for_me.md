### Post by isantop on 2011-12-14
Vs. the Pavilion:

The Pavilion does have a faster processor, though you can add that to the Gazelle.
They're about equal in memory.
The Pavilion has a larger screen, but it uses a lower resolution (17.3 inch at 1600x900, vs 15.6 inch at 1920x1080)
The Gazelle's graphics card is faster, though it has less vRAM.
The Pavilion has a larger hard drive, but it is much slower. The hybrid drive in the Gazelle will be 50% faster at its slowest, and up to 150% faster at its fastest.

Price: The Pavilion is $1029.99, and the Gazelle is $1179.00

Vs. the Envy:

Same processor
The Envy has 2 GB less RAM
The Envy has a smaller, lower-resolution screen (14.5 inch 1366x768 versus 15.6 inch 1920x1080)
The Gazelle has a much faster graphics card with more vRAM.
The storage on the envy is weird, but you can get a similar hard drive or SSD in the Gazelle.
The Gazelle has an additional USB 3 port, and a combo USB 2/eSATA port.

Price: The Envy is $1299.99 and the Gazelle is $1179.00

In either case, with us you'll receive dedicated Ubuntu support, including software support, that HP can't offer. You'll also know that your system was specifically engineered to work with Ubuntu.

### Post by mgolden on 2011-12-20
I got a Gazelle Professional:

i7-2760QM Processor ( 6MB L3 Cache, 2.40GHz )
Nvidia GeForce GTX 560M Graphics with 1.5GB GDDR5 Video Memory
16 GB Dual Channel DDR3 SDRAM at 1333MHz
120 GB Intel 510 Series SATA III 6 Gb/s Solid State Disk Drive
8X DVD±R/RW/4X +DL Super-Multi Drive 

on which I am running Kubuntu 11.10.  If you were to buy the machine now it would cost $1652.

I have been very happy with both the machine and the OS.  My quibbles with it are:

*) It has a camera and it works fine, but there's no light indicating it is on.  This is a security problem.

*) The machine is a bit heavy, which i knew, but the included adapter is just under 2 lbs, which brings the total weight up.  I am looking for a lighter one.

*) Don't use the included free carrying case (they don't even point out that you'll get one).  I carried it around for a couple of days and the strap broke, and now the corner of my machine is dinged.  (It would have been helpful if a little slip of paper had been in there saying something about that.)

*) Read the knowledge76 page I wrote about using the nVidia graphics card.  Unfortunately not everything is as smooth as you'd like if you use the proprietary driver.

Note that in my machine I opted to get a small, but very fast SSD drive.  That works for me but you might want to make a different choice.
