### Post by EJJ on 2011-02-20
My only real dissatisfaction with the Starling is the battery (aside from no bluetooth!.) Don't get me wrong though, the life has been satisfactory....but I'd like it if it lasted longer.

I haven't exactly timed my Starling as far as how long the battery lasts...though it's charging right now, so I'll time it and let you know.

There could be some other factors at play with your battery life though....how bright is your screen? What tasks are you running on the machine? If you aren't using wireless internet are you turning off the card?

I use my Starling primarily for writing, so I'm usually running open office and I've made it a habit to keep the screen pretty dim (not all the way, I'd say about 25%) and to make sure the wireless card is turned off.

When I use it at work (I teach guitar and general music) I frequently use Tux Guitar. Playing the midi notes and editing those files drains the battery significantly more than if I'm just writing.

If you aren't running things at full blast and you're still getting 1 1/2-2 hours of life you might want to try letting the system run till the battery dies completely. Then let the machine charge fully to max, then drain the battery down again and charge all the way. I usually find this process helps for most things with rechargeable batteries, phones, ipods, etc.  Incorrect charging habits are usually the culprit behind most battery complaints.
