### Post by cmnorton on 2009-06-28
Does hibernating consume battery life? If so, how much and how long would a battery last at 80% or greater charge?

There are times when I'd like to leave things set up, and hibernate by Gazelle Ultra without having to reboot.

---

### Post by lswb on 2009-06-28
Hibernating does not run down the battery, but batteries do slowly discharge even when not being used. IOW, the 80% charged battery should last equally long on a hibernated laptop as on a laptop that was shut down.

### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-06-29
...and hibernate practically *is* shutting off the computer. The re-boot time after hibernating isn't all that much (if at all) different than a fresh start. The difference is, the applications that were open when you hibernated will be open when you resume.

With suspend, you use a little battery life, but not much. The applications you had open are stored in RAM (as opposed to being stored on the hard drive swap partition), and this takes a small amount of power. The advantage of suspend is that the machine can resume to it's pre-suspended state in a matter of seconds.
