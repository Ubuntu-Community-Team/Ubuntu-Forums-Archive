### Post by rorschachwalter on 2013-08-14
Battery life doing a normal load (maybe 60% brightness, web surfing, a short amount of movie watching) is 3-3.5 hours.

Framerate in TF2 was pretty lousy. My 5-year-old Dell kicked the GazP9's butt all over the place, playing TF2 at 1920x1200. On the GazP9, I really couldn't up the resolution to native 1080p without lots of spotty framerate. The CPU temp sat at about 70 C, the fan was going super loud, and the framerate was about 30 fps, often dropping below that. If you drop resolution to less than native, you eek out a steadier framerate, but it's still low (35 fps). I played with settings, but they simply didn't make any noticeable difference.

Now, Phoronix has demonstrated that graphics performance for Haswell should improve greatly with the 3.10 kernel and Mesa 9.2. I tried updating to these, but ran into all sorts of weird issues after I did so, and I didn't see any performance increase at all, so I can't comment on the real life improvements. That said, I would expect *some* improvement for the HD 4600 going forward, but I wanted to cry after I realized I spent ~$1000 on a laptop that couldn't even play TF2 reliably.
