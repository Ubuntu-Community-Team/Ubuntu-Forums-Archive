### Post by imhavoc on 2007-08-26
I would post photos, but my camera died, and I'm too snobbish to replace it with less than what I want.

Here's my review of the Darter Ultra 2 (black):
I love it.

It's not perfect. Some things don't work. Power management is buggy (looks like a BIOS or firmware issue, and not a System76 or Ubuntu/Linux problem). The camera, fingerprint reader, HDMI, and card reader don't work.... yet. (They will, I'm convinced.)

Other than that, I can get 4 hours of batter life. I can do all the stuff I want and need to do. It's not less capable than my desktop, and I have a year of "free upgrades" as we get drivers running for the other features. I don't even use the desktop at home any more. I take the DarU2 to work, and use it at work and I use it when I get home.

I've had mine for just over two weeks now, and I already have a scratch on the lid -- that's a sign to me that It's going with me everywhere, not being babied, and doing everything I bought it to do.

I am one happy System76 customer.

### Post by shingalated on 2007-08-27
To get it working in cheese, I had to download the latest version (0.22) compile that.  Open a terminal and type gstreamer-properties.  Open the video tab and change the plugin to "X Window System (No XV)"  Make sure default input is the "v4l2" driver, and device is USB 2.0  You may also have to hit the 'P2' button above the keyboard before running cheese.
