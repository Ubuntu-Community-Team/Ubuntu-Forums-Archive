Hi Everyone,

System76 Driver 2.3.7 has been released. This update is for Starling Netbook users. The release installs a new wireless driver on the Starling which increases range and fixes the wireless LED.

Change Log:

[LIST=1]
[*]Extend wireless range for the Starling Netbook (star1)
[*]Fix wireless LED on Starling Netbook (star1)
[*]Add Leopard Extreme (soon to be released)
[/LIST]

Go to System > Administration > Update Manager and click "Check" and then "Install Updates".

Go to System > Administration > System76 Driver. Click the "Install Drivers" tab and click "Install". The new wireless drivers will go into effect after rebooting the system.

It is confusing.

When you double click the system76-driver-2.3.7.deb file or select it in synaptic you are installing the application "system76-driver".

One of the things that application can do is install the System76 drivers, but you have to run it and select that option for it to happen.

I think the reason they made it a two step procedure is so that one application can download and install drivers based on the model of machine it is run on.
That's a more complex process than can be handled by a deb install.

### Post by ShowMeGrrl on 2009-08-10
OK, I've done some further testing of the Starling wi-fi with the new driver (Version 2.3.7) and the results are better.

I compared my Starling to my Lenovo R60 laptop. It's much beefier than the Starling. It's got 2 GB of RAM and a better, faster processor. Of course, it cost me $1800 vs. $359 for the Starling and weighs twice as much ;-)  

I compared them in three scenarios and the results varied for these three scenarios: 1) in a coffee shop where the signal is relatively weak and where I previously had trouble connecting my Starling while everybody else around me was connected; 2) sitting outside a library in my car; 3) sitting inside the library where the connection is strong and where previously I had been able to connect with no problems.

Scenario 1. The access point was not in the coffee shop itself but rather across the street.  This time I was able to connect my Starling to this network. The signal strength was only two bars.

The Lenovo, which also showed two bars, seemed to connect more readily than did the Starling. Also its wi-fi performance was signficantly faster.

I was able to bring up the New York Times front page in 18 seconds on the Lenovo compared to 1 minute 40 seconds for the Starling. (This is the time it took for the word "Done" to appear in the status bar on the bottom of the Firefox browser.)

Scenario 2. We then went to a local library, which hadn't opened yet, and tested the two computers in our car. There, the Wall Street Journal home page came up in the Lenovo in 8 seconds, compared to 2 minutes 30 seconds in the Starling.

Scenario 3. The library opened and we went inside, where signal strength is strong. The performance of the Lenovo and the Starling was comparable. It took the Lenovo 10 seconds vs 18 seconds for the Starling to bring up the NY Times, but it took the Lenovo 18 seconds vs 15 seconds for the Starling to bring up CNN.

Also, the Starling in all cases was able to recognize more local wi-fi networks than the Lenovo. At the coffee shop, the Starling saw 13 networks, while the Lenovo saw 7. Don't know how many of those 13 the Starling could connect with because most of them were secure and required passwords, etc. 

My conclusion is that the wi-fi performance of the Starling is improved but is slow when the signal is weak.

### Post by luptinpitman on 2009-08-25
Found my own answer.

from thread:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1202015&page=10](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1202015&page=10)

> **jediborger said:**
> Rectagonal,

Try the following and see if it helps.
Open up the terminal and type
```
ifconfig -a
```
If you don't see any wlan devices then press Fn+F2 and run the command again until you see a wlan device. Now try the following:
```
sudo ifconfig wlan# up
```
Where # is the number of your device from the first command.
If you get something about Operation not permitted then slide the switch on the front of the starling to the right once and let it slide back, it's spring loaded. Now try running the above command again.

You should now see networks and be able to connect.

I ran iwconfig and saw interface was set to wlan1

Following instructions above solved it 100 percent.  Wireless light lit right up and wicd sees everything around.  Happiness reigns.
