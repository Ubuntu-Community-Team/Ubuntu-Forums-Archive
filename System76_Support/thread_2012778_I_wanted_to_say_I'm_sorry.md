### Post by allquixotic on 2012-06-29
This coming week will be the last week that I can use to decide whether to keep my Lenovo ThinkPad T530 or to go with a System76 system. After Wednesday, I will not be able to return my ThinkPad with no restocking fee, so I'll be committing to keeping it.

As someone who REALLY wanted to support System76, give them my money, deny Microsoft my money, and support a vendor shipping Ubuntu by default (you guys rock!), I wanted to say I'm sorry.

I cannot -- I repeat, **cannot** buy a System76 system, for one and only one reason.

**System76 refuses to sell me a TrackPoint.**

Yes, it may sound petty to you, but a TrackPoint is an **essential** part of any laptop for me. I simply cannot use a laptop without a TrackPoint.

Why? I am a programmer with wrist problems. The TrackPoint allows me to type and mouse without moving my hands off the keyboard. It avoids wrist strain and saves time, since programming IDEs often require a frequent mix and match of mouse and keyboard, which is frustrating when using any other pointing device.

Touchpads?

I've tried them from Apple, Lenovo, Dell, HP, and even System76. I can't use them. They're just not the same.

I encourage anyone from System76 who reads this to tell your CEO or other executive officers that you would get my business if you could just offer this one simple thing. ALL of the other major vendors, except Apple, offer at least one laptop series with the TrackPoint. Lenovo, HP, and Dell all offer them, with Lenovo offering them **by default** on nearly all of their units except the IdeaPad.

And don't assume that just because I want a TrackPoint, that I also want an energy star certification label and Intel vPRO and other enterprisey features. Some manufacturers only offer TrackPoints on devices that are sold exclusively to businesses. IMHO this is unacceptable. The TrackPoint is an excellent pointing device for **everyone** but especially for software engineers.

I would have purchased a high-end 15.6" unit with the works if only for this small, but **critical** detail. Instead, I'm going with your competitor, who has a deal with the devil (Microsoft). :(

I'm sorry. Please step up to the plate with this feature, and I will give you my money and brand loyalty for a long time to come.
