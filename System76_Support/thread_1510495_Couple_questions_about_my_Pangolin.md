### Post by isantop on 2010-06-15
A: Check your settings for your screen saver. When it puts the display "to sleep", it shuts off the backlight. If your screen is simply dimming, I believe that that's a screen saver thing.

B: Unfortunately, there is no way to disable Fn+F4. I find I do this too from time to time. Make sure that under the power management preferences, in the General Tab, the action for the sleep button is set to "Suspend". this will minimize the impact, though It doesn't solve the root of the problem.

Glad you like your laptop! I hope it continues to serve you well long into the future.

### Post by isantop on 2010-06-17
You can reassign the function keys (i.e. F1 through F12) but the Fn+Fx combos are actually basically hotkeys, like the Mail, Web, and Mode buttons on the upper left. Like the Mode Key, which enables the silent mode feature, the suspend key is mapped directly with the system BIOS in order to provide identical operation across many operating systems.

You can, however, remap the volume keys, and move them somewhere else away from the sleep button.
