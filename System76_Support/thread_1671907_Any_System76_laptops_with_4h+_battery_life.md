My wife uses a macbook pro 15. It gets around 4 hours of battery life in real world usage. Sometimes less if she is doing lots of real work that uses the discreet graphics.

I think there are probably three things at work here:

1) marketing hype vs. real world performance
2) linux - power management isn't as bad as it used to be, but it's not as good as under Windows or OS X. My macbook gets 2-3 hours under OS X and 1.5-2 hours under Ubuntu.
3) Apple R&D - This is wild speculation, but I think the situation with patents / intellectual property in the US probably prevents smaller companies from leveraging improvements in battery technology.

### Post by jml on 2011-01-20
I believe that both the Starling and the Lemur if configured with the larger battery will give about four hours of battery life.  The Lemur has a 13 inch screen.  It can be loaded with a boatload of RAM and has several Hard drive configurations including solid state drives (think MacBook Air.) 

The Starling has a bit longer battery life, but being a "netbook" has a more modest CPU and may not be ideal for more demanding applications.  So it really depends on your needs.

By the way, I believe that the Lemur uses the same Lithium Ion Polymer battery technology used in the newest Macs.  Polymer technology simply lets laptop manufactures design batteries in custom shapes to maximize battery volume and performance.

### Post by isantop on 2011-01-21
Please note that that's a quote from the old Serval, the [SerP6](http://knowledge76.com/index.php/SerP6). The new [SerP7](http://knowledge76.com/index.php/SerP7) gets 2.5-3 hours. 

The CPU's will all scale back as needed to conserve battery life. The CPU thus doesn't affect too much until you start looking at different architectures (i.e. Atom vs. Sandy Bridge vs. ULV vs. Arrandale) Thus far, Sandy Bridge is much more efficient.
