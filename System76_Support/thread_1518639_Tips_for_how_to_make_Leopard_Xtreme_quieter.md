### Post by cascade9 on 2010-07-01
> **christopherbalz said:**
> Thanks everyone for the helpful suggestins.  So the stock fan is not really the quietest one available?  Also, regarding the window, it is cool but I'd rather have quiet - wouldn't it work in terms of noise dampening to put the noise-dampening foam on it?

For foam on the window, the noise is transmitted much better through a metal side panel than through a metal panel with a plastic window. The window is thicker than the metal side panel, and the window/side panel is heavier so it 'drums' less, and it is harder for the noise to get through in general.

Without actually hearing a leopard extreme (or any other system76) run, or checking the hardware its impossible for me to know.....but AFAIK, the quietest fans are noctua, and I really doubt that it comes with noctua fans (they are pretty expensive, and all the recent models are a districtive poo-brown and tan). 

Its much easier to actually cut the noise down from the source than it is to stop the noise getting out of the case ;)

### Post by mobius357 on 2010-07-06
It's probably not a good idea to only replace the fan on a stock cooler. Stock coolers need a fair bit of airflow and the quiet fans are low airflow, though they do push more air per decibel. Heres a list of recommended heatsinks on silentpcreview. [http://www.silentpcreview.com/Recommended_Heatsinks](http://www.silentpcreview.com/Recommended_Heatsinks)

The large tower coolers can be barely audible at all while still sinking more heat than the stock. They are heavy and harder to install though. Good chance you'd have to remove the motherboard.
