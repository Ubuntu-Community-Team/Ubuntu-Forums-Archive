### Post by adakite on 2012-07-29
Hey Folks,

I wonder why System76 does not offer any laptop with a discrete GPU, at least for their high-end/Pro versions. This is something which IMHO is missing.  While I understand that light IGP might be enough for a lot of people, having a heavy-duty GPU is also something that some people (me) need as well. Battery consumption is not relevant, for at least two reasons: 

(1) people who need discrete GPU don't look for mobility as the highest priority

(2) HP, Dell and Apple (at least) offer laptop with real GPU and at least >4 hours of battery life which even better than my current ThinLemur (without any GPU, but only a weak Intel IGP). And those battery life time is based on my everyday own experience, as I am using these system at work, so I can tell, that having a heavy-duty GPU is not incompatible with +4hours battery life time. One could says that, "yes but Optimus does not work under Linux, but only Windows."  I could say that Apple is able to offer its own IGP/GPU switching system using nvidia GPUs without Optimus. In addition some open-source attempts look pretty promising: [http://bumblebee-project.org](http://bumblebee-project.org). So IPG/GPU switching under Linux/BSD is possible. 

So offering a System76 laptop with Quadro Option (for the Pro line products) would be great. 

thanks for considering such feature in the near future.
