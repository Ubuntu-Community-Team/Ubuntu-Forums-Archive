### Post by drewbenn on 2011-10-06
I have a panv3, and it is a Compal FL-91.  I pulled out a lot of dust and cat hair a few times from around the fan; if there are pets around or just a lot of dust, it could certainly build up in there.  I have a manual I downloaded a couple years ago but it's 15MB (bigger than ubuntuforums.org's max filesize) and there isn't much inside it about disassembly or cleaning.  If you really want it and can't find it anywhere I'll try to find a place to upload it.

Here, though, are a couple pictures of my laptop that might help a lot:

First is the bottom, **unplugged and with the battery removed**, to show the location of the 4 (smallish phillips) screws that need to be removed (2 are short, 2 are long; should be obvious where to put them back: the long ones won't fit in the holes for the short ones, so she doesn't need to worry too much about getting them back correctly).  Then I had to really pry the cover off; today it came off pretty easily but I remember having a lot of trouble with it the first few times.  She might need a flathead screwdriver or something to pry that up.

The other picture shows the fan after I removed that cover.  The arrows point to where I've usually found the most dust & cat hair; it gets pretty matted up so it's pretty easy to pull out.

I'm kinda jealous of your daughter, that's a fantastic laptop; a couple years ago I dropped mine while it was plugged in and jogged the power jack loose, and last month (just 8 days away from 4 years since I got it) the power jack finally got loose enough that the power plug wouldn't fit right anymore, and it stopped charging.  I really miss it :(  Good luck with yours!
