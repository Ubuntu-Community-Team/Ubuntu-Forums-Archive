### Post by jml on 2009-12-23
Teasdale, I did not mean to imply that the hardware was in any way inferior to other laptops.  In fact, compared to some low end laptops, the Starling is built like a tank, (in a good way;-) )The only hardware specific issue that has been brought up is the fact that the compact size of most netbooks results in smaller radio antennas being installed into the netbook.  This theoretically impacts wireless performance.  In my opinion, wireless works fine.

I also completely agree with ShowMeGrrl. In my experience, the Starling has been powerful enough to do everything that I need it to do.  I also have read reports that trying to play high def. video (1080p) results in stuttering of the image.  Standard def is not a problem.  My reference to CPU/GPU intensive applications would  include things the netbook was not designed for such as running Photoshop in a virtual Windows machine, video editing, animation, or transcoding very large multimedia files.  Truth be known, given my modest computing needs, the Starling could be my only computer.

Joe

### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-12-26
Given the exact same wireless cards and drivers, a notebook is likely to outperform a netbook (as far as wireless goes).

Typically, the wireless cards antenna wire goes up into the lid of the computer and attaches to sheets of aluminum foil for receptivity. The sheets of foil are quite a bit larger in a notebook than they are in a netbook for obvious reasons.

Of course, this isn't a guaranteed rule, but it does seem to be what we see in our shop.
