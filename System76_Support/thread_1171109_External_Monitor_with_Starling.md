### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-05-27
You can switch back and forth between the monitors using the Fn-F3 key combination, and you can set up cloning and various spanning configurations by going to Preferences > Display.

The specs say it supports QXGA resolutions (i.e. up to 2048×1536 pixels with a 4:3 aspect ratio). We've not tested resolutions that high.

I'm sure you will get a resolution mismatch between the LCD and a projector.

### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-05-27
We only test computers with the monitors we sell as options (as those are the only ones we keep on hand)...

26" KDS Widescreen LCD (1920 x 1200)


The computer is going to detect the resolution of the projector, and it should get that correct. However, it's probably going to mess with the resolution on your LCD. This is pretty typical in Ubuntu.

Unfortunately, I do not have a projector to do detailed testing with. That's just from past experience.
