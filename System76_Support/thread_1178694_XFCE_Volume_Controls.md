I have Xubuntu installed on a Daru2 & the labeled keys ( Fn-F7 Fn-F8 ) work, I don't know about the Panp4n.
It might make a difference whether you installed Xubuntu or just got xfce4 with apt-get or synaptic.

If you can't get the keys to work you might try the Mixer applet.
Just right click on the toolbar to add it.
Right clicking the icon brings up a menu to set sound properies.
Left clicking the icon brings up a mixer panel.
Scrolling on the icon changes the volume.
