### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-04-09
Most BIOS upgrades provide support for Windows.

So, unless a) you are running windows - and it sounds like you are, and b) the upgrade is fixing a known problem that you have, you usually can safely ignore it.

Typically, if an upgrade makes a difference under Ubuntu, we will recommend it and provide instructions for performing it. 

The problem is, if you do an un-recommended upgrade, and it hoses your bios -- thus hosing your machine permanently -- it would not be covered under warranty.

It's your machine, so you can do whatever you want with it. Just be aware of the potential risks.
