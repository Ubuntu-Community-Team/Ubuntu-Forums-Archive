### Post by thomasaaron on 2008-11-03
Val67,

> 1. why has this new ubuntu version not been properly tested and s76 is shipping laptops that are not fully functional?

It has been properly tested. We do not ship a new version of Ubuntu until Canonical officially releases it (which was Friday). This does **not** mean that people upgrading from 8.04 to 8.10 will never have a problem. It means that our computers, running a fresh install of 8.10 will indeed be functional. There are a million ways, though, that an upgrade can go south.

> 2. if we wanted to buy laptops with the previous LTS (8.04) ubuntu, is this still an option?

No. We only ship the most recent version of Ubuntu. But if you want to re-isntall 8.04 on it, we will still support it.

abulluck,

> System76 has said they are testing Intrepid and will have the bugs worked out soon, and suggest never upgrading to the latest until a few weeks/months have passed anyway.

Our goal has always been to release the computers with the newest final release of Ubuntu. I think we've pretty much always met that goal. The problem is basically that everyone rushes to upgrade on the day a new final version is released, and the quality of the upgrade is dependent upon Ubuntu's servers -- which are usually stressed to the max on upgrade day. So, there are typically a number of corrupt downloads.

There's always a chance that we've missed a bug, but we try to fix that within a week of the upgrade date.
