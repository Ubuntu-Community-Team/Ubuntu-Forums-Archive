### Post by ubarry on 2006-10-18
I'm late, but for what it's worth.

I'm happy with mine. I'm new to Ubuntu, coming from Fedora, but I'm getting used to it. I've moved one of my other machines here. I think Redhat shot themselves in the foot with Fedora, and its short window of updates. The biggest thing I miss is package selection on install, but you're getting a preinstalled system here.

But about the Serval:

It works fine. It's linux so I have a minor unsolved problem or two but mainly because I haven't had time to fix, both these are quirky and specific to my setup. I use this mostly mostly as a desktop I can move around easily, so it's usually plugged into a regular monitor and keyboard.

It's not hot, and very quiet (one of the reasons I wanted a laptop).

Suspend is OK

Battery life is short compared to some smaller machines with big batteries (I have a Dell X1 with a six-cell and it lasts 6 hours +), but that's not a fair comparison.

The monitor and graphics card are cool. 

It looks and feels fine. 

I have two gigs of ram in mine but this is a matter of what you do, not brand specific.
