### Post by isantop on 2012-01-04
I should add that around mid-way through 2011, we implemented strict new performance standards to ensure an optimal Ubuntu experience. All of our laptops and desktops are required to meet these criteria, else we don't sell them (the Starling Netbook is an excellent example of this).

The current Lemur sails through the standards easily, and is one of the best out-of-the-box Ubuntu experiences out there. HD video, multiple browser tabs, and most computational work (even light 3D) works very well on the Lemur, particularly with a RAM and Processor upgrade. Ubuntu performs smooth and snappy on it, and as long as you aren't looking to do heavy gaming you should be fine with the Lemur.

(Also note that the Lemur and the Pangolin are identical in terms of starting configuration, and will both offer very similar performance for a given specification. If wanted something with a larger screen, then the Pangolin would work great.

### Post by cephalopoda on 2012-01-28
I've owned my Lemur Ultra for about a month now and I am very happy with it. I do both scientific computing, including some heavy simulations, as well as personal entertainment e.g. streaming video. The Lemur has performed beyond my expectations. Also, the tech support I received through this forum has been quite good.

Specs of my Lemur:
2nd Generation Intel Core i7-2670QM Processor ( 6MB L3 Cache, 2.20GHz )
8 GB Dual Channel DDR3 SDRAM at 1333MHz - 2 X 4GB
500 GB 7200rpm SATA Hybrid Hard Drive with 4 GB SSD
Intel Centrino Advanced-N 6230 - 802.11A/B/G/N Wireless LAN + Bluetooth Combo Module




I priced out a mac with about the same specs and it was >$800 more expensive.


The only minor complaints I have are with Ubuntu itself, i.e. touchpad sometimes stops working, but there are easy workarounds for those problems.


Overall, I highly recommend System76.
