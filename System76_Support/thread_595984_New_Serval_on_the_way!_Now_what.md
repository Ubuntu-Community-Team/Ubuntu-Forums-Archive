### Post by jml on 2007-10-29
For help with printers, first check this out:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/7.04/printing/C/index.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/7.04/printing/C/index.html)

It only goes up to Ubuntu 7.04, but it will give you a place to start.  I am sure others have different opinions, but I have been very satisfied with Linux's support for HP printers.  Now the one caution is to avoid cutting edge designs, there will be less support for them under Linux.  If you find a printer that meets your needs and has been around for 6 - 12 months, you will increase your chances of getting a supported printer.

Wireless, I also agree with the recommendation for the Linksys router/wireless access point.  Don't think you need to spend the extra money for an 802.11n device for several reasons.  The 802.11n standard has not yet been officially ratified, so all of the hardware is marketed as complying with the "draft standard"  A standard which could change.  Second, there is virtually no support for the 802.11n standard yet in Linux.  It will eventually arrive, but one never knows how long it will take.  A Linxys router/access point that supports 802.11b/g is very inexpensive and can be configured via a web browser so it is OS agnostic.

Finally, after adding my favorite apps, I usually add the third party/restricted codecs I need for full multimedia support.  But most important, enjoy your new toy!    ;)
