### Post by thomasaaron on 2007-05-14
> 1. If I replace a piece of hardware does that void my warranty?
It could, if the new piece of hardware damages the existing hardware.

> 2. I know it's possible to swap out the wireless card for another, but is it possible to swap out the video card? I'm not sure how this works in laptops.
Nope. Not on your Pangolin.

> 2a. Do you have any wireless cards based on a non-centrino chipset that you know work well with the other hardware in the system, and are supported by Linux drivers (MadWiFi) rather than having to bother with Ndiswrapper.
No, the only wireless cards we offer are the ones that ship with the system. You could possible get a PCMCIA wireless card to work. But I don't know anything about package injection and what capabilities you would get out of it.

> 3. Can I buy an extension on my warranty? Or is buying a warranty only available when you purchase a new computer?
If your current warranty has not expired, you can still upgrade. But bear in mind that if you damage the system attempting upgrades, that will not be covered.
