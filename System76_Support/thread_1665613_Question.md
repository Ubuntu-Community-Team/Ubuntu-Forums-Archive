### Post by satsujinka on 2011-01-14
@isantop
I haven't seen anything that would indicate that Intel's Atom lineup can be said to compete with AMD's Zacate. I'm not real up to date on the situation but last I was aware, there was no functional open source driver for the gpu on the Atom line. While I know that there is a fully functional open source 2D driver for Zacate (with partial 3D support.) On top of that, Atom is significantly slower than Zacate in the first place.

As for mobile computers that aren't in the low power spectrum Intel is the obvious choice. Better battery life, better performance, not too large a difference in price.

Desktops are a bit more complicated with no clear winner as far as I'm concerned. AMD can provide substantial performance for significantly cheaper, but if you need the absolute best you have to go with Intel. Generally speaking, if I were still using desktops I would choose AMD 99% of the time. Simply because it's cheaper and the difference in performance isn't worth the money to me.

Anyways, I had really wanted a Zacate based computer (preferably in the 13" space as netbooks are just too small,) but I decided that I wasn't willing to wait until at least March (as no Zacate based platform will be released before then) and just bought a Lemur.
