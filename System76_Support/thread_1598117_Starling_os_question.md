### Post by Carl Hamlin on 2010-10-17
If I'm reading you correctly,those aren't separate instances of the operating system so much as choices concerning which operating environment to log into.

Were you to install KDE, it would add itself to that list, however, you'd still only have the single installation of Linux on your system.

### Post by jml on 2010-10-17
I agree with Carl.  There really is nothing to remove.  Those different configurations don't take up much space, and there is little reason to delete them.

Safe mode is useful when you trouble shoot a system.  It loads the bare minimum to get your system functional so you can poke around and find out what is "broken"   Just my two cents worth.
