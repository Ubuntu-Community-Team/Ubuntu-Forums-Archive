### Post by samalex on 2010-02-01
It's funny how since Apple announced the iPad everyone's talking about tablet computing.  I guess Apple does set the trends :)

But for me here's my wish list for a PERFECT tablet computer:
- Must run Linux -- full distro of my choosing (which would be Ubuntu of course)
- Decent processor... though I'm not looking for a gaming system
- Screen that's not so bright that I can't read books for hours at a time, but something that'll work in direct sunlight
- 10"-12" 16x9 screen
- Decent amount of storage, possibly 100-250 Gigs for movies, music, etc
- Wifi
- Expansion ports to add G3 or other cell phone network wireless after market (don't care to have it built in so it's heterogeneous to all networks)
- A dock like the iPad to add a keyboard and possibly other devices
- Bluetooth
- Decent battery life with replaceable battery
- Light weight
- External battery life display to see what battery power is without turning it on
- Change orientation as computer flips (like iPhone and iPad) but also lock in one position via external button
- Webcam with decent resolution
- Internal microphone and stereo speakers plus mic input
- Possibly the ability to add a 2.5" hard drive for future upgrades
- Touch screen, but also work with stylus or mouse

For me by the tablet running full Linux I'm not too worried about software capabilities since Linux can do anything.  And as long as the hardware requirements are satisfied and it doesn't cost an arm and leg this would make a pretty nice Tablet computer.

And all this for around $500 to $800 :)  I can dream, can't I???
