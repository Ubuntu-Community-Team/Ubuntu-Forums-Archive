### Post by bro brian on 2010-02-21
[SIZE=2]  I finally went ahead, and installed Karmic. I grew tired of having to see the "update" button on the update manager window.
  During the beginning of the process, the upgrade did warn me that unless I had enabled the "Community-maintained" software, I would lose some of the applications, so I cancelled the upgrade for the moment, and enabled the Community-maintained software by doing the following:
System>Administration>Software Sources
  While in "Software Sources" (in the "Ubuntu Software" section), I just put a "check mark" in all the boxes, and hit the "Close" tab [/SIZE][SIZE=2](located at the bottom)[/SIZE][SIZE=2]. Then, I proceeded again with the upgrade.

  When the upgrade was finished, I didn't have any sound coming out (seems to be a common problem for many who have done the upgrade -** at least people using System 76 computers**). The "fix" for this is found here:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1306773&page=5](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1306773&page=5)

  I think the "sound problem" was about the only thing I ran into. Everything showed up. All my files were still where I had them, and everything worked fine. I did put a music dvd into the drive to see if it would play, and it did - however - using the "Movie Player" application, I had no sound. So I tried it using the "VLC media player", and I had sound (I don't remember if this was a problem before the upgrade to Karmic or not).

  I am happy with the Karmic 9.10 so far, and am glad I took the plunge - even though Jaunty was working well for me. There was a few things I wanted to do that I may have had more difficulties attempting to do had I not upgraded - like an attempt to install the KDE desktop in addition to the Gnome desktop ( KDE desktop topic found here:[ http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1404775&highlight=KDE+desktop+Karmic]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1404775&highlight=KDE+desktop+Karmic") )

Hope I didn't bore anyone with my lengthy reply! Bro Bri
