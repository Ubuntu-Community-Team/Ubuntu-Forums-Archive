### Post by psylem on 2015-01-15
It's not a happy story, but It's all relative. I want to share my story here so far because all the System76 hate I'm seeing around the place is a bit out of proportion. Hopefully I don't live to eat my words ;)

I decided to take the risk of getting a Bonobo shipped to Australia to replace my Macbook Pro which had recently been rendered an expensive paperweight after the graphics card died just out of warranty. You think anyone's going to shed a tear for me about that? You don't get to see many of those stories, they're pretty easy to express as a "me too" post on someone elses thread for starters, otherwise they just get drowned out through noise of the fanbois.

I was determined to find a big screen, powerful laptop with a life expectancy of more than just a couple of years this time. Looking around, System76 has a small number of damning reviews on their support. However, if you look at them carefully, the issues are minor compared to some of the horror stories I heard from friends trying to deal with big players. Response from Apple for instance to one of my friends who ordered a Macbook Pro that never powered on, they refused to replace it, instead sending them a refurbished one (despite the fact that's illegal under Australian law). Apple would have received a small fee/fine or something from the government that they probably don't even pay via some tax loophole scam.

This time I wanted something that didn't have a 'self destruct as soon as warranty expires' chip in it. The only vendors that I trust to provide reliable support and never try to mess you about in my experience are Dell and HP (within warranty). I also wanted to actually own my computer again, which meant getting off OSX and Windows. A vendor actually being cool with me doing my own hardware upgrades is unheard of. We have very few vendors that do Linux laptops in Australia, Dell keep making false promises that never make it to our shores. Mention Linux to anyone and the conversation ends, even with the small vendors. Laptops often have non-standard functions and devices in them that I wasn't prepared to risk buying something that I wasn't certain would work. Looking at the specs for what HP and Dell had on offer, I pretty much had to go for a goofy looking Alienware laptop which would have blown my budget and just been way more powerful than what I need. Bonobo on the other hand was far more flexible with the specs I wanted and budget I had in mind. I could have gotten something a little cheaper from a local vendor considering the import taxes, and perhaps the support risk for hardware faults would have been less, but like I said before I have no idea what the Linux support would have been like. I also wouldn't have been able to touch the hardware without breaking my warranty. I got the impression from System76 that if something did go wrong, say a hard disk failed, that I could explain the situation and just post back the parts that needed replacing instead of sending the whole laptop for a trip around the world (meaning I could swap something in to keep me going in the interim). Hopefully I don't have to test that theory, but I'm prepared to take that risk. Under Australian consumer law, System76 would have to cover the cost of freight anyway, so it would be win/win (assuming they respect Australian law, kind of hard to enforce unless you actually have a presence here).

All this stuff I took into consideration before I decided that even in the worst case scenario System76 can't possible burn me as bad as I could get from the other available options. On the scale of vendors that have customers best interests at heart, System76 is well over on the opposite end from Apple, that's pretty clear to me. Also, the Bonobo reviews that almost turned me off the purchase I've got to say were a bit off the mark. Maybe the internal fans in the older model used to be noisy, but these ones make far less noise than any of the other laptops I've owned. I don't even notice them unless it's a hot day and the aircon is off. The touchpad is indeed useless though.
