### Post by alphaamanitin on 2014-03-26
Hello Everyone,

So a little more than a year ago I posted a thread o here about my panp9.  I mentioned some issues, like the crappy power button.  For those who asked if it was on AC power when I had a difficult time turning it on, yes it was.  

Now, to my 1.25 year experience with a panp9.  I have had zero issues with the device that prevented the use of the machine. Until last week.  True my SD card reader never worked, but not a big enough issue for me to care.  I already had a USB adapter for a computer that did not have an SD reader.  It does feel cheap and made of thin plastic, but I never had any issues.  I always use the computer on a zallman laptop cooler that has two fans and a aluminum stage for the computer to sit on.  I put 16 gigs of ram in it with the i7 and would run dna sequence alignment and tree building programs as well as a virtual machine of windows 7.

The thing has been a workhorse for me.  I was a bit concerned at first, but it has really done very well for me.  

However, finally last week the power button just decided to stop working.  I am pretty sure it is the power button as well because if you could hit it just right it would turn on and work fine.  It is a real pain in the ass right now, to the point that it isn't even worth trying to use.  Luckily, a coworkers friend was just going to toss a macbook pro that had a cracked screen and had a soda spilled on it make the touchpad not work and the shift, caps, and command keys stuck.  I saw lucky because I took it apart and cleaned everything up with 100% ethanol and it works perfectly.  The cracked screen cannot even be seen unless you are sitting at a severe angle to the monitor.  I still want to get the panp9 back though.  I am searching for parts.

Also, I finally found some helpful guides for taking it apart and the like.  I don't know about others, but my panp9 is a Clevo model W25AEU laptop.  Here is a link to a website with instructions to disassemble the computer.

[http://www.pugetsystems.com/labs/support-hardware/Puget-B550i-Component-Replacement-Guide-466/](http://www.pugetsystems.com/labs/support-hardware/Puget-B550i-Component-Replacement-Guide-466/)

I am still searching for a place to buy parts.  One of the most annoying, and in my opinion stupid, things that system76 does is force you to have an account and tie your device to the account before they will even discuss issues.  Well, I don't want to talk to you guys and I don't want to open a god damn service thread.  I just want to buy a freaking part.
