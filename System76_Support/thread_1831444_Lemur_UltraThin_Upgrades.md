### Post by isantop on 2011-08-25
Thanks for your questions!

> 1. I'm going to upgrade the RAM. Can I do 204 pin 800mhz up to 6GB?

Shouldn't be a problem!

> 2. Can I swap the CPU from an i3 to an i5?

Since the processor is soldered onto the motherboard, it can't be replaced.

> 3. I want to replace the HDD with an SSD. I know what to do here.

Not an issue at all.

> 4. I noticed under the battery that there is a sim card slot. Does that work is there a 3G radio in it? If I got a data plan with t-Mobile or something?

No, there isn't a 3G radio in it.

### Post by drewbenn on 2011-08-26
> **szale9001 said:**
> This is good to know. However, I read somewhere that the RAM slot is underneath the keyboard. I only saw one screw underneath that pertained to the keyboard and was having trouble figuring out how to remove it. What is the proper procedure for gaining access to the additional memory slot?

I have a lemu2, and I'm not positive this is the right manual, but it worked for me to get at the WiFi card (which is next to or above the RAM): [http://www.clevo.com.tw/en/e-services/download/USRManualOut.asp?model=S3100/S3100M/S3101](http://www.clevo.com.tw/en/e-services/download/USRManualOut.asp?model=S3100/S3100M/S3101)   I took out that one screw you mentioned and then pushed in 4 tabs above the top row of keys (F1, F5, F9, PrtScr); there's sort of a trick to pushing the tabs in (I used a flathead screwdriver with a slightly larger bit than the phillips I used to pull out the screw on the bottom) so that the keyboard pops out from each tab.  You should be able to see the tabs if you have good light and look for them at the right angle.  If you don't see them, you must have a different model and you shouldn't follow my instructions :).
