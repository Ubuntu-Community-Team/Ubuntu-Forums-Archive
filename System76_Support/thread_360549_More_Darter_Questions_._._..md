### Post by jml on 2007-02-13
I know this is stating the obvious, but it really depends on how you plan to use it, and your needs and preferences.  I've used laptops as my primary computer for about five years now and I do have some definite opinions.  ;) 

First its intended use, If you plan to use this primarily as a portable device away from desk, power outlets and network connection, then battery life becomes important.  Darter is rated at 4.5 hours, but expect to get about 4 on average.  The Gazelle is rated at three, so expect about 2.5 hours on average.  Size, if you plan to carry this thing around airports for business travels, then size really does matter and here the lighter and smaller the better.  I find it a pain to carry full size laptops around places like airports etc.  

If you plan to use the computer primarily at a desk like I do for my work unit, then the 13 inch screen may be a bit small for day to day use and the Gazelles 14 inch screen would be a better choice.

Third, if you plan to at some point move away from Ubuntu and try other distros, you may run into wireless issues.  The Darter does not come with a PCMCIA card slot, but a more recent PCI Express card slot.  This is not a criticism, most manufacturers are adopting this slot.  Problem is, there are very few cards that support this slot yet and virtually none for Linux.  So if you have problems getting wireless to work with the internal wireless card you will not be able to fall back on a PCMCIA wireless card.  The Gazelle still has a PCMCIA card slot.

Finally, durability, by all accounts System 76 sells very reliable and sturdy products, but as a rule of thumb, small light weight computers may tend to be a little less durable than a slightly larger model, all other things being equal.  Now I've used small computers for years and have not had anything break, but I tend to be very careful handling them.  So your mileage may vary.  As I said, just my opinion.

### Post by Paranoid Randroid on 2007-02-13
> **MarkID said:**
> So, are you saying that the Darter is a little sharper, while the Gazelle is a bit less sharp, but a little bit bigger?

Yes, I suppose that's a good way to put it. The Gazelle has a bigger screen, but what's displayed doesn't have any more information in it. It's like digital zoom on a camera. You won't have any more room to, say, arrange windows on the screen.

> Ah, but why -- except for things like price and the webcam -- is everything overwhelmingly in the Darter's favor?Well, that was a little hasty on my part, I guess.

Here are some things that are in the Darter's favor:

* Lighter
* Suspend/hibernate guaranteed to work (I don't think that's been officially confirmed for the Gazelle)
* Smaller overall volume
* the aforementioned PCI express slot
* considerably more battery life
* bluetooth

And in the Gazelle's favor:

* Slightly thinner
* The ability to buy a dedicated Nvidia card (but it destroys the price differential)
* The ability to use half a gig more memory
* webcam
* Lower price
* One more usb port (4 vs 3)

The bluetooth and battery life alone would be enough to shove me in the direction of the Darter; lack of suspend would be sufficient to keep me from buying any notebook.

edit: by volume, I mean the amount of three dimensional space the thing takes up, not its loudness.

### Post by cleentrax on 2007-02-13
Another couple of things in the Gazelles favor:

1. The availability of a DVD-R drive
2. 2gig of matched RAM. The latest RAM configurations work better with matched sticks, and the Darter can't have matched RAM sticks at higher than 1gig. This is especially a killer because of the need to share RAM for the Intel 950 graphics. I am a fan of the 950 graphics (for heat and battery life) but they do require extra RAM.

I try to use a laptop for at least three years, so I try to future-proof my purchase. Right now that means core-2 duo at (at least a T7200 for the extra cache), 2 gigs of RAM and the ability to do 802.11n wireless.
