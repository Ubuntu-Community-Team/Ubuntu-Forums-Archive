No problem.  Just go ahead.  The meta-package mentioned is already  installed and the update is just one of the needed parts of the meta-package that has been updated, not the meta itself.  A meta-package is simply a small wrapper, a bit like a script, telling the system what actual packages to install or update to fulfill the meta-package requirements.
