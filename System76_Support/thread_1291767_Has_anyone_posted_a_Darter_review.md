### Post by jml on 2009-10-15
I have had a DARU3 shortly after it started shipping.  I have not written a formal review, but I will be happy to share some thoughts.

Positives:  Compact, light weight, very well built.  Everything I have tried has worked out of the box.  Wireless is very reliable.  System speed is very good even with the least expensive processor.  Screen resolution and image quality is very good.  Sound quality is acceptable, about what you would expect from a compact 12 inch laptop.  Sound through headphones is flawless.  Have not tested the fingerprint reader, Express Card slot, or 802.11n functionality.

Negatives:  The glossy black plastic housing is a fingerprint magnet.  In fact, my Darter was shipped with a hot pink, microfiber mit to clean away the smudges.  It works well, however, I don't bring it out in public.  ;-)   The Glossy screen is a bit problematic.  I prefer a mat screen, but one is not offered.  It makes viewing the screen in very bright light challenging, but not impossible.  The final quibble is battery life.  With my Darter set at the default power settings as shipped from the factory I get a little over two hours of battery life with "average" use.  I bought a second battery for it and opted for the larger 8 cell battery which does improve battery life as expected.  The problem is the added weight and the fact that this battery raises the back of the laptop by nearly an inch.  Not a deal killer for me, but it does detract from what I consider to be a very attractively designed laptop.

Final message:  I would buy it again if I had to.  I am very satisfied with it. When I need long battery life and light weight, I take my Starling along in place of my Darter.

Joe

### Post by Murrquan on 2009-10-21
I bought a Darter of the latest model last year at the after-Thanksgiving sales, so it's been out at least 11 months. Anyway, theZoid's pretty much dead-on. It's very pretty, but there are lots and lots of fingerprints involved. And he got a hot pink microfiber mitt too? Heh.

I just wanted to add that it runs hot sometimes, especially if you use Compiz / Docky or watch Flash videos or play certain games or do just about anything requiring graphics performance. The fan can be pretty loud, and it scales for no apparent reason sometimes. There's a button to set it to "silent mode," but if you do this for too long while running it hot it *will* disengage and the fan will go into overdrive.

I worry about heat issues with it. But, I haven't had any after almost a year now, and the overall feel of the unit is solid and well-made. I'm kind of fond of it.
