### Post by zitch on 2011-07-05
It's likely the lens on the CD Drive picked up some dust or something (like oil from a finger rubbing across it) that affected the laser or the reader from reading CDs and DVDs cleanly. I'd suspect using a soft brush (or a 'Q-Tip') and a small amount of Isopropyl Alcohol might have been all you needed.  

I "restored" an old XBOX 360 similarly (Friend gave it to me after it stopped reading his discs and bought a new one), though that I had disassemble it to get to the lens. Brush with a small bit of Isopropyl Alcohol, let it dry, and put the unit back together and it's reading discs perfectly fine now and I got a free XBOX 360 ;).

At least with laptops with pop-out CD trays, the lens is exposed right there (Just have to turn off the system to clean it safely), though I'd suspect slot-loaded drives would be a real pain to work with.
