### Post by amasiar on 2010-02-04
I had my new pangolin for few days, and would like to post few thoughts for those that are still on the fence. I got few upgrades from the default configuration, so keep that in mind.

First of all, it's a speed boat! Four cores, i7 and its fast fast fast. I can have a windows vm running and I won't even know. I tried to really tie it up, and only bottleneck would be the hard drive speed. Somehow the combination of linux and i7 chipset, everything just seems so snappy! It's definitely the fastest computer I've ever owned. 

The oled screen is just superb. It's bright and clear. Unfortunately the external monitor will support only up to whatever your highest resolution is ... so if you have a huge 22" display like I do, it will go up to only whatever your laptop resolution is. 

As far as the hardware goes, it's really top notch. The case is well built out of some hard plastic, the keyboard has just the right amount of click. It reminds me one of those fancy Apples actually. 

Overall, it's 10 out of 10. I have no qualms about the hardware whatsoever, and I'm you will be satisfied. As far as software goes, well - it's open source, and Ubuntu, so that equals "awesome"

### Post by Lee_Machine on 2010-02-12
Sorry to hear about your laptop delay mookiemeister. Chinese New Year is celebrated here in Japan also, and people get days off. 

For your questions...

1) Ubuntu Linux is the best to start from, and one I keep as my main Linux distro. I try and leave, but I just keep coming back. 

Some good books are The Linux Bible, and I remember [this]("http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ubuntu-Linux-Fourth-Thomas/dp/1430219998/ref=dp_cp_ob_b_title_2") being a good book. 

I find that distro specific books tend to get outdated fast, so I would stick to a generic Linux book. Like the Linux Bible. 

2) Device support is great for things like scanners, and printers. Especially with HP devices as they are one of the top CUPS (Common Unix Printing Service) contributers. So most HP printers will work out of the box. I would recommend installing the hplip, and hplip-gui package. It provides extra features, and an easy to use GUI. I recently bought a new wireless HP all-in-one printer, and it works great. 

[HERE]("http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/downloads.html") is the official HP website about hplip. 1,952 printers are supported :)

Also the camera should work too, they usually mount as a flashdrive. If not, memory card adapters work too.
