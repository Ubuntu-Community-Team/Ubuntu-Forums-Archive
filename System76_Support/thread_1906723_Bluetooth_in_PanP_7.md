### Post by isantop on 2012-01-10
I don't think we sold any PanP7s without Bluetooth. My guess is that the module is turned off. Press Fn+F12 first, then you should see a bluetooth icon in the panel. 

As a note, the BIOS in the PanP7 is configured to turn the bluetooth adapter off at boot to save power. You'll want to turn it on every time you start up. You won't need to re-pair the headphones every time, though, so it will be as simple as pressing Fn+F12.
