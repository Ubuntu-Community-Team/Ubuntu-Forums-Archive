### Post by bakelitedoorbell on 2010-07-22
I have some experience with trying to get an analog video encoder working on my Ubuntu laptop.  My advice would be to stay away from MythTV unless you want to set up a dedicated media PC.  For just watching TV on your laptop there are smaller and easier programs available, such as "Me TV" and "TVtime Television Viewer".

My tuner didn't work with these because its analog, and the TV programs in the repos all seem to require a digital tuner.

You can get a tuner with a built-in hardware encoder, which is more expensive, but it uses less of your computer resources.  The tuners without an encoder will depend on your computer to process the signal, so they'll bog down your system, but they are cheaper.

Make sure the tuner you get has a linux driver.  There are some drivers that are built right into the linux kernel, so you won't have to compile them.  Check the USB section of this page:
[http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Tuner_Card#USB_Capture_Cards](http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/Tuner_Card#USB_Capture_Cards)

Some possibles:

Hauppauge WinTV-HVR-950Q - software encoder, will use your CPU to do software encoding ($80 at Newegg)

Hauppauge 1192 WinTV HVR-1950 - has a hardware MPEG-2 encoder that allows your PC to run at full speed while recording ($130 on Amazon)
