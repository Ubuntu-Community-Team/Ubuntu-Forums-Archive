### Post by watchpocket on 2010-01-05
[I posted this in Absolute Beginner Talk with no responses yet, so thought I 'd try here also] 

Three or four times now in the last few weeks, while in my text-editor, vim, I've pressed <CTRL>-F (which moves you forward in your document by one page), and suddenly the gnome calculator pops up. 
 
A second later, without my having done anything else, it pops up again, and again, and again, ad infinitum, the status bar at bottom filling up with ever-smaller calculator icons.

I do have a setting in calculator for <CTRL>-F to bring up "financial" calculator, but this still shouldn't happen even when I have the desktop (instead of vim) in focus. (Rather, it should happen only once.)

Suggestions?

---

### Post by thomasaaron on 2010-01-06
Go to System > Prefs > Keyboard Shortcuts and change the "Launch Calculator" shortcut to something other than "Ctrl-F".
