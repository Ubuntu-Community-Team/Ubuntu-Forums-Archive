### Post by HhtFBFn on 2016-04-14
This is on a brand-new Lemur. I'm keeping my large hands well clear of the trackpad while typing. But the cursor is visibly jumping while I type, usually by small increments but sometimes larger, and sometimes clicking on things. So I find myself typing in the wrong place, or refocused on the wrong window, or even having a window suddenly maximized.

I see through Google that various laptops of different makes have had this problem in the past. My own experience, largely with ASUS laptops, has not included this problem. There's a thread in this forum from 6 years ago suggesting turning off the trackpad every time you type. That wouldn't work for me, and I'm often both typing and moving between tasks, pasting things between them. Is this a common problem with the Lemur? Is the design of the hardware inherently compromised, or do I just have a faulty trackpad on mine?

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-04-16
This is not uncommon in light laptops with 13.3 & 14.1 screens.    Almost all brands are affected - possible excluding Thinkpads.   (I had 3 laptops with this issue to a greater or lesser degree).

For me, it's not problem just tapping the Function Key + the F1 key - - takes a second to toggle on/off.  If I'm doing a lot of typing I've also learned to use  hot keys, tab key, etc. to traverse through various applications (and a small wireless mouse as needed).
