### Post by isantop on 2011-11-03
The price and specs are exactly why they don't really compare against each other. It's like comparing a Sports Car to a Family Car, or a Tablet to a Netbook. They all have very different goals in mind.

That said, some people do need or want that kind of power. There are games available for Ubuntu (Warsow, Flightgear, etc) that will puch the capabilities of even the Bonobo, if configured right. some people run heavy computations. Others might want to do heavy virtualization. It all depends on the use case. I wouldn't recommend a Bonobo to someone who only wants to browse the web, read email, and watch movies. For that usage, the Lemur or Pangolin will be just fine. But more power will always perform better and smoother, applications will load faster, and they'll be more responsive in use. It's hard to describe until you've tried it.

The same argument can be applied to someone who wants to buy a fast car. With speed limits, it's unlikely that they'l ever be able to reach the top speed off of a track. But that won't stop the person from buying it. If you don't see the point of the power, then I'd recommend a less powerful, less expensive model. Other customers have different needs and wants, and it's impolite to tell them they're wrong.
