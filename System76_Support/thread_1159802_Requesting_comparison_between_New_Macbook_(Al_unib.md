### Post by bill516 on 2009-05-15
Leemachine is right, this is essentially about applications, but let me make a few quick points.

I am running a new Pangolin and I have a family of ardent Mac people with multiple machines.  I have spent time on Macbooks, in particular.

1.  The Pangolin is far more cost-effective.  Ubuntu as done by System 76 is a true turn-key system.  Repositories are very well stocked.  You cannot get Excel, of course, which means you cannot do pivot tables.  If those are essential you can either get the Mac or install XP on the Pangolin (dual boot) so that Excel is there when you need it.  Open Office will do most of everything else M-soft Office does.

If you program you want a Linux machine, get the Pangolin.  Tools are far less costly and very good.

2.  Pangolin runs very well.  Fan noise can be louder than a Mac, but not objectionable at all.  Keyboard is stiffer than the Mac so a "touch adjustment" probably will be necessary. Might take you two weeks.  Touch-pad is by Synaptics (on my machine) and it works very well.  Having used both Mac and Pangolin, I rate them equally with regard to navigation.  Both machines run Skype easily if you do that.

OS-X deserves the praise it receives.  But customization of Ubuntu far exceeds that of any Mac.  Buy a Mac and you do it their way.

3.  Pangolin battery life between charges is much inferior to the Mac.  If this matters you need the Mac or a different S76 machine.  Figure the Pangolin for 1:45 to 2 hours between charges.

4.  Re battery I believe battery replacement on the Mac is no simple or inexpensive matter.  Apple really thinks you will trash the machine before you replace the battery.  This is to assume the battery has no problems.  Meanwhile battery replacement on Pangolin is simple and they are available for a bit over $120 last time I checked web prices.

5.  Mac styling is seductive to be sure, but it does no work in the end.

6.  Be very careful to compare carefully with fully optioned Mac against comparably optioned S76.  Add options and Macbooks get expensive in a hurry.  I wound up looking at a nearly $1,700 Mac against a roughly $900 Pangolin.  Guess who won that argument?

7.  Build quality?  All I can report is that my Mac family said "Make sure you get the extended warranty, you may need it."  The Mac slot-CD drive can be a problem, apparently.

My take?  Apple Mac is the ultimate "computer as appliance."  You turn it on it works.  And in stylish circles you get points - for which you have paid handsomely.

Pangolin (take a look at the Serval if you have Mac money!) comes very close to being similarly turn-key.  S-76/Ubuntu is not a geek system unless you feel like geeking.  Turn it on and do your work.

Problem:  You get no style points.  Get ready for "You bought a what from who?"

Just grin.
