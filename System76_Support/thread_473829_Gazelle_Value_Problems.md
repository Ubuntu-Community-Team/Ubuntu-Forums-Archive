### Post by sxkorean on 2007-06-14
Ordered: 05/03/2007
Delivered: 05/10/2007

Before purchasing my laptop I looked around for a laptop that would work with Linux and I found System76. After reading through their site I looked for modem support, because I only have dial-up where I live and I'd need it if I wanted to do anything. I searched through the knowledge base and found this page, which led me to believe the on-board modem could be made to work.

[http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Laptop_Modem_%28sl-modem%29_on_Ubuntu_Breezy](http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Laptop_Modem_%28sl-modem%29_on_Ubuntu_Breezy).

My laptop was shipped quickly, and when I took it out there were minor cosmetic flaws that I was willing to overlook. It wasn't worth the shipping, it was a graduation gift from my parents, and it was still functional. The top of the laptop where it said "System76" was peeling at the edges. Some of the other stickers were, too.

After checking to see if it turned on, etc., it was put into storage [it was a graduation gift]. 

After I graduated [yay!] I took my slick black laptop to school to use their internet to get all the packages and libraries I needed. I followed the directions at the above link using the sl-modem-daemon package. Things started acting up. A lot of things, and I didn't know why.
Speakers became distorted, sound would sometimes just not work at all.

I called Tech Support and they were more than patient. A nice man attempted to help me fix my mysterious problems, but in the end I had to do a fresh install. I went to school again for another hour of apt-get and everything was working fine - except the modem. I started looking around for a solution. I found quite a few but none of them worked, and I was trying to be cautious (I'm pretty new, I don't want to mess around with kernel modules if I don't know what I'm doing).

I called tech support again and told them what I had found. I had experiences a similar problem on my desktop, but I decided to buy an external serial modem instead. Because my serial modem won't work with my laptop, I thought it'd be nice to have my modem working. The same guy told me there was a lot to read, and he'd look into it. I didn't have a chance to call him the next day, but I shot an email to [email]support@system76.com[/email] and he emailed me back saying that the "solution" in the knowledge base wrecks ALSA. Well, that explains a lot, heh, but I still didn't have a modem.

I went to CompUSA and bought a Serial to USB adapter, but the modem would connect to the ISP and then log off after sending a password, for some reason. It didn't show the final part in the log of gnome-ppp where it shows the assigned IPs.  Considering the fact that I'm moving soon I was going to let it go. I wasn't going to need dial-up where I was moving to, so I thought everything would be ok once I left.

There was only one more problem. After playing a grueling pool match I shutdown the laptop and put it in it's case. I unplugged the AC adapter [it was running on AC power before] and put everything away. Less than five minutes later I take it out to charge it and no light turns on for the battery icon. I pressed the power button and nothing happened. I looked at the adapter and the light was on. I looked at the metal-tipped end and tested it - it was working. The outlet and both parts of the adapter was working, but the laptop was not.

I emailed system76.com to ask about an exchange. The representative I spoke with in the tech support had told me there is usually a restocking fee. I was slightly disappointed with the laptop, and even more so that I'd be paying to give it back. The email has gone unanswered since it was sent a few days ago. I'd like to be able to use a System76 laptop, as I had read a lot of glowing reviews and had read quite a bit about how they had a great support staff.

- Drew.

P.S.:
I'm currently on my desktop, using Ubuntu. I've also used Zenwalk and DSL distros. I'm not completely new, but I'm not very experienced.
