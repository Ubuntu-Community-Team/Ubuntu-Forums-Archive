### Post by miniyak on 2009-09-27
the physical speakers themselves are capable of being very loud. In my observation this has just been a matter of what type of media your listening to. For example compare a .wav file at full volume, as compared to and mp3 at full volume. Depending on how the files were recorded and transcoded, naturally they will have different volume levels. Unfortunately this difference sometimes means that you can't put the pangolin's Master or PCM volume control high enough to compensate for something like the lower volume levels of a DVD. (try watching a movie with a wide sound range like the matrix... prepare to pull your hair out)

may be there is a way to adjust file volume levels in real time, but i have yet to here of it. (you can transcode to higher levels, but that is kind of tedious)

just to be sure, you have also put the PCM option to max in volume control correct? (the option is not present by default, click preferences in volume control)
