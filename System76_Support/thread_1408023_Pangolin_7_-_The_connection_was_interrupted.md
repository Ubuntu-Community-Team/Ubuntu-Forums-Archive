### Post by thomasaaron on 2010-02-16
```
Perhaps something screwy with the LAN driver?
```

Probably not. The LAN driver is provided by Ubuntu, and I've seen precious few problems with it... and definitely not *that* problem on any of our computers. 

So, since it's only *your* Pangolin on that *particular* router, that rules out your Pangolin (providing you've tested it on other routers).

("But none of my other computers on this router do it!")

Yes, I know. But routers have a number of ways they can fail, and this is one of them. It could be the port. It could be the firmware. Or it could just be old (how old is it?). 

You can try resetting the router. That sometimes helps.
