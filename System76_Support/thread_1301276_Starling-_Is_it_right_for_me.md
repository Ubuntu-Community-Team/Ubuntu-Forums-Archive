### Post by jml on 2009-10-26
hillarymichelle, I would recommend that you buy one.  I have purchased three laptops over the years from this company and I have never been disappointed.  The company offers great customer support.  I have had a Starling for several months.  I have found it very well built.  Runs very well, powerful enough for what I need it to do.  Price is very competitive.  Judging by what you list as the tasks you want to accomplish, I thing that you will be very satisfied with it.

Going over your needs:

1.  Battery life is about 4.5 hours running the Starling with the factory setting unchanged.  I could squeeze more time out if I wanted by dimming the screen and turning off the wireless when I did not need it.  But quite frankly, 4.5 hours is enough for me.

2.  Note taking, will be very easily accomplished by either OpenOffice.org, or gedit, ( a text editor) both installed with Ubuntu.   If you have special requirements, you have the entire Ubuntu repository to choose from.

3.  Social networking, e-mail and other web based needs will be easily handled by the applications that come with the Starling from System 76.

4.  Memory, this will definitely not be a problem for your needs.  It comes with 2 gig. of RAM and a 150 gig hard drive.  This should be more than enough for your needs.

Hope this is of help.

Joe

### Post by Rayve on 2009-10-26
I'm actually using my new Starling right now at a coffee shop - just got it Wednesday, specifically for internet and homework, so far it's been great.  Some small adjustments to get the wireless working, but now it's working perfectly.  I have had no issues with battery life, I get a full four hours or more, it is light weight, and heat isn't a problem.  It's plenty fast.  I was worried about using a netbook because it looked so small, but I can type just as well on my Starling as I do on a large desktop keyboard.

And of course, support from System76 and other users here on the forums is amazing.  Shipping and packaging was even fast (the only things I changed were an external cd/dvd drive and an upgraded support package), so overall, I have been nothing but pleased.  :)

If you order through System76, and do get a Starling, I suspect you'll be just as happy.
