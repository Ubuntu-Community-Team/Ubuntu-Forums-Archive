Inspired by your experience I downloaded Gutsy 64-bit beta. I am using DARU2 and can happily report that at least at the moment (I am using the LIVE-CD):

1) Suspend works and goes to s3 sleep with the default setting. It wakes up from this mode too.  This almost alone is worth for me to go to 64 bit. Under 32-bit Feisty DARU2 just sleeps S1 sleep.
2) Wireless works
3) Battery monitor seems to be working without any fixes. This was also the case when I booted into Mandriva 2008 RC2 live-cd (32-bit).

Bad news:
1) Screen Brightness keys don't work. They bring the right "image" into the screen but using those keys brightness does not go beyond very dim level. Brightness applet allows for adjusting above that level.
2) I can't change screen resolution at the moment.
