### Post by isantop on 2011-09-19
I'm not particularly familiar with current RTS titles, since I don't dedicate much of my time to Gaming (and, admittedly, Minecraft won my heart for when I do). However, I can say with a high degree of certainty that the Srval, when properly specd, is the fastest 15.6" laptop on the market. If you need a laptop to do it, the Serval can. In their stock configurations, pretty uch any of the professional line models will be able to run pretty much any game. You may not be able to pull out all the stops and run every game with completely full graphics settings, but you won't be stuck on the lowest settings either.

As to the memory question, with multiple VMs, you'll want to add as much RAM as you can afford; the amount of RAM will have a bigger effect on the performance than the speed will. If you have to choose between 8GB of 1600 MHz RAM or 12GB of 1333 MHz RAM, you will want to go with the 12.

If you have doubts about the weight, then I'd choose the Serval. It feels lighter than it is, and is actually decently portable for a laptop with that much power.

The i7's are quad core, and for VMs, all the parallelism you can get is a good thing. Having more cores won't hurt the gaming aspect of things either.

We're seeing SSDs that last as long as traditional hard drives at this point. The only real downside to SSDs are price. They are still very expensive given what you pay.
