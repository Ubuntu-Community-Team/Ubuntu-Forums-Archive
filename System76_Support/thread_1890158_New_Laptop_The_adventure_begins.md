### Post by Dragonbite on 2011-12-05
I am seeing a number of people running Gnome-shell in Ubuntu (11.10) and the instructions look pretty clean.  

In Ubuntu 11.04 Unity and Gnome-shell are not happy co-inhabitants but that changed in 11.10.  Going forward, who knows, because Unity and Gnome-shell may move further and further apart, but that's in the future.

To install Gnome-shell ```
sudo apt-get install gnome-shell
```

Then when you go to log in, click the little "cog" and select "Gnome" (as opposed to "Ubuntu" which will do Unity IIRC).

I got the quickie information from here : [_How To Install Gnome 3 Shell in Ubuntu 11.10_]("http://techhamlet.com/2011/10/install-gnome-shell-in-ubuntu-11-10/"), which was from October.

### Post by jaimeaux on 2012-01-21
Alright, so... Initial review of the laptop.
 
Aesthetics: 10. This thing is friggin' gorgeous! I've had it for a little while now (new years eve) and it's rather good at staying clean, despite it's glossy exterior. 
 
Keyboard: 8. IMHO this is the smoothest keyboard I've ever worked with. I'm giving it an 8, because of it's resemblance to a Macintosh keyboard, and the fact that my wife deems it "Clacky," but I find it to be smooth, and easy to work with. Minimal flex (but not overly unmoveable), responsive, and good key placement.
 
Screen: 8. The screen leaves a little to be desired, mostly due to size. Also, the glossy screen is naturally less clear than a matte screen. 
 
Touchpad: 7. This thing drove me nuts until I could figure it out. I didn't realize that it used two-finger scrolling (nor did I understand the idea behind two-finger scrolling); thus, viewing webpages was tricky.
 
Speed: 10. For a computer with only 2G of ram, this thing is BLAZING fast. I plan to upgrade to 8G of ram shortly. I blame the i5 quadcore 2.4 Ghz processor. AMAZING!
 
Sound: 7. The speakers on this computer are the obvious weak point. I'm not sure why they decided to put the speakers on the bottom of the computer, but they did and I have to live with it. Seems fine for movies though, as well as music. Headphones recommended though.
 
 
 
All things considered, this computer is a great buy. Everything is responsive, and works great. 
 
Bonus: The computer not only survived my home-life, it also has survived a trip to Afghanistan! I'll keep updates coming on it's survival. No injuries yet!
