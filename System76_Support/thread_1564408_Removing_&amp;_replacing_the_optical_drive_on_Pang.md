### Post by Eldera on 2010-08-30
You can verify your model by going to System>Administration>System 76 Driver>System Information Tab. I have a Panp4. It fell from a footstool unto the floor about an 18" drop. This damaged the Cd/DVD drive in such a manner that I could not close it. This happened when I was first using the pangolin. Tom Aaron was kind enough to get on the phone and talk me through removing the drive. He had me take off the larger panel on the bottom of the computer so I could see where the screws lined up. The drive was at the other end, not under the panel. There was one screw in the middle of the bottom of the computer to loosen and the drive slid right out. Tom told me to look for two screws about a half in apart from each other but my computer only had one. 

I had a replacement drive ready to put in but when I tried to push it in I discovered that the case had also been bent in the fall. I could not seat the new drive into the connectors. I eventually bought a USB external Cd/DVD drive and use that.

Warning if you have a panp5, it might be different.

Have a good day, Eldera

The screw, or screws, goes into a little metal tongue. Take the screws out and the drive will slide out the end of the computer.

### Post by isantop on 2010-09-01
Not too much to add here.

The screw you need to loosen is actually accessible without removing any panels. Its labeled M2X8 and is on the bottom of the computer, just to the side of center. It's located between the label on the bottom and the edge of the large, main access panel. Simply remove it and your drive should come right out.

If you need a replacement drive, I can recommend a Toshiba Samsung TS-L633. It's  the model we have in shop, so I'm pretty sure it'll  be compatible. Though in theory, any slimline SATA drive should work.

You'll need to remove the bezel on the front of the original DVD drive to cover the new one. It should clip on.
