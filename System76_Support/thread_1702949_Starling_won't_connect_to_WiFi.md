### Post by EJJ on 2011-03-08
Try deleting all the "Auto-" network settings from the edit connections. Then try to reconnect. I had no wireless for nearly two weeks and on a whim just deleted my 'Home' router from the auto setting and then reconnected to it. Also, just double check that the card is on-Fn-F11-it has been reported that the networks are visible even when the card is off.

If none of this works, try connecting to Wifi after booting from a live disc and let us know how that goes. If you can't connect from a live disc boot, it could be a hardware issue. However, I tried booting from a live disc when I had the wireless issue and still couldn't connect. Everything was solved after I cleared the saved connections. Haven't had ANY connection issues since.

If you could also connect to a physical ethernet source and download any potential updates that might be beneficial.
