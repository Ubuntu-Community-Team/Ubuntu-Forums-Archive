### Post by patjennings on 2011-01-25
Just got a new lemur ultrathin, after reinstalling a 32 bit version of 10.10 desktop edition and installing updates and all of the applications that I wanted. I installed the system 76 drivers, then went to the restore tab and pressed restore. After I rebooted the computer my entire top panel was missing no menus or bars of any type left. The only thing on my desktop is docky with my terminal and firefox icons. How do I get my menus back I dont have the patience to start from scratch.

### Post by khamil8686 on 2011-01-25
try pressing alt+f2 and typing gnome-panel and pressing enter, if you restart and the panels are missing again just hit alt+f2 and run gnome-panel again and then add gnome-panel to your startup files :) hope this helps!

---

### Post by patjennings on 2011-01-26
I figured out why I lost my panel, After doing some research on all the linux forums I found someone who had the same problem as me. It turns out I lost my panel because i uninstalled evolution from synaptics and it is somehow connected to gnome panel. I ended up doing a fresh install of 10.10 and kept evolution . Everything is back to normal. thanks for the help.
