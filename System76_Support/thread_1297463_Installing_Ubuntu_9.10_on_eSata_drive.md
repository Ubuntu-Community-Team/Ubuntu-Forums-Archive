### Post by vttay03 on 2009-10-21
I don't have a PanP5 so I can't comment on the eSata port on that particular machine.  However, one of my Ubuntu disks is an eSata drive on my main desktop PC.  It works like a dream, just as fast as my internally mounted Sata drives.  The only thing I will point out (which you probably already have figured out) is that you'll need some separate way of powering it.  I power mine through a USB hub, but you could also buy a little wall wart if you'd rather do it that way.
