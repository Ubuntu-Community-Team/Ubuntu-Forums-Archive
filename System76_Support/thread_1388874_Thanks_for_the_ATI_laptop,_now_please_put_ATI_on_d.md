### Post by thomasaaron on 2010-01-25
> Please offer ATI card on your desktops (it should be much easier there as you can just pop in another card). Nvidious has been a foe of Linux and FOSS for a long time, and it's time we start boycotting them!

That's funny. Five months ago, **ALL** of our desktops were ATI, and we received unending encouragement to go **BACK** to nVidia (primarily because configuring ATI was prohibitively difficult). So, we're back to nVidia, which has a much more usable UI.

While we HAVE noticed that ATI cards do provide a little more bang for the buck performance-wise, we've had to put about the same level of technical support work into both of them to work around various issues. And we've not seen fixes from ATI be more forthcoming than fixes for nVidia on our particular machines.

In the end, we've found that our customer base just wants their systems to work. So we don't really engage a lot in nVidia vs. ATI, or AMD vs. Intel debates.

We always have ATI and AMD on our radar. And when ATI gets their act together with their UI, and AMD develops a partner program that is even HALF as good as Intel's, we'll certainly be taking a closer look.
