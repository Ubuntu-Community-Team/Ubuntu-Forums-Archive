### Post by jml on 2010-01-23
The Starling has a 6 cell battery that give about 4.5 hours of use at the factory default settings.  With a little tweaking, you should be able to squeeze a bit more time out of it.

I do not know what chip set is in the Starling, but that chip set seems to be the one weak point in the hardware configuration.  When originally shipped, the wireless had very short range.  That was fixed with a driver update.  Then the upgrade to 9.10 broke wireless completely.  That has been fixed with the most recent update.  My experience is that Linux support for the Starling's wireless card has been spotty over many distros both .deb based and RPM based.  At least the ones I tested.

I don't know about the scaling issue.  Hopefully Tom from System 76 will read your post and answer the questions I can't.

I don't believe System 76 does custom distro installs, but I will let Tom answer that question as well.  

Joe
