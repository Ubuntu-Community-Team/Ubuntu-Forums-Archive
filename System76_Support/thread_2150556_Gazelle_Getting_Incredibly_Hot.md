### Post by Shrek01 on 2013-06-01
Nothing like a good vacuum cleaner, to the rescue!  You don't even have to open your laptop.   Just went for the main ventilator and the HD one. I had the same problem with mine and the overheating stopped.  I plugged my vacuum on the CPU ventilator grid and the HD one.  Of course I did this while the laptop was unplugged, turned off, and at my own risk :D  It did the trick for me. I have to do it every 6 months or so, and that fan doesn't go wild anyomre.

### Post by chez17 on 2013-06-02
So I did end up opening the bottom and taking off the fans on the advice of a friend. There was a layer of dust covering the vent. I got rid of it and everything is back to normal. Thanks guys.
