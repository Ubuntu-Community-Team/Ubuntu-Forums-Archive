### Post by jaylittle on 2012-12-16
Buying a System 76 machine was one of the best purchasing decisions I've made in years.  I purchased a panp9 on 5/1 and I've totally fallen in love with it.  I just purchased my second System 76 machine, a bonx6 and my wife will be inheriting the panp9 once it arrives.  System 76 has taken what used to be a pipe dream for me (switching to Linux entirely) and made it a reality.  Even though I'm not an Ubuntu user, other distributions benefit from the work they do when it comes to sourcing the appropriate components and modifying the BIOS/EFI portions of the machine for maximum compatibility.  Because of that, buying a System 76 machine takes the guesswork out of trying to figure out just how compatible with Linux the machine in question actually is.  As my past experience has taught, you never really know how compatible an OEM machine is with Linux until you install it and try it out for yourself.  Except with System 76.

### Post by Ubun2to on 2012-12-17
High quality and great support.
Support picks up fast on the phone and replies quickly online. And they actually support Ubuntu and provide drivers-they do not just pick out hardware that requires no extra drivers and abandon it.
I bought a Lemu4, and I was not let down-the only problem I had was a problem fixed by a kernel update.
They may not support Windows, but fixing Windows is easy enough (if virus scans do not fix problem, format & reinstall), and they will still support the hardware, regardless of the OS you use (you can install Gentoo, MS-DOS, FreeBSD, and even leave it without an OS and they will support the hardware).
