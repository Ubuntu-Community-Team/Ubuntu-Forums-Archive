### Post by samalex on 2010-04-30
ubuntu-10.04-desktop-amd64.iso is the correct file.  I've heard a few reasons why 'AMD64' is used even though it applies to both AMD and Intel 64-bit processors, but I'm not sure which is accurate.  But yeah, regardless of whether you have an Intel or AMD 64-bit processor, amd64 will work.

Update: I found some info on why the naming convention is what it is:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X86-64#Industry_naming_conventions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X86-64#Industry_naming_conventions)

### Post by bill516 on 2010-04-30
I own a Pangolin and my wife recently purchased an HP laptop with Windows 7, so let me respond to hedgeborn.

An inexpensive laptop such as an HP or Dell running Windows 7 is an alternative.  But do bear in mind the downsides of that.  On-line support does not begin to compare with the work done everyday by System 76.  You will not have a true Windows 7 installation disk unless you purchase one.  What you get is the vendor's "set up" or "rescue" disk which is not at all the same thing.

Now in fairness Dottie's machine works quite well for her.  She likes it.  But I have to say I find the Windows 7 interface on her machine to be terribly cluttered when I compare it with my Pangolin.  I am also reminded that she has to put up with the AV software I no longer need.  Just one man's opinion but I see nothing there that makes me want to run out and purchase such a thing.

Finally let me point out that I work my laptop heavily doing the various things it sounds to me as though you want to do, hedgeborn.  I have a Windows XP desktop in this room and I cannot remember the last time I had to resort to it for anything beyond Microsoft Flight Simulator.

Last point:  If you need Windows software there is a good chance that something like WINE might run it.

For the record I do flight simulation with X-Plane, a considerable amount of photography and editing, a good bit of writing and spread-sheeting and I watch an occasional movie when I am traveling.  Flash is essential and I have no trouble with it at all in my Firefox browser.

Getting Ubuntu multi-media ready is perhaps a 15 minute exercise using the medibuntu repository.  It just is not hard at all.

My current machine is a PanP4 running a 64 bit Ubuntu 9.10 installation I'll upgrade in 2 to 4 weeks.

Given your description I would be very surprised if one of the current Pangolins would not work very nicely for you.

Good luck!

### Post by Futurian on 2010-05-06
A year ago I replaced my MacBook Pro with a Pangolin panp5 with a 64 bit Ubuntu distribution, since upgraded to Karmic Koala 9.10. I use the Pangolin as my primary machine. I am glued to it for hours a day. I love it.

Sometimes I have to work on Windows XP machines for reasons beyond my control. I find the experience quite irritating now. Every windows machine I control has Ubuntu on it (put there by yours truly), either as a dual boot, or in VirtualBox. When I have to work on those machines, I use Ubuntu whenever I can.

Let me relay one experience with Linux. I have a multi-function (print, scan, copy) device at home. I had used it only as a printer, but then suddenly needed to scan something in a hurry. The printer vendor's documentation said to start by pushing the scan button on the scanner after installing the vendor's scanning software into Windows. I followed the instructions, but nothing happened; no scan. After working on the problem in Windows for a while, without success, I switched to Linux, and again followed the vendor's instructions. Still nothing.

I was close to giving up, even though I really needed that scan, and quickly. I did a quick search in these Ubuntu forums. Someone had posted an answer to a similar problem, something like "Use XSANE". I installed XSANE (trivial through the "Ubuntu Software Center" on the Applications menu). XSANE came up, but offered me a lot of options I didn't want. Now completely ready to give up, I noticed a button, "SCAN", on one of the XSANE windows. One click, and the scanner instantly came to life, for the first time ever. A few seconds later, I was looking at the scanned image I needed.

The Linux community rocks.
