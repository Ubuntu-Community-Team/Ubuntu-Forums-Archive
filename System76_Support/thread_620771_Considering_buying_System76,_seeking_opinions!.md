### Post by octathlon on 2007-11-23
Wow, I can't answer most of your questions, but I can address some, as someone who bought one of the "early" new Pangolin Values (before they had the big silver thing on the back or the webcam & nvidia options).

1. Lid: Mine had no sticker.  The lid surface is slightly indented with a raised "frame" (bevel?) around the edge and a small circle in the center.  It looks perfectly fine with nothing on it.  (There was supposed to be a System76 logo on it, but they were out of them at the time; they are supposed to be sending me one later).

2. Keyboard/Touchpad:  I think that is a subjective thing.  Overall I find the keyboard comfortable to use.  A couple things I don't like are that the arrow keys are a little smaller and more scrunched together, so more difficult to hit accurately than on other keyboards.  The Del key is in a strange (to me) place at the upper right.  But I got used to these things fairly quickly.  Home, PgUp, PgDn, End keys are conveniently placed vertically along the right edge.  Touchpad:  I don't really like using the touchpads on any laptops; this one seems about like most others.  I usually use a USB trackball.

3. Screen:  I love the display.  It is very clear, crisp and easy on my eyes.  But, I usually use this at home under regular lighting.  I noticed when I took it to the office that under the fluorescent lights there was much more glare.

4. quality/hinges/solidity:  Everything feels solid including the lid stiffness and hinges. The surface surrounding the keyboard is plastic even though it looks like metal.  So far everything has held up very well.  Of course I'm pretty gentle with my hardware compared to some people. :)

5.  Built-in mike - I just tried it out once and it worked but did not sound as good as a regular plug-in mic.

Hope this feedback helps a little.  I also found the System76 prices to be very competitive with other companies and I consider the System76 support to be worth a lot too.

### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-11-23
I have a Gazelle Value which I purchased in February. It's a few versions back, so some things have changed, but I'll answer to the best of my ability.

> **Mr. Picklesworth said:**
> 
Those big brushed metal stickers on the Gazelle and Pangolin Value notebooks; are they cleanly removable?...
Can't speak to that, as mine came with a paperish sticker that had to be re-secured with packing tape after about a month.

I find it to be as tolerable as any laptop keyboard for typing. My only major objection is the lack of a num-pad, but I bought a USB one for about $10.
