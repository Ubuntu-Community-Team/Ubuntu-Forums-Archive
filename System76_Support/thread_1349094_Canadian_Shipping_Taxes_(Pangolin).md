### Post by thomasaaron on 2010-03-08
> Canadian shipments ship via UPS Express service. Generally shipments are delivered within 2 business days of shipment. UPS will ship your computer to the nearest Canadian customs office. Once customs clears the package, you will be contacted for delivery authorization to your residence or place of business.

UPS Express service includes shipment fees, insurance fees, **and carrier brokerage fees (excluding any GST brokerage fee)**. System76, Inc. does not charge taxes for orders being shipped to Canada. Customers will be billed Canadian Harmony Tax and any Provincial Tax C.O.D. when you order arrives at the customs office. To determine the duties owed, if any, you can contact your customs office with both the description of your order and its total price.


Hope that helps.

### Post by nobodysbusiness on 2010-03-29
Well, I've been using my Pangolin for a while now, and I have a few other thoughts. I've been really happy with it, overall. It's super fast and Ubuntu is great (obviously). And I apparently didn't even take as much of a hit as I was expecting on the taxes/shipping/etc... I am very happy with the purchase and would buy it again.

Anyway, I just want to clarify that although I'm going to spend the rest of the post complaining, that doesn't mean that I'm unhappy with the laptop. This is just what a review should be about, so future buyers will know what to expect.

I now feel that it would have been nice to have the opportunity to buy a 9-cell battery instead of the default 6-cell. The battery life is (as others have stated) about 1.5 to 2 hours. This is fine most of the time, but sometimes it would be nice to have a little more battery life. Unfortunately, based on other threads, there isn't a 9-cell available, even from a third party. I'm going to look into trying to find some efficiency-boosting software, though.

Another minor issue that I have relates to multimedia keys. The Pangolin only has three buttons up at the top-left, which can be a bit annoying because I got used to using the keys on my old Dell computer for Play/Pause, Mute, and so on.

The last issue that I can think of is that the little light for capslock is on the front of the computer where it is usually hidden by my wrist. This can sometimes result in confusion about whether or not capslock is enabled.

(Yes, I realize that I'm nit-picking. But when it comes to criticism, it's the best that I can do. Good job, System 76!)

Edit: Wait! I just remembered one more little thing that some people may want to know about! The "Windows Key" on the keyboard has the little windows logo (presumably) covered up with an Ubuntu logo. This is good. However, the Ubuntu logo is printed on a sticker that is stuck onto the Windows Key, rather than it being a key printed the same way as the rest of the keys on the keyboard. This leads me to have some concerns about the Ubuntu logo rubbing off with enough use.

Edit again: I've decided to set the "Mail" key to Play/Pause and the "Web" key to Mute, which helps to nullify one of my criticisms a bit. I just noticed, the little "M" key (the rightmost of those three) doesn't seem to do anything. Is this normal?

Edit yet again: There may be a fingerprint reader problem going on, but they're [working on it]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1427493"). And one more thing (hopefully the last). The screen tilts backwards only a little less than 45 degrees from the vertical and then stops. This isn't really an issue, unless you're sitting on the toilet and have the laptop on the floor in front of you.
