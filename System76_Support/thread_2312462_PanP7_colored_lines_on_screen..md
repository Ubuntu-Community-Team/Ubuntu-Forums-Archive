### Post by re1d on 2016-02-11
Tank Jr - see my other thread, but it turns out the 4570 in the Pan P7 is not replaceable. ATI apparently sold it as a discrete board that plugged in, or as an integrated MB component. I understand that in the Pan P7 it's integrated.

They have offered send a quote for replacing the MB - not sure whether I'll do it. This has been a great computer, and still has lots of life left in it - even at almost 6 yrs old, it's faster & more responsive than a friend's new Win10 box - but we'll see what the damage is for the MB replacement first! I've used it primarily as a desktop replacement, so if it's too much for the MB I may just replace it with a Meerkat. 

Good luck!
