I wouldn't worry about they contacts, they'll be fine.

I don't know about a dead battery, but I leave a live one in mine all the time.

I have a Daru2 that's over 2 1/2 years old & the battery still gets over 3 hours, I leave it plugged in all the time.

Lithium Ion batteries are very different from the old nicads.
If the charging circuit, which is in the battery, not the laptop, is working properly it won't overcharge the battery.
If its working properly the battery should stay cool unless it's charging or discharging.

What is really bad for lithium ion batteries is heat and repeated deep discharge.
They will also lose some capacity over time whether they're in the computer on on the shelf.

Some folks say you should never leave the batteries in if it's on AC, and they might be right, but from technical reading I've done and my experience with them, I tend to disagree.
I like the protection I get when the lights flicker, they make a great UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply).
