### Post by kend650 on 2011-07-27
I received my System76 Bonobo Professional laptop earlier than expected.  That was much appreciated, as my old Sony Vaio (running ubuntu 10.10) was dying (display).  I spent the next week migrating my data from the old and dying laptop (when I could get the display to work).  After three weeks of working and using the laptop, I am pleased with the results.

I do have the following concerns:

1)  The fingerprint sensor does not work.  But per your comments at [http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Fingerprint_Reader_Usage](http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Fingerprint_Reader_Usage) I will wait for notification from System76 that this issue is resolved.  Can I assume you will notifiy me when this issue is resolved?

2)  I tried to resolve an issue with my mp3 player (Sansa Clip+).  The desktop would not recognize it.  At the time, it was connected through a 7 port external hub (USB DUB-H7).  After several days of frustration, I connected the Sansa directly to the laptop, and everything worked fine.  So my issue is with which hubs work properly with this laptop.

A secondary issue along these lines is how to identify which ports are USB 3.0 and which are 2.0.  This would be identified in the User Manual, which the above link states is being worked on.  Again, can I assume you will notifiy me when the User Manual is available?

3)  My next issue is how to detect wireless networks.  I've had the laptop in internet cafes and the only wireless network it shows is my home network.  I'm not sure how to resolve this one.  I've installed the Wicd application as an interim (final?) solution.

4)  There is no file search option.  When I open Places > Search for files... a dialog box opens with no place to enter file names or any other search criteria.  I am currently using the find command in a text window.

5)  Unchecking the "Disable touchpad while typing" in the Touchpad tab of the Mouse Preferences has no effects.  I am constantly touching the touchpad and sending the cursor elsewhere while typing.  Obviously, this is very annoying, and I would think that unchecking this box would disable the touchpad from moving the cursor while I am typing, but any time my thumbs get near the touchpad, the cursor goes scurring to another window, or another line of my current document.  I would be very much appreciative if System76 could fix this.

6) I was connecting wirelessly to my dlink router with a pcmcia air card (verizon).  I am going to be gone for a couple of months, and want to take this laptop with me, but there is no pcmcia slot, so I upgraded the aircard to a UMW190 usb.  It didn't work out of the box on ubuntu, but after installing verizon access manager on my xp machine and getting the aircard activated properly, I popped it out of the xp machine and into the System76 machine and it worked perfectly.  I am impressed!!  Now I have to upgrade my router when I get back to allow the rest of my lan to work properly.

All in all I am very happy with this new laptop and I can work around these existing issues.

Ken

### Post by shebaw on 2011-07-28
Yes USB 3.0 cables are blue instead of white/black in the inside. It is to make it easy to differentiate the USB 3.0 ports from the USB 2.0 ports. See [this]("http://www.trustedreviews.com/opinions/usb-3-0-what-is-it-and-do-you-want-it") for more information.
