### Post by sportscliche on 2012-02-11
I've been running Arch on my home desktop and laptop for about a year and a half.  I still have my critical work computer on Xubuntu and have no plans to change that.

With Arch you are a power user, entirely responsible for the setup and maintenance of your system.  You have to be careful...it requires time and attention. A simple pacman -Syu can get you into trouble.  The benefit is that you can make an ultra-powerful, high-performance system that does exactly what you want.  

At the job, I don't have the time for that.  I need my box to just work, with minimal intervention from me outside the usual updates.  Sure there may be some performance compromise and I won't have instant access to the latest kernel and all the software package upgrades.  But I don't really care.  I just want it to work reliably and Xubuntu does that very well.
