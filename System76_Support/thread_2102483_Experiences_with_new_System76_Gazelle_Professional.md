### Post by arendjr on 2013-01-07
Past Friday I received the Gazelle Professional laptop from System76 with Ubuntu 12.10 preinstalled. It is a really great laptop, and I am very satisfied with my purchase and the service from System76...

But there are two glitches that are spoiling a little bit of the fun.

First, I'm coming from a MacBook Pro as my previous laptop. It's a really great laptop too, even though it's more than 4 years old by now. As such, you might imagine I got used to the "Natural Scrolling" that is default in OS X in recent versions. In short, Natural Scrolling reverses the scroll direction for mice and touch pads from the old default (and the Windows default), so that it matches the swipe direction on touch screens. Being the owner of not just a MacBook, but also an iPad and iPhone, I can appreciate a consistent scrolling experience very much.

Because of this, on my Gazelle Professional, the scroll direction feels backwards, and I immediately went into the System Settings to reverse the scrolling direction. (I also got a Linux workstation at work, with OpenSUSE and the KDE desktop, where this option is available by default). Soon I was disappointed to find this option is not available in Ubuntu, though it is when I install the Ubuntu Tweak utility. So I installed the Ubuntu Tweak utility and reversed the scroll direction, only to find it doesn't work in certain applications. It works fine in Chrome, in LibreOffice and in KDE applications, but it doesn't work in GTK3 applications like the file manager, System Settings and others.

I have already searched for bug reports related to this problem and found several:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/nautilus/+bug/951123](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/nautilus/+bug/951123)
[https://bugzilla.gnome.org/show_bug.cgi?id=674716](https://bugzilla.gnome.org/show_bug.cgi?id=674716)
[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu-tweak/+bug/995748](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu-tweak/+bug/995748)

Unfortunately, it appears that noone (other than the affected users) is taking these bug reports very seriously.

As such, I would really like to stress how unfortunate this bug is. It was the very first setting I wanted to change when I started my laptop, and it failed.

The second glitch I encountered is less severe, but still annoying nonetheless. I'm a really big fan of the Amarok music player, but somehow it seems to inherit some really ugly colors from the default Unity theme. The center panels are slightly pink/salmon. But no matter what or where I try, I cannot seem to change these colors. Changing the Unity theme doesn't change the colors, installing KDE System Settings and changing themes doesn't change these colors. I simply cannot figure out how to get rid of these pale pink panels in Amarok. Fortunately, the application is for my listening pleasure, and not so much to look at :)

Other than these, I'm really happy with my new laptop! :)

Cheers!
Arend jr.
