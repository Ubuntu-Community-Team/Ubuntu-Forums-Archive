I posted this elsewhere, but I thought I'd share here too.

I just bought the [Buffalo 1.5TB]("http://www.buffalotech.com/products/external-storage/drivestation/drivestation-usb-30-hd-hxu3/") USB 3.0 external HD at a local electronic store, along with the PCI-e adapter.


The drive was just over $99, and the card $20. 

Sadly, the 3.0 card does not work with Ubuntu 9.10, but works well with Windows 7, and I'm sure Linux support will be soon. 

Even when used as a 2.0 device on Ubuntu, I am getting in between 50-60 Mbps write speed.

According to Gizmodo, the drive will cost $250 at U.S. retail, so I would get it from a Chinese online store :P
