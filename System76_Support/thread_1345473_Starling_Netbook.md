### Post by hoodwink on 2009-12-12
> **mysteriousdarren said:**
> I was wondering what people thought of the Starling Netbook. I've had my computer for a while and was considering adding a smaller computer to my collection. Thanks for the advice.

I can recommend the product with few reservations.

1. The keyboard is just large enough for comfortable typing.

2.  If you stick with the pre-installed netbook remix, everything just works, but if you try any upgrades, your wireless will go dead, and you'll need to use ndiswrapper (search for a reported procedure). I recommend that inexperienced users stick with remix until Sytem76/Ubuntu have sorted out the problems. It appears to be kernel related.

3. Like many laptops, the builtin touchpad is a POS. You can't type without the cursor wandering off. I've got to find my travel mouse. Also, the system settings option to tune the touchpad seems not to work.

4. I've installed sidux (a Debian unstable distro that I've used for years on my desktop system), and the wireless works just fine out of the box. The Livecd boots and activates wireless with no problems. The Livecd uses a later kernel 2.6.31, and after intitial updates it goes to 2.6.32. As a plus, sidux does not use Network Manager (I despise it) for wireless management. NM s much improved recently, but I'm happiest without it.

5. I'm generally happy recommending Ubuntu to new users, but I don't really prefer it for personal use. I've tried the xubuntu-ubuntu-kubuntu flavors, and they're just not my cup of tea. I'm currently using an xfce4 desktop.

Enjoy your Starling. It's a great product.

According to what Thomas Aaron of System76 has posted on this forum, if you are running Karmic on the Starling, then you probably will be having problems with the wireless. System76 is working on solving that problem and has recommended Starling owners not upgrade to Karmic until the fix is completed. If you are running Jaunty and are using the proper System76 driver, you shouldn't be having problems with your wireless.

To turn the touchpad off on the Starling, go to the "Preferences" section and click on the "Mouse" icon. Then click on the "Touchpad" tab and uncheck the "Enable touchpad" box.

I use a mouse with my Starling and found that my typing experience was vastly improved once I turned off the touchpad -- no more inadvertent brushes of the touchpad.
