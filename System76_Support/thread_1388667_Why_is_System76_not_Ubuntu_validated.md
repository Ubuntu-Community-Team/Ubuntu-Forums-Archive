### Post by jml on 2010-01-24
A certification process is meant to reassure a future customer that the computer that they are interested in buying will work with Linux/Ubuntu.  I would guess that since System 76 has built it's business model on only selling computers with Ubuntu installed, and since they have demonstrated their commitment to both Ubuntu and their customer base over the years, they do not have to go through the process and expense  of certification to reassure their customers.  In my opinion, certification would be useful if I were going to buy a computer from a company that did not officially support Linux.
