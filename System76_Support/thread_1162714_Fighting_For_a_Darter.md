### Post by riseringseeker on 2009-05-18
> **AlexInBlack said:**
> After copious amounts of research on a laptop to take to college, I've settled on the Darter.

My parents, who will be the ones paying, are strenuously against my choice.

Being a parent myself, I understand their concerns, especially when they are paying the bills.  

No one reading your post can divine the situation that you are in though. If they are open to argument, I'll throw my 2 cents in, if no, as the first reply stated, it's over.

> Their reasons:
1. As a non-mainstream company, system76 will not provide adequate support.

I doubt if you will find anyone who has used the support that System76 gives who will agree with that.  The support they give is nothing short of amazing.  

Many of the problems that I have had were of my own making, but Tom patiently and expertly went through each of them with me, and seemed happy to do so.  

I have only had a couple of problems with System76 products, but they mostly were not the fault of System76, but of bugs within Ubuntu with certain hardware combinations.  The fixes were sometimes from Ubuntu development, other times from the System76 driver writers.  Tom always found at least a work-around for the bugs that were not immediately fixable.

> 2. As a non-mainstream company, system76 will have a large probability of failing during this recession and thus deprive me of long term warranty/support (I'm probably going to go for the 3-year warranty, and they've said that this laptop will be long term)

Size alone does not guarantee longevity, if it did, a lot large banks, insurance companies, brokerage houses and others would not have failed in the last 12 months.  Being small *can* equal being nimble and being able to respond more quickly to market conditions, which would make them *more* likely to survive, not less.

(I have no idea of System76's current financial health, but size alone is not a good indicator, unless the Federal government decides they are "too big to fail" - which is another thread entirely, and not one for this forum.)

> 3. A 12 inch screen may be inadequate for required reading/pictures

I have a Darter, and have to disagree.  I find it quite adequate for everything I do, but of course, that may not apply in your case.

> 4. A 12 inch screen will cause eye strain

It hasn't in my case, but as they say, YMMV (Your Mileage May Vary).

> My reasons:
1. I don't run any memory intensive applications, so smaller is the way to go

Smaller and lighter is why I went with the Darter - having a small laptop to tote around the world is much nicer than a big/heavy one.

> 2. I've checked out laptops with 12 inch screens for a half an hour at a store and watched a classmate play starcraft on one and found the screens satisfactory

I have used the Darter quite often and have never found the screen size to be a problem

> 3. system76 seems to have much better support and a warranty than the mainstream companies (don't all of the major players have their warranties voided if you install another os?)

Can't speak for what other companies do, but having had an HP, I know they were unwilling to talk to me once I could not click on the start icon.  Again, System76 provides awesome support.

> 4. system76 has reported 61% growth since 2008 1st quarter (my dad says the original volume may be miniscule and the growth rate thus deceptive)

I know they are growing fast, but didn't know it was that fast!

> I've presented my case to them without much result. In particular about system76's stability as a company and the screen size's comfort, does anyone have any data that I could look at?

I don't have any hard data, and doubt that System76 is willing to release numbers more than they have publicly already.  I do know they provide a quality product backed by better support than you are likely to find anywhere else.  

Just so you know, other than being a loyal customer (both a white Darter and a Wild Dog desktop machine), I have no affiliation with System76 whatsoever.  I will sing their praises though, given the amount of time that Tom has worked with me to solve mostly self-inflicted problems.

In the end, it's your parents money being spent, and unless they are willing to reconsider, you should still be happy they are willing to buy you a laptop at all, regardless of what it is.

### Post by perce on 2009-05-18
I have no idea how healthy System76 is financially, but here is an argument against them failing because of the crisis: they are a company who focuses on a niche market, rather than relying on mass sales, moreover heir typical customer is probably an above average person both for wealth and education, who looks for a specific product which is still not very common to find in the mass market. 

I can tell you that their software support is far better than what could you expect from any mainstream company, but I can't tell about the hardware support because my Darter has never failed. 

The only thing you should be careful about is that their products come without a Windows licence, so if you discover that you need one, you'll have to pay for it on top of the computer price.
