### Post by flukeairwalker on 2009-05-12
To answer some of your questions:

1) Windows drivers are available for download on the System76 wiki in each model's page. They are available for XP and Vista.
2) I'll let someone who uses Kubuntu answer from experience.
3) I tried running a few other distros on my Pangolin but in the end I stuck with Ubuntu. It's the only distro that the System76 driver fetching program works on and it makes a big difference. System76 optimizes and tests their computers to work with Ubuntu so I would recommend you stay on the safe side and stick with it.
Personally I like the 6 month release cycles. I've only tried Hardy, Intrepid, and Jaunty, but with each new one I have seen remarkable improvements in support and stability. But I'm just along for the ride. I don't mind the adventure in upgrading twice a year. Your mileage may vary.

### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-05-12
> 2) Does the System76 driver support Kubuntu? 

We do not currently install (or officially support) Kubuntu. However, we have a large number of customers who do use it. While we will always try to help you out in whatever way we can, the best support for Kubuntu currently comes from this forum...

The System76 Driver (which supplies model-specific patches to our computers) will run on Kubuntu and generally fixes basic issues like sound, microphone and fingerprint reader support, etc... However, since we do no testing with Kubuntu, we can't guarantee that everything will work perfectly.
