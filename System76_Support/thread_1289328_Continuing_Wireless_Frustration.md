### Post by jmwink on 2009-10-12
Since receiving my gazu1 this summer, I have been immensely pleased with basically everything about it, and really applaud System 76 for making my transition from proprietary to open-source operating systems smooth, painless, and for making me feel comfortable about the risks that switch entailed.

I can't help but notice, however, how unreliable my wireless networking seems to be compared to my friends' and partner's connections. I work in a major research university in the United States with a fairly accomplished IT staff, and an extensive and well-maintained wireless network. The amount of times I am bumped from the network compared to others with whom I'm working, or the length of time it takes me to connect vs. others, or the quality/speed of connection, etc. makes me wonder if there isn't a something larger than random chance operating here. I find the same issues whether working with my home network, or on semi-public networks in coffeehouses, or other places away from either my home office or my University office. 

Thus far, I've given System 76 the benefit of the doubt, but I must admit I find myself a little bit frustrated about the persistence of this issue, and my own ignorance as to how to accurately troubleshoot the issue. Within the last two months I have experienced the problems described above (relative to someone else with a 5 yr. old laptop running Windows XP) on three specific networks, each with different brands of routers, and at different ranges from the router, etc. I have a spare USB wireless networking adapter; would testing that give me any better explanatory leverage on the problem?

From previous forum posts here, I understand that folks at System 76 have not encountered similar problems, and seem to lean on the argument that particular combinations of hardware at particular distances to different brands of routers, perhaps on different wireless channels will explain most of the variance in stability and performance. These variables are not providing tremendously robust explanation of my particular case, and I wonder what other variables could be controlled in order to better get a handle and a solution.

I should note, however, that at the moment, given the length of this post, I have been experiencing pretty stable, but slow connectivity.
