### Post by wyliecoyoteuk on 2009-11-17
It is a common anti-fraud measure.
The address of your first order will need to be the same as the billing address of the credit card, or they will refuse the order without further proof of identity.

Using a credit or debit card to ship to an unproven address nullifies the seller's fraud insurance, as it is a common scam.

Because I think you are supposed to call your credit card company before you place the order to add that address as an alternate shipping address. As mentioned above, it is an anti-fraud measure. 
 
Then, when system76 calls your credit company to verify that the different shipping address is, in fact, associated with your account, there shouldn't be a problem. Otherwise, for all they know, someone could have stolen your credit card number to use it to buy a computer and ship it somewhere else.
 
Not sure how much of a delay that creates, though.
