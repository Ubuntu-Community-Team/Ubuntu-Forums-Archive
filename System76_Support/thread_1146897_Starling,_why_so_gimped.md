### Post by jml on 2009-05-22
I have owned a netbook since the original ASUS EeePC 704 came out.  I have also owned an ASUS 900 16G (with the notoriously slow SSD and battery discharge problem,) a 900 HA with 160  Gig hard drive.  All were reliable, but the small keyboard has always been a problem.  My Starling seems to me to be the perfect compromise of size performance and price.

One caution when considering a netbook with an SSD is to research what the read/write speed is.  This information is often hard to get from the vendor. The faster drives tend to be much more expensive than the slower ones.  For example, I have read on several netbook forums that the ACER Aspire one, while very competitively priced, is shipped with a relatively slow SSD.  And my former experience with the ASSUS 900 16G was very painful waiting for the drive to catch up to my typing, (which is very slow by the way.)

While SSD's are theoretically more rugged than a traditional HDD, that does not mean that a netbook with an SSD is "impact resistant."  While an impact on an SSD equiped netbook may not damage the drive, other components may be at risk.  I have noticed significant variability in  the quality of the construction of netbooks from different manufacturers.  In short, my Starling seems to be a very ruggedly built device that has more than adequate performance for my need.
