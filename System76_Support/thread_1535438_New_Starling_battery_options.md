### Post by jbelmonte on 2010-07-22
Hi Greg,

The key part of isantop's response is:

> **isantop said:**
> The Star3 chassis comes with a 3-cell battery

When they order the barebones Starling (version 3) laptop from the supplier, it comes with a 3-cell battery. If S76 offered just a 6-cell version, they would have to order a separate 6-cell battery for it and they would be left with a spare 3-cell battery for each 6-cell version sold. And the cost to S76 of the Starling with a 6-cell battery will actually be the cost of a base Starling with a 3-cell battery plus a 6 cell battery, so I doubt S76 will be able to provide a discount for a 6-cell battery only version of the Starling. Personally, if I was going to be paying the same, I would prefer to get both the 3-cell and 6-cell batteries.

### Post by greghk on 2010-08-20
I got the Star3 with the Intel 40G SSD instead of the hard drive.
Weights are:
Chassis with no battery: 855g
(I left a few grams of packing material on the cover)
3 cell battery:          165g
Total                   1020g = 2.25lbs (higher than the 2.0 advertised weight)
6 cell battery:          315g
Total                   1170g - 2.58lbs

There is no sleave/case.

The 6 cell battery sticks out about an inch and down about a half inch from the chassis. Star3 dimensions with the 6 cell battery are width the same at approx 10.5in. Max height on a flat surf 1.375in. Depth on a flat surface 8.25in.
