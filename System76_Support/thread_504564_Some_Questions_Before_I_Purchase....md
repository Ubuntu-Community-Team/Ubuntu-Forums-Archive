### Post by thomasaaron on 2007-07-19
> 1a) From reading other posts, I gathered that I would have to have already purchased my own S-video or Composite cables to get it hooked up to my TV. That's true? No video cables of any sort come in the box? I don't have to buy a power cable for it also, do I?

It comes with the power cable. It does not come with video cables.

> 1b) Once I have the cables, do I need to do anything fancy to get the image to display properly on the TV? Can I just run the cable from the TV-out of the PC and to the input on the TV and be golden? (I realize there may be issues with setting up video card options or some such, but I was referring mostly to hardware concerns.)

There likely will be settings, but, other than cables, that should be about  it.

> 2) Does the Sable have any issues with running hot? Reason I ask is that the best spot in my current entertainment shelf is right next to the TV, in a cabinet-like area, where there wouldn't be tons of space around it for air to ventilate. There'd still be some space for warm air to escape, but it's a pretty tight fit. Last thing I wanted to do is get a brand new PC, put it in next to the TV, and then have it melt on me from overheating...

The Sable exhausts out the back and the left side (as you're looking at it head-on). As with ANY computer, you've got to account for ventilation. That said, I know of no overheating issues with the Sable, but, of course, that doesn't mean that it will survive any amount of abuse.
> 
3) Is the only difference between the Sable Value and the Sable Performance the dual-core processor? Looking at the system specs, that looked like the only difference.

Correct.
