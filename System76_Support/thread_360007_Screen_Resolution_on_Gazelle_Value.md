Wifi worked without a hitch, installing Beryl was a breeze, I was pleased to find a button that enabled and disabled the touch pad, but I've got a couple of bumps. The screen resolution is 1024x768, despite having a wide screen monitor. When I booted the computer, the screen was stretched. After I tested suspend, the screen is a square with black bars on the side.

I went to System -> Preferences -> Screen Resolution, and 1024x768 is the only option. I looked in xorg.conf and the screen section has several other options listed, but the only one that appears as available is 1024x768.

I've browsed the forums a bit, and it seems other Ubuntu users have had this problem, but I haven't found any clearly written solutions.

Any advice?
