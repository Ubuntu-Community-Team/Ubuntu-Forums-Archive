### Post by re1d on 2010-05-16
My Pangolin Performance arrived late last week and I promised some impressions for those who might be curious or considering buying one! So, here's a quick review:

_Battery Life_ – Might as well get the one dim spot out of the way first: the Pangolin has pretty poor battery life. Mine lasts about 90 minutes without trying any conservation measures, which has been well-covered in other posts so I knew what I was in for. I'm mainly using it as a desktop replacement so that's fine with me – in fact, after “conditioning” the battery with a number of complete full discharge/charge cycles, it is currently out of the computer. I'm keeping it out while using it at my home office desk, and will insert it every few weeks for another full cycle before taking it out again to keep it fresh. It's possible that several times a year I'll have to take the Pangolin along for some reason, and the battery will be ready for that eventuality. But, if your use pattern is going to have you on the road or in coffee shops a lot, this will be a negative for you.

_Touchpad/Buttons_ – Not exactly weak areas, but compared with some computers the touchpad is pretty basic. It works great and I love the touch and responsiveness but it only does traditional touchpad stuff – no gestures or anything. This is also something I knew from reading posts and reviews and doesn't bother me. In fact, using the computer at my desk it's plugged into a Unicomp keyboard with a pointing stick ('cause I'm not that crazy about mice, either – I like the stick). But if you like touchpads, you'll be OK with this one; it's better than most I've used. As for buttons, these are positive in feel but kind of small – they could have made the whole touchpad/button assembly bigger, or put the fingerprint reader somewhere else, or done something else to increase button size a bit. But they're OK.

_Screen_ – A thing of beauty! I got the HD+ and am glad I did. It's very shiny, and whether you like that is up to you; but if you don't you can get antiglare overlays that some folks like better than the stock antiglare screens. Personally, I like the clarity and brilliance of this screen – watching movies on it really makes them pop! Great screen.

_Keyboard_ – Everyone has their own preferences in this area, and as an old Thinkpad user I'm used to a good keyboard. The Pangolin has a pretty good one in my opinion. It sounds a little hollow to type on but the action is good and I find I type accurately on it. I wish the right shift key was bigger, but it's not been a problem. I did a lot of typing on it at first, to get used to it, and it wasn't hard. So the keyboard is not a big plus, but it's still a plus and not a minus.

_Ports_ – It's got 'em! I plan to use the eSATA for a backup storage drive. USB ports on both sides are handy (wish they were USB3!). Good port layout & variety.

_WiFi_ – Only used it for a couple of days to make sure it was configured for both home and office and working right. Seems to have good range, though I didn't test its limits. Fast hookup to the router when you start up. Seems smooth and quick.

_Speed_ – This thing flies! I invested a little extra in an i7-720QM and 6GB RAM. I'll be using a virtual machine to run Windows 7 occasionally – company software I can't run otherwise – so I thought the extra horsepower and memory would pay off. Also, my laptops have to last no less than 5 years, and I wanted this to be fairly future-proof. Haven't run any tests, but it's the fastest computer I've ever used – apps open fast and small tasks are accomplished immediately if not sooner. Even the Web seems faster. You won't be disappointed with the performance – and the price/performance ratio is unbeatable!

_Quality_ – This was important to me, as I need durability. I've been disappointed in Dell and Gateway, in particular, in the past. The Pangolin looks like a well-built machine to me, apart from some lid flex and the hollow-sounding keyboard. I'm not sure these are durability issues, as to go by the posts and reviews I read in researching this purchase, past Pangolins seem to have served very well in demanding use. Overall it seems strong and well made, with something of the understated quality I've liked in Thinkpads I've had.

_Appearance_ – Businesslike is the word that comes to mind. Nothing fancy, but certainly not cheap-looking or ugly. You'll never mistake it for a MacBook Pro, but it's a handsome machine in my book.

_Other Issues_ – None to speak of – some configuration was needed at first to fully enable DVD playback, and I had an update issue that was fundamentally my fault. But both those served to illustrate a great strength of the Pangolin, and all the System76 machines: their stellar support, both directly and through the excellent forums. You really feel you've joined a dynamic working community here, so no problem should go un-addressed. And if System76 stands behind the hardware in the same way (and reading some of the posts regarding hardware problems leads me to think they do) then you can't beat the support experience anywhere I've been. 

[I]My Pangolin: HD+ screen, i7-720QM, 6GB RAM, 320GB 7200 HDD, Intel WiFi Link 5300, extra AC adapter, 3-year warranty & support.:)
[/I]
