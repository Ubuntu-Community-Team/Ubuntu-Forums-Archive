### Post by roscoetuff on 2008-12-01
Finding that once Intrepid was out that the new 76 driver meant for Interpid resulted in polling issues for updating my Hardy... I upgraded and the "problem" was solved.

However... it caused me to wonder whether this hardware specific driver was more of a problem than a solution as it forced me to upgrade an otherwise stable system to keep from getting updating errors.

I'm not going to say that this was the right solution... but simply that it was one that left me thinking... "What the heck... upgrade." And after a few days... everything was back to normal. I upgraded an old Dell desktop on Hardy to Interpid and it bombed. So that got me back into trying new Distros... which creates an unfair comparison of a "clean" reformatted drive against the upgrade that failed by freezing the machine (to which no one replied with a solution)... but I can report OpenSuse11.0 is very nice and stable out of the box.. that pretty much does what you want with non-chocolate coloring (for those who care - and apparently some do). 

But the question is this: What do we get for the trouble of this System76 driver? I don't actually know. Upgrading brings issues... least of which is that the best approach is a clean re-install. Doing this every 6 months is more than I necessarily want to do with Ubuntu. And I can say that having tried Mint's version of Ubuntu... where everything you want to do is installed right out of the box rather than as an upgrade...that you have to figure out... Mint is just far superior!!! .... so I have to ask why bother with the driver? or for that matter with this sort of unfinished Ubuntu when a more polished version exists? 

And I ask this because I'm inclined to wipe the Ubuntu 8.10 off my Pangolin and put Mint on it. Thanks!
