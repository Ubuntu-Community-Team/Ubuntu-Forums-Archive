### Post by thomasaaron on 2010-08-13
> 1. There are 3 wireless cards offered and could use some guidance. Are they all fully and well supported by the linux kernel or do some require system76 drivers? Are there significant perfomance advantages to a particular choice (802N support aside)? After futzing about with the Starling drivers for some time -- dealing with wireless stopping working after an upgrade and a shortish range -- I'd like to make the most reliable choice. (I love the Starling, incidentally, despite that.)

The 6200 card is capable of 300 Mb/s, the 6300 is capable of 450 Mb/s. Either of these would be good choices for streaming video and such. I'm not exactly sure of the speed of the stock card... will have to research it.

All of the cards are reliable. The Starling wireless issue was sort of a one-off situation with a certain Realtek chipset, although most Realtek cards are pretty darn good.

> 2. A review indicated that the lemur runs somewhat hot. One thing I adore about the Starling is how cool it runs. Will a solid state drive make a significant difference in heat?

This is always a tough question to answer, because people that worry about laptop temps are *often* more sensitive to heat than most people. But not always. I don't have a way of actually quantifying the temperature. But from a support perspective, I can't really remember any complaints about the temperature, although we did have a few Lemurs that were overheating due to a flaky CPU fan. This has been remedied in the new Lemur.
