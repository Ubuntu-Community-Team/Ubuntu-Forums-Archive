### Post by isantop on 2010-09-24
Upgradeability depends on the system. Generally, the more powerful systems are more so than the more portable ones. 

In general, if we can configure a system in one way, you can upgrade it to those specs. For example, you can configure an upgraded processor on the Serval at purchase time, so you can upgrade it later. But, you can't configure an upgraded processor on the Lemur, so you can't upgrade that later on. (The Lemur and Starling processors are soldered to the motherboard)

### Post by PabloH on 2010-09-26
I have a Serval Pro. It is a desktop replacement. I do lug mine around to client sites and I do use it when giving presentations to my college students. I like the power of the i7 and the solid state disk as I run virtual machines and compile millions of lines of C++ and Java code. Otherwise, I would just use a less powerful laptop.

My two cents: I would say that the Serval is an exceptionally poor choice for a student. As I student I have used several laptops, and what a student needs is the opposite of the Serval:

You want a computer that can fit on the tiny student desks in most lecture rooms and still leave room for a textbook of moderate size next to it (i.e. a netbook). 

You want a computer that has a long battery life (Serval lasts an hour) so you can use it in class without plugging it in all the time. 

You want a computer where you can afford to buy an extra battery and charger to leave in the back of the room and swap out, if need be.

You want a computer with a small ac adapter that does not weight a ton and take up half a back pack. The Serval's is a large brick. 

Buying a laptop for school is useless if you can't use it when you need to. You should be taking all lecture notes on the laptop, it is much easier to type them than write them and you can quickly search them on the laptop. You want to use the laptop to replace most of the uses of paper and pen. If your purchase does not do that, it is a poor purchase.  

Look for a Starling or Lemur for portability and buy an easily expandable performance desktop to augment its use when you are home (if necessary).

For student use (even in Computer Science) there is little that a Lemur with plenty of RAM can't do for you, but that a Serval will. There are plenty of things a Lemur can do for a student, that a Serval will never accomplish.
