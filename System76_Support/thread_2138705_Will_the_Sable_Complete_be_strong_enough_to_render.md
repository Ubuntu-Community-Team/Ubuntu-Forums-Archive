### Post by Deer Hunter on 2013-04-29
Jackalope, it's your money to spend, but I think you're going to find you've spent too much and gotten excessive power than you'll use. Do you seriously know what kinds of applications will need more than 16 Gb of RAM? That's already a massive amount. Check out [http://www.zdnet.com/how-much-ram-do-i-need-early-2013-edition-7000011139/](http://www.zdnet.com/how-much-ram-do-i-need-early-2013-edition-7000011139/) in terms of RAM.

To make an analogy, your situation seems like a man who has a dog struggling to pull a cart and decides (correctly) that the dog is too small and can't really do it. So, he decides to go bigger, but instead of going with a horse, which would do the job nicely, he decides to get the absolute biggest animal he can find, and buys an elephant to pull the cart, in the name of having more power and muscle. Granted, the elephant has no difficulty doing the job, but it's what is known as "overkill," and our man might even end up looking slightly foolish for having spent more than he needed to, on power he'll never use. Get the analogy?

BTW, I know this is a System76 subforum, but if you're do end up going for a linux-built AIO, check out [http://zareason.com/shop/zima-930.html](http://zareason.com/shop/zima-930.html). IMHO, the features that put it above the Sable complete are: USB 3, SSD for OS and HDD for storage, wireless chip included in base price.
