### Post by ahood on 2012-04-09
Hello,

I hope this email does not break forum rules, after all, I have no help to offer. 

The purpose of this message a couple of items:

First I am grateful to System76, as well as the other providers, for specializing in computers that supporting Ubuntu/(GNU)Linux out of the box. There are not many Ubuntu/(GNU)Linux providers for the general consumer (IMHO).

Second, as of this morning, I am now a repeat System76 customer. The first System76 computer I purchased was a Darter1 in April 2007. I purchased the Darter1 machine for my mother who had never used a computer (not even Windows or Mac). Just to make things challenging, I trained my mom to use Ubuntu from a distance of 1,500 miles apart (thank goodness for Remote Desktop!, hehe).

The Darter1 aged over the years quite well. I upgraded the RAM from 1GB to 2GB, and replaced the optical drive with a faster one (original was quite slow). It currently runs Ubuntu Lucid (10.04 LTS) very happily. The only problem encountered with the hardware is a sticky 'N' key, and the battery doesn't hold the charge like it used to when it was new, which is to be expected.

Recently, the Darter1 is acting its age. It wants to sleep more hours of the day compared to when it was a young pup. Sure, giving the Darter1 a good nudge (i.e., reboot) will wake it up, but then after a while of no use, it falls asleep again and doesn't want to awaken unless nudged.

Rather than troubleshooting and finding a fix, we figured this machine has served a useful life and it is time to replace it.

I was amazed that my original account from 5 years ago was still in the System76 system. Seeing the Darter1 in my account from 5 years ago was great. Boy, has pricing changed (hehe)!

Thanks System76 for providing a machine (Darter1) that lasted so long. We look forward to the new machine (Pangolin Performance). 

:D
