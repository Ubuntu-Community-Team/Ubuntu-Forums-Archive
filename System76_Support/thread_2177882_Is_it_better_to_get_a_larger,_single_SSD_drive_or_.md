### Post by hawkmage on 2013-09-30
I really depends on how you want to manage your system.  It is simpler to just have a single drive with a single partition that holds all of your files but having a separate drive for the home directory so that you can do whatever you want with the OS without effecting your home.

### Post by isantop on 2013-10-03
Having a single, large drive (with a single, large partition) is by far the simplest setup to manage, and short of distro-hopping you'll still be able to upgrade/install from the live disk without erasing your home folder.
