### Post by Leslie Dorner on 2013-06-13
System76 unveiled their new Gazelle Professional and Galago UltraPro laptops last hour. Now, they have a modern mobile product line up that is highly competitive with the industry. I say good for them. I've been hammering this company to get its act together for quite some time. They managed to lower their prices for these two new products as well. As it stands right now, they are reasonably priced and they are highly competitive with the industry. While I would not get the Galago UltraPro, it is good to see that it is offered. For me, I still need the quintessential built-in optical drive as I rent and copy many DVDs and CDs each week. I like the fact that the mSATA option was recently added as this makes System76 products very competitive with leading edge laptop designs from other brands. mSATA is also cooler and more durable than SATA with the added advantage of being smaller which means that thinner and lighter form factors can be achieved such as the Galago UltraPro.

If System76 can improve their technical support department to actually provide useful solutions to their customers instead of the standard re-install Ubuntu and choose the default configuration alibi to solve all technical support problems, then we may have a glimmer of hope that a real company may emerge from the shadows. This seems to be the Achilles Heel of the company right now. They don't offer real support for custom installations relying heavily upon third-party software applications.

I've got System76 on my mind again. I don't think that they need to add more desktops or laptops to their product catalog now. They have to focus on Ubuntu powered tablets and smart phones in order to play catch up with Apple, Google, and Microsoft. I'd gladly buy an Ubuntu powered tablet or I would gladly root my Droid Bionic and install Ubuntu if they supported this old phone. My understanding is that this will become a reality sometime in April through August 2014. I would be due for a systems upgrade at that time, but I think that I'll hold off for another three more years to get the most value from my current investment. I've had laptops last as little as 22 months under heavy usage up to 5 years with light usage. 4 years seems to be the right time to replace my Lemu4 from last year.

I like to see this company grow and succeed, but it's highly dependent upon Canonical getting their ducks in a row. The hardware may be available, but the seamless Ubuntu and Ubuntu Touch software OS needs to be developed and refined to offer the best user experience possible with no glitches or gotchas. So far, System76 is following the right principle to release only when all the hardware features and software controls are fully baked and ready for the masses. I've not had any problems with Ubuntu or my Lemu4 ever since I upgraded to a more modern Linux kernel.

Kudos to System76. Now, work on your mobile product line up and we'll talk over the telephone to do business yet again in the distant future.

My lesson learned is that it's just not worth spending the extra $200 for up to three years of support and warranty.
