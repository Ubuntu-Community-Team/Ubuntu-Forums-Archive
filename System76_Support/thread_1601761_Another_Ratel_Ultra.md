### Post by 3Miro on 2010-10-21
> **HotShotDJ said:**
> (For the nay-sayers who think that Linux is only for the young and technically proficient... Bah!  She's been using Ubuntu since 2006)


"Technically illiterate" people have no trouble with Linux; for them one OS is just like another. The most they know is how to get FireFox up or start a card game, you can teach them everything they need to know in about 5 seconds. Linux is actually much better for those people because of the improved security, nothing is 100 percent fool-proof, but you are taking a much smaller risk. My grandparents have never used a computer, but if they ever do, I will make sure it is Ubuntu. System76 does a great things by providing very well working "out of the box" Linux machines.

"Technically proficient" people have little trouble with Linux. The system is very transparent and things make sense from technological point of view. It is easy to explain all the "obvious" advantages. Also, they are quick to learn new things.

The people that complain are the ones in the middle, they sort of know how to work on a computer, but they only know windows and switching to Linux will take hit on their productivity. They are somewhat willing to learn, but they don't want to start (almost) from scratch. They know things about windows, but not enough about technology to make sense of things from technological point of view, so learning for them is harder. They are by far the largest number of people out there.
