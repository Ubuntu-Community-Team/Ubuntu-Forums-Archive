### Post by isantop on 2011-01-05
I'm not sure of any compatible adapters right off hand, but anything with matching specs should be okay. Check the plug size before ordering, also check that the output is 19V at at least 1.58 amps. Also, negative is on the outside.

We do have a car adapter available. We don't have it listed on our website, so you'll have to place your order over the phone. Just call us with your card information handy, and use extension 2 to get to customer service.

### Post by themrfreeze on 2011-01-05
Kensington makes a universal notebook power adapter that works with a wide variety of laptops.  Simply set the desired voltage range on the power supply, attach the appropriate connector to the end of the power cord (it comes with at least a dozen different ones), and you're good to go.  I use them here at work (sys admin who supports a lot of laptops), and it works with 95% of the PC laptops around here.
