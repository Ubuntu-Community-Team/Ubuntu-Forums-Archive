### Post by VCSkier on 2008-03-08
I've been wanting to find a laptop that is completely FLOSS compatible.  I've been looking at the systems that System76 offers, and comparing it to the information shown on [Ubuntu's Free Software Drivers]("http://www.ubuntu.com/partners/hardwareprogramme/freesoftware") page, and the FSF's [list of supported hardware]("http://www.fsf.org/resources/hw"), and I'm unsure of some things.

Is it possible to build a system from System76 that would not need *any* non-Free drivers or firmware?  If so, it should be noted what is Free-compatible, and what isn't.

I really think that for a company that specializes in Linux-compatible hardware, the hardware should be selected for ideal compatibility with completely Free software.  If this was the case, distributions like Gobuntu and gNewSense would run perfectly, out-of-the-box.  This would not only offer benefits with regard to convenience and security, but it be a way that users and companies like System76 could really communicate to hardware manufacturers how important openness is.  We can sign petitions and write emails and file bug-reports to these proprietary-driver companies, but many of them won't listen until they start losing customers because of it.

Basically, I guess what I'm asking is, what affordable laptop can I purchase that would contain the most Free software supportive hardware.  I think this would be a System76 laptop, but I think even System76 could be doing better (at least by clarifying what would require non-Free drivers/firmware).
