### Post by kec on 2013-11-09
I have a gazp7 and am OK with the keyboard. I generally use it with an external mouse and disable the touchpad. The touchpad senses things that I don't want it to if I leave it enabled.

It sounds like the keyboard is important to you and you've read  comments that may you doubt it will be acceptable. Real life experience  will rarely be better than comments in reviews.

Don't buy it unless you find someone nearby that lets your try it and you decide the keyboard is great. Otherwise, I bet you will waste time and money returning it.

### Post by moschops on 2013-11-09
I have one and have posted elsewhere on this forum - the other major keyboard thread.  It's an issue for me even though I got the second generation keyboard with strengthened back to the keyboard.  After a month of use I still loose about 5% of all key presses.  Some keys seem worse than others but it basically requires you to pound on the keys like an old klacky keyboard even though it is a keyboard that feels like a soft touch chicklet keyboard.  

So I have two words of advice - firstly: they have a 30 day return policy.  Use it if  you have any doubts.  Sure you'll probably have to pay some return shipping fees, but think of it as renting a computer for a month. Secondly I recently exchanged messages with System76 and it seems like they are getting some new keyboards in soon.  I don't know f they are superior, redesigned or what, but I would call them up and ask about it. 

PS. The touchpad is a problem for me too.  As is a broken ethernet port door.  The former may get fixed in a software update - the synaptics touchpad can certainly support palm detection even if the driver does not.  The latter I'm getting a replacement for, but if you plug in often you should certainly be careful with the door, the port is non-functional without it.  Other than that although the case is plastic I feel like the overall system is sturdy, the screen is good quality and I'm happy with it.  Being a quad four processor battery life is nothing like what a dual core 4th Gen MacBook Pro will give you, but 3 to 4 hours is usually enough.
