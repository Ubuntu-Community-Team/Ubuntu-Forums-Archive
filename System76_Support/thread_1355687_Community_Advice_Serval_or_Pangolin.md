### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-12-15
The Serval only gets about an hour of battery life. The Pangolin gets about 1.5.

Performance-wise, both will handle the job (particularly if you configure the Pangolin with some memory and CPU upgrades). 

The Serval is designed to be a desktop replacement system and is quite powerful. It also has a beefy graphics card that's aimed towards gamers. It doesn't sound like you have a lot of need for that, though.

### Post by HotShotDJ on 2009-12-17
I can speak to the resolution issue on older eyes.  I wear bifocals and neither the distance lens nor the reading lens works well for computer use.  Just like Samalex, I have a PanP5 with the discontinued high-res (1680x1050) monitor, and I agree with his assessment... it is the most beautiful display I have ever used, including my 1440x900 external monitor.  However, it can be difficult to read web pages.  BUT, the Ctrl + "+" (or, alternatively, Ctrl + scroll) comes in handy for that.

All other things being equal, I think that ThomasAaron's assessment is right on the money. And based on your description of how you will use the computer, you will definitely want the 8 GIG RAM no matter which one you choose -- those are some pretty RAM intensive apps you plan on running concurrently!  I should also add that I recommend that you choose one of the 7200 RPM Hard Drives.  That extra speed comes in handy with virtualization.

In my opinion, the Serval is probably "over-kill."  I would opt for the Pangolin -- in fact, I DID opt for the Pangolin. :P  It is an excellent (not to mention cost-effective) compromise between mobility and desktop power.  However, whichever model you choose, I have no doubt that you will be happy with your System 76 laptop!

### Post by mickie.kext on 2009-12-20
> **Eldera said:**
> Edit: Just re-read the thread and noticed virtex uses VMware. I am guessing that VT-x would be even more important for a VMware user, because VMWare seems to be considered a "heavier" program.

VT is very important for everyone who uses any virtualisation software. For example, KVM and XEN wont ever run without VT. VirtualBox will run, but you are limited to 32 bit guest OS-es who do not support PAE. For PAE supporting 32-bit OSes and all 64-bit OSes, you need VT or AMD-v. Note that AMD (all K10 and all newer K8 models) and Intel Core i5/i7 processors are way batter for virtualisation (than Core 2) because they support nested paging and Core 2 do not.
