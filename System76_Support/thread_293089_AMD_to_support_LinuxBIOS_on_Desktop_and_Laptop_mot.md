### Post by DRM_free on 2006-11-04
[LinuxBIOS]("http://www.linuxbios.org/") developer and AMD employee Lu Yinghai has asked on the [LinuxBIOS mailinglist]("http://www.linuxbios.org/mailman/listinfo/linuxbios") which desktop and laptop motherboards people would like to see supported by LinuxBIOS:

[http://www.openbios.org/pipermail/linuxbios/2006-November/016632.html]("http://www.openbios.org/pipermail/linuxbios/2006-November/016632.html")

This is a great opportunity for System76 to tell AMD what desktop and laptop motherboards you want pre-installed with LinuxBIOS. LinuxBIOS is one of the most important [ campaigns by the Free Software Foundation]("http://www.fsf.org/campaigns/free-bios.html") because of the scary treacherous computing and bugs that can plague proprietary BIOSes. If System76 starts selling Linux-BIOS desktops and laptops, I have no doubt that the [ Free Software Foundation]("http://www.fsf.org") will put a **huge ad and link** on their front page pointing people to your products (and the FSF sure has a lot of dedicated followers).

Here's a link to the new [LinuxBIOS wiki page]("http://www.linuxbios.org/Desktops") detailing plans for supporting LinuxBIOS on desktop and laptop motherboards. I highly encourage you to get in touch with Lu Yinghai on the LinuxBIOS mailinglist to let him know about the AMD-compatible motherboards System76 is selling or plans to sell.

### Post by DRM_free on 2006-11-19
The fact that AMD hires one of the most prolific LinuxBIOS developers speaks a lot about their good support for Linux and Free/Open Source Software. AMD has also curageously refused to put any DRM on their flagship media platform, [AMD Live!]("http://amdlive.amd.com"), despite strong pressure from the entertainment cartels (RIAA and MPAA) that want to control what you can watch and listen to on your own computer.

Furthermore, the Free Software Foundation (FSF) [strongly endorses]("http://www.fsf.org/campaigns/free-bios.html") AMD processors that are compatible with LinuxBIOS:
> AMD has been cooperating by releasing major chunks of their BIOS source code and making their technical experts available. You can help our campaign by buying AMD CPU chips and not buying Intel, and by publishing statements about what you're doing.

As for booting Windows XP with LinuxBIOS, this is possible but hard. Please see the "[booting XP]("http://linuxbios.org/pipermail/linuxbios/2006-November/thread.html#16793")" thread started this month on the LinuxBIOS mailinglist.
