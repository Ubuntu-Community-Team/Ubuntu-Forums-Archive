### Post by Paranoid Randroid on 2007-03-08
> **AusIV4 said:**
> A student discount means they've jacked up the price for everyone else, and give you a break if you're a student. System76 gives the best deals they can afford to everyone. In other words, if System76 gave student discounts, everyone else would be paying ~$50 more, students would be paying the current price and think they were getting a deal.

Ehhh. This is what economists call price discrimination, which is not as bad as it sounds; its effects are not so clear as all that.

I'm assuming that System76 makes a profit off of every machine they sell. What if, by lowering the amount of profit they make per student sale, they could sell more than enough extra computers *to students* to make up for the change? This wouldn't necessarily correspond with an increase in prices to other demographics. Of course, they could charge you more, if they thought you'd pay it, but that's an entirely separate issue.

In general this is *good*, because a finer-grained pricing structure allows more people to get what they want at the prices they're willing to pay.

Now, for a firm like System76, it probably wouldn't make sense to charge less for students &#8211; there'd be the overhead in managing two different price points for everything in stock; losses to fraud; probability of choosing the wrong price due to lack of market research ...

Nah, their pricing model is just right for a business their size.
