### Post by samalex on 2010-01-18

> Heyo, Id just would like to hear your guys opinion on system 76's overall 
quality. im thinking of buying a new laptop!

Hi,

I ran Linux exclusively from 2000 through 2005 but switched to an Apple iBook as my primary system.  About 6 months ago though I decided to retire the iBook since PowerPC processor had become legacy and move back to the penguin.  I'd been following System76 and a few others for years, but in the end I went with a Pangolin P5 laptop.  

My experience from pre-order questions to ordering was superb, as was the laptop when it arrived.  The only problems I ran into were related to Ubuntu and not the laptop, but regardless of the problem the folks in this forum have always been eager to help.

I'm still running Ubuntu 9.04 which shipped with the laptop because quite honestly it works great and I don't upgrade just to upgrade.  I may move up to Ubuntu 10 when it comes out, but I'll have to see what they add that might entice me :)

They've changed the system alittle from the PanP5 to P6, but my system has the 1680x1050 upgraded screen, P8700 2.53 Ghz processor, 4 Gigs/ram, and 500 Gig 7200 RPM HD.  I splurged on the faster drive, more ram, and nicer monitor ... all of which were worth it. Battery life is the only blah with it going 1.5 - 1.75 hours tops.  If you're hitting the hard drive or CD-ROM allot then expect it to be closer to 1-1.25 hours on a full charge.  For me given this is my 'desktop' equivalent I don't mind the shorter battery life, but it did take some getting used to coming from my iBook with a 3-4 hour battery life.

I don't use a desktop, so I wanted something powerful in a laptop, which this works great.  I do software development using NetBeans, MonoDevelop, and I'm slowly getting into Eclipse. I also run Win XP through VirtualBox, and it works like a champ.  There I run SQL Server 2008 Standard, Visual Studio 2008, and a gambit of other apps that just don't play well on Linux.

All and all I've been 100% happy with this system... though much of that is a true testament to Linux itself regardless of what hardware it runs on.  I can't speak for the folks running other System76 models, but honestly the PanP series is quite good.  Also for me coming from a Mac I had VERY high expectation because I think Apple builds the best laptop hands down ... but an Apple with similar specs to what I got from System76 would've set me back about $800 more than what I spent, which was around $1200 with taxes and shipping.

Good luck in whatever you choose ...

Sam

Bottom line is I *will* buy my next computer from System 76, and recommend it to anyone looking for one.

### Post by dabrowsa on 2010-02-02
I bought a wild dog in October.  The hardware is great.  Ubuntu is good. The integration of the two is mediocre.

I was unable to get the audio line-in jack on the sound card working without uninstalling pulseaudio, the backbone of the Gnome audio system.  I'm inclined to think System76 should have investigated this and switched to a different card before shipping the system.

I got the 500 GB hard disk.  System76 alloted only 15 GB to the root partition, with the result that I started getting "no space left on device" errors within two months.  What were they thinking?  I then had to boot from a rescue disk, redo the partitions, and change the mount point of /usr.  

Initially the video card offered was an ati.  I had read that nvidia cards work better with linux, but I figured System76 knew what they were doing.  Shortly after ordering I got a message saying they were switching to nvidia cards because of much improved performance.  Thanks, but it feels like a close call - if I'd ordered a week earlier I would have been stuck with the ati.

The overall picture is of a company that's a little careless about how they combine and configure components.

None of this may matter in your case.  You may get lucky.  Or you may not be someone who pushes a system to its limits.  But if you are, ask some probing questions before placing your order.  I don't think I'll order a desktop system from them again.
