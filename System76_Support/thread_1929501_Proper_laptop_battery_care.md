Hello,

I am curious about the best way to take care of a laptop battery.  I've had a gazelle professional (gazp6) for a little over a month now.  I run it a lot on battery, but sometimes I leave it plugged in for many hours after it is done charging because I am doing intensive tasks that work better when the machine is plugged in.

I've been reading online about laptop battery care and some places say to take the battery out of the laptop if you are leaving it plugged in for a long time to prevent it from overcharging or something... ### Post by isantop on 2012-02-22
The batteries we use in our laptops automatically prevent overcharging. You won't need to worry about that. Furthermore, you shouldn't need to worry about overheating, since our laptops generally stay very cool. 

You should be fine following most general battery care guides available. But it won't hurt much if you don't take any special precautions either. Modern Li-Ion batteries are very resilient, and it's difficult to cause lasting damage as long as you follow good practices. 

There is one thing I do recommend. If you're not going to be using the computer for a while, then I recommend discharging the battery to about 40%, then remove it from the computer. Store it in a cool place. At that point, the battery is primed for long-term storage, and you should be good to leave it for extended periods of time.
