### Post by palintheus on 2007-08-08
The Darter is great I bought one recently. I upgraded to 1.8Ghz cpu, 1Gb of ram, and 80Gb hhd. I am extremely happy with it, as far as installing programs, most of teh time you just open the Add/Remove Programs and search for the program check the box and hit apply and your computer will handle the rest. Ubuntu will notify you of updates for every program you have installed that is avail in the Ubuntu Repositories, which should be just about everything. Also if you do need to dual boot, they include the driver cd for windows. Yes, if you use it everyday you will start exploring/tweaking your system and learn a lot in a few months.

### Post by ackey on 2007-08-08
My story is very similar.  I needed a laptop and wanted to buy one operating linux as a method to make myself learn linux.  I have had substantial help setting things up from someone very knowledgeable, but without the help it would have worked great right out of the box.

I really like the darter - it is tiny and light, but still very powerful and functional.  My only two complaints so far would be the power monitor and headphone control.  Both are submitted as bugs already, and I am optimistic that System76 will have them fixed.  I have started dual-booting ubuntu on my desktop and have had far more problems with configuration and set-up than I have had on the Darter.

Over the past 2 weeks I have become a believer in the Darter, System76, Ubuntu, and linux.  I encourage you to do the same!
