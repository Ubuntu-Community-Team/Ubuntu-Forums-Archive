Certainly, you can install Ubuntu, or another up-to-date distribution on a wide variety of hardware. I have had good results installing Linux on a variety of platforms including laptops.

But the advantage of buying from System76 and ZaReason, or other Linux laptop vendors is that they have done the research and troubleshot any compatibility issues for you.

If you contact them on a pre-sales question you will reach one of the techs.  All they install and support is Ubuntu. And one of their techs is very active on this forum. 

That should get you started on your own decision making process.

### Post by GeneSalamin on 2012-08-16
I have been running on a ZaReason computer for several years with only  minor problems, and these were all my fault.  While I have not yet been a  System76 customer, I can say that ZaReason support has been every bit  as helpful and wonderful as the descriptions in this Forum of System76  support.

These are both small companies, and cannot provide the  wide variety of hardware choices available from the big guys.  For that  reason, you should examine the product offerings from both to see which  best meets your needs.

If you plan to do scientific calculations,  you will want an i7, and 3rd generation (Ivy Bridge), since that has a  built in hardware random number generator.  Pay attention to Intel  graphics vs. Nvidia, and examine the Forums for the  problems people  have been reporting with Nvidia.  If you want a laptop, be aware of  glossy screen vs. matte.  In my case for example, I want i7 Ivy Bridge  and Intel graphics, no Nvidia.  For a Laptop I want matte screen and  Crucial 512 GB SSD, for ruggedness and because I like Crucial.  My  laptop choice is the System76 Gazelle.  For a desktop, it's the System76  Ratel vs. the ZaReason Limbo 5660.  But I'd prefer the Limbo because it  can be configured with 32 GB RAM and 3 TB HDD.
