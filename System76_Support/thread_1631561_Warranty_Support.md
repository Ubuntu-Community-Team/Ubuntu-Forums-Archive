### Post by Eyore15 on 2010-11-26
While I was typing today, the "S" key must popped off the keyboard of my Starling, and I can't reseat it.  No problem, I thought.  I purchased the three-year support package.

I went to the System76 Web site, and with a minimum amount of digging around found the warranty section. (I wish the information was in the "Contact" section; the general category for warranty and returns is buried at the bottom of the screen.) I then scrolled through all the standard boiler plate and in the middle of all that, I found a phone number to call (888.468.6482.).  Calling the number is an exercise in frustration because you get the message that the number is no longer in service.

There was an email address, so I've tried that.  Will see how that turns out.

I was super-pleased with the service and attention to detail when I purchased the Starling.  The folks on this forum, both users and company reps, have always been helpful.  I've recommended System76 on different mailing lists and to friends.  I thought the machine was well-built, but now I'm beginning to wonder. I'm not a heavy-touch typist, so it shouldn't have popped off like it did; it makes me worry about the other keys.

If my past experiences are any gauge, the repair process should be rather simple and pain-free. The discontinued phone number gives me pause though. I haven't seen anyone talk about warranty repair service here.  I'll let you know how it goes. 

mcm
