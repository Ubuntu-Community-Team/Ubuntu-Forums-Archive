Actually, I did a lot of research and landed on this: 

[http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003BEDQYY/ref=oss_product](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003BEDQYY/ref=oss_product)

although I haven't used it yet (it's a Father's Day present) and in the meantime have been using a simple block of wood to slightly elevate the back of the computer, and that has worked fine (though not so elegant). Mine, a quad-core P7, never gets hot - I have a little utility that reports the temp - but the fan runs a little and I thought the pad would minimize that. So the one you linked to would probably work great too. 

Good luck, report back on what you get and how it does!

(Edit) - Well, Father's Day came and I'm now using the Logitech linked to above. I like it - it fits the Pangolin well, it's pretty quiet (though you *can* hear it, but it's quieter than the laptop fan) and the laptop fan has only come on once since I started using the cooling pad. 

Processor temp has dropped a couple of degrees in normal use but the big change is that the laptop fan virtually never comes on any more, which I infer means that internal temps are cooler, therefore heat stress to the computer has been lessened. It didn't get hot before, but I figure cooler is better. So I'd recommend this one. 

There are probably other good ones as well, but I think for use with the Pangolin - where heat is not a big issue to begin with - that getting one with multiple fans would only suck more power and make more noise. And the Logitech quite good-looking, simple and elegant - I don't mind looking at it every day. It got good reviews on several sites, and I have to say that initial impressions bear them out.

Again, good luck and let us know what you get in the end and how it works!
