### Post by Transhumanist on 2013-01-03
I am planning to buy the Lemur Ultra.

I have the option of buying extra RAM, a faster processor, a better wireless radio, and an SSD instead of HD.

Of these options, I was thinking of opting for:
8GB of RAM
The baseline processor
The baseline wireless radio
The 128GB Crucial M4 SSD

My reasoning is:
* 4GB of RAM is not enough for the future
* The baseline processor is more than enough for a coding and development laptop for a computer science student. After all, it can't really play 3D games with that Intel 4000 HD GPU.
* I don't know the difference between the two wireless chips
* An SSD is more resilient to physical knocks and drops than a moving parts HD, and consumes less power

Is this setup optimal to maximise battery life?

I wonder if 16GB of RAM would be a better choice because it might mean the SSD could be idle longer and hence at a lower power longer? Because apparently Ubuntu uses spare RAM as a disk cache?

The faster processor uses a larger wattage PSU and runs at 45 watts TDP instead of 35 watts TDP. Even accounting for the power savings gained by running the CPU for less time due to it being faster, this still seems like it'd use more power on average than the baseline CPU. Thoughts?

Does anybody know the power consumption differences between the two available wireless radios? Since WiFi A is so old, I doubt I need that. Which really only leaves power consumption and signal strength. Does the $20 extra wireless radio have noticeably better signal strength? One significant use case will be using the laptop in university classrooms which don't always have nodes inside them (hence sometimes low signal strength).

What are your thoughts on optimising battery life on the Lemur Ultra?

Also, what kind of battery life can I expect on the Lemur Ultra?
