### Post by philbert on 2014-04-18
I have no affiliation with System 76, except that I own one of their laptops.  However, I did work for a small systems integrator back about ten years ago and found that even small integrators, and I put System 76 in that class, cannot get the pricing that Dell, HP, Lenovo, Asus, New Egg or Tiger Direct for components. We could barely build a comparable system with only the cost of parts for the price Dell was selling entry level models. The big vendors get Windows OS for a song, whereas we had to by the OEM version. We could be competitive when building the high end models. Yes, if you want to save money, buy the entry level memory and hard drives, then upgrade and sell off the extra parts on ebay\craigslist. If you pay the extra have System 76 upgrade it for you, as I did when I bought the laptop, they own the support on it. Of course, the laptop has been rock solid, and I have not had to call in support.   In the end, I want System 76 to succeed. I can build towers easily enough, but don't care to build my own laptops. So am hoping they are around when it comes time to buy my next laptop.
