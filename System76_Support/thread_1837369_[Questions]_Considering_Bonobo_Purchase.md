### Post by isantop on 2011-09-01
> **tommytomatoe said:**
> Hey team s76,

1. Does the motherboard support UEFI? 

No, the systems do not natively support UEFI. Can I ask why you need to use a GIUD partition table?

> **tommytomatoe said:**
> 2. With direct comparisons with the Dell XPS 1702, I would seemingly get more for less if I went with Dell. Can you make a non-biased suggestion?

The first thing I notice about that GPU is that it uses Nvidia Optimus with it's graphics. I've heard that Optimus laptops can become stuck on the integrated GPU in Ubuntu, which would obviously negate the performance benefit from the dedicated Nvidia card. In addition, while it has more vRAM available, the GT 555M uses standard DDR3 RAM which is much slower than the GDDR5 RAM used in the GTX 560M (total memory bandwidth of 43.2 GB/s in the former vs 60 GB/s in the latter). It also has a much lower clock speed (675 MHz max vs 1550 MHz). Overall, the GTX 560M is a much faster card in a very different league from the GT 555M.

The screen on the Bonobo is also much better, despite having the same resolution. It uses an LED backlight, which consumes less power and produces brighter colors and deeper blacks. There is an LED backlit option on the Dell, but it adds $50 to the price and reduces the resolution to 1600x900.

The Bonobo comes stock with a WiFi/Bluetooth Combo module, which is a $30 upgrade on the Dell. The stock module in the Dell does not have bluetooth, and is not as fast as the default WiFi module in the Bonobo.

The Bonobo uses THX TruStudio Audio, and sounds comparable to most Desktop Audio Systems.

The Bonobo is specifically engineered to run Ubuntu, and the Dell will have a hard time running it due to the general performance issues and Optimus. 

> **tommytomatoe said:**
> 3. How flexible are you with pricing and further accommodations? Say, I would the system sent without a hard drive and I would instead use my own, would you make pricing changes? Feel free to direct me to an email or some other resource if this isn't the right place for such a question.

We can only ship complete systems, and don't offer any discounts.

> **tommytomatoe said:**
> 4. Can you explain how the "caddy case" would look if I were to select optional storage as opposed to the optical drive bay.

The Caddy case appears identical to an Optical disk drive; it uses the same bezel and face as the optical drive.
