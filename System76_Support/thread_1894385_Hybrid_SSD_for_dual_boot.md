### Post by isantop on 2011-12-13
I'm not sure about performance issues with the cache, but while somethings will be faster, and there may be less that feels faster in each operating system, the base drive is still a 7200 RPM drive, which is faster than the standard. So the 4 GB SSD on there will make a difference in performance. 

Additionally, since it caches what you use the most, if you always boot into Ubuntu and load Firefox and LibreOffice, those will get cached. It's not a bad investment in general.

As for the screen, I would definitely go for it if you plan on doing any graphics or video work. If you're more of a casual user, or you aren't focused on graphics, then it's not a game changer, but the screen is still pretty spectacular. For reference, the stock screens are 60% NTSC. Here's a comparison photo:

[https://plus.google.com/photos/107517157558649473024/albums/5660155414850384961](https://plus.google.com/photos/107517157558649473024/albums/5660155414850384961)

The screen on the left is the standard display, and the screen on the right is the 95% display. Both have Matte finishes and are identical in every other way.

The 95% screen represents colors that the standard display simply can't display, and the screen looks brighter and more vivid as a result.
