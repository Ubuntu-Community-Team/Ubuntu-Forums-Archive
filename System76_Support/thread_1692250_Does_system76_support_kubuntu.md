### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-02-21
kubuntu is a flavour of ubuntu, just like UNE (ubuntu netbook edition) is a flavour of ubuntu, so there is no netbook edition for kubuntu.

you can off coarse install kubuntu on your computer. Just search in synaptic for the package "kubuntu-desktop" and install it. Then you can choose between kubuntu or UNE on login.

Watch out: this will install all kinds of proframs twice. You will have two browsers (Firefox for UNE and konqueror for KDE), two text editors (gedit and kate), two music players (rhythmbox and amarok) ... This is no real problem, but your menus will get kinda full.

### Post by isantop on 2011-02-21
We don't officially provide support for Kubuntu; however, it will have the best non-ubuntu compatibility since they are the same code underneath. The drivers should continue to run just fine on Kubuntu.

However, the solution sanderd17 offered is probably the best one. That way, you can have Gnome installed for support issues, and KDE for daily use. Plus, you don't have to reinstall the operating system.

### Post by Derath on 2011-02-21
I haven't had a problem with KDE/Kubuntu loaded on my machines, but they are much bigger than a netbook. The drivers are all the same, everything works as normal. I am unsure how it would look the netbooks, it might appear fine, but I don't know if the apps would work well on the small screen (i.e. "Okay/Cancel" buttons being below the screen and the like). You could try "sudo apt-get install kde-desktop" and use KDM to change between the two, at least if kde doesn't work, you can always get back into the UNE desktop.

also, I would look at trying the kubuntu cd, just to see if the screen would work for your use.
