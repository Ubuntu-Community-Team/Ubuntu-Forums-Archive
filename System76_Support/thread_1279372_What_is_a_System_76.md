### Post by Eldera on 2009-09-30
System 76 is a manufacturer of Computers with Ubuntu installed.
You can check out their line at the link amsum gave. 

You will find that they have laptops, desktops, and servers. They are not a "store like Best Buy." Unlike Best Buy they have a research department that tests hardware to find out which components work well with Ubuntu. 

They maintain a Knowledge base 
[http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Main_Page](http://knowledge76.com/index.php/Main_Page)
about their computers and provide a package of updates and patches called the System76 Driver as ongoing support for what they sell. They also have this sub-forum for specific help for people who own computers made by System 76 just like there is a subforum for people who own computers made by Dell.

Definitely more than a store.

### Post by miniyak on 2009-10-01
Just to elaborate the point,

 System 76 is a PC/Server vendor that sells shiped with the latest version of Ubuntu installed

So, System 76 is kind of like dell. Except that the hardware they sell is decent and the support staff is pleasant to deal with. 

> System 76 is a manufacturer of Computers with Ubuntu installed. 

The OEM's (Original Equipment Manufacturers) make all the parts. So it would be incorrect to say that S76 "makes" their computers optimized for Ubuntu. Though I'm sure they choose the best computer arrangements for the job. It would also be incorrect to say vice versa that System 76 optimizes Ubuntu for their computers. However they do provide drivers and a driver update system that is integrated with ubuntu's update manger. Their updater which can be simply downloaded as a deb(for s76 computers), I would say is far more convenient then their competitors update methods. They seem to do a lot of the hardware(driver) particular bug squashing themselves but im not exactly how that works. Maybe its a partnership with OEM's? Idk  

I wouldn't get too hung up on the business mechanics though, because thats the hassle resellers are there to alleviate.  

If I've got anything wrong here I'm sure Tom, S76 support guy will chime in.
