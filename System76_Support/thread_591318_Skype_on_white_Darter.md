### Post by riseringseeker on 2007-10-25
Thought I would pass on my experience with installing Skype on a daru1 under 7.10

I was incredibly happy when I installed skype from the skype repository.  For the first time I could use the built-in mike, and it also recognized if I plugged in a mike - no more having to select which mike I was using, and often loosing the mike in the process.  I've made 100's of calls to skype test since I've had this, and was frustrated before this install.  (Skype is **very** important to me since I travel extensively and am often overseas.)

I have medibuntu also as a repository.  One of the updates had updates to skype.  I blithely clicked on install, and didn't think anything about it.  Next time I went to use skype, it refused to work.  I could hear the caller, but they could not hear me - no matter what I tried.

Reverting to the "official" skype got it back, and can again make calls without plugging in the headset.  To keep it from asking me to "upgrade" to the non-working version from medibuntu I locked the version in synaptic.  Now it works, the only drawback is that I will have to periodically check the skype website for updates.
