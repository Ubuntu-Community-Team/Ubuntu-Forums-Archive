### Post by EJJ on 2011-02-10
The Starling is a wonderful machine and System 76 is a wonderful company. I highly recommend the Starling. 

You won't have to go through the hassle (not that it really is a hassle) of installing Ubuntu on the machine (as compared to if you would get a windows machine).

I have spent long hours typing on my Starling and I can assure you that the keyboard is by far the best keyboard I've ever used on a laptop or netbook. As long you lock the mouse with FN + F1 (if it's not locked I've found that it jumps all over the place since the pad is SUPER sensitive)

My main machine is a mac and so when I considered getting a small machine I thought about either the iPad or an Apple laptop. The iPad was neat....but not ever being able to upgrade the memory, storage, and no USB ports for portable hardrives (seriously, what good is that thing?) so that ended up being a no.

The smallest apple notebook is an 11 inch screen and is about 1000/1300 USD...a bit too far out of my price range (though the new airbooks are pretty cool..) and once again you CANNOT upgrade memory or storage....why apple does this is beyond me.

Since I refuse to windows I thought about getting a netbook and putting Ubuntu on it. Dell stopped offering the Mini with Ubuntu and about that time I came across System 76's website and learned about the starling. The choice was obvious. The hardware is great, the software is free and reliable. Why would you get anything else? 

Should I have a need to get a new machine (between my mac and my starling I'm great for now) System 76 will be getting my business. Whether it's for a desktop of laptop.

### Post by jml on 2011-02-13
Another good way to achieve the same thing is to configure both panels to autohide. Then when you set your cursor over the area where the bars were, they pop up, wneh the cursor moves off the area, the bar hides.  To configure hover over a panel, right click, choose properties and then check autohide.  This way, I have a clean desktop, get a bit more vertical space for my application.
