### Post by isantop on 2011-09-26
> **bodam said:**
> Shopping for a laptop.  My current one is an older Serval that I'm passing down to my son to use for schoolwork.  I'm comparing the Serval to Bonobo.  While looking at the config specs, I noticed a second hard drive option on the Bonobo.  Am I correct in understanding that the Bonobo can hold 2 drives at a time?  If so, this also means that even with 2 drives installed, I still have an optical drive (e.g. 2 drives and the DVD simultaneously - no swapping)?

That's correct; the Bonobo has two internal HDD bays and an ODD bay. You can have up to 3 hard drives if you give up the optical drive.

> **bodam said:**
> One other question, and I know the System76 response but has anyone had any experience with running either Linux Mint Debian Edition or Debian itself on either the newest Serval or Bonobo?  I was a big fan of Ubuntu for many years but I just do not like what they've done since about version 10.10.   I can live without OS support but I would like to know that everything will work (e.g. Wireless, camera, etc. I can live without a fingerprint reader).

We don't do any testing, so I couldn't tell you for sure. The above poster would be a good person to contact for help with that.

With that in mind, you might want to give 11.10 Beta 2 a try on a testing machine. It's not stable yet, so expect bugs, but it does have a good feel for what the release will look like. It's got some good changes, and a lot of the people who didn't like Unity in 11.04 seem to like it more in 11.10.
