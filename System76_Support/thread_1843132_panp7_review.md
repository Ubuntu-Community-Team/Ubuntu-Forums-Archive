### Post by malachus on 2011-09-12
I purchased my panp7 in late December 2010 and I received it in early January.  I ordered it with upgraded RAM (4GB) and a faster than stock hard drive.  I was very excited and I had high expectations.  I was upgrading from a three year old Dell laptop that, while useful, was getting a bit cumbersome in terms of performance and weight.  

My first disappointment came shortly after I ordered, when I noticed that the price of the RAM upgrade, which I had questioned before ordering, dropped by about $20.  That was a bit of a bummer, but not a huge deal. 

My second disappointment was when I received the laptop and I found that the bottom cover, where the RAM was installed, was not properly seated when it was replaced, so I had to get out a screw driver and put the cover back on properly.  Again, not a huge deal. 

Everything else seemed pretty smooth, although I did not like the feel of the keyboard as I did the more typical laptop keyboard of the Dell.  That, however, is just a minor detail.  My bigger problems have revolved around the touchpad, the wireless adapter, and general stability.  

First, the touchpad.  Basically, no matter what driver setting I have selected, nor which version of Ubuntu I have used (it was supposed to be fixed in 11.04), the touchpad has behaved very erratically.  The most vexing issue is that it will sometimes respond as though I have touched it when I have not.  This usually occurs when I am typing and it moves the focus to wherever the mouse cursor happened to be, which is very disruptive. 

One might assume that I am accidentally touching the touchpad when this happens, but it has happened when I am certain that I am not touching anything other than the keyboard.  

The touchpad also sometimes becomes totally erratic when in use, although this generally only happens for a few seconds and discontinuing use briefly will generally restore proper operation. 

I have corresponded with System76 tech support multiple times about this issue and the responses I have received have varied from "it's fixed in the next version of Ubuntu [11.04]" to "it will probably go away once it has been broken it a bit more".  None of this has resolved the issue to my satisfaction.  The only other suggestion that tech support had was to disable the touchpad completely when I want to type long documents, which is non-optimal. 

The wireless issue I have been experiencing causes the wireless card to drop its connection and not be able to reconnect.  I have two WAPs at my house and once this occurs I can still see both of them but I cannot connect to either one until I reboot the machine. Supposedly support will send me a replacement card, but I have not received any response to my formal request a month ago.

Most irritating, however, is the fact that when I leave the laptop running for a "long" time (usually less than 24 hours), it will lock up hard.  Once I have shut it off by holding down the power button, I have to turn it on then turn it off again before I can turn it on and boot.  The first boot after failure just gives me a series of beeps. 

I have been working with System76 tech support on this issue for months trying to figure out what the problem is, including running a live CD for several days and doing a system restore by upgrading from 11.04 to 11.04.  Nothing has resolved the issue, and it still persists to this day.  

Because this is my personal, home computer, I do not have a lot of time to spend trying to make it work. Quite frankly, I had higher expectations of both the laptop and of tech support. My old Dell was much more stable in general and seemed to be better supported the one time I did have an issue.  

This panp7 has been a huge source of frustration and I deeply regret purchasing it.  I would be much better off if I had spent my money on something else.  Sadly, I cannot recommend a System76 machine, despite how much I would like to.
