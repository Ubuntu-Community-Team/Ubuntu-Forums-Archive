### Post by isantop on 2012-07-27
> **mevun said:**
> How many empty RAM slots are there on the motherboard if I wanted to upgrade the memory later myself (when hopefully prices have come down for the 8 GB stick)?  Or would I have to discard the installed RAM to upgrade?

There are only two slots. They are full-sized 240-pin slots.

> **mevun said:**
> Does the PC come with the bezel pictured for an optical drive even if I don't order a DVD drive?  If Linux ever gets better Blue-Ray support, I would want to install a Blue-Ray drive.  Since the PC also has an option for internal SD, maybe this PC comes with a different case depending on the option chosen?

The CD bezel pictured is only included if you order an optical drive. We use a standard blank bezel if don't order the optical drive. The card reader gets mounted in the optical drive bay, and in that case no bezel is included.

> **mevun said:**
> For upgrading or adding drives, what are the internal bay sizes (3.5 in or 2.5 in) for the first drive and the second drive?

There are four total internal hard drive slots. There are two 3.5 in. slots and two 2.5 in. slots. Keep in mind that there are only four total SATA ports on the motherboard, and only two are SATA III.

> **mevun said:**
> Will any of the above mentioned upgrades void the warranty: memory, optical drive, storage drive?

Not necessarily. We obviously couldn't cover the upgraded parts under warranty, but every else would be good, as long as the upgrades aren't the cause of the problem.

> **mevun said:**
> I know this is subjective, but is it difficult to upgrade any of the above-mentioned parts because of the small case size?  I can imagine that perhaps quite a bit of dis-assembly is required to reach some of the parts.

Everything you mentioned except for the optical drive is fairly easy, particularly the hard drives. The Optical drive requires removing both side panels to mount properly, and the motherboard is mounted to one of them, so care should be taken.
