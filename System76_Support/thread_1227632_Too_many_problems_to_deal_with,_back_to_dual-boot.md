### Post by bill516 on 2009-09-08
At the moment I am on a  Quest Actiontec M1000.  At home I use a Trendnet Wireless G 432BRP.  Both work flawlessly with Ubuntu 8.10, sidux 6-2 and antiX M8.2.  I use WPA-PSK and one of these has a hidden SSID as well.  They each work flawlessly and setting them up was easy.

I have used a D-link only briefly but it also posed no problems.

I used a Linksys earlier and it worked very well with both Linux and Windows before it developed odd electronic problems.  The Trendnet unit replaced it.

I have used Windows XP on the Actiontec and the Trendnet as well with no problems.  I do not usually bother to hide the SSID, but I do rely on WPA-PSK.

One  suggestion.  The Trendnet may need a supplemental hi-gain antenna if you must do a long signal run between your PC and the router.  Barriers between you and the router also can affect the quality of that signal.  The good news is that a hi-gain antenna does not cost much at all.

### Post by bill516 on 2009-09-09
Correction:  Being in a hurry I incorrecty identified the Qwest router as wireless.  It is not.  The wireless router in use at the moment at this location is a Linksys Wireless-G WRT54G.  Like the others it performs reliably.

I use Network Manager 0.7.0, wicd and Ceni to link to these routers.  They all work.  Knetwork manager also works, but it is not my preferred tool.
The fastest of these once configured is Ceni, but it appears to be available only for sidux and antiX.  Next best in terms of speed would be Network Manager 0.7.0, although wicd has strong support from many people.

### Post by vgrisham on 2009-09-09
To the OP,

Are your screen freezes primarily happening in OpenOffice? I'm having a heck of a time with graphics in that program (so much so that I've switched to Gnumeric spreadsheet and google docs occasionally). 

One of the philosophical points that you brought up early in the thread was regarding LTS releases. I've been on Ubuntu for 3+ years now after dumping windows. In my experience, it's always paid to follow each release up. I tried just going with LTS releases, but despite the name, they don't get a lot of updated programs, particularly 3rd party stuff such as video and wireless drivers. When I was on Dapper, I kept having to add 3rd party repositories just to keep up and after a while it no longer made sense to stick with the long-term release. Ubuntu's repositories are already six months or so behind. I've also learned to wait a bit after each release comes out to make sure that the kinks are worked out. Jaunty has been the best release so far, imho. 

Good luck.
