### Post by 3Miro on 2009-07-03
To put couple of thoughts about my experience:

First of all, I love my Pangolin. For less than 1K in cash I got the best computer deal of my life. For the past few months I had to work on my dissertation and I don't think I could have been successful without my laptop. It is not just the convenience of working on the road (and the time saved), it is also the ease with which I can get things done in Ubuntu. What do I mean:

- great and very functional interface that lets me do all the LaTeX fast and effective
- the advantage of compiz's shortcuts that let you navigate fast between many simultaneously opened windows
- at the same time, the power to run scientific computations on it and the time it saved from having to run everything on my desktop
- when I had to give the final presentation, I got the projector up and running in seconds and was able to give a non-linear presentation with movies and spinning compiz cube. That did impress some people (I hope my work was more impressive, but I am not sure about it)
- I never really needed support help (as in an emergency), but all the questions I eve had were answered promptly. Great support!

The question that I have and I don't think anyone here can answer is: why would anyone not buy a sys76?

Having said that, I have couple of questions:

1. Why does the system76 driver list my machine as Bonobo. I wanted Pangolin, I payed for a Pangolin and according to lshw I got a Pangolin. The Driver however says: Bonobo Professional, bonp2. Now everything is working great camera + mic + sound + video and so on, is this some small bug with the driver?

2. There is one thing that System76 can improve in future models (if they have not already done that): the laptop has heat issues. On regular use (web, mail, docs), it is fine (a bit on the hot side, but fine). When I ran the numerical simulations the CPU temp went to the high 50C - low 60C, which is not bad. When I tried to play a 3D game, the graphics chip temp (as reported by nVidia X Server Settings) went over 80C.

I know that should expect some of those issues with a laptop, I do have the large desktop with 5 - 6 different fans that plays games fine, however, the laptop could be designed better. The laptop case is completely closed from below so there is no way for me to provide extra cooling. A laptop fan pad has absolutely no effect or whatsoever. If there were more vents, then I would have been able to play games with the fan-pad. It is now kind of sad, I have the hardware to play games aay form home, but yet I cannot do so because of the heat.

Sorry I should have added more info:
Pangolin NVIDIA 9300M, 4GB RAM, 2.4 Core 2 Duo 1066, still using the 8.10 that came with it (and I am not changing it any time soon)
