### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-07-27
> I'm about ready to pull the trigger on ordering a Pangolin Performance, but in watching this forum for months now I have to ask if anyone knows why so many little issues are creeping up for most people.

That's a really good question. I think miniyak is right. There are always going to be some issues with Linux, or any other operating system. But all in all, Linux is very solid and reliable.

Interestingly, though, the people who post on the forums and send email support requests make up a small fraction of our total customers. And they tend to expect a lot from their machines -- as well they should.

Just an observation... I would classify the people who post on our forums into three main categories:

1. Beginners that run into snags of their own making as they tinker and learn the ropes. This is to be expected, and that's why we are here. Without them, Linux wouldn't grow.

2. Advanced users who want to make Ubuntu better. They notice and report issues and work together to resolve them. Linux would not advance as rapidly as it does without these folks.

3. People who have been badly bitten by a bug or have hardware issues.

Please don't ask for percentages, I don't have the time to crunch the numbers right now. But, suffice it to say the vast majority of our customers never contact us for support.
