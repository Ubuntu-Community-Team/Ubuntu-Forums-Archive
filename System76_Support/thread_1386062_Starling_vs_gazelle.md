### Post by jml on 2010-01-20
Having bought three laptops from System 76, I am sure that you will like whatever computer you will get.  That being said, here are a few things to consider:

1.  Battery Life:  The Starling gets between 4 - 4.5 hours of battery life running the default power settings.  The Pangolin gets about 2 - 2.5 hours of battery life.  I think the Gazelle is somewhere in betwen.  Depending on how often you plan to use the computer on battery, it may influence your choice.

2.  Screen Size:  You mention that you, your husband and father will be using the computer.  The 10 inch screen is great for a net book, but if it were my only computer I would consider connecting it to a larger monitor for extended use.  (I'm 55 and require bifocals.)

3.  Optical Drive:  The Starling does not have a built in optical drive (not a criticism, just an observation.)  If you plan to use an optical drive a lot, the USB drives seem to be a bit slower, and a bit more fragile than the ones installed in bigger laptops.  Plus if you plan to travel a lot with your computer, having an extra piece of hardware to keep track of can be a hassle.

4.  System performance:  Since the applications you list using are not resource hogs, the performance of the Starling would be just fine.  I find my Starling is just fine for simple text editing, web surfing, and e-mail.  I would not want to edit big image, video files with it.  But it souonds like you are not planning to any way.  Hope this is a bit of help for you.

Joe
