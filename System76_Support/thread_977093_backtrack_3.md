### Post by jml on 2009-01-16
I have both a first generation Darter, ( the white one,) and the current Darter Ultra.  For the record, both are very Linux friendly laptops.  Lee_Machine is right.  I have played with BT3 a bit and it runs well on the DARU3 as a live cd.The one thing I have not tested extensively is the wireless compatibility.  Several of the pentesting tools require that the wireless card be placed into something called "passive mode"  Kismet is one of the applications that come to mind.  I would suggest that you find out precisely what wireless card would ship with your Darter, and then check on the BT web side to see if the card is listed as being one of the compatible ones.

I have played with several live distros on my Darter, and the only problems I have had were with some more obscure distros handling the WXGA (wide screen) resolution, and occaisional problems with recognizing the wireless card.  However, if you strick to the more main stream distros, you should not have any problems.
