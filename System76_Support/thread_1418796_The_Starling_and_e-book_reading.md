### Post by ShowMeGrrl on 2010-03-01
Hello All - I have been trying to use my Starling as an e-book reader. Recently, I downloaded several books from Project Gutenberg in the epub format. (That's an open format that Adobe has been using and that is a leading e-book format.)

Unfortunately, the FBReader that came preloaded with my Starling apparently could not handle this epub format, because after I loaded one of the e books into the FBReader library, I was no longer able to get the FBReader application to open. Also, I read somewhere on the Web that FBReader support for the epub format is incomplete.

I then downloaded the Calibre e-book application. This program has some definite pluses, but I have found it to be poor at viewing or reading an e-book, which is the main thing I want to do. It boasts a lot about its ability to organize your library, a feature I'm not that interested in.

The good things that Calibre does include 1) You can use it to convert html books to epub format; 2) You can use it to download for free a number of newspapers and magazines in the epub format.

The newspapers include The New York Times and the International Herald Tribune. There is a fairly long list of publications. The curious thing to me about this is that most of the current e-readers on sale, such as the Sony PRS, the Kindle and the Nook, charge for downloading these publications, yet in Calibre it is free.

After searching around on the Internet, I came across an e-reading application that is working very well for me. This is a Firefox plug-in called "epub reader." 

You get it the same way you get other Firefox add-ons. (Go to the Tools menu within Firefox, click on the Add-ons item, when the Add-ons window pops up, click on the "Get Add-ons" button. When this page loads, type "epub reader" in the the search box. Or you can just go to this URL in Firefox: [https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/45281?src=api](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/45281?src=api) )

So far, I've thrown about six epub books, newspapers and magazines at this epub reader plug-in, and it has done a fantastic job of rendering them all. The hypertext links all work. The images are shown properly. In a pane on the left is the  table of contents. For the New York Times, the pane on the left lists all of the articles organized by sections such as "latest news," "world," "opinion," "sports," etc. 

I feel as if I now have met some of my e-reading needs. I use Calibre to convert html documents to epub format and to download newspapers and publications. I download epub books from Project Gutenberg. Then I read these in my Firefox browser, using the epub reader plugin.

What may still be a problem is buying more current books than are available at Project Gutenberg from online retailers, such as Powell Books or Kobobooks or Barnes & Noble. Or checking out books electronically in the epub format from the library.

That is because you need to use Adobe Digital Editions to download books from retailers and the library. Adobe Digital Editions provides DRM (digital rights management) support that publishers and authors demand to protect their copyrights. But, I am sorry to say, Adobe has not seen fit to make a Linux version of Adobe Digital Editions.  

So I can't say how successfully the Firefox plug-in would handle these books from commercial retailers and the public library. If anybody else has more information on this issue, I'd be happy to hear it.

I just thought I would share my discovery of Calibre's strong points and the epub reader Firefox plugin, which I heartily recommend.
