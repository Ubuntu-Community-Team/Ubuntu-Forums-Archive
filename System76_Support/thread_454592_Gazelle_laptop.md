### Post by jml on 2007-05-25
One of the benefits of buying from System 76 is they make sure everything works out of the box, so my answers are:

1.  I am pretty certain that there will be no issues with the graphics card.

2.  The web cam should work, but I do not know which applications are supported out of the box.  I would suggest sending System 76 a personal message asking them.  I sent them several messages with questions about their products before I ordered my Darter and they usually answered within 24-48 hours.

3.  As far as I know the networking protocol standards are international, (unlike cell phone systems,) so you should not have any problems on that point.

4.  Don't really know why suspend/hybernate issues arise.  There does not seem to be any pattern to the problems.  But considering the fact that once a laptop gets into the hands of the owner, different applications are installed, different updates may be installed, its not too suprising to hear about occaisional problems.

5.  The one thing that I would suggest is not do a major upgrade when it first comes out.  For example, I recieved my Darter with Ubuntu 6.10 installed.  I waited several weeks before I upgraded to 7.04.  The upgrade did cause some issues with several of System 76's products, including the Darter. I simply waited until the System 76 folks announced that the problem was fixed and they offered detailed instructions for the upgrade.  Hope this helps.

### Post by jml on 2007-05-28
It primarily depend on several factors.  If you run applications that load data from the hard drive often, then you will probably notice a difference between a 5400 rpm drive and a 7200 rpm drive all thngs being equal.  As for the difference between two 7200 rpm drives, there might be a noticable diference since there will be more disk space to search, but I suspect that you will not notice the difference in that senario.

That being said, you can reduce the impact on disk access by installing as much ram as you can afford.  That way, your system will not have to use the swap partition as often and your disk access should be less.  

Personally, when I ordered my Darter, I ordered it with the biggest 5400 rpm hard drive they offered, and the maximum amount of ram.  It runs fast enough for me, but I do not use any applications that require a lot of hard disk access.
