### Post by msrinath80 on 2010-09-28
I own the first generation lemur (lemu1). What you see on the website is a lemur too (but a refreshed model:lemu2). For my lemur, I see a practical battery life of about 2-2.5 hours (under continuous moderate usage with reasonable brightness levels). The CPU is a Core 2 Duo SU7300 (10 Watt). The new lemur (lemu2) has an 18 Watt core i3 CPU.

On the lemur, I have absolutely no problems suspending/resuming/hibernating etc. It's like this laptop was built for GNU/Linux. Compiz is smoooooth. As is GNOME. I have 4 Gigs of DDR3 memory, and I rarely, if ever see any swap usage.

Make an informed choice. But please oh please if you do get the Macbook, please don't put Linux on it. That's about the worst thing you could possibly do! OSX has been highly tuned for the Macbook and therefore is the optimal choice. I may be a free-software zealot, but when it comes down to practical use, I have to recommend OSX for the Macbook.

PS: I have never owned a Macbook. My opinions on Mac-related subjects are therefore based on discussions I've read over the past several years in such forums.

Hang in there and wait for a couple other users OR the S76 devs to respond :)

### Post by Magneus on 2010-10-26
A few weeks late to the party, but let me chime in and say two things:

1. If you love Mac OS X, get a Macbook. I have one for work (iOS development), and I can see why Apple guys love this thing. 

Sadly, it's not for Linux zealots like me. The hardware is very good, but I'm not a big fan of Mac OS. The iffy driver support in Linux is a bummer, but a bit of a non-issue for me since I use this thing for native iPad/iPhone development.

2. If you're sticking with Linux, I highly recommend the Lenovo (formerly IBM) Thinkpad. I've had two T series models (T40, T400) and they're very good. Battery life is surprisingly good. On idle, wifi off and low brightness, you can get them to ~9-10 watts, if memory serves.

If you're looking for a 12", I hear nothing but good things about the X200/X201. 

Good luck!
