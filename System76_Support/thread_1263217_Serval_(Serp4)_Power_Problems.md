### Post by bodam on 2009-09-10
I have a Serval Performance laptop (Serp4?).  That has started to have significant problems that I know are hardware related.  Whenever I push the laptop hard (e.g. video rendering, importing 6000 pictures into F-Spot, importing music collection into Banshee, etc) the laptop completely loses power.  It's like a lamp when you turn the power off.  There's no warning - just off.  When this happens, I have to disconnect the power supply for a minute or two to get the laptop to restart.  When I say dark I mean it, not even the LEDs that show the power is plugged in are lit.

Because I leave my laptop plugged in 99% of the time, I did an experiment and ran through the list of processes that typically crash the laptop while on battery power only and I have yet to have it crash while on battery power?

Any ideas here?  Could it be as simple as replacing the power adapter or is it more likely to be something in the laptop itself?  Do you even sell the power adapter as a one-off item.

### Post by thomasaaron on 2009-09-11
Yes, remove the bottom panel and blow out the dust bunnies.

Also, if you have your battery in and your ac adapter connected when it shuts off, and your battery has a charge, it's *probably* not the ac adapter. Definitely sounds like overheating.
