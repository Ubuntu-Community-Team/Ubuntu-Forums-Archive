### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2013-09-09
Check the SMART status of the drive. It can sometimes detect disk problem before a catastrophic failure but not always. To be on the safe side backup anything you don't want to lose. I personally wouldn't replace a disk if there are no SMART red flags and it still functions normally, but I also already have everything important backed up on a regular basis so I'm just considering downtime and not potential loss of data.
