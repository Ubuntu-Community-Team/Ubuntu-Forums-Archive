### Post by thomasaaron on 2008-02-07
Wireless cards do work out of the box.

For an exact list of what is supported, you can click on the item on the website and check out the list just to the left of it. If it's not on the list, it is not supported. For example, for the current Gazelle...

Display: 14.1" Widescreen WXGA (1280 x 800)
Graphics Card: Intel GMA X3100 or nVidia GeForce 8400M GS 256 MB
Audio Output: Realtek ALC 268
Networking: Ethernet (LAN), WiFi
Wireless: 802.11 agn
Expansion: Express Card
Ports: VGA, 4x USB 2.0, MIC-In, Headphone Out, FireWire 1394
Card Reader: 3 in 1 Card Reader
Battery: 6 Cell Lithium Ion
Battery Life: 3.5 hours
Dimensions: 13.26" x 9.65" x 1.45" (W x D x H)
Weight: 5.4 lbs.

So, since it has no s-video listed, that port is not supported.
Also, if you upgrade it to the nVidia/webcam option, the web cam would be supported.

### Post by thomasaaron on 2008-02-08
> I see that this is true of the SERP2, as well...

Yep. Same.

> Can you recommend a modem?

Honestly, not really. I've never used a dial-up modem with any of our systems. Interestingly, I've never heard back from any customers who were trying to set up dial-up modems. It would be nice if a customer who has done this with a System76 computer could write a how-to. Seems like there are more people asking about it these days. Odd.

> Why USB in particular? 

Seems well enough documented. I've been referring people to this for a while...
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UsbAdslModem](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UsbAdslModem)
...and I've never had anyone come back and say it didn't work. Of course, nobody has ever told me it works either.:(

> Do you know of any cardbus modems that might work on the SERP2? I would like to bring faxing capabilities with me on my laptop.

There was a PCMCIA card Modem called "The Gold Card" that did work with a number of our older computers, including the SerP2. (Hey, I guess I *can* recommend one!!)
NOTE: This will not work on the new line-up as they have Express card slots, NOT PCMCIA slots.
