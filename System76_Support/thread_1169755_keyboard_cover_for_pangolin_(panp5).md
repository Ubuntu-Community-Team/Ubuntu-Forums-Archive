### Post by HotShotDJ on 2009-08-19
> **PGScooter said:**
> I just thought that kids would have trouble understanding that they can't use the keyboard attached to the screen.Elementary school kids, even the very young ones in Kindergarten, are very good at understanding the idea of "don't touch."  (Drunk adults, not so much).  All you need to do is point to the laptop keyboard and tell them in a very serious (but friendly) voice -- "this keyboard is only for grown-ups, but I have this one especially for kids!  Isn't it cool?" -- then just make sure there is adult supervision.  Although there is always one or two in the group that might "forget," the rest of the kids will gasp and say "I'm telling!" if they get anywhere near the laptop itself.

Something like [THIS]("http://www.google.com/products/catalog?hl=en&safe=off&q=flexible+keyboard&cid=6658121900308964173&sa=title#p") or [THIS]("http://www.google.com/products/catalog?hl=en&safe=off&q=flexible+keyboard&cid=9352968758612685290#p") might be good.  It is virtually indestructible and the kids will think it is the coolest thing they ever saw.
