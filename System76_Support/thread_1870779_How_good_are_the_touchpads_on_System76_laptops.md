### Post by mcpetersen.dsm on 2011-10-29
I have a Core Duo Macbook Pro that I've installed Ubuntu (10.10, then 11.04, now 11.10) on. I'm about to remove that, reinstall Snow Leopard and put the machine in the closet until I have a specific need for it. Much of the reason is the trackpad, which I have been unable to configure to perform consistently and without "goofy" behavior. That may just be my failure to understand the settings, but I don't think so.

Five months ago I got a System76 Serval Pro laptop and have had absolutely no problems with the trackpad (or anything else, for that matter). In contrast with the Macbook, the Serval's trackpad operates very smoothly and consistently. There's no jumping around (after changing the settings to disable clicks with the touchpad). I've been very pleased with the machine and the track/touchpad.

### Post by mgolden on 2011-11-03
I just got a Gazelle Pro and find the trackpad a bit on the sensitive side, but it seems comparable to what I have seen on other PCs.  Sometimes it misses the multitouch.  I am not dissatisfied with it.

My wife had a MacBook and the trackpad got very wonky over time.  In fact, it was because the battery was dying and was expanding, pushing against the trackpad from the bottom.

One thing I like quite a bit is that the synaptic trackpad manager makes it very simple to TURN OFF the trackpad when a mouse is present, which is a boon.
