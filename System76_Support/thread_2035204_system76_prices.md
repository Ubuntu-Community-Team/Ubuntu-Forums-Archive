### Post by isantop on 2012-07-31
> **Thisislaw said:**
>  As I said above, I would like to see another company try to have low-cost Ubuntu laptops.

The issue with this is that any company that did it would go under quickly. ASUS, Dell, and larger companies (who only [or predominantly] do Windows machines) can afford to do this because they can pump money into staying afloat. But most consumers would rather pay more for an awesome product than pay less for a crappy one. The more expensive machine is always a better use of the money, since there's less frustration and longer lifetime.

Combine that with the fact that smaller companies (like System76) are generally built on their reputations. Selling "cheap" computers wouldn't increase sales, it would hurt it by hurting the company's reputation and the reputation of Ubuntu.
