### Post by joe4ska on 2012-06-10
I can only say that the Crucial M4 SSDs tend to have more positive reviews on sites like Newegg.com and from reviewers on YouTube. [Tom's Hardware]("http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/m4-ssd-capacity-comparison,2957-11.html") was positive as well. However, Intel is more Linux friendly so it might be a tradeoff if firmware ever needs to be updated.

I for one would get the Crucial M4 128GB rather than Intel's 120GB drive.

This thread on Tom's discusses the [intel vs Crucial debate]("http://www.tomshardware.com/forum/284760-32-intel-crucial") in detail. In the end it comes down to the part's warranty.

I figure the Intel 520 might be the more compatible but, you know, twice the storage.  The jump to the Intel 240 GB is a little much for me. You are far more likely to run into firmware issues with the Intel drive, because it doesn't use an in-house Intel controller. Instead, it uses a SandForce controller, which historically has been highly unreliable. Just the other week, it was discovered that the specific controller used in the Intel 520 series (SF-2281) doesn't support AES-256 encryption, despite SandForce claiming that it does. In light of this, Intel recently offered all owners of a 520 drive a full refund.
