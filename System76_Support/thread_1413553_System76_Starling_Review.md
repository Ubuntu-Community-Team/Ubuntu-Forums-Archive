### Post by whistlingtony on 2010-02-22
Ok,

So, I wanted a netbook, and I didn't want any money to go Microsoft's way. I shopped, and it came down to either the Starling by System76, or a Dell netbook. I couldn't decide... The Dell was $300, the Starling $370, but I wanted to vote with my dollar. I considered that giving the money to Dell would show them that there is a market for this sort of thing. In the end though, I decided to give my money to the little guy.

The Starling has some good features.

* A proper right shift key. Hey, the Asus EEEpc had a tiny annoying right shift key, and it made typing rather annoying. 

* A good solid 4.5 hours of battery life.

* Nice little touches. The Ubuntu key, etc. 

* The starling came with beautiful documentation. I really like that and want to encourage that.

* System76 is very responsive. They're on these forums, and they answer emails well, and they seem to want to try to be "good folks".

However, the Starling has some problems.

* Wireless is Hinky. It uses the RTL8187B wireless card, and the drives suck. System76 has a driver... add on... thingy. It doesn't seem to work for me. I'm sitting in my living room, I can see the router, and I get dropped connections. Forget it if I leave the room. This is a very basic functionality that I expect to work well in a netbook. They should have gone with a different wireless card.

On a related note, hey System76, why don't you put your fixes into an apt repository, so they'd get updated automatically and we wouldn't have to think about them.

* SD slot. Hey, I put in an SD card... it doesn't work. I think the hardware is old, and doesn't recognize newer cards. Lame.... Just to test it, I put in an 8MEG (that's meg, not gig) SD card from the days of yore. It didn't see it either. My other computers can read it...

* The battery falls out. The latches come loose at the drop of a hat, and the battery constantly falls off inside my bag. This Sucks. I complained, and the recommended solution was to "put tape over the latches". Ok, yeah, that would work, but... That's just stupid.

* Sound output is Shite. There's an audible hum on the line that's really annoying... I'm willing to blame PulseAudio for this one, but only because I really hate PulseAudio (and sound daemons in general)

* The "/" key is small and hard to get to. It's a small thing, but when you do a lot of command line work, the "/" key gets used a lot. 

* It's kind of loud. The EEEpc was too, so maybe it's just a form factor thing.

I did ask for a refund, and was denied. Hey, it's cool, I know they barely made a profit selling the thing to me, but I even offered to take a partial refund. 

There are good things and bad things. If asked today though, I'd recommend the Dell. Some of these things are stupid gripes, I know, but the battery falling out, the sound, and the wireless issues are big problems.... I still think System76 are "good folks" and probably their other boxes don't have problems, but I can't recommend the Starling netbook.

-Tony

### Post by bvandev on 2010-02-25
I got my Starling in October and have had no regrets.  It's a fine little machine.  Yes, the / key is a little small, as are the comma and period, but I've become accustomed to them.  All netbook keyboards are small and include tradeoffs.

I'm having a great time with my Starling, using if for all of my home tasks, including programming.  Yes, my battery did fall out once when jostled around in my backpack, but I just put it back in.  No harm done.

The wireless works quite well, providing a good connection at 50 feet, definitely better now on Karmic than on Jaunty.

The SD card slot works fine with my 2GB card, though I've read it doesn't have the new protocols necessary to read newer, larger cards.  (I don't know much about SD card technology.)

In short, I was happy enough with my Starling to get another for my son.

Most of all though, the support offered by System76 is excellent.  This very forum and the attention paid to it by System76 provides more and better support than Dell or any other vendor provides for day to day usage and configuration problems. 

My two cents.

I have never seen such customer service anywhere. Anyone who says S76 has poor CS is either lying or hasn't really dealt with them.

### Post by isantop on 2011-05-16
> **EJJ said:**
> As far as the batter falling out...one part of the latch is not spring loaded, so you might want to double check this. I've never had the battery fall out.

Just to clarify, there are two latches on the battery. One is a snapping lock that stays in a particular position (either locked or unlocked). This one locks the battery in place. The other is spring loaded, and will always return to the locked position when released. This one releases the unlocked battery.
