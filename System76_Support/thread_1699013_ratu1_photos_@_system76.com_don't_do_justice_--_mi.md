### Post by StephenDavison on 2011-03-03
My black Ratel Ultra was delivered on Monday, and I'm very excited about this computer.  The Lian-Li case is nicer than one would think from the System76 website photos.  The brushed aluminum exterior was a significant factor for me buying this machine.  There is a silver and black system76 logo beneath the front USB ports (missing on the website photos).  The case actually looks better with the logo than without it.  *The front USB ports appear to be mounted upside down, but it looks like that can be corrected, perhaps after the warranty expires.*  The power button lights up bright blue when turned on.  The small reset button also lights in red for HDD activity.  All things considered, I think it looks much better than the alternatives I considered (Zareason MediaBox or Silverstone SG-05/06).  Also, there is no case fan to make noise.  Compared to my last desktop machine, I can hardly believe how quiet it is.  

I ordered the Ratel sans optical drive because I loathe a CD/DVD drive tray.  For now I have a Sony Optiarc (AD-7690H) slot loader mounted in a USB2 enclosure sitting atop the Ratel. The [Syba Connectland enclosure's]("http://www.amazon.com/Syba-Connectland-CL-ENC55001-5-25-Inch-Enclosure/dp/B002NKM1XE") brushed aluminum finish matches almost exactly to the Ratel: it is just slightly darker black.  And I can still use the drive with the netbook should I wish to.

Peeking through the side mesh and looking around back, I see the 1.5TB hard drive is Western Digital, and the 380W 80plus power supply is from Antec.  I'm happy to see the WD drive as they've always served me well.  I don't know, but it looks like there's room for a 2.5" drive underneath the 3.5" one?  Swapping out the 3.5" drive for 2x2.5" might be worth looking into too.

The hard drive is divided into two (or three depending on how you count) partitions.  A single primary partition consuming all but 3.5GB of the drive provides a root ext4 file system.  The remaining 3.5GB is an extended partition containing one logical partition for swap space. I like a separate /home file system, so soon I'll have to try booting from a USB live disk to "fix" it.

System76 delivers Ubuntu with GnuCash installed.  Other than that and the System76 Driver Installer in the System->Administration menu, it looks like a standard Ubuntu Desktop distribution.

I have this ratu1 connected to a 24" 1080p ViewSonic monitor by HDMI. The monitor has built in speakers, but they're not so great (and that's being generous).  Nevertheless, I'm getting sound out of it after changing the audio profile from Analog Stereo Output to Digital Stereo (HDMI) Output. I just had Ratatouille playing off to the side while doing other things and it looked great.

So far I'm very pleased with this purchase.

PS: I just learned that ratel is Afrikaans for honey badger, and that this is one [bad *** animal]("http://www.badassoftheweek.com/honeybadger.html").
