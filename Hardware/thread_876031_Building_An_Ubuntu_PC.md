### Post by blazercist on 2008-07-31
> **phidia said:**
> There's a hardware database [here]("http://www.linuxquestions.org/hcl/") that covers just about everything. Google is also a great tool-when you find a component and want to know if there are problems search the manufacture name and model with "linux".

No offense but that guide doesn't cover nearly everything it doesn't list half the devices I have in my box that are supported and working just fine.

The second part of the statement is probably your best bet, use google.

Aside from that I can tell you that there are only a few caviats when considering hardware for linux, at least the main hardware.

You can use almost any Mobo you want, almost any CPU you want, x86, x86_64, dual cores, quad cores, AMD, Intel, it really doesn't matter.

As far as Video Cards go, I would go with something new (not bleeding edge new, but maybe a month or two old) and I would recommend Nvidia.  Mind you that ATI/Nvidia dispute is a hot topic in these forums and ATI has improved significantly in the past few months but they still have a way to go IMO before they match the reliability of Nvidia's drivers.

Ethernet doesn't matter what card you use although 99% it will be some generic built-in to your mobo so no need to worry.

Internal Harddrive should be no problem, unless you want to do some crazy RAID it should be plug it in and just works, SATA/IDE/etc doesn't really matter.

RAM obviously makes no difference.

Monitor makes no difference, other than the fact that if you are planning on dual displays I would say that you MUST use an Nvidia video card. (ATI will work but Nvidia support for dual monitors is just plain better)

If you are planning on using Wifi then you need to do some research.

Printers: you need to do some research.

Mouse/Keyboard: USB/PS2 usually no problems at all, but if you are going to go wireless/bluetooth I suggest you do some research.

Can't think of anything else.
