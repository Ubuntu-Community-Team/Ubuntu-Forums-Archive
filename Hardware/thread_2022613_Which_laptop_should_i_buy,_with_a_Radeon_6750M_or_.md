### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-07-11
I'd pick the ASUS because of the NVIDIA card and good experiences with ASUS products.  Also it appears to have just the one adapter rather than using "Optimus" with both an Intel and an NVIDIA adapter. These newer hybrid laptops can be a pain in the neck to get working with Linux.  For details, see the "[Bumblebee]("http://bumblebee-project.org/")" project.

My daughter's HP dv6t laptop has the same ATI 6750 card as the Samsung.  I could only [get it to work]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11244073&postcount=2") by upgrading the firmware and changing the BIOS to "fixed" graphics.  However there have been rapid [developments]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1930450") on the ATI side as well.  I just don't follow them as closely.

### Post by Ubun2to on 2012-07-11
I got my ATI card to work just fine with proprietary drivers installed (dual monitors).
[http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/linux/Pages/radeon_linux.aspx](http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/linux/Pages/radeon_linux.aspx)
Also, AMD is supporting this card for Linux.
[http://askubuntu.com/questions/86221/does-ubuntu-support-radeon-hd-6750m-or-6490m](http://askubuntu.com/questions/86221/does-ubuntu-support-radeon-hd-6750m-or-6490m)
The NVidia 650M is supported on Linux.
[http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux-display-amd64-295.59-driver.html](http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux-display-amd64-295.59-driver.html)
Also, keep in mind that these are 64 bit drivers. I'm assuming you're getting 64 bit, as it's hard to find a laptop without at least 4 GB of RAM these days. It seems they are equally matched, so you could probably get either one.
I hope this helps.
