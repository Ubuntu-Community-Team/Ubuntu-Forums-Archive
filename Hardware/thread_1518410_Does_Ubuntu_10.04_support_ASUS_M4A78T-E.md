### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-26
Should work fine (but IIRC, I've seen a complaint or two about the sound quality).

But I wouldnt get that board.....the newer 8XX series ATI chipsets are out now, with SATAIII support (which will be very nice in a year or two, as some SSDs are already faster than SATAII can support). A lot of the newer 8XX series motherboards have USB3.0 as well. 

Also, the 8XX series board with onboard video have upgraded cards, so if you want to use the onboard video they are better boards......and if you dont want onboard video, get a 870 chipset board, they come without onbaord video.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-26
Yes, they work fine with linux distros. 

BTW, you have to be careful- some of the 8XX chipset motherboards have old SB7XX southbridges, and the SB7XX only has SATAII. Personally, I wouldnt get an asus myself, but if you really want an asus board with onbaord video, there are 2 choices- 

M4A89GTD Pro 

M4A88TD-V Evo 

The 89GTD has a slightly faster video chip, the 88TD-V has 128MB 'sideport' memory (for the video chip) onboard.
