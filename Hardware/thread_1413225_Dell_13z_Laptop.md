### Post by TBABill on 2010-11-29
I think that machine would be great. If I'm not mistaken, the 1501 Dell wireless is a Broadcom device. Not necessarily a bad choice, but if you want it to just work out of the box you could check with Dell to see if they have an Intel wireless device. That way you won't require a proprietary driver and it should be much easier to just work. I have searched and cannot confirm the chipset of the 1501 device but perhaps a call to Dell to configure by phone would save some install pain later with the Broadcom drivers. They work fine for me, but they're more work than my laptop with an Intel wireless device that just works on install. May be worth a few bucks and a few minutes of time to ask and order, if available.
 
My signature has a link to the compatibility list for devices compatible with Ubuntu. Dell has more than any other manufacturer so you are usually safe with one of their laptops.
