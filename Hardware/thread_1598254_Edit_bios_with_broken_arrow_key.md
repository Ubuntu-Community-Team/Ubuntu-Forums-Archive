### Post by Brian88 on 2010-10-16
If the up & down arrow does not broke, you can temporarily take those keys out and place it on the place of the left and right arrow. 

For long-term replacement, you can buy another keyboard for your laptop if your laptop is fairly new -- or buy another laptop (preferably a broken one) with the exact keyboard as yours, and take their buttons.

It should work, if your keyboard is an PS/2 one. Most laptops had USB Legacy support disabled on default, hence an USB keyboard could not be used for editing the BIOS without enabling this option first.

Some Phoenix BIOSes had mouse-controllable BIOS setup, you can use your touchpad to set the BIOS.
