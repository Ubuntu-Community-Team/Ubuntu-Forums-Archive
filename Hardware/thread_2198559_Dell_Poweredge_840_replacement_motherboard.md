### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-01-09
It's 90% compatible.  Only until you pull it apart and try to fit things in you will find one hole, or one connector, or one slot position that is slightly out-of-whack.  So you may need to not use that hole, or modify/swap/solder a connector, or shave a card to get things to fit.  If it works without modification, then you have lucked out.  If you have to make some modifications, that is the price you paid with a cheap machine.

I don't think Dell does this on purpose.  Dell machines generally have good access features--slide out cradles, power supplies held with one screw, etc.  It makes field repairs of these computers simple.  Some of these access features require modification of standard ATX fixtures, so you get a motherboard that is 90% compatible and some frustration.
