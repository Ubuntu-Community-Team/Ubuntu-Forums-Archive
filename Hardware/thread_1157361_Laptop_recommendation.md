### Post by Scipio_Publius on 2009-05-12
Personally I avoid the laptop bandwagon unless 
1. I have to be mobile with it.
2. they are free or close to it


If you thinking of it in terms of a desktop replacement or a game machine or dammit you must have one then go with something popular and buy the longest warranty as you can.

The reason I say this is because laptops in many cases have proprietary parts.  So if it breaks parts are often hard to come by and repairs are way to expensive.  I regular tower or a minit tower has pretty much multiple vendors that make standard parts.  Much better situation.

At any rate I have a Toshiba Satelite which has lasted me 2 years so far and runs Ubuntu well.  I just picked up an Acer Aspire One netbook which I am still have some trouble getting everthing working on it with Ubuntu...might have to go back to XP on that one.
