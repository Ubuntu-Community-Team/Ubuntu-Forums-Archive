### Post by GhettoMrBob on 2013-06-02
ThinkPads have very good build quality and a fantastic keyboard. If you're doing a lot of typing, I'd pick the ThinkPad hands down.

I work at a shop that services all makes (including Apple) and personally would never spend on a laptop with bad build quality. Lenovo (specifically with their ThinkPads) and Apple by far have the best build quality in my opinion. There's a lot to be said for having a sturdy chassis vs cheap, plastic chassis that flex/break.

All that aside, the T420's are pretty fantastic machines as well and can be picked up for quite a bit cheaper than the T430's if you shop around (maybe those savings could go towards that SSD).

Also, if it means anything, the T430 is an Ubuntu certified laptop.

I don't have any experience with Crucial per say but I can recommend against OCZ. We ordered a batch of OCZ's (various makes/models) for client upgrades.
