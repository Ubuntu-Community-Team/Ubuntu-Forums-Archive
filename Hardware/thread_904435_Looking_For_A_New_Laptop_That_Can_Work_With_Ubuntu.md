### Post by nicedude on 2008-08-30
To all ASUS is a top notch company that has been making quality motherboards for years and I have used them in Windows builds for years. When I saw they are super linux friendly I felt stupid for buying an Acer 5520 but I have gotten my acer to do everything I need but it was allot of work to write custom battery power savings script and to enable my wifi with madwifi custom driver ( learned allot though in the process ) and in future I am only going to buy form top name that also support linux fairly. If there are small companies out there making laptops with Ubuntu then just do your homework as to what quality level of components they use before purchase, they could be great but if they go broke etc and disappear then so would any waranty or support so just be sure what ever brand you buy is going to be there and that they care about Linux compatibility.

So before you buy a certain model google the crap out of it and see what kind of reviews and Linux user feedback you can find before making a purchase, that way it will be much harder for you to get burned :-)
