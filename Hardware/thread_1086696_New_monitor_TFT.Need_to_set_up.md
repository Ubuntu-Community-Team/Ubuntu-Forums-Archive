### Post by kestrel1 on 2009-03-04
I must say that is news to me. Been using Ubuntu with my monitor for about two years now & not seen any sign of the monitor giving up.
I would say that who ever said that was scare mongering. If a monitor is going to go phut it will go phut, if it is conected to an OS using xorg or not. I personally wouldn't listen to this sort of thing. I have used computers for many years, I have had monitors die, but this is due to age & nothing else. You might get the occasional one that dies in it's early life, but you will be fairly unlucky. I have monitors at work that have been kicking around for ten plus years (CRT) & still going. The newer monitors that we are getting in come with a three year rtb warranty, so they are covered. I cannot see a manufacturer trying to say that because you actually used the monitor on a computer running an OS other than Windows, that was not approved by them that they wouldn't repair it.
