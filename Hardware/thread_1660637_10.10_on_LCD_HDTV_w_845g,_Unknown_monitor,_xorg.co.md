### Post by jeffcox65 on 2011-01-05
Howdy -

I installed ubuntu 10.10 on an old Dell Gx20 connected to my living room LCD HDTV that is capable of 1366x768 resolution. lspci reports that the Dell has 82845 onboard graphics.

The default installation booted the screen to 1024x768 (which is better than 800x600) but the screen was very jittery, so I knew I needed to do something about that resolution.

Since it's on my living room LCD HDTV I want to play DVDs on it, so I popped in a DVD. Totem started successfully, but only achieved about 2 frames per second. I installed VLC and it was able to play a DVD fullscreen -- nice!

This is when I began trying to change the screen resolution to stop the jitteriness. I brought up the Monitor Preferences and it reports MONITOR UNKNOWN and gives me no options for changing the resolution or refresh rate. So now I know I have to play around with xorg.conf.

But I can't find xorg.conf anywhere. I poke around and discover there isn't one by default so I create one by booting to recovery mode and typing # X -configure

I mv the resulting xorg.conf.new to /etc/X11/xorg.conf and reboot.

Now Monitors Preferences gives me plenty of options for resolution, so I pick 1368x768 and the jitteriness stops immediately. Nice!

When I go to play a DVD, Totem exits immediately. VLC opens the DVD and plays audio, but there's no video.

Okay, so here are my questions:

- why was the default configuration, before the generated xorg.conf, good enough to play DVD video, but the generated xorg.conf breaks DVD video playback?

- why does Monitor Preferences always report MONITOR UNKNOWN? Where does it get the monitor definition if not xorg.conf? Am I supposed to be telling ubuntu somewhere about my monitor? How did it determine supported resolutions while configuring xorg.conf but not before?

- how do I determine what was correct for playing video in the default configuration, before configuring X? how do I convince VLC that nothing has changed and that it's okay to play video?

In searching for answers, I found lots of old (2006, 2007, 2008 ) responses about configuring xorg.conf to work with 845g onboard graphics, but with xorg.conf missing from 10.10 and then auto-generating one, I feel like I took one step forward (found a good nonjittery resolution), then one step back (no dvd video), and I'm not sure what steps to take next.

I will post the log entries for booting without xorg.conf, then the generated xorg.conf, and then the log entries for booting with the xorg.conf in the next reply.

Thanks in advance.

- Jeff
