### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2010-06-26
The metal cage and rubber inserts are likely for protection from vibration and impact. Never heard of a heating element for a hard disk, but I guess its possible. Note if you replace the disk with one that doesn't have an accelerometer then the drive will be vulnerable to damage from impacts unlike the factory hard disk. Most notebooks meant for rugged use will have disks that automatically park their heads if the accelerometer senses an impending impact (like zero G when the notebook is falling). Very few disks on the market have this feature. Cheers!
