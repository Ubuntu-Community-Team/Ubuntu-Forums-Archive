### Post by DFlame on 2009-09-03
This is really a personal preference decision. You'd have to take your current funds, the cost of a repair and the cost of a replacement into consideration.

I had a Toshiba Satellite P10-304 laptop about a year ago. The HDD failed a month in and the battery went too, about 4 months later. I finally decided to make a decision during the fourth year of service, when the soldering around the power supply connector degraded.

A new battery and a repair job (soldering is **not** my forte) would have cost somewhere close to £150-£200. I decided to get a new one instead and sell the Toshiba as spare parts, making about £70 from its component pieces.

My new laptop (in the sig) cost £600 and is coming up to the end of its first year with no signs of slowing down. If I had continued to use my old one, I think it would have only been a very short amount of time before something else went wrong too.

I'd buy new. Selling the old one can ease the costs a little and of course, new equipment means new warranty. That's something you don't get with a 4 year old ;)
