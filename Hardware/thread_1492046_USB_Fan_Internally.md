### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-24
Depending on the card, it might be safe to run without a fan- the lower end cards are more likely to survive, gaming cards will probably die faster. 

The USB fan isnt getting the video card anywhere near as much airflow as the original fan was. You could always just get a 40mm fan (if thats the right size) and bolt it back on (whcih could be hard if the previous owner has thrown away the bolts... Or you could get a zalman ZB123 (fan bracket that 'floats' a fan over the PCI slots, or CPU). 

BTW, whoever screwed that computer togther had some really weird reason for doing what they did, or doesnt know anything about computer building. Putting a (full height) sound card next to the video card isnt a good idea. Better to have the soundcard in the bottom PCI slot, which is free. Or placing one of the other, smaller cards next to the video card would have given much better airflow- and its possible that the noise from the card was caussed in part by how close the soundcard is. 

I'd be a bit sus on that being a 'high-end' video card- looks like a ATI all-in-wonder to me. Its also a VIA motherboard chipset, and quite possibly a VIA Kt266a/333/400 from the look of it, so maybe he meant 'high end video card circa 2003-5'.
