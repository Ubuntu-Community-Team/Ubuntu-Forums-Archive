### Post by 4Play on 2009-07-16
Indeed the current kernel's touch screen support is not the best, but expect Ubuntu 9.10 (Karmic) to have very good touch support. I have a dell latitude XT (touchscreen tablet) and I installed the 2.6.30 kernel from karmic and the touchscreen works even better than in vista (snappier, more accurate response). To top it off, there are several pretty cool apps out there for touch (easystroke, etc). Multitouch is still in the works, but there are a couple of patches milling around the mailing lists that implement it.

Just a side note on the whole 'DIY' approach: open source is great for many reasons, but being able to improve the operating system yourself is one that very, very few people can make use of. Just writing a driver for linux is a pretty daunting task, I study computer engineering and have a very hard time figuring out the ins and outs of the monolithic linux kernel.
