IMO....

WAY too much for a mobo.  check out this one [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813157229&Tpk=ASRock%20P67%20EXTREME4%20%28B3%29%20LGA%201155](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813157229&Tpk=ASRock%20P67%20EXTREME4%20%28B3%29%20LGA%201155). ### Post by oldfred on 2011-10-29
Your initial selections were the top end which may be appropriate for a gamer or developer who needs the very best. But I do not need either and look for a system one or two levels down from the top. Price is a lot lower and performance is still good at that was the top end system 1 or 2 years ago. 

I would also add at least one more HD and since cost does not look like an issue a boot SSD. The SSD can only be 60GB or less and just used for booting. All data is on the spinning drives, but you want two as all drives eventually fail.

### Post by b0b138 on 2011-10-29
That psu is fine.  Pretty much all the corsairs are good.  You can drop the price a few bucks and get one a bit less wattage and without the fancy blue light.  You really just want to make sure it provides enough wattage to power your system.  Heres a general wattage calculator. [http://educations.newegg.com/tool/psucalc/index.html](http://educations.newegg.com/tool/psucalc/index.html)  Having a second video really jumps up the usage, so if that may be a possibility either now or in the future, go ahead and get a high watt psu.
