### Post by pixel2 on 2014-03-06
Since its HP Proliant microserver I'd go for some WD drive (1TB and above) if one, or two WDs (2*500 GB) if two. They are reasonably priced and (whats even more important) they do work well in RAID's (whereas many other HDD - Samsung, Seagate etc - does not work so well in RAID)

### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-03-06
You will go crazy reading the reviews.  Every disk model has problems.  There can be a good brand, but a small, bad batch of drives that cause issues and lots of bad review posts.  There can be a cheap, crappy brand, but decent reliability, but you won't hear about that because nobody bothers to write reviews when things are working.  Green drives don't tend to work well in RAID configuration.  For a media server, just about anything will work, including mud and straw stuffed into the slot.  

I have a couple of old Dell PowerEdge rack servers and I found some used disk drives for $6 each.  Out of 12 drives only one was bad.  The rest were fine and make nice hot-swap backups.  If you go with mud and straw, you want quality mud.
