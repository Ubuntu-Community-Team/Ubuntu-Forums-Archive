### Post by Futant on 2012-08-29
There are many good laptops, it all depends on what you need. I would say get a refurbished one, you can get a lot more for your money - I got a Dell XPS L502X fro the refurbished section of Dell's website for half the price it usually goes for. I'd never even heard of Ubuntu at the time but dual booted on the advice of a friend and now I never use Windows at all. Hardly had to do much tweaking, Ubuntu 11.10 and now 12.04 worked 'out of the box'. It's one of he best laptops I've used (out of not many, admittedly) sturdy, reliable and the speakers are brilliant!

### Post by yeehi on 2012-08-30
PCSpecialist is great for configuring a laptop. Novatech make good systems, too. They have good customer service and will let you try out a live CD on a system before you try it, if you go to one of their branches.

Do you really need a laptop, though? Bang for buck, it is much better to get a desktop. How often do you expect to carry around your computer? Every day? Maybe you should get a tablet for that. (There is a new [Nexus 7]("https://play.google.com/store/devices/details?id=nexus_7_8gb&feature=nexus7_campaign").) A tablet would be much more convenient for carrying around. 

If you do go the desktop route and want to get a good configuration, try [overclockers]("http://www.overclockers.co.uk/productlist.php?groupid=43&catid=1951"). They have a fantastic selection of options and good prices.

Alternatively, you could try and go for the really cheapest desktop route and buy the components and put them together yourself. I think the cheapest way to do that would be to shop at [TigerDirect]("http://www.tigerdirect.com/"). They ship to the UK.

If you are building a desktop yourself, I would say the most important component is the monitor. Don't cut corners there. Get a  nice IPS one! (Check reviews.)
