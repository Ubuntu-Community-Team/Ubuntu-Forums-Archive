### Post by cascade9 on 2010-09-07
About the only choices you would have (at least as far as nVidia goes) would be 9500/9400GT, 8400GS, 6200, which is as low as I would even think of going). FX5200, MX4000 and MX400 PCI cards arent hard to find, but in the case of the MX400/4000 cards, the proprietary drivers wont be supported by the newest versions of ubuntu, and its only a question of time untill they wont be supported by any linux distro. FX cards were pretty awful, and probably best avoided. 

IIRC, according to nVidia, the 8400GS needs a 350watt or higher power supply unit. That IS nVidia playing 'pya' (protect your butt LOL) as the maximum draw on a 8400GS is around the 70watt mark. 

As for the manufacturer....depends. Almost everybody who has worked with hardware for a while has favourite brans, and brands they wont touch unless its totaly necessary. But it shouldnt make a huge amount of difference to be honest.

*edit- sparkle a 'off brand'? I'd actually rate sparkle higher than PNY myself......
