### Post by him610 on 2017-06-09
Hello millers.dada and welcome to the forum,

Dell, HP, and Lenovo all offer All-in-One PCs for the size you want from around $550 upwards. I have never owned one, but there should be no problem installing Ubuntu on current models, or even last year's models if you can find them.

Here is a link that lists five sellers that sell systems with pre-installed Linux,  [http://www.zdnet.com/article/the-top-five-linux-desktop-vendors/]("http://www.zdnet.com/article/the-top-five-linux-desktop-vendors/")

You might want to check the manufacturer's websites - dell.com, hp.com, or lenovo.com for product descriptions; although, their primary systems sales are Windows machines.

Another option would be check online sellers - amazon.com, newegg.com, etc. Newegg seems to have the most detailed product specifications.

It is encouraging to hear of someone using Ubuntu in an office environment. All-in-one PCs are sort of a niche market (although they make a lot of sense from in an office environment) , and to find one with Ubuntu pre-installed might be difficult. A few years ago, System76 offered one, but I checked its website and found none listed. You may have to settle on buying a Windows All-in-One and then installing Ubuntu on it.

Yet, another option might be to buy a mini-computer (these are really small) and mount on the rear of your chosen monitor. Refer to [https://system76.com/desktops/meerkat]("https://system76.com/desktops/meerkat")
System76 will sell you both, the mini-pc and a 24" monitor with hdmi cable and mount for less than $800. You might consider calling System76 Sales.

Hope you find what you are looking for.
