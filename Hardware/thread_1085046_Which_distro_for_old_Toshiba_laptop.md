### Post by smilinggeek on 2009-03-02
I'm no expert yet, but what I did was make a bunch of bootable CDs of a number of different distributions of Linux, BSD, even OpenSolaris, then booted my machine with all of them to figure out which one would work the best on that machine.

I picked Ubuntu 8.10 for an Acer Aspire 5100, but it was a close run with Mepis Linux and a couple others. Just took my time to test things out and see which one worked best "out of the box", because I didn't have much interest in faffing about with editing obscure files in the depths of the system. 

I suggest you do the same, then pick the distro that works best for you on that machine.
