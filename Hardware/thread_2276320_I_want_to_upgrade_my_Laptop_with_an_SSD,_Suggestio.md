### Post by kerry_s on 2015-05-01
just get what you can afford. you want at least 32gb minimum. what you need to watch out for is read & write speed.
i'm currently using a sd card sata adapter for my drive, traded a friend for it. it has 2 90mb/s sd cards that it runs in a raid 0. so i got to do a little extra work when installing. it works just fine though, feels just like the ssd i have in my other laptop.

i think it's just the no moving parts thing that makes the most difference, so read & write speed is what you should look for mainly.

### Post by efflandt on 2015-05-01
As long as your laptop does SATA it should not be a problem. When I first got an 80 GB Intel SSD (sometime before 11.04 was released) I put it in my old laptop from 2006 to try it out and put Linux on it. On SATA1 it was 4 times faster than the original 5400 rpm drive in the laptop. When I put it in my SATA2 desktop it was twice as fast as on SATA1 (and twice as fast as my 7500 rpm desktop drive). But they are likely faster than that now.

I recently put a 512 GB Crucial M550 mSATA SSD card (tiny little thing) in my MSI gaming laptop to install 14.04 and to boot grub from, with enough room for Steam games. That worked without a hitch. Its hard drive has Win7 and 13.10 that I had installed earlier that I will either switch back to a data partition or use some of for swap (w/8 GB RAM I am currently not using any swap).

Some helpful hints for optimizing SSD for Ubuntu [https://sites.google.com/site/easylinuxtipsproject/ssd](https://sites.google.com/site/easylinuxtipsproject/ssd)
