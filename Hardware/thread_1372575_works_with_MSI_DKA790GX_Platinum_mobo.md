### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-05
Yes, everything should run. 

I wouldnt get that motherboard myself.....790GX is onboard video. Which you really dont need if you going to run a video card, and the price difference between a MSI 790GX platinum motherboard and a decent 770 chipset motherboard and a video card would be virtually nothing, or the 770+ video card would be cheaper. 

Unless you are planning on using an older AM2/AM2+ CPU, an AM3 socket (with DDR2 or DDR3) would a better idea.

Flash can be a bit more of a pain with 64bit, but normally its not that bad. I'd use 64bit myself, its a lot faster on some tasks.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-05
780 is onboard video as well. 

Here is the breakdown, simplified- 

No onboard video-
770- 1x16 PCI-E slot. 
790X- 2x16 PCI-E slots
790FX- 4x16 PCI- slots (not all 790FX chipsets will have that number, its a maximum) 

If yuor not planning on running corssfire video cards or a 16/8xPCI-E hardware raid card, 770 should be fine. 790X is what I'm running, I decided that it might be worth getting it just in case I wanted to run hardware RAID at some point. 790FX is the 'top end'- slightly more features than the 770/790X, and slightly better overclocking. Not really worth it though, they charge a premium for the 790FX boards.  

Onboard video- 
760G- Radeon 3000
780G/E/V-Radeon 3100/3200
785- Radeon 4200
790GX- Radeon 3300
