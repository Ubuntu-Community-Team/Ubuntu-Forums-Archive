### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-09
As far as I know, there was no MSI KTV4 'Ultra' version (the ultras normally had active cooling on the northbridge and extra IDE ports). I could well be wrong though...

If you think your board/CPU is overheating, check your CPU/northbridge temps. There are programs to do this in linux/windows, or you can just reboot and go into the BIOS and check in there. As long as the CPU is under about 70c and teh northbidge under about 50c, its porbably not a heat issue (but if the temps are that high, I would try to bring them down. Post the temps here if they are high and I'll find a few  hopefully easy solutions for you) 

Or, the 'easy and dirty' way to check this is to take the side off and point a nice desktop fan into the case (this may not work if you have a heatsink and/or thermal paste issue).

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-09
44C at idle is...well, to hot (unless its 30C+ in your house) 

I would guess from the 'to smush' comment you mave have excess thermal paste under the heatsink. Yuo want the thinnest layer possible. 

As for USB fans- meh. Dont bother, just get a 'normal' case fan. 

The 80mm fans are nowhere near as good as 92mm or 120mm, but if you have a 80mm mount I would guess its right 'behind' the CPU/heatsink, and under the power supply. Virtually any 80mm fan should be fine, the only differences between them is speed, flow and noise. I'd try to deal with the heatsink 1st though.
