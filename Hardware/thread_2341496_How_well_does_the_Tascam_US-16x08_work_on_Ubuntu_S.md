### Post by stephane-roy on 2017-05-04
Hey Javier... I know it's been a while since october and it may be to late, but just in case...

... by the way, I joined this ubuntu studio forum just a few minutes ago.


I bought the 16x08 you're asking about a few months ago after reading the post of a guy saying that it worked great on ubuntu studio.

On the first tries, I had a lot of noise interference issues. At 44.1kHz, it sounded like a scratch on a CD when it goes:«tic, tic, tic,...» and at 96kHz it sounded more like sudden bursts of radio interference. It happened randomly, wether multitracking or just listening to regular stereo. Tough it was at relatively low level, you could still hear it over the music. Somethimes it could be 10 or even 20 min without hearing a thing and then suddenly you may have it every 10 sec or so.

I was using the 16x08 with a small laptop with a not so powerfull processor running on a Linux plateform so I cheked with my son's laptop, wich was about the same as mine but on windows and we had the same issues. Then I checked with my girlfriends computer, wich is also on windows but is a much newer and powerfull one and... it worked great!

So if you plan to buy this thing, just be aware that you need a very fast and powerfull processor and a lot of ram, at least 16G to be safe. This being said...


This is a very nice peace of gear! I bought it mainly for live rap jams so I didn't expect that exquisite preamp sound, like the focusrite stuff, but with a good laptop on the first jam, I plugged in the condensers and... wow! The voices came out amazingly clean and clear and the music track too. You could here no noise or coloration, everything sounded balanced and natural. After that i tried it for a recording session, tracking a voice with one condenser and an acoustic guitar with two others at the same time. The thing also came out as good as with the live session. In fact, it sounds so good that I doubt I could hear a difference between a recording made with the 16x08 or my Scarlett 2i2. I plan to use it for a drum session...

The headphone amp sounds good too and has a lot of power, more than the 2i2, it has little more bass also, not sure if you could mix with cans on it because of that.

Also, the unboard compressor and EQ works very nicely... on windows only, you don't have access to them or any of the internal settings on Linux but plug it in your daw and use every inputs and outpouts you need up to 24bits/96kHz and it works just fine.

The only bad side to that gear is the totally black face with these tiny grey markings. If the place you're in is even just a little dark, you don't see a thing. Apart from that, I love the gear and I would shurely recomand it!
