### Post by mutnik on 2010-11-01
I have been researching a similar issue with the Synaptics touchpad on my Panasonic CF-19.  

In my opinion at least part of the problem lies with the kernel module "drivers/input/mouse/psmouse".  I have found a change that was made to the psmouse driver back in July of 2010 to enhance Synaptics touchpad detection.  Details can be found in a Gentoo bug report here: [328527]("http://bugs.gentoo.org/show_bug.cgi?id=328527").  

Today I downloaded and compiled Linux Kernel Source 2.6.36-trunk which includes the change outlined in the Gentoo bug report above, but it does not fix Synaptics detection on my PC.  I think more changes still need to be made to the psmouse module to detect a wider range of Synaptics touchpad hardware.

Without proper detection by the kernel, the Xorg Synaptics driver does not even attempt to load.
