### Post by slooksterpsv on 2011-05-21
It looks like it's a BIOS problem. Which means, if you can't boot or anything or get into the BIOS, you gotta reset the BIOS.

How  you say? Well that's a bit complex, but simply put:
Power it off
Unplug it,
Remove the battery
Open up the laptop and remove the CMOS battery
Hold the power button down for 30 seconds, leave the computer like it is for 15-30min.
Put CMOS battery in
Put in battery
Plug it in
Power it on.

You may want to get a computer tech involved if you've never worked with internal components of a computer, especially a laptop. This is a "DANGEROUS" procedure, and one that happens a lot apparently. I guess it's because it tries to boot the USB before the BIOS is processing.
