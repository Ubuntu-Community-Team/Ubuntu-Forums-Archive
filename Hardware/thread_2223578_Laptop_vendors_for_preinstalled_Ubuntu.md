### Post by takowl on 2014-05-11
I'm starting to think about buying a new laptop, and I'd like to get one with Linux - probably Ubuntu - preinstalled, both to be confident it will work smoothly, and to support vendors selling Linux laptops. I know enough about Linux to deal with problems if I have to, but I'd rather not have to ;) . The kind of thing I'm looking for:

[LIST]
[*]Size: 13-16" screen. Portable, but not too cramped keyboard.
[*]Price: Doesn't need to be cheap, but $1k-$1200 is a soft limit.
[*]Hard drive: Maybe an SSD - I'm not sure yet how much extra I should pay for this.
[*]Keyboard: Considerable bonus points if I can get UK layout.
[*]In the US, or can ship there at a reasonable cost.
[/LIST]

System 76 has a couple of models ("Galago" and "Gazelle") that seem to meet all my specs, but I've found [several]("http://www.reddit.com/r/linux/comments/1pwwr0/system76_is_screwing_me_need_a_laptop/") [terrible]("http://blag.depotwarehouse.net/2013/09/system76-galago-ultrapro-galu1-review.html") [reviews]("http://bitflip.net/system76-galago-ultrapro-review-clevo-w740su-259/"), particularly concerning the keyboard (which I do care about), though one from December says [it got fixed]("http://therealkatie.net/blog/2013/dec/4/review-system76-galago-pro/"). ZaReason can do most of it apart from the UK keyboard; a [review]("http://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2012/11/zareason-ultralap-430-a-penguin-friendly-ultrabook-that-doesnt-suck/2/") seems to say it's decent but unremarkable. Thinkpenguin seems to have what I want, but I haven't heard of them before, and the website doesn't look super professional. EmperorLinux resells Thinkpads, but at a hefty markup. Los Alamos Computers sells Thinkpads at a better price, but still with no choices for the keyboard. Dell's XPS Developer Edition still has an old version of Ubuntu, and no options on the keyboard.

Has System 76 fixed their issues now? Is ThinkPenguin a safe bet? Is there another vendor I should look at, or a way to get hidden options from some of those vendors? Should I give up on preinstalled Linux and buy from a mainstream brand like Lenovo?
