### Post by sackbj on 2008-05-08
I have a Lenovo X60 tablet and I have installed Ubuntu 7.10 on it previously.  I had a couple of problems.  First, it was difficult to get my audio working.  I had to do a lot of searching in several different places to find a fix that worked - but even that was temporary.  I had to edit my xorg.conf with a few extra lines.  This leads to the next point: the wacom tablet pc functionality.  You might not be looking for a tablet.  If not, then this will not be an issue for you.

The tablet function of the X60 did not work "out of the box."  I had to find another fix for this, and edit my xorg.conf again.  Unfortunately, this did not fix the problem AND it broke the audio again.  I eventually gave up.

### Post by yavez on 2008-05-08
I have a Clevo m520n 12" which cost around £550 a year ago.  Everything works fine on it apart from the integrated webcam (still working on a solution for that one) and the Realtek ALC833 (INTEL HDA Audio) needs Alsamixer to get headphone out. The matshita drive crapped out about 6 months after purchase, but I expect that, every Matshita laptop drive I've ever owned has failed pretty quickly.  I bought an LG replacement for around £25 and it's been great since. Other than that it's been a solid laptop, looks pretty good too.

Currently running Ubuntu 8.04 with no major problems.
