### Post by wieman01 on 2008-04-28
I just bought an inexpensive Samsung ML-2010R Printer today and I must say I am impressed. It installed without a hitch, I simply had to turn it on and connect it to my PC, that's all. It worked out of the box on Kubuntu Hardy Heron.

For 69 EUR, it's a very pleasant surprise. Printing quality leaves nothing to be desired, it's got an "energy star" label, and prints around 20 pages a minute. I can recommend it to any user who looks for an inexpensive printer that is fully Linux compatible.

### Post by wieman01 on 2008-04-28
Just an advice... If you happen to run Kubuntu, don't use the KDE printer configuration tool, but CUPS itself which is running on port 631. You can easily access it via your favorite browser:
> [http://localhost:631](http://localhost:631)
You'll save yourself a lot of trouble if you set up printers that way. KDE printer config tool is plain useless and confused the hell out of me.
