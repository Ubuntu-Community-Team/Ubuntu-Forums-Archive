### Post by amn108 on 2008-08-29
I am writing this from a Thinkpad T61 with a Intel 4965AGN which works absolutely flawlessly as far as I am concerned. People who say they have problems should first check how it works under other systems (read: Windows). There are always problems for somebody under some circumstances.

You should pick Intel for several reasons. First they make reference wireless cards and chipsets, which all of the market trusts on, since they may only fail once. Second, Intel supports Open Source driver development, and contributes to the source themselves. This is much better than having to reverse-engineer many other wireless chipsets, firmware and cards. As far as I know only Intel and Buffalo Tech are actually contributing.
