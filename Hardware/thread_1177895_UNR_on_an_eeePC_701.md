### Post by baruch60610 on 2009-06-03
I've had an eeePC 701 for over a year, running the Xandros flavor of Linux that it came with.  This distro wasn't being updated, and it lacked many features I wanted, so I decided to try the Ubuntu Netbook Remix (UNR).  I have to say that this turned out to work quite well for me, with a few glitches.

I followed the usual steps for installing the OS using a USB flash memory ([http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download-netbook).]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download-netbook")  As usual, the installation went smoothly.  The Ubuntu remix didn't seem to take up more disk space than the Xandros installation had taken; possibly it was slightly less.

When I finished the installation, I wound up with a working Ubuntu distro on my tiny netbook.

I have to say that I do not like the Gnome desktop, which is what you get with UNR.  This dislike is more a matter of taste and habit, than of any deficiencies in Gnome itself.  I've used KDE almost exclusively since 1997 or so.  My opinion is of course biased.

I found the newer, "Jaunty" interface distasteful, more of a hindrance than a help.  I therefore chose to use the "desktop switcher" to go to the Gnome "classical" view.  THIS IS BUGGY.  When you switch, everything works fine.  Unfortunately, when you reboot, Gnome forgets all your panels and such.  You wind up with a blank desktop showing, at most, your peripheral disks.

All is not lost.  There is a fix for this, available at [http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=7081942&postcount=8](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=7081942&postcount=8).  I recommend you follow these instructions *before* trying to switch desktops.

So far, I haven't found any problems aside from the vanishihg panels.  I had wanted to use a program called sagemath, which wasn't available through Xandros.  I wanted access to gcc, which also wasn't feasible through Xandros.  Finally, I use Open Office quite heavily.  In the Xandros version, there were no language aids (hyphenation, thesaurus, spellcheck).  That, plus typing on the tiny keyboard, meant I had lots of errors that could have been avoided.

One curious note.  When I first installed UNR, I got the sluggish mouse pointer that others have noted.  I'd move the cursor, and it would lag by a good while, perhaps a second or so at times.  It was as though the OS were very busy and couldn't update the position regularly.  I had intended to try to track down a fix for this problem, but I realized that somehow this problem had vanished.  No clue what caused it, nor what made it go away...

I would definitely recommend using UNR, if you've got a netbook and aren't happy with whatever OS it has on it now.
