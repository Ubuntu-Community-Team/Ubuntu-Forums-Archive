### Post by kurt18947 on 2011-11-11
> **boast said:**
> I've had good luck with Brother printers on linux. I think only WLAN printing doesn't work with brother, not sure.

Brother has pretty good Linux support including wired and wireless network printing.  Installation may or may not be plug & play but drivers are available.  Google 'brother+linux drivers', you should get a few pages listing supported models.  The one thing to be aware of with Brother is at least some and I think most printers only have 32 bit drivers available.  The drivers can be used on 64 bit machines without a problem but must be installed from the command line AFAIK.  Copy & paste are my friend there.  I think HP is easier but Brother is quite usable in my experience.  

The nice thing I've found about Brother is that the cartridges don't have 'kill me' chips in them so they're pretty easy to refill or buy refillable cartridges.  For heavily used printers ink costs can far exceed initial purchase cost.
