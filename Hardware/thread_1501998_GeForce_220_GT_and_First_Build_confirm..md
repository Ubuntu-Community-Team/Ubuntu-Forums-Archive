### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-05
I wouldnt call it a 'fault' exactly, but why get a 785 chipset (with onbaord video) when you can get a 770? 

I'd get this board instead- 

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128431](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128431)

Or better yet, this (slightly more expensive than the one above, or the asus)-

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128443](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128443)

USB 3.0, more expansion slots, and a better quality board IMO, and the 870 has SATA III (6Gbit/sec, compared to SATA II 3Gbit/sec). 

If you do want a board with onbaord video for some reason, get a new 8XX version (probably an 880). SATA IIII support is good, there are already SSDs which can saturate SATA II, and they are going to get a lot cheap, and more common, very soon....
