### Post by William_Klee on 2015-03-03
I have a 2 in 1, a Dell Inspiron 13 7347. I just spent most the the past weekend trying different distros on it, and ended up with Ubuntu as the one that worked the best out of box. Best, however, is not the same as perfect. It's not bad, though, as I only see two real problems.

1: I can't seem to find the orientation sensor, so I can set up screen auto-rotation. No big thing, I just have 4 scripts, rl, rr, ri and rn (rotate left, rotate right, rotate inverted and rotate normal) I can call in guake. The only drawback I've found so far is that, for some reason, I get a cursor / arrow right where it should be, that works fine, and a second arrow, pointing the direction I just came from, ie, if I run rl, then rn, I have a second arrow pointing down and left; down and right, if I go rr, then rn. The second arrow does nothing, so I assume it's just some sort of artifact.

2: currently, my trackpad does not work. It worked yesterday, and the day before, but today, no joy. The left-click / right-click buttons at the lower don't work, either
Edit: after shutting down & rebooting, trackpad works alright, except that lower-right / right-click corner / button still doesn't work. However, I logged into kubuntu & set up 2-finger-click = right-click & 3-finger-click = middle-click, so that's okay now.

Other than those two issues, I like it quite a bit. It's got a 2.4 GHz i7, which is much faster than the AMD64 in the 2007-era HP it's replacing. Pulling the 5400 rpm, 500 GB hd it came with and replacing it with a 960 GB ssd doesn't seem to have hurt anything.

Why did I pick a convertible? I could give you several reasons, but, honestly, the first and foremost reason was "It's cool!" All my other reasons are really just after-the-fact rationalisations.
