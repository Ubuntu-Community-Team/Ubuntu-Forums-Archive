### Post by chitowner2 on 2010-02-12
This is a help post, not a question!

I recently had this AMD error message appear during boot. I could not get into BIOS and the boot would not proceed beyond this warning. So, naturally, I googled it.

I found a wide range of statements in several different forums, frequently encountering a self assured claim that it was a CMOS error.

It is NOT a CMOS error.

How do I know this?
Because I fixed the problem without touching CMOS, the battery or doing anything in BIOS- because I couldn't _get_ to BIOS!

What I observe- as many veterans here can attest to- is that the typical plaintiff neglects to mention *important stuff* when they ask for help. Been guilty of that myself, I'm sure.  :)

This is a HARDWARE problem guys!

How do I know this?
Because shutting down, removing a **recently added** PCI card and rebooting was all I needed to do. I was then able to do a clean shutdown, replace the PCI card, and boot normally. It's fine now.

SO- my conclusions: 
This is a hardware problem that makes no difference what your mobo or OS is. Obviously it is an AMD-related error, but that is about as far as one can go with it. REPEAT: "AMD Data Change" error has nothing to do with operating system or motherboard. What you PLUG INTO your mobo *IS* important.

I just wanted to post this here b/c I am a member. I am sure googlers will find it!  :D

### Post by chitowner2 on 2010-02-12
UPDATE

OK guys, I figgered it out. Have to make changes in router (I have dd-wrt on a Buffalo with wireless-N) with receiving WICD *offline*, THEN connect. Otherwise it apparently can not track the change. 

So I'm good. No more conflict with my nice Adesso wireless keyboard. No more cable and no need for line-of-sight nonsense either. Coooool!

Still have no idea why channel 6 was a problem and channel 11 is OK, but there ya go.   :P
