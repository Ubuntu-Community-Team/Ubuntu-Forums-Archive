### Post by Sam Lars on 2008-08-19
Dell sells laptops with Ubuntu preloaded.  I got one similar, so here are some things I've noticed to watch out for:
[LIST]
[*]As much RAM as possible, of course.  I got 3 GB, which seemed like plenty, but once I started manipulating huge images and running my laptop for extended periods, it seems smaller and smaller.
[*]Intel Core 2 processors are 64-bit capable, as are a lot of newer chips.  It can be nice to have (or not nice, but you can choose what to run)
[*]Avoid Intel graphics.  They are alright, but some things don't work.  For example there is no blur (they're working on it), no cube caps, no skydome... the list goes on.  One of my greatest regrets is that I didn't get a Nvidia card.
[*]Get a big battery, you probably will benefit from it eventually.  Maybe even get an extra.
[*]I got a 320 GB hard drive, which is really nice.  More space = better.
[*]The card reader reads SD fine, but not MemoryStick, and I heard of problems with xD cards.
[*]Avoid Broadcom if possible, try to find wireless that would work out of the box, and well.  But you can get Broadcom to work, just check for the wireless compatibility.
[/LIST]

Although I would definitely recommend a Nvidia card, I can confirm from experience that Intel X3100 integrated graphics (965 express chipset family) works out of the box with Hardy with all compiz effects supported (cube caps, skydome, etc).
The main difference I noticed from my other pc with Nvidia is that if I have a movie playing and I rotate the cube using Ctrl+Alt+MouseButton1, the movie screen turns blue and I cannot see the movie playing. When I stop rotating the cube, I can see the movie just fine.
