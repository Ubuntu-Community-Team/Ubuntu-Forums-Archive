### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-05-28
I am not a fan of Lenovo laptops.  I would never buy one.  One reason is that they (like HP) limit your ability to upgrade your wireless card; they "whitelist" the wireless cards to specific models and the computer will not even POST if you install an "unapproved" wireless card.  Dell, Toshiba, and Acer (and others) do not have this absurd restriction, and I have successfully upgraded numerous wireless cards in those brands of machines.

To be fair to Lenovo, though, they do not make hard drives. Hard drives fail all the time, in all brands of computers.  Sometimes they fail the first week, sometimes the 53rd week (one week after warranty expires as in your case), sometimes not for ten years or until you finally recycle the laptop.  I would not blame Lenovo for a failed hard drive.  I consider that a routine type of failure and always make backups.  One year is an unfortunately short life for a hard drive, though.

Batteries are similar: they can last years (though they lose some of their capacity naturally as they age) or they can fail very early.  A Dell laptop I bought several years ago came with a second battery (extra cost).  One of the batteries failed just a week or two BEFORE the 1 year warranty expired, so I was lucky enough to get a replacement in time.  My other battery lasted for years.

Sometimes it does make sense to buy an extended warranty for a laptop.  I purchase computers and electronic equipment with my American Express credit card, and they automatically extend my warranty on these products by an extra year.  Otherwise, I might consider purchasing an extended warranty, depending on the product and the cost.

I am sorry for your poor experiences with Lenovo, but they could easily happen with a Dell or an HP laptop as well.

### Post by jjiggens on 2012-05-29
Most consumer grade laptops will most likely have a small 3 cell battery, getting a year out of it only is expected.

If you want quality you should really look into business grade laptops, most of those ship with 6 or 9 cell batteries out of the box as well as better warranties and higher quality and sturdier builds. Stay away from lenovo's think pads though, they are just as dodgy as their consumer grade models. The brand has lost all its reputation when IBM sold it. 

Business class laptops 3 year warranty, consumer 1 year.

Being a pc tech by trade i see the low end budget consumer laptops all the time for repairs, and its not a rare trend for them to fail shortly after the 1 year warranty expires.
