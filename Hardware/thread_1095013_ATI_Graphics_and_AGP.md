### Post by TruthOrDare on 2009-03-13
I want to upgrade my graphics card from an nVidia GeForce FX 5600 (don't laugh :( ) to a better card.

However finding a decent nVidia AGP card is hard and when I do find one, they are at silly prices.

So for the first time I am considering ATI. However their reputation is legendary in that they have the best bang for the buck cards but only if you can get them working with their crappy drivers.

I am constrained by a 350W power supply and if I have to replace the power supply, it just isn't worth upgrading. I have rounded it down to two cards.

The X1650 Pro and the 2400 Pro. Neither of these are particularly positive in their reviews

Everywhere I read, the X1650 Pro is that the fan is poorly built and the card overheats. Also, ATI consider it a legacy product now.

The 2400 Pro doesn't have them problems but has a problem of it's own in that the driver support is meant to be terrible. This forums hardware incompatability thread lists that card several times and google is littered with negative results.

So what should I do? The machine needs an upgrade, however I would rather stick with the working out of the box nVidia card than a semi-working more powerful ATI card.

Maybe I am being too negative, however back in 2003 when this machine was built, nVidia was recommended because of poor ATI drivers. Six years on, it looks like nothing has changed.
