### Post by Cracklepop on 2011-02-16
I'm suddenly getting really weird behaviour on my eee 1005 netbook.

Yesterday it was fine, but today while on skype this happened, in this order:

1. Battery power, microphone plugged in (the netbook has it's own mics but I use a headset with a mic) 
-- everything fine, no noise on the mic except my voice

2. Switched to AC power (plugged the AC cord in)
--  mic noise went to 100% and stayed there, producing a very loud low hum  on the other end of the skype call (and of course meaning that I  couldn't be heard).

At this point I could cycle between 1 and 2 by switching between AC and battery power

3.   Removed the power board/adapter from between the laptop AC cord and the  wall socket that I used for the first time today and is the only change  from yesterday, and plugged the laptop AC cord directly into the wall  socket

4.  Noticed that while on AC power if I put my finger on the metal around  the VGA port next to the power port that mic noise went down almost to  zero, almost as though I'd pulled the AC cord out

5. Tried a wall socket in a different room
 --  no difference, but I also tried unplugging the headset mic here(use  built-in mic), and got a third state: noise level goes to around 25%  (not very stable) and semi-randomly jumps up to between 50-100%

6. Returned to the original room
 --  now touching VGA port does nothing, and the power source has become  irrelevant: there are now just 2 states, depending on whether the  headset mic is plugged in.  100% noise if the mic is in, jumpy 25% if  the mic is out (power source has no effect at all either way)<br/>

7.  Find yet another state: if I unplug the mic, and put the earphone cord  into the mic port it get a very low level of noise, maybe 5% (not  stable).  This is also regardless of whether it's on battery or AC  cable.

This is weird, weird, weird.

I  am assuming it was the power board (turn 1 socket into 4) that started  this all because I can't think of any other change, but now the power  source has no effect - only the mic cable has an effect...

There is no problem with the laptop sound output, btw, just the mic.

### Post by quadproc on 2011-02-16
I don't know enough about your particular computer and microphone to know for sure but it sounds like you have a broken ground connection somewhere.  These problems are often found where connectors are attached to the ends of cables - the mechanical pulling that inevitably happens to the cable stresses the conductors inside the  cable and eventually the conductor breaks.  Sometimes the problem happens to the connector mounted in the computer but this is less frequent.

If you can, I would suggest substituting another mic to see if the problem is with the mic.

quadproc
