### Post by Perfect Storm on 2010-06-19
No, it won't show up in the list. Only restricted drivers (close source) are shown in that list. Open Source drivers comes automatically with Ubuntu.

Unfortunately, the Open Source driver for ATI is poor when it comes to running games and 3D applications, so you'll be out of luck when it comes to games.
ATI have stopped supporting your cards in their restricted drivers, that's why no restricted driver is shown in the list.

If you're looking for a laptop or Desktop you can use linux + game you need a nvidia card or wait a couple of years with the ATI Open Source driver to mature.


Regarding games via wine/Play on Linux/Cedega /etc. will never be the same as on Windows (performance wise), as the game are made for Windows. So expect a performance hit.

Your best option might be dual-bboting in this case. Windows fore gaming and Linux for anything else.
