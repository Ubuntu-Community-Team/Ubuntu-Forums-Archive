### Post by NewbieGirl on 2008-09-05
Had almost pitched a nice old perfectly operational printer.... just couldn't get it to work on Ubuntu. According to all of my searches, there just isn't a Linux driver for it.

Well, I set it up on an old Windows machine - and here is what I saw on the printer Test Page under Driver Name: HPPLC5MS.DRV.  Well, guess what... HP Laserjet 3P is the only one in the HP list that has "PCL5" in the driver name.  So my dear old Canon LBP860 found its new Linux life as an HP Laserjet 3P... works just fine.

Moral of the story... obviously, sometimes printers have a second identity... try to look up what the Windows driver name is... your unsupported printer may after all be supported - just under another name.
