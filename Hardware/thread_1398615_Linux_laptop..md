### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2010-02-05
I have installed several different distros in HP hardware, always with great results.  I have at least tried 7 different laptop models and a couple different desktops, always getting hardware recognised at once, with one exception in an old desktop whose broadcom wireless card I couldn't get to work.

I would also recommend you buy HP professional models.  The are supported  and remain available longer, usually never sporting cutting edge hardware which may be untested, but tried and solid components.  That does not mean they are not powerful, much the contrary, but you are not likely to get nasty surprises.

In addition, don't get too caught up with powerful machines.  Ubuntu is very well optimised, and I am yet to see my machine allocate 1 GB of RAM, or get the CPU beyond 60%, even when running daily demanding tasks.  What I am trying to say is that the usual Windows oriented thinking when buying a machine (the more powerful the better) does not apply in Linux that heavily.

Good luck!

### Post by djchandler on 2010-02-05
> **pizza-is-good said:**
> 
I have heard a lot of complaining about ati, a lot more than nvidia.
~pizza

I wasn't trying to "get" you or argue.

In fact, you are not wrong if you look at the "big" picture. But since we were talking pre-installed on newer hardware, that changes the focus.

What is happening lately is newer versions of the proprietary Catalyst drivers, that in fact do address many of the old problems, are not including support for the older GPUs.

Cards and IGPs prior to the 2xxx series (R-5xx GPU and older) are not getting fixes in Linux. If you have integrated graphics with the AMD 760G or newer chipset or a discrete graphics card starting with the HD 2400 and up, then you shouldn't be having much in the way of problems recently if your driver is current. But if you have an older GPU, you probably should (if you can) replace your graphics capability if Linux is your main OS. It can really negatively impact the whole experience.
