### Post by Wulfrunner on 2008-07-15
If you plan to have your laptop plugged in for a long period of time, you can disconnect the battery. This will prevent charge/discharge cycles that can reduce the life of your battery over time. I do not plug my battery in unless I deliberately want to charge it or I want to use it wirelessly, simply because I want to keep the battery as healthy as possible.

### Post by sergiom99 on 2008-07-16
Sorry, I thought I explained it to you in another post, that's why i didnt tell you why:

HP recommends that you "calibrate" your battery every three other months or so.
You do it by setting "do nothing" for low battery level in your power settings manager (In Kubuntu I do it by clicking the battery icon). So you leave your laptop on until it just shutdown and then when you press the power button nothing happens b/c the battery is drained.
Then you charge your battery with laptop off for 4-6 hours and there you are.

Hope this helps.

### Post by jlh68 on 2008-08-29
I normally leave the battery in when operating on 120V AC, because usually it is for a short time.  When I leave the laptop off and I know the battery is charged, I disconnect the power connector.  I have two batteries and I alternate using them (one is 2200-mAH which provide about 70-min run time, the other is a 7200-mAH which provides 4-hours runtime).  I will periodically allow the computer to discharge the battery and then I will recharge while NOT using the laptop.  I guess that is close to the "Calibration" mentioned  by **sergiom99.**
