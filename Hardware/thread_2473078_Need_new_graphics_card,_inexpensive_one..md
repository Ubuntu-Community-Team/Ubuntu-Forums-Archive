### Post by him610 on 2022-03-22
If your PSU is capable, most cards just work. I don't know how to choose an incompatible card 

The way I normally choose a GFX card is to decide on my budget first.
Next, I go to Newegg (because they have better written specifications than Amazon) and search for GFX cards within my budget.
Study and compare the specifications including recommended PSU rating. Make sure your PSU will handle the GFX card. 
Next, I look for buyer ratings and read reviews then narrow my search by eliminating the cards that don't measure up, along with a few other things too.
Once I have it down to two or three acceptable cards then I start comparing prices of different sellers.
Choose the one with best price including shipping. 

I have never had any issues with either Nvidia or AMD (ATI) GFX cards,  or Intel GFX chips. I don't recall the last time I purchased  a GFX card; I mostly use castoff cards from people who wanted to upgrade their original cards to the latest and greatest who also sometimes overstressed(burnt out) their systems.
