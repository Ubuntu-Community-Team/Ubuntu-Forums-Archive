### Post by gradedcheese on 2008-06-21
Hmm.. well, they are a 'white box' reseller, that is they take a reference design (Asus, I think) and repackage it (tons of companies do this).  They have very little control over what graphics chipset is used, though here they specififed Intel because it's the best for Linux drivers.

Anyhow, there was nothing wrong with your hardware.  compiz is a new feature that depends on driver support.  It's disabled by default (until recently) and some drivers have to be blacklisted until they improve.  You should not expect to have working compiz support from a manufacturer at this time.  That said, if even without compiz enabled, your external display looks like that, it might indeed be a problem with your cables or something like that.  In a rare case, it might be a hardware problem (around the VGA connector) but it's not likely.
