### Post by rossyp83 on 2010-01-15
I called up Buffalo about the HD-PXU2, which I believe is similar. Tech support told me unless I bought a lower-spec device, which didn't have encryption (and looked rubbish) there was nothing they could do, just repeatedly told me that using Linux is "unsupported". The guy I spoke to did however explain that, by installing the encryption software onto a windows PC, you can go into a config page and remove encryption. This is installed on the virtual CD-Drive that is created when the device connects. Whether this is the same for your model I'm not 100% sure. I was lucky, mine came with encryption turned off and I'm not turning it on, I'd love to see either Buffalo support Linux or a new project to support their hardware encryption. Buffalo have some really nice hardware, its a real shame they aren't more Linux-friendly.


Hope that helps.
