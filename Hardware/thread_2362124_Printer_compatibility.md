### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-05-24
Brother are very Ubuntu compatible as are HP are compatible. Canon are good.

I would always go for a Brother personally. Easy install. HP equally as good. Also takes not long to install. Both easy.

Avoid 'the general run of printers', presuming that means the first printer that looks good off the shelf regardless of brand or model, and go for something specific by a brand known to be Linux friendly and fully compatible with Ubuntu (most Brother, HP and Canon printers and there are others, but not familiar enough to me to pass comment).

Hope that helps and good luck.

(PS: No, Linux  usually doesn't get a mention on the box and I would take absolutely no notice of that and check the model online in a search with the printer name and 'ubuntu' appended. Even printers that are VERY well supported in Linux may not have 'Linux compatible' on the box. The manufacturer is not getting paid by Canonical or any other Linux organisation to whack that on there and the manufacturer is in no way obliged to put it there, even if they fully support Linux and are 'Linux friendly' company. :))
