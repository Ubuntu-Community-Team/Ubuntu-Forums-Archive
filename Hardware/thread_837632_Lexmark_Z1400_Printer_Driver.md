### Post by phillyk on 2009-02-18
I know this is a very old thread.
I bought this printer a while back, not expecting to ever need to use it with linux & now I find myself using linux more & more.
So I wanted to print to it.
But saw that the manufacturer did not support it. Then I saw they make a MAC os driver. Mac os Is based on BSD. I took a look & found that BSD uses some cups & linux compatible printing standards.
I then decided to try some things. I use cups on a centos server & when I went to new printer setup, CUPS detected my printer!
Cups needed drivers &  I did not have any, but it did ask for a PPD file. I poked around & found one in the installed files on a MAC I have.
I gave that to Cups & it added the printer & seems to be detecting it & acting like its getting messages from it. I sent a test job thru cups & it seems like it might have printed! Unfortunatly I am 25 Miles from home & do not have a webcam monitoring my printer to check....
