### Post by 1clue on 2015-10-28
My biggest problem with inkjet printers is that I only print about 10 pages in 6 months, and it's usually all on the same day.  So 10 pages is one set of cartridges, since they dry out between printings.

I got a color laser and been on the same printer ever since.  Got married since then, my wife prints a lot more than I do (I actually ran out of toner already!) and now she wants to print from her phone.  My printer is evidently not compatible with that.

Anyway, if you're a very occasional printer and keep getting that 'cartridge expired' thing or get dried out cartridges, then I highly recommend a laser printer.  More, I recommend a small office printer to get around that home printing 'ink tax' from insane cartridge prices.  Laser toner is expensive per cartridge but you can get a lot more pages out of a cartridge.

### Post by mastablasta on 2015-10-30
> [SUP][SIZE=2]5[/SIZE][/SUP] Fax supported means that PC initiated fax send is supported using hp-sendfax, once an appropriate CUPS fax queue is set up. Fax support varies on Linux distributions, based on the availability of python-dbus version 0.80 or greater (versions before 0.80 will not work with HPLIP). Click on the model name of each printer to get more detailed information.

> [SUP][SIZE=2]8[/SIZE][/SUP] ("Required") A downloadable driver plug-in is required for printing support. ("Optional")  A downloadable driver plug-in is optional for printing support and may increase the speed, quality, or other aspect of printed output.  ("No" or "None") A driver plug-in is not required nor available. Driver plug-ins are released under a proprietary (non-open) license and are not part of the HPLIP tarball release. For more information, please refer to this [KB article]("http://hplipopensource.com/node/309")
