### Post by Dr._B on 2013-10-21
I am running a Brother MFC-7860DW printer in Ubuntu 13.04 (64 bit).  It has been working perfectly for over a year, including working perfectly after I updated to 13.04.   Sometime in the past few weeks it began shifting the printing up and to the right; depending on the margins of the document, this sometimes cuts off part of the printout.

I am assuming this is an error introduced due to an "update" somewhere along the line.  I've read extensively and found that past incidences of this are due to the paper type settings being changed - and indeed, this appears to be what happened here.  I've updated every setting I can find back to 'Letter' size (it was on A4), changing all of the following:

[LIST=1]
[*]The settings under 'printer' in the "Systems Settings" dialogue box
[*]The paper size in the document viewer, open office, and system print dialogue boxes (all of which were 'A4')
[*]I checked the settings on the CUPs web-interface and printer web-interface - both of which were correct
[*]I checked the printer info files as outlined [here]("http://askubuntu.com/questions/64823/printer-output-shifted-on-a-brother-dcp-135c").
[/LIST]

None of that worked, so I uninstalled and then re-installed the [driver]("http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/download_prn.html#MFC-7860DW").

None of that worked - the print-out is still shifted.  I noticed that in the system print dialouge box the paper type & output tray options are greyed out, which might mean something.

Anyone have any insights.
