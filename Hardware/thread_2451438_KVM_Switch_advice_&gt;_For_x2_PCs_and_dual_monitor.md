### Post by TheFu on 2020-10-04
Probably. But VGA is a dead technology.  

I have a 4 port kvm, a Belkin onmicube. 

I would look at using dvi or hdmi for the KVM.  At some point, your technical dept will need to be paid.
So will mine. I have 4 systems connected. 2 have VGA as the only output. The other 2 support multiple output types.

If the dvi is dvi-d and not dvi-a, that can be used a hdmi with just a plug converter. Also, mode monitors made the last 15 yrs support more than just VGA. Most support dvi/hdmi/vga and newer ones would have DisplayPort.

But only you know the trade-offs between all the different connections for the monitors and GPUs.  A few months ago, I looked for a replacement that supported 4K resolutions. Those got expensive. 

KVMs are one of the thinks where I'd want a reasonable return policy.  I had 2 before finding the belkin that worked.

### Post by DuckHook on 2020-10-04
Links are acceptable if they are clearly only for purposes of reference. Given your history and prior postings this is not a problem.

KVM switches are difficult to advise on because they differ so much in quality and implementation. Most are designed to work well with Windows. Their OEMs rarely test them with Linux. These forums are filled with past threads asking how to make a KVM switch work with Linux. Sometimes, it's like pulling teeth, especially if the switch sends wrong or nonstandard EDID info to your video card. I'm not familiar with the model you are looking at, but make sure that you can return it for a full refund if it causes problems.

If I were you, I would leave the current DP/HDMI directly hooked up to its existing monitor. The switch can be used to toggle only between the two VGA outputs. The mouse and keyboard would be hooked up to toggle likewise.

Your setup is actually relatively simple and doesn't necessitate any extraordinary design.
