### Post by IcarusR on 2009-07-23
Best way, like all_gears said, is to just give it a good blowout with an air duster aerosol. 
Computer components are susceptible to electrostatic discharge damage, (can fry ram chips, cpu, etc) so is not a good idea to touch the components.
I regularly clean my server out with air duster.

### Post by markbuntu on 2009-07-23
If its just dust do not use liquid cleaners. If you spilled something then use windex and make sure the board is completely dry before applying power. Unplug everything, remove the cpu and ram and clean them separately. Be careful about static electricity.

Dry everything overnight with a fan blowing on it.

ESD trouble is way overated. Unless you are in a very dry environment walking around on rugs with rubber or leather soled shoes the chances of causing ESD damage are not very big. Just be sure that the arm you are using is touching the case when you mess around with stuff.

### Post by markbuntu on 2009-07-25
> **wirepuller134 said:**
> For really dirty or wet systems (which we get into a lot) we use CRC products to clean them.

CRC is pretty good but in my experience it does not do such a good job for removing sticky and salty substances. It will displace water and is good for initial treatment but often leaves some residue in difficult places when the water is not clean.

Actually, we usually used pure water and then CRC and then windex and then water again for final cleaning of salt-water and coffee immersed electronics. black coffee or tea is not so bad but when you add cream and sugar it can be very tough to get clean.
