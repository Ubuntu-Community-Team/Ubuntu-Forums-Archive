### Post by jasonab on 2009-11-02
Reboot your PC, check what is reported  frequency of your CPU is on the POST screen. I have a AMD and after many years the BIOS started to do a wierd thing, it changes the clock frequency from 133 to 100 and my 2.0 GHz CPU then runs at 1500 MHz (15*100) instead of 2000 MHz (15*133). This ussually requires me to go into the CMOS Setup and adjust it again. The post screen is ussually the first indication of it. You may have had an error in the past that required you to press F1 or something similar. I wish I could remember the exact message (but it's not the CMOS battery).

As for your second question. I cannot say for certain but check the above and report back. In my case the OS seems fine but video playback tends to stutter a bit every now and then.
