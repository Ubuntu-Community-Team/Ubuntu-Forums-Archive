### Post by earthboundbob on 2017-08-10
My build has onboard HDMI, and my graphics card (GeForce GT 620) has a mini HDMI port, as well. I'm trying to use my TCL Roku TV as a monitor, but Ubuntu won't detect my HDMI cable when I plug it into the onboard port. I haven't tried the graphics card port yet since I don't have a mini-to-standard cable.

I've heard that using a separate graphics card disables onboard HDMI. Is this true? And if so, is there a way to re-enable it? Or am I better off just getting a mini HDMI-to standard HDMI cable and using that?

Thanks for your help!

### Post by efflandt on 2017-08-11
Yes, usually if you add a graphics card it automatically disables onboard graphics unless you change a setting in BIOS.

If the GT 620 has a DVI port you could alternately use a DVI to HDMI cable instead of mini HDMI to HDMI. DVI-D (or digital pins of DVI-I) are same digital video signal as HDMI. Not sure about GT cards, but GTX cards since 2xx series have also been able to do HDMI audio through DVI to HDMI cable.
