### Post by 0004tom on 2011-01-05
> **o891 said:**
> I was looking at the ATI HD 5770 and some mention of the eyefinity stuff, but I would rather ask some experienced hardware experts!

The eyefinity cards require at least one monitor connected from display port. The thing about dual DVI and HDMI cards is that one of the DVI ports shares something with the HDMI port, so if one of them is in use the other won't work.

But the eyefintiy setups you can use Displayport adaptors, that will give you a DVI or VGA connection for your third monitor.

Any of the HD5xxx series eyefinity cards will happly run your 3 monitors, if your not doing any intensive graphical stuff, modelling gamming etc etc. If you are planning on this, then the 5770 will be a good 
start.

Edit ignore me rambling on about the HDMI and DVI stuff i misread the second paragraph. You'll be able to run the 3 screens 2 with the DVI and the third with a Active DP > DVI adaptor.
