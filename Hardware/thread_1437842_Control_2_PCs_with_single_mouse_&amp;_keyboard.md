### Post by srs5694 on 2010-03-25
It sounds to me like you want a Keyboard/Video/Mouse (KVM) switch. These are hardware boxes that enable you to hook up one keyboard, mouse, and monitor to multiple computers. You press buttons on the KVM box to switch between computers. (If you want each machine to have its own monitor, you can just leave the monitor out of the mix and use the KVM switch for the keyboard and mouse alone. There may even be boxes designed specifically for this variant configuration, but I've not looked into it specifically.) I've got one of these that I use with three computers, and it works quite well. The biggest problem is that some OSes want to see all their devices attached at boot time or they get misdetected, so I've got to switch over to the target system while it boots rather that let it boot while I use another computer.
