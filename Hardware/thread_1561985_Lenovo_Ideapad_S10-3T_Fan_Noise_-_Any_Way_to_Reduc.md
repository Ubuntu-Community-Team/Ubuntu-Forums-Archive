### Post by ptptaylor on 2010-08-27
I wouldn't advise actively trying to slow down the fans as they are spinning for a reason!
Instead (if the laptop is a year old or more) try cleaning out the dust from the insides (use compressed air to blow it out or a vacuum cleaner).
Failing this, you could scale down the cpu frequency so it's not running full throttle.

Remember, overheating causes damage, irreversible damage - unless you have a warranty ;)
