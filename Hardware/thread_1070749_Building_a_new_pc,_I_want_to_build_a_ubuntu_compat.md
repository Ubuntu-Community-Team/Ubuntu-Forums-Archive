You don't want a gaming machine but you want something to handle multiple memory heavy programs without slowdown.  Translation: you want a gaming machine.

Seriously, no offense intended, but I have found that, as a non-gamer, what would be a good "gaming" machine is generally also very good for heavy usage for other type programs as well.  So that means lots o' RAM and lot's o' speed on the ol' HDDs.

Speaking of HDDs, unless you are talking laptop (I'm assuming you're not) I would think about as many as three HDDs but two should be sufficient to start with.  That way you can increase your speed in any number of ways including a RAID configuration (which I will leave to someone with that kind of experience) or at the very least, place your /boot and /swap on one drive /root /usr /tmp on another and /home on yet another if you have a third.

Also, I would give yourself at least 2 GB of RAM and make sure you have the video RAM on your graphics card to handle the load.  While any one program will not likly chew up your video like a game would, running multiple desktops can, especially if you go with KDE or even XFCE with Configurator on.
