### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-02-09
I am glad that you now have output to an external screen. I am assuming that you are using 11.10. Have you tried running the Displays utility in System Settings? That has a button called Detect Displays.

You may be using a proprietary video driver. If so, then you should have a configuration or settings utility installed with that driver. That also may have a Detect Displays feature.

I am not an expert on this. I only have one monitor and I am using a desktop. I am just suggesting things that I might try myself in that situation. As a last resort, as the internal screen is broken you could open up the casing around the screen and disconnect the screen by unplugging it.

I think that you will need to set Ubuntu to not suspend. That too is in System Settings under the Power Utility. You could also see (now that you got an external screen working) if there is anything in the BIOS that will turn off the internal screen and switch output to VGA and also switch off the suspend or hibernate feature when the lid is closed.

I do not know if any of this will help you.

Regards.
