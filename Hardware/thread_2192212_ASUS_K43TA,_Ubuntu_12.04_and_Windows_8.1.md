It is unusual to ask about Windows problem in Ubuntu forum but the problem is caused by Ubuntu, so I think it is the right place to ask.

I recently install Ubuntu 12.04 LTS on my ASUS K43TA laptop to do my work on it. Everything is fine until I reboot to Windows 8.1, then I noticed that there is no sound comes from external speaker (everything works as it should except no sound comes from external speaker but internal speaker) and I can't turn Bluetooth on or off (Bluetooth settings in Windows 8.1 is greyed out, but Bluetooth itself is on and discoverable). When I reboot to Ubuntu to check if those problems are caused by broken hardware, both sound and Bluetooth work fine in Ubuntu. So I decided to re-install both sound driver and Bluetooth driver to solve the problems but it will come back again whenever I boot to Ubuntu, and sometimes re-install the drivers doesn't solve the problems.

If you guys know how to solve these problems or workarounds, please let me know.

UPDATE
Workaround: disable and re-enable speaker, which is in audio inputs and outputs, will fix sound problem, but I have to do every time that I boot to Ubuntu 12.04.
