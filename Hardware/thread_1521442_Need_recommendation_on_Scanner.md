### Post by SoFl W on 2010-06-30
I purchased an[COLOR=Blue] Epson Workforce 610[/COLOR] All-in-one recently.  It will scan directly from the scanner to a PDF and store it on the unit.  The unit has a slot of a compact flash /SD card and will act as a network drive (or usb drive if directly connected to the computer) where you can retrieve your scanned files.  You can scan to a pdf or a jpg image.  It will allow a multi-page one-sided scans to PDF.

Overall I am happy with the purchases but the unit takes a while to warm up before printing.  Printing is very slow in high quality mode but fine in standard or draft modes. ### Post by tvillamil on 2010-08-06
Figured it out.  It's interesting... what you can find out when you read the instruction manual... :)

You need to select ADF (Auto Document Feeder) as the document source within Image Scan!.

Click on Preview to activate the scanner.  Once it has previewed the first page, place it back with all the other sheets in the Document Feeder.  

Click on Scan, and it will rerun the previewed page as well as all the other documents (upto 30 pages).

Here  is the confusing part...

Once it has reached the last document, it will give you a message to place more sheets on the Document feeder.... It gives you an option to continue or cancel.  It seems like it will terminate the scan if you click Cancel... but what it does is tell the scanner you have no more documents and completes the scan process.

It's not the fastest, but since it auto feeds, all you need to deal with is the annoying sound of the fax.... but I guess an annoying sound means it is working... so I guess I will take that any day....

I explored this same topic over a year ago, and let's face it, this is not a hot area for Linux advancement.  It isn't just a Ubuntu issue, but the whole clan being effected by a general lack of progress in this area.

What I mean is, if you want some real recommendations, spread out your search range, not just the Ubuntu Forums, and then just hope for a method that is rumored to work with certain scanner models, but you won't really know until you try.  I found a big chart of scanners listed by make and model that included what they thought might work wirh each as of that time, but it really wasn't current.

That is when I decided that I could just live with a Windows client under VirtualBox and have this as one of its tasks until the picture changes, if it ever does.  A Windows client does just fine for the job, because it is after all, still able to do its own thing, though to a lesser extent in other areas, like real time game play.

Oh, and if you do find something, consider coming back here and posting an answer to your own question.  Others might find that very helpful.  What I do know is that the most common advice is, don't buy your next system, particularly the scanner, until you determine if it is supported under Linux by drivers from the various venders involved.
