### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-07-19
Take the keyboard apart and clean it.  There are several videos on youtube how to do it.  You are not the first.  Take a picture of the keyboard in case you drop the keys on the floor.  Typically corrosion or dried mineral deposits will cause a keyboard to go wacky.  Cleaning the circuitboard with a Q-Tip and alcohol will remove the residue and possibly restore function.  It may have damaged the key-scan microchip--so if cleaning doesn't work, time to get a new one, and invest in a sippy cup.
