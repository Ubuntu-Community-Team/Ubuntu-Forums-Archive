While working on a friend's computer I encountered a strange one that I can't quite put my finger on. I've been scratching my head for a couple of days. So I'm testing the waters to see if any of you have any ideas.

On a cold start everything works fine, it boots up normal and will run for days. You can restart, log off, hibernate, all programs work. With a steady boot up everytime. 

The strange part comes from a shut down. When you shut down from the computer and wait a minute or two, then manually start back up from the front of the computer the boot sticks. It will not let you into BIOS or anything. Now this is strange to me considering it would be a warm start. You can't start back up until you unplug the power supply and let the supply drain; ie let the green light out go out. Then everything boots up properly.

I don't think the processor is over heating, he has three fans, 1 for the processor, 1 for a back blower and 1 on his power supply. All fans work from start up till shut down.

If it was the power supply why would it start on a cold start and not a warm start? It's only when all components are shut down and then have to be started back up again.

I'm trying to pinpoint the problem but am having a hard time. Thanks for reading and any help will greatly be appreciated.
