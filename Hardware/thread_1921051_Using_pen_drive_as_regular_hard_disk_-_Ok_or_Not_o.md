### Post by cwklinuxguy on 2012-02-06
Using an external flash drive to boot to Linux and store data is NOT harmful, in fact it is widely practiced. If you used UNetBootin to create your bootable flash drive, however, it will not store your data between restarts. You will be able to use your flash drive again as a regular flash drive once you wipe it and restore it to FAT32 file system (for maximum compatibility, although you can also use HPFS, EXT, or other filesystems depending on your needs).
