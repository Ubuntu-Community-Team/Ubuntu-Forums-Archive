### Post by penguinpete on 2008-12-28
Luckily there is a Micro Center near where I live so I was able to check out some netbooks rather that rely on reviews. They had a good selection on display, the HP 2133, several of the eeePC and several of the Aspire netbooks. 

I almost bought the original eeePC netbook but the 7 inch screen was just too small and it just did not have a quality feel to it, but is is not suppose it's a $300 laptop. 

The next netbook that I checked out was the HP 2133, a very nice laptop but spendy and it come with Vista, that was a deal breaker.

My wife and I were at Costco one day and she pointed out a HP Mini 1000, she knows I am a laptop junkie. It looked great, the 10 inch screen was bright and much more usable than the 7 inch on the eeePC. It even came with XP instead of Vista, although I would have preferred Ubuntu or Mint. I really like the keyboard on the Mini, it is the largest by far on any netbook, and that's important for a challenged typer like me. Costco was only asking $400, The deal breaker was the 60 gig hard drive,also the unit did not have a monitor port (although a USB video out dongle is available), this is a big deal for me because I need to connect my laptop to a LCD projector from time to time. Another potential problem with the Mini is it does not have an expresscard slot for expansion. I don't have a need for an expresscard slot now but you never can tell what the future holds. I went home and did a little research to see if I could upgrade the hard drive and it looks like HP intentionally made it hard to replace the hard drive and ram.

During my quest for a netbook I read a lot of positive things about the Lenovo Ideapad S10, I bought the Lenovo S10 without ever seeing one in person but every review I read was positive. I went with the S10 because of the 160 gig hard drive, 1 gig of ram, Intel Atom processor, and Windows XP (Linux would be better). I have had the netbook for several weeks now and I really like it, I get compliments from everyone that sees it. The only gripes that I have about the S10 are minimal. The keyboard is smaller than the HP Mini but it is still very usable. My other issue is the battery life from the three cell battery, I haven't timed it but it might be under two hours. I've found 6-cell batteries for the S10 for about $70 on Ebay.

To recap, if you don't mind a small hard drive (that cannot be easily upgraded) and a lack of ports, then the HP Mini is a good computer. But if you want the 160 gig hard drive go with the Ideapad S10.
