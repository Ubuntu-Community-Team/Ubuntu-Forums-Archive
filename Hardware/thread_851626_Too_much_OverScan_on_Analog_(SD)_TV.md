### Post by doas777 on 2008-07-06
Hello all,

I'm having some trouble adjusting my Nvidia 8500's TV output to my Analog (SD) TV.  
I've been searching for answers to this one for months now, and though I find many simmillar issues, none of them seem quite the same, and I havn't been able to get any of their fixes to work for me. Most of them were about HDTVs or Myth.

On my TV, all of the edges of the desktop extend past the edge of my TV screen, so that I can't see about 5% on each side no matter what resolution it's set at. Based on what I find online, the problem is too much overscan.  I had no problems with an older NV card on this TV. The problem is similar to one [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-445091.html").

My nvidia-settings applet does not contain settings for overscan (this appears to be not uncommon).
Furthermore the driver seems to ignore the “TVOverScan 0.0” configuration in the xorg, as it makes no difference if it is set to 0.0 or 1.0. I've played with the TV Format settings and the output type settings, and have changed the media that I'm connecting with, trying both composite and Svid. At this point I'm thinking this isn't a problem I can fix with a configuration in xorg. 

I also tried xvidTune, to adjust it, but it objects to every modeline and won;t apply any changes.

I'm starting to run short on ideas as to how to handle this one. Does anyone have and other avenues to persue on this issue?

Thanks everyone,
Franklin
