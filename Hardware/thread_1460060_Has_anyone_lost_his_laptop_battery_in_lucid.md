### Post by Lepy on 2010-04-22
I have a eee 701 that ran a few distributions of ubuntu and won't charge anymore. However, I think my problem is either a bad power jack or a crap charger with bad capacitors.

It won't charge unless I press the the connection at the jack to an extreme angle and even then the charger makes an awful squealing sound, like a dieing squirrel or something, and the charge light stops blinking after about 5 minutes. The wall charger started squealing and my latop started charging less until it stopped almost altogether. I have yet to try replacing either part, awaiting others responses. In my case, my bet is on the wall charger.

Besides a HDD, on most laptops, especially the EEE which is meant to be moved around and unplugged a lot, the power jack is probably the most vulnerable part. I'd do some charge testing and fudding with the jack before you blame charge malfunctions on software.

### Post by Macchi on 2010-05-01
(I am sorry to only confirm some critical problems with no solutions yet.)

We have two EEEs, a 701 and a 900, both having power management issues with 10.04 Lucid Lynx.
Processor scaling is not working and the last full capacity of the battery is erroneous since the EEE battery reports this as a percentage and not in mA (Thus showing a last full capacity of 100mA, that is too low!). The fan for the EEE 701 is not running and the netbook gets HOT.
These combined factors give a very poor battery performance and possibly even risks for hardware damage on the 701.
