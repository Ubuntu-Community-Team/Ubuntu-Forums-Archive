### Post by six30two on 2010-03-01
If you're ever interested in learning to build your own desktop, start looking for parts on sites like [www.newegg.com](www.newegg.com) and tigerdirect.com. Both are cheap and offer fast shipping.

It's always cheaper than buying prebuilt models - not to mention better, since you're hand picking the internals.

There's lots of places you could go for help in building one, forums I'm sure somewhere. Just a suggestion ;)

If RAM is important to you, get the most RAM. I'd also suggest multi-core processors, if you're running many programs at once. The multiple cores will allow each program to be run indpendently with their own core, instead of trying to share a single fast one.
