### Post by efflandt on 2010-05-07
The key word to test what works is **xrandr** (what resolutions does that list in a terminal?).  Also see what /var/log/Xorg.0.log shows what X recognizes (and might or might not use).  And see **man xrandr** and [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Config/Resolution](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Config/Resolution)

xrandr can temporarily set and test resolutions.

Although, I do not know how to permanently set resolutions, because recent versions of Ubuntu have no xorg.conf by default.  I only use xrandr commands from a script when I connect VGA on my laptop to an HDTV.

If you had a digital connection (DVI or DVI to HDMI or HDMI) you would normally automatically have the correct resolution if your graphics card/chip is capable.  Not so automatic with VGA.

Although, I have not done anything with non-default or proprietary video drivers, so someone more familiar nVidia may have suggestions.
