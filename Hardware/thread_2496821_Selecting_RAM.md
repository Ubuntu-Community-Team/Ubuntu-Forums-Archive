### Post by hyperlinxe on 2024-04-14
Good choice with the AMD cpu, those are awesome! (if you're gonna be "ripping" any threads)

You need to understand your motherboards capabilities to figure out what kind of ram it will support, whether or not it's ddr4 or ddr5, or ddr6, to do that you should find the documentation for your motherboard from the manufacturers website, ideally, or look at the general information about it.

You want to be especially careful with the motherboard component, and spend some time figuring out basic questions about it, does the manufacturer have good documentation available to you for it? Do they provide, and update, the bios/uefi drivers for it? Can you download them? Do they respond to phone calls if you have a problem that needs to be resolved? How long will they provide support for that one specifically? 

The brand name of the ram doesn't reference the general architecture of the ram, broadly, such as ddr4 ect...

You want to understand in advance, primarily, what your goals are for your computer. And these might actually look really expansive if you spend a lot of time developing them, but they will be constrained by your ideal budget.

Also you want to be as patient as possible, because you will have your computer, and have to live with the decisions you make, for a very long time! Make sure to use reliable sources, and attempt to actually speak with real human beings, find people you can trust, to provide your system components, and support them!

### Post by TheFu on 2024-04-14
I'm running G.Skill 3200Mhz DDR4 RAM in both my Ryzen 5600G systems.  I have Asus B450 motherboards.  Be certain you buy all the RAM together in "matched sets".  Ryzen is VERY picky about RAM being matched.  

I started with 2x8GB which worked great at 3200Mhz for over a year. Very stable.  

Then added another 2x8GB with the exact same part number and the system wasn't stable.  To make it stable, I had to slow the RAM down to 2666Mhz. Any higher speed and it would crash a few times each week.   

A few years later, DDR4 started becoming cheaper, so I bought 2x16GB matched sets for both systems and sold some of the old RAM off to friends. Nothing wrong with it, provided the matched sticks stayed together.

Just thought you should know this.

For my 2nd Ryzen build, I could have gotten a B550, but decided against it due to problems with the 2.5Gbps onboard NIC drivers.  I think those are all corrected now, but a few weeks ago, I did see someone posting here where the installer had selected the wrong driver for the RealTek version. Beware you may need to blacklist wrong selected driver and install the correct one manually.
