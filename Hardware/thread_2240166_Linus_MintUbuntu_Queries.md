### Post by Mark Phelps on 2014-08-18
Windows gaming, in general, works poorly at best in Linux -- due primarily to two problems.  First, you have to use Wine in order to run games written for Windows and only SOME games work at all in Wine.  Second, gaming puts heavy demands on the video drivers and the Linux video drivers pale in comparison to those written for Windows in particular, for "gaming" machines that come preinstalled with hybrid/dual graphics which, due primarily to the lack of Linux drivers, do not work well.

If you look around in the Gaming section, you'll see lots of folks retain dual-boot Windows/Linux machines so they can continue to do Gaming in Windows.
