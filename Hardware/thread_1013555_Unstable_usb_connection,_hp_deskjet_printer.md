### Post by Vpc on 2008-12-17
I have a HP Deskjet 3745 printer that I'm running on Ubuntu 8.04, Gnome desktop. I've noticed the usb connection gets lost from time to time i.e. lsusb shows no device connected after I find I have trouble printing. This also happened during the installation of the printer (with hplip) and after I aligned the cartridges (using hplip).

After losing the connection, I go ahead trying each of the usb ports until lsusb shows the printer connected again. I've tried two different usb cables and I am able to establish a connection with every usb port, front and rear. I am able to print when there is a connection. 

Running hp-check shows no errors except for the warning:
warning: Printer is not HPLIP installed. Printers must use the hp: or hpfax: CUPS backend to function in HPLIP.

Is there a way to fix this? Is it a problem with my printer?
