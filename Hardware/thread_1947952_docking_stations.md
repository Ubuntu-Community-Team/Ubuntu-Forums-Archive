### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-03-29
I've used a couple of different docking stations on two very different laptops and found them to be OS-ignorant (meaning, the laptops work the same in either OS (Windows or Ubuntu) regardless of whether they're docked or not).

The challenge I found, for older laptops, was finding ORIGINAL docking stations that could still be purchased.

They didn't come cheap -- so whichever one you choose, I would strongly recommend you get one that comes from the same manufacturer as the laptop. That way, you can be reasonably sure it will work without problems.

### Post by Bill Z on 2012-03-30
Docking stations in the past were connected to the bus of the laptop and the user didn't see any reduction in speed.  Many docking stations had extra video circuits to provide exceptional pictures on the extra monitors.  Yes, they made it possible to use your laptop screen and an external at the same time.

This looks like it doesn't really do that.  It looks like all information flows through the USB interface.  Some of the reports I just read indicate there is a lag and reduction in picture quality.

Business laptops have slots in them for docking stations.  Most commercial laptops don't.  Maybe the reason for the price increase in the commercial ones.
