### Post by d-cosner on 2015-09-17
I am having a little trouble with your description, lot of information there! Take the side panel off the PC and double check that everything is seated properly, especially the memory. Carefully look at all of the capacitors on the motherboard for any that are swollen. If these things look alright, you might try resetting the CMOS. There should be a jumper to do this but you can just remove the CMOS battery and put it back to get the job done. Swollen capacitors indicate problems with the motherboard.

If the PC still will not boot after doing these things, it could be the power supply has failed. You could try a power supply from another computer to test if you have another computer available. Just guessing but I kind of think the power supply might have failed from your description of the problem. Processors usually survive everything except excessive heat, they can fail but I have not experienced that type of hardware failure for years.

If another power supply does not get it working, the motherboard is likely the culprit. To make things simple you need, the motherboard, RAM, graphics card or on-board graphics and the power supply to test with. Nothing else need to be hooked up for the machine to post.

Edit: did you try the computer with the original memory it came with? Maybe the new memory is incompatible?
