### Post by mossman44 on 2008-07-17
I have a 4850 and I absolutely screams through games on windows and hardy. I installed the 8.6 catalyst drivers on hardy and it runs compiz just fine. The only problem is that it idles at 80 degrees, which seems a little hot, but I guess the card is built to withstand the high temps. If you use windows, you can adjust the fan speed to lower the temps but I have been unable to control the fan speed in linux. In short, its a great card and I recommend buying it. BTW, the instructions to installing the 8.6 drivers can be found at [http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Hardy_Installation_Guide](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Hardy_Installation_Guide) as the restricted drivers manager doesn't support the card. If you run windows for gaming, the fan fix for the card can be found at [http://forums.techpowerup.com/showthread.php?t=64267](http://forums.techpowerup.com/showthread.php?t=64267)

### Post by Timbu on 2008-07-19
I bought one of these cards yesterday and I must say that I am pleased so far.  I upgraded from Nvidia 8400GS, which is the low end 8000 series.  I have always used the low end cards and found that there were limitations to what I wanted to accomplish.  I did not want to slap down too much money on a card and all the reviews I read said that the HD4850 was the best bang for the buck.

I was very skeptical because of what I read about the ati drivers with Linux.  I thought it was worth the risk and so far I am pleased.  It took a while to get the right drivers installed.  I first tried the link in the above post and that did not work for me.  I then tried Envy and that seemed okay.  Envy installed the 8.6 catalyst and all seems okay right now.  I have had two lock ups since the upgrade but I hope it doesn't happen frequently.  I am running 64 bit.

The graphics are outstanding on games.

Thanks to mossman44 for the links.

Hope this is good insight!
