### Post by dBuster on 2010-12-27
You were not trying to over clock the chip?  Bios settings are tricky and if you are not careful you can fry things rather easily!  Trust me been there done that.  Sounds like you may have a bad board definitely.  Especially if everything else works when you swap out the mother board.  Now here is a question, have you tried, that is if you can, swap out just the CPU, the main processor and see if it makes a difference?  Doing so though may come at a cost, experience speaking again, if the processor is toast it may just share the toast with the swap out board and vice versa.  It is a risk I took and was willing to.  

Up to you...

It could be faulty memory. Install the program memtest86+, reboot and run it from the boot menu. Let it run overnight and see if it catches anything. Also, take the memory modules out and put them back in.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-12-27
You are my 4000th bean!!  I would try to declock the CPU in BIOS--assuming you can even get into BIOS.  I would clean out the machine, reseat all cables, check voltages with a meter, and pull out every non-essential card.

I would also do as Mr James suggested, cleaning and replacing the thermal paste.

It wouldn't hurt to replace the coin battery as well.  You are essentially rebuilding it and hope that it will be stable.  If it runs at a lower clock speed, then keep it at that speed for a few days.  Then bring it up slowly until you get to full speed or until it starts to act up again.
