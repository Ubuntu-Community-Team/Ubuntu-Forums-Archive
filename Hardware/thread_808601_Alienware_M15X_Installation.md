### Post by KAding on 2008-05-27
The only real installation problem in Hardy was the speakers not working and the wifi not working out of the box. Installing the wicd network manager did the trick for me though. Not sure about the speakers in Hardy (they work in Gutsy).

However, my m15x felt sluggish on hardy heron. In fact, it appears slow on all the new distro releases I tried (Fedora 9 and OpenSUSE 1.1 beta 3 all 64-bit). It runs quite well in Gutsy though, everything worked right of the box. I don't think compiz is too blame, since it feels even slower when disabled. It might be the new kernel or the newer gnome release, I really don't know. Maybe someone should see how it runs in KDE? I have also not tried a 32-bit release. Seeing as Gutsy 64-bit works fine I doubt it would help.

I'm not that knowledgable on linux and I'm having a hard time pinning it down :(. 3D acceleration was somewhat slower in Hardy, Fedora 9 and OpenSUSE 1.1. Glxgears reported about 5000 FPS, compared to like 7500 in Gutsy. Yet, even 5000 FPS seems enough? I also have to admit that I mostly noticed the sluggishness while browsing in Firefox 3 (scrolling, opening new tab, clicking a link, etc...). So if it feels too slow for you as well you might want to try just using Firefox 2. This whole "it just seems slow" feeling is just that, a feeling. Somehing is wrong somewhere, just can't pin it down. And now that I installed Gutsy I've stopped trying to be honest.

I have to admit though, that the thought of newer linux releases not working well is scaring me :(. I generally update my distro within weeks after a new release. The thought of being stuck on Gutsy for years does frighten me :(.
