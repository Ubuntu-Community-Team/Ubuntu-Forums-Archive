### Post by Fiable.biz on 2010-01-25
As a rule of thumb, Hewlett Packard is usually a good choice for everything. If you want to add a video card, avoid NVidia except if you're sure it'll work.
   The best is to buy a computer with Ubuntu pre-installed and all the devices you need together from the same reliable shop, and have it specified in writing that all these are mutually compatible. This way, the possible compatibility problems are not yours, but the seller's. If your shop is a physical one, you can check yourself before purchase that the printers prints, the scanner scans etc.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-29
The 5770 isnt a bad choice at all. ATI has had drive issues in the past, and they still arent perfect (then again, neither is nVidias drivers) but its probably worth it for the lower power consumption and better price. If you havent seen this review, its worth a look-

[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=amd_juniper&num=1](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=amd_juniper&num=1)

BTW, if you have no plans to run SLI/crossfire, then 500-550 watts is as much as you will ever need. Not that it makes a huge difference in price, but a few dollars saved in never a bad thing. The slightly lower rated power supplies tend to be more efficient as well (not that they are more efficient pre-say, but power suplies tend to be more efficient at higher loads, so if your running 550 @ 50% load, its better than 650 @ 40% load)
