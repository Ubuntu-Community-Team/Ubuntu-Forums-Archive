### Post by Carl Hamlin on 2009-02-15
I've been looking at the tiny Sony "P" Lifestyle PC pocket laptop for a little while. The other day, I went into the Sony store at my local strip mall and asked them if they sold just the hardware with no pre-installation, or alternatively a package with Linux pre-installed.

They told me that they *all* had Linux pre-installed, that the pre-boot environment *was* Linux. Then they proceeded to prove that they had no idea how to get *to* the pre-boot environment, which did little to bolster my faith in their claims.

So.

From what I've been able to gather on the Internet, the factory default is to ship with Windows Vista pre-installed, accessible via a Linux 'fast boot' underlayer, reminiscent of the Sony PSP default interface. Ew.

I'd like to know if anyone has had any luck smoking the entire drive and installing Ubuntu (or another distribution) on one of these. I love the idea of a fully functional laptop computer that fits in my jacket pocket, but I'm not too keen on being stuck with Windows Vista.

### Post by abeck409 on 2009-04-12
I have a Sony Vaio P and I have Ubuntu v9.04 beta installed on it.  The whole thing installs fine (I got rid of Vista and XMB Linux).  The only trick is the resolution.  You have to edit the xorg.conf file to get it up to 1600x768.

You could likely get Ubuntu v8.10 going as well, but I wanted to try the most recent version.
