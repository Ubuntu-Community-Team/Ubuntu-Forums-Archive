### Post by danboid on 2011-12-03
We currently have a Kodak ESP 5250 which is a fantastic AIO printer/scanner under Windows so sadly we have vbox installed just so we can print and scan stuff properly as Kodak offer no Linux support for this device at all. Under Linux the only driver is reverse engineered and its colour print quality and speed are very poor but what stands out about these Kodak printers is their cheap running costs- they claim a black and white A4 sheet costs around 2.25p to print, while a colour sheet works out at around 3.79p. Low running costs are what many of us are after but Kodak's total lack of support for Linux and other free OSs means my next printer will not be a Kodak.

So, what I'm looking for is a printer for low volume, personal use that can match or excel this printer in both running costs and photo output quality but with full support for Linux. Ideally it will not require any binary blobs to fully use the printer under Linux and it will also be able to accept 3rd party refills. I would consider both colour inkjets and colour laser printers, old and new so long as refills are cheaply and easily available.

A built-in scanner and networking are not essential but are plus points- certainly if it is a good quality scanner that works well with SANE and easy wifi setup. I have seen the LinuxDeals site and that is a great Linux resource but unfortnately you cannot list the printers in terms of cost per colour and BW page. 

I know HPs don't 'officially' support but still offer generally excellent support for Linux but I'm not sure if any of their printers can compete with Kodak for running costs? Kodak are thankfully increasingly in the minority now as it seems like most of the other big printer manufacturers do offer at least some support for Linux these days- you really can't afford to ignore the penguin any more!

EDIT

I should add that I have a pretty tight budget - £500 is about the upper limit I have for buying a printer but I wouldn't expect to be paying much more than £200 if it was an A4 inkjet. I would also consider A3 inkjets if they fall into this price range and can compete with the Kodak on consumables/ run cost.
