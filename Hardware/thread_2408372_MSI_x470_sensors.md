### Post by CatKiller on 2018-12-17
That looks like a standard sensors output. You'll often have some sensor lines that are gibberish because the standard off-the-shelf chip the motherboard manufacturer has used can do more than they have a use for.

### Post by CatKiller on 2018-12-20
There wouldn't necessarily *be* a sensor for the RAM voltage. That tends to be set rather than detected.

The thing I tend to do is to look at the readings in the BIOS, and then pair them up with the sensor readings.
