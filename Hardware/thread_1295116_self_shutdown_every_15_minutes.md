### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-19
Run the memtest86 option from the boot menu. For a couple of hours. Any error in memtest86 will be fatal under any operating system.

If you can't get memtest86 to work, it would be a mistake to boot into something more complicated.

Did you cheap-out on the power supply? Cheap power supplies will often deliver only half of their rated power, and that will cause symptoms like this.
