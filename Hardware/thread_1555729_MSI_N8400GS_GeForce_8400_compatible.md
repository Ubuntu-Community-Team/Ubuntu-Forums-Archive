### Post by Fafler on 2010-08-18
I have several 8400GS cards running round the house. Ideal for low end 3D and, and most of all, video decoding and most of all, low power requirements, video decoding on the GPU and great Linux support. If you want a new card for $30, it seems you're getting a 8400GS. Going up to $40 gets you a GT 210 [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127454](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127454) it's two generations newer (or one, depending on how you count) but not much better performance. Faster clock, same amount of cores and RAM. Your 8400 board and other components, BTW, are made from a reference design from Nvidia. Every 8400 looks exactly like that and they all act the same within a few % difference in 3D performance for clocking reasons.
