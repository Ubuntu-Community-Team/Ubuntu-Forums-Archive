### Post by TheFu on 2022-06-14

Nobody here works for Canonical.  I was a programmer for years, but have little interest in spending 6+ months creating a driver for a device that I don't own, need, want, without compensation.  Plus, I'm older now and know that $50-$150 for a new scanner is much more cost effective.

In the last 2 years, I have created some software (not drivers) for personal itches, so I'm still programming, but choose what I'd like to work on  .... or get paid to do.

Drivers are really the responsibility of the vendor.  Why support any vendor who refuses to support the OS you prefer?  You aren't the first person to learn this.  I've been burned a few times .. with scanners, printers, even HDDs, modems, and RAID controllers over the decades.  I'm stupid. It took me a few more times that most to learn to spend my money only with companies that are excellent at making Linux drivers.  Most recently, I decided to stop buying nVidia GPUs.

I've even been burned by a RAID controller card that specifically said "Supports Linux" on the box ... so I bought it only to discover the driver was for a 3 yr old kernel and no newer drivers were released.

Live and learn.  Spending the extra $10-$20 for a well-supported scanner with excellent Linux support is worth avoiding the frustration to me.  The same for some NICs (network adapters). I don't buy anything from Realtek anymore ... after poor performance and HW failures.  Life is too short to deal with crappy vendors.
