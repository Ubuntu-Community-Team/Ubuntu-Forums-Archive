### Post by 98cwitr on 2012-01-03
So since I bought my gf a macbook last Christmas, I've periodically used her old Dell Lat. 600m (1.5GHz Pen. M, 512MB, 60GB 5.4k rpm hd) as something to tote around on vacation to upload photos and browse the web. She has some sentimental value in it, so we keep it around.

 Even running Ubuntu (11.10), this thing was dog slow. So for Christmas this year, I asked her for the following:

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820145068](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820145068)
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820208529](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820208529)
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16812186098](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16812186098)

The old hard drive in this thing averaged 22MB/s and grinded away just trying to open the browser...total garbage. So the HD is the bottleneck, but it could use some more RAM too.

So got the parts in, and first thing first, upgraded the BIOS from A11 to A17...big jump.

RAM went in fine, but the mobo wasn't seeing the HD. To make a LONG story short...a few parts later and some "fabrication" made to the enclosure, I got the damn thing to read in BIOS.

Now if you guys have seen the prices of old IDE harddrives, you'll know you can't get one for under $100. Granted, they're over 100GB, but for downloading torrents to move to a file server or just for photos and browsing, this thing has really come back to life with this SSD. I was hoping to see the 90MB/s advertised, but will definitely settle with the 51MB/s avg. I'm getting.

Install of 10.04 LTS took all of 20 minutes from a USB flash drive, and system boot time was cut in half. Browser now opens with just a small blip from the new SSD. I can now finally have multiple tabs open and run more than one program at once! YAY!

Moral of the story, if you can live with 32GB for a primary HD, this is the cheapest and best option on the market. $86 for a 32GB IDE SSD that is capable of 90MB/s.
