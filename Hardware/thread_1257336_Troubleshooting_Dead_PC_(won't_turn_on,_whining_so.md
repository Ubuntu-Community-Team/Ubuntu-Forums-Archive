### Post by Griff on 2009-09-03
I'll make a long story short.  A PC was given to me a couple days ago and it won't turn on.  I know it was operating normally until well....it stopped turning on.

I popped the case and when the PC is plugged in and off there appears to be a whining sound coming from the psu.  I couldn't remember if this was normal or not.  It doesn't go away though.  If you hit the power button nothing happens.  Any advice in exploring this thing to confirm if it's a bad psu or something else (like a bad mobo)?  The only problems I've ever faced are fan and hdd related.

Thanks,
Griff

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2009-09-03
I've heard PSU's whine before when plugged in. It's probably some annoying (but harmless) coil whine from the standby circuitry.

-Disconnect/remove anything unneeded, like add-in cards (including video card), hard disk, etc.
-Double-check the PSU connections (especially main and CPU power connectors)
-Double-check the case's power/reset button connectors
-If you have multiple RAM sticks, try using just one (and try different ones).
