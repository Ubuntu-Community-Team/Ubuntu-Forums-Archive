### Post by valkyrie on 2009-06-26
I have an Asus N51 laptop, just a few weeks old. It came pre-installed with Vista. I installed Ubuntu 9.04 as a dual boot. And for the last few weeks I've owned and used it, everything worked just fine. Now the wireless will not turn on, in either Vista or Ubuntu.

It actually stopped working while I was in Ubuntu using Firefox; it was suddenly "trying to connect" to various wireless networks available, and despite having all correct WEP keys and even a couple open networks, it could not establish a connection despite being just fine a few minutes before. (I had not been in any hardware or software settings, just using Firefox. I also wasn't on any different website than I had been on for the past hour.) Something similar had happened once or twice previously, and the solution had been to turn off the laptop, come back after a while, and then in Vista the "diagnose connection problem" dialogue would point out that the wireless adaptor was off and offered to turn it on.

This time, however, Vista just states the wireless is off and needs to be turned on without saying it can turn it on. The function key combination does not work to turn the wireless on or off. I have looked through BIOS and "turned on" everything that is related to wireless, with no changes to the problem (even disabled everything, rebooted, enabled everything, rebooted, and still no change). The function key combination does not work in either OS any more (it did work in both before), and I can't get the wireless to turn on in either OS.

I know it WAS working perfectly fine, I just can't find where to turn on the wireless card. (I'd prefer not to have to turn it on in Vista, since I'm not using that as the OS most of the time and I am confident there's a better solution inside Ubuntu anyway... I just don't know what it is :D )

Thank you in advance for any advice!
