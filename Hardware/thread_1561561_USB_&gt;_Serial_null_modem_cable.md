### Post by jarviser on 2010-08-26
Back in those days we didn't have 8GB pen drives for the price of a cable. If it's a one-off that may be quicker.

Nullmodem crossover RS232 cables are now usually replaced by ethernet crossovers.  If you have ethernet sockets on the two machines you could use a crossover ethernet cable and "network" the two comps together (I've only done that on Windows so don't ask me for more info on Ubuntu networking)

I think the soap-on-a-rope so-called USB nullmodems usually need their own windows compatible software.

EDIT: Maybe on re-reading the post, the Amstrad may not even have ethernet?
