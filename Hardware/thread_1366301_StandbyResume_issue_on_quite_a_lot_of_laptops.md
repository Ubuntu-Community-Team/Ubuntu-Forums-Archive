Hi there,

while browsing through the Ubuntu forums, I'm sort of astonished that this problem appears not to have arisen so far for any other people than me. I have quite a lot of different laptops, as there are:
- 2x Dell Latitude D830
- Toshiba D350
- IBM/Lenovo Thinkpad R60
- Dell Mini 9
- Lenovo N500 (Celeron core)

On ALL of these different platforms, the following problem occurs: on roughly every 15th or 20th resume process after a decent sleep period, the system wakes up submissively, the monitor lights up and the logon screen reappears (screen is locked after going to standby). I start typing in my credentials, but either while doing so, or after having pressed <Enter>, the system IMMEDIATELY goes back to sleep/standy mode. That's it! When I immediately wake it up again (2nd time), everything works as expected: either login continues or I can finish the logon process. Everything is fine then.

I just can't believe this issue has passed unnoticed so far. My pm-suspend logs are clean and no unusual failure is taken note of.

Did anybody else experience this problem? Any way to resolve this? It's just getting on my nerves to wake up the system twice, and it's not good for hdd health, I suppose.

Thanks in advance!
