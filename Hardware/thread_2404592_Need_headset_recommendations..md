### Post by 1clue on 2018-10-25
Hi,

I currently use a Corsair over-ear USB gaming headset with a microphone on the boom. I hate it. It has its own sound card built in, its own volume and mute buttons.

The problem is, any of these buttons is treated by Linux as though it were a mouse click. So I push (accidentally or not) one of the buttons and I'm halfway into a mouse click (but not a release) and then my entire system is messed up until I unplug the headset, wait at least 15 seconds or so, and plug it back in.

I like the idea of a built-in mute button, I like the sound quality and the microphone works well when it works at all.

What I want though is a similar headset that plugs into my sound card with the traditional TRRL connector. Preferably one that fits my phone too. I've tried external microphones hooked up to the sound card, but they work sporadically at best and sound terrible.

What I'd like from the group is models of headphone that have good sound quality for music listening, plus a microphone that works well with Ubuntu, without a built-in sound card or any of the extra whistles. A mute button for the mic is OK, but no volume knob and no weird behavior on the system when I use it. Don't need a volume wheel on the headset either.

I have a good sound card, I just don't have a line on a good stereo headset with microphone. I don't mind spending in excess of USD $100 for the headset, you have to spend money to get something decent that you can wear all day IMO.
