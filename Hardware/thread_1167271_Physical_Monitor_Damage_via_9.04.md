### Post by RayBluer on 2009-05-22
I've been showing off Ubuntu 9.04 -- even though I still use and like CentOS -- and have good luck with it. I decided to load the Live CD on my son's computer to see if I could at least get him to dual boot Linux and XP. He had an older Dell CRT monitor that was capable of millions of colors at 1024x768 resolution. Apparently Ubuntu decided to go above that resolution limit and somehow fried the monitor. It no longer works above VGA resolution -- which I was able to confirm. Fortunately I have a few CRTs stored in my garage that a company was throwing away (similar model, a bit newer) so I was able to replace my son's monitor without issue. But this has definitely cooled me to the idea of "showing off" Ubuntu.

This leads to a comment and a question.

Comment: It seems to me that Ubuntu is beginning to worry a bit too much about the surface (flashy interface) and a bit too little about content (a solid OS). It may be that I notice this because I'm usually using trailing edge hardware and Ubuntu's thing seems  to be to compete with Windows on newer computers. Up until my son's computer I had had good luck with 9.04, but 8.10 would crash every time it timed out.

Question: Ubuntu is a great way to show off Linux but now I'm nervous about using it. Is there a way at boot up to make Ubuntu test the monitor before going into a really high resolution? I don't want to destroy anyone ele's monitor.

Thanks for letting me vent a bit.
