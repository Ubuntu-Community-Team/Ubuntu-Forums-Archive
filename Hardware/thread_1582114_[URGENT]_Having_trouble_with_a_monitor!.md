### Post by Fafler on 2010-09-26
Try pressing ctrl+alt+f1. Does the console appear on the second monitor? And are you knowledgeable enough to use it to rescue your data from it? If yes, them just go ahead. Or ask again.

It would be nice to know what graphics card you have.

Or just do what sanderd17 says. If it's a recent laptop (less than 4 years old) it's likely to use a SATA drive. It will plug into another computer using standard SATA connectors. If it's older and using IDE, you need a 40 to 44 pin adapter for it. Or one of those USB to IDE cables that provides a 44 pin connector.
