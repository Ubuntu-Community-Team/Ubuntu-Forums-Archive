### Post by Crunchy the Headcrab on 2009-07-08
I have the X1.

Regarding the cd rom issue I have a fix (found it while trying to install Suse, which wouldn't even install).

> In the default configuration you will have a nasty surprise of all kinds of errors sooner or later during the installation process. This is all because of the *Enhanced* SATA setting in the BIOS. Before wasting good CDs trying to burn again the installation kit, try rebooting the laptop and pressing F2 during the flames to open BIOS settings, then from *Advanced* tab switch to *Compatible* mode instead of *Enhanced*.[http://monalisa.cern.ch/blog/2008/09/16/ubuntu-on-asus-g50v/](http://monalisa.cern.ch/blog/2008/09/16/ubuntu-on-asus-g50v/)

This fixes installation errors as well as issues regarding mounting the disc drive.  In my case it would work for a few (30 or so) seconds and then say it couldn't mount, at which point I could not access cds/dvds and the button to open the drive would stop working.  Following that fix, everything works fine and the disc drive mounts and unmounts perfectly and the disc drawer button works great.
