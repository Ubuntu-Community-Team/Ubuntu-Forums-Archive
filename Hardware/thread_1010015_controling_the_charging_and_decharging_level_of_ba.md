My Lenovo X300 is dual boot ubuntu and windows XP. How can I do the following under ubuntu?

Under Windows XP, there is a tool (part of the power manager) that allows one to control the charging and decharging level of the battery: the battery will be charged only if the charging level falls below a certain customizable level (say 20%), and it will charge up to a customizable level (say 50%). The documentation of this tool promises an increased battery life (which appears reasonable). In particular, it recommends that the battery should not be kept fully charged (100%), if this is not actually needed. That's why I specified a 20%-50% cycle which fits my everyday needs.

I'd like to control that under ubuntu, too. Fortunately, the hardware for battery charging somehow memorizes the configuration specified under windows XP. (Otherwise it could not work when the fully switched-off laptop is connected to a power socket.) But once it happened to me that the laptop "forgot" these settings (I don't know what triggered this) and the laptop started to keep the battery fully charged (100%). Booting the laptop once under Windows XP (I rarely use it!) brought me back to the 20%-50% cycles.

Any tips will be appreciated!
