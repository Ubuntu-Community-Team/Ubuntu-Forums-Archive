### Post by Bashing-om on 2014-03-20
mogsareus; Hi !

My 2 cents worth.

Now-a-days it takes some hosses to run the flagship editions of (u)buntu.
> 
RecentIy bought a Panasonic CF-29 Toughbook. Its 1300 MHz, 760MB ram,

I think the minimum ram is 1 gig, and 2 gigs is recommended for a good experience. 
Might I suggest that ya try installing (Lubuntu) ? and see what results ?
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Lubuntu/GetLubuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Lubuntu/GetLubuntu)
Verify the .iso download:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM)
Burn to disk at a s l o w speed:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto)
Boot to the liveDVD's boot options screen and verify the burn ->
Boot the liveDVD, soon as bios screen clears depress and hold to right shift key -> language screen, escape key to accept the default ->
boot option screen -> choose "check disk for defects".

Now choose "try ubuntu", if all performs as expected then I once more suggest installing (L)ubuntu !
