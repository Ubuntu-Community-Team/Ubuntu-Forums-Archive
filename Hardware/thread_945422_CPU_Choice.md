### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2008-10-12
The computer system industry is actually having a boom in cheaper, smaller systems that consume less power because we're seeing diminishing returns with CPU speed/cores in "day-to-day" tasks. Other things like faster video cards, solid-state hard disks, and having a sufficient amount of RAM are more important for a responsive system. An AMD 6000+ will handle most workloads smoothly. Heavy math/science apps, software development, server use and Photoshop/video/audio editing are the main areas that will see benefit from more powerful CPU's).
