### Post by coffeecat on 2011-10-16
Hi, and welcome to the forum.

Sony Vaios vary a lot in their hardware specifications. For some Ubuntu just works, and for others users experience certain difficulties.

My VGN-NS20S, which originally came with Vista, has worked very well for me with every version of Ubuntu since 9.04/Jaunty, with the exception of Maverick/10.10 when the Fn-key combinations stopped working. A recently acquired VPCEJ gives poor graphics with its nvidia GPU with the default nouveau driver, but this is solved by installing the proprietary nvidia driver. But I haven't been able to get the Fn keys to work in 11.10. The brightness notification appears on-screen when I do the Fn+F5/F6 combination, but the brightness doesn't change.

Best advice is to run 11.10 from a live CD or live USB and see what works and what doesn't work. What's the GPU on your machine, and do you know the wireless chipset?
