### Post by em3raldxiii on 2010-09-05
Haha, glad you pointed out the batteries, because that's the first thing I was going to suggest.

I am not precisely sure what is at issue, but I would say the strongest contender here would be the radio connection with the base/antenna.  So, ensure you have fresh batteries, ensure the base/antenna is connected *firmly* to a reliable USB port, ensure the antenna/base is not being interfered with by wires (power cables are a known culprit), and ensure the mouse is close enough to the base.

Finally, and I know this seems stupid, check your mouse pad.  What I have discovered in the past is that the lens on my mouse slowly becomes either dirty or out of focus.  The solution has been to:  1. cover my mouse pad with a piece of clean white paper, 2. raise my mouse slightly by taping something thin to the bottom.

Let us know if *any* of these solutions seem to help.  Good Luck!

PS.  It's not likely a software/ubuntu issue.  While it's possible, I suppose, I strongly doubt it.
