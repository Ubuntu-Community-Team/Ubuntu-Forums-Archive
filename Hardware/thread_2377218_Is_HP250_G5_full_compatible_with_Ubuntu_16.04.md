### Post by DuckHook on 2017-11-10
The usual method to test for compatibility is to run a LiveUSB on the thing to see. The last time I tried this at Best Buy, the sales staff were very accommodating and allowed me to do so, but removed the SSD first so that it could not be endangered. So long as you explain your reasoning politely to them, they are usually very understanding. After all, would they prefer that you try out a floor model, or buy and then return to them a used and opened product (under their 30-day return policy)? If courteously pointed out to them that way, they usually see the sense in permitting a LiveUSB. Some are even quite curious about Linux!

### Post by DuckHook on 2017-11-10
Then I'm afraid I can't help you, as I have no experience with that laptop. Your concern with the wifi is quite valid. It's one of those areas where Linux support is sometimes spotty. Have you compared specs between the two models? Googling for specs will often (though not always) get you the details you need. However, it bears noting that manufacturers will sometimes switch wifi cards in the same model. They don't seem to care much about this detail so long as the component works with Windows.

Make sure that you can return the machine to your online vendor without a hefty restocking fee. I know. Easier said than done, but there are many who will accept returns for full money back, though you would likely have to pay for shipping.

You might also consider some of the Linux vendors too, which would be guaranteed to work with Linux and come with Ubuntu (or another distro of your choice) preloaded.

### Post by wildmanne39 on 2017-11-10
The specs says it has an Realtek 802.11b/g/n (1x1) Wi-Fi, it will probably work with Ubuntu if the has been out for a while but if the computer just came out on the market then it most likely will be several months before it will work.
