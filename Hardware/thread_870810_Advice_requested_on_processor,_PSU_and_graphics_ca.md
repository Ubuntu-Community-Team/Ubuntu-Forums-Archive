### Post by markbuntu on 2008-07-26
The new ATI HD 4870 is kicking nvidias *** and is fully supported by the ATI driver. There is a review at phoronix and a comparison with the nvidia 8800. The 4850 or even 3850 might be a better choice if funds are tight. 

Also, ATI has promised crossfire support for linux in the next quarter.

All of these cards need a beefy p/s (450w or better), even the nvidia 8800. And yes, the 8000 series is better than the 9000 series.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2008-07-26
Overkill on the PSU unless you aspire to have multiple video cards. Save yourself 20£ and go with the Corsair 520. If you can find the VX-450 cheaper, consider that.
A modern, high-powered system (with a single GPU) under full load uses 300W at most, even with a Core 2 Quad and X38 mobo:
[http://www.techreport.com/articles.x/14967/10](http://www.techreport.com/articles.x/14967/10)

There's only one site I trust with PSU reviews and that's SPCR
[http://www.silentpcreview.com/article699-page1.html](http://www.silentpcreview.com/article699-page1.html)

Also, if you have a mobo that OC's, you can save yourself some money on a "slower" CPU.
