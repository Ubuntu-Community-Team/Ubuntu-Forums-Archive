### Post by cascade9 on 2010-11-29
I can see the video card + rough roads issue (I've had it myself). Still, I'd rather get a 870 chipset motherboard + a cheap ATI (or nvidia) card. You should be able to get a gigabyte 870 motherboard and card for about the same cost. (that should be faster, and/or use similar amounts of power). 

As for the heatsink- those big, tall heatsinks arent as heavy as what they seem. The backplate system that most of them use makes them much less likely to cause damage than the 'old days' when you would get 700gm CPU coolers mounted on sockets designed for a max of 454gm, and held on only by a few plastic notches....  

I dont know what the socket mounted weight on the  Coolit Systems Eco (all they say is "keeping the solution well below the maximums specified by Intel and AMD"). Still a lot of them are over the AMD max weight, and not everybody will do what I used to when my computer went down lots of rough, dirty roads. I used to put foam around the heatsink, and in one case I ziptied the cooler to the case LOL. 

If you (well, whoever you are building it for) is really worried about weight, then there is an easy solution- the stock cooler + a zalman FB 123. Mount a 120mm fan on the FB 123, presto! You'll get much cooler temps and/or less noise than stock. A nice bonus is that there is even less weight on the socket than stock. Cheap, easy.

### Post by Kir_B on 2010-12-21
Update time.

We placed our order, with it assembled by the company we ordered it from. The final components had changed a little bit. As you can see below, we decided to put a hold on the video card, in order to see how the build performs without it, including the performance of the onboard HD 4290 graphics chipset. We also wanted to wait and see other peoples experiences with the HD 6850/6870's on Ubuntu (last time I checked the drivers for Linux were still not available on AMD's website).


CPU: AMD 1055T X6

Cooler: Stock cooler (works fine so far, as we aren't over clocking yet)

Mobo: Asus M4A89GTD PRO/USB3

Case: STORM  SCOUT GAMING CASE

HDD: Western Digital Caviar Black 1TB SATA3 

RAM: G.SKILL F3-12800CL9D-4GBRL Ripjaws PC3-12800 ddr3 2X2GB 

GPU: Onboard HD 4290 chipset

DVD: Samsung SH-S223C 22X DVD Writer SATA Black OEM

PSU: XFX 750W Black Edition Single Rail Modular ATX 12V 62A 24PIN ATX Power Supply 80PLUS Silver 

We changed the motherboard from the Gigabyte to the Asus because of a great deal ($125 canadian) and we picked the XFX power supply for the same reason. An after market cooler will probably be purchased in the future, and the graphics card is another story.

So far our experience with the ATI graphics are both positive and negative. The HD 4290 performs okay, but the proprietary drivers from the Ubuntu repository are not up to date! With the drivers installed from the repository, we experience artifacts (little horizontal lines) at the login screen, which makes us have to login by memory. We are also stuck with a watermark that comes with no way to disable, and is annoyingly located in the bottom right of our desktop (it says "AMD Unsupported hardware"). Apparently, this is because the drivers are not current enough. The graphics work well enough for the compiz desktop effects, but until we install a relatively demanding game, we won't have a clear idea of the 4290's true capability (we're not expecting much from them, but may be pleasantly surprised). My only experience with on board graphics, was with the Nvidia 6150 SE, and the 4290 appears to be much better, but time will tell how much better. I'm not sure if we'll wait for canonical to get the updated drivers in the repo, which would be preferred, or if we'll go ahead and install the [drivers from the AMD site]("http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/linux/Pages/radeon_linux.aspx?type=2.7&product=2.7.4.3.5.3.1&lang=English"). I'm not sure which version of the ATI drivers we have installed, as the version naming scheme that I see in synaptic makes absolutely no sense!

There's still some money left ($320) in the gift fund, so we'll be picking up a cooler and a new graphics card sometime in the near future, but we'll wait and see how peoples experiences go with the HD 6850 and HD 6870 before we decide on ATI or Nvidia. We'd really like to go with ATI's cards for the performance, cost and low power consumption, but it does appear that their support on Linux just isn't as good as Nvidia (feel free to correct me if I'm wrong.).

Other than the graphics driver issue, everything else appears to be well supported on the Ubuntu 10.04.1 (maintenance release) we installed. The installation went flawlessly - great job Developers.

The only other issue we have, is a huge issue! We watch a fair bit of hockey via the internet, but doing this in full screen mode via Firefox or Chromium, results in a complete system lock up (approximately 6 times per two and a half hours). I believe this is a known bug that has been eluding everyone for months now, so we'll have to wait for a fix. The work around that we now use for this issue, is to run the Ultimate Edition 2.8 gamers live dvd. This works flawlessly and is one heck of a live dvd that loads the drivers during the boot (Awesome job guys). I'm not sure why the Ultimate edition is not plagued with this issue, but something tells me that if I was to install it, the first (Ubuntu) update would probably start causing the same issue (or a new one).

That about sums it up, but if I think of anything else worth adding, I'll come back and post it here.

Peace. Kirby ):P
