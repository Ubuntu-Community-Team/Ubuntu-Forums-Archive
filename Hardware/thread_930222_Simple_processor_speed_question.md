### Post by tstack77 on 2008-09-25
A few years ago it seemed that processor speeds were over the 3ghz mark, but now everything you find is in the 2-3ghz range. 

What has changed? 

Is it due to dual-core systems actually multiplying the speeds or are they simply more efficient at these same speeds?

---

### Post by ElectroGrav on 2008-09-25
A single core of my dual-core 2.1GHz laptop is over 3 times faster in the SuperPi benchmark than my 3.0GHz Pentium 4 laptop. A program using two cores would theoretically be up to 6 times faster.

GHz is a totally unreliable comparison of CPU speed unless it's between CPUs of identical architecture.

---

### Post by Ja38 on 2008-09-25
Speed has become less important than FSB (front side bus) speed and core efficiency. A new E8600 CPU FSB runs at 1333MHz and is around two to three times as fast as an old Pentium 4. It also has more cache.
