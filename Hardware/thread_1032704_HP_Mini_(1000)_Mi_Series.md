### Post by Ubuntu Joe on 2009-01-15
All right, here's what I've experienced:

First of all, I'll answer my questions above:

1. I don't think it is Netbook Remix, just HP's proprietary shell . . . maybe a little remix, but I'm not certain.

2. The terminal is disabled, but one need merely ENable it!  So simple . . . it's in the settings, and exactly where you'd expect it to be.

3. Absolutely.  I installed a few programs for fun, though only from the Ubuntu repos . . . I'm pretty certain it would be no problem to gedit some other repos in and download additional software with ease, though I didn't test.

4. I don't think so . . . here's the thing, the HP desktop shell (which I cannot turn off to save my life,) is inextricably linked to the OS as is . . . which is bad for a few reasons I'll discuss below.  I don't see any way to upgrade from 8.04 to 8.10 . . . sigh.

NOW FOR MY THOUGHTS:

This little device broke my heart, and here's why:

The good:
So beautiful!  This little thing is hot; my wife even giggled when she saw it . . . in this case, a good sign.  At first I was smitten too . . . the keyboard is HUGE compared to other netbooks, and the screen looks beautiful . . . I got the 10.1" 2G RAM version, btw.  So light yet so solid . . . a great concept.  Surfing online is a breeze; audio & video loading/streaming without a hitch.  Even the HP desktop shell was cleverly designed . . . HOWEVER:

The bad:
I ended up returning this device today . . . I'm very lonely now, but it had to happen.  The desktop enforced upon you by HP, while creative, doesn't work as it's meant to.  From left to right: the email third of the screen would NOT except my Thunderbird account no matter how many times I entered it and restored and tried again and fiddled and tinkered . . . just kept saying my email account wasn't set up yet!  BAH!  Nonsense . . . I click on the 'Set up Your Account' button and it took me right to Thunderbird with all my emails sitting right there!  Working FINE!  Once into the Ubuntu OS proper, everything worked fine, but HP's integration with their own "clever" desktop SUCKED.  The center section was fine linking to Firefox, though it is not customizable and not extremely useful . . . the right section however is irritating in that, while being non-customizable, it also forces you to use the LOUSY media center software, ELISA . . . I hate Elisa, and of course you can't change it to something else . . . crummy . . .

There's more too . . .

The CAMERA?!  WTF?!  The camera itself is probably fine considering it's most likely the same as in the one HP uses in the XP chassis, but the drivers they have for it, blow big time.  The effect of which is a camera which renders you in total darkness only remedied by the equivalent of a 1,000,000 candlelight flood . . .  Essentially useless . . . unless you're sitting in direct noon-day sunlight.  I even got the HP tech to get one and try to video himself . . . he had the same issue, so it's clearly one of the bugs they just ignored hoping to firmware-fix it later.

BOTTOM LINE:

I sent it back because I'm still within my 21 days of purchase, so I can get a full refund.  With my money in hand, I'm going to keep checking around the forums and with HP and see if these bugs are fixed.  I think that's a good plan.  Maybe in a month or two, the Mini Mi will perform the way it was intended and I'll get me another one . . . with the imminent 6-cell battery to boot!  I was very sad to pack the Mini up, but I simply expect more for my dollar, and while I believe HP (more likely, you guys) will fix these bugs, I don't feel like racking my brain to make my consumer netbook work as advertised . . . not my job.

Your thoughts my brothers and sisters in Ubuntu???

### Post by vanzandtj on 2009-04-26
On my mini 1000 mi, I could only connect with wifi by running "dhclient wlan0" in a terminal.  Setting "automatic" didn't help.  Then I added this to /etc/network/interfaces:

 auto wlan0 
 iface wlan0 eth0 dhcp
     pre-up (ifconfig wlan0 up && iwconfig wlan0 ap any && iwconfig wlan0 essid any)

(Please add spaces before "pre-up".)  Now, it's connecting well.  There ought to be a more user-friendly way to set this up, though.

I assume HP will eventually offer Jaunty from their repository.  I will be running settings | Update Manager regularly to check.
