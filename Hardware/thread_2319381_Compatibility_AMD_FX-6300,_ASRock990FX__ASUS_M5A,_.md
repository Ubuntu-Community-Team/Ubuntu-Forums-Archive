### Post by gordintoronto on 2016-04-05
Welcome to the forums!

I have no direct experience with any of the components you listed, but I pay a lot of attention to what works and what doesn't.

I think most people would prefer an ASUS motherboard to an ASRock. Neither one is perfect, of course.

Spend the extra $30, get a Western Digital Black hard drive.

Generally, if you build a computer with the latest parts, you will have a better experience with the latest Linux. Ubuntu 16.04 will be released in less than three weeks, and you should plan on using it.

You didn't mention the case and power supply. You should get a case that has good air flow to keep things cool. And since you will be installing everything into it, you want to avoid sharp edges!

Don't try to save money on the power supply. When a power supply fails, it might take out the motherboard and memory as well.

Is this your video card? [http://www.newegg.ca/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814487024&cm_re=GTX_750_Ti_2GB-_-14-487-024-_-Product](http://www.newegg.ca/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814487024&cm_re=GTX_750_Ti_2GB-_-14-487-024-_-Product)

When I search the reviews for ubuntu or linux, I only see good reviews.

I find the reviews on Newegg helpful, although you never know when vendors enter good or bad reviews.

If you have never built a computer before, keep grounded and take your time. Good luck!
