### Post by freelook on 2009-06-20
Hello,

I'm looking for a good solid general purpose printer for Ubuntu.  Here's what I want:

[LIST]
[*]Has to "Just work", without having to find somebody's homemade drivers, and with a strong preference against going to a website and downloading somebody's drivers.  (apt-get is fine)
[*]Should print fast.  I've had printers before that let me easily choose between good quality and fast printing.  I like that.
[*]Be reasonably priced.  I'd like to not spend more than $100, but definitely not more than $200
[*]Have full support for the printer.  For example, I should be able to monitor ink levels, clean the heads, etc. all from Ubuntu, and preferably Ubuntu's interface, not a third party program.
[*]The newer the better (I want to buy it from the store, not from eBay).
[/LIST]

I'm looking for particular models here, not brands in general.  Also, if you have any information about ink replacement for your recommendation, please leave that as well.

...but I can't find any easily accessible information.  I've looked at the OpenPrinting database, the forums, and Ubuntu's community documentation, but I'm afraid the information is often not very helpful.  For example most of the time it's not even mentioned how easy the printer is to set up, and it doesn't talk about stuff like whether ink levels can be monitored and stuff.  I could complain further, but you get the idea.

Any recommendations, personal success stories?

Much obliged.

### Post by kaibob on 2009-06-20
HP Deskjet 6988. It meets all of your requirements and can be purchased for $90.00. Price per page for black only is around 3.5 cents, which is very reasonable for an ink jet. I usually get the cartridges on sale or with a coupon, which brings the price down further. 

[http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/us/en/en/WF05a/18972-18972-236251-14438-64340-3422858.html](http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/us/en/en/WF05a/18972-18972-236251-14438-64340-3422858.html)

My only complaint is that it shakes the table a bit when in fast mode but, fortunately, there is a button that slows things down. You check ink levels with HPLIP, which is in Synaptic.
