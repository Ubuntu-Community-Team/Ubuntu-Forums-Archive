### Post by George_Ronald_Mayfield on 2013-08-23
I thought I'd use the forum to help me buy the right kind of computer for my home. It's been awhile since my last purchase (2006-ish), and maybe what I'm looking for isn't even a traditional desktop. 
You guys probably have better ideas of what's out there and how I could get the requirements I need at the most affordable price. I'm in the $1,500 price range.

In order of importance:

* handles videos easily - I have burned all my movies to an external HDD, and use PS3 Media Server to send video from my HDD to my PS3 through my PC. The program streams the videos in a format that the PS3 takes, so it's using a bit of processing power. I'd like to put all of its switches in High Quality mode.

* minimal problems with Ubuntu - I'd rather not have to fight with compatibility issues when I open the box. I don't have a problem installing Ubuntu and drivers myself, though. I don't plan on dual booting. 

* power consumption - I almost never turn off my computer except for an occasional restart. 

* eSATA ports - I have two SATA external drives.

* HDD space isn't too important. Because of my external HDDs, I've been fine with only 250GB so far. Even economy PCs have more than that nowadays.

I also use an Ubuntu laptop sometimes. Another thought I've briefly entertained is using a server for all that above, and just using the laptop for general use. I don't know much about servers, really, but it kind of seems that's more of what I need one for. If that's the answer, please be detailed.
