### Post by TheFu on 2014-10-14
Forget clock speed.  It isn't possible to compare apples-onions using clock speed and hasn't been for many years.

Make a list of things you thinks are important, prioritize them, then go looking for laptops that meet those requirements, in order.  Look for supported wifi, if that is important to you. Pretty much everything else will be supported out of the box these days.
[http://blog.jdpfu.com/2010/04/23/buying-a-laptop-stuff-to-know](http://blog.jdpfu.com/2010/04/23/buying-a-laptop-stuff-to-know) might be helpful.

### Post by frank75 on 2014-10-15
Personally, I'd look for a good, used laptop on Ebay or Amazon with a Core 2 Duo, say 2.2GHz or so and then bump the RAM up to 4GB, add an SSD drive and spend the rest of the cash on something else.  You don't need the "latest/greatest" hardware to run Linux on and actually getting some of the newer stuff with UEFI can just make it more of a PITA to do a Linux install.  It's very easy to add bluetooth, a good 802.11n wifi card adn anything else that you want to a laptop and it doesn't cost all that much either. 
  I'd figure for around $200 or less you can get into a very nice laptop and have plenty of cash left over for any little upgrades that you'd like to do.  JMHO, YMMV.
