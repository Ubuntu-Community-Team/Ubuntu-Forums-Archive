### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-07-01
You can make it work, but Linux support for that WiFi card (made by a company called Ralink) is poor.  If the laptop had had an Intel card it would have worked out of the box, like mine did.

Google for "presario cq56 ubuntu wireless" and you'll find lots of people with the same exact problem in older versions of Ubuntu.  You can try following some of their instructions and solutions, but most of them seem to require typing commands in a terminal window.

The newest version of Ubuntu, 12.04 LTS, may not require these hassles - it may support your wireless card automatically.  Have you tried it?
