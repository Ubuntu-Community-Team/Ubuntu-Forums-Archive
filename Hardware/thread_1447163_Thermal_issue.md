### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-04-05
Laptops are especially prone to building up gunk over time.  When you couple that with the tiny fans and cramped conditions, it's a miracle that more laptops don't shutdown with heat issues all the time!

If you're not up to taking your apart, suggest you get one of those cans of cleaning spray (generally compressed air or some inert gas) and spray it through the heat vent(s) on your laptop.

Yeah, I know this sounds simple, but I have a several-year old laptop that started overheating recently and that cleaned out the vents, the CPU fan, and now it's back running at normal temps.
