### Post by Ares Drake on 2012-09-28
> **HuitZiloP said:**
> So the problem is I can't get to my monitor's native resolution and am stuck  with either 1024x768, 640x480 and even 320x240. 

What is your monitor's native resolution?

> **HuitZiloP said:**
> From what I understand, the problem is my monitor not being detected properly due to lack of proper EDID info.

[LIST]
[*]In case your monitor is connected via a blue VGA cable and both monitor and PC have the white DVI connector, you might be able to easily avoid EDID problems by using a DVI cable. Usually the monitors provide propper EDID via DVI.

[*]You can also force your monitor to use a resolution not advertised via EDID by using xrandr in terminal. Beware that the proprietary NVIDIA driver does not alaways support xrandr, if I remember correctly. There are a lot of How-Tos for xrandr around, just google. The following two links are some I had stored, but you might find even better ones at google.
[/LIST]
