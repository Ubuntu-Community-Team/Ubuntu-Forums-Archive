### Post by mysticreporterr@yahoo.com on 2011-03-22
This forum is a discussion regarding setting up the Lexmark X4650 All In One Printer.  I am using:

uname -a
Linux ******** 2.6.35-27-generic #48-Ubuntu SMP Tue Feb 22 20:25:46 UTC 2011 x86_64 GNU/Linux

I am actually on the phone with the Lexmark Linux  support team as I write this.  Apparently the Lexmark printer driver  mentioned in the article and it’s variants such as  lexmark-08z-series-driver-1.0-1.i386.deb.sh.tar.gz 



 At any rate the support team is trying to be helpful.  Apparently  they seem to feel that to have full support for x86_64 you would need a  64 bit driver but they only have 32 bit drivers for this printer.  Mine  works in print but not scan.

They  said they will be notifying product engineers to add a file that is  missing from the existing driver to support x86_64.  Not sure what the  turn around time will be.


Lexmark now supports Linux and when you go to the [www.lexmark.com](www.lexmark.com) website, and go to Support/Downloads you can enter your product identifier in the search box, and when you see the downloads you can filter by operating system (and Linux is supported).  The online chat does not support linux but you can call 1-800-LEXMARK and select the Linux support option (Lots of audio menus before getting to the support) and you will get to talk to a fairly well informed individual (at least in my case).


I am hoping they update the driver for my printer soon.  It doesn't make sense to not support it when the fix is fairly simple.

BTW the driver file name is: lexmark-08z-series-driver-1.0-1.i386.deb.sh.tar.gz

in case you were wondering.  Prints but no scan.
