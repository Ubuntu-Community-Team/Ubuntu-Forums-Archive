### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-04-25
Yes - your hard drive is toast.  Let's hope you have already copied off any important files to another drive?

I have a 1545 myself (Ubuntu 10.04 LTS).  Replacing the hard drive on a 1545 is about the easiest thing you could ever do.  With the laptop right-side up, the drive is under your left hand as you type.  Turn the laptop over and remove the drive with the two phillips screws that hold the drive in; slide it out the side and then remove the bracket (two more screws).  I can swap out one of these drives in about 30 seconds.

But you'll have to re-install Ubuntu most likely.

If you ever want to upgrade the CPU (in case you have a Celeron or something), that is pretty easy to - I upgraded my Pentium Dual Core to a faster Core 2 Duo that runs cooler too.  Took about ten minutes.
