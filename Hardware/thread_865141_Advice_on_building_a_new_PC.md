Looks pretty sweet, although you may want to consider upgrading the GPU to an 8800GT or one of the 9 series. The 8600's are underpowered for their price. ### Post by s3a on 2008-08-29
You will actually not see the whole 4 GB in 32 bit (you will probably see 3 GB), so even with 4 GB you'll want to use 64 bit. 64 bit Linux has very good support. I know because I use it. Go for 2x2GB. 64 bit spends more RAM than 32 bit but on the positive side, the CPU is faster in 64 bit. And with 4 GB RAM, do **sudo apt-get install preload**. That command will download a package that preloads your most used applications to RAM making your computer even faster than it should already be!
