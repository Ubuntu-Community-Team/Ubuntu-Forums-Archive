### Post by Intelligitimate on 2008-05-24
This is just a warning to anyone else trying to fool around to get the S-Video working with these brand of cards. I have a gpc2 that I wanted to upgrade, and the best card it would took was the ATI Radeon x1550 (no AGP or PCI-E slots). I got the card, it worked great, only minor installation trouble that was easy to solve. So I wanted to utilize all the capabilities of the card, and get the out put to the TV working. Well, I downloaded Gatos and fglrx-control from the Synaptic Package Manager. I started usr/bin/firegl-control and switched it to clone mode, and it told me I needed to restart the X-server. So I hooked it up to the TV, and rebooted. Nothing but black on the TV, and nothing but white on the monitor (no grub, no commands, no splash screens, just white as soon as the power comes on). Removing the graphics card didn't help either. It fried something on the motherboard.

Now, the only other thing that might have had something to do with it was the fact I used regular RCA cables. I tried to look on the internet to see if you could just switch them out, and some seemed to say yes, or no, depending on if the signal was HDTV or not. But no one said anything like it could fry electronics, so I don't think it was that.

So anyone gooling in the future, trying to get the S-Video working with their ATI Radeon x1550 or similar card, I'd advise extreme caution.
