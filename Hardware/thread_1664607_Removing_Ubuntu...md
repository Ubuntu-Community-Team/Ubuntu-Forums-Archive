### Post by TBABill on 2011-01-12
Just make the Win 7 DVD from your download and boot from it. It'll take care of the rest, including formatting the drive and installing its bootloader. And relax a bit...asking how to install from a torrent download of Windows does send up lots of red flags in people's minds because it gives the perception of illegal activity by downloading a copy of Windows. Whether it is or is not is up to you to deal with, and if you have the key code the point is moot, but the claim of a downloaded copy of Windows appears to have started the hostility. Well, that and the appearance that the software has problems without investing sufficient time to find out completely what it is all about, how it works, what it can do and what it cannot, ways to work around some compatibility issues (using VM, Wine, etc.) and others.
 
It's not easy to switch from Windows in all cases and if it doesn't run Windows software it's because it wasn't designed to. It looks like you were able to get the OS that will support your requirements and just following the normal Windows install steps will care for your concern with removing Ubuntu and starting fresh. Just keep in mind if it's a full blown copy of Win 7 and not a OEM copy, the time to install will be much longer because you will be getting each driver individually and having many updates to install. I just went through those steps and you can count on a 2-3 hour install process, especially if MS Office, iTunes, printer and other drivers are involved. It's quite tedious and that's from experience.
