### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-04-18
Can't help asking, but why?

Do you have on of each, and one has a dead hard drive, the other good hard drive but other problems?

If you're thinking about buying a cheap laptop to canibalize the harddrive, that's a bad plan.  You can get a much better hard drive for WAY less money by just buying one.

In any case, I agree with the other posters.  They're both the same manufacturer, and judging by the model numbers, I'd guess they are just different configurations of the same laptop.  Hard drives are almost universally interchangeable, even in laptops.  Any laptop made in the last several years will have a SATA HDD, and with very few exceptions, they are completely interchangeable.

There's a few ultra-high-end models that either have thinner than standard solid state drives, and no room for a normal hight hard drive, and one manufacturer just came out with a 2.0GB drive that is slightly thicker than most other 2.5" drives, which means it won't fit in some laptops.  Beyond that, they're completely interchangeable.
