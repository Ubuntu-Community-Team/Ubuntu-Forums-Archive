### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-07-12
Ubuntu is a Linux-based distro, and is NOT going to cause a hard drive to fail -- anymore than any other OS would do this.

What can happen is that you install an OS and you use it a lot (because it's new) and that puts more wear on the drive than the PC just sitting idle.  

So, if the drive is failing (as yours appear to be), any increased usage is going to hasten that process.

Given the huge jump in bad sectors, using the drive is a recipe for disaster.  You need to replace the drives -- you can't "fix" a failing hard drive (short of taking the drive into a clean-room environment, disassembling it, and rebuilding it).
