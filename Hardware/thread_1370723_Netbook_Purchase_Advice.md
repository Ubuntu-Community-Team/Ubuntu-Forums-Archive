### Post by ziphi on 2010-01-02
Hi everyone,

I'm writing in this forum because I've been using (X)Ubuntu for four years, and for the last 1 1/2 years, this forum has solved nearly all of my computer-related problems, almost always by searching.
Today, I'll need a bit of advice: I consider purchasing a netbook as complete replacement for my desktop PC. I'll use Ubuntu, probably the netbook remix, as operating system.


First of all, to save your time:

The main reason for me to go mobile is I'm going mobile myself: Starting in August, I'll spend a year in the middle of nowhere in China. Does it make a lot more sense to wait for the next LTS release and make a fresh install on a new device in May or so? Will the prices sink considerably until then?


Now for the real thing:


How much am I willing to spend at most (if necessary)?

Budget: 350€ / 500$ excluding equipment


What is very important to me? What am I going to use the netbook for?

1. durability / size / weight: A screen size of 10" to 12" will suffice. I'll carry the netbook around in a messenger bag on my bike every day. I won't use it as a punching bag, but it's supposed not to break two days after the warranty has expired. Should I look for SSD to prevent hard disk failures? Will this be very expensive?

2. Playing music and videos (rhythmbox, vlc): Silently running in the background, playing music will be the most common task.

3. textprocessing / latex / mail / browsing / instant messaging

4. connectivity: (W)LAN and at least 3 USB slots are indispensable. Digital output isn't necessary, but very welcome.

4. sound / video quality: I'll have to work in a very bright environment at times and the screen should meet this requirement. The netbook will replace my desktop PC. At home, I'll use good stereo equipment / screen / keyboard / mouse. About the resolution, I've never used a notebook or netbook, so I don't know what's "good" or "readable" or if I can set the machine to a higher standard resolution on a different screen. VGA output is alright, digital would be better.

5. battery life: Most of the time, the netbook will be used in mains operation, but 2 hours of video or 4 hours of text processing should not pose a problem.

6. Ubuntu Netbook Remix: Out-of-the-box compatibility is not needed, but there should be a way of getting things up and working without having to write my own source code. I can use some of the given tools, but I'm not a programmer.


What do I NOT need the device for?

1. gaming: My gaming days are over.

2. heavy image / video / sound editing: It's not supposed to be a cutting edge "Deep Thought" megamachine.

3. infinite mass storage / dvd / CD writer: I'll keep an external hard disk / dvd writer at home.


I live in Germany, so please refer to European prices if possible. That's about it. Please, tell me what you think is possible.


Thanks for your advice!

ziphi
