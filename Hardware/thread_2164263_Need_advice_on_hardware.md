### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2013-07-31
You'll get the best bang for your buck by upgrading the GPU, so I'd go with a GTX 650Ti (good midrange card): [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127725](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814127725)
Don't assume your PSU can handle it, though. Check the Amperage ratings on the power rail(s).

I wouldn't upgrade the CPU, because it probably won't help you as much as you think, and your PSU doesn't need the extra power draw. Also, you couldn't move the CPU to a new system like you could with the GPU, so you're basically dumping money into this system, which is a bad idea if you're a gamer.

The only other upgrade that would interest me for such a system is an SSD (also reusable in a new system).

> First, it might be a good idea to leave that old ATI card behind since you could be left with a non supported card in the next couple of years
AMD has an open-source driver for their video cards, and it's finally getting to the point where it maches/beats the proprietary driver, so that's a non-issue.
