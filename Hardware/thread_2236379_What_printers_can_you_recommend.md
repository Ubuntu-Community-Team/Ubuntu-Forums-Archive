### Post by 1clue on 2014-07-26
I wouldn't recommend putting that old a printer into service again, but that's up to you.  I imagine any parallel port card is good if you want to do it that way.

Lasers are fairly affordable now, so if you want one then I recommend it to get away from the ink tax associated with inkjets.  I'm pretty sure your printer drivers are still around somewhere, you might have to find it on the net though.

I have a Canon MF8050Cn color multi-purpose.  It works fine now, although when I bought it there was no driver yet for Linux.  It's a little flaky in that driver support sometimes breaks, and it's a non-standard driver so an old-school lpr print (for postscript) doesn't work unless you have the specific driver.

My printing needs are small, so my inkjets were literally drying out before I printed my next batch of documents.  With ink costing about the same as the printer, it's hard to deal with it that way.  So I bought the laser and figured I had 10 years of toner in the first set.  Of course, about a year after I got the printer I also got a GF who turned into a wife, and she's printed a tree and a half worth of paper since then.
