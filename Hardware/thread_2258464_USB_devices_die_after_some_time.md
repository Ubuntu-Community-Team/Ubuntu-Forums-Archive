### Post by cybrsaylr on 2015-02-08
> **kyariester said:**
> ... in fact, it seems that the USB ports themselves stop responding, as unplugging and plugging them back in don't seem to do anything, and newly-plugged-in devices don't work either.....

Have you tried cleaning the usb ports out?
Same thing used to happen to an old desktop. Didn't really use some of its usb ports much but when I tried to, they didn't work. After cleaning and vacuuming  them out all was good again. Don't forget usb ports get air and dirt sucked into them clogging up their contacts. Now I keep a strip of saran wrap covering over seldom used usb ports and they always work fine when needed.
