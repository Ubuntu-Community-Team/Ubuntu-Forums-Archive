### Post by sikander3786 on 2010-10-08
Definitely a heat up issue. You can check for your processor/motherboard temperatures in Bios menu after the PC shuts itself down.

Check the processor heatsink connection with processor. Is it loose? Is the processor fan spinning? Are the air ducts obstructed by dust? Use compressed air to clean up the inside of your CPU.

### Post by sikander3786 on 2010-10-08
For me, most probably, it is the processor.

Secondly, look at the motherboard, can you see any leaked capacitors?

And yes it can also be any of the chips heating up. I've had some bad experience with the main chip on Dell GX280. You can check the chips by placing you finger on the chip while the system is running. Normally they shouldn't feel like burning your finger. **Be a lot careful**.

At last, it might be RAM.
