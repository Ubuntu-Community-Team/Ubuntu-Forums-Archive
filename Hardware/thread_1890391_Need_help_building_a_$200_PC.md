### Post by Kenjitamura on 2011-12-04
Edit: Nevermind, that motherboard lists Pentium CPU's as supported: [http://www.biostar-usa.com/app/en-us/mb/introduction.php?S_ID=547](http://www.biostar-usa.com/app/en-us/mb/introduction.php?S_ID=547)

MoreOrLess is right that the Intel solution will have worse integrated graphics.  But, the Intel build using less power should help you out if you ever need to add a graphics card.  I think the best graphics card out that a 400w power supply can support is a 5670: [http://www.superbiiz.com/detail.php?name=AT-567VGA5](http://www.superbiiz.com/detail.php?name=AT-567VGA5) (Newegg is soldout thus the superbiiz link)

Benchmarks to show what that looks like: [http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/radeon-hd-5670,2533.html](http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/radeon-hd-5670,2533.html)

There is hope though, in 2012 both Ati and Nvidia are releasing 28 nm graphics cards, they will use much less power and provide much better performance.

What's most important about the power supply to determine graphic card upgrade ability is the Amperage rating on the 12v line, that 400 watt power supply does considerably well at 30A.
