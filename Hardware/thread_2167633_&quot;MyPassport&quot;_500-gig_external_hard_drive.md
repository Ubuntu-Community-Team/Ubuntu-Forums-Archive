I solved a 500 gig "pass port external hard drive" failing to mount.  Thought some troubled users might appreciate these tips..  To get it working out of the box, format it, then run synaptic to add software that handles NTFS..  This 500-gig hd wouldn't start.  It whirred softly inside, and made a few soft clicks, and the light flashed 9-times then just stayed on, but the device wouldn't mount..  WD troubleshooting files said "it might be shot, or "might be a damaged cable", and to "plug the thing into the computer, and reboot".. I tried that on Ubuntu & Scientific Linux notebook and desktops with same no-mount symptom.. I tried it on several computers with same symptom.. Tried it on a Windows-7 notebook..  It didn't mount.. Got a message that it was defective.. Rebooted with the hd plugged in.. It mounted.. "ohhh Yayyy & ohhh Sighhh.. I didn't lose 450-gigs of data..  Hahhhhhh!..  Maybe it was that I used my friend's cable..?  Maybe something else good happened..? What ever happened, I'm stripping my 450 gigs of files off that thing into my friend's notebook, then I'll rush out and buy me a new Pass Port ext hd, a terabyte one, plus put everything to DVD's in case this ever happens again.. WhooH!.. I'm hoping there's a solid state external hard drive..?  Looks like the solution is to just keep rebooting the computer with the thing connected, and to try another's cable..  plus I tapped the thing gently with a fingertip a few times while rebooting the notebook.. Don't know if that helped for anything..? I thought of freezing it in the freezer, in a plastic baggie, as a last resort, in case the fault was a heat problem solder joint...  One important tip a computer tech gave me about these Passport hd's, is to never twist nor bend the cable, nor try to straighten it out,.  That can very easily break the wires to the connector ends..  Replacement cables are from the manufacturer only...  Back-up your PassPort to DVD today...
