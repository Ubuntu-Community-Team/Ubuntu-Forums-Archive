### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-06-04
Sometimes you can "exercise" pixels to get them to work--by making them change colors rapidly, but more likely it is a manufacturing defect in the etching of the glass that is causing those pixels to fail.  Each manufacturer has different rules about screen replacement including placement (center field versus outer field) and number of defects.  I don't know of a linux tool but you could use a drawing program and try manually changing the bad pixel.

In your case, I would not be surprised if they say that you need 5 to 7 bad pixels in the center field before warranty replacement.  That used to be the criteria for Samsung displays.
