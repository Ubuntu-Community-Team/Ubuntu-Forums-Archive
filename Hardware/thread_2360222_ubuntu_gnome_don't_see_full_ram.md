### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-05-02
Welcome. New sticks you have just put in? I'd start by working out if you have a dead stick. To do so, leave one in slot one and take the other out then boot the machine. Working? If it is, switch off and pull the stick that's in there and replace with the other stick. Boot up. Working?

If these are new sticks the one that's not working may just need a little 'convincing'. This is not unusual. Seat it and reseat it a couple of times in the slot. Make sure it is properly seated. May same crazy, but lightly rub the contacts with a pencil eraser (lightly!). This will remove residue/traces of factory gunk, then try again.

Hope that helps. Seems obvious one is being seen and the other one is either dead, not seated properly or is not making a clean contact in the slot.

* Just noticed something else which probably shouldn't cause a problem but might. This is not a 'matched' pair and the speeds are different. One is 800mhz and the other is even slower at 667mhz. These are old? They are showing up in your system, but that doesn't mean they are both functioning as they should. Try them one by one, as suggested.
