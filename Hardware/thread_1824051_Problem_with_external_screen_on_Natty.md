### Post by mindless1973 on 2011-08-13
Hello,

I have a Lenovo Thinkpad T410 connected to an BENQ 24" external monitor via VGA. This combination worked brilliantly on Lucid and Maverick, but since Unity I experience big problems on every single reboot!

Without the external screen connected, Unity starts fine on the Thinkpad, nothing to complain.

When I boot while the external monitor is connected, everything works fine until I log in (until that, I get a cloned picture on both displays, the external screen and the Thinkpad display). 

After I log in, the Thinkpad Displays turns off (as intended), but the external screen just starts flickering. When I now try to bring the desktop to the Thinkpad display by pressing FN-F7, I usually end up with a black screen and the mouse pointer (so, the screen is turned on, but the Desktop seems not to be displayed). 

The only thing I can do in that situation is to switch to a text console (CTRL-ALT-F1) and reboot the systems where the game starts again....

By now I found two workarounds:
1.) to disconnect the external monitor physically while booting
2.) to turn off the external screen with FN-F7 fast enough after logging in and before Unity starts to get the Desktop on the Thinkpad Display.

After having the Desktop established on the Thinkpad Display, I CAN start "Monitors" and set my screens as desired (which would be external screen on with 1920x1080, Thinkpad screen off) and I can even close the lid of the Thinkpad afterwards.

However, it usually takes me two to three reboots every day until I get my system working as required. This is quite annoying...

Any idea how to solve that problem?
