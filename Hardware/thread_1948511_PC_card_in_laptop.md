### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-03-28
I'm not clear on what you mean when you say you think you will need some special card to "help with downloads."  But, you can plug various cards into a PC Card slot such as wireless cards in case your laptop doesn't have built-in wireless or you want to add more USB ports or something.  (A six year old Gateway laptop should have built-in wireless.)

I'm not sure if you can even get a graphics card for a PC Card slot - but I've never heard of anyone needing one.  I have installed Ubuntu on older laptops than yours and used a PC Card only for a wireless card because the laptop was too old to have built-in wireless.

How about installing Ubuntu first?  Worry about other possible needs later.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-03-29
If by "PC Card slot" you mean a slot in the side of the laptop, I too have not seen any Video cards made for that. I've seen networking, serial/parallel port, and USB cards -- but not video cards.

Also, the video card itself plays no roles in the downloads other than displaying you the desktop.  So, replacing the video chip in the laptop with a card (even if that IS possible!) will not help you in downloading.
