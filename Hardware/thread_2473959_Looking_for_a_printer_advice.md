### Post by SeijiSensei on 2022-04-20
I've had good success with Brother printers. That said, the combination of color scanning and color laser printing is going to cost you a lot more than 200 euros. The contemporary equivalent to my Brother HL-3170CDW is [https://www.amazon.com/Brother-HL-L3230CDW-Automatic-250-sheet-White-Bundle/dp/B09SCV2YDJ/](https://www.amazon.com/Brother-HL-L3230CDW-Automatic-250-sheet-White-Bundle/dp/B09SCV2YDJ/). It's over $450 and has no scanner. My printer has been on sale from time to time; I bought it new from Amazon for about $200. 

How often do you need to scan documents? I've found in many cases taking a photo with my smartphone works just as well. OTOH, if you're an attorney and need to send around fifty-page documents, that's not an option.

### Post by him610 on 2022-04-20
Sometimes one can find practically new, refurbished printers (MFC devices) for less expen$e than a new one. A few years ago I purchased a refurbished commercial grade Brother MFC-7360N (B&W only) device for about $100 from Staples while a new one was around $350, also paid for a three year maintenance contract - it all still cost less than a new one. I still use it after all these years printing, copying, scanning, faxing - send & receive. Never had any problems with it. In my experience, Brother provides good support with their Linux drivers, and their toner cartridges are relatively inexpensive.
