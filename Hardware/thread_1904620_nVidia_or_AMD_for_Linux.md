### Post by dj.houba on 2012-01-05
I read some news about present drivers. There was many problems with ATI drivers few years ago, that's true. But it was at time, when these GPU were made by ATI. ATI wasn't so frindly to Linux. But ATI was purchased by AMD. All presents "ATI" graphics are made by AMD and they tries to do better drivers - hey communicate with open source developers. But they tries to improve their proprietary drivers also. They are doing so good work, but nVidia's proprietary drivers are still better.
And about open source drivers: for AMD are pretty good now, they climb to 80% of performance of proprietary drivers.
nVidia open source drivers are't so good.

Link: [http://www.reddit.com/r/linux/comments/jzhfr/whats_the_current_status_of_graphics_cards_amd_vs/](http://www.reddit.com/r/linux/comments/jzhfr/whats_the_current_status_of_graphics_cards_amd_vs/)

If you want better performance under Linux and wine, but with closed source, take nVidia.
