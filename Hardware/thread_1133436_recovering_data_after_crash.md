### Post by cariboo on 2009-04-23
If the BIOS isn't detecting the drive, you may be out of luck. The only thing I can suggest is to stick the drive in the freezer and let it sit for a couple of hours, then reconnect it to see if it is recognized. I would not get my hopes up, as you have to put the drive back in the case in order to get at the data which causes the drive to heat up faster than if you could keep it external and sitting between a couple of ice packs.
