### Post by The Cosmic Hobo on 2008-06-10
I can't say this loudly enough: **NO!**
The switch on the PSU is to tell it which mains voltage you're using. If you set it incorrectly you can fry it, along with other PC components, and possibly blow the fuses.
A PSU is basically a transformer. It takes mains voltage, which is alternating current, and "rectifies" it to the direct current that PC components need. It also does something horribly complicated that I don't understand which changes the properties of that current. Physics was never my strong point.

**Your PSU is fine set to 110v, _do not_ move that switch!**
Other than that, I'm afraid I can't help. I have no idea how to calculate what sort of PSU you need, but I think it may be on the low side. Hopefully someone else will be able to give you more advice.
