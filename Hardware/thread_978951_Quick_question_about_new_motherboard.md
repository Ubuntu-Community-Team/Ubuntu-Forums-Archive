### Post by Afkpuz on 2008-11-11
Hey everybody, I'm sure this question is in the wrong area, but here goes.

I'm getting a new motherboard for my p4 cpu.  I've done this sort of thing before, and I always run into one problem: the black stuff on the bottom of the heatsink.  I never know what I'm supposed to do with it. 

Whenever I take off the heatsink, I always notice that the black, rubbery looking patch that was on the bottom of it has chipped off.  Some parts stick to the heatsink and some to the cpu.  My understanding is that this is a heat transfer medium.  

So my question is this: what should I do with this gunk when I transfer to my new mobo?  I have some artic silver thermal grease, but don't know if I should scrape off the black stuff.  Should I clean the black stuff away or put the arctic silver on the CPU and leave the black stuff alone?  

Oh, and I will be installing ubuntu on this.  

thanks in advance

### Post by immerohnegott on 2008-11-11
scrape it off. gently. if you've got something plastic like a pallette knife, it works well. just don't use anything metallic, you don't want to scratch the bottom of the sink.

after you scrape it off, clean the bejesus out of the cpu heatspreader and the bottom of the sink with isopropyl alcohol (the higher the alcohol percentage, the better. I recommend at least 90 percent - you don't want residue).I like to use a coffee filter for this, as it doesn't fall apart and produces no lint, etc.

let both pieces dry a second, apply the arctic silver as per manufacturer instructions, and attach everything. 

just don't bloody put the arctic silver on with the old heat gunk. or god will start killing kittens.
