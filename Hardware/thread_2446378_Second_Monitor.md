### Post by CelticWarrior on 2020-06-29
It's as easy if not easier than in Windows.
Connect it and boot, that's it. At this point you need to Settings > Screens and adjust your preferences. Typically there's nothing else to do but occasionally old monitors (and some newish) with analog connection aren't detected correctly and their native resolution isn't available. In such cases you may need to use xrandr.
