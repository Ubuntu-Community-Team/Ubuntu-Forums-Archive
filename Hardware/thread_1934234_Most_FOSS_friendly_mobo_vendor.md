### Post by kurt18947 on 2012-03-06
I believe ASUS boards are pretty compatible.  I have 2 Gigabyte MBs.  The only problem I have with one using an AMD 780 (I think) chip set is that it won't boot a Live USB unless that Live USB is formatted with Windows.  It's actually a problem with the Award modular BIOS, I believe.  Other than that no problems and no issue booting a live CD or booting off a hard drive.  A bigger issue is -- or at least has been -- is graphics chipsets.  Certain ATI/AMD are not well supported. Nvidia seems a safer choice for Linux.

### Post by QIII on 2012-03-13
Over the years I have had exactly ONE problem with Gigabyte:  a faulty RJ45 jack.  RMAed and replacement in 10 days.  In terms of compatibility with Linux, exactly ZERO problems.

Recently a low budget MSI board smoked on me.  But that was after 5 years of 24/7 full bore 100% CPU flogging.  4 of those years were with an AM2+ Phenom II x4.  That was certainly longer than I had any reason to expect from a low end board.

But my experience, and any other single person's experience here, is anecdotal.  You risk hasty generalization by making a choice based on anonymous authority claiming universal knowledge.

Check a lot of reviews of any board that catches your eye.  Phoronix would be a good place to start.

"I heard ..." and "Everyone says..." are without value.

### Post by OGpmpdog on 2012-03-14
Hi all,

I believe MSI is a wise choice as Ubuntu, or FOSS-friendly, mobo.  The BIOS is awesome.

I also believe MSI mid to high-end mobos are the most reliable - beyond the 3-year warranty.  Look at the build quality...

For those who are building PC's, please dont go cheap on the mobo or PSU.

I'd budget $150 for a future-proof 1155 (or 1361 for overkill)MSI mobo, and $60-70 for an Antec or OCZ PSU.
