### Post by cjazz on 2010-08-08
The consensus is that Hewlett-Packard is the Linux-friendliest printer maker around, and most HPs work out of the box. I should add that Epsons generally seem to be a good pick, and Brother has a whole page of proprietary drivers for its printers. (I happen to own an HP and a Brother, which both work flawlessly.)

No matter what model you buy, my advice is to check it first at the OpenPrinting site [here]("http://www.openprinting.org/printers").
