### Post by cybrsaylr on 2011-05-17
When using my laptop in the car with only a mobile inverter used for power and the laptop battery removed, the touchpad arrow pointer is very unstable and jumps all over the screen and is very jerky. It is so bad the laptop is basically unusable. When the battery is put back in and the inverter is disconnected the Toshiba works perfectly. But if I plug the inverter back in while the battery is powering the laptop, the pointer arrow controlled from touchpad acts very unstable again. Same thing happens when using a usb mouse plugged into laptop. 

I have a 150 watt Adapter [CyberPower AC Mobile Power inverter, model # CPS150CHI.]("http://meoutdoorpowerconverterbatteryalley.blogspot.com/2011/04/cyberpower-cps150chi-ac-mobile-power.html") It is 3 years old. It ran OK first year, acted up a little last year but this third year it is acting up so bad the laptop can't run well using it. I bought another new CyberPower Inverter and right out of the box it did the same thing! Touchpad pointer arrow jumped all over the place and was very unstable. Returned the unit to store a couple hours later after testing it out. 

This Toshiba laptop runs perfectly well at home with the Toshiba AC adapter and with it's own laptop battery. It only acts up when attempting to use the mobile inverter alone or when the laptop battery is left in the laptop, while the inverter is connected to the laptop.

Anyone have any ideas on what the problem is? I am in the process of looking for another mobile inverter that works well. What are some good brands to look for?

### Post by noidian on 2011-05-18
I have a Toshiba Satellite as well. I presume your laptop works well with just the battery supply as well without the AC power.

As mentioned earlier i would presume the car inverter adapter noise rejection ratio is very poor. 

If your highly motivated : scope out your signal and see if its noisy.

Just to be sure, ensure you try it out on another car.

>>
If the noise is generated at the DC end of the adapter

1.) you could try putting in a small decoupling capacitor between the supply (DC) and gnd (spikey noise) or a large cap between supply and gnd will remove low frequency noise.


2.) Or buy some voltage stabilizer for your Car inverter. 


>>

Your mousepad is a capacitive device, thereby highly sensitive to voltage variations.

### Post by cybrsaylr on 2011-05-19
The tech guy at the auto sound shop suspected it was a low wattage problem straightaway. He said he had seen this happen before. He knew the CyberPower inverter I had, while it said it put out 150 watts at peak would only deliver 75 watts continuous which he said wasn't enough, therefore causing the instability touchpad problem. He said continuous watts are what is more important.   

Tested out the new inverter some more and so far it is powering the laptop fine. 

I was surprised and disappointed both CyberPower and Toshiba tech support didn't know about this or even ask about it when I had phoned them. The guy at the auto sound shop seemed more knowledgeable than both of them.
