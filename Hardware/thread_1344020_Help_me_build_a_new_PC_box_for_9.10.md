### Post by jeepinpete on 2009-12-02
My wife's computer is an old eMachines T3256.  It has served us well, and would still be except XP is so bug ridden, I haven't been able to get it clean.  So I installed 9.10, but the nVidia driver is not working very well with the integrated MX graphics (occasional, random lockups).  I bought a new AGP video card for it and went about installing that last night but could not get the new card to work. 

So rather than throwing money into an outdated box, I am thinking about building a new one with hardware that is robustly compatible with Ubuntu 9.10.  I'd like to reuse the emachines case (mATX), hard drives (IDE), and less so the power supply (300w).

Must haves: wifi (I have a PCI card that could be reused), ieee1394 (firewire), ide and sata connections, printer port.

CPU: I'm agnostic.  Anything made in the last three years will be a huge step up from the Athlon XP 3200 that the machine currently has.

So recommend me a motherboard/cpu/memory/etc that is plug an play with 9.10, and keep my budget below $200 :D

### Post by jeepinpete on 2009-12-03
Well, looks like I will have no need.  I gave it another go tonight.  Turns out there is a tall capacitor on the motherboard that is in line with the AGP port.  The front edge of the board must have gotten held up on it last time.  I moved it out of the way, now everything works :)
