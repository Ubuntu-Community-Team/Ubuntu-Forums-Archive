### Post by Adapted.Cat on 2009-03-12
I have an amd64 system running ubuntu 8.10, hooked up to a standard TV via a UHF modulator. I need an AGP graphics card that has open source drivers which fully support TV-output.

Over the years I have tried ATI and NVIDIA cards, but the open source drivers have only basic TV-output support (e.g. you can't reposition the screen or adjust overscan), and the proprietary drivers start off working fine, then after a year or so the new driver switches to B/W output because they've changed the signaling on the SVideo pins, then they stop working altogether because the card is "too old" to be supported. In addition, ATI cards take forever to get any drivers to work, and all the NVIDIA cards I have tried are LOUD!

I would just use an old card I had from 10 years ago - that had brilliant TV output, but it's a 3.3V card and won't fit in my 1.5V AGP slot.

I realize that there are a number of things I could do to fix this:
1) Use a monitor instead - too small to be seen at a distance
2) Buy a new big LCD HDTV - too expensive
3) Buy a new computer and use binary drivers - too expensive and might not even work

The card does need to have 2D acceleration for DVD playback, but I don't need the latest and greatest video card - it doesn't take much memory to display a 800x600 or 1024x768 screen for a TV - and I'm just watching video on it, not playing fancy video games. I'm pretty much precluded from buying a brand new graphics card now anyway because I just have the AGP slot, and all the new ones are PCI-E.

Is there another company out there making (quiet) graphics cards that have TV-output which is fully supported under linux? Even if they're not sold anymore if I could get one second-hand that would work. Any recommendations of specific cards that would work for me and where I could get them?

Thanks!
