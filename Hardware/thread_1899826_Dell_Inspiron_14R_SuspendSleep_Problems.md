### Post by hithirdwavedust on 2012-03-23
Look for options concerning screen lock, or lock screen. Your session can be locked without sleeping, and can also sleep without locking. You may actually want your screen to turn off at some point of inactivity, but it sounds like you don't want to have to unlock it every time it does.

Also, I agree, ten minutes is a small time, especially when sleep will crash an install, and a lock will kick you out of a live session. (because there's no password, so you can't log back in)
