But when I connected it, I found that I had to (or so I thought) either cut off the top and bottom of my screen or leave a blank space on each side--the former leaving my screen size the same and the latter actually *reducing* my screen size considerably.

I'm sorry to say this makes no sense to me.

Thanks in advance.

### Post by Autodave on 2017-04-25
Looking at the specs that you posted and the specs that I found online for that machine, a couple things come to mind. First off, that machine has onboard graphics of only 256M. That may not be able to support that monitor. You may need a new machine or at least an add-on video card.

Secondly, how is the monitor connected to the PC? Is it by the VGA cable (15 pin) or by a DVI (white connector) or HDMI (much smaller, flat connector)?
