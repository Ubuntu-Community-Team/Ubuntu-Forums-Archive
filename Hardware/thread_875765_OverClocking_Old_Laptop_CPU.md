### Post by tamoneya on 2008-07-31
Overclocking laptop CPUs is very difficult because the BIOSes on laptops are designed to not over clock.  you will probably find that there are no options in your bios that allow you to change clock speeds or multiplies.  This means that if you want to overclock you would probably have to do some sort of pencil mod or replacing some of the oscillators on the motherboard.  

If it was me I would just leave it the way it is.  If it doesnt have a problem there is no need to fix it.

### Post by etomic13 on 2008-07-31
Minus the gaming that laptop should run all those apps just fine the way it is. Overclocking in a laptop is difficult/risky like tamoneya said its usually a difficult process because they are not designed to be overclocked. Secondly laptops have issues with cooling because they are in such small cases which often have terrible air flow (especially the P4 processor which ran especially hot). If its not a laptop that your rely on and you dont mind possibly breaking it go for it, it might be a good experiment but otherwise I'd say leave it.
