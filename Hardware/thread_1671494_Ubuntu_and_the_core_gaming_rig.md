### Post by pi/roman on 2011-01-21
You should have been ecstatic to receive 2 replies since many questions on this forum go unanswered.  There are many very knowledgeable computer techs on these forums that are willing to spend their time here for no other reason than to help others, but the number of topics I believe are just overwhelming so when you get a reply be appreciative and when you don't then you should be understanding.  If you get no replies then just wait a day and bump your topic.  If you start a discussion and someone hijacks it by going off topic and talking about something trivial such as post count then you can let it go and wait a day before creating another thread if you don't get any more on topic replies.

I don't know if your hardware is compatible but you may be able to get an idea by searching for individual devices on here. The particular pieces you would want to research are the mainboard, the video card and the amount of ram.  The ram will play a part in how you install linux because you will want to get the full use of it. Personally I would not put linux on a gaming system just to surf the internet because I would not want to continually reboot to switch operating systems. Also the amount of power consumption of a gaming system does not make it ideal for web surfing.  It would be much better to get an old pc or laptop from a second hand store and use that instead.  That way you could increase the life of your gaming system by not running it needlessly.
