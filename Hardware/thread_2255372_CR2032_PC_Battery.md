### Post by jean noel on 2014-12-04
The battery is there to keep bios settings. If you were to remove the battery for some time, the bios would default to its factory setting. The time would revert back to its original setting, and other settings; for example first boot squence would default. As for life expectancy of the battery, my guess would be around three years.

---

### Post by TheFu on 2014-12-04
They keep power for the BIOS and system clock when there isn't any power available.  Depending on the power situation, it may last 10 yrs or 2 yrs. The most common ways to know whether it needs to be replaced are 
a) BIOS settings aren't remembered between reboots
b) the system clock is always wrong

Since I leave my systems running 24/7, I haven't had to change one of these batteries in about a decade.
