### Post by cascade9 on 2009-11-23
A couple of options-

1- New AMD 45watt CPUs- Athlon II X4 600e/605e, Athlon II X3 400e/405e, Athlon II X2 235e/240e. Probably best if you actually need much in the way of power. 

 2- Intel Atom- Great TDP on the CPU, horrible TDP on the chipsets they use with the desktop Atoms (if you see a pic of the atoms, the heatsink with the active cooling is actually the northbridge, because it uses quite a bit more power than the CPU) See here- 

[http://www.techfuels.com/attachments/motherboards-memory/5332d1221294585-intel-atom-enhanced-motherboard-intel-atom-enhanced-.jpg](http://www.techfuels.com/attachments/motherboards-memory/5332d1221294585-intel-atom-enhanced-motherboard-intel-atom-enhanced-.jpg)

3- Intel Core 2 Duo. If it was easy to use teh laptop CPUs in a desktop, these would edge out the 45 watt AMDs. But thats not exactly an easy option. Probably your best bet would be a Core 2 Duo 8300. Either of the other 2 options would be better IMO though.

### Post by snowpine on 2009-11-23
Atom is the lowest-power choice by far.
The new Ion chipset has much lower power consumption than the Atom boards with the Intel 945 chipset.

I've been fairly happy with my Foxconn R10-S4 Atom 330 barebones.
