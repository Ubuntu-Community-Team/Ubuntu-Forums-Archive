### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-24
I wouldnt say that all the cards after the 9XXX series are 'a bit rubbish'. The GT2XX cards are fine cards (not that they are what you would be looking at as a gamer), the GTS250 is just a 9800GT given a minor upgrade (and that was just a 8800GT with a minor upgrade anyway). The GTX260-275 were good gaming cards. GTX 280-285 were a bit too expensive, and the GTX295...well, lets just say that its great, if you want a heater/video card at the same time. 

That being said, I wouldnt touch a GTX4XX with a bargepole.

### Post by urbanus on 2010-06-24
I've tried the HD4870x2 and 3850AGP cards in Linux. With the binary drivers, you get 3D preformance, but they don't work completely smooth with many things. Video tearing is also the reoccurring theme.

I've managed to set up Linux on my media pc using the HD3850AGP card and remove video tearing, HD videos also play.
([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1512915](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1512915))

I like ATI cards, they are cheap and fast, but the drivers need to catch up. The Catalyst 10.6 drivers seems to have helped a littlebit in my experience.
