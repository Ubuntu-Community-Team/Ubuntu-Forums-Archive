### Post by oldfred on 2018-10-07
I know you said you only want a laptop.
But I am somewhat anti-laptop, but they may have use in some cases.

For normal use a laptop on a desk has keyboard too high, monitor too low or bad ergonomics.  And screen is too small for longer term use.
They always advertise that new laptops are as powerful as desktop. They are not as they have to save power.
If you want to really use computer get a good desktop and for the occasions when you need portability a smaller laptop. You can probably do that for the same cost as a high end laptop. Your desktop then can have a 20 to 30" monitor and a full keyboard. And be much more powerful.

If you really want a high end laptop, get a separate keyboard & monitor to correct ergonomics & give you a bit better screen & keyboard.

### Post by sp40140 on 2018-10-09
Simple thing (mostly): you pay for quality. 
Dell XPS is very good laptop, well built and has Ubuntu drivers. You can go for Dell Precision series as well. Costs more but has better warranty, easier to repair / upgrade and also comes with Ubuntu pre-loaded. 
I've been using same HP EliteBook for 8 years. Haven't had problem and don't even feel like upgrading. Has all the Ubuntu drivers.
All the OEMs make cheap (price and quality) laptops and quality laptops. Better ones cost more. And typically Thinkpads, EliteBooks, Latitudes have better driver support for linux over consumers lines like inspiron, pavilion.
