### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-05-22
Optical drive bay HDD caddy? Go ahead if that is what is most appropriate for your needs. I have a thread about them somewhere here if you have a hunt.

The only thing to remember is, make sure the caddy is exactly the same fit as your optical drive bay caddy. Is your motherboard capable of SATA3 speeds? Either way, as mentioned, SATA3 is backward-compat with SATA2. Whatever speed you are getting from the internal drive you will get (at least) from your optical drive bay disk (of course, SSD will be faster regardless of SATA2 or 3, cos they're just faster at processing information than a HDD, but they are still limited by transfer speeds of your system once the data is sent from or to the SSD).
