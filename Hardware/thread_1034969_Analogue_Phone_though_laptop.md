### Post by sentofuno on 2009-02-02
in theory, all you need is a microphone and the modem cable that came with your laptop. assuming your laptop has speakers.

in (my experience) in the UK of the 16 different combinations its possible to wire up an RJ11 to BT cable, 3 are in common use. what i would call a 'win-modem' cable may be called something completely different by anyone else, but it should be the same as the modem cable for your laptop (the other 2 options are straight and crossed, straight being for desktops and crossed for.. well i've never used or sold one to be honest)

to start with, if you have it plug in your modem cable that came with the laptop and set up a dialup internet connection, with the ISP telno as your mobile phone number (i'll assume you have one) and attempt a dialup connection. if your phone rings and emits an awful screech when you answer, you just succeeded in calling yourself with a data connection. 

it should then be possible in theory for you to use your modem to dial any number for voice calls as well. let me know if you get that far and i may be able to offer more advice. as for any software that may make the experience more user friendly, i have my own questions [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=6664608#post6664608]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=6664608#post6664608")
