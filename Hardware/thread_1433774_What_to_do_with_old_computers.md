I noticed where you're from. So you could use the best of the bunch to save yourself a bundle on phone bills by installing a text-only distro and Asterisk 1.2 or 1.4 (1.6 is probably too resource hungry). If that machine has a PCI slot spare, you can add a TDM card (about £60) and integrate your existing PSTN or ISDN lines. ATAs can be got off eBay from approx £20 for a Linksys PAP2 (but make sure what you get is unlocked), which can serve 2 analogue phones each.
  
Since I went over to Asterisk, my phone bill has dropped from over £300 per quarter to about £90 since the only calls I'm paying BT for are a few to mobiles and also 08xx. Included in that £90 is a ten Euro + VAT per quarter to buy a rolling 300 minutes per week from a Betamax clone VOIP provider.

OK, it costs a couple of hundred plus your existing PC, but for me it paid for itself in the first quarter and I've been saving ever since! Er.. and it's a bit of a steep learning curve, but I suspect you might be the sort to welcome the challenge!

Geoff
