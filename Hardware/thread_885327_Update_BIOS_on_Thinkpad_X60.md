### Post by tcpip on 2009-05-11

> My Thinkpad is running quite hot. As a first step in my quest to tame the CPU, I would like to  update the BIOS. But I am new to Linux and I I do not know how to do that. Could anyone guide me?
A BIOS upgrade is always recommended when IBM (now Lenovo) releases one. The firmware upgrade upgrades many things, including the internal power management firmware, ACPI engine, etc.

I have been using Thinkpads since 1999, always with various flavours of Linux, and am on my fourth Thinkpad now. I have not learned of any other way to upgrade the firmware other than to retain a Windows partition and run the Windows-based upgrade utilities provided by IBM (now Lenovo). If you have a Windows partition, then it's a very smooth process, and you just have to follow the instructions given online (including the instruction of executing the entire upgrade while keeping the laptop on mains power). It has always worked, and an upgrade has never degraded or broken anything in Linux or Windows for me.
