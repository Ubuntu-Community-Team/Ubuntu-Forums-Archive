### Post by karachuonyo on 2008-08-03
I had a 512 333 mhz and a 512 266 mhz stick in my laptop and had no problems under Kubuntu. Obviously the speed indicated was 266 mhz. Windows (I'm dual-booting with XP) refused to load after POST however until i removed one of the sticks.
So, not recommended 'cos your computer my blow up and burn down your whole neighbourhood but give it a go and if boot does not go past POST, then just remove one of the sticks.

### Post by munkyeetr on 2008-08-03
Most often 400Mhz DDR RAM will "clock down" and run at the lower speed. I haven't *personally* had any issues mixing 266, 333, and 400, but I have heard that there can be timing issues when mixing sticks.

As stated above, give it a shot. If your system seems unstable (or doesn't boot) then you'll want to remove one of the modules.
