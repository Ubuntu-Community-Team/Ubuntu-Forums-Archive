### Post by Skripka on 2008-11-09
The differences in ALL these cards you post is in the technical details, specifially:

-How many processing cores do they have?
-What is the core clock?
-What is the RAM clock, in addition to how much RAM?
-Cooling ability (2-slot card with fan & exhaust, versus sngle-slot and no exhaust)


In general, the more cores, you have and the higher the clock speeds-the better the card.  Another thing to bear in mind, is that there is a WIDESPREAD theory (I do not know if it is true or not), that Nvidia 9800 cards are just BIOS-flashed 8800 cards from the last generation; ergo if you got a Nvidia8800 card you could get near the same performance at half the price.

Also PCIx16 2.0 cards are backwards compatible with PCIx16 (1.0) rails, with no real loss in performance.



It depends on you and your modeling--if your modling is complex enough to eat your entire GPU of memory, then by all means spend and get a higher clocked card with more memory.  Also, I HIGHLY recommend getting a 2-slot card with cooling duct and exhaust-you thermostats in your computer will love you, it is worth the added expense in GPU intensive processes.

Another thing to bear in mind--MOST GPUs in the last year or so NEED at least a 450W power supply....many card manufacturers specify a minimum PSU in their specs, or you can look around to be sure.  My GTX260 needs a 500W PSU with 2X6pin PCIe power inputs on it.
