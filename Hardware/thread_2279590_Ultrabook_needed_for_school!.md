### Post by oldfred on 2015-05-24
I do not know now what the definition of an ultrabook is.
Microsoft required vendors to speed up booting and with Ultrabooks they added a small 16 to 32GB SSD just for cache for booting. And to add value they included another video card.
But if you have a full SSD, you do not need the separate cache. And unless gaming a lot the add in video cards are not required as the internal Intel video is now where lower/mid range video cards were just several years ago.

I like the suggestion one user posted where he buys a better system, but with hard drive. But before even booting Windows removes hard drive and installs SSD and Ubuntu. Then later he can sell system with an unused Windows, so he does not have any of his data on it.
