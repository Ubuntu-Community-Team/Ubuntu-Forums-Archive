### Post by gwaar on 2008-07-21
I think it would be hard to get a complete overview of all of these manufacturer's cards, but I can give you my experience with ATI. My card is an old Mobility 7500, but I've had few problems with it. Originally I did have to do some major editing of my xorg.conf to get acceleration to work, but after that, compiz, nexuiz, glxgears, vlc etc. worked perfectly. My card uses the standard ATI driver, not the proprietary one from ATI itself, in fact i've never been able to install that one, but it hasn't hurt me at all. So pretty good experience so far with my card.

### Post by falcon61102 on 2008-07-21
I've got an nVidia 8600-M in my laptop and I haven't had any problems with it.  I use quite a few desktop effects just because I'm not too concerned about performance and I like them but to be honest, I don't really have any performance issues.  I've turned on the benchmark plugin and rarely do I drop under 50fps when I'm spinning my semi-transparent cube with windows open on all four desktops.  As far as graphics go, the card handles all the recent games I've played with no problems including graphic intense games like Ghost Recon Advanced Warfighter 2.

As far as Firefox goes, I haven't had a problem for either Ubuntu or Windows.  I often have multiple Firefox windows open throughout the cube and rarely use full screen, and haven't had any issues...

Maybe I'm just really lucky, but from my past experiences with nVidia, this is just par for their equipment.  Hope this helps.
