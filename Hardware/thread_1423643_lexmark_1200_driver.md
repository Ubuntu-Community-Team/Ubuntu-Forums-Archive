### Post by xtnsgo on 2010-03-07
I am very new to Ubuntu, and even newer to the forums--but i have run into a similar situation noodling around with an old Dell 926 i picked up on the cheap (free!).  As you may have found out already, Dell printers are basically Lexmarks, and from what i'm able to gather, Lexmark printers are notoriously difficult to get working in Linux of any flavor.  I'm not actively searching for a solution at the moment, but i have a few links that might hopefully get you started in the right direction.

From the Ubuntu documentation pages: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters/LexmarkPrinters]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters/LexmarkPrinters")

There is a long, and still developing, thread at: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=49714&page=64]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=49714&page=64")

This looks promising: [http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-489903.html]("http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-489903.html")

And this pertains specifically to 1200 series printers (x1270):  [http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-933616.html]("http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-933616.html") Also a possiblity? 

From the Lexmark site: [http://support.lexmark.com/index?page=content&id=OS4&locale=EN&userlocale=EN_US]("http://support.lexmark.com/index?page=content&id=OS4&locale=EN&userlocale=EN_US")  
If i remember correctly one of the main workarounds for Dells/Lexmarks takes advantage of some driver cross-functionality between models.  Maybe this is a possiblity for you?

There are others, but the Ubuntu forums are by far the most helpful and polite, from what i've seen.

From what i can tell, though, whatever solution you might find could be rather involved and may require a little work.  

Hope this helps, or at least gets you started.  Good luck!!!
