anakynblade,

This is quite a list of things to do.

Some information you should know before I start making wild suggestions:

* Ubuntu can be installed on a hard drive from one machine and then move the hard drive to another machine with little or no trouble.

* Windows cannot be installed on a hard drive on one machine and moved to another machine unless the hardware is identical.  This is why the HDD from the Toshiba runs Ubuntu, but not XP on your Vaio.

Right now you cannot install a fresh version of Ubuntu on your Vaio because your CD/DVD is broken and you cannot boot from USB.  For this system, if you would backup your documents and then move the hard drive to ANY other laptop with a working CD/DVD, you can do a clean install of (say) 10.04.02 LTS and then be operational again.  You do not have to upgrade the bios on that laptop if you do this.  Once that hard drive has Ubuntu installed, you can move it back into your Vaio.

Getting XP loaded on that same drive is much harder. It has to be loaded on this laptop, or one just like it for Windows to work.  Let me think about this for a bit...
