### Post by bitscarre on 2010-11-16
Look out for a model you may like, go to manufacturer website, grab specification list, lookup for possible hardware problems for every component / whole laptop, on all the forums. 

Also see what other people say about it, any problems they encountered, like noise levels, heating up, battery life, screen brightness, touchpad efficiency etc.

I rekon, you should spend about 2 weeks for finding the perfect buy, just spend 10-20 minutes every day or two and look really carefully for something you like, works well with linux and lasts at least 3 years.

If you follow this, you'll never have any problems you won't know about.

Also make sure you check the store prices vs online prices, as C is coming and a lot of websites do better deals than retail shops. 

Oh, and lastly, open eyes wide open for dead pixels / screen stains.

Good luck.
