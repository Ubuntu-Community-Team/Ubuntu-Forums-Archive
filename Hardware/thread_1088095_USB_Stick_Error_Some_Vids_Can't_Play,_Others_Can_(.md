### Post by OzzyFrank on 2009-03-05
OK, I am perplexed by what happens to some vid clips I stick on my 16Gb USB stick. It is not a filesize limitation, since a 1.5Gb clip can be fine, and ones as small as 700Kb can get "corrupted" (not sure if that is what is happening, but seems to be). And the files involved are invariably the same ones, ranging from 1.1Gb down to 500K.

When I took my stick to a friend's place, most of the clips wouldn't play, either off the stick or when copied to his drive. Looking at the stick contents, you could see some had picture thumbnails (they worked either off the device or once copied to his PC) and others had the generic vid clip icon (these reported correct size, but wouldn't play off the stick or off his drive, which leads one to assume they got corrupted).

Now the funny thing is that when I got home and deleted the offending clips, then copied them over again, the preview thumbnails appeared, and yes, the clips played fine. However, unmount and then remount the device, and - you guessed it - those same clips now have a generic icon and can't be played. Copy them back over to the hard drive, and they no longer work!

Let me just stress again that these clips DO play fine off the stick, as long as I don't unmount the device. As mentioned, it can't really be a filesize issue, since small clips are affected, and I have larger clips that work fine. Also, in case you're wondering about file extensions, it's not that either as, for example, some WMVs work and other don't etc (same with the other filetypes). Besides, the stick is a storage device, so shouldn't even care about filetypes etc. And I doubt the stick is corrupted, since I can delete all the clips, and copy them over again, and the same ones are fine, and the same ones are not fine after remounting it.

Any ideas on what is happening here?
