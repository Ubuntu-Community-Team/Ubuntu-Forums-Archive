A 550W supply should do you for most individual cards (my PNY GTX260 needs a 500W PSU minimum, with 2X6pin PCIe power inputs).  If you're on Hardy and only want to deal with drivers in Synaptic/AddRemove (and avoid having to run install scripts via CL) then stay away from the latest and greatest Nvidia cards (the "GTX 200" cards) as they need the 177.80 Nvidia driver or better (and that is only available through easily through Synaptic on Intrepid Ibex).


Best advice is to shop around on NewEgg they'll probably beat prices of anyone (I live behind a BestBuy, and I always NewEgg my hardware in), check and snipe clearances/deals.  Do you know what your budget, and what you want to run on it?  For general purpose computing, you can get away without spending that much, contemporary games on Windows tax even the most expensive boards to their limit.  Lest you're playing Crysis etc you don't need a pricey nuclear-reactor powered GPU.

### Post by Skripka on 2008-11-08
Hmmmm, another factor to consider, is your box....do you know what platform motherboard you have, and how much physical space you have in your box?

From the photo in your other thread, I'll bet money you don't have a full ATX size motherboard, how much distance do you have between your expansion slot covers and your harddrive bays?  My roomy tower box with a full ATX board has 10.5" of space exactly (my gtx fit with no room to spare between the HDD bay, actually).

As I said in your other thread, for heat purposes, I always recommend shopping for a card with a ducted exhaust on it--these are usually a little more expensive (an exta $50 or so from without), but you end up being very greatful for anytime you are running graphics intensive processes.

For ~$240 you could afford a gtx level card if you really wanted, though lest you're doing Crysis, or Photoshop in Windows or anything similarly computationally demanding in linux, it is *really* overkill.  Currently GeForce8800 boards are getting cleared out (they are the old top of the line), that would be a good place to start looking-NewEgg has various models of those for a little under $100USD and up.  With the bonus, that you wouldn't be feling very obsolete soon.  Again, foor getting compiz to run smoothly, I doubt you'd even need that much power.


I have an old Asus built Nvidia 8500GT TOP board laying around Me Casa, that could kick butt--even running Half-Life 2 under Wine (with all high settings)--for comparison.
