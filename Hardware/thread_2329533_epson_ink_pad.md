### Post by jgw on 2016-07-09
I think I will mark this as solved.  Newer epson printers have something called ink waste.  They handle it in two ways.  The first is the line the entire bottom of the printer with absorbent material and the second is the put absorbent stuff in a box.  In both cases the absorbent stuff gets full and then drips all over whatever its sitting on.  Epson took care of this by counting the number of times you have printed something and then get get told your printer is worn out and, basically, forget it.  The fact is that your printer is not worn out and you can google this stuff and buy a reset key.  There are also free keys, which may, or may not, work.  You can also hook up an external waste receptacle and capture the waste there.  The waste, apparently, is all black so you can re-use the waste if you have refillable cartridges installed.  

I think this is an effort, on the part of epson, to stop people from messing with their printers (apparently they think your printer is theirs).  Anyway, I was just wondering if anybody had dealt with this one before.  I have now installed an external receptacle for the ink and it seems to be working.  

Thanks to all for the replies.
