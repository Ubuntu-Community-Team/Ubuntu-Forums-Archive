### Post by theophiles on 2010-10-28
Well, here it is nearly three years later. I struggled for a few days with the tiny screen, then decided I didn't care that much. I basically just decided that this ancient computer was too old for a modern operating system. I probably should have recycled the old thing, but I am glad I did not. To make a long story, which I will tell anyway, short: I am typing this post using Xubuntu 10.10 on my olt Toshiba Satellite 4080xcdt.

A few days ago I found a copy of Ubuntu 6.06 in a drawer. I remembered that I had this computer running with a full screen under that operating system. Since then, I have learned a great deal more about Ubuntu and figured I could probably do that again. I pulled the old machine out, put in the CD and hopped to it. A bit of fiddling with the xorg.conf and there it was at full size. I almost giggled to see this 11 year old laptop working again. Then I had an idea. What if I copied the version of the xorg.conf and installed a later version of the software. I might be able to make it work and have an old system running a modern operating system. Since I am working at a college that is struggling with the hardware costs of moving from XP and Vista to Windows 7, the idea really appealed to me. I knew that the computer would struggle with the RAM requirements of Ubuntu, so I went ahead and went with Xubuntu. I put in the xorg.conf and now it is running great with an up-to-the-minute operating system on a very old computer.
