### Post by Kayone on 2009-10-06
Silly questions:

Did you get a virus scanner?
Did you reboot?
Are you on a KVM?
What has changed (since it worked last)? 
Is the problem fixed?

Doubt a worm would do it.. by definition, worms don't really have that kind of capability. I would think that a virus wouldn't necessarily move the mouse for you. I guess it technically "could" make something like this happen but I doubt it. I doubt it is a trojan. If you had a trojan and someone was messing with you, the movement would typically be identifiable of a person. I assume the movement is erratic. If someone actually hacked you and got a trojan on the box, I am going to say it is pretty safe to say that they wouldn't mess with your keyboard.

If rebooting didn't help, there are troubleshooting steps you could take to islate it. For starters, you could always get a virus scanner and clear that question completely.
