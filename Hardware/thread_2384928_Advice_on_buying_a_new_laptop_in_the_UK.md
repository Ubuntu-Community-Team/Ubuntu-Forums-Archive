### Post by foxy123 on 2018-02-14
I am planning to buy a new laptop some time in April and I am looking around. I have decided on three options and wonder if anyone could have any comments.

1. Dell XPS 13 (Ubuntu edition) - bassically one of the most often recommended laptops. I am thinking about i7 option. It looks great and it is a well-known brand. Ubuntu is pre-installed. The drawback is that it is quite pricey.

2. Asus Zenbook (either UX430UQ*or a new UX331) - it also looks great and again I am thinking about the i7 version with 8Gb memory and 256Gb SSD. It might be cheaper than Dell (I've only seen it released in the US at the moment). However, it comes with Win10, so I have to install Ubuntu myself. On the one hand, it could be a good thing as you have two OS on the laptop and I have no issue with installing Ubuntu myself. On the other hand, I have always had a dual-booted laptop for the last 10-12 years and I almost never had any reason to use Windows. Also as it would be a Windows laptop, something could always be incompatible or it might take time to resolve incompatibility issues.

3. Slimbook KATANA II Intel i7 - on paper it is a great laptop with very good specs and it costs slightly less than Asus and a lot less than Dell. There is an option to get it with Ubuntu preinstalled. The issue is that I could not find any decent reviews even of their previous KDE Slimbook model so it is difficult to see the quality of the laptop. Also it is a Spanish company, so if anything goes wrong I will have to send it back their, which always takes a lot of time. Another thing is that a laptop with a British keyboard has a lead time of 1 month at the moment and I feel a bit nervous to pay that long in advance (I can always buy a Spanish/US version).

Anyway, if someone has any comments it would be great to read.

Build quality is very good for both.
