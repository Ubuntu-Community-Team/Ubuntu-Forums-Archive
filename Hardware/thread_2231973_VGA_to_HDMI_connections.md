### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-06-29
Yes, you're right about the cables. They're limited to very specific devices (a few projectors and some earlier Atom based Windows tablets come to mind) that can output analog signal trough the HDMI port. In this case the HDMI specifications aren't being used, just the socket for convenience. The aforementioned early Atom based Windows tablets didn't even support HDMI and yet they came with that physical port but for standard analog VGA and meant to be used exactly with those adapter cables or similar. Understandably a standard Dsub connector is too big for a tablet we want slim.

The cable in picture you posted from eBay looks exactly like any other similar cable. Are you sure they claim to have a converter built-in? Seems a blunt lie to me. It simply there's no space for the electronics needed for the required conversion, unlike the other one you posted which I immediately recognized as a converter (quite typical form factor actually) and not just a cable.
