This thread follows on from my earlier one at

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2154383&p=12691002#post12691002](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2154383&p=12691002#post12691002)

To summarise, I bought a "Neostar" USB record deck and a few spare styli, intending to transfer a large collection of vinyl records to the computer. I reasoned that a price of around £200 should give a reasonable quality transfer without the need waste a fortune on something that would be redundant once the job was done.

[http://www.mysmartbuy.com/p-404-Neostar-Vinyl2CD-USB-Turntable.html](http://www.mysmartbuy.com/p-404-Neostar-Vinyl2CD-USB-Turntable.html)

The turntable rumble and speed variations seemed to settle down if I left it running for a while, but the pops, clicks and surface hiss were completely unacceptable, much worse than even a badly worn 78rpm record played on a wind-up gramophone, and it would persistently skip backwards, whatever I did to the tracking weight.

I checked the stylus with a low-powered microscope, only to find that there was **no crystal** on it, just a spike of what seemed to be a blue-green glue. No wonder the output was so bad!

I checked some of the other styli, and they were hardly any better, though they did at least have crystals attached.

Trying to play a 78rpm record (with the appropriate sylus) was even worse. The sound was breaking up so badly that it sounded as if the arm was "bouncing" across the record, and some records wouldn't play at all.

I can't say the Neostar deck is rubbish because they'd probably sue me, and this has been my only experience with it. Perhaps I was just unlucky and bought a "dud" (with several dud styli as well ???), but I certainly won't be buying anything else from that company.

Their website has a lot of very positive "customer reviews", but when I attempted to add my own comments, surprise surprise, the link didn't work. I wonder why? One could almost be forgiven for thinking they don't **want** any **real** customer feedback.

I contacted the company, who expressed their "concern", but did nothing to rectify or ameliorate the situation, and they ignored my follow-up e-mail.

That's the history lesson over. Sorry it's so long, but it seemed important to warn people who may be about to make a similar mistake.

What I need now is a **decent** record deck, possibly USB, and I'd like it to be a current model so I can order enough spare styli for the job. Can anyone advise me? I'm in the UK if it makes a difference.

I couldn't afford, and wouldn't want, to pay over £20,000 for a top-of-the-range model from a manufacturer like SME. My hearing isn't as sharp as it once was, so I don't need "best possible" quality. Good enough is good enough, and cheaper is better, consistent with reasonable quality.

I thought £200 should have been enough, but if I have to, I'm prepared to go to a maximum of about £500.

I'm no expert in audio or electronics, so I'll need something that's ready to use without a lot of extras or too much fiddling about with the settings, and I'll be using Audacity.

One final point. Someone advised me to save the "master recordings" in .flac format prior to reworking to .mp3. Is .flac better than .wav? I'm not very knowledgable in these matters.
