### Post by TheFu on 2014-12-10
There are many, many, existing how-to guides for specific models of chromebooks.  It is NOT beginner friendly at this point.  I have an Acer C720 and still have to rebuild the touchpad drivers manually after every kernel update.  Ok - so that rebuild is scripted, but still ... 

There are many frustrations in using Chromebooks as normal PCs to offset the wonderful weight, size, power, and speed.  Mine cost
* $212 (after tax)
* $100 (new 128G m2-SSD; 16G ain't enough)
* $20 HDMI-to-VGA (for presentations)
* $30 USB3-to-GigE ethernet adapter (wifi sucks)
----------
$362 - to become a usable configuration.
For that price, I'd have been better off getting an 11inch Dell with a normal keyboard, RAM that can be expanded, normal SSD/HDD size, built in ethernet, VGA out, and more USB ports ... still with the same 8.5 hr battery life.  

The chromebook battery is amazing. I go out to meet with customers all day and do not bring a power cable.

I really dislike the chromebook keyboard. It doesn't have a Delete key.  Think of all the times you need that.  Mapping ctrl-backspace **is not** the same. Plus the Power key is where the DEL key is on all my other systems - ALL OF THEM. As a touch typist, for the first 2 months, I was powering off my UBuntu-on-chromebook 15-50 times a day. It was extremely maddening.

I've had the C720 for 10 months and still wish I'd gotten the Dell 11 inch instead - mainly due to the keyboard issue, but the lack of RAM expansion is proving to be a major issue too and the lack of touchpad drivers being included in the stock Linux/Ubuntu kernels all these months later is crazy too.  I need to check into the HW-compatibility kernels ... 

Get the dell ($280), dude. There are a few Chromebook vs other option threads here. Carefully review those before jumping. please.
