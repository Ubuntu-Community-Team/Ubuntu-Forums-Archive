### Post by wdaniels on 2008-08-04
It does get slightly complicated because the total maximum output in Watts gets split between the different voltage lines coming from the unit. Usually the output is given as a table showing the ratings (current in Amps) for each voltage. You have to match the Amp requirements for the GPU with what can be supplied by the PSU at the correct voltage (usually 12v).

245W seems too low for the whole PSU, are you sure you read the correct part of the sticker? For reference, I have a good-quality 350W PSU which it says can supply 18 Amps at 12 Volts (18A x 12V = 216W). I hear that the 8800 GT draws about 10 Amps on 12 Volts at full load so the 400W PSU you mention should be OK for that, although it could be borderline (it does depend a little on what other devices you have in your system too).

But unless you actually need that level of performance for games or something I would go for a lower-end card. I have a GeForce 7600 GS myself and have never found any reason to consider upgrading it. I play Anarchy Online sometimes, for which it performs admirably, but other than that it only gets used for compositing effects on the desktop.
