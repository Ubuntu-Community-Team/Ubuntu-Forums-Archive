### Post by BrandonBan6 on 2008-12-15
To update the processor, you would need one that your motherboard supported (i.e. matched the socket type and wattage of your current one). Your AMD Sempron sits on an AM2 socket. So something like this might work: [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103773](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103773)

If you can't find a processor that your mobo supports, then you can add a new mobo to the shopping list, but then things get sticky from there. As this is a manfactured machine, it is unlikely you will find a new mobo with matching stand offs, so then you have to get a new case. LOL. 

Most video cards should work. Looks like you have (1) PCI Express X16 slot, so I would utilize that with a newer GeForce 8 series. 

If it were me, I'd turn that box into a file server, and build a new machine. You can build a descent rig for about $500.00 and even a gaming rig for about $1000.00.

### Post by Titan8990 on 2008-12-15
Alright, here is the deal.

In order to upgrade your CPU, you will need a new motherboard.

A new motherboard will cost about $70-100 for AMD and $90-125 for an Intel board.

An AMD CPU will cost around $100, and an around $150 (you should note these are the price ranges of products I find to be in a good range for price/performance).

If you also want a video card, a good one will run you about $100-150 (with your needs a $100 card should be just fine)

If you do a video card upgrade you will most likely need a new power supply for about $70-100, or $30 if you don't plan to keep your computer for longer than a year or two.

That leaves you RAM, a case, and a DVD drive away from a full new machine.

The option is yours. I personally continuously upgrade the same machine, but that is my preference.


If you have a budget, I can make some recommendations on products. 

Just let me know what you are thinking and keep in mind that all of those prices are estimates and can be increased/reduced as needed.
