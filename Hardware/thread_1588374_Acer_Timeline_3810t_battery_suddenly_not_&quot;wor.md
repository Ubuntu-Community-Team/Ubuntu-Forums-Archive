### Post by quickdry21 on 2010-10-04
Hey, the strangest thing happened to my Acer Timeline 3810t laptop, running the Meerkat 10.10 RC. This laptop has been running Ubuntu exclusively for over a year now without any issues.

Anyways, I was using the laptop as usual, and I had just finished compiling and installing the latest Compat build for my Intel 5100 wireless card. I was using the laptop on battery at the time, and when the drivers finished installing I turned the laptop off as I headed off to my next class. In class I went to turn my laptop on (on battery), and absolutely nothing happened, no indicators, no monitor, no fan, nothing, despite the fact that it had over 1/2 the charge remaining when I turned it off less than 4 minutes ago.

After class I plugged in the AC and it started as if nothing had happened. I checked the battery indicator applet, and it showed two batteries, neither of which were charging or discharging. 

I preformed a "battery charger reset," (or something like that) as per the Acer Support website, which basically means unplugging it, taking the battery out and holding the power button for 30 seconds, but this didn't change anything.

Also I upgraded to the latest BIOS and still nothing.

Any ideas? Are there any indicators from Ubuntu that would explain such a sudden, catastrophic battery failure?

I'm fairly convinced this is a HW failure. Could it possibly be something that Ubuntu did? If it is HW, is it the battery or is it the laptop itself? The Timeline series have an indicator LED on the front that is orange when the laptop is charging and blue when the laptop is fully charged. When I turned the laptop on, post-incident, the orange light would flash once or twice during the bootup and then remains dark.
