Super-cheap upgrade: thoughts?
I'm on a really tight budget. Partly because of being low-paid and partly because I'm ultra-practical, believing there's no point in paying for features (in any item) that won't be used. I do functional, doesn't have to be pretty!
However, my aging old-style P4 is starting to struggle a little and adding more RAM is looking like being less long-term cost effective than my proposed build. Getting it up to it's 2Gb max will cost almost 1/3 of my list but still leave me with a seriously out-of-date computer.

Current System in brief: (It's a self-build, much the way Frankenstein's monster could be so described)
P4 2.5MHz
IBM M/B, transplanted from an 83xx series, possibly 831x, the boards are basically the same.
2x 256Mb RAM, PC2700/333, supports max 2Gb at same spec
Nvidia GeForce 5200 with 128Mb RAM and AGPx8 but m/board only does AGPx4 :-(
160Gb IDE Hdd
Multi DVD/CD writer
Generic CMedia8738 5.1 PCI soundcard

Proposed System in brief:
AMD 4050E Athlon 64 x2 2.1GHz
ASrock N68-S /Nvidia 7025 on-board
2Gb DDR2 800

Should be able to bring it in at £100-120, re-using drives/case/monitor/keyboard and mouse. I had some concerns about 20 pin to 24 pin PSU but I think my pretty modest setup would be fine ignoring the extra 4 pins. Should I stick in a graphics card at a later date, I'll probably be looking at a new PSU. Current one is 420W btw.
Ubuntu 9.10 is running great atm

I mainly use my system for pretty low-end user stuff. Surfing, WP, watching DVD's, playing music sort of area.
I propose to use the on-board graphics for now, the likelihood of me ever needing anything more than one PCIe graphics card is zero. I'm no gamer, discounting Frozen Bubble and the odd whirl at Doom3... :-) I even turn off Compiz/Beryl, it just annoys me. Likewise, I needed IDE: I can't squeeze a SATA HDD just yet.
Now and then, I'll see something I like as source code and compile it myself.

I'd like to go for Ubuntu 9.10 64bit, fresh install. It'll be a while, maybe 2yrs before I upgrade all my hardware again. I wanted to be as cost-conscious as possible yet still have a system that'll take minor upgrades (graphics, RAM, CPU) in the meantime.

The 'net in general and these forums are full of info but so much of it is contradictory. I'm particularly concerned about Firefox and Flash.
So, basically, whilst I think my reasoning's in order, the Devil, as they say, is in the detail. 
I'd appreciate any comments generally but specifically on anything I've overlooked, please.
Many thanks
Mike

I don't suggest moving to previous version of ubuntu (it has no benefit)... 
You will get improvements by switching window managers.. You are using Gnome, which is a full fledged desktop environment, it gives you allot of functionality (allot it you are probably not using) but at a performance cost. There are window managers that require very little memory and cpu usage, so switching to that will feel like a hardware upgrade...  

Some examples are: xfce, lxde, openbox, fluxbox, but there are countless more... 

You can install them on your existing ubuntu install, and boot them when you login to your system.
