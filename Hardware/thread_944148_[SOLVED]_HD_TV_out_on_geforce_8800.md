> I am trying to get a full HD (1080p) signal out of the tv out (S-video to components) on my geforce 8800 in ubuntu. I can get the full HD signal in windows.

NEVER!

```

docter[~] lspci | grep VGA
01:00.0 VGA compatible controller: nVidia Corporation GeForce 8600M GT (rev a1)

```

VGA is analog, you'll never get a digital signal out of it. Sure, you could get a solution like 
VGA->DVI->HDMI adapter/konverter

but you'd still have an analog out. Not even windows may do such magic :)

S-Video is limited to sth below 800x600. Anything above is scaled or stretched.

I have a 8600M GT, a laptop with 1440x900 and my LCD-TV with 1366x768.
But I'm using VGA-out, as if it was a 2nd Monitor.

This works just fine for me, compiz works too, and with some tuning on your graphics cards settings you'll get a quite reasonable Resolution. 
But it's still not Digital, and never will be.
I assume you know the differences between digital and analog output, do you?

### Post by Reisrdok on 2008-10-12
I added these to xorg.conf under tv's monitor section, problem solved. I can now choose correct resolution.

```

    Option         "TVOutFormat" "COMPONENT"
    Option         "TVStandard" "HD1080i"

```

Reading nvidia driver's readme helps a lot ;)

[http://us.download.nvidia.com/XFree86/Linux-x86_64/177.80/README/index.html]("http://us.download.nvidia.com/XFree86/Linux-x86_64/177.80/README/index.html")
