### Post by srs5694 on 2010-03-15
Try to track down a CPU compatibility list for the motherboard. If your new CPU isn't on the list, chances are it's not compatible and will never work. If the new CPU is on the list, then either the CPU is defective or you need to do something board-specific to get it to work, such as set a jumper (unlikely even on a 10-year-old board) or upgrade the BIOS. Sometimes a CPU that's not on a board's compatibility list will work with a motherboard, but for the most part you should stick with CPUs that are officially supported.
