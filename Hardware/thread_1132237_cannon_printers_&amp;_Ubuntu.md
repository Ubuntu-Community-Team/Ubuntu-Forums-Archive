### Post by Gemnoc on 2009-04-22
Short answer: not really.

Long answer: as long as she just wants to print B&W or color graphics on regular paper, it's fine. But, if she wants to print on photo paper like 4"x6" prints (10x15cm), it's a no go without the purchase of TurboPrint, a commercial printer driver that will set you off 30 euros. To my knowledge, the generic Linux drivers don't support more than 600dpi on the Canon printers. It doesn't look good, I tried with an iP4000, and the ink smudges because it doesn't bond with the paper.

The printer company that's most Linux-friendly is HP. They maintain a Linux driver, HPLIP. But even then, it's no walk in the park to figure out how to print borderless 4x6 photos on Linux. I managed to do it with my HP C7280 All-in-one and HPLIP 2.8.7, but after I upgraded to 3.9.2, it doesn't anymore.
