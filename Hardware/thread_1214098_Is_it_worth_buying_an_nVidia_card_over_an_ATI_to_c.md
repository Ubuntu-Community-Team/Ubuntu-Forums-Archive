### Post by Bohemenian on 2009-07-15
Basically, I'm really liking Linux, but I feel like a problem is stopping me from crossing over from Windows any more, the problem being the ATI graphics driver. So, to be able to continue using Ubuntu more and more I got the idea to actually buy a new nVidia graphics card to replace my current ATI Radeon 4870 (it stings a little extra to do this when my current card is such a good one though).

But before I spend that kind of money, I wanted to double check a few things on these here

1. The Blender UI is really hard to use, with submenus blinking 
2. A lot of games doesn't work with Wine, because of the ATI driver
3. VLC movie rendering doesn't reall work, and HD videos can make the entire computer just hang
4. Inkscape is a little slow when changing colors and very slow when moving panels

Maybe this is a hard question to answer, but I thought I'd still ask:
Has someone else actually bought an nVidia card because you got tired of the problems with the ATI driver? And did you consider it worth the money, or has someone who hasn't bought a new card any opinion on whether it's worth buying a new card.

Btw, if anyone wants to just give me the gist of what the ATI driver lacks that the nVidia driver has, that would be really great.
