### Post by Double.J on 2012-01-22
Hi there!

In terms of support.. we're now at a fantastic place with hardware support - most things can be got working with little efort.

A couple of things remain true - popularity is worth it's weight in gold - the more popular a device, the more support threads, outside hardware guides, mod tips etc. As well as often having more native support.

You get what you pay for - two laptops with the same specs and a large variation in price; one is cost cutting; maybe a less common webcam/touchpad etc.. thing to consider.


Finally - support is still "ported" - unfortunately we're still not at the stage where a hardware vendor releases linux drivers with all it's new things - last year's releases are often the best for the support/features/cost ratio :)

Personally I think the most important thing with laptops is to go to a place like PCWorld (without your card) and get the sales guy to show you their range - try before you buy; you want to see the webcam, hear the sound and feel the keyboard/track pad - when I choose my netbook I chose one without HDMI over one that did at the same price because it felt better to use and 3 years on I don't regret it ;)

### Post by aquarius18 on 2012-01-22
If you are not afraid to build a machine to your liking then you could try to get hold of a 3 or 4 year old desktop from a friend, relative or even from a company who upgrades their machines on a regular basis.

Then buy a Linux supported NVIDIA card for around 100-150 bucks, maybe stick a larger additional HDD into the box (another 80 bucks or so), load 11.10 or better wait for 12.04LTS and away you go.

My wife and I have not bought a new machine for years, always got some used 3-4 year old box and modified them to our liking.

Recently I got hold of a tower from my neighbor, WinXP and riddled with viruses which killed the MOBO. Installed a MOBO and CPU from another "inherited" machine, reformatted the HDD, loaded 11.10 and then XBMC, bought a video card for $89 and now have a fully fledged HTPC. Total cost: $89 and 3 or 4 days of installing and configuring.

You could do something similar with a 2nd hand laptop, just check that it has sufficient RAM, an adequate CPU and HDD space.
