### Post by SeijiSensei on 2019-05-21
When I was supporting dialup and UUCP mail services for clients, I always bought these:

[https://www.amazon.com/Courier-Everything-Analog-Modem-us-3CP3453/dp/B00006BA2H](https://www.amazon.com/Courier-Everything-Analog-Modem-us-3CP3453/dp/B00006BA2H)

At the time they cost $300 each.  Now you can pick one up for around $50.

Pretty much any modem works with Linux.  I ran some faxing tests with Hylafax and this modem many years ago. It worked fine.

The bigger problem might be whether your computer has an RS-232 serial port.  They're much less common these days.  Amazon has a USB-to-RS232 converter cable:  [https://www.amazon.com/TRIPP-Serial-Adapter-5-Feet-U209-005-DB25/dp/B00TWFKEFA/](https://www.amazon.com/TRIPP-Serial-Adapter-5-Feet-U209-005-DB25/dp/B00TWFKEFA/)
