There will never be an fglrx driver for 9.04 and cards older than the 2xxx series. ATI has said so.

Your options are to stay with 8.10, or to try the open source drivers with 9.04. The open source drivers provide no 3d acceleration and no power management features, but are fine for desktop use.

What happens is: ATI dropped the support for older ATI cards (bastards!) and now you're forced to install one of the open source drivers (which are quite nice, but not that polished yet).

I know this because I just popped in the 9.04 CD and installed from there without thinking too much about it. Then I tried to install Fglrx and discovered all this.

So far I have a few complaints about the open source driver, I had lock-ups before, but it seems that, with updating, it becomes more stable.
