### Post by TheFu on 2024-06-26
I'd only buy locally available laptop hardware that has local support channels and parts readily available, unless you can be without the machine for 6+ weeks at a time or travel to other countries where support is possible without too much hassle.

Every laptop I've ever owned has failed at some point.  The most common failures recently have been keyboards, but I've had screens, GPUs, and needed replacement batteries after one L-Ion battery was expanding enough to cause the entire case to bulge. The old battery was a fire hazard.  I've replaced these things myself and learned that some replacements are trivial (batteries and some keyboards), while others are very complex or impossible. One laptop, the external barrel power plug was hurt by a small drop. The fix was a new motherboard for $650 ... when the laptop was $750 new.  Fortunately, the credit card I used to buy it had a "double the warranty" clause, so that insurance just paid me $650 and I bought a new laptop. I was lucky. Of course, I had to pay $50 to get someone certified to determine the issue and create an estimate for the fix so the insurance claim would be paid.

I took a router to a 3rd world country, set it up for a monastery there and told them to call/email me if there were any issues.  A year later, they had replaced the beautiful router I'd brought them with one that was locally available, but less capable.  Having local expertise was more important to this client than anything else.  BTW, I have the exact same router hardware in my house and it has been working flawlessly since 2015. I expect it will work another 10 yrs, since it is sealed and all cooling comes from how the metal case contacts the hottest chips inside it. Effectively, there are ZERO moving parts.  I may need to replace the mSATA storage in a few years, but that's it.  If that happens, it can run from a USB-flash storage device just fine for months, if necessary.

If it were me, I'd stick with locally available hardware.  You may have excellent luck and if you only need the laptop to work for 24 months or less, then I might be tempted to get what I want regardless of available parts/support.  If you think this laptop will be used for 10 yrs, then support WILL be needed, so plan appropriately.
