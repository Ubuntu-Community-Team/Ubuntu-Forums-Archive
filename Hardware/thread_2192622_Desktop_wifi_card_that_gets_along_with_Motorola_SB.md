### Post by kjkrum on 2013-12-08
TL;DR: **Does anyone know of a PCI network card that plays nicely with the SBG6580?**

I bought a Motorola SBG6580 cable modem + wireless router, which may have been a poor decision.  I have 12.04 on two machines: a netbook in the same room as the router, and a desktop in the next room.  The netbook reports about 90% signal strength and works perfectly.  The desktop reports 100% signal strength (obviously a lie) and the connection fails every 5-10 minutes.  It says it's still connected, but I can't connect to anything, not even the router's config page.  I have to toggle wifi to get it working again.

The funny thing is that the desktop worked perfectly and reported about 70% signal strength with my old Linksys router.  There are many reports of similar problems related to the SBG6580 on a variety of operating systems, so I suspect that either it does something slightly wrong that some network cards don't tolerate, or it doesn't tolerate something that many network cards do wrong.  It's too late to return it, so I'm thinking I should just replace my network card...

### Post by JDorfler on 2013-12-17
I'm in the middle east right now, and I have this "Wimax" modem/router.  I have never trusted any router features in a ISP given device.  In fact, when I was living in the States I bought my own cable modem.  You'll always get better service and options with a third party router than you will with a modem/router in one system.  Let the modem modulate/demodulate, let your router route, and get a switch so it can switch for LAN connections.

I know this really doesn't help with your original concern, but it is what it is.  Good luck.
