### Post by Hur Dur on 2010-12-23
SliTaz would probably be your best bet. It's in active development, runs on about 24mb of RAM, and has a rather large repository. Not as large as Debian, Gentoo, or Ubuntu's repository, mind you, but it has over a thousand packages in its repository. Although it is small, it comes with quite a few packages already installed. The iso is about 30mb in size, and I tried it on my parent's old computer (Pentium III, 256mb RAM) it was snappy, and was only using about 20% of the available memory with Midori and Mplayer open. It's also pretty easy to use. It's not as easy as Ubuntu, but it's not as difficult as muLinux. The repository is [here](http://pkgs.slitaz.org/).

Other than that, Puppy Linux, Slax, Slackware, Debian, Tiny Core and Puppy Linux are great alternatives. Do not use DSL, though. It is outdated and no longer being worked on.

If you have a newer computer, I recommend you try some of the ones I listed using 128mb of memory and see which one you is snappy enough for your liking, then install it on your old laptop.

You could also check this topic: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456)
