### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-14
You can buy two 1 terabyte drives for less than a very small SSD and I'll wager you won't notice a performance drop...even if you try to find one with a benchmark program.

SSDs and laptops go together well for one reason: they can stand being dropped a little better.

Are you likely to drop your desktop system while waiting in line at Starbuck's?

Yes, SSDs are theoretically faster. You can't prove that with available hardware, so why would you spend 30x as much per gig for a mere 2% boost?

If you edit pictures and movies, your money needs to be allocated according to this priority:

Monitor (get the best you can afford)
Disk (get as much as you will use, 1TB is a good start)
CPU (I'd go with the Q6600 - you can run them at 3.2Ghz easy and they're cheap)
RAM (The more, the merrier, but 4GB is plenty and 1GB will do)
Power Supply (Just cuz it SAYS it's 700 Watt, doesn't make it so)
Motherboard (Stick with Asus or Gigabyte and get only those features - sound, lan, etc - that you'll use)

Fancy graphic cards won't help much, but nvidia has fewer driver problems. Go for cheap.
