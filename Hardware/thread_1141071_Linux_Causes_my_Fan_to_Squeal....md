Example, I hear the squealing, then I click on an application(or anything in particular that makes the hard drive kick in and the computer start thinking), the squealing cuts out until the application loads the the computer returns to an idle state. The computer also appears to be functioning just fine, but this little squeal is kind of annoying in quieter environments such as on an airplane.  Has anybody else experienced this and know why an operating system would cause a audible noise from a piece of hardware?

### Post by Danielkida on 2009-05-10
I think it runs at different speeds depending on the temperature (ie activity) of the cpu.

Check the system monitor to see what % the cpu is running at.

If its low..maybe it's because linux isn't constantly hammering your cpu, perhaps its the sound it makes when its running more slowly.

I have noticed with my laptop, the fan doesn't seem to run as much under ubuntu as it did with windows.
