So from one noob to another, welcome to the Ubuntu comunity. Here you'll find lots of experts, unfortunately for you I'm not one of them... But I can offer noob advice (in other words - a checklist).

1). Have you updated as much as possible via "Update Manager"? Cos sometimes Ubuntu fixes itself (don't ask me how, it just does... and when it doesn't - one shakes a fist towards the sky)

2) Have you activated the proprietary software under "Hardware Drivers"? You should see video card programs similar to the ones in Windows (i.e for ATI/AMD video cards - Catalyst Control Centre ; for Nvidia - Nvidia Driver Settings ?¿ i think).

It should be better suited to successfully tweak the display settings, refresh rates, extra monitors etc.  I emphasize the word "successfully" because there are many programs out there in Ubuntu Software Centre, that 'might' do what you need it to do 'but' it always involves a little elbow grease.

3). The veterans of WW2 always dug their own fox holes. i.o.w Try using google - type in "external monitor black flicker 1440x900 Ubuntu 11.10" . No, seriously... you'll be surprised at how much help is available outside of this Forum. N.B: always include the word Ubuntu &/or your distribution code, cos if you end up in a windows or mac forum (don't say i didn't tell you so).

4). This one is my favourite, as quoted from Roy from the 'IT Crowd'... "Have you tried switching it off and back on again?"

When in doubt, try try and try again. Otherwise that spider will move in and overstay its welcome.
