### Post by snowpine on 2010-02-16
I have a Dell Mini 9 (very similiar to the 10v) and Ubuntu 9.10 ran great on it. The only minor inconvenience is it will take you 5 minutes to get the wireless working. Lots of great tutorials on this site:

[http://www.ubuntumini.com](http://www.ubuntumini.com)

To answer your specific question, you will need to do a fresh install of 9.10 (you can't upgrade from 8.04), so it doesn't really matter if you buy it with Ubuntu preinstalled or not.

### Post by snowpine on 2010-02-22
> **rcaca0 said:**
> Why can't I upgrade from 8.04?

The Dell Mini does not come preinstalled with "regular" Ubuntu, but with a special "Dellbuntu 8.04" that is not 100% compatible. Dell hasn't made an official announcement yet whether they plan to offer an upgrade.

A fresh reinstall is easier, if you feel you need a newer release. (8.04 will be fully supported through April 2011.)

### Post by snowpine on 2010-02-22
ps I forgot to answer your question about wireless. Depending on the version of Ubuntu, you may need to install the Broadcom STA wireless driver by going to System->Admin->Hardware (while connected to the web with a wired ethernet connection). This is a one-time inconvenience that should take no more than 5 minutes.
