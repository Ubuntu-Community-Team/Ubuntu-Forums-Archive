### Post by JayCarman on 2009-01-14
According to some of the users posting on the launchpad.net bug discussion I mentioned in my last post ([https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/258357](https://bugs.launchpad.net/bugs/258357)) this workaround will solve the problem:

A workaround for this is to increse the GdmXserverTimeout from 10 seconds to 60 seconds. Its seems that X needs more time to perform its required task. You can find the parameter in /etc/gdm/gdm.conf

I tried this on my system and it did not work, but maybe some of you will have better luck.

I have figured it out in my T61 : I upgrade my nvidia driver to 180.29 which was downed from the [www.nvidia.com](www.nvidia.com) and delete the two lines of the xorg.conf  which was configured by nvidia-xconfig:


	HorizSync       60.8 - 73.0
	VertRefresh     50.0 - 60.0


Then run /etc/init.d/gdm restart
It really works and I loged out and come back with a normal welcome screen after the xserver restarted.
Good luck for you.
