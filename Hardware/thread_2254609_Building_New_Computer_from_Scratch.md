### Post by Dennis N on 2014-11-28
> I want to start buying parts now (I will buy parts over time). 

Better to save up and buy them at the same time.  Components may have a limited time to return them to the retailer for exchange should something turn out defective - often 30 days from purchase. Not good to buy a motherboard, and 2 months later when you finally are ready to assemble find that it's defective. Also, new items tend to get less expensive as time goes on.

Read reviews online and reviews by purchasers on online retail sites who may comment how well something works in Linux (or not).

Intel is usually a good bet for Linux.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2014-11-28
You might like to check the link in my signature (BUILDING A LINUX COMPUTER?) for some ideas. Fairly old but the principles remain. The main thing is get a decent, name brand power supply unit. It is the heart of the machine, so avoid the generic silver box type PSU. They are not energy efficient and if they blow they can take other components with them. Good luck. It is enjoyable process building your own and gives great satisfaction when using something you've built. ;)

### Post by gordintoronto on 2014-11-29
+1 to Dennis N: get everything more or less at once. When you are doing the build, don't rush.

I have used two resources: the pcmech web site, and newegg. I built my primary system five years ago. I went for the top rated parts on Newegg, and that has worked marvellously. On Newegg, you can search the reviews for "ubuntu" and "linux" and sometimes get useful details.

Since I built my system, I upgraded the memory from 4 GB to 16 GB for a work project, and upgraded the optical drive to blu-ray, because a free blu-ray drive became available. My best decisions: going with a WD Black hard drive, and a really good power supply. (Antec 650).

Good luck!
