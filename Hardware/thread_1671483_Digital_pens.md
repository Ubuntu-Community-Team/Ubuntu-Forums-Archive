This archived thread talks about digital pens. That was 3 years ago now. 

What's the state of it now? 

To quote LadyOwl:

>  Digital Pens
I have been googling around this, and found in practice nothing execpt somebody saying he had been "able to discover/pair & connect to a Logitech "io" digital pen using a cheap Bluetooth USB dongle but so far, have not been able to access data on the pen." Also on the Logitech website it says the pen isn't produced anymore... (This may be very recent, I think in the autumn it still was.)

BUT, luckily, there are other pens. Quite many, actually. These seem promising from the Linux point of view:

Nokia Digital Pen SU-1B seems to be possible with Linux too. It works with special paper just like IO2, but you can also print the paper yourself (though somebody had managed only with a laser printer). The link explains what tweaks make it work with Linux directly, but if your mobile phone is of the right type, you can use it directly, If I've understood correctly. Nokia site has plenty of information about the pen.

I also found EPOS digital pen. Sounds interesting, cause it works with ANY paper. In practise it seems to be sold with name Zpen, see here. "...the notes can be read only by a special viewer, came with the ZPen and compatible with Windows, Linux and Mac OS. An application, only this time Windows will then change his notes in PDF documents more easily exportable or classify them on his hard disk. Finally, there is a module for recognition of handwriting."

Of the rest I haven't got a clue (at least not yet) about whether they work with Linux or notm only a a bad feeling that perhaps not...

Smartpen from LiveScribe seems also interesting, but it's not available for sale quite yet.

Digital Scribe
Mobile NoteTaker
FlyFusion (requires special paper)
magicomm G303 Digital Pen and Maxell Digital Pen (requires special paper) seem to be the same pen.
Chatpen by Sony Ericsson (requires special paper)

I also got interested in the Cybertop Digital Notepad. It seems to need the pad, but let's you use any paper you want.

I notice something nobody is taking into account is how you fill the pens up again.
