### Post by WinRiddance on 2010-12-11
Well, according to the [manufacturer page located right here]("http://www.airlive.com/product/product_3.jsp?pdid=PD1234297283834") there are no Linux drivers that are supplied by the manufacturer. On top of this the USB stick uses such new, still uncommonly used technology, that Linux Ubuntu may not even be able to recognize this device. Until you wrote your post I never even heard of 11n-HT20 and 11n-HT40 which is what that receiver uses in order to achieve those speeds. Keeping in mind that this is nothing more than a glorified USB WiFi antenna/enhancement, your actual upload and download speed still comes through the box of your DSL provider, whomever that might be. And if your provider has got you set up for a certain amount of upload/download speed, then guess what? That's right, your connection will be limited to whatever access has been granted to you with your account.

Normally Ubuntu recognizes such USB WiFi sticks by default. You may need to left click on your Ubuntu network symbol (should be on the panel) to view your available connections for that device. I've never had problems with them before, ever. But then again I've always used standard technology that was commonly accepted worldwide as a standard. The bottom line for 99% of all internet users is that your actual upload and download speed is assigned to you by your internet service provider. Buying a faster USB WiFi stick is not going to change that.

On the other hand, if you're just after the additional distance, then you need to wonder if that 11n-H20 or rather 11n-H40 technology has something to do with that as well? If so, then you might not be able to get the desired distance out of this new device either.

REMEMBER ... The account that you're signed up for with your ISP ... the hardware compatibility on your machine ... the driver availability for whatever hardware you're using ... and specific types of wireless technologies ... all work hand in hand together to provide the actual, physical access that you're getting in the end. Simply making a change to one of these may not provide you with any results at all.
