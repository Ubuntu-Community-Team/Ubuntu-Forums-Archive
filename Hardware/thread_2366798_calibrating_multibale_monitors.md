### Post by efflandt on 2017-07-22
Not quite sure what you mean by calibrate. If using digital connection (DVI, HDMI) about all you should need to adjust is brightness and contrast. For VGA, besides that and sizing/centering the image, you would typically just make sure that colors are set in the middle (unless you want them warm or cool). I don't know if DVE (Digital Video Essentials) is available where you are on DVD or BluRay. But that can be used for stand alone video players as well, including audio.

When I wanted to calibrate default settings on my color laser printer, I found a reference image with all sorts of colors on the Internet, and kept adjusting settings on the printer itself until it printed the same photo realistic (initial default colors were too bold). Those same settings worked when we got a similar color laser printer at work. I don't know where to find that particular photo of a number of people and various colors at a fair. But if you web search "calibration test image" you can likely find something with various flesh tones, color pallet, and various shades of gray to black to adjust color, brightness, contrast.
