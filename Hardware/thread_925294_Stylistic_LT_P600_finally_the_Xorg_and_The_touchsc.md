######Second###################################

Tips: If you have changed the serial port in the bios and you want to know wich one is the serial try:

cat /dev/ttyX 

touch the screen and see if some ascii caractes appear on the screen.
######Third###################################
Once you know the state of your ports precede with the configuration:

setserial /bin/setserial /dev/ttySxxx autoconfig

setserial /dev/ttyS1 uart 16450 port 0xfd68 irq 5 low_latency baud_base 115200 spd_normal skip_test

This solved my battle with the great Stylistic LT P-600! it Appears that ttyS1 is the default serial. If you are having trouble (like me) go check the BIOS settings for serial configuration, this was changed from auto to serial 3 (or 4) with irq 8 i believe
