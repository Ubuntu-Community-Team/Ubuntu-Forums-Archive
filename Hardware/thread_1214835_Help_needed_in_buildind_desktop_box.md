### Post by DownTown22 on 2009-07-16
Case - I like CoolerMaster cases.  They typically have excellent air flow.

DVD drive - I think all basic DVD burners are fairly cheap these days.

Audio - I've never had issues with onboard audio before for basic computer use.

CPU - Might as well go with a 64bit (a lot of CPU's are 64bit these days anyway it seems).  I actually have the 710 in one of my computers right now...seems to running pretty good actually.  My other computer has an Intel Core 2 Duo E-something in it.

Video - Can never go wrong with nVidia.  I like eVGA and XFX cards - currently I have an eVGA 8500GT and an XFX 9600GT (or is it 9800GT....I don't remember off hand).

Power Supply - Look for ones that are certified 80+ or better (an energy efficiency rating) and have nice big fans in them to keep things cool.  I like Corsair, Cooler Master and OCZ - the CoolerMaster I have is a little louder than the others, but overall, it's not really that loud.

Building computers is so much fun!
