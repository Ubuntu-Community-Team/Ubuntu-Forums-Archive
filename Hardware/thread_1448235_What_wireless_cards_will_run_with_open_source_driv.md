### Post by oldsoundguy on 2010-04-07
the point is ... Broadcom does not support Linux.  Not every card that works in Windows will work in Linux and Broadcom is one of those problematic companies . They change the architecture of their chips as often as I change my sox, so writing open source drivers for them becomes a game of catch up and the results are spotty and iffy!!  Lots of times ndswrapper has to be installed around the Windows driver.

For 20 bucks you can save the grief and get something that will work on both Windows and Linux.
