### Post by beew on 2011-05-06
No. It is not supported because Nvidia doesn't provide the driver, as long as this is the case it will never be supported, it has nothing to do with the Ubuntu version. 

You may want to write to Nvidia  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1712278](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1712278) It is one thing that they don't support graphic switching in Linux, but it is too much that you can't use the Nvidia card at all if Optimus is enabled (unless there is a BIOS switch to disable Optimus to use the discrete card)  Since most new Nvidia laptops are shipped with Optimus and without the BIOS switch Nvidia is basically pulling the plug on laptop Linux, they should at least be upfront about it so people don't waste their money on  garbage (it is worse than garbage because while you can't use the Nvidia card it continues to suck power and generate heat)

However, if you cannot return the laptop you may want to check this out.
[http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=162171](http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=162171)

Don't know if it works or how well it does (when it does), but it is worth investigating.
