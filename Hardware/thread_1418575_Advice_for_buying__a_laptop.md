### Post by foresthill on 2010-02-28
Are you going to be doing any gaming? If not, you could save a lot of money just getting regular integrated graphics. I have Intel graphics on my Core 2 Duo system and it handles desktop effects fine. Also, you would not have as many problems with excess heat and / or graphics chips burning out prematurely, as has been known to happen.

### Post by foresthill on 2010-03-01
Hey, 15.4" screens are cool. I just bought a Gateway notebook with one of the 17.3 inch 16:9 screens. The thing is really wide and narrow, and not really that much bigger (area-wise) than a 16:10 15.4 inch screen. Web pages look really stretched out on a 16:9 screen, I don't like it.

I went back to my old laptop with the 15.4 inch screen, the 17 inch is just too damned bulky, especially the bottom half of the notebook where the keyboard is. Speaking of keyboards, watch out for the really flat ones that some of the cheaper models have, they're a pain to type on.

### Post by Schotty on 2010-03-02
I will second the Asus motion.

I have a G51 and it rocks with most distributions.  The factors involved were the nVIDIA GPU (G260m), good CPU for gaming, and the sound, WLAN, and HDMI all work fine.  I would burn an extra copy of a live CD/DVD and bring it to the store you plan on purchasing from.  I went to hell .. ahem, Best Buy, and luckily the salesman was sure that linux worked fine and was willing to boot the 9.04 install disc on the store model and go into live mode so I could see.  The only external driver needed was for the nVIDIA card.  Everything else worked fine post install.

If you use Crossover or Cedega to aid in your gaming addiction as I do, there are some extra debs/rpms that are needed in the latest ubuntu and fedora releases, fwiw.
