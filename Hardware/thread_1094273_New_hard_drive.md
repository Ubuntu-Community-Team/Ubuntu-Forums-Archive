### Post by lindsay7 on 2009-03-12
Look at the mother board and see if you have sata ports on it. It is the better way to go if you can. Most times you do not need to do anything except putting in the Ubuntu startup disk. Sometimes witha sata hd. You may need to put the cd with the drivers and setup files that comes with the sata hd, into the the cd drive and  let it load before you put in the Ubuntu startup disk.  The documentation that comes with the hd should tell you.  Ide hd's generally do not need to do this.

Whenever you do anything inside the computer, make sure that the powere cord is out of the back of the computer. If you leave it in, even though the swith is off, there is still power on some of the circuits and you can damage the hardware if you start unpluging things.
