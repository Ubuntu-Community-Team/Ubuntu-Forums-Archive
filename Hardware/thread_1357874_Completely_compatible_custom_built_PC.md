### Post by michael-wd21 on 2009-12-18
At the top of the page is a link which says "Hardware compatibility list", you might want to look there first! :)

The biggest issue I have had with linux and hardware compatibility has been in the area of wireless support but that's much better these days. Also you should be careful with choice of graphics card - Intel is a very good choice for compatibility as the drivers are fully Free but the performance is probably not the best. Most modern cards are supported though with recent linux kernels and Xorg releases and I have happily used both NVidia and ATI cards - a few years ago things were much worse.

The strategy I find useful is to design the system according to hardware and cost requirements first then check each component for compatibility by doing web searches. Then refine the design.


Hope that helps
