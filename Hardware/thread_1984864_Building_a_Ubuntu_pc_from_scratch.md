### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-05-22
I'd start by getting a different CPU.  With such a low budget, why blow $120 on a high-powered CPU you probably don't need?  A media PC does not need a lot of CPU power - unless "light gaming" becomes "intense gaming."  By comparison I'm running two media center PCs (mostly used for viewing content on big screen TVs) both based on a 2.5GHZ Celeron Dual Core and that's been plenty of CPU horsepower.

Also, do you really want a 125W CPU for a media center box?  Really?  I'd be shooting for something that runs way cooler (when you run cooler, you not only generate less heat but also can get away with a quieter fan).

Personally, I'd go for a 35W i3 for not much more money:

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819115094](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819115094)

or even a Pentium G630T (budget version of Sandy Bridge i3 - only has dual core no extra threads) for only $85, should be plenty fast enough for a media PC:

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819116407](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819116407)

Your power supply looks great if you go with a lower-power CPU but 380 watts may not be adequate if you stick with that AMD heater CPU.  I'd probably shoot for a 500 watt PSU in that case.

Your video card might be fine but if you are on a budget, I'd probably opt for a cheaper card.  You can routinely find cheap HDMI video cards for $20 or even less after rebate.  They are passively cooled and not blazing fast, but for what you need that may be sufficient.  FYI, in the Intel Sandy Bridge the integrated video is much better than it used to be (the new Ivy Bridge is even better).  I'd personally just try that initially instead of buying any  video card at first.

Of course, if you go with an Intel CPU you need a different  motherboard.

My personal philosophy in building new computers, if I'm not in a rush, is to wait for bargains over a few weeks.  Wait for a CPU to go on sale, wait for a case, wait for a motherboard...monitor the deal sites.  None of the components you list originally seem to be on sale, anyway...
