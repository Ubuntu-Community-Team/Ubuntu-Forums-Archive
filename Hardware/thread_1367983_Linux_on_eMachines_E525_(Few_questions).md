### Post by volobiz on 2010-07-04
There must be different models of E525 from eMachines because I can google these laptops as far back as 2008. Underneath the laptop, although my vision must be going because these are some teeny tiny characters, I read E525-2632 as the model number.

I picked mine up for $298 from WalMart and it's been a delight.

Anyway, I have the very latest Ubuntu 10.04 LTS on it, and here's what I noticed:

* The processor is an Intel Celeron 900, so 64bit dual core 2.6Ghz.
* The laptop is cool to the touch, so no more heat issues on previous models.
* The battery has been charging well now and when I unplugged it, it said the battery life was about 6 hours, which was better than Windows 7 because Win 7 said 4 hours.
* It runs super fast -- I have full 3D acceleration on with the video and full effects and no issues.
* I could even hook up a second monitor and the new display control panel from Ubuntu 10.04 LTS absolutely rocks -- it detected it and let me change the resolutions or where it was sitting on my desk, and I can drag windows back and forth between the two even with full video effects.
* I had no issues with the wireless net card or the wired network either.
* The touchpad works without issue.
* The drive is quiet and doesn't do this "tick" sound like you say.
* I connected a USB keyboard to it and a USB mouse, and both work perfectly in all respects. The touchpad and internal keyboard on the laptop itself also still work at the same time in all respects except for the brightness keys.
* The microphone and speakers work fantastic.
* The keyboard seems good. It's not bad, although I've seen better keyboards. I give it an 8 out of 10, which is better than many laptops actually. Many laptops have bad keyboards where the keys fly off with too much usage, or they make too much sound.
* The fan is quiet.
* The touchpad buttons are very quiet, and that's fantastic because my last laptop had super noisy and hard to push touchpad buttons.

The only negatives about the laptop are that it only has 2 USB ports (so one needs to carry a small USB hub if doing anything elaborate), it lacks bluetooth (so one needs to plugin a bluetooth adapter if they want that), it only has 2GB of RAM when 4GB would be nice, and the brightness applet as well as the brightness keyboard controls do not currently work.

I'm still trying to figure out what to do about the brightness controls because that's my largest problem. It's bright when you're in a dark room and want do dim the screen slightly.
