### Post by umhelp on 2009-01-15
I use esata on my dv5t without any problems. I don't really see why you would ever have to reformat your drive in any case???

As long as ubuntu finds your drive then you should be able to mount it. 

It is really an issue of ubuntu detecting your esata port as aposed to what filesystem your drive is formated for. If ubuntu doesn't have the correct drivers for your esata port then your drive will not be detected at all and you won't be able to use it.
