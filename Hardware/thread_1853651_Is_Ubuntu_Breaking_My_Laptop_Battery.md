Nothing to do with ubuntu or any other installed software, just a broken battery. Just order a new one :)

### Post by diasf on 2011-10-03
It is NOT linear.

Once it starts breaking, goes fast. So it is normal to go down from 50 to 40 in a week, it's already (badly) broken.

You can screw your battery in a number of ways, none of them have anything to do with ubuntu. High end games, burning DVD, among other high power operations are not recommended when on battery for instance. 

Again, the battery was broken already. Broken, gone.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2011-10-04
You will typically get 300 cycles or 3 years whichever comes first.  The  aging of the battery will cause internal resistance to increase and cause less current to be delivered--this will reduce life.  After 25% reduction, the drop off occurs pretty quickly.
