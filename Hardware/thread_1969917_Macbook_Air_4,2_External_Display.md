### Post by Umbra Diaboli on 2013-02-15
Dear All,

I apologize for bumping-up this thread.

I am having the same issue with my 4,2 MBA and my 27" Apple LED Display. If anyone has found a solution to this problem I would really appreciate their advice.

Thanks in advance!

PS: If a mod could move this thread to Apple Users it would also be greatly appreciated :)

EDIT: I have a somewhat solution for the problem - I use the magnet trick (this trick is used to keep any Mac laptop with it's display shutdown while the laptop itself is open and plugged in to the external display - it helps the laptop breath better, and avoids the Mac from running 2 displays simultaneously [https://discussions.apple.com/thread/3192733?start=60&tstart=0](https://discussions.apple.com/thread/3192733?start=60&tstart=0)). It's supposed to only be used with OS X Lion or higher (Apple pisses me off).

However, I perform the trick in a slightly different way:

1) With the MBA powered off, plug in the external display.
2) Press the power button. 2 seconds later, place the magnet on the mac laptop slightly below the left USB (Here, both screens may do weird things or even stay black for a few seconds until the login screen pops up.
3) Login - once in, the resolution from the external display will look quite wacky, don't worry for now.
4) Once logged in, I remove the magnet for 1-2 seconds and place it back, just half a second later I tap a key on my external keyboard - after a few seconds the MBA's display will be shut down and the external display will be the only one active (the resolution will adjust by itself too).

It took me a while to perfect this technique, for some reason it only works with the correct timing, and no idea why it works. Still, I would like to know if anyone has found a permanent solution to this issue that doesn't require so much messing around.

Thanks a lot,
