### Post by pmrlaw on 2015-08-24
The HL1030 FOR CUPS driver (a pre-installed Brother printer driver in Ubuntu) works perfectly well as a driver for a Brother DCP-L2500D monochrome printer.

A lazy way out (if you want to avoid downloading the tar.gz driver from the Brother website before using alien to convert it into a .deb file etc).

Connect your Brother printer to your Ubuntu computer by the USB port and turn it on. Open System Settings, Printers, Add (printer) and select the DCP-L2500D.

Ignore the recommended 'Generic' drivers as they do not work. Scroll down to the Brother Drivers and select the HL1030 for Cups driver. Then just forward away until it is installed and print a test page. Sorted.

Discovered it by accident and hope it helps ):P

Edit: If it is not pre-installed, you can download the HL1030 FOR CUPS driver from the Ubuntu Software Centre. You will see the available Brother Printer drivers by typing 'DCP' into the software centre search bar
