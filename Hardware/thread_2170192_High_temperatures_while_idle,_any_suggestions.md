### Post by Thee on 2013-08-25
I had a Packard Bell laptop from a friend some moths back that was overheating. It got so hot that it kept turning off after working a little, and in time the HDD failed and had to be replaced. Seems like Packard Bell really has some serious heat issues with their laptops.

Anyway, I had to take the entire laptop a part to be able to reach and clean the cooler, it was a horrific experience, but after I did that and applied some thermal paste on the CPU, the temperatures normalized and the laptop stopped turning off.

What I'm saying is, that either you need to clean the cooler better / apply new thermal paste or you need to replace the cooler altogether, because it may be faulty and it doesn't spin fast enough to cool the components right.

That's all I can tell you.
