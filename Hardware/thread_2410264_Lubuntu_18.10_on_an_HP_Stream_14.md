### Post by tea for one on 2019-01-13
I have a two year old HP Stream 11.6 with Broadcom 43142, 2Gb ram and Intel Celeron N3050.

I currently use Xubuntu Core [https://unit193.net/xubuntu/core/](https://unit193.net/xubuntu/core/) which runs fine.

The wireless performance with the Broadcom 43142 using the Linux drivers was OK if you were near to the router.

I eventually removed the Linux Broadcom drivers and purchased a tiny wireless usb device Edimax EW7811 and the wireless performance is improved.

This Edimax device is marketed as suitable for the Rasberry Pi so that info gave me the confidence to purchase it.

A couple of years ago, I installed Lubuntu 16.04 on my netbook and, if I remember correctly, the OS was fine other than the intermittent loss of wireless connection.

Good luck
