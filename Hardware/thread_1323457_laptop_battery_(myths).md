### Post by Diphallia on 2010-01-05
Batteries in general do like to be fully discharged and then fully loaded.
And some batteries may become overcharged if they're in the charger too long.
But I suppose that modern laptopts don't overcharge.
But If you're running on AC power, the laptop doesn't turn of the usage of batteries (They must be in standby to be ready for the AC-cord to be plugged out, right?) and the battery will decharge and charge all the time.
I have no idea what difference it makes in the short run, but eventually it will effect the battery.

I hope that answers your question.
You can probably google it to see what other people have experienced.

### Post by Hillshum on 2010-01-05
Modern Lithium-ion batteries have no memory, so not fully charging or discharging makes no difference. Once a month or so, it is a good idea to discharge it to maybe 10% to reset the capacity meter. 

Taking the battery out might reduce the heat it absorbs, depending on the laptop, but that is about all it does.
