### Post by em3raldxiii on 2008-04-30
I suspect it might have something to do with USB versus ps/2 keyboards.  I am going to bet you are using a USB keyboard.  I am also going to bet that if you put a little usb to ps/2 adapter, it will work with no troubles.

Alternatively, there may be some entries in your BIOS that allows you to do something like, "enable usb keyboard".  Also, if you turn OFF "plug and play" or "PnP" (makes your BIOS configure your devices instead of your operating system), it *might* do the trick.
