### Post by Kevbert on 2008-06-17
Hi.  Some comments.
1) What does he base his figure of 550W on ?
2) Power supplies are very cheap to manufacture (<$1 each!) and testing would be minimal (probably a batch check, 1 in every 20).  So faulty PSUs are possible, also like most electronic goods failure normally occur early in life (have you seen the bathtub diagram for life expectancy?)
3) Customer is always right, but I assume you've tried to do him a favour, so he should give you some slack.
4) He's taken the PC apart!!! What antistatic precautions has he taken ?  Even the CPU could have gone.
5) Are we saying the AVR is faulty ?  Is it new or has it been tried with something else ?
6) Storms can put large spikes on the lines and in worst cases cause a blackout.  Even though I'm in the UK and the mains power is pretty good I still use a line interactive UPS to filter out mains problems. 
Good luck.
