### Post by cascade9 on 2011-01-27
> **lolium said:**
> Thank you for your reply, but what im asking is could you possibly point me in the right direction as to which cards would be compatible with the computer?

Sod-all now. 

Its only got AGP and PCI slots, and the vast majority of cards now are PCIe. AGP cards are hard to find, and unless you know a good source for 2nd hand cards, outrageously expensive. 

PCI is even more expensive, and the most common current PCI card is the nVdia 8400GS, which has been known to cause problems with the 845 chipset. 

If you can find one, a nVidia 6XXX or 7XXX AGP card would be your best bet.

### Post by Sylos on 2011-01-27
First you need to set what you want to be able to do with the computer. Although most cards are now PCIe if you arent looking for HD or high level gaming then you can easily get away with an old AGP card.
