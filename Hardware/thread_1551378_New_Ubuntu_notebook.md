### Post by zolr on 2010-08-13
There is a simple program by the name of "Cheese" that can be downloaded through the Ubuntu Software Center for viewing your webcam feed and capturing photos and videos.

Within the system preferences there are options related to power management and what happens when you close the lid.

Basically, laptops that come with Ubuntu pre-installed are geared toward users who need or want a new computer but don't want to spend more on a laptop that comes with Windows 7 when they plan to format the hard drive and install Ubuntu the second they take it out of the box anyway.

If you are positive that you want Ubuntu, I would recommend purchasing a laptop that comes with it pre-installed, unless of course you can find a better deal that happens to come with Windows. Plus, as the company designed the computer to be completely compatible with Ubuntu, you should have no issues. Just because a laptop comes with an open source operating system doesn't mean it wasn't designed as well as one that comes with Windows.

That being said, I am currently running Ubuntu 10.04 on a laptop that originally came with Windows Vista and I have had no problems whatsoever. (Except for having to reinstall Ubuntu after a failed attempt at resizing a partition with GParted.) Also, if you purchase a computer that comes with Windows pre-installed, be assured that installing Ubuntu couldn't be easier.

Either way, good luck with whichever laptop you choose.
