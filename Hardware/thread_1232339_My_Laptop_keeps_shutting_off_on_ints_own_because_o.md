### Post by The Real Dave on 2009-08-05
Well, seeing as your laptop came with such a powerful GPU, its probably designed to cope with the stress that GPU causes on your system, namely how bloody hot its gonna get =] So, I don't agree with the idea that you'll need to get a new lappy, but I havn't huge experience with laptops, so this is just my logic two cents ;) 

Dust causes a lot of heat. Enough said :lolflag: My PIV was idling at 50, spiking to 60-65 once I put load on it. So, IMO, a can of air could help at least. You would want to out a vacuum cleaner near the output of the vent, and spray the air into the intake. Those little straws on the compressed air are useful for getting out the niggly bits of the dust.

Then again, seeing as yours is so powerful, it might have one on the GPU also. If not, you'd have to open the case to clean it properly. Which would void your warranty. So talk to the guys you bought it from, and see if they'll pay to just have it cleaned by a pro, keeping your warranty. 

Remember that your laptop shutting down is a good thing, protects it, and shows that you've a problem :lolflag:

After the laptop has cooled down, run some canned air through its exhaust vents, blow out the dust and debris both directions, being sure to work the air so that its knocking the stuff loose. You can likely also pull the RAM bay door loose without ruining your warranty, blowing air through there as well. Other great places to push air through and hopefully dust out, are the usb openings, mini pcie openings, etc...

The warranty is almost a detriment to the laptop as to properly clean it you need to break the seal and get after it thoroughly; for now just keep as much blown out through the vents as you can.

The little red straws they tape to the sides of those cans of air can be very helpful for this sort of thing so be sure to plug it in. I find that having 3 or more cans to work with is best, as you use them, the gas inside the can cools, making the product less effective; so, I use one until the can is cold, then pick up the next one and keep round robinning it like that until I am satisfied with the job. 

Be sure to park the laptop on a hard level surface during warmer times, ventilation is key. It may also be worth investing in a laptop cooler, a good one, with its own external power source, not one of the usb driven units.

### Post by motoperpetuo on 2009-08-05
one nice thing about most acer laptops i've seen, at least those made in the last few years, is that you can clean the heat sink and the main vent without taking the whole computer apart. there's a big panel on the bottom, and removing it exposes the fan, heat sink, memory, and a bunch of other components. then you just pop off a few screws, remove the heat sink assembly, and spray all the dust off with compressed air.

i did this with a good friend's laptop that was very slow and constantly shutting down due to overheating, and it fixed it right up. the other acer laptops i've looked at in stores seem to have the same panel. i wish more laptops were designed this way.
