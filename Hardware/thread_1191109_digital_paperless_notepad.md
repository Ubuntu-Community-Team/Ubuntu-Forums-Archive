### Post by msbentley on 2009-12-02
Interesting question - I was looking for something similar a year or so ago. In the end I bought a "digital notebook", equivalent to the Adesso CyberPad[ (http://www.adesso.com/products_detail.asp?productid=294]("http://www.adesso.com/products_detail.asp?productid=294")) that needs real paper, but transcribes a copy that you can then sync onto your PC.

If you don't want real paper, then it means you need a screen to see what you've written. You *can* use the CyperPad without a paper - the (electronic) pen also has an inkless refill for use as a graphics tablet, but using this without the PC would be tricky at best. Imaging trying to turn back a few pages and add a note, when you have nothing but a blank sheet in front of you ;-)

By the time you have a device with a screen and touchpad you are already talking something more complex/expensive, and I'm not sure the market for a dedicated device is large enough - you're basically 90% of the way to an ebook reader with annotation support, for example.

So I would suggest either get a device with paper, or look for a cheap netbook tablet (e.g. the Asus T91) which is <$500.

Let us know what you decide/find!
