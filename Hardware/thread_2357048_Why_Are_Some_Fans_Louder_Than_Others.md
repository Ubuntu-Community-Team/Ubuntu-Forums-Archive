### Post by TheFu on 2017-03-29
It is the same reason that calm days outside are quieter than very windy days.

Depends on a few things.
* quality of the components used - quiet usually costs more
* amount of air required to be moved
* size of the fan
* size and efficiency of any heat dissipation components.

Moving lots of air through a small fan is noisy.  Moving lots (same amount) of air through a larger fan will be a little quieter.  Moving lots of air through a BIG fan will be quieter still.

Power supplies that are more efficient tend to put off less heat, which means they can use quieter fans.  After all, fans for computers are all about heat management for the components inside.  The more efficient components used tend to generate less heat.

Generally, the included CPU coolers are NOT going to be the most silent. They are designed primarily to cool the CPU appropriately. Noise is a 4th or 5th level consideration.

Arbitrarily slowing a fan and lead to overheated components.  Definitely monitor the heat for different parts of your systems if you screw around with that stuff.

Google will find more info than you can stand to read.
