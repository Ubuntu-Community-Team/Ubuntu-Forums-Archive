### Post by Olav on 2014-03-02
I see you have not got an answer yet. Let me try.

I believe, but I am unsure, that your T5082 does NOT support SATA drives, only IDE drives. Those may be hard to find nowadays and if you do find a new one, it will probably be a bit too expensive. So your best option would be to find a second hand one. Ask any computer hobbyists you may know, small local computer shops and such. They are likely to have a few of these lying around. Of course there is always Fleebay and such.

Don't worry about compatibility too much. I have never found a generic/cheap DVD-writer that didn't work with Linux. Unless you come home with something very exotic, it is probably not going to be a problem.

As for your doctor, he or she may be content with a copy of your stuff on a USB stick.
