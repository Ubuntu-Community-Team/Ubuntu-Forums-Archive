### Post by SiathLinux on 2011-09-22
I've just been given one of these (HP Pavilion zv6000 - AMD 2GHz / 512MB DDR / ATI (not sure which yet)...

This thing shuts down very suddenly, sometimes before finishing booting, so I did a lot of research on this thing (since it would be an ideal computer for me - even if I can't get wireless with Ubuntu 11.04 since I can just plug up to my network easily).

From what I found - HP laptops in general (both new and old - including 'Compaq' branded) overheat. With the zv6000 / Compaq R4000 - an upgraded internal fan along with a good cleaning should fix it.

I've ran across information that the 'heatpipe' could go bad - but have not confirmed this, and most sites show that they had a good amount of the goo (can't think of proper name) for placing on the heatsink to cpu contact. 

On general, just from looking at the casing it seems 'very closed' compare to my previous laptop (Acer Aspire 5720Z) or my Wife's laptop (Dell Insperon 1525) both of which have plenty of air slots on bottom... and the HP only has two small fan size circles of air slots.

Maybe while I have mine apart I'll 'case mod' it and add some cooling inlet slots on the bottom. Especially since I plan to keep it on my Themaltake T1000 (120mm under cooler).

I did run into several threads about the powersupply/battery issues known to this 'series' (seemed to include all zv models and their Compaq counterparts). Though that problem is similar - it kills all power to the laptop (if you problem is this you will note that the charging light goes out when the laptop shuts off - the overheat problem this light stays on).

Just what I've found so far.
