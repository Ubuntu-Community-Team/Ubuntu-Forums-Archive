### Post by weatherman2 on 2014-10-02
I have an Acer AO756.  I bought it a year ago, but it was already a close-out by then so it's really two year old technology, I guess.  The thing I dislike the most about it is the touchpad, but I use the thing every day, often for hours, and have gotten used to it for the most part.

It does support Ubuntu (12.04.2) very well.  I had some issues getting secure boot w/Windows 8 dual boot to work (it came with Windows 8 and I decided to keep it).  Newer versions of Ubuntu might be easier.  Otherwise, it is extremely reliable.  The CPU is a tad slow (Celeron dual core Sandy Bridge CPU) but really fast enough most of the time, light years faster than my old Acer Atom-based netbook that was slow, slow, slow.  I would buy another one of these.  Apparently, the Acer V5-171 is a much faster version of this laptop (with USB 3.0 ports) - I have considered buying one of those as a step up, if I could find one cheap enough.

I upgraded my Acer to 16GB of RAM, an SSD, and a dual band wireless card with bluetooth.  It's very easy to upgrade: one screw to remove the panel on the bottom to get to everything.
