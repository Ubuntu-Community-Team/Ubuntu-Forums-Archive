### Post by coffeecat on 2010-10-01
> **fra_pennaz said:**
> so even if a ATI graphic card has drivers by ATI, maybe it is barely working with 3D?

No, not quite. I'm very happy with the 3d performance on my ATI cards with the open-source driver, but then I'm not a gamer for which you really need the proprietary driver. The problem with ATI is partly with old, legacy cards, but that won't bother you if you are going for a new laptop. There are problems with some of the newer ATI cards, so the best things is, if you find a laptop you like with an ATI card, search the forum for other people's experience of the particular card. Make sure you look for experience on a recent version of Ubuntu. What was problematic in Karmic might be just fine in Lucid.

Generally speaking, you are safer with nvidia. And safer with Intel if you do not game.

You still have not said what you want the laptop for. If you're not playing games, the choice is wider.
