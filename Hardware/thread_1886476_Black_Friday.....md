### Post by gordintoronto on 2011-11-26
HP makes a huge number of different laptops. For example, when I went to the bestbuy.ca site, I found 67 HP laptops. My G62 works very nicely with Ubuntu or Kubuntu 11.10, but I can't vouch for the others. In my case, that includes the wireless and webcam, as well as the more standard parts. Everything "just works."

One of the big current confusion factors is having a discrete graphics adapter on Intel-based machines which have a graphics adapter on the CPU chip. If you turn off the discrete graphics adapter it saves power, at the cost of graphics power. I'm not convinced that Linux is handling this feature well just yet, but I could be wrong. You basically need to have both Intel and (for example) Nvidia drivers available, with the ability to switch back and forth.

I think Acer also has a huge number of different models, but I have no hands-on experience.
