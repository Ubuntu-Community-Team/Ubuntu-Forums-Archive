### Post by Squid Spectre on 2009-01-28
I've forgotten the specs but they were similar, I ran Edgy on a Fujitsu B series with 64mb of ram for some time. Installing it was a bit of a trick because the thing didn't have a CD-ROM, but it ran well enough (really slow) for what I needed it for. The key difference was the HDD constraint, I had a 30gb drive, you have a 3gb. If I were you , I'd skip the fancy desktop environment and go straight for Xubuntu or better still DSL if you want to install it locally. 

Or you may look into just investing 20-40 bucks into a USB drive thats 6gb just install/boot Ubuntu off that.

EDIT: Assuming, of course, your Fujitsu CAN boot off a USB drive. I know mine couldn't.
