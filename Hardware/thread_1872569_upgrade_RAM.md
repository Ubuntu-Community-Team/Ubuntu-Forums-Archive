### Post by lindsay7 on 2011-10-30
Look at the specs for your motherboard and find out what type of memory you have, ie, ddr2 or ddr3 and find out what the max speed is for you MB, also find out how may memory slot you have and what the max capacity is in gigs,  then buy memory from Tiger Direct or Newegg and change it out.  You can read all about this stuff using google search.

### Post by 2F4U on 2011-10-31
When I change RAM the first thing I do after the technical side is complete is boot up the machine with a liveCD and run memtest. I usually let it run all its tests, even if it takes hours. I found this to be the best way to recognize incompatibilities or damaged RAM.
