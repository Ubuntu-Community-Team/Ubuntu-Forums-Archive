### Post by lisati on 2009-09-21
My first laptop, a Toshiba A100, came with a recovery DVD. My current laptop, one of the Toshiba M200 range, came with software to create recovery DVDs - I'd suggest using a DL one if you can.

Yes, Ubuntu can tinker with the partition sizes and make room, but be careful: I've read reports that suggest that it's better to use Vista's partitioning tools to resize Vista's partition if you can. It would also be a good idea to make your recovery DVDs first, along with backups of important data.
