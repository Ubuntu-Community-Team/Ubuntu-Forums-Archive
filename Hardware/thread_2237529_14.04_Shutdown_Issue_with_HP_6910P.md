### Post by divergex-oss on 2014-08-02
I have an HP 6910p notebook that works pretty well with 14.04 after a few tweaks. The one lingering issue I am having is with shutdown. Sometimes everything seems to shutdown fine, but when I press the power button (the next morning, for example) The notebook will not turn on. In order to get it going, I have to unplug the power, and remove then re-seat the battery. This works every time, but of course I would rather have a clean shutdown.

I have become handy with editing config files and the like, but I have no idea where to start looking for the cause of this issue. My specs are:

3GB RAM
320GB HD
Core 2 Duo T8300 @ 2.40GHz
ATI X2300, Gallium 0.4, DPM on
Intel PRO/Wireless 4965 AGN
Bluetooth

Any ideas/help are greatly appreciated!

---

### Post by divergex-oss on 2014-08-07
It seems I have solved my own problem. On the 6910p, the BIOS setting for Wake on Lan needs to be enabled. Once I enabled that setting, shutdowns have been perfect and the notebook boots back up without issue.
