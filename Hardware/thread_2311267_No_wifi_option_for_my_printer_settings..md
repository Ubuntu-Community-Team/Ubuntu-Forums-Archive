### Post by weatherman2 on 2016-01-25
You first need to connect your Samsung printer to your wireless network.  This has nothing to do with Ubuntu.  The "Samsung wireless network" is an ad hoc network used for the initial connection to the network, in case you can't set the WiFi password on the printer itself (with keys on the front or something).  If it's possible to enter your WiFi password into the printer with keys, try to do it that way.  Every printer model may differ in how you can connect it to WiFi, and I don't know how yours works.  If your Samsung has an ethernet network connection, you don't even need to use the WiFi connection to get it on your network, if you can just wire it to your router or something.

Once your Samsung printer is on your network, then you can try to add it as a network printer on your computer.
