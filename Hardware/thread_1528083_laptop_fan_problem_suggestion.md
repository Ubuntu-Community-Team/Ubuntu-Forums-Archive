### Post by bazzal1941 on 2010-07-10
The problem you are facing is that your internal fan is positioned for the airflow to pass over critical components. Unless your external fan can replicate this you are likely to continue to overheat and damage your system.

### Post by pastalavista on 2010-07-10
I use a vacuum cleaner nozzle on the exhaust vent of an old Acer laptop I keep in the garage and it cools it down nicely and hasn't hurt anything I can tell. Laptop fans can be replaced but I don't think it's worth the cost. I have to monitor the temp when I use it (it sleeps most of the time) but only intense gpu or cpu usage overheats it and I just switch on the vacuum for a couple of minutes and it's back to normal and a couple more minutes, it's back to start-up temp. I try to do intense stuff on one of my other PC's.
