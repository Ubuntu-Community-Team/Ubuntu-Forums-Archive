### Post by Rhubarb on 2008-09-01
If getting the screen replaced is too expensive, and you want to try to clean it yourself (even if that means you may destroy the whole screen in the process), then read on:

Laptop screens can be tricky to take off, there are usually several clips and screws to remove.
Please remember that the LCD screen is made out of very thin glass, and as such, it's very fragile, so treat it with care.
Beer should be cleaned out as soon as possible, it's probably not as corrosive as cola.
I recommend using a damp cloth / very mild soap.
Then wipe down with a slightly moist cloth with water / window cleaner.
Carefully assemble back, and see if it works.

I'm no expert at doing this - but it should work fine for you.

Aside: One of my laptops that had a touch-screen where the touch sensor had failed, I found the cause of the problem and fixed it.  But I'd had neglected to realise the fragility of the screen, and had fractured it.
So, now the laptop with the broken screen is now my home server (web server, squid proxy, apt-cacher, murmur voip, ssh).

### Post by IntuitiveNipple on 2008-09-01
> **figgles said:**
> BUT, I'd bet the brighter bulbs that visit ubuntuforums have better suggestions?
I've stripped down many TFT displays (laptop and stand-alone) to remove the annoying bugs (midges) that come out of the wheat and barley at harvest-time and creep behind *everything*.

As long as you are methodical, take precautions against causing static discharges, keep the miniature screws safe and compartmentalised to avoid forgetting which goes where (a piece of packing foam stops the rolling), it usually takes about 15 minutes.

The thing you'll need to do is carefully separate the protective layer from the LCD (if the beer has got between). Be careful and check first though, in case they are bonded in any way... don't try to force anything!

Use a lint-free cloth so it doesn't leave fibres behind, slightly damp with no detergents on it, just warm water, and gently soften and stroke the contamination away. Work from the centre of the screen outwards so any residue is pushed to the edge.

Let it stand to evaporate any remaining dampness then give it a gentle polish with a soft lint-free cloth (spectacle-cleaning cloths or wipes for PDA screens are excellent) to remove any smudge marks.

Before you start, do some searching for any photo's other people have posted on the net of disassembling that model since it may well save you time and frustration working out how it comes apart.
