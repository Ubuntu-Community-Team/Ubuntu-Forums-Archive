### Post by tgalati4 on 2011-05-21
Understand that newer drives have specific, digital, tuning parameters that are burned into the PCB boards.  Simply swapping the boards may not work because the tuning parameters (drift, location, read/write power, etc) are different.

I was successful is swapping boards years ago to rescue some data from a Fireball 80 GB drive.  I haven't done it lately.

### Post by satanselbow on 2011-05-21
I think everyone here might be able to give you better quality answers if you let us all know the exact model of drive you fried ;)

You do realise that switching PCB is slightly more technical that pulling a couple of screws and jabbing in the hot iron don't you? Harddrives are rarely fused internally so you will be very lucky if the powersurge didn't cook the platters on the way through :(

If you have a USB enclosure or SATA/IDE to USB you might coax some life out of it and is a far safer method - to the pc you are testing it on as much as anything else - than plugging it onto a good machine blind ;)

IMHO switching PCB has not been possible for about the last 5 years due to the reasons already stated elsewhere in this thread :(
