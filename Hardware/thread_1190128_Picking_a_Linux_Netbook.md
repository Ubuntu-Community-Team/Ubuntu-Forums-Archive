### Post by timcredible on 2009-06-17
a friend has a dell mini 9, we erased the ubuntu 8.04 NBR that came with it and installed a normal 9.04 install, everything works perfect out of the box, and it's much faster than the NBR that came with it.

my granddaughter has an acer aspire one netbook, 9 inch screen also.  it came with windows, i dual-booted it with ubuntu 8.10, normal install, everything worked perfect except wireless, which was fixed by installing linux-backports-modules via synaptic.  

the dell was only like $199 on sale, but only has 8gb sd drive, whereas the acer was $299 (i think) but has 120gb hard drive.

another friend has a dell mini 12, which cost a lot more, like $500, and it came with ubuntu 8.04 NBR, which worked fine, but a little slow.  we looked into installing 9.04 instead, but there's issues with the video driver, which are probably known and workarounds available by now.

almost forgot, the dell mini 9 is about 18 months old, the mini 12 is about 5 months old, and the acer is about 8 months old, all of them are used daily and there's been no problems with any of them.
