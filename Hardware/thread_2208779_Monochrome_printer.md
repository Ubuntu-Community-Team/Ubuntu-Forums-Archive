### Post by TheFu on 2014-03-02
Don't know what wifi or "administer entirely from Ubuntu" means.
I've been happy with cheap ($50) laser printers from Samsung and Brother.  The ink never dries - I'm still using the same $35 toner from 4 yrs ago.  These are printers ... not much "administration" to them.  The Samsung must be 10 yrs old.  I prefer the brother laser printer. Plugged it into the USB port and it was recognized - has been working since then.

I also have a Brother All-in-One inkjet. Never print with it - just fax and scan. Ink costs WAY TOO MUCH and dries out every year.

I'd recommend checking the Linux Printer Project HCL. [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Printers](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Printers) should have a link. Strongly recommend printers which 100% built-in support. Avoid downloading anything outside the package manager for the happiest results.

### Post by panorain on 2014-03-03
I have had some situations with HP inkjet's failing to print after 'cyan, magenta, or yellow' becomes low. I recently purchased a used Brother MFC-295CN. I took a chance that it would be supported with drivers from Brother. The printer works very well under the Ubuntu 12.04 LTS kernel. The Brother website has pretty decent documentation for installation as well. So after I purchased ink cartridges I have 24.00 in the printer. Xsane works well also for scanning with this model. You can use 'CUPS' to easily create a 'print server' that will enable your printer to be used by other 'GNU/Linux based computers in your home or office also. I have not been able to get the Fax to work properly though which is a let down. 

 [http://www.openprinting.org/printers](http://www.openprinting.org/printers) has a reasonable list but not every supported printer is on the list.

A laser printer could be ideal because no need to replace ink cartridges as often. I like this Brother though because the refills are so cheap compared to some other printer makes out there.
