### Post by MaindotC on 2008-05-21
Computer failure when installing/Removing physical components is not really an issue.  If your car is running fine, you pull it out of the driveway, then drive it back in and suddenly the car doesn't start - well yes that is a problem but it's not really related to the act of moving the car in and out of the driveway.

Spend a couple bucks and get the latest A+ book.  It's soooooo nublar - you can't possibly remove/install something wrong if you follow it.  Find your components, or the type of components.  Different hard drives (IDE, Sata, eSata, FC) have different ways of connecting to a system, different memory modules, processor chips - they all have their own little way of doing things.

Get the A+ book - you've got all summer.

I just got an R61 also (with XP) and am hoping to dual boot with kubuntu. With the kubuntu 8.04 live-DVD, I was impressed that everything I thought of worked out of the box - wireless networks automagically detected, function keys I tested (even the silly keyboard light) seem to work. (Have nothing to test firewire with. Same with bluetooth - might not even have bluetooth!)

So I'm about ready to try an install.
UPDATE:
INSTALL WENT SMOOTH AS SILK!
My machine is a R61i model 7732-CTO
specs: 
Dual core 1.6 Ghz 533 MHz 
100 GB 7200 rm hd
2x1 GB PC2-5200 667 Mhz ram
dual layer DVD Read/Writer
SBB 11ABG wifi (intel)
NO BLUETOOTH, NO CAMERA, NO FINGERPRINT 

So far everying I've tried has worked just fine.
Specifically, sound works, headphones work, most (if not all) function keys properly mapped, including supspend, hibernate, sound up/down, brightness up/down, silly little keyboard light on/off. 
Battery life seems relatively reasonable. No actually timing, but I've no reason to believe its worse than the windows side. Maybe even a tad better?

Wireless worked straight out of the box.

I have not tried, having no interest, in more graphics intensive things like gaming or Compiz, etc. So don't know about that.
Dual boot works fine. Took a small tweak to edit the grub menu.lst file so that XP is the default.

So far the only "problem" is that when it autmagically detects a second monitor, and automagically shows both screens, one of the two screens often is not quite the right size, but then again windows xp has related issues in my hands.

So I've been very impressed!

As stated by others, lenovo often has sales these days, and in addition it seems easy to find additional coupons on the web. Between the sale and the further discount, price was WELL below 1000. With shipping, taxes, and the  I'm-a-sucker warranty extension (which I bet installing linux voided, sigh), price was about 800 dollars US.

So I recommend kubuntu 8.04 on lenovo R61i whole heartedly!
