### Post by impact on 2008-12-29
1)You probably mean Linpus Linux Lite, which is a Fedora based distro shipping with Acer's netbooks.

[http://www.linuxhaxor.net/2008/09/15/linpus-linux-lite-review/](http://www.linuxhaxor.net/2008/09/15/linpus-linux-lite-review/)

[http://www.linpus.com/products_2.php](http://www.linpus.com/products_2.php)

I am sure you can install additional programs. I have no idea how hard or easy it is. 

2) You can use an external DVD drive that plugs into an USB port or you can use a bootable USB stick (if you already have a computer with Ubuntu, there is a tool in the System menu that allows you to create the bootable USB stick). 

3) It depends on the power management settings. Dimming the screen and enabling laptop mode is a good start.


Do your research first, some netbooks (MSI Wind and it's clones) are quite easy to use and pretty much everything works on them out of the box. Others (Samsung NC10 comes to mind) will need a lot more work to play well with Ubuntu.

Edit: This site should give you an idea about what's happening in the world of netbooks - [http://www.liliputing.com/](http://www.liliputing.com/)
