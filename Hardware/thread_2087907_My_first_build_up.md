### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-11-24
This Gateway appears to have four RAM slots, with a 512MB DDR2 stick in two of the slots.

You could get four 1GB RAM sticks (replacing those two 512MB sticks) to hit 4GB.  Or you could compare that to the price of getting two more 512MB sticks to hit 2GB.  Or just two more 1GB sticks to  hit 3GB.  I'd do the 4GB option if it's cheap enough.  I don't know what your budget is.  The 2GB option would probably be super cheap and with Ubuntu should be reasonably fast.

DDR2 PC4200 would work for sure.  PC5300 would probably work too.  If you look up your machine on Crucial.com they recommend DDR2 PC6400 so those would probably work too but I'm not sure you can mix them with the PC4200 sticks you have already in there.

You want non-ECC desktop RAM.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-11-24
Depends on how much RAM your machine is capable of using and if you want to add more later. 4x1Gb = 4Gb but your computer can possibly/probably take 8Gb, 4x2Gb=8Gb. Therefore 2x2Gb will leave you two empty slots to keep adding if you like. 

Check the capabilities of you hardware re maximum RAM.

### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-11-24
The manufacturer spec states it will recognize only 4GB of RAM maximum.  If I had 2GB sticks of RAM laying around to try, I'd sure see if would take more, but I wouldn't risk buying 2GB RAM sticks myself if I didn't already have them.
