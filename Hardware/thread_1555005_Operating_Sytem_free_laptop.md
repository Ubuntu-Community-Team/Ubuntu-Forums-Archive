### Post by embrodak on 2010-08-17
I'm not sure how it works in the UK, but in the US if a laptop comes with Windows on it already, as long as you don't accept the EULA, you can get refunded for the cost of the OS. However, this is usually a huge hassle. You have to basically video-record yourself booting up the laptop and not accepting the EULA and then wiping the drive, and you have to keep a copy of the license agreement to point out to the manufacturer where it says that if you don't accept you can get a refund. Also, it can take a couple months on the phone arguing with people. 

If I were you I'd try to figure out if the EULA is different in the UK, and if so, I'd try to look it up to see if there's anything written in there about getting refunded for an unused OS. 

Cheers
