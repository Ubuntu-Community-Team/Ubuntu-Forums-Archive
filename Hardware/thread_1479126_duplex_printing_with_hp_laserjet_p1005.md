### Post by guano on 2010-05-10
Hello all, I just installed a new HP P1005 LaserJet on a Ubuntu x64 Lucid, and installation was VERY smooth. I connected the USB cable, and in the _instante_ I hit the power button, a msg appeared asking if I wanted to install the driver. I did and printing works fine for one-sided jobs (resolution 1200x600, 600x600 doesn't work).
Now I wanto to print on both sides (duplex), but I don't know how. If I select this option, the first side is printed correctly, but then the 'warning' red led starts blinking and there's nothing else I can do. I tried to put the paper again, but it won't pull it. Also, this model doesn't have any button besides power, so, I don't know how to tell the printer that it can go ahead and print the other side of the paper.

TIA

### Post by aleboco on 2010-05-18
Well, I had the same problem and solved it with a bit of trial and error few days ago: when the red light blinks, pick up the sheets, reinsert them into the paper tray...

AND

gently lift the gray cover of the printer (the one where you put the toner) -- just a little bit.

As soon as you fix it again the printer starts printing the second side!

It may be a bit of a crude workaround but, missing a button or a GUI, it certainly works!
