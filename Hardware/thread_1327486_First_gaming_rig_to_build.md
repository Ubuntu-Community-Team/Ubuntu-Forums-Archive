### Post by mivo on 2009-11-15
I wouldn't get an ATI card for gaming in Linux. The open source drivers won't be ready for 3D gaming in the forseeable future, and the closed drivers are inferior to Nvidia's, plus you cannot control the fan speed, so the card will be loud almost no matter what you do. I'd go with a GTX 260 (would need a 700W PSU for the GTX 285 and 295, if I recall correctly).

I would go with a "be quiet!" PSU, but the Antec one seems to get good reviews, too. I'd also go with two smaller HDDs instead of one large one, though that is personal preference ... I like to play it as safe as I can. :)

### Post by Rayve on 2009-11-15
Note: a lot of the following is dependent upon your budget, too!

Do you have a monitor already, or will you be buying one through NewEgg?  Same with keyboard/mouse/speakers.  Did I miss the audio card, or are you sticking with the integrated audio on the motherboard?  I second using an NVIDIA graphics card simply because it tends to do better with Linux, not as many workarounds as ATI I've heard, but to each their own.  If you don't mind putting the data from a CD/DVD on the hard drive and then copying it to another CD/DVD, a single CD/DVD drive works, but personally I prefer having two drives - one read/write and another just read, copying between the drives is much faster.  That's just me, though.  :)

Re: HDDs, one of the books I was looking at for building a gaming computer recommended using two smaller 10000 RPM HDDs in a RAID format for speed and then a larger, slower drive (i.e. 5900 or 7200RPM about 500G+) for storage.  Not by any means a requirement, just a suggestion!  Either way, be sure you have room for backups, whether by media (CD/DVD) or external hard drive or cloud storage.

Be sure your mid-tower case is big enough for what you plan to do now and also in the future - enough bays, enough room for your motherboard and video card(s) - or else when you upgrade a few components in a few years (add more hard drives, get a second CD/DVD ROM, put in a card-reader bay or fan-control point, get a second/larger video card, etc) you may find it cramped.

The anti-static wrist straps are great, and I also went out and bought a small set of non-magnetized tools just for computer work, to be safe.

I'll be doing the same in a few months - good luck to both of us!
