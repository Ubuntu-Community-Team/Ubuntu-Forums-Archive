### Post by Artemis3 on 2011-06-26
These are rgb "component" video, its basically the same as the small vga connector, using separate bnc plugs for each thing (rgb, sync, etc).

Some monitors had these plugs in the back, and a cable to convert into vga. They could be plug into video equipment using the separate components, or another cable with scart or similar end.

Of course in 1994 could have been just about anything: multi-sync/fixed sync vga (15 pin) (640x480@60hz max), EGA, CGA and MDA (all 9 pin); or even a composite one. 14" was common then.
