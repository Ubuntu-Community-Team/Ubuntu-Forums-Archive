### Post by vendax on 2023-02-14
Laptops often have very abysmal RAM memory options and often lack BIOS support for virtualization extensions. You cannot run advanced operating systems on many laptops, and 16GiB is not much RAM either for let's say firefox, let alone some filecache. For comparison, my OrangePi 5 SBC comes with 16GiB RAM and costs little more than $100. Laptops should have 64GiB RAM minimum.

Really depends on your usecase though. If you do not install privacy addons in your browser and are a light user, you can easily get away with 16GiB RAM.

### Post by BBQdave on 2023-02-14
> **Laffen_macc said:**
> Look like Lenovo Thinkpad is the best choice for me so far if it is Linux Ubuntu compatible.

I wished there were more choices for affordable laptops with Linux preinstalled…

Lenovo offers a guide on how to reset or shut off *secure boot*, if Windows is pre-loaded. This allows you to easily load Ubuntu or other Linus distros.

Other manufactures may offer similar information on how to reset bios or start-up security, to load a Linux distro.

Lenovo even offers a way to turn on *secure boot* after you have loaded your Linux distro.

I do not fully understand *secure boot*, perhaps another can clarify, but *secure boot* seems to be a Windows function. I do not believe it is necessary for Linux.
And I have ran multiple Linux installs on multiple hardware without *secure boot* and no problem.

So my thought is, most hardware (especially a couple years old) is good for Linux, just have to reset bios and disable secure boot (Windows) to allow Linux.
