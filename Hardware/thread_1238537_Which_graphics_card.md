### Post by balaknair on 2009-08-12
The nVidia **8800GT** is a pretty solid card, will fit in your budget(~, and should be good for 2-3 years, more if you're not a gamer or multimedia professional. 
Another good nVidia option is the **9500GT** or **9400GT**(with 1 GB DDR2 should be ~40-50 GBP, 512MB ~35 GBP).

I've got an old Sapphire 8400GS with 512MB which used to perform pretty decently till I decided to avoid nVidia and ATI till they released open source drivers. As long as you don't try to play Crysis or do some heavy video editing, it's a pretty decent buy at ~25 GBP.

**Future-proof**
You might want to consider the **AMD Radeon HD 4770** especially if you want a future-proof option, since it features cutting edge tech(40nm manufacturing, DDR5 memory) better than even that in high-end cards. It would have to be my first choice for the best budget card. It's also not very power hungry for such a powerful card.

It also works fairly well on Ubuntu with the Catalyst closed source drivers
[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=amd_hd4770&num=1](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=article&item=amd_hd4770&num=1)

Plus the rate of progress on the ATI open source drivers(not that great for the 4770 at the moment but bound to improve) should give 3D support soon.

The drivers are the only thing holding me back at present(I'm sticking with Intel GMA till AMD/nVidia go open source).

The price might be a little high though in the UK(last I checked in June it was retailing for ~70 GBP)
