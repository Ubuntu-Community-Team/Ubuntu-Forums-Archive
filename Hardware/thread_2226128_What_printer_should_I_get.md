### Post by squakie on 2014-05-25
Same here except with HP.  I don't think you can wrong as long as you do your homework ahead of time:  see if it's supported in Ubuntu before you buy it.  Ask here on the forums specifically about a given printer, whether it works in Ubuntu, if anyone is having any trouble, etc..  If it is mutli-function, be sure to include questions about all of the functions working as well.

I had an old Brother laser printer and it also worked out of the box with no problems - just as gifford has indicated above for their multi-function printer.

Just pick out a model or two, then post some very specific questions about it.  You can also do an internet search using something like this is the search string:

ubuntu 14.04  <brand and model of printer>

A lot of times you can find some great answwers that way - you just have to wade through things to find what you are looking for.

Good luck! ;)


You can also take a look at [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters). It shows a link for each manufacturer that will show other people's experiences.

### Post by QIII on 2014-05-26
In my experience (which does not by any means represent the entire community) HP printers of recent manufacture have proven to be a breeze to use.  I have two HP wireless AIO printers on my network and with HPLIP I simply found them on the network and installed the driver.  To set up scanning, I did have to temporarily connect them directly to my main machine via USB, but just had to disconnect them when I had the scanners set up.

What I do find a bit irksome about HP is the expense of the ink cartridges -- so don't forget that cost.
