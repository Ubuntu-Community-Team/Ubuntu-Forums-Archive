### Post by Ben Page on 2010-07-23
Celeron M 560 is better speed-wise, multitasking wise, and raw CPU power wise, but can get pretty hot really quickly (it doesn't loose speed that way, but fan can get loud on the Vostro this way). Plays HD flash an HD videos just fine (1280x720). Average office suite use battery life is 3,5Hrs - constant flash video playback drains the battery in 1,45Hrs.

Atom, on the other side, is very battery friendly, but weaker atoms can load firefox for whole 1,2 minutes! There are newer atoms and dualcore atoms that are better, but by my standards, Atom is a pure netbook CPU concentrated on low power consumption and low heat emission. Atoms are good at what they are meant to do, save battery and last circa 7-10hrs (4,5 flash video drain), but their performance is very poor in terms of CPU power. 1,2 minutes to load FF, up to 2 min to load MS office! And forget about editing of any kind. Playing HD flash will be choppy in fullscreen.

Celeron Ms also have twice as much of cache memory 1MB vs 500KB, which can significantly speed up your apps, like office suites and video/foto editing etc. 
The bottom line is that Atoms are crap for any task that need CPU power (flash demands CPU power), but are ideal for battery savings, I would never look at Atom CPU, Celerons are not gold either but can be considered as a decent CPUs at a price, can run moderate apps in moderate multitasking, even Ps and W7 64bit if you have at least 2GB RAM.

My vote goes to Celeron, although this topic is old, many people in 3rd world countries are having this dilemma, so I hope this helps someone. Stay clear from cheap Atoms if you want to actually use your PC's.

### Post by PresenceofMind on 2010-07-23
Hello!!!!

I use an Atom and I'm very impressed with its performance as a low-end processor. Despite its name, it actually turned out to be much more 'potent' than I thought. 

Celeron probably has better horsepower and the Atom may only have 1/6th the power of a Celeron, but for me....the Atom simply does the job. Note that Atom performs better with Linux than with Windows.

Gaming and HD-content (and Multimedia in general) used to be the downfall of the Atoms, but NVidia has introduced the Ion platform which allowed Atom processors to make some excellent progress in those fields. Dual-Core Atoms are even better... 

The only point I would like to make is this.......don't underestimate the Atoms.
