### Post by chaceos on 2011-09-13
I am experiencing the exact same problem on hp pavilion dv6000 series.
Can't find any workarounds.


NEW INFO April 7 2012

Ok, this is wierd, I never started a thread but it happened though the  thread kept the title that mentions a different HP series. But OK, all  well till now.
The problem is that I found a workaround that may help someone, but on a pavilion dv6000.

The whole story: After I started having problems with other keys as well ( f, g, f4 and more) I decided to buy a new keyboard.
Before I order it I wanted to check if I could be able to replace the  old one on my own. After having a very helpful how to tutorial  
(  [https://kcoppock.wordpress.com/2007/10/19/howto-replace-keyboard-on-laptop-computer-hp-dv6105/](https://kcoppock.wordpress.com/2007/10/19/howto-replace-keyboard-on-laptop-computer-hp-dv6105/)  ) by Kevin Coppock (THANKS) I wanted to try it just for the fun of  unscrewing. After everything went well before closing it I had the wierd  idea that it could do a difference if I unplug the keyboard from the  motherboard and plug it back again. It sounded stupid in my mind, (like  moving the wire of a sound system to get a better touch) but I also  thought that it could be a preparation for the real replacement. 

Well after I unplugged and plugged the keyboard, and closing the laptop  again, everything was working like a charm, the keys (f, g, F4 and more)  but also the media volume control touch buttons! I never ordered a new  keyboard. Now 3 months after the tweak, the touch buttons for the volume are again dead but everything else works like a charm.

Is this thread SOLVED? Surely not for a pavilion dv9000.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-12-12
When you say back "two or three releases", do you mean back in Ubuntu 8.10 days?

Asking because I have a tablet PC that is no longer running Linux because, back then, the media buttons worked -- but they have NOT worked with every Ubuntu version since 9.04.  Some changes were made to the underlying Linux kernel such that, after Ubuntu 8.10, it does not "see" the buttons anymore.

You could do a search for "fjbtndrv" (the fj button driver).  I have seen posts in the past from Favux where they talked about how to get THAT driver installed and working -- but I think that has been "broken" now for 11.10.
