### Post by TheFu on 2020-09-11
I've had issues with every non-USB microphone that I've attempted to use. Low levels, poor quality, always some problem.

I don't have a recommendation for a really cheap microphone, sorry, but I can recommend a middle-tier microphone - the Samson Go Mic. For the price, about US$35, is is a real value.  It has 3 modes, selectible through a switch on the side of the mic.  Omni, uni, and bi, directional.  It doesn't provide the deep warmth that a Yeti for 4x the price does, however.  Here's a review: [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AJAuhSNcGmY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AJAuhSNcGmY) I've had mine for years now. Always works well.

I'm assuming you want voice recordings. Not music. Not extremely high-quality for professional broadcasting.  The Samson is a value microphone for new podcasters and joining those meetings for work.  Often, a webcam will have a good enough built-in microphone for work meetings, BTW. A local LUG has weekly meetings and the best, no-hassle, webcams are Logitech C270 (US$40) and C920 Pro (US$90+) users. They have acceptable cameras and acceptable microphones.

All of these devices "just work" with Ubuntu. Plug them in and they are recognized, drivers are automatic, life is good.

Just depends on what you need.
