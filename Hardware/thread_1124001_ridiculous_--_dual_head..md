### Post by gnix on 2009-04-12
I have wanted to migrate to Linux for a long time.  This weekend I took the plunge and installed Ubuntu (for the second time, I tried a few months ago but had some sort of problem recognizing my external monitor).  

Installation went smoothly but I couldn't get it to extend my laptop screen onto my external monitor, as I do in Windows ("dual head").  I spent literally two entire days trying to rewrite my xorg.conf file and learning xrandr commands and now I'm giving up.  Two days!  In the process, I found dozens and dozens of sad stories on the web from other people trying to do the same thing who either succeeded (after some number of days) or failed and gave up like me.  There appears to be no standard solution (completely hardware specific) and no simple utility for auto-detecting/configuring ones hardware.

I *want* to use linux, really, I do.  I'm a computer nerd at heart -- I love emacs, I do a lot of perl hacking and am constantly in cygwin so I figured it was time.  But what the hell is wrong with the Linux community that they can't get relatively simple stuff like this to work smoothly?  I intend this post both as an angry gripe but also as a serious question.  Why is this so hard?  If it was 2001 I would understand that XWindows just wasn't ready yet, but I thought all these new distros were supposed to be user-friendly enough to compete for average users.

I was expecting Ubuntu to be nearly idiot-proof.  Everything looks very smooth, but if it's not possible for average users to make relatively simple adjustments to their hardware/peripherals then this won't get off the ground for a while yet.

Anyway, I'm mostly just mad at myself for being too stupid to figure this out.  If anyone wants to see the 65 xorg.conf files I tried I'll be happy to post them.  I bought a netbook with Ubuntu pre-installed that should arrive tomorrow.  I have my fingers crossed that the manufacturers configured the drivers perfectly out of the box because if I have to go through this nightmare again just to get the microphone working or some bullsh*t then I'm just plain finished with Linux.

PS - I tried this with Mint and Puppy as well, and had the same problem so this gripe is not really targeted at Ubuntu... but I thought Ubuntu was supposed to be the easy one for n00bs that "just worked".
