### Post by Therion on 2009-03-27
> **shateredsoul said:**
> ... I'm not sure about the mobo, but I think it's a Asus A7A266-E motherboard based on a question asked on a website.  
That would be a good thing to know for sure.  Reason being is that particular motherboard has a somewhat unusual memory-slot layout; with three slots for SDRAM and two for DDR.  

Your video card options, while limited to AGP, are still worth looking at.  On-the-Street-Performance-Wise there's really not that much difference between AGP 4X and AGP8X.  You're going to have a hard time finding an AGP video card on the shelf at a brick-and-mortar place though, so I hope you don't mind shopping online.

Just digging around on google I found this:
[http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/product?product=90829&lc=en&cc=us&dlc=en&lang=en&cc=us](http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/product?product=90829&lc=en&cc=us&dlc=en&lang=en&cc=us)

It appears it will hold a max of 1gb of ram (unless a bios update makes it capable of more), it already has a decent legacy Nvidia graphics chipset (GeForce4-MX420 64 MB DDR)in it, so that gForce 2 would be a downgrade. I'd recommend one of these: [http://shop.ebay.com/?_from=R40&_trksid=p3907.m38.l1313&_nkw=6600gt+agp&_sacat=See-All-Categories](http://shop.ebay.com/?_from=R40&_trksid=p3907.m38.l1313&_nkw=6600gt+agp&_sacat=See-All-Categories) While they are 8x, if memory serves they will step down to 4x so you should get max performance from your motherboard, and have a card that is an actual upgrade.

For what the video and ram limitations are, the Pentium 4 2.53 GHz / 533 cpu should perform very nicely. If you can get a cheap 6xxx series nvidia card (I like the 6600gt's) and another 512 mb of ram, you should have a very solid little daily internet machine.
