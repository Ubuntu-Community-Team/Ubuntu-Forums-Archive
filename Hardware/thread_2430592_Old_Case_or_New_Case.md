### Post by CatKiller on 2019-11-04
Whether it's adequate depends on how much heat you need to remove. 

At load my CPU is putting out some 100W and my GPU is putting out 250W, which is a lot of heat that needs to be shed. I would much rather do that with massive fans spinning relatively slowly than with small fans spinning fast and making a lot of noise. My fans only come on when there is heat to be removed; they are off the rest of the time. If you have more modest cooling needs, you don't need to move as much air.

Cases aren't that expensive as PC components go. If I wanted quieter or more cooling, or required a USB 3 port on the front, I'd go for a new case. If my cooling was fine and I was fine with USB 3 on the motherboard at the back, I probably wouldn't bother.
