### Post by Kevinator on 2010-03-24
Does it go through the shutdown proceedure or just turn off as if someone had pulled the plug? When people report spontaneous *reboots* I always suspect the power supply first. This seems to be one of the most common (yet not-so-widely known) points of failure for PC hardware. Spontaineous power-down could be that also, but it would be instant.

Shutting down in response to a hardware emergency such as a malfunctioning fan or overheating component is the other obvious possibility. I don't have any experience with this, but I think it would actually go through all the usual motions for shutting down, taking a minimum of a few seconds. If it gets "sluggish" first, that could be due to the CPU being clocked down in response to overheating.

It would help if you clarified your post. It's not clear exactly what happened with windows, whether or not disabling features fixed it, and if so what those features were.
