### Post by dvlchd3 on 2010-06-28
I'm not sure of the correct answer here.  I have always been under the theory that, you should fully charge a battery, let it completely discharge, then charge it again.  This supposedly helps prevent the battery from developing a memory, and increases the overall life of the battery.

On the other hand, I've heard people say that process is not good for batteries...Regardless, over time, batteries are going to develop a memory no matter what you do.

Either way, if your battery is only holding a two minute charge, you are probably at the point of no return.  To recover a battery from that state, you need professional equipment...If it really bothers you that much, you could always drop $100-$300 for a new one...:neutral:

It wont affect your computer in any way. However, it can affect battery capacity as you've discovered. From what I understand Lithium Ion batteries are not supposed to suffer from the "memory"" problem but I've never really believed that and I've heard other stories too. 

The now ancient NiCad's were notorius for that, you basically had to discharge them down and then fully charge up to keep them at top capacity, if not they would lose capacity over time. I'd suggest you try running on battery alone and discharging right down, then charge fully up and so on. Maybe you can recover some of that capacity..
