### Post by coffeecat on 2009-06-29
Canon are one of the manufacturers to avoid if you are running Linux. (The other is Lexmark. :wink: ) My Hewlett-Packard Officejet 6310 All-in-one works just fine with Ubuntu - printer and scanner. It's a network attached machine and it works just fine over the network too.

Generally speaking, HP devices work out of the box with Ubuntu. And you can download the hplip-gui frontend in Synaptic, which is useful for when you're working with a HP machine.

Be warned though that HP doesn't produce Linux drivers for all its machines, although it's probably the best manufacturer for Linux compatibility. Before you buy, check out your intended machine here...

[http://openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi](http://openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi)

... for printing, and here...

[http://www.sane-project.org/](http://www.sane-project.org/)

... for scanning. Unfortunately, the SANE website isn't as well-maintained, or as well laid-out, as the Openprinting one, so it's worth also checking the HP website (if you go for a HP machine that is).

By the way, if you need to do photoprinting, don't get an OfficeJet like mine. It's fine for everyday colour printing, but photoprinting is markedly inferior to my HP Deskjet machine. And I should imagine that a Photosmart machine would be better than a Deskjet - you can get all these series as multifunctions.
