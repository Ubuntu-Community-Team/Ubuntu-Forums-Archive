### Post by Autodave on 2018-07-25
My first thought would be the heat condition. Are fans turning as fast as they should? Any noise from them?  Try leaving the side off of the computer and see if that helps: that way it will get a lot more cooling.  (I *always* save any fans that I can for future use/testing.)  

My second thought would be the RAM failing: either from age or just from getting too hot from lack of ventilation.  Again, leaving the side panel off could alleviate some, if not all, of that problem.

Lastly, it could be the PSU.  Have you checked to see if it is full of dust?  I have saved more than one PSU by disassembling it and blowing the dirt out.

The DVD sounds like a different problem altogether. The fact that you cannot open it with a paper clip through the access hole tells me that something has broken or jammed.  Check you boot order and make sure that the DVD isn't in there before the HD: that *could *cause the error that you are getting.  After removing it from the boot order, disconnect it and try that way to see if the rest of the system is stable.  Again, I believe that this is a separate problem from the shutting down issue.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2018-07-28
None of the symptoms you mention make me suspect the PSU. PSU's tend to feel warm (like power adapters for laptops).
I would start with the CPU heatsink. If your CPU is giving high temp readings and the heatsink doesn't feel warm, then you should remove it, clean it (blow out dust and remove old thermal compound with rubbing alcohol) and then reattach it with quality thermal compound.
