### Post by Autodave on 2017-11-17
Since no one else has jumped in, let me tell you what I would start with. If this doesn't work, I have nothing else to offer, but it is worth a try and always the first thing that I do when I get some odd behavior on a laptop.

  	 	 	 	   1. Power down and disconnect power cord.
2. Unplug all peripherals.
3. Remove battery.
4. Wait 10 minutes.
5. Reinstall battery.
6. Connect power cord.
7. Boot computer and test to see if condition is gone.
8. If good so far, now you can reconnect peripherals.
