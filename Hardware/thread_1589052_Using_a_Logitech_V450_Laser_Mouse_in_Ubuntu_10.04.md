### Post by heroofhyrule on 2010-10-05
Hey everyone, some of you might be wondering how to use the Logitech v450 laser mouse in Ubuntu 10.04.  Here's the easiest and simplest way to do it.

Before we begin, a few notes:

[LIST=1]
[*]This particular mouse does not have out-of-the-box support; if you plug in the USB antenna, the entire computer will freeze.
[*]Double-check your model number (M/N) before you continue.  It can be found on a sticker inside of the battery compartment.  The V450 M/N is "M-RBR125."
[/LIST]
First, we are going to need to install HIDPoint.  Head over to [www.hidpoint.com]("http://www.hidpoint.com") and click install.  Once you have downloaded the .bin file, follow all of the installation instructions on HIDPoint's website; they are very, very clear, even for the novice Ubuntu user.

During the installation, you might get a warning stating that your kernel is unsupported.  Disregard this message and proceed with the installation.  Once the installation is complete, you might get another warning message about your kernel not being supported; once again, disregard this message.  

Plug your V450 into one of your USB ports and it should work instantly.

REMEMBER:  You must follow all of the instructions on HIDPoint's website.  They are very clear and lay out exactly how to install the software.  

POST-INSTALL NOTES:

[LIST]
[*]The scroll wheel works :)
[*]When actually utilizing the click button that the scroll wheel has, it will paste whatever text you have copied.
[*]The mouse's speed is super fast. :P
[/LIST]
