### Post by otakuj462 on 2010-06-21
Hi all,

After my HP Touchsmart tx2z has proven to be an epic fail, I am now on the hunt for a new laptop. The most important thing to me is that it guarantee a top-notch Ubuntu experience. To me this means flawless suspend/resume support and excellent battery life (at least 5 hours for intense usage). As far as specs are concerned, I'm looking for something high-end, in the range of 4GB RAM and Core i5/i7. The processor should have support for hardware virtualization, and I'd much prefer Intel over AMD. Discreet graphics are not required.

Another important thing is support. If the hardware fails, I'd be willing to pay a premium in order to talk to someone competent, and who won't balk at Ubuntu being installed on the hardware.

If anyone has any recommendations for something like this, in terms of success or failures, I'd greatly appreciate it if you could let me know.

Thanks,
