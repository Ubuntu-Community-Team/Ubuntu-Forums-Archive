### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-25
AFAIK, most mini-ITX boards with full sized PCIe slots will take a video card. Its fitting the card into mini-ITX cases thats a bigger issue. 

The mini-skeleton will take a optical drive. 

[http://www.tweaktown.com/reviews/2992/antec_mini_skeleton_90_open_air_mini_itx_case/index6.html](http://www.tweaktown.com/reviews/2992/antec_mini_skeleton_90_open_air_mini_itx_case/index6.html)

I havent seen a mini-skeleton, but someone I know has a skeleton (OK, apart from boneless brian, everyone I know has a skeleton, but only one person has a skeleton case). Its a dust magnet. :| 

For somethign really 'slick', have a look at this- 

[http://www.silverstonetek.com/products/p_photo.php?pno=lc09&area=usa](http://www.silverstonetek.com/products/p_photo.php?pno=lc09&area=usa)

That could have some issues with an fitting a standalone video card, and it (like the skeleton) only has a tiny, weenie DC/DC power supply. Which means that it will have an external AC/Dc power brick (something I'm not fond of). Also, to run some of the more power hungry CPUs, or even a video card, you would need to upgrade the DC/DC power supply. 

You could always look at the shuttle computers, they make some neat, slick little systems- 

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856101083](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856101083)

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856101092](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856101092)

Or even just a normal miniATX tower. Yeah, they doesnt look as small as the skeleton, but without the external power brick, its not going to take up that much more space- 

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811112219](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811112219)

Or a mini-ATX HTPC case- 

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811163158](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811163158)
