### Post by efflandt on 2012-07-05
Removing an internal battery or resetting the BIOS does not necessarily clear a BIOS password, especially for laptops.  Otherwise someone who steals a laptop, or whose business locks them out of CMOS setup, could easily get around that, which would totally negate the security of setting a BIOS password.

While desktops may have a way of resetting BIOS password with a motherboard switch or jumper, laptops or other portable and easily stolen computers often require sending it to the manufacturer to clear a BIOS password.  So don't forget it, or if buying a used computer, make sure that CMOS setup is not locked with a password (which could be a signed that it was stolen or the actual owner does not know it is being sold).
