### Post by dtset on 2010-04-11
Thanks to all - I think I've fixed the problem.  Short story: opening the case and resetting the BIOS permitted Ubuntu 9.10 to recognize the hard drive.  From there, it was a matter of partitioning, mounting, etc.  Oddly, the BIOS reset also fixed my ACPI problem: no longer do I have to insert acpi=off into grub2.  

-------------------------------------
The longer story is intended for those with similar problems in the future:

Resetting the BIOS should not be done lightly: for Buddha's sake, be sure to back up everything as you might bork your system.   I *had* to do this, since I couldn't locate the password to enter the BIOS.  With my motherboard's manual and some instructions on the internet, I was able to locate the jumper that would reset the password to the BIOS as well as the factory defaults.  (The other option involves unplugging the battery and leaving it out for an hour or so.)  

Resetting the BIOS caused me to lose sound in Vista, though not in Ubuntu oddly enough.  The sound drivers that Vista automatically installed didn't work.  So I had to install stuff from the sound card's CD.  But now I have some associated crapware on my PC that I'll have to deal with... Again, the point is that you should be prepared for the unexpected when messing with the BIOS.   (I am working with a back up PC, so none of that posed a real problem for me.)

Once I was able to recognize the hard drive, I used a number of links including this one:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/InstallingANewHardDrive](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/InstallingANewHardDrive)
