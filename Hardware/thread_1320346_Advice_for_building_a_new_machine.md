It give 246watts for your i5 build, with a 2x120mm fans and 1x80mm fan. I think that 400watts is fine. Mind you, I would probably get the 430watt seasonic M12-II myself. 

I'm not fond of the newer antec power supplies, they just arent as good as they used to be (dont take that as anti-antec, I'm still running an Antec 400watt from 2002 in one of my systems, and I bought an Antec Solo case for my last upgrade) 

If your still thiniing about a full-ATX case, I would consider a Silverstone PS02B-
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811163126](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811163126)

True, it doesnt have a power supply, and that will cost you more, but I have my reasons. 

*all following in IMO only, and is not hard fact by any measure* 

My philosophy with computer building is get a good PSU, case and motherboard. Why? Because you can keep a case for years, through many upgrades, so get a good one. PSU really matters, its probably the most critical component. If your 220/110 volt to 12/5/3.3 volt conversion isnt good, the chance of problems is increased, a lot. Even minor sags, etc., can have a major effect on stability and lifetime of the stuff in the box. Motherboards? Get a good one, cause its a right pain in the **** to change out motherboards. CPU, video card, ram, optical drive and hdd changes are easy, and fast. I think that virtually anyone could change a CPU, video card, and ram in a matter of minutes, and optical and hard drives only takes a tiny bit longer. Changing a motherboard is a 30 minute job, at least, and can take a lot longer. 

For myself, I would not be spending the majority of money on the CPU for just that reason. Its an easy upgrade, Intel and AMD keep marching on. While a $200 CPU is not a bad thing, it would be easier to get a lower-end CPU now and a really nice board, and plan on upgrading to a faster CPU in, say, 18-36 months. 

I dont know how possible that will be with Intel i5s right now though, for all we know Intel will change socket....again...or never release i5s that are much faster than current offerings. 

I would suggest an AM3 motherboard/CPU (or even AM2+ board and an AM2/AM2+ CPU, and upgrade to an AM3 CPU later- while it sounds crazy, yes, that is possible) for a better upgrade path but I might come across as an AMD fanboy. Opps, already typed, too late LMAO.
