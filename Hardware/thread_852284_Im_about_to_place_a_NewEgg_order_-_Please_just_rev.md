> SATA II Misnomer

The 3.0 Gbit/s specification has been widely referred to as "Serial ATA II" ("SATA II" or "SATA2"), contrary to the wishes of the Serial ATA International Organization (SATA-IO) which defines the standard. That power supply has the following connectors:
1x 20+4-Pin Main Connector
1x 4+4-Pin ATX12V/EPS12V Connector
1x 6-Pin PCI-E Connector
1x 6+2-pin PCI-E Connector
6x 4-Pin Peripheral Connector
4x 5-Pin SATA Connector
1x 4-Pin Floppy Connector 

This is looks sufficient to me.  You have enough sata and PCIe.  All the mother board connectors you need and then floppy if you ever want one as well as the old 4 pin molex connectors.  What you really want to make sure you have is enough SATA cables to connect the harddrives and such to the motherboard.
