### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-02-09
You say it powers up outside the case OK? And, you say it powers up with one stick of RAM OK?

If you have more than one stick, have you tested with each of the others individually, and do they all work?  Have you combined them, and then, do they still all work?

If everything works OK outside the case, one possible problem is grounding inside the case -- especially if there are old motherboard standoffs that are touching the board.

The other possibility is the power switch on the case and/or the power switch cable connection to the motherboard header pins -- but this would only be true if you are "shorting" the pins outside the case to turn on the motherboard.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-02-09
*Insists* is the operative word here.  It's possible that the turn-on transistor has failed.  Look for a video tutorial to help, but you might be able to power it on with a paperclip shorting black to green on the motherboard power connector.  I have two machines that I need to do this on, as over time the power-on transistors failed, but the machines run OK otherwise.
