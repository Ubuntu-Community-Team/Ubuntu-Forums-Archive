### Post by Mark_in_Hollywood on 2008-05-28
Maybe, depends on what is on your hard drive and what you want to access.

Try this:

from the panel, click Places/Computer/<name of your hard drive>, highlight the device. Right click and select MOUNT. If the device mounts, right click on the mounted device and open or browse it.

If you want to copy or cut&paste, that might require doing it under sudo, which means that the files moved/copied/transferred may need to have their permissions changed once they are on/in another device.
