### Post by tommygunner on 2009-02-06
I have 4 monitors on my system and two video cards. These are setup and working in Windows XP just fine with 3 of the monitors rotated 90degrees and the other monitor being used mostly for movie watching.

Doing a dual boot setup, I tried to do the same thing in Ubuntu. I had problems getting the video card drivers to work in Ubuntu, basically got locked out with a blackscreen.

I removed all of the nvidia drivers and installed one directly from Nvidia. By manually editing the xorg.conf I got two monitors working on Video card #1, along with the rotate on one.

However, the rotate does not seem to save after I change it in the Nvidia GUI tool. It stays rotated while logged in but when logged out, it goes away, and I have to rotate it again. The other monitor I have selected as a twin view one so it seems to stay upside down or at a 90degree angle even though the monitor is in normal position.

I haven't found any info on having multiple video cards in Ubuntu. I did see one post where someone got it working but it was using a vesa driver on the one and not an nvidia one.

I'm wondering what would happen if I install a restricted driver for the other video card. Maybe it would work considering that the other one was installed directly from Nvidia?

Most likely I'm just ranting and nobody else knows what do do either, but maybe someone does. 

BTW, Compiz isn't working for me either. The icons are white blocks. I have to shut down compiz using Compiz-switch or else I can't move windows around with the mouse. As a result, I ran compiz-check and got an error that the program can not continue because it has detected multiple video cards.
