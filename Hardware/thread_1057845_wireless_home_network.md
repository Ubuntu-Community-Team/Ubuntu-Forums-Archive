### Post by dkev on 2009-02-02
Look at your routers documentation. You have to log onto your router through your browser. From there you can set up your security and encryption. I highly recommend you do this as soon as possible. Any one can log onto your router and, at the very least, use your bandwidth. If you have a server, they can log onto it. Never run your router with out the security enabled.

No, that router should be setup for DHCP by default.  Just plug in your cable modem to the Internet port and reboot the modem.  AFter that plug in your PCs and all is good.

If you are going to do the wireless thing make sure you setup encryption (WPA or better).
