### Post by zipher on 2012-10-14
Hello,

Are you dual-booting or running this a Ubuntu-only machine?

Toshiba are famous for tweaking the specs on their machines so they work with a designated operating system, right down to a specific release of windows.

That said, the bluetooth stack and other drivers are fairly easy to obtain, either as binary files or .exe type, from the manufacturers repository.

I'm currently on a Toshiba project, with a much older machine though. What I have discovered is quite interesting but just seems to get more complicated as I go on.

There are quite a few people adapting Toshiba machines to open source (Try running a search on source forge) so you may get some machine-specific advice there.

But ...... and please don't take this the wrong way (I am talking with the lean and mean voice of frustration and Homer simpson style DOH).....
My honest opinion is, find a Toshiba licenced engineer and get a professional Windows reinstall. Minus the bloatware.  I mean dude, your laptop is current!

Once this is stable and running, try setting up a dual-boot with a non-bleeding edge release and have a play.
