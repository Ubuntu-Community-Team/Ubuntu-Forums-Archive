### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-05-21
Does it also use AMD's graphics, and if so, have you installed the proprietary driver from "Additional drivers"?

If you're using unity DE I think you can get to the driver application using the dash (top left icon).

---

### Post by asearle on 2013-05-21
Dear AJGREENY,

Many, many thanks:  This has fixed the problem!  All I did was a quick search on "Radeon" and came up with the Binary ATI-X.Org Drivers.  I installed these and then re-booted and, after a few moments where the screen seemed to re-jig itself the screen came up.  No special action (by me) was required.

Now I have a longer battery life, the fan runs less frequenctly, the case is much cooler and the system will wake up from sleep.

Many thanks for your help.

Rgards,
Alan
