### Post by Fafler on 2013-01-29
I have taken the board out of a broken 1st gen Atom netbook, think it was a Samsung NC20 or something. The board works great, and as it is quite low power and still able to run from battery, it seems it would be a good NFS root and iSCSI server for my other machines around the house, paired up with a 500 gb 2.5" drive. To speed it up a bit, i was thinking about b-cache, to get some of the benefits of a SSD without having to buy one big enough for iSCSI Windows images.

The board have two interfaces of interest: USB 2.0 and the SD card. So which is faster in a real world situation like this? A USB 3 thumb drive in the USB 2 port or a UHS-1 speed SD card? Read speed is the most important, as theres not going to be much writing anyway.

Alsa, if anyone has any first hands experience with b-cache, i would like to know. It's quite new and theres not much info out there.
