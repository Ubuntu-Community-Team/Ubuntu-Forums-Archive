### Post by newboon2 on 2012-06-23
I have an older model of that line (Toshiba NB555D with the C30) and when I first got it, suspend and hibernate did not work either, failing exactly in the manner as you described. That was under 10.04.

The machine was swapping out too much with the original 1 GB RAM so I picked up a 4 GB stick on sale and put it in, and a welcome side effect was that suspend and hibernate began working after that.

I'm too new to Linux to know if that makes any sense, but that was the sequence of events in my case and hibernate and suspend have continued to work through upgrades to 11.10 and now 12.04.
