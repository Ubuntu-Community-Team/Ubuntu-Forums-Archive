### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-08-04
I have a 365 module in my Dell laptop (Inspiron 1545) and with Ubuntu 10.04 LTS 32 bit it works fine.  I use bluetooth all the time to tether my cell phone.

What version of Ubuntu are you using?  I have booted 12.04 LTS I believe from live cd but don't recall if bluetooth was detected or not.  I suspect it was.

Are you sure bluetooth is enabled in your BIOS?  Restart the computer and hit F2 and check there.

Also, Dell laptops tend to enable/disable the WiFi card with the same key that enables/disables bluetooth.  On my Dell, Fn+F2 turns WiFI and Bluetooth ON and then both OFF.  I haven't found a way to enable only one and disable the other.

### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-08-04
My 1545 did not have an internal card, either, when I got it.  I bought the 365 card on eBay for about $20 USD shipped.  Mine was easy to install: pry off the top cover above the keyboard, plug it in, one screw to secure it.

You can always buy one of those cheap USB bluetooth dongles for about $1 shipped on eBay ($1 from Asia to US, anyway).  I use a couple of them for various projects and they work well in Ubuntu.  But for laptops I find them awkward and prefer an internal bluetooth card, even if it costs a little more.
