> **Kiyamudeen said:**
> I have a fingerprint reader on my HP Pavilion dv6-2170ee, and want to use it with Ubuntu 11.10. I haven't found any drivers/programs for it yet. My main need is for me to log in to Ubuntu with a swipe of a finger. Any drivers or programs would be appreciated.

Maybe you're better off with it not working.  Not too long ago I heard that fingerprint readers, even dedicated ones protecting doors to buildings, mis-read about one time out of ten.  That means they don't let an authorized person in 10% of the time.  It also means they might let an *unauthorized person* in 10% of the time.  A typed password is better, or consider a USB or Bluetooth security dongle.  The advantage of a Bluetooth device is that you wouldn't need to plug it in and it would only allow access to your computer while you're in visual range of your machine.  Of course, if you opt for the dongle solution, you'd need to have a typed password backup, so that if something were to happen the dongle, you'd still be able to log in. ;)
