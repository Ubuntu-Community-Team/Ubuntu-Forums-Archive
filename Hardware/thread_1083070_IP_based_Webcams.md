### Post by bmachia on 2009-03-02
I have two; one for the front of the house and another for the back.

My Aviosys IP-Kamera 9060-SL is fixed wide angle and fairly good in low light.  Depending on where you buy, it can cost between $60 and $80.  My backyard is covered by a Panasonic BL-C111A.  This camera can remotely pan, tilt and zoom.  Costs range between $110 & $200.

Both of these camera’s can be setup with motion areas and will email you a picture if it senses movement.  I’ve found this very informative; you might be surprise who walks around your yard when you are not home. 

Both of these cameras are directly connected to my network via RJ45.  I tried the 802.11G stuff, but kept having drop outs and never knew when the camera would be on the network or not.

Over the years I also bought a Very expensive D-Link security cam.  It lasted about a year, until just after the Warranty period expired, and died with a hardware problem.  D-Link does not have a repair depot, so when their camera’s die, that’s it.

Hope this helps.

Bill
