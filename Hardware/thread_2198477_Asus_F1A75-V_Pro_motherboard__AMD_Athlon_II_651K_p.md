### Post by QIII on 2014-01-08
Hello!

That CPU and motherboard are both electronically and *physically *incompatible.  Not only will that CPU not work with the electronics on that motherboard, it will not *physically *fit in the the socket.

The CPU, if of recent manufacture, will be made to fit an AM3 socket, but the socket on the motherboard is FM1.

Don't buy that motherboard.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2014-01-09
The Athlon II 651K is on the supported CPU list, but it needs an updated BIOS. [http://support.asus.com/Cpusupport/List.aspx?SLanguage=en&m=F1A75-V%20PRO&p=1&s=41](http://support.asus.com/Cpusupport/List.aspx?SLanguage=en&m=F1A75-V%20PRO&p=1&s=41)
If the mobo you're getting has an older BIOS, you may be screwed, or you may be lucky enough to get it to boot far enough where you can update the BIOS.

As far as the components of the mobo, I don't see anything that would cause a problem in Linux/Ubuntu.
