### Post by aqua_scummm on 2011-01-26
I have a Gateway LT2106u.  It came preinstalled with Win7, and now I have the drive partitioned about in half, with Xubuntu 10.04 on the other side.

Xubuntu does get noticeably better battery life.  I assume it's just because it has fewer background processes and services running (it's also noticeably faster).  Not a huge amount, maybe an extra half hour of use, or 45 minutes of dim-screened standby (such as torrent downloading).  Speed is significantly better just in the number of Chromium tabs I can have open.

Ubuntu still doesn't do everything I need (which is why Windows is still there - Netflix is a major contender in this fiasco), but it does enough where 80% of my time is spent in a faster, lighterweight Xubuntu (compared to Windows 7, which I do love).

And get the bigger battery, it's always worth it IMO.  I got the larger battery for my laptop, even though it's usually plugged in, the few times I haven't had power (plane rides, car rides, etc), the longer battery has proven its worth.
