### Post by markbuntu on 2008-12-28
I recently changed to that amd 6000 X2 cpu and can offer a few observations. It is dirt cheap, 1/3 the price of a phenom wich has more processors but is slower.

It is wickedly fast compared to my previous 3800+ single core. 

I run Hardy 32 and 64 bit and Intrepid 64 and Mandriva 2009 586 and 64 bit OS is far faster than 32 bit even with 32 bit apps running with 32 bit libs on the 64 system.

If your interest is games and graphics, a good gpu matched up with that cpu will be all you need. Get a 6000 and you can also get a good gpu for the same price you would pay for comparable intel. Then you can offload the graphics processing from the cpu and that makes a huge difference in rendering etc.
