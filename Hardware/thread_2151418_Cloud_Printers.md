### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-06-04
Depends on what you mean by a "cloud" printer.  I've been around long enough (recently retired) to know that the "cloud" is nothing more than the new "word" for an offsite network. 

So one question is that of printing stuff you have stored in a "cloud". This can mean either getting the item to a printer also connected to that cloud; or pulling it out down to your local PC or network and using a printer connected to your local PC or network.

HP's latest round of all-in-ones and other printers offer something similar known a e-Print.  This allows you to send a document (typically from your smart phone) to your home-networked HP printer by assigning it an IP address that is in HP's "cloud".  So basically, you transmit your document to their cloud which sends it to your printer using an IP address administered by HP.

I'm sure there are other schemes as well.  I personally don't use ANY of them because it means sending my PERSONAL documents to someone else's network.
