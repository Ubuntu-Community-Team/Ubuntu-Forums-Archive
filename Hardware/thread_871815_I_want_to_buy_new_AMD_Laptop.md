### Post by argosreality on 2008-07-27
Is there a particular reason you want to stick with an AMD CPU? No offense intended since Im actually running this on an AMD based laptop (HP dv6745us) but at this point the Intel Core 2 Duo's are both faster and generally better battery life for laptops as well. You'll also often get purely Intel based components (video and wireless) which are quite a bit easier to get working. I'd imagine thats why most suppliers don't use AMD components in Linux based systems. AMD means you either get ATI graphics (which still require binary, proprietary drivers for the best capabilities) as well as often times crappy wireless (Atheros and broadcom Im looking at you)

### Post by kejava on 2008-08-02
I just had a nice discussion about this subject in the #ubuntu-us-pa channel.  So far, the only place I've found that sells amd-based laptops with ubuntu pre-installed is at [Linux Certified in the US]("http://www.linuxcertified.com/linux-laptop-lc2464dc.html").  And it looks like they're trying to get rid of them.

I don't know of any in the UK.  After looking around on [tuxmobile.org]("http://tuxmobil.org/cpu_64bit.html") (64-bit amd), I have to agree with argosreality about the sucky hardware that comes with amd-based laptops.  I didn't look at them all but [Fujitsu-Siemens laptop]("http://antti-juhani.kaijanaho.fi/fujitsu-siemens-amilo-pa-1510.html") looked like it had great hardware for Linux (w/the exception of the Atheros chipset).

My recent interest in finding a laptop with an AMD cpu is partly because I've heard that the open source ATI Radeon drivers are working quite well, even the 3d stuff :)  If you go with an Intel-based laptop, your options for video chipsets are Intel and Nvidia.  Intel isn't bad but if you want something higher end, you're stuck with Nvidia.  So far, all I've seen are bad things happening in Ubuntu when using nvidia cards (open and closed driver).
