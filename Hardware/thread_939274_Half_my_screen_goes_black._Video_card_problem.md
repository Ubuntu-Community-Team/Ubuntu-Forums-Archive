### Post by patrickballeux on 2008-10-05
Have you checked your video cable, to make sure that it is not loose?

But as the previous comment stated, you probably have a problem in your monitor.  Could be time for a brand new LCD 22" 16/9 display... :)


No driver/software can fix this.  Or it could be your graphic card overheating.

When this is happening, shutdown your computer, and let it cool for a while (15,30,60 minutes).  If it works after cooling, then you have an overheating problem.  If that does not seem to help, that it could be a mechanical problem (cracked PCB, loose cable,etc...)

Good luck!

### Post by patrickballeux on 2008-10-06
Yep, it could or simply check that there is good ventilation inside your PC.  Sometimes dust can accumulate in there.

Unplug your PC, and clean-up inside with a small vacuum cleaner (carefull) or with a towel.  Make sure that all the fans are working also.  Sometimes, they fail and we don't know about it until it blows up :)

That would explain why it happens more in WoW since there is more power used when the game is running.

Another explanation could be also that your power supply may be dying slowly, not giving enough power to your PC.  A simple test is to unplug everyting, keeping screen,mouse and keyboard only.  If the problem is less frequent or disappear, then you could have a power problem.  Another power supply (400W minimum) is around 30$...

Good luck!
