Ubuntu 16.04LTS, Logitech Trackman Vista T-CG10, Samsung 4k 40" TV as monitor

Is there a way within Ubuntu to change the mouse scaling?  To be clear, I am not talking re mouse speed which is set in System Settings>Mouse and Touchpad.

On a 1280 x 1024 pixel screen, slowly rolling the ball in the X direction from one extreme X to the other, it takes about 2-1/3 “rolls” to get the cursor to move between X extremes of the screen.  On a 3840 x 2160 pixel screen (4K), slowly rolling the ball in the X direction from one extreme to the other, it takes about 6-1/4 “rolls” to get the cursor to move between the X extremes.  

The ratio, 2.3/1, ie, 2.3 rolls of the ball to traverse from one side of the screen to the other, is optimal.  However,  the ratio of 6.3/1, ie 6.3 rolls in the X direction for the cursor to move from one side to the other, experienced on the 4K screen, is annoying and diminishes the beauty of the 4K environment.  Making changes to mouse speed with the 4K screen sort of helps, but really is woefully inadequate.  A web search surprisingly does not produce suggestions regarding this 4K issue.

Note:  I am not getting responses under Desktop Environments so posted here also as can be thought of as a hardware issue.
