### Post by kubug on 2009-11-13
I finally got rid of that crappy BCM4311 Broadcom Wireless card in my Dell Laptop. I replaced it with an Intel 4965AGN. 

I can't even begin to describe the differences.

I had high hopes for Karmic to finally fix the kernel panics that the Broadcom drivers used to give me. But alas, it did the same things. In fact, it almost ruined my install because whenever it hard locked (flashing CAPS lock and NUM lock LEDs) it would corrupt system files. 

I used to spend a lot of time fixing these files later on and I was just getting tired of it. All this is behind me finally and my laptop is working like a dream for me.

What is interesting is that I didn't go out to buy a new Intel card, rather I asked one of my co-workers if he was willing to swap his wireless card with mine (sucker!). I figured that the drivers under Windows must be better. But he called today and said that the Broadcom card keeps dropping connections, doesn't have near the range and it seems to have issues with hidden WPA2 networks. Hmmm... maybe it's not all a driver issue. 

I guess I will be going out and buying another one of the Intel cards to replace *his* Broadcom. But at ~$50 they are not a high price for what you get.

And I realise that replacing hardware is not always the solution to get things working (and may get frowned upon in here), but I tell you, currently you couldn't convince me otherwise.
