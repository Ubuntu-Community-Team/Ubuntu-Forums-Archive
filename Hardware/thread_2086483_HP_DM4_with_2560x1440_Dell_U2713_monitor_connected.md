### Post by tomtom12 on 2012-11-21
Google did not help me to find out if I could connect a new Dell U2713 monitor with a display resolution of 2550 x 1440 pixels to my HP DM4 1150ed laptop. This laptop, now on 12.04,  has Intel graphics only, HD3000, and no display port, only HDMI.

When I first connected, the monitor would not go any higher than 1920x1080. But the good news is, after some I found out what to do, with the help of these posts:

[LIST]
[*][https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Config/Resolution/](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Config/Resolution/)
[*][http://askubuntu.com/questions/63681/how-can-i-make-xrandr-customization-permanent](http://askubuntu.com/questions/63681/how-can-i-make-xrandr-customization-permanent)  (post of John Roberts)
[/LIST]
What did not work, even though I purchased an expensive (€20) HDMI 1.4 cable, is to connect on 60Hz. 

I experimented with commands like cvt 2560 1440 30  and xrandr. The solution for me was:


**Solution**


1. I created a new file  /usr/share/dell_2713_display_video_resolution.sh with contents:

> xrandr --newmode "2560x1440_30.00"  146.25  2560 2680 2944 3328  1440 1443 1448 1468 -hsync +vsync
xrandr --addmode HDMI1 2560x1440_30.00(used gksudo gedit)
And made the file executable.


2. I added  to the bottom of /etc/lightdm/lightdm.conf

# for your desktop session; add another resolution
session-setup-script=/usr/share/dell_2713_display_video_resolution.sh

That was all.  Restarted the system and in the Settings for Display the new 2550x1440 resolution became visible... and selectable.

[B]Conclusion

[/B]Do I miss the 30Hz versus 60Hz?  No, not really. I do not play games. Also I do not play video's other than Youtube, but what I see is that it works ok for me. Of course the Dell U2713 is like the U2711 a sublime monitor with a rock solid picture and great colors. Highly recommend for Ubuntu!

So, if someone tells you that you can not control higher than 1920x1200 over an HDMI cable from a HD3000 chip... well..this is simply not true. You can! With half the frequencey, sure, but good enough for many purposes.  It works great... typing this text on that screen now ;) :P
