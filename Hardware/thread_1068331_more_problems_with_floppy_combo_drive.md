### Post by mtbsoft on 2009-02-12
Wow, it's a long time since I saw one of those.

I only ever used mine under Windows, quite a few years ago, so can't remember much.  However, if I remember correctly, changing which plug on the floppy cable (first or second) was plugged into the drive would determine which of the drives was seen as a: or b:.  Try swapping over the cable and see if it sees the 3.5" drive.  

From what I recall it was necessary, under Windows, to load a special driver to enable using both drives without swapping the cable about.  Certainly your system will need the bios set to permit multiple floppies.
