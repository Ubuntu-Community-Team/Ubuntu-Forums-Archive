### Post by Ghost|BTFH on 2011-08-24
#1: The case/PSU combo won't work - the PSU is underpowered.  I'd go with an 80+ bronze certified PSU, myself - it really helps reduce the power consumption.  Just find something decent to put one in.  I'd consider [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817371033](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817371033) - a nice Antec for about $40.

#2: The APU isn't bad from the test results I've seen, but since you're only looking at GIMP as a major video resource hog, it shouldn't be that bad.

#3: Go with an Asus board, something like [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813131764](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813131764) - it's worth the extra money to have something that's reliable and solid performance.

#4: For a hard drive, I'd go WD Caviar Black series - they're reliable, excellent warranty and not horribly priced.  Can't go wrong there. [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136284](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136284) - $89 and you won't be crying in a year or two about it dying out on you and wiping all your data - been there, done that.

Now, this will jack up the cost about $100 or so, but if you want a disposable system you won't really be happy with, go the other route.  If you want something that'll actually last you five years, the above modifications would be my suggestion.

Hope that helps.

Cheers,
Ghost|BTFH
