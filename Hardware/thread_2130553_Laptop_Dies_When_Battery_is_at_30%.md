### Post by lordnemo on 2013-03-29
I'm running Ubuntu 12.04 on an Acer Aspire 4730z. i recently installed a new battery (following the recomended instructions on the Acer website) and everything functions normally except when the battery runs down to approximately 30% it immediately dies and refuses to boot until i plug it in. After plugging it in it will boot and charge normally until i return to battery power and discharge it to 30% again. Any ideas on how to fix it?

---

### Post by tgalati4 on 2013-03-29
That might be normal.  If you are running Gnome, the battery manager needs several (perhaps 20) charge/discharge cycles to calibrate its battery life estimator.  Look in the BIOS for a battery level for emergency shutoff.  Set it from 15% to 30%.  It's possible that you have a defective battery or one that is made of cat food.
