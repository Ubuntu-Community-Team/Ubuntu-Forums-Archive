### Post by Ceza on 2009-03-10
I don't think your Video can play WoW that well to begin with ( no offense intended at all please ) but your best chance is with DirectX. When I moved to Kubuntu and played WoW with my Nvidia card I had to move to OpenGl and that dropped my frame rate in half. Intel isn't much for good drivers to begin with and I'm not sure if they have much/any OpenGl support but you can probably expect that any card that is weak in DirectX will fail in OpenGl. Meaning it may work but will probably drop off to be below what you would consider playable.

A link that might help you to find out for sure: [http://intellinuxgraphics.org/download.html](http://intellinuxgraphics.org/download.html)

Sorry I can't pursue it with you but I don't have that chipset and I myself am to new to know otherwise. My guess is you will need a discrete card.

### Post by Chemical Imbalance on 2009-03-10
Yeah, that integrated chipset from Intel (basically any chipset from Intel) is not meant for any gaming.  You can play some light games and maybe a few more advanced ones (like WOW) maybe on the lowest settings if you're lucky.

Good luck though.  Oh, and yes Intel is fully supported in linux and there are no proprietary drivers that you need for Intel chipsets (intel does not make cards, just integrated solutions)

Like the OP above, I recommend you get a card, like an Nvidia 8XXX series or above.
