### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2021-01-19
> **daniell59 said:**
> I was wondering, how much would it cost me to buy a graphics card that would be equal in performance to the APU on the CPU?

Unfortunately, AMD doesn't make any discrete GPU's that are equivalent to APU performance/features while still being reasonable in terms of power/noise/price. The RX550 would probably be the closest thing from AMD, though it lags behind the Vega graphics on the APU a bit in terms of features (no VP9 decode acceleration).

The closest thing from Nvidia is probably the GT1030. The drawbacks there are use of proprietary driver and poor support for VA-API, which browsers use for decode accel.

I'd personally stick with the graphics on the APU unless you're a gamer. I found the graphics on my 2400G to be better than a GTX950 I own when using Linux. The GTX950 has more raw 3D power, but I'm not enough of a gamer to take advantage of it.

### Post by CelticWarrior on 2021-01-23
AMD graphics is currently easier because its drivers are open-source and are always installed by default, whereas with Nvidia you'd need to select the option to install third-party drivers, etc. in order to automatically install the Nvidia proprietary drivers, or install them later. The end result in either case is the same.

It's not a matter of one being more or less compatible.
