### Post by slickymaster on 2013-10-01
Try the following command at a terminal, with a disk in the drive:```
sudo mount /dev/sr0 /cdrom
```If you still get the same error message, the most likely reason is a faulty drive. Unfortunately dvd/cd drives are a lot more fragile than most people realise - a piece of fluff or a scratch on the lens can render them useless. Even worse, laptop drives that have the lens on the carriage that is ejected from the device leave them very susceptible to physical damage.
