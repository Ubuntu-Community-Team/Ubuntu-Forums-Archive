### Post by #&amp;thj^% on 2024-02-05
I wish I had your hardware in front of me, but i don't.
So now I warn use this at your own risk On ASUS laptops.
You can also at boot into a TTY session to undo this change:

Be Aware this is something off the top of my head, ok now run:

```
sudo nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf.d/00-keyboard.conf
```
Add this to that file:
```
Section "InputClass"
        Identifier "Asus WMI hotkeys"
        MatchProduct "Asus WMI hotkeys"
        Option "Ignore" "on"
EndSection
```

If you also have Nvidia GPU don't forget to add those lines to
"/etc/X11/nvidia-xorg.conf.d/00-keyboard.conf"
Reboot and Good Luck
