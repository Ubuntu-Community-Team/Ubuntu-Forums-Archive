### Post by Vinddraken on 2010-05-16
I got a temporary solution, google for cpu frequency scaling ubuntu (hopefully the steps will work in Zorin) when finished you can set your CPU Frequency at different levels (maximum will be 2.0ghz) and then you will have setting going down until 800mhz i think. If you are going to surf the internet, watch a movie and so on that don't need a powerful computer then you can have on the lowest settings, this will slow down the computer and save battery time, but as i said this will slow the computer down, but using it to play simple games, watch a movie, surfing the internet will still work and also your battery time might go back to the same level as with windows or even longer.
