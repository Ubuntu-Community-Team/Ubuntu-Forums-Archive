### Post by rg4w on 2012-02-28
You probably won't save much buying Windows-free, since the Windows machines are often sold in such volume that the economy of scale usually more than offsets the OEM Windows license cost. 

Also, trying to go Win-free means having a relatively small subset of systems available, making shopping harder than it needs to be.

But there are other reasons to go Win-free, not the least of which is to simply deny Microsoft your money. ;)

In addition to the other good vendors listed above, you might also check out rjtech.com, which carries a good selection of laptop bare-bones.

And for complete systems you may want to look at ZaReason.com, and LinuCity.com, both exclusively Linux.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-02-29
Regarding MicroCenter, last time I checked, they offered "bare bones" desktops but not laptops.

It's unlikely any laptop seller is going to be willing to drop their price to sell you one without Windows on it -- as they pay practically nothing for Windows in the first place.

Also, a bigger problem you will face is that laptops are infamouse for customized hardware -- which requires cutomized drivers -- which are often NOT available in Linux.

So, anything you DO buy, be certain to check into any return policy they have so that, in worst case, if it simply won't work well with Linux, at least you haven't lost all the money you paid for it.
