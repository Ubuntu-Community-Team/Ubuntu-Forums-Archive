### Post by dsm iv tr on 2009-04-01
I'm happy with my AspireOne A110, from Acer (120GB HDD version).

I get about 7 hours with the 6-cell battery (costs about $30 more, but worth it) after tweaking the kernel a little. Ubuntu installs flawlessly, there's a couple issues with sound and wifi that I fixed by compiling ALSA and ath5k myself to fix, but they may indeed be fixed by Jaunty/9.04's release.

All in, I paid about $360 CDN or so. 8.9", keyboard is big enough for me to type normally on (certainly better than the eee's, imho), and the performance is excellent for a netbook.

Check [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne) or my cleaned-up guide for 8.10 on the AA1 at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AA1](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AA1) .

I understand your dislike for Best Buy, but this is one case where they're useful - you can go and try out the keyboards on various netbooks to help you decide if they're comfortable for you.

Good luck!
