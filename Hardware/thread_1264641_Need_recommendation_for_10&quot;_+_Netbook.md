### Post by brallan on 2009-09-28
As always in linux, a lot depends on what your time is worth to you and how much you want to fiddle with it or if you want it to JUST WORK.

I bought the linux version of the eeepc 901 and though I was amazed at the price, I was horrified to learn that it had a horrible interface (xandros) that it wasn't compatible with 8.04, and even as of karmic there are still lots of issues for lots of peple with the eeepcs. I've had to deal with the idiosyncracies of eeebuntu, easy peasy, crunch-eee, getting the array kernel to work with a vanilla version, the annoying interface of UNR, bad processor use and battery time, flaky wifi and webcam issues - I probably spent enough time tweaking this over the past year and a half to have paid for something double the price with ubuntu installed. If you want something that just works, and works just like your desktop only with a small screen, you should look into another hardware company than ASUS. I will certainly pay more next time to know that the manufacturer is going to use hardware with free & open source drivers.

along these lines, the dell mini 10 with ubuntu preinstalled looks pretty enticing, especially since you can get optional GPS.  

Don't believe the hype about the advantages of SD drives. they may be faster but the disadvantages of having to worry about space and having the chip welded onto the board are quite serious. my 4gb drive failed right away and gave me hell ever since, since i couldn't even disable it in the BIOS.

all that said, the netbooks are indeed a wonderful thing and have wonderful prices. make sure your keyboard is comfy (most are NOT) and your screen has a minimum resolution of 1000x600. I have also been held back quite often by the 3 usb ports on the ASUS, so certainly don't get less.
