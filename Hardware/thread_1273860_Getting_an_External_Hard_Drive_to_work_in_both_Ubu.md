### Post by Shelbsterina on 2009-09-23
I run Ubuntu Hardy on my desktop at home, and I have an 80 GB external hard drive, which I used Gparted to format to Fat32, under the impression that I would be able to access the files on my hard drive in both my Ubuntu box, and a friend's laptop running Vista. While I have absolutely no problems with my HD in Ubuntu, Vista refuses to recognize it. When I plug it in, I hear the cute little sound Vista has to notify you of a new device being connected, and the same when I unplug it. In the taskbar, there is an option to "Safely Remove Hardware", and my hard drive is actually listed by name in Vista's Disk Management Tool, but it doesn't show up in "My Computer" at all. Clearly, Vista knows it's there, but doesn't want to do anything about it. I'm not looking to reformat my hard drive, I just want to know if there's something I'm overlooking or not doing correctly to get this working.

---

### Post by chris1950 on 2009-09-23
What are the permissions set at for the ext HD?? Try 777

---

### Post by Shelbsterina on 2009-09-23
Oh jeez, false alarm. I just went into disk manager and assigned it a drive letter, now it's up in "My Computer" just fine. I feel dumb now. But thank you, Chris 1950, for your reply. I was getting to test out your suggestion when I discovered the problem.
