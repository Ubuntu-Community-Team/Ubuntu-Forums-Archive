### Post by kurt18947 on 2011-10-09
HP is probably the most Linux friendly printer manufacturer. HP also provides a GUI printer administration app (hplip-gui) I don't have an HP laser so have no recommendations.  Another manufacturer that is pretty good and pretty inexpensive is Brother.  I have 2 Brother networked multi-functions and have gotten them to work easily with Brother's drivers and instructions.  Here's a link to Brother's Linux driver page, you can check supported models there:
[http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html)
There have been people on this forum that have had trouble installing Brother printers.  I have not, in fact my MFC-7820 is in the foomatic data base so it pretty much installs itself. I did find it worthwhile to install the brother cups wrapper driver from Synaptic and use that.  The Brother CUPS wrapper is much faster on second and subsequent pages than the default drivers.

Cheers

Hi

I've used lots of different HP printers with network cards and haven't had a single one which didn't work.  Occasionally they have problems with large images which power-cycling the printer seems to always fix.  The one next to me is CP1515n, a colour laser, which works very well, but isn't especially cheap.

If you're just printing black and white and don't need a new one, I'd look for a refurbished HP 4000N series, which are a bit big but very robust, or a 2000N series; one sees these in the £50 range on Ebay.  If you get one with a network adaptor there's no driver, and it's great not to have another cable.

Look for suffix N (for networked) and D (duplex) is nice too to save paper.

I've haven't had much experience with USB printers and Ubuntu, but the last one I tried was an HP Photosmart and it worked without me touching a thing after plugging it in to Ubuntu 10.04 on a laptop.  (Admittedly much to my surprise.)  Cartridges painfully priced however.

Hope that's of some use.

Jonathan.
