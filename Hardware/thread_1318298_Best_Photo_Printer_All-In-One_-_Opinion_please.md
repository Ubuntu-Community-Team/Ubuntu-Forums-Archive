### Post by dh003i on 2009-11-10
The [HP printers generally have great Linux support]("http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/supported_devices/index.html"). 

I'm currently looking at the HP OfficeJet 8500 and HP PhotoSmart Premier c309a, both of which are fully supported. The OfficeJet is more document & efficiency oriented (it beats out many laser-jets for TCO). The PhotoSmart is aimed at photo-applications, and has better photo-quality & also can print to printable CD's or DVD's.

The Epson Artisan 800 is supposed to be a multifunction, even more photo-oriented than the HP Photosmart c309a. [But I'm not sure about it's Linux support]("http://www.linuxprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Epson-Artisan_800").

### Post by coffeecat on 2009-11-10
There are basically three Hewlett-Packard ranges: Officejet, Deskjet, and Photosmart. The quality of photo-printing rises in that order. You can get multifunctions in all three types.

Beware: my networked Officejet 6310 is an excellent machine, works flawlessly with Linux, but the photo-printing (using the same cartridges as my Deskjet :? ) is disappointing. And I've tried it with Ubuntu, Windows and MacOS - the same. But my (usb-connected) Deskjet puts out good quality (good enough for me) photo prints even on generic photo paper.

If you want photo quality, go for a Deskjet or, better, a Photosmart.
