### Post by TheFu on 2023-07-18
If you stick with printers that support "driverless printing", then you should be ok.  Google that term for a list.

You cannot assuming the entire priinter line will work with Linux. Each specific model has to be researched.  Some HP models do work well with Linux/Unix, but those tend to be the non-cheap ones.  Also, it is best to avoid ink, since ink is priced higher than platinum these days.

For example, I bought an inkjet and a laser printer at in 2008.  Over that time, both devices still work. They were both $70 new.  I've purchased 2 laser toner cartridges at $35 each and I'm still using the 2nd one.  The ink for the other printer is $35 per cartridge, but they dry out annually, so a new one **must** be bought - that's over $400 just for ink, 4x more.  It truly is a situation of pay me now or pay me much more later.

I'd avoid HP because the company has been doing some anti-consumer stuff, especially with their printers, buy requiring internet so the printer can phone home about your printing.  Do you really want that?

If you are going to print lots of pages, then a laser printer makes 10x more sense.

BTW, the all-in-one I have is a brother MFC-240C from 2008.  I wouldn't buy it again today, because it doesn't support driverless scanning or driverless printing. If I have to download anything from anywhere that isn't in a core Canonical repo, then that's a failure and I don't want it.
