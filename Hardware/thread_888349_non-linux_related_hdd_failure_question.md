### Post by linux_tech on 2008-08-13
You should always backup your data; hard drives often fail.

HD clicking is known issue to Western Digital. Most of the time this is an 
indication the drive is going bad. You can run diagnostics on the drive. 
Western Digital has lifeguard diagnostics available on their web site. If the drive fails the diagnostics and is still under warranty it then you can RMA it to them for a replacement.  

I had a western digital that made loud clicking noises for about 2 weeks
then suddenly stopped making the noises.  The drive is still working,
I'm using it now.  I don't worry about a drive going, my data is backed up 
I have 2 spare hard drives already formatted  that can be installed in 5 minutes.  For my OS and apps I use ghost imaging, so if I have a drive fail I  just install the drive, transfer my most recent image to the drive, restore my data and I'm back in business.

For power supply testers I use the Ultra power supply tester.  It works great.
