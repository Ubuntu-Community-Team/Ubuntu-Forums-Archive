### Post by GreyGeek on 2009-05-19
I also own a Sony VAIO VGN-FW140E.

Ubuntu has never worked on my Sony.  It can't configure my video or my Intel 1500 wireless chip.  BUT .....

I found that Kubuntu 9.04 (Jaunty Jackalope) installs and runs perfectly on it.  Everything works!!!!   Accelerated video (3D), sound, USB, DVDROM, Webcam, ... everything.  

Try the Kubuntu 9.04 LiveCD and see what I mean.   My Sony came with VISTA Home Premium installed, but a couple months ago I switched from dual booting it with Kubuntu to giving Kubuntu 100% of the HD.   I run a licensed copy of XP in VirtualBox.

I also used the methods shown on the MediBuntu website to get multimedia working the way I want it to.

I also upgraded KDE4 to KDE 4.2.3 and it works beautifully.  Nothing has crashed on me.  Kubuntu/KDE4.2.3 is rock solid and fast.

The SecondLife 1.22.11 Linux client works beautifully as well.

Stellarium, KStars, TuxRacer, FlightSim and many 3D graphical apps work well, but the ones which require a high end graphics driver (TUCS) stutter because this Sony only has the GM45 midrange video chip.
