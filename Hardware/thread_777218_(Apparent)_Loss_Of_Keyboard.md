### Post by ekred on 2008-05-01
I just went through something that drove me mad so I decided to share it here so somebody else can save the hours I've lost on that!

So I'm working on my 1 week old installation of Ubuntu 8.04 Hardy when all of a sudden I entirely loose my keyboard! My mouse still works but not a single key on my laptop is working. Even the volume knob is gone. Hmmm... what's going on? I try to logout, my keyboard works at the login prompt... great. I login... keyboard gone again. WTF!?!

Let's completely reboot the machine... same thing. 

/*INSERT A COUPLE OF HOURS OF HAIR PULLING HERE*/

What's the problem? **Assistive Technologies!!!**

In fact, my keyboard was working, but the option "Slow Keys" was on so it was waiting for me to hold every single key like 2 minutes before actually considering it!

How to turn this off?
**System -> Preferences -> Keyboard -> Accessibility tab -> Uncheck "Slow Keys"**

Now, how did that checked itself? I never intentionally checked it... Well my guess would be the first option in that same window "*Allow to turn accessibility features on and off from the keyboard*" I must have pressed the wrong combination of keys and boom, I did something I shouldn't have done. I other words, I suggest your turn this one off as well!

Maybe someone can tell me how a keyboard can toggle accessiblity features on and off?
