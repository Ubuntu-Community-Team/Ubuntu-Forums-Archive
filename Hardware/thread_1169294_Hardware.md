### Post by akash222 on 2009-05-25
A [personal computer]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_computer") is made up of multiple physical components of **computer hardware**, upon which can be installed an [operating system]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_system") and a multitude of [software]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software") to perform the operator's desired functions.

Though a PC comes in many different form factors, a typical [personal computer]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_computer") consists of a [case]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_case") or chassis in a tower shape (desktop) and the following parts:
 
 **[[edit]("http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Personal_computer_hardware&action=edit&section=2")] Motherboard**

 The motherboard is the "body"[*[citation needed]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed")*] of the computer. Components directly attached to the motherboard include:
 
[LIST]
[*]The **[central processing unit]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_processing_unit") (**CPU**)** performs most of the calculations which enable a computer to function, and is sometimes referred to as the "brain" of the computer. It is usually [cooled]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_cooling#Spot_cooling") by a heat sink and fan.
[*]The **[chipset]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chipset")** mediates communication between the CPU and the other components of the system, including main memory.
[*]**[RAM]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAM")** Stores all running processes (applications) and the current running OS. RAM Stands for Random Access Memory
[*]The **[BIOS]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BIOS")** includes boot [firmware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware") and power management. The **B**asic **I**nput **O**utput **S**ystem tasks are handled by [operating system]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_system") drivers.
[*]**Internal [Buses]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_bus")** connect the CPU to various internal components and to expansion cards for graphics and sound.
[/LIST]
