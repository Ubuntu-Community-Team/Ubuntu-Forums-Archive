### Post by Xorlium on 2010-06-20
Hello!

Can anyone recommend a good ebook reader (like Kindle or Nook or Kobo or...) that satisfies all the following properties:
1- Works with a PC using Linux.
2- Can read at least PDF, txt, html, djvu
3- Has sound capability (like ogg, mp3, etc)
4- Embraces the open-source spirit. I want to be able to change whatever I want without worrying about DRM or other things. For example, if I could install fbreader or other software, that would solve many problems.
5- Has a large (preferably) screen size and adjustable fonts.
6- e-ink, no LCD. I don't want my eyes to hurt.
7- Sells in Canada (so no Nook).

Thanks!

### Post by SartoriusGenuflectus on 2012-04-07
I have a Kobo Touch. Its good for reading, looking up definitions and searching for word occurrences - all things I expect from an ereader.
It doesnt do sound, but it can view pdfs and Html (in fact it as a web browser as a non-supported extra). It was recognised by my PC running Ubuntu 8.04 when I connected via USB and I was able to copy all files from the Kobo onto my PC.
Its most useful feature, so I thought, was the ability to highlight text and write notes. However, having made many such annotations in all the ebooks I have read, I find the mechanics of accessing those notes very laborious on the Kobo. Also there is no facility for extracting those notes into a more useful medium such as a word processor. 
I am currently having a major problem in that most of by ebooks have got corrupted and the Kobo will no longer open them. The help desk recommends deleting the ebook and reloading. That is fine for getting back to reading but in the process the annotations are lost for ever.
P.S. I think its made in Canada.
