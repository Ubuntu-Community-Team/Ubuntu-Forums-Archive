### Post by saunders on 2008-10-09
I've got an Acer Aspire One, my girlfriend has an Eee 701.

I've found pro's and con's to both. (I know you're thinking about an Eee901+, but this may still be of interest)

The Acer Aspire has a better keyboard for typing, it's just that bit bigger than the 701. I tend to find myself accidentally hitting other keys on the Eee.

The screen size on the 701 means that you're constantly scrolling sideways on webpages to see the whole page. I think the 901 has a bigger screen. The One doesn't have this problem.

I've put Hardy on both, and both have had their ram maxed. The One is slightly more prone to 'greying out' than the 701, especially with FF3. Not sure what the problem is there, may be flash related. I now tend to use Epiphany on the One. Both have pretty good boot times in my experience, about 40 sec. All the programs I've tried run well on both (apart from the FF3 issue on the One).

Had no problems installing Hardy on either. The Eee has it's own Eeebuntu distro now, and there's one in the pipeline for the One (OneLinux?). Wireless works fine with both (had to install MadWifi and WICD for the One). Battery life for both is good - the 701 beats the One, but the One is nowhere near as bad as some reviews have made out.

I stayed away from the hard disc memory as I don't want it breaking if I give it too much of a knock. I've got about 4Gb spare on the One (from 8Gb) which I find plenty.

I'm guessing the 901 will be better than the One, but then it costs a bit more...!
