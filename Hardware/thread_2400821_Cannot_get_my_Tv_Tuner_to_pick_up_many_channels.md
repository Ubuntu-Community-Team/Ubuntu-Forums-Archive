### Post by TheFu on 2018-09-10
Go here: [http://tvfool.com/index.php?option=com_wrapper&Itemid=29](http://tvfool.com/index.php?option=com_wrapper&Itemid=29)
and enter your street address an the altitude for the antenna.  

If you are on the north side of the building, then you can't get Austin stations without placing the antenna south facing and as high as possible.  If there is a building blocking that view, buy CATV or Netflix.

I guessed an address in Round Rock and got this [http://www.tvfool.com/?option=com_wrapper&Itemid=29&q=id%3d9038454c54c53a](http://www.tvfool.com/?option=com_wrapper&Itemid=29&q=id%3d9038454c54c53a) which says you need to point the antenna SSW towards the transmission towers 15 miles away.

Using rabbit ears, you'll only get Fox which is on VHF channel 7.  All the other major stations are UHF which is what your leaf-like antenna should pick up.  Forget the channel numbers that they advertise. Those don't matter at all, it is the frequency that they broadcast on that matters.

Univision is NW, so you should be able to get that from the north side of a building.
PBS is on UHF-46 almost due north, but I suspect an unpowered antenna wouldn't pick it up.

If you'd like more info, I have a long blog article about antennas and how important the right antenna, facing the right direction, at the highest altitude is.  I was getting 19 stations initially with the pole antenna that came with the USB ATSC tuner, but by moving higher and with a better antenna, now get over 90 channels.
