### Post by phidia on 2008-06-16
I purchased a HP Photosmart C5180, new and on sale; it's a discontinued model. I did research on this all-in-one HP and according to the data at openprinting.org it works "[perfectly]("http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=HP-PhotoSmart_C5180")".
I have the correct driver installed and the printer sets up fine and it does print but the quality of the prints-specifically photo printing is quite poor. 

I did a test by scanning in a photo and then printing out that scanned image. Admittedly doing that has to result in a lower resolution print. However using a G5 iMac with this exact same picture and printer resulted in an print that was hard to tell the difference from the original while even reducing the size of the print to 2x3", in ubuntu resulted in a noticeably pixelated and really unacceptable print.

I have checked and rechecked settings. I even installed the hplip-gui. I didn't find much help out there on improving resolution on this model series for linux so I wonder if good enough in linux (I'm sure it's not just a ubuntu issue) is sort of subpar for other computing platforms.

If anyone has this printer or series working for printing out photos in linux let me know how you achieved that please-thanks.
