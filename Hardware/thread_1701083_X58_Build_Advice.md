### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2011-03-06
If audio doesn't work right, you can always get a good discrete card: The Xonar DG works with ALSA 1.0.24 and it has very good sound quality: [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16829132020](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16829132020)
$20 after mail-in rebate. I have one on the way and I'm going to put it up against my aging M-Audio Revo 5.1 to see which one I like better.

Personally, I'd go with the Gigabyte as it has eSATA and a PATA option, as well as both PS/2 ports, and I have a personal fondness for gigabyte boards/BIOS when dealing with intel chipsets. Asus is just too hit and miss for me.
