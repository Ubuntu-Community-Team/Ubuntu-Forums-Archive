### Post by nixscripter on 2008-10-06
I've installed linux on an older G4, and I found it to be as reliable and stable as advertised. However, a few things did annoy me.

1. Wireless was a pain to get working. I do have an old thread you might try if you are interested.

2. Binary-only packages not compiled from source by Ubuntu aren't very well supported. This includes things like the Adobe Flash plugin and other people who have a "linux version" as a precompiled x86 binary.

3. The track pad tap and "scroll edges" act differently than on the Mac interface. As an ex-Mac person, I found this so annoying, I had to hack a script to disable them.

4. No second and third mouse button. In GNOME, that drove me crazy at first. There is a configurable kernel option to set a keybaord key (which can be changed at runtime). I believe the default is F11 = middle click and F12 = right click.

I would suggest trying a live CD first and make sure these and other things don't bother you too much. Good luck!

ChromeKaldra - I think you probably have an Intel Macbook Pro in which case you could use either the i386 (32-bit) or AMD64 (64-bit) versions of Ubuntu we all use on PC's.

What nixscripter was describing was his experience on a G4, which was a PowerPC chip, and an entirely different architecture. 

You experience will not bet the same as his, and will be more like what you would experience on a PC.

Just wanted to clarify.
