### Post by Daeboy on 2009-05-19
You more than likely need to edit your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file. I would suggest backing it up first, but when you look at it, scroll 'til you see something like this...:
```
Section "Screen"
        Identifier      "Default Screen"
        Device          "Intel Corporation Mobile 945GM/GMS, 943/940GML Express$
        Monitor         "Generic Monitor"
        DefaultDepth    24
        SubSection "Display"
                Modes           "1280x800"
        EndSubSection
EndSection
```

That part by "Modes" contains your resolutions. Try adding it up there and see what happens. ;) [Oh, you'll have to change the resolution via the normal method though. This just adds that resolution to the list]. Hope it works!
