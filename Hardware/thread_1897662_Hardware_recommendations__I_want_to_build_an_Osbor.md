### Post by 1clue on 2011-12-19
Hey everyone. 

I want a box that isn't quite a laptop, and can't exactly BE a laptop. I'm thinking of the old first-generation luggable from a company called Osborne, only with new hardware.

I'm interested in hearing what hardware anyone knows of, or even a form factor to search for. 

I'm after decent hardware from decent manufacturers, but money definitely factors in here. I can't swing modern server hardware, and noise would be an issue. I want it quiet. 

My original concept came from looking at a Mac Mini with an i7. But that's not quite enough. 

Here's what I have in mind: 

[LIST=1]
[*]I have a pair of ViewSonic 22" LED monitors with 1920x1080 resolution. 
[*]I want a good quality dual or triple-head video card with good Linux support. 
[*]I want to make a transportable/luggable system that doesn't have batteries, but which can be pulled out of a case and booted up in a minute. 
[*]I want a small footprint motherboard with a 6+ core processor (probably AMD) with support for more than 12G RAM and decent KVM support. 
[*]At least 2 high quality gigabit ethernet ports. Built-in wireless N would be neat too, but not necessary. 
[*]USB3 
[*]One or two solid state drives. 
[*]The ability to add sata/sas drives, preferably external and removable.
[/LIST]

I'm going to put together some sort of folding framework for the monitors, so I can just pull the framework out of the bag, throw it on the desk and unfold it to put everything in working order. 

I'm thinking of a CPU case that can lay flat or possibly a couple small cases, one for the CPU and one for the cards. Or whatever. I'm thinking of just mounting the CPU case in a bag and have ventilation in and out of the bag, so I don't have to pull everything out. I'm willing to put in extra fans, but I also definitely want a washable air filter. 

Worst case would be a 1U server case, but I'd like to hope it could be something smaller. 

I think it would best go into a large backpack style case. The monitors sandwiched together won't quite fit into a 17" laptop case.
