### Post by sudodus on 2013-08-01
USB sticks are not 100% standardized. 

- "All of them" work in Windows (they are made and tested for Windows) unless they are bricked.

- "Almost all of them" work in Linux as storage devices.

- Most of them work as boot devices (that you can boot an operating system from them).

I have an old 1 GB Kingston pendrive that works in Linux and is bootable. I have Sandisk and Transcend USB 2 and USB 3 pendrives that all work as storage devices in Linux and are bootable.

See these links for more details about pendrives.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick)
