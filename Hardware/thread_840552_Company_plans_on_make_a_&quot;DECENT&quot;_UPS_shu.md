### Post by wpshooter on 2008-06-25
Please let me first say that this is not an advertisement for and/or endorsement of the Tripp Lite UPS manufacturer.  To my knowledge I have no finacial interest in the Tripp Lite company.  And if some other company wants to do the same thing that Tripp Lite is current working on, then the more "**power**" to them.  

In talking to one of the Tripp Lite technical representatives this week, I discovered that they currently have a **GUI** interfaced shutdown software that will run natively on a Ubuntu/Linux O/S computer thru a serial port interface.  

He said that this software will allow you to do the same type of functions, i.e. set a timed graceful computer shutdown after an AC power loss (and other functions) thru a GUI just like those that are available on most other Windows based UPS shutdown software packages.

And now here is the **really good news**.  He went on to tell me, that Tripp Lite currently has under development the same type of UPS shutdown software that will be able to work thru a **USB** interface.  He said that this software is expected to be released in the next 3 to 6 months.

I certainly hope what he told me is true.  And as I told him, that whatever company is first to come to market with this type of shutdown software that will run natively on Ubuntu/Linux is going to sell a whole slue of UPS hardware product (and he said that they knew that and that's what they were planning on doing).  Because unless I am wrong, this is one of the things that will really solve a big minus point against using Ubuntu as your O/S.

Therefore you might want to keep this in mind and monitor it's progress.

It will be great to have a UPS that you can count on to do a decent timed-set graceful shutdown of your computer in case of power loss without having to be a Linux programmer.

Thanks.
