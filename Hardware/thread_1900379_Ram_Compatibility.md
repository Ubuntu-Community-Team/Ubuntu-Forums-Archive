### Post by 2F4U on 2011-12-26
As far as I know, you can overclock only all RAM chips together, but not selectively. If you do nothing, all RAM chips will be operated on the speed of the slowest chips, so that the fastest chips would operate at a lower speed. This is usually not a problem. Overclocking, however, could be a problem. Not every chip is acting the same way and you need to do a lot of testing to find the right settings. Overclocking can cause instability and even harm your system.
