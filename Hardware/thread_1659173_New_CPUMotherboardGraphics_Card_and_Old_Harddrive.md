### Post by Fafler on 2011-01-04
Linux is very well suited for booting on any hardware you want. It doesn't really keep a list of drivers it needs, like Windows does. Instead it probes and configures everything during boot.

You could also put the drive in a USB enclosure. That would give you a nice external drive the minute you are finished playing around with the old installation.
