Hi.

Sorry for my language. I got to excited... I will pay more attention to that manner.

As for what happend, apparently I do have a very small sized SSD in addition to the drive that came with the computer. So the computer came with SSD, which is physically separate from the one I had. I always thought that when they write HDD+SSD drive they mean to some kind of combination between the two, that there is one drive that is hybrid and have to modules that work together. 

But I was wrong.

HDD+SSD means two different cards. One in the bay (the HDD) and one on the motherboard (the SSD). mine was 24GB Sandisk. Just a bare card hooked up to the motherboard.

If it might solve someone else's problem in the future...

### Post by efflandt on 2017-06-14
The SSD card on the motherboard is probably either mSATA or M.2 depending upon age of the laptop. I had an MSI gaming laptop from 2013 that in all spec's did not even mention mSATA and it actually had (2) mSATA slots in it. I had added a 512 GB mSATA for Ubuntu, but the laptop no longer turns on properly, so I am currently running Ubuntu 16.10 on my PC from that mSATA SSD card in a SATA adapter.
