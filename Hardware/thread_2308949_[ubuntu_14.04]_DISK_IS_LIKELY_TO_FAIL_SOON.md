### Post by weatherman2 on 2016-01-06
Not only buy a new hard disk but try to save the information on your hard drive before it fails completely.  That would be my biggest worry.  As your hard drive continues to fail, eventually it could fail completely and you may lose everything on it.

If you don't care about any of the files on your hard drive now, just buy a replacement and re-install everything.

### Post by weatherman2 on 2016-01-07
Hard drives fail all the time.  They are complex devices that are both electrical and mechanical, with constantly moving parts.  They simply aren't that reliable.  At some point they fail in various ways.  It's like a car engine that eventually wears out; some may last longer than others but eventually they'll all start to break down.

What's happening to your particular hard drive?  I'd guess, with so many bad sectors, that the magnetic surface has begun to fail.

It's extremely important to make regular backups of your data for several reasons, but a failing hard drive is one of the biggest reasons.
