Heya!

I'm considering getting myself a netbook, for the sole purpose of running Linux, and being an absolute beginner, I thought I'd start with Ubuntu.

I am, however, a bit divided on exactly which Netbook to purchase, I have been looking at several models, the ever popular Asus EEE, the MSI Wind, the Samsung N350, but I have yet to find something which makes me truly sure about my purchase.

So, here I am.

Mainly, what I'm looking for could be summarized in these criterias:


[LIST]
[*]Processor - An Intel Atom, or equal, preferably dual-core.
[*]RAM - At least 1GB & Upgradeable to at least 2.
[*]A screen no bigger than 10.1"
[*]A sleek design. Don't get me wrong, I'm not looking for a Mac, but I am so sick of these standard "corporate grey plastic"-machines.
[*]Ubuntu compatibility - Well, duh... It'd be neat to have everything compatible "out-of-the-box", as I'd prefer not to get any extra peripherals (such as USB-network cards, wireless adapters etc)
[*]Shinies! - Any extra, cool feature that isn't inhibiting is always a plus, but not really a requirement.
[*]Batter life - What good is a laptop if it dies after an hour of use?
[/LIST]

What I'm looking to do with this computer is mainly Internet work, writing, possibly running some rudimentary games, or taking up notepad-style coding (if I can ever figure out how). No big things really.

Any help or recommendations are greatly appreciated.

Thanks in advance!

I just got an Acer AspireOne D255 and I'm very impressed by it so far. :)

1 GB of ram but I'm pretty sure you can add more. 250 GB hard drive. dual core intel atom processor. 6 cell lithium ion battery (lasts quite a while, especially if you're not on the internet!)
