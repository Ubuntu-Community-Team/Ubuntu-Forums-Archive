### Post by 3Miro on 2011-07-26
Hi, we had a discussion on the testimonial forum and I should have looked at your thread earlier.

When you ask a question like that, the odds of getting an answer are slim. Look at how many people have looked at your thread. The only way one of them could have answered is if they owned such a camera. It would have been better for you to put the camera model in the thread title. A good thread title should help you with popular hardware like video cards or CPU, however, it may still be insufficient for something like a special model of a camera.

Google is your best friend. Here are couple of links that I found in couple of minutes:

[http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Hardware-HOWTO/](http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Hardware-HOWTO/)

And if you go to "Camera" the you go to gPhoto (g is for Gnome, which is best supported under Ubuntu). Then you get the list of compatible cameras:

[http://www.gphoto.org/proj/libgphoto2/support.php](http://www.gphoto.org/proj/libgphoto2/support.php)

Good luck finding a good camera.
