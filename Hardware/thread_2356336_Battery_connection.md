### Post by Autodave on 2017-03-22
My first thought is that you have a battery that needs replacing. However, before doing that, try one thing: charge the battery fully (computer powered off and wait until the charging indicator tells you that it is fully charged) and then make sure computer is off and that power cord is unplugged, and remove the battery. Wait ten minutes and reinstall the battery. Power up the machine. Use as normal and see what happens.

A lot of times when something weird goes on with laptops, most can be repaired by simply powering everything completely off, removing battery for 10 minutes, and restarting.
