### Post by cascade9 on 2009-10-08
Depends on how much you want to spend. With PCs you can go over $10,000 U.S. if you really want. Thats going to the point of stupid though, I would consider a custom spec computer of $2500-3000 US to be the last sane point in 'high end'. 

Also, what you want to do with it- Since you are getting everything from scratch, you can build in performance in areas you use and not worry about areas you dont use. 

E.g. if you are planning on running compiz, or play lots of games etc, you will want a bigger video card than if you are going to be running without, and spend the money you saved on faster hard drives, and/or faster ram and CPU. 

Or a much nicer speaker setup (which is good for those of us that play a lot of music), or monitor. I see a lot of computer speakers and monitors that have outlived 2-3 boxxen, which is a point worth thinking about as well. 

But even low-spec computers these days run things like ubuntu very very well. There is a point of diminishing returns, once you go past that you spend lots more $$$ for not much difference at all. (That starts somewhere about $2000 US IMO, but thats just my version)
