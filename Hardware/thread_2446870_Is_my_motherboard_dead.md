sometimes they work but not good enough or stable enough.

still i think in this case it does look like the motherboard is faulty.

to test PSU, plug in the CPU , cooler and fan and get some power to it. if fan does not spin then the PSU is at fault. if it spins, then motherboard is likely at fault. but oyu can keep adding individual components testing each as you go.

to confirm ram you would have to plug it to another PC. 

you can also check capacitors on motherboard if any of them are bulging it would be a bad sign. also if you can see in PSU, sometimes capacitors there are bad. 

i had strange boot behaviour, but once i change the PSU it all worked. after a while computer was acting funny. so either there was poor driver support of it was dying.

To be accurate, you have to check the voltages while the unit is under a load.  A voltmeter tells you voltage but does not tell you how much wattage is available.
