### Post by markbuntu on 2008-10-05
There are some good $100 mobos with decent nvidia and ati built ins that will give you far better performance than any $25 plugin. Intel is also very open source friendly but their chipsets are sort of plain vanilla stuff. 

But if you plan on using this machine for a while, go ati. They are very committed to the linux community now and are fully supporting the open source effort with both money and information. They are now in a catch up phase so, while they are currenlty lagging behind nvidia, they are making rapid progress with both the proprietary and open source drivers. Their current goal is to have the proprietary linux drivers fully equivalent with the windows drivers by early next year. Progress in the open source drivers is strictly limited by the open source devs resources, ati is doing all it can to speed them along.

ATI is working on crossfire support for igp and pci card combinations for linux drivers so expect that by the time you can afford a real improved graphics card.
