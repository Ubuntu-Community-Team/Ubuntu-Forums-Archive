### Post by cpbl on 2010-10-06
I just installed Ubuntu 10.10 on my laptop, next to 10.04. After the install, my wired ethernet does not work. Not under 10.10, not under the old OS / older kernels, not even when booting from a USB. The ethernet cable works fine on another machine. It looks like there is no connection anymore on my laptop. The contacts all look good, but something seems disconnected.

The only weird thing I did was to start a "battery calibration" at some point from the BIOS when I was switching boot devices to the USB. I aborted this calibration during the discharge phase (stupid thing didn't tell me how long it would take... I think it runs the battery flat!).  

Is it possible that something in firmware has been turned off to disable the ethernet port?

Any ideas for what to test / how to diagnose further??

This would likely be the end of my laptop, I guess, if I can't fix it. (Wireless works fine still, incidentally, but I need the wired connection to be able to access the machine at work from home...)

Thanks!

### Post by cpbl on 2010-10-06
Well, an obvious test (?) was to go through the BIOS' battery calibration and wait it out. I did that, and upon rebooting, the wired network works again. 
Spurious?  I had tried a lot of reboots, etc with no luck, before this. 

So, it's solved, I guess. I'd love to hear from someone who can corroborate the theory.
