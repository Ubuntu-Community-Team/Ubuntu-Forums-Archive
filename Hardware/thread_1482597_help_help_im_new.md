### Post by arkham on 2010-05-13
Hi Mike,

The wiki is often a good place to start for this type of thing, followed by a search for similar questions previously on the forums.  For example, on the wiki you will find this pretty comprehensive guide to mounting and unmounting external USB hard drives:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Mount/USB]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Mount/USB")

Also, as an aside, there are no more "drive letters" in Linux - that is a purely Windows convention.  Everything in Linux is just mounted under a single root filesystem, regardless of how many physical drives there are.  While this can initially seem confusing, it allows you to place drives in more meaningful places than "E" and "Z", though you could certainly use /Z and /E if you really wanted to :)
