### Post by Sorivenul on 2008-09-29
Well, yes and no.

You *can* build a laptop from "scratch", but it's more or less the same as a barebones model.  You can search on eBay or whereever you wish to find old laptop cases.  eBay seems to have a multitude of empty Dells.  

The problem with doing it from "scratch" versus barebones is that the barebones already has *some* things in place for you, allowing you to easily upgrade the parts.  With a scratch system, if you leave something out or something is assembled incorrectly, you run the risk of owning a dead machine.

If you really want to do this, my suggestion is the barebones route to get a better working understanding of the innards of laptop systems.  I've disassembled many, and still would not attempt a "from scratch" laptop.  This is much the same way as many Linux journeys on the software end.  Many start with Ubuntu because it provides a lot of the basics out of the box and lets you customize, and then branch out to thinks like Slackware, Arch, and LFS as they learn more.  What it comes down to is baby steps.

Good luck!
