### Post by GodBane on 2009-11-05
Take a paperclip and short the GREEN and BLACK wire on the 20/24 pin cable.
There should only be one green wire - the power-on switch - and a handful of blacks - they're grounds. I'm 99% sure you can't be electrocuted doing this as the green wire doesn't pull much.

What this does is tells the PSU to turn on, it will then spin up it's fan(s). Odds are if the fan(s) do(es) not turn on, the PSU is dead. If they do turn on, then the PSU could still be sort of fried, or it could be other components.
