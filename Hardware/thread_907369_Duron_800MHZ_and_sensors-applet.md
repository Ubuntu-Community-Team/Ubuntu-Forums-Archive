### Post by dublinfireman on 2008-09-01
If you run your mouse over each of the temperatures, it will tell you which device that specific temperature is for.  You can also right-click any of the temperatures, go to preference and change them to Fahrenheit if you wish.  Red is bad, if it's red, it's way too hot.

### Post by dublinfireman on 2008-09-01
Temp 1 is most likely your M/B temp and temp 2 your CPU temp.  In the sensors tab, this is how my "temps" are in order.  I didn't change the order when I installed the applet.  I had the same problem you had, my hard drive was actually getting too hot, until I got my fan, the only solution I had was to take the side of the case off and point a box fan towards it to cool it down.
