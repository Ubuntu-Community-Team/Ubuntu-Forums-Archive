### Post by TheFu on 2014-07-01
I have not.  The 8hrs means a new Celeron CPU. Those are usually placed in chromebooks, so lower resolution. Thought I found one with a Dell 11 inch over Xmas, but it had a touch screen (which I hate too).

So .... nope. Haven't found what you want.  I ended up settling on a chromebook C720, but it fails on many levels too - no wired ethernet, terrible keyboard layout, picky touchpad drivers (I have to manually rebuild them every time a new kernel is loaded).  RAM is soldered in - no upgrades and the SSD had to be swapped, but only a funny, tiny, M2 form is supported. I almost always carry a 500G external HDD because of the limited (128G) storage.  So my netbook has become a did-I-bring-the-extra-stuff-boook.

OTOH, it is relatively fast, has great battery.

Oh ... and what screen size can you live with?  That is really where all laptop specs start.

> **VivaTaiwan said:**
> Thanks for you reply. This doesn't sound very good. If I shouldn't be able to find anything usable, I'll just buy the next Macbook Pro and run it with OS X.

Lots of people I know (20+) run Linux on their Apple notebooks.  I understand that Apple makes great hardware, but folks aren't so happy with the last OSX update.  Something more to consider.

I've had the same Dell 15" for 4+ yrs. It is the best laptop that I've ever owned. However, when it is time to get on an airplane, I take the 11in chromebook. I do miss vertical resolution, but compared to the system it replaced, it has almost 200px more.  For most laptops, 1080p is the top resolution for now.  That drives me crazy since in 2006, I had a 1440p (4x3) laptop from Dell. Then those damn TV marketing people took over.  Once 4K-TV gets popular, we'll have higher resolution laptops again.

BTW, the performance between the C720 chromebook (Celeron Haskell CPU) and Dell Core i5 laptop (3-4 yr old 1st-gen) both running 14.04 LTS - is about the same. The comparison really isn't fair, but an about 2lb C720 ($211) vs 5.5lb Dell ($850) means less backache from airport lug-time.

So, if high resolution really is the primary need for you (which I can completely understand), the Apple Retina offerings are worth considering at [https://www.apple.com/macbook-air/](https://www.apple.com/macbook-air/) - let me know if you learn how to remove itunes from OSX. ;(  I just can't see spending $1400 for a laptop when remoting into a $400 server elsewhere will still blow the laptop away on performance.
