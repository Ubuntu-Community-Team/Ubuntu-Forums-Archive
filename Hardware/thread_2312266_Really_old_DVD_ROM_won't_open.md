### Post by KayeNg on 2016-02-03
Hi guys.  I connected an old DVD ROM to my desktop computer.  When I press the eject button, the light blinks but it won't open.  

Months ago I revived an old desktop computer by installing Lubuntu in it via the DVD ROM drive (I had a live CD).  It wouldn't open either, but all I had to do was straighten a paper clip and insert it thru the hole in the front of the dvd rom, and push it in while pressing the eject button.  That's how I opened it and eventually installed Lubuntu in that old desktop.

This time though, poking it with paper clip won't cause the tray to open.  Any ideas?

### Post by J_Me on 2016-02-03
You should only be using the paper clip when the computer is powered off, there's a lot of moving parts which can get stuck.[br]1[/br] [br]1[/br] This video may be a bit boring but worth watching as it explains what happens inside the drive. [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=axP8LfkPvF0](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=axP8LfkPvF0)  I suspect the tray hasn't fully closed which will affect the paper clip hole trick. Try this, first power off the pc then press your thumb down across the whole face of the tray, hopefully you should get a tiny bit of movement now press paper clip in the hole. Repeat process until you can pull the tray out with your fingers.[br]1[/br]  In future if you use the drive again give the tray an extra little shove when it's closing this will help it settle in place but TBH just buy a new drive.
