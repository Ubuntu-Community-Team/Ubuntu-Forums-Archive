### Post by cymbaline42 on 2011-04-30
I can vouch for a Thinkpad.  I own a R61i for just over three years now.

They are very sturdy machines.  I dropped mine screen facing down from four feet onto a wooden floor and it survived without a scratch (not recommending you try this, of course).  Not counting how many times I have bumped my laptop against hard surfaces in the bus, trains, corners of walls etc.  

Since your budget goes up to $1,000, I would recommend getting something with a decent graphics card (something that I rue now since I wanted to save some money and got only an integrated graphics card).  Seeing how Ubuntu is going in the direction of Unity and Gnome 3 already out, I think it is good to have a decent graphics card to support hardware acceleration.  Also useful for playing games and video editing etc.
