### Post by eigenman on 2010-07-20
Hi,

I recently got annoyed at the slow resume from hibernation on ubuntu (on my HP Pavilion dv2, with no program aside from evince running, it used to take few minutes to resume from hibernation), and after a quick search, decided to give uswsusp a try. This seems far superior to the default option. Not only hibernation and resuming from hibernation are much more reasonable, the suspend to ram option solves the ati problem with the mouse cursor as well. So, my question is, are there any well known draw back to uswsusp? Are there people who experienced a worst experience with uswsusp compared to the default option? If not, maybe it is worth considering this as the default hibernation procedure. 

I must admit that I make this suggestion with a lot of hesitation, since suspend and hibernation has been a lot of pain for the ubuntu community, and it seems that it has finally stabalized to some extent. However, at this point, on my laptop at least, the hibernation is completely useless. 

Best,
Eigenman
