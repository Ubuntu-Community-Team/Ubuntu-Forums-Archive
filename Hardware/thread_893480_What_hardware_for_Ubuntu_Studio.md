### Post by markbuntu on 2008-08-19
You could give it a shot. The processor should be OK for audio and graphics but you might need to up the ram as long as you don't need to do live recording or live djing with a ton of applications open. 

For video editing you might need a more powerful dual or better processor because video processing is very cpu intensive and may be very slow on that machine.

Anyway, ram is cheap these days and what would you lose, a little time and a very little money. You can put that machine to work rather than gathering dust.
