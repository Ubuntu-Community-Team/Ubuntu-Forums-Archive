### Post by TheFu on 2018-04-24
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Scanners](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Scanners)

Get one with an automatic sheet feed. I'm using a color multi-function printer from Brother (240C?) that does fax and scanning too.  It is old and hasn't been produced in about 10 yrs, but it still works.  Do all the research BEFORE buying. Once you get a short-list, search for problems, issues related to Ubuntu and Linux on the web.  Be extremely specific about the models, since support varies wildly between different versions.  Don't get burned.

I'd avoid wifi models too. wifi connections always seem to cause hassles; I don't know why.

When the ink dried out, I never replaced it, since replacement ink was more than the entire device. I had a sheet scanner previously, but it was a big hassle when scanning mutlipage docs and it took much more space.  I use a separate, cheap, Samsung laser printer for printing.  The 2 combined were cheaper than a single device.  Laser printer toner lasts a very long time. Think I've had mine about 15 yrs and only changed the toner twice.

So, when picking a device, I'd check the Linux printing/scanning project for well supported devices too.  Google should find it.

My scanner isn't perfect, but it does work. I use **gscantopdf** as the front-end.  This is a nice GUI over other tools.

### Post by him610 on 2018-04-24
Hello muroshi,

Yes, people still use fax to send documents to others. One doesn't need a computer to receive an email with attachments, just an inexpensive fax machine and phone line. Besides facsimile transmitted documents can be recognized as legal documents in most states. Not sure about emails since they can be altered.

I use a Brother multifunction, MFC-7360N. I don't think that particular model is sold anymore, but there are similar, newer models available. Brother provides good Linux support for its multifunction devices. That said, you will need to go the Brother website to download the functional software and drivers.  I have mine configured to print, scan, copy, and fax; however, I normally key in the destination fax number from the panel on the MFC-7360N;
I have also faxed as many as 15 pages in one transmission - don't know what the upper limit is.

TheFu suggested something that I missed - get a model that uses laser technology to print instead of inkjet.

Do your due diligence by finding a model well supported in Linux by the manufacturer.
