> **fredbird67 said:**
> Can anyone tell me what brands of motherboards tend to work best with Linux?

I've used one each of Abit, Aopen, ECS and DFI motherboards and they all worked 100% with Linux. But not all of those are considered top-notch makes so I don't necessarily recommend them. The reason they worked for me was that I researched the chipset first. Avoid the very latest release chipsets because the drivers may not yet have made it into the version of the kernel your distro is using. With that proviso, if you go for Intel, Nvidia or VIA chipsets you should be all right. I have no experience of AMD chipsets, but I've never come across the sort of adverse reports that I've seen for SiS. Don't forget that the chipset doesn't just include onboard graphics (if that is included). There are the IDE, SATA and USB controllers, ethernet, sound, etc, etc, all of which need kernel drivers.

Find a motherboard that suits your budget and then search these forums or google in [www.google.com/linux]("http://www.google.com/linux"). Hopefully, you'll soon find adverse reports if a board isn't suitable. And you might be lucky and find a positive recommendation.

I'll let others recommend specific makes/models. With the present hoo-hah regarding FoxConn, you might think it prudent to avoid them. I get the sense that it's all going to turn out to be a storm in a teacup and the allegations won't stand up to scrutiny, but time will tell.
