### Post by efflandt on 2010-06-19
I had no problems with suspend or hibernate in 9.10 32 or 64-bit.

In 10.04 64-bit with all standard modules my desktop PC tended to not turn off its ATI X1300 video, or complete suspend or hibernate, until I disabled kms with **nomodeset** (or **radeon.modeset=0**).  Then suspend or hibernate seemed to work consistently, until one time it when it reversed Caps Lock LED indication when woke from suspend.  The resume login thought that caps lock was on when I had not turned it on.  The caps lock key turned the LED on and Linux then though caps lock was off.  I only remember that happening one time.  It was back to normal after reboot.

The problem I have lately is that sometimes when it resumes from suspend, it just locks up with no video (screen says no signal), and blinking caps lock and scroll lock keyboard LED's.  When that happens and I hard power down and boot, networking is disabled (ethernet) until I check **Enable Networking** from the applet.  In pm-suspend.log everything always appears normal for suspend, but there is nothing in any logs about resuming when that fails.

So far I have had no trouble with hibernate, but waking from that takes almost as long as booting.  I have had no trouble with networking waking from hibernate or a clean boot after normal shutdown.

So I am trying to figure out what could cause occasional failure to resume when nothing logs what went wrong?  All modules are standard and it has all updates.  Somewhere I saw something about an issue if networking was interrupted while in suspend, so the network failure after suspend failure makes me wonder.

It is an HP a530n from 2004 with early Athlon64 3200+ (2 GHz, 1024K cache), 2 GB RAM (which tests fine) and other hardware:
