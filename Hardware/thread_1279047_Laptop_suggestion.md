**Brands :**

I get good mileage out of my IBM Thinkpad R51, which is built to **last**. (In Australia, we would say 'built like a brick outhouse, mate!')

IBM's laptop divison are now owned by Lenovo, and they still make laptops worthy of the Thinkpad name. If you don't like Thinkpads, Dell also make reasonably durable laptops (try the Vostro line, which are intended for business users.)

I have found both IBM laptops and Dell laptops to have excellent hardware compatibility with Linux.

Don't buy Toshiba, Compaq or HP. In my job (IT Support Officer) I've seen these laptops die on people alarmingly often, and the hardware support is shocking. (I *never* found working drivers for the Toshiba I owned.)

**Processor (Intel/IBM)**
IMO, you *should* get an Intel processor. Here is my reasoning :

  1) Battery Life
Current Intel notebook processors deliver much better performance per watt, which means that your CPU runs cooler and your *battery lasts longer*
Battery life is an important criteria for a laptop. Who cares if your notebook if powerful enough to play COD4 if it can only do it for 30 minutes before shutting down?

  2) *Your concern about cost :*
You are going to spend at least $1000* on a laptop which is going to last you for three or four years, at a minimum, if you treat it right. *Why are you trying to shave pennies off the cost?* Get the better machine, so you won't need a new one in two year's time.

Compatibility :
Intel make very good motherboards and chipsets. I have never had a compatibility issue with any Intel hardware (if anything, Intel hardware is as standard and compatible as it gets.)

Reliability :
I have also found Intel hardware to be rock-solid (again, built like a brick outhouse.) Most of the Intel Desktop motherboards I have seen have lasted at least 5 years; i have specimens that are 12 years old (in my server) and still work. This is a good indicator of build quality.

*$1000 Australian dollars gets you an entry-level laptop.

Off to bed for me!

### Post by dwinks on 2009-09-30
I have an Acer Timeline 5810tz.

Out of the box, 9.04 everything works with a few exceptions, graphics are a bit slow due to kernel regressions (fixed in Karmic) and poor Intel drivers (also fixed in Karmic).  The WIRED network also doesn't work, due to the wrong driver being used.

Out of the box, Karmic all current updates: Everything works.  Graphics are as fast as windows (it's integrated intel, so not fast period), networking works great, compiz works natively, etc.

My ONLY gripe at this point with the system is that Windows gives me ~8 hours of battery, while Ubuntu only gives me ~5 hours for some reason.  However, compared to most other $600 laptops, even 5 hours is quite a bit more than they get.

It's less than an inch thick, has HDMI out (sound and video), VGA out (somewhat pointless with HDMI), and overall a VERY nice laptop for the money.  I highly recommend it if you are looking for a laptop with the following:

Long battery life
A decent sized screen (not a netbook 10")
Near-netbook portability
Reasonable CPU power (Core 2 Solo ULV, compared to the usual Atom in netbooks)

Note that it will NOT play games.  It is NOT a desktop replacement.

I got it because I wanted a portable, mobile, decently sized laptop for web-surfing, movies/video, music, and office program usage, and it fits that bill perfectly for me.  Note that I also have a Phenom II quad core at 4.7GHz a core at home with 16GB of ram, so it can do all my "heavy lifting", which makes the apathetic ULV processor in the Timeline a moot point.
