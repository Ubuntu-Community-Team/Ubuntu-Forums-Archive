### Post by Tindytim on 2008-09-02
Not enough information.

You can't judge 2 different processors simply by their clock speeds, unless they are similar processors. And in this multi-core day and age it makes it even more difficult.

For examples, the original Pentium 4s were clocked higher than the top of the line Pentium 3s, but those P3s out performed those P4s. 

So you can't judge things based purely on clock speed.

And CPUs are the only part of the PC that contributes to speed. Considering that other components could bottleneck performance (RAM, Motherboard). Then you have to look at graphics cards. Depending on what you'd be doing (Gaming, Multimedia, 3D Rending) a GPU may contribute to speed considerably.
