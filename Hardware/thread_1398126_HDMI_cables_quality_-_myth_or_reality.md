### Post by Jose Catre-Vandis on 2010-02-04
On my new net top and LCD screen I have hdmi connections, so logic follows that I should connect the two together by hdmi cable.

Did lots of googling and reading up about cables, and the general consensus was there was no need to buy expensive, just ensure that the product met the specification.

So I eBayed a 1m cable for @ £5.99, 19 wire, 1.3 spec, gold plated, 1080p compatible.

Plugged it in, switched on, and from the moment the bios kicked in I had switching between black screen and image, mostly black screen. Didn't matter what I booted into Xubuntu or W7 the problen continued.

Nothing I could change with configuration.

So bit the bullet and shelled out on a "quality" QED Classic 1.3 branded cable @ £25. Plugged it in, perfect picture.

This may not be your experience, and cheaper cables probably do work in most cases. But as hdmi becomes more prevalent as a connection method, it may be worth having at least one "good" cable available in order to test connections.

Some useful advice. Power down and unplug all equipment before connecting the cable.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-02-04
There are at least 5 levels of HDMI with different bandwidth levels--all using the same connector.  1080p would be the most stressing bandwidth.  So if a cheap cable works at 1080p without sparkles, stars, dropouts, or other weirdness, then you should be OK.

Avoid Monster cable.  Their cables are full of plastic and very little copper.  Go ahead and cut one of your $90 cables and compare it to a $6 cable.
