### Post by blackened on 2008-12-01
If you're really dead-set on the ~$200 range, then craigslist or ebay are going to be your best bet. Narrow your search on ebay by price range and machine specs and you should be good to go. Keep in mind the machine that you'll be getting will likely be used, so the seller posting good close-up pictures is absolutely necessary. Ask alot of questions: does the machine function as it should, does the display have (many) dead pixels, has the machine been dropped or damaged in any way, etc. The more you ask, the more recourse you will have if something goes wrong or the seller misrepresents the product.

### Post by nobleguardian on 2008-12-07
I wouldn't recommend an Acer laptop.  They have a high failure rate with the motherboards (especially the ASPIRE).  After a while (about a year & 1/2, give or take 6 months) the keyboard, mouse and USB ports suddenly quit working.  I've attempted to fix two laptops with this problem, and the failure is quite common with ACER laptops (Something to do with the Southbridge chip). Unfortunately, it's more expensive to fix, than to buy another laptop.

From what I've heard, ASUS eee's are good with Ubuntu, (know someone with that setup).  However, ASUS tech support ain't so good.

You'd be better off buying used.

Brand-new laptops ~$200 are a rare find.

Maybe this(I know it's slower but it does have wi-fi and the screen's easy to see in sunlight):
The OLPC [XO laptop]("http://laptop.org/en/laptop/index.shtml").  

Specs [here]("http://laptop.org/en/laptop/hardware/specs.shtml").

Instructions on how to install Ubuntu on the XO [here]("http://sprocket.io/blog/2008/05/ubuntu-hardy-heron-on-the-olpc-xo-1/").
