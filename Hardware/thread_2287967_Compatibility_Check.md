### Post by TheFu on 2015-07-23
That is a low-end CPU for gaming.

For everything else, you'll need to be exact on the chips to know anything about compatibility.  
Generally, more compatible systems are at least a year old and use extremely mainstream chips.

For example, we cannot say anything about the wifi - no chips specified. Also - I doubt you want a 2005 PCI wifi card - sure that isn't PCIe?

Gaming GPUs tend to need a large PSU - is 400W enough?
For the CPU specified, the amount of RAM seems like overkill.  Get half the RAM and spend $100 more on the CPU.

I'd much rather see a normal Intel PRO/1000 network card than any wifi - especially on a gaming rig.
