### Post by SeijiSensei on 2019-09-18
Boot from an installation medium and choose "Try Ubuntu".  It will leave your computer untouched.

I recommend 18.04 LTS for a new user.  There are multiple "flavors" of Ubuntu.  You can try the standard [Ubuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/bionic/daily-live/current/"), or something like [Kubuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/kubuntu/bionic/daily-live/current/") (my choice) or [Xubuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/xubuntu/bionic/daily-live/current/").  The basic operating system is the same. What differs is the desktop you see and some of the associated applications.

The "LTS" versions have "long-term support;" 18.04 LTS will be supported through April, 2023. Interim versions like 19.04 have only a nine-month support period.
