### Post by dsteven1 on 2009-05-12
[SIZE=3][FONT=Comic Sans MS]I bought my wife an Aser Aspire 5315 2513.  It had Vista on it, and it worked great for about 10 months then it started shutting down.  If I let it cool down for awhile,, it would start back up and run fine for awhile then it would shut down.   I could hear the fan running, so I knew that wasn't the problem. Eventually  I got brave and decided to open it up since it was out of warranty anyway.  The back cover was a little difficult to remove.  There are 4 screws that hold it in place, but there are several tabs around the edge, and so one must carefully pry between the cover and housing to remove it. After getting the cover off and putting on my ANTI-STATIC wrist band, I looked at the fan and it looked a little dusty.  I decided to remove the fan which was held in place by 3 small screws.  When I took it off, boy, was I surprised.  There is a small radiator that is attached to the cpu heatsink, that the fan blows air thru to cool the computer. BUT  mine was completely CLOGGED with dust and lint.
Using compressed air i blew all the dust away, reassembled the laptop,  and it works great,  NO MORE OVERHEATING      hope this works for you too    :guitar:
