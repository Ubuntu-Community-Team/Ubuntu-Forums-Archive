### Post by Rockbuster on 2009-09-06
sorry to say...but 10 gigs of RAM *is* the latest and greatest. I don't know a single person with that much RAM...lol. Most I've ever actually seen is 6. Core 2 Duo is a pretty generic, but solid, 64-bit architecture. It seems to be more reliable than those AMD or Pentium deals. My own Core 2 Duo is a lot steadier than my  buddy's Pentium 4 64-bit, even though his clock is faster.

Also, if it's your type of thing, find a processor that's really overclockable -and at that, a good heatsink and power supply. I can't say much about a new motherboard - I've actually never replaced one. But make sure it doesn't have an integrated Intel graphics card. Those are terrible pieces of work (some can't even run Windows Aero), and you probaby don't want one for video editing.

### Post by NoXiS on 2009-09-07
Really how much of this old machine are you planning on reusing?

If you are planning on using this for video editing you are going to need a decent amount of storage space. With more recent drives also being noticeably faster than 7 year old drives surely a new hard drive will be required?

If your old machine doesn't have a DVD rewiter, you'll need one of those too surely?

In case you do just plan on reusing the existing hard drive and optical drive, make sure you get a motherboard that does have a couple of PATA connectors. Many motherboards, including mine, these days only have 1 PATA connector (or possibly less? - maybe).

As far as a CPU goes I would probably go for a Pentium Dual-Core, something like an E2200 or something or (depending on what motherboard bargains you can find) the bottom end AMD Athlon II (which are under £40 new, but too new to be found second hand).

AMD Socket AM2+ boards do tend to have better on-board graphics than Intel Socket LGA775 boards though. Are you planning on reusing a graphics card or just going with the on-board?

Forget about this 8-10 gig RAM obsession until you really consider what other parts you need in accordance with your budget. If you want to be able to increase your RAM just make sure you get a board with 4 RAM slots and stick 2 2gig sticks in for now.

Really though without any idea of what kind of performance you are looking for or any kind of idea of your budget I don't know of how much help any of us can be. Is it really going to be a video-editing machine or is it a general purpose machine that you will sometimes do some video editing on? You really do need to consider what is is going to be used for, because I'm damned sure that if you were building a dedicated video-editing machine you wouldn't need to be asking us what you should get.

Help us help you.

### Post by NoXiS on 2009-09-07

@op

Luckily there are some cracking deals to be had on recent hardware. Just don't fall into the trap of thinking that used/old hardware is cheaper than brand new low-end stuff (that is still very powerful).

You may find a good bundle deal for motherboard/CPU/RAM on eBay but if you end up getting each separate component from different sellers, you may as well go to a proper retail site.

I doubt you'll find, for example, DDR2 RAM any cheaper on eBay.

Check some prices on new stuff and see how they compare, then you'll at least know what kind of a deal you're getting. Make sure you get at least a 'Pentium Dual Core', which is the cheaper variants of the Core2Duo range and avoid Pentium D's.
