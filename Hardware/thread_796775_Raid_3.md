### Post by Eric the Red on 2008-05-16
I'm building a PC right now with 1 HDD. However, in the future I'm going to be upgrading to RAID 3 and place 2 more drives in the computer (for a total of 3).

My question is.. (and please don't answer unless your 100% sure), can I successfully upgrade from 1 disk (no raid) to having 3 drives in raid 3 **without** formatting my disk?

 I.E, My Motherboard has support for raid 3.

---

### Post by kidders on 2008-05-18
Not if you want to use the entire surface of all three disks in the array, and part of one of them is already in use ... which seems to be the case. Having said that, your motherboard's RAID software *may* be capable of magically preserving the contents of your disks, but I very much doubt it.

Incidentally, RAID 3 is a bit obscure, and is only suitable for a small number of applications. I just thought I'd mention that in case you didn't already know.
