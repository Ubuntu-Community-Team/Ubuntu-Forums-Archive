### Post by ride4mud on 2011-02-11
For the past year I have been running an Ubuntu LTSP environment in a k12 school with 30 thin clients.  The server supporting the thin clients is an IBM x255 with 8 gig of ram, 4 Xeon single core processors, and 10k sata drives.  This setup has worked fine for most of the classes with 10 - 20 students but a lag is noticed when all 30 clients are running.

I have watched the system performance with all 30 clients being used by and the server is definitely CPU bound.  My plan for the next year is to split the LTSP environment to utilize two servers (15 thin clients each) with a common home directory.

The question I have is hardware related.  The x255 is many years old and is starting to show it's age.  With recent advances in desktop processing speed it almost makes sense to buy two, or three, desktops running quad core AMD or Intel chips and 7.2k sata drives and use these to run the thin clients.

Comments?
