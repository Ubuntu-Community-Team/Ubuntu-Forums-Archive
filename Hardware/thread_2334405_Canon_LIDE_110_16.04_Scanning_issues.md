### Post by grege2 on 2016-09-03
I do not know the solution to your problem but to say mine works fine with xsane. My installation is a clean install of Ubuntu Gnome 16.04 64 bit running on a Intel H97 motherboard with an i5 and running a stock Ubuntu kernel.

There was an ongoing issue with this scanner and USB3 connections that should be resolved in 16.04. In older versions you had to turn off USB 3 in the bios and force all ports to USB 2 and it would be fine. However my system is most definitely working with USB 3 active although the scanner is plugged into a USB 2 port at the back.
