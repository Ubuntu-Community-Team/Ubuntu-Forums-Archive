### Post by dabl on 2010-07-08
I'll tell you what I did, and you can decide whether it makes sense for you.

I bought a nice netbook (Toshiba NB205) that came with Win XP Home Edition pre-installed.  I never booted the Windows OS -- not even once.  I connected a big externally-powered hard drive with a large empty partition on it, via the USB connector, and then I booted (using an external USB optical drive) a Linux Live CD that happens to have Clonezilla on it, and I cloned off the pristine hard drive in the Toshiba to the empty partition on the external hard drive.  Once I did that, and was certain that I had a good image, I re-partitioned the Toshiba's hard drive and installed Linux on it.

Now that the 12 month warranty is over, I guess I can free up that backup hard drive partition -- I have no use for the image that's on it.  :P

But, if the Toshiba had died within the first year, you can bet I'd get the hard drive out of it, use Clonezilla to put the image back on the hard drive, and then run it one time before I put it back in the Toshiba and sent it back for replacement.  :P

Of course, had it been the hard drive in the Toshiba that died, then ....

### Post by ugm6hr on 2010-07-08
It is common practice by many manufacturers to not support any OS that isn't pre-installed.

This is because the manufacturer has to go through standard diagnostic questionnaire (often on the phone) to ensure the "fault" isn't a software problem (as most are).

In theory, you should be able to remove or wipe any HD / SSD before returning the device for data security reasons.
