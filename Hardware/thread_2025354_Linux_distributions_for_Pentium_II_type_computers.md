### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-07-14
Take a look at Puppy Linux: [http://puppylinux.org/](http://puppylinux.org/)

Note that these computers may be too slow to be useful to anyone.  Even if you can boot Linux on one, you may not be able to use a modern web browser like Firefox.  And that will make the computer useless to a charity.

As an example, one of my friends just gave me an old Dell with a Pentium II 450MHZ, 96MB of RAM, and an 8GB hard drive, to clean the drive off to erase personal info.  I could probably boot Puppy on this (I booted DBAN to erase the drive and that worked, so I know I can boot Linux.)  But no one will want this computer even if I try to give it away - I've tried to give away faster computers on Craigslist before and no one responded.  I will take it to the local computer recycler here, but they will tear it down to components.  They only reuse machines with at least a 2.4GHZ CPU (unless a dual core).
