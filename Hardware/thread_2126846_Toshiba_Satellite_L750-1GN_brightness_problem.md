### Post by agurk on 2013-03-24
MadLuke, there is something wrong with the ACPI driver or it's missing outright. While looking for a solution, perhaps you can enter the boot menu (you may have to hold down <Shift> during boot) and adjust the brightness before continuing to boot.
At the boot menu stage, there is no ACPI driver involved at all and your BIOS should hopefully be able to dim the display.
You may want to check for BIOS revision updates as well, it might just be that your current revision has a buggy DSDT ACPI table.

### Post by Toz on 2013-03-25
Another suggestion: try the **acpi_osi=\"!Windows 2012\"** kernel parameter instead of "acpi_osi=Linxu acpi_backlight=vendor". 

The line in /etc/default should look like this:
```
GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT="quiet splash acpi_osi=\"!Windows 2012\""
```

And make sure to run:
```
sudo update-grub
```
