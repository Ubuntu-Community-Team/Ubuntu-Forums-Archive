### Post by oldsoundguy on 2010-06-27
TOAST!!!  IF you got shocked from it, something went to ground (most likely a blown capacitor in the power supply) and the "built in" circuit breaker that is supposed to protect the user and equipment FAILED.  Salvage what you can .. dives, cards .. but most likely, because the breaker failed to activate .. lots of stuff got eaten!

One reason to replace the power supply in off the shelf computers as soon as you take them out of the carton.  OEM power supplies are usually the cheapest ones that the manufacture can buy and corners got cut in the construction.  Several good brands out there .. shop them and read the reviews (I use Enermax, but that that is not the ONLY good brand).
Good ones available on eBay all the time!
You will then have power to ADD items and features to that box and it will be stable and reliable.
The power supply is the foundation of the computer .. bad foundation, bad computer!

### Post by IcarusR on 2010-07-14
Static (ElectroStatic Discharge, ESD) damage is permanent, ie non reversible. Computer chips have minuscule power & data rails in them which get damaged by  ESD.
This is why sensible people use an anti-static wristband connected to an anti-static plug when delving inside computers.
One may think, having added ram for example, that no harm has been done. But there is such a thing as 'Latent ESD' ie damage that shows up later.

Bottom line is something is damaged for good !!
