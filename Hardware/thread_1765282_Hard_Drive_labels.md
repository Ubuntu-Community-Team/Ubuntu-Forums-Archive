### Post by oldfred on 2011-05-24
When you mount drives in /media it shows it twice in the left panel of Nautilus. I just mount in /mnt and link in the folders I want to avoid the extra entires.

Another workaround:
Duplicate mounts of partitions in Nautilus left panel
use /dev/disk/by-uuid/xxxxx...
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1561929](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1561929)

The more partitions you mount the longer the boot and NTFS partitions are slower as the system has to check that it can mount it. Linux does not mount NTFS partitions that need chkdsk to prevent further damage that chkdsk might not be able to fix. Have you run chkdsk regularly on your NTFS partitions? Linux automatically run filechecks on your boot partition every 40 or so boots.

Another possibility might be to remove the entries from fstab to speed booting and then use a script to manually mount after booting.

### Post by oldfred on 2011-05-26
If you have two partitions with identical UUIDs you much have cloned them with dd or something. If you do get both to mount you will not be sure which is written to. You have to change one of them to a new UUID.

Example is partition X on drive sda.
Change UUID see also man pages:
uuidgen
sudo tune2fs /dev/sdaX -U numbergeneratedbyuuidgen
or:
sudo tune2fs -U random /dev/sdaX
