### Post by Ranko Kohime on 2011-06-06
Almost all laptops suck air in through the bottom, expelling it out the sides, or in some cases the rear, below the display hinge.  Therefore, the pads should blow air up into the laptop.  Plus, heat rises, sucking it down doesn't make a whole lotta sense.

This is just based upon logic, I could be entirely wrong.  :p

### Post by colin.p on 2011-06-07
I have a Dell 1545 that sucks air in through the bottom and expels it at the back. I bought one of those Antec coolers that suck air down from the computer. Now that sounds like it would conflict with the Dell's fan pulling air in the opposite direction, but, the computer runs at least 15 degrees cooler with the Antec (40-45 degrees, 60-70 without). Just using FF and TB, the Dell's fan never runs, but without the Antec, the fan will cycle.
