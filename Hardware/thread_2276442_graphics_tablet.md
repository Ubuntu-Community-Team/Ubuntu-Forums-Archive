### Post by mcduck on 2015-05-04
Sorry, I don't have any kind of list of the ones that might require tweaking, It usually is just a case of a device being so new it's not included in the list of device code the driver is looking for (even though it will work fine with the driver once you get it recognized).

So I guess the only advice is to avoid any brand new product models, while anything that's been on the market for a while should already be recognized by the drivers. Or just pick the model you like, and then do a quick google search to check if anyone is having troubles with it, if not, then you should be fine...

(other than that, I personally find the better pen in Intuos series good enough reason to pick one of those over Bamboo, but it really depends on how big tablet you want and how much you want to pay for it.)

(And my old Intuos3, while maybe not as pretty as the new ones, still beats brand-new Bamboos in pretty much everything, so it's definitely worth considering if you want a better tablet for the same money. Plus you will avoid any possible driver issues with brand-new models.)
