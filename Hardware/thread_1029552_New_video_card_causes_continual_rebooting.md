### Post by Naddiseo on 2009-01-03
I brought a Radeon HD 4850 the other day, and after discovering I don't have a good enough PSU I brought a new one yesterday. So I got home excited to install everything, and, after modding my case to fit the PSU, put the video card in and booted. The power comes on, and the LED on both motherboard and video card come on. A second later my computer enters a loop of clicking sounds, as though it's rebooting, and the fans slow during it. I'm sure it's rebooting. So, I remove everything in my computer, and slowly put everything back in to check if I'd 'caused a short to something, and it appears as though all my HDDs/DVDroms, PCI cards are fine; which leaves the video card. Again I remove everything, and just put in my video card; same thing, if I left it it continues to reboot, for about a minute, then the LED on the video card turns off and it appears to continue booting, but I can't see anything through my monitor. Actually, while the video card is in I don't even see BOIS. At this point I'm thinking one of two things: either my motherboard doesn't support the graphic card; or two: I've somehow broke my video card.
Is there any way of testing for the second?
My Motherboard is an [Asus M2V-MX]("http://www.asus.com/products.aspx?modelmenu=2&model=1418&l1=3&l2=101&l3=324"). If it does turn out to be an incompatibility issue, I can buy a new MB/CPU - though I'd rather not. but if the video card is broke I'm going to have to return it.

Any ideas on what I should do?

Thanks

** Solved, the motherboard didn't support PCIe x16
