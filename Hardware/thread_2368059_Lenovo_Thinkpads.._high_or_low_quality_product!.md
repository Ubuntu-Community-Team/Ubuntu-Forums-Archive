### Post by vocx on 2017-08-06
> **kurt18947 said:**
> ... **The Thinkpad line is the 'business' line, the Lenovo line is more the student/consumer line, I think.** 

You are mistaken. "Lenovo" is the manufacturer. "Thinkpad" is the generic name of their series of laptops. And the business model is the T-series, such as T420, T450, T470, etc.

As, I said previously, the T-model is the original workhorse. The other series, Thinkpad E, P, L, etc. are cheaper and cater to general consumers and students.

So, this topic really depends on the segment you are looking at. If you want to know if the T-model is a high quality product, I'd say, yes, it is. If you mean the E-model, my answer is, maybe.

It's the same with mobile phone manufacturers. Samsung produces several different models for different markets. For power users, the prime line is the S-series (S6, S7, S8, etc.). For other segments they have A-series (A3, A5, A7), J-series (J1. J3, J5), etc.
