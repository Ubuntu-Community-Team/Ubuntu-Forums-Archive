### Post by Votlon on 2014-06-05
I needed a few extra fans for my computer, just as a temp fix. So I ended up using an old power supply unit and hooked up all the fans to it, but to make the power supply run I ended up having to put a paperclip from the green cable to one of the black cables. When it comes to electrical how bad is that for my PSU, and can leaving the paperclip in the PSU (as I explain before) be a fire hazard?

---

### Post by andyfied on 2014-06-06
As long as it is a fairly thick paperclip, you won't do any damage at all. The voltage running through that is only a control voltage and very low power. Of course, any bare wire may be a fire hazard if it shorts to another piece of metal so I would recommend a thick gauge single core insulated piece of wire over a paperclip. Then put a bit of insulating tape over it, just to make sure.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-06-06
That is a common repair to older, white-box PC's that won't power on using the front switch.  Typically the motherboard power-on transistors give out.  I solder some wires and pull them out to a switch in front of the case to rescue these machines.  Fire risk is low, but you should keep the inside of the machine clean and keep it a few inches off of the floor, and don't stack a bunch of recycled papers on it and you should be OK.
