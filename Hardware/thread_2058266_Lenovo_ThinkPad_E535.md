I also am looking at laptops with lenovo as a specific manufacturer in my search. I do not currently own an E535 but was drawn to it for the integrated 10 key on the keyboard and always on USB port for charging my phone without having my laptop on.

As a plus, Ubuntu made the official documentation--
[http://www.partnerinfo.lenovo.com/partners/nl/products/downloads/thinkpad-edge/E435_E535_DS_WE_14JUN12.pdf](http://www.partnerinfo.lenovo.com/partners/nl/products/downloads/thinkpad-edge/E435_E535_DS_WE_14JUN12.pdf)

For this specific model it looks like only the sound on the HDMI video out is a problem-- which isn't a problem for me. I never connect my laptops to TVs and the presentations I make never have sounds I don't make myself (there is nothing like standing in front of very serious people and making explosion noises-- try it some time)...

I am also considering a similarly equipped z585-- but it was second on my research list for my OS of choice for every day use. The z585 is their similar 'gaming' consumer grade line offering. It is a little less expensive.
