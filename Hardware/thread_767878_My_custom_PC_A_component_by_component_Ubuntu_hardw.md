### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-04-26
A while back I had to put together a new desktop PC to run Ubuntu only. This is just a summary of all the components and how well they work with Ubuntu in case someone might find the hardware reference useful. I put it together from newegg, so I'll link the items to there so you can take a look at them if desired.

LITE-ON Black DVD Burner - OEM
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16827106247](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16827106247)
:This works perfectly, though the tray opens  and closes a bit slowly in my opinion. Perfect burns so far, no trouble.

mushkin 550200 ATX12V 550W Power Supply
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817812002](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817812002)
:Produces stable voltage. it's nice that it's modular, but I wish it had some single connectors. As an example, A modular SATA power supply cable has 3 or 4 SATA power connectors. For me, this means I have 2 connectors unused, which annoys me, and seems to defeat the point of going modular slightly. It still saves space, though. Very nice greenish metallic mirror finish. Overall quite nice. So far as I can tell, its voltages are within tolerance and stable.

GIGABYTE GV-NX86T256H GeForce 8600GT 256MB 128-bit GDDR3 PCI Express x16
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814125070](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814125070)
I mostly got this card because I wanted something both relatively inexpensive and fanless. Further, after being annoyed with my laptop's ATI x700 for ages, I thought I'd try out an nVidia card. This card performs quite well for everything I do except when it comes to high definition mkv files, which my CPU must decode. Despite performing quite well for my needs, this card can also make some weird hissing noises. I am guessing they somehow relate to the heatsink, but don't have much of a clue. It tends to run at a stable 48-60 C, which I think is fine for a fanless card rated to keep running until 115 C. While this card is decent overall, certainly smooth with compiz and fast with open gl, gtk windows and whatnot, in the future I will strongly consider going ATI only on my desktops for the better video playback. I've found that the mode-setting and detection for the monitor is much better than ATI's, but it doesn't matter as much on a desktop as it does on a laptop.

 APEX TX- 381 Black Steel MicroATX
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811154071](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811154071)
I mostly got this case because I wanted  a uATX with a handle. Overall, very satisfied with this case. Pretty solid and easy to work with. No quick release systems or anything, but that doesn't bother me. Did not use the power supply or fan that came with it. My one gripe is that the PCI slot covers break out so there's no way to put them back again once out. It also would have been nice if the internal audio jack pass through had about a .5" longer cable just to add some give. Overall, quite happy with the case.

ASUS M2N-MX SE Plus AM2+/AM2 NVIDIA GeForce 6100 Micro ATX AMD Motherboard
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813131249](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813131249)
This motherboard requires one BIOS tweak (an acpi option) to work properly with Ubuntu. Otherwise I believe it functions properly, though I am not using its network port (on wireless) so I can't verify that. It does have one oddity. I've connected the internal audio header on this mobo to the headphone port on my speaker case. And true enough, if I plug headphones into the headphone port, they work. However, the external speakers do not shut off. I am not sure if this has to do with Ubuntu, the Mobo, or the Case. Finally, it's important to note that this mobo will NOT overclock at all, and that it doesn't have any manual voltage control. Because of this, though it is rated to 'ddr2 1066' I would go more on the basis of ddr2 1.8 volt than on the basis of its speed. Have not used the onboard video.

Crucial Ballistix 2GB (2 x 1GB) 240-Pin DDR2 SDRAM DDR2 1066
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820148069](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820148069)
Stay away from this product. It's either dead on arrival, or dies shortly thereafter. With the motherboard I chose, there didn't seem to be any way to get it to run at full 1066 speed, either, though crucial's own website lists this a recommended option for the M2N-MX SE Plus mobo. Just stay away from this product. It doesn't perform consistently and exhibits a high failure rate. 

 Seagate Barracuda 250GB 7200 RPM 16MB Cache SATA 3.0Gb/s Hard Drive - OEM
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822148262](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822148262)
Works perfectly. I've only been using Seagate drives for years. No problems with any so far, though I do have a laptop HD that might be on the way out. This drive seems fast enough and works fine so far. Haven't done any benchmarks, though.

 Thermaltake TT-9025A-2B 90mm Case Fan
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811999108](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811999108)
This is one of the two case fans I'm using. The only reason I bring it up is because it's significantly louder than I expected. It does the job, though.

 SUPER TALENT INT-AIN1-C All-in-one USB 2.0 Card Reader
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820609198](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820609198)
The SD card reader and USB port work. I can't vouch for the other readers as I don't have the cards, but I'd guess that they work, too.

 AMD Athlon X2 BE-2350 Brisbane 2.1GHz Socket AM2 45W Dual-Core Processor
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103231](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819103231)
When I got this CPU it was near the same price as the 65W version. It runs at about a constant 31 C. Performs decently. Low power draw. Only negative about this CPU is that there's some sort of problem with its internal temperature probes. Not a big issue as most mobos have their own CPU temp probe.

 Kingston 1GB 240-Pin DDR2 SDRAM DDR2 800 (PC2 6400)
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820134487](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820134487)
I'm now using this stuff instead of the crucial ddr2 1066 reviewed previously. Adds some heat to the case because it doesn't have sinks, but overall quite stable and performs somewhere in between ddr2 800 and ddr2 1000 speed. I'd say maybe a hair under 900.

 SMC SMCWUSB-G IEEE 802.11b/g USB 2.0 EZ Connect 2.4GHz Wireless Adapter 
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833129080](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833129080)
Works out of the box with ubuntu. No trouble, no problems, supports WPA2, and gets better reception than my laptop's wireless card in the location I put the desktop. Wide, so I recommend using the provided extender cable. Probably not be a good choice for a laptop because of its size. But great for a desktop.


Overall, these parts seem to work well together and provide a stable system that should meet the needs of a typical Ubuntu desktop user. If there's a weakness, it's that the nVidia card doesn't power high-def mkv playback and that its video scaling algorithms seem slightly inferior to ATIs. However, it does work great with compiz, and plays nexuiz fine (80 fps + on most maps) @ 1680x1050 resolution and most settings high.

So no problems. It all works with Ubuntu so far as I am aware. Maybe later I'll run phoronix suite and link the results now that I've got my RAM issues squared away.
