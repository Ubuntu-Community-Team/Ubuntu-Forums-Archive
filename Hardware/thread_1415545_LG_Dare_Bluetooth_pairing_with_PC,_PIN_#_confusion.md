### Post by EndersJ on 2010-02-24
Couldn't find anything else on the forums regarding bluetooth connection for an LG Dare. I am trying to connect my LG Dare for file transfer purposes. I am running Ubuntu 9.10 and am using a D-Link Bluetooth DBT-120 dongle due to lack of integrated bluetooth support on this pc (HP Media Center PC m7350n). 

When I plug the dongle in a Bluetooth symbol appears in my notification bar at the top of the screen. I set the computer to be "discoverable" in the preferences there as well as named it. After doing this I am able to see the computer under the name I gave it by searching for bluetooth devices on my phone. When I click the computer (on the phone), it asks that I enter a passkey.

If I go to the bluetooth icon at the top and "Set up new device" it sees nothing. I have the PIN options set to "Automatic".

On the phone when I click on the computer (since it is able to find it) it asks me to enter a passkey. If i enter "1234" my computer pops up a window that says:

> 
Device 'LG VX9700' wants to pair with this computer
Please enter the PIN mentioned on device 'LG VX9700' (00:21:FB:CB:00:50).


At this time the computer is able to see this same device on the bluetooth device search I have running. If I select it, it gives me a random passkey to enter on the phone. 

So this is the problem I am faced with; I have one window telling me to enter the pin the phone is giving me (however, the phone gives me nothing, just sits there trying to pair), in addition the ubuntu bluetooth search is giving me a pin to enter into the phone (which the phone will not let me do).  Anyone have any experience with this or know where I may get some help with this? Thanks.

---

### Post by EndersJ on 2010-02-24
After writing the previous post I continued to fool with it and almost immediately found that if I enter the passkey that I entered into the phone ("1234") into the window that pops up when trying to connect, it connects perfectly. I can now click on the bluetooth icon at the top and browse files on my LG Dare. Hope this is able to help someone else out there if they run into this problem. Thanks.
