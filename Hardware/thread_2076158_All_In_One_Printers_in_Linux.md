### Post by kurt18947 on 2012-10-25
HP is generally the easiest,  just download hplip & hplip-gui.  I've  had very good luck with Brother.  Their linux support pages are here:

[http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html)

It looks more intimidating than it is, in part because Brother relies on command line installations.  They have an install script that makes installing the printer function very simple once you're familiar with bash scripts.

[http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/faq_prn.html#f00104](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/faq_prn.html#f00104)

Epson usually gets good marks for Linux compatibility.  Consumer grade Lexmark stuff is frequently a pain.  It seem Canon drivers are more  frequently found on non-North American sites - Australia,  Malaysia and Singapore seem to have more Linux related drivers etc.
