### Post by IcarusR on 2010-03-13
Whilst I'm not up on current h/w I would go for AMD over Intel as I believe they are slightly cheaper. Someone will probably correct me on this !!
HDD, personally I'd go for WD Green series drive. Uses less power, run cooler, quieter & specs are generally better. Price difference .... WD is £1 cheaper.
Case, I'd go for cheapest that has enough space for my drive requirements.
PSU get a GOOD one, quallity, generally, goes up with £s & will last longer & have tighter voltage tolerances.

Don't forget to use an anti-static wrist band. Or risk latent damage !!

Good luck & have fun building.

### Post by DownTown22 on 2010-03-13
Awesome setup there.

I've built a few computers in the past few years and I don't see a single thing wrong with that.

I've never used Antec stuff so I can't really comment on any of that.  I like CoolerMaster and Thermaltake cases and OCZ and CoolerMaster PSU's.

This looks like a great setup, i've heard great things about that intel chip set, but if you did want to go cheaper AMD would be better and with Linux you really wouldn't feel the difference in power. 

Please make sure you partitions that drive when you get everything running. A drive that size will see a performance drop running at 7200. If you dont partitions it it will have to access the full drive every time you try to access at program or open up a picture or play a video. If you partitions the drives you can cut down on that fetch delay. 

I for one will look into solid state hard drives when i build my next computer. I will install Ubuntu and the programs on the solid state drive and all the music and video and pictures on a much larger disc drive.

I would also suggest a more powerful power supply 750 should be one to grow with because as time goes on you will add more memory and then probably a graphics card or two since you have SLI support. Your makeing this to last. 

And just a last suggestions. Im not sure if there making them yet but since you're going to have a motherboard with HDMI i figure you going to get a monitor that HD you should think about getting a blue ray drive. It just seems to make sense.
