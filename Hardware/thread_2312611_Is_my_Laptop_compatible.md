### Post by gordintoronto on 2016-02-05
There is a good chance that your laptop is 100% compatible with Ubuntu. The most likely source of problems is the Wi-Fi adapter, and companies generally don't publish exact details of the Wi-Fi adapter, because they often change suppliers without rebadging the laptop.

The good news is that you can run Ubuntu, or any of its variants, in "live" mode. You boot from the DVD or flash drive, and select "try Ubuntu." If it says, "Wi-Fi networks are available," that means the Wi-Fi hardware is compatible.
