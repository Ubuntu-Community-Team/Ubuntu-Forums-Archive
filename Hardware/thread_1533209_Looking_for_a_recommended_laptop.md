I've used Ubuntu since 7.04 and it's revolutionized my computer experience.

I'm currently travelling overseas (without my own computer), and out of curiosity I had a look at the laptop market back home and laptops seem to be ridiculously cheap these days! Here's my wishlist:
[LIST]
[*]Run Ubuntu 10.10 flawlessly (I know it's not out yet, but it'll be released before I return home)...should be easy to manage, but if a laptop has serious compatibility issues with Ubuntu on launchpad and fixes are not expected by the release of 10.10, the machine better have an amazing price/value and shouldn't require more than 20 minutes to figure out how to make it work following instructions on the forums
[*] Atheros N-range card (should be easy to find) - strong preference for this, I am a fan of the MadWifi driver I'm convinced that the Ath9k/Ath5k should be amazing drivers by the time 10.10 is out...in 9.10 there were some issues
[*] ExpressCard/34 slot - strong preference for this. I have a Belkin ralink ExpressCard WLAN card and I would like to use it with a future purchase
[*] Size should be a 12-14" screen. 15+" is way too big, and anything smaller is a netbook. I want an optical drive so I'm not looking at netbooks, I want a primary computer
[*] I would prefer 64 bit over 32 bit, and I would like at least 3 GB RAM (OEM), I don't care too much whether it's DDRII or DDRIII, seeing as with 3GB RAM, Ubuntu should run insanely fast
[*] Anyone have experience with insisting that the retailer remove the Windows OEM and discount it from the retail price? Seeing as I would remove the Windows OEM anyway, I would like to get the WinTax reimbursed if possible
[/LIST]

Am I asking for too much or should I just get a Mac? Apple computers still tend to be a little expensive...I can pick up a MacBook for a grand, but it has decent hardware it seems. It's usually trickier to install Ubuntu on them in my understanding.

If the computer is awailable in Canada, I'll more likely buy it there than in the US, because I probably wouldn't gain from the price difference after considering the travel expenses. I am willing to order online, though I would prefer to buy it at a retail store.
