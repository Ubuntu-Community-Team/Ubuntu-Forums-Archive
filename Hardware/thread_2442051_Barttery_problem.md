### Post by Autodave on 2020-04-29
Could be.  But before buying a new one, try a few things:

Shutdown the computer.  Remove EVERY cable from it. Remove battery.  Let it sit like that for at least 10 minutes.  Reinstall battery and cables and power on.  Does the power screen tell you something different now?  If it does, you may be OK now.

The other thing you can do is to try and condition the battery.  With the charger unplugged, run the laptop's battery all the way down: until the machine shuts itself off.  Now charge the battery all the way up.  Repeat the process at least 2 more times.
