### Post by Apal on 2012-06-27
As some of you might already know, Vizio is coming to the personnal computer market.

They will offer 3 line of products, **Thin+Light** (Ultrabook-like), **Notebook** (the classic one) and **All-In-One** computers.

They have drawn attention for their intention to bring tight hardware/software integration to the table. They'll indeed try to get their touchpad to work perfectly with the installed OS (which happen to be Windows), an area where Apple have been the natural leader for years. They are also to offer a clean install without all the crapware coming with most PC nowadays.

I asked them already about Linux support, they don't plan to support our OS of choice as of now, but they may be open to suggestion. I've been directed to the "Give Feedback" link from their support page to adress my remarks.

Perhaps things could change if every Linux user interested by their products get to this page and ask them to bring perfect drivers and/or specifications of their hardware to the open source world.

Other big vendors, as*HP, are already selling Linux certified computers. Lenovo did, but stopped a few years ago. Dell tried, stopped, and is trying again with the Suptnik project (XPS 13 with native drivers for Ubuntu). Asus is coming back to Linux for at least a few of its netbooks.

If you think it's a good idea and would like to try, here is the link :
[https://vizio.custhelp.com/app/chat/chat_launch/](https://vizio.custhelp.com/app/chat/chat_launch/)

You can find the **Give Feedback** at the right of your screen.

PS : If you think an open letter or a petition could weigh more in the end, feel free to write it and ask for signatures here. I know this is probably an utopic idea, but considering they are just entering the market, they may be more willing to listen the community than older competitors.
