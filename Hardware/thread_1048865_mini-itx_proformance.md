### Post by jfr1tz on 2009-01-24
mITX can contain a wide range of processors, which is what sets the performance. Most well known  boards come with VIA processors or Atoms integrated, or let you fit a core 2 duo mobile.  Either way ubuntu should run on that kind of hardware provided the processor  and integrated (graphic) chipset is also supported. 

Best to check the board you are interested in against the known list of hardware/chipsets that are supported. 

As far as performance goes, i'm sure there are some hardware benchmark that give an indiciation of the performance of the higher end VIA processors and atom boards out there. The latter is the same material used in most netbooks which seem to run the recent ubuntu editions quite well.

These kinds of processors however are notably slower then for instance a core 2 duo mobile,but the c2d also consumes considerably more power, and is very expensive, the cheapest i've seen is around 300usd, a prefitted atom board you can pick up for as little as 60-80.

EDIT, good speed is kind of subjective which makes it so hard to answer, it depends on what you want to do with it at good speed ;)
