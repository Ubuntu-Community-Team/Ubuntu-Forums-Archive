### Post by gdi2k on 2016-04-24
I've installed Xubuntu 16.04 from scratch. 

I have an Intel Broadwell laptop (Lenovo X250) with integrated Intel graphics. I have it connected to two external Dell displays, which works fine. 

However, when I move my mouse cursor from one screen to another, this (sometimes) causes one of the displays to blank for 2 - 3 seconds. Sometimes it also happens when I am not moving the cursor from one screen to the other. The screen that blanks is always the active screen (where the mouse cursor is positioned). It happens around 10 - 15 times per hour, making the machine unusable. 

I have checked all logs (dmesg, syslog, Xorg logs etc.) and there is nothing related. 

I would like to try other Intel drivers, but can't see much available for 16.04. Any tips? It's driving me crazy.

### Post by gdi2k on 2016-05-04
I was finally able to save my sanity and resolve this by installing the [Intel graphics drivers from Intel]("https://01.org/linuxgraphics/downloads") despite not being officially supported by 16.04 at this point (up to 15.10 only - hopefully the next release will officially support 16.04). I followed this helpful guide to get it installed: 
[https://allanbogh.com/2016/01/05/ubuntu-16-04-installing-the-intel-graphics-drivers-using-the-intel-graphics-installer-for-linux/](https://allanbogh.com/2016/01/05/ubuntu-16-04-installing-the-intel-graphics-drivers-using-the-intel-graphics-installer-for-linux/)

All my graphics woes are now resolved, including horrible tearing during scrolling of text / web-pages, incessant blanking issues and all the hairy kernel messages shown in the prior post. 

Now to figure out why wifi keeps dropping, why I lose my mouse cursor on resume from suspend, why sound resets to 100% during 30 mins of screen locking (surprise, mf) and why my scanner requires root access before being able to be access by a regular user, none of which occurred on 14.04. Enjoying Xenial goodness doesn't come without sacrifice!
