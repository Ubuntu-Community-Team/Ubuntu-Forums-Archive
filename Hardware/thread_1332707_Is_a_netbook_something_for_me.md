### Post by slumbergod on 2009-11-20
My partner bought a netbook so that gave me the opportunity to use hers whenever I go to a cafe for a coffee.

If you buy one make sure it has an Intel Atom CPU. Unfortunately, my partner was convinced to buy one with VIA CPU and it is crap - it runs hot and won't support Ubuntu Netbook Remix.

The keyboard is supposedly 93% of fullsize but I struggle with it whenever I use it. I am used to touch typing on a fullsize keyboard and I am forever knocking adjacent keys. If you two finger type or look at the keyboard as you type it might not be an issue for you.

I've found the 8" screen too small to be useful - even reading news sites is a bit awkward. I can increase the text size in the browser but then even less is visible. I doubt writing a text document would be much fun on such a small screen, let alone writing code in an IDE.

My conclusion is that a 13" *LAPTOP* (as opposed to a netbook) is the best compromise - cheap, small, and light. I think Asus has some 11 inch displays and perhaps they'd be OK too. I'd never buy anything less though!

### Post by movieman on 2009-11-20
> **sgosnell said:**
> The Atom can be problematic, and doesn't seem to live up to its hype.

Well, it's designed to give decent performance at low cost and low power consumption while running x86 programs, and it's still hard to beat at that. ARM has better performance per watt, but not if you're trying to run Windows, and some of the newer x86 CPUs are getting their power consumption down to the point where you might be able to get a similar battery life without having to vastly increase the size of the battery so they may be an interesting alternative.

Personally I bought mine because I wanted something small and light I could carry around anywhere while running Linux or Solaris; it's my second Atom and does everything I want it to do. Though, oddly, with the exception of running some older games in Dosbox which would have run OK on a Pentium.

It's got to the point where if I want to do something quickly I grab the netbook instead of my big laptop because booting Ubuntu on the netbook is faster than Windows coming out of hibernate on the laptop.
