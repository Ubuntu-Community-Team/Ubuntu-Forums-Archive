### Post by pdc on 2012-11-30
........ for its printers, Canon generally offers two packages: a common one to be installed first, and then a specific one for the printer you are using ........

..........so for your ip1980 ....................

1) If one goes here

[http://support-in.canon-asia.com/contents/IN/EN/0100159003.html](http://support-in.canon-asia.com/contents/IN/EN/0100159003.html)

(*I went to the Canon India website and selected support*)

and downloads and installs what Canon call the [COLOR="Blue"]common package[/COLOR] for your printer ....... [COLOR="SeaGreen"]cnijfilter-common_3.00-1_i386.deb[/COLOR] ....I have selected the debian package as ubuntu uses debian 

.....as you click to download your system is likely to offer to install using gdebi installer...........let it do so ........

2) if one then goes here

[http://support-in.canon-asia.com/contents/IN/EN/0100159101.html](http://support-in.canon-asia.com/contents/IN/EN/0100159101.html)

and downloads and installs the [COLOR="SeaGreen"]**cnijfilter-ip1900series_3.00-1_i386.deb**[/COLOR] by doing as above

3) you don't tell us if you have the 32bit ubuntu installed...if so all should be good.....if you have the 64bit you may need to make a symbolic link to tell the programme to look in lib64 whereas it may only look in lib
