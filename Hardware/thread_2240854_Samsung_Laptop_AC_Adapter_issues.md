### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-08-23
It could be the charging circuitry on the motherboard.  You have done the correct sequence of troubleshooting in order of expense.  It could be a simple as the jack needs resoldering to the motherboard needs replacing.  It's possible that the weak solder connection in the jack only allows enough current through to power either the laptop or charge the battery, but not both.  Or the charge circuitry is getting hot because of a failing component (capacitor, resistor, varistor, zenier diode, copper trace, power regulator IC, etc) and only allows enough current for one or the other, but not both.

The next course is to take the laptop apart and examine the power section on the motherboard with magnifying glasses and see if anything looks burned.  You can also check and resolder the power jack.
