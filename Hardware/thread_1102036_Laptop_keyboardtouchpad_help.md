### Post by frytek on 2009-03-22
Just in case you are tapping the touchpad - try to disable it and see if this helps. 

No digging in the system will be necessary. Just make some use of the  "Pointer Capture Applet".

Just right-click on one of your Gnome panel bars and choose Add. Find the applet and drag it to some convenient position. It looks like a green rectangle. 

Now right-click it and choose "Properties". You can change the size (25 pixels is good enough) and add some keys to be pressed while clicking to release your mouse. 

You must add some hot key that you don't use while typing (like Alt, for instance) because without it, TAPPING alone would release your mouse every time. So check the "Alt".

Now, click your text, and move your pointer to the applet and click it. It becomes red (on my screen is brownish?) and you can type without jumping cursor. 

When you want to release back your mouse, press the key you chose (Alt) and the touchpad button. 

I hope this helps.

### Post by frytek on 2009-03-22
choosing the wrong applet is not going to be harmful. i would even suggest experimenting with them a little. they usually display things about your system (e.g. you might find it interesting to have the one which shows if your caps lock is activated: my eee pc does not have a LED indicator for it). others can show the weather report and so on. i personally like the online dictionary, for instance. it is always there, so i just type the word to look for. :)

and of course all the aplets of this type can stay there at all times. they are designed for it. 

i've suggested alt key because for sure i use shift while typing capital letters. it COULD happen that i would accidently tap with shift pressed, so the cursor would get released. and alt is very rarely used. you can use ctrl if you like. 

and only for release. in my opinion it is very difficult to click the applet incidently, because it's small. so there is no need for extra key while capturing the mouse. while releasing is a problem because while tapping we are clicking everywhere on the screen, right? this is what makes us crazy...

by the way: my wife was angry a while ago, because the mouse was not responding... so you better inform other users about mouse capture issue, if you share your computer with anybody. ;)
