### Post by vonHumboldtFleischer on 2011-12-15
I've had a ThinkPad X200 for several months now. When I originally got it, I installed Natty (11.04) and it worked flawlessly. But a week or so ago I started fiddling with custom PPAs for the Intel GMA HD 3000 graphics card (this: [https://launchpad.net/~f-hackenberger/+archive/x220-intel-mesa](https://launchpad.net/~f-hackenberger/+archive/x220-intel-mesa) and related) and got major graphics problems, with whole windows not rendering properly. Being too dumb to know how to fix it, I simply did a full re-install of Ubuntu, first to 11.10, which I didn't like, and then back to 11.04 (full re-install again; now 2.6.38-13-generic). But, to my surprise, Natty does not work flawlessly anymore. These are the problems I have:

1. Every now and then I get minor graphics glitches: e.g. an orange, slightly wobbling, rectangle (it's possible this is my orange-y wallpaper "shining through" a window). The rectangle likes one position best, namely just slightly off the lower-left corner of the screen.

2. Sometimes a small parts of a window, e.g. a menu bar, doesn't render properly, often it's weirdly interlaced.

[Moving windows around a bit usually solves 1 and 2, but only for a while, as the same glitches often return to their original positions.]

3. Sometimes the system does not shutdown properly and freezes at the Ubuntu splashscreen, the one with dots underneath. 

4. Sometimes "Suspend" works like "Lock Screen", both through the Fn-F4 shortcut and the menu. I stress it used to work 100% reliably with my previous Natty installation.

5. Sometimes, and I think only when I had manually powered down the machine due to 3, the system loads with an ugly button&window theme.

My guess is that 1-4 are related, and 5 would go away if they were solved.

Any ideas? In particular: it is possible my fiddling with those PPAs physically damaged my graphic chip? The glitches I have now do look like scaled-down versions of the major issues I had with my previous installation.

Thanks in advance.
