### Post by matthinckley on 2010-02-24
OK This seems like a question I should know the answer to but I can't seem to find a solid answer from google.

I have a Diamond ATI Radeon 4870x2.  It takes up two slots and has the squirrel-cage fan with vents on the plate (at the back of the pc case)

The thing gets really hot at idle and I noticed that the fan is pulling air in from the back and expelling it into the case.  This seems backwards to me.. shouldn't the fan be pushing the air out of the case?

I'm thinking about pulling it apart and reapplying thermal paste to the heat sinks but I'm still concerned about the fan.. every squirrel-cage fan I have ever seen pushes the air from the center out.. not the other way around.

---

### Post by matthinckley on 2010-02-24
well I pulled it apart.. found that the fan wasn't backwards.. just the heat sink fins on the first GPU (towards the fan) were so dirty that there was absolutely no airflow and was pushing air back out the fan opening.. so I'm cleaning it all up and will be putting new heat sink grease on it tomorrow to put all back together.. glad I found this when I did.. :)
