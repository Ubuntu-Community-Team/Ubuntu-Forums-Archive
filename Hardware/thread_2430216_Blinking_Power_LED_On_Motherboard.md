### Post by CatKiller on 2019-10-29
The PSU being able to provide *some* power doesn't necessarily mean that it can provide *all* the power.

It could be either side: if the PSU was marginal it might no longer be sufficient for the load, or the power circuitry on the motherboard - or external USB devices - may be drawing more than it used to.

How much time you want to put into troubleshooting a 10-year-old machine is entirely down to how much you value your time, and how much you enjoy the process. It's standard stuff: unplug all but the minimum, swap out for known-good components, and so on.

### Post by him610 on 2019-10-30
A friend of mine had same issue a few months ago on a computer about 8-9 years old. We tried two different power supplies. Turned out to be the mainboard which was replaced. Found exact replacement on eBay. It is working now with replacement mainboard and original PSU.
