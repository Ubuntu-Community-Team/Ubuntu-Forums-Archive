### Post by checksquid on 2010-01-09
Solved, I think.
I was able to recreate the same failure by backing up my steps. Earlier in the day, I believe I made a typo in /etc/fstab that tried to mount two drives to the same mount point. Oddly, the drive NIGHTLY that failed later was not one of the duplicate drives. After removing that line and disconnecting the affected drive, the problem started with NIGHTLY.
Doing the same thing intentionally recreates the problem, so that must be what caused it. Live and learn. :)  Hopefully this will be helpful to someone in the future.
