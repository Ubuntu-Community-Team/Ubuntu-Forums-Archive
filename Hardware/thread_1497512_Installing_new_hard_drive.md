### Post by BoneKracker on 2010-05-30
Power off the system.

Unplug the power cord.

Take the computer somewhere where there's good light and no carpet on the floor.

Open the case.

Disconnect the power and data cables from the back of the hard drive and lay them aside, out of the way.

Your case may have the drives mounted on a removable rack.  If so, unscrew and remove it, noting how it goes back in (you may find it easier to do this before disconnecting the cables per above).

Unscrew the mounting screws that hold the drive into the case or rack and place the screws where you won't lose them.

Slide the hard drive out of its slot (note which side is up, front, etc).

Check the jumpers on the back on the new drive to make sure they are set to "Cable Select".

Slide the new drive in (make sure it's right side up and facing the same way).

Replace the screws (do not over-tighten and strip the holes).

Plug the cables back into the drive.

Close the case.

Plug the power cable back in.

Boot up.

Press ESC (or whatever you have to press) to go into your BIOS setup.  Make sure the drive is properly accounted for.

Exit the BIOS and pop your installation CD in.

Boot the installation CD and install your OS.
