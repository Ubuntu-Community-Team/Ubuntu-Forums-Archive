### Post by cascade9 on 2010-04-09
'Add more RAM' is a common response, but if you arent running out of memory, or using a lot of swap space with your current RAM, ithen what you have is already enough. If its 'slow', then check your RAM and CPU use with top/htop (no, dont use gnome system montior- nothign wrong with the program but it sucks more than its share of CPU cycles and RAM and can skew resuls). 

As long as your RAM isnt heavily used, and your CPU use is OK, then whatever slowness you get is just the price of desktop effects.
