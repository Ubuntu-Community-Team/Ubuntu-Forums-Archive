### Post by hardyn on 2008-08-01
the Thermalright devices are well priced for CPU heat pipe, they do not come with fans, but most packaged coolers come with crap for fans anyway.  I have the Thermalright XP-90, as it is pretty low profile, and cant go wrong for 30$ including fluid bearing PWM fan.

PSU... just find something that advertises temperature controlled fan, you will have to pay alot more than 20#, probably more like 40# but not hearing a fan drone away is well worth it.

I am currently using a samsung 750gb sata hard disk, probably the quietest drive i have ever owned, and cheap, 105$ CAD.

aftermarket heat pipe cooling for the GPU is nothing that i have ever done, but the GPU fans tend to be loudest in most peoples systems.  there is just not payback for the vga card manufacturers to put anything good onto the card from the factory.

good luck.

### Post by evets25 on 2008-08-06
That onboard card will be more than enough. Unlike Vista's aero effects, compiz-fusion has surprisingly low hardware requirements. I've gotten compiz to run smoothly on my ancient ATI Rage pro 128 (32MB of ram), as well as on various old integrated intel graphics chips. An integrated geforce 7 will be just fine for compiz. :) 

As for the power supply, 400 watts should be plenty, especially if you are just going to use the integrated graphics card. If you're going with a separate fancier card, then perhaps you *might* need a 500 watt PS.
