### Post by Sir_Sword on 2014-10-07
I have an HP DeskJet 3050A printer.

I used to use Ubuntu 13.10 and the printer printed flawlessly.  It looked great.  The print looked just as good as printing from Windows or my iPad.

Then I installed 14.04, and the print quality has never been the same.  I use the same printer settings:  black only grayscale, plain paper, normal print quality, just as I always have, but a lot of the print comes out streaky, like certain letters will have streaks in them, or be missing part of the letter.  The letters don't print out as smooth as they should... curves and lines are often more jagged than they should be, and some of the letters looked slightly smudged, like a few thin streaks were printed beyond the edge of the letter.  This includes when using a fresh ink cartridge and freshly aligned print heads.  Changing the print quality from Normal to Best doesn't really improve things any.

By the same token, Windows and the iPad continue to print flawlessly.  In fact I'm having to do a lot of printing from Windows now just so it'll look professional.

I'm sure there's smeg-all one can do about this issue, but has anyone else experienced such a thing?
