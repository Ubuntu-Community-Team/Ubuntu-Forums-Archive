### Post by Jagoly on 2011-09-12
Where did you find an 8GB SSD? You wouldn't fit a lot on that.

Also, blu-ray movies don't really work in Linux yet (data disks work fine and movie support will exist eventually), just thought I'd point that out.

A 60-80 gb SSD will make a huge difference as an OS drive, primarilly in general responsiveness. But don't try dual booting one. You can mount data-hungry locations (infact any place you want) like /home on a big drive.

As for the video card, it depends on what processor you're chucking in. If you use a second gen i-series intel chip, then the integrated graphics will blitz WOW. An i3-2105 (feturing intel HD graphics 3000) would be a great choice.

### Post by Jagoly on 2011-09-12
not yet. Blu-Ray movies are heavily riddled with drm and copy protection, and can only be played by authorized devices. DVD movies used to have a similer problem, but the encryption was eventually decrypted. Blu-ray's are getting there. they can be played, but it currently involves ripping the entire disk (up to 50GB) to a HDD first. If you get a blu-ray drive now, support will exist eventually. Tons of progress has been made in recent months.

On the SSD, I don't think you can get 8gb SSD's. The USB images are super compressed to fit on the small space. A 60GB SSD would be a good choice. Remember program data should be there or you won't get any speed increase.
