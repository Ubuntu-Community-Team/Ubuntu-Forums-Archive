### Post by kidders on 2011-04-06
Hi there,

The answer to your question depends on what the cards are, and what you do with them. In general though, adding more cards to your PCI bus at least has the *potential* to lower the time/bandwidth available to the others. It may also mean your computer has to load more drivers, and manage I/O to more hardware.

Adding the extra cards may have no discernible performance impact at all ... then again, it might slow your machine to a crawl. For forum members to even make a guess, you'd have to post fairly detailed information about your motherboard & connected PCI devices, and hope someone had the patience to go through all the specs with a calculator! It might be easier to just plug all the cards in & see what happens. If you're not happy your machine is as fast as it should be, take them back out again.

I hope that helps.

### Post by wt8008 on 2011-04-06
Performance wise I doubt you would notice anything. If the cards generated a lot of interrupts, it could keep your CPU busy, but I do not know how exactly the card is setup and used.

Other considerations could be:
* power consumption of the PCI cards, although if they are idle it should not be much
* heat generation (see above)
* air circulation, PCI cards are close to the hot video card.
