### Post by Handssolow on 2011-02-03
I installed 10.10 on a new Sata drive about a week ago bit and the red hard drive led light has been permanently lit, I've never seen it not lit except during the Post process. Search results mostly refer to windoze and point to a program unknown to the PC's owner which is accessing the hard drive.

I've discovered that often if I just lightly touch something metal like a screwdriver or even 40cm piece of wire against the outer metal part of usb socket on the front panel, the hard drive light goes  out. It will often remains off until the hard drive is accessed but then will remains lit. After a few minutes though I can repeat this procedure to turn the light off. This solution doesn't always work but will again if I wait a few more minutes.

I'm applying no force to the usb socket. The only thing I've found with my cheap multimeter is about 4 microamps current will flow between the case and the metal of the usb socket (PD is 1.5 microvolts). Grounding the usb socket to the outer case doesn't extinguish the hard drive light. When I unplugging the USB header feed to the front panel I was still able to extinguish the hard drive light. The front panel also has two audio sockets for microphone and headphone. I haven't yet tried unplugging them from the motherboard because they're a bit more hard to reach.
Is there some piece of hardware not properly grounded? I did not had this issue when I booted from my IDE drive.

Any suggestions about cause, effect and cure?
