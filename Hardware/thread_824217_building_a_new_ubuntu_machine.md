### Post by MockY on 2008-06-10
This mobo only has one IDE channel, so only two ide devices can be installed at once unless you get a controller card. It comes equipped with 4 SATA channels though, so you should be pretty set for the future.
Also keep in mind that your case does not come shipped with a power supply, so you have to purchase one. Though your old power supply most likely would work, this mobo (and everyone else that is sold now) has 24 pins and if you want your system to be as stable as possible, a new PSU is highly recommended.

### Post by MockY on 2008-06-10
> **The Cosmic Hobo said:**
> how do you calculate what your system will need?

Many motherboards simply require more juice in order to even POST. This is generally true on Asus boards that use nforce chipset, something this board does as well. Look in the manual for power requirements. 
As a rule of thumb, the more fans and expansion cards you use, the greater the power consumption gets.
In ljonesj's case, 300w is just fine if the board supports that low of wattage.
