### Post by AlanR8 on 2010-03-08
Sony tends to have some proprietary  stuff installed and they can be temperamental.

That said, I've been running Ubuntu on this Sony Vaio VGN-FZ21S for almost three years now and have an installation routine that has become easier and easier as the versions of Ubuntu have changed. The main challenge was getting the Web cam to work but that's dead easy now but won't work out of the box. Needs a tweak. The multimedia keys all work, the sound and vision is great. The only function I've not fixed is the FN F5/F6 key combinations to access the brightness controls. No show stopper!

Your model has an ATI graphics card, mine Nvidia which is meant to be better supported but that said ATI have come on leaps and bounds this last six months!

You should be OK, most functionality should work out the box like wireless, blue tooth and wired networking. Most everything else you'll probably have to find tweaks for.

### Post by coffeecat on 2010-03-08
You might want to look at some other Vaio models before making up your mind. On my VGN-NS20S (not a current model - not quite a year old), everything works out of the box in Jaunty and Karmic: wireless, webcam, special Fn key combinations, SD card reader, and so on. It has Intel graphics and Intel wireless - which helps.

If I was buying another one now, I would go with Intel or Nvidia rather than ATI. At least until the open source ATI driver has improved a bit. (Although I must say my experience with Lucid Alpha and the ATI open source driver on another laptop is good - so the future looks brighter for at least some ATI cards.)
