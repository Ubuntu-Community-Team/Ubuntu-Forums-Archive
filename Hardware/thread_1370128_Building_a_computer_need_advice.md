### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-02
Asus M4A79T? I wouldnt get one of those, for 2 reasons-
1- I'm not that impressed with asus now, they used to be much better (throwaway comment, really, but I couldnt help it). 
2- Its using the 790FX chipset. Which is pointless unless you want to run multi video card crossfire. (OK, its worth a 1% in benchmarks, but really, who cares about that). About the only other advantage that board has over a few other options I'll list below is the on board sound, but for the price difference you can get a really nice sound card. 

I'd use on of these 2 boards myself- 

Gigabyte GA-MA 790 XT (for if you actually needed a 2nd full size PCI-E slot for, say, a RAID card)- 
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128378](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128378) 

Gigabyte GA-770TA-UD3 (with SATA 3 and USB 3....for half the cost of the asus with SATA 2 and USB 2) 
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128419](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128419)

I'd take the WD RE3 over a hitachi myself. Yeah, its an 'enterprise' level drive, but it should be at least as fast as the hitachi (depending on what benchmark you want to poke) and it should have a longer life (higher MTBF). 

I would get a nVidia card over ATI right now. The ATI drivers are gettign better, but nVidia still have the better support an drivers for linux. 

Your not going to need 650watts + on that setup. If you have the money, I would get one of these-

Corsair CPMSU-620HX
_[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817139002&cm_re=17-139-_-17-139-002-_-Product](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817139002&cm_re=17-139-_-17-139-002-_-Product)_

Very nice power supplies. I'm running a 520watt version of that, and its been the best power supply I've ever used. If the sticker shock is a bit much, maybe one of these?-

SeaSonic SS-550HT
