### Post by Whiffle on 2009-09-04
If you open up a terminal and run "xrandr", it will show you what outputs are available.  Alternatively, System > Preferences > Display should give you similar information, except in pretty GUI form.

### Post by Whiffle on 2009-09-04
In theory, yes.  However, it may be worthwhile to connect the cable, and then reboot the computer with the cable plugged in.  Sometimes the video hardware doesn't tell the OS that there is something plugged in unless it is plugged in at boot.  Thats how my laptop is, anyway.
