### Post by cascade9 on 2010-02-06
Its nowhere near as good a deal as it seems. 

The upside- 
Because its an IBM, and a Xeon server chip (even though this is an 'entry-level' system) it should be pretty well made. 

On the down side- 
2x 3.8Ghz xeons are no-where near as fast as what you might imagine. Sure, they are hardly that slow, but a newer CPU will be faster. 2x 3.8Ghz Xeons are also going to eat a lot of power under load (far more than a newer CPU) and will put out a _lot_ of heat. The case its in isnt that big on expansion slots as well (2x5.25'', 1 used for CD/DVD drive and 4x3.5'', 1 used by the floppy and 1 by the HDD). 

For web surfing, its far more than what you need. For video...well, its go enough guts to output full HD 1080p, but its going to use a lot more power to do that than a newer machine (with an nVidia card that support VDPAU anyway).

You could build an upgradable box that would use far less power, have similar or more processing power to 2x3.8 Xeons for a similar amount of money. Netbooks are cheap, but a desktop is more upgradable and will have more processing power.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-02-08
I've never seen a direct comparison between Irwindale Xeons (which is what the 3.8 Xeon is) and Core 2 Duo. I've seen this though-

 [http://ixbtlabs.com/articles2/cpu/intel-cmp-vs-smp.htm](http://ixbtlabs.com/articles2/cpu/intel-cmp-vs-smp.htm)

Pentium 4 EE 840 (dual core) vs two 2 x CPU Xeons. Not much between them...pretty much showing that 2 x Xeons wont be any faster than a dual core P4. The Core 2 duos are a chunk faster than the Pentium 4s, see here- 

[http://www.tomshardware.com/charts/cpu-charts-2007/benchmarks,14.html](http://www.tomshardware.com/charts/cpu-charts-2007/benchmarks,14.html)

Even 3 year old CPUs can (noticeably) faster than 2x Xeon 3.8s. With newer CPUs, that gap is just going to get a lot wider. 

I'd get something newer than the Xeon system myself.
