### Post by omelette on 2010-02-03
Seem to be going around in circles with this.  I need to transfer files to an old Atari computer via floppy but I've hit a snag.  First, I find that Jaunty (which my floppy-based PC has) has a bug where the floppy isn't automatically mounted;

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/linux/+bug/255651](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/linux/+bug/255651)

Following the "work-around" at the above link, I got Jaunty to register the floppy at start-up, displaying "floppy drive" when I open the 'Computer' window.  Problem is that although the drive is detected, when I click on it I am told that the media cannot be mounted.  Selecting 'Re-scan' causes the drive light to come on and the disk to spin, so no problems there.  Further searching led me to this link;

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=204736](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=204736)

- which basically says, "right-click the floppy icon and format the disk!...".  Unfortunately, when I right-click the floppy icon, there is no option to reformat available!  Anyone?
