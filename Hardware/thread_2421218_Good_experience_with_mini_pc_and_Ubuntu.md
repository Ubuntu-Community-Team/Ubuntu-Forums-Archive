### Post by TheFu on 2019-06-18
> **j2ee said:**
> I am thinking to buy a mini pc like Intel nuc. How is your experience? ~o)

I find the NUC devices to be $150 too expensive for the performance provided.  There is a huge premium on size.  

Long ago, I had one of the early generation Core i5 NUCs. It had a terrible overheating issue and burned out the GPU. I gave it to a college student so they could take it apart and learn. It never worked after the burn out. The CPU couldn't be replaced, at least by me. Everything was soldered to the tiny MB.

If you can live with a box the size of hiking boots, then the prices become normalized. By using an iTX motherboard and you regain much more, though not wildly more, expansion.  I've owned 2 "Shuttle" computers and 1 home build ITX system. The ITX box was a tiny bit over $100 total, including the industrial steel case, APU/GPU/RAM and PSU. Used it as a media center box for years, until HiDef video became more than the GPU could handle.  It was converted into a router for a few years after that with a 4-port PCIe NIC.

A 2-yr old NUC might be a good value. I haven't looked at the prices.  My experience with the ITX MB was a good one, so that will be what I choose for smaller builds.
