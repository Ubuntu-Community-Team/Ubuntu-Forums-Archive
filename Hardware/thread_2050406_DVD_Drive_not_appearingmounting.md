### Post by dandnsmith on 2012-09-01
Optical drives are always more difficult with laptops, as the tend to be special drives and are less accessible.
Your sort of problem could be a cable partially unplugged - not too likely as you say it revs up (but does it only do that at boot time, or will it do it if you insert a disc?)
Another possible reason is that the drive has aged, the laser has degraded so much that it cannot perform (drive needs replacing)
The most positive thing you can do is to use a lens-cleaner disc, which can remove grot and, possibly, get the whole thing working again.

Do post back when you have more info.
