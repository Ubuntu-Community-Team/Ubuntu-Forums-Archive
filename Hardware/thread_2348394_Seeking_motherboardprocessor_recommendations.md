### Post by pakro on 2017-01-06
I personally prefer Asus. Only runned in one issue (within the warranty time) in 10 or 15 years of hardware experience. I would not recommend products from MSI. Every single product from them (be it a mainboard or a graphics card) didn't manage to hold it much longer than the given warranty time for me.
Given the usage scenarios I also would stick with a integrated gpu (cpu and gpu on one chip). Here I'll prefer stuff from Intel (i5 4th generation and upwards), but I'll think an AMD also would do the job. If you buy the newest CPU generation you should verify that the currently used kernel in the Ubuntu distro is supporting the CPU (I've remember about some issues with Intel Skylake CPU in the beginning). But I wouldn't recommend buying the newest generation as it is normally more expansive than older ones and the performance benefit is normally not worth the extra money. You maybe can look for a CPU + Mainboard bundle, however you should not buy any packages with overclocked hardware.
