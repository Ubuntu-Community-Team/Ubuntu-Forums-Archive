### Post by Jive Turkey on 2010-04-04
What you need to figure out is if your motherboard has the apropriate slot for the card.  Generally there are 3 types of slots your motherboard might have.

1) PCI Express the best option if you have a slot on your mobo.
2) AGP its not really found on curently manufactured motherboards but it was top of the line 5 or 6 years ago.
3) PCI the most generic addon slot that pretty much any motherboard will have, NICs, Audio cards, modem cards, and all manner of accesories are available for PCI including video cards.

Some of the cards require an additional connection the power supply, particularly the ones that fit into PCI-X and AGP slots.

An easy way to figure out what slots you have is to look up the specs for your mobo at the manufacturer's webpage.  That first card should work just fine for you if you have a PCI Express 16x slot it doesn't require an additional power connector as far as I can see.
