### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-04-24
What I'd probably do is pick out a few motherboards you're interested in and read through reviews of them - probably on NewEgg and elsewhere.  Many Ubuntu users seem to shop there, and they often mention success or issues with installing Ubuntu on hardware they bought at NewEgg and are reviewing.

What you really care about on any motherboard is probably the chipset.  So before buying a motherboard, figure out if the chipset has good Ubuntu support - by googling for "NAME_OF_CHIPSET Ubuntu" is probably how I'd do it.
