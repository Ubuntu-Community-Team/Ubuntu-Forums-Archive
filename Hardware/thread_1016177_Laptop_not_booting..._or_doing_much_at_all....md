### Post by J_Man on 2008-12-19
I have a problem with an old laptop of mine.  It's a really old machine and I wanted to give it a new lease on life by turning it in to a digital photo frame.  I completely gutted the laptop and reassembled it, but (surprise surprise) it now won't boot.  I can hear the hard drive spinning up and seeking, the cd drive ejects when I press the button, the power button lights up. and the scroll/caps/num lights flash once as you might expect.  But that's where it ends.  The cpu fan does not switch on, although the area of the heat spreader where the cpu is gets a bit warm.  The screen does nothing, just stays black.  If I hold down the power button for a few seconds, it switches off again.

When I was dismantling it, I disconnected what looked like a small plastic pack of button batteries.  I didn't realise I'd done it as it was bundled in some speaker/microphone cables but I plugged it back in.  I assume this is the CMOS battery, although it's a long way from the CMOS.  I looked around for a jumper for the CMOS but couldn't find one anywhere.

Does anyone have any idea what I could do to get it up and running again?

Many thanks!

### Post by markbuntu on 2008-12-19
Most likely your bios is gone from the CMOS. There is probably a few pins sticking up from the mobo that need to be jumped together so it can be reloaded from ROM but many older machines do not have this and the bios needs to be reloaded from a floppy, especially old laptops that have very tweaked OEM bios for the display etc. 

If you are really really lucky, you might be able to find someone with an old bios you can use or the OEM might still have it on their server.
