### Post by TheFu on 2015-01-26
Avoid Seagate HDDs.  They have been found to be much, much, much worse in reliability compared to any other brand over the last few years. One very large study found Seagate drives were dying at over 25% rate.  There's an Ars Technica article about this in the last few weeks.

Don't think you need more than 4G of RAM - if you want to cut costs. OTOH, 8G is reasonable.
Don't know anything about the other stuff.  For a desktop, I'd be inclined to get a Core i3 at the min.  It is only highly specialized use machines like media players where I'd go less.  OTOH, I do use a Celeron 2955U CPU and find that to be quite acceptable performance.  Any CPU with better than a C2D E6600 performance will be fine.  Youtube if flash is decoded in the CPU sometimes. mpg2 and h.264 should be handled by the GPU in hardware decoding.

You didn't mention the GPU.  Any $20 GPU will be fine.

Rebates don't always come ... ever.  About 80% do arrive ... so you might want to plan that.

I wouldn't bother with the SSD at all.  The extra RAM will be used for buffers anyway.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-01-27
People here know I always bang on about paying a little extra for a name brand, 80-85+ PSU, and I'm glad to see you have done that. Avoid generic silver boxes at all costs! They can be dangerous and can cost a lot more in the long run if/when they go bang and take out some or all of the other components in the box. They are generally less than 70% efficient (good as heaters in winter) and lack appropriate safety switches. 

You should be able to put your hand on the back of the box where the PSU is seated with a good 80+ PSU and not burn it. Mine runs cool, even after the machine has been on for a couple of hours. Good luck with the build. It is a very satisfying experience when you're sitting at a machine you put together from the ground up. ;)
