### Post by Victormd on 2008-06-13
I guess it all comes down to how you use it. I've had a WD 120GB for over a year now and no sign of problems. Basically an external HD is an internal HD + [enclosure]("http://www.newegg.com/Store/SubCategory.aspx?SubCategory=92&name=External-Enclosures") that allows you to connect it via USB. Maxtor, Western Digital are brands that I've used for a long time with no issues. Keep in mind that, if you're not going to be transporting data from one place to another, the internal is not only cheaper, but faster (connected directly instead of USB). I don't think you'll find an [external]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=2010150414+131021357&name=400GB+-+750GB") for less than $100.00. [Internals]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=2010150014+103530113&name=400GB+-+750GB") on the other hand, you can find for less. I posted the links to newegg for you to take a look...

thanks!Again, it comes down to how you will use the drive.  If you will be moving data often between computers, get the external.  Even if you are limited to PATA, it will still be faster.  USB is limited around 23-25 MiB/sec while most PATA drives will do 60-70 MiB/sec.

Since someone mentioned life, I'll comment there also.  Buying *any* hard drive is a gamble, you don't know when it will fail.  I've had internal drives last substantially less time than an external.  Granted, externals usually don't have good air flow (if any) and will run warmer which can shorten its life.  If you have a fan directly over your internal drives, I would suggest going that route.
