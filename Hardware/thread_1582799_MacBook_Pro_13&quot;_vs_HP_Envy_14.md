### Post by eightballd on 2010-12-12
I was in a similar situation earlier this year, deciding between the Envy 14 and Macbook 13.  I went with the Envy 14 (got it early, and have a Radiance screen).

While I'm not unhappy with the Envy, the battery life is disappointing (3-4hrs in Windows..2.5 in Ubuntu).  I also dislike how the integrated graphics card cannot drive an external monitor.  You have to be on the ATI card.  

Also multitouch support in Linux for the Envy is missing.  While the ATI support is shoddy (fglrx won't work on the Envy), the xorg radeon driver will get you external monitor support.  Supposedly rudimentary 3d is working in the edge version of the driver, but I haven't tried it yet.

---

### Post by eightballd on 2010-12-12
I should mention that the radeon driver (current stable) cannot drive the laptop panel AND an external monitor at the same time.  You have to disable the laptop panel using xrandr to have usable output on an external monitor.

One other positive that the Envy 14 has over the MBP 13 is that it can (supposedly) drive multiple external monitors.  Though this isn't a configuration I use in Linux ever (don't know if it will work).  I have used it in Windows and it works fine for me there.  I can't drive the laptop panel in Windows with 2 external monitors though.  Others can, I'm not sure what my problem is but I didn't spend much time on it because it was more of a 'hey look what I can do' thing.
