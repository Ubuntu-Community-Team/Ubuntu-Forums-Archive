### Post by Kirboosy on 2014-04-30
I have a MSI Twin Frozr GTS 250. Its a great card even though its getting up there in age now. However, I would say the biggest thing to watch out for when you buy a card is the cooling system on it. The last thing you would want is for your expensive card to burn up after a short period of time. The PNY I found on-line that you mention doesn't have a big cooler on it. I would research to see what the temperatures of the two cards run at and how they differ.

Also, benchmark websites will definitely help you find the card that has an edge.

Hope that helps!

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2014-04-30
> Any one have any input on these two or other manufactures of nvidia cards or any input as to features to look for
I doubt anyone that doesn't do hardware reviews for a living has worked with a large enough sample size to tell you that PNY or MSI is "better".
A lot of cards don't stray too much from the reference design, so look at:
- Warranty
- Clock speeds (you can OC yourself, but it usually voids the warranty)
- Price (rebates)
- Number and type of outputs (more important for multi-monitor)

I'd usually throw the cooler on the list to consider, but see below..

> **Caboose885 said:**
> The PNY I found on-line that you mention doesn't have a big cooler on it.

The GTX 750 (Ti) is a very efficient card, so it doesn't need a huge cooler. Look at the reference design: [http://techreport.com/r.x/geforce-gtx-750ti/ref-card.jpg](http://techreport.com/r.x/geforce-gtx-750ti/ref-card.jpg) and also check out the thermal/acoustic measurements: [http://techreport.com/review/26050/nvidia-geforce-gtx-750-ti-maxwell-graphics-processor/10](http://techreport.com/review/26050/nvidia-geforce-gtx-750-ti-maxwell-graphics-processor/10)

Big coolers can also pose problems fitting into some cases, so I think I'd actually avoid something like a "Twin Frozr" cooler for a card like this unless I was planning on major OC'ing.
