### Post by oobuntoo on 2009-03-05
The only way to be sure if you really like the case is to see it in person. I made a mistake of just looking at pictures and read customers' reviews on newegg and purchased a case (Rosewill). It looked nice and sturdy, got good reviews, and cheap. When I actually got, it was flimsy, had sharp edges everywhere, front door hinges were loose and the whole door sometimes fell off when moving the case around. Worst of all, the case/fan rattled when turning on PC. It was cheap for a reason.

If your local stores has the case you want, go look at it first to see if the build quality is acceptable.

I used to build custom PC's for a local vendor for a living. We used variety of cases. In my experience, the most sturdy, well-built, no-none-sense cases are from Antec. They used mostly steel, so they are very heavy.
