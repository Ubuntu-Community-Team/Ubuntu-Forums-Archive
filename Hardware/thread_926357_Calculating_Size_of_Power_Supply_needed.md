### Post by IntuitiveNipple on 2008-09-22
Caculation of PSU ratings has become an art. It isn't enough to ensure the overall power rating is sufficient. You also need to consider the power on each of the DC voltage outputs (3.3 volt, 5 volt, 12 volt, etc.).

If the PSU rating is close to the requirement it is possible that the total load on one voltage rail could be exceeded by the combination of devices.

Try using these PSU power calculators:
[list]
[*] [PSU Watts And 12V Calculator](http://web.aanet.com.au/SnooP/psucalc.php)
[*] [Thermaltake Power Supply Calculator](http://www.thermaltake.outervision.com/)
[/list]
