### Post by snowpine on 2008-12-19
Hi Albinootje, 

My favorite way to get Ubuntu up and running on the eee is to install the custom kernel from array.org, which adds support for all of the eee's hardware (wireless, wired, webcam, etc.) and works with Ubuntu/Xubuntu/Kubuntu hardy or intrepid: [http://array.org/ubuntu/](http://array.org/ubuntu/)

There are also unofficial Ubuntu derivatives that work well on the eee out of the box. eeebuntu and ubuntu eee seem to be the two most popular right now. They both are available with the ume launcher you like. That would be my suggestion in this case. Good luck!

### Post by BigSilly on 2008-12-21
We've just bought a 701 for our daughter this Christmas, and found like you that the default Xandros is pretty naff all said and done. It's not a *bad* interface, but there are too many restrictions, such as the WPA issue you mention.

I've just tried out a few different distros, and ended up settling on EEEBuntu. I would seriously recommend you give this excellent distro a shot. The latest iso on their site only came out a couple of days ago, so it's bang up to date. But everything about it is more polished and capable than the default distro. You can update and add software without issue, and it's based on Intrepid so it's very new.

Good luck. :)
