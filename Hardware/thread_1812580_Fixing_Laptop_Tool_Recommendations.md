### Post by jramshu on 2011-07-26
The tech said he is using a thermal scanner to monitor temp. Those are usually more than a laptop. Generally around $2000.

You could use a reflow station and maybe, big maybe, find one for a couple hundred bucks, that's for a cheaper one without a bottom warmer(You could use a customized  electric griddle for that). Still have to be careful with overheating then. (Lot of people use them to reflow xbox gpu's. Best to get a reball stencil and reball the gpu, different subject though. Probably debatable too. lol)

You could take your chances, but I personally wouldn't advise it. A thermometer just won't do it, not accurate enough with flames that close.
