### Post by ian-weisser on 2022-11-24
Much of the information in that database comes from a team of Canonical employees in Taiwan.

OEMs send their hardware to that team for certification. The team also provides assistance with getting new hardware into the Linux kernel.

The team looks at what is sent to it, which may indeed differ from what shows up on a store shelf. OEMs have no obligation to be consistent.

Again, that Canonical team tests the hardware that OEMs provide, and  accurately records data for that hardware. Expecting anymore from that  team seems unrealistic.

Complain to the OEM if you believe there to be "data inconsistencies." It's *their* inconsistency.
