[*]Now open a Terminal (select **Applications** -> **Accessories** -> **Terminal**) and enter the following commands:sudo mv /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf.old
*(you&#8217;ll be prompted to enter your password, do so)*
sudo mv /home/*your-user-name*/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf

If the worst happens and something goes horribly wrong (which is unlikely) &#8211; you&#8217;ll have a backup of your xorg.conf. Just rename xorg.conf.old back to xorg.conf and you&#8217;ll be using the default system file.
Now sign out of Ubuntu by hitting ctrl-alt-delete on your keyboard
Sign back in again, and select **System** -> **Preferences** -> **Screen Resolution**
