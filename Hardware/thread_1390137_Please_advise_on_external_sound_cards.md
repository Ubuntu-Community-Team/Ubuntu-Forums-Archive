### Post by Mariane on 2010-01-25
I am looking for an external sound card to plug into my laptop via usb socket, and with jacks for speakers and microphone.

I read m-audio products had some Linux support so I asked about them the (only) distributor in Iceland. Here is his answer: 

Yes we are the M-audio distributor in Iceland and we do carry their line for external USB audio interfaces. 

Unfortunately none of them is officially supported by a Linux or Ubuntu driver. There are some 3rd. party developers making these but they are not supported by M-audio or even recommended.  I honestly cannot say that running any of these soundcards on Linux is a great idea after reading though the forums I browsed this morning.  Some guys seem to have this working, but most user seem to be experiencing problems and in-deapth knowledge of the Linux operating system seems to me to be essential.  If you have this knowledge and are willing to dive at the deep end the Fast track ultra seems to be the most popular choice for Linux users .

Fast track Ultra is not available at the moment, but is on order and we expect it in stock mid-february.  We do have the Mobile pre wich has the connections you requested available, but like the FTP is have no Linux support. 

BTW I do not have "in-deapth knowledge of the Linux operating system", not to the point where I would be able to code a driver or modify the kernel. All advice and comments welcome. 

Mariane
