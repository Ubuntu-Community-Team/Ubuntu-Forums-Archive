### Post by sdennie on 2008-06-01
I say this on a lot of laptop overheating threads but, there are so few vents on a laptop that, when they become even slightly filled with dust, the laptop can get very hot.  My current laptop CPU is running nearly 10C hotter than it was when I got it (2 months ago).  When I buy some pressured air and spray out the vents tomorrow I'm quite confident it will drop down to it's original temperature.  Regularly cleaning out laptop vents with air probably extends its life more than any other thing you can do.

### Post by AYBABTU on 2008-06-05
You can try checking for BIOS updates (or older versions).  I was running Gutsy for two weeks without any issues.  I figured it was time to update the BIOS on my HPDV6000 notebook.  Big Mistake--my CPU temps were constantly running critically hot----115F (idle) and 133F (under load).  I was able to revert back to the old BIOS and now it runs cool again.  

In my case it was reverting back to an older version, maybe you can solve your issue by upgrading to a newer version.  Just posting what worked for me.  Good luck.
