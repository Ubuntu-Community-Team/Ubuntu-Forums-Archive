### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-09-10
You got it wrong. Your graphics chip is not only old and very limited, it's also buggy even in Windows with proprietary drivers. And any chip/card of that time work poorly if at all with the current Unity or Gnome desktop environments.

Unlike what you're thinking, what you need is a variant with a lightweight desktop environment _but of current 14.04 branch where the support for old SiS Graphics with the open source driver has improved noticeably_. In a nutshell, LUBUNTU should be your best bet.
