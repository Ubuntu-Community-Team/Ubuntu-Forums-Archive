Before you buy a printer Google for the model name and CISS (Continous Ink Supply System)
So for example I just searched for "Epson R300 CISS" and got many results.

There are two forms of CISS, this one has external ink tanks. Very easy to fill but makes moving the printer difficult
[http://www.ebay.com/itm/Continuous-ink-system-for-Epson-R200-R220-R300-R320-/400153994845](http://www.ebay.com/itm/Continuous-ink-system-for-Epson-R200-R220-R300-R320-/400153994845)

The other version is re-fillable cartridges. More fiddly to fill but the printer is easily transported
[http://www.ebay.com/itm/empty-CISS-cis-for-Epson-R200-R300-R220-R320-R340-/170557762162?pt=LH_DefaultDomain_2&hash=item27b608ea72](http://www.ebay.com/itm/empty-CISS-cis-for-Epson-R200-R300-R220-R320-R340-/170557762162?pt=LH_DefaultDomain_2&hash=item27b608ea72)

### Post by kurt18947 on 2015-02-24
I too am a fan of Brother because getting them to work on linux machines is pretty simple and their inkjets often have refillable cartridges available. One point made elsewhere about inkjets is that they don't like sitting for a week or more without being used. The ink will dry out and if the ink cartridges don't also contain the printheads - which is the case with Brother - one or more cleanings will be required to restore print quality. Cartridges containing the printhead could also dry out but I've removed the cartridges and soaked/rubbed the printhead portion of the cartridge and restored function that way. If a separate printhead is too clogged to restore via the machine's printhead cleaning routine, the cost to replace the printhead can exceed the cost of a new printer. Something to keep in mind when considering inkjet printers.

Another option less talked about is Samsung. They seem to have pretty good linux support, they offer an installer script very much like Brother which is a bash script run from terminal with SUDO privileges. I was able to get mine from Newegg on sale for $89 factory refurbished. Newegg has Samsung lasers for $59.99 and up for factory refurbished machines.  Factory Samsung toner cartridges seem pretty pricy but there are 3rd party cartridges and refill kits available.
