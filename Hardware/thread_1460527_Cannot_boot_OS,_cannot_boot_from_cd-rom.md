### Post by Ozdemon on 2010-04-22
I built a new PC last week.  It has 2 x 1TB drives.  On sda1 I have installed Ubuntu 9.04.  Sdb1 has data.  All was working, except I kept getting crc errors.

It seemed to me, after checking various possibilities, that the most likely culprit was the motherboard.

So I took out the motherboard and replaced it with an identical one.  Hooked up all the connectors.  The drives, PSU and cd-rom did not need to be moved.

Now when I switch it on I get the message "Cannot boot from OS". I checked the power and sata conections.  All good.

I cannot even boot from cd-rom.  I have the bios set to cd-rom as the first boot device.  I checked the connections, all good.

I changed the sata cables on both hard drives.  No difference.  Whatever I do I get the same message.

I am really stumped why this would happen when all that I have changed is an identical motherboard.

I also have never had a situation where I could not boot from a cd-rom, including sysresucue.

So I am running out of ideas.  On startup, the hdd and cd-rom are recognised.
