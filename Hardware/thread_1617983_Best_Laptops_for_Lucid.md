### Post by divergex on 2010-11-10
I have been using Lucid on my Acer Aspire 5740 since its release, and for the most part it's a pleasant experience. However there are still some hardware/compatibility issues. Unfortunately Maverick exhibits the same issues while introducing an error with the Intel HD graphics.

I have blacklisted acer-wmi to get rid of the annoying error at boot, and added acpi_osi= to the Grub parameters to get the brightness keys working.

The biggest issue for me is that power management just isn't working as I would like. For example, when unplugging from AC, the screen does not dim, nor does it dim while idle. I have tried adjusting settings with no success.

This has become a great annoyance to me, so much so that my next laptop will likely not be an Acer (I have owned four and have had good experiences with all).

So, long story short, are there any current or fairly recent (core 2 duo or above, or AMD turion or above) laptops out there that are 99% compatible? For me automatic power management is very important, as I want to be able to walk away from my laptop and not come back an hour later to it still going at full brightness and fans blowing audibly.

I have read some suggestions for Lenovo and Toshiba, but I don't have much experience with them other than old Pentium 3 and 4 ThinkPads. Any suggestions (for models or solutions for my Acer) are greatly appreciated.
