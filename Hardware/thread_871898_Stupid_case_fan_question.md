Ok this is probably going to sound like a dumb question, but here goes.  We all know the fan in the front of the PC case is for air intake, and any fans on the back of the case are for exhaust.  What about a fan mounted on the top of the case?  What do you use it for? ### Post by argosreality on 2008-07-27
Generally a front and side based fan will be used for intake while rear and top based fans will be used for exhaust. This way you create a solid channel of air flowing from the front over the hottest areas of the case. However, with your current fan configuration you'll create a "negative" pressure and suck air (and thereby dust) through any open holes in the case as well. As long as you regularly clean the case out, or clean your house fairly often it won't be a major issue but its something to keep on eye on. My current case gets by pretty well with a 120mm fan up front, a 120mm exhausting out by the CPU and the powersupply's 140mm fan blowing air through the back.

Still have to clean it pretty regularly though :(
