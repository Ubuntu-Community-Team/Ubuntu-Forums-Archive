### Post by cascade9 on 2010-08-10
Looks liek everything is comptible to me. I havent checked th asus page for RAM comptibility, but corsair is normallypretty good like that. 

A few questions though- 

Why a M4A785TD-V Evo? Since you are getting a video card, the M4A87TD is a better choice- SATAIII support (not SATAII from the Evo) and USB 3.0 ports (Evo is USB 2.0 only), core unlocking (not sure if it you can unlock a core on the Athlon II X3, but you sure can with the phenom IIs), newer southbridge, etc.. From what I can see, its only a couple of quid more. 

You could get a Phenom II X2 550 for about the same as the Athlon X3. Its a better gaming CPU than the Athlon II. 

For HD video, go nVidia.....VDPAU (nVidia hardware video decoding) actually works, XvBA (ATI hardware video decding) is...well...buggy is being nice from what I've heard. A GT240 should be about the same price as the 4670, its slightly faster for gaming, and has VDPAU.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-08-10
I wouldnt get one of the GDDR3 versions. GDDR3 is a lot slower than GDDR5, and when you only have a 128bit bus (like the GT240 has) more bandwidth really helps. 

If you check the pages on that site, you can see the difference in bandwidth- 

> Palit GT240 1GB GDDR3
MEMORY: 1GB  GDDR3 128bit 1600MHz (800x2)

[http://www.ebuyer.com/product/179747](http://www.ebuyer.com/product/179747)

> ASUS GT240 512MB GDDR5 
Memory Clock    3400 MHz ( 850 MHz DDR5 )

[http://www.ebuyer.com/product/189262](http://www.ebuyer.com/product/189262)

I'd probably go for the Asus 512MB GDDR5 version, even though Idont like asus much LOL. People get far to worried about if its a 512MB card or a 1GB, when it really doesnt have that much impact on gaming. Its only really usefull on VERY heavy games (eg crysis), which are probably too much for a GT240 anyway. See this test-  

[http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/geforce-gt-240,2475.html](http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/geforce-gt-240,2475.html)
