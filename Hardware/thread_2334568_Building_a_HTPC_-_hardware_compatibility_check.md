### Post by deadflowr on 2016-08-21
Looking at the [linuxtv page for dvb-s2 pci-e cards]("https://linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/DVB-S2_PCIe_Cards"), tbs has excellent linux support for their cards.
That stated, of course, without the model number, nobody can tell you if the card will indeed work well on linux.
Based upon how well they support other cards help lean in the direction that it will, but nothing can ever be certain.

But that said, if anything to look for as far as your other hardware goes, for an htpc, you might want to increase the hard drive capacity.
240 GB can get eaten up rather quickly, especially if using a tv recording program.
(unless, of course, you set it to sacrifice quality, which is up to you if you like watching large pixelized television, in which case doing that can lower the sizes of the recording significantly)
But that's just my 2 or 3 cents.
