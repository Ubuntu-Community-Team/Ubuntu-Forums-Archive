### Post by paulderol on 2009-02-02
be sure your particular model is fully supported. some hardware changes over model numbers cause issues. I'm using the stock xandros right now, as i am about to send it in for a repair, but easypeasy was nice as ubuntu-eee, but i am wary of their aim to include all netbooks, which increases configuration issues. eeebuntu is built specifically for the eee, and i find that to be preferable, although there remain some tweaking issues that must be addressed with my particular model [900]. i belive the eeebuntu website is eponymous, but i will not risk the leaky link in assuming such.
