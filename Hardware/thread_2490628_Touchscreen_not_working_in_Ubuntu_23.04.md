[COLOR=#232629][FONT=-apple-system]I recently purchased a "no-brand" mini laptop off eBay that came with Windows 11 pre-installed. The device has a built-in mouse and capacitive touchscreen that works fine under Windows. The pre-installed version of windows was not activated and would not activate so I suspect it may have been counterfeit. Rather than purchase a windows license I opted to install Ubuntu 23.04 to try and get the latest hardware support. Everything worked well other than the left mouse click, sound, and the touchscreen. I was able to get the left mouse click to work by switching from Wayland back to X-Org and using some xkbset commands in a startup application. The sound I fixed by creating another startup application to issue some alsa commands to change some sound levels. The touchscreen however is giving me issues and I can't figure out how to correct the problem. It's being detected as a Goodix Touchscreen and it's completely unresponsive.[/FONT][/COLOR]
