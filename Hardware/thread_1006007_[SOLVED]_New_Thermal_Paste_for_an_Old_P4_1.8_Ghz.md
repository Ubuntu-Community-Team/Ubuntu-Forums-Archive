### Post by solitaire on 2008-12-08
I'd clean off all the old thermal paste and redo it. (use cotton buds and swabs to clean it all, you can get Thermal paste remover)
You don't need much just a VERY thin layer.
Once you have spread it on the CPU set the Heat sink back on then take it off. 
You'll see where it's touching and where it's not. 
If it looks roughly even then just secure the heat sink back on then fire up the box and see what temps you get..

My P4 2ghz laptop runs from 45c (idle) up to 69c (full load)  it's currently at 56c which is around 133f so your temps sound ok.

Just make sure you got a good quality heatsink and fan :D
