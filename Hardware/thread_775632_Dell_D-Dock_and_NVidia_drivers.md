### Post by aloyr on 2008-05-09
Simon,

I have one of those M4300 from Dell with the Quadro FX360M now running on 8.04 with kde3, but also got it working on kde4/8.04 and on 7.10. They work pretty well with the Dock once everything is configured. I am using the nVidia restricted drivers. Have you checked the VertRefresh settings of your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file? It could be that X is trying to send the video signal on a frequency that is out of the range of what your monitor can handle.

here is the monitor section of my xorg.conf:

Section "Monitor"
    Identifier     "Monitor0"
    VendorName     "Unknown"
    ModelName      "DELL2407WFPHC"
    HorizSync       30.0 - 83.0
    VertRefresh     56.0 - 76.0
EndSection

Perhaps limiting your VertRefresh to 60.0 - 60.0 might help you.

I've found that if i use the display option under the system settings app, it hoses my configuration and I can't/don't know how to fix without editing the xorg.conf file. 

In one of my setups I have a dual-monitor configuration. In order to get that working right I had to use the nvidia-settings application.

Hope any of the above helps you.

-Aloyr

### Post by Simon Brown on 2008-07-28
Thanks all for your posts. 
I did try evan_c72's suggestions, and on reboot the Nvidia drivers would no-longer work, even if I put the old working xorg.conf file back. 
After quite a bit of frustration, I stripped everything back and reinstalled the Nvidia drivers with ENVY. Great, but still no external  screen. 
Eventually I was able to get hold of a dock that allows you to open the lid of the laptop. After much trial and error I was able to use the Nvidia config manager to set it up to only display to the external monitor. Shut down, restart in the old dock with the lid closed and perfect. 
Final test, I copied the config file, reinstalled Ubuntu (8.04 64-bit rather than 32-bit), and copied the config back. Perfect first time.
