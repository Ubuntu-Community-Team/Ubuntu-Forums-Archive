### Post by MishMich on 2010-06-18
I recently got a Nokia n900, and was so taken with it and reading about the move to Meego on future Nokia ARM devices, tried out Meego on my Advent/Wind netbook.  Loooked OK, but didn't pick up the WiFi card (Moblin did, but couldn't connect to my network).  So, I had just read about NBE (which last time I looked only worked properly with EEE), and downloaded it, booted from a USB...

Wow!  Fast.  Picked up the WiFi and got me connected in minutes.  And it looks slick.  A dream.  I decided to run through the dual-boot install.  Wow.  Fast.  Has to be the easiest linux install ever.  Shifted the old XP installation, created a partition for linux, and installed itself without a glitch.

I am so impressed.  And it is great to have my netbook back and usable - with XP it was so slow, and ugly, and boring, and it kept switching itself off whenever I switched on attached to the mains - now, it just works the way a Netbook should.

Thanks guys.

Now, if only there was a way to get an ubuntu squeezed down to the n900.  I wish you guys could get together with the Meego project and create something truly spectacular for this little smartphone/computer - it has the potential to knock the spots off any other handheld device.

Mish.
