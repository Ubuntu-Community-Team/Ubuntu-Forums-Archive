### Post by cascade9 on 2009-12-27
There is a good chance that MartyBuntu has recommended gigabyte over asrock because of brand loyalty- but the 'Ultra Durable' gigabyte boards are some of the better motherboards around now. 
Possibly bad experiences with asrock. 

No idea on your budget, or where you want to get the stuff from, but a Core 2 Duo motherboard with DDR3 will be easier to upgrade RAM on later. They are more expensive than the DDR2 motherboards though.  

Both those boards have no trouble with ubuntu. All the other stuff is compatible as well. 

Personally, I wouldnt get a seagate 7200.11. (the benchmarks on the seagate 7200.11 drives are not great, and I've had issues with seagate in the past). I'd rather have a WD myself. But that is just me.

### Post by jonest1 on 2009-12-27
Just a quick note on the RAM.  You will need to install the pae kernel to see all 4GB.  This may not be a problem from a fresh install. I upgraded my desktop from 2 to 4 and could only see three until the move to a pae kernel.

### Post by cascade9 on 2009-12-27
IMO, Run 64 bit not PAE 32 bit. 

PAE 32 bit is still i386 (with ubuntu). That is circa 1985-1989 instruction sets. 64 bit uses all the newer instruction sets (MMX, SSE, SSE2, etc)
