### Post by AnotherBrian on 2017-06-30
I overheated my e7450 and it has issues and wondered if some hardware types might point the way - whether the system is toast or fixable.  

I have two of these systems so I have been able to isolate the issue.  Moving the disk (SSD) to the other system boots just fine and runs fast. So its not a software issue. 

The damaged system only boots without the lithium battery.  Once booted it runs slow (cpu blowffish scores a poor 36 versus 4 on the good system same SSD moved between systems) 
Character input is real slow and jerky. USB Mouse usage sucks too but is fine on good system. The arrow works fine but clicking around on firefox results in slow webpages and random behavior.   Internet speed test runs fast as heck on damaged system (88mb on my 100mb internet ISP access).  

Dell bios sometimes comes up to test the system and it shows all ok (cpu, fan, etc) except can't find battery. 
The design is that the battery sits below the  arm rest and mouse pad.  There are about 5 ribben cables that go into that area (just below mouse pad). So thought maybe keyboard controller is damaged from overheated battery (a theory).  
Fan pumps out air and there is no blocked air vents (in or out).   
I wondered if perhaps the cpu paste / heat sink might have gotten to hot and so the heat sink might not touch the cpu but that requires a teardown to see it.  

Any hints - or maybe its just toasted.  Thx for any pointers.

### Post by Autodave on 2017-06-30
Laptops are so much fun to tear apart. They are. Putting them back together though is an entirely different story.

Generally speaking, operating any laptop w/o a battery is not going to make it run properly for several reasons. So, you may want to try and swap batteries or look on the 'net to see what a replacement one is worth: it may surprise you had how cheap one might be. Short of the battery fixing it, it is probably time to replace it.

Do you have any idea how hot it got and why? Again, generally speaking, they will shut down or just slow to a crawl w/o doing any damage to themselves.

I just did a very quick search: $41.00 on Amazon and I am sure that you could probably find a cheaper one than that.

I don't think there is any point is trying to find the fix any further. The only parts worth replacing in a laptop are HDD (SSD) or RAM. Battery is optional depending on your use case.
And as you don't need to change SSD (you already tested it). Only thing is RAM. From your symptoms, it doesn't look like its RAM either. But you can keep checking RAM constantly to see if there is anything unusual there.
Apart from those things, any other part replacement is going to be highly risky and highly expensive that a replacement laptop makes much more sense.
