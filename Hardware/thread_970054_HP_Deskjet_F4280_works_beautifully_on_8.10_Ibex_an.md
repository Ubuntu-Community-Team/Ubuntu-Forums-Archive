### Post by mark2741 on 2008-11-03
Just wanted to give anyone who is interested a heads up:

Circuit City (at least in my area in Pennsylvania) has the HP Deskjet F4280 Printer/Scanner/Copier on sale for $64.99. 

On Ibex, there is no need to install a driver. Just unpack it, plug it in, install the ink cartridges, and then Ubuntu will recognize it automatically. Even the scanner, much to my surprise!

I gave up on Ubuntu a couple of years ago because my Lexmark X5470 Printer/Scanner/Copier wouldn't work with it. After trying out Ibex and wanting to go back to Ubuntu as my primary OS, the one thing holding me back was the printer. So I looked around and was surprised to see an all-in-one so inexpensive, and I remember seeing that HP Support for linux was good/better than the others so I gave it a shot and am glad I did.

And best part - my wife's laptop (which I installed Ubuntu on about a year ago, but unfortunately she's always complaining because it wouldn't print to the Lexmark) - her laptop prints via the wireless to the printer (via my Ubuntu/PC) without needing any configuration/driver setup on her machine. I simply went into my System > Administration > Printers and then enabled the network sharing for the HP printer and it works great.

mark
P.S. It may sound like it but no, I do not work or am connected with Circuit City or HP in any way. I just wanted to contribute to the community with what I see as a good deal for a good printer that works great with Ubuntu.

### Post by Mahinva on 2008-12-06
Just wanted to add my $0.02!

Today, I found the HP Deskjet F4280 on sale at Best Buy for just under $55.

I followed the setup instructions until the point where they became OS-specific (either do X for Windows or Y for Mac). After connecting via USB, Ubuntu 8.10 registered and added "Deskjet-F420-series". I was able to configure and print a test page with ease.

This printer has been running for only 5 minutes but this printer will definitely replace the Lexmark that I had to "hotwire" for printing and the aging Dell AIO (yes, horrible) that was lingering around for scanning.

Not sure how long the sale will last, but even if I paid the normal price of under $80, I'd be a happy girl!

Cheers!
