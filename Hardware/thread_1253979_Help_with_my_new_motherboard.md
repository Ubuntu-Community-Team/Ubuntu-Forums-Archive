### Post by tgeer43 on 2009-08-30
Although Gigabyte's website does list a Celeron 2.53GHz processor in its compatibility list, your CPU appears to be a socket 478 whereas the new motherboard requires a socket 755 CPU.

And before you go buying a new motherboard you might want to think things through a bit.

First off, you are not going to see any noticeable speed improvements by upgrading the motherboard alone.

Secondly, your memory is not going to be compatible and the same may be true of some of your other hardware. Since you don't have money for a processor upgrade, you probably don't have it for an upgrade to your memory and other hardware either.

Better to wait until you can do it right. You'll save yourself a lot of frustration and disappointment in the long run.
