### Post by kidders on 2009-05-24
Hi there,

Imo, which to choose depends on what's important to you. For example ...[LIST]
[*]SSDs can't be damaged by magnets, which (arguably) makes your data safer. On the other hand, anything that damages *any* of an SSD is much more likely to damage *all* of it, so if you do experience data loss, it will probably be total.
[*]SSDs are (obviously, I suppose) not affected by sector-level fragmentation, which improves long-term filesystem performance.
[*]SSDs use significantly less power than HDDs, and you don't have to wait for them to spin up when they've gone to sleep.
[*]SDDs tend to have much shorter lifespans than HDDs. Defragmenting an SDD even once could knock months off it's life ... not that you'd really ever need to do that. One way or another, it'll pack in long before a good HDD though.
[/LIST]

It's also worth pointing out that not all SDDs are much faster than a good hard disk. Many also tend to write significantly more slowly than they read. Before you make your choice, you should compare the specs of the specific devices you're looking at.

I would suggest choosing the HDD, unless the solid state alternative gives you something that would actually make a difference to you. For instance, it's all very well to say opting for an SDD will let you run for longer without recharging, but if you're never away from a power source for extended periods, who cares?

Anyhow, I hope that helps.
