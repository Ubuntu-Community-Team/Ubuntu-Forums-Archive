In short, "No," to both questions.  

When purchasing components it's better to stick with well established names however.  You might pay a little more, but you get what you pay for 99.999% of the time.  All all too frequently that payoff comes in the form of things like, not having sharp edges that slice you up like a Ronco Veg-O-Matic, holes that align properly when you go to screw things together, the $2 cable you can't finish the job without that you now have to order online because it's proprietary... Things like that.  I've built rigs using "cheap" parts and let me tell you: You pay.  One way or another you wind up paying out the a-- for so-called cheap parts. Oh yes... Yes you do.

But WHAT name is a good one to stick with depends on the component in question.  User reviews on sites like NewEgg are an *invaluable* source of information.
