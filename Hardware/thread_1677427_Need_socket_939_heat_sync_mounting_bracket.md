### Post by paulisdead on 2011-01-30
[http://www.google.com/search?client=ubuntu&channel=fs&q=socket+939+backplate&ie=utf-8&oe=utf-8#q=socket+939+backplate&hl=en&prmd=ivns&source=lnms&tbs=shop:1&ei=DNNFTerCN5P4swPZq_3KCg&sa=X&oi=mode_link&ct=mode&cd=5&ved=0CA4Q_AUoBA&biw=1578&bih=557&fp=9caa5df788507897](http://www.google.com/search?client=ubuntu&channel=fs&q=socket+939+backplate&ie=utf-8&oe=utf-8#q=socket+939+backplate&hl=en&prmd=ivns&source=lnms&tbs=shop:1&ei=DNNFTerCN5P4swPZq_3KCg&sa=X&oi=mode_link&ct=mode&cd=5&ved=0CA4Q_AUoBA&biw=1578&bih=557&fp=9caa5df788507897)
There's tons of them available on google.

To remove the busted one, you should just need to unscrew the plastic piece from the top of the board.  The backplate is likely glued on on the underside of the mobo.  You can carefully warm it up with a heat gun (a blow dryer will work as well) and it'll pop right off.  Just keep moving the heat gun in circles and don't let one spot heat up too much, and it'll be fine.  I've pulled tons of those things off to put different heatsinks on.  You're not trying to get it burning hot, just warming it up enough so the glue softens and you can pull the backplate off.

And like I said, if it's just the plastic bit that broke, there's tons of heatsinks that don't use the plastic part, and come with their own backplate.  Zalman had a few.
