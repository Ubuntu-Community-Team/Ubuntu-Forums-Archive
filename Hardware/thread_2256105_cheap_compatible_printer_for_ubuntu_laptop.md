### Post by kurt18947 on 2014-12-10
If time is not too critical for you, check what models are available at your WalMart (or wherever else). Then go to the manufacturer's web site and look in the support and downloads section.  See if there is any mention of linux. Another place you could look would be openprinting.org though there may not be a many current models there. I recently bought a Samsung laser multifunction for $89 from NewEggbusiness.com after making sure there was linux support available. I was pleasantly surprised at how easy it was to install and Samsung laser printers can sometimes be found for a pretty low price.  HP & Brother are pretty well supported in my experience. It seems like cheap-to-buy printers are sometimes  pretty expensive to run - expensive ink and short lived. if you plan to use it much, a better quality machine on clearance because it's being replaced by a newer model might end up being a better value.
