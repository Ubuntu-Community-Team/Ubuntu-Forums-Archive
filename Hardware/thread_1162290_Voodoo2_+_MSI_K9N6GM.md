Hi there, I've been doing some research to setup dual monitor:
 - I have an integrated GeForce 4100 (from the mobo, MSI K9N6GM).
 - I added an old Voodoo2 to my pc.

A year ago, I did something like this (integrated vga + voodoo2) on Windows, and worked perfectly (integrated vga -> monitor1 , voodoo2 -> monitor2).
Now, I want to do that with my new PC, in ubuntu.

I tried LOTS of configurations for xorg.conf, every driver I found, etc., and the X just doesn't seem to recognise my Voodoo2.

After trying lots of things, some xinerama tutorials, etc., I realized that the problem is not the dual monitor configuration: **the problem is the VOODOO2 configuration**, since It doesn't work either with dual monitor config, or the voodoo2 alone.

Ubuntu comes with a "voodoo" driver, but it doesn't work.

I think the problem is that the Onboard VGA is "blocking" the Voodoo board, is that possible? Have you some ideas?
(I already tried exploring the BIOS and that stuff).
