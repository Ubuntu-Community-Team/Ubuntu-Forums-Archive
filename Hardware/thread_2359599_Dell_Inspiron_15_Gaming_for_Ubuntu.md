### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-04-26
You could do that and you could do a search for that machine and Ubuntu, [like this one]("https://duckduckgo.com/?q=Dell+Inspiron+15+ubuntu&t=h_&ia=web"), and discover it is Ubuntu certified and have a look at the experiences others are having with it (remember, you will be using a current, supported release of Ubuntu so older info may not be relevant). You have a better chance of finding the info you need on whether it is compatible 'out there' before asking 'in here' in the hopes that someone who is using the same machine is going to spot your post. That could happen in an hour, a week or never here.

The main issues Ubuntu has with any machine are wireless card and video card. If they are compatible, probably good to go. As mentioned, Dell are generally Ubuntu-friendly. Also, if these drivers do not 'automagically' work in a live session does not mean they can't be made to work in a hard drive install. Another thing to remember is that anything you do in a live session is lost on reboot.

Good luck with it. ;)
