### Post by madscience on 2009-01-27
I just bought a Lenovo S10 last week.  I didn't even boot it from the XP install, just install Ubuntu immediately.  OOTB, all hardware worked fine, including Fn keys, camera, CardBus, and Power management.  The only issue I'm having is that if I boot the machine on AC and then unplug it, the lid switch won't automatically trigger suspend and gnome-power-manager won't adjust the backlight automatically.  Fn keys for both of those issues work fine, however.  I've upgraded the RAM to 1.5 GB, as there is 512 soldered to the board and one DIMM slot.
The display on this machine, while a lot smaller than my previous notebook, is crisp and has great viewing angles.  The keyboard has a great feel to it, and I can live with the odd placement of the right shift key.
The only complaint I have is the short battery life on the 3 cell battery, but I'm rarely away from the mains for long and I get > 2 hours.
The biggest reason I picked this machine was build quality.  My previous notebook is an Asus, and the hinge broke internally and split the lid.  This netbook is solid and does not look or feel like a "toy".
