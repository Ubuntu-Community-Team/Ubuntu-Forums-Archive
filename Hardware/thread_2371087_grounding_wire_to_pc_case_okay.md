### Post by jeremy31 on 2017-09-10
I would usually frown on grounding something to a computer case but another ebay auction had these for instructions
> RadioShack Cable Modem Surge Protector 61-2145

Cat.No. 61-2145

Installation

Your RadioShack Cable Modem Surge Protector is the ideal tool for 
protecting your cable modem against surges, such as electrostatic 
discharge and ground surge.

          If your home does not have a three-conductor wire configuration
          (hot, neutral, and ground), then attaching your surge protector
          to an AC outlet will not provide proper ground protection.
          Attach the ground wire to the back of your computer.

1.  Turn off your computer.  Then turn off your cable modem's power.

2.  Disconnect the data cable from your cable modem and connect it to the
    surge protector's IN F connector.

3.  Connect the surge protector's OUT F connector to your cable modem.

4.  Use a screwdriver to loosen the screw on a nearby grounded AC outlet
    wall cover and slide the end of the surge protector's ground wire onto
    the screw then tighten the screw.

    CAUTION: Do not attach the ground wire to an AC outlet that does not
             have a grounding slot because the surge protector will not be
             properly grounded.

5.  Follow the instructions supplied with the modem for connection to your
    computer.
Those instructions don't make any sense to me after thinking about it, if you don't have three wire construction, connecting it to the back of the computer isn't going to provide a ground.  If you have three wire install, you should be able to ground it through the computer case
