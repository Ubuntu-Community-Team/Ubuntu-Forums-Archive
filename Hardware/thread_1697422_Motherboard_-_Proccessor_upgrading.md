### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-02-28
Try going on the Asus web site. There you will find this:

> Intel® Socket 1155 for Intel® 2nd Generation  Core&#8482; i7 Processor/Core&#8482; i5 Processor/Core&#8482; i3 Processor/ 
   Support Intel® 32nm CPU

So, that is three CPUs that this motherboard will run i7, i5 and i3.

You could also do some research on what Intel says about the Socket 1155 and the CPUs that fit in it and any CPUs that Intel might produce in the future that will fit in it.

You could do the same with AMD CPUs. The key to this is the socket size and what fits in it. You will find that all motherboards will accept a range of processors.

If you check out some on-line retailers look for the specifications of the motherboards.

### Post by Artemis3 on 2011-03-01
You can tell because the processor is not soldered in, but in a socket that allows it to be replaced, and thus, upgraded. Most mobos are like this.
