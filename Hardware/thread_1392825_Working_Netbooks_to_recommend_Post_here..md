Hi all,

I have been raking the internet for information about netbooks that will work with Ubuntu Netbook Remix (or indeed any other up-to-date netbook orientated Linux distro) without any major hacking, and have found it all a bit disjointed.

Bluetooth works - tested it last night. The netbook hibernates on shutting the lid and wireless reconnects on opening. All the Function keys do what they are supposed to.

Running UNR 9.10 it works very well - the only issues are:
1. The integrated webcam isn't recognised - it's a Bisoncam Pro and there may be ways of hacking it better but I've yet to crack it. An external USB Technika webcam works fine with it.
2. I have not tested the onboard Memory Card Reader (but I have to reason to expect it not to work).

NOTES: The unit came preloaded with UNR 9.04 which worked fine until updated when it lost its wired ethernet ability. A fresh install of UNR 9.10 via Memory Stick (along with changing my wireless security from WEP128/Shared Key to WPA) solved the problem.

Please recommend away...:D

### Post by garryknight on 2010-01-28
**EeePC 701 4G, Kubuntu 9.10 full version installed from scratch**

Almost everything onboard seems to work without any problems with the possible exception of the onboard mic. While my 2 generic headsets (one "leave it plugged into the PC-sized", one "carry it in my rucksack and plug it into the netbook-sized") work with Audacity and Skype, I couldn't get the onboard mic to work with either, but to be fair, I didn't spend very much time trying and I probably won't have time to in the near future. In any case, I can't see myself sitting in the coffee bar shouting into a tiny hole situated underneath the front of the netbook.

WiFi works fine, webcam works with Cheese and in the Options dialog of Skype but I haven't tried it in a live call; no reason to think it won't work, though. All function keys work, though it won't hibernate to SSD as I turned off swap (the SSD is only 3.7 GB). Also my web dongle (Huawei E220) works fine with the Vodafone Mobile Connect software. It would probably work with KNetworkManager but I haven't bothered to try; I'm not one of those "if it works, fix it until it doesn't" types.


**Advent 4211-B (MS Wind clone), Kubuntu 9.10 installed as an upgrade to 9.04**

Works pretty much as well as my EeePC with 2 differences: 1) the Microdia webcam doesn't work, though I'm pretty sure it did in 9.04; 2) it has an 80 GB HD and dual-boots with XP Home, so it has a 2 GB swap partition and both suspend and hibernate work fine, and even the WiFi picks up and reconnects soon after resuming.


The EeePC has a 7" screen and even fits in one of my pockets (photographer's vest, not a regular jacket) so it's great to just pick up and go, and to use in the way that netbooks were intended to be used: web, email, IM, etc. The Advent (Wind clone) on the other hand, is more like a scaled-down laptop. I've used it in the field on a photo shoot to post-process photos in Lightroom then upload them to Flickr; I've used it to catch up on BBC TV programs I missed seeing the night before (on iPlayer with my 3G dongle or downloaded and played as MP4s); I've used it for word processing and spreadsheeting, and so on. I sync my data files from and to my PC using Grsync, and if my PC went down it would be my main machine - at least until I could get another PC.

I'm not a Gnome user (been with KDE since version 1) so I can't say how regular Ubuntu would work on either of these machines, but I can recommend Kubuntu; KDE 4.3 works smoothly on both netbooks, including all of the compositing tricks. And I can also recommend both netbooks, though I hope the webcam and internal mic problems get sorted sooner or later.
