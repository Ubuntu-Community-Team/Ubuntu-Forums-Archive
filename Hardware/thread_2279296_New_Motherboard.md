### Post by mattharris4 on 2015-05-23
If you are going to the trouble of changing out a motherboard and CPU (and a very expensive i5 CPU at that) why not change out everything else while you are at it (if the case is adequate you can re-use that although if you decide to replace everything you might want to leave the old computer assembled and let your kids/grandkids/nieces/nephews/etc. use it)?  The biggest cost of a computer with an i5 CPU is the CPU and motherboard, for another $200 or so you can have a new DVD drive, power block, hard drive, etc.  If you choose an SSD and a Blu-Ray drive you would probably have $350 extra wrapped up in a complete component replacement.

AMD is cheaper but be sure to check the Passmark score (do a Google search for the processor model's Passmark score) for the i5 you are considering and any AMD CPUs you think are equivalent to make sure they actually are.  For example the Intel Core i5-2430M @ 2.40GHz has a Passmark of 3268 (I picked a low-line i5 on purpose, with that score it would probably serve you well).  A higher line i5 CPU like the Intel Core i5-4670K @ 3.40GHz scores a 7654.  Here is a Passmark list for higher-line CPUs, there are several AMD CPUs in the 3268-7654 range.  This range is toward the middle of the list, some Xeon CPUs score over 10K:  [http://www.cpubenchmark.net/high_end_cpus.html](http://www.cpubenchmark.net/high_end_cpus.html) .
