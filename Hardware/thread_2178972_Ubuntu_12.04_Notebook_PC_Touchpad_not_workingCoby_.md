### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2013-10-15
Doesn't look good. Still some ideas might be worth trying (because what have you got to lose?):

email the maunfacturer

do the searches with the strategies I suggested. Might want to use a metasearch engine, rather than plain google.

Try to find out what part of the mobo is responsible for managing the touchpad and if the chips involved are bought from some other manufacturer, like you see a lot of mobos with onboard vidio by nvidia. If they are, email THAT manufacturer. They might be more willing to toss you an idea if you make it clear you're not insisting on a sure thing but just something that MIGHT work to try at your own risk.

Download as many different drivers for touchpads, of any and all makes, as you can find and try them one at a time.

You might look for a user's forum dedicated to this particular machine. Probably there isn't one, but you never know until you look.

Finally, if all esle fails email the manufacturer and tell them you want to try to write a driver and can they send any information that might help.

Good luck.
