### Post by Tip the bartender on 2011-10-07
In spite of the laptop supposedly being "supported" by Ubuntu, I quickly found out that it wasn't the case.

While my other older laptops worked perfectly without a single glitch, my x220 will produce weird video glitches at random. Usually part of some menu bars will become filled with weird colors, and the computer will end up locking up randomly, especially if it is undergoing a task where the CPU use is higher.

I tried Ubuntu 11.04, along with 11.10. The latter won't even let me use Unity 2D, and the bugs are even worse.

Lubuntu and Xubuntu don't seem to have the same problems, however I don't like the interfaces of these two distributions. Kubuntu shows corrupt menu bars, same as vanilla Ubuntu.

I've scoured all I could find on the Internet but there seem to be no tips that I know of to get rid of these performance issues, which seem to be plaguing many other distributions of Linux that happen to use GNOME.
