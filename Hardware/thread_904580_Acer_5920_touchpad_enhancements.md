### Post by tonymaro on 2008-08-29
I've consolidated some tutorials from various places into one tutorial that explains how to enhance your touchpad on the Acer 5920 laptop.

You likely have noticed that the multimedia buttons on the right don't work as expected.  Turns out, they are a second touchpad.  The tutorials that explain how to make a touchpad disable for a few seconds while typing don't work because it ends up disabling the media buttons touchpad instead.

I would have posted this in the older thread about the touchpad, but it's been locked as read only.

[COLOR="Blue"][Here's the tutorial.  http://www.ossramblings.com/disable_touchpad_in_ubuntu_with_acer_5920]("http://www.ossramblings.com/disable_touchpad_in_ubuntu_with_acer_5920")[/COLOR]

This will enable the media buttons for actual use, and set the touchpad to disable tapping when you are typing.

I also recommend [COLOR="Blue"][this tutorial]("http://linuxtechie.wordpress.com/2008/04/24/making-intel-wireless-3945abg-work-better-on-ubuntu-hardy")[/COLOR] on updating the wireless driver to enable the wireless LED.  [http://linuxtechie.wordpress.com/2008/04/24/making-intel-wireless-3945abg-work-better-on-ubuntu-hardy]("http://linuxtechie.wordpress.com/2008/04/24/making-intel-wireless-3945abg-work-better-on-ubuntu-hardy")

All in all, I'd say I love Hardy with my Acer Aspire 5920.  Everything worked straight out of the box. 

Side note: to enable sound on this laptop, enable and turn up the volume for "surround" in your mixer / volume control.
