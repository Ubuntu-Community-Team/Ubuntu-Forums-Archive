### Post by amn108 on 2008-11-05
So I have noticed a very strange thing, and it absolutely beats me (and I am a programmer) how on Earth this happens. It borders on paranormal, although I am sure a physicist might explain it.

What happens is this. I have a desk, on which there stands a regular, AC adapter powered (60W) Thinkpad T61 14" laptop. It just stands there, on a wooden desk, surrounded by papers, pens, and such. Also on this table stand two magnetically shielded, fairly large, amplified and powered (by the wall socket) M-Audio BX8a studio monitor speakers connected through a stereo cable to an M-Audio MobilePre USB soundcard that is connected to the laptop (via USB, of course). Besides the laptop on the desk, **there is a lamp**. It is a fairly basic desk yellow lamp powered by the wall socket, with a small finger sized bulb, rising over the desk.

All that electronics, more precisely laptop AC adapter, two thick 220V speaker power cables and the lamp power cord, **all share the same wall socket, through a usual splitter thing (don't know the proper English term)**.

The funny part is, (almost) every time I switch the lamp (on/off), it appears that soundcard or Ubuntu or something resets. Yes, that is right. Ubuntu then plays its funky sound, and all applications playing any audio through the USB soundcard hang. In other words, the effect is as if you re-plug the soundcard via USB port again. For the life of me, I cannot figure out the nature of this phenomena. Can anybody hint at what is going on? Is it electric phenomena? Fluctuations of power? But most of the stuff is shielded, there are no bad-wires as far as I can see, nothing short-circuits. It could be the lamp is flaky. But how does it all make the soundcard reset. Can it be fluctuations in USB 5V power or something. I am out of ideas here...
