### Post by TWO on 2008-10-03
Hello

I've been running (K)Ubuntu on my Dell Inspiron 1300 laptop ever since Ubuntu Dapper Drake 6.06. 

No release will ever be bug free but one bug has consistently proved to be an annoyance and that is the **touch pad**.

In my experience, when it is enabled, it is responsible for moving the text cursor whilst you type which in turn messes up what ever you might happen to be typing. In addition to this, there is also the problem of web page hyperlinks, live feed links or other buttons in program windows being selected without your intent. 

I have tried to use the program gsynaptics but this has not solved the problem. Disabling the touch pad and using a USB mouse stops the problem completely but this defeats the point of having a touch pad. 

A bug was filed to Launchpad on the 27 August 2007 reporting the issue:

**"mouse pointer moves cursor while typing" link:**
[COLOR="DeepSkyBlue"][https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/xserver-xorg-input-mouse/+bug/135062](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/xserver-xorg-input-mouse/+bug/135062)[/COLOR]

From that date until now, users that update with every new release would have gone through 3 distributions. 

This bug relates specifically to Dell laptops but it is probable that other models may also be affected. 

I imagine that there will be Dell and other model laptop users who may not experience the problem, but may I ask of those that do to contribute to the bug report by giving details of your problem on Launchpad? If you have the expertise to find a solution to the problem, please join Launchpad and help to find a solution!

Also, if there are any Ubuntu Brainstorm users out there, may I ask that you put in a good word for this problem to be resolved?

I know there are hundreds of persistent problems out there, but as laptop users, this one is a really problematic aspect of the (K)Ubuntu experience. 

Many thanks

TWO
