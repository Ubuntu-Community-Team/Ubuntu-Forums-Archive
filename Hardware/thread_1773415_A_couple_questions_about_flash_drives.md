### Post by johngreth on 2011-06-02
Theoretically it should never be a problem to remove a drive even with files open because of all the buffering and write redundancy that should be used, but I've seen enough stupid designs/drivers/hardware etc to know that Murphy's Law always applies and manufacturers get lazy and problems happen when you don't do what they say. Anyway, if you are using the flash drive on a Windows computer then it is probably a virus or some other hidden thing in Windows causing the problem.

As for speed, it probably doesn't matter that much unless you are using it to move your 30Gig music collection between computers. For general use you probably won't notice any difference.
