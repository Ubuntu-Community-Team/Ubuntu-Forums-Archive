### Post by 00b00nt00 on 2008-12-19
Supposedly, LCDs are immune from burn-in. However, I've read that some LCDs develop 'ghosting', which is similar to burn-in. Unlike CRT burn-in, ghosting is not persistent, and can be cured by displaying a pure white image on the LCD for a few hours.

Regarding CRTs, I've only seen burn-in on really old displays where no power saving / screen saver was used. Actually, I think that colour reproduction is still better on CRTs.

### Post by druellan on 2008-12-19
As many have replied: yes, they have a similar effect. Its called "image retention" and happens when one or more LCD cells become less responsive to electrical stimulation due to a residual charge on them.

In practice if you left the LCD screen for too long showing the exact same image, you can end up with some kind of ghost showing a static image on the screen. For how long depends by the monitor technology or even humidity or ambient heat.

The effect is not permanent. As soon as the residual charge on the cell vanishes all gets to normal. Usually a quick turn off and turn on is enough, sometimes you will need to left the monitor unused for a long period of time.

About procedures to avoid image retention: a good recommendation is to use the powersave functions and turn off the monitor after 10 or 15' of inactivity. If you're recovering from a previous image retention, a plasma screensaver, kaleidoscopes or even Totem's visualizations can help.
