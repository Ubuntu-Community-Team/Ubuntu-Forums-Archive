### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-11-09
At those prices the choice will be a bit limited, but the thing to be most wary of is the wireless chipset, which can be very difficult to find details of in laptop published specs; they tend to put just the b, g, & n spec without noting chipset.  The graphics card detals are usually put in more detail, so you can check that.  I suggest you look for an Intel graphics card chipset, most of which work very well, and if you can find the wireless, Intel again is usually very easy to deal with.

Have a look at Novatech in the UK, which is where I assume you are having priced in £s, and also make use of their good linux forum.  They will sell any machine without an OS, so can be very useful for we linux users.
[http://www.novatech.co.uk/novatech/](http://www.novatech.co.uk/novatech/)
[http://forum.novatech.co.uk/forumdisplay.php?f=53](http://forum.novatech.co.uk/forumdisplay.php?f=53)

Apart from that there is the Linux Emporium [http://www.linuxemporium.co.uk/hardware/hardware-laptops.html](http://www.linuxemporium.co.uk/hardware/hardware-laptops.html) but they seem to be more expensive, though great quality, from what I hear.
