### Post by wirepuller134 on 2009-04-08
In a pinch in the field we did this.  We put a small temp sensor on the cpu heat sink and used a separate power supply for the fans.  We put the temperature sensor in series to both fans.  I don't have a part number for the temp. sensor but we got it from an old desktop with an after market fan on the cpu.  It worked until we could get back to the shop and fix it correctly.
   I understand the drowning of the laptops, some of the processing plants we have had to work in use large water chillers.  We have had more than one take a bath and had to wait 45 minutes for it to come out the other end.  Those ones didn't fair to well.  The most problems we run into, is sanitation workers spraying our equipment down.
