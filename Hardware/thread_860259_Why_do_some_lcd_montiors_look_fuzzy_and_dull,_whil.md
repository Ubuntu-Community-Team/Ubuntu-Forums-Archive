### Post by silkstone on 2008-07-15
22" widescreens run at 1680 x 1050. At least, all the ones I've come across do!

Regarding contrast ratio, since black can't be darker than black, the only way to get very high contrast ratios is to make the white brighter. That's fine perhaps for videos and gaming, but it's far too bright for office applications, photo processing and anything where you're working on a white background. It's usually necessary to turn down the brightness (or the contrast if that controls the white point) to allow comfortable viewing, in which case there's no real need for a very high contrast ratio in the first place.

A very fast response time is good for videos and games, but not necessary otherwise. A response time of 8ms is OK for most things.
