### Post by MauiKaren on 2009-12-02
After I ran my laptop battery down to nothing, my System76 laptop (many, many years old) wouldn't turn on -- I mean NOTHING, no lights even when plugged into AC. (Apparently one of our frequent Maui power surges tripped something in the electronics)

Fix was to reset the CMOS.

The System76 tech support guy responded INSTANTLY and solved my problem.  If you want to get an Ubuntu computer that has fantastic and quick tech support, I can really recommend System76.  I think my old Darter is one of the first that they made years ago and their tech support is just as good now as it was years ago.

Thank you System76.

To reset you CMOS:

old Darter - unplug, take out battery, stick a paper clip in the teeny tiny slot at the front right corner of your computer (you can see it on the same surface as you keyboard and touchpad but well over to the right bottom)

New Darter - 1. Remove your battery and ac adapter.
2. Remove the largest panel on the bottom of your computer.
3. Remove both memory cards (just press the retainer arms outward, and the cards will pop up).
4. Near the memory socket is a small white connector attached to the motherboard. It has a black wire and a red wire going into it. Remove the connector from the motherboard.
5. Wait about a minute.
6. Replace the white connector, your memory cards, the panel (don't forget to plug in the cpu fan).
7. Replace only your ac adapter and start your computer.
