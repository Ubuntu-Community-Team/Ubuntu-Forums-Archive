### Post by Petro Dawg on 2012-08-29
Yes they do have a natural read/write life time expectancy and can "wear out", however that wear will probably result in corrupted data and bad sectors, not immediate full failure.   While they are not too susceptible to physical wear, they are sensitive to electrical surges, its possible someone plugged it in and it received a surge that fried it.

> If security people have analyzed it, could they have screwed it up in some way? What on earth could they have done to it?

It's very easy to destroy a USB flash drive.  You can ESD one (destroy it with Electro Static Discharge).  All it takes is a tiny static shock and you will destroy all the semi-conductors on it.  (E.g. walking on a carpet.)  I have destroyed a few by accident and known immediately, because I felt the spark.

I am totally not surprised your drive was destroyed.  Something small like that, left at an embassy???  It could have been a threat, a listening device, whatever; they could have done all kinds of security tests on it.
