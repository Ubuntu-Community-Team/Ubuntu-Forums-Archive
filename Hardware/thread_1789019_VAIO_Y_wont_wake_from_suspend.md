### Post by 3602 on 2011-06-23
I have the same issue on another brand of computer. This is mostly a BIOS incompatibility. You *may* have to risk a BIOS update (flash) if this is an important issue. However, please note that flashing a BIOS poses a number of problems:
* The problem may not be solved at all
* The problem may get worse (mine did)
* Your computer may be no longer compatible with the Linux OS
* Even if you prepare for unforeseen consequences, a BIOS flashing may result in a destroyed BIOS assembly. Since most of such assemblies are board-carried, this would mean that your motherboard is wasted.
I regret to inform you that living with these inconveniences is part of the fun.
Welcome to Linux.
