### Post by weatherman2 on 2014-09-03
In my experience, you can use old, slow hardware (4-5 years old, anyway) for a media server without issue.  If you plan to hook it up to a TV, an HDMI port would be nice, and if your motherboard doesn't have one, you'll need to get a video card that does.

The biggest drawback of using old hardware for a media server - assuming it is in your living space where you watch TV or listen to music - is that older hardware is more likely to use more power and run hotter than modern hardware.  The more power a CPU uses, the faster the fan runs...the more noise it makes, in general.  Newer hardware is much more power efficient.  The video is integrated directly into the newer CPUs and that saves a lot of power.

If you don't already have a motherboard, I'd probably start over and get a modern CPU that uses less power with a motherboard to match.  Even a Celeron might work fine for a media server.

### Post by weatherman2 on 2014-09-03
I'm not a big fan of AMD CPUs, so if you want one, ask for a recommendation from someone else.  The newer Intel Atoms - being marketed as "Celeron" recently - are multi-core, multi-threaded, and will not be very fast but should be extremely low power - and fairly cheap.  You may find them integrated directly onto the motherboard, a new trend that will probably be the norm in 2-3 years with most CPUs and motherboards.  Older Ivy Bridge-based Celerons (and "Pentiums") will be a lot faster and may be had for $50 or less - but you still need a motherboard.

If you want to try to use your older hardware instead, compare the price of buying a motherboard - and RAM(?) and video card(?) vs. buying one with integrated HDMI, etc.  If you can save a lot of money and build it with mostly what you already have, do it; if it costs only a little more for a much mower power-efficient system, do that instead.
