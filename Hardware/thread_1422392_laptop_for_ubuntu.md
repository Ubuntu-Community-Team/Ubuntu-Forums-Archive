### Post by TBABill on 2010-03-05
Way too broad a question because you did not state your needs - high power, lots of RAM, minimal cost, big or small size factor, casual user or power user?
 
My recommendation would be to stick with one with a well supported video driver, and if possible a well supported wifi as well. Those seem to be the biggest challenges, although there are others. nVidia will now have the open source driver so you'd likely be safe with an nVidia card, although new does not always mean supported. But nVidia does a great job supporting Linux compared to ATI.
