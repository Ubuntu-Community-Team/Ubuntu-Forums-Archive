### Post by bob120 on 2015-04-21
This applies to Brother Model MFC-J475DW, it will work for all cartridge types having a moving float inside (visible).
This is only a temporary fix as colors are still used up in "print head cleaning". (or permanent!)

There is liquid remaining in the empty yellow and blue cartridges (C), how long this will last for head cleaning is not known yet. Place tape over the open ink port, angle the C the same as installed, then rotate to the left so the ink port is up, watch for the float indicator, see the on or off positions.
This float has a circuit inside that matches the contacts on the outside (I guessed this). Possibly a magnetic sensor, something generating a minute electrical current causing the printer to quit when the ink gets low. How doesn't matter as the solution works to bypass the off position.

There are several methods to lock the float in an ON position, drill and glue or melt a hole to lock the float. I used a small pointed file, heated on my electric stove, I will silicone the holes later today to stop evaporation. 
Reinstalled both Y & B C's, selected new C on the screen, results in "full cartridges" for Y & B.
