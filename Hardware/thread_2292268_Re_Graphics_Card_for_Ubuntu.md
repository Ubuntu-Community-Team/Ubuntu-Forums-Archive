### Post by Old_Printer on 2015-08-26
Can I jump in here? Just a comment, something I learned. It may not apply, but it may. I work with fonts all the time, and I need a pretty good level of clarity. HDMI from the computer to the monitor is nice -- it can even take sound to your monitor speakers, but your monitor is sort of treated as a television. The computer controls the resolution. When you use VGA and DVI, the monitor tells the computer what to do. Using HDMI for typesetting was a bit blurry, where VGA and DVI are crystal clear. If I were watching movies, and perhaps gaming on my system, I would definitely go with HDMI. For typing, I go with VGA -- and run an audio signal wire from my sound card to my monitor. Newer DVI setups can deliver sound with the audio.

I could be wrong on this, but it's what I've learned here with my experiences. If anyone wants to tweak my understanding, I don't mind at all. Not a bit. I like to figure out why things do what they do.

Peace,

Old_Printer

### Post by efflandt on 2015-08-27
I would not expect (and have not experienced) any difference between HDMI and digital DVI on my 32" 1080p HDTV, since both use the same digital signal and can communicate their capabilities to the source with the EDID. I am actually using a DVI to HDMI cable (which can do audio, but I use 2.1 PC speaker system with subwoofer). Properly adjusted VGA can look just as good, but may require adjustment of sizing and centering if your display does not have a way to automate that. My LG HDTV has a mode called Just Scan (I think Samsung calls that Fill Screen) that automatically sizes/centers whatever signal it is receiving for DVI or HDMI, but not for VGA.

If you were planning on doing any gaming, nvidia does work best with Steam. But the GTX cards would be better for gaming than GT cards. The new 750 cards with Maxwell chip are pretty efficient (limited to 60 watts max), so the desktop in my sig with MSI Twin Frozr Gaming GTX 750 Ti OC uses a total of only 150 watts max (AC input measured with Kill A Watt meter).
