### Post by Dark_Stang on 2009-05-27
After you connect to the add-hoc network you'll want to configure the printer through your web browser. You can do this by typing in it's IP address into your web browser. Then you'll want to secure the wireless on the printer (WEP please, HP has some issues with WPA on their printers). And then every time you want to print you'll have to connect to it to print.

To be honest, Ad-Hoc printing is a massive pain in the ***. I'd just get a wireless router if it will work in your situation.
