### Post by RichardUK on 2009-10-18
XPS 420
6 gig ram
PNY GeForce 9600GT (with custom fan)
Ubuntu 9.04 64bit (amd64)

Check you card in windows first before doing what I did and blaming Ubuntu for the full speed fan. 

I have a XPS 420 which had an ATI card in it, one of the rubbish ones that don't have real vram but uses a big cache on card and the system memory. So when the latest 9.9 drivers were not quite working, they did do better than the ones that come with 9.04 but still had issues, mainly the display would sometimes boot corupted, I decided to go and see what cheap NVidia card I could get.

I found a GeForce 9600 GT @ £40 a very good deal. So I snapped it up. After installing the GPU fan was on full speed. To cut a long story short after breaking my UBuntu install trying different driver versions I tried my windows disk to just make sure it was not the card. **IT WAS THE CARD!!!!!** Ubuntu is off the hock and to my shame I have to admit putting the blame at it's door. Seems the PNY card has a fan with only two wires. So after grafting on a spare fan that has the full 4 wires and spending a few hours fixing the mess I made in Ubuntu I now can not work in silence and also control the fan speed!!! :)

Sorry Ubuntu.....
