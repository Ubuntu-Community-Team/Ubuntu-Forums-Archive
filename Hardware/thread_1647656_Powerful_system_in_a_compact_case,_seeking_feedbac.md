You could have issues with clearance between the power supply and heatsink with the Q11. While I think that the SCSK-1100 should fit, even if it does you'll have interference problems with most ATX power supplies. (the CPU fan blowing 'down', and the power supply fan sucking 'up' in the opposite direction). 

If you can find one, get a power supply with the fan on the 'end' not the 'bottom'. Or you could get a slightly bigger case and microATX, then you can run a bigger, cooler heatsink/fan, whatever power supply you want and get better case cooling. (no, I'm not expecting you do go for this, but it never hurts to try). Silverstone TJ08s look pretty nice- 

[http://www.silverstonetek.com/products/p_photo.php?pno=tj08&area=usa](http://www.silverstonetek.com/products/p_photo.php?pno=tj08&area=usa)

If your after the absolute maximum performance, getting the fastest RAM you can then underclocking it to the fastest yur motherboard supports is generally the way to go (compability issues notwithstanding). BTW, CAS rating is important. You would be better of with DDR3-1666 CAS 5 than DDR-3 2000 CAS 8. Also, if you get high MHz RAM, as you drop the MHz you should be abel to decrease the CAS rating (lower CAS = faster) 

8400GS? Noes! Your right, the current cards do run cooler. Well, for consumer level cards anyway, the gaming beasts are still hot, power hungry and huge. A GT210 should do the job, a GT220 would be as far you would need to go now, if your not doing some fairly serious gaming.

### Post by fjgaude on 2011-01-17
I've had fun getting all the stuff into the PC-Q11, but done right it is a beauty both inside and out.

The key is the SILVERSTONE ST45SF 450W SFX12V 80 PLUS BRONZE power supply. It's 450 watts and much smaller than the normal ATX supplies. It comes with an adapter plate to fit nicely into the ATX slot of the Q11. Gives lots more space all around, including between the CPU cooler clearance, and add-in video card. I didn't get an Intel stock box fan but ordered the SILVERSTONE NT07-1156 90mm CPU Cooler. It is good for up to 95 watts. All seem to work fine with the i5-655K overclocked to 4.0GHz and 1600MHz memory. I use the onboard video. It is fast enough for HD movies, etc. But likely not for upper-end gaming.

You have to be sliding that supply partial out to get all the front-panel wires in and dressed, and to put the cooler on after you install the side panel with MB on it. Follow instructions closely or you will have to un-do somethings to get it right, to get it complete.

Have fun!
