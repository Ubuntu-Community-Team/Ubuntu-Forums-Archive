### Post by lawfulfalafel on 2011-01-10
Hi there,

For those that google this later, I have an:
Acer Aspire Timeline X 4820TG, which happens to have an Intel HD card as well as an ATI Radeon 5650.

Now before I whine about Linux, I want to make clear the absolute travesty the drivers are on the _windows_ side. The drivers are not available from the ATI site. In fact, Acer basically made these laptops with their own mishmash of custom-outdated drivers (the only working drivers required using device manager and a sketchy zip file from an acer forum) The point of this diatribe is that I have a super-special-screwed-up card, so I find it unfathomable that the ATI linux proprietary drivers would somehow support a card that ATI doesn't even acknowledge for windows. So please don't mention trying ATI's drivers.

You are probably asking yourself, well, what does he want help with then? Well basically, in the ideal world, someone could perhaps direct me in figuring out how to turn off the ATI card and force Linux to use the intel one. The BIOS for my laptop lets me choose between "switchable" (intel hd + 5650) and "discrete" (5650), but Linux likes choosing the ATI card either way, so I can't really force it's hand. Right now I am using "switchable" because I like pretending I have a chance of it using the intel card when I boot up.

I'm sorry if I come off as rude, it's just that I am pretty angry at being tricked into buying this laptop. This Acer ATI switchable technology (I'm not really sure which manufacture should be blamed) is highly unfinished, and as if to prove the point, this laptop has been removed from Amazon after barely a month and a half. If anything, I want to ask for a bit of help, but really, deep down, I just really don't want to see another person suckered into this broken technology.

Don't buy an Acer Aspire Timeline X 4820TG.
