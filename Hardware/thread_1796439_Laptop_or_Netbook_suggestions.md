### Post by 3ncrypt0 on 2011-07-04
I have the HP DM1Z netbook that has Backtrack on it which is Linux.Debian based. I chose the DM1Z because its dual-core AMD Radeon.....It has 4G RAM and 500GB HD. Its good for gaming also.
You can order the base model from HP with 2g RAM and a 250 GB HD. There are just a few other netbooks that can hang with the DM1Z. If you want a cheaper model, go for one with the N455 processors or higher, but there are lots of better processors than the N455..... plus get a dual-core.....buy the best for the money you want to spend.
The DM1Z passed other netbooks in benchmark tests. You may have to install some drivers, such as wireless drvers to make the internal wireless card work in Ubuntu, but also you can install your own wireless card and/or use a usb device, or do a dual boot install of Ubuntu. At any rate there are lots of support for any Netbook to install Ubuntu. :D
