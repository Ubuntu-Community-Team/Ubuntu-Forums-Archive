### Post by pirateghost on 2010-02-03
A video cards bit-width has ZERO to do with your OS and everything to do with the speed at which it can process video data.

put the nvidia back in.  a 64bit video card is actually on the low end of the spectrum....the big boys on the block are running 512 bit, 448 bit, and in the case of the nvidia gtx295 - 896 bit (448x2)

find me an 896bit OS.  ;)

### Post by pirateghost on 2010-02-03
the video card does not care what OS is running.  as long as the OS can communicate to the card through the PCI/PCI-express BUS.  The OS may care about drivers, but thats a completely different story.

that card will work on any machine that has a pcie slot.
