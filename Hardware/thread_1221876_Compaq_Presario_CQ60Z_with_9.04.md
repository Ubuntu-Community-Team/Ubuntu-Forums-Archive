### Post by rgb1701 on 2009-07-26
The Wifi chip in the CQ60 from Walmart is the Atheros AR5BXB63, which appears to work under Ubuntu 8.04+

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=865971&page=4](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=865971&page=4)

[http://blog.rtg.in.ua/2008/11/madwifi-hal-dkms-mini-how-to.html](http://blog.rtg.in.ua/2008/11/madwifi-hal-dkms-mini-how-to.html)

The wifi card is easily removed via a panel on the bottom, so it may be possible to replace with a wifi card that is better supported than the Atheros (full speed/power, default DKMS support, etc)

You could also just use a good USB wifi adapter with antenna for better speed/range.

I have used these with great plug and play success on Ubuntu-

[http://www.geeks.com/details.asp?invtid=PT-H5D&cat=WLS](http://www.geeks.com/details.asp?invtid=PT-H5D&cat=WLS)

Excellent range.  Just use 1' USB extension cable and some Velcro to mount to the back of the screen when needed, and remove when you move the machine around- you get FAR better range/signal strength than any internal wifi adapter on most notebooks...

### Post by rgb1701 on 2009-07-29
> **ignavia said:**
> I have the walmart laptop too, and I have video problems. Which nvidia package do I need to install to get the 8200M working? None show up in the hardware drivers panel.

The Nvidia 180.xx driver installed fine for me in 9.04 via the Hardware Drivers panel, which should pop up in the systray a few minutes after you boot to the desktop after installing 9.04.

If it doesn't pop up automatically, just go to Admin menu and select Hardware Drivers there.

One thing I noticed- I think it's better to install the Nvidia driver AFTER installing all the auto-updates (security, etc) for 9.04 FIRST, then rebooting, then installing the Nvidia driver either from the Hardware Drivers panel (should be v180.xx as of 7/28/09) or the installer at nvidia.com if you want the latest driver, which is 185.18.29 as of this morning-

[http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux_display_ia32_185.18.29.html](http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux_display_ia32_185.18.29.html)
