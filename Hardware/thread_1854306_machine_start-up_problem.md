### Post by diasf on 2011-10-04
You have a hardware problem. A serious one. 
(It will probably require the replacement of parts, so my advice is take the machine somewhere to be repaired).

Usually the motherboard would beep the errors, but either way that can be processor going nuts, memory, motherboard or even power source.

And do not try to boot it, you are probably making things worse. If it's a power problem, all that booting and rebooting might "spread" the problem to another components.

### Post by diasf on 2011-10-04
You have a *hardware* problem.

If it was booting, I could suggest memtest+, check temperatures etc. But it isn't.

That means, for instance, that your power source can have a defect. If you try to keep booting (to see a log that doesn't have anything to do with the problem), it can overheat and start a fire. Yes, physical fire. And yes, I saw it happen a couple times.

So grab your PC and take it to a repair shop. Let them deal with it, because as it seems, you would not be very comfortable taking your PC apart and part of the solution is to replace systematically parts of your computer to locate the defect.
