### Post by akashiraffee on 2010-02-15
Your hardware won't get damaged -- it will shut down when it overheats.  Meaning it will just get steadily harder to use, if I'm right about the problem.  I am guessing it is at least a year or two old.  

It may have been less apparent under windows because you were not monitoring the temp and windows may have made it's own decisions about the fan, whereas now most likely it is just the BIOS's own perogatives.

Read this:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=8829411&postcount=3](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=8829411&postcount=3)

I had a similar problem with a Satellite and apparently it is very common due to the design of the heat sink.   Mine got to the point where the fan stayed on and the machine would not stay on for more than 20 minutes.

After cleaning it was good as new.  However, DO NOT take this to mean that applying compressed air or a vacuum cleaner will help (they may tell you that at a store to sell you a can of air).  The dust clog is right inside around the processor and there is NO POSSIBILITY of blowing or sucking it out.

You are in luck in the sense that there are lots of illustrated how-tos online for cleaning out Satellites.  Look around until you find the exact model.  Give yourself at least 4 hours to do this, quietly, on a large surface, with the proper tool (little weeny philips head screwdriver).   I bent the little white do-hickey at the back that works like a fridge light switch with th top up/down:  after I put it back together it did not turn on, I thought I was going to cry -- it was just that little thing hadn't popped up right.  I still need to flick it with my thumb after the lid has been down...but everything else works great, the overheating stopped completely.
