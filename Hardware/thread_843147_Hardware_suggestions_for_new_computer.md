### Post by markbuntu on 2008-06-28
If you are compling c++, large datas sets, etc, you should look for something with a quad core processor and at least 4GB of DDR3 dual channel ram. If you are going to use 64bit you could up that to 8 or 16GB. Make sure you get a mobo that can use that much ram, a lot of them are limited to 4GB. Make sure you get SATA/Raid drives, they are far faster than PATA.

At least a 300watt ps. You can use the onboard sound and ethernet etc. Most high end mobos come with 5.1 or 7.1 sound, ethernet, etc built in.

A plugin video card with 512MB ram will offload some of the cpu/bus load and free up system ram, besides, they are cheap these days, less than $100 if you are not needing to play the latest games. Something that can handle dual monitors would probably be very useful for what you do. Some mobos come with very good built in video so this is not strictly necessary. 

You could probably do all that for about $600-700 minus the monitors. Newegg has a wide selection of barebones computer boxes with ps, mobo, cpu, etc already put together for you.
