### Post by kurt18947 on 2012-12-21
For me there's Thinkpad and everything else.  I really like have 2 pointing devices and the nice keyboard.   I have the older series T61 and it's perfect with *buntu.  You might take a look at the hardware forum.  One of the stickies is 'laptop compatibility list', another is 'laptop incompatibility list':p.   I had an Asus EeePC netbook which was plug 'n' play.  

-I'd stay away from the glossy/shiny screen; they seem to reflect overhead or wall mounted lights too much and really show smudges or finger prints.

-I'd avoid 'hybrid' graphics, some are able to get them to work but they seem like a work in progress at this point.  

-I'd make sure I could disable 'secure boot' if it's present.  

-Personally I'd prefer to avoid the complications of UEFI BIOS if possible but it can be made to work with a little research.
