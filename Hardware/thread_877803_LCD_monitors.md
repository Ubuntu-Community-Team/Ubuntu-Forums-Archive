### Post by Rhubarb on 2008-08-02
LCD monitor backlights last more than a year.  They should last for at the very least 5 - 10 years or more (about the same as for a CRT monitor).
You should always use the default resolution that your LCD monitor natively uses (or 1/2 or 1/4).  This is because image quality will be significantly worse in it's non-native resolution.
However, there isn't much of a need to use any other resolutions.
If your text and icons are too small with an LCD monitor, simple increase the text and icon sizes, and everything will still look crisp and sharp like it should.

There's nothing wrong with LCD monitors, they can be used for FPS games, for everything.
Unless you're a digital artist who does detailed painting / photography / cinematography, 3D (and even then, there are high end LCDs that can do this), stick with LCD, it's plenty fine for the job.
