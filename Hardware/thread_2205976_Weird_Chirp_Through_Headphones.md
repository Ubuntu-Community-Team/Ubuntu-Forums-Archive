### Post by justbinfishin on 2014-02-16
I have a dell latitude e6520 that is making weird chirping noises when the 3.5mm audio jack is connected. it is intermittent (sometimes every 10 seconds or so, sometimes minutes go by between the noise) and there is never any noise through the speakers when the audio jack is not connected. I tried turning off the internal mic, thinking there was feedback going on. Didn't help. It happens with 2 sets of headphones and one set of earbuds (all different brands). This is one of those dells that shocks you every now and then through the aluminum wrist rest. Could this be related to poor grounding and some sort of electrical discharge going through the audio out jack? I doubt it, as it happens with both wall and battery power, but it's a thought. Oh and I tried removing the battery and just going off the jack, it still happens.

Any ideas would be helpful!

It happens when booted in both windows and ubuntu, but much more frequently (and loudly) in windows.

thank you for your time.

Justin.

---

### Post by justbinfishin on 2014-02-17
I updated my BIOS to the new version, it fixed it!  Now it only chirps once at startup and once at shutdown. I have exorcised the sparrow's ghost from my laptop!  It seemed to be associated with poor sound quality, so I wondered if the sound card wasn't being controlled properly. It's gone in both Ubuntu and Windows now, and I can listen to music while I study without being driven crazy.
