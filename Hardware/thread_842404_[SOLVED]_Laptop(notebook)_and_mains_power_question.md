### Post by dogscoff on 2008-06-27
I don't think the problems of memory effect apply so much to modern batteries as they used to, so I don't think you can go too far wrong. Even partial charging, which used to be a big no-no, isn't a problem these days. 

That said, I am still in the habit of discharging the battery now and again (pull the power out and work on the laptop until the battery is nearly empty, then plug it back in) and I also try to avoid leaving it charging when it's already full.

I have no idea if this is a good idea or not, but it seems to have served me well in the past.

If you're really worried, look up the type of battery you have (ie what chemicals are inside it) and do some research on the internet.

EDIT: I just looked up the type of battery in my laptop and learned something new. It seems it doesn't matter how or when I charge or discharge it, it will lose its effectiveness over time at a fixed rate regardless: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li-on](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li-on)
