### Post by JohnFDay on 2012-04-08
Okay, I did some serious searching and, in UBUNTU WORKS, I came across an answer that seemed to work.  I removed Ubuntu 10.10 and installed Ubuntu 11.10.

The wireless worked until I restarted the machine, then it froze.  I read in UBUNTU WORKS about the problem.  Re-inserted the Ethernet cable and rebooted.  The computer came up properly.  Rebooted again, but hit the F2 and altered the boot sequence so that the "network boot" was first in the list.  Booted up, turned the wireless back on, then RE-BOOTED 4 times to check whether the wireless was working.  This "fix" worked!

Up and running now with Ubuntu 11.10, and wirelessly.

I have to admit, I'm not overly thrilled with the new interface but I think that, with a little bit of usage, I'll become familiar enough with it.

SO.....This thread is being closed.
