### Post by Objekt on 2010-03-11
Brother provides Linux drivers for almost all of their printers.  HP provides Linux drivers for pretty much every printer they've ever made (almost 2000 models).  Often, an HP printer will work as soon as you plug it in, because Ubuntu already has the drivers installed.

I just bought a Brother MFC-7440N.  All of its functions are fully supported in Ubuntu through Brother's Linux drivers, but it is a bit more than what you wanted, both in price and capability.  The MFC-7440N uses very affordable consumables compared to many other laser printers, so maybe there is a simpler Brother model which uses the same toner cartridge (TN-330 or TN-360).

I paid around $250 for my MFC-7440N.  I think if you shop around, you can get a Brother or HP mono laser for under $200, not sure if it will be networkable though.

A good place to research your purchase: the Open Printing database ([http://www.openprinting.org/printers]("http://www.openprinting.org/printers")).

Brands to stay away from:
-Canon (very spotty Linux support)
-Lexmark (like Canon)
-Dell (almost NO models have Linux drivers)

Hope this helps.
