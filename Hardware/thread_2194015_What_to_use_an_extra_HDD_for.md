### Post by Dale61 on 2013-12-16
We use external portable HDD's instead of flash drives now.  We have a 2TB external desktop drive for ALL major data, then we use portable 1TB drives to swap data between a desktop and laptop.  As I am the family PC tech, I also have an external drive that has required diagnostic tools, and a separate copy of Ubuntu 12.04 LTS is used to access a Windows HDD if the booting process has been messed up.

What you use the HDD's for is entirely up to the end user.

### Post by Rick St. George on 2013-12-17
I am assuming by "steam" games you mean streaming. And by Backup HDD I assume you mean 1TB or enough to handle ALL your BUs, or use something like Acronis (image BU). Having BUs handled (DO NOT use the Cloud - ie, someone elses computer as security is out the window), that only leaves the option of Optical Drive. My choice would be a BlueRay drive as then you can burn CDs, DVDs, BRs, including all sorts of media that can accompany these disks.  I can't imagine someone with a computer (not a tablet linked to some 'cloud') that cannot even burn a disk. It is the ONLY permanent backup of most important work, as any HDD can fail.

Rick
