### Post by marvlush on 2008-05-01
A while ago, my work assigned me an older D600 laptop with WinXP as my company laptop.

Rather than stick to the WinXP...I partitioned off 1/2 my hard-drive and installed Hardy (hope the IT guys dont get bent out of shape).

Install went great.

At work I have my D600 in a dock and an external 20" LCD hooked up to the back of the dock. I wanted the laptop's 1400x1050 LCD to work in conjunction with my external LCD (1600x1200).

At first, I fell back what I knew before...and tried adding Xinerama to my xorg.conf...and obviously had some problems...

Then after some googling, I remembered as of last release...xrandr is the new way to do multiple monitors.

So I tired several xrandr commands...but since I'm new to xrandr....I of course screwed it up a few times...

Finally...I read about Hardy's new integrated xrandr config tool under: 
 
  System->Preferences->Screen Resolution

DOH! Could it really be that easy? It was. Click, grab monitor, move to the right. BAM! Slick guys, very slick.

For the first time I was able to configure multiple monitors...WITHOUT sifting through man pages for command line options for various tools or editing conf files.

Ok, so...if you read all that, thanks for sticking around.... 

The *real* question is....now that I have a 1400x1050 laptop screen and a 1600x1200 external screen working together as one big desktop on my laptop's ATI Mobilty 9000...HOW DO I GET DRI WORKING????

I've tried several things...but I always get the following in my /var/log/Xorg.0.log:
