### Post by t0mppa on 2009-04-27
Sorry for possible thread necromancy, but if the OP is still looking for feedback on this: MX5500 on my laptop worked just fine on both 8.10 and 9.04 of normal Ubuntu.

There's a slight problem with the usb dongle working a bit differently than users expected it to, but there's a fix to be found for that. Also, all the special keys don't work out of the box, but the ones I often use do that (unlike Flip 3D for instance, which is just a silly shortcut for Alt + Tab / Alt + Shift + Tab anyway).

Seen a lot of people take a look at it and say: "Money well spent, the small display is never going to work with Linux." but it worked out of the box, so can't see what they're freaking out for.

### Post by t0mppa on 2009-05-06
Well, I did initially install the drivers on Windows, where everything worked and could easily be configured. So, when I ported it over to Linux, the LCD simply continued to work, didn't have to take any extra steps to activate it or anything. The only annoying thing about it was that I only managed to set either the language to English or the clock to 24h format, but not both. Temperature is on Celsius though, even though the other setup is US based, so go figure. :/

Anyway, just try setting things up on Windows (use a friends computer, if you don't have one of your own). The drivers upload the settings to the keyboard, so there's been no need to do anything after the initial set up.
