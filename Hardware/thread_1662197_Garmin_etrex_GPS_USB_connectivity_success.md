### Post by celem on 2011-01-07
FYI - here is a report on something that just works - at least with Lucid 10.04.

I have a rather dated Garmin etrex GPS receiver. While I have a serial cable for it, I no longer have a serial port on my PC, so I wanted to use a USB port. I ordered a special cable from a vendor on ebay that has the funky etrex connector on one end and a usb connector on the other. The product description on eBay is "*USB PC data Cable for Garmin Etrex Emap Legend GPS*" I paid $18.97 e/w delivery. I purchased it from seller *kawagps* - KawaMall.

Of course, the product description makes no mention of Linux compatibility, but I purchased it anyway. I am happy to report that it works perfectly. I used the cable to connect my etrex to a usb port. Next I used the [viking]("http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/viking/index.php?title=Main_Page") program to download the GPS data within my etrex, for display on a topographic map. In my case, I selected ttyUSB0 to access the etrex.

I hope this helps those of you with similar GPS receivers.
