### Post by Andrew_Lucas on 2013-12-09
Hi all,

I recently built my 14 year old brother his first gaming machine. Some of the key parts were salvaged from a machine I built in 2008, but the motherboard had burnt out, and getting an old board was going to be prohibitively expensive - so the RAM, CPU and motherboard was replaced, but the case, PSU and GPU kept. The PSU is a good quality 650w modular Xigamatek model (either 2 or 4 12v rails, I don't remember the details). The GPU was a 9800GTX Nvidia card.

The card however is now dead, and I've taken it out of the machine. Weird green lines began appearing on the screen, and this didn't get any better after reseating the card or cleaning out the fan. The rest of the computer still works well, but he's not able to use it for what it was built for without a decent graphics card. Currently, an ATI (from when it was still ATI, lol) X1900GT is in the machine...but the driver is ancient and pretty unstable as soon as anything more demanding than displaying the desktop is required of it.

Consequently, I'm in the market for a new card! ATI seem cheaper, and a HD 7850 is currently what is powering my gamung machine. Its a little bit of a stretch of the budget, however...the price is still around £113, when ideally it needs to be around £75, stretching to £100 at most. Also, I'm not sure if the PSU (given its age and relatively low wattage) will run that at full throttle. The other hardware in the PC is:

8GB (2x4GB) DDR3 RAM
1TB eco hard drive
Biostar TA970
Sound Blaster Audigy LS
AMD FX 6100


Recommendations would be appreciated! I'm currently thinking it makes more sense economically to get a high end HD 6xxx or top end HD 5xxx, rather than a low end HD7xxx. However, currently installed is a dual boot of Windows 7 and Xubuntu, and driver support might be an issue under Linux (e.g. there is none with the current card, whereas it's maintained as a legacy model under Windows). Ideally the computer should last another 4 years. He mostly plays FPSes so gorgeous graphics aren't a priority (in the way they would be in slower paced RTS or RPG).

### Post by sudodus on 2013-12-11
I'm using ***nvidia*** cards. There can be issues with them too, but I think it is easier to make them cooperate with linux for good performance than AMD/ATI/Radeon cards. Avoid the latest and greatest, and chances are better, that there is a good proprietary driver so that you can run advanced stuff like VDPAU.

My graphics cards are fairly old. The newest one is a GeForce GT 430, but I guess your 14 year old brother wants a more powerful card. Let us hope you get more tips about models, that work well :-)
