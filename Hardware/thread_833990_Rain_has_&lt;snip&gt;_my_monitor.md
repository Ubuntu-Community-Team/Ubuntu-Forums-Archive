### Post by Opensource-student on 2008-06-19
If the rain/water has been there for a day or two it may have soaked into the pixels or control units and shorted them.
I dont know what you mean by island - is there a wet patch on the screen?
You should try and get a service center to repair the screen - There is a risk of eletrocultion if you muck around with eletricity and water.
Depending on the laptop you should be able to unsrew the screen from the main unit and unsrew further. It shouldn't be hard to replace the screen, If you dont feel comfortable doing this any good IT place should be able to do it.

The promblem with the screen would be the presence of water behind the pixels where the circuts and control unit is. Capasitors may still hold charge, some many hundreds of volts -that could shock you.

To get to the point, You probably could remove and drain the screen, wherether the screen would ever work again is another matter. I cannot be held responseable for anything you do you should try and get it fixed by a pro/someone that knows exactly what they are doing if you are unsure but you should be able do it yourself pretty easily **The longer the water is there the more potenial problems may arise BUT dont rush You are more important than a laptop screen**
