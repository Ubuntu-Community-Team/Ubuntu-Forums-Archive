### Post by martibs on 2008-12-03
I have a x86_64 system with an integrated Intel grapichs chip (X3000), 2GB ram, Intel Core 2 Duo 1,86GHz.

With this system I was running Ubuntu 8.04 with Compiz enabled smoothly. I can't remember having any performance issues really, except with some of the effects in Compiz (water effects I think). But after upgrading to 8.10 everything graphical is very slow and choppy! Compiz isn't performing _that_ bad, actually, but Gnome in general is. Especially scrolling with Firefox and Nautilus is very slow, to the point of unusable. Starting applications (loading the windows) also take a long time.

I've read some bugreports of the Intel driver, is that what's causing the problem? If so, is there a fix? I've tried turning off Compiz, doesn't improve performance. It's really annoying to upgrade your system only to find out that it almost is unusable.
