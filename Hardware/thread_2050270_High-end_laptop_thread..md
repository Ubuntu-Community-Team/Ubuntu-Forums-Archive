### Post by 1clue on 2012-08-30
Hi,

This is yet another laptop thread, but my requirements seem to be a bit different from most of what's already here.

First thing, this MUST be at least easily transportable.  I already have a desktop, and I'm a professional programmer who needs to have the same system at home, office or anywhere else.  We often go to a restaurant for a code review or other meetings where something is presented from computers.

Second thing, I usually have at least two virtual machines open.  My current laptop is a dual core with 8G and I'm very clearly out of resources a lot of the time.

I'm looking for a high-end laptop.  Here's what I want:
[LIST=1]
[*]Option to NOT buy an operating system license.
[*]3rd-generation Core i7 quad core or better
[*]One or more decent but not outrageous graphics cards.  Not for gaming, but I use high definition video often.
[*]1920x1080 native screen resolution or better.
[*]Support for one or two external high resolution monitors.
[*]12G RAM or better.
[*]Support for two internal drives, one for SSD and another for spinning disk.
[*]BD-R drive
[*]USB 3.0
[*]ESATA or better
[*]Prefer 4 hours of battery or better, but I can get by with external batteries for this.  I'm usually not so many hours away from a power receptacle.
[*]I'm in the USA.
[*]I do NOT care about weight.
[*]I do NOT care about size, although it would be nice to have a 17" screen or larger for less eye strain.
[/LIST]

Seriously, I've considered a micro desktop box similar to a Mac Mini and a couple small 1080p monitors.  Or with 2 27" Apple thunderbolt monitors.  I've also considered a 27" iMac and an external 27" apple thunderbolt monitor.  Can't figure out how that would be easily transportable though, and there's no way any of this could be battery operated.

I don't know of any laptop with these hardware specs, even without the operating system preference.  I would be happy with a luggable setup as long as it's not too huge.  I commute on a train.

I would appreciate any serious recommendations.

Thanks.
