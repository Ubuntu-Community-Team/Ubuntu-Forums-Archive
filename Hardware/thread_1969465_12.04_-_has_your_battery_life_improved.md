### Post by shomati_sen on 2012-05-01
Hi,

I run Ubuntu on Lenovo x220. I haven't seen any improvement in the battery life after my upgrade to 12.04. However, the performance seems comparable to Windows 7 (according to what others have quoted as the Windows battery life). 

I get about 8-10 hours on x220 with the most common things I do (browse, write, email). I set my display to 60% brightness, have the usual parameters on boot for better power ([http://www.williambrownstreet.net/blog/?p=387](http://www.williambrownstreet.net/blog/?p=387)). With 11.10, when idling, the discharge dropped to as low as 7.72 W. With 12.04, it only drops to about 8.9 W.

Hope this helps,

Shomati

### Post by HuttonIT on 2012-06-23
I am running Lenovo X1 4Gb, 256Gb SSD, and can confirm that battery life in Windoze 7 64 is about 3.5 hours but Ubuntu 12.04 is less than 2 hours.  Windoze slows processor, dims screen and switches bluetooth off automatically when the power is disconnected.  Ubuntu appears to do none of that.  If I dim the screen there is only a small improvement presumably because the screen is LED so uses very little power anyway. Turning off Bluetooth manually gives another 10 mins life.  I think it is the processor, Intel Quad Core i5 vPro, which is using the most power and can see no way to limit or reduce it's performance in Ubuntu.
