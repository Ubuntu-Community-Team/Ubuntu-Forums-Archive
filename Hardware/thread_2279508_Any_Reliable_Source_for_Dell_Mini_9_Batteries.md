### Post by acedia2 on 2015-05-24
If you are getting BIOS problems, read the battery for its voltage  ect, you might need to look up the serial number and google what others  have done for bios preferences. Then go into your system and make sure  its on the same setting. What is probably happening is the battery is  not connecting to the computer because what your system is saying it is  and what it actually is are two different things.

This might sound ridiculous but I have taken apart many laptop batteries for their lithium cells that I use for solar application. I bought them off alibaba. You never know what your going to get though, the batteries mostly come at 80-70% compacity because they have been on a shelf for 4+ years. (they don't last forever) My suggestion is to open the battery and replace the cells inside with modern LiFePo4 cells. I am in engineering and transferring into electrical engineering so I don't know how hard this would be for someone who has never done anything like this before. I'm sure someone has done it and posted on the internet. A quick search brought up hundreds of links but do so at your own risk.
You might ask why anyone would ever do this, well your battery will have a capacity that makes anything else on the market laughable.
