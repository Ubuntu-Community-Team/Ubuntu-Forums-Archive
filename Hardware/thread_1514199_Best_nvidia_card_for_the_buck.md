### Post by SeePU on 2010-06-21
I would look at the GTS 250 card.  Try to get a 1GB ver. if you have a monitor with a resolution of 1680x1050 or higher.

That card is a bit old but they're around $100 or a bit higher but for light gaming and an all-around card, it's pretty decent.  I'm considering such a card since I'm not too sure about HD 5xxx cards in Linux.  If I was mostly in Windows or gaming in Windows, that would be the series of card with no hesitation.  I'd probably get a HD 5770.  But, in Linux, there's still some issues that aren't getting fixed yet and the progression (for fixing bugs and optimizing features) is really slow.  

You should be able to get your card working, at least to do most of what you want.  Catalyst 10.6 is supposed to be working.  If you have trouble, post about it or try the phoronix forums.  ATI/AMD in Linux lists Ubuntu as one of the distros officially supported so there are reports of the BINARY driver working for that card.  

Try the install instructions for your card at the AMD website and see what happens.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-21
GTX465- loud, hot, power hungry. Outclassed by some of teh old GTX2XX cards as well (apart from DX11). 

GTX260- entry level gamers card from the GTX2XX series. Still not a  bad deal, but if you dont play games, or only play light games, pointless. 

GTS250- a rebadged 9800GT, which is a rebadged 8800GT. Good for what it is, but pretty old.
