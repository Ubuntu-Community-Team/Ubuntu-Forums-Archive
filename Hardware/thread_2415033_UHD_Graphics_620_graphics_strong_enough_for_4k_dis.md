### Post by CatKiller on 2019-03-19
I would rather it were 4K, actually. That model was just before battery and screen technologies were quite at the point where 4K was plausible in something that small. 4K gives integer scaling to the ever-present 1080p, which means that 1080p stuff is slightly fuzzy on that screen. Not ever so, since the pixels really are tiny, but a 4K screen would be sharper still for that kind of content. But I needed a laptop then, rather than being able to wait for the next-year's model, or maybe the year after.

Dell's Linux laptops are part of an initiative called Project Sputnik, if you wanted to find out more about it. The XPS 13 was the first model they did, but there are other models too now.
