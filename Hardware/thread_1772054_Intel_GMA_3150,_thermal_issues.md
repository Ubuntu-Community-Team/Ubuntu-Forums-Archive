### Post by cafejunkie on 2011-05-31
Hello Everyone.
I have been spending weeks trying to solve this problem. In any Linux distro my netbook (Acer Aspire One AOD255, pineview chipset) runs hot. In Windows my idle temp is generally in the range of 40*C to 43*C. Never gets hotter than 52*C, even while playing games (SimCity 4 deluxe for example) and immediately cools back down to 43*C as soon as I quit (thanks to proper CPU frequency scaling down to 997Mhz). 

In Linux, I have cpu frequency scaling working (down to 1000Mhz), did all of powertop's suggestions and still this netbooks idles around 50-52*C. As soon as I start doing something as simple as say opening firefox, the temperature shoots up to around 60*C. It also takes much longer to cool down. At idle CPU usage is almost nothing and it scales properly. One day I noticed the vga port on my netbook was extremely hot (enough to burn my finger). I was really surprised as intel support has generally been good for me with previous integrated intel GPUs. Well, not this one.

My question, does anyone else have the GMA 3150 chipset? Do you have heat and performance problems? (For example, kwin refuses to do any compositing what so ever even though the card is quite capable). Any kind of 3D performance is horrible in Linux but runs beautifully in Windows. I really, really despise using Windows (haven't used it since Windows 98!) but at the same time do not want to shorten the life of my precious netbook due to thermal issues (it's my main computer). 
So, please, if you have this chipset and have managed to get it to run quiet and cool please offer some advice.
Thanks!
