### Post by BitRogue on 2012-02-02
I have a Dell Latitude E6410 running a new install of Oneiric 64 bit.
I installed it on Sunday to replace Maverick since it was older than the hardware and had started glitching. But the glitches continue even worse. This whole week its been hanging, forcing me to hit the restart button up to 10 times a day.

After some severe head scratching and hair loss, I started to notice a pattern. The disk does a half squeek/click and after that event it (usually, but not always) still keeps on operating but if I do something that needs disk access then it fails. Applications cannot be started and current open documents cannot be saved. Basically, it seems like the disk has been unmounted. On more rare occasions, the screen corrupts and everything freezes.

In summary, it seems Ive hit the seemingly popular issue with aggressive disk unwinding and parking, but the part that seems unique to me is that on parking (or on trying to unpark and wind back up) it simply unmounts the disk, inevitably resulting in me hitting the power off for 5 seconds because there is no recovery from it.

Modifying the hdparm parameters seems to improve it, as does running it on battery. But all the tricks only really delay the inevitble. The hard drive will park at some stage, and then its the power off button.

This is a work laptop so I can't afford much downtime and fiddling around. If I can't find a solution by the end of the weekend, Im forced to reinstall a different distro, or go back to (shiver) W1nd0w$.

Any insight appreciated.
