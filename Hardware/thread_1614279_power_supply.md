### Post by cloyd on 2010-11-05
I have a Gateway MD7818u. It is indirectly responsible for my converting to Ubuntu.  It is a very good machine, but I noticed a hardware problem: The socket where the power supply plugged into the machine did not seem to be holding well. It worked, but a little movement of the cord caused it to lose contact. Fearing it might go out, I bought a netbook with 2gig ram for a backup.  The netbook had Vista. This worked so poorly, it prompted me to investigate Linux. 

I've been using that netbook at work and play for about a year now.  I realized that the MD7818u was a very good piece of hardware that I was not using. So, I loaded Ubuntu 10.10 on it and was amazed at how it ran on this machine. However, the plug issue still persists. I took it to be repaired, and the technician told me it was simply poor design. Nothing was broken, but the socket was just too far inside the case. I brought it home, and am using a rubber band to hold the plug firmly in the socket. It will probably work, but is not very esthetically pleasing. Does anyone have a suggestion for a better solution? The socked is located about 1/8 inch away from the interior of the case, and it is hard to maintain firm contact.

This is too nice a machine to let sit around. 4 gig ram, and a 64 bit processor ( I am only using a 32 bit pae kernel).  In ubuntu, I've never used 2gig of ram . . . but this machine plays You Tube videos considerably cooler.
