### Post by coffeecat on 2009-03-27
By openprinting.org I suppose you mean [http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting](http://www.linuxfoundation.org/en/OpenPrinting) . That's the usual place. If you find a printer you like and it's listed on that site as being Linux compatible, I suppose you could then go to the manufacturer's site and check to see whether they list Linux in supported OSs for that particular model.

Alternatively, go to a suppliers website. For example here, in the UK, Staples is advertising the Samsung ML-1630 and under specifications is showing:

> OS Compatibility: Windows 2000/XP/2003 Server/Vista Various Linux OS, Mac OS 10.3~10.4Which means that under UK law, if I find that it won't work with Linux, I can take it back to Staples and get a refund. But I imagine it would work fine, because my old Samsung ML-1210 works a treat with Linux.

By the way - you're still showing 'Gutsy Gibbon Testing'. Don't you want to join the Jaunty generation? I'm posting from Jaunty now, and I'm very impressed with it. Prints just fine to my Samsung laser printer too. :wink:
