### Post by mollie4168 on 2014-07-30
I just bought a refurbished Brother DCP 7065-DN multifunction printer, and scanning is really important to me.  When I scan multipage double-sided documents, it will scan the odd pages fine (I go back and check the previews).  When I scan the even numbered pages, it turns some of the odd pages into the image of the even page, creating duplicates.

For example, the page numbers on the gscan2pdf document go from 1 to 10, but the scanned images that the previews show might be 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 6, 8, 8, 9, 10.  Some pages in the same batch scan correctly, while others don't.  It only appears to be an issue with the "reverse" image as opposed to the facing image, but I haven't done an extensive amount of scanning yet.  It's happened on 3 different documents that are 9 to about 30 pages.  I haven't had a document scan completely successfully on the first try yet.


I'm not sure if it's a problem with the scanner or the program, but it's frustrating to have to rescan some pages and add them in and renumber, etc.  I want to figure it out while I still have the opportunity to bring back the printer if that turns out to be the problem.  

Any thoughts?  I'm using 12.04 and I'm at a beginner level on Ubuntu usage.
