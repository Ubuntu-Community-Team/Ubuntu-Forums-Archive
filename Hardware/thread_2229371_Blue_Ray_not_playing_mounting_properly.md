### Post by T.J. on 2014-06-13
I'm sorry I can't offer you a solution, only some comments as to what you are dealing with.

If the blu-ray disc you are trying to play is encrypted with AACS (most movie discs are) it would not play properly on Linux without a licensed decoder.  Discs without AACS should work fine.   Because of the nature of AACS, and the laws regarding its use, it is unlikely you will ever have 100% reliable playback.  Even on Windows, with a fully licensed decoder, there can be frequent updates or playback issues.

If you try to add the software for AACS to your copy of Ubuntu, you may face criminal and civil penalties in your home country, unless you purchase a license from the AACS-LA.

Honestly, I do not understand why anyone would prefer BD over DVD - given all the problems that the AACS causes.
