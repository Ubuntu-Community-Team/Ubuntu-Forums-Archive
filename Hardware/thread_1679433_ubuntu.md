Welcome to the forums!

_*Please*_ do some research before posting. There are many sites and many posts on these forums regarding this. Here's but one:

[http://news.softpedia.com/news/Installing-Ubuntu-10-04-LTS-141550.shtml](http://news.softpedia.com/news/Installing-Ubuntu-10-04-LTS-141550.shtml)

Please also provide a descriptive thread title ('Ubuntu' tells us nothing and many helpers will just ignore) and as much detail as you can. This will help us to help you. 

Do you have an empty hard drive to install to? Do you want to dual-boot with Windows? Netbook, notebook, desktop?

For a new comer I would advise 10.04 LTS (long term support) as you might get a smoother ride. You also get much longer support cycle (until April 2013). You can always upgrade  to the more experimental 10.10 (or install to another partition) when you know the ropes a bit better and are more able to make any fixes required. ;)

### Post by Rubi1200 on 2011-02-01
If you want to install 10.10 and you are also booting Windows then beware!

The installer has been known to wipe Windows installs out:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=10145206&postcount=15](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=10145206&postcount=15)

Use manual partitioning only.

Alternatively, and I agree with Bucky Ball on this one, go for 10.04 as you will not face these issues and will have a stable, secure system.
