### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-21
> **mactece said:**
> My system runs fine and there's nothing wrong with it, so of course I need to replace something.

The graphics are ok but a bit slow and enabling compiz really slows things down. Would a graphics upgrade help? What would a sensible upgrade be (rather than just grabbing the most expensive card I can afford)? 

I cant say for sure that a newer card will help with compiz, but I wouldnt be surprised if it did. The old 3450 hasnt got that much power.
 
As for a decent card to replace it with, depends on your budget and how much of a gamer you are. But guessing from the 3450 I dont think your much of a gamer, so here is what I would look for (BTW, only listed nVidia as I much prefer the nvidia drivers under linux)- 

8400GS (minimum), 9400/9500GT (slightly faster) or GT210/220/240. Don't bother going past 8600GT/9600GT/GT240 for normal use...its just going to be louder, hotter and use more power for 0 gain. If it was me, I would get a GT220.
