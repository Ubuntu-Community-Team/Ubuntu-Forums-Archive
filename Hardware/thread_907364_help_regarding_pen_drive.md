### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-09-01
You should still be able to unmount the drive by right-clicking on its icon on the desktop and selecting 'eject.' As long as it's not mounted, there's risk of data corruption when removing it.  Besides, the risk of causing a problem is really quite minimal anyway, even if the drive is not unmounted first.
