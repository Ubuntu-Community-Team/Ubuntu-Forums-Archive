### Post by flamingswrd on 2008-09-09
Seagate is a good choice for hard drives. I have a 500gb external and it works fine with Ubuntu. I don't know what price range a Seagate 1TB is but it would be reliable. 

If money is no object you can get a TB worth of space but for your money smaller is better. For example, when I got my drive (about a year ago) the 500GB drive was $150 US. The TB model was over $350.  
500GB Drive-------$150--------$0.30 per GB
1TB Drive---------$350+-------$0.35 per GB

Its not much of a difference but it was when money was an issue. I'm willing to bet that they are considerably cheaper and more reliable now. Consider how much you need to back up and then consider how much your willing to spend, but Seagate is a good option. 

My model: Seagate Freeagent 500GB 7200rpm, $150 @ Best Buy
