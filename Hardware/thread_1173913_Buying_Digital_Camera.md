### Post by seuzy13 on 2009-05-30
I am thinking of buying a new digital camera soon in the $100-$160 price range. I was hoping I could get some recommendations from you guys as to what is the best bang for my buck. Let me list a couple things I would like in it:
1) A few digital cameras I have worked with refuse to take decent pictures in low lighting without a flash. If you turn the flash off, even when you do not need it, it will blur the picture. This seems to be a theme in cheap cameras. I don't want a camera that will do this.
2) Most cameras also show the picture you have just taken to you for a few seconds. I would like the option to turn this off, so I can go quickly from shot to shot. I also want to avoid cameras that have a significant delay between shots.
3) Also, I edit the pictures on the computer later. I don't know if any cameras take RAW shots in that price range. (Truthfully, I don't know much about it). But that would be a plus.
4) Other than that, a decent zoom (optical 3.0 is fine) and megapixel amount (7 or so).

Thank you!

### Post by bukwirm on 2009-05-30
I bought a Canon PowerShot SD1000 ($200, probably can find cheaper) about a year ago, and I've been pretty happy with it. It does take fairly good pictures without the flash as long as you hold the camera steady, it starts up and takes pictures pretty fast, and has 3x optical zoom and is 7 megapixels. It also has lots of options you can set. However, I don't think you can save images in any format other than jpeg. It's also quite small and light, and it works fine with any operating system.
