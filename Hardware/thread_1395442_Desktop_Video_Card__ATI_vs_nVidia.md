### Post by linux4life88 on 2010-02-01
In my opinion the ATI proprietary drivers are hit and miss. One release they will work fine and then the next they will mess everything up. Although, what is nice is that the ATI cards do get open source support. I bought a new laptop a year ago and then nothing worked with the open source drivers, not even the screen resolution. Then with the release of Ubuntu 9.10 the screen resolution was now detected, not sure what program added this support. With the latest release of the Kernel, 2.6.32, ATI R600/R700 cards (the one I own is a R700 card, Radeon HD 4650) are supposed to have 3D support. Have not tested this yet. Also, I've heard rumors of battery saving support coming soon to the open source world.

In summary most ATI video cards will eventually be supported in the Kernel or Xorg. Once released the ATI card will work fine but it can be a waiting game. If you go out and get an R600/R700 ATI graphics card, open source drivers are in heavy development as I type. New features are being added all the time. But if you want something to simply work and work now, Nvidia proprietary drivers are far superior. This is just my two cents and hoped I helped.
