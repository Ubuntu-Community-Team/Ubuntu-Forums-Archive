### Post by 2F4U on 2012-12-06
> I noticed there are a LOT of Lenovo Thinkpad T61's available for just under $200... but it's NOT ["Ubuntu Certified"]("http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/")  and I don't want to have compatibility issues again. I could upgrade  hardware but I just want my installation to actually work... so I can  work.

ThinkPad's are usually very Linux-friendly. Of course, it depends on the hardware configuration you are looking at. Keep in mind that a ThinkPad, at the time it has been released, uses somewhat older components, which are known to work. It is nothing for those people looking for the latest and greatest in notebook technology. To keep it short, these are business notebooks, which also means you will be able to get replacement parts for at least 10 years.
It may be worth upgrading the RAM and, if your budget allows it, look for a new battery.

I have several ThinkPads running here with Linux and all of them run well. Just keep in mind that these machines are not made for gaming. They usually come with a low spec graphics card and the monitors are not sufficient (non-glare, not too bright).

### Post by linrunner on 2012-12-06
Hi,

the T61 runs fine with 12.04. 

Important: I would strongly advise NOT to buy a T61 model with Nvidia graphics chip (this includes all T61p's). The Nvidia chips of that generation (Quadro NVS 140M, Quadro FX 570M) have an excessively high failure rate.

Models with Intel graphics (X3100) are safe, but usually more expensive.

### Post by linrunner on 2012-12-07
I don't own a T61 myself but I set one up for a friend some time ago. GIMP runs fine as it doesn't need graphics power. Games I don't know (and don't want to know).

You're right about the T400: it is one significant step up from the T61 &#8211; which means faster processors, DDR3 RAM instead of DDR2 (cheaper), SATA II instead of SATA I and lower power consumption (especially models with LED backlight). Plus you can have AMD Radeon HD 3470 graphics (switchable). Dimensions and weight are identical (for 14" wide).

If you can get it at the right price, you should prefer it over the T61.
