### Post by markbuntu on 2008-07-17
Crossfire is working:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=854719](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=854719)

Also, you do not need to reinstall the latest driver when the kernel updates as happened to many nvidia owners with the latest -19 update.

I have an HD3650 1GB and it works just fantastic. I was amazed I could get such good performance from a $70 card. And the ATI driver just gets better and better. The latest one 8.6 has major improvements over the 8.4 in the repos. It includes the Catalyst AI which was previously only available for windows and advanced anti-aliasing controls, etc. Both the 8.5 and 8.6 drivers have been very stable.

I have no problems in both 32 and 64 bit with this card or its driver. Besides that, ATI has begun packaging the linux driver with its new 4800 series cards though many resellers are not including it yet. The drivers they release are not betas, they have their own closed beta program, they are releases and the release notes are very good about what is fixed and what is not and what is improved. They are on a scheduled release program for their linux drivers and have hinted at some very big things coming out in the fall.

For sure, ATI dropped the ball for years in supporting linux but they are now very committed. You can even go here, where thy have an ask the developers thread that actually gets answered.

[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Main_Page](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Main_Page)
