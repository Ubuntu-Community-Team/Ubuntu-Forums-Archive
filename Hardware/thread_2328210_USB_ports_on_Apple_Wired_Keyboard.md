### Post by DuckHook on 2016-06-19
> **raamizh said:**
> ...On Windows there ports are underpowered...This is a clear indication that you don't have enough power to sustain the ports in the keyboard. USB follows standard electrical conventions: if the keyboard itself is using up most of the draw from the USB port, there won't be enough left to power anything else.

For the general user out there, USB connections cannot just be daisy-chained indefinitely like some people try to do with cheap unpowered 7-port hubs (for example). As far as I'm concerned those hubs are false advertising and a real menace to data integrity, especially since so many people then attach storage devices up to them.

Powered hubs are a different matter, but they are much more expensive and rarely found in retail computer outlets because general consumers are woefully ignorant of the differences between the two and will always reach for the cheaper one.

Even if you could get those keyboard hub ports to work, I would not trust them for anything, especially involving data. You could connect your keyboard up to a powered hub that outputs high amperage, but then, why not just bypass the keyboard altogether and connect your peripherals directly up to the hub?
