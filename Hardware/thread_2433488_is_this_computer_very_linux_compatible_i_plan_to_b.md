### Post by CatKiller on 2019-12-20
The xps 13 has a 13 inch screen. There is an xps 15 with a 15 inch screen. You can find which of Dell's models come with Ubuntu pre-installed by looking for either "Project Sputnik," which was their internal name for putting Linux on their computers, or "Developer Edition," which is how they're marketed. I understand they've got quite a few now, but it started with the xps 13. 

There are other manufacturers that pre-install Linux, like System76, if you'd like an alternative to Dell. 

A useful thing to do is to check the components of whichever laptop you're interested in against those that have Linux pre-installed; you'll know that whatever part can definitely be made to work with Linux if someone's already done it and is selling it.

The likely trouble spots are those I highlighted in the first post: wifi, sound and weird resolutions. I would personally avoid Optimus (integrated Intel graphics paired with dedicated Nvidia graphics). It can be made to work, but is generally a pain. Nvidia is fine on the desktop but integrated Intel or integrated AMD on a laptop is much easier.
