### Post by cbanakis on 2011-12-28
I have always stuck to a vigorous combination of rubbing alcohol, cotton swabs, vacuum cleaners, and canned air.

Nowadays, I have a very nice Kirby vacuum cleaner, and it has a blower attachment that works nearly as well as canned air.

I do not agree with air compressors, because of the oil, and the condensation they can spray.  (Dangerous)

The best bet is to take apart as much of your system as you are comfortable with, and clean everything with a combination of compressed air, and a vacuum cleaner.

Pay particular attention to your processors heat-sink, and don't overlook your video card.
90% of the dust in your system will be on the processor heat sink, and on your video cards heat sink.

If you have pets, or smoke in your house, the heat sinks will be pretty gummed up.

When I had a roommate, he had dogs, and we smoked inside.

I would actually disassemble the processor heat sink, remove the fan, and wash the heat sink in the dishwasher to get all the crud off.  Then scrub the fan blades down with alcohol and cotton swabs.
(Keep in mind, if you do this, you will need to thoroughly clean off the top of your processor with alcohol and swabs, and apply new thermal paste before re-assembly)

Be very careful not to apply too much paste, as some kinds conduct electricity, and some excess paste can short out your system.  (Better to use too little, than too much)

Once again, I want to remind you to only go as far as your comfort level allows.

In most cases (Without smoking, and pets) you can easily get away with simply removing the cover of your system, and blowing the dust out of your processor, and video card.

DO NOT, throw your heat sink in the dishwasher unless you have considerable experience installing/upgrading processors.
And obviously, if you were to do so, be sure to remove the fan assembly from the heat sink first.  ;)
