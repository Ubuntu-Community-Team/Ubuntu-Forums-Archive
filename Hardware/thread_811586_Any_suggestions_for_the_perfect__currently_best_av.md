### Post by sshlyk on 2008-05-29
If you need perfectly compatible with Ubuntu laptop stick to the rules:

1) Intel CPU (hence Intel chipset)
2) Intel Wireless
3) Intel Video Card

Suspend works all the time, wireless restores the connection, you can connect any external monitor without any troubles. [Have two laptops and none of them have any problems with Ubuntu]

I disagree with you that you need NVidia to experience Compiz.
Compiz works flawlessly with Intel Video card, in addition you save battery life, because your laptop radiates less heat + suspend works out of the box.

In general, stick to the hardware Vendors who supports Open Source ;-)

### Post by sdennie on 2008-06-01
If you buy a Dell or IBM with Intel graphics and Intel wireless, you will probably be just fine.  Surprisingly, because of terrible nvidia mobile driver support (specifically PowerMizer) for 8 and 9 series cards, Intel graphics are probably better on compiz than nvidia unless you plan to do heavy gaming (this isn't opinion, these are numbers that are very easy to reproduce with benchmarks).  My Dell XPS m1330 is probably the finest machine I've ever owned with 1 exception: I wasn't able to choose the video card and so have been stuck with an nvidia 8400M GS.  I was VERY disappointed to install Hardy a friends box with an Intel 945GM (so, 2-3 year old Intel graphics) and find that it worked much better out of the box with compiz.

Edit:
As a fun test, monitor these forums for a while and see how many posts begin with "I have Intel integrated graphics and it's not working right".  Then find how many posts begin with, "I have an nvidia/ati graphics card and" 1) Nothing works 2) Compiz doesn't work 3) The card sometimes decides to destroy all humans within a 50 meter radius.
