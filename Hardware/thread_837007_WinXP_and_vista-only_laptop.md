### Post by amyst on 2008-06-22
You might find the lengthy explanation below helpful

[http://www.mydigitallife.info/2007/10/23/windows-xp-setup-could-not-detect-and-find-any-sata-hard-disk-drive-on-ahci-mode/](http://www.mydigitallife.info/2007/10/23/windows-xp-setup-could-not-detect-and-find-any-sata-hard-disk-drive-on-ahci-mode/)

The best solution is probably look for an existing solution that your laptop manufacture might have posted online. 

I once had a Dell laptop pre-installed with Vista. To get XP to recognize the sata drive, Dell technical support suggests configure the BIOS as well as download a dozen drivers made available on their website. 

In short, it is possible to get your laptop recognize XP, and many people with your model probably have already asked similar questions before. However the solution wouldn't be straight forward.

### Post by Lantesh on 2008-06-22
There are plenty of people who have already done the work of slipstreaming in all the latest drivers into custom versions of XP.  A quick search of some torrent sites will yield results.  I'm assuming that based on your statements that you want to keep things as legit as possible.  As long as you use your own XP serial number it makes no difference what installation media you use.  Just make sure the version you grab matches the type of serial you have...retail, OEM, or VLK (volume).  Grab a torrent, burn a disk, and use your legal serial number.
