### Post by TheFu on 2023-10-26
3/4 inch drill initially, then take the drives with some thermite pushed into those holes and ignite ... er ... from a distance. Use a normal fireworks sparkler to get the thermite to start burning. 4500 deg is cool. Probably best to have a dirt firepit to contain the heat and result while it cools.  I have an incinerator for outdoor debris, but still use a post-hole for this instead. Don't want to hurt the incinerator. ;)

I wouldn't trust a single LUKS encryption layer as sufficient safety. OTOH, 10 seems excessive.  Maybe 5 passes would be reasonable.  There are tools specifically created to randomly write data. Those would be less computationally intensive, while meeting the desired outcome.  **shred** is one. [https://www.howtogeek.com/425232/how-to-securely-delete-files-on-linux/#the-shred-command](https://www.howtogeek.com/425232/how-to-securely-delete-files-on-linux/#the-shred-command)

For SSDs, only physical destruction should be trusted.  Might even want to remove the chips and destroy each, then drop the different parts into different bins around town, never together.  If there are 10 chips, put 1 in your house garbage, 1 in your work garbage, 1 at the grocery store, 1 at the lunch cafe, ... you get the idea.  Perhaps I'm a little paranoid?
