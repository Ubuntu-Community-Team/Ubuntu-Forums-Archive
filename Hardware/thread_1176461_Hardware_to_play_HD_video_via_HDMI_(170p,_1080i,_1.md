### Post by frankvw on 2009-06-02
Dear Ubuntologists,

Can anyone suggest a mini-ATX platform that can be used to play full HD video (smoothly and reliably) via an HDMI output using Ubuntu desktop?

I am in the process of developing an application that will play HD video clips (both 720p and 1080p), probably using VLC (but if I really have too, I could use another player as well). The application will run on a mini-ATX mainboard that will be built into or on the back of a display. The OS of choice, needless to say, will be Ubuntu. :-)

So far I haven't been able to find any hardware that performs to satisfaction. I've gone through various A/Open and VIA mini-ATX mainboards, both with and without hardware video acceleration. Results varied from lack of performance (100% CPU load and slow or jerky video with dropped frames) to badly supported video hardware (noticably Intel chipsets that only give 800x600 resolution on HDMI outputs or that X won't run on at all), Intel drivers that wouldn't compile as a result of some insane dependencies (e.g. libs that are not part of any mainstream distro yet) and on the VIA board any video player will briefly work and then crash X. All these results were largely version-independent: from Hardy LTS (desktop) to Jaunty (desktop) the results have pretty much been the same.

[EDIT: for now we'd be even willing to settle for a desktop configuration... so a standard mobo with a mainstream video card, e.g. ATI or nVidia, just to get us going...)

My clients are getting worried. I am getting headaches. All suggestions will be greatly appreciated!!

Tnx!
