### Post by remu on 2008-09-19
I have had the dv2700t, and now I have the dv4t. I'll share my experiences with both.

dv2700t - The reason I don't have this one any longer is because I was having problems with the Video Card, in Vista and Ubuntu. I sent it in for repair 3 times, and each time it came back with a replaced motherboard but the problem wasn't solved, so HP decided to replace the thing completely and sent me a new dv4t. The problem was that with the nVidia drivers (Vista and Ubuntu) I would get a black screen and nothing would show, but using the generic drivers in both OSes gave me a display, though video was bad because of improper drivers. Anyways, as far as the hardware is concerned I had the Intel 3945ABG and that worked out of the box, I was able to originally get the nVidia 8400m GS setup with envyng with no problems (before my hardware issues popped up). I didn't have bluetooth on this laptop, but the webcam worked just great! Sound worked out of the box too if I remember correctly.

dv4t - The nvidia 9200m GS works great with envyng, installation was a breeze, though when you get envyng to autodetect the proper driver, it says that it doesn't know what to use, so I just did a manual install through envyng and the driver worked fine. I do have bluetooth on this one and it worked out of the box. The wireless card that comes in mine is the Intel 5100, this I had to do some messing around to get working, though it wasn't too bad (and it works out of the box in Intrepid). I'd have to search for the post which helped me get the wireless working, but it was more or less straightforward, I just had to load the new drivers and what not. The main issue I had was with the sound, that took me a little while to get working, the dv4t uses the Intel ICH9 family codec, the only way to get that working properly was by adding the pci=noacpi boot option, theres a thread that I posted in where the problem that I, and some others were experiencing was discussed, and the workaround is in there as well, I can look for that too if you would like. One thing that I haven't been able to get working so far is the brightness control, it doesn't seem to work under Hardy, but with Intrepid it does, so when that comes out, you should be fine. I haven't searched a whole lot on how to get the brightness controls working, so I might still be able to get Hardy to do it properly one of these days.

It might seem like the dv4t was causing real problems, but it wasn't that bad, and the wireless and audio issues aren't too difficult to fix. Plus the wireless is fixed in Intrepid, so when that releases that won't be a problem anymore, and it will work out of the box.

I don't know what other hardware component I should mention, so just ask and I'll let you know how its behaves in either machine. And if you need those links, let me know.
