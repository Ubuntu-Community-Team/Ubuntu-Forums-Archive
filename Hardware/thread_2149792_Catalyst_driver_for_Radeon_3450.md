### Post by dffx on 2013-05-30
I just recently made a post requesting some help with a catalyst issue I've been having on my 7970M. Through the few months I've had this laptop (with switchable AMD graphics) here's what I can tell you:

1. Desktop AMD cards generally work well. There are a few bugs here and there, but ever since the Steam launch on Linux AMD seems to be putting a fair amount of effort behind fixing their desktop-tier drivers. Experiences, from what I've seen, tend to be acceptable-to-decent.

2. Laptop cards are still a huge mess. AMD's Enduro tech -- that is, the AMD/Intel swappable graphics -- is, as far as I can tell, still experimental. I *can* get the AMD card to work, but with some significant caveats: specifically, I've personally had issues with Xrandr support both in Ubuntu and Arch Linux.

If you're going to get a desktop Radeon you'll probably do alright. If you're looking at laptops, make sure you either get one that does not have switchable graphics, or else one that has a mux switch.

At the end of the day I would still recommend Nvidia over AMD.
