### Post by Floker on 2009-03-20
I assume your dad's not completely uncomfortable with computers.
On that basis, i think gnome will be perfect (not that it woudln't be perfect if he indeed was totally uncomfortable with computers).

What i tell people who come from windows is this:
If you're not sure what to do, do whatever appears to make the most sense. It *will* be right. 

Additionally, i tell them to just click all the buttons and press all the keys, which helps a great deal. Windows users seem to have this fear that a bomb is going to go off if they click a menu item which is slightly unclear, make it plain that nothing bad will happen, as long as they read all the dialogue boxes and trust their judgement.

Windows users tend to learn routines rather than understanding what they do, you have to make it really clear as well that this is not the way to do things here, and more often than not, they will find that Gnome is just a brilliant desktop for any computer user, from six year old kids to beardy hardcore hackers.
