### Post by bmullan on 2011-08-04
I recently bought a WeTab 11.3 inch Atom N450 tablet.

The WeTab is produce in Germany and sold only in Europe.

However, the WeTab's hardware is actually manufactured by a division of ASUS called PegaTron.

The very same hardware is used by a Canadian company which sells a Tablet called the ExoPC.

WeTab comes in 2 models:
[LIST=1]
[*]16GB mini pcie SSD, 1GB Ram, 2 USB, 1 SDHC, miniHDMI port, Bluetooth, WiFi b/g/n
[*]32GB mini pcie SSD, 2GB Ram, 2 USB, 1 SDHC, miniHDMI port (w/Crystal HD codec hw), Bluetooth, WiFi b/g/n plus unlocked SIM slot for mobile
[*] BOTH come preinstalled with an enhanced MeeGo OS called WeTabOS ... VERY Cool if you've not used MeeGo linux before.
[/LIST]

ExoPC comes in 2 models 
[LIST=1]
[*]difference is ONLY in whether you get 32GB mini pcie-SSD or 64GB and whether you get SIM slot for mobile.
[*]ExoPC defaults to 2GB of RAM in both models
[*]Bluetooth, 2 USB, WiFi b/g/n
[*]ExoPC has mini-HDMI connector and also includes the Crystal HD codec in both models I believe
[*]ExoPC comes preinstalled with Windows 7
[/LIST]

On the Intel AppUP website you can find detailed instructions in how to install Ubuntu in a multi-boot setup (or by itself) on either of these great tablets.

[Intel AppUp Developer Website - Creating a Multi-Boot EXOPC/WeTAB Tablet]("http://appdeveloper.intel.com/en-us/blog/2011/07/07/creating-multi-boot-exopc-tablet")

There are quite a few YouTube video's on upgrading hw or sw for these tablets (remember they are the same HW inside).

For instance it took me 5 minutes to change the WeTab from 1GB RAM to 2GB RAM.

The ExoPC website's Community section is in both English & French.

The WeTab website is primarily German but they have an English section as well.

I installed Ubuntu 11.04 on my WeTab last nite... so far so good and eveything seems to be working including two-finger capacitive touch screen.
