### Post by TheFu on 2016-05-21
I wouldn't do just Ubuntu. Go with what the Linux Kernel supports, help more people.  You'll probably need to separate out all the major subsystems to list what works and when support was both added and removed by the kernel.

For example: [https://wikidevi.com/wiki/List_of_Wi-Fi_Device_IDs_in_Linux](https://wikidevi.com/wiki/List_of_Wi-Fi_Device_IDs_in_Linux) - wifi support, but doesn't list confirmed issues, like constant disconnects or poor range.

14.04 systems could still be running 3.2 kernels, so that would be the support starting point for me (if I were doing this).
