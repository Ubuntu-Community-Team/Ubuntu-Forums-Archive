Hi there! 

Sadly, my trusted netbook (Asus Eee, a few years old ... 1001HD I believe) has just decided to die on me. I have been running Ubuntu (think "perpetual newbie") since Warty on various machines, as well as various other distros before that (and besides that). 

Despite all the sources of information online (like this one!) for people trying to determine Linux compatibility, I'm finding that most such sources are behind by enough that current shelf items at big-box stores like Best Buy are often not addressed. (I look forward to more models being available on the new Ubuntu Friendly site.) Part of this is because there are so many models in sum, and part because often model number isn't enough to truly know what's inside -- makers sometimes change major as well as minor components, and with Linux the *particular* chipset often makes a big difference. 

I've been searching this evening for several of the model names that are for sale right now at Best Buy, in fact (because one is handy to me), and having very little luck. 

Rather than a laundry list, though, I'd like to ask whether anyone (you? someone you know?) has had success right out of the box with either of these two (very similar) HP models: 

1) DM4-2165DX (On sale for $580, regular list price $650) - 6GB RAM, claims "up to" 8hr battery  
2) DM4-2015DX (regular price, $530) - 4GB, claims "up to" 6:45 battery life

Despite some personal prejudice against HP, these both seem to be reasonably sized, amazingly specced vs. the last time I bought a laptop, even sort of nice looking. But ...

a) I've read (posts on this site and elsewhere) that some HP laptops, at least, are very unfriendly for dual-booting. That's mildlly annoying but basically fine with me; when I realized I needed a replacement laptop, I decided provisionally to keep Windows on as a novelty and amusement (and perhaps it will one day prove handy), but I don't much need *dual* boot as reliably working in the first place, and "in the first place" (meaning from the install media, not an endless game of chasing / compiling / reconfiguring) is my biggest priority. If *not* dual booting is a path to that (back to Linux only, that is), I'll take it. (cf. [http://h30434.www3.hp.com/t5/Notebook-Operating-systems-and/Pavilion-dm4-Windows-7-with-Ubuntu-BSOD/td-p/342303](http://h30434.www3.hp.com/t5/Notebook-Operating-systems-and/Pavilion-dm4-Windows-7-with-Ubuntu-BSOD/td-p/342303) -- that's from earlier this year, and about a very similar model, at least, perhaps the same one -- the user says only "DM4," which would mean any of several variants ...)

b) I don't know what kind of wireless system they have, or whether (as with my Lenovo problem, described below) there's more than one option, and you can't tell in advance. 

Longer aside, as background:

I was hoping to get away with a cheapie solution, one of the $300-400 ones, and in fact gave one a try -- the Lenovo B575, on sale for $299. Looked good on paper ;) But it turns out to have wireless difficulties; not sure if that would have been true if I'd had the version with the Atheros wireless in it, but I didn't -- mine had a Ralink 3090 wireless chip, and googling "Linux Ralink 3090" is depressing. 

I had other problems with that laptop as well (it bluescreened several times in the few days I had it, and I returned it). But esp. with Windows, I sort of expect the occasional bluescreen; far more significant to me that the wireless was not supported out of the box for any of the several recent Linux distros I tried. (Three Debian-related, including Ubuntu 11.4 and 11.10 beta, Linux Mint 11 and Linux Mint Debian Edition, and 6.0.1 squeeze, and also the newest Fedora beta.)

With the Ralink 3090, apparently, some people have it working, and many others besides me have ongoing trouble -- I'm happy to cut and run, when the solution involves compilation. What I want is one that works out of the box; I don't mind if that means G-only speeds instead of N, but my hurl-computer frustration point is low and I know it ;) 

So: does anyone have any "Yep, it worked instantly and with no tweaking!" stories about those HPs? Or should I beware of a "download this proprietary driver as a deb file, recompile your kernel, do a rain dance" sequence? Imagine I am your dumbest elderly relative when it comes to installing the OS ...
