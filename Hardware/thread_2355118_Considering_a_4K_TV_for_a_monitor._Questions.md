### Post by DuckHook on 2017-03-09
I considered your alternative when looking into a big monitor, but after much research, eventually settled on this proper monitor instead: [https://pcmonitors.info/reviews/philips-bdm4065uc/](https://pcmonitors.info/reviews/philips-bdm4065uc/)

My research showed the following:

TVs are not built to be monitors. Using them as monitors give rise to the following issues:

[LIST=1]
[*]TVs do not have the same colour depth nor colour profile as monitors.
[*]TVs have lower frame rates.
[*]TVs have built in overscan.
[*]TVs do not have DP or DVI. Only HDMI with its attendant limitations/DRM-crippling.
[*]As a result of the above, TVs should be less expensive, but any potential savings is offset by the presence of the tuner, which is superfluous to usage as a monitor.
[/LIST]
Of the above, I found the most irritating limitation to be #1. It's surprising how much a bad colour profile offends the eye until you actually see the results of a badly profiled photo, but it is more than you would think. I started out very much in your camp: not really a gamer, not really a video editor—just wanted lots of real estate. But so glad that I sprang for a proper monitor (I do have one laptop hooked up to a TV, and it's no comparison).

I believe that my manufacturer now has a 43" model.
