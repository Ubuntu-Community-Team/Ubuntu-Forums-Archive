### Post by ajgreeny on 2008-12-22
One thing you could try is adding the correct information to your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file in the format from mine, where the resolution was detected wrongly for 8.10, but is now all OK.  Use your own figures, obviously, but in this same pattern and it may work for you too.

```
Section "Device"
    Identifier    "Configured Video Device"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
    Identifier    "Configured Monitor"
            HorizSync    30-80
            VertRefresh    50-75
EndSection

Section "Screen"
    Identifier    "Default Screen"
    Monitor        "Configured Monitor"
    Device        "Configured Video Device"
    DefaultDepth    24
    SubSection "Display"
        Modes        "1280x1024" "1280x960" "1152x864" "1024x768" "832x624" "800x600" "720x400" "640x480"
    EndSubSection
EndSection
```Good luck, I hope it works.
