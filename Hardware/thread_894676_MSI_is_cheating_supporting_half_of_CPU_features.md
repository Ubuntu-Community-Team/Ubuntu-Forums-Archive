### Post by Kubunteando on 2008-08-19
Yes, cheating I think is the appropriate word here. Here goes why:

I bought a MSI Fuzzy GME965 Motherboard that MSI says it supports the Mobile Penryn CPUs. So I bought it and installed on it an Intel T9300.

But the surprise was that the lastest BIOS does not even support the basic CPU Power Management features like Frequency Scaling nor CPU power saving states. Yes, in the year 2008!!
Of course those does not work even in Windows.

I consulted it with the kernel ACPI people and a very helpful person from Intel confirmed (by disassembling the BIOS code) that BIOS does not support those features. So the problem is on MSI, with whom I opened a Case and after 3 weeks they have not even replied... Holidays? Will see how long more it takes...

Why I call this cheating? If MSI can tell that the BIOS is supporting the Core 2 Duo Mobile CPUs without not even supporting the basic power saving features, then I can tell that I can speak Chinese because I am just following the Olympic games and I now how to say Beijing. Is that truthful?

Have you had any good/bad experiences with the support of any motherboard manufacturer? Are there really willing to solve the problems?

I would like to hear those.
