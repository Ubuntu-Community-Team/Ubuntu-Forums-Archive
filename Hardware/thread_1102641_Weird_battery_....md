### Post by sdennie on 2009-03-22
The graph you've posted could be caused by the battery not being calibrated correctly with regards to its maximum charge.  As battery get older, they can't charge to their original levels but, they may not calibrate properly unless you completely discharge the battery and then completely recharge it.

### Post by kestrel1 on 2009-03-22
> **sdennie said:**
> The graph you've posted could be caused by the battery not being calibrated correctly with regards to its maximum charge.  As battery get older, they can't charge to their original levels but, they may not calibrate properly unless you completely discharge the battery and then completely recharge it.
This works with Ni-cad batteries, but not Lithium-ion.
The recommended procedure with Ni-cad is a total discharge & recharge, but you need to do this a few times. I have found the best way to do this is to use the machine until it is saying you have about five minutes left, then restart & enter the BIOS & leave it in the BIOS until the battery has drained, do this a few time then recharge it. I had a battery that would last about ten minutes & it is now good for 1 hour 30 mins or more.
Lithium-ions should be discharged for a short time & recharged, not been able to achieve good results with LI batteries though.
one of many links about laptop battery usage: [http://mobileoffice.about.com/od/usingyourlaptop/tp/laptopbattmtc.htm](http://mobileoffice.about.com/od/usingyourlaptop/tp/laptopbattmtc.htm)
