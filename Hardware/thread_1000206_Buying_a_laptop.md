### Post by buttertoad on 2008-12-02
I know Dell used to put freeDOS on computers in the past if you didn't want Windows and they even have models that come with Ubuntu now.

I think your best bet is to call places and tell them what you want. They my be able to help you beyond what a website offers.

If all else fails when you get your computer don't accept the user agreement for the software and contact the laptop manufacturer tell them you won't accept the EULA. I talked to someone who did this, sent back the software, and was refunded $200.

Good luck.
