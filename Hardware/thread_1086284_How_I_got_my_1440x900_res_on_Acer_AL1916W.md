### Post by wadelewis4 on 2009-03-03
I just accidentally stumbled into a fix for my issue with Ubuntu not allowing my favourite 1440x900 wide screen resolution. A quick history: Ubuntu has ALWAYS detected my video card and monitor correctly.. lucky I guess. But suddenly, since Hardy and Intrepid (coincidence maybe..), I lost the luck when I realised I could no longer get 1440x900. When the "screens and graphics" application was taken away in intrepid, I was really upset because I couldn't get the resolution right at all - even after rebuilding the xorg and trying every nvidia driver and envyng--repeatedly.. I realised that it could just be my monitor not reporting it's abilities correctly to the system, but could find no way to make that change. Anyway, this is what I did. I went to download "TeeWorlds" from their website.. filename: "teeworlds-0.5.1-linux_x86.tar.gz" for my system. Saved it to the desktop and ran the executable from the extracted folder. When the game opened I got a resolution settings error from the OSD of my monitor. So, with the message floating about, I clicked through the video settings menu and selected "1440x900" as my resolution... the screen flashed and then suddenly I was viewing in wonderful widescreen beauty.

There is no technical guarantee of this as it was purely accidental. All I can say is if you are having the same trouble that I was, give it a shot!:P
