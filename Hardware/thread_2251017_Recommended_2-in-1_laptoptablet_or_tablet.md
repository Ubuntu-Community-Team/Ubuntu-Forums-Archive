### Post by Mark Phelps on 2014-11-02
> 3. Input data via voice recognition(ideally) but a touch keyboard that doesn't suck will work too

Regarding the first part ...

I used to do work on one of the initial "tablet PCs" a long time ago and I found the voice recognition to be one of the most valuable features -- second only to the ability to use a stylus to not only write on the screen, but also to do handwriting recognition offline so I could transform all my written notes into text.

But ... and here is what is probably the BIG impediment to your search -- it was a Windows tablet!

And, I tried for a LONG TIME, after loading Ubuntu onto the tablet in dual-boot mode, to find Linux apps that could do the same -- but without any success.

I won't claim to be an authority on voice recognition in Linux (or more properly, the lack thereof), but what I've read has been discouraging.

Same is true of handwriting recognition -- it's just not there to any useful degree in Linux.

Regarding the second part ...

IMHO,  ALL touch keyboards suck, period!  Since "suck" is such an ambiguous term (although we all KNOW what we mean when we say it), if you want advice on touch keyboards, you'll need to be specific about what you want in detail.  We have no idea if a touch keyboard we like is one you feel "sucks"!

In my case, whenever I needed to use the tablet for a lot of typing (which wasn't often due to the handwriting conversion feature), I would plug in a USB keyboard.
