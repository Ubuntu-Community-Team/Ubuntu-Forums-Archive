### Post by tamoneya on 2008-07-26
the USB wires and the Firewire wires look similar make sure you differentiate them.  If you put them on the wrong headers you can blow out the header.  They should be labeled though so it shouldnt be a problem.  Put all of them that you have on the headers.  You most likely will have some spare USB headers in which case you can just leave them disconnected.

As for the graphics card it will do fine in ubuntu.  It is not a gaming graphics card and therefore it doesnt draw a crazy amount of power.  As for that controller it most likely just controls the fan.  When not connected the fan probably just runs at full speed so you should be all set if you dont use the controller it will just be a little noisy.

### Post by tamoneya on 2008-07-26
by the time you know you fried your mobo its almost always to late to do anything.  Symptoms you may notice:
1. smoke
2. funny smells
3. sparks
Although if you have done everything right and taken your time you shouldnt have any problems.  I personally have yet to fry a mobo(knock on wood).

You can have the ubuntu disc in at first power up but it doesnt really matter.  Unless something fails though you shouldnt need to fiddle with the bios.  I would put it in on first boot.  If it doesnt launch ubuntu then start looking into the bios.

As for 64 bit vs 32 bit there are many articles in the forum about it.  Ultimately what it boils down to in my opinion is how much ram you have (or plan to have).  If you have 4GB+ you want to run 64 bit.
