### Post by fj401971 on 2013-05-19
There's no real way you could have messed up the BIOS by installing or messing with a thumb drive.  Even if the Ubuntu partition did get corrupted some odd way, the BIOS should still show something.  

I would be willing to bet that you've got a power problem, either your charger if you don't have a battery that works or maybe whoever repaired your laptop caused some other problem internally with your laptop.  Or like you're guessing some piece of hardware just gave out randomly such as your motherboard, power supply to the motherboard or something along those lines.  Either way, it is a hardware problem and not a software problem.
