Here is the convention for fan direction:

Front fans suck the cool air in.

Rear fans blow hot air OUT of the case including power supply rear exhaust fan.  Power supplies can also have a fan in the front or the bottom that suck in.

Just remember suck in front and blow out back/top/sides.

CPU fans blow cool air onto the CPU.

If your CPU fan is mounted up vertically and not flat then you should position the CPU fan to blow WITH the ariflow of the case.

I have an Arctic Cooler CPU fan that stands up and I positioned it to blow with the airflow of the case.  Do you have an Intel LGA775 processor?  Let me know.

### Post by thideras on 2008-06-20
If you are talking about putting it directly behind the other, that would hurt airflow and create more noise.  It takes special fans (counter rotating fans) to be able to "stack" them.  Even if you were able to do that, it doesn't increase total airflow, only pressure.

If you want to increase airflow, cut those finger guards out that they put on cases and get a good fan on it.  You could also use a 4.75" hole saw to create a 120mm hole to bolt a fan to.

To answer the other questions, yes a 120mm fan will work fine on a heatsink.
