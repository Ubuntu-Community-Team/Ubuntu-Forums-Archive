### Post by mooz123 on 2008-10-27
Have you ever heard this old saying: "when God invented the computer, the devil invented the printer" ?  I think this is still true after buying 2 failing printers for my Ubuntu system: one is a Canon Pixma iP 1200, and the other a Brother DCP-110 C. Neither worked out of the box, and despite clicking through many helpful posts neither ever printed a single character. I am fed up with this, I paid for these printers and I want them to work. I am fed up with the arrogance of these companies (Brother and Canon): how many million Linux users are there in the world? Still they think it is acceptable to dump their products on us and then let a lot of good willing amateurs try to figure out how to get it working, while all the time some guy at Brother and Canon knows exactly what it would take to make it work. Why all this secrecy, do they all have shares in Microsoft or what?

I am not a personality that can rant for a long time without coming with some positive twist, so here comes an idea to chase the printing devil away from Ubuntu.

I envision a new menu choice, under System, Administration: an Ubuntu shopping guide. Here we find a list of all hardware that works without a problem, or that a normal human being can get to work. Also it could give a negative buying advice for products and suppliers that will give you a headache. (not like "Brother? Forget it, brother!" or "Canon does not care if you'll print or perish", but a bit more constructive)

The advantage is that this will give suppliers a clear benchmark and an incentive to make their products compliable by supplying drivers, which will result in a better Ubuntu experience for us and more sales for them. (It wouldn't hurt if external hard disks and USB hubs got included too.)

I have said.
