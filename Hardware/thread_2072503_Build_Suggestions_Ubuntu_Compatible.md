### Post by Jagoly on 2012-10-20
That is a great board,  I've actually used it in a build before. Unfortunately it's an m-atx board and not m-itx, which is smaller. One thing to note about m-itx boards is that they only have one expansion slot. Luckily, a lot of m-itx boards have built in wifi, so you don't lose the slot to a wireless card. So you can use a graphics card if you wish.

Plus for that sort of system (with a 6770) I would be looking around 400w for the PSU. 

Also, if you can spare a bit of extra cash for a 6850 instead, the performance boost is massive. The 6850 is awesome for bang-for-buck. I'd probably recommend a 500w for that. I personally prefer corsair power supplies BTW, such as the cx-500 which I'd recommend.

Edit: having a look around on micro center, they don't seem to have much of a range of itx motherboards. But they do have [http://www.microcenter.com/product/392376/Z77E-ITX_LGA1155_Z77_mini_ITX_Intel_Motherboard](http://www.microcenter.com/product/392376/Z77E-ITX_LGA1155_Z77_mini_ITX_Intel_Motherboard) which looks good. I'm unsure what wifi chipset it uses, I'll have a look around for you when I can (on my phone right now). ### Post by Jagoly on 2012-10-22
usually the 8 pin cable from the PSU will be 2x4 pin plugs that can clip together (for backwards compatibility with old boards). But according to the [page]("http://www.microcenter.com/product/318189/HERCULES_400_Watt_Micro_PS3_ATX_Power_Supply") on microcenter, that unit lacks 8pin power.

In regards to the power button, refer to the board's instruction booklet to find exactly where to plug them in. The front panel headers are on the bottom right of the board.

EDIT: with regards to the PSU, 400w is plenty of power, however I wouldn't trust the quality of that particular unit. Try and grab on of these: [http://www.microcenter.com/product/399979/Builder_Series_CX430_430_Watt_ATX_12V_Power_Supply](http://www.microcenter.com/product/399979/Builder_Series_CX430_430_Watt_ATX_12V_Power_Supply)
