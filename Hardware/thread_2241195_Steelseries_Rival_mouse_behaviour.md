### Post by imac_89 on 2014-08-24
So I bought a new mouse and was playing with it on OS X. Swapped it to my Ubuntu box and it doesn't work!

As it turns out, customizing the buttons on the mouse is persistent. It seems obvious now, but it wasn't just a few short minutes ago.

In OS X, using Google Chrome, the command for going forward and back in the history are "cmd+]" and "cmd+[" respectively. Ubuntu doesn't like this and only after holding them down did I see:
> [[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]]

Doh! So going back to my OS X machine, I restored their default behaviours and it works!

So just a little forward thinking to those with mice that switch between computers, be aware of whether your mouse actually stores its profiles!
