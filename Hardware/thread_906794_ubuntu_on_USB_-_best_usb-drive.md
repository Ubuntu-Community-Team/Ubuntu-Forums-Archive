### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-08-31
I have this drive:
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820609296](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820609296)
(Which IMO is a bargain after MIR)

I gave it 2 partitions using fdisk and mkfs.*, one small, one large, and it works fine booting DSL so long as the mobo supporys usb: floppy-forced or usb: zip  emulation or usb boot.

Performance wise, quite good for individual and large files, but if you want to copy over 30,000 very small files at once, expect it to take 1+ hours. I have not benchmarked its performance, but anecdotally, I believe it to be better than most I've used.

The thing I love about this drive is that it's very small and very solid. It's about the size of metal part of a normal USB plug, only a bit longer. No problems keeping on a key chain (smaller than most car keys).
