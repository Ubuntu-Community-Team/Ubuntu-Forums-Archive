### Post by bgates on 2009-03-01
I have the 900 with the 16 GB solid state drive.  I got the one that came with Xandros linux on it, but I have blown it away and installed Eeebuntu.  

No problems at all, everything works.  The one thing I really had to tweak was removing the IPv6 support, it slows down my transfer.  But, I have to do that on my desktop computer as well.  The nature of netbooks is taking them around and using them on a bunch of different wireless networks, so even if your whole home network supports IPv6 and there are no slowdowns there, I might recommend to disable it on the Eee anyway, since you might eventually run into a router where it's an issue.

After that, the only thing is that the keyboard is about half size, so it's not for everyone because of that.  I have pretty small hands (it is not obvious from my username but I'm female), and it still takes some getting used to for me.  I would suggest that even if you've decided to order it online already, still go to a store that has a floor model (doesn't matter which operating system is running on it) and try some typing and see if you think that will drive you crazy or if you can get used to it.

But yeah, I like it a lot, no issues other than my terrible typing.  It works really well, boots fast (even faster if you leave Xandros on it, and I'm still tweaking mine to boot as fast as possible), connects to wireless immediately, is light and convenient to carry, etc.  Going to a meeting?  Why not bring Eee to take notes instead of a paper notebook, it is no more cumbersome than that.

IOW: The Asus netbook also serves as my main system. Entirely pleased with this classy mini-PC.
