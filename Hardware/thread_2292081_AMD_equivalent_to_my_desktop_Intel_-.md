### Post by TheFu on 2015-08-25
Intel or AMD - either are fine. No concerns with going for one over the other, except that the details matter like power use, onboard GPU, heating, and cost.  

APUs from AMD tend to have better onboard GPUs which matters for gaming, but not at all for video/audio.  F/LOSS drivers for Intel are much easier to work with than either AMD or nvidia drivers.

Without exact specifics we cannot help.  General models isn't specific enough. C2D is vague. There must be 20 different versions some are slow and some are fast.  

Total performance is made up of a few different items.
* CPU speed - compare different benchmarks like "passmark" scores. Don't fret over less than 200 differences. For you and me, close enough is close enough.  1000 difference matters, 500 might matter - maybe not for some faster CPUs.
* Amount of RAM - the speed barely matters anymore. 4G is really the min for anyone
* Disk speed / storage / I/O controller 
* network connection speed - Don't go cheap on wifi - if they over a better "n" for $20 more, get it. Pre-check the wifi chips to verify they are linux compatible. Be very careful with broadcom wifi - exact chips matter. I have an Atheros AR9462 Wireless that works great except with Netgear wifi.

So --- I have a chromebook with a Celeron 29950U CPU.  According to "passmark" benchmarks, it is about the same speed as an old desktop I had with a Core 2Duo E6600 CPU.  I find that very believable - actually the Celeron feels faster because it has an M2.SSD for storage, not a 7200rpm spinning 320G disk.  Both have 2G of RAM.

Does that make sense?

BTW - I will avoid buying anything consumer-level from HP again. Been burned a few times - desktop, camera.  They make great, expensive HP-UX servers, however. ;)
