### Post by bdoe on 2008-12-11
After updating your BIOS, you may need to go in and select the "Load Default Configuration" option in your BIOS to clear out and reset CMOS registers that might have become invalidated with the update; then go back in and edit whatever settings you need to.

### Post by bdoe on 2008-12-15
I would just go back to the last BIOS version that worked for you and be done with it. As a general rule, I don't update the BIOS on any of my computers unless the new version has a feature or fix that directly affects me or that computer, since the risks associated with a BIOS update are far higher than the risks associated with software installation.
