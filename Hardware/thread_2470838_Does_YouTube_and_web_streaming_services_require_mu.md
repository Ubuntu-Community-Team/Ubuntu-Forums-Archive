### Post by TheFu on 2022-01-13
If you just want to watch youtube and do nothing else, get an android tablet or a chromebook. These come with GPU drivers and acceleration just for video playback.  As long as the video service uses the most standard video and audio codecs, both those types of devices handle the videos very nicely.  It is actually kinda crazy how great youtube is on chromebooks running ChromeOS.

Since there are 20,000+ web streaming services, nobody can answer for every one of them and Linux is often blocked by some of the streaming services.  After fighting for a month, I gave up and bought a Roku to watch DRM'd videos. I stopped wasting time thanks to that Roku. OTOH, I waste time trying to limit the tracking that Roku does - only Plex Server seems to be worse on my LAN.

I bet that nearly all web streaming services go out of their way to work with chromebooks and ChromeOS.

I have to admit that all my computers used for media viewing have at least 2 cores. Some have 4+.  I do watch 1080p content using a raspberry pi v2 on a projector here.  If it is mpeg2, it often stutters, but h.264 content plays very nicely.  The pi cannot handle any h.265 (AVC1 or HEVC) at all. I think the v4 Pi has hardware and driver support for that, but the v4 Pi is a quad-core SBC.

If you can outline the main purposes for your Ubuntu computer, we'd have better luck making cost effective suggestions.
