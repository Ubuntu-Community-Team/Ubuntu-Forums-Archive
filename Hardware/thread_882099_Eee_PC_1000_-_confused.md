### Post by comradekingu on 2009-01-03
Both ubuntu eee and eeebuntu have everything working right out of the box.
If you want to accomplish that with a regular install of ubuntu you need the kernel from [array.org]("http://www.array.org/ubuntu/setup.html")

[Ubuntu eee]("http://www.ubuntu-eee.com/") uses the netbook remix environment by default, [eeebuntu]("http://www.eeebuntu.org/") can be had in different versions depending on what you like. It comes in three versions. Standard (full desktop) Base (stripped version) and Netbook(Netbook Remix)

[Quote=eeebuntuFAQ - What is Eeebuntu?] [/quote]

[Eeebuntu is a customised distribution of Ubuntu specifically developed for the EeePC range of netbooks. By incorporating the three key technologies of Ubuntu, the Array Kernel and the EeeConfigure system, users of EeePC's who want to try running Linux on their machines now have an easily installed distribution which works out of the box with no configuration needed.]("http://www.eeebuntu.org/index.php?page=faq")

You can configure ubuntu or any of the two other to do what you want, they are the same with some differences between the, however you are most likely going to find what you are after out of the box with one of the two custom spins.
