### Post by sulekha on 2010-08-27
I recently read about recommendations for increasing the life span of optical media as follows:-


1) storage at constant temperature in the range 41F-68F with relative humidity 30%-50%
2) no exposure to direct sunlight
3) marking only with water soluble markers

can any one explain me how water soluble marker becomes a factor in the life span of an optical media

---

### Post by shzln on 2010-08-27
Water soluble means its not soluble in alcohol/ether/any other organic solvent.
Organic solvents often dissolve plastics (depends on type of solvent and type of plastics). 
As a result , since the solvent that your marker is based on can destroy the label that your data is written on, the data can be damaged.
