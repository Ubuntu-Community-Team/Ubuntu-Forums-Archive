### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-12-06
I got 1600 speed RAM when I built my newest PC because it was part of a package deal.  I ran the PC at 1600 for several months.  Then, I made the mistake of upgrading the BIOS and it would not boot at 1600 anymore; had to slow it down to 1333.

Turned out there was a "bug" in the older BIOS that was "fixed" in the new version.

But, in the weeks since the slowdown, I have not noticed any reduction in performance in any way. I even restored the earlier BIOS version and stepped up the RAM speed to 1600 again. Still, no noticeable change in performance.

So, if the faster RAM is available for the same price, I'd say go for it -- but you're unlikely to see any performance gain resulting from spending more for 1600 vs. 1333.

### Post by JohnSmithNot1882 on 2011-12-07
After posting this question here, the Linux forum and reading discussions on several other forums, I concluded it makes zero difference for my needs.  Even changing CAS latency numbers from 11-7 makes no significant changes in performance for anything I need.  The only significant advantage of 1600MHz 8-8-8-24 ram is it is more stable when overclocking a processor.  Laptops with over clocked i7 2920QM or faster with high end video cards like a GeForce GTX 580M and solid state hard drives benefit from this 1600MHz ram.  Placing the same ram in a laptop with an i7 2670QM, GeForce 560M (1.5GB) and 7200 RPM hard drive is not a good investment.  The gain is probably zero due to the hard drive and certainly not noticeable[COLOR=Black].  I am going to avoid the 1600MHz ram and buy my laptop with the 1,333MHz.
