### Post by c0rd3l1a on 2012-03-04
I have an Asus Eee PC 1015 netbook running Ubuntu 11.10. Everything was working quite well (with 4+ hour battery life) before 2 days ago. Then I neglected to plug it in and the battery drained after many hours, forcing the computer to shut down. I plugged it in when I discovered this. Since then, the computer does not recognize the AC power. I left it plugged in overnight. The light on the power block lights up, but the light on the computer indicating that it is plugged in does not light up when the computer is off or when the computer is on. I can turn the computer on and it boots fine, I can log in, I can get online, but the battery indicator says "Battery is discharging 0%" even though it is plugged in to AC power and within about a minute it shuts down. If I remove the battery and keep it plugged in to AC power, the computer will not boot at all. I'm not sure what to do at this point. Could there be a problem with the software at all? The power block seems to be fine (as it lights up) but perhaps the problem is the other half of the power cord, or possibly the port on the computer itself? Any ideas or suggestions?

### Post by 2F4U on 2012-03-04
If the battery doesn't even charge when the computer is shutdown, I would say it is not a software problem. My guess would be that the battery is damaged.

### Post by Irihapeti on 2012-03-04
A damaged battery wouldn't be causing problems when not connected. More likely, there's a problem with the netbook itself or the charger.

The plug on the charger (the one that plugs into the netbook), or the wire leading to it, might be damaged. That happened to me with an EeePC 900. I replaced the charger (couldn't get a new plug) and everything worked fine.

### Post by c0rd3l1a on 2012-03-05
After further inspection and comparison with detailed photos online, I've determined that a little piece of metal from the DC IN port on the computer has broken off and is stuck in the plug. Now to go about figuring out how possible it will be to replace that myself. Thanks for your ideas, everyone.
