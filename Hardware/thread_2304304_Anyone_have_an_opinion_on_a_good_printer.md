### Post by kurt18947 on 2015-11-25
Any machine with decent Ubuntu support will fit the bill. How heavily will this machine be used?  Most 'business class' machines will have ethernet, the light duty home use machines may have only wifi & USB. Does your client need color? I have Brother inkjet and Samsung laser MFDs. Both are easy to set up and reliable. HP printers & MFDs have an excellent reputation with linux. Canon & Epson get good reviews but I have no experience with them. IMO the list of machines that are not suitable is much shorter than the list of machines that are.  One thing I do is go to the manufacturer's support section of their web site. See if there's mention of linux drivers and support.  Watch consumables cost/page, those can vary quite a lot.
