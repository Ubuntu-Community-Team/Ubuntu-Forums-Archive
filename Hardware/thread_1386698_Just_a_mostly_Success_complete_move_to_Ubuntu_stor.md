### Post by MercilessMing on 2010-01-21
hey fellow ubuntu users and possible converts,

Well I have played with linux many years first install(play with) slackware around 97~98, of course when I was a PC gamer I was a windows user, but now with console(s) being what they are I am satisfied and work(being Microsoft house) I finally have made the complete conversion over at home.  

I have an Asus m3a78 pro, quad core amd, 8 gigs ram, onboard 3200 and additional 3450 addin ati card, and bought a WD Velociraptor  hlfs 10k drive and decided make the more permanent host install of linux (ubuntu 9.10 64bit) instead of the guest linux vms I played with over the last few years.  

Everything works great (3 monitor setup), I would suggest using  nvidia card(s) from what i have read, because with my setup I seem to only be able to 3 monitor separate cubes or xinerama desktop not combined.. but everything else is GREAT, I haven't accurately timed my bootup but it is around 15seconds..  PS3 Media Server serves all media I need to my console for streaming and my non Microsoft at home learning/play programming works with LAMP and tomcat. 

Hope my success helps with any potential converters, don't worry as well an "oops I screwed up" playing with linux configs for stuff like x config Ubuntu is still forgiving and allows for "safe mode" boots for corrections to the rest of the noobs like myself. I still use virtual box for windows guest for any at home microsoft stuff, but I can't be pro Microsoft. Once I learn eclipse and java programming (coming from 8 years of c sharpe) I will be 100% happy and I am sure ati driver upgrades and ubuntu 10 will perfect my gui interface. As a noob I can see myself doing a few reformats but I will be formatting every drive from NTFS and being complete linux all around.

I intend to buy some ubuntu merchandise to show my support and advertise ubuntu and linux and do my share to help a almost free or/atleast reasonable software community, and beg any other potential converts to join in on the not domination of windows or mac but just a playground levelling freedom of choice movement.

And don't mind some of the "pure" aficionados who proclaim ubuntu as the easy way out from using "pure debian", test play and chose either but ubuntu has done a wonderful package/distro job that truly excels and gets better with every release.
