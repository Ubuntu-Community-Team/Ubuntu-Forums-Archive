### Post by bonestonne on 2008-12-24
well, in all honesty, it could cost more to go this route than to actually have a whole computer on your desk.

[http://www.embeddedsys.com/](http://www.embeddedsys.com/)

that website is among the most popular. at the same time, you also have VIA which makes their own, if you've ever heard of the VIA C3 processors. AMD also has their own, the Geode series, which is not necessarily new, however they do have newer models in development, some of which reach around 800mhz.

You also have the current Intel Atom release, which sports more SATA than IDE or CF slots.

[http://www.mini-itx.com/](http://www.mini-itx.com/)

that's a great website to use for research, you'll see many projects that people have created around the ITX platform.

do be careful though, because ITX is beginning to diverge away from embedded everything, and it's turning into something you can throw an intel quad core into.
