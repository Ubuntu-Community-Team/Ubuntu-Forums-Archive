### Post by DuckHook on 2018-06-18
It is really not possible to answer such a subjective question with any sort of objectivity. Aside from purely technical issues, like whether a specific bluetooth/wireless set works at all, it's mainly a matter of taste. If your set is a plugin set, then only you can decide whether the sound is good for you.

Example: I use a cheap Logitech H600 headphone/mic set that most audiophiles would laugh at. But with my diminished hearing, it's more than good enough for me. However, it has very limited dynamic range, and is also limited to a low volume. So the answer to your question depends on so many factors (what you are using it for, corded or cordless, comfort, personal taste in sound profile) that it really is unanswerable.
