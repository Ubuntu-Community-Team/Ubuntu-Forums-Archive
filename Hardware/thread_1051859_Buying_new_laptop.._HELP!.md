### Post by Squid Spectre on 2009-01-27
I recently conducted a similar search. Its hard to find a laptop that doesn't come with Windows with that kind of quality in the hardware. Reason being is most vendors don't want to support Linux but still have to support the hardware, so most force you to install Windows. 

You CAN buy some laptops with Linux installed or no OS installed from Dell, Lenovo and certain shady 3rd party vendors:

**Dell:**
[http://www.dell.com/content/topics/segtopic.aspx/linux_3x?c=us&cs=19&l=en&s=dhs](http://www.dell.com/content/topics/segtopic.aspx/linux_3x?c=us&cs=19&l=en&s=dhs)

**Lenovo:**
-Used to but stopped last September :(

But the one this you'll notice with those laptops was the hardware was unsurprisingly low-end and generic, with little savings to show for it. It depends on your priorities but I would recommend just giving into to buying _another_ copy of windows and simply formatting.

On the bright side, if you're like me, you can use your numerous windows license stickers to make something cool like a fancy-top hat to show off at parties, better than nothing right?
