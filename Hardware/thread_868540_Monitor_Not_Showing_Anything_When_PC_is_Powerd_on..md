### Post by evets25 on 2008-07-24
If you turn on your computer, and the computer powers on, and the moniter powers on (and you know it works), but nothing appears on the screen, then you know that for some reason, the moniter is not getting a signal from the computer, or they can't communicate somehow. So, trace the problem backwards, and start checking things in this order:

- Double check power cable on moniter and computer
- VGA/DVI/whatever cable: is it plugged in? are the little screws tightened? Is the cable itself damaged or cut in any way?
- Is the graphics card working/plugged in correctly/receiving power?
- Is the power supply working?
- Is the ram installed correctly and working?
- Is everything plugged in the right slot and secure on the motherboard? (a PCI card half-in the slot can cause this problem) 
- Is the motherboard working?

As you can see, the farther down the list you go, the harder it become to answer the questions. If you need help figuring out any of these steps there are things I can suggest along the way to help, just let me know. Just think through it carefully and logically. Ask yourself: What needs to be in place to make this work? What could possibly go wrong? Form a hypothesis, then test it through trial and error. All computer problems can be solved this way.

### Post by silkstone on 2008-07-24
If this followed a power surge, I'm afraid it's likely that something has been fried inside the machine. If you have a meter, you can test the power supply (or get an electrician to do this). If that's OK, it sounds like the motherboard, since the graphics are on-board.

I know it's too late and of no help now, but it is worth running a computer through at least a surge-protected outlet or, preferably, an uninterruptible power supply.
