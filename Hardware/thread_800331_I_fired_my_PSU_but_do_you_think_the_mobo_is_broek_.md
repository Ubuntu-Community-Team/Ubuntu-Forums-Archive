### Post by Keith_Beef on 2008-05-19
A few years ago my PSU failed... an electrolytic capacitor exploded on power-up. I heard a loud "POP" noise as it happened, and could see through the aeration vents what had blown.

The result was a fried mainboard, two CPUs, memory, mouse and keyboard.

Hard discs and optical drives were OK, as were the Adaptec SCSI PCI card and the AGP Video card.

You'll need to find a new PSU and mainboard that are known to be in working condition, and be sure that the mainboard accepts your CPU and memory, then just try plugging in components to test them.
