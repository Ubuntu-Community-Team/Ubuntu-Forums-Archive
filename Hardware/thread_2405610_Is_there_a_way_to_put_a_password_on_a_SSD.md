### Post by #&amp;thj^% on 2018-11-08
This is considered overkill by many including myself, but here is a very concise link: [https://www.zeitgeist.se/2014/09/07/enabling-ata-security-on-a-self-encrypting-ssd/](https://www.zeitgeist.se/2014/09/07/enabling-ata-security-on-a-self-encrypting-ssd/)
Please note some ssd's come self-encrypting.
If your notebook/lappy dies, you may not be able to use your user and master passwords anymore, which means your SSD will remain locked without access to read, write or erase any data within the device. In this case, your SSD is toast.
