### Post by Solid Shadow on 2010-05-02
The day Ubuntu 10 came out I downloaded & installed it on my Toshiba Satellite A215. While I found improved performance in many areas (specifically graphics; the last version of Ubuntu did not work well with the ATI X1200 video adapter) I found that it will not work with my Atheros wireless adapter. The previous version of Ubuntu worked with it just fine. Oddly enough, when running the live cd to "try" Ubuntu it detects and uses the wireless adapter just fine. As soon as the OS is installed, nothing. I'm pretty new to Ubuntu and can't figure out what to do. Any suggestions?

EDIT: Okay, so oddly enough, I found that if I suspend and resume the OS, it can use my wireless adapter. Also found out that my SD card reader wasn't mounting until after the suspend/resume as well. It's a workaround, for sure, but I'd be interested in a permanent solution.
