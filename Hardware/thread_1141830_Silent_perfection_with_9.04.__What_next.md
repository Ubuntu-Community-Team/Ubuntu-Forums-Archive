### Post by eurgain on 2009-04-28
Hi

I have just upgraded my darling Dell X1 from what was computing delight to something nearing perfection.

The Dell X1 was a machine the size and weight of a netbook but with a compact (11") *full-resolution* (1280x768) display and a full-spec 1.1GHz Pentium-M (Centrino) processor.  The i915 graphic chip can do the compiz tricks if you want it to.  It is about 1kg with a battery.  It looks a bit like a netbook, but it is without their limitiations (but it was damned expensive in its day).  Mine has 1.5GBytes of ram, so is not swappy.  All X1s are fanless, so quite quiet.

You get the picture - small, light, powerful, quiet.  What could be better?

How about silent?  How about faster?

Smartd was telling me my hard disc was getting tired.  So, when 9.04 was released, I decided to install the OS onto a new disc, and to use a solid-state drive.

Now, an X1 uses a 1,8inch drive, rather than the usual bigger 2,5 laptop drives, and there are few of them available. Worse, it uses the micro-ATA interface and there are virtually no drives available.  However, there are drives with the ZIF interface and there are adapters.

So I am now running 9.04 from a Diamond Point MTron 32G SDD on the X1, and begad, is it ever fast!!

I use a single ext4 filesystem with a swap file (not a swap partition) but my machine has enough ram not to swap.  Laptop mode is permanently on, data is on writeback, and /var/log is on tmpfs, all Good Things as far as an SSD is concerned.

The machine is faster, battery life has nigh-on doubled, and it makes *no noise at all*, apart from keyboard and trackpad clicks.

My sadness is that I know that this lovely computer will one day die.  It is used intensively, and little respect is shown to it.  (It is already looking very worn, and slightly bent having been dropped from 1,5m onto a solid floor while running with the panel open!)  The trouble is, that no-one seems to be making its replacement.  Certainly not dell!

If anyone can suggest a small, silent, powerful replacement to me, I would be delighted.  It is especially the low display resolution of the netbooks that turns me off.  Also, they all seem to have fans.  If my Centrino did not need one, why on earth does a modern Atom need one?

So, one day my X1 will die, and I will need a replacement.  I just hope that by then there will be something available that can replace this wonderful little machine.

A
