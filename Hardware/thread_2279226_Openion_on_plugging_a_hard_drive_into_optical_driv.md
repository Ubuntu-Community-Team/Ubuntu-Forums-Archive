### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-05-22
I used one in an old notebook. The caddy contained a SATA to PATA adaptor but it worked fine. Not the same as the original optical drive aesthetically - it looked like something was missing there - but not something eye-catching either.
Now, if you're talking about a desktop, similar caddies exist, they look good, and you can plug them to any SATA port available, something you can't do in a notebook.
Either way, any SATA II or III should be fine. SATA III won't work at its full speed though.
