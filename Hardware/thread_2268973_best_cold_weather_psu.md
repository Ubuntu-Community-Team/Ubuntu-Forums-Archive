### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-03-13
Welcome to the forums.  

Any recent, high-quality, retail power supply from Corsair, ThermalTake, etc can handle up to freezing temperatures.  What problems are you experiencing?  Normally PSU's generate a lot of heat, so it's hard to imagine a malfunction due to low temperature.  It could be humidity.  Temperatures below dew point will result in condensation and that can cause a problem.  Put a stick-on temperature and RH meter on your computer--one that holds maximum and minimum values so you can see the temperature and RH ranges.

Put a temperature monitor on your desktop panel and enable logging so you can see how the motherboard temperatures fluctuate.  After a few weeks of data, you will have an idea of how your computer behaves.
