### Post by cogier on 2010-02-20
You could try the following with both screens on and connected. Go to

System>Preferences>Display

If "Mirror Screens" is ticked/checked remove it. You should now see your 2 screens.

Click on the screen you want on and set it "On" and change any other settings as required. Click on the other screen and turn it "Off". 

In my experience you can then boot the laptop with the lid closed and the other screen will be on.

To double check you can ensure that the power options (right click the battery on the task bar and select "Preferences")for both battery and AC power settings are set to do nothing when the lip is closed.

Hope that helps.
