### Post by mivo on 2009-11-09
The Zoostorm has the typical netbook specs (1.6 GHz Atom, 160 GB HDD, 1 GB), but the screen is smaller than usual (most have 10.1"). I never heard of the brand, so, it is hard to predict whether it will work well with Linux.

My netbook is a HP Mini 2140, and I have yet to find a way to get hibernate/suspend to work properly in Linux, which to me is a key feature, since I close and open it often. I just kept XP for now, because it provides full support. Common issues with netbooks are hibernate/suspend, wireless, microphone, LEDs, sometimes the touchpad.

If Linux is a requirement, I would definitely go with a system that 100% supports it, so something like the aforementioned Dell Mini 9 may be the safest bet. (I believe there is a HDD version as well, but haven't looked it up.) I generally like HP's offerings for the really good and nicely sized keyboards, but for Linux use I can't really recommend the 2140, though that aside it is a remarkable netbook.
