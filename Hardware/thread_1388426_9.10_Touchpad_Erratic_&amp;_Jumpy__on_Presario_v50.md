### Post by linkinrally on 2010-02-03
Hi,


I was having more or less the same problem, including wanting to use special options for my touchpad. I also upgraded my HDD on an Alienware m5550 and in the process got fed up with trying to migrate Vista from the old HDD, so here I am now :D.

Obviously you've seen the options on the Preferences>Mouse section are not enough.

The problem with the erratic touchpad is not controlled by the JumpyCursorThreshold (quite illogical, because it seems to be the perfect name for the required parameter), but by the MinSpeed parameter.

Try setting MinSpeed to zero. It worked for me. I also like the LockedDrags option on, for easy selections.

Here are some resources you will need to make those changes permanent:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1249489&highlight=synaptics+touchpad+config](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1249489&highlight=synaptics+touchpad+config)
[http://www.bhagwad.com/blog/2009/04](http://www.bhagwad.com/blog/2009/04)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SynapticsTouchpad](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SynapticsTouchpad)

By the way, after setting MinSpeed to zero, your cursor might feel slow, so you should adjust the acceleration factor in the normal graphical mouse preferences.

Leonardo
