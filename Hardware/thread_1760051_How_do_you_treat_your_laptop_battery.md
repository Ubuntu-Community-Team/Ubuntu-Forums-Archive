### Post by ZaHACKieL on 2011-05-16
I've read so many different opinions about Lithium battery treatment and I hope everyone can share it's own experience taking care of laptop batteries. Here goes mine.

I've had 4 laptops. I've used them as desktop replacement connected to AC around 16 hours a day. The batteries of my first 2 laptops were dead after 1.5-2 years (which is normal I guess) but I didn't do anything special with them.

With my third laptop I used to take out the battery (even when the laptop was off) and use it partially or full once a month aprox . After two years the battery still has 49% of its full capacity but I ran into a problem.

I started having issues with the BIOS, things like loosing time configuration which is usually related to a bad CMOS battery. I changed the CMOS battery and it worked well for 2 weeks and then started having the issue again. After that, I kept the Li battery in my laptop all the day (most of the time plugged to the wall) and the BIOS problem disappeared so my hypothesis is that because of not having the battery in my laptop, somehow, it messed with the CMOS battery or so.

I got my fourth laptop couple of days ago (Dell XPS17 9 cell battery) and I'm thinking about which method should I use to take care of my battery.

What about you guys? Any story? :popcorn:
