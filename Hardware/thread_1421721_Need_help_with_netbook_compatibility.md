### Post by snowpine on 2010-03-04
System 76, Dell, and a few other vendors sell netbooks with Ubuntu pre-installed. That is the "path of least resistance."

Otherwise, check [this list]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport/Machines/Netbooks") and of course here on Ubuntuforums.

It is natural to think of a "netbook" as a new and unique type of device, but really it is just a small laptop. So long as the wireless card, graphics card, etc. are supported it should "just work." If not, you may need to install a non-free driver (such as the Broadcom STA driver for Dell Mini's or the Nvidia driver for the new Ion netbooks) which is a slight inconvenience (maybe 5 minutes out of your life).

New hardware on the market sometimes takes 1 or 2 Ubuntu releases before it works "out of the box". (The original Asus eee's are a good example, they didn't work 100% out of the box with 8.04, but no problems since 9.04).
