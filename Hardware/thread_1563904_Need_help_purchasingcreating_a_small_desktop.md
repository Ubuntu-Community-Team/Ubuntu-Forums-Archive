### Post by him610 on 2010-08-29
> I've considered the shuttle PCs, but can't find good information about how Ubuntu-compabile they are.
Newegg.com is a good place to find all the components you need to complete the final assembly of a current barebones Shuttle system. My Shuttle, a model SN68GS has been chugging along on Ubuntu beginning with version 7.10 through 9.10. I have never had any problems with it since I assembled it three years ago. Try this link for a Shuttle that appears to meet your desired specs.
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856101098]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856101098")
All you need to do is add a CPU, memory, hard drive, and CD/DVD reader. Once you gather all of your peripherals, you can complete final assembly in a couple of hours followed by installing Ubuntu in less than an hour. It should work great.

Have fun.
