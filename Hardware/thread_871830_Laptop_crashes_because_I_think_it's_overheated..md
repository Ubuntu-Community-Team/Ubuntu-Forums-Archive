### Post by argosreality on 2008-07-27
Make sure there's clearance under the laptop when your using it and that the bottom vents and rear exhausts are clear of dust. If that doesn't work you can try a laptop cooling pad (they'll often have one to three fans in them that'll blow cool air on the bottom of the laptop and power from a USB port) but if that doesn't work then most likely there's a hardware issue with the machine itself. It could be a component has worked loose (CPU heatsink could be a big culprit for that which if its not under warrant you could disassemble yourself, put new thermal compound between the heatsink and cpu and then put it back together but...YMMV and it def voids the warranty) otherwise it could be the board or CPU itself have gone bad.
