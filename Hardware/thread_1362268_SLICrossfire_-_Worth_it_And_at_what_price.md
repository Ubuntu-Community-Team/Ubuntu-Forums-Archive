Total system cost or just for the cards? 

For just card cost, its not even worth looking at SLI/Crossfire unless your going to spend over $600 US on 2 cards. At least 2x GTX 275 or 2x 5850...and 2x GTX 285 or 2x 5870 ($800+) is probably a better idea.

So with a CPU to match, you are probably looking at a system cost of $2000 US, at least. Without a monitor. 

But that depends on what you mean by 'worth it'. Getting 2x 9500 GTs in SLI might be worth it if your doing it for pose value LOL. Considering your talking about running ubuntu, I would never get SLI/Crossfire unless you are hitting the top end cards. Besides not getting as much out of SLI as you would with windows,  there is also the heat issues, power issues and general 'pain in the butt' issues.

If your looking for riced up, maybe spending money on something more useful would be a better idea? Water cooling or phase change cooling, or a nice hardware RAID card and a couple of big HDDs (or even SSDs) in RAID 5/6.

It would depend on what Socket 775 CPU you already have...but honestly, if your buying a 2nd hand board then I would just ignore SLI/Crossfire. Even if you've got a really nice Socket 775 CPU/board.

BTW, watch out, you need to be really careful with socket 775 boards and CPU support...just because you have  socket 775 CPU doesnt make it work in all socket 775 boards.
