### Post by jhilp on 2013-11-22
I have an older Dell Dimension E510 pc that I'm keeping around as an extra/backup machine.  I recently upgraded it to 4 GB of ram and loaded Ubuntu 12.04 on it with all the upgrades installed.  We used it for several days and I was very pleased with how well it worked.  A couple days ago I unplugged it and moved it out of the way so I could do some work on another computer, and this morning I hooked the Dell back up to start using it again. I can't figure out why, but it would start up normally, and soon after booting into Ubuntu it lost power to the mouse and keyboard (both are USB connections). I've tried resetting the CMOS by removing the battery for a few minutes and moving the CMOS jumper.  I've also tried a different power supply and CPU (trying everything I can possibly think of).  Also reset the BIOS defaults.  After fooling around with various things it suddenly started working again, but then failed after a few minutes. It's gotten progressively worse, and now won't even recognize the keyboard in the BIOS, so I can't boot up past the BIOS at all now.  I also notice that the BIOS seems to be loading considerably slower than before.  I've checked just about everything I can think of.  All the connections seem to be good, and the BIOS is the latest (2007!) version available.  I've also tried all the USB's, and none work, even when I plug in something other than the keyboard or mouse.  A couple of times when it failed to boot past the BIOS, it gave me 2 alerts: one being a past fan failure, and the other one said the CMOS battery was critically low.  I tried 2 other CMOS batteries with the same results, and if the fan failed and cooked the processor, I assume the different CPU would have at least temporarily solved the problem.  Sorry to get so long winded, just trying to get all the info out there.  Thanks for the help!
