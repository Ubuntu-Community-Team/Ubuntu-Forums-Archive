### Post by deadflowr on 2013-05-22
Press the windows symbol looking key on your keyboard and then when the dash opens, type 'additional drivers'.
When that app show up pick it and run it.
If your system can/needs run closed(restricted)drivers, it'll show up here.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-05-22
Linux is not Windows -- meaning -- you do not start out hunting down drivers, hence the lack of anything like Device Manager.

The drivers are obtained and installed as part of the initial setup.  Unless Additional Drivers offers some to you, there are no other drivers to install.
