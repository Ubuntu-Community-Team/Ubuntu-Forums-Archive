### Post by Malakai on 2010-01-30
You either fried the PSU, or the mobo. The CD drive itself is almost certainly fried.

Id say the PSU has the strongest chance of being what burned out, as the only thing connecting the cdrom to the mobo is the ide/sata cable, and it would have had to be a pretty unlucky short for it to happen to travel right down that cable into the mobo. Or back through the psu into the psu cables attached to the mobo. I suppose its also possible tho unlikely that you could hav3 surged & killed everything attached to the psu heh :(

So I would drop that PSU and CDROM out of the box & see if it works then.

Also check the wall socket and make sure you didnt somehow cause a breaker trip for that outlet i your powerbox, unlikely but possible.

Never ever do work inside a pc case with the psu powered on, You should have the PSU unplugged at all times when you are reaching your hands around in there. Build good computer habits right from the start!

Also touch metal on the case before unplugging the PSU to discharge any static electricity you have built up. Too many times have I seen someone touch a component and that little spark jumps from their finger to a video card or whatever, its like oops bye bye component!
