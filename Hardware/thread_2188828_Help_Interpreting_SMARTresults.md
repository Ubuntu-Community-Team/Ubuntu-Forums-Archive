### Post by powder on 2013-11-19
I would recommend you first make a backup of your important data just in case.

As far as SMART goes you can open the "Disks" utility and run a self test on the drive.

Also, if you have a desktop system you may be able to connect this drive as a secondary drive and then use Disks to put it into Standby. That should help you determin if the drive is chirping or not.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2013-11-21
When I had a mobo with coil whine, the intensity was dependent on CPU frequency and system load. If there doesn't seem to be any correlation between the noise and system load, then it is more likely a moving part.
