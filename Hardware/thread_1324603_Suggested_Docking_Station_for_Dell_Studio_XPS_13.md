### Post by SmokeyMaverick on 2010-03-10
Hey Nate:  No, I didn't find any docking stations to work with the Dell and Ubuntu.  

I made me own, just like this:
[http://www.flickr.com/photos/oldwisemonk/tags/laptop/](http://www.flickr.com/photos/oldwisemonk/tags/laptop/)

I poked out holes in the back of this laptop stand, so that on the 1st shelf, below the laptop, I have a 7 port USB Hub.  On the 2nd bottom shelf, I have my Seagate external HD that I backup my data and OS to.  So when I "dock" my laptop, I have cable catchers on the laptop stand and have to plug in monitor cord, USB, power cord and maybe cat5 if I don't want to use my wireless.  While that sounds like a bit of a hassle, its really not all that bad - I only undock my laptop about twice a week.  

For the external monitor, I used some simple scripting and a disper ([http://willem.engen.nl/projects/disper/](http://willem.engen.nl/projects/disper/)) to jump between 1 screen (laptop) or dual screens (laptop + external screen) with a mapped shortcut key in Ubuntu.

Hope this helps some - good luck!
