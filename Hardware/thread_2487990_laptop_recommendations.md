### Post by TheFu on 2023-06-20
Get a Lenovo or Dell or from one of the dedicated Linux vendors if you want the most Linux compatible solution.  I think you can get a $500 laptop that will meet those needs and if you are willing to get a refurbished one off a corporate lease, you can get around $300.

You will have a pay a premium to have Ubuntu pre-installed.  Is $100-$300 really worth 15 minutes of your time?  Basically, you are pre-paying for support, which is 100% fine. People do that all the time.

There's a vast difference in video editing needs.  Resolution and video-codec will matter more than most anything else.  Will you be working with 8K, 4K, or 1080p?  The hardware requirements are exponentially different.

I'd like to get you to consider something else.  Get a cheap laptop for when you need to be on the do - $300, then get a capable desktop for when you need real computing power for sub-$500.  You'll end up with the best of both worlds.  You can remotely connect to the desktop from almost anywhere in the world to get real work done that is beyond the laptop, but the laptop will still be able to do the things you've listed ... except video editing.  

I've been working in this mode for about 15 yrs.  I started using an Asus Eee as my laptop. Moved to a chromebook  where I loaded LUbuntu, then got another chromebook with more RAM, faster CPU, better screen, but lighter weight. Then MS-Windows laptops finally became cheaper and it was possible to find great performing laptops, used, for $300.  I'm have a used Core i5 8th-gen laptop that I got in 2018 still.  OTOH, my desktop systems have improved performance hugely from 3000 passmarks to nearly 20,000 passmarks, all while staying under $500 for the system upgrades.  Last fall, I swapped just a CPU for a $120 and got a 40% performance improvement to a desktop and higher performing iGPU.  Can't do that with a laptop.
