### Post by towheedm on 2011-03-29
Depending on the age of the old components, and the heat it was subjected to, yes, it would be a good idea to clean out the old paste and replace it with a fresh layer, even if you can replace the components without removing the heatsinks on them, eg some older GPU chips.
Over time, as the paste hardens, it's ability to conduct heat away from the component and onto the heatsink is reduced.  Thus increasing the possibility of a overheat condition, especially if the user is overclocking.
Look for a paste with a good thermal conductivity, especially for the CPU, these are easily idendified visually as being slighly grayish in color as opposed to the normal white ones that fairly common.
