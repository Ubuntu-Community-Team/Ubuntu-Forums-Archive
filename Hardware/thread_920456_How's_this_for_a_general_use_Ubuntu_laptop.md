### Post by Idefix82 on 2008-09-15
It looks to me like the WiFi card is a Broadcom card so you better check in these forums how Ubuntu handles it before buying it. Having said that, my Dell has a Broadcom card and to get it to work with ndiswrapper was one line (after other people have spent weeks looking for the right driver, I guess :) )

P.S. Oh and I guess you may as well go for the 64 bit version straight away. That's what I did with 3gig RAM, anyway. Firstly it will handle the two cores better (will distribute the work more sensibly) and secondly you won't have to install a completely new system if you upgrade your RAM.
