### Post by spacesamurai on 2009-05-12
I have an ASUS EeePC (a very old version), having installed [Ubuntu 8.10] on it. Have very few issues after installing once I replaced the kernel with one specialized for EEE PC. 

Kernel used: linux-image-2.6.27-8-eeepc

Have to say that firefox likes to die on me, but for new versions this may not be such a problem.

As for EEEBUNTU, I tried it but did not care for it much. Oversimplified, so it is easy to use, but leaves a lot out. Just standard Ubuntu gives you so much more to play with.

### Post by MysticGold04 on 2009-05-12
I used to have an Eee 1000HA and I miss it dearly...I just could not handle the keyboard on it.. too small for my hands, plus I use a full size keyboard all day. 

  I was running 8.10 + the Eee kernel on it and it ran awesome.  If you decide to use 9.04, you will not have to use the specialized kernel, most everything will work out of the box... as to which version to use... the Netbook remix transform Ubuntu into an interface specialized for the smaller screen of the netbook.  I myself never installed that because I am a tinkerer, and wanted the regular Ubuntu interface to play with. 

I tend to agree with the opinion above regarding Eeebuntu and EasyPeasy. 

No wrong choice, just a preference.

### Post by Macchi on 2009-05-12
> Asus Eee 1000
Works very well with sound, webcam, ports all reported to work correctly. Minor problems include pulseaudio interfering with sound recording.

Known issues: * Bug 354620 - Recording from microphone stutters when pulseaudio is running 

From: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport/Machines/Netbooks](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport/Machines/Netbooks)

I have two EEEs, a 701 and a 900 running a standard Ubuntu desktop 9.04. Most things work nicely with no tweaks directly after installation. Definitely much better that the Xandros and certainly better than XP. One exception is the buil-in microphone that has a noisy sound. A minor tweak might be necessary though in order to move windows above the top panel on gnome, specially when dialogues have been designed for screens larger than a height of 600 or 800 pixels.
