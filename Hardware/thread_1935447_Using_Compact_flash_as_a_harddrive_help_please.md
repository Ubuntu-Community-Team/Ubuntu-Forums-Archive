### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-03-04
If your computer has USB 2.0 ports and can boot from USB, it's probably much simpler (and probably cheaper) to get an 8GB USB flash drive instead and boot from that.  The other obvious advantage is that it's easy to move that USB drive to another computer.  I regularly see 8GB flash drives on sale for under $10 each.

I have a couple of CF-to-IDE adapters I've used in the past, but they don't support UDMA so they aren't very fast.  Maybe the newer ones do.
