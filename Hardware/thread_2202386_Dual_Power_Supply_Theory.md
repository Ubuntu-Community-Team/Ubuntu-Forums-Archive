### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-01-29
Dell power supplies are not suited for gaming.  Dell had a billion dollar write down a few years ago replacing faulty power supplies.  Many failed due to bad capacitors--probably made with cat food.  In theory you could double up the leads to get twice the current at the same voltage.  In practice, you will burn up your computer because your motherboard--and the thin copper traces--were not designed for the extra current.  

Let us know how long it lasts.  I give it a few days.

### Post by king.of.random on 2014-01-29
Definitely not worth it. The components used in higher specced psu's are of a much higher quality and thus allow a more stable voltage to the cpu and graphics card as demand goes up. This is the main reason why you pay more for a decent psu. I would also caution that a psu failure can and probably will cause damage elsewhere. I once had a failure that destroyed my motherboard and two hard-drives. It was an expensive problem as technology had moved on and I needed a new cpu and ram that would work in the replacement motherboard.....
