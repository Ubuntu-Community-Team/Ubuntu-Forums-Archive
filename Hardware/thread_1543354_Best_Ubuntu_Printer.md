### Post by sgosnell on 2010-08-02
HP and Brother both make very good Linux-compatible printers.  I don't think you'll find any, of any brand, that work only with Linux, though.  There just isn't the market for that.  Most of the all-in-one printers will do fine for what you want, scanning and printing, plus they work as a fax if you ever need that.  All inkjets that I know of cost more to operate than they should, because they all work on the Gillette model - give the razor away for free, make the money on the blades.  Printers are cheap, but the ink makes the money.  I've seen several printers, mostly Lexmark, which were complete disposables, because it cost more to buy a set of ink cartridges than it did to buy a new printer with cartridges included.  If you only need to print text, you can get a laser printer that is more economical, and the ink lasts much longer, but it's expensive when you do have to buy a new drum.  There are certainly laser all-in-ones available, that will scan, fax, and print.
