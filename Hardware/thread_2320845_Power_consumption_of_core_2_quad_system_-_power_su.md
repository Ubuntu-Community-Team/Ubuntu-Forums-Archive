### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-04-18
Have a [look here]("http://outervision.com/power-supply-calculator"). Some of your components are old and may not be listed.

PSUs are one of my things, and at a punt, I'd guess around 350-400W would cover it, probably less, but better more than less. The PSU is the heart of the machine so don't scrimp by getting a generic silver box. They are very inefficient (less than 70% generally) and a safety hazard as far as I'm concerned. 

Go for something that is 85+ efficiency rated by a reputable manufacturer. The extra thirty or forty bucks will be worth every cent. They have a heap of safety switches and features, will last longer than the rest of the components (probably) so you'll be able to use it for the next build. A generic silver box could disintegrate at any time taking one, some or all of the other components in your box with it, or worse, causing a fire (I have witnessed this happen). 

Good luck and hope that helps. ;)

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-04-18
320W or 400W if a PSU calculator has give you just over 200W. Only you know whether a larger power supply will be required in the future. I can't imagine anything you can put in that box that is ever going to get you to requiring 600W! And no, more power available doesn't mean more power used. 

It is worth remembering that running a 600W PSU with your system would actually not be a good idea as the load would be VERY low and that is definitely not the best way to get the most out of a high-efficiency PSU. They are best around 50-60% load and above generally. 

Only you can know whether a larger PSU is going to be a benefit for the future. What we do know, though, is for now, you need at least a 350W PSU and at most 500W, and that would be overkill.
