### Post by sdennie on 2008-09-15
It's fairly common to do that and, as long as your machine isn't exceedingly hot to begin with (so, it has good ventilation), it's probably not anything to worry about unless your laptop has outtake vents on the top of the laptop (not a common configuration I don't think).  In fact, if you go to System->Preferences->Power Management and, set the "Put screen to sleep after X minutes" to the lowest possible setting, the laptop will turn off the backlight fairly quickly (which reduces power and heat).
