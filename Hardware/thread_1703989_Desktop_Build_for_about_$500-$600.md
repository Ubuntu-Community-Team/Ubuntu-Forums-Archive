### Post by VastOne on 2011-03-10
Use Newegg

Make sure you look at user comments and approvals, I use the comments to always check on Linux use and approval

I use Gigabyte motherboards and swear by them - the GA-MA790X-UD4P is great

I use the 965-BE AMD quad processor and swear by it, the 955BE and 945BE are also great

The above combination can be overclocked pretty easily from the bios which gives you even more bang for the buck

You can get a nvidia 9500 o6 9600 with 1 gig of ram dirt cheap and they work great.

4 gig of ram is ok, 6 is great and 8 is incredible... Any of these that fit your budget

Look to find any of these packaged on Newegg and you should be fine

I use the Rosewill Blackbone Black Steel case on all my builds..

I have done hundreds of them and have used Newegg and something similar to the above on all of them ranging from home computers to massive database servers for a web farm and for school districts.

Good Luck

Hmmm. 

An i5 2400 & 8GB of RAM is overkill, unless you will be working with extremely high resolution photos, huge HD videos etc. If it's mainly programming, you can easily get away with an i3 2100 & 4GB of RAM. But it really depends on the scale of the projects you are programming :D

As for the PSU, any 80+ bronze rated Earthwatts, Corsair or Seasonic will do. Try not to buy any more than 450w for a system like yours, as you will lose efficiency.

Finally, I tend to buy Samsung drives, especially the F3's. An F3 500GB is faster than the seagate, and I've never had a Samsung drive fail on me, ever.
