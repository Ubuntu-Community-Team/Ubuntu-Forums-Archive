Lastly can I check that you are formatting a partition or partitions and not the completc disk, ie, the whole device without any partitions. I'm not even sure if gparted will do a whole device without partitions but I understand they are seen in Windows sometimes.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2023-02-03
***sudo mkfs -t ext4 /dev/sdl***

There, possibly, is your problem, ie, you are not creating a filesystem on a partition but on a device, the whole disk, dev/sdl
You really must create partitions and then create a filesystem in a partition, not a whole disk.

### Post by dkolars on 2023-02-07
Marking this as solved... Here's the end result...  

I had bought a bare (internal) HDD, and put it into an old enclosure that I had (have a few).  Thinking that perhaps the cables/connections were not secure, I put the drive into a different (Omega) enclosure to try that.  Suddenly the 5.46 Tb drive was read as a 1.5 Tb drive???  And, could not be "manipulated" by Gparted, Disks, or command line.  Hmmm?   Put it back into the original enclosure I'd put it into, and it's again a 5.46 Tb drive, but still non-functional... wants to be root:root instead of me:me, etc etc.
Returned the drive and got an external HDD **with enclosure**, plugged it in, Gparted could not read it (Gparted has acted funky thru this whole deal), but Disks could read it fine.  Added the UUID to my fstab file, changed ownership & permissions, and 'poof', I have a functioning drive in about 5 mins.

So, it would seem that one cannot simply put an internal drive into just any old external enclosure?  Used to be able to do that, no problem.  Oh, well, fixed now, so onward we go.
