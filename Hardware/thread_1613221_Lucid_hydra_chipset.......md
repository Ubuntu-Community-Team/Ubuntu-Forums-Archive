### Post by P4man on 2010-11-04
The hydra chipset is a chipset, its hardware, not something you can install on any pc. If you look at actual reviews of that hardware, you will find its pointless. More often than not, the combination of an ATI and an nVidia card ends up being slower than either the nVidia or the ATI card alone. So buy an extra card, and get worse performance.

The idea of hydra is compelling, and if ever they get it working then more power to them, but as of today, its not a real world solution on windows, and you can safely forget about it on linux.
