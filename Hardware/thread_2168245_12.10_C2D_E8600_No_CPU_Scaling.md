The description of the two seems to contradict each other. Anyway, I agree to check dmseg.

Edit 1: By the way, typically acpi_cpufreq will not show with "lsmod" because it is normally built into the kernel and is not a loadable module. See the "CPU Frequency scaling" section of your kernel config file to know for certain which are modules ("m") and which are built in ('y').

Edit 2: I have one computer that has frequency scaling and another that does not, and I have been trying to find some log entry difference between the two that indicates what the kernel determines. I have not found such an entry. In the past I have always used the lack of directories and files such as "/sys/devices/system/cpu/cpu*/cpufreq/scaling_driver" as the indicator that the CPU doesn't support frequency scaling (or least the kernel doesn't think so).
