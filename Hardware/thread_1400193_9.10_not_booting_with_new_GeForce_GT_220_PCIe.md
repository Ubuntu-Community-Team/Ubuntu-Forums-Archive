I swapped out my old Nvidia GeForce 6200 PCI card for a new Nvidia GeForce GT 220 PCI express card.

My computer fails to boot and only gives me an error as follows:

PANIC: early exception 0e rip 10:ffffffff812d0760 error 9 cr2 ffffffffff400ca4

If I put the old GeForce PCI card back in it boots normally. I tried changing the preferred video from PCI to PCI express in the BIOS, but this seems to have zero effect either way.

If I have both cards plugged in the machine will boot and show both cards in SYSINFO, but onlt the old PCI card will work.

I am running 2.6.31-19-generic Kernel on x86_64 DELL Core2 Duo machine.

The Nvidia driver in use is: NVIDIA UNIX x86_64 Kernel Module  185.18.36  

any help much appreciated.

### Post by smilingralph on 2010-02-06
No guarantee this will solve your problem but the 185.18.36 drivers do not support the 220 GT. You'll need the [190.53]("http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux_display_amd64_190.53.html"). The easiest way to install these is with the [Vdpau team PPA]("https://launchpad.net/~nvidia-vdpau/+archive/ppa").
Good Luck
Ralph
