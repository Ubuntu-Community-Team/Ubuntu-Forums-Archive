### Post by Welly Wu on 2012-07-14
So, my friend bought a new Acer Iconia 18.4" desktop replacement computer and he decided to purchase a Seagate Momentus XT 750 GB with 8 GB SLC NAND FLASH SSD hybrid hard disk drive. Naturally, I told him to download and install Ubuntu 12.04 64 bit Long Term Support which he did so bare metal. At first, the initial cold boot was pretty fast and he was able to boot Ubuntu in less than 30 seconds. I told him to shut down Ubuntu and try a second cold boot. This time, Ubuntu booted in less than 20 seconds. I told him to shut down a third time and try it again. On the third cold boot, Ubuntu booted in under 13 seconds. He paid the a little more of a price for Seagate Momentus XT 750 GB with 8 GB SLC NAND FLASH SSD hybrid hard disk drive as he would have paid for a single 128 GB Crucial M4 SSD, but he wound up getting 750 GB of storage capacity. So, the more that he uses his computer to do frequent tasks, the faster it will learn his patterns and it will respond much more quickly. It learns as he uses his computer. I think that Seagate has a winner on its hands. They cost a fraction of a current generation SSD and they perform almost as fast, but you get 750 GB of storage capacity. It does not matter which operating system or software applications that you use as the Seagate Momentus XT hybrid hard disk drives will optimize performance dramatically especially for repetitive tasks.

Now, if Seagate winds up creating a next generation Momentus XT with 1.00 TB 7,200 RPM of storage capacity and 16 GB of SLC NAND FLASH SSD, then I will definitely buy it for my next System76 laptop PC in April 2014. This is a really good compromise between storage capacity, speed, performance, and price. It gets the balance right for most users especially performance enthusiasts on a tighter budget that can not afford to splurge on a high capacity SSD.
