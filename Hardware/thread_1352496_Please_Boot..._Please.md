### Post by pricetech on 2009-12-11
geek squad is a waste of money anyway.

Start by disconnecting things.
Disconnect your Hard Drive, Optical Drive, Floppy if you have one.
Remove your add on video card and any other add on cards.
The only thing plugged in to your PS/2 or USB ports should be your keyboard.
Your monitor should of course be plugged in.
Remove your CMOS battery or set the jumper to clear the CMOS.

See what happens.

I'd also remove and reseat the RAM.

If it still doesn't boot, you have a Mobo problem.  You should at least get a display complaining that you have no boot device or some angry beeps from the on board speaker.

Good luck with it.
