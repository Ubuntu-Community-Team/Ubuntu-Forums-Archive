### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-11-27
The Pentium Dual Core is a slightly slower version of the Core 2 Duo CPU.  It's basically the same chip but the Core 2 Duo has more cache so will improve performance in some cases.

Otherwise, a few other differences:

- one has a dedicated graphics card, the other has integrated graphics.  The dedicated graphics will give you better graphics performance but may use more battery.
- one has a "combo" drive (DVD ROM + CD burner), the other just has a DVD ROM (reader)
- one comes with a dock, the other doesn't.

Personally, both seem expensive to me given that over black Friday you could get much newer/faster machines for not much more money.  Best Buy had a Dell Inspiron for about $250 - new, not refurbished.  Yes, people like Thinkpads because of the design and build, but they are still very old machines now.

### Post by PhilGil on 2012-11-28
One thing to consider before you ditch Windows is if any of your university classes are going to require software that won't run on Linux, or access to websites that use Windows-only tech.

For various reasons (which I won't go into here), computers with Linux pre-installed tend to be more expensive than Windows machines with equivalent specs. Two companies that specialize in Ubuntu PC's are [System 76]("https://www.system76.com/laptops/") and [Zareason]("http://zareason.com/shop/Laptops/"), but their offerings are out of your price range. ASUS said they will be producing a couple of laptops with Ubuntu pre-installed, including the [X201E]("http://www.asus.com/Notebooks/Versatile_Performance/X201E/#specifications"), with pricing starting at about $300. At 11.6&#8221;, the screen is considerably smaller than the Thinkpad and the base CPU isn't anything to write home about, but it's a nice-looking ultraportable.

For $200, I don't think you're going to find anything better than the Thinkpad. You may be able to snag a new 10&#8221; netbook for a bit over $200, but it's going to have a less-powerful CPU and a small, low-resolution screen. I've seen a few new, full-size laptops selling for under $300, but performance is significantly compromised to get them down to that price.

If you want to try snagging a new, cheap laptop keep an eye on Fatwallet.com or Slickdeals.com. Dell and Lenovo both have outlet stores where they sell refurbs at decent discounts (although it's unlikely you'll find anything under $300) and Acer sells their refurbs on ebay. An excellent resource for comparing CPU benchmarks is [Passmark]("http://www.cpubenchmark.net/cpu_list.php"). 

Hope that helps a bit.
