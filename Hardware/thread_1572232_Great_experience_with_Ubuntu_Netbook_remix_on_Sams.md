### Post by EricBaenen on 2010-09-10
Just thought I would share my latest Linux on a netbook experience.  :D

The background...

I had been thinking about getting an iPad - but I started analyzing what I would use it for:
- email (typically use gmail or webmail off my personal Zimbra server)
- web
- photos (prefer Picasa by far over any other tool I've used)
- videos/music while away from home
- ebooks

I also thought about how I would use it.  I typically end up using my Droid Eris smartphone in landscape and I prefer a real keyboard if I want to type much at all.  I also wanted a device that had a long battery life - but not be too expensive so I wouldn't feel bad about taking it with me everywhere.

My choices were the iPad, wait for a decent Android or Linux based pad to come out, or a netbook.

If I chose the iPad or an Android based pad I'd want some kind of stand for watching movies or reading for any length of time and probably a separate bluetooth keyboard for typing (adding to the expense and number of bits I'd have to carry around).  Add to that the fact that the iPad is not designed to dock while in landscape position and my choice became pretty obvious.

I ended up going with a Samsung N110 netbook.  1GB RAM, expandable to 2GB; 1.6GHz Atom cpu; 160 GB hard drive; three usb ports; 6 cell battery, 10in screen, and the clincher for me - bluetooth.  A factory refurb through Tigerdirect was $230.

The netbook came with windoze xp - which I left on the machine -- shrank its partition down to just 20GB - mostly for the Kindle ebook app, the windows version of Skype which is much more advanced than the Linux version, and Netflix which unfortunately uses microsloths proprietary silverlight.  The ebook apps and Netflix are the main reason for keeping xp around though (for the time being).

Then I followed the instructions on the Ubuntu web site for making a usb thumb drive into an install media for the netbook remix and installed Ubuntu 10.04 on the netbook.  

A bit to my surprise - but great joy - every single thing works with no tweaking required!  No muss, no fuss - Ubuntu just works on this system!

- wired network works perfectly
- wireless networking works perfectly with WEP and WPA1
- webcam works flawlessly
- sound input and output works perfectly
- battery life is phenominal (7 to 8 hours depending on what I'm doing)
- SD memory cards from my camera work perfectly in the SD slot
- bluetooth works perfectly
-- set up my Motorola sterio bluetooth headset that I had been using with my Android smartphone
--- the N110 only supports bluetooth 2.0 so its either sterio headphones or mono headphones with mic for calling
-- set up a Targus bluetooth mouse that I bought on Ben's Outlet for $5 (I hate touchpads) - works great and no extra wireless dongle

Installed Skype for Linux - works great - audio, video... everything.

Added the Google stable and testing repositories to synaptic and installed Chrome and Picasa - work perfectly.  Hulu in Chrome works great.

Went to Gmail and downloaded the new Google Talk .deb installer -- tried installing, but it was missing a package (not the best deb packaging job) - installed that via synaptic -- then the Google Talk .deb installed just fine - then proceeded to call my parents land line in another state through Google Talk (free calls to land lines!) - and talked to them for an hour.  Sound quality on both ends was excellent, and that was using the built in microphone and speakers on the N110 - they thought I was using my regular phone.

All in all - Ubuntu on this netbook is phenominal and completely painless!  I HIGHLY RECOMMEND IT for anyone thinking about getting a netbook and personally for me its far more capable and versatile than an iPad - all with a built in 'stand' and keyboard.  ;-)

Sending this from the netbook!

Eric
