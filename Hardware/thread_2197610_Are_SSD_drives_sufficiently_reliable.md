### Post by dsc1596 on 2014-01-04
In my experience they are quite reliable. I put a machine together back in February/March of 2010 with an 80 GB SSD as the system drive, and it's been running fine since. Been through a couple of reinstalls of Windows 7 since then, as well as a liquid cooler failing, but the SSD itself is going strong. Although, it does not get used 24/7 either, it's turned off every night, but used almost every day. I remember reading somewhere that SSDs can actually last longer than HDDs since they don't have any moving parts.
As far as whether or not you want more than 20GB, that's entirely dependent on what you intend to use the machine for. One of the benefits of SSDs is that the launching of application is vastly improved. If you plan on installing large applications or games on the machine, you may want to have more space.

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2014-01-04
SSDs still have predicted lifetimes as there is a limit to the number of I/O operations an SSD will support.  

That said, I have three SSDs on my PC and the shortest predicted lifespan is a little over seven years.  I'm sure that within that timeframe, I will be replacing the SSDs.
