### Post by sandy8925 on 2011-01-23
As far as I know, Intel graphics cards work best in Linux with open source drivers. They're supported out of the box. 

In the case of ATI cards, I have a Mobility Radeon X2300(OpenGL 2.1 no longer supported by ATI), and the support is good enough for compiz and I'm able to play OpenArena in full detail (without Anisotropic Filtering). I'm also able to play 720p HD video smoothly. So, most ATI cards should work well (although very recent ones might not have good support). This is on Ubuntu 10.10.

Never used Nvidia on opensource drivers.

Using xorg-edgers ppa and latest kernel might give you better performance, but might be unstable.
