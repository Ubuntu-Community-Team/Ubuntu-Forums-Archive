While I was reading I heard of a case kind of like my old computer where the computer died and would not boot. Someone recomended replacing the battery and it worked.

### Post by chrisfu on 2009-11-29
Short answer: no.

All it'd mean is that you have to reset your BIOS settings and reset your time on every cold-boot.

### Post by lisati on 2009-11-29
I agree with chrisfu that a dead CMOS battery is unlikely to kill your computer completely.

If you have replaced your CMOS battery and are able to enter the BIOS setup utility (usually by pressing something like "Esc", "Del" or one of the "F" keys while the manufacturer's logo is being displayed), you might need to reset the BIOS settings. The exact procedure is can vary according to which make and model computer you are using.

### Post by owise1 on 2009-11-29
If the computer tried to boot at all and a BIOS test failed then it should have made a series of beeps.  You can then use that code to diagnose the cause of the failure.  

I have had a video card fail and make the PC un-bootable but the series of beeps led me to the right answer.  I have also had a flat CMOS battery and the PC still booted but I needed to set some BIOS settings.

Nothing from the PC at all would indicate a pretty failure
