### Post by cascade9 on 2011-06-16
NZ prices are awful. I was so shocked that I checked staticice NZ, and it seems that store arent the best prices around in NZ but arent that bad either. 

[http://www.staticice.co.nz/](http://www.staticice.co.nz/)

You might find some better prices there. 

I dont think I would be getting a LGA1155/i7 2600K myself. If I did, I'd probably try to get a more overclockign friendly board, and a better heatsink/fan setup. 

RAM is better in dual-channel mode, but I have the feeling this is a build done on a budget, and you migth be planning on getting a 2nd 4GB stick later? I'd go for DR3-1600, maybe even higher if you can afford it.

I'm not convinced that the GTX 550 Ti is worth the extra cost over the GTS450. Spend the extra and get a GTX460- 

[http://www.elive.co.nz/asus-gtx-460-1gb-video-sy2757.php](http://www.elive.co.nz/asus-gtx-460-1gb-video-sy2757.php)

Or save some money and get a GTS450. 

 AV-GP Edition Hard Drive? Why? 

'GP' = 'Green Power'. They are slower 5XXX RPM drives, the faster drivers are 7200RPM (or even higher for WD raptors). 'AV' drives are made to be used 24/7, so they cost extra. Just get a caviar black, or a hitachi 7K100, or caviar blue. 

8500watts is far, far more than you will need, even if you are overclocking-

[http://www.xbitlabs.com/articles/cpu/display/power-consumption-overclocking.html](http://www.xbitlabs.com/articles/cpu/display/power-consumption-overclocking.html)

 Save some money, or spend it somewhere else, get a smaller power supply. IMO you can get a better power supply than the antec for less money- 

[http://www.elive.co.nz/corsair-hx-650-atx-psu-ac0725.php](http://www.elive.co.nz/corsair-hx-650-atx-psu-ac0725.php)

*edit- I'd even consider going to less than 650watts, its still more than you need.
