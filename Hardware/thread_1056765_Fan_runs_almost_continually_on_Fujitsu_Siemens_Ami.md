### Post by ulibumpa on 2009-10-08
Probably not a good idea to try to turn it off with those temperatures you've got...there's a reason there's a fan in there
 
I have an old dell laptop I almost chucked out last week because of the noisy fan. Well, the fan was going on full blast since it was overheating. Why? After 5 years the amount of accumulated dust prevented any air cooling. I found on the net a guide on how to open it up and clean it out. Google your model and I'm sure you'll find something. I've never done it before but it was actually very easy as long as you're careful with those tiny screws. Now it's humming very quietly (well it's still a dell but anyway) and the fan only kicks in when it should. Very happy :)
 
 
I'm not familiar with your model but here's a link. Maybe it will work for you too otherwise google on...
[http://overheatingguide8820.blogspot.com/](http://overheatingguide8820.blogspot.com/)
 
edit: I didn't have to deal with any cooling paste on mine. It was just a matter of getting rid of a massive lump of dust.
 
Good luck
