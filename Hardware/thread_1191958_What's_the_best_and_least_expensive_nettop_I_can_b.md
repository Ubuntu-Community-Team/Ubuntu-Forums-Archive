Hi all, I'm looking to build a nettop.  This is my first time trying to build a PC from scratch or barebone though I'm familiar with upgrading RAM and hard drives and such.  Apologies for a possible double post: I have a similar thread, but it's more of a poll about what hardware people use rather than a request for hardware suggestions.
 
I'd like it be very small, inexpensive, low power, quiet machine for basic web browsing and email.  Being able to watch the odd Youtube video is a plus but not necessary.  I would like to to be very quiet and very cool, ideally passively cooled most of the time or even fanless if possible while still maintaining a good browsing experience. Booting from USB or Compact Flash would be ideal if it will get rid of the noisy hard drive.  Any needed files would be stored on a USB key or local NAS. Hopefully I can install from a USB image.  Otherwise, I might need to get a USB or internal SATA drive to use just for the install.

So far the lowest spec system I've found is the [MSI Wind PC Intel 945GC 1]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856167032") which is $120 + about $11 shipping after a rebate. I'm not too familiar with the other low power processors out there.  Are there combinations of Via Nano, AMD Geode or C7 processors and motherboards that can push the total price of the system lower while still maintaining decent web performance?  Newegg has the Atom 230 motherboard for $65 shipped or the 330 motherboard for $87 shipped, so I'm not sure if it's possible to go much lower than that.  One reason I might consider another processor is if it runs cooler than the Atom and does not require a fan.  However, I have a feeling Intel may offer the best price / performance ratio since they seem to be able to push the price down by shipping more units than their competitors.  Any thoughts or suggestions on what to research next?
