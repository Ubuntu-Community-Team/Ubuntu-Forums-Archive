### Post by TheFu on 2022-05-21
Canon isn't great at making Linux drivers.  Sometimes a commercial driver is needed from a 3rd party.  About a decade ago, someone gave my mother a beautiful Canon color printer and it didn't have any Linux drivers.  The model was an IPxxxx (I don't remember the numbers).  At the time, there was a German/European company selling Linux drivers for unsupported printers for $50. Everyone seemed to like that company and said the drivers were excellent. I think it is still around.

Mom had to choices.
a) Buy the commercial driver license for Linux
b) Buy a replacement printer that was well supported by Linux - effectively plug-n-play.

She gave the Canon to a granddaughter that uses MS-Windows and I bought her a Brother laser printer and 10K page toner for about $100.  She said that 10K pages was likely longer than she would live.  Sadly, that was true.  But she never had any printer issues again.

Anyway, the first stage is actually providing some information - like the OS you are running, which DE, which printer model and how is this printer connected to your Linux computer?  Without that, nobody can possibly help.   [https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/](https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/) might be helpful.  Be certain to know the exact version of Ubuntu and the DE, so you refer to the correct desktop guide and find the section on printing.

With supported printers, for the last 10 yrs, especially since 20.04, the printers seem to be automatically recognized and correct drivers are installed, IME. Not even 15seconds of effort needed.

What I heard so far is "I have a blue car. It doesn't work."  No mechanic can help with that tiny bit of information.  We need facts.
