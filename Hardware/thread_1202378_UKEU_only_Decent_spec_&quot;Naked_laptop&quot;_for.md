### Post by Apostle_Monkey on 2009-08-13
Hi,

I was informed of this laptop of a friend, toyed with the idea and eventually gave into to my on desires and bought it.

I thought i would post to help share the facts about this one first hand. 

Its a great laptop, no frills and has exactly what is mentioned in the spec. a couple of starting pointers though; enter the BIOS straight away, navigate to the advanced screen, here you will find a option for installed OS, cycle through the options (XP, Vista, Linux and Other) and change the Embedded Share Memory option to 256 otherwise your graphics will be very poor and running on 128. I also found this nessercary since all Linux distro's i attempted before this chance failed to a black screen during boot or install.

Webcam!
No need to alter a thing. I installed cheese and the cam worked first time like a charm.

WIFI!
Had me stumped for a minute or 2, but it seems as though all you need to do is on start up use the function (fn+f11) to enable the wireless and your away.

Graphics!
In Ubuntu the graphics drivers are unknown and the max resolution by default it 800x600. As mentioned in other forum posts 3rd party divers are available. 2D drivers are meant to work, although i am sill unable to get them to work. I'll post again with an update if i am able to get any to work. Also I am dual booting with windows 7 64bit and i am unable to get any theme other than the basic to run. so if your looking for a good 3D experience maybe this is not the laptop for you. If your not bothered about graphics this laptop is 5star.

If anyone has a more specific question let me know and I'll see if i can test it for you.

### Post by Apostle_Monkey on 2009-08-14
Right. Windows 7 was not playing ball, so i reverted to XP and installed all the drivers from the disk supplied with the machine (no problems with that apart from its windows)

Back to Ubuntu, just to clarify i am using 9.04 standard. the graphics still are not fantastic however i have managed to get a better res (of 1280x800) after installing the first 2 .deb packages from this site:

[http://ncc-1701a.homelinux.net/~linux-sis/index.php?page=Downloads](http://ncc-1701a.homelinux.net/~linux-sis/index.php?page=Downloads)

I am still unable to use any visual effects and i would imagine anything 3D is out of the question. but its makes all the difference to the feel overall. :P

And finally, your battery expectations. well i was not expecting a lot with a more powerful machine such as this and i was correct. The Ubuntu battery meter suggest 2 hours approximately. I've found that to be correct, constant usage will give you a bit over an hour. Left alone and you will get more towards 3. not that great.... but not the worst in the world either. :)
