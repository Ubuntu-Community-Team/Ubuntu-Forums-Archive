### Post by bcollier on 2011-07-13
I thought I would update this, I just bought this keyboard and am running Ubuntu 11.04.  In addition to the ones that work above I discovered you can easily get the zoom buttons to work.  
1.  Use KeyTouch Editor ([https://help.ubuntu.com/community/KeyTouch](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/KeyTouch)) and create a keytouch file.  When it says press a key, press the up zoom key.  Then do it again for the down zoom.  I mapped them on to "scroll up" and "scroll down".  

2.  Then save your configuration, launch keytouch load the configuration file in keytouch and go.  

3.  Then within the Keyboard Shortcuts application in the Ubuntu menu, you can map anything (say maximize window) to either of those buttons.  I mapped them onto minimize and maximize.

In addition to the keys above that work, I've found that FN+F7 launched your browser, FN+F9 launches you email (Evolution in my case) and FN+F10 open Nautilus search.  FN+F12 does Eject.  You can customize what each of those does in Keyboard Shortcuts.

Lastly, the power button in the top right by default on my keyboard sends my computer into hybernate.

Two things that were strange, there are two buttons that don't even register with KeyTouch editor, the configure button (top left of keyboard) and what I assume is supposed to be alt-tab switcher in the bottom left of the keyboard.  The other thing that is strange is that the Music key and the Media center key map onto the same keycode, both in KeyTouch editor and in Keyboard Shortcuts.

If anyone finds ways to improve the use of this keyboard I'd love to hear about it.  Great keyboard all around (and mouse)!
