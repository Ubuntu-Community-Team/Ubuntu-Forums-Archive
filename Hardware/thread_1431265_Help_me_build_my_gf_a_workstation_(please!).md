### Post by jrssystemsnet on 2010-03-20
Quad-core CPU.  Hands down, period, don't consider anything else.  Yes, your apps will use it, yes, it will make a HUGE difference for her video editing.  Linux will tend to utilize it better than Windows, but either WILL use all 4 in multithreaded apps.

No, don't look at that dual core CPU, it's not "good enough", that'll only save you $50 if you're lucky and you're going to have to use this thing for years.  Seriously, I'm gonna smack you, get away from that thing and go back to the quad-core section of the store!  AMD quad core if you're really hurting for budget, Intel Q8x00 family if you're *slightly* hurting for budget, otherwise Intel i7 family.

MEMORY MEMORY MEMORY MEMORY.  As much as you can cram in... you *did* say you're going to be editing video, after all.  4GB bare minimum.  8GB better.  16GB if you can afford it, but that starts getting expensive.

Video: nVidia or Intel.  nothing else.  Intel onboard video on the motherboard is just fine and dandy.

64-bit: YES. YES YES YES.  No need even to think about it on Ubuntu, 64-bit YES.  On Win7... well, probably still 64-bit, but it's a little bit stickier.  You want 64-bit for the memory usage, but it's not going to help much if her *app* isn't available in 64-bit flavor.  Hopefully it will be.  Even if it's not, you probably still want 64-bit Win7.  You may have difficulty with drivers on some hardware (multi-function printers, that kind of crap).  Or maybe you won't.  The Windows ecosystem is TERRIBLE at 64-bit support, but to paraphrase Lancelot's arrow-shot sidekick, "it's getting better!"

Mac: ain't all it's cracked up to be.  The days of the Mac being the premier multimedia editing platform are, frankly, long gone.  Unless she is very specifically looking at working someplace that is an all Mac shop... just don't do it, you'll just be crippling her for when she inevitably has to work on stuff that ISN'T a Mac.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-03-20
Youwont find many single core CPUs around anymore. Pretty much everything, apart from some older CPUs (eg socket 775 P4s and celerons), a few bottom of the line CPUs (AMD semperon) and some of the intel atoms are dual, triple or quad core. None of which are really what you would buy from any sort of video encoding duties 

Unless your really broke, go for a quad core. Video duties will run a lot faster. Dual core will work, but you are only looking at $50 more than a botom of the line dual core (in AMD). 

Linux runs fine with duals/triples/quads. So will WinXP. Well, XP SP2+ has no problem with quads, I _think_ that pre-SP2 it might have only used 2 cores, but I'm unsure on that.....and you should be using XP without it being upgraded to SP2 at least, and SP3 is a good idea.  

@ jrssystemsnet- I would write off the AMD quads quite that badly. I would, however, write off the Core 2 Qauds- these days they cost more than the AMD offerings. Depending on your local prices, this might vary, but here, and in the US, a Core2Quad 8400 costs more than a Phenom II X4 955. The X4 955 will do video editing faster than the 8400, and is generally faster all round. 

@ javyn999- got an idea on your budget levels? What sort of hardware you get would vary a lot on this. IMO, the AMD Phemon II X4s are really good if you dotn want to spend a lot of money...Intel i5 or i7 might be nicer if you have more money to play with.
