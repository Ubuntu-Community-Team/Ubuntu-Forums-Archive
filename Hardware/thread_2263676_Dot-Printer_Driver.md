### Post by magpie03 on 2015-02-05
redbunny,
I have an old Citizens 500 dot matrix printer hooked to the parallel port on a Ubuntu 14.04. Unless you want to experiment, I would leave the dip-switches alone. Turn off the computer and hook up the printer. Then turn the printer on. Then turn on the computer. As the computer boots up, the printer should reset. You should here it. Over here in the US its called poking the printer on boot up. If it works good. As for a driver, I would choose a gernic one. There are no drivers for my Citizen but I chose the Epson driver and it works good for me. Epson, Okidata, and Panasonic were the popular printers in there day and one of those drivers should work. Just remember, if it is a 9 pin head, choose a 9 pin driver.
Hope this helps.
