### Post by biggyfred on 2008-10-09
> **Captain Chaos said:**
> How do I access my BIOS?

Ruh roh.

Usually you hit delete while your computer is booting up to access your BIOS settings. The initial set of white text on black background when your computer starts up is your BIOS at work (though some manufacturers like Dell hide this process behind their corporate logo). Your BIOS is the instructions your motherboard issues to get everything up and going. 

Find out what kind of motherboard you have, find the motherboard manual, and go looking for BIOS settings related to case fan management. When you make changes, don't forget to save before you exit BIOS. 

You should tread very carefully in your BIOS. It typically includes settings used to set how much power your chip receives and things of that nature. You can do permanent damage to your hardware by screwing around in places you shouldn't be, and with the frying of hardware comes the (outside) chance of a fire. I don't mean to be an alarmist, but you need to know the risks involved.

Having said all of that, there should be something in there about case fan speeds. It should be relatively easy for someone with BIOS tweaking experience. I would ask a friend to help your first time in there. 

Good luck.
