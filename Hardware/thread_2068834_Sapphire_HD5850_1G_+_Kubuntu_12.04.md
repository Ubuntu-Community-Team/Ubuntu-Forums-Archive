### Post by betlog on 2012-10-13
Well, I finally got around to doing it.
The drivers are as simple as the nvidia ones in that they are just 'additional drivers' and install trivially. Although the 'post-release updates' version just fails to install.
Driver functionality via the GUI seems decent, and command line options seem to have all bases covered for GPU and fan speeds etc.
I'm not entirely convinced that wine is properly detecting the amount of VRAM, even after I tell it via wine registry.

Most of all it's likely to get the HD 5850 removed and the 8800GT reinstalled because the ATI card just locks up after playing TF2 for a while. 
It takes the whole OS down with it, even though it seems to still be running in the background, no keyboard shortcuts nor power button binding work(to shut down nicely).
Maybe my PSU is slightly insufficient, but it's hard to tell why it would suddenly drop its bundle when all the voltages and temperature look fine while it's running. Pretty hard to tell after its locked up.

Not very impressed with radeon on linux so far.
Regretting it, as expected.

### Post by betlog on 2012-10-15
So after the last few days of the HD5850 being installed my system has performed poorly and erratically in TF2, sometimes framerates seemed like they were quite good, but would then plummet to face-melting 10's and 20 fps for no apparent reason. At all times during 3d acceleration it felt clunky and even mouse control was adversely effected.

Tonight it crashed my system for the last time.
I took the HD5850 1024Mb out and put my 8800GT 512Mb back in.
The difference was immediate, and a significant improvement.

I'll be in the market for a new card before too long.
Will I be even considering an ATI card?
On this system and with this OS? Absolutely not.
