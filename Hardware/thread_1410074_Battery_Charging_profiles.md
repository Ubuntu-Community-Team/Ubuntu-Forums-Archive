Is there any way to have control over battery charging?  My battery is dying after one year.  I want to get a replacement, but I have an idea for a better solution to help me maximize the life of the next one I buy.

Charge profiles.

Many sources that I've read say to maximize the working life of the lithium ion battery, you have to store it at 50% charge.

How about a selectable profile?  For instance, under normal use, I want my battery to charge up to say 60% and discharge down to 30% and then my computer should suspend automatically so it doesn't go too low.

For another profile, I manually tell the laptop to charge completely, and I let it discharge completely.  I only really want this to happen on purpose (not accidentally without my consent).  There have been many times where it didn't suspend properly (i thought it did) and my laptop ended up discharging the whole battery for no reason (also gets really hot in my laptop case for an hour while it does this), lowering the effective life of the battery.

Anybody know how to get this functionality or if it's possible in the first place?  I think it would give ubuntu a nice usability edge.
