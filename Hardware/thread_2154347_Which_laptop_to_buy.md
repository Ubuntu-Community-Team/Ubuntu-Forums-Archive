### Post by user_of_gnomes on 2013-06-23
You can't "permanently" damage your hard drive by dual booting. Hard drives are only damaged by wear and tear. And abuse. 
What happens when dual booting goes wrong is that one of the operating systems overwrites data of the other. This can usually be repaired.

If I were you I'd buy that new laptop, make Windows recovery DVD's and then just overwrite everything with the Linux installation of your choice to see if it works with the video editing and power consumption.

Not to your liking? Pop in the Windows recovery DVD's and you're back to where you started.
