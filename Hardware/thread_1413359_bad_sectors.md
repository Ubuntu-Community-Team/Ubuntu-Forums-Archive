### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-02-22
Bad sectors are often physical defects to the surface of hard drives.
So in this case a re-format won't fix it up.

Having said that, a re-format will let you use the HDD as if it were new, so you won't get corrupted on existing known bad sectors.
But this a good thing to do, as you have some bad sectors, you're bound to get more bad sectors as the hard drives ages.
So, you should either throw the HD out in the rubbish (or recycle it if you can), or you might get away with using it non-critical applications, such as a big swap partition.
