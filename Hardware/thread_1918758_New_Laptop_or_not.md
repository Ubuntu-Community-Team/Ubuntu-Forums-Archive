### Post by GraeW on 2012-02-01
If you can afford it, I would say go for the upgrade of a new computer. In some ways the last few years of technology advancements seems to have slowed for computers in general, pushing for the smaller mobile platforms. But the changes and improvements for laptops are still ongoing, and it still is pretty true that once you purchase your next computer, it will be obsolete almost as soon as you take it home. 

Get what you can best afford. Don't just settle for something right at the edge, but get what you need so it can last you for a while. I've been five years on my Acer Aspire 5050, and am looking at a similar situation as you, ready to upgrade. Good luck to you.

### Post by sudodus on 2012-02-02
Since you are and will be running tough jobs editing and converting graphics, I think you should get a computer that

- runs without getting hot / low energy consumption, so a modern CPU and an SSD drive would be good.

- Try to compare intel and nvidia graphics!

- You can store data on an external eSATA or USB 3 drive (faster than USB 2.0) and format it to ***ext3*** (which is a journaled file system and faster from linux than ntfs)
