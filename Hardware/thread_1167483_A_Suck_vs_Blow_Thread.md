### Post by pbpersson on 2009-05-22
From front to back, all fans need to go in the same direction.

Therefore, if your power supply blows air out the back (that is the standard now) then all fans in the back blow air out of the case and all fans in front suck air into the case.

Now when I build a computer I always make sure I have two in front and two in back, in addition to the power supply fan

I'm with pbpersson; if you've mounted it at the front you want it to be an intake fan. If you've mounted it at the back you want it to be an exhaust fan. If you've mounted it on the side then you want it as an intake fan (unless setting it as an exhaust fan gives you lower temps for some reason).

### Post by pbrane on 2009-06-01
One thing to remember is that CFM in should equal CFM out. If you have fans pulling and fans pushing they should be equal (or close) in cubic feet per minute of air flow. Too much pushing will cavitate the fans, as will too much pulling. This is inefficient and may result in higher case temperatures. e. g., 4 fans in front (pulling) should equal (or be close to) the same amount of air flow as the 2 fans in back (pushing). Include your power supply fan as this will pull in air from the case.
