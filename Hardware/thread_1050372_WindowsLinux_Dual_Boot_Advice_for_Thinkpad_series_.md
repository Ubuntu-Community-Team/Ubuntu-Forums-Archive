### Post by Bitmouse on 2009-01-25
So to put it in short.

Your laptop's bios is set to reserve a 8-10 gb portion at the end of your harddrive for backup purposes. In the bios there is a toggle to turn this section off. Meaning it should be accessible to you. 

In some cases windows will freak out if you use this portion. Despite Bios upgrades, harddrive firmware upgrades, etc... (there may be some solution, but none that I have tried).

Simple solution is to just leave this portion of the harddrive empty and un partitioned. Now your dual boot should work. No more Blue Screen Of Death.
