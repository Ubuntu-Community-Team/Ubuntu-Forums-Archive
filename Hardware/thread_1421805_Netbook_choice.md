### Post by tom4jean on 2010-03-06
I have a Compaq CQ10 that I got about 2 weeks ago and am very happy with it.
It came with windows 7 starter edition and I made a new partition and made it dual boot with Ubuntu 9.10 Netbook Remix on it.
The one thing I like is it has a large keyboard for the small size. I have seen some netbooks you could hardly type on.
The one negative is the speaker design is poor. 
They are at the bottom of the screen so when you open the top they end up down between the back of the computer and the bottom edge of the screen rather then above the key board where they should be, so to me they are somewhat muffled. I can hear it fine most of the time if I am alone on it but in the living room with my wife watching tv forget it. I use headphone and will eventually get external speakers, But I doubt most netbooks have good speakers with their small size.
It has a 160gb hd and the atom n270 processor with 1 gb of ram, upgradeable to 2gb RAM, I haven't yet, but will.

Ubuntu is faster then windows 7 and "starter edition" of windows 7 annoys me with the limitations compared to the windows 7 home professional I have on my desktop but I can access all my files on my desktop on my home network using the built in tools it has.
However, I primarily use Ubuntu because I like linux a lot better then windows. 
Everything works "out of the box" on Ubuntu net-book remix including the built in web-cam, microphone, touch-pad and even the keyboard shortcuts for volume, screen brightness etc...
I have not tried it, but I know other users on the forums have successfully ran the regular version of Ubuntu 9.10 on it and reported everything worked.
This is my first net book so I can't compare it from experience with others, but I like it better then some I saw in stores. I bought it on line from HP.
It takes about 1-2 weeks to get from them and ships directly from china.

### Post by AlanR8 on 2010-03-14
Got the COMPAQ 110C-1120SA on Saturday and I must say I'm impressed! Mine came with XP rather than W7, but my son went out and bought the HP version on the sane day and he's running W7. Ubuntu is MUCH faster than W7 by the way.

Had some issues at first installing Lucid Alpha 3. The initial install was fine, it was the updating that caused some issues. I had to go to recovery mode several times. Such is Alpha software.

The Hardware device finder gave me two options for the Atheros wireless card and I chose the wrong one! No Blue tooth. Quick install of the correct driver and all was well. Everything works from wireless, blue tooth, web cam, volume and brightness controls on the FN keys.

Very happy with the choice and like Tom above I would recommend fully!

BTW, it comes with a 6 cell battery so the battery life should be awesome!
