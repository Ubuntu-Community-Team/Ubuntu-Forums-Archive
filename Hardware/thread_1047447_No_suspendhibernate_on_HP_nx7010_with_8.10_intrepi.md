### Post by inspired on 2009-01-22
Hi folks,
I have recently installed Ubuntu 8.10 onto an HP Compaq nx7010 laptop. Everything works great... except that it won't suspend or hibernate. The system appears to all shut down but an underscore/cursor remains blinking in top left corner of screen.
The only way to proceed from there is to do a forced shutdown (hold powerbutton for a while).

Whilst looking for a solution to this I notice a few people mentioned that the nx7010 is the European version of what is the USA is called a x1000. If that is correct, then I assume any workaround for the x1000 might work on this machine.

I was not able to find a solution or even any info directly related to this issue on this particular model with Ubuntu 8.10.

Any assistance would be greatly appreciated. One of the things I like about Linux is that in the past (on a desktop) I found I could put my machine in and out of suspend and hibernate for weeks with no issues. I find Windows XP generally freaks out after it has gone into suspend a few times in a row.

I'm assuming you have integrated wireless. Try disabling wireless in network manager and see if that works. That's one thing I didn't try.
