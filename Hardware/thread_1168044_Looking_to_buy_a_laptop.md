### Post by sgosnell on 2009-05-24
For what you say you intend to use the laptop for, I would recommend a netbook, probably some model of Asus Eee-PC.  They can be purchased for much less than your budget, in the vicinity of $300 more or less, depending on the model, and work well.  They will run Ubuntu easily, albeit with some tweaks necessary.  There are actually several eee-specific versions of Ubuntu available, which work out of the box.  The Eee comes with a custom version of Xandros, which although it works, it isn't nearly as good as a Ubuntu variety.  You get an SSD or HDD (I prefer the SSD by far) and relatively standard hardware, along with great portability, low weight, good battery life, and cooler operation.  Personally, I'll never buy another full-size laptop.  They're just too big, too heavy, too hot, and too expensive.

The Eee has dedicated forums, [Eee user forums](http://forum.eeeuser.com), which are very active, on the order of this forum.  The downside of such an active forum means you have to wade through a lot of noise, but but upside is that with that much activity, there will almost certainly be someone who has seen your problem and most likely has a fix for it.  IMO, having a very active support forum is a major selling point for any computer.
