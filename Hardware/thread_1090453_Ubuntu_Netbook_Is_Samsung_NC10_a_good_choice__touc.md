### Post by w-sky on 2009-03-08
Hi there, I'm searching for a 10" netbook to use it with Ubuntu. Eee PCs (1000H and S101) only available in Windows versions here in Germany, so there is no obvious reason to prefer those.
First research suggested to me that the Samsung NC10 might be a good choice and work nicely, but digging more into this I found out that there are many problems, some of them still unsolved:

Link: [Ubuntu Community Documentation NC10]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NC10")
There are 2 WiFi sections here, as it seems, one simple solution and a more complicated one. But it says that WiFi can't be turned off (not even with the Gnome applet??), which, I guess, would significantly reduce the battery life when WiFi is not needed?

Link: [NC10 Ubuntu Blog by "jb"]("http://nc10ubuntu.wordpress.com/")
The touchpad x and y axis are having a different speed, and 2-finger functions do not work. At the documentation it says "work in progress" about the touchpad problems - might this be perfectly solved anytime soon?

Link: [Another blog by ???]("http://nc10linux.wordpress.com/")
Some more problems mentioned here, but some of them are solved afaik.

And either way, it's nothing like out-of-the-box. You have to complete many tweaks and always be careful about kernel updates. :(

If I want Ubuntu on a Netbook and want it easy and fully functional, maybe the Eee PC is still the best choice? Especially because there are two specialized Ubuntu distros, [EEEbuntu]("http://www.eeebuntu.org/") and [Easy Peasy]("http://www.geteasypeasy.com/"), that promise to do work out-of-the-box with EEEs.

Yet another hot netbook candidate is Acer Aspire One, but at the [community documentation]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne") again there are many problems noted that are still not solved.
