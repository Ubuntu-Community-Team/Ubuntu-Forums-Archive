### Post by psusi on 2010-03-02
There is no way the drive got hot enough to deform the metal chasis.  That looks like manufacturing defect to me.  The metal did not quite fully fill the mold leaving some small pockets of of air.  It doesn't look that bad though.  Most likely that was one of the first shots of the run so the mold wasn't fully warmed up yet.

### Post by 23dornot23d on 2010-03-02
I agree with psusi .... there are no signs as far as I can see in these photos ...... of excessive overheating ........

What you are probably looking at are the original casting marks .......

It would be almost impossible to get up to the temperatures needed to deform the metal casing of a hard drive ..... without it being in a furnace .....
