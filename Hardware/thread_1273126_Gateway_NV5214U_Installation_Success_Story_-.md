 I recently purchased a Gateway NV5214U from Best Buy to use exclusively as a linux machine. I recently started Graduate School in Engineering, and it was either

 a) Keep using Window/OSX and pay literally THOUSANDS for specialty software or
 b) Switch to Linux as a main OS and spend that money on beef jerky & Fireworks instead. :P

Hardware Specs: 
**just go **[http://www.bestbuy.com/site/olspage.jsp?skuId=9370441&st=NV5214U&lp=1&type=product&cp=1&id=1218093004316](http://www.bestbuy.com/site/olspage.jsp?skuId=9370441&st=NV5214U&lp=1&type=product&cp=1&id=1218093004316) <==HERE

 I upgraded the HD right out of the box to an WD 500gig HD and off we went.  Everything went absolutly perfectly. The Software recognised the wireless card right off the bat.  After realizing that I want to use ALL the RAM, I upgraded to a -64 bit installation and 

  **Wireless**: Just worked
  **Graphics Card**: I manually installed the Propriatary ATI/AMD Driver in the system>administration>hardware Drivers. restart. Done. Enable cool graphics and install games. 
 
  **Sound:** sound, Mic, jack, all just worked.
 **USB, Video Out: **All just worked. Haven't checked the HDMI yet. Need a cable. 
 **Little Camra Thing**: Haven't checked. WTF do I need that for anyway? I'll get around to testing it sometime. 

 That's about it. Perfect installation. I mean, comeon, last time I put ubuntu on an old pentium 3 and Corel Linux on my old laptop, I had to type stuff in the consol compile drivers by hand. What's up with this? I thought Linux was supposted to be a nerd club or something. Anyone can do this nowdays. Sheesh. :P

 I hope this finds it's way to someone looking for a Good $450 laptop that works well with linux.  NV5214U - GOOD BUY!!!
