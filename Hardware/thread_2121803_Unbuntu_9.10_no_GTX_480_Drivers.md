### Post by sudodus on 2013-03-03

Welcome to the Ubuntu Forums :-)

Yes there is a lot of hope for you. Your Ubuntu version has passed end of life, and Ubuntu 10.04 LTS will soon pass its end of life (April 2013). So you need a brand new Ubuntu version, and the hardware support is much better for new devices.

Instead of trying to upgrade to a new version, which must be done in several risky steps, I suggest that you

1. Backup your personal data (documents, pictures, video clips etc), and if you have mailboxes and web browser bookmarks. Back it up to an external drive.

2. Let us know about your computer, at least cpu, ram, (graphics chip/card we know) and wifi card/chip! And tell us what kind of tasks/programs you want to run! This information helps us give advice to balance performance (speed) versus eye-candy.

3. Decide which version, 12.04 LTS or 12.10, and which flavour Lubuntu, Xubuntu, Kubuntu or Ubuntu you want to install. It is a good idea to download one or several iso files and make CD/DVD or USB boot drives. Try to run from the external drive, do ***not*** install anything before you know that it works with your computer.

4. Come back here and discuss again what to install! Probably you can re-use the partition where you have Ubuntu 9.10 now.
