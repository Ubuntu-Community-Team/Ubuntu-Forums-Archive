### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-03-09
I handled this by not using my main laptop to watch movies on my HDTV.  Instead, I built a dedicated home media server (tower) and use that solely to watch media.  I have a wireless mouse to control it and have configured it mostly not to require any keyboard interaction (but you can get a wireless keyboard too if you wish).  If need be, I can remote desktop from my laptop to do updates and things requiring keyboard interaction.

My media center box is Ubuntu 10.10; my laptop is 10.04 LTS.  Picture is great.

I built this box very cheaply - found a deal (two years ago now) on the motherboard + CPU at Fry's, used an old case, spare hard drives, etc.  PCI-E video cards with HDMI and no fan (passively cooled) are cheap on sale, easy to find them for under $20 after rebate.  New motherboards may have HDMI built in so you won't need a separate card.


I find the idea of having to plug in my laptop to my TV kind of awkward anyway and like the idea of having a separate box much more practical.
