### Post by happycube on 2009-02-25
Theoretically any Centrino-branded laptop (so you get intel wireless) with intel graphics should work well in ubuntu.  There are some exceptions for brand new chipsets, but I think it's been long enough since the last chipset releases.

It mostly depends on how well the particular model's engineered, and searching on a particular model'll with ubuntu help you find quirks.

In the US basic but reasonable pentium dual-core (low end core 2 duo) laptops are in the $450-600 range.  I'm not sure how that translates into prices there, though.  And avoiding Acer might be trickier in Europe, since they also own eMachines and Packard Bell these days.
