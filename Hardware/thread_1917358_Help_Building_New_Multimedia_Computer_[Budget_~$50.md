A 550ti should run three moniters for desktop use, but no chance of running games across them. A 550 won't cut it. I've got a 550ti in my little brothers rig, and a 560ti in mine. ### Post by Jagoly on 2012-01-31
Well I've got a 560ti and my bro has a 550ti, and my other bro has a 520. Sometime in the near future I intend to directly compare all three in my own personal computer. So that may help your 550/560 issues.

And definitely I do recommend a 2x4gb ram setup. That leaves room for upragde to even 16gb if you ever find the need.

On the note of overclocking, the most annoying thing about intel sandy bridge is that only cpu's with the a "k" at the end of their name can be overclocked. So unfortunately that only leaves the i5 2500k, i7 2600k and i7 2700k. But boy do those chips overclock. My 2600k can run stable at 4.7ghz, and a friend of mine had his 2500k running at 4.6ghz. But remember that most comparisons and graphs on the net will be at stock speeds, which are still awesome on any sandy bridge CPU.

I definitely do reccomend waiting a few extra months and getting something really awesome. I was savage up for my build for just over a year, and it was Definately worth it.
