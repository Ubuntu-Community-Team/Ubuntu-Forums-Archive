### Post by paulisdead on 2009-11-02
Ya, he should be able to use an older processor in a new board, and it should detect it and run it at the right front side bus.  I'd try to at least borrow another power supply, and try to see if it posts with that before you go and buy a new mobo.  Also, try unplugging anything not absolutely needed to boot up, unplug drives and any add in cards just to narrow it down.  Check the sticks of RAM and the slots too, to make sure he didn't install one backwards.  Might not be a bad idea to blow out the contacts in the memory slots with some canned air, as well.

I have seen a few motherboards, that required you to really push the ram in pretty hard in order to get proper contact.  Not enough force to break or flex the board, but a decent amount of pressure.
