### Post by sulekha on 2010-08-14
Hi all,

canonical should provide a web page specifically meant for online/offline installation of recent drivers, firmware, manuals, and related support utilities with free and paid support options, following the lines of [http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html) or [http://www.driverguide.com/](http://www.driverguide.com/)

With the help of volunteers, compile a massive database of drivers, firmware, and support documents 

The site should have the following features 

Provides an easy step-by-step process for finding and installing drivers. 

Offers a huge searchable archive of hundreds of thousands of driver/firmware files, manufacturer information, and links. 

Includes discussion boards that give members the opportunity to interact with others with their hardware type, and learn from their experiences (very useful!). 

Offers a drivers found area where members can upload new and hard to find drivers for others to download. 

Provides a driver request board where members can make requests for specific drivers. 

Provides links to helpful tutorials, valuable utilities, and other resources. 

**Offers a great collection of old, out of date, and hard to find drivers. **

Membership is absolutely FREE & Members are given lifetime access to this site.

### Post by IcarusR on 2010-08-14
Why should Canonical provide this ?? It would cost them a lot of time, server space & money to create & maintain.
Canonical already provide you with an OS with 6 monthly new versions & regular updates on LTS versions, all for FREE.
Most drivers in linux are built into the kernel source. The ones that are not are either proprietary, or manufacturers do not provide them.
A lot of this sort of info is available on the net to people who can be bothered (a lot don't before posting here) to do a google or forum search. 

If you want it why not create & maintain it yourself ?? Linux is after all about the community getting involved !!
