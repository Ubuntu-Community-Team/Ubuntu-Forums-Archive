### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-01-27
Avoid the GX620 if it has a Pentium D CPU.  That's an ancient dual core - two Pentium 4 CPUs in one package in effect.  The newer Core 2 Duos are far superior.

Personally, I'd look for deals on newer motherboards and just build one.  NewEgg recently had a motherboard with integrated CPU - just a Bay Trail-D Celeron J1900 quad core - but still, much faster than that old Pentium D - for only $33 after rebate (deal is no longer available, but these come up from time to time).  Not a speed demon but will have modern features like HDMI, USB 3.0, etc. and will use a lot less power than those old CPUs.  Keep your eyes open for other components on sale - cheap cases and power supplies etc.  They go on sale from time to time.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-01-27
> **weatherman2 said:**
> cheap cases and power supplies etc. 

Avoid cheap power supplies that are generic silver boxes! If you decide to build your own, pay a little extra for a name brand, 85+ PSU which will have good safety switches and not go bang and take out other components. If you can find one on sale, all the better.

The PSU is the heart of the machine so a generic silver box is not worth the risk (and may cost a lot more in the end than a few extra bucks for something decent if it blows and takes out your MB, RAM, CPU, vid card, etc ...).

PS: Some cheap cases are badly designed and can be a nightmare to work inside. They can also be LOUD! ;)
