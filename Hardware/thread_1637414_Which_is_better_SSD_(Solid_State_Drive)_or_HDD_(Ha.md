### Post by sgosnell on 2010-12-04
Better for what?  SSD is generally faster and more durable, but HDD allows much larger capacity and is much cheaper.  So if you want speed, lower power consumption, and bullet-proof reliability, then SSD is better.  If you want high storage capacity for a reasonable price, then HDD is better.  It depends on your individual requirements.

### Post by sgosnell on 2010-12-04
I've been using a relatively inexpensive, and relatively small, SSD in my netbook for a long time, and I'm satisfied with it.  I don't need huge amounts of storage, and my 32GB SSD is enough for me.  I don't store huge amounts of music, photos, videos, etc, so I have no need of a large HDD.  My music is stored on microSD, for use in my phone and mp3 player, and while I have an external HDD, I rarely use it for anything other than backups, which need to be on external storage in any case.  But some people do need vast amounts of storage, and for them HDD is the only way to go.  SSDs are not yet available in terabyte capacity at reasonable cost.  IMO, better is always a subjective opinion, based on things I may not be aware of, so I think it's pointless to say unequivocally that one is better than another.  It always depends on one's individual needs and desires.
