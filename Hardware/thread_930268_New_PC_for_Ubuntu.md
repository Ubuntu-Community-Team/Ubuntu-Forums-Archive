### Post by travm on 2008-09-25
That would work, Ubuntu, and linux especially is more scalable than windows vista.  Depending on what you want to do that should work great.  I would recommend a better GPU however.  Perhaps something with an nvidia or ATI chipset (8200 or 780G).  
On the other hand if the price is right, and fancy desktop effects (wobbly windows, and fancy minimize and maximize) mean nothing to you, then sure what you chose should work.
I am unsure if Intel has a workable 3d driver for that GPU on linux.  There is an open source option but it requires the CPU to do the 3d rendering which will slow your slow cpu to a halt when rendering anything (ie OpenGL screensaver, etc.)  Other than that the rest of your hardware should work out of the box.
