### Post by pzaw on 2013-04-27
hi,

I am after a USB2.0 PCI card. That is, it provides "TRUE" USB2.0 rated speeds on each port that it provides.

The cards that you can get around the trap seem to be rated USB2.0 for one device.
This is just my observation from my limited experience but the 4 port USB 2.0 cards that you get or even the mother boards that you can buy
don't do true USB2.0 on all USB2.0 rated ports. ie, if you have 2 or more USB devices that are rated at USB2.0 and you connect all of them 
into USB2.0 ports you will only have one, ONE that is working at the USB2.0 speed (480MB) speed and the rest will have USB1.1 speeds (12MB/Sec).

To make it worse, the trouble is you can't pick and choose which one gets the high speed connection. Generally the one that is connected/boots first is usually the one
that is connected with a higher speed.

This is because they only have 1 chip on the card rated at USB2.0 serving all 4 ports and total transfer rate cannot exceed 480MB/sec USB2.0 speed.
I would suggest that it is the same with USB3.0 cards. Don't really see why I should by USB 3.0 devices or computers when I cannot use 2 USB3.0 
devices at the same time and receive USB3.0 speeds on both Devices.

So my Question is, do you guys know if there is a manufacturer that builds PCI cards with TRUE USB2.0 rated ports?

pzaw
