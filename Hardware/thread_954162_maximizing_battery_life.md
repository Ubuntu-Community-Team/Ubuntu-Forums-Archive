### Post by swoody on 2008-10-21
Yes, you can edit the brightness with Configuration Editor. If it's not under Applications -> System Tools, you may need to install it, or edit your menus. 

When you run Conf. Editor you can adjust the brightness by using the menus on the left hand side to go to Apps -> Gnome-Power-Manager -> Backlight and then setting all of the numerical values to 0. Under Apps -> Gnome-Power-Manager -> Buttons I changed the "lid_ac" and "lid_battery" settings to "nothing" so the brightness doesn't go back to bright after I close/open the lid on the computer.

As far as saving battery in other ways, vor has done some great work in his writeup, I found it very useful while maximizing my battery life. Check out the info here:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=729644](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=729644)

I also just replaced my original battery from HP with one I bought from eBay for $70. My battery life is now 7-8 hours :D
