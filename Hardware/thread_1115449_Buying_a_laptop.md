### Post by dwaldrop on 2009-04-03
I cannot answer most of your questions, but I feel compelled to advise against HP.  I run a ministry at my church that helps people with their computer problems.  I have seen a disproportionate number of HP systems.   I had to call HP once to help a family order a replacement install disk.  Their support was the absolute worst I have ever had.  The wanted to charge $79 fee just to tell me the price.... that was considered a service call.

Please consider Lenovo.  I have a CHEAP Lenovo N100 running Ubuntu 8.10.  Never a single compatibility issue.   Wi-fi, screen effects, everything just works!   I had a problem with the CPU fan and their support was superior, to say they least.   

On the Lenovo :
- Battery life was about the same between Vista and Ubuntu.
- The Intel graphics card worked fine.  Handled screen effects!
- I do not use sleep mode much, so I cannot tell for sure.  If this is a deal stoppper, I would be happy to experiment on mine. 

I hope this is helpful.
