### Post by efflandt on 2010-02-19
You may be having trouble auto-negotiating speed.  For an old Celeron 300 server in my basement (still running), I eventually had trouble with its Dlink DFE-530TX+ with older 8139too module negotiating 100baseT speeds with certain devices.  It would go into a frenzy causing 25-50% packet loss on my LAN unless I forced it back to 10baseT speeds (full duplex still worked) with mii-tool.

If you have a faster nic, maybe it is having trouble trying to negotiate a faster speed than 100baseT, or between faster and slower devices.  You could try having it advertise 100baseT (which it appears to be connecting as).  I am not familiar with ethtool, but probably something like **sudo ethtool -s eth0 advertise 0x008**, or if that does not work maybe 0x002.

If you list the output of **sudo lspci -v** related to your nic someone else may have other suggestions.
