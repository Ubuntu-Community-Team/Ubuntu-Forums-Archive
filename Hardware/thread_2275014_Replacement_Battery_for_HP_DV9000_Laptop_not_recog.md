### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-04-23
Possibilities: the new battery may in fact not be good or not compatible with your laptop (where did you get it?).

I've never personally needed to update a BIOS to get a compatible laptop battery to be recognized.  I suppose it's possible it may fix your problem, especially if the battery is a genuine HP battery.  But I'd guess not.   I wouldn't spend any money to update the BIOS just to find out.

First, go to HP's website and verify that they actually have a newer BIOS available for your laptop.  Sometimes there are release notes in BIOS updates telling you what was updated, too; if you see notations about recognizing newer batteries, then maybe that's a good bet.  You used to be able to download trial versions of Windows off the web from Microsoft but not anymore I believe.  See if you can borrow a Windows install disk from someone and install Windows temporarily just long enough to run the BIOS update, if there is one.  It doesn't even need to be activated.  There are other solutions you may find out there such as trying to make a FreeDOS boot disk or something - that may be workable too but you are taking a risk updating the BIOS.  If you are doing it in a non-conventional way, you risk "bricking" the computer (making it unbootable) if something goes wrong.

Another possibility is that your charger isn't working properly.  I know it sounds unlikely that the charger worked fine the last time you used the laptop but not now - but I suppose it's possible.  If you bought the battery off eBay or as a generic replacement, though, my guess is that it's simply a bad or incompatible battery.
