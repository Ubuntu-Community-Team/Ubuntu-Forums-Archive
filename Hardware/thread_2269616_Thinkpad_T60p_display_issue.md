### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-03-17
The LCD cable runs through the hinge in a braided, stainless jacket.  The wires are unbelievably small.  I will repeat that.  The wires are unbelievably small.

If you take the laptop apart (watch some YouTube videos) and examine the cable, you will probably see that it has worn through and is now shorting out.  You can replace the cable.  It is a pain to do.

[http://forums.lenovo.com/t5/T61-and-prior-T-series-ThinkPad/Procedure-to-install-LCD-video-cable-on-the-Lenovo-T60/td-p/842941](http://forums.lenovo.com/t5/T61-and-prior-T-series-ThinkPad/Procedure-to-install-LCD-video-cable-on-the-Lenovo-T60/td-p/842941)

[http://support.lenovo.com/us/en/documents/migr-62730](http://support.lenovo.com/us/en/documents/migr-62730)

The good news is that parts are available.  The alternative is to not to open and close the screen.
