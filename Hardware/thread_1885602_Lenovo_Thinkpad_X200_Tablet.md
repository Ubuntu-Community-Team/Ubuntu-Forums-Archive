### Post by Favux on 2011-11-23
Hi darksourcetech,

I'd advise keeping Vista.  It is always better to dual boot so you can update the BIOS and firmware in Windows.  Much easier in Windows.

Haven't seen much on Oneiric but with earlier releases they got pretty much everything working.  At least one user says Oneiric is fine:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11369993&postcount=215](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=11369993&postcount=215)  The Thinkwiki.org is always a good source of info.:  [http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Category:X200_Tablet](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Category:X200_Tablet)  and  [http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/ThinkWiki](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/ThinkWiki)

Back a while ago, approximately the X60t and X61t time frame, IBM/Lenovo had a program where developers got Thinkpads cheap.  So Thinkpad support in Linux has tended to be pretty good.

With the X200 it only goes up to Karmic (9.10):  [http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Category:X200](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Category:X200)

For rotation support consider Magick Rotation:  [https://launchpad.net/magick-rotation](https://launchpad.net/magick-rotation)
