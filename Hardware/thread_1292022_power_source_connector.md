### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-10-15
> **eutu said:**
> I read the manual it shows both connectors and does not mention whether to use one or both

Does the motherboard have a 20-pin connector, or a 24-pin connector?

If it has the 24-pin connector, use the PSU's 24-pin connector.

If the PSU doesn't have a 24-pin connector, use its 20-pin connector and the dangling 4-pin that is probably in the same sleeve as the 20-pin connector.

Do NOT force the connectors. They are keyed, but soft plastic. It is possible to force the wrong cable onto the motherboard. You'll probably have to stand on it to do so, but it can be done. So don't stand on it. ;)

Tip for next time: *I* always buy Asus or Gigabyte boards. Better build quality. Better parts. Better documentation. Better support/service.
