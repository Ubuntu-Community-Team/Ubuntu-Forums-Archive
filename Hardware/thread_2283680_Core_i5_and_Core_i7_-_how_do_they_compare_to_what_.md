### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-06-23
"Core i7" and "Core i5" are the names of dozens if not hundreds of different Intel CPUs made over the last 5-6 years.  These are marketing names that get applied to new products every year.  A Core i7 from 2010 is going to be a lot slower than one made in 2015.

But - any Core i5 or Core i7 you buy today will be much, much faster than any Core 2 Duo CPU you have in an old computer - guaranteed.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2015-06-24
The Core i7 often has a bit more cache and they usually support Hyperthreading(HT), while the i5 usually (not always) doesn't support HT. How much the cache and HT help depends on what you're doing. Personally, I'd rather have a 3.2 Ghz without the HT than a 2.8GHz chip with it.

If you're going to spend money upgrading from a Core 2 Duo, make sure you get a Sandy Bridge or later: [http://techreport.com/review/20188/intel-sandy-bridge-core-processors/19](http://techreport.com/review/20188/intel-sandy-bridge-core-processors/19)
A 2.8GHz Core i7 is definitely not a Sandy Bridge or newer. The i5 could be, but you need to give a model number or link to make sure.
