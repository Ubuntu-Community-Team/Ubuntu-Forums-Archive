### Post by guisar on 2010-11-16
I use an older eepc (1000HA); the eeepc-applet will allow you to control the scaling of the processor. The panel applet is available from the repos- just search for it in synaptic. My experience is that the eeepc will default to "on demand" unless you specify otherwise (performance) and while the two performance levels may sound close together, putting it to performance makes a BIG difference in latency if not absolute speed. 

I also have 2G of memory. Not sure if that makes a tremendous amount of difference but I just setup a new machine for my daughter and noticed it was swapping quite a bit so I think I'll upgrade hers as well. It's only $20 or so bucks to make the switch. 

As an aside, not sure what you are used to but I've found the machines absolutely acceptable for work- it's all I use. Yes, it's not a quad core but it works just fine. My daughters uses the newer N525 chip which is allegedly quicker but didn't seem all that different during the few hours I was using it. I didn't do any timing etc, just my perception.
