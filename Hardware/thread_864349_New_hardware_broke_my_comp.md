### Post by jerome1232 on 2008-07-19
Once that new case comes in it's time to swap out parts, hopefully you didn't shock your mobo or something, try and re-seat your ram, make sure it's in the correct DIMM slot, somewhere on the motherboard you should be able to flash CMOS, just turn everything off, move that jumper (usually says CMOS next to it) leave it for 20 secs move jumper back and boot her up. If you just have a blinking cursor on a black screen that usually indicative of bad RAM or CPU.


Once when upgrading my RAM I pushed it in to hard and the case was flimsy, flexed the motherboard and snapped a pin on my processor so now I take CPU's out when installing RAM. and make sure I have some backing to push against if the case is filmsy.
