### Post by cascade9 on 2010-04-03
I'm not going to help, much but hopefully a little. 

[http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/uk/en/sm/WF06a/321957-321957-64295-3955552-3955552-3974409.html](http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/uk/en/sm/WF06a/321957-321957-64295-3955552-3955552-3974409.html)

There area few things that vary over that model laptop- 

Wireless (I think that some of the wireless chips will at least need some mucking around with on linux, I'm 99% ure that the intel wireless works out of the box) 


CPU- get the Intel Core2Duo version if you are planning on using virtualbox- they have more power than the Celerons. 

Graphics- 4500MHD should let you use the external monitor to extend the desktop, and according to the intel data sheet should do up to 2048x1536 @ 75Hz (analog) or 60Hz (digital). I cant confirm that, I'm pretty sure I've seen laptops with that graphics setup the the laptop manufacturer says will only do 1600x900.

I qwould hope that with a HDMI port it can go to at least 1920x1080 . If your buying it from a store, ask them to hook it up to a HDMI TV or monitor and check (I'm pretty sure  that if i will display a resolution in windows, it will with linux) 

I think from reading the HP/Compaq site above that there is an option to have an ATI 4330, but it seems to be US only. If the intel graphics cant go to the resolution you want, then that might be worth looking at...if it is available in europe.
