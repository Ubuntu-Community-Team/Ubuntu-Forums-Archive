### Post by linux4me on 2008-12-16
I've got a Toshiba Satellite A205 that has run fine on both 8.04 and 8.10. The only issue I had with it was with wireless. On 8.04 I had to use madwifi to get it to work, but on 8.10 all I had to do was enable the restricted driver it found on install. In both cases, once I got it working the wireless worked great. Both were clean installs, not upgrades.

I loaded 8.04--downloaded from Ubuntu, not Dell--on a Dell 1525 (that came with Vista) for a friend and it worked out of the box. Even the web cam worked. 

Of the two, the Toshiba seems like it is of better quality construction on the outside. From my admittedly limited experience, I don't think you have to write Dell off completely, but of the two, I would go with Toshiba. If you do go with the Dell, buying it with Vista and then installing Ubuntu was cheaper than buying it with Ubuntu preinstalled when I last looked.

### Post by InlawBiker on 2008-12-18
All laptops use a mix of the same hardware, it's a matter of making sure there are drivers for that particular hardware.

I use a Lenovo T61 with very Linux-friendly drivers and even still I have some problems with the dock.  But, for the most part it works because almost all of the internals are Intel.  Intel wireless, sound, gigabit ethernet, chipset, NVidia graphics, all are solid on Linux.  Once you get into hardware that doesn't have open source drivers you start running into headaches.
