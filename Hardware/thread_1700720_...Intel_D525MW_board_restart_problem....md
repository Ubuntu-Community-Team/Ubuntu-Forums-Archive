### Post by gsam_fan on 2011-03-05
Installed Zentyal after Ubuntu Lucid 10.04.2 LTS...
working as a gateway...
the problem:
when ever a restart needed (ISP connection problem etc...) have to go and press the power off button to bring the "LAN" and "WAN" up.
Zentyal admin site (https:/"Zentyal static IP"/) has a system "Halt/Restart" but guess what..routers don't need to go and press the power off button!!!](*,)

pre-fix:
although  that Intel released a Bios Fix (0074) didn't help me.
over the net found the 2 problems that together don't let the board to "SoftRestart":
**1**. Ubuntu and Linux at general don't like Hyper-threating on Atom Dual Core
**2**.the VGA-out on that board is the **"Second Display"**..the first is the **"Laptop Display"**:lolflag:
actually if you notice near the **[*CPU heatsink*]("http://www.google.com/search?q=CPU+heatsink&hl=en&client=ubuntu&hs=Lob&sa=X&channel=fs&prmd=ivns&source=univ&tbs=shop:1&tbo=u&ei=FXhyTe2TBs30sga-g4mEDg&ved=0CFAQrQQ")**

 is a "multi-pin position" ...._**THIS IS THE FIRST DISPLAY**_ !!!!!!!!!!!!

_**fix(the easy way):**_

1.go and disable the Hyper-threating into Bios..actually it run faster with stock Ubuntu kernel...


2.
...run "plymouth-resolution-fix.sh" to take control over frame buffer...

...restart--->log in--->disable laptop display from "System-->Preferences--->Monitors".
if the mouse cursor desapears (hehe) press Alt+Control+F1 and then Alt+Control+F7.

...restart

...run  "plymouth-resolution-fix-revert-back.sh" to *release* the frame buffer to Ubuntu-stock again

**what we just did?.... we created an "initramfs" without the problematic frame buffer and EDID modules...and this will follow up every kernel update**

:popcorn:


_**the ugly-fix:**_
recompile the kernel..but you don't want to do that on an Atom CPU and the stock Ubuntu config with 2500+ modules....
