### Post by weatherman2 on 2016-02-23
No, I don't think the fan would consume anything close to 8.5 watts.  If you have a "watt" meter, plug the AC adapter into it to see how much power your laptop consumes.  You can hear the fan come on and see if the power jumps (though make sure the CPU load isn't also jumping - obviously when the CPU works it gets hotter and the fan comes on; you could run something to get the CPU working and not, then stop it and the fan will stay on for a few seconds longer to cool it but the CPU should be idle and not drawing that much power).
