### Post by Keith_Beef on 2008-06-04
Three days ago, my computer just hung while doing nothing particularly demanding.

I had put a blank DVD in the drive, thinking I'd burn a set of photos to send to relatives overseas, and went on playing FreeCiv.

The display froze, and the numlock LED on the keyboard stayed on. Since I didn't have a network cable to hand (left it in my laptop bag at work), I couldn't telnet in from my other system... and anyway it was late at night and I was dog tired.

The next day, I didn't try turning it on.

Yesterday, the same thing happened while reading e-mails.

Tonight, I switched on the computer and walked away. When I came back to it, the video card's startup message was still displaying. I tried to reboot, but the computer just wouldn't boot.

The video card fan would spin up, then between two and six seconds later the CPU fan would spin up, then around two seconds later the whole system would restart. The UPS front panel showed that the computer, monitor and printer together were drawing around 1 Watt before switching on the computer. This went up to a normal 120 - 135 Watts as the fans began to spin.

Thinking back to the first hang, when I'd put a blank DVD in the drive, I wondered if I'd somehow introduced something (conductive dust, fibres) that could have made a short in the drive.So I opened the case and disconnected the drive. No joy.

So I disconnected all four SATA hard drives. Still no joy.

So I disconnected the PS/2 mouse and keyboard. Still no joy.

So I disconnected all USB devices. Still no joy.

So I took out both sticks of RAM (Mushkin XP2-8500 5-5-4-12) from slots 1 and 3. No joy, but the computer made a cryptic BEEP BEEP BEEP [pause] followed by eight more BEEPs.

So I put one of the sticks back in slot 1 . JOY! The system boots, and has ben stable for, oh, about an hour and ten minutes as I type...Not impressive, I know, but it's a start.

I suppose I'll have to remove the stick that works, and try the other in the same slot... then try each stick in other slots to see if one stick is bad, or if one slot is bad... and hope that it's the stick so I can send it back under warranty.
