I would think that they all would work just fine. An external hard drive is just like an internal hard drive, but obviously external and connects through, most likely, USB. You just have to make sure that it is formatted into a file system format that Linux understands. Most that I have run across come in FAT32 format, which will work fine with Linux, Windows, ect... I own a Seagate FreeAgent and it works just fine.

### Post by DownTown22 on 2010-02-24
I have 2 Seagate externals and they work just fine.  A 500GB FreeAgent GO and a 320GB (I think that's the size) FreeAgent Pro.  The Pro is rather large and bulky and requires a power cord, whereas the GO is small and just runs off the USB port.

I recommend the GO, they're fairly cheap (cost-wise) and very portable.  They come with some software preinstalled, so I just formatted mine as soon as I got it and it was all good after that.
