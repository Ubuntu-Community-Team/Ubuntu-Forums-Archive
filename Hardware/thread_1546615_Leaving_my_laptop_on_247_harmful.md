### Post by Nathan.Flow on 2010-08-05
I first want to ask what do you mean by "well ventilated".

My experience, and this my not be the case for you. Don't leave your laptop on 24/7, mine always got very hot, like dangerously.

The normal system with adequate ventilation can take temperatures up to about ~40c, depending on the hardware, some can go a little higher. So I'd take a thermometer, or other temp measuring equipment and measure the ambient temp in and around your laptop after it's been running > 2 hrs. If the reading are with in the manufactures recommended operating ranges. (Look at your manual I know it's a lap top, but their will be a clause stating suggested operating ranges.) I'd say your OK but still not for 24/7 a few hrs at a time, yes but not 24/7. Laptops tend to bake the air inside them. The parts will eventually fail because of prolonged exposure to those temps.

Defiantly take out the battery if you plan to run it that long(longer than 3 hrs, if the battery's already charged.) As most systems will constantly charge the battery while it's plunged in. Keep the inverter(the thing that looks like a brick) free, some will get hot enough to cause a fire in some cases.
