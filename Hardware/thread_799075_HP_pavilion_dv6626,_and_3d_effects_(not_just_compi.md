### Post by ASULutzy on 2008-05-18
BSOD's shouldn't happen in Vista from trying to do too many things at once. (I'm not trying to sing the praises of Windows Vista here, but it's certainly not like it was in Windows 3.1)

BSOD's generally happen because of a bad driver, or of course because a component failure.

If I were you I would try playing around doing GPU intensive things in Windows for a while. If a crash occurs there, then it's most likely a hardware issue if it happens in Ubuntu as well)

Whether the component is bad, or if the temperature is getting too hot, would be possible, albeit not easy to differentiate. Get some thermal monitoring tools and check the temps under load. If they seem reasonable then perhaps a component is going bad.
