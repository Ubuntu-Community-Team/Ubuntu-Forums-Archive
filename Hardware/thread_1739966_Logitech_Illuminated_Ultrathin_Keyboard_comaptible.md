### Post by LinXNut on 2011-04-26
You should be perfectly fine. I would keep the old keyboard on hand just in case something

 goes wrong. If the keyboard does not work during the installation of the Ubuntu CD, plug 

the older keyboard in, and use that until you have installed and updated your Ubuntu 

system. Once it is finished, plug in your new keyboard. You can then bind keys to certain 

things if you like. The problem with the BIOS was the version that Dell was using. (It was 

outdated for the logitech keyboard). I read that dell has also had similar problems with 

"logitech" items as well. System 76 makes very good computers, and I hope everything goes well! :popcorn:
