### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-03-02
How old is the power supply?  As the MOSFET's age, the current they handle is reduced somewhat.  So a 350 watt power supply is probably only capable of putting out 290 watts (without voltage sag) after a few years.  You can upgrade to a 500 watt supply for 1/3 the cost of the video card.  If the power supply burns up, it can take out your motherboard, your harddisk (and the data) and your video card.

So a new power supply becomes cheap insurance to keep the rest of the system functioning.

Or just use the old power supply until it burns up.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-03-03
Power supply calculators always overestimate the real power consumption in my experience. Have a look at this-

[http://www.xbitlabs.com/articles/coolers/display/system-wattage_6.html](http://www.xbitlabs.com/articles/coolers/display/system-wattage_6.html)

That page is probably the closest to your current setup, but I'd go back to page 1 and at least read the introduction. BTW, the calculator you linked gives 246 watt for the system that tested at 189 watts, max load. 

As for how old your power supply is- its newish to medium. I've got power supplies that are 7-8 years old that are still going well, but they are decent brands. A lot of the 'yum cha' (cheap chinese junk) power supplies have less power than the sticker says, and will not last anywhere near as long a decent brand. 

I'd guess that your computer would be fine with that card and power supply. That is just a guess though, and would vary a lot depending on who it was made by.
