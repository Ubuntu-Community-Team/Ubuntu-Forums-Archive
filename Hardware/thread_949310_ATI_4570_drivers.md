### Post by BMWDriver on 2008-10-16
A graphics card is hardware. ;)

I strongly suggest visiting the [Unofficial ATI Graphics Drivers Wiki]("http://www.wiki.cchtml.com") where you will find very well organized information on how to install, verify the install and troubleshoot - among other subjects relating to the ATI drivers. Simply enabling the drivers is as you say insufficient.

I had a lot of trouble myself with both my ATI 9600 Pro, and X800XT until I visited that site. I also recently installed the drivers (Catalyst 8.9) for my HD 3850 AGP card with great success.

After you install the driver, visit the Configuring section of the Wiki, and enable 2D acceleration. This way you will be able to get video upscaling and any other capacities enabled by the drivers. Otherwise video playback may get choppy at full screen.

This was the best site I found for solving ATI problems relating to drivers.

Good luck !
