### Post by oldefoxx on 2010-07-03
Many Y-cables are reportedly for the PS/2 mouse and keyboard to USB connector purpose. but in many cases, there has to be a power feed to the keyboard, and in some cases, to the mouse.  What I found is that the power feed from the USB port is not always evident, and that means it won't work.  Your idea about a 4 or 8 port adapter makes sense, if it works.  I picked up some 4 port adaters a couple of years back at Big Lots for $4 each, which was something of a bargain.  Each as a light inside that tells you when it is drawing power, and that can help too.  USB hubs offer a big advantage to notebook and netbook users as well.

Don't trust a PS/2 to USB or USB to PS/2 adapter cable just because two connectors are colored to match the color of the connectors sported by the keyboard and mouse.  First, the name difference shows that the connection is sort of one way, so you have to get one that goes the right way for your needs.  Second, it may not work as advertised anyway, so see if you can find something on line to guide you to one that does work.
