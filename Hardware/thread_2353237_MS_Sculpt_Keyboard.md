### Post by Sigma1 on 2017-02-20
Hello,
I've set up a Microsoft Sculpt Keyboard on Ubuntu, with no real problems of detection. However, the application internal keyboard shortcuts (e.g. Shift + f5 for "retrieve all new messages" in Thunderbird) don't appear to be working as the built-in MS keyboard shortcuts take precedence. Could anyone suggest a fix for this, please? Many thanks in advance.
G. R.

### Post by him610 on 2017-02-20
Oftentimes in the past, when one purchased an aftermarket keyboard, a disk (cd/3.5in) came with the keyboard that permitted the new owner to assign special keys and key combinations as the owner saw fit. This was true for me with an IBM branded keyboard I purchased years ago. I have lost or misplaced the original CD with the setup utility, but the assigned special keys still work as originally configured around fifteen years ago.

You might look around in your cache of peripheral equipment support CDs and discover the one that may have come with your MS Sculpt keyboard, or possibly  download it from Microsoft. You probably need to perform any keyboard reconfiguration from a MS-Win environment.

### Post by Sigma1 on 2017-02-24
Thanks for the answer, him610.
I worked it out for myself in the end... embarrassingly. At the top right of the keyboard is a switch marked blue on the left, for the MS built-in functions, or white on the right, marked Fn. It was just a question of flipping the switch to the right for me to get my old custom functions back.
I'm posting this in the hope it might help someone as slow as me to the right answer!
