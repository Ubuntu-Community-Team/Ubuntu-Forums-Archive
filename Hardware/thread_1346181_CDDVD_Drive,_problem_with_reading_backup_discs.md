### Post by ram2500 on 2009-12-04
You mentioned that "professionally-made" discs work well with both systems, however, with discs you burn, you said that they don't work either in Ubuntu or Windows. It's very likely that when you are burning CDs, there are a variety of problems. First and foremost, if your drive isn't BURNproof (to prevent buffer underruns) or the like, if you run out of memory or the hard drive/memory cannot feed the information to be written to the disc in time, you have a buffer underrun. This can be remediated only by replacing the drive with a BURNproof model (almost all newer and better-quality drives are BURNproof) or adding more memory. It could be because there's a scratch or a fingerprint--fingerprints may seem harmless when burning, but in all actuality, the laser cannot get to the surface where the data is written--you can sometimes see light spots around fingerprints or marks. Scratches, fingerprints and the like means that the disc is coaster, whereas, it's unusable at that point.
