### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2011-01-13
The first thing I would recommend is that you try out Ubuntu before committing to using it with a new build. You may find it will not have all the software or features you need.

Regarding hardware:  
Go for an Nvidia GPU, any onboard chip is fine or a gt210 for a dedicated card if you have the cash. Dual monitor setups can depend on what displays you are using. I have seen numerous problems with TVs not accepting the right resolution, so try researching beforehand. 
Good thermal paste is not exactly necessary, but it is easy to acquire so I would use it. Stock cooling fans are fine unless you want to overclock or you want noise reduction. 
Make sure you keep grounded when assembling parts.

### Post by cascade9 on 2011-01-13
Asus, IMO, mainly lives of its previous reputation. They used to make great boards, these days they are alright but gigabyte is making better boards IMO. 

Onbaord video- works, but ATI video (the majority of oboard AMD video)  tends to have more issues than nvidia with the closed drivers. If you are planning on watching much in the way of media, nvdia has the edge (hardware video decoding, ATIs XvBA is buggy, VDPAU works well).

You dont need a video card, but its a better option IMO.  For any given feature set, you pay more for a motherboard with onboard video than without, so it might cost a little more, but not much (you can get GT210s for $35 US).

Phenom X4 965 is a nice CPU. They are cheap, and run well. No, you wotn need a aftermarket CPU heat/sink fan, or thermal paste. Provided that you arent running your CPU very hot, heatsink/fan will make no difference to the lifespan of the CPU.  

Power supplies, whatever you do, DONT get a cheap one. Cheap power supplies cause more problems than anything else. Get a quality brand- corsair, seasonic, maybe antec or OCZ. 

Buying local might be worth it, if the prices are right. I wouldnt pay more than 15-20% more than newegg or the other online retailers. If you live in a decent sized city (or near one) you should be able to get good prices, somewhere.
