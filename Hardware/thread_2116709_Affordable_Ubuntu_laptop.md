### Post by MoebusNet on 2013-02-16
+1 for System76; I've been quite pleased with mine :)

Have you considered trying out a Ubuntu version optimized for older hardware? I run Xubuntu 12.04LTS on my 2002 Dell Latitude D800 (1.4Ghz Pentium-M) and it is quite acceptable for normal web browsing, working with LibreOffice and similar activities. It is certainly no slower than the original WindowsXP that came with it. You can create a Live-CD or USB image, boot from it and try out Ubuntu on your notebook without changing anything on your HDD. If you find it suitable, then installing it to your HDD will run much faster than running from CD/USB.

I recommend Xubuntu 12.04 because it is more compatible with older hardware and will not demand as much from an older graphics card as Unity in Ubuntu 12.04 would. I also recommend the 64-bit version if your notebook is 64-bit capable.
