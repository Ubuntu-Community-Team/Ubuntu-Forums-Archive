### Post by dsm iv tr on 2008-08-24
I like my Dell Vostro 1000.

1. When I bought it, it was $400 CDN incl. shipping and large-capacity battery.
2. 15.4"
3. Dell.
4. I get 6 hours and 45 minutes to 7 hours on the large-capacity battery, which only added about 1 pound to the weight, if that. That's with brightness down to nearly-minimum, which I normally find comfortable anyway and cpu frequency scaling enabled.
5. It's durable, and the keyboard has a mylar seal under it for minor crumbs/spills.
6. Dual-core athlon64 x2, 1.7ghz.
7. Radeon Xpress 200m, more than sufficient for moderate video work and the fglrx drivers work perfectly for me.
8. See #6.
9. 80gb, sata.
10. it has a dvd burner, and I *think* it's dual-layer. But you'll have to check the Dell site.
11. Works fine with Ubuntu 8.04.1, and Debian 4 if you were so inclined.

It's fairly light, I'd say about 6 or 7 lbs w/ the bigger battery. It has an ExpressCard slot, wired/wireless lan, a softmodem, and 4 usb ports.

I've had a few issues with getting the hotkeys for volume/brightness/VGA to work, but I just wrote up some simple ACPI scripts to attach to them, and I think they're fixed in the latest testing branch anyway.

Don't forget you can customize the hardware dell puts in. For the money, it was WELL worth it to me.

[http://configure.dell.com/dellstore/config.aspx?c=ca&CS=cabsdt1&l=en&OC=N1000T2V_F_1E](http://configure.dell.com/dellstore/config.aspx?c=ca&CS=cabsdt1&l=en&OC=N1000T2V_F_1E)

Good luck.
