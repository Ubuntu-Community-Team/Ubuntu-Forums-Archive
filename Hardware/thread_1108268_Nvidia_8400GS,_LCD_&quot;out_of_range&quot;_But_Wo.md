### Post by SynthesizersFTW on 2009-03-29
"Out of Range" means the vertical refresh is set for too high a rate, as most LCD panels have a hard limit of 60Hz (they aren't variable, it's 60Hz or nothing at all).

You might want to edit your xorg.conf to have a "generic" default monitor setting.  Check out mine:

Section "Monitor"
        Identifier      "Generic Monitor"
        Option          "DPMS"           
EndSection                               

Section "Screen"
        Identifier      "Default Screen"
        Device          "ATI Technologies, Inc. FireGL Mobility T2 (M10 NT)"
        Monitor         "Generic Monitor"                 
        DefaultDepth    24                               

        SubSection "Display"
                Depth   24
                Modes           "1400x1050"
        EndSubSection
EndSection


Perhaps you can import the settings if you have a Windows driver .inf file, I recall reading about being able to do something like that when Ubuntu doesn't automatically recognize a LCD's properties.

Also this link might help you:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto/Nvidia](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto/Nvidia)

Don't give up, my LG widescreen LCD never auto-detected prior to 8.04 and I had to hand-tweak the resolution in the xorg.conf myself.  I recommend looking at the manufacturer's spec sheet for the right monitor settings to plug in.

Good luck!
