### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-02-04
I have a couple of the BU-353's.  They use the SirFSTAR III chipset.  They are fast and accurate and have a magnetic base so you can stick it on a bike or car hood.  Definitely worth the price.  They are somewhat weatherproof as well, but I don't have long-term outdoor experience with it.

I use mine with a nokia n800 and Maemo Mapper (based roughly on gpsdrive code).  MS Streets is certainly best in class for the price.  You can find used versions with a GPS dongle (but they use the older SirFSTAR II chipset--which is not as fast or accurate).

Depending on your needs, you may just want a Mio Walker, or Tom-Tom, or Garmin, Magellan, hand-held or car navigator.

### Post by Marvin H on 2010-02-04
If it was the 2008 version (or one of the first US shipments of version 2009), then the receiver was a little square translucent one. The sticker on the back of it should read: 'GPS-500'.

You could very inexpensively buy this old version of Streets & Trips w/GPS Locator (Amazon sells old versions).

GPS-500 was included in some late shipments of version 2006. Versions of 2007 and 2008 included it. And also some first shipments of S&T2009 in the U.S. included it, too (not in Canada).

P.S. I'm not sure about Linux compatibility with this one, though. To find out more, search for 'gpx-500 linux' :-)
