### Post by Norami on 2009-12-04
I might not be able to fix your issues, but I can give you some suggestions.

First off, the difference between Ubuntu, Kubuntu and Xubuntu is the UI. GNOME, KDE and Xfce are desktop environments. Xfce is practically the same as GNOME, just a few differences. The biggest thing about these differences are the applications. KDE has a set of apps written for it, and GNOME and Xfce have their own set. Plus there's a variety of apps made for either or. Personally, I like the GNOME based environments better, but that's mainly personal preference.

Assuming this netbook has a 10.1" screen, the resolution should actually be 1024x600. I'm running Xubuntu on my Aspire One. Now, if your videos are "squeezing" on the sides, then, getting your resolution set correctly should fix your video issue. For some reason, the movie player likes to fit videos to whatever aspect ratio you have. When I had my netbook set to 4:3, the videos in 16:9 would squeeze down to 4:3. Kinda weird. Once I set it right, though, It started playing them correctly.

As far as scrolling goes on the trackpad, that also depends on some settings and what hardware is in your netbook. I have my trackpad set to side scrolling, and it gets a little wonky at times, but it's only because the processor is a 1.6GHz Atom, and the RAM is only 1GB. So naturally, sites like facebook and myspace will scroll oddly, even if you have a 3-button mouse.

On top of all this, also make sure that you have Kubuntu freshly updated, and I recommend turning off the visual effects in the system settings menu. Visual effects on a low power machine like this can be a death sentence.
