### Post by heartburnkid on 2009-02-08
Try updating the motherboard to the latest firmware.  A lot of times, older firmware won't support a dual core chip.  I had to do that with my socket 939 board.

Do you know what chipset your motherboard uses, and what core the CPU uses?

I think it's fairly unlikely you fried the chip; IME, when that happens, the motherboard gets fried too.

It could also be the power supply; a dual core chip draws more power than a single core, and if you were already taxing your PS... well...
