### Post by spotted zebra on 2010-06-04
what software are you using to burn the disk?

i know this is going to be frustrating but download the .iso again and do the MD5 check sum to make sure it is good ( I am not exactly sure how to do one but google i am sure will explain it). then download a copy of imgburn and burn the .iso at the slowest possible speed x1 or x2. don't try to do it really fast cause it will mess it up.

or if you have a flash drive you can format it and use unetbootin to create a bootable flash drive with the new iso files you have. this is my preferred method because it is usually faster and you can reuse the flash drive over and over again.

hope this helps.
