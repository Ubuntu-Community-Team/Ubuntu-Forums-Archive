### Post by t0p on 2011-12-21
> HPs are generally cooperative with linux.

You'd think so, wouldn't you, what with hplip and the rest of it.  That's partly why I bought a HP Deskjet D2660 - it was claimed "compatible" with Linux, and it was cheap.

Unfortunately I have yet to find a Linux set-up which will work natively with the D2660.  I'm currently using Ubuntu 10.04 LTS on my desktop machine, and I am able to print only because I've installed a virtual XP in VirtualBox.  The printer works when it thinks it's dealing with XP, but not when it speaks directly with Ubuntu.  Ubuntu *sees* the printer, it even claims to be printing jobs for it... but no printing happens, even when I've waited up all night long hoping for a miracle.

HP probably is the best way to go, especially with newer printers.  But I would add a caveat: if you can possibly see the printer working with your set-up, do so before spending your money.

### Post by The Cog on 2011-12-26
I use an HP Deskjet 1280. It's an A3 printer though to be honest, I've never actually printed anything A3. 

One thing that is worth looking out for: Some printers have the print head built into the ink cartridge, and some have the ink cartridge "bolt on" to the print head. I would definitely favour one where the print head comes with the cartridge. Otherwise there is no way to fix a clogged head other than buy a new printer. I am very happy with my printer now, and the previous 3 were all replaced because they clogged.
