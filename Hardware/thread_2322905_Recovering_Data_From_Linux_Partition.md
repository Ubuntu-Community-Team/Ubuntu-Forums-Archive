### Post by weatherman2 on 2016-05-01
Boot a Ubuntu disk (DVD or USB) on any PC, then you can connect your old laptop drive with the USB adapter you have, and then you can copy your old Linux files to any other disk - another USB drive, whatever.

If you want to use your old Acer again, try cleaning out the heat sink/fan of dirt and dust, so that it can cool properly.

### Post by him610 on 2016-05-01
As long as your 500GB drive is not corrupted, the data can be recovered. 

Windows normally does not recognize Linux partitions so that is probably not a good option. 

If you are planning to install Ubuntu along side Windows 10 on your new laptop, you can probably connect your 500GB drive to a  USB port using an external drive case or an adapter.

Re: Acer 8930 - Did you consider removing the panels from underneath and giving it a good vacuum? Sometimes lint and other crud get sucked into the case and accumulate around the air ports, fan, and cpu. 
I too have an Acer 8930 - extra nice, low mileage, only operated by a little old lady (spouse of the house), still runs great with Ubuntu 16.04.:)
