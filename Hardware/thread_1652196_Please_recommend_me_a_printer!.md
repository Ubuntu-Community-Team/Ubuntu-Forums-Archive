### Post by perspectoff on 2011-01-01
I have 5 Brother MFC-7820Ns, which I picked up each for $179 at Best Buy. They changed my life (seriously) because all my business (and personal) documents are electronically scanned and stored, printed, or received or transmitted by fax.

These inexpensive machines replaced some very expensive industrial Minolta MFCs that didn't network as well. Employees used the Brother machines (this model is B&W laser) in preference to the Minoltas so we ditched the expensive Minoltas.

The laser cartridges for Brother products are the least expensive, which was a deciding factor.

Check out the price for HP laser cartridges before getting one. Inkjet, you say? You must not be a high-volume shop if you use Inkjet, which is incredibly expensive. 

I like them a lot and have them peppered at work and home.

I use my Brother printers with (K)Ubuntu (and with Windows XP/Vista/7). My setup instructions are at:


Ubuntuguide.org:
[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/MFC-7820N](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/MFC-7820N)

or Kubuntuguide.org:
[http://kubuntuguide.org/MFC-7820N](http://kubuntuguide.org/MFC-7820N)
