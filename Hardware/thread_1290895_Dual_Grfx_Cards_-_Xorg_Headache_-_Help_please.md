### Post by nzadLithium on 2009-10-15
I've been looking at a how to for a setup using 3 monitors over two cards:
[http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Multiple_Monitors](http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Multiple_Monitors)

I noticed in the device section they explicitly state which screen each device is handling. Perhaps you should try specifying that?

I was reading another forum and saw that you can't load the 'nvidia' and 'intel' drivers at the same time. Perhaps you can't load any two different video drivers at the same time? All the configurations i've seen with multiple cards + multiple monitors have used chipsets supported by a single driver, ie they use a  nvidia 7300 and a nvidia 6150 or they used a pair of ati radeon 9600's. 
This link shows pretty much exactly what your trying to do with two different nvidia cards:
[http://aufrecht.org/blog/one-entry?entry_id=32962](http://aufrecht.org/blog/one-entry?entry_id=32962)

I'm thinking however that its not possible to do with two different video drivers. Perhaps you could try the vesa driver on both cards? Yeah things would slow down, but it might get your dual displays up.

Also as far as i've seen they all use xinerama as well, you might want to try sticking that on and seeing if it works, twin view might not be possible.
