> reproducing good prints from colour photos would be nice.

I have always used Epson with Linux, no idea about others. Currently using a TX210 all-in-one, with LCD display and memory card slot.

Everything works fine, out-of-the-box, including photoprinting.

Inkjets are sold on the "razor blade" model; the razor (=printer) is cheap, the consumables (blades=cartridges) expensive. So I always  use a CISS (Continous Ink Supply System). CISS for Epsons are cheap and easy to install and use.

I do photo prints, t-shirt transfer prints and a lot more.

Hope this helps. Check with openprinting.org to ensure your model's compatibility before purchase.

Here is my take on printers.  HP is pretty much plug -n- play.  I have no experience with cheapy HP1000 series printers but have seen posts about getting them to cooperate with Linux, plus they seem rather flimsy.  Other HP models seem fine.  Epson has traditionally been very strong with photo printing.  I've had very good luck with Brother all-in-ones but Brother reviews don't speak highly of their photo printing.  Canon linux support has been spotty but if there are .deb file installers for a chosen model, it would probably work.  If I were looking for a new printer, it'd be HP or Brother.

If you were to choose Brother, they offer an printer installer script which works quite well.  Installing the scanner component is more involved.
