### Post by AtomicSource on 2015-02-06
I'm posting this for the sake of anyone else goingling how to use a the Xbox 360 controller (or any controller or joystick) as a mouse in Ubuntu, and in reply to many old threads that never got answered.

Antimicro works much like Xpadder in Windows. It's available through a [PPA]("https://launchpad.net/~ryochan7/+archive/ubuntu/antimicro"). (For intructions on installing for a PPA, check out this [article on How to Geek]("http://www.howtogeek.com/142414/how-to-install-software-from-outside-ubuntus-software-repositories/") under the heading Personal Package Archives (PPAs). Here's the Antimicro's website: [https://github.com/Ryochan7/antimicro](https://github.com/Ryochan7/antimicro)

To launch it, search for antimicro in the dash.

There's even a bunch of pre-configured profile for various games: [https://github.com/Ryochan7/antimicro-profiles](https://github.com/Ryochan7/antimicro-profiles)

There's also qJoyPad available through the Ubnutu repository, but I couldn't get it to work witht the 360 controller, and the interface isn't quite as nice.
