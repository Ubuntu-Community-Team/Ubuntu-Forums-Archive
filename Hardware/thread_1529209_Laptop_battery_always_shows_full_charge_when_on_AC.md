### Post by chessnerd on 2010-07-11
Well, I might be the bearer of bad tidings if my theory is correct...

My sister's Dell laptop a few months ago started giving her this message under Vista: Plugged in, not charging

I booted into a 9.10 LiveCD to do some diagnostics for her (because that kind of thing is easier for me to do in a Linux environment) and it told me that the battery was finished charging, but that it was only about 70% charged. Her power cable, for whatever reason, was failing to charge the battery. The solution was to buy a new power cable.

I can't say for sure that this is the case on your system, but it could be. To determine if it is try this: unplug it and check to see what the charge percentage is. Plug it back in and turn the machine off. Let it sit for a while. Come back and see if the percentage has risen. If it hasn't you either have a faulty cable or faulty battery.

If it does charge, then the good news is that it is a software problem, not a hardware one. I have no idea how to fix it, but at least you'd know your computer itself isn't broken.
