### Post by gordintoronto on 2012-08-03
Generally, motherboards, CPUs, disk drives and memory work fine with Linux, unless you get into some really oddball stuff.

I like the PCMech web site, although it is not focussed on Linux. The most common advice there is to get a great power supply, and make sure your case has excellent airflow. For your applications, those factors are especially important.

Does the software take advantage of multiple cores?

If I were buying a system for what you said, I would probably begin with an Intel Core i5-3570K. Antec EA 450 power supply, Cooler Master Centurion 5 case with an extra fan or two, WD Black hard drive. ASUS motherboard with SATA 6 GB and USB 3, and whatever memory ASUS suggests. You don't need a video card. A great system should cost well under $1,000. You don't need an SSD, it really only speeds up booting and program loading.

Sometimes new Ethernet adapters are not supported. That's the only component I would worry about. (The Ethernet adapter in my three-year-old computer was not supported when I bought it, but it has never had an Ethernet cable inserted, the computer has always been wireless.)

Let us know how you proceed!
