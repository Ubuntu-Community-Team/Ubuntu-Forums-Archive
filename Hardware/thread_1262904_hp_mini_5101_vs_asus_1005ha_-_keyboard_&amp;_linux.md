### Post by SergeantBort on 2009-09-10
I am runing an Asus 1005ha myself, with a dual boot set-up.  I used wubi, to install ubuntu dual boot, only issue was getting the drivers for the wifi, but once I got those and installed them, it has worked like a charm, sometimes even a better connection to the wifi then the windows side.  I will give you the keyboard takes some getting used to.

I have been using it as my primary computer for about a month now, I have a fullsize laptop and desk top as well, but this is just light weight and I use it for my schoolwork. 

I have been running the ubuntu for about a week now, and once I got it working, I have only booted into the windows side of things once, I like it.. and wubi is a great tool, for dual boot out of windows.

I personally liked the feel/set-up of the asus, over the HP's that I tried out.  Also the hardware seemed to work together better, because it loaded and ran things with less problems then the HP did, although these tests where done in windows, so it could be the extra's HP installed in windows that caused it to slow down.  Have not played with HP's linux version of their netbooks, I decided to just get the windows netbook, because you really are not saving money by running linux, and sometimes I will have to do somethings in windows for school.
