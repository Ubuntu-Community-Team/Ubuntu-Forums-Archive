### Post by stephanie-s on 2020-03-21
Hey everyone.

I discovered an issue with the Broadcom driver on my older MacMini.  Usually the first thing I do after a new installation is go to the Additional Drivers and install any that are missing.  So I installed the driver listed, and wifi works great.  This time I decided to use my Apple keyboard and mouse instead of my old Logitech bluetooth key board and integrated trackpad.  I went to the bluetooth settings and got them running, which was great.  But!  The mouse performance was not smooth, the right click function would stop, and Bluetooth would suddenly stop working.  After some searching and reading I discovered that the Broadcom driver also has something to do with Bluetooth functionality.  So in the Additional Drivers section I selected "Do not use the device" and voila, the Apple Keyboard and Mouse work perfectly in every way.  Without the Broadcom driver I can't configure Wifi in Ubuntu, but I don't need it as my MacMini is wired directly to my router.  I'm a happy camper.:p
