### Post by ready4thebreak on 2012-05-03
I'm posting this more for clarification and hopefully be a resource for those who are looking for it. I have the Logitech Combo MX5500 and MX Revolution(both Bluetooth models that came with one receiver to pair them with). 

I'm using the Ubuntu 12.04 as a Live USB install with persistence at the moment(wanted to test Unity thoroughly before committing). I hadn't used this combo of mouse and keyboard in previous versions of the distro(it was a recent purchase), but I was able to find old postings of people not having much luck[ (Example of a bug posting for Ubuntu 11.10)]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/870227").

At first boot, I was a bit worried, because magically, no movement or typing. I plugged in my trusty Microsoft optical and everything was fine there. I had to activate the Bluetooth applet and pair both devices by pressing the red 'Connect' button on the bottom of each device while the Bluetooth applet is searching for the devices. So the nice thing is Ubuntu allows you to turn the provided "Logitech Receiver" into a Bluetooth receiver that can be used to pair anything!

So it appears that the Logitech MX5500, MX Revolution Bluetooth(and non-Bluetooth model, I have both), and the receiver provided to you are supported by Ubuntu 12.04 on a fresh install. You will need to have an extra mouse handy unfortunately, but at least it works!

Oh and unfortunately the Search button, left scrollwheel, and the scrolling "clutch" (to switch from smooth scroll to line-by-line scroll) don't work. The forward and backward buttons do work. The media keys on the keyboard are spotty at best. The Volume keys appear to be about all that work.
