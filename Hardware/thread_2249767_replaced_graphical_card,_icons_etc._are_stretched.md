### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2014-10-24
HDMI IS an option regardless of what TV manufacturers say or don't say... The reason why some recommend a DVI-HDMI adapter - DVI at PC and HDMI at TV - is because some known interferences that outputting audio through HDMI can occur in certain usage scenarios. It isn't however a rule of thumb nor nothing of the sort. As a matter of fact, it seldom happens. There's also the overscan issue. Most TVs are factory set to compensate underscan from TV boxes and other non-PC devices. The result would be the borders going out of the frame but any good TV nowadays has a setting just for that. 

Bottom line: For any new TV/monitor **always **use a digital output (HDMI or DVI), never VGA, let alone with adapters.
