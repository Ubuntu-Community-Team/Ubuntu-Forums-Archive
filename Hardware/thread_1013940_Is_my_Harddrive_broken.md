### Post by Gimble on 2008-12-17
Several hard disk vendors provide software that can test their hard drives.  For example, here's a link to Seagate/Maxtor's web site:

[http://www.seagate.com/www/en-us/support/downloads/seatools](http://www.seagate.com/www/en-us/support/downloads/seatools)

In addition, with Windows loaded, you can run a disk test including a free space check.  If the drive is in reasonably good shape, it can remap the bad sectors to a good sector, preventing problems.

If both of the above don't solve your problem, then a new hard disk is likely the best solution.  The Acer 5672 doesn't appear to e particularly old, so a SATA replacement 2.5" drive shouldn't be difficult to find ( installation may depend greatly based on the laptop ).
