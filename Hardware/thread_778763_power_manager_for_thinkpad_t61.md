### Post by mariner09 on 2008-05-04
There's a number of power saving utilities on Ubuntu.  There's a power applet that runs in the notification area to the upper right and you can control the screen brightness on battery vs plugged in.

You can also set the screen to blank after a period of non-use.

Powertop can help pinpoint power hungry apps that you may or may not always need.  Keeping wireless on when you don't need it or bluetooth when you don't need it will drain the battery.

There's a CPU throttling utility cpufreqd that will slow your CPU down when you don't need it..

Google around.  I will say that power conservation is usually a little better on windows than linux, but with the proper tools you can get close...
