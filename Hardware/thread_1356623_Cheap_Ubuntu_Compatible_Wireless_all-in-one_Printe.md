### Post by =not4prophet= on 2009-12-16
I have had a lot of luck using Brother printers with Ubuntu.  They have linux drivers available for almost all of their printers (most of them available through the Ubuntu repos) and their network printers tend to be significantly less expensive than printers from HP.  I'm not sure how fast their inkjets go through consumables, but you can get a network multi-function inkjet printer for less than $130.

### Post by sgosnell on 2009-12-16
The HP OfficeJet J4680 works.  I've used one at work, and it was simple to set up.  It's problematic under Windows, but my Ubuntu laptop prints and scans without problems.  The J4680 has built-in wifi, and can connect to an access point wirelessly if that's what you need.  The other printers I've used are wired, using ethernet, to wireless routers, and they work fine, including several HP and Brother models.
