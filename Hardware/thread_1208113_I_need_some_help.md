### Post by shatterblast on 2009-07-09
Like every decent manufacturer does, a name brand should exist on the case of your computer.  After that, there should be some kind of model number.  If your system is custom built, then you may need to open up your computer.  If you have a technical person you frequently work with, he or she may have specifications on your machine.  (It could be out of friendship or potential profit.  Either way, good technicians commonly do that.)  After that, you can visit the manufacturer's site for the BIOS upgrade software.
