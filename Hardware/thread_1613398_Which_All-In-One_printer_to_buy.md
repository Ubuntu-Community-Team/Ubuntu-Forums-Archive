### Post by ghostborg on 2010-11-04
My HP Photosmart C5480 AIO Non-Wireless seems to be handled OK.
 It's now on a dual boot system (Win7/Ubuntu 10.10).

My personal feelings/experience in the past is Lexmark Ink cost was $65 for a $40 printer.

I did not like a Brother AIO wireless 480? its software was a pain with Windows so you could imagine what it would be like in Linux.
The printer also started to print black poorly with Brother Inks,  eventually I sent it to pasture. I could never get Ubuntu 9.04 to print to it. Software driver installed OK, saw it but hit print nothing.

As far as Epson goes I called with a question of whether I needed to replace my scanner lamp and they would not even talk to me without fronting my credit card, which I declined and went to the store and bought an HP scanner which within manufacturer warranty the top lid needed to be replaced and they did free of charge. I used to purchase Epson products.

I had a cheapo Canon printer (S220?) that seemed to last forever
and was sad the day it died, ink was cheap too.

I would lean towards HP or Canon brands.
