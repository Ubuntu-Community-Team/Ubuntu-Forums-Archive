### Post by kevinatkins on 2009-09-01
OK, sounds unpleasant!

It's fairly normal for certain components to get very hot during use - eg, the CPU heatsink will generally be very hot to touch, similarly any heatsink on a graphics card will also feel hot, as will RAM chips and hard disk drives. That doesn't necessarily indicate a failure.

Also be reassured that, unless something absolutely dreadful has happened, your hard drives and data are probably intact and could be transferred to another machine if required.

You say the machine isn't starting - does that mean there's no life at all? No fans, etc?

What I'd suggest is that you disconnect everything from the motherboard that isn't absolutely necessary (eg, CD-ROM drives, PCI cards, USB devices, etc), so you just have CPU, memory, video card, hard drive, and try booting again. Also try reseating all components, including the CPU. If the machine starts but you get no video, then based on your 'orange screen' description, try swapping out a different graphics card and see if that works. It's really a process of elimination..

Good luck!
