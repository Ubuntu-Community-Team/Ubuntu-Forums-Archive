### Post by MoebusNet on 2009-09-22
I've been running Ubuntu on my computer(s) for three-plus years now. Currently my only computer is my Dell Latitude D800 (1.4 Ghz Pentium M, 2 Gb RAM, 32 MB video card).

I changed to Ubuntu after spending a lot of time & money on antivirus software, malware software etc. but still kept getting viruses & bloat on WinXP.

Ubuntu has improved dramatically in the last three years, but still has areas that can use improvement. Battery life is one of those areas. Dell pays programmers to optimize its code for battery life for each model of laptop. Ubuntu, being community-supported, has tools for you to optimize battery life for yourself. In my estimation, Windows probably still does a better job on battery life for most people.

I've tried different Linux distos; Ubuntu has worked best for me. The strong community was a big factor in my selecting Ubuntu.

I still have my old, smaller original Dell WindowsXP hard drive for my laptop, but I rarely if ever hook it up as an external. My new bigger, faster hard drive is Ubuntu-only and has been since I bought it. There isn't anything I need to do that I can't do in Ubuntu. YMMV.
