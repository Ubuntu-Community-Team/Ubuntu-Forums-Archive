### Post by GregBrannon on 2010-05-02
I tried to find a picture that shows the electrical path from your video card to the display, but I had no luck.  In words, the fact that you have a whole line out in your LCD suggests (strongly) that there's an electrical short in the signal that drives that row.  The signals that drive the LCD's rows usually pass through a ribbon cable connected to the edge of the display at 'tabs' which are bonded to the glass.  Failures usually occur in the signal path after the ribbon cable.  Repairs can be as simple as "pressing" everything back together to replacing the tabs bonded to the glass.  The simple repair may be temporary.

Here's a link to a site that shows a simple cell phone LCD repair but also has other, more complicated LCD repair projects along the right side of the first page.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-05-02
Gently take your fingers and press on the bezel (the plastic part just outside of the glass screen).  You want to pinch on the areas right where the vertical line touches the bezel.  If the line of pixels comes back with pinching, then take a small binder clip and put in on the bezel where you were pinching.  

Otherwise, you have a small crack in the screen that is really not repairable.  There are etchings on the glass that conduct electricity.  If one of these etchings gets broken you lose either a vertical or horizontal line of pixels.

If the line flickers when slowly opening and closing the lid, then it's possible that the cable that runs through the hinge is worn out.  It can be replaced, but it's a supreme pain to do so.
