### Post by coffeecat on 2009-04-01
The 'R' key didn't work for a time in Vista and then did. Three keys didn't work in Ubuntu, then did. Sounds like an intermittent hardware fault - possibly a poor/loose connection with the keyboard connector. This is usually a tiny and very delicate ribbon cable in laptops.

It's unfortunate that you mentioned Linux. It gave the customer support people an excuse to fob you off.

If the fault doesn't go away soon by itself, can you restore the Vista partition(s) back the way they were and remove all traces of the Linux partitions? If so, do so, assume an innocent air, and tell them what's being happening in Vista only - don't mention Linux. In fact, run Vista for longer and I bet you those same three keys will misbehave again.

Another thought. Here in the UK, the onus is on the retailer, not the manufacturer. If the product is not fit for purpose, then the retailer **must** replace or refund. Refund may be optional - I don't know. Even better, if you buy by distance selling and return the product in the same condition you received it within 30 days, you are entitled **by law** to a refund. You don't have to give a reason; you just ask for your money back. Of course, some suppliers can be awkward, but that is the law here in Blighty.

I don't know what country you are in and whether your consumer laws are similar, but check your consumer rights. But before you exercise them, **get rid of Linux**. It's not fair; it's not just. But suppliers/retailers will use the presence of Linux to try to wriggle out of their legal responsibilities, even when it's an obvious hardware fault.
