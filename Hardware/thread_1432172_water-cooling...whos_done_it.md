> **c0nfusedami said:**

That's right. Pure distilled water doesn't conduct electricity. Hook a wire to one battery terminal and put the other end of the wire in distilled water.
Connect the other battery terminal to a light (12v with a 12v battery) and the other side of the light to a wire and put it in the water.

No light. Add a little salt and stir and watch the light come on.

Still wouldn't try it with a pc. The salt in the oil on your skin will make water conductive too, that's how you get shocked in water.

It was on one of the Beakmans World episodes.

I havent checked the prices laterly, but for my whole watercooling setup, the prices were about this- 
 Waterblock- $80 (IIRC) 
Pump- $50
Heatercore- $20 (could vary, I know how to deal with wrecking yard monkeys) 
Hoses and clamps- $20

Because I made a seperate box to bolt underneath my computer case, I have to add about $50 on steel and $20 on paints. You wouldnt need to spend that, it was just my choice, it made working on my computer a lot easier than having ghe whole radiatior/pump setup in the case.
