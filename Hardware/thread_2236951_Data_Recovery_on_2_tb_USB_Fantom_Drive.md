### Post by Mark Phelps on 2014-07-30
You didn't say which of the three "restore" options you used with win8, but basically, they are the following:
> If you're having problems with your PC, you can try to refresh, reset, or restore it. Refreshing your PC reinstalls Windows and keeps your personal files and settings. It also keeps the apps that came with your PC and the apps you installed from the Windows Store. Resetting your PC reinstalls Windows but deletes your files, settings, and apps&#8212;except for the apps that came with your PC. Restoring your PC is a way to undo recent system changes you've made.

So, if you chose Reset, it did exactly what it was supposed to do.

>  I went to a win8.1 forum, all they said was to use a re partition tool program or a data recovery program, but both of those only would restore 1 gb unless I purchased the entire program for $69.95.  This is typically the going rate for Windows data recovery tools.

An alternative Windows data recovery program is Recuva -- and it is free; but my experience in doing data recovery with Windows tools is you get what you pay for -- so, it if doesn't work, don't be surprised.
