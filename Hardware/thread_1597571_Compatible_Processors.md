### Post by srs5694 on 2010-10-15
> **grindboy said:**
> The motherboard is AM2 compatible so any AM2 processor should work

Unless there's something specific about AM2 that I don't know, this probably isn't true. Motherboards require support for specific CPUs, not just socket types. Electronic issues like voltages, frequencies, etc., vary between CPUs, and may not match between motherboard and CPU. Even if all those obvious factors match, the BIOS may not support the CPU.

I once forgot this and brainlessly ordered a new Intel CPU for an older motherboard. The socket type, voltage, and all other physical specs matched, but the CPU didn't work. *Then* I remembered to check the motherboard manufacturer's CPU compatibility list. My CPU wasn't on it and I couldn't find any updated BIOS. I ended up buying a new motherboard and memory (since that also wasn't compatible with the new motherboard) to go along with the new CPU.

The moral: Track down a CPU compatibility list for the motherboard, or plan on upgrading the motherboard and perhaps other components along with the CPU.
