### Post by mattharris4 on 2015-04-28
I don't directly know the answer to your question but I do have one suggestion.  I have priced gaming laptops and most of them go for $1800 plus (many well over $2K).  I have seen gaming desktops for about $1200 or so on Tiger Direct (Newegg probably has them too) with a high-line processor, 16GB of RAM, a separate video card with its own RAM, etc.  From my research the battery life on a gaming desktop is less than three hours (many only 1 1/2 hours), the cases get too hot to use on your lap and they are very heavy.  If you don't have a monitor a large one can be bought for less than $300 (much larger than the display on any laptop).  Since you want to help develop Unity 3D (it is great that you have the programming skills for that, programming is something I know next to nothing about) and evidently intend the computer for that purpose maybe a desktop is the way to go.  You can also have a good computer repair shop build one with exactly the features you need if that would help you (not too many builders build laptops nowadays).  Having one built also allows you to make sure the hardware runs well on Ubuntu assuming the shop knows and regularly deals with Linux.
