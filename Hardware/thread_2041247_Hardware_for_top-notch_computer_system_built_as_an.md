### Post by Xero Xenith on 2012-08-12
If you're just looking for something that will play videos very well, it's worth mentioning that you don't need to spend a lot of money any more. The Raspberry Pi, which retails for about £30, has sufficient hardware to play 1080p Blu-Ray quality videos very well over HDMI. (I'm not actually suggesting you use one, just using it as an example.)

Would it be acceptable for me to suggest a standard HTPC, which you can equip with a Blu-Ray drive and a ton of external storage which mirrors itself with rsync, as an inexpensive solution?

I've also been looking into entertainment systems, and an idea I've been toying with is a projector. Reasonable-looking 720p projectors start at around £350 and 1080p ones at £570. It may be worth considering an sexy 120" screen rather than a feeble 60" :P

EDIT: These projectors also support 3D when hooked into an nVidia GPU - just add glasses!

### Post by typhoon_tip on 2012-08-17
There are really plenty of solutions out there. Look at something QUAD core for the CPU, a very good motherboard and an AMD or NVIDIA video card (the hardware 2D video decoding capability of XBMC is supremely wonderful, it allows also to sync a display with frame rate set on the playback device !).

As for system, I would suggest to go for Ubuntu 12.04 64 bits with XBMC installed (auto-login, so you can choose either to login in XBMC at boot or Ubuntu for web surfing, maintenance etc. !).

I can speak loud for audio anyway: what is your requirement ? Audiophile or else ? And, do you have an amplifier already or you just want to somehow connect your 7.1 output directly to speakers ?

First of all, the audio card. Forget the integrated one. If you are audiophile, you want the top... This is what I am using and blown away by it:
[http://uk.asus.com/Multimedia/Audio_Cards/Xonar_D2PM/](http://uk.asus.com/Multimedia/Audio_Cards/Xonar_D2PM/)

Even an MP3 sound incredible with this card. In my case is connected with digital to the amplifier, but you can use the analog 7.1 output directly.

For speakers brand, in either cases, I would take a good look at Dynaudio entry level amplified speakers (would allow you to work without amplifier), they sound just as good as their bigger brothers, and they do not need an expensive amplifier (you connect the audio card directly to the speaker !).

[http://www.dynaudio.com/int/multimedia_sound/mc_15.php](http://www.dynaudio.com/int/multimedia_sound/mc_15.php)

You can use 7 speakers all same for a 7.1 system, with a sub-woofer of course (adjust the level of the speaker in the HTPC).
