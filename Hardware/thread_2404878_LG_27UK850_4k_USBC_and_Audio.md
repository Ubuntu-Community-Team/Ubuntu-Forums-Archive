### Post by StevenHarper on 2018-10-29
Just a post to help other users.

I have a **Dell XPS 13** 8th gen intel processor.
It can be powered over USBC (thunderbolt 3) and send out  a 4k signal with audio.

To complement this I have the newly released **LG 27UK850 monitor.**
By default it does "just work" -  the usbc cable supplied powers the laptop and also accepts the 4k signal.

In windows, the audio to the monitor works simply, however in Lubuntu & Ubuntu  it ins't easy to spot.
In Lubuntu I open the **pavucontrol** (Volume Control), in the tab that is off to the right is **Configuration**.

In here the Built-in Audio has a Profile selector - this list has audio channels for 4 HDMI slots - 1 for each thunderbolt port (yes you get 4)
Simple find the one that is not marked as (unplugged) and select it. - **Digital Stereo (HDMI 2) Output**

[IMG]https://i.imgur.com/xYocngr.png[/IMG]

That is it; you now have glorious monitor sound - well it's better than what is built into the XPS 13

I also have an Dell XPS15, it works perfectly with that too.

I hope this helps someone.
