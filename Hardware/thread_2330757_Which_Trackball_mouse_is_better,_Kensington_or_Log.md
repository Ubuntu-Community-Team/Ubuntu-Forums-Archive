### Post by kurt18947 on 2016-07-15
I've used both. I prefer the Kensington but found the scroll ring became harder to move after 3-4 years and my attempts with dry lube didn't help. I found the Kensington more comfortable if it were sloped away from my wrist. I found the included wrist rest about useless and made a wedge shaped wooden base with the fingers end about 10 mm. lower than the wrist end. Right now I'm using the Logitech M570. I think I'd prefer the Trackman Marble if it had a scrolling device but once I got used to using my thumb to manipulate the ball it works fine. The Kensington  Orbit Trackball is about 150 mm. wide so is wider than the Logitechs if that matters. Kensington's scroll ring is sweet though.

### Post by giga+bytes on 2016-07-17
My household has used three different Logitech trackballs over the last few years: the cordless optical trackman, the one you mention, and the M570 wireless. The first one was our favourite, and we would've bought several if we knew they were going to be suddenly discontinued. The one you mention wasn't used much because it wasn't as comfortable a design, and it lacked a scroll feature which quickly got annoying. The M570 took a little adjusting, then was good for a while, but about a year later the left-click button double-clicks most of the time, making it unusable. I found out that's a common problem with that model trackball, and they clearly aren't going to fix it (their own forums have years-long threads full of the same complaint, and no response from Logitech).

We recently decided to try the Kensington you mention, and after everyone trying it out we're all switching to it. It's not perfect, but it's the best design and comfort level we've found out of the small selection of options.
