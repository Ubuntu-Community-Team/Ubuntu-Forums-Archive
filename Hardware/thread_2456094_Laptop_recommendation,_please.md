Anyway, enough with the ranting :)
Any Linux users out there who found a good laptop that happens to be in line with my weird requirements?

Overall Needs:
I have a top-tier tower computer for my gaming and ML work. I need a laptop I can carry around to watch movies on flights, check email on vacation, no heavy duties...

_Display_
A. 15-16" of size
B. FHD (1080p, not 4k, not 800x480...)
C. GREAT color, contrast, brightness, viewing angles, etc... My eyesight is going, I want to enjoy it while I can!
D. Not too reflective
_Keyboard_
E. Reasonably ergonomic with decent key stroke and feedback
F. Very useful-to-me keys like End, Home, Delete, PgUp/PgDown not hidden as a 3'rd-level alternative to the "upload to TikTok" button
G. Backlit
_Trackpad_
H. I know I am wishful-thinking at this point, but any chance to still get physical left/right-click buttons?
_CPU_
I. I will be perfectly happy with a strong ARM or a 10-gen Intel. Could consider AMD, if the laptop is overall good on battery
_GPU_
J. I am perfectly fine with any modern integrated graphics. However, I know sometimes it's cheaper to buy the model with the GPU (I'll just disable it myself)
[U]Chassis
[/U]K. Metal or a very solid plastic case with robust hinges
L. Decent cooling solution to enable reasonable work without too much noise and throttling
_Battery_
M. +5 hours of light work, at a minimum
N. BIOS-controlled charging manager - or, at least, the laptop has known good drivers for the charging manager, for linux
_Price_
O. Under $1000. Can do $1200 for something really worth it
_Dimensions_
P. Under 2.6kg of weight
Q. Under 36cm in width. I don't really care about length or thickness

Of course, the idea here is for a linux-able laptop. I don't want to buy something and then "there are no drivers for half the components" and "the battery is dead after 2 months"

### Post by goinglinux on 2021-01-05
Where To Buy Computers With Linux Pre-Installed

If you want a laptop guaranteed to run Linux, purchase a computer with Linux pre-installed. You can get these from [System76]("https://system76.com/"), [Dell]("https://www.dell.com/en-us/work/shop/overview/cp/linuxsystems"), or [Linux Certified]("https://www.linuxcertified.com/linux_laptops.html"). If you want to broaden your selection, and install Linux yourself, and still get the best performance at the highest quality and guaranteed to run Linux, look at the [Ubuntu Certified Hardware]("https://certification.ubuntu.com/desktop") list or similar published lists.
Not in the US? Try a model from Tuxedo in Germany. [Tuxedo Notebooks]("https://www.tuxedocomputers.com/de/Linux-Hardware/Linux-Notebooks.tuxedo")
Or UK-based [Entroware]("https://www.entroware.com/store/") provides a range of desktops, laptops and servers pre-installed with Ubuntu.

I have some additional information in this article: [https://goinglinux.com/articles/HardwareDesignedForLinux_en.htm](https://goinglinux.com/articles/HardwareDesignedForLinux_en.htm)
