### Post by zipperback on 2009-09-10
If you are simply installing a faster processor of the same type, say for example a 2ghz 32bit pentium in place of a 1.6ghz 32bit pentium, then you shouldn't need to reinstall.

However if you are changing the type of processor such as a 64bit dual core Athlon in place of a 32bit Pentium, then you should do a fresh install.

Also be aware that different processors have different hardware requirements, for example some processors might require a "Socket 7" cpu socket, whereas another processor might require a "Socket 370" cpu socket.

Make sure you check to determine what kind of socket your new cpu needs.

There are alot of different types of cpu sockets & motherboards, so you have to make sure your new CPU is compatible with your currently existing motherboard hardware.

Here is a page that gives some information of various types of cpu sockets.

[http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/mother-cpu-charts-part-1,943.html](http://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/mother-cpu-charts-part-1,943.html)

I hope this helps you. Good luck with your hardware upgrade.
