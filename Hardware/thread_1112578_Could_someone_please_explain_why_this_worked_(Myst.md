### Post by DirtDawg on 2009-03-31
I have two hard drives installed in my desktop computer. One with Windows, the other with Linux. This morning, I installed the latest Ubuntu updates, which required a restart (simple enough). But when I restarted, the computer could not find either hard drive!

I switched the hard drives (master to slave/ vice-versa) but no luck. I tried each hard drive individually, but neither was recognized. I tried the Repair option from the Ubuntu Alternate Install disc, but the partitioner couldn't find either drive. Same problem when I tried a fresh reinstall. Nothing was working and I found it hard to believe both my hard drives died at the exact same moment.

After several hours, I called a friend for advice. He told me that, as strange as it sounded, I should try replacing the hard drives to their original position, then switch the power supply wires between the two. I almost didn't do it because it sounded so silly, but since I had tried everything else I could think of I figured there was nothing to lose. So I switched the power supply cables and, much to my astonishment, it totally worked! The computer booted up as normal! :confused:

Could someone please explain to me why simply switching those cables would make a difference? And why would this problem happen in the first place? My friend with the suggestion has no idea.
