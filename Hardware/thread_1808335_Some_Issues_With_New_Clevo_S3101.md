Hi everyone,

I recently bought a new laptop through Clevo (well one of their resellers): the S3101. I haven't been able to find much info about it on the forum, but maybe it's because those laptops are "rebranded" by the reseller before being sold and I don't know about the other names.

The hardware is as follows:
Processor: Intel Core I3
RAM: 8GB
Video Chip: it seems to be integrated to the Core I3, though I have 3 references there (GS45, HM55 and GMA4500HD).

And I don't think the rest matters much for my current issues, but if it does, I'll update this.

_Issues:_
1. It can't play HD video files. I have tested to read a few HD movies (either through Movie Player or through VLC) and they always freeze and are unable to render the image properly.
This might be because of some drivers for the graphic chip, but my knowledge stops here and I don't know how to check about this nor how to solve it.

2. It's very hot with fan always on and noisy even with slow to no use of the laptop.

From what I have read, Ubuntu does not by default override the BIOS to take control of processor frequency scaling and fan speeds. The BIOS does not seem to have a "Cool and Quiet" option or "Reduced energy" or anything that would look related to that.

I have seen a few threads dealing with this aspect of heating issues, but from one thread to the other blog post, information is such contradictory (some people telling you to uninstall what the previous were saying was the solution) that I prefer to ask before killing something.

Any advice welcome, and thanks!
