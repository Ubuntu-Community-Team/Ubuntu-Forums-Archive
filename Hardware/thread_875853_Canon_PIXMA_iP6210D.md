### Post by jbo5112 on 2008-08-26
I know of two options.

1) Contact Canon customer support and as for a driver.  Then purchase turboprint for anything from about $45 (USD) to $320+ :shock::shock::shock:, depending on the number of computers and if it is for business use.  While they do have a free trial, the dpi limit (IIRC 300) is too low.  IIRC any settings over the limit will print a rather obtrusive logo across every page.  Anything within the limit is quite washed out (blacks become medium greys) due to the printer's small ink drop size. :(

2) (My personal choice) Contact Canon asking for a driver (link on the bottom of their page or 1-800-828-4040), encourage everyone you can to do the same, and hope Canon stands behind their products enough to actually provide a Linux driver. ](*,)

I suppose there could be a third option of hacking the protocol and writing your own driver, but I assume anyone who could do that would probably know there wasn't a driver and post on a Gentoo, CUPS or Foomatic developer forum.  I don't know what's wrong with a company using standard printer languages like postscript or PCL (which was developed for Inkjet printers long ago and kept modern).
