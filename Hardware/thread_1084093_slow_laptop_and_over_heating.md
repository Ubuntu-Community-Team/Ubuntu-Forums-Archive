### Post by haagen dazs male on 2009-03-01
ok had to add my bit into this and to help people with what turned out to be quite a easy fix in the end. i had a problem of my inspiron 9100 laptop been so slow with the fans at full speed it almosted stoped and was so hot it reach about 78C far too hot for a laptop!! after talking to a IT friend and telling me heat was the bigest problem laptops face i desided to take my laptop apart. it seamed quite a big thing to do at the time as i had never taken one apart before but i had nothing to lose so i took it apart bit by bit. took a while and there was a lot of screws, just remember where they go after. you have to strip out every thing to get to the problem part which is the last part you have to remove which is the heat sink and processor (all one part) inside the air intake vent which just so happens to be in the middle of laptop where no one can get to without striping the laptop down to its smallest part is a heat sink vent which was so so blocked with fluff it was impossiable for air flow to cool the proscessor. so after a good vacum and clean and remembering how to put it all back again i now have my old laptop back good as new with quite fans and a lot faster. so if you fancey a hour or so with a screwdriver and a vacum and you got nothing to lost then go for it. hope this helps someone and yet another over heated laptop ready to be fried by dust build up.
