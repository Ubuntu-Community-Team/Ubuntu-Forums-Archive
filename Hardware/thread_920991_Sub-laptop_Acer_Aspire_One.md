### Post by ceratophyllum on 2008-09-15
I just got the 120GB Aspire One w/XP. Wanted linux but the guy at bestbuy didn't know what I was talking about! Anyway, even though I followed all the relevant steps in the Ubuntu Community Acer Aspire One page, I still find Wifi and sleep to both be very flakey:

Sleep sometimes fails to wake, but usually works fine.

Wifi sometimes just stops working. I tried using madwifi, but nsdis wrapper is vastly easier to install (using the gtk install front end) and seems to be more robust.  The suggested madwifi driver doesn't always survive a suspend/wake and simply unloading the wifi drivers isn't enough to make it reliable.

I suspect there are minor differences in the different Acer Aspire One laptops besides just the hard drive.  This machine is just too flakey for primetime running Ubuntu.

I didn't try installing Linpus Lite, because I imagine it won't work much better. (Acer probably massaged special kernel modules just for their particular AAO models.)
