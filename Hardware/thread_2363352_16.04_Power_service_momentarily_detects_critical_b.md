When I boot my UX302LG without the power adapter, the power service momentarily detects the proper battery level (90~100%) and then temporarily drops to 0%, causing a critical battery signal and starting the hibernate process before immediately jumping back up to 100%, but by then the hibernate process has started and the computer shuts off.  If I power it on again the cycle repeats.  The battery is a little old, but still functioning properly on my Windows partition.  As things are, I can't use Ubuntu without my power adapter.  What can I do about this?

### Post by Autodave on 2017-06-10
Since no one else has jumped in, here is a thought that I had. Boot it with power cord in and go to settings and then power manager. Go to the section for when on battery. There will be a place that asks you what to do when battery gets down to a certain point. Change that setting in the drop-down menu to "do nothing". Try that.
