### Post by newtoubuntu on 2009-05-01
I recently upgraded from Ubuntu 8.10 to 9.04 on my T61 Thinkpad, which has an NVidia Quadro Card.  On 8.10 I was notified that I could use a proprietary driver (almost immediately).  Also if I went to System and started drivers application.  It would recognize that I had an Nvidia Card.  And suggest a couple of drivers.  
On installing 9.04 that was not working.  When I went to the System/Hardware Drivers App, it was not recognizing that I had an NVidia card or recommending any drivers.  When I went to Add/Remove software application (I was looking for the Nvidia Drivers)  What I found was that the Hardware drivers app was NOT installed.  After installing it the app immediately recognized that the Card was there and allowed me to install the driver.  And now everything is working. 

Hopefully this will help some of you having similar issues.  Note It seems like a screw up on the install disk.
