### Post by The Ratman on 2010-09-18
I found this on an Eee PC user forum - it doesn't fix the way Ubuntu Netbook Remix gets its information about the battery, but it does stop the false message coming up.

[FONT=Fixedsys][COLOR=Blue]to get rid of the annoying " the battery may be broken",just simply press Alt+F2 and typing gconf-editor, go to

apps ->gnome-power-management ->notify, uncheck low_capacity. 

easy fix![/COLOR][/FONT]

### Post by nathan726 on 2010-10-28
Just thought I'd mention that this also affects regular Ubuntu and other Linux distros.

The problem is that Eee reports battery life as a percentage instead of a standard mAh value. So when power management sees design capacity as 5200mAh and last full capacity as 100mAh it freaks out assuming your battery is damaged.
