### Post by sleepingdragon on 2008-12-10
I've had a DX8400 delivered today with some mixed results. The good news was that Amazon delivered as promised, practically to the minute. Fantastic.

Bad news was that the scanner drive belt had jumped off the gear wheel which meant that the scanner was an absolute no-go. The DX, taking great care to ensure that all of its components worked properly, politely informed me that the scanner was malfunctioning and asked me to switch off the DX. Oh goody...

So, here's what happened. Some of us make a meagre living as electro-mechanical engineers. It was actually quicker to take the scanner bed apart and thread the belt back over the drive gear than it was to get hold of Amazon to try and sort out a replacement (I cancelled that later).  Remember kids, don't try this at home as it will cripple your warranty and any hope of getting a replacement in the future...

I was genuinely surprised that the DX was capable of being disassembled - it had normal screws in obvious places. I was also very disappointed that the DX left the factory in that state. To dislodge a belt like that (in the way it's been built on the DX) would require considerable force in transit and would certainly have damanged other parts. This can only be down to lack of care during manufacture. 

I've always considered Epson to make solid machines, I've never had one fail until recently when my old CX3600 finally failed due to solid hard use over several years.

On the upside, the libsane-extras picked up the scanner immediately and the printer is working really well. So, I got what I paid for, but the DIY assembly to the scanner left a lot to be desired.
