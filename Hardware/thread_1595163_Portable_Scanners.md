### Post by desertdog on 2010-12-29
For a list of portable sheetfed scanners see:[https://sites.google.com/site/roadwarriorscanner/](https://sites.google.com/site/roadwarriorscanner/)

The travelscan 464 has been supported the longest and should work out of the box.

Support for most of the others was added in version Sane-Backends-1.0.21. I think ubuntu still ships version 1.0.20, so you may have to upgrade. For instructions, see:[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Scanners](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Scanners).

Some of these scanners can be found used on ebay for $40-60. Almost all are USB powered, so just one cord is needed. Most can do 600dpi. I find that 150-300dpi is usually enough for documents. A plastic protector sleeve helps with flimsy receipts and newspaper clippings. Photos are better done on a flatbed scanner, but can be done on these.

The duplex scanners (two sided) such as Visioneer XP300 and Docketport 485/487 are not fully supported at this time.

I think they are all made in China.
