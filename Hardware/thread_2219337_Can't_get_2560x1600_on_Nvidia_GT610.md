I saw that on nVidia's site but it carefully does not mention resolution with HDMI. Normally TV with HDMI just needed 1080p.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HDMI](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HDMI)
It depends on version of HDMI supported?
> The maximum pixel clock rate for HDMI 1.0 was 165 MHz, which was sufficient to support [1080p]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1080p") and [WUXGA]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WUXGA") (1920×1200) at 60 Hz. HDMI 1.3 increased that to 340 MHz, which allows for higher resolution (such as [WQXGA]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphic_display_resolutions#WQXGA"), 2560×1600) across a single digital link.
