### Post by tgalati4 on 2009-08-12
CCFL's are good for 10K-15K hours.  So with heavy use, say 240 days/year*8 hours/day*4 years is 7700 hours.  So in 2300 hours it will die.

### Post by jflaker on 2009-08-14
I had worked as a bench tech repairing laptops as well as supporting them for my company supporting remote sales people who use and abuse their systems heavily.  


With remote people depending on their systems in a corporate environment, I would replace the system....as a user in control of your own system, I would use it until the backlight dies.  The LCD is a full part.  You used to be able to replace the backlight only, but it is integrated and the LCD, backlight and plastics are usually one piece/part and ordered in the color of the system case.

In the mean time, unless you WANT to replace your system...I would canvass eBay in search of the same make and model laptop for parts...most of the time, the systems for parts will have a working LCD panel, but have something else wrong which prevents the system from booting or even powering on.  Do some research...you could probably find a parts laptop for under $100, far cheaper than an OEM NEW part.

once the LCD dies, dismantle your system and replace the LCD.  Can be well worth the hour or two replacing your LCD.
