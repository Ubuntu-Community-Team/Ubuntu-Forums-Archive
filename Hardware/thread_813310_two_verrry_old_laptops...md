In my opinion, no. Xubuntu is really just the standard Gnome Ubuntu with a thin layer of XFCE over top. So Xubuntu would burden those machines almost as much as regular Ubuntu would. Try it for yourself and see if you like it, but I think you'll find them to be just as sluggish as if you had given them the full Ubuntu suite.

You'd do better to look for other distros, or to build up from a minimal Ubuntu installation. Here's a thread that lists very small distros, according to their ISO size.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456)

Here's an entire subforum for other-than-Ubuntu talk.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=147](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=147)

And here's the wiki page for a minimal installation.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems)

Ask around if you have any problems; your situation is not unique. ;)
