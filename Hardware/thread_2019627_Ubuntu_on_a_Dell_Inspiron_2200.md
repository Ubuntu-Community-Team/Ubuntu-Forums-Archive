### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-07-07
Can you be more specific by what you mean by "slow?"  Slow in dragging windows? Booting up?  What?

The slow Celeron CPU in this laptop was probably slow the day it was purchased.  I wouldn't expect it to be fast on a modern OS.  I know this is beyond the capability of the average user, but I like to upgrade these slow CPUs (even in laptops) when I can.  Upgrading this Celeron M to a Pentium M would probably increase battery life/reduce heat, too, because that Celeron M lacked "enhanced Speedstep" support (the ability to slow the CPU down when idle).  Some laptops are easier than others for upgrading the CPU.  It's not something worth paying much for, though.

Bad and redundant. They are not supported and haven't been for a year or two. Doubtful you could find them anyhow.

What you want is a lighter system. You could try installing xfce4 or lxde desktop environments (log out, choose it from 'Sessions' pop-up, log in) or just reinstall with Xubuntu or Lubuntu, 12.04 or 10.04 LTS, they are both long term-support, the former for five years, the latter for a year. (... and HDD is also probably a slower 5400RPM in combination with the Celeron).
