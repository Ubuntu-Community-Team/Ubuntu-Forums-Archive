### Post by hibliss on 2009-08-06
Look for a router with more ram, and with more flash memory for firmware upgrades.

The two best I have personally used are the Linksys WRT54GL (L stands for Linux), which also has a decent amount of flash ram and the capability to be upgraded to DD-WRT, Tomato, or OpenWRT. Available for under $50 here - [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833124190&Tpk=wrt54gl]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833124190&Tpk=wrt54gl")

Another more expensive option is the ASUS wl500gP. This is a beast, this is the best consumer router that is reasonably priced. It can act as Network Attached Storage if you plug a USB hard drive in to it, you can VPN straight in to it (no more broken SSH connections), and it can be upgraded to all of the same aftermarket firmwares (to allow more features because it has USB ports and a better processor). I love this router. Here is a link -- [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833320030]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833320030")

Stay away from the new Linksyus WRT54G/GS series, they are making them cheaper and cheaper every day, and putting VXworks on them (garbage firmware).
