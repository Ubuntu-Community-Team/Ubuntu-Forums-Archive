### Post by dantesinfernos on 2008-05-26
hello all! My question is this. I'm upgrading my computer soon, and I'm dual booting vista 32/Ubuntu, but I have a few options. I have need for speed, so I was considering raid 0 with two 500 gb hard drives from WD because I already have one. Also, I could get a 150 gb Raptor drive for the OS and save the 500 gb for storage to share between the two OSes. Lastly, I could just split my 500gb hard drive into the partitions 100/100/300 and use the 300 gb as shared storage. Will getting the Raptor drive really speed up ubuntu by that much? I would rather not spend the money on the raptor if it is insignificant. Thanks ahead of time.

### Post by tamoneya on 2008-05-26
i would go with the 2x500GB raid 0 instead.  The speed increase will first of all only be really noticable at boot up due to the way ubuntu handles RAM and swap space.  Also you will get an additional speed boost due to RAID 0 spanning the data across both drives so it should narrow the margin significaltly.

### Post by sdennie on 2008-05-27
While using the system for prolonged periods of time, you'll probably see a bigger speedup by investing more in RAM than in disks.  Depending on your system usage, a RAID0 array isn't that useful for speed if everything you are using is cached in RAM.
