### Post by Wiebelhaus on 2009-08-25
I've used Multi-channel KVM's for 8 years 10 hours a day , Some hardware just HATES KVM's and other times it's the KVM that sucks , the worst KVM I ever had was a Belkin 4 channel manual switch the second worse was a 4 Channel I/O Gear USB KVM , that stupid thing hardly ever worked and the only time it would was when I re-plugged the stupid thing and relied on Plug & PLay to pick it up. If you have to rely on P&P (like with the USB versions) your experience will be horrible , There's a tech that works near me that uses a P&P KVM (I can't recall the brand)  and at least once a day he curses the stupid thing.

A side note:

In the early years I used Debian and for the last 4 or so years I've used Ubuntu both OS's as main full time tech boxes. The OS seemed not to make much of a difference other then Debian getting confused with so much P&P plugging and unplugging from time to time.
