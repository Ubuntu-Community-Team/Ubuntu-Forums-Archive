most any decent name mobo will work well with linux, but you have to watch out for hte cheap ones. I tend to buy asus and gigabyte, and sometimes intel. I used to really dig abit, but they no longer do mobos, so...

I don't know if I have used that board specifically, but it looks alright. the intel gma is weak, but you have a pcie slot to upgrade with. the biggest worry for me is that there are only 2 ram slots. it also worries me that the 1333 speed is with overclocking.
I would probably buy this one, with an atx case instead of micro, and a video card:
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813131639](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813131639)

but thats just me. I perfer atx as there is little you can do about thermal problems if you are in a very small box, but then again, the core2's run pretty cool.
