Has anyone trid to connect a computer modem (using the RJ11 jack) to a cell phone (via the headphone/mic jack) to do dialup using a cell phone?

This is not Ubuntu specific, but this is my favoriate forum. Maybe someone can point me to a place where this has been discussed elsewhere.

I have a need to test a modem service for a friend and I don't have a land line, and I also don't want to enable the data package on my cell phone service.

I would like to use a cell phone headphone, cut the wire (discard the mic and ear piece) and connect it to a (also cut) phone line that I plug into my laptop's RJ11 jack.

I see 2 or 3 issues with this, 

#1, I don't think the audio is really full duplex. It seems when someone is talking and I try to interrupt, they cannot hear me till they shut up.

#2, would be the relative signal levels on the cell phone from earphone output vs. mic input. The mic is a very low level compared to the earphone output from the phone, some kind of impedance matching seems to be in order.

#3 delay. I tried to call another cell phone and listen to both and there was a noticeable delay between when I spoke, and I would hear myself.

I envision an old style modem where one would place the phone headset into a box with a mic and speaker but I would like to convert without going through audio. Just straight analog to analog.

For this test, I wouldn't even mind using the cell phone mic and speaker if I had one of those old style modems.
