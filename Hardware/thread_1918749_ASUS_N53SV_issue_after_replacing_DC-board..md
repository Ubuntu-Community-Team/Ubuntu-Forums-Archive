### Post by SlowGeorge on 2012-02-25
Wireless:
1. Perhaps the wireless on-off switch on the board did not engage with the switch next to optical drive door when you installed the dc board. (Does the switch seem to work?) 2. Or perhaps the dc board was overheated during soldering. 3. Or maybe the cables to the dc board are not fully seated.

Keyboard:
Maybe the ribbon cable is not fully seated.

I'd try taking the laptop apart and assembling it again.

Of course you already know how to do that, but for anyone else...here's a video: Assembling ASUS N53SV Laptop after DC Board Repair - [http://youtu.be/KN7i5nBUZqc](http://youtu.be/KN7i5nBUZqc)
