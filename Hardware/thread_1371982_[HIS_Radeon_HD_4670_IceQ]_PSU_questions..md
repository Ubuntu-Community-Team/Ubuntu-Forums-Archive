### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-04
It should work, even on 300watts (I can find real power consumption tests using a 4650 that use far less power than people would guess). One of the reasons why they say '400 watts+' is because of 'yum cha' power supplies. You know, teh horrid things you get in $40 cases, where they change the sticker on the power supplies every few years to reflect current power supply sizes. A decent brand 300watt is probably going to be a much better PSU than some 'eagle star brand' with a 400 watt sticker on it. 

But honestly.....I wouldnt risk it. $100+ on an AGP card is excessive. Its probably quite a old PSU, and years dont help them. Lots of extra Hdds and CD/DVD drives will just push up power consumption. Besides that, there isnt much point getting a newish card for an AGP system, IMO anyway.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-05
AGP works, but its a lot more expensive than PCI-E now. That card in AGP is $124 at newegg (yes, I know about the mail in rebate but I enver count them LOL). The PCI-E version is $80. DDR1 is also a lot more expensive than DD2/3. For the extra money you are going to spend on AGP and PCI-E you would be a long way toward a new system...

You really should be looking at getting a new power supply with that card, while I said it should work that doesnt mean you wont have nasty issues from an undersized power supply. 

If its for gaming, it wont just be he geforce4 holding you back, lots of newer games like dualcores, and more CPU power than the old P4s ever had.
