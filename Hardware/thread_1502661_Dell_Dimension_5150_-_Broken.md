### Post by efflandt on 2010-06-06
If you cannot even get the BIOS splash screen, the video may be toast.  If you get that, but the CD blacks out it may be a different issue.  Pop it open and see if it has a card or is built into th motherboard (and whether you recognize a brand name on a chip.

We had integrated video go out on a Dell Optiplex at work once, but it was under warranty.  That was my first experience changing out an entire motherboard.

Google "Dell Dimension 5150" for the manual.  There are 4 diagnostic LED's.  After normal boot, all 4 should be on.
