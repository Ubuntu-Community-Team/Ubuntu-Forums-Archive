### Post by opto-laptop on 2009-03-18
Yes, it may have been fine with xp, but even running full speed it shouldn't overheat no matter what O/S.
Check the vents are clear. I have to clean my fans every few months. I even pulled the CPU heatsink off and found a lack of thermal compound out of the factory so had to apply that myself and re-assemble.
I wouldn't suggest everyone goes to that extreme but I've heard of the same problem in more than one brand of laptop.
Another strategy is remove the battery. If you are on a desk and plugged in 90% of the time, charge it then remove it. It will increase battery life and reduce heat.

### Post by Weidbrewer on 2009-03-18
Yeah, that's the funny thing about laptops - they're not really made to work on laps.   Air flow doesn't get to them right, and then they suck up a lot of fuzz.  (I know you said you've been using it on a table, just saying.)

But, as they are designed with form in mind first, and then function, they tend to have a lot crammed in and no where for the heat to go.  Under the best of circumstances, they are barely in balance.  A little fuzz, a little too long on the lap, a battery heating up - and everything goes out of whack.
