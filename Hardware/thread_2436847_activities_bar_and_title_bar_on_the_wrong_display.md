### Post by CatKiller on 2020-02-14
What you're looking for is to set the monitor you're interested in as the primary monitor. By default, that's the first one that the system discovers at boot (screen 0), but it's configurable. In KDE that's simply part of the monitor settings, but I don't know how Gnome handles it.

### Post by vanadium on 2020-02-17
In standard Ubuntu, go to Settings, Displays. With the "Join Displays" active (i.e. different content on the two screens), change the "Primary Display" setting to the monitor where you want the Gnome Shell controls to appear.
