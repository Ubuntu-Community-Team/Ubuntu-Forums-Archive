### Post by dabl on 2010-02-25
Your ability to boot from the SDHC card has nothing to do with Ubuntu.  In your Acer's BIOS is probably a "boot from USB" option, and you need to "x" that.  Then you have a "Boot Device Sequence" option, and you need to move USB to the top of the list.  Then, if the interface to the card reader is USB (most are), and you insert a bootable SDHC card with an operation system on it, yes, Ubuntu or any other Linux system will boot that way.
