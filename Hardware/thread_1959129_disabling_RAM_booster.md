### Post by xyzzyman on 2012-04-18
He probably means 'boot booster' which on those eee pc's does have to be disabled while changing out ram sticks... The RAM shouldn't be causing it to overheat and shut down. You either have a fan or vent plugged up, you're running it on top of your bed, the heatsink isn't seated right, or the fan is disconnected... Or it's completely defective and the guy sold it to you knowing this.

(There were some eee pc's that shipped with 900mhz celeron m's downclocked to ~650mhz becauuse of overheating at 900mhz. Some people realized this and reclocked them up, maybe this guy did that?)
