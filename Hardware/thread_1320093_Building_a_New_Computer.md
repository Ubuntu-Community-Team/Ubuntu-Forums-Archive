### Post by tamasrepus on 2009-11-09
I'd eschew the Velociraptor hard disks (I have one myself, I'm not that impressed with it) and get an SSD instead. At the moment the Intel X25-M has the best price/performance ratio of SSDs currently on the market.

AFAIK you can't burn or play Blu-ray on Linux yet. If you're going to be pro-open source please vote with your wallet and not buy proprietary/closed products (there's a rant in here about NVIDIA, but they're dedicated enough to the Linux community to let it go).

I'm personally never buying Thermaltake anything again (something about their products being loud, difficult to use, and low quality relative to the price paid), but everyone to their own.
