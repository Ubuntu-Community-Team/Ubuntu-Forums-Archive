### Post by SaintDanBert on 2010-09-23
Please don't panic and go away mad or simply go away.

In general, "thinkpads" are very linux friendly. I have similar issues with my Thinkpad X61-tablet (tablet-PC). I even have an entire thread asking why I'm not getting any answers. Keep bumping...

Many of the issues are not specific to *-buntu, but are hardware or drivers or configuration. You need to find out what to do about those where ever you find answers then return here to discover how to deploy those solutions under *-buntu.

Have you found [http://www.thinkwiki.org/](http://www.thinkwiki.org/) on the net. There is information there. 

Have you found [ur]http://www.linuxquestions.org[/url]. They have been lots of help for me.

With Ubuntu Lucid (v10.04 LTS), they have mostly completed a fundamental shift in how things work. Mostly gone are any sort of directed-graph, sequential, synchronous system start. The old way was called "system-V init" (that's roman numeral equals 5). The new way is called "upstart". As the kernel finds hardware and loads drivers, it will "throw events". The upstart configuration "catches events" and causes configured jobs and tasks to run. These actions do all sorts of things -- including "throw events" -- and devices get created and configured and start-up applications and services start running.

The most difficult (for many) aspect of all this event-driven processing involves X11 and Xorg configuration and start-up. With Lucid, things auto-magically create a pretty stripped /etc/xorg.conf file. The real X-config parts are elsewhere and different. 

Upstart and "udev rules" are joined at the hip in all of this. To sort out boot-time hardware activation you must follow the bouncing ball through your upstart jobs and tasks and "udev rules."

Bonne chance,
~~~ 0;-Dan

### Post by knightlie on 2010-12-26
Hi,

I've got the headphone jack working on my IdeaPad Z560 using the instructions at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HdaIntelSoundHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HdaIntelSoundHowto) - basically add the line 
"options snd-hda-intel model=ideapad" to /etc/modprobe.d/alsa-base.conf.

Hope this helps.
