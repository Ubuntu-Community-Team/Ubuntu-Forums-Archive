### Post by I2k4 on 2011-06-07
A few points:

1) You can get everything you list for $350:  you can get a pretty good and very portable netbook with very good 6-cell battery life for $250-$350.  You've alread mentioned a desktop screen and wireless keyboard/mouse combo for about $150 more.  Those extras will equip you for serious work.  A netbook for real work on the road still whups any fancy pricey tablet alternative, which will run you closer to $500.  I won't pump any brand - the best choice of hardware (all use the same reliable Asian components) is based on your personal preferences for keyboard layout, screen quality, look and feel.  These personal preference factors are very different for different makers, and it's worth a visit to a big box retailer to compare how they look and feel in your hands, since you plan to live with the thing for years.

2) You don't have to worry much about processing power if all you want it for is basic operations like Google Docs and email.  Apart from high intensity uses like games and video, almost anything on the current market will do fine.  You should look for 11n wi-fi instead of 11g for faster present or future broadband compatibility.

3) For the money you're talking about, SSD storage will be much more limited than a regular hard drive - I'd say the main advantage to SSD is if you're going to be packing the unit over rough terrain or throwing it around.  If you wanted hard drive speed you'd spend more.  

4) Anything pre-owned (aka pawn shops or eBay) is at risk of wasting all your money or fighting with some jelly-roll haircut fast talker for months about any problems.  You'll get a good no questions hardware warranty for at least a year from a major manufacturer, which will suss out any defects to the equipment for the years ahead.  Any warranty on used stuff is worth the paper it's scrawled on plus the price of a gooey salesman's smile.
