### Post by wintercorn on 2010-05-17
I'm having problems writing on external USB drives. 

Data transfer starts at fairly ok USB 2.0 rates but quickly goes down after the first 100-200 MB and eventually stabilizes at around 600 kB/s. Backing up my 250 GB home partition takes more than 1 week (or that's what Nautilus estimates, I obviously didn't wait that long). Writing on the USB device also maxes out both of my CPU cores so, in addition to that extremely slow writing speed, the computer becomes unusable during that time. Reading from the USB drive becomes extremely slow, too.
 
After un- and remounting things go back to normal, however, after having transfered the next 150 MB, here we go again. These problems occur for both my external USB hard drive and flash memory sticks.

From the many bug reports and questions I've found, it seems that many people experienced similar problems. However, even though some of the reports date back to Ubuntu Hardy, I couldn't figure out a solution or even a valid workaround from those threads. Using cp instead of C&P in Nautilus makes no difference.

I'm willing to put up with some inconveniences but being able to copy a movie to a USB stick and, most important, to backup my data is essential. Has anybody with similar issues found a way to get around this?

My system: Ubuntu 9.04, fully updated, on Thinkpad T61
