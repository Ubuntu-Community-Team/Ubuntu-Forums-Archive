### Post by TheFu on 2022-01-27
Inkjets all dry out.  If you want printing just a few times a year (like me), then get a laser printer. Toner lasts years and can be shaken to even out the printing when it starts becoming lighter.  I have a toner cartridge in my laser printer that has to be almost 10 yrs old. Think it was $35 new.

I had an epson injet, but it wasn't supported on Linux, so it was "gifted" to a Windows user.  I'd say to be extremely careful buying Epson and Canon printers. EXTREMELY careful.  Many of their models have ZERO Linux support. Also, check whether the model mandates "phoning home" to work or may report to the corporate home-office about use and levels. All of those requirements are non-starters for me, but we each have different requirements.

Look through the printer configuration tool for the vendors and models that are already inside there. Pick one of those.  Really want "driverless" printing.  Any postscript printer should support IPP and "just work."  

Probably want to stay away from wifi printers too. Those seem to have the most issues, just look on the internet for wifi issues with printers.

### Post by QIII on 2022-01-28
Toner is not known to be carcinogenic, but the WHO does list it as a *possible* carcinogen because in contains carbon black.  In the amounts one might reasonably expect to encounter as a user, any risk is vanishingly small.  There is risk to everything, of course.

Toner might be dangerous to your health in two ways, however:

1.  As a fine particulate, it can be irritating to the eyes and mucous membranes.  It can also be breathed in and cause lung irritation in much the same way as fine dust might.

2.  Some toners contain noxious chemicals that may or may not be toxic.  I also know that it is not biodegradable because it can contain iron oxide, styrene, acrylate and naptha.

Best bet?  Wear eye protection and a mask.  But I wouldn't personally lose any sleep over handling a toner cartridge once a year.
