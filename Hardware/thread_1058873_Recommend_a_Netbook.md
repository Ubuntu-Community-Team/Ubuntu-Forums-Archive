### Post by Chibone on 2009-03-08
I've been looking for a netbook too. Some recommendations from my searches:

Eee 1000HE - uses an Atom 280, which is a bumped up Atom 270 (FSB 533 -> 667 MHz and clockspeed 1.6 GHz -> 1.66 GHz.)  No misplaced shift key like on the Dell Mini 9 and earlier Eees.

Acer Aspire One (various models) - These are the cheapest in price netbooks I could find without being absolutely crippled in some department like low storage, non-Atom processor, or weak battery.  Run around 250 dollars when I checked some deals sites.

HP Mini 2140 - An HP 2133, but with Atom instead of VIA processor, upped screen resolution, metal instead of the HP Mini 1000's plastic, and includes an Expresscard slot.  Also a pretty good keyboard.

Samsung NC10 - Probably best keyboard.  Also if I remember correctly the battery life on these is super great.  Like 6 hours or something.  Of course if you buy extended battery on the other models it's probably a wash.

MSI Wind / Mini 9 - I would pick these netbooks if I were going to boot OS X from them.  Otherwise not really standout in my opinion.

### Post by m_duck on 2009-03-08
I have an eee 1000 that came with Xandros, it's currently running Windows 7 (just wanted to see if it could - it can) but has had other distro's beforehand. Cruncheee was very nice, based on Ubuntu with Openbox WM and ran nicely. I also had a regular install of intrepid with the [array.org lean kernel]("http://www.array.org/ubuntu/setup-intrepid.html") and [acpi scripts]("http://forum.eeeuser.com/viewtopic.php?id=49081") from the eee forum which also ran well (array kernel gets wireless etc working and acpi scripts get hotkeys and other buttons to work as well as keeping the fan speed down).

Of course, I've had no real experience of the other netbooks so have no basis for comparison but the eee 1000 meets my needs. The specs all seem to be largely similar anyway so in the end I guess it would be personal preference and hardware compatibility that would make the decision.

### Post by Chibone on 2009-03-08
Whether recommendations work with Ubuntu or not:

EEE 1000HE - anecdotal, but I helped someone who asked about the Atom 280 in the 1000HE and they said everything worked great

Aspire One - [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne)
Card readers don't work that well yet, and hibernate doesn't work on the A110L (maybe, depends on swap, not sure)

HP 2140 - should work, there's a tweaked Ubuntu (MIE - Mobile Internet Experience) for the Mini 1000, with similar hardware. Not sure though.

MSI Wind - [http://wiki.msiwind.net/index.php/Ubuntu_8.04_Hardy_Heron#Option_2:_Compiling_Drivers_for_the_Supplied_Wireless_Card](http://wiki.msiwind.net/index.php/Ubuntu_8.04_Hardy_Heron#Option_2:_Compiling_Drivers_for_the_Supplied_Wireless_Card)
Wireless and suspend are the only problems, apparently fixable with some work

Dell Mini 9 - [http://www.ubuntumini.com/](http://www.ubuntumini.com/)
You can get Ubuntu on it from Dell, so should work with a Non-Dell Ubuntu.
