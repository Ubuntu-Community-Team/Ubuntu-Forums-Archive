### Post by Sisyphus48 on 2008-11-15
This isn't a slam on any video card.  I wanted to share this experience in case someone else might find the info useful.  I built my present computer a year ago.  It has AMD Athlon(tm) 64 X2 Dual Core Processor 6000+, 4 GB of DDR2 Ram (although Ubuntu only recognizes 3), a WD 80 GB HD of which 29 % is use and I put a PNY Nvidia GeForce 7300 GT video card in it.  I play some video games (Nexius and Alien Arena mainly) and watch a lot of TV shows on the internet.  I thought maybe an upgrade in the video card area might make all the above a little more enjoyable .  So, I purchased SAPPHIRE 100248L Radeon HD 3850 512MB 256-bit GDDR3 PCI Express 2.0 x16 HDCP Ready CrossFire Supported Video Card.  It basically had double or better of all the specifications like Core Clock speed and DDR2 memory.  I put it in expecting a noticeable improvement but not expecting to be WOWed by the difference.  The result was zero, nill, nada or at least nothing my 60 year old eyes could detect.  I checked and rechecked the drivers and the installation and apparently all was as it should be.  So, after fiddling with it, and checking all the forums to make sure it was working properly , I boxed it up and sent it back to NewEgg (my favorite place to buy computer "stuff").  Although NewEgg, as usual, had no problem letting me return it, by the time you added shipping and the restocking fee it cost approximately $20 to return it.I hope this helps someone.

### Post by taurus on 2008-11-15
Just so you know, the reason Ubuntu only detects 3.2GB of your 4GB of RAM is because you are running 32bit version.  If you install the 64bit, it will detect/use all 4GB of your RAM.  It is the same as if you install 32bit windows.
