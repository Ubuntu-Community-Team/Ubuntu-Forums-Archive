### Post by pjmed on 2009-05-05
Hello Everyone.
This is my first post and my first day using Linux. I am currently running Ubuntu 9.04. I am having some problems myself which I hope I can get fixed but I do have a suggestion for your heat problem. I myself have a VAIO S260. I still don't know if I have a problem with heat but my fan is not running all the time. I did notice that I have the visual effects set to NONE so I don't know if that makes a difference. I did this because of the "poor" performance I feel I get when there are any visual effects turned on. Besides that, right click on the top bar and choose add to panel. From the list pick CPU Frequency Scaling Monitor. What this will do is let you know at the frequenzy your CPU is running and you will also be able to pick between PERFORMANCE, POWERSAVE, ONDEMAND, ETC. With this application you should be able to keep the computer cooler by choosing a lower frequency or a different power performance setting. Give me a few days and I will let you know if my laptop get hot. I will mention that right now it feels much warmer than with windows xp so time will tell. The heat seems to me concentrated under the right palmrest.

My main problem is the video performance. There seems to be too much tearing and sluggish performance when moving windows around and stuff like that.It is not as smooth as it should be, feels like the video card driver issue. I haven't installed any video drivers, the system is "as is" since this is a fresh install.
