### Post by RamonBianco on 2011-03-18
(You know, the "Most" is redundant.  ;))

But your question is too general - "top of the line set of components for Ubuntu that I could actually take advantage of?"

What would you want to run, if not games?  How do we know what you could, or would want to, take advantage of, now or in the future?

And what price range do you have in mind?

I'm looking at a pretty good, but not top-of-the-line, build myself:
[LIST]
[*]BIOSTAR TP67B+ LGA 1155 Intel P67 Motherboard
[*]Intel Core i5-2500K 3.3GHz CPU (planning to overclock to 4-5 GHz, so the next, $30, item)
[*]COOLER MASTER Hyper 212 Plus CPU Cooler
[*]Mushkin Enhanced Silverline 4GB (2 x 2GB) DDR3 1600 RAM - Model 996946
[*]ASUS ENGT430/DI/1GD3(LP) GeForce GT 430 (Fermi) 1GB Video Card
[*]Antec Three Hundred case + BP430 power supply
[*]HITACHI Deskstar 7K1000.C 0A39261 160GB 7200 RPM Hard Drive (small root partition, and low-usage /shared partition)
[*]HITACHI Deskstar H3IK5003272SP (0S02859) 500GB 7200 RPM Hard Drive (swap and /home partitions)
[/LIST]
**About $700 U.S.**  Add $100 to get the Intel i7-2600K, and you're closer to the top, but not optimal, I'm sure.  :P

Or save $160 and get an AMD Phenom II X4 955 Black Edition 3.2GHz CPU and a GIGABYTE GA-880GA-UD3H motherboard with on-board graphics.  (And being older tech, Ubuntu 10.x should support it "out of the box", unlike the other Sandy Bridge options [new this year] that I list above and below.)

Or save $xxx by getting an Intel H61 or H67 motherboard and a socket 1155 CPU with build-in graphics, and drop the video card and CPU cooler (those boards won't overclock any CPU more than a few percent, but unlike the P67, they can use the CPU graphics, though you might have to tweak Ubuntu so it can use them).

Go to newegg.com, create an account, and then use their "wish-list" feature to build a few alternate prospective PC builds and check the trade-offs between optimal cost and optimal performance (though the latter can be somewhat hard to judge).
