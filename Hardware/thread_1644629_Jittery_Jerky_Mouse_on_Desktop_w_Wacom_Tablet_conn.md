### Post by bitterbug on 2010-12-13
I managed to troubleshoot this myself but thought it was obscure enough that someone else might benefit from it being posted.

I logged in to my desktop box and found that the mouse was acting extremely jittery without being touched.

First assumption was cat hair on the mouse tracking sensor.
Cleaned the mouse, no improvement.

Second assumption was stylus resting on or near the Wacom tablet. 
Nope. Stylus was resting under the monitor.

Third assumption was that my trusty six year old mouse was dying. *cry*
Nope! I disconnected it and the jerking mouse pointer persisted.

So I examined the tablet more closely.
My netbook power supply was sitting beside it. I moved the power brick and the jitter went away. The EM field from the transformer was interfering with the Wacom input.

I had no idea it was capable of that. But now we all know.
