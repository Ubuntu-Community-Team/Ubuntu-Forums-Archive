### Post by Baldrick_NZ on 2011-03-14
Last week we bought a Panasonic 32" LCD with PC and HDMI inputs. After plugging my Ubuntu desktop into the PC slot of the TV, I found the best resolution I could get was a measly 640x480 - the TV is full HD (1980x1080i).

The PC is fitted with a nVidia GeForce 6200 video card, which has provision for s-video out and another weird connection I've not seen before (similar to that pictured).

The resolution rate remains the same when I remove the card and use the on-board graphics card.

I've noticed the resolution does change to 1080x768 when it boots up to grub, then reduces to 640x480 while loading up.
Curiously, I can get XP to work at higher resolutions on the same TV. Which is fine, but it's not my chosen OS.

I see that our local computer store has an adaptor that 'converts' the unknown connection to an HDMI connection. Would it help to buy one and then connect it to the TV through an HDMI port? Or would I just be wasting money?

Can anyone suggest something else to help resolve the low-res issue?

Thanks in advance.
Baldrick.

---

### Post by Baldrick_NZ on 2011-03-19
It appears the unknown socket on the video card was a DVI socket. So, I bought a DVI-HDMI adaptor along with an HMDI cable & plugged into the TV. Rebooted the PC, and viola! Prob fixed.
