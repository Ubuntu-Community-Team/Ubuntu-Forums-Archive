### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-07-14
It will help to figure out which wireless card it has.  The wireless compatibility issues are mostly due to make/model of wireless card, not due to the laptop manufacturer.

I don't buy Lenovo (or HP) laptops because they "whitelist" their wireless cards, meaning you can't upgrade them except to an "approved" wireless card stuck in your BIOS (so maybe two or three different approved cards, at most).  I've upgraded plenty of wireless cards in Dell, Acer, Toshiba laptops without issue, so that's what I buy.  Personally, if you are going to get a Lenovo laptop, I'd make sure that it has not only a good Linux-compatible wireless card but that is also supports the newest 802.11ac standards.  You may not care today, but in 2-3 years, assuming you still have the laptop, you'll be glad you can connect to faster routers, and you won't be stuck with a non-upgrade-able wirleess card.  If you buy a Dell with a slow wireless card today, in 2-3 years you can upgrade it to an 802.11ac card.
