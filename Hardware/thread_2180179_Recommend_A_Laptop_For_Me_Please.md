### Post by vuniverse143 on 2013-10-12
I have been looking into dualbooting Ubuntu on ChromeOS as well, and the most outstanding hardware option right now is probably the [HP Chromebook 14]("http://www.engadget.com/2013/09/11/hp-chromebook-14-with-haswell-hands-on-video/") while a personal good-design award goes to [the 11" model]("http://www.theverge.com/2013/10/10/4822576/hp-chromebook-11-review"). I don't know how much power is needed to render video edits at a respectable quality, but Chromebooks don't come any better than that (unless you want the Pixel in which case you might as well go with a Haswell MacBook Pro).

[Crouton]("https://github.com/dnschneid/crouton"), a solution for dualbooting Ubuntu on ChromeOS, was written by a Google Employee, so in all likelihood, they aren't going to break compatibility for a newer model on their side. Which version of Ubuntu can you run is another question, but I suppose not a critical one.

I can't get my hands on any of these laptops for another 2 months (the 14 won't come out until the first week of November anyway), so if you do decide to give it a try, let me know how it goes.

EDIT: The 11" HP Chromebook has a few interesting points, all of which are covered in [this more lengthy review]("http://www.computerworld.com/s/article/9243129/HP_Chromebook_11_review_Affordable_style_for_life_in_the_cloud"):
[LIST]
[*]The design is decent. Sleek, minimalistic, with cool lights taken from the Pixel.
[*]The speakers are hidden underneath the keyboard.
[*]It's charged using a microUSB port that's the same as the one going into smartphones/tablets.
[*]IPS display @ 1366x768
[/LIST]
