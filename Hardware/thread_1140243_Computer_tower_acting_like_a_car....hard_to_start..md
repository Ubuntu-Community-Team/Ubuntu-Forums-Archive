### Post by coffeecat on 2009-04-27
Older motherboards (more than 4 years old) had only twenty pin connectors, so that's an old PSU on a new(ish) motherboard. Even good PSUs decline and die after a few years, so it sounds as though it's time to replace the PSU.

Quite how it works at all with 4 pins unserviced, I don't know. You can get 20 -> 24-pin adaptors, but they're not an optimal solution. But just leaving four sockets unfilled! Interesting. :?

AH HA! Now we get to the good stuff.

Yes, it is PERFECTLY FINE to use a 20 pin PSU on a 24 pin motherboard provided the PSU is powerful enough to run it (Certain rails have to have certain amperages) so it WAS good enough at one point, but now that your PSU has gotten older, its output has declined... So it'll only SOMETIMES (After warming up from "trying" a few times) give enough power to boot.

Solution: Buy a new PSU. :)
