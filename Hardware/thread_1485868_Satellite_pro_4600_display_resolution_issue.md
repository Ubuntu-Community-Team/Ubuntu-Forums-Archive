### Post by ajgreeny on 2010-05-18
the **xserver-xorg-video-trident** driver is probably already installed in your system as part of the default graphics drivers that come with the system.  Have a look in synaptic to see if that's the case, if not install it, then if you still get nowhere try using your own version of the xorg.conf file posted by trundlenut.

You may not need every section of that file but the sections on **device**, **monitor** and **screen** may help, using your own figures, of course, for resolution and refresh rates.  The resolution you know about for your monitor, and the refresh rates you can probably get from your monitor specs.  If you don't find any refresh rates published, delete those two lines completely, as they are probably not needed any more.
