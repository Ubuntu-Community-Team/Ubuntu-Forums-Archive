### Post by forkandles on 2015-05-09
Canonical has joined forces with HP, AMD and Ebuyer to launch a range of laptops in the UK that will run Ubuntu out of the box.

[http://www.theinquirer.net/inquirer/...tops-to-the-uk](http://www.theinquirer.net/inquirer/...tops-to-the-uk)

The entry-level HP ProBook 255 has an AMD A4 processor and 4GB RAM, the ProBook 355 has an AMD A8 and 4GB, and the top of the line HP ProBook 455 (above) has an A10 with 8GB.

All have Ubuntu 15.04 installed and Bluetooth as standard. The screen is a backlit LED 1366x768, so full HD, and the laptops come with an optical drive and a 720p HD webcam.

The speakers are described as 'dual' with HD audio support.

Port-wise, there are two USB 2.0 and a USB 3.0, HDMI, VGA, stereo headphone/line out, RJ45, media card reader and, of course, a power socket which links to the handily included power supply.

Battery life is modest with a three-cell battery that is projected to offer four hours in optimum circumstances.

Prices start at £199.98.

[http://www.ebuyer.com/ubuntu](http://www.ebuyer.com/ubuntu)

**In addition**, there is an even better offer available until 31st May 2015, featuring the HP 255 G3 with a cashback deal of £30, making the net price **£169.98**.

This laptop has **W8.1** installed but it is easily wiped and a suitable Linux distro (Ubuntu or another Linux) installed instead.

Ideal for weaning Windows users from XP, Vista, W7, W8.1 and also for introducing total computer novices to the online world at a reasonable price for a brand new machine.

[http://www.ebuyer.com/669147-hp-255-g3-quad-core-laptop-k7h92es-abu](http://www.ebuyer.com/669147-hp-255-g3-quad-core-laptop-k7h92es-abu)

I have introduced many older people to computing by putting Linux on this particular laptop.
