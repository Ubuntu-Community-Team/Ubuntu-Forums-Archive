### Post by AoSteve on 2009-06-30
Ok laptop 101..  There's VERY few laptops that have removable video cards...   RAM on a 32-bit system shouldn't go over 4gb because of how it's managed with the processor.

Some tips for you.

The slowest thing in your system, no matter how fast and elite, is the hard drive.   The drives only read so fast, and they are a bottle neck.   If you're going with an SSD, get something like an OCZ Vertex, Samsung, OCZ Summit, or Intel SSD.  The rest are pretty much.. well crap after a while and you can find out why from anandtech.com.   They have a REALLY good article on SSD's and how they slow over time.   But I doubt that's your problem either way.

Video cards are the next bottleneck..   The video card in most laptops, like this one here, are onboard and use shared memory that is restricted heavily by the FSB of the system.  In a glance, this machine runs at 533mhz FSB and that's what the "shared" video ram will run at.  Take my GeForce4 for example, the memory channels on it run at 600mhz and for a 7 year old card vs. my 2 year old intel, you do the math.   The onboard, unless built for power, usually sucks and there's no getting away with it.  Face it, most laptops aren't designed to have fast video.

Ram, if 2GB isn't enough, there's a problem.   I ran Vista Ultimate for over 2 years with only 1GB of ram.   And it's horrible with ram.   Virtual memory and disk cacheing becomes your issue here.   2GB should be enough unless you are encoding video, pictures, or doing some virtual boxes or the like.   I have 2gb of ram in this old 1.73ghz T2080 powered system and it's not bad at all!  The intel video card drags it down with compiz however.

Processor..  Processors are no longer a major weak spot in computers.  Unless you have a single core processor with low speed, you have something good enough for ubuntu and/or windows.   My 1.73 dual core runs just fine with Ubuntu Jaunty and with Vista.   Heck, my old AMD Athlon XP 1700+ runs great for being 1.466ghz with 1GB of ram on 9.04!

So what you need to do first, find out about your hard drive.  From the looks of what you posted, you have a decent enough HDD.  An SSD will do a LOT of justice, but for price comparison, it's not worth it in the least unless you "have" to have that bleeding edge.  

You ram should be fine, but you can upgrade it if you feel the need to.   I'm not going to say do it or not, but ram is cheap now and I just got a set of 2x1GB 800mhz 200pin SoDimms for a laptop for $25 on NewEgg.   

See what all you have running, this is the biggest thing.  Running compiz, AWN, and some other stuff at the same time can really start lagging my ol' Intel integrated graphics down on this machine.  So I run a simple gnome panel with a few of the compiz settings on.  The difference between full compiz and AWN and like I have it now is probably 100FPS on compiz and over 300mb of ram.    

I hope this post helps you decide what to do here.   I know what you're feeling as I go through it myself.  But what can I say?  I picked this laptop up for $600 two years ago and it works like a charm on 9.04.   It won't perform like the Alienware that I would like to have, but it does what it needs to do!  :)

That's not a bad system.  But if you are going to upgrade to something better, you want something totally 64-bit so you can use anything more than 4GB of ram.  I use 4GB on my desktop and have NEVER used more than a few mb in my swap partition.   

The biggest thing is going to be the hard drive.   You might get a nice performance boost from an SSD, but you must remember there's a LOT of things associated with SSD's.  They are still in an infancy state where they aren't near as capable as they may be three years from now.

A good system for ubuntu in my opinion would be the following

2.0 Dual core processor or better
4GB or more ram
160gb 7200RPM drive or better.  The drive speed makes a BIG difference.  I went from a 5400rpm 80gb to a 160gb 7200 and my loading times were cut by at least 5 seconds from 30 to 25 or less.
nVidia or ATi built in graphics, they make a big difference.  Look at the [Alienware](www.alienware.com) laptops for a good idea of what to look for in graphics in notebooks.  They usually have the most high end that you can have at the time.
For wireless..  I'd get an expansion card with an antenna..  They make a BIG difference in range compared to internals.  :)
