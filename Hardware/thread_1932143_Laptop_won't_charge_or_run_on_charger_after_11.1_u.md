### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-02-26
Sounds like nothing to do with Ubuntu.  Your laptop is a few years old.  Computers break at random.  Just because it worked two days ago doesn't mean much.  Every computer worked great two days before it didn't.

That said, Dell chargers are notorious for being unreliable.  I've had a couple of Dell laptops and replaced a number of them (long before I switched to Ubuntu).  Fortunately, you can buy generic replacement chargers cheaply on eBay for about $10 shipped.  You probably want a PA-10 (90 watt) or PA-12 (65 watt) compatible charger. 

Still, I don't know if your charger is your problem or not.  That laptop should work without the battery installed if the charger is working.  Did you try simply removing the battery and running it off the charger?

Sadly, it could be something even worse like the motherboard - not a big shock for a laptop that's a few years old (2007?).  If by chance the power jack on the back of the laptop is loose, could be it was damaged at some point(?) and maybe now finally won't make a reliable connection.  Loose/bad power jacks that were damaged can be replaced.  Motherboards generally can't be fixed thought power issues like yours (if the motherboard is the problem) may have power boards that can be replaced.  Depends on the exact model.  I have worked on a few 1501s but have never taken one apart.  Look on Dell's website for troubleshooting tips and even a service manual should you desire to take it apart.
