Can't offer much of an advice since you pretty much know the specifics you want in your new laptop. On the other side, you are on the right track with Dell if a backlit keyboard is a must -- one of the best I've experienced is on Dell Precision M2400 and the LED screen is also immaculate. However, I don't know how Linux would work. 

Re. ASUS laptop -- great laptops. I use [ASUS G1]("http://www.notebookreview.com/default.asp?newsID=3403") (gaming series) with Ubuntu 9.10 -- everything that matters to me works right out of the box (NVIDIA GeForce Go 7700 w/512MB VRAM, wireless, wired, even the freaking media buttons work +email +browser +sleep buttons work). With just a bit of downloading also retail DVD movie playback and other multimedia works too. I haven't tested the built-in cam or some peripherals that I never use regardless of the OS installed. I've noticed that hardware clock/messages display (above the keyboard) is not working and the OLED lights on the screen sides are not working in Ubuntu which is understandable since they need ASUS-specific Windows utilities software. Overall, Ubuntu is rock solid on this laptop and the excellent 15.4" 1680x1050 screen makes it look awesome. I also had a very good experience with ASUS A8Js running Novell SuSE Linux. Anyways, just to put a few good words about ASUS laptops :) . Did you try looking at the [MSI laptop offering]("http://www.msimobile.com/level2_productlist.aspx?id=6")? (they are also the original hardware manufacturer as ASUS, but the overall quality may be just a notch down from ASUS) -- regardless, except for some keyboard flex and a bit dimmer screen in early models I am very impressed with their GX620 (I haven't tried any other MSI notebook models)

Anyways, good luck and hope you find the right notebook for you.
