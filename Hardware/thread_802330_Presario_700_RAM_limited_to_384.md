### Post by prshah on 2008-05-21
> **tstack77 said:**
> There is no official updated BIOS for the 700, I asked the rep while we were chatting :(
Is there another place beyond compaq's site to find an upgrade? Are the motherboards generic?

They told me that too; but I checked my BIOS and found out that a later BIOS was available.. and was desperate enough to take the risk.

I got it from [www.driverguide.com;](www.driverguide.com;) you have to register, membership is free. At the time when I got it, all access was free; now looks like you have to pay for some "premium" files. A workaround: If you find a suitable looking BIOS, google the file name.

There is no "generic" BIOS; that can kill your laptop. You need to find the specific Compaq BIOS for your laptop; the BIOS identifier codes can help.

It may turn out that there is no such available BIOS; in which case I recommend Xubuntu.

Good luck.

### Post by kznight on 2009-03-24
I had a 512MB PC133 SODimm in my Presario 700US for years with no problem. I also had the latest BIOS update installed as well. I wish there was a 1GB chip produced, as I would try that out as well. The 700 is a great notebook for its age. Nice design, great sound, and a good performer.

I don't have it with me, but I think mine was etiher a 704US or 705US. It has an AMD Duron chip at 1Ghz in it.

Also worth noting, many of these laptops had overheating problems. I solved mine by ENABLING power management in Windows. I believe its OFF by default, which makes the CPU run full speed at all times and causes the overheat. This chip has PowerNow technology and driver support is built into Windows XP, but you need to install a driver for older Windows versions.
