### Post by mastablasta on 2019-07-29
> **bjohas said:**
> Do you not think a compatibility list would be helpful?

it would be super helpful. even better if they all had linux compatible mark or something.

problem is there are thousands of models and iterations each year, there are also a large number of Linux distributions, each with different kernel (and thus different drivers). who would maintain such a list and how?

for example you have the old HP DM1. there was model with E-350 and E450 chip. then besides that there was different version for "Australasia", Asia, US, EU and probably also for latin america and africa. and it's not just the plug that was changing. you also had different ram and wi-fi chip being used. so for that model alone you would need test at least 16 machines. say the keyboards were different - more testing needed and that's just for one distro. now multiply that with a few hundred and list quickly grows. and this is just one old model. each brand has a bunch of models. you maybe see only 10, 15 at your local shop or in your area. but worldwide, there reeally are a lot of them.

Some manufacturers worked with Ubuntu and we got this list: [https://certification.ubuntu.com/](https://certification.ubuntu.com/)

but many times new PC, particularly, laptops are not found here. there are also many others that are not on the list but will still work well or need just a minor work around.
