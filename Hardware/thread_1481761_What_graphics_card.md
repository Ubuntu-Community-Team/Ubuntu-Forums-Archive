### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-14
Honestly? I'm not a fan of crossfire/sli. Its got its uses, but as a start point its not the best way to go IMO. Easier to get a single card, and then if/when you feel the need for more frames per second, get a 2nd card the same as what you have now...with crossfire, you need to scrap both cards and then get a whole new videocard setup. 

But to be short, from what I know 

5770 x 2 in crossfire- 
Good points-
shorter cards
slightly more frames for your dollar. 

Bad points-
more power draw.
more heat. 
if the game doesnt suport crossfire, the 5850 will be much faster. 

I'd get a crossfire capable motherboard (790X, 790FX, 890FX..I dont think that the 890X chipsets are out yet) and a 5850 and a 890FX myself ;)

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-14
As far as I know, all the 8XX chipset AM3 motherboards support the new X6 Phenom II. The older 7XX chipsets wont all support X6 CPUs. I *think* that prety much all the AM3 motehrboards support X6, doesnt really matter if they are 7XX or 8XX..but I'm not sure on that. 

Considering what you are going to be spending on the CPU and video card, I'd get a 890FX. (its worth it if only for the SATA3, 6Gbit/sec support from the newer southbridges) 

BTW, if you are really unsure about the CPU support on a specific motherboard, most of the manufacturers have 'CPU support' lists on the page for that model motherboard. ;) Not something you want to check for every motherboard, but its not that bad an idea to check when you make a short list of what boards you might want.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-14
My (basic) reasons- 

790FX- SATA2 (3Gbit/sec) 890FX- SATA3 (6Gbit/sec). From the upgraded southbrige (SB850, 790FX has SB750) 
A-link express 3.0 with 890FX. 
Gigabit ethernet on the southbridge. 
The SB850 has quite a new featues that the SB750 hasnt got. 
Should have a better upgrade path as well. 

There are some other reasons- the new gigabyte 890FX motherboards have USB 3.0 (a mjaor jump over USB 2.0). I'm not sure if the NEC USB 3.0 controller works with linux, but I think it should. 

Considering the cost of the MSI vs a gigabyte 890FX, I'd be getting a gigabyte. I admit my bias- I have an MSI motherboard now and its not been the best board I've used, not even close.

*edit- the MSI 790FX-GD70 use a %&*#$&#% jbmicron expansion SATA RAID controller. Those things are junk :|
