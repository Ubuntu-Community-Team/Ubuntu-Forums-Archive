### Post by shatterblast on 2009-07-10
Apparently, there exists a slight legal issue with "libdvdcss2" if a trading ban exists between nations or something to that effect.  You would probably know if you can't buy DVDs or even CDs for that matter.

Usage of the "w32codecs" or "w64codecs" implies that you have a Windows XP or higher license I think.  It's one of those "free" versus "non-free" things.  (Note:  "Proprietary" doesn't always mean "non-free," but I also think LGPL can dip into the area of "proprietary.")

The desired software should be "libdvdcss2".  I believe it has the tools to adjust DVD regional settings.  Also, it has a very distant relation to "reverse-engineering."  That shouldn't be an issue over all.
