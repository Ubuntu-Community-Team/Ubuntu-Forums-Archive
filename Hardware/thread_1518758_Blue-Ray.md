### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-06-29
It's also worth pointing out that if you buy a laptop now, and a year from now discover that blue ray really is the future instead of the last gasp of a dieing method of data storage, you can always just replace the drive.

There are already slimline (read that as "laptop compatible") after market blue-ray drives available.  You can expect to pay $250-$350 for one today from a major retailer, but you can shop around places like "the big auction site" and get them for half of that or less.

When and if they do become "standard" they'll probably be more like $50-$100 from big retailers and proportionally less on auction sites.  Granted, you'll void your warranty by installing one, but a year from now your warranty will be gone anyway.
