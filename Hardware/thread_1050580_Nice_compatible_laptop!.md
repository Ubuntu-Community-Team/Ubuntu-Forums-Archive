### Post by ArgentSilver on 2009-01-25
I just bought a new laptop for my wife; an Acer 4730z (specifically the model was AS4730-4516. Pentium dual-core 2.16GHz, 2Gb ram, 250Gb hard drive, 14.1" 1280x800, Pioneer DVD-DL/CD-RW). After hosing off the Vista that it came with, and installing Intrepid, I was extremely pleased to find that everything important worked out of the box! Wi-fi turned out to be Atheros AR5B91 chipset, and immediately picked up my access point; just needed the WPA password to connect right up. The wi-fi toggle button worked, though the light under it did not. Sound worked. The Intel Graphics accelerator was immediately active, so compiz was turned on. SD card reader worked. Only thing I haven't checked is the webcam (probably won't get used).

Best of all, for those of us in the USA, Best Buy is selling these for $449. I actually got mine for $399 on sale last week. For the price, this is a super deal for a small lightweight Linux-compatible laptop. I was a little jealous, since it wasn't for me!
