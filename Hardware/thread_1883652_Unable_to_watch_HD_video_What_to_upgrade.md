### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-11-19
Probably the whole computer, I'm afraid.

If you can only accept an AGP graphics card you have a fairly old machine and I therefore suspect you will be unlikely to get it to play HD video with any great success.  To do so I think you would need a new mobo, faster cpu, and a much better socket system for your graphics card, in other words, it will not be worth upgrading, or not specifically for that purpose alone, and you would do better to get a newer, better, faster machine.

### Post by papibe on 2011-11-19
There are a few PCI cards that support video hardware acceleration. For example the GeForce 8400 GS. Check this Mythtv [guide]("http://www.mythtv.org/wiki/VDPAU"). Unfortunately, as you may read, it is partial acceleration. You will end up with lots of movies that you won't be able to play. 

You can even find some 9400 or 9500 PCI versions too. However, for what I have read, PCI don't have enough bandwidth to make the experience worth it.

IMHO, sadly, I would have to say that just a new graphic card, won't be upgrade enough to play HD video.

If you are interesting in buying, or building a new system I would be happy to give you some recommendations.
