### Post by Fafler on 2010-03-26
Something based on the BeagleBoard [http://beagleboard.org]("http://beagleboard.org/") would be great for the purpose. It's a small single board computer based on a 600 mhz ARM core. It is able to run Ubuntu, Android and several other Linux distributions. A ready-to-use handheld computer based on BeagleBoard is the Pandora [http://www.open-pandora.org/](http://www.open-pandora.org/) although it's not shipping yet.

Asus has also made a touch version of the Eee line, but i can't remember what it's called. And when apple releases iPad, were going to get a lot of small ARM and Atom based touch devices to play around with. Just look at the amount of iPod and iPhone look-alikes.

A small ARM device would use much less energy than a Atom device and still be more than fast enough for your project.

One thing you should note is that depending on design, touch screens work with either finger touch or a small pen. You would want one you can use with your fingers.
