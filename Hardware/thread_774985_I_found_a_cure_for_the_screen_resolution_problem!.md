### Post by fredbird67 on 2008-04-29
When starting up the live CD of Hardy Heron, I've found that, after selecting your language, if you press F4 and select "Safe Graphics Mode", you get bigger screen resolutions available.  In my case, I originally didn't do this and couldn't select a screen resolution higher than 800x600.  However, I just tried this just now, and it gave me a 1280x1024 screen resolution, plus my preferred 1024x768 resolution is available, too.

However, I can't adjust the monitor refresh rate, as it's stuck on 85 (it's my understanding you should use 60 at a 1024x768 resolution), but that doesn't seem to be causing any problems, but this is a definite improvement nonetheless.

Again, if you've found yourself unable to select a screen resolution greater than 800x600 on Gutsy, after selecting your language, try pressing F4 and selecting "Safe Graphics Mode", and then starting Ubuntu/Edubuntu/Kubuntu/Xubuntu/whatever.

I've tested this so far on Ubuntu and Xubuntu, and it works there, so I would expect it also probably works with any other Ubuntu variants.
