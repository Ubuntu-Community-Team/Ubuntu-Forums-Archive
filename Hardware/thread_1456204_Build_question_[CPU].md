### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2010-04-16
If I was doing a lot of video encoding, I would get a quad core since video encoding scales well across 4 cores (and Athlon II X4's are very reasonably priced). If you have a mobo with an AMD SB710/SB750 southbridge, and a decent BIOS, there's also the possibility of getting a cheap, high-speed quad-core for the price of a dual-core through the magic of unlocking disabled cores (though there's no guarantee it will work): [http://www.anandtech.com/show/2927](http://www.anandtech.com/show/2927)
