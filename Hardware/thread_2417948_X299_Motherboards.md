### Post by rbmorse on 2019-04-29
What on Earth?

First I've heard that Linux isn't compatible with the X299 chipset motherboards.  I know that driver availability can be an issue immediately after a new generation CPU/support chipset are released (this is an old and sad situation that goes back to the beginnings of Linux and yes, I'm looking at AMD/ATI as a prime offender), but these are usually resolved fairly quickly.  In terms of general compatibility there should be no problem at all. 

Asrock and Linux are usually best buds.  I've been buying ASUS boards the last couple of years, but if I needed to build quick and there was a feature compliant Asrock board available on sale, I would not hesitate to use it.

Michael Larabel at phoronix.com tested the i9-7900X on a  MSI X299 SLI Plus motherboard and it ran his full suite without issues.
