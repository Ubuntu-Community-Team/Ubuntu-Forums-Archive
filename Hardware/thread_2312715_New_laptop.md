### Post by ajgreeny on 2016-02-06
It's not easy to be sure as manufacturers have a nasty habit of changing the wifi adapters and using whatever they can get that is best value to them (cheapest) so the chipset may change from week to week.  If you can find a machine that is built to your spec you might be able to get a wifi card that is known to work with Linux.

However, having said that it is getting easier and easier to get wifi to work and even some of the cards that were a problem in the past, eg Broadcom, are now supported, and others have drivers available from the makers' websites direct.

### Post by weatherman2 on 2016-02-06
One way to fix an issue with a poorly-supported wireless card is to replace the wireless card with one that is well supported.  I have upgraded many laptop wireless cards.  In most cases it is easy - a few screws, two or three wires to unclip, slide the wireless card out of its slot and slide the new one  in, then to clip on to the new wireless card.  Occasionally I find a laptop where the wireless card is buried under the keyboard or something but usually it is under a panel on the bottom of the laptop.  There are plenty of YouTube videos demonstrating how to replace a wireless card on a laptop if you want to see how to do it.  Look up your exact make/model to confirm if you want to check before buying one.

I never buy HP or Lenovo/IBM laptops because they "whitelist" their wireless cards, meaning only a very few cards will be supported, otherwise the laptop will not even boot until you remove the "unapproved" wireless cards.  Dell, Toshiba, Acer, etc. do not seem to whitelist their cards.  I have upgraded many Dell laptops successfully. I prefer Intel cards - most of the Intel cards I have upgraded to have worked great.  A good, cheap card I have had great luck with is the Intel 6235 which is dual band + bluetooth.  It is only 802.11n though (not the newer 802.11ac).  I have added an Intel 7260 802.11ac card into my old Acer laptop and it works, but it was not designed to work well in this laptop so it has had issues.  I have put the 6235 into numerous laptops without issue.
