### Post by jak on 2008-05-26
I used to have an Eee PC 4g, and I swapped that over to Ubuntu easily enough. Now I made no changes to the hardware itself (i.e defaul 512MB ram), and Ubuntu still ran very well! There were some limits to multitasking, but a cheap and cheerful ram upgrade to 2GB sorted all that out.
If there was any problems it would have to be the 7" screen size, but thats only if I *have* to say something bad about it as it was hardly an issue for what I was using it for, simple internet browsing, music and movies on the go. I did quite a bit of python coding on it, so you should be fine when it comes to that.

The keyboard is about three quarters of the size of a regular one, which took a couple of days to get used to but it was easy enough. The right hand shift and up arrow got a bit confused at times :-)

So yeah, basically, if you go for the Eee, stick Ubuntu on it (the eeeuser.com forums and wiki are great) follow the few instructions for wireless and overclocking utility and dont sacrifice the Gnome desktop! It runs compiz with ease!

You will probably notice I've written "had", I sold it after getting a HP TX2050ea which was better for my personal wants and needs, and due to the HP laptop being light at about 2kg I had little need for the Eee.
