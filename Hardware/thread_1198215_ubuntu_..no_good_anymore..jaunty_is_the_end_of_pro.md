### Post by randcoop on 2009-06-27
I disagree with the suggestion that the non-LTS releases shouldn't be expected to perform.  The OP is right to think that any release of Ubuntu ought to be able to use a standard wireless card.  

I didn't install Jaunty when it came out (I'm still on Intrepid), but I had one user who did (against my advice).  In that case, the only way I could get the wireless to work was by uninstalling network manager and moving to WICD.  But that shouldn't be necessary.  

There have been other major bugs in Jaunty, including the sound problems mentioned by the OP (many Skype users can't use Jaunty, which seems to also have major problems with microphones).  

Perhaps my biggest problem, though, with these kinds of problems is the responses that are sure to follow here: you don't need sound; buy a different wireless card; don't complain about free software; if you don't like it then good riddance...etc. etc. etc.  In other words, instead of addressing the failures of the system, the effort will be made to blame the victims (the end users).
