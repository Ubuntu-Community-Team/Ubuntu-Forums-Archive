### Post by sajro on 2008-06-06
We're going to be getting a second internal hard drive to back our stuff up to, since our system doesn't have a slave drive and externals are more expensive. Our [current] main HDD is ~80GB and we'd probably be getting around ~100+GB for the second one (in my area, it's hard to find a new one smaller than that).

My question is: should I use the 80GB as the main or the backup? We don't need to backup the whole OS, just the documents, media, configs and such. So 80GB should be exponentially more than we NEED. The 100+GB would be advantageous for stuffing more...umm, expendable media (brought through the nice folks in Sweden) to use. Especially music (now that only partially comes from Scandinavia; I have a lot of physicals), to back up what's on my player (inc. 3 full Pimsleur courses). All in all, I think the 100+ would be nice for master and use, whereas the 80 could be slave and backup. Any thoughts?

I've fallen in love with Arch as of recent and I'm thinking it'd be perfect; it is **much less** unstable than it wants you to think. Properly configured, it's ROCK-solid. Also, I'm a stickler for speed and low resource consumption, so that'd be great. Should I stick with *buntu or jump to Arch (I know it's a bit biased on an Ubuntu forum, but nevertheless...)?

### Post by thideras on 2008-06-06
It really is up to you.  I would trust the newer drive more than the current one you are using.  Either way, you can back up your "mission critical" data to the other drive, lessening the chance of data loss.  If you are re-installing, I'd suggest using the newer drive.

I can't comment on other any other distro since I don't have any experience with them.
