### Post by falconindy on 2010-01-12
(My) rule #1 of PC building: Don't skimp on your PSU. You'll be sorry if you find a short in the wiring down the road which fries every other component in the box. A higher quality PSU will also consume less power, and it's likely to be more efficient. I notice you have a "green" hard drive listed so this might be of interest to you. I've been very happy with Corsair units -- particularly the modular ones.

Also, buying another stick of RAM "later" for dual channel is somewhat of a risky proposition. Dual channel pairs of RAM are matched, to ensure that they will run smoothly in a dual channel setup. I've, on occasion, found 2 "identical" sticks of RAM (same size, speed, JEDEC) unable to POST in dual channel, but run flawlessly as a single channel.

Of the components you've listed, I wouldn't expect any compatibility issues.

### Post by viper250 on 2010-01-12
youve got good equipment but I would make sure youve got good cpu cooling. also if you can find them get the round ide cables (less profile to block cooling air)and make it a habit of cleaning out the dust monthly. as a pc specialist I can tell you that dust buildup causes excess heat retention and early failure of components.
as your first build make sure that your connections are snug and take your time to check your jumper settings on your drives are correct.
check out your memory carefully because some memory sticks are designed to be installed in pairs. 
as with any installs ste the bios to autodetect the drives and read the manual that came with the drive. 
any new drive will need to be partitioned but you are installing ubuntu so it will be easy for you.

### Post by audiomick on 2010-01-16
Hi Rick.
I don't know how much trouble it might give you if you buy a second RAM stick.
The thing is, when things are matched, it usually means that some effort has been made to make sure that they are really the same. Production tolerances mean that "identical" things aren't really, when it comes down to the nitty gritty.

for instance, with high quality microphones, matched means that someone has measured a whole lot and chosen two that have really the same frequency response, with elecronic components (discrete components like resistors and such) it means that individual components have been chosen from a production run that have identical values and not just within the production tolerances.
