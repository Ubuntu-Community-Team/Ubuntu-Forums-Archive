### Post by varunendra on 2011-09-18
_**Transcend: Love it!**_
Never used a SanDisk. I like Transcend, have used many versions and types of it and it never gave me troubles. Speed is as good as USB2 can provide, can partition it, use any of those partitions to install and boot Ubuntu (and derivatives)/Slax/HBCD.... whatever. In short, I feel that anything that can possibly be done with a flash drive - is done easily with transcends.

_**Kingston: Good with speed, Uncertain with booting**_
I've experienced some failures with kingstons (where transcend worked). From the point of view of a storage device, they are as good as transcend. But you can never be sure if it is going to let you boot from it. I also have an 8 GB kingston, which will boot fine as long as it has a single, windows formatted FAT16/FAT32 partition. But as soon as I use gparted on it, or even reformat using winxp (1st partition only, as xp can't see subsequent partitions) after partitioning it, it fails to boot no matter which partition I install the live OS upon.

**_PNY_:**
Umm.. I just have experience with one - a 1GB tiny one (which resembles a thick memory card). I currently have Slax on it, and all I can say is that it is reliable for me. It is a bit slow when transferring files, but never gives me problems with booting whatever I install on it. Gets detected immediately - that's something I love about it. Whenever a friend's laptop fails to detect my other flash drives, it gets immediately detected and boots up!

**_Verbatim_:**
I doubt if many of you have heard about it (I didn't until I got one). I've currently two of them, both 4GB, but of different generation and model. Both of them are moderate with speed, slightly problematic with booting. That is, boot fine if the OS is installed on the first partition, but will fail if installed on another one. My overall feeling about it: cheap in price, cheap in quality.

**_Umax_:**
I used a few almost 2-3 years ago, then switched to transcends due to  much superior speeds. So can't say how's their current performance, but  when I used them, all 3 (of two different models) I used failed to boot (on  multiple PCs), on which transcend and kingston booted fine.

I have used some more of them, but don't remember as I didn't care much about them at those times. Transcend was the first that impressed me with its speed (kingston's speeds were same as others then).
[I]

**_PS_:**
This is my personal opinion based on experience with not more than 20-30 flash drives (of which, more than a dozen are transcends). So please don't consider it as a professional or even a general opinion about these brands. Other mambers' experience may radically differ.[/I]
