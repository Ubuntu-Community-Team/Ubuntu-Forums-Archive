### Post by jespdj on 2008-05-17
Before you do that, lookup the manual and specifications of your motherboard to make sure that it can handle the Core 2 Duo.

Just because the socket for the P4 and the Core 2 Duo is the same, does not mean it is going to work. The Core 2 Duo needs a newer version of the power circuitry on the motherboard than the P4. So check the documentation carefully.

If your motherboard supports the Core 2 Duo, you might also need to download the newest version of the BIOS and flash your BIOS.

If it all works, then you will not need to configure anything in Ubuntu, it will recognise the new processor automatically.

### Post by nicedude on 2008-05-17
Listen to first poster about checking that new CPU works with your Motherboard & BIOS version. Sometimes a BIOS update will then support newer proccessors. But in general socket 775 is the easiest to work with since it is pinless and its the one I don't mind pulling at all really. You might get your self a 8$ thing of silver thermal paste for best heat transfer between CPU and heatsink. 

But in general read up on this and verify everything before you start doing it.
