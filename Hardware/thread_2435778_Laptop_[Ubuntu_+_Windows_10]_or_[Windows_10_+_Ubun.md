### Post by therealdps on 2020-01-25
I can vouch for the HP Elitebook laptops being an AWESOME choice to dual boot Windows 10 and Linux based operating systems. I have two actually (840's). Look into those, they're really sweet. Most (at least mine) have a Serial ATA port for an HDD or SSD, and an extra PCIe M.2 slot for an NVMe based m.2 SSD (2240 size I believe) or just a regular M.2 SSD can fit also. 
  They also usually come with another PCIe (M.2 form factor) slot for a WWAN card, so you can stay connected while you're out, if you get a SIM card from a company with a data plan!

*EDIT* Plus, you could probably even get one for a decent price, and a nice CPU.

### Post by CatKiller on 2020-01-25
I can only speak about Dell machines, since that's the only laptop I've owned.

All the stuff that they do for their Linux machines is upstreamed: you won't be missing out on some Dell secret sauce by installing Ubuntu yourself. You could call up the specs of a model that ships with Ubuntu and make sure that your Windows machine is configured the same so that you don't end up with iffy WiFi or whatever. Installing Ubuntu yourself is *way* easier than installing Windows yourself.

Their XPS 13s (which is what I have, and which come with Linux pre-installed) are generally well-regarded: you wouldn't be slumming it with a Linux machine.
