### Post by Pablo_Reyes on 2014-12-21
Hi! Miguel

I just wanted to tell you, I found out that by disabling the secureboot option inside the bios setup, do not forget to type the special code that the machine will ask you to type and press enter after that reboot once and wait at least 1 minute and reboot again, then shutdown the laptop, wait another 1 minute, while is not connected to the ac adapter, remove the battery and push the power button a few times slowly, then replace the battery and turn on the laptop, let it go to the login screen and wait a couple of minutes to stabilize the system, then login then again wait a couple of minutes, then shutdown again and wait a few minutes before trying to work on it.  This worked flawlessly in mine, to get the mouse to work normal, I still see the error "ignoring:BGRT" at boot up though, but the mouse/touchpad is working at least, good luck in yours.
