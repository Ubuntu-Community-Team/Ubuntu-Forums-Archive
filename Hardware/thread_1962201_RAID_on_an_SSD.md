### Post by jerome1232 on 2012-04-20
You need mulitple drives to use a RAID (Redundant Array of Inexpensive Disks) At the very least for RAID0 (which isn't *really* raid) requires 2 disks to stripe across, which gives you a speed increase since your reading/writing to both disks simultaneously. 

You can read more about the different raid setups and the minimum disks needed here [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_RAID_levels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_RAID_levels)

The type of disk (ssd vs traditional) is largely irrelevant.
