### Post by Vaedrah on 2009-04-13
From the link the model from Acer doesn't seem to get very good press - especially in "support".

I also note 4.0 GB of RAM - I have 4.0 GB installed in this Acer Aspire 5920 and it reports 3.0 GB(Bios reports 4.096GB). This may be resulting from 32 bit address space but some people argue that 3.5 GB will "appear" but 3.0 GB is probably what Ubuntu will report on the new machine despite having 4 GB physically installed (unless Ubuntu reports wrong)

I have tried Ubuntu on an older Acer notebook Aspire 3003. The Wireless broadcom modem needs a wired Internet connection to download files before it can work but newer models seem to work OK from install. Also this older model works well with 8.04 but the screen is limited on 8.10 and also on 9.04 beta. Again, newer models will probably be OK but I appreciate the idea of trying to return a notebook to the retailer with a different OS installed isn't attractive!

They will also wiggle out of any service obligations if they can!

Also, if the new notebook comes with Vista (as it most likely will) then it may not have recovery DVDs. You may need to make these. Even so, they may not work as I found out on this Acer!

I suggest you try VirtualBox or VMWare sometime - then you can test run Ubuntu on top of the host OS without damaging anything. VB is free and there are some excellent threads in these forums.

I am looking to upgrade in future but notebooks quickly go out of date (I overheard a retailer salesperson trying to sell a 4 year maintenance contract to a customer the other day!) so I suggest you may want to look for dual core or higher, or 64 bit with proviso's or Windows 7 instead of Vista. What seems "adequate" todays will quickly look feature shy in a year or two despite what people may claim. In any case, good luck AMC25114 :popcorn:
