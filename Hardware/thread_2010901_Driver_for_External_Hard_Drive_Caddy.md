### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-06-26
It should not need a driver.  I've used five or six different enclosures and adapters in Linux and all has always worked in every version of Linux I've ever tried.  Even the cheapo SATA laptop drive enclosures I've bought from Meritline work.

If this is a self-powered enclosure (no power cord, just USB), it's possible that a single USB port doesn't have enough power to power the drive.  Does the cable have two USB plugs on one end?  If so, try plugging them both into your computer at the same time and one end into the enclosure.  This can allow it to get 2X the power and enough power to power the drive.  I have a couple of desktop computers that have this issue particularly with the front panel USB connectors.
