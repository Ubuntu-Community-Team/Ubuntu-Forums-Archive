### Post by Enigma12 on 2014-11-20
> Can anyone recommend a compact, easy to install, inexpensive, reliable scanner to go with a Lubuntu 14.04-equipped X40 Thinkpad?

I don't know if you'd consider it compact, since it can scan a full 8 1/2" X 11" page. and it is a flatbed scanner, but I am using an Epson Perfection 1200U scanner on my Lubuntu 14.04 desktop computer and getting excellent results. I downloaded the driver(s) from the Epson website and added Image Scan! for Linux for tweaking scans. Since it is a not one of the newest scanners, I was able to get it quite inexpensively off eBay. As for reliability, so far it has always done what I wanted. Works for me.

Update: I was at an office this afternoon and saw a scanner that should fit your compact requirement. The person using it said it is a Canon 2050-C. It is mostly vertical and has a fairly small footprint. I am also aware of very small scanners that you move along a page manually. No one I know has one, so I can't give even a second hand eval of them. I do recall that you would need to stitch images wider than they scan. And as irv said, older scanners should be very cheap.
