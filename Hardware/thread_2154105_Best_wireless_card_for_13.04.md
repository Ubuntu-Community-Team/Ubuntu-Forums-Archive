### Post by kurt18947 on 2013-06-20
I've tried a few and if you're happy with N150 speed, TrendNet's TEW-649UB or other RealTek 8192SU based adapter seems to work very well.  Plug 'n' play, good signal strength and range, good throughput speed.  Manufacturers have a habit that irritates me.  They'll change chipsets on the same model number, just change the version number or letter.  There's no way to know which version you're getting until you open the package.  

Dlink did this with their DWA-131.  The orignal used the RTL8192SU chipset and was a twin to the TrendNet adapter.  Worked great.  They switched to another RealTek chip, called it ver. B that was NOT plug 'n' play.  It'd be nice to be able to tell the difference prior to purchase but I don't know of a way.
