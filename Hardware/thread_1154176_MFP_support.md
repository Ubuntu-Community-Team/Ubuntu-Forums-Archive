### Post by kunjalo on 2009-05-09
Hello to the community,

This is not a request for help, but an obsevation of an area in which I find Ubuntu (and all distros) lacking.  

I run an IT services company, which serves mainly SMEs.  We make extensive use of OSS, particularly on the back end, with Ubuntu server, Zimbra and Alfresco DMS.  These solutions are extremely popular with SMEs struggling in the current commercial climate.

However, whenever we look at replacing their desktops with nice reliable Ubuntu, we always hit the same snag - MFPs, faxes and workgroup scanners.  In almost every case our customers have invested in a fair bit of this sort of kit, and more often than not they don't work or 'sort of work' under linux.  To my customers, they idea of replacing all this hardware with stuff that happens to be supported by Linux prevents them from moving to Ubuntu.  

In all other areas, I find Ubuntu's hardware support so good that we rarely have to worry about it, but in this one critical area it suffers badly, and that's a real barrier in my arena.  I wonder, is there a wide recognition of this problem and a movement to address it?

Just my two cents!
