### Post by roop_s on 2010-12-17
> **Hamburglar03 said:**
> Desktop, I wanted to repartition the drive; so, i used gparted and then installed ubuntu (from a live usb) from the desktop.

i have a tm2-2150ca (canadian version). i'm running 10.10 on it because people using 10.04 reported problems with the touch drivers.

10.10 had no issues for me, the screen is nice and bright, dims when idle, etc. i didn't make changes in the bios.

to install 10.10 i put the hard disk in another computer (with a cdrom) and installed it from the 10.10 cd. put the hard disk back into the tm2 and it booted up. i had to connect to wired ethernet for the broadcom nic drivers to install (automatic 3rd party driver notification). after this was done, i didn't have to do anything special, i just started using it and it works great. i wish HP made a larger version of the tm2 - the screen it too small and the keyboard is cramped. optical drive would be sweet but for $800 brand new with win7, it's hard to complain.
