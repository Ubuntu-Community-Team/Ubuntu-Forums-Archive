### Post by FrankVdb on 2010-10-23
I want to buy a powerful laptop.

Instead of buying one and having to solve all kinds of installation problems afterwards, I would prefer to know what laptops are Linux-compatible and which ones are not. There isn't one single website where I can find find that type of information. How come? This is 2010. You guys will probably tell me, "make one yourself", "this is an open-source community", "we don't get paid to do that", etc. Obviously, you're right. But that doesn't help me...

The problem is simple. Laptop manufacturers don't give a sh*t about Linux. I bought a Dell XPS 1330 laptop three years ago and apart from the hardware problems I had (you really don't want to know...), their support just sucks. I won't tell you about the Dell customer service. If guns were legal here in Belgium, I probably would have killed a couple of Dell people over the last years. Dell stopped supporting Ubuntu a long time ago, so why bother buying their stuff anyway? 

The ONLY way to get manufacturers to change their minds is turnover. So why don't Linux users express their preferences for brands? The reason is probably that there is simply no such thing as a Linux-friendly computer manufacturer. 

I'm an interpreter by profession and need a small-sized laptop to take with me in the interpreting booth. Thirteen inch screen, long battery life, lots of power to do a *lot* of things, including editing music, virtualising Windows for a couple of things, etc.

I am willing to spend 3,000 euros on a powerful laptop. The only condition is, it will have to able to run Linux or I won't buy it.

So I discovered that Sony has this powerful Z series. Linux compatible? Of course not. So I probably won't buy it.

What's the alternative? I don't know. Is there any? The Linux community needs some examples of fast-booting, powerful laptops that seamlessly work with Linux. 

They don't seem to exist. Or do they?
