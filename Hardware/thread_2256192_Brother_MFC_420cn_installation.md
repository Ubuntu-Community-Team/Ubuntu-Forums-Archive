sudo bash linux-brprinter-installer-2.0.0-1 MFC-420CN

and that should do all the work for you ..................

### Post by bilkay on 2014-12-14
First of all, you need to know the correct name for your printer. Look in /var/spool/lpd - it should be there. Once you find it, lpq -P{name} should show show the jobs in the queue. You can then delete jobs by their number.

When you add the printer, when you get to the list of all Brother drivers, scroll through the entire list. Brother seems to give them strange names, so the one you download from their website may not be anywhere near where you'd expect it to be.
