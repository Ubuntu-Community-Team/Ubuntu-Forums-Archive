### Post by hcollier on 2010-09-17
I like Ubuntu. I really do. But it is a bit of a nightmare when it comes to installing a printer. I have an HP LaserJet 1020 (network). It installed easily. I have an HP LBP 5000. It won't work with Ubuntu. I have an Epson PhotoStylus R360. It installed easily. I have just bought a Kodak 5250 for use at home. It won't work with Ubuntu. I have learned that, with Ubuntu, things either work easily first time, or you give up before spending useless hours trying to make things work.

But the printers are a pain, since I like using Ubuntu at home where I only have one printer. Now, when I wish to print at home, I have to re-boot in Windows (where the Kodak machine installed itself painlessly). A big minus for Ubuntu, these printer driver lotteries.
