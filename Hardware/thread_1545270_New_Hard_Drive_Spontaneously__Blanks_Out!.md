### Post by Eric Boring on 2010-08-03
I hope someone here can provide a little insight to a problem I'm having. I don't think this is directly related to Ubuntu, but I trust the input from this community, so I figured I'd post it here.

I have an older Gateway laptop (MX6920). I've recently been trying to upgrade to a bigger hard drive--a western digital 500 gigabyte.

Despite the inherent superiority of Ubuntu, my wife is keen on keeping Windows around. So I've install Windows 7 alongside Ubuntu 10.04. Installation works fine (especially on the ubuntu partition!)

Anyways, to the crux: In either OS, after the computer has been on for 1/2 an hour or so, it just turns off. No BSOD, no warnings, no shutdown. Just... blank. (Well, Ubuntu flashes a string of kill messages, but Windows just turns off--I haven't been able to capture any of those kill messages yet).

The computer has always run hot. And I noticed that after it craps out, it won't turn on again for awhile. Ubuntu also tells me that one of the processors is pretty much always running at 100%, even when I'm just looking at a blank desktop.

So, it seems to me that the heat is making the hard drive shut down in self defense. This doesn't happen at all with the old hard drive (120 gig -- not sure of the brand just now.)

I've updated the bios to the latest version. That has helped some. It seems to crap out later, but it still craps out.

Any ideas as to where I should look next?

Thanks so much for your help!
