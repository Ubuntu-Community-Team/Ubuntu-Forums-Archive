### Post by Favux on 2012-08-01
Hi Rehellio,

It does sound like a "sticky" key doesn't it?

I'd google for fixing laptop keys.  You're doing the first step by taking the keyboard out:  [http://www.insidemylaptop.com/disassemble-hp-pavilion-tx2000-tablet-pc/](http://www.insidemylaptop.com/disassemble-hp-pavilion-tx2000-tablet-pc/)

My cat use to attack one of my old laptops.  I'd walk into the room and discover she had pried out several keys.  At least on that laptop underneath the keys were several pieces of plastic that fit together like a little jigsaw puzzle and formed a little hinge/spring.  I figured out how to put them back together and was able to fix the keys.  A couple may have been chewed a little but I got them all working.  She was hooking a claw or two under the key and prying it off.

So if you're slow, methodical, and careful you may be able to fix it.  Likely something wedged in there or maybe something has gotten dirty/sticky.  Just try to do a little research.  Like what's good for cleaning the pieces?
