### Post by sgosnell on 2009-04-23
You don't need hpsetup.  Just open System/Adminstration/Printing, and select Add a new printer.  Ubuntu should find the printer, and let you tell it the make and model if it doesn't find it.  Just take the defaults if they make sense, and it should work.  It should go out and get the hplip stuff and install it, after getting permission, and then scanning, fax, and printing should work.  HP provides good Linux support, so it shouldn't be a big deal.  My HP all-in-one works perfectly under Ubuntu, but it has never scanned anything under Windows, and the Windows software simply wouldn't install, even after hours on the phone to India and having a new CD shipped.  Printing under Linux works.
