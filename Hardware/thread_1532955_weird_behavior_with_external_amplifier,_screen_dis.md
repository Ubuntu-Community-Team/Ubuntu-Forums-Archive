### Post by checcouniud on 2010-07-17
Hi all,

i have ubuntu 10.04 on a computer with an Asus P5G41-M LE motherboard.
I am trying to connect an external amplifier(trends TA10.2) to the pc to listen music with good quality speakers.
Unfortunately it does not work. As soon i connect the amplifier to the headphone plug the power led of the amplifier starts to flash instead of been continuous. And absolutely no sound is transmitted to the speakers. A disturbance in phase with the flashing led appears on my screen. The funny part is that the power led of the amplifier is flashing when connected to the desktop even if the desktop is turn off. And the disturbance on the screen is present even if the amplifier is turned off. 
As soon i disconnect the amplifier everything is normal again. The amplifier is working fine if i connect it to any other source ( i tried an ipod, my dell laptop and a macbook) and the desktop work fine if i connect my headphones. 

Does anybody has an idea of what is going on? is it simply a compatibility problem between the two components? 

reading the forums i find that the control volume or alsa mixer should have a switch option for external amplifiers but i can not find it.
Can you help me activating that switch?

Does anybody has any kind of suggestion or idea on what is the problem?

Thank you everybody!!!!

---

### Post by checcouniud on 2010-09-11
After days of testing i realized that the problem was caused by the faulty AC/DC adapter of the amplifier.
Once the AC/DC adapter of the amplifier has been replaced the amplifier worked fine.
