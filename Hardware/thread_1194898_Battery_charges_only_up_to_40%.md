### Post by linuxmagick on 2009-06-27
I'd drain the battery completely and then see what happens.  Which I believe is what another poster has already suggested.  However, I recall hearing that this can be a good or bad thing depending on the type of battery.  Don't remember much more than that.  I'd say that if you still have the problem after that, it's time to buy a new battery.  Unfortunately, hardware failures do happen and they often don't care about the age of the device.

On second thought, if you have a spare power cord, you may want to try it too.  I've seen cases where the battery was fine, but would not properly charge because the power brick needed to be replaced.  It's more likely to be the battery itself, but thought this was worth mentioning too.
