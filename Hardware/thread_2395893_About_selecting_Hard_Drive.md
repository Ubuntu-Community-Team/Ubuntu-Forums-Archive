### Post by TheFu on 2018-07-08
[https://www.pugetsystems.com/labs/articles/Understanding-the-WD-Rainbow-674/](https://www.pugetsystems.com/labs/articles/Understanding-the-WD-Rainbow-674/)

If you want a long-lasting, fast HDD, get a black. They have 5 yr warranties.
If you want a disk for media storage and viewing, get a red.  They have 3 yr warranties.
If you want a disk for video surveillance  recording, get a purple.  3 yr warranties.
If you want a disk to be used in a RAID1 or RAID10 home system, get a gold.

Use the warranty period as a guide for how well-made any HDD is. Manufacturers don't warrant for 5 yrs unless they believe in the product.  I've had reds fail before 4 yrs of use.  I've never had a black drive fail or show any issues unless I dropped it while it was spinning.

I've had blues and greens fail too and I have a few blues that have been working well beyond their warranties - over 5 yrs now.  Green drives have been merged into the "blue" coloring for marketing purposes.

Whenever adding new storage, I include a plan for backing it up.  If I can't backup the storage, then I won't bring the primary up. I've been burned too many times.

For data-driven HDD decisions, check out the backblaze quarterly reports.  4TB disks are pretty safe, if you avoid some problematic lines.
