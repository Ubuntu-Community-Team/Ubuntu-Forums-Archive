### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-01-01
They're generally doing that for one of several reasons ...

They are only wanting to try out something, so they don't want to mess around with their current partitioning -- to prevent accidentally breaking something.  Example of this is trying a pre-release version, or trying out a new version -- without changing what is there today.

Or ...

They want to have a portable Ubuntu bootable solution -- that they can plug into various PCs, and be able to use Ubuntu without having to install it.

Or ...

Their current PC came preinstalled with Windows, and also with the maximum of 4 primary partititions -- and they don't want to fiddle with this, or use Wubi, to be able to run Ubuntu.

Or ...  (whatever other reason they want)
