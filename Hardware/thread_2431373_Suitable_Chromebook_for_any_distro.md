### Post by TheFu on 2019-11-19
After years of using chromebooks as cheap Ubuntu laptops, I finally gave up and switched to using used laptops from the off-lease sellers in the area instead.  I've been 10x happier doing this than fighting with chromebooks.

Chromebooks bring all sorts of issues because they are made with really cheap hardware that is missing about 10 keyboard keys, the keyboards wear out well before the chromebook is EOL, and unless you pay $400 for a chromebook, you'll likely end up with a terrible CPU where a C720 Celeron 2950U would be faster.  Always check the performance of any CPU BEFORE you buy. The CPU numbers don't mean anything - a 5015U isn't necessarily faster than a 5010U. Check each one so you don't get burned.

Don't forget that chromebooks require a physical modification to boot any other OS and you'll almost certainly need to swap out the 16G storage for something at least 32G in size.  Not all chromebook models have replaceable storage. Some are soldered onto the board. If you don't do the storage modification, you're stuck with a limited OS.

I know nothing about touchscreens, except that we saw complaints here a bunch when they first became popular.  I don't know why those complaints stopped. Did they give up or did all of them magically get solved?  Considering all the GPU issues that still happen, I doubt every touchscreen issue has been fixed forever.
