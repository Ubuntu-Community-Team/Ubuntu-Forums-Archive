That would be my first step.  Obviously, if you put the old one back in and it works, then you have something wrong with the new chip: defective chip, bent pin, etc.  However, if you put the old one back in and still have nothing at all, you need to look for something you may have knocked loose.

### Post by Merovius on 2010-01-03
Are you sure the board supports that CPU? Is the memory you have correct for the new CPU? Did you plug in the cpu fan? Or, is it not pushed onto the fan connector tightly enough? Many boards will not boot without the fan connected correctly. Check that all cables and memory are seated and tight. If that doesn't do it I would go  back to the old CPU and if it boots you may have a bad cpu. Did you guard against static? (strap?)

Just my thoughts.

### Post by adam814 on 2010-01-03
If the fans don't turn on and none of the LEDs light up at all it sounds like a power supply issue.  Is it possible you don't have the main power supply cable to the motherboard connected properly?  Or maybe you need a higher-wattage power supply with the newer processor?

Although you can test them with a multimeter, I'm a fan of the testers you can buy at RadioShack.  Mine cost about $14 and it's a lot easier just to plug everything in and you can see all the info at once.
