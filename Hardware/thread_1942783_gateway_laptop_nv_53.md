### Post by Mark Phelps on 2012-03-19
You said "stopped working" -- implying that it WAS working, but is not now, right?

Sorry, but laptop keyboards are fragile and tend to break easily.  I have two older laptops (5 years +) and have already replaced the keyboards in each of them.

To rule out config problems with the installed Ubuntu, boot from a LiveCD and see if the keyboard works with that.

Also, reboot back into your BIOS screens and see if the keyboard works with those.

If it fails all the way up through the BIOS screens, then it is broken and will need to be replaced.  Keyboards are typically around $100 and are not hard to replace -- if you're very careful working with fragile prts.
