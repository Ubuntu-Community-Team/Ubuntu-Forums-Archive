### Post by darkenemy on 2010-03-03
I have now built 4 computers, albeit with relative difficulty, I would suggest that you take the time do look up each part specifically.  The Hardware compatibility lists are great, as far as linux goes, but there are other important things to worry about when building a computer from scratch.

for example, here are some common worries I have found myself having:

is the power supply gonna have enough power for the graphics card?
is the onboard sound driver gonna conflict with external sound hardware?
is the stock cup fan gonna be enough to cool the cpu?
does the motherboard support the frequency of the ram i am getting?
does the cpu support the frequency of the ram i am getting?
will the motherboard support blu-ray, *and* if it does, do my cpu and gpu also support it?


My point is this, even after checking a precompiled list of hardware that works well with linux, there is still the daunting task of assembling all those individual pieces in a way works.

Now, maybe you already know all this stuff, but I hope my answer helps at all.  It seems the best bet is to:

a) decide on your budget
b) gather all your individual pieces (cpu, mobo, gpu, psu, etc.)
c) check whether all the pieces will work well with linux
     -from my humble experience, the only things to worry (at all) are sound and graphics.  In the last 4 years, Ubuntu has come LEAPS AND BOUNDS in the hardware compatibility department.  If it appears that at least some people are reporting to have working systems similar to the one you are trying to build, then you should be ok.



I hope I have helped.
