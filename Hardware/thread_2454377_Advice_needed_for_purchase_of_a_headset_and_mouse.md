### Post by CatKiller on 2020-11-28
Any wired headset should be fine; it's the wireless ones that might have issues. Or you could just get a directional microphone.

For mice with extra buttons, features, or lights, checking that they're supported by Piper is a sensible step. That is able to configure all of those things.

Your mouse issue sounds like your mouse just isn't very high quality rather than it not being targeted towards gaming: either the switch is getting stuck or the connection is getting dropped.

### Post by CatKiller on 2020-11-28
> **SeijiSensei said:**
> My (adult) daughter is a pretty avid gamer and only uses wired keyboards and mice to avoid lag.

Me too. Plus, no chance for interference, nor reliance on a (potentially shoddy) Bluetooth chip and tiny antenna. 

> **makem2 said:**
> I will check what 'Piper' is... 
Looked at piper buy a bit lost lol.  I see a 905 listed on [libratbag]("https://github.com/libratbag/libratbag") but again lost :-( 

The G903 that you've picked out is on the [list of supported devices](https://github.com/libratbag/libratbag/tree/master/data/devices). Logitech stuff is, in general, a reasonable choice: they have reasonable (but not amazing) build quality for the price, and they don't do anything really weird as a "value add." Razer stuff fails on both those counts. I use a Steelseries Rival 310, since it works well, has good specs, and doesn't look too hideous, although I'd personally prefer it if it were a bit heavier. 

I don't know if it's a function of the Amazon links, but they were showing wireless headsets to me. If you specifically *want* a wireless headset then you'd want to establish that your current mouse problem isn't actually a Bluetooth problem. I've got some (AKG) Bluetooth headphones that work really well with my laptop and phone, but I've not had Bluetooth on a desktop that wasn't really flaky: it's one of those YMMV things.

When I was still doing multiplayer gaming I used a Steelseries Siberia, that they don't make any more, and it was fine. They were pretty terrible as headphones because they put so much prominence on speech frequencies, but that's actually what you want for multiplayer comms. The microphone was clear and directional, and rolled up into one of the cans when you weren't using it. I'm sure the hardware on the newer versions will be solid with the wired versions. Going wireless, though, means that you have a battery to worry about and a (potentially unsupported) sound card over a (potentially flaky) Bluetooth connection.

### Post by CatKiller on 2020-11-28
> **makem2 said:**
> The G903 looks ok but it does not say how it charges or anything about the dongle that I saw but I do need to investigate further. 

I think Logitech wireless stuff all uses the same dongle, so if you have a mouse and keyboard, say, you only need the one dongle. It looks like it plugs in to charge, or you can buy an optional wireless charging pad. 

>  Any suggestions for a headset like the 7 without a puck to drag around when I move place

I would personally steer away from the wireless audio. Wired audio has been essentially unchanged for a century, so there's not a lot of scope to mess with it. The wireless stuff is more vulnerable, and the Steelseries Engine software is exactly the kind of "value add" thing that companies like to use to selectively uncripple their hardware. For my mouse I didn't care because it was a standard USB mouse and I knew that Piper could configure the rest, but for wireless audio it's possible to make it so that an OS can't even see the device without the manufacturer's special sauce. If you do go that route, make sure the place you buy it from has a good returns policy, just in case.
