### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-02-03
Keep in mind that different manufacturers produce variations of the same GPU. I have just searched Google for the GTX 260 and I have seen two different photographs. The first picutre showed the GTX 260 with VGA, DVI, and HMDI connectors. The second showed 1xVGA and 2xDVI. No connection using HDMI. If you are buying this type of card then you will need an adapter that converts a DVI connector to a HDMI connector. It may be included in the box.

I have noticed that recent TVs have HDMI connectors but DVI seems to be going out of fashion. What is the difference? Is there any lost of picture quality? This you need to answer or buy a card that has a HDMI connector. What connectors do you have on your screen? This is what you have to match up with.

VGA is OK if you are just using the machine as a computer but DVI or HMDI is better as it is digital in the computer to digital in the screen without the need to convert to analogue and back again. This is what happens when you use VGA to connect to a screen.

DVI- no audio, just video. HDMI- video + audio. 

> As of 2010, there is a transition occurring for home TV monitors away from DVI towards [HDMI]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HDMI") as the common connector format. While DVI is pin-compatible with HDMI, the DVI specification does not specifically support audio, while the HDMI specification does. This might create an audio hardware limitation for people who want to connect their desktop computer (which typically uses DVI) to an LCD or plasma monitor (which may only have HDMI inputs).


 The computer user may in some cases be forced to run audio separately from another audio connector on their computer to the TV monitor, using either 1-channel analog RCA cables or a digital audio cable. It is not possible to "inject" a [SPDIF]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SPDIF") digital audio signal directly into a DVI to HDMI adapter as the audio signal is transmitted along with the video information in a single TMDS data stream in HDMI.
