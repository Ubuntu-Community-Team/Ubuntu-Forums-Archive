### Post by Crash.hm on 2009-02-26
I suppose I should start by saying that I am a moderately skilled computer user. I'm new to linux but I used to use unix machines. So I'm not brilliant with ubuntu but I hope at least competent.

My computer usage:
I've had the mini 12 for about a week now. This is my primary computer. My overall impressions are favorable, but you have to understand that I am a very low demand user. The most demanding thing that I do is open a PDF and sometimes visit hulu. I mostly write papers, read journal articles, Make presentations, and surf the net. So I think the mini 12 is ideal for me since my biggest concrns are size and battery life.

My dell mini 12 specs:
atom core 1.6ghz z530
1gb ram
gma 500 graphics
bluetooth module
48whr bat. (6cell)

Construction:
I like the feel of the over all package. It feels solid and pretty sleek. I has some venting on the bottom that makes me feel a little ill at lease because the vents lead right to the underside of some chip sets. The laptop barely flexes when I pick it up by a corner. The hinge is nice and stiff. It's also the only thing that keeps it closed, so who knows what will happen long term. The 6 cell battery creates air space underneith which seems to help keep it cool. I've run the laptop for hours streaming video and it barely heats up. My only concern in the construction is that the battery feels a little loose. I don't think it's going to suddenly break contact of fall out, but it's a small concern that sits in the back of my head.

Trackpad and keybord:
The track pad feels good. It's big enough and responsive enough to be fucntional. since there isn't alot of spec to rest my wrists they start to ache very quicky. I'm probably a little more sensitive than most people to this. 
The keyboard is ok. I has a nice tactile feel and it is functional. I could probably tollerate typing a few pages possibly more it if I had to. It is bigger than most net book key boards, but i was dissappointed to find the HP 10" has a nicer keyboard. In terms of key sizing/spacing the HP 10" has a slight advantage ~1mm per key, which I would have liked. Dell chose to make the arrows key the standard inverted T rather than placing them a in a row. The trade off is that it moves the shift key in and compressed the "," "." and "?" keys. I found that annoying at first but I'm used to it.

Ubuntu on the mini 12"
generally I'm happy with ubuntu on the mini 12. The one thing that stands out to me is that the standard ubuntu package may be a bit much for the lower spec 1.3 ghz processor. I felt that a few of the programs and the file manager weren't fast enough with the 1.6ghz atom, so I would probably have hated the 1.3ghz atom.

The first thing I did when I got the computer was turn off the dell desktop and switch to the standard ubuntu. For those who are wondering it is running version 8.0.4 of ubuntu. 

Early on I felt inclined to slim dow the system. It's not horribly slow but slow enough to annoy me. The file manager would freak out an stall when I was trying to drag and drop batches of files. I switched to thunar which is working well. I also installed epiphany web browser and abiword.

Generally speaking everything worked well out of the box. The only real problem I've had has been hooking up to an external monitor (but more on that later).

Load times:
ubuntu ~60sec
firefox ~12sec
Epiphay (gecko) ~4sec
Abiword ~5sec
Open office writer ~14sec
Evolution mail ~10 sec
Adobe Reader ~12sec

hooking up an external monitor:
i hooked up a viewsonic VA1926w (19" widescreen). It will work but you probably won't get to work at 1440x900. Dell will lie to you (they lied to me) and tell you that it can run up to a 24" monitor at appropriate resolution. In my experience 1280x960 is the max the dell mini 12" will tolerate. This was confirmed by dell's ubuntu tech support which is as much of a joke as you'd expect. I found that at higher resolutions the monitor likes to spasm occasionally. If anyone's been following the gma 500 discussion, it seems that the gma500 is largely unsupported, hopefully this changes. Whether or not the gma 500 is directly responsible for my problem I'm not sure.

Battery life:
As I said, I'm a low demand user. I have the 6 cell, and I'd guess that it gets 3-5 hours of continous low demand use. I have bluetooth shut off, but I generally keep wifi enabled.

Conclusion:
 I generally like this laptop. It does more or less everything I need. I would like it to support higher resolutions on my monitor. I would also like it to be able to stream hulu full screen rather than just a small window. overall I give it 4/5 stars, but if i'd had to pay the full 600 for my config I'd probably give it 3.5/5. Hopefully they get someone to address the growing concerns about the gma 500, but who knows.
