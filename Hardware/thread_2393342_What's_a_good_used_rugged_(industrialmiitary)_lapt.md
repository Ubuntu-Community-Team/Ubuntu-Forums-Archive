### Post by him610 on 2018-06-02
Ahoy jeff162,

At one of my previous employers I was issued (in 2012) a Panasonic CF-53 for testing software on construction machinery. It was perfectly suited for the job. I am not sure I would recommend a used one though because they are liable to have been pretty well used and beat-up during it's lifespan. You probably can find good used ones though. On the inside the ruggedized computers use the same motherboards, processors, and peripherals that regular computers use. You might try the Dell Business Outlet store that sells refurbished, scratch & dent, and returned laptops. Newegg and Amazon also sell refurbished machines. Consider replacing the HDD with a SSD if you get one.

### Post by sudodus on 2018-06-02
+1 to look for a refurbished computer.

- It need not be extremely rugged, but replacing the HDD with an SSD is a good idea to make it 'shock-proof'.
- You can also consider covering the keyboard with some plastic film (polyethylene or similar) to protect against smoke and dust.

### Post by kazakore on 2018-06-04
All Lenovo ThinkPad T series and HP EliteBooks are MilSpec tested to the below specification, basically the reason why they're pretty much the only laptops I'll buy for travelling with.

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIL-STD-810](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIL-STD-810)


Note that Tested (or Built/Designed To Pass) doesn't always mean Passed. Somewhere HP has a pdf of the results for all tests of their enterprise laptops from a few years ago but I can't find it right now. Lenovo don't seem to be quite so open about things. But I would still trust these more than the average consumer laptop and they generally run Linux pretty well IME.
