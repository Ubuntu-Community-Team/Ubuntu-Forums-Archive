### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-03-26
First of all -- quit bumping!! One bump every 24 hours is considered the rule.

Second of all, the xorg.conf file does not do autodetection.  When it is built, it reflects what Xwindows knows about your hardware.  If it has "generic monitor" or something like that, it was not able to detect your specific monitor brand or model.  Editing it by hand is not going to change what it detects.

You can try System --> Preferences --> Screen Resolution.  I believe there is a button there for detecting the displays.  If it doesn't detect your specific make and model monitor, you will have to set the values manually.  You can do that by editing xorg.conf (which you've already done) or you can experiment with monitor selections in the Screen Resolution applet.
