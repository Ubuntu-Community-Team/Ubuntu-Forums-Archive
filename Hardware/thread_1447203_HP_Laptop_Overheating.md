### Post by reservedforgreatness on 2010-04-05
Hi, 

I also have an HP laptop that heats up, the simplest option is to reduce the processor speed. Right click on the taskbar, and add panel. You can then click on add panel, and add the CPU frequency scaling monitor. Once added, you can manually lower the clock speed of your CPU to prevent overheating.

### Post by sakundiak on 2010-04-05
I've also run into this problem with one of my hp laptops and the only thing i can say is make sure your heat sinks are working to full capacity. I pull the laptop top apart and cleaned all the fins out in the heat sink and now it still runs very hot but will play video around the clock. The only version that ran okay in regards to heat on my older hp was 8.04, now don't ask me why but it ran very nice and somewhat cool. Good luck
