### Post by TheFu on 2019-10-18
About a year ago, saw some Core i7 16G 14 inch Dell laptops for under $300 on eBay.  But not all Core i7 processors are created equal.  Be certain that that CPU performance you want is clearly understood.  Check some benchmarks on the CPUs in your list.

BTW, 2-internal disks isn't something a 12inch laptop will have. Those will be SSD-only, probably m.2 format. Optical discs aren't seen much in laptops anymore.  I can't imagine needing 6TB of storage on a laptop.  Use the network for storage.

I ended up with an 8th-gen Core i5 which was 2x faster and much better on battery than those older i7 versions. It is a 15 inch, no touch screen (I hate touch), 8G RAM. $305 with a tiny blemish that cannot be seen, just felt.

### Post by oldfred on 2019-10-23
Are you sure you want or need a laptop?

While laptops always say they are as powerful as desktop, they are referring to older desktops.
Laptops are not ergonmic, screen is low & keyboard high if on desk. And screen is small for a lot of use.
You can add keyboard & larger screen, but then it is not portable with those.

I suggest a good desktop that meets your requirements and then a lightweight laptop for when you need mobility. Probably lower in cost than expensive high powered laptop.
