### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-07-07
TBH, I haven't needed them very much, so I don't have a truly reliable estimate.  Most of the time when they kick in is during brownouts, or when we pop the breakers.
My desktop chews a lot of power, I'm not sure what the wattage is on it, but its a core 2 duo with 2 flat panels.  I've never had more than a brownout on this setup.
The server uses a 240 watt power supply and I think it's had easily 30 minutes to an hour or more of power.  I don't make a habit of pushing the backup's limits because, well, it's a game server and I'd rather not run it out of power.

During blackouts, I primarily use my battery backups to give me heads up time to safely shut down machines when it gets to the point that the battery backup is beeping continuously, meaning its about to run out of power.

When you purchase a battery backup, it should tell you how much you can expect to get out of it.
