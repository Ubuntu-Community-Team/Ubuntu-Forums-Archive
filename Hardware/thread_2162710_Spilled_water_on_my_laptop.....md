### Post by snowpine on 2013-07-15
I've had good luck with the "bag of rice" technique:

Dissasemble computer and remove battery.
Place components in a large ziploc bag full of dry, uncooked rice.
Leave overnight.

However, you  may have already damaged the laptop by turning it on while it was still wet. :(

### Post by Ranko Kohime on 2013-07-15
You might get lucky, but you may have screwed it up.  Only the results of the rice drying will tell.

Any time that any electronic device gets wet, unplug it, remove the battery, and then dry using the rice technique.  Turning it on while it's still wet inside is a sure way to kill it off, assuming the water didn't do that already.

_**NEVER POWER ON OR ATTEMPT TO POWER ON A WET ELECTRONIC DEVICE**_
