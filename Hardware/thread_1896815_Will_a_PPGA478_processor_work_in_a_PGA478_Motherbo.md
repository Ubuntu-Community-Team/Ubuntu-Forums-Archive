### Post by xyzzyman on 2011-12-18
[http://download.intel.com/support/motherboards/mobile/mgm45rm/sb/rm_e3504002.pdf](http://download.intel.com/support/motherboards/mobile/mgm45rm/sb/rm_e3504002.pdf)

Mobile Board MGM45RM supports the following Intel mobile processors in the 
mPGA479 package: 
&#8226; Intel Core 2 Duo mobile processors with a 1066/800 FSB 
&#8226; Intel Celeron mobile processors with a 667 FSB 

You should be good. Update the BIOS from Dell before you swap the CPU's, and make sure to clean the heat sink of old thermal compound and apply fresh. Not too much, not too little. Look up a guide on google with pics to see. YOu are doing this at your own risk. lol

### Post by MoreOrLess on 2011-12-18
Be very careful. Intel made a few sockets with 478 pins. The CPU in question is a Socket P: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socket_P](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socket_P)

The Inspiron 1545 came with different CPU models which used different sockets, though most of them were Socket P.
