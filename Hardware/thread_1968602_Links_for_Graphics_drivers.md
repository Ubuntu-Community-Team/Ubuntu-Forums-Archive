### Post by ubiquitin.jf on 2012-04-29
The beauty of Ubuntu (and other modern distros!) is that you don't need to do any of this! Ubuntu comes with an open-source graphics card driver and the vast majority of drivers for other pieces of hardware (such as wifi cards) on the disc. Once installed a user-friendly tool will ask you if you'd prefer to use the proprietary drivers available for your hardware and install them for you with just a click.

The Lenovo software won't work in Ubuntu and doesn't need to because Ubuntu has its own power management and backup tools. If you're finding your battery life is a little poor, try installing Jupiter for a little extra juice.
