### Post by SeijiSensei on 2015-09-14
I have an old ASUS Eeepc that runs Kubuntu, and the ASUS desktop I'm using right now with dual-boot Kubuntu and Win7, so I'll give their products a recommendation.  I did a little browsing online and found this item if you prefer a physical hard drive: [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=9SIA6R432A7474](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=9SIA6R432A7474).  The SSD models only include a 32 GB drive like [http://www.amazon.com/ASUS-X205TA-11-6-Laptop-Intel/dp/B00NY29UIO/](http://www.amazon.com/ASUS-X205TA-11-6-Laptop-Intel/dp/B00NY29UIO/).  You could, of course, buy a larger SSD or add USB storage.  I might choose the [SanDisk Cruzer "Ultra Fit"]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820173098R") USB drives which sit flush against the side of the machine. You can just insert the Cruzer into a USB 3.0 port and forget it is there.  The [64 GB version]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=9SIA7HK38J4797") is considerably cheaper than the 128 GB model and may do the job for your friend just as well. The 32GB SSD is plenty for a standard Ubuntu installation.  Format the USB to ext4 and mount it as /home.
