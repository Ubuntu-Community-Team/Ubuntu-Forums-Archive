### Post by steev182 on 2009-09-23
Remember, the limitations on a lot of these phones is how much can be displayed at one time now. The hardware speed/capacity are better than or equal to desktop PCs of 10 years ago.

The graphics on the iPhone are good enough for games that look as good as that on the N64/PSone, which although these were around 15 years ago, they were dedicated to gaming, so I feel that's pretty good going.

I used to think that Physical keyboard was a deal breaker, but now I feel like it's a limitation, and really, it makes me want a laptop with touchscreens on both halves instead of one half having a keyboard/mouse. Having a 3.2" to 3.5" screen for video in widescreen is brilliant, and the quality is great depending on how you encoded it, basically, you can have DVD quality on a phone. Plus 32GB space on the iPhone 3GS and 8GB + 32GB on the Samsung Galaxy isn't an insignificant amount of space to have!
