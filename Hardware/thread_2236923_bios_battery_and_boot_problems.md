### Post by jgw on 2014-07-29
I have been messing with pc's for a very long time.  During that time I have never even bothered to check the bios battery as they are ALWAYS good.  A couple of weeks ago my main machine would not boot, it wouldn't even get to the bios.  I checked with friends and we determined that the motherboard was fried so I replaced it.  Not long after my wife's windows machine was booting with a very dim screen which hung.  The general internet consensus, on that one, was to reset the bios.  There are normally two ways to do that, there are usually pins, on the motherboard, when shorted, will reset the bios.  Another way is to remove the battery, wait a bit, and put it back in.  I choose to remove the battery.  I then, just for the heck of it, decided to check it to make sure it was good (I KNEW it would be!).  It was not, barely alive.  I replaced the battery and restarted my wife's machine.  It came right up (and strange happenings also stopped, like not being able to read memory cards, etc).  I then remembered I had not yet chucked the old motherboard so I thought I would check that battery too.  It was flat out of juice.  Replaced that battery too and - it was just fine!

Anyway - this may be posted elsewhere but, when you have having strange problems, have tried everything else, cannot boot, etc (irregardless of operationg system), check the bios battery (Its one of them round ones (cr 2092 I think)).  If that is not alive then your machine cannot get the bios.  The bios controls all the basic functions of your motherboard and without it you are doomed.
