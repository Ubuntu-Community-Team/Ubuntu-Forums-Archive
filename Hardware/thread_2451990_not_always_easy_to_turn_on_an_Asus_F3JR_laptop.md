### Post by Autodave on 2020-10-14
I find that about 1/2 of the laptops that I get for repair can repaired by doing just what you did.  Removing the battery just clears all of any memory going on.  Even your battery has a mini-computer in it that has to work in cinjunction with the rest of the laptop.  

I usually leave the battery out for 10 minutes to make sure everything is wiped.

### Post by CelticWarrior on 2020-10-14
As above but it must be stressed that the battery is long past its due date. Problems such as this can and should be expected. Either remove the battery for good (and send it to the nearest recycling center) or replace it with an inexpensive one as it makes no sense to pay $150 for an old laptop worth less than $50. This is a financial decision, not technical. The technical part is that battery is as good as dead, persisting in using it can create other problems.
