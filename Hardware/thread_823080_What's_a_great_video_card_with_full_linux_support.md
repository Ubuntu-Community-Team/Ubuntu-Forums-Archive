### Post by thideras on 2008-06-09
What are you going to use this computer for?  If you won't being doing anything 3D (games, etc), then look for a midrange card (7xxx series, 8600).  If you want to do 3D, the G92 cores (8800 GT, 8800 GTS 512) are fully supported and work very well.

### Post by The Cosmic Hobo on 2008-06-09
I've been pondering the same question, as I'm researching new tech to build my own system - the last one I built was in 2002, and things have changed a lot since then!

I heard that Nvidia do the best Linux support. The current issue of [Custom PC magazine](http://www.custompc.co.uk) reviews a number of ATI Crossfire-capable and Nvidia SLI-capable cards, and off the information in there I researched a couple of cards. The one I've decided to get is the GeForce 9600 GT, which is reasonably priced, reasonably powerful, and has a low power draw. Linux drivers are [available](http://www.nvidia.com/object/linux_display_ia32_173.14.05.html) and there are [several](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4558708&postcount=37) [different](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=5086971&postcount=6) posts around the forum on installing them
