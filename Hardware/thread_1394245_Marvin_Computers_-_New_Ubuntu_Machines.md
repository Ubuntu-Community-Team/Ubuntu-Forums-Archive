### Post by disciple3d on 2010-01-30
Hi cascade, thanks for your feedback.  I'll try to address your points -

1. Yeah, I'll specify the HD brand, thanks.  I'm intending on the Samsung Spinpoint F3 at the moment, which I've heard good things about.

2. I should be clearer about the motherboard and socket, thanks.  I know that strictly speaking the LGA1366 platform is faster, but my personal opinion is that it is going nowhere in the future, and I'm hoping to make PCs that will be more easily upgradeable in the future.  To be honest, they'll probably be a whole new chipset/socket by then, but nevermind :)

3. Thanks for pointing that out, it should say **5850**! ;)

4. Yeah, the 6GB of RAM is just to meet a certain price point, but I see your point regarding dual channel vs. triple channel.  It would probably make more sense to go with 4GB and reduce the price a bit.  I think I'll do that, thanks :) Oh, the RAM I have is Crucial Balistix PC-12800 I think.  I'm going to try to keep it at least that fast, but if the prices come down a bit, I'll try to get better timings too (I think the Crucial is 8-8-8-24).

5. I'll specify the motherboard, which is an Asus P55D Pro, or something akin to that on the DarkMatter (I'm not good at remembering motherboard names).

6. No AMD options yet, just because the first few PCs I've specced are performance centered, and the Intel processors seemed better 'bang for the buck' at the top end at the moment.  I know the Phenom IIs are excellent though, so I'll do a bit of research on those too and try to spec out some AMD platforms as well.

7. PSU?  Well, for a single card crossfire system, you need at least a 500W PSU to support it (with a high end i7 system).  The card draws a whopping 170W at peak! It's a bit ridiculous when you consider the processor itself is 95W.  It's not really possible to get anything less than a 700W power supply if you want 4xPCI express connectors (this then supports two cards, since each 5850 needs 2x 6-pin PCI-E).  I could have put a 500W in, but that won't allow for the addition of another 5850 later, without upgrading the power supply, so it made sense to fit in it the first place.  

Thanks a lot for your feedback, I'll try to address the points you've made, which were very helpful.

Chris
