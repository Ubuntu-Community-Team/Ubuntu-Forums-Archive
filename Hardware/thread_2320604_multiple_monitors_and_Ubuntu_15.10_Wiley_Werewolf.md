### Post by T.J. on 2016-04-16
Edges: Depending on your desktop environment, you may have "edge resistance" set or your monitors may be misconfigured. I'm sorry I can't give you more. I do not use Ubuntu/Unity. I'd check your settings. 

Wallpaper: Gnome based desktop environments, such as Unity, Mate or Cinnamon, the answer about individual wallpapers on each monitor is "no." On XFCE (Xubuntu) or KDE (kubuntu) the answer is "yes". " It's a deliberate design choice by the designers. The Gnome designers wanted things "dumbed down" i.e. simple without extra options.


If you are using the Ubuntu default, on login the password entry follows the mouse to whatever screen is active.  It has no effect on the system once you are logged in.  You should be able to reconfigure the login screen manually.  I am not aware if Ubuntu gives you access to it inside your desktop.
