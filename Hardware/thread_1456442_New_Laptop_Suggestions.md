### Post by P4man on 2010-04-21
They have to give you a rebate if you dont need and use the windows license. Some OEMs make this painfully hard though, when i asked Acer here in the benelux, they told me that after purchase, I had to ship the laptop at my expense to their HQ, where they would remove windows, and send it back (again, at my expense) within 2 to 4 weeks (!) and then I would get a €50 rebate. Thanks, but no thanks.

You can often get ubuntu (or no OS) preinstalled from most OEMs when you talk to their professional department though. For businesses this is rather common, but unfortunately that often means you are limited to business class machines, with a nice price premium. Lenovo does this for instance. Then again, that price premium may be worth it as the business laptop really tend to be higher quality builds.

Lastly, if you walk in to a brick and mortar shop, its usually easy to get an OS-less laptop. You do have to ask and insist, but it tends to work.
