### Post by WinRiddance on 2010-12-11
Any graphic card or computer with built-in HDMI jack should be able to provide what you're looking for. The purpose of HDMI is to receive top HD quality sound and video on whichever device (a TV) the HDMI cable is running to. As is always the case with computers though, if you plan on watching movies and working on the computer at the same time, you'd obviously be looking for a graphic card with the most amount of RAM that you can find (512 MB would be my **minimum** recommendation), as well as having plenty of CPU power and RAM on the computer.

Optimally I'd say that the HDMI graphic card should come with at least 1 GB of RAM, the CPU having quad core technology, and the RAM in the computer consisting of high speed  4 GB. If you can put that together in any way, you'll have an awseome HDMI setup on a workhorse of a machine. Plus a super nice 5.1 or 7.1 surround sound system of course .... ;)
