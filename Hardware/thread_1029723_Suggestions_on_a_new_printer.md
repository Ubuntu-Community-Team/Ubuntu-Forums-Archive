### Post by Paul Miller on 2009-01-03
I'm looking to buy a small laser printer for home use.  My requirements are:

[LIST]
[*] Auto duplex

[*] Reasonable monthly duty cycle. 

I'm a graduate student, so it's not unusual for me to print 100+ pages a day.  On the other hand, that's not an everyday thing, so, I'd say a 2000 page duty cycle would be more than adequate.

[*] Low cost per page.  (See the previous point.)

[*] Good print quality.

[*] Reasonable price for the unit itself (under $300 would be ideal, but cost per page takes precedence).

[*] And, of course, it has to work with Ubuntu.
[/LIST]

Nice to haves would include:

[list]

[*] Color.  

Honestly, I don't print a lot of stuff that would demand the use of color -- mostly journal articles and such.  But, I'd like to have the capability.

[*] Networkability.

[*] Fast printing speed.

Obviously, the faster the better, but I don't mind hitting "print" and going to get a cup of coffee or something.

[*] Postscript would be ideal.
[/list]

### Post by zob on 2009-01-14
I'm very happy with my Brother HL-2140 which is fast, cheap (printers price), cheap in cost per page, not to big (though it IS bigger than the standard ink-jet printers). Print quality is fine. And best of all. You can get linux drivers:
[http://solutions.brother.com/linux/en_us/download_prn.html#HL-2140]("http://solutions.brother.com/linux/en_us/download_prn.html#HL-2140")

By the way just below it on the drivers page you see the HL-2170W which is only about $30 more and comes with network possibility.

By auto duplex, I'm not sure if you mean that the printer has to turn the pages for you. In that case it doesn't. But through the driver you can choose to write only left pages and printer writes on one side of ALL the papers and afterward you can choose to write right pages. So it is easy, but the windows driver has more features I must admit. But I still prefer that to my dads printer (canon pixma-something) which does indeed turn the paper but takes forever to do so. Maybe in the future there'll be more functionality in the drivers.

This printer is only about $120 so you can get a lot more functionality for the price you suggest. Have a look at these:
[http://www.brother-usa.com/Printer/BW_Monochrome_Laser_Printers/]("http://www.brother-usa.com/Printer/BW_Monochrome_Laser_Printers/")

I don't know maybe HP is good to. But I've been happy with my new Brother...
