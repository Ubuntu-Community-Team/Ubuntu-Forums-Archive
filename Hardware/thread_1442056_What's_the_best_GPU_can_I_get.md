### Post by clutch| on 2010-03-29
I would suggest losing that mobo in favor of one that has PCI-E.  With a $450 budget you should have no problem getting a decent motherboard and a much better video card.  

You could get a GTX 275 in the three hundred dollar neighborhood.  The remaining 150$ should be plenty to get a decent motherboard, depending on your processor.  If the whole computer in general is from the Pre-PCIe days, its more or less ancient and you should probably just save your pennies and replace everything.

I just recently did a brand new Core i5-750 build for around $900.

/edit, sorry, just saw that you have a P4.  Idk, that's sort of old, but if its still got the power you need stick with it I guess.  I had a P4 with similar specs in my last machine and the upgrade to the i5 was a huge difference.  Of course, given the new socket, that's going to mean a whole new computer basically.  You can save a lot of money by cannibalizing the hard drives and optical drives from your P4 machine though.
