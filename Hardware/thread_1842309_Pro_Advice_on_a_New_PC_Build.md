### Post by 1clue on 2011-11-12
You mentioned a VM in the initial post.  Make sure your motherboard is supported by KVM or whatever virtualization software you choose.  Even if you don't think it's important now it might be interesting to you later, and it sucks to not have it work right.

I'm using an older version of what you seem to be building, and I missed that.  What a drag.  Seriously hurts VM performance.

I don't see that you mentioned clock speed enhancements.  FWIW if you're not bumping the clock speed then there's no reason for fancy cooling.  I have 5 fans in my box, they're all hooked up to the CPU-control and I rarely hear a thing in a home office.  I can tell it's running if I think about it but it's definitely NOT noisy unless I'm running 3 VMs and a load of tests that hit all of them.

***Edit:***  Oh yeah.  Get a much bigger power supply than you think you needed.  And figure on $300+ for your UPS.  I've been through 3 lightning storms so far that shut down power for over an hour.  the UPS has already paid for itself just by lightning protection.

I probably will go with water cooling eventually. But not a h80, because based on pretty much every bench run of all coolers in corsair's H series, the only one not out-performed by air coolers a third of thier price is the H100.

A lot of the info regarding h series coolers assume you set them to blow into the case, it would seem that getting them to blow out not only has a very small impact in cooling ~1-2C - but it also has a large impact in reducing heat in the case. 

If you are going to overclock the cpu I recommend liquid cooling - it is more effective  - i definitely think a h80 will have a more significant cooling effect than any £20 cooling set up.
