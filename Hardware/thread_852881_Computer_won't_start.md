### Post by crazyness003 on 2008-07-08
Dont take offense to this, but im gonna assume you're talking about a desktop. Also this has happend to me and the problem i had was a faulty boot switch. 
Best way to figure it out is to locate the 2 pins on the mobo that connect to the power switch and touch them together with a paperclip or something. The cmos battery may also be a problem, but usually it does start up, but then tells you that theres a problem. Im gonna guess its the switch. I know it sounds dumb, but it has happend to me (took 3 days to figure out too. Stupid...mumble...wire...mumble...button...)

### Post by starcannon on 2008-07-08
A blown cap will smell a bit like rotten fish, maybe have some gunk oozing from it, and if its leaked long enough it will corrode the pc board that its attached to.

Some other things to check out would be to make sure all jumpers if any are set right, clear cmos (usually a jumper or some contact points near the cmos battery or the cmos chip consult your motherboard manual for exact location)

Worst case scenario is the mobo is toast, and perhaps you can grab a better one on ebay for next to nothing or goodwill or where ever, and maybe scavenge the cards, ram, cpu, etc... off the emachine board.
