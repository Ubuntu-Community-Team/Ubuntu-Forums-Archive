Hi,

I have recently made the decision to take my PC and fully switch from Windows to Ubuntu.

While the installation was very quick and easy getting my graphics card working properly was anything but. I tried using the "Additional Drivers" for fglrx that were found via the Software & Updates but this led to a host of problems

One of which was causing my PC to boot into a **purple screen** which froze on each restart. I tried several different steps to try and resolve this some more sucessful than others (GRUB menu, quite splash, nomodeset, ....). Also part of my problem is that I have 3 different monitors and getting them working with the drivers found by Ubuntu was very problematic.

By the end I decided to do a clean install and look for the proper steps for setting up Ubuntu to work with my video card.

I found a wiki page which gave me step by step instrustions to follow on setting up the proper drivers and Catalyst control software. This worked great for me and solved all my issues with my graphics card (6950).

[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Raring_Installation_Guide](http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Raring_Installation_Guide)

Hopefully this helps someone else out stuck in the same situation.
