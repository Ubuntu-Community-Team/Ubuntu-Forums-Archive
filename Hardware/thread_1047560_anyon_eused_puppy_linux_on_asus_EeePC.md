### Post by snowpine on 2009-01-22
Hello Cmay, I have experimented with Puppy on my eee 900ha (bigger screen and hard drive compared to your 701). It is very fast and runs well from the hard drive, a USB stick, or an SD card. You can even do a "frugal install" to your hard drive to make a dual boot without the need for extra partitions. I have tried 3 different versions of Puppy:

1) The current version of Puppy (4.1.2). My favorite option because it is the most up to date. The only necessary "hack" was to install the correct wireless driver: [http://www.murga-linux.com/puppy/viewtopic.php?t=34159](http://www.murga-linux.com/puppy/viewtopic.php?t=34159)
2) Pupeee. This one is a bit older, only tested it out briefly but it seemed to work okay.
3) Breeezy. The oldest of the three. It is tiny and super fast, but I did not like it because it doesn't really support the newer model eee's. Might be perfect on your 701 though...

In a nutshell, Puppy is a great option for the eee's because it is small and fast. It makes a great backup/dual boot with a more full-featured Linux. I am not currently using Puppy because I prefer an Ubuntu-based distro (Cruncheee).

Hope that helps.
