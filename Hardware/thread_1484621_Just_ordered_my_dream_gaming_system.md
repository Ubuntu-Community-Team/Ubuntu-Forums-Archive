### Post by antieuclid on 2010-05-15
I won a sweepstakes recently, and made a deal with myself that as long as the rest of the money went straight into savings, I could spend up to $2k on building a very shiny gaming rig. Since I was working with a fairly large budget, I decided to spring for an OEM copy of Windows 7, and now I'm trying to decide how I want to organize things once the new system is set up.

Basically, I will have one top-of-the-line (A) and one perfectly serviceable (B) system. I can install Windows on one of them. The only truly Windows-specific things I need to do are syncing my iPhone in iTunes and running Google Sketchup from time to time. I greatly prefer to do all my web browsing and day to day computing in Linux for security and usability reasons. As I see it, I have three options:

1) Windows on A, Ubuntu on B. This way I would have access to all the latest drivers for the good hardware, and wouldn't have to worry about WINE compatibility problems for games. On the other hand, it pushes all of my non-gaming computing onto my second best computer
2) Ubuntu on A, Windows on B. Computer B basically gets a cushy retirement as an iTunes alarm clock off in a corner somewhere, and I do everything else in Linux on Computer A. My concern here is that the limitations of WINE and linux drivers mean that the true potential of the hardware won't be utilized.
3. Dual-boot on A, send B to live on a farm with a nice family. On the one hand, this means both operating systems get to play on the nice hardware, but on the other hand I can't use them both at the same time. I don't like having to reboot all the time, and I suspect I'd leave it in one or the other OS most of the time.

So what would you do if you were me? The most relevant hardware stats are NVidia GTX 480 (possibly going up to 2 of them in SLI at a later date), AMD Phenom II X6 3.2Ghz, and 4GB of DDR3 1600 RAM, going up to 6 or 8 in a few months. I mainly play WoW, though I occasionally dip a toe into the Final Fantasy franchise. How much of a performance gap am I likely to see between Linux and Windows on a system like this?
