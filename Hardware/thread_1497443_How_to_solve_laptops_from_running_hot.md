### Post by DDDa on 2010-05-30
I have a very nice, aluminum Antec cooler for $21 ($4 cheaper). But the cooling pad is not the answer, because:

When someone complains about heat and power usage, you don't suggest a cooling pad because that solution (it is a partial solution) fails when we need it the most: when on the road. I don't bring cooling pads when I'm on the airplane. And you probably won't, too. And that's when you will suffer from suboptimal battery times.

It also feels like there is nothing wrong with either Linux in general or, at least, our configurations (that's when we come to forums like these, sharing knowledge and experiences), when in fact there is.

During the years, I've tested many distros on some notebooks that I had. Compaq, Toshiba Satellites, one Mitac, a T400 and finally my 20 days old Lenovo X201. I even posted a thread just now sharing my experience and also asking for this very same question: how to improve power management on my specific hardware. On all laptops I had, Linux in general performed worse than Windows, be it XP or 7 (the two I ever had on laptops anyway).

So the point is not really to use cooling pads, but instead how to tune the system to use less heat, and improve the notebook longevity even when using a pad is not possible.

Best regards!

### Post by sdennie on 2010-05-30
Does the laptop have an nvidia graphics card?  If so, you may need to install the proprietary drivers to get the machine to cool down. By default, Ubuntu doesn't enable many possible power savings features and using less power means generating less heat.

### Post by cabinetman on 2010-05-30
I added the system monitor to my panel. It shows you the percentage of CPU that you are using and when you are using 100% is when you laptop heats up. It will also show you what process is using it. By keeping you eye on this it will help  you to control the tempature. On my laptop the fan starts running faster when it gets hot. One of the times it was trying to find a network printer that was not available. When I stopped cups the usage went down. Also programs like farmville will use a lot of CPU. Sometimes you may have more than one tab opened in your browser. Hope this helps. You can add it by right clicking on your panel bar and selecting it and adding it.
