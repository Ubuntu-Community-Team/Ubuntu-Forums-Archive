### Post by QIII on 2016-11-15
While it is possible you may get some files off the drive, the unfortunate fact is that mechanical drives are very sensitive to shock.  The chances that the drive is mechanically/physically damaged are high.

Were I you, I would work first on getting the easy stuff recovered.  Just remember that if the drive is physically damaged (it is, no doubt), then the more you use it the more damaged it will become.  You may find that although it appears as though you have 1/3 of the data recoverable, you will end up with substantially less as the drive becomes more damaged.

Think of a bent fender rubbing on your tire.  It may not seem as though there is much damage to your tire, but you may find you've had a blow out in just a few more miles of driving.

I genuinely do hope you can get much of your data recovered, but mechanical drives are not meant to be dropped from any height.
