### Post by pieman711 on 2010-02-21
I know that this isn't strictly a ubuntu problem but my 8800GT video card has stopped working! It was fine before, although I remember I had some difficulty getting it to work when I first got it over two years ago, and I've even had dual monitors running quite nicely with ubuntu.
Last night my friend's computer had stopped working and she thought it may have been the PSU. I offered to let her try mine to see if that helped diagnose the problem (it didn't) and now that I've put the PSU back in mine I no longer get any video output on either of my screens. It sounds like it is booting up fine, although the gpu fan stays spinning at full speed. Other than the lack of display on my monitor it all sounds fine.
I'm sure I plugged everything back in the right place and I've re done it just to make sure nothing was loose. It can't be a compatibility or faulty card because it was already working and I doubt I damaged the card because I didn't need to touch it to remove the PSU, other then teh plastic of the power cable that goes directly into teh card.

Any suggestions?

### Post by pieman711 on 2010-02-28
I fixed it! While removing the PSU i must have bumped the RAM I think. After reseating it it booted fine!

Alot of people have been having a problem similar to mine with this card when they first installed it, and I think alot of people  assumed it was DOA.
When I first got the card it wouldn't work with my mobo, I assumed it was DOA too but a bit of searching suggested that maybe they weren't fully compatible (despite it being a pci-e slot in my MOBO) There was a guide suggesting back flashing the 8800gt to make it more compatible with the mobo. Unfortunatly to do this you need a PC that does work with the card and all of my friends have laptops. In the end I just bought a new motherboard and it all worked (make sure your PSU is powerful enough too!)

So if you've bought a new 8800gt and you have the symptoms I mentioned in the first post then the card may be working fine but not compatible, it would be worth trying the card with a different motherboard before giving up on it!
