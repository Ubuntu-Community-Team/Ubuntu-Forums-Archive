### Post by chombee on 2014-03-14
Hi, can someone who owns one let me know how well the Apple Magic Trackpad works on Ubuntu 13.10?

I have googled it but reports vary and are mostly talking about much older Ubuntu versions. But from what I've seen, my guess is that it'll work more or less out of the box and do what a typical laptop touchpad will do on Ubuntu: point, left- and right-click, and two-finger scrolling, but more advanced multi-touch gestures may require some setup.

Does it work out of the box without any messing around installing drivers? Does basic pointing and clicking work (left and right mouse buttons)? Two-finger scrolling? More advanced multi-touch gestures like those that OS X supports?

More advanced gestures are a bonus for me, I'd be happy with pointing, left- and right-clicking, and two-finger scrolling.

I'm considering getting one as an alternative pointing device because my mouse has been giving me RSI. I'd welcome any suggestions of other alternative pointing devices I could try.

I originally looked at the Wacom Bamboo Pad, but it looks like that doesn't work on Linux yet. Other Wacom tablets are more expensive, aimed at artists not for simple desktop users, and at least the most recent ones are hit and miss on Linux compatibility from the looks of it. So I think the Magic Trackpad might be the closest thing to a Bamboo Pad that works on Linux.

I've also tried a couple of vertical mice, a DXT Precision and a 3M Ergonomic. The DXT is horrible, sets off my RSI much worse than a normal mouse. The 3M is better then a normal mouse but not great, and only works right-handed (swapping hands is very useful when you're getting RSI symptoms).
