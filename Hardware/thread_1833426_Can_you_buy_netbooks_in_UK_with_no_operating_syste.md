### Post by coffeecat on 2011-08-27
There's a couple with Ubuntu pre-installed at Linux Emporium:

[http://www.linuxemporium.co.uk/hardware/hardware-laptops.html](http://www.linuxemporium.co.uk/hardware/hardware-laptops.html)

(Except that the cheaper one is out of stock atm.)

I haven't searched for netbooks specifically, but have a look on Naked Computers:

[http://nakedcomputers.org/](http://nakedcomputers.org/)

And/or on LXer:

[http://lxer.com/module/db/index.php?dbn=14](http://lxer.com/module/db/index.php?dbn=14)

That all being said, the irony is that a Windows pre-installed machine is often cheaper than a non-OS one or Linux pre-installed one. This is because the large manufacturers can negotiate a very low price from Microsoft for each licence (a fraction of what a retail copy of Windows would cost you or me) and that the income from the pre-installed bloatware more than covers the cost of the Windows licence.

My advice - unless you object in principle to a few more £££s going to Microsoft. Find a netbook you like at a good price and simply remove or don't use Windows.
