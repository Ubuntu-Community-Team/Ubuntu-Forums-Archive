### Post by The Real Dave on 2009-05-23
Two reasons that I can see;
1) Using Internet Explorer. Im presuming that when you say that you "open it" in Ubuntu, that your talking about Firefox browser, as opposed to Internet Explorer. Its a long known thing that, though Firefox is one of (if not the) fastest browser in operation. However, it takes much longer to start than other browsers ](*,)

2) The XP machine you used was a public machine, designed to contain only the pieces of software necessary, so as to prolong the life of the machine. Computers set up like this, tpically clear clogging files at log-off or shutdown, such as temporary files, browser cache's etc. This removes alot of the clutter from the pc, allowing it to run quicker. Also, its possible that the pc is a slave pc, connected to a central server. In this instance, its possible that much of the processing of the pc is being handled by the server, again speeding up the pc.
