### Post by TheFu on 2017-01-04
I've had reasonable luck with dell laptops.
Bad luck with Acer, Toshiba, SONY, HP, Compaq, and Asus - all laptops.

Last "desktop" I bought was a Compaq which never worked correctly running the OS it came with. It would lock up after a few hours of being booted, even if I never touched it.  Support was a joke. They had fresh firmware and updates weekly. Basically, they were trying to convince me NOT to return the machine before the return period ran out. Life got in the way and that $700s was wasted. It was never stable.

A client gave me a Dell workstation (it was a CAD system with fast CPU, professional GPU, 10,000k spinning disks).  It was big, noisy, and at the time, required Dell replacement parts because they'd elected to use non-standard stuff internally. I would never have purchased it.

Since that time, I've been building my own desktops. Usually spending about $300-$400 and reusing old components. 45 min of work for a solid, stable system.  Have 4 of them running here now and I couldn't be happier.  No funny components. I got to pick parts that are compatible with Linux, no hassles. Got to avoid a few known things that cause issues, like realtek stuff.  I start with a CPU and work backwards to MB, Case, PSU, and other stuff. Looking for price, brand, and versions that work with the HW I already have (usually RAM, NICs, GPUs) and linux.  On a desktop, I don't usually worry about much more.  pcpartpicker.com has pre-designed systems, if you want to follow someone else. The only real tricks is knowing when a CPU cooler is included or not and when thermal grease is needed.  Usually takes me 30-45 min to put a system together and 15 min to load an OS.  Of course, this assumes odd cases aren't used, which sometimes require dremel work. ;)

Get a Dell from Dell.com. Many of the Amazon ones were picked up when Dell had a great sale (often on badly engineered versions) and the seller is trying to make a quick $150. How much are the hassles worth to get a tiny bit cheaper price?  I wouldn't buy a full desktop again. Between youtube and a screwdriver - all will be fine.

I bought an ASUS from Newegg that I configured to dual-boot Windows and Kubuntu. Works fine though the BIOS takes forever to boot. I used [Rufus](https://www.ubuntu.com/download/desktop/create-a-usb-stick-on-windows) to create a UEFI boot device. I tried other options but Rufus was by far the easiest.

I'd avoid HP. Their machines are much too Windows-centric. I've had good luck with Dells.
