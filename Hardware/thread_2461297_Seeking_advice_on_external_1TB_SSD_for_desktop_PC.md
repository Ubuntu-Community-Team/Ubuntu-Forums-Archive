### Post by mastablasta on 2021-04-28
i just did this frew months ago. i got (single) disk enclosure on "sale" and i got internal samsung SSD drive (also on "sale"). works well and it cost me less than external SSD drive. as a bonus i can move it inside the laptop in the future. 

the enclosure was so cheap i got a second one for slightly used laptop HDD drive i got from my friend for free.

here is the combo i got:
Samsung SSD disk 860 EVO 500 GB - 72 EUR
Orico 2159U3 - USB 3.0 UASP v SATA - 9 EUR

both support linux well. if you have money there are better enclosures out there (they cost 20+ EUR) that take in more disks, different disk sizes or are even able to clone them.

my kid uses the disk for games, because internal drive on laptop is too small. 

USB port type will affect transfer speed.

edit:
> [COLOR=#000000]1. Whether the connection is ONE USB connector for both power and signal/data? No additional power supply is needed?[/COLOR]
one connection only (USB).

> [COLOR=#000000]2. The USB port of my desktop PCs are not of same standard, some old PC not with the latest USB port. Can it works on all my desktop PCs? Although the transfer speed of my old PCs may be slower?[/COLOR]
that's OK. USB3.0. is backwards compatible and will work on 2.0, just at slower speed. i have external HDD drives that are USB3.0 and use them on my 17 year old PC just fine. but the transfer speed is slower.

### Post by oldfred on 2021-04-28
I have converted two old SSDs to external.

One was my first SSD from about 2011, only 60GB. I just bought a USB to SATA cable and it worked but only really enough room for a test install.
But I tried using adapter with an old HDD and HDD would not spin up. It needed external power to have enough power to work.

My 2016 build had M.2, but NVMe was really expensive, so I got a M.2 SSD. Recently upgraded to larger NVMe M.2 and put M.2 SSD into external M.2 to USB3.1 adapter. That has worked so well, I may not buy anymore flash drives. I already have a large pile of flash drives I use for backup or repair tool installs. And remember when the smaller ones were expensive.

Have thought about the external SSD as they would have better shock or water resistance than the case I am using.
