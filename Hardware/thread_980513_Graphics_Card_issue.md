### Post by Mardoct909 on 2008-11-13
Keep in mind though: A heatsink and fan are NOT the same. Heat is generated on the CPU and other hardware because electricity running through it causes a great deal of friction, and this friction causes heat. Heatsinks dissipate this heat amongst a lot of aluminum and/or copper, then cools the metal down. This is FAR more effective than applying cold air directly to the hardware during operation, even if it helps.

That, and the when the BSoD lists hex codes (like 0x0000008E) it is a pointer. A pointer is a specific point in the RAM, and if I'm not mistaken the BSoD is telling you that there are problems with locations that pointers are listing.
