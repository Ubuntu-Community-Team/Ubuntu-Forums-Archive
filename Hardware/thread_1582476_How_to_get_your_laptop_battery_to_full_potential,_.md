### Post by PresenceofMind on 2010-09-26
Keep the brightness of the screen to its minimum. Lower the brightness, lower the chances of the screen sucking up the battery. This also applies to the more efficient LED screens as well as LCD screens.

Avoid turning things on and off frequently. This applies to things like the Wireless Lan, bluetooth and, more importantly, the laptop. Use the Hibernate option when you want to switch the laptop off for a while.

Use the power save option (available on Windows 7 and Ubuntu) when working on the laptop. It may be less impressive on graphics, but it will save you some extra minutes.

The wireless device can suck up battery too. Keep it switched off unless you want to browse the internet.
