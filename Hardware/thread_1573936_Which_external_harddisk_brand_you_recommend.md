### Post by TimEnid on 2010-09-13
I have a 1tb lacie, had it for about 2 years now. Havent had any issues with it. I also own 1 tb cavalry, a 2tb cavalry, a 1.5 tb Buffalo, a 1.5 HP MediaSmart and a 640gb usb 3.0 Buffalo Ministation. I was going to suggest the lacie to you, but you probably are not willing to try them out again. My Cavalrys are pretty good, had one of them for about a year and a half, the other, a few months. I use them both for storing movies (hooked up to my wd media player). The Buffalo DriveStation is a hd that does not have a power button. Its a 2.0 device. So when i turn my pc on, the hd boots up and mounts, same with the usb 3.0 buffalo, thats a portable. The HP is probably the slowest one. 

With that said, i would recommend a buffalo. if you have usb 3.0 slots, Lacie just release 2 new portables. might want to check them out (in case you change your mind.

### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2010-09-13
Disks go bad, that's life. Doesn't matter what brand or model because none are immune to failure. I'd suggest getting a Western Digital or Seagate internal disk and then purchasing a third party enclosure from a reputable company. For example these two.

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136284](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136284)
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817371008](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16817371008)

In buying them separately you have more control over it. For example if the disk dies you can use the enclosure again, or if the enclosure dies you can just get another enclosure instead of mailing it in. Or both the disk and enclosure can be used separately like if you get a new computer later on the disk can be used internally.
