### Post by jimv on 2009-06-24
I bought half a dozen old Dell Optiplexes (the thin cases) that sit in my geothermally heated basement.  I run various servers and Folding@Home on them, and they haven't had any issues.  After shipping, they were a little under $80 each.

You have to remember, a processor that idles at 40C is already 104F, which is about 30F hotter than most people feel comfortable with.  A moderately warm room isn't going to harm a computer, especially one that won't be doing much that is processor intensive.  Just make sure that it gets dusted out every now and then.

### Post by learningcurb on 2009-06-24
You may want to try an Atom based nettop. If you're up for assembling a barebones system, the MSI Wind's nettop may do the trick.  I've heard that it only uses about 30-35 watts even under heavy load:  [http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856167037](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16856167037)  

If you want to build something from scratch, try [http://www.mini-box.com/Atom](http://www.mini-box.com/Atom).  Newegg also has these parts.

The desktop Atoms come in two flavors: Atom 330 and Atom 230.  The Atom 230 is slightly cheaper and may work just as well for a proxy.  There's also the AMD Geode and VIA C7 processors, though I don't know as much about them.  You can also just get a fully assembled system like the desktop EEE PC or Ion based nettops if you want to spend a bit more money.
