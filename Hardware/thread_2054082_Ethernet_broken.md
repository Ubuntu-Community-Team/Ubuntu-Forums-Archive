### Post by Magzimum on 2012-09-06
My computer goes offline after several minutes. When I boot it is online and internet runs smoothly. But after a while (can be anything between 1 and 30 minutes) webpages stop loading, and I am disconnected altogether. 

Sometimes, it comes back on after just a minute. Sometimes I have to wait longer. But eventually it always comes back online (so far). 

This problem occurred some time last week, after running Ubuntu 10.4 smoothly for 2 years... It happened somewhere in the middle of a Youtube movie. I made no changes on anything at that moment.  

It's a wired connection. I checked my DSL modem. My other laptop is online all the time. 
I checked the cable (using that other laptop), and the cable is fine because the other computer went wired online. Also, my problem just happened again with another cable. I have to conclude that internet, modem and cable are all fine. 

I thought that it might be some Ubuntu issue, since my symptoms did seem to resemble some other issues I found on this forum. But since I am not really an expert, I thought that it might be easier to just do a fresh install of a newer version (12.4 LTS) rather than tinker with my old 10.4 Ubuntu. So I did a clean install of Ubuntu 12.4. And although my Ubuntu has increased in awesomeness, the problem surprisingly stays. 

When I try to reach my router, when I am offline, I cannot reach it. I know I have the right IP, because I do reach it from the other laptop, and it responds. 

So, does anyone have a clue what could be the problem? Right now, I fear it might be my hardware that is breaking down. If so, is there any way to check that, before running to the store to buy new toys? Can my hardware (ethernet) fail in such a way – a slow and horrible death? (Since I am pretty certain it's not Ubuntu – how could it be - maybe I should be asking this on a different place on the interwebs, but I generally think this forum is awesome). 

Thanks in advance.

### Post by Incarnadine on 2012-09-06
It indeed does sound like a broken Ethernet port on your laptop. If you had a desktop I would suggest to buy a cheap PCI ethernet card, but since it's a laptop, you can only hope that the Ethernet jack is not part of the motherboard and possibly buy a new one from Amazon.
