### Post by PhilGil on 2015-03-14
> **lampface said:**
> thanks for the help mate! however just one more question... how am i to install Ubuntu? does this come with USB ports? DVD? etc. or shall i need to buy them seperatly?
This particular kit does not come with a CD/DVD drive (many people don't find them necessary any more). If you need one you can purchase an external or internal one - they go on sale regularly for $15-$25.

This particular kit comes with 2xUSB 2.0 ports in front and 4xUSB 2.0 ports in back. Ubuntu can be installed from a USB drive: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStick) .

I would strongly encourage you to look at the other options available in your price range before making a decision. For example, the motherboard on this kit has a PS2 mouse and keyboard port. Unless you have a very old mouse and keyboard those ports are unnecessary and you would be better off having two more USB ports. You might also want a USB 3.0 port. Additionally, since you will be using the onboard graphics, be sure the motherboard you choose is compatible with your monitor (VGA, DVI or HDMI).

### Post by mattharris4 on 2015-03-14
> **MartyBuntu said:**
> I'd suggest buying a used system from a local seller.

You're not going to build a $200 computer of any quality.

A local seller may be dicey, unfortunately.  Some are very good, others not so.  At least in my area refurbs from Tiger Direct or Newegg are cheaper and some more fully featured anyway.  You can buy a 3-4 year old computer that has (supposedly) been tested and worn parts replaced from either retailer starting at less than $100 shipped (although I recommend spending more to get more in this situation).  For $140 or so shipped you should have a system with a DVD-RW drive (not DVD or DVD-ROM -- be sure to check this), 2 GB RAM (more than enough to run Lubuntu, Kubuntu or Xubuntu although Ubuntu and Edubuntu would be dicey on a 2GB RAM computer nowadays), a 160GB or higher drive (check this as well -- some are only 80GB, too small for a dual-boot with Windows 7 which most come with) and a one year warranty (check this as well, some refurbishers only warranty for three months -- too short IMO).  The $140-$175 shipped range ($100-$140 listed price w/o shipping included) is the sweet spot here for value.  These systems should also have a traditional BIOS instead of that dreaded UEFI (especially if it is an HP originally with Win 8 or 8.1 from what I read) so a dual-boot system should work on the first try.

I bought mine from Tiger Direct, it was refurbed by Joy Systems four years ago (it was about $220 shipped but refurbs and new computers both cost more then), it is about equal to the current computers in the range I recommend, the first one had a defective motherboard that caused it to unexpectedly and immediately shut off but once we figured out that the mobo was defective after a couple attempts at repair they sent a replacement and I have been using it ever since without major issues -- Joy Systems was actually pretty nice about it and easy to deal with.  Once XP turned into a pumpkin I put Edubuntu on it, eventually increased the RAM to 3.5GB and continue using it to this day.
