We all want exactly what you are looking for.  However, the most important part is having a boot-loaded that can be unlocked.  Without that, you will be stuck using a cluge solution like running ARM/Ubuntu inside a chroot environment under Android, then connecting to that OS with a VNC client.  I've run this and it was cool for about 5 minutes until I found that the keyboard and mousepad drivers sucked making it completely unusable as a PC.  

I travel with an Atom-based netbook still, if that is any indication of where these installs are.

In short, don't hold your breath. Canonical seems to have learned from Microsoft about pre-announcing lab experiments as products that we will never see unless a big company decides to pay.  Not a bad business decision, but terrible if you make real-money decisions based on it.

I hope I'm completely wrong, I really do, but I've been in the IT industry over 20 yrs. The names change, but the tactics do not.
