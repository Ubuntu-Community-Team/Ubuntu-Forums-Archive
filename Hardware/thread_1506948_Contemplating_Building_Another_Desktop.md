### Post by nirvana21 on 2010-06-10
The only components I have seen Ubuntu have trouble with are NICs, graphics cards, web cams, and few other small items. Just research to confirm that other people have had success with the same hardware you choose. Specifically check the motherboard (network interfaces). Nvidia seems to have better driver support, but more recent ATI seems better supported then it used to be. Don't expect crossfire or SLI to work, so stick with one card. Again, the best way is to just check what hardware other people are using. If you plan on using RAID with that motherboard, check compatibility of the south bridge, AMD SB850.

Don't worry too much about processor, memory, hard drives, optical drives, and power supplies for typical usage. If you are going to use a solid state drive, definitely do some research on TRIM support. I am not sure if Blu-ray is supported either.

That is all that comes to mind, hope it helps.
