### Post by mtbsoft on 2009-02-17
Adding a cooling pad is just treating the symptoms, you should first try to work out why the lappy is running so hot in the first place.  Overclocking a lappy is generally considered a bad idea due to the limited cooling capabilities.  Does it have enough airflow around it?  Ambient air temp. too high?  Fans working properly?  Are the running processes maxing the CPU out all the time?

Powered fan bases will increase the cooling air flow to some degree though require power from the lappy, but are not addressing what is overheating the thing in the first place.  The passive thermal mats don't require power but are only really suitable for units on laps on the train or similar.

Cure the heating problem and you may not need a mat at all.  If it's a desk based unit, stand it on a few lego bricks and turn a desk fan on it for now!

### Post by dzark on 2009-02-17
For what it's worth, my Laptops CPUs & GPU all slow down when not needed. 

I run the Nvidia PowerMizer, which drops my GPU from 450Mhz to 100Mhz, and cpufreqd & cpufrequtils packages keep me at 1Ghz when idle, 1.8 when needed.

A quarter of GPU clock, and almost half of CPU clock makes a big big difference. Very noticable when I'm only browsing the web, or listening to music. Increases my battery life quite a bit too!
