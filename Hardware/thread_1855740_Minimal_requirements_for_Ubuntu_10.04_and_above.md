### Post by mikewhatever on 2011-10-07
> **wolfen69 said:**
> Consider buying a laptop from System76 or Zareason. They come with Ubuntu preinstalled and are guaranteed to run perfectly.

Second that.
However, if Zareason and System76 are not an option (perhaps you need Windows as well), check out the list of Ubuntu certified hardware.
[http://www.ubuntu.com/certification](http://www.ubuntu.com/certification)

Any modern and compatible laptop or netbook should run Ubuntu reasonably well. Most of them are Intel based, and you may find that 10.04 lacks support for the recent Intel's chips, such as Sandy Bridge. Test before installing.
There is no need to buy an external hard drive for installing Ubuntu. A flash drive, 2GB or larger, should do.
