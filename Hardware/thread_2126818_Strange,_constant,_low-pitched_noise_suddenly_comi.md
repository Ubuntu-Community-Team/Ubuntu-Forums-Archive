### Post by veroslav on 2013-03-18
Hi,

I am running Ubuntu 12.04 (64-bit) on a 2-year old HP desktop. It used to work very very silently until around 2 months ago, when I started hearing a strange, low noise coming from it. It is hard to describe it, it mostly reminds me of a power drill drilling into the floor (but MUCH quiter, it is barely noticable for untrained ears :)).

It starts as soon as I boot the computer and is constant all the time (doesn't change the pitch when computer is under heavy load for instance) until I shut down the computer. It sounds louder when listening from the above the desktop, from the sides it is almost unnoticable.

My first thought was that it might be a failing hard drive, so I checked the S.M.A.R.T. status and it was ok (no bad blocks, everything green).

Next up, I opened the casing in order to clean the dust from the fans. There was a bit of a dust on one of the fans but cleaning it didn't help. Unfortunately I couldn't reach the GPU's fan to clean it, as it was covered with tick wires. I can see that there is some dust in there though.

While the casing was removed, I booted the desktop and tried to locate the source of the noise. It appears that it is coming from the part where the hard drive and dvd-rom are located, nothing else there. My main suspicion is thus still the hard drive.

Could anyone give me any suggestions what I could do next? The computer is still operating without problems.

Thanks in advance.

Kind Regards,
Veroslav

### Post by coldraven on 2013-03-18
Press with your finger, first on the hard drive and then the chassis where it is mounted. Does the noise stop or change?
Maybe just the screws need tightening. 

Or with the power off unplug the power cable to the hard drive then switch it on and check for noise.
If there is no noise it must be the drive. Make a backup!
