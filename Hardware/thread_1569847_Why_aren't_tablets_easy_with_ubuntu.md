### Post by Fafler on 2010-09-07
You should look into the JooJoo. Theres some dispute over who actually owns the rights to the design, but it ships, cost $500 and most important, there's a (albeit small) Linux community around it. It, by the way, holds a Atom with ION chipset, which means decent performance, even in 3D, and there's 3rd. party upgrades to increase storage etc. [www.thejoojoo.com](www.thejoojoo.com)

The reason tablets are bad supported is that there aren't that many in the Linux community. Most improvements and bugfixes are made because people spend time developing them, and finding and reporting bugs. Not much commercial interest in running Ubuntu on tablets compared to Android, but Ubuntu would IMHO give a much better tablet experience.
