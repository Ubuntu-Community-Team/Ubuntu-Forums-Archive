### Post by phatqao on 2011-08-15
I'm running Ubuntu 11.04 on a mid-2007 Macbook. When plugged into AC power, the software battery indicator shows that the battery is charging. If the power cord is removed, the battery indicator immediately shows 0% and a warning appears saying that the computer is about to hibernate. I turned off automatic hibernation, but it still goes to sleep whenever the power cable is removed, often causing a tricky series of states that results in a hard reboot.

When the laptop is booted with no power connected, the battery indicator still reads 0% regardless of the charge shown on the battery itself, and there is usually a kernel panic within ten minutes of using the laptop with no power plugged in. Regardless of the battery status and AC power status at boot, removing the cord always causes an automatic sleep state. 

I should note that the lights on the battery itself indicate that the battery is charged when this happens. Essentially, the only way I can use my laptop is with the power cord plugged in at all times without removing it, shutting down every time I'd like to change locations.

My question is: how do I get Ubuntu to recognize my Macbook battery? Thanks.

### Post by phatqao on 2011-08-15
