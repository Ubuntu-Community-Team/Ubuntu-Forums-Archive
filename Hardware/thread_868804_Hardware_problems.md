### Post by evets25 on 2008-07-24
There's always some basic troubleshooting stuff that you could try like these:

- take out the new graphics card and see if it works
- try a different graphics card
- double check all connections and cables, make sure everything is in the right slot and secure (including graphics, ram, cpu, HDD, etc.)
- plug it into a different outlet
- Try a different power cord
- if you're comfortable with it, take the power supply out of another computer, try it in your, and then put it back in the original computer
- remove components one by one to see if you can get the power supply working with that graphics card by making the system draw less power. (to help nail down the problem)
- run memtest on your ram somehow (plug it in to another computer if necessary)
- try one stick of ram by itself, then the other, then both, then both in different slots. 
- visually examine the parts for heat damage - i.e. see if stuff smells "burnt", or looks slightly blackened or melted
- when all else fails, take out your frusteration on it by giving it a good kick. (I actually got a computer working once by doing this.) 

Hope something in there helps. Remember, some of these things will be not necessary once you nail down the problem; this is just troubleshooting to find out what the problem is.
