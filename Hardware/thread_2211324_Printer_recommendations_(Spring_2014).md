### Post by gifford on 2014-03-15
For compatibility, I have always used Brother printers with Ubuntu. Never have had any problem and they have excellent support for Linux.
As for a specific printer, I am now using the MFC-495CW all in one. Have had it for a number of years without problems and am not sure what model has replaced it. 
The main issue for me has been the Linux support from Brother and the robustness of their printers. As you can observe from the forum, there is a lot of frustration with printers and getting them to work. 
Good luck with your search and if possible, post what you decide on and why.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-03-16
Be sure the printer works on Linux, of course, and that usually means that a driver is available, either open source or from the vendor.

Odds are that connecting a printer via a USB cable will be the easiest approach.

If you do not really need color printing, look at inexpensive laser printers. They're comparable in price to inkjets and don't gobble ink cartridges.
