What problems would you expect to fix with this update?  If you don't know or don't have any real problems, then I wouldn't bother with the BIOS update myself.  It may not change a thing for you.  (Sometimes these updates do things like add support for newer CPUs etc.)

Even then, my preference is still to do a BIOS update with Windows.  Flashing the BIOS always entails a small risk; I have done many and have "bricked" only one motherboard with bad flash (though I knew I was taking a risk to fix a nagging problem - long story).  I have spare hard drives and Windows disks; I usually just do a quick install of Windows (don't even need a product key for a short trial period) then do the BIOS update, then go back to my original hard drive.  Doing a BIOS update in some non-standard way increases the risk and, as I said, may not help you anyway.
