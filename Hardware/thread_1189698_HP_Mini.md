### Post by inigomontoya on 2009-06-17
I have an HP mini 1000 series netbook.  I like it alot.  I'm currently running linux mint tweaked for netbookage.  I'd be happy to answer your questions.

1. I think some of them come with bluetooth and some do not.  I'm not sure if there are any other differences.  Best to double check with HP.

2. Yes you can easily install ubuntu over the distro it comes with using a USB key.  HP provides a downloadable image if you ever want to reinstall their distro.

3. Runs ubuntu and ubuntu based distros very well with only 1 gb ram.  I don't think it's ever used over 512mb. I'd stick with Hardy or Intrepid though.  There are a few problems with Jaunty, notably sound and video problems but those can be fixed if you are inclined to do so.

4. I have no experience with other netbooks so I can't really recommend any but this one. 

Hope this helps.

### Post by Jesse the k on 2009-06-21
My HP Mini 1000 was my introduction to Linux (Apple user since 1985). Like you I was attracted to the all-solid-state system (this netbook replaced my much beloved AlphaSmart DANA.) Luckily I had a sysadmin friend who installed the first distro, and I've been climbing the learning curve since then on my own.

I'm running 8.10 Xubuntu right now on 1GB RAM, and it's quite functional. OTOH, I'm not using the GIMP or even OO Spreadsheet or other processor-intensive tasks: web browing, writing and the occasional YouTube video is all. 

Drawbacks:

HP Mini has a nice keyboard, yes, but it also has a seriously shiny screen. I've never been able to use it outdoors due to reflections. Many times can't use it indoors on sunny days or with many overhead lights.

The screen resolution is **just** a little short vertically (not 600, but (depending on who I read) 576 or 590 pixels) which means that the control buttons on dialog buttons don't show on screen for many packages. Yes one can alt-click the windows to move their tops under the window title, but it's a constant annoyance. I found that Xfce in Xubuntu offered the easiest tools for reskinning the interface to work around this issue.

Battery life stinks. I'm usually not an early adopter, and I surely paid the price this time around: I got this box on February 13th and better alternatives abound *already*. Even with the SSD, I'm lucky to get 3 hours.

Finally, it takes forever to connect via WiFi. Perhaps Apple is just really fast, cause my MacBook Pro and iTouch take five seconds to log on to known nets and 10 for new ones. My HP Mini, on the other hand, takes up to 30 seconds on known nets and 60 seconds on new ones.

(I'm dl'ing 9.04 UNR right now to a USB stick, just to try it out.)
