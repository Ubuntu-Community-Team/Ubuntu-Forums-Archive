### Post by SeePU on 2009-07-15
> **mr_gourami said:**
> so all nvidia card will just work? 

i thought creative cards were a pain in the butt to work with linux.

No, Nvidia cards are just easier to work with than ATI cards, generally speaking.   Not always the case but usually.  To use the newest drivers, it can be a bit of a battle to get it configured but trust me, it's easier compared to headaches ATI card owners have.   The newer ATI cards usually require the proprietary drivers known as fglrx.

[http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Fglrx](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/Fglrx)

But, so many ATI owners have claimed to have major problems or too many restrictions or issues so I would rather avoid it (too time consuming for what I'd rather spend my time on) and just go with NVidia until ATI support in Linux improves drastically.  That's just me.  ATI fans might disagree.

I am used to Nvidia installs and installing the latest driver in Ubuntu/Kubuntu as well as other distros so I would recommend Nvidia cards since the troubles don't seem as serious.  There's enough 'how-to's' to guide you along but don't get too frustrated.   There can be roadblocks with Nvidia as well and unfortuntately, they mostly have binary drivers.

Just google for info and install instructions.  Nvidia's website for Linux support has the initial install steps.

Creative's older generation sound cards have good Linux support.   Look for a Creative Sound Blaster Audigy 2ZS card.   I have one and it was just plug and play.  Way better sound than onboard sound from the mobo, too.
