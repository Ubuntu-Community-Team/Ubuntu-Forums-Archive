### Post by jasonsjunk on 2009-02-07
Buy an Nvidia graphics card or use Intel graphics.  ATI is so horribly supportted in Linux that is just isn't worth the hassle.  If you are just building a basic box then Intel onboard graphics are well supported and you won't have any issues.  

Most on-board sound chips are supported these days but if you run into a problem just buy a SoundBlaster Audigy (or better) card and your problems should go away.  

For video capture cards I have had really good luck with Hauppage cards but more brands these days have some linux support.  

There is a linux driver that works with Ubuntu that restores most funtionality of the Logitech G11 and G15 keyboards.  I have the volume control and many of the other special keys working which is nice to have.  

As for printers I have heard stay away from Cannon.  Epson and HP printers seem to have good Linux support.  My HP laser printer works flawlessly but I am in the market for a scanner so I will have to do my research before I buy.  

Also watch which wireless card you purchase.  Not all wireless cards work with Linux.  The reason I believe is similar to the Winmodem problem where some of the functionality was done in software not hardware and without the Windows driver you are SOL.  I have had good sucess with Linksys and AirLink cards but I have a Dlink that will go into the trash when I convert another computer to Linux sometime next month.
