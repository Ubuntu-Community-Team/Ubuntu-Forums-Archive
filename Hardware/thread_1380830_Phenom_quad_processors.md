### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2010-01-14
The real question you should ask is, "Does my motherboard support Phenom quad core?" Pay particular attention to the maximum power your mobo can deliver and to the TDP of the CPU you want. Hopefully, your motherboard can support 140W TDP Phenoms, which is probably what most Phenom quad cores are rated for.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-14
The Phenom qaud cores ( in particular the Phenom II X4s) multitask better than a X2. They are faster all round, and they also have extra cache- there is 512K cache per core, so you will be getting at least an extra 1MB cache over a dual core 

Extra cache helps, a lot. 

Most dualcores are only 2x512K, while the new Phenom IIs are cores x512, with an extra 6MB level3 cache. 

Just how much better a quadcore phenom is than any given dualcore depends on the dualcore model and the phenom model though.
