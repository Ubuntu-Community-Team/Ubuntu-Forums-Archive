### Post by motang on 2010-03-25
> **Minotauraus said:**
> In the meanwhile, I'm wondering if there are any more options out there? Anyone?

I had my HP for over 3 years and it has been pretty good with Ubuntu, with the exception of sleep when the lid is closed. Since it was aging a bit, I was in the market for a new notebook. My father was at our local BestBuy and saw an [Acer]("http://www.bestbuy.com/site/Acer+-+Aspire+Laptop+with+AMD+Athlon%26%23153%3B+Single-Core+Processor/9555769.p?id=1218127632130&skuId=9555769&st=acer") for a low price, and he told me that he would buy that one and I can use that and he would use my old HP (seemed like a good deal to me), the Acer isn't the more powerful and don't have the greatest stuff but it's comparable for my use and everything works out of the box.

*I will list the stuff that it has*:
It has a modest AMD Athlon64, with ATi HD 3200 graphics, 3 gigs of RAM, no webcam, but it does have a SD card slot, wifi, DVD burner, 2 USB ports, no bluetooth, and came with [s]rubbish[/s] Windows 7 Home Ultimate (it's ok but after being on Ubuntu for many years it's hard to use Windows). The battery life is okay 2 and half hours, and the hard drive capacity ain't too bad, I got is for $300 so I am expecting it be ground breaking.

**So what you have been asking for, what works?**
Compiz and wifi worked out of the box without a hick (on my HP I had hook it up to the network and download it as it was a broadcom based wifi chip), SD Card reader works out of the box, I even hooked up my notebook on to the projector for a presentation yesterday in class and it worked they it was suppose to (which my HP had a problem with), and the sleep works...yes I close the lid it goes to sleep and open it up bang it's up and running! :D All the special function keys work (out of the box) for instance skip song, brightness, etc. (they worked on my HP as well).
