### Post by |{urse on 2013-05-31
Whelp, it could be a few things, if you don't want to take it apart you really only have one option, take it to a pro. If you are in the Cincinnati, OH area, bring it to me and I'll diagnose it for free.

-Since you aren't even seeing the BIOS screen:

It could be a bad vga chipset (if so that means buying a new motherboard or having the chipset reballed or reflowed with a heat gun) it could be the backlight, it could be that the screen itself is bad, it could be the power inverter that supplies the ccfl bulb (if it uses a ccfl, if not, and it is LED then the entire screen is bad). It could also be that your power jack needs to be repaired (perhaps a bad connection, intermittently getting power), it could be bad ram that is intermittently allowing you to boot and other times not. One thing is for sure, though, it isn't ubuntu that caused the issue. Sounds like a trip to the shop is in your future. If you decide to try to repair this yourself, ask for help! I bet myself or someone else here will walk you through repair attempts.
