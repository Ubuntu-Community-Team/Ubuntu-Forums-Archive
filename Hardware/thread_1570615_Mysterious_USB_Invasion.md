### Post by BothEyesShut on 2010-09-08
Hello Linux Community,

I have an excellent mystery for you.

Windows was killing my friend's 5-year-old Dell Inspiron, so I suggested to him that we play around with the latest Ubuntu release without making changes to his hard drive, a suggestion he agreed to wholeheartedly.

We loaded Ubuntu onto my 2G USB stick, booted from it without installing, and Ubuntu recognized everything but his lappy's network card, something we likely could fix later if he so desired.  He loved Linux, but needed to get files from his Windows first; however, we were out of time for the day, so he closed the lid on his lappy, packed up, and went home.

Five minutes later, he calls me.  His computer is booting to Linux -- without my USB stick in the drive -- and he can't boot to Windows.

I worked on it for hours after that.  The BIOS could no longer find his Windows-bootable HD, and when I repair-installed his Windows in desperation, the Windows partitioner found both Windows and Linux, the Linux being labelled something like <USB Bootable Partition>.  Deleting this partition had no effect.  The compy still booted to Ubuntu.

I didn't know what to do.  Having tried everything I knew, and having no more time, I installed Windows atop his previous installation without formatting, and wonder of wonders, all was restored: his files, his desktop -- everything.

So, problem solved, but what the hell happened?  Anyone got any theories?

Cheers everyone,

-Both
