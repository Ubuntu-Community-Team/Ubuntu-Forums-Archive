Everyones got different eyes, so what works for me migth not work for you. 

That said, I found a 19'' CRT (cathode ray tube, aka 'old' monitor) was better for reading than a 17'' CRT. Its not that much bigger, but enough to help a little. I've tried  a 20'' CRT and it was just to big IMO. 

The bigger monitor are better for video watching and spreadsheets. The 16.9 widesceen models in particular (16.9 aspect ratio). Rather than go through all the resolutions, have a look here- 

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Vector_Video_Standards2.svg](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Vector_Video_Standards2.svg)

Most 16.9 monitors are 1920x1080. Which is, IMO, just to wide. 

For reading and writing, IMO, the best setup would be a 16.10, 1650x1050 or 1920x1200 monitor..in portrait mode. Not that many monitors that actually do portrait mode these days (you need a monitor that allows you to swivel the screen to 90 degrees to the standard landscape mode) but it really, really helps with reading and writing. Mainly because almost all documents are in portrait mode, so you dont have 'wasted' space on either side of the document. 

BTW, even 1280x1024 monitors work better for document use in portrait mode. It looks like the 17'' you linked to can rotoate the screen, but  cant be 100% sure. Samsung dont really talk about  portrait mode much anymore...its not even mentioned on the page for the samsung monitor I have now.
