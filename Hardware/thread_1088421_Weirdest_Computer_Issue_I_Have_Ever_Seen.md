### Post by Maheriano on 2009-03-06
I mentioned in another thread that my girlfriend's Compaq Presario V3000 died from a faulty motherboard but didn't really explain why so here's what happened and after what I found tonight, I have to post this.

A month or 2 ago it wouldn't boot up randomly one day. The lights came on but there was no sign of life....nothing on the screen, no fan noise, nothing. I took it completely apart and made sure everything was hooked up, put it back together and threw it on my bedroom floor until today.

Today I decided to turn it on and see exactly what it was doing and to my surprise, the hard drive light was flashing like something was happening and the CD ROM tray would eject no problem. From what I could tell, it was booting into Windows but there was nothing on the screen, this is good news. I put a really bright light to the screen but still couldn't see any activity so it's not the backlight and I had it plugged in so it's not the battery.
Then someone online said they had the problem and managed to get it to boot by rapidly pushing ESC after pushing the power button. This is the stupidest and lamest troubleshooting idea I have ever heard but I gave it a try and IT WORKED! For some unknown reason, this machine booted up. I even rebooted it and it worked again. I shut it down and pressed the power button with no ESC key and it worked again. I was all ready to tell my girlfriend I spent hours fixing her computer until I realized the keyboard didn't work, I must have forgotten to hook it up when I took it apart the first time.
So I took the computer apart, hooked up the keyboard, and with everything hanging everywhere, I booted it up, tried the keyboard and everything worked great. I popped the pieces back in place, screwed everything back together, flipped it back over........NOTHING. I'm right back where I started with the machine apparently booting but nothing on the screen. WTF do I do now? Pushing ESC doesn't work anymore.
