### Post by garvinrick4 on 2009-11-03
All BIOS about the same.  Some times f2 or f10 just do what it says when you are
booting up. When in BIOS use arrows to go up and down enter to open the shaded 
selection. Put CD in first boot position then look at bottom of screen to see what to hit
to save selection and quit. 

You only have 2 or 3 seconds to press key to get into BIOS so keep an eye on screen. 

If you get confused just get out of there until someone you know can help you.

Just change boot from hard disk to CD first and leave rest alone.  Sometimes older machine will
also have a floppy boot selection also. Just ingnore.
