### Post by ronparent on 2009-06-08
You are getting into a murky area here that I may not fully understand. But generally in windows raid drives implemented with a mb chipset are  accessed with 'drivers' provided by the mb manufacture. If that is the case, and, if your raid mb chipset is supported, the purpose of the windows drivers is served by software called dmraid. Otherwise if you are using a dedicated raid card you would need drivers specifically for linux. I guess the question is what is your raid?
