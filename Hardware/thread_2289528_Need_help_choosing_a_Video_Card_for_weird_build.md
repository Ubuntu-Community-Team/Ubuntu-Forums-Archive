### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2015-08-04
I love AMD's open source drivers, but they do take time to mature for newer products, so I just can't recommend an AMD card for your intended use. A GTX 750 is an ideal video playback card. From all reviews I've read, the GTX 750 is very efficient/cool/quiet, and it doesn't even draw enough power to require an extra 6-pin power plug. As far as installing a recent Nvidia card on Ubuntu, this PPA makes it a lot easier: [https://launchpad.net/~mamarley/+archive/ubuntu/nvidia](https://launchpad.net/~mamarley/+archive/ubuntu/nvidia)

> I did get one recommendation for a [http://pcpartpicker.com/part/msi-video-card-n7402gd3v1](http://pcpartpicker.com/part/msi-video-card-n7402gd3v1) but I'm not liking what I'm reading. 
A GT740 is definitely not worth it at anything over $50. Nvidia is releasing a new GPU (GTX 950) later this month, so you can find some really good deals on the GTX 750 as retailers try to clear them out, For example, If you don't mind mail-in rebates, you can get a GTX 750 for about $70: [http://pcpartpicker.com/part/msi-video-card-n7502gd5oc](http://pcpartpicker.com/part/msi-video-card-n7502gd5oc) 
I'm not 100% positive it would fit your case, but it seems unlikely it would not fit any MicroATX case.
