### Post by Sidneyaks on 2009-02-01
Ok, so at first my problem was that if I plugged headphones into the jack my external speakers would still play from my laptop. I ran alsamixer once and it seams to have permanently fixed that, so if I plug in headphones it disables the external speakers and only puts sound through those.

For some reason though, this seams to have created a new problem. The volume knob *only* affects headphones. It's not hardware based, it acts like the volume knob on a multimedia keyboard (that is I can bind it to some other key mapping if i really want to) and it changes the external speakers when I'm running vista. Is there anyway to make it so it just adjusts the master volume channel instead of the headphone volume channel?

---

### Post by Jorge_C on 2009-02-01
Try opening System->Preferences->Sound
At the bottom part (Default mixer tracks), you can select which channels your volume knob affects.

Hope this helps!

---

### Post by Sidneyaks on 2009-02-01
Erm, sort of? I did that, and now, yes, my volume knob works for my main speakers, but whenever I plug in my headphones now, they put nothing out. I remove the headphones, nothing comes out of my external speakers. The only way to re-initiate volume again is by restarting the entire system.

Edit- Nevermind. I switched it to headphone control, increased the headphone volume (left it on 0) and then switched it to master. Works fine now.
