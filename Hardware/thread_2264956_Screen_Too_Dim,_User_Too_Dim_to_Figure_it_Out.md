### Post by Mike_Walsh on 2015-02-11
Hallo, graysunlight.

Well, if yours is an antique, then my 2002 Inspiron 1100 must be prehistoric..! However, you having a Dell should make this easy.

Dells normally have brightness controls on the keyboard itself, which are direct hardware controlled, NOT via the O/S. You should find, if you look around the keyboard, that there are a couple of buttons (probably marked either above the key, or on the key in a different colour.) These are accessed via a Function button (this will be marked 'Fn'); hold this down, at the same time as operating the brightness controls. They're normally marked on Dells with a picture of a sun, and either an 'up' or 'down' arrow.

Hope that helps.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-02-12
Most likely, your backlight inverter has worn out.  It's the circuitry that pumps up the voltage to run your fluorescent backlight.  Then the inverter goes, your display goes dim, and pink or orange in color.  The good news is that Dell is still in business, and parts are probably available--$100 US, or so.  The bad news is that it is not an easy repair.  Watch a youtube video of the procedure to see if you are up for the challenge.

It's also possible, although less likely, that the fluoresent bulb is worn out.  The inverter could be OK, but the bulb is past due.  That would require an entire backlight and/or screen replacement.  More money and time.

Plug in an external monitor and use that.
