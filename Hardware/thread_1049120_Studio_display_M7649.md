### Post by electrogeist on 2009-01-27
As you've probably noticed, that monitor has a funky ADC connector like this:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Display_Connector](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Display_Connector)

There are gender benders to convert ADC to DVI.  Maybe ADC to VGA too, if not you'll need a DVI to VGA in addition to ADC to DVI.  Make sure you get them going in the right direction 

...and the bad news: ADC gender benders are not usually cheap.

the good news is it is a Genuine Apple component and you could probably sell it and buy another used monitor!

<rant> Seriously ADC was a half baked proprietary idea of Apples that they later kicked to the curb.  They ran power for the monitor through the fscking video cable.  Not even enough power for the bigger monitors.  And the power went from the motherboard, thru the AGP slot... Sure they were unused pins...at the time...then it caused compatibility issues with say later AGP cards which *did* use those pins and sure didn't expect all that juice running thru them.  So you have people with exacto knives and tape blocking off those pins in order to upgrade their Mac's video cards.  And yes I have done that procedure LOL </rant>

**EDIT**:  I just remembered. I was wrong about the power pins - while power does come from the motherboard thru the AGP card, it is a seperate connector in front of the normal AGP slot... there is another issue with Apple's proprietary use of an AGP pin that did require the tape mod.
