### Post by prcoy on 2009-11-22
I reinstalled ubuntu 9.10. and it fixed it.. but wait there's more..

Copied my /home data back in and rebooted... the problem was back! So now I had to find which of the 12,000 files was causing the problem. I poked around and finally came across ~/myhome/.config/monitors.xml. I edited the line "1280" to "1680" and reran System>Preference>Display and there were about 20 choices instead of the default 4 for resolution. I chose "1680x1050" and everything works fine again.

 I have no idea how monitors.xml is tied to Display program as it only showed one resolution (1280x1024), but it worked. (this time :D )
