OK. nac, there are some things we need to be clear about first.

As far as I'm aware, there is no such animal as a computer peripheral which accepts multiple HDMI inputs.  
You will therefore need to purchase a dedicated i/o box to do this, then connect your computer, dvd, ps3 and whatever to this box.  
This box will come with it's own remote, and you store that and get a Logitech Harmony  (I use the 1100i bit expensive but brilliant)
With this touch-screen remote you'll be able to control all your stuff.
I use Mythtv, a pig to set up, but ultra-configurable, see the dedicated multimedia forums on this site.

Best of luck, Tony.

NAC-

I don't believe anyone yet has any inputs for HDMI.  Think about the kind of hardware it would require, it would need hi-res processing plus hi-def audio processing simultaneously, and the market for such a beast would be highly specialized and hardly for the average punter.
You may find some specially designed and produced studio cards, but I'll bet they'll only fit a very specialized machine.
There are many capture cards which have component video inputs, you may have to compromise and fit a couple of these.
I still reckon a dedicated HDMI box is the only way you will effectively garner the solution you seek.

BTW, I have been a home theatre installer for many years in Perth, Western Australia but too long in the tooth to clamber through ceilings now.  (I was a TV Engineer before we became a throw-away society.)
