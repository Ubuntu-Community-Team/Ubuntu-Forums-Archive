### Post by hotrod6657 on 2008-07-19
I don't know for sure but what I've always been told is that your MB just has to be the same or faster (ie larger FSB) than your CPU.  

Think about it like a road with a 300 MPH speed limit (your mother board's FSB) and a car that can only go 200 MPH (your CPU's FSB).

On the other hand, if your car (CPU) can go 400 MPH but the speed limit is still 300 (motherboard) you're not going to go any faster than 300 MPH because your computer is a good citizen and follows the rules to a T.  lol

I know this is probably a very basic way of looking at it, but i think i'm right.  Any one disagree?

Now, how this all effects overclocking i do not know.  I would imagine that since you can overclock by increasing the CPU's FSB that having a motherboard with a higher FSB than your CPU would again be a good thing.

If i am wrong about that please, someone let me know because i'm about to make a newegg purchase myself and i would like to make sure that my thinking is correct.
