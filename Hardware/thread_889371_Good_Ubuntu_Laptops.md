### Post by griz on 2008-08-14
I bought a Toshiba Satellite A200 installed with vista.  Had all sorts of problems with it.  I wanted a laptop without NVIDIA graphics because I'd always had trouble getting them to work properly in Linux.  Graphics would continually freeze, had all sorts of probs with system just stopping etc.  Dual booted with Hardy, only had dual boot for short period of time, then got rid of Vista all together.  I'm now using XUbuntu and love it.  The only thing that i can't use is my GPS device software.  Had some problems with my webcam, until i realised that i was suffering from a lack of light.  Everything worked out of the box, wireless connection is fantastic, sound works fine, I'm a lot happier now that I'm running Linux on this laptop.  So, in my opinion, the Toshiba Satellite A200 is fully compatible.  I suppose that there may be some people that have had problems with them, but as a medium-term user, but by no means an expert, I have to say that I had no problem setting this system up.  I've even got Samba working so I can print to a remote printer.


Griz.
