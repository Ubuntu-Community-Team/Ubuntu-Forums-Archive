Problem is, I've consistently had trouble getting decent battery time on my Toshiba laptop (Satellite A135-S4427). I specifically bought this machine because of its all-Intel chipset--I thought it would be well supported. As of right now, I'm running Intrepid (8.10), and when I booted up and unplugged, my battery icon told me I have 50min of battery time on a 100% charged battery. A year ago (running Gutsy) I got more than double that. When I right-click on the battery icon in the notifications area, the powersaving options for On Battery Power are already checked (dim display, reduce brightness, and put to sleep after so many minutes). What am I missing? Are there settings I just don't know about? Any help would be hugely appreciated. Thanks in advance!

### Post by iggykoopa on 2008-12-23
There are a lot of power saving options that aren't shown by default, I'm writing a gui to control them if you want to look into it here [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=988309](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=988309) . Also though if your getting half the battery life now compared to gutsy your battery may be dying, try running the battery diagnostic in your bios (if thats an option in yours).

edit: by dying I just mean losing it's ability to hold charge overtime, not that its going to blow up on you or suddenly fail.
