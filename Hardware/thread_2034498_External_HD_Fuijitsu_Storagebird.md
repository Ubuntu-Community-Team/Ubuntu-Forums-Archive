### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-07-28
Hard drives can and do fail all the time.  With an external drive, besides just the drive itself failing, the power supply can fail and so can the controller the drive is attached to.

If you can, try plugging the drive into another computer to see if it is recognized there.  If not, try booting a live Linux CD (maybe the one you originally used to install Ubuntu) and see if the drive is recognized there.  Your computer's BIOS may also "see" an external drive as a boot device if the drive is plugged in and working.  Can you do this?

Does the drive seem powered up?  Lights are on?  Makes the typical drive "hum" of being on?
