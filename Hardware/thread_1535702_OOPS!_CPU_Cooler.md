### Post by Rhubarb on 2010-07-21
It should be fine.
But I guess it really depends where the thermostat is.

If it's a reasonably new CPU, it should be on the CPU (which is good in your case - so don't worry about it).

If the thermostat is on the heatsink (most aren't, but a few are), then there is good reason for concern.
- As poor heat transfer from CPU to heatsink would bring about an ok temperature reading for the heatsink when the CPU could be burning hot.

I would recommend you put some thermal paste on sometime soon when you have time - certainly before you do any big CPU usage or any future very hot days.
You don't necessarily need the good thermal compound, you can just use silicon grease which is quite cheap.

To sum it up: you should be fine, don't worry about it.  Just fix it up in your own time just to be sure when you can.
