### Post by tentimes on 2012-12-19
Until you do your first laptop disassemble the idea of opening one up to fix something like this seems like a "no-no". But, having had to take apart a Dell a year or two ago, I know it's not as hard as it seems.

You might not be able to put the key back from outside and it might be much easier detaching the keyboard and getting at it from inside. Be careful though when removing the actual keyboard - you need to keep them level sometimes or the keys fall off.

I would have a look for a maintenance manual for your model and see what the keyboard assembly is like.

Failing that, hit it with a mallet ;)
