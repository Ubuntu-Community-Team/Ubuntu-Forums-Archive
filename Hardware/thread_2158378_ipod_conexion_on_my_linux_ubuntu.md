### Post by cwsnyder on 2013-06-29
If you have a Windows or Mac machine or virtual machine available and can load iTunes, that would be simplest.

Assuming you are still reading, it may be possible to do what you wish via the methods in [http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/transfer-music-media-ipod-ios-device-linux/](http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/transfer-music-media-ipod-ios-device-linux/) if your sister's iPod Touch has an iOS version 4.0.0 or older.  See Wikipedia article [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_iPod_managers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_iPod_managers) .  For a newer version of iOS, you may be waiting a while for an update to the libraries.  As of the latest articles I have read, you will be only able to read the files on the device, not write to the device if you have iOS 4.1 or later.

You may also want to read Canonical's article [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/PortableDevices/iPhone](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/PortableDevices/iPhone)
