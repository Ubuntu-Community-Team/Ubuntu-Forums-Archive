### Post by Mark Phelps on 2015-02-13
> It seems to me that Hard Drives eventually fail, but these SSD's have no moving parts and are more likely to last much longer. 

That was my presumption too, especially since SSD Life indicated that my SSDs has 10 years of useful life left.

Then, one of them started crashing the filesystem late last year. I migrated the install to a spare hard drive -- and while that was slower, in two weeks of usage, I did not encounter one filesystem problem.  So, I imaged off the current install and restored it to the SSD.  Within minutes of rebooting using the SSD, I was encountering problems indicating of filesystem corruption.  On the next reboot, the machine would not boot into the OS.  

I went into another OS on the desktop and ran utilities against the SSD -- and everything checked out OK.

I sent the SSD off for replacement (under warranty) and since I needed to use that OS in the meantime, I bought another one (same size, different brand) and never had one problem, the whole two weeks it was in use.

I posted these results on another forum, and folks guessed I had a "bad" SSD (whatever that is) but I had been using it for over 18months before any problems started, and when they did, within a week, the SSD was all but unusable.

This is the third SSD that has gone "bad" on me in the last two years (since I started using them), so the 10-year lifetime predictions from SMART utilities is worthless.

I now do backups every week, and have had to rely on those to restore working systems much more than I did when I was only using Hard Drives.

### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-02-13
SSD prices are falling rapidly, as Moore's Law has finally made manufacturing them en masse affordable.  Meanwhile, it seems the increasing need for more storage space on a local computer hard drive has leveled off.  500GB is plenty of space for most computer users.  In 2-3 years 500GB SSD drives will be $50 USD or less.  Only people who still need to store large data sets - videos, backups, etc. - will really have the need for large-capacity hard drives.
