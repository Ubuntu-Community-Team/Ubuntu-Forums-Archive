### Post by Mike BFD on 2013-02-14
Hi,
I have just finished examining the same case with my wife's colleague's laptop ;)
E3276 doesn't want to work; the workarounds known so far (picked from other forums) are "for geeks only" - I'm not a geek, though.
However, I've read a "healing potion" is being prepared and most likely will be included in some future software update.
For the time being, I solved the problem stupidly exchanging the stick with an older model. For example, E173 works just like a charm with Ubuntu. In fact, E3276 is just too new and all we need to use it is to wait for a couple of months, or even less.
So, in order to avoid grabbing through forums and command prompt lines you may not fully understand, try to buy/borrow/steal (the last option might be illegal in some states, watch the local law!) an older stick and put your sim-card there. Not perfect, but extremely reliable solution!
BR / Mike
