### Post by seth.rattan on 2008-10-05
I have emailed the following to HP's CEO using the this webpage: [http://www.hp.com/hpinfo/execteam/email/hurd/index.html](http://www.hp.com/hpinfo/execteam/email/hurd/index.html)

> OPEN LETTER TO HP

Sirs,

Several months ago I purchased an HP Pavilion tx2500z laptop.  I, along with millions of enterprise and home customers worldwide, am a Linux user.  I am not an open source or free software evangelist, I am simply a user who does not like bloat on his computer.  I called HP to check for known issues installing Linux on the tx2500z before my purchase of the laptop.  Like many Linux users, I am used to second rate hardware support from vendors and manufacturers.  However, I was optimistic when told by an HP representative that there were no known issues installing Linux on the tx2500z and I decided to purchase the laptop.

Upon booting Ubuntu 8.04 LTS (a Linux distribution) I discovered that ACPI support for the Linux kernel was broken, either by the BIOS installed on the tx2500z or by the motherboard (perhaps by both).  I called HP to inquire about this issue, only to be informed that the only support for Linux was paid.  After examining the comments from other Linux users who had also purchased the tx2500z in various forums, I decided to wait for a Linux kernel revision in the hopes that such a revision would fix ACPI support on my laptop.

Well, that revision has arrived.  I am typing this letter in OpenOffice.org running on the Ubuntu 8.10 beta, and ACPI works on my tx2500z with a newer version of the Linux kernel.  It appears that my confidence in decentralized software development (open source) was well placed.  My cynicism concerning central planning in the software industry (closed source development) seems equally well placed.

Given the hostile, dishonest, and dismissive attitude I encountered while dealing with HP customer support, I do not see myself purchasing any products from HP in the foreseeable future.  This is not a boycott, and I seek no concession, support, or apology from HP.  I am simply finished purchasing substandard hardware from a company which does not warn users that some of its products are incompatible with the Linux kernel.

I realize that HP, like any computer company, strives to achieve a streamlined and friendly user experience.  I do not berate HP for this fact, but such a user experience should not come at the expense of choice.  So, until HP is willing to engage in more transparent and accurate promotion of its products, I simply cannot risk another investment in HP hardware.

I have posted this letter on the Ubuntu forums, where users may comment on my letter, revise the letter as it applies to their situations, and sign the letter by posting on the thread.  I hope that in the future HP takes a more open stance in respect to the community and in respect to its users.

Sincerely,

Seth Rattan


Any other tx2500z users who want to send this letter can edit it as they deem necessary and send it using the link above.  I'd appreciate any feedback, criticism, or support from Ubuntu users out there.
