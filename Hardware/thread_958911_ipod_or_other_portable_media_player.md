### Post by mperry on 2008-10-26
I've had very good luck with my 80g video ipod and ubuntu. I turned off auto mounting and installed rockbox on the IPod since I prefer to just see the music without some messy ipod database inbetween. Now when I want to sync music from my nfs mp3 collection to the ipod, I just rsync it over to the directory on the Ipod with the music in it. You can find a graphical front-end to rsync like grsync which can help a bit too. I've used MTP based players like Creative's and I prefer just using something which makes the player a usb hard drive. I also don't have a lot of love for the apple ipod interface.

I don't know about the new classics but there were issues before with syncing them and rockbox does not support them. The video ipods just work for me and are easy to get working either with rockbox or not. I just prefer a basic sync tool that can be scriptable and does not require some strange database thing.
