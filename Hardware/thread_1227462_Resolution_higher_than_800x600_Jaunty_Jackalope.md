### Post by davidmohammed on 2009-07-31
X's log file (/var/log/Xorg.0.log) should tell you why other resolutions are being rejected. If you want more information in there, you can put

        Option          "ModeDebug"                     "True"

in the Device section of xorg.conf.

### Post by davidmohammed on 2009-08-01
Looks like Xorg isnt recognising your monitor - might be worth filing a xorg bug report

also try the following (or something similar )in your xorg.conf

```
Section "Monitor"
 Identifier "Configured Monitor"
 Option "DPMS"
 HorizSync 31.5 - 37.9
 VertRefresh 50 - 70

EndSection

Section "Screen"
 Identifier "Default Screen"
 Monitor "Configured Monitor"
    DefaultDepth 24
    SubSection "Display"
        Depth 24
        Virtual 1024 768
        Modes "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
    EndSubSection
EndSection
```

might be worth trying with or without the virtual option 

My only experience of nvidia stuff was back in hardy days - had to use the vendor hardware drivers - best to use envyng (from synaptic) (run from terminal envyng -t to install.  You can then use nvidia-settings to play with the resolutions you want.
