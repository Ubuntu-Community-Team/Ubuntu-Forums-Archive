### Post by weatherman2 on 2014-11-28
There are lots of components on the motherboard.  Any of them could fail and disable some but not all functionality of the motherboard.  I do recall one Dell laptop that had a bad IDE controller and was unable to boot any hard drive plugged into it but could boot from CD (and probably USB, never tried).  So it's certainly possible.

But your case sounds different.  How was the tech able to install Windows again and have it boot, at least for a while?  I suppose it's possible some components on the motherboard are marginally failing.  I've seen many that worked OK when cold but would begin to fail when warmed up - or just the opposite.

In any case, it doesn't sound like the tech fried your motherboard.  If it is doing the same thing now it was doing before, it was probably fried before (if that's really the problem).  Your best argument is to say they should have tested it more thoroughly before re-installing Windows and charging you for that.

As for installing a new motherboard: almost certainly you could find a replacement (used) on ebay.  A computer repair shop may be unwilling to install used hardware - or they may simply refuse to warranty it (you buy the motherboard; they it for per-hour fee; the day after you get it back, if it fails again, you can't get your money back from the repair shop).  But is it really worth the cost of a replacement motherboard, even a used one?  Laptop prices fall every year.  You can probably get a new laptop that's much faster than the old one for not much more money than the cost to repair an old laptop - maybe even less.

Some might use that description for a motherboard that can't boot a hard drive (a pretty basic function of a modern computer) even if everything else seems to work.  Others might use that description for a board that won't do ANYTHING (certainly not boot an OS, probably not even POST or allow BIOS Setup.

Then again, I am only going by your descriptions of your problem, not from actually seeing it myself.  As I said, motherboards can fail in all kinds of weird ways.
