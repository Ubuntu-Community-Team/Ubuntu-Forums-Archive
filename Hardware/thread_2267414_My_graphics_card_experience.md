### Post by michael-piziak on 2015-03-01
HP Compaq dc5800Small Form Factor Intel Core 2 Duo 2.8 GHZ x2, 4 megs ram, running Ubuntu 12.04 LTS

I installed the Nvidia Geforce GT 610 1 gig graphics card.

Installation went fairly smoothly and "Additional Drivers" found the drivers for me.

My main purpose for the card was to enhance my game console emulators, which I have 5 of.

The graphics card did enhance my system, making the workspace switcher quick and smooth, and I was able to download the game 0 A.D. and play it with ease whereas I couldn't play it before (too choppy).

However, I'm an emualtor gamer.

For some odd reason, my Snes9x emualtor, on occassion, would stall and drop too many FPS to play.  Same with bsnes.  I opened up System Monitor and noticed that now my system was maxing out on one of it's 2 chips when emulators were running.  Previously, no 100% maxing out occurred.   With PCSX it also was maxed out on one chip at 100%.

So I uninstalled the graphics card and am once again have a much better emulator experience.  I thought the advanced OpenGL in the graphics card would have enhanced my experience - but nada.

So I'm curious if a graphics card can actually tax ones system - especially an older system like mine, instead of helping it.
