### Post by lindsay7 on 2009-03-14
Tiger direct has good "bare bone" deals all time. Look for something that has 2 gigs of memory. Built in video card (nvidia is you can get it as this seems to work well with Ubuntu), A minimum of 350 to 400 watt power supply.  A lot of the bare bones systmems are layed out this way. You will problable need to get a cpu cooling fan as extra but sometimes the cpu will come with it and sometimes the cpu is included in the ket. You will need a hard dive, how big depends on what all you want to store on it but I would guess at least 250 gigs would be as small as you want to go. You will also need a dvd drive. They are cheap and you will want one that reads and writes. A inexpensive monitor will run $75 to $100, wired key board and mouse can be $10 to $15 each. So I would say at a minimum you have a shoping list somethin like this

mouse      $10
keyboad    $10
monitor    $100
dvd drive  $25
hard drive $60
cpu        $60       
kit        $150 (case, power supply, motherboard. ram)

Total      $450

Shop around, but this going to be a minimum system.

### Post by CorvisRex on 2009-03-14
Under $300?  maybe not, Depends really on what you are going to do with it.  I mean you CAN get a AMD 64x2 and a asus microatx mobo for like 120 at newegg...but...It severly restricts upgrading, flexability, etc.

One problem really is the case and the power supply.  They cost.  You can get cheap ones, but it is a trade off that is not recommended going too cheap.  Cheap case=poor air flow=overheating and cheap power supplies really can damage equipment.  One thing you do not want to skimp on too much is the power supply, cheap ones can over heat, burn out, deliver poor quality power, surges etc that damages the equipment.  

I usually think that the cheapest for a mid-tower would be about 500, and that is cutting some corners, but still at least relying on a descent power supply.  

The one I am building next week is only $850, and it will be pretty damn powerful.  

Best advice is to dig around NewEgg.com for a month or so, you can set up wish lists for multiple machines and save them, checking them now and again for the price.  Newegg also does free shipping on alot of their equipment, and with a case and power supply, free shipping can save you a bundle.

That's what I do. but Personally, anything less than 500-600, I would be very concerned about the quality of the parts, It does not save you money if you done gots to replace it in 2 months.

just my 2 cents.
