**EDIT - PLEASE DISREGARD**. Stupid rookie mistake on my part. As it turns out, i8k fan control did not start up immediately after installation. I should know better already, but I finally wised up and did a reboot, the module loaded and it's quiet as a mouse now. Sorry for crowding the forums with another post, but for future reference of those who have the non-dual graphics version of the 3521 i8kutils ***do*** indeed work! **/END EDIT**

Original post: Hello all. Decided to come back to Ubuntu after what has been almost four years to the day. Anyway, I installed 14.04.2 LTS on my Dell Inspiron 3521 laptop and the fan now runs at top speed, non- stop. It doesn't appear to be running any warmer than normal, the fan just goes all out as soon as I boot into Ubuntu. Idles just fine in Windows so I suspect it may be more to do with the OS/ACPI than firmware/BIOS. I've searched both Google and these forums and read *many* complaints but not found a single reliable fix yet and this seems to date back to at least 12.10 as far as I can tell. 

There are a few different variants of the 3521, mostly with Intel/AMD hybrid graphics. Mine is not one of those and only has the 3rd gen Intel HD graphics chip, 4gb RAM and an Dual Core Pentium 2127U processor. I have tried installing i8kmon/i8kctl, updating the BIOS and changing a few lines in GRUB as suggested on a couple of other pages with no effect. As mentioned, I've already found tons of threads here but no definitive solution. Hoping against hope that time has changed that and someone has a good answer by now. Thanks!!
