### Post by cascade9 on 2010-06-26
You wont have any major issues with those systems. About the only places you could run into trouble is with the intel core iX support, wireless networking, add-on RAID chips and rarely, sound chips. 

BTW, I wouldnt touch the ASUS M4N68T-M DDR3 myself. Its probably an OK board, but its a very, VERY old chipset (NVIDIA Geforce 7025/nForce 630a). The VIA VT1708S isnt as good as the realtek sound chips as well, IMO anyway. ;) Better off with a ATI chipset with no onboard video IMO (eg a ATI 870).
