 What GT/GTS/GTX means can change, but currently is like this- 
 
 GT- 'base' model, low power draw, low heat. 
 GTS- 'midrange' model (GTS450 only, GTS250 + GTS240 = 9800GT = 8800GT) 
 GTX- 'top end' model. 
 
 For cdesktop use, you wont notice any difference between them, and getting more than  aGT is a waste. For blender, I'm not 100% sure, but I dont think that blender will use your GPU to render, etc. If thats the case, then the onlky advantage of going for a more expensive 'gamers' card would be for video RAM.
