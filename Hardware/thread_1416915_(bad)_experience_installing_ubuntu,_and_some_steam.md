### Post by unhappyuser on 2010-02-26
Hi, I am completely new, and I must ask forgiveness before starting my rants... this is my first post here, and probably my last, because what I'm going to say is probably quite harsh. This is probably also my way of letting some steam out after TWO ******* DAYS WASTED.

I am a software developer, I've been arround since the days of the 8bit micros, and now I work in a company developing desktop aplications... so I consider myself fit for giving critics. I also admit I am mostly a windows user, but I really would be willing to see a real alternative to windows, and I've been willing to try linux for a long time.

The other day, a friend of a friend (zero computer knowledge) came to me for help because his windows was full of virus. Given that he was not willing to pay for a windows license nor an antivirus, I suggested him to try Ubuntu, now that it's starting to take momentum through non-expert end users, so I simply installed the Ubuntu/Gnome, vanilla from this site, no modifications.

The installation went well except for one thing: just after the installation (before user login prompt), the computer expended what it looked to me like 10 minutes, with a message from the monitor: "out of range"; I was about to reboot the machine when the desktop suddenly came it. I thought: "well, windows also has its moments when you install it".

After that it went like a charm, I liked very much the user interface and the placement of different elements, it was natural and easy to learn, so I thought it was a good choice.

Then I began updating and setting up the hardware, and the charm went to Afganistan.

I ran a "hardware update", it detected some nVidia drivers, it suggested one, which I chose and installed, it requested a reboot, and that's what I did.

after rebooting, and logging in, a blank screen with an "out of range" text, I waited, and nothing.

Okey, the installer warned me that these were propietary drivers, but come on, these are official nVidia Drivers!... anyway... its not Ubuntu's responsability...

until I chose to login using gnome safety mode... blank screen again

NO KIDDIN, a BLANK SCREEN IN SAFETY MODE

this is where all my alarms went on.

Come on guys, no fall back system? nothing to detect that something went wrong, that the user can't see anything and fall back to previous working mode?

But what happened next was what made me realize why the linux community is still not ready for the non expert end users (all of you can begin bashing me right now, I don't care)

I began searching for solutions, what I found was totally and absolutely horrible: lots and lots of forum replies, articles, and ways to solve the problem...

In the console, and as Root user.

What's worse, I've read many replies and ways to solve the problem, many of them totally different than the others.... like if there's not a clear, obvious path to solve these things.

Also, many of these solutions implied to use console commands that where not available in the system (it was newly installed), which made me realize that, most of the applications for linux are still developed to run as console apps, which is okey for administrators and developers, but definitely not okey for end users.

At the end, the solution was easy, I canned ubuntu and installed an old copy of WindowsXP. The guy will probably come back in 3 months with his machine full of virus, but its probably better than if he comes back in 3 months with a blank screen monitor related problem, and because I preffer to solve things clicking buttons than learning esoteric console commands.

Now, I would like to add some bits

User interfaces are not only for when things go well, they are also there for when things go *bad*, so, if something breaks by clicking buttons in a GUI, the end user should be able to solve the problem clicking buttons in another GUI; sending him to the console is NOT an option.

And what I found on internet when I searched for help was console, console, and more console commands.

I beleave Ubuntu is going in the good direction: Root is not as accessible in Ubuntu as in other linux flavours.... and developers should realize that this is for a reason: they should stop relying in that the end user has access to it, and find new ways to reach the end user and solve his problems (ie, using GUIs)

I would also suggest, in the beginners section, for people offering help, to forbid giving solutions that require using the console..... if there's a problem, and the only way to solve it is by console, them there's a GUI application missing there to solve that problem. And ultimately, will make people realize how many pieces are still missing.

I really would love to see linux pick up, and have a serious user base, because I really want to move out of windows.... but although its close, it's still not there.

Sorry.
