### Post by dude123dude on 2012-10-30
Hello!

I currently have HP c309g, which works FINE using opensource HP drivers. And I love it.

However, there is one particular thing, related to technology, that I dislike about my printer.


**Firstly**, it uses heat to transfer ink, ie "bubblejet" and thus the printing head can overheat.

**Secondly**, the color inks are dye based. This means, that any color image is not water resistant (unless on special silicon paper) AND is way less resistant against aging and UV.

Pigment inks do have own problems, like more clogging and less color variance, which are offset using cleaning fluid and six inks (instead of three).

Epson seems to be ideal device to match my needs, however I am aware that Epson drivers (epikowa) are proprietary. 

However, big part of "SANE" project and "Gutenprint" supports many Epson printers via opensource driver. 


So, I am exactly searching a good pigment/piezo All-in-one (printer,  scanner) that is fully supported by SANE and Gutenprint or similar  opensource drivers.

PS.
I also know that Epsons have "page counter", that stops printer after certain total page count. I am capable to replace the ink collection pads, so it would be nice if there is a known way for the printer you recommend to reset this (after parts are replaced).

For example,... RX700 is fully supported and matching. However its a bit old.. :/ Newer printers would be prefered..

Anyone? :)

### Post by dude123dude on 2012-12-12
The best all in ones seem to be SX series 2xx-6xx, with 5xx-6xx wielding best results.

Also, if one want to use best aftermarket ink, the only pigment ink now is from DCTec/Hongsam. Inktec, Inkmate, OCP etc are pigmented, not true pigment.

In the end, I purchased SX525WD and proud :)
