### Post by irv on 2012-08-15
Something happen this morning that I want other to have a heads up on.
My laptop started to slowdown this morning so I thought I would just do a restart. What I did is powered it off to clear all memory but when I went to restart it, it ran very slow in bootup on the BIOS. It came up with a message that my power supply didn't have enough to charge the battery. I actually have three power supplies so I just switch it out. But before I did I went into the BIOS and it said the power supply was not recognized (the one going bad). After plugging in the other one it came up in the BIOS as a 90w and booted up very fast.

To make sure the other power supply was going bad I plugged it into a different outlet, this time I got the BIOS to see it but it said it was a 65w. Now I believe it is a lower wattage but it has been working for many years. I think it was a loose wire in where the cord plugs into the power supply, but seeing I have three of them I will just use the good 90w power supply from now on.

The interesting point is my laptop boot up and runs much faster with the 90w. The point being if your power supply is to low or failing it will slow your computer down.
