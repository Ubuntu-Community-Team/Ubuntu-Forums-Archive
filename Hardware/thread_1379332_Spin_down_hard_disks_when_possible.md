### Post by nortexoid on 2011-04-15
The problem with spinning down is that the OS often wants to write to the disk even when running on battery off a "laptop" power profile, so it will spin down for a minute (sometimes less!--and sometimes more) and then spin right back up again. This is obviously extremely hard on the hdd. If you're reading one long pdf and doing nothing else, for example, it might save you some battery power to spin down--ASSUMING nothing causes it to spin up again (which is unlikely), but if you're surfing the web then your best bet is to keep it spinning.

The question is: how much battery life can one save by spinning down and how hard is it on the disk? Under normal use, almost no battery life is saved and the process of spinning up/down/up/... can be quite hard on the disk, decreasing its lifespan considerably.

I'd keep the option to spin down DISABLED.
