### Post by max-power2717 on 2010-05-11
Hi Forum,

I was just hoping to get people's opinions about a choice of RAM configuration for a desktop motherboard.

I am upgrading a friend's PC, and there's an opportunity to increase the RAM. The motherboard has two DDR RAM slots (no dual-DDR), and it seems to function fine with both 400MHz as well as 333MHz RAM modules. With the spare parts that I have available I can either install 1x256MB + 1x512MB 400MHz modules or I can install 2x512MB 333MHz modules.

Which option would you choose and why? 

My preference in this situation is to choose the faster option (768MB @ 400MHz,) but I have no real explanation why that's the better option - 'tis just my gut feeling. I'm very keen to hear other's opinions. 

Does the OS impact on the decision? For example, would a Linux OS prefer one configuration, while an MS OS prefers another? 

Any input would be greatly appreciated.

Thanks.

### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2010-05-11
Personally, I would take the extra 256MB if it's a Socket 754 AMD. The 333MHz sticks may have less bandwidth, but they probably have tighter timing, which is just as important for an Athlon/Sempron. If this is a Pentium/Celeron, I would choose the 400MHz RAM, since those CPU's are more bandwidth-limited (because of their long pipelines).
