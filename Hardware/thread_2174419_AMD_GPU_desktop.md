### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-09-14
It will depend on the exact integrated GPU.

I believe, though I may be wrong, that 13.04 is having problems with some of the higher numbered AMD GPUs, the 7xxx series and above in particular, so maybe you would do better and face fewer problems with an older but still supported nvidia GPU.

Steer clear of the hybrid graphic motherboards and cards at the moment as they are having big problems with the use of bumblebee, as I understand it.  Should you decide to go with an Intel system, I can tell you that the newer i5 and i7 CPUs with HD4000 graphics work superbly, but they are more costly that AMD, I accept.

### Post by acaciajnv on 2013-09-14
You won't face many issues with modern AMD/ATI graphic cards or their APU's integrated cards.

However, AMD/ATI drivers aren't that great (Still, they are much better now than before)... so going for NVidia is the best choice for discrete graphic card or Intel's IvyBridge/Haswell integrated cards (HD4000+, specially Haswell's IRIS PRO one).

In my opinion it all depends on wich software you are going to run, for media editing and demanding games go NVidia... but for casual gaming and general purpose, go for Intel's integrated card.

Good luck!
