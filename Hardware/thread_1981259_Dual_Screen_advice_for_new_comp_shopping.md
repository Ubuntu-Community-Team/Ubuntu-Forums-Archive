### Post by wyliecoyoteuk on 2012-05-16
I would advise nVidia, simply because they always seem to be easier to set up in Linux.
Some motherboards have DVI and VGA outputs, and often they can both be used at the same time, a DVI>VGA adaptor will work, but you don't need that.
I have this setup at the moment on one PC with a builtin Nvidia card.
If you have VGA and HDMI, you will need at least a HDMI>DVI adapter. (I also have this on an AMD motherboard using a Samsung syncmaster (DVI with a HDMI>DVI adapter)and an ACER (VGA).
Both are running 11.10, but I have tested it on 12.04, as I am trying to write a Multiseat article.
