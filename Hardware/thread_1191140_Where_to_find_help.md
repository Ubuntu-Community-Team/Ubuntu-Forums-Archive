According to two reviews (they're a year or two old though) at...

[http://www.ubuntuhcl.org/browse/product+hp-deskjet-f380?id=151](http://www.ubuntuhcl.org/browse/product+hp-deskjet-f380?id=151)

...it should work.  You probably do not need to get any extra drivers for the printer.  They might be already included in the Xubuntu installation.

Have you tried plugging in the USB cable, powering on the printer, wait a few moments and then checking the Printer Settings in your Xubuntu Desktop.

I don't know where the printer settings are (in Xubuntu), but there should be a place/menu where you can check for existing printers and add printers.

If you are not able to get the printer to work using the above ideas or anyone else's help, you may try downloading [HPLIP](http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/index.html) and follwing the [*HPLIP Automatic Installation Instructions*](http://hplipopensource.com/hplip-web/install/install/index.html).

Hope this helps.
