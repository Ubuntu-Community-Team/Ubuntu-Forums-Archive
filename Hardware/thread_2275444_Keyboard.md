### Post by efflandt on 2015-05-01
I have used the K400 with a Raspberry Pi (which uses a special Debian Linux for its ARM cpu). You can also associate a mouse with the Unifying receiver, for things like gaming that may be more difficult using a touchpad (although, I used Windows to associate the mouse with Unifying receiver, have not looked into a Linux way to do that). I have a Logitech Anywhere MX mouse that is supposed to work on any surface, although, I am currently using that on my desktop PC. The Anywhere MX mouse eats batteries, but I have 2 pair of rechargeables that need to be swapped after about a week of Steam gaming.
