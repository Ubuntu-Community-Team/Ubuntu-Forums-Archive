### Post by Autodave on 2023-05-11
I have no experience with an Alienware machine, but normally, there is a processor inside of the battery itself that determines the state of charge, charging rate, etc.  My first thought is that that processor has or is failing and the battery needs replaced.

I am assuming that the battery is an easy remove/replace.  If so, try one thing before you plunk down money for a new battery: remove the battery from the laptop.  Remove EVERY cable from laptop.  Let it sit like that for 10-15 minutes.  Reinstall battery and then plug in charger ONLY.  Power up and see if that helped.
