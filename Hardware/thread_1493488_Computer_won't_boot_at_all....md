### Post by cascade9 on 2010-05-26
What motherboard is giving you a '00' post error? AFAIK, the post codes can vary depending on the BIOS manufacturer. 

There is a chance your power supply is dead. There is also a chance that your motherboard is dead (and if you have had a previous power supply die when hooked up to that board, the chances of a dead motherboard are higher). 

> **Vanillalite said:**
> Still no fix here. It could maybe be the CMOS battery so I might try and pick one of those up at sometime today.

CMOS batteeeries only provide power to allow the BIOS to keep its settings. Even with a bad CMOS battery, your computer should still start, just all the settings will be gone.

### Post by picarune on 2010-05-31
It could be a LOT of things, so just buying a new PS might not  be the best way to go.  Probably a post code of 00 means never got to first base.  Generally they run through a sequence, 00, 01, 02, etc. as BIOS reaches each stage of bootstrapping. The fact that you aren't getting different beeps when you fire it up hints that it isn't memory or other attached device.  Might be worth investing in or borrowing a power supply tester, a good thing to have anyway.  The red indicator light is probably just telling you the main board is getting  3.3V+, and doesn't really mean the rest of the power supply isn't hosed. 

It does sound like either PS or MoBo.
