### Post by cascade9 on 2010-02-06
Just get a nVidia 8400GS, or maybe a 9400GT/GT210. Its a major move up from the Intel 865 onboard video, and still pretty cheap (those cards should be $50 US or under). 

*edit- depending on what model Optiplex GX270 you have, you might need to get a 1/2 height video card. That can make things a lot harder, there is less choice in the shorter cards. 

> **higashi said:**
> The other day, I decided to see how many Ghz my processor was just out of curiosity. I found out that I actually have 2.6ghz which shocked me because I always found that my computer was quite slow.

2.6Ghz can be fairly slow. CPU speed isnt just about Ghz, theres lots of other factors as well, cache being one of the most important. That is why a 2.6Ghz P4 feels faster than a 2.6Ghz celeron- the celeron only has 128K of cache, the P4 has 512K.
