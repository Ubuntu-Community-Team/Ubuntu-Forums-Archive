### Post by skiv71 on 2015-04-02
Hi,

Can anyone tell me if its possible to have Ubuntu mimic the type of operation I'm used to within the windows environment.

Specifically, whilst you can enable 2 finger scrolling in Ubuntu, the 2 fingers must contact the touchpad, side by side.

This require's a very awkward and not natural positioning of the wrist, your hand needs to be below the touchpad.'

The natural position for the right hand is to approach the touchpad from 4 o clock position (at a 45 degree angle).

So, when you use 2 fingers to contact the touchpad and initiate scrolling, the fingers, are in effect, almost on top of each other.

In windows, the alps drivers for my dell e7440 cope with this just fine, but in Ubuntu (or any other linux distro I've tried), this does not work.

You either get skittish cursor movement or nothing at all.

To get scrolling you have to adopt the hand/wrist position stated above.

Is there a fix for this?

Up to now, the only one i've found is to use Ubuntu as a VM, but I'd much rather use it natively.
