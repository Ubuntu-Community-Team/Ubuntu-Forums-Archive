Hi all,
I have been a desktop man for the last decade, only helping others with their laptops.
Recently I was given an Acer Aspire 3680 with a faulty battery and HDD and for just over AU$200 I had myself a reasonable laptop  with Celeron M processor, new high capacity battery, 160GB of HDD (up from about 60-80GB but the best I could do since it was IDE) and 2GB of RAM (up from 512MB).

Over Xmas I dual booted it with Win7 and Ub10.10 and it was to be a knock about test machine.

Why am I telling you all this? Well the battery came with a note saying it needed to be charged and flattened a few times to get calibrated. When I first charged it, Ubuntu said I had over 4hrs of battery life but as it ran down it died before it could give me a low battery warning. 

On subsequent recharges the total estimated battery life was reducing but it would still die before reaching a warning level. I decided to try charging and discharging it under windows and (damn it) it worked perfectly. I got a 10% warning, a 7% warning and then it hibernated at about 5%.

In case it was a fluke I tried it again and it worked again (damn it). I hoped that this just meant that it had finally calibrated itself so today I tried ubuntu again and I just swiped the touchpad a little while ago (and then typed in the password) and it shut off. Looking at the battery history it seems to have gotten down to about 12% (approx 30mins remaining) before it died but I don't know if I got a warning message (since it died as soon as I checked).

I will try again as soon as it is charged but has anyone had any experience with the battery meter not being quite accurate enough for reliable use? Any tips for me?
