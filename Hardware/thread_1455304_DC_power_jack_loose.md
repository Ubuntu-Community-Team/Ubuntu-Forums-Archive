### Post by tgalati4 on 2010-04-19
If the whole bottom half of the laptop flexes, then it's time to tighten all the screws.  That is something you can do yourself.  If it still flexes after that then it was probably dropped (and has internal support cracks), or is just cheap construction.

Some power jacks use a ribbon cable, so that they are loose by design.  Search the web for a disassembly sequence for your model and you can see for yourself.

### Post by plus2plus on 2010-04-26
If you take the laptop completely apart, which you will have to do, it is the wire inside the silver square protective cover where you would plug in the A/C power supply. If you take off the silver square cover and put some solder on the top of the wire and then on the opposite side where the wire comes through the other side of the board, it will work. Be sure to put enough on or you will have to disassemble it again and repeat. It took me about an hour and a half to take it completely apart, solder, and then reassemble.
