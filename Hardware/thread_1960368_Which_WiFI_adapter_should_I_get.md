### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-04-17
I've also had great luck with Intel wireless cards in my laptops.  Driver support has been built into the kernel.

I probably wouldn't buy a Lenovo laptop though (or an HP) because of the way they limit your ability to upgrade your wireless card.  If you find a nifty new wireless card next year you'd like to put in the mini-PCI slot, the Lenovo BIOS will say, "Sorry, that's not an approved card" and refuse to POST until you remove it.  Dell, Acer, Toshiba, etc. laptops do not have these same restrictions like HP and Lenovo do.

### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-04-17
I don't  know if ALL Lenovos can't be upgraded.  Actually you can still upgrade to an "approved" wireless card but it must be "whitelisted" in the BIOS of the Lenovos and HPs I've seen.  That is, if a certain model Lenovo laptop was sold with two different wireless cards (like this one mentioned by the OP), you could upgrade from one to the other but that's it.  Some other wireless card model used in a different Lenovo laptop still won't work - at least, in the cases I've seen.  I have tried a few of these types of upgrades in frustration.  By contrast, Dell, Acer, etc. never have issues with "whitelisting."

HP and Lenovo also have a different version of same model wireless card than the model used by other manufacturers.  For example, I have bought a few Intel 5100 cards to upgrade Dells, Acers, etc.  You can use a 5100 card from a Sony, Toshiba, etc. and they generally work fine in the other brands.  However, the "HP/Lenovo" version of the 5100 isn't supported fully in a Dell.  (Actually, the Linux kernel may be OK with it but it won't work in Windows at all!).  For sure you can't put a Dell 5100 card in a Lenovo - even if that Lenovo laptop was sold with an Intel 5100 wireless card, you have to get the HP/Lenovo version of that card to work in there!

I prefer simply to avoid HP and Lenovo altogether, because I like the freedom to upgrade things.
