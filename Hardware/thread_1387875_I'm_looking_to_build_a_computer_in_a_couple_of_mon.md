### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-24
I have no idea what computer prices in Zambia are like. But in most places, the cheapest i7 is about as much as the top of the line AMD X4 and a motherboard to suit. Also, the i7 comes in 2 different socket formats- LGA 1166 and LGA 1366. LGA 1166 is dual channel, lower performance. Thje LGA 1366 is triple channel RAM, higher performance. If your going to spend the money on an i7, get a LGA 1366 version..but watch out, not only is the RAM more expensive, but the motherboards have high prices as well.

### Post by cascade9 on 2010-01-24
Just the chip? LOL

Phenom II X4 965 Black Edition- quad core, 3.4Ghz, 4x512K Level 2 cache, 6MB Level 3 cache. Unlocked  from the factory for overclcocking/underclocking. 

Probably a good choice for a motherboard would be a ATI 790X chipset. 2 x PCI-E 16 slots (just in case you want to use crossfire, or run a high level raid card). The 790FX is more 'high end' but you pay a lot more for them. The 770 is cheaper, but only has 1x PCI-E 16 slot. If you never plan on running crossfire, or highend RAID, its a good choice. 

Video card? How much of a gamer are you? If you game a lot, maybe a nVidia GTX260 or GTX 280. If its more of a casual thing, maybe a GT220 or 9800GT. 

I'd also chuck in at least 4GB, and maybe 8GB. Depends on budget.

Edit- Gah, I forgot. MacOSX is 'apple branded hardware only'. It will run on x86, but its a 'hack' only (And IIRC, you have to use some downloaded version of MacOSX to even get it to run at all. I'm not 100% sure on that though, I dont use apple software....or hardware.....or 'lifestyle gadgets' LOL.
