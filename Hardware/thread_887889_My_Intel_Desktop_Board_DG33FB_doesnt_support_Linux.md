### Post by starcannon on 2008-08-12
Most hardware manufactures will not officially support Linux. Thats their way of saying "even if we provide linux drivers, or linux provides our hardware with drivers, we don't provide linux support". Keeps them from spending money on phone techs that could help. It's much cheaper to have someone working for peanuts somewhere, reading windows support scripts from a monitor.

As stated above, pop in a liveCD and see what happens, I'm relatively certain you'll find that it just works. I'd also recommend reading up a bit on a dual boot, or wubi install before you click on the "install" icon. A few minutes of research now will save you a headache later. And as always, before you mess with your hard drive BACKUP everything that is important to you, then proceed in a blissfully untouchable manner.
