### Post by dabl on 2010-07-15
To learn whether a Linux driver exists for your printer, you need to check it out here:  [http://www.linuxfoundation.org/collaborate/workgroups/openprinting/database/databaseintro](http://www.linuxfoundation.org/collaborate/workgroups/openprinting/database/databaseintro)


The KDE printer utility is pretty useless -- best to stay away from it.  Use your browser and the CUPS interface -- enter "localhost:631" (with no quote marks) in the browser URL window, and follow the instructions there.

Also, see #13 here: [http://kubuntuforums.net/forums/index.php?topic=3099811.0](http://kubuntuforums.net/forums/index.php?topic=3099811.0)
