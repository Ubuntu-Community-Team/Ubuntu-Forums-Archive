### Post by farlies on 2018-06-17
I have a Lenovo Ideapad 510 laptop running Bionic--it had previously run Artful. Ubuntu generally works very well on this hardware.
However Ubuntu (A & B) does/did sometimes indicate the battery's remaining charge suddenly drops from 99% down to <20%, falling quickly from there, even though the machine is on the charger and has been fully charged for some time. When it does so,* the keyboard becomes disabled*: no keystrokes register in terminal or anywhere; the mouse continues to work. Rebooting Ubuntu will not restore the battery charge estimate.  However booting into Windows-10 seems to cure it: subsequent boots to Ubuntu will immediately show the correct charge state.  I haven't seen this problem while running Windows.

Are there ACPI tweaks or other tools/techniques that would solve this, or at least keep me from having to stop everything and boot Windows in this state?
