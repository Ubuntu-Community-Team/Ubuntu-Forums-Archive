I have a Gigabyte Z170 and it has worked well.
 oldfred's new SFF system Skylake with 16.04
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2315007&p=13449024#post13449024](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2315007&p=13449024#post13449024) 

I think any system can work. And just about all vendors will say they do not support Linux.
 
But as with any very new hardware, you may need newest Ubuntu or even version being released this month 17.04. Most vendors do not update Linux drivers. So Linux developers have to modify drivers, test them, include in Kernel & support software and then be in a distribution. That can take a while before everything then becomes easy to just install.

Often boot parameters or newer drivers may be required to get system to work well.
