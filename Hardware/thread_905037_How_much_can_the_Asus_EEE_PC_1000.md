### Post by malrost on 2008-08-30
I've had a 1000 for two weeks now. Briefly:

-text files and internet? not a problem
-programming? not a programmer, cannot answer.
-anime? not a fan, cannot answer. However, I have run into problems w/ Flash video on Ubuntu both on my desktop amd_86 8.04 install as well as the i386 install on the eee, so that is probably not hardware specific. The same sites/videos are unviewable in either.
-keyboard is very usable. Biggest weakness IMO is that the function key is necessary to toggle for home/end/pageup/pagedown, they're on opposite ends and thus require both hands.
-The Xandros default linux install is weak sauce. I tried to stretch it as far as I could, but lack of 802.1x support (enterprise wireless, used at my school) was a show stopper for me. I tried the manual madwifi driver & wpa_supplicant fix, but what a PITA. Ubuntu supports 802.1x by default, and speaking of Ubuntu on an Eee...
-a few critical Eee drivers aren't supported by the default 8.04. Namely wireless. There are already reputable customized kernels for this if you can deal with that. Also, Ubuntu-eee just came out last week, with the customized kernel preinstalled.
-there are some problems with Bluetooth, and the microphone and camera sometimes require tweaking
-If you have a USB CD drive you're fine with installs. You'd also be fine if you didn't but had an USB thumb drive 1 GB or larger.

I've heard rumors that Ubuntu 8.10 intends to support all Eee hardware, rendering every issue above moot.

I can't compare it to a CoreDuo, but obviously they'll perform faster than the Atom. The Eee's SSD, battery life, size and weight are all the unbeatable features that sold me on it. The 13 inch screen is fine; depending on what AV you need for your anime that may be a showstopper for you.

I replaced the 1 GB Ram module with a 2 GB module, which is doable on the cheap (and won't void your warranty.) I'm biased, but that said I think it was a great bargain, particularly as the 1000 comes with only SSDs.

Also, this machine has the fastest Ubuntu boot I've yet to achieve, under 20 seconds.

You can also get much more specific technical answers at the Eee forums:

[http://forum.eeeuser.com/index.php](http://forum.eeeuser.com/index.php)

### Post by richardh9936 on 2008-08-30
:)Oh boy do I have good news for you. :)

I have owned my eee-1000 for about 5 days, and yesterday I transformed it into an UBUNTU HARDY eeep.  I relied on adamm's process and resources: see [http://www.array.org/ubuntu/]("http://www.array.org/ubuntu/")
and this ([http://www.array.org/ubuntu/setup901.html]("http://www.array.org/ubuntu/setup901.html")) in particular.

Asus changed the ethernet and wireless hardware or firmware at least, so they won't work until you install adamm's bits.  That's why Mandriva's "spring 2008 ONE" works fine for 700s but not 901s or 1000s.  I suspect the same issue affects all other distros.

I found adamm's site from the official Ubuntu-eee page ([http://www.ubuntu-eee.com/index.php5?title=EeePC_901]("http://http://www.ubuntu-eee.com/index.php5?title=EeePC_901")).

Remember: do as adamm says, and install Ubuntu-eee first.  This is an Atom chip, and may differ significantly from i386.  Follow adamm's instructions.

I am happy to answer the VGA question - it can handle VGA out.  The easiest way is to plug in the VGA cable and restart X (with CTRL-ALT-BACKSPACE).  On restarting you'll find that it's adopted the external monitor's settings.  Disconnect the cable and re-start X to go back to the EEE PC's lcd screen.
