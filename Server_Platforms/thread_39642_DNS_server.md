### Post by Gtaylor on 2005-06-05
All you need to do is go to a registrar like [http://godaddy.com](http://godaddy.com) and buy a domain for about $16 a year. You can then point it to an account on [http://zoneedit.com](http://zoneedit.com) which in turn points your domain to your webserver, unless you have space on an ISP with its own DNS servers. In this case you'd just point your domain name to them.

So you don't actually need your own DNS server since you can do the same thing free, not including the domain registration costs.

### Post by Gtaylor on 2005-06-05
You could use something like [http://dyndns.org](http://dyndns.org) which is also free. If you want a top level .com/.org, etc you are going to need to buy the domain. DNS servers don't let you have domains, they are where you point domains that you own to. No amount of software (free or not) will get you free top level domains.
