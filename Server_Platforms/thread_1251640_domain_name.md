### Post by jasonsjunk on 2009-08-27
First see if your ISP will offer you an static IP.  I have 3 websites running out of the house on a 10 meg DSL connection with a static IP address.  I run all of my email through google apps->cost is free for first 50 email addresses.  Even if my webserver goes down my email stays running.  

If your ISP doesn't offer a static IP option you can always use Dynamic DNS dyndns.com and use thier free service with one of thier subdomains or pay for an upgrade to use your own domain.
