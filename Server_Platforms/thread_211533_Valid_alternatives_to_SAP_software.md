### Post by yager on 2006-07-20
I have no experience with any of the following, but this link may point you in the right direction.

[http://www.roseindia.net/opensource/erpcrm.php](http://www.roseindia.net/opensource/erpcrm.php)

I use SAP at work, which is a large international corporation.  I am not a fan of SAP, way too complicated and not very logical in its structure.  As a user of several different MRP/ERP systems over the years, I suggest the following.  The biggest factor that you should consider is the underlying structure of the system and what was the original purpose of the system.  SAP grew from an accounting package and therefore is very logical to people with accounting backgrounds.  If you want to handle physical objects in a logical process that is similar to how it is handled in a production process, SAP is the most convoluted software I have ever seen.  It is close enough to look like it knows what it is doing, but there are too many complicated requirements to work around artificial barriers.  When our production plant converted from a legacy system from IBM to SAP, we had to hire additional staff to feed the beast.  New software is supposed to make things more efficient.  In addition, we had to make many sacrifices in what we had because everything we originally had was an extra cost option from SAP.
