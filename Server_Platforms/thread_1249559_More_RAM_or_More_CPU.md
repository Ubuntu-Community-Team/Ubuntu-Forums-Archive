### Post by volkswagner on 2009-08-25
You can happily run a basic server with the Pentium 3.  One thing to consider is how your son will use the second machine.  If he needs a GUI, then I would give him the 2.6GHz unit, as he won't even be able to run Flash on the ~450MHz unit.

Other things to consider:  If the server is running 24/7 power usage may be a concern.  The Pentium 3 machine will most likely consume less than half in electric.  As you have noticed, upgrading old hardware can be very expensive.  Depending on your Internet connection, your upload speeds my be your biggest bottleneck.  I second the "SATA connectors on the 2.6GHz is a huge plus when adding large disks" for the lowest $/gig.

Have fun!

Newegg.com has some of the best prices on ddr memory.  You can probably get 1Gig for $25-30.
