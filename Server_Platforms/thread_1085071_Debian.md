### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2009-03-04
I waffle between the two.  Currently release/support schedules are the biggest difference, but Ubuntu is working on certification with a lot of ISV's.  That makes a difference if you're chucking down five figures for a piece of software like Oracle or something.

I still tend to choose Debian, unless a particular service is just implemented better in Ubuntu (LTSP, for example -- though Lenny might have come up to par on that one too).
