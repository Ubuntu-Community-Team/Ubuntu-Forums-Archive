### Post by Glut on 2006-08-13
Yes, we have a few as production servers.

It's been fine, few problems but nothing very interesting, either. It's hard to pick between them and Debian.

Debian's (and by association Ubuntu's) great strength is package management and stability. This can also be a weakness: The packages in Debian stable are fairly old, so if you need newer packages or to support new hardware then your choices are basically Ubuntu or Debian Testing. It's arguable which is better. Testing has a better reputation, but you're at the mercy of devs -who could make changes at a whim (The chances of this reduce drastically as release date approaches). Paradoxically Ubuntu should be more stable (in terms of major changes), but it's reputation is as a bleeding-edge desktop distro. Ubuntu has commercial backing, so support is available. That's important to us. It may be to you, too.
