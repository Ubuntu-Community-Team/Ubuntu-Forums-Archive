### Post by TheFu on 2012-01-25
> **samosamo said:**
> Thanks very much. Alfresco looks very interesting. Redmine looks geared more towards software development.

We use both at my company. 
* Alfresco for document management and 
* redmine for project management. 
Yes, we use redmine to work software projects, but we do not use any of the software-specific parts like version control at all, just the action items, wiki and documents.  A checkbox removes those parts from the redbox GUI.  The forums have been enabled, but training users how to use them effectively has failed. Sad, I know.

Wikis can work, but I've only seen them fail due to the non-intuitive editing wiki-text required. In a tiny office of just 4 people, I was unable to get anyone else to edit wiki pages that someone else created. We did full office training and one-on-one training and they still didn't understand.  The trivial wiki-text formating failed for them as well.  They learned bulleted lists - nothing else, numbered lists, headers, bold, italics - were all beyond them.  The users preferred to use a word processor and to upload HUGE text documents into Alfresco. Alfresco looked like a shared folder to them, it was easier to understand in their minds.

Collaboration can mean different things to different people. Are there any specific features you are interest in using besides the wiki?
