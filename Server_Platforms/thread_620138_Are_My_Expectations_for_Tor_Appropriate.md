### Post by Ubuntist on 2007-11-22
I figure that if the CIA, MI5, Mossad, FSB, <insert favorite spy agency here> want to track me on the net, break my passwords, etc., there's not much I can do about it.  However, I would still like to keep Google, newspaper websites, etc., from tracking my reading habits and such.

For casual daily browsing, I set Firefox to accept cookies for the session only, I usually use Tor and Privoxy, and for sites requiring registration I get a login ID and password from BugMeNot.

For sites where I actually care about protecting my password, however, like my e-mail provider, I don't use Tor on the theory that using Tor increases the risk that somebody will skim my password.

Recent stories about some Tor exit nodes skimming traffic give me cause for concern, but it seems to me that the strategy outlined above still makes sense.  Does it?  Am I being too paranoid?  Not paranoid enough?
