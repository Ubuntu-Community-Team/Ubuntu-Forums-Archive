### Post by mbeach on 2008-07-20
if talking about production servers, and not something just running apache for home use and maybe some file sharing with samba I'd suggest not.  You 'can' of course, especially if you could cluster a few together but I wouldn't.  A proper network of cheap servers with cheap parts is how Google got started I believe (not sure if that's still the case).  

Proper servers with dual power supplies, hot swapable drives, rack mountable, multiple nics, etc provide certain advantages which a laptop is not quite capable of (imo).

### Post by jflaker on 2008-07-20
Laptops are certainly powerful enough, but the reliability isn't there for commercial use, IMHO.  As a low power option for home, I think it may be a good idea

another option: You can get PC's from companies who update their desktops every few years and must pay to dispose of the old systems.  I have about 4 systems over the years from my company and they were more than powerful enough to run simple daemons like file shares or even apache servers.  Best of all, the systems are designed for desktop use so they can be placed on a desk and used as a headless system and don't need to be cooled like high end systems.

Use the laptop for yourself.
