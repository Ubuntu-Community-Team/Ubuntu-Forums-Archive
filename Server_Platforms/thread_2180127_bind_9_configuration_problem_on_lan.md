### Post by SeijiSensei on 2013-10-11
Since 12.04, making changes to /etc/resolv.conf is the not the correct method for specifying nameservers.

As a test, remove the entry for 127.0.0.1 from /etc/resolv.conf, or put it below the entry for your DNS server.  If that fixes the problem, add the line "dns-nameservers 192.168.1.7" to the entry for eth0 in /etc/network/interfaces as explained [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/12.04/serverguide/network-configuration.html#name-resolution").  (Note that "dns-nameservers" ends with an "s."  Leaving that off is a common mistake.)

As it stands now, your client never talks to your nameserver because dnsmasq is listening on the localhost interface and handles the request.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2013-10-14
> **jamesbon said:**
> I am not clear with this line of yours.

Ubuntu now installs dnsmasq which acts as a nameserver and listens on the localhost interface 127.0.0.1.  Unless you specifically include your nameserver via dns-nameservers or editing files in /etc/resolvconf/resolv.conf.d/, dnsmasq will not pay attention to your local nameserver.  If you look at the output from dig you posted above, all the requests were being directed to the root nameservers, not to yours.
