### Post by MJN on 2006-08-08
Assuming a dynamic IP from a DHCP lease it depends entirely on your ISP as to how they manage the address assignments. You could try what works for me (Blueyonder here in the UK) - turn your router off (completely) and wait... if I give it around an hour (often less) I'll get a new address next time I turn it on. I've seen reports of others (on the same ISP) having to wait overnight so try giving it as long as you can. No guarantees this'll work for you of course but it's probably as good a chance as you're going to get.
