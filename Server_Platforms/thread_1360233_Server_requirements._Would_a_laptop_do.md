### Post by hugmenot on 2009-12-20
Shouldn&#8217;t be a problem. I have an old laptop with a bad screen under my bed that runs 24/7 with no issues at all.

Regarding resources, it depends. Regarding wearing out, just make sure it doesn&#8217;t overheat. You can switch off the screen so the lamp in it doesn't age. The battery will age, but when you don&#8217;t cycle it too much it won&#8217;t be so bad. It also gives you protection from power outages. ThinkPads can lower the charge to 40%, which minimizes aging.

> BTW: is there any way to sort of.. calculate how much power it would be using, and maybe figure out how much our energy bill would go up?
No magic here.

Remember that V x A = W

The average sort of laptop power is 19V, 4A so approx 80W.  A unit of electricity in the UK is one KW hour.

I am sure you can work out the rest for yourself.
