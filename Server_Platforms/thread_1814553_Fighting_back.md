### Post by lisati on 2011-07-29
If you're running your own email server, there are possibilities for keeping the junk out. I use Postfix for my email server, and among the tools I use are greylisting, spamassassin and DNSBL/RBL lists to help reduce the amount of spam.

### Post by Habitual on 2011-07-29
> **Mr. Beekeeper said:**
> ....what all of you do to keep it to a minimum, or possibly spam the offending email addresses back.

1.) **Don't post *your* email address in a public forum**. Ever.
2.) Get (a) throw-away email address(es) specifically for forum use, as hakermania has suggested.
3.) employ greylisting, spamassassin and DNSBL/RBL lists on your mail server as lisati has suggested.

It looks like shared hosting with WHM/cPanel from here. If it is, open a ticket and ask them for help with greylisting, spamassassin and DNSBL/RBL. 
WHM has RBL lists that you can add your own filters to.

Personally, I employ all of these methods.

re: "possibly spam the offending email addresses back." Don't even try, it's a lost cause unless you have the money and time/resources to pursue the offenders.

Good Luck.
