### Post by craigp84 on 2007-05-13
Hi Dalebuk,

There will be a firewall of somesorts on your router, it's probably more effective to use that. Just ensure you keep it's firmware up to date, and that you power cycle it every so often.

You probably don't need any anti-virus, unless you are delivering files to your customers for download (maybe a PDF invoice or similar, would be good practice, though not essential, to run that through a virus scanner).

Reading between the lines here though, it's probably *much* better if you pay for some professional hosting. When you're hosting an online shop, you really want the customer's experience to be fast and reliable - otherwise they'll likely click away :( A slow loading site, hanging off an ADSL connection is not going to impress me, mr customer, very much at all. Even if you did put it all together yourself technically.

Also, it's good to be able to shout at someone else when it goes down, rather than have to spend time figuring out why the site went down, and how to stop it happening again.

Save your home ADSL bandwidth for your own browsing, have someone else host the site :-)

Hope this helps,

Craig
