### Post by silurius on 2007-04-03
I'ill second Drupal as I've used it for lots of diverse projects and it never fails to meet my requirements.  I'd swear by it.  The best thing about it is a huge community of friendly, active developers and a wide range of possibilities - lots of support there.

Mambo and Joomla are somewhat popular I think, but I'm not sure if they're as good as Drupal.  I set up a couple of sites on those platforms and the experience wasn't too bad.

Plone is gaining attention in my circles, but I have limited experience with it.

MediaWiki is another option to consider.

Wordpress is infinitely simpler and easier to deploy, but not as feature-rich as the above.

I see a lot of vBulletin / vBulletin Advanced sites.  Very functional, great way to manage articles and their associated comments, but I find the design possibilities of that approach to be very amateurish.

More ideas?  The suggestion to look at [SourceForge]("http://www.sourceforge.net") and [Freshmeat]("http://freshmeat.net") was a good one.

### Post by huygens on 2007-04-03
You can use a wiki or blog which are easy to set-up. A CMS (Content Management System) like Drupal, Joomla, etc. are more complex (eventhough as easy as the other to install). By complex I mean to arrange the content and administrate them, they are more difficult to start with than with a wiki or blog, but they offer much more possibilities.

One more word, basically wiki and blog are a simplified form of a CMS.

To choose a wiki (there are many wiki engines), you could try [Wiki Matrix]("http://www.wikimatrix.org/").
For a blog, you have [Weblog Matrix]("http://www.weblogmatrix.org/"). (not as up-to-date as the wiki matrix)
For CMS, there is not really a matrix with a wizard to help you pick up the correct one, but you have this comprehensive web site: [http://www.opensourcecms.com/](http://www.opensourcecms.com/) you can then view features of the various engines, and even try them out (both user and administration interfaces)
