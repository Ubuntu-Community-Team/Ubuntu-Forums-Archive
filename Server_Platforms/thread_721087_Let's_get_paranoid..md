### Post by netlogic on 2008-03-11
What is your PC security inside your building? These days, it is almost impossible to bypass the firewall. Most data thieves are working from the inside once they have software or devices in any of the nodes. 

1.Bypassing grub password
         a.get a screw driver, open up the case, take the battery out for 30secs, boot to livecd and rehash the password or remove the entry.
         b.solution: pad lock usb ports and remove optical drives
         c.issue: it avoids the warranty from your manufacture.
2.Large PC case
         a.friend of mine found a pico-itx case hooked up to their network inside a tower case. He doesn't know how long it has been inside the case.
         b.4&#8221; motherboard isn't too hard to fit inside the large case and it isn't so hard to cross connect the cables.
3. c.Encrypt the /home /root partitions
         a. maintaining passwords for 1000 workstations can be a nightmare.
4.Thin and VM Client machines
         a.VM are located in the server. Data thieves have no physical access.
          b.It is a really hard sell to other departments to go 100% thin clients and store all the machines through VM and Blades.
5.Running IDS system inside every subnets. 
        a.The false alarm will go off every minute. It is very difficult to determine what should be alarmed.

What are you doing to prevent data thieves stealing your company's data inside your building?
