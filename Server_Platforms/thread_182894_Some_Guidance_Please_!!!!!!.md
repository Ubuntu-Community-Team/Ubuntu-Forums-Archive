### Post by linuxone on 2006-05-28
Hi,

[QUOTE=NeoGreen]Can you recommend a book that will help me learn the Linux OS and how it is confiugured, step by step.[/QUOTE]

from my own experiences I recommend the following:

Start with:  [Linux in a Nutshell](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/linuxnut5/index.html) (current release: 5th Edition)

Depending on your needs and interests you may continue with (for example):

[Running Linux](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/runlinux5/index.html) (5th Edition)
[Linux Server Security](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/linuxss2/index.html)
[Linux Cookbook](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/linuxckbk/index.html)

Basics:
[Linux Network Administrator's Guide, Third Edition](http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/linag3/)

Before buying a book you'd check [Safari Books Online](http://safari.oreilly.com/) first.

More books about vi (very best text editor), bash (Linux shell), postfix, Apache, MySQL, PHP, DNS & Bind .... were available. Enough to read for the next years .... :)
