### Post by darkod on 2012-05-28
How are you loading your rules at boot?

Don't forget that you can load iptables rules one by one, and create the file with rules like the one you posted, but unless you load it at boot, your rules will not exist by default.

Maybe this is happening and not letting you enter by SSH because no rules (including the one about SSH) are loaded.

### Post by spynappels on 2012-05-29
Do you have Out-of-Band access to your server, through a console of some sort?

If you do, check the iptables rules immediately after a reboot and see what they are.

You may also find it useful to use iptables-save to save your ruleset once you are happy with it and use iptables-restore on boot to restore the rule set after all the interfaces have come back up.
