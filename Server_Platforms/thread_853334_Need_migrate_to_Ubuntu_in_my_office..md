### Post by dca on 2008-07-08
Ubuntu contains OpenOffice which is a free suite of Office applications.  OOo does not offer nearly the features of MS Office but it doesn't cost anything and does everything most users I know need.
 
The biggest question is does your company or office rely on a particular application (point of sale, quickbooks, etc, etc) that has no port or equal in Linux.  Some server-based versions (not full client install) of apps only run on Windows Server 2xxx OS, also...

### Post by bluefrog on 2008-07-08
it has all you need to run your office (see above point though about a specific windows "only" apps).

The real problem you have to think about is how much are your people willing to move to Linux.

Don't overtake that problem or the change will be very costly for you (money wise, relationship wise). You might end up going back to Windows. 
I have seen that happen with "only" a move from MSoffice to Openoffice inside windows where the tech guy has underestimated his people.

Switching one or two PC to start with, and with people who wants the challenge. They will need to be trained as well as if they cannot do something the "it's easy, find out by yourself or look at help files" will come back to you as a rocket: "I do not need the help files in Windows, I know how to do".
