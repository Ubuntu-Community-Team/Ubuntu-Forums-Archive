Greetings and salutations:

I would like to talk about a piece of software ( as well as start a trend of usage) available to assist your security.

TinyCA.

TinyCA is available to us to allow us to create certificates to use in our email and for our local (evel live, but it's a nogo mostly) websites.

It's a cert authority on your machine that uses ssl as a backend.

Thus far I have only been able to use the appy to create certs that let me sign my emails...but I can ( and you) also use any number of front ends for this purpose:
Kgpg
Seahorse
etc.

My intention with this initial post is to fully understand how to locally use this piece of software as well as getting more people (everybody) using it or something similar to assist the authenticity of our correspondance.

Nobody needs to be reminded of the plethora of scams abounding on the web and in emails, etc...do we?

Perhaps someone can respond to this thread and tell me how to make a local cert that I can use with evolution or kmail or any other email app that will allow me to encrypt my email, should I find it necessary.

Creating a cert to sign your emails is simple enough, and I also found this site if it's more your flavor:

[https://www.cacert.org/index.php](https://www.cacert.org/index.php)

I think it's important for ourselves to safeguard our email and not wait for some corporation to develop a proprietary or otherwise self-serving scheme.

We run Linux - it's what we expect, ey?
