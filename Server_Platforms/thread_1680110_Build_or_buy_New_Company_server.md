* You can spec what you need
* You spec upgrade-ability (plan for the future)
* Not proprietary (like Dell, Dell is so bad at proprietary) (not a fan of Dell)
* Potentially lower costs (especially if you buy from NewEgg)
* Your choice of Form-Factor

### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2011-02-02
Having a warranty on server hardware has saved my backside more than a few times.  It's worth it, IMHO.  We moved from in-house hardware to Dell servers a few years ago, and haven't regretted it.

### Post by doogy2004 on 2011-02-03
My suggestion is to buy a used Dell server from a place like [http://www.xbyte.com/](http://www.xbyte.com/) they have good prices an there hardware, all hardware is sold without an OS.  With branded servers if something breaks you can get parts cheap and easily.  Since you are doing this for a business you should look into systems with built in redundancy (dual power supplies, ECC ram, etc.) you can get this easily with a used branded server.  Don't be afraid to buy used enterprise hardware, enterprise hardware is designed to be robust and stable (unlike consumer hardware that tends to wear out over time or at random).
