Hey folks,

I have a number of computers in my house which have various uses. A Dell desktop runs our movie projector, an old Toshiba Laptop is in the kitchen for internet radio and podcasts while I cook, and some other computers. I have several more computers that were given to me because they were "old" and I have possible uses for them. For example, I'd like to have a computer in the bedroom to stream TV or music to the bedroom. All of these computers access shared data on my Ubuntu server which sits in the garage. 

My concern about multiple full machines is three fold:

1) The power consumption
2) The noise of fans
3) The excess heat produced (it's hot enough in the summer!)

I am starting to look into thin clients as an alternative and I have a few questions:

1) Can I take a typical desktop computer and strip it down to nothing more than a motherboard and use that as a thin client (keeping the necessary PCI cards for video and network). I'm thinking that if I do that I would have virtually zero noise, way less power needs, and less heat generated (no disk drive).

2) Can each thin client be customized somewhat for its use? For example, can I have the client connected to our movie projector boot up straight into a program such as XBMC?

I'm not diving into this idea too deeply until I get a feeling for others' experiences.

Thanks!

Garfonzo
