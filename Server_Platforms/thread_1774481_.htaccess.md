### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-06-03
.htaccess files are disabled by default in Ubuntu Apache.  If you look at /etc/apache2/sites-enabled/000-default, you'll see the defaults as defined in the "<Directory />" stanza.  The directive "[AllowOverride]("http://httpd.apache.org/docs/2.2/mod/core.html#allowoverride") None" disables .htaccess files.  You can either change that, or put the rewrite rules directly into the <VirtualHost> container for your site.

### Post by Baumbart on 2011-06-04
I didn't ever get too much into that reg expression stuff but I accomplished a similar task with a slightly different rewrite rule:

RewriteRule ^(.*)$ /index.php/$1 [L]

You see I work with directories instead of GET params, but the principle is the same.
The only difference is that I use * instead of + , /index.php instead of just index.php and that [L] afterwards.

### Post by Baumbart on 2011-06-04
Ah, the famous missing dot, the horror of every debug-session.

The L flag means "stop processing after one rewrite". In .htaccess files it's actually a bad habit because it doesn't act as expected.
