### Post by arrrghhh on 2011-05-20
I'd be careful about running (public) websites from a residential internet connection - many providers either block the traffic, or will not be happy with you if the sites get a lot of traffic...

At any rate, you can certainly host a site with that rig... several in fact, those specs are way overkill for a webserver :p.

So are you wanting to go Dyndns so you don't have to pay for domain registration?  Even if you host the sites on your machine, if you want your own domain you'll still have to pay to register the domain - certainly a lot cheaper than full-service hosting costs, but of course with full-service hosting you don't have to worry about hardware, internet connections, etc... That's the tradeoff.
