### Post by Coreigh on 2009-01-23
I vote no.
8.04 is an LTS release, meaning it will be serviced and updated for 3 years (possibly longer) Where the releases that are not LTS, like 8.10, are supported for a shorter time. In theory the LTS releases will be or become more stable and have fewer problems, bugs or glitches. The non LTS releases, while many work very well, are in reality the beta or testing versions for the LTS releases. They may work near perfectly and give you no trouble, but they might not. And they aren't supported as long. So unless your purpose is to run a testing server or you simply enjoy doing the upgrades and bug tracking etc. then stick with the LTS versions for your "production" level servers.
