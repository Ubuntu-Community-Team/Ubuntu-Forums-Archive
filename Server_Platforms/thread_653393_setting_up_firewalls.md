### Post by rivalarrival on 2007-12-29
Typically, you log into your router's configuration page and configure it under "wireless security" or a similar tab.  

It's a little different for each router, but typically you just point a web browser at [http://192.168.1.1](http://192.168.1.1) to get started.

Your best bet for good information is the manual that came with your router.

### Post by Giradman on 2007-12-29
You might want to give some more details of your setup, e.g. brand/model of your router & types of computers that you're trying to link to your home network.  I'm using a Linksys 'wireless' router and the manual is excellent at explaining the options and their configuration - if you no longer have a manual, check the website of the router's maker (usually a PDF file can be downloaded); finally, WEP was one of the first encryption options offered and is easily broken; WPA and its variants are more effective - you'll need to explore the best security choices available on your router - good luck in your choices - :)
