### Post by Thirtysixway on 2011-01-31
I would also suggest wordpress. It's very easy to extend functionality with plugins and themes, and is used by thousands and thousands of sites.

Search around online for some best practice tips on wordpress to help fight spam.  The one downside to wordpress is there are a lot of bots/software created to automate spam comments and whatnot.  But it's not hard to secure it, and I believe the newer releases of wordpress have the ability to filter spam comments out of the box.

> **weedeater64 said:**
> Have you searched the packages?

Or

  mahara

Remember wordpress from the repos is coming out of universe. This means it's up to someone to take wordpress updates and push them out.  If you download from [www.wordpress.org](www.wordpress.org) you'll have more control over the version and security updates.
