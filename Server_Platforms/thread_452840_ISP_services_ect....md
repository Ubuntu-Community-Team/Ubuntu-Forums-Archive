### Post by dbott67 on 2007-05-24
If you were your own ISP, you could do whatever you want, however, there are a number of issues.

Do you want to just host other people's websites or do you actually want to sell them some sort of internet service (dial-up, DSL, cable, wifi, etc.).  To become an ISP (Internet Service Provider) you would need some sort of infrastructure (multiple phone lines, banks of modems, accounting and bandwidth monitoring software, a big honking synchronous internet pipe such as a T3, help desk, etc.).  I'm guessing you just want to host websites.

Having said that, most residential ISPs prohibit running web servers, mail servers, etc. from home.  They also prohibit "reselling" their service (i.e. allowing a neighbour to pay you a fee to share your connection).  They would require you to purchase a commercial access account, which would provide you with static IP addresses, synchronous bandwidth (most connections are asynchronous, meaning that they give you fast downloads, but limit the upload bandwidth which is what you need if you're a webhost).

As for web design & flash, I'll leave that to others to answer.

My recommendation:

For personal knowledge & experimentation go ahead & setup your own server, however, if you plan to sell hosting services, become an agent/reseller of your ISP or some other commercial hosting provider.  They have the infrastructure, staff, expertise, bandwidth, etc. to provide proper hosting services... all for a very reasonable monthly fee:

[http://www.1and1.com/](http://www.1and1.com/)
