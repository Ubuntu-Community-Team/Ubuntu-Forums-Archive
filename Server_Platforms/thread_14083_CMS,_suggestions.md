### Post by jakeslife on 2005-02-04
I am looking for a versatile, easy to learn and manage CMS for a new web project I will be working on soon. I want to make this clear first though: I Love Mambo.

I use Mambo on my personal site and design site right now, and have been playing around with lots of the modules and components. I just want to make sure that the CMS I choose will provide what I have needs for. Here's what the idea is:

There will be a site. Obviously. It is a pay site, linked through a third party bill processing system (CCBill, etc.). I looked at eZpublish, It seems nice, but I was turned off by their backwards attitude towards free software.

You cannot offer a product under the GPL and go and sell a commercial version of the same thing.  Especially when some of the most sough-after features are only available to the proprietairy version.  They seem to be confusing the GPL with freeware or shareware.

This is another example of a company which uses the GPL for marketing reasons.

Not that I am against companies making money.  This is not the way to make money from free software.  It is not a sustainable practice.  You either end up closing off your product, or opening it up all the way.  (Usually  after you go bankrupt and so there goes all the support for your product.)

Until they get it straight, I would not use their product, let alone base my business on it.
