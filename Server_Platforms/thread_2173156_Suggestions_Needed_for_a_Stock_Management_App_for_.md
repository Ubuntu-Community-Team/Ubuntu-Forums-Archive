### Post by Chris of Arabia on 2013-09-08
I'm trying to find something that will help my wife out in sorting out her stock for her handmade jewellery business. It is a very small enterprise, but with beads, wires, findings and assorted cords stuff in every cupboard, she needs something to keep track of both raw materials through to finished product, so she can track purchase costs against sales. There's no requirement for POS, but it would be good if this were done as a web-based app that can be installed on my LAMP server (13.04) which she could then access via her iPad. As I say, it's mainly the stock control side that is needed, but if other functionality is there that could be used at a later date, then I'd have no objection. Does anyone have any recommendations on something that would suit a 1-person operation?

### Post by volkswagner on 2013-09-08
Perhaps Google Drive spreadsheet will help.  You can build a tabbed workbook, which can be edited on the iPad.  You can also build forms for easy input of bulk purchases, which can update the spreadsheet as a backend.

If your into building something, check out [Xataface]("http://xataface.com/"), or any other CRUD type software.  Xataface will allow you to build a web frontend/with a MySQL backend database.
