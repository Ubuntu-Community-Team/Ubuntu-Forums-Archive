### Post by TheAddict on 2008-06-07
Joomla! will certainly solve all of your problems, but from the description of your requirements, it may be over kill, and the control panel/dashboard/admin area/whatever you want to call it can be quite intimidating to unfamiliar users, especially those with little knowledge of web publishing.

Personally, for something simple like what you require, and for ease of use for all users I would recommend you look further than the big name full blown CMS. Something like wordpress *might* do what you want, or even simply a simple forum like BBPress. There are others out there as well, like ExpressionEngine, but its neither free nor open source, so for a lot of people that counts that one out (although I personally have trouble using anything else).

Check out [http://www.cmsmatrix.org]("http://www.cmsmatrix.org"), where you can compare a whole bunch of different CMS', and [http://www.opensourcecms.com]("http://www.opensourcecms.com")
will let you try them out before you choose one

### Post by molotov00 on 2008-06-08
If easily uploading pictures is important, I highly recommend Gallery2 (aka G2). I haven't used it in a couple years now, but there was a great Java client that allowed uploading entire albums. This is much better than the typical "Browse" buttons and other browser-based implementations.

I'm sure there's a G2 plugin for most popular blogs. I agree that a full fledged CMS is overkill.

I'd look into a basic blog app with a G2 plugin/extension. Restricting access is something virtually all blogs will support. Hope that helps.
