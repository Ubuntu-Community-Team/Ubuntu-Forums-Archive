### Post by machiner on 2005-03-02
Any company telling you that they give you 120GB bandwidth monthly is talking out it's arse, esp for $7.95 per month if paid annually.  You try actually using that amount and see what happens.

I have helped many find suitable hosting companies for a few years now and I have noticed one very important thing:

The best ones are not on some website's list.

What are your real needs? Will that company allow you more than 8mb per script? Will they respond to questions quickly (mine has every and all issues solved within 10 minutes. Always)

Will your hosting company give you a static IP address for your security cert? Or, will they force you to use their shared one...scaring customers away.

Do they charge a setup fee to create a directory? This is bogus.  Will they host you on linux?  WIll they give you SSH access? How about emails, mailing lists and subdomains?

Will they give you one or more mysql databases?  WIll they give you a dedicated server for a reasonable price? Do they own their own hardware, or is it leased, rented?

Can they promise you 99.9% uptime and back that up with proof?

Are you signing up with a reseller that buys an IP address and then aliases 100 off that address selling each for $?? monthly?  DO I even need to go into the cons of a shared IP address.. or expecting service from some "trunker"?

Do you get Perl, PHP, Python, TCL...etc?


It's difficult to find a decent company out there to host your site...my advice is to do it yorself, or - since this really isn't feasible for everyone...ask someone that runs or has run a business online...these people know what makes a good hosting company....Will your scripts work? Are you allowed to install any scripts? Do you have FTP access 24/7 (you'd be surprised at how many do NOT give you this)...or do they make you do all file maintainence through their crappy and DOG SLOW "control panel"?

The most important things that I have needed are more memory for badly writted scripts and immediate problem resolution.  My sites are all hosted at the same company and I'll never leave them... I pay $40 per year for each site.

With ICDSoft my sites have:
never been down
never been compromised
always ranked very well with google, et all (but there's some effort involved, and YES hosting plays a part)
hosted on linux and I have the freedom to do whatever I want (no porn or anything illegal - just like ALMOST every other company) at any time. Oh - all my sites were up, and DNS resolved within 1 day. Not 3.


However - to this point, when my current sites were active ( I have 2 left - down for maintainence) , I never got more than 1000 visitors (not hits) a day.  I cannot vouch for the reliability of their servers for more traffic than that...Oh, they let me install anything I want - they even offered to install any shopping cart program of my choice when they couldn't boost script memory over 25MB for the one that I was using - it coupled with Mambo was a ton of memory. (Yeah - that's a crappy script...any script using that much isn't worth your time)

Good luck with your search - just watch out for the crooks.

### Post by jfdill_2 on 2005-05-20
In the end, it came down to three choices for Ubuntu VDS in the US--I skipped some sites in the UK and elsewhere in  Europe, so I don't know if they offer Ubuntu.

quantact.com located in CA, US (San Francisco) offers Warty
linode.com located in TX, US offers Warty
unixshell.com located in GA, US offers both Warty and Hoary

For quantact and linode, you can upgrade to Hoary via apt-get after the install.

I decided to go with unixshell, because they offer more disk space and bandwidth for the $, and they are a bit "closer" to me via the Internet.  You can also keep 1 snapshot included with the plan except for with the "32".  And they offer Hoary as an install, so that is more convenient than installing Warty then having to upgrade.

All three have excellent network infrastructure, like Gb/s peering with multiple ISPs through a massive data center.

My Ubuntu Hoary VDS will be coming soon to www2.jfdill.com  I'm planning to use it for some development on a "knowledge base" type of system for my clients.  I haven't decided on the base CMS that I am going to use, but it will incorporate Owl and Coppermine Photo Gallery for storing documents.
