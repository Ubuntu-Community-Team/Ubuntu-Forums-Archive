### Post by Jonathan L on 2012-04-28
Hi

> **wirepuller134 said:**
> It's also about a 50 to 60LB server that makes a lot of noise with all of the fans running. []("http://www-947.ibm.com/support/entry/portal/docdisplay?lndocid=MIGR-56806#DOCPRODUCTS")

Do not underestimate the volume these things make.  I have similar equipment from Dell, HP etc, and though it's amusing for a short time when your colleagues complain, it really can be a problem.  I did a demo for a client with this kind of equipment and the client was seriously concerned about noise and referred to it in every subsequent meeting ("They're not going to be as loud as the ones you showed us, are they").

Most of the experience you'll need about server software you can get from almost any piece of kit.  I use near-silent nettops (from Acer) as they use very little power and are quiet.  You'll learn more from something which stays on: much server work is really about uptime and availability.

Having said that, if you've no experience of a big solid rack mount server (with all the fans, onboard RAID, more fans, lights-out remote controllers etc), it's really good experience.  I took a quick look on Ebay and there are loads of servers at $200, and plenty for $120, $150.  You might also easily find one for free.

Hope that's helpful.

Kind regards,
Jonathan.
