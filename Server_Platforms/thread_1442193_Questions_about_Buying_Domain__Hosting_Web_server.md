### Post by bpalone on 2010-03-29
I use DNSExit, they are registrars and they also provide Dynamic DNS services for free.  They are not the cheapest for Domains ($12.95) but I figured that since I was using their Dynamic DNS for nothing, I could pay them a bit more for the Domains.

I haven't used anyone else's dynamic dns service, so I can't compare for stability.  DNSExit's service will go for a month or two without a hiccup, then I will have string of days where it has shut down and failed to check.  (Never more than a day or two.)  All it takes is just checking it everyday and if it quite to do the sudo thing and stop and start it again.  My ip doesn't change very often, so it isn't that big of a deal and my web sites get very little traffic.  

As for private whois data, I don't know if they offer it or not.  I didn't really care who knows.

You can set up for mail, provided your ISP isn't blocking the port. 

Here is their site: [http://www.dnsexit.com/](http://www.dnsexit.com/)
