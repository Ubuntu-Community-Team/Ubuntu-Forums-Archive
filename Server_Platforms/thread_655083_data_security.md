### Post by shad0w_walker on 2007-12-31
There are many different types of encryption, ranging from takes maybe a few minutes (Very rarely used) to millions of years require to break it. 

The average solution you will find for hard drive encryption will be using something roughly in the middle. So you still get a reasonable amount of performance with out sacrificing a large amount of security.

The important thing of course is not to do something stupid and write down your password otherwise there is no point in even bothering with the encryption. Also, make sure to keep backups as you can't recover encrypted data if you forget the password.
