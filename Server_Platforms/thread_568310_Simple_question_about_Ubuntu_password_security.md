### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-10-05
Unless you encrypted your hard drive, anyone has access to all the data with a live CD. They can set a new password for your user with a live CD.

If you encrypted your Hard drive, they can likely crack it given sufficient time.

If you encrypted your had drive, anyone can install another OS and use your computer.

Bottom line, physical access = bad news.

### Post by netlogic on 2007-10-05
That's why you have to backup of your keys, so you can login and change them. You don't have one set of house keys do you? Call your providers and arrange something to change your passwords right at this moment. Treat your stole data as stolen credit cards, do it now.
