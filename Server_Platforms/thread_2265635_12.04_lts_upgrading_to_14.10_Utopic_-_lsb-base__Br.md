### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-02-16
If it were stock Debian, you would edit the sources, then do a dist-upgrade (not a mere upgrade)
If you were upgrading from 12.04 to 14.04, you could use the Ubuntu-specific command 'do-release-upgrade'
12.04 to 14.10 has not been tested, and is not supported. Nevertheless, we will try to help.

Your upgrade included at least one PPA. PPA software may cause version conflicts...very much like the version conflicts you have.

Look at the explicitly blocked packages (lsb-base, netbase, upstart-bin) in apt-cache policy and apt-cache show.
For example, lsb-base breaks upstart. You need upstart, so try to uninstall lsb-base.
