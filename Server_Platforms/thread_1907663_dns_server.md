### Post by volkswagner on 2012-01-12
Depending on your domain provider, you should not need your own DNS server.

Do you currently own a domain?  If you do, see if your registrar will handle your DNS.  If you use GoDaddy, they will have DNS settings to point to your home ip address.

If you don't have a domain, you can get a free one from places such as Dyndns.com, or no-ip.com.  These will help if you have a dynamic ip assigned by your isp.  Many routers have built in support to update the dynamic host provider, when your home ip changes.
