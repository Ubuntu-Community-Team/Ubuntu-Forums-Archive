### Post by crtlbreak on 2008-06-17
The wider racks are not  just finger room - they also allow for more cable room internally in the racks (something that is always a problem) - thecable runs up and down the insides of the rack between the servers ad the rack sides is obviously more in the wider racks.
Obviously a deeper rack accommodates longer servers such as Dell 2850's etc. The shorter 1800's are equally at home in a shallower (front to back) rack. It also once again depends on whether
[LIST]
[*]you are going to have a cooled room environment (that ownt necessitate cabinet doors on front and rear?)
[*]you are going to have Rack fans on top (extracting/pulling air)
[*]the sizes of your intended servers
[*]the amount of cabling (is it just an IT envirnment - or like mine is a broadcast environment where almost every machine has multiple audio inputs and outputs - this obviously needs more cabling!)
[/LIST]
This list is not exhaustive as there are many more parameters - Ihave named the most important - hope I havet raised too many questions again?
