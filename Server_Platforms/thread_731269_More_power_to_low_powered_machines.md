### Post by netlogic on 2008-03-21
Do you feel vendors are starting to cater more devices to Linux than Windows? Intel has been getting ready to launch many different variants of low power processors that can't run Vista. We all know about Vista hardware requirements to run word processors and Firefox at the same time. Is this mean Intel is planning to depart more away from Microsoft? They already partnered with Apple with their computer lines few years ago.  It seems like Intel is a bit tired of Microsoft's crap of pushing their views to rest of the world. Anyway, I am pretty excited. I used to bash Intel and favored AMD. Anything that adds or help to break up of the monopoly in technology is a good thing for us. Yea, it means more things to learn, but it also mean your days will be less ruined with products that don't work or products that your IT directors have made for you.  Little more freedom for more sweat is always a better choice.  It seems like Intel Atom 230 will be running at 1.6GHz with a 512KB L2 cache. It seems to similar to Celeron M series but it uses a lot less power. I also heard Silverthorne will have various clock frequencies of up to 1.8GHz with a 0.6W - 2.5W TDP. That is insane. This might mean we might finally have Linux laptops that will  be running off these chips with flash drives that can last up to six to eight hours off six cell batteries. This also mean Linux will appear cooler than Vista to the public's eyes. More Linux distros need to start working with hardware vendors, so we might have one day when devices have no supported drivers for Windows.  However, I wonder how this will affect virtualization market. I love VM, but if these processors are going to be cheap and use low energy, and if you already have iSCSI and San, why bother with VM? What are your thoughts? Do you think this might hurt VM market?
