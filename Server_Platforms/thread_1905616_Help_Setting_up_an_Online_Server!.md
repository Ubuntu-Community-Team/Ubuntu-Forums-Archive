### Post by collisionystm on 2012-01-07
> **Phixion said:**

You don't need anything that ridiculous. 

You can average a connected user needs 45-75kbs to play and transfer info back and forth during regular game play. 
If downloading levels they will use as much bandwidth as they can, but that is not full time, and thanks to a router it will even the bandwidth out between users.

A 5mb cable connection will suffice for what the OP is trying to do.
However a good comcast 25mb or even better a fios would be awesome.

### Post by Dangertux on 2012-01-07
Something else you might want to consider, not to be a downer here. But when I was that age I messed around with the same things and ran a bunch of low end nix servers in my room 24/7. My parents thought it was awesome that I was learning it, right up until they started getting 400-500 dollar power bills. Running a PC (particularly an older less "green" PC) 24/7 can get VERY expensive in energy costs. Make sure that's something your guardianship will be okay with BEFORE you do it.

Anyways , you also might check out ebay, not as good as the free recycling alternative, but if that doesn't pay off you can usually pick up old hardware (even server hardware) very cheap. I grabbed a Dell 1750 1u server for 135 bucks, granted that is a rack mount piece of equipment but it was a steal. You can get regular desktop systems for as low as 30, some places even offer free shipping. 

Good luck ;-)
