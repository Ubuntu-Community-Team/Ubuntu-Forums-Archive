### Post by SuperMike on 2009-02-09
Well, my hopes are on Canonical. I love that company. I could evangelize for that company. All they need to do here is think from the enterprise customer perspective, collect the best open source tools to solve the problems, and then create web-based wizards to roll this functionality out. If those tools cost too much money to build, then charge small, reasonable sums for them as add-ons to Ubuntu Server.

When this is complete, Canonical needs to create Novell-style training books on how to use it, and then push Ubuntu Server certification training courses.

Once those courses are beta-tested and the polished, Canonical needs to turn around and offer free training for the top 500 web hosting providers but for a limited time. This will help Canonical get mindshare in the web hosting providers away from RH a little.

From there, Canonical needs to just keep focusing on Ubuntu Server with surveys of their clients, asking what works, what doesn't, and keep honing that product's tools.

Eventually Canonical needs to pay for third-party evaluation studies of Ubuntu Server versus the competition and be as fair as possible. If they get bad marks in an area, then they need to learn from that and beat those problems down.

And then, after that, it's a cycle of marketing, self-evaluation and improvement, training upgrades, improving automation tools, and occasional free training to web hosting providers.
