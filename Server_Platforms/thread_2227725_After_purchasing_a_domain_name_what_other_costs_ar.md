There are 3 things needed to have a website/domain.
* registered domain (commonly called a "registrar")
* DNS that connect the domain to an IP - 
* hosting provider/server

Sounds like you are running the server at home ... so the ISP costs should be added.

The DNS may or may not be part of your registrar agreement. I don't know. Running your own DNS can be dangerous. I've been hacked over BIND.  $20-$40/yr makes this problem go away.  I like to keep the DNS and Registrar separate to prevent 1 company from having too much control over my websites.  It is certainly easier to have an all-in-1 solution, however.

Registrar costs vary wildly from $2/yr (1st year only) to $100/yr.  I normally renew my domains 90 days in advance of the expiration and buy 10 years at a time to keep annual costs to $7/yr.  Why 90 days in advance?  Some disreputable registrars lock accounts for the last 60 day of an agreement, making changing providers harder (called a "transfer").

So ... the costs for each of those things needs to be included.
