### Post by chris.zeman on 2008-07-03
Unless IXWebHosting has changed hands, stay far away from them. Do a little research on the company and its owner and you'll understand why. I was with them once, and it wasn't a good experience. My first sentance in this post is based on facts I discovered during my battles with them. DreamHost also offers exactly what you're looking for.

That said, you'll find large numbers of people that hate any particular host. I've been with DreamHost for many years and have been quite happy with them. You will, however, find a great number of people that hate them. Sure, they're not perfect, but they are the best host I've ever had. I've been with quite a few web hosting companies, too!

The bottom line is that whatever recommendations you receive for hosting should be used as a starting point in your search for a new host. Research each company. Talk to current and former customers if you can. Don't go with a company because a plan MEETS your needs. Be sure there is room to grow, because you never know when your needs will grow. 

One more thing... Remember, most hosting companies oversell. Find out what each company you look at has done to users for consuming the resources given to them. Some people have been treated the same as users who use more than they're alloted.

Chris
