### Post by slt on 2008-10-22
If you're worried about quiet, you need to look at the case, fans, and PSU first and foremost.  Most of today's hdds are fairly quiet.  I know they're not cheap but I've got a couple of Antec Sonata's that are amazingly quiet and can be found (in the USA) on sale for $100 or so with a good quiet PSU around 350W to 400W.  From there, you sound like you're already in the right frame of mind - basic mATX motherboard w/ onboard video from a decent major manufacturer (Asus, Gigabyte, MSI, Foxconn, etc) with an AM2 chip will be lowest cost.  I always recommend WD or Seagate hdds as they've always been the lowest failure rates in my experience.  One of the aftermarket "quiet" cpu coolers would be a good investment too...  Zalman, Antec, et al.  Think "large" fans at low rotational speeds instead of small fans blowing hard.  That's where most of the noise comes from.  I had an old Socket A system I used to call "The Helicopter" since the CPU fan was an early "high performance" unit that moved lots of air from a small fan at high rpm - and HIGH SPL.
