### Post by Permafrost91 on 2008-05-08
Hello, I am a medical student and at least once a year we need to record ourselves interviewing standardized patients and then review our videos and grade our performance.

The situation: we have 12 rooms, each with a camera, TV, and VCR. Anytime we need to record, we get a tape and off we go to our little room.

The problem: most of us don't have VCRs at home to review our tapes and with only 12 VCRs for a couple of hundred of students, it's obvious that we need a better solution.

The idea: use the cameras we already have (standard A/V or S-video connections) and record into a digital format (MPEG, AVI, MOV, etc.) Then serve out those files via a web server to the students.

The question: has anyone else dealt with this kind of situation before? Has anyone found a solution? We have looked at several commercial solutions none have been quite satisfactory. We're also hoping to employ more open source software and go away from our expensive proprietary contracts.

Thanks!
