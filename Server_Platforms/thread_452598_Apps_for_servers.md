### Post by craigp84 on 2007-05-24
Hi M0rThden,

It's more productive to think of it in terms of "i have problem X, what solutions are available" rather than "what solutions are available, and i'll see if i have the problem they solve".

(Unfortunately) the number 1 tool for most small businesses still has to be Microsoft Excel. I'd suggest getting that one down first (and learn it if you're not sure about it).

OpenOffice calc (open source attempt at Excel) is usable for basic stuff, but unfortunately still lags a long way behind :-( There's another spreadsheet package which is pretty good on ubuntu called "gnumeric" it's streets ahead of OpenOffice calc, but still behind Excel :-( also it's no longer as actively maintained i don't think - which is a real shame!

May be wise to invest in a copy of CrossOver office, and run a licenced copy of excel in that.

What kind of business are you running? How much are you prepared to spend? What value will the money bring to you? Go to your local chamber of commerce, there's more and more linux consultants poaching in these places now, speak to one of them, they'll expect to withdraw some cash from you, but normally well worth it.
