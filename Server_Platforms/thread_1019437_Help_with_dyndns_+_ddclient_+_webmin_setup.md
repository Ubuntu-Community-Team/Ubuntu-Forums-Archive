### Post by bluefrog on 2008-12-23
/etc/ddclient.conf
pid=/var/run/ddclient.pid
protocol=dyndns2
fw-login=your-router-user, fw-password=your-router-user-pwd
use=fw, fw=192.168.0.1/info.html, fw-skip='ppp_8_35_1' # change accordingly to the page on your router where you can find the info of your public adress
server=members.dyndns.org
login=your-dyndns-login
password='your-dyndns-pwd'
your-domain.dyndns.org

Then it is just a port forwarding matter for port 80 from your router to your machine.

### Post by dragos_iliescu_2005 on 2009-01-06
Sorry for late reply, I'll try to explain:
- about static routing, considere your Netgear DG834G as having two interfaces -one to the outside world (internet) and the other to your machine. First one is called "WAN" and it should have the connection parameters for your ISP. The second one is called "LAN" being the interface that your machine will use to connect to internet.
Between WAN and LAN communication is possible in both direction: from your machine to internet there is no need to make any settings inside the router. But from internet to your machine, there must to be set a "route" or a "forward" whatever will be this called in your case. That means that you must to declare a WAN port to redirected to a LAN address;port. In your case, to access the webmin from inteernet, you must to set WAN port 10000 to be forwarded to LAN <your_IP*>:10000
_all the settings described here m ust to be operated inside your router via web access interface.

*)It will really help that you will have a statip IP address for your machine.
