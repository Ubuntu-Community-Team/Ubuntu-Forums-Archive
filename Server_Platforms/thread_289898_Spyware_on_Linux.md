### Post by eeGhoong0ais on 2006-10-31
> **Aleksandersen said:**
> Are there any spywares at all on Ubuntu Linux?

The problem with spyware is that it isn't a very well-defined word. That said, if anything you could remotely describe as spyware ever came with a default Ubuntu install, there would be an uproar the like of which has never been seen.

Unless you go out of your way to install something non-free like, for instance, Opera, the source code is available for everyone to inspect, and anything suspect would most likely be spotted and removed.

So, basically, you'll be fine if you stick to the officially supported software, and probably even if you don't.

In the many posts on these forums I've seen (and, believe me, I've seen **many** posts), I have never seen a legitimate case of someone reporting having received spyware on Ubuntu (either through Firefox or some other means).

[quote=Mimsy]the spyware I pick up is in the form of cookies that I am too lazy to block.[/quote] Okay, well... if you're going to include cookies in the definition of spyware, then, yes, there will always be spyware on any operating system you use with any browser you use, unless you go out of your way to block the cookies.

Of course, if you use Konqueror, technically even cookies couldn't qualify as spyware, since the default behavior in Konqueror is to ask you every time you get a cookie how you want it handled.
