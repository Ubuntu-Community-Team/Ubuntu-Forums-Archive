### Post by Jussi Kukkonen on 2007-04-03
That depends entirely on your specific needs (And also the level of tinkering you are ready to do to get everything Just Right). Try to think what you want out of your system and try to evaluate different products based on that -- the underlying OS is not that important IMHO (if you have no preference on the issue), but different products have very different feature sets and goals... I'm not qualified to suggest Windows software, but on the Linux side you should look at least at Myth, VDR, My Media System and Freevo.

Personally I'm more than happy with my box running Dapper: It has VDR for digital television recording and slimserver as a music server for my Squeezebox player. The combination does everything I want, is fully remote-controlled, never crashes, is very customizable and is built entirely on Free Software. On the other hand getting VDR to even run was a lot of work even for an experienced *nix user...
