### Post by nutznboltz on 2010-02-01
I found this news article claiming that the open source driver for the Ramsan-10 and Ramsan-20 PCIe SSD flash storage cards (which are not attached via SATA or other classic disk controller) has become available.

[http://www.wwpi.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=8250:texas-memory-delivers-pcie-flash-for-open-source-community&catid=221:other-storage-media-ssd-hdd-blades&Itemid=2701223](http://www.wwpi.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=8250:texas-memory-delivers-pcie-flash-for-open-source-community&catid=221:other-storage-media-ssd-hdd-blades&Itemid=2701223)
> Texas Memory Systems, provider of the Fastest Storage line, announced that it is delivering open source drivers on Linux and Solaris for the RamSan-20, a high-performance PCIe flash-based solid state disk (SSD) storage product for accelerating workstations and servers. 

The Ramsan-20 page is here [http://www.ramsan.com/products/ramsan-20.htm](http://www.ramsan.com/products/ramsan-20.htm)
