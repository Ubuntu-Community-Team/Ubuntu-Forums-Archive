### Post by Jeff_From_VA on 2007-09-11
I wrestled with this decision on my latest pc build.  Previously I had used 32 bit, as I had a 32bit chip and no other option.

I went with 64 this go round for a few reasons. 

Most everything works fine out of the box, there are only a few things I could find that are not available in 64bit, and so far I have found workarounds for each.  

There is a performance increase for 64 bit across the board. In most apps it is very small fairly insignificant increase, in some apps it is fairly big increase.  I happen to do a lot of media encoding type tasks, and 64 bit shines in this department!!

Lastly, the whole world is moving towards 64 bit, and applications being optimized, developed, etc. to perform better in 64 bit environments will be on the rise, I think.

I am not trying to suggest 64 is for everyone, or sell 64 bit to anyone, but it certainly is for me.  I won't be going back.  

I would encourage anyone considering this to read up on it, and weigh your computing needs, and wants, against the pro's and con's of 64 bit to see if it is right for you.
