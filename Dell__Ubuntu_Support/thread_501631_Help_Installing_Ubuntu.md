### Post by gulmer on 2007-07-15
I ran into the same situation, wanting  have my cake and eat it too. I have a Dell Dimension 4550 desktop with a 40G hard drive w/ XP installed, computer came that way, then I installed a second, 80G hard drive, and installed ubuntu onto it. When I want XP, I just choose it at start up, the computer gives you the option to choose which O.S. you want. Mine always defaults to ubuntu if I leave it to it's own. Hard drives are very inexpensive now and this gives you an easy way to have both O.S.'s on one computer.
