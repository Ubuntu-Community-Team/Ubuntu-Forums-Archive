### Post by ChaosParser on 2007-12-23
Optiplex GX270s have bad motherboards.  Don't buy one. 

Optiplex in general, are great. 

BEFORE you buy any of Dell's machines, do yourself a favor.  Go to support.dell.com.  Click Drivers and Downloads, and select the model.  Change the operating system to RedHat.  

The Optiplex line is for business, and thus Dell actually has Red Hat drivers for a lot of them.  Any driver for RedHat can be made to work with Ubuntu using something called Alien.  Instructions here: 
[http://www.howtoforge.com/converting_rpm_to_deb_with_alien](http://www.howtoforge.com/converting_rpm_to_deb_with_alien)
[RedHat uses .rpm, while Ubuntu is .deb.]

I have two Optiplex GX1's.  They're ancient.  533Mhz Processors, 128MB Ram.  Grabbed all the RedHat Drivers, converted them and everything worked.  I'm not about to play games on them, but they work fine for basic internet browsing and word processing.  I loan them to friends as needed when their windows machines crash. :)
