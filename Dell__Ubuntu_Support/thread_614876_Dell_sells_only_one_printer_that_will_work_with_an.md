### Post by WrathofthePenguin on 2007-11-16
So I recently bought a Dell Inspiron 530 Ubuntu pre-install system. The person I bought it for wasn't ready to buy a printer, but now is. I decided that the easiest way to pick a printer would be to simply call and ask Dell what printers were supported with the system I bought.

The call was answered by somebody who did a surprising amount of work in a short period of time. She told me she would go and check and asked to put me on hold. She was back relatively quickly. She'd called some Dell techs who supported the Ubuntu systems and been told that only one printer that Dell sold worked with the Ubuntu systems, and it was a laser printer (I've forgotten which one she told me it was, unfortunately) which sold for far more than my friend is willing to spend. She went on to say that the Linux Foundation listed Dell Inkjet printers as "Paperweights". I told her I appreciated her thorough work and that the info would save me some headaches.

I'm very surprised that Dell has gone to all the effort to put together some fantastic systems to sell with Ubuntu pre-installed and hasn't made sure they had at least one ink-jet printer supported for those systems.

Off to figure out which HP to buy!

### Post by Dellfan on 2007-11-16
The issue with most Dell printers is they are based on Lexmark engines, which are not Linux friendly.

I would strongly recommend buying a small laser over a inkjet, especially for someone who just expects it to work. Inkjets, even high end ones, have issues with the nozzles clogging up, if used sporadically. Having to replace the cartridges once, pays for the difference between a decent inkjet and a small laser.

Samsung ML-3051 is a great cheap laser. For under $200 you get a network ready laser capable of duplex printing. Works great out of the box with Ubuntu. Plugged it in, connected to the network, all my Ubuntu machines detected it, knew what it was, and I was able to set them up via the Printer GUI. The HPs are great but you are definitely paying a premium for the HP label.

If they absolutely have to have an inkjet, HP is the way to go.
