### Post by tmshih on 2008-03-20
I've been running Ubuntu on my Inspiron 1501 for over 6 months now without any problems. If you are concerned about going back to Vista at some time in the future I would suggest having the installation resize your Vista partition to make room for another space to install Ubuntu onto (I have mine on a 20Gb partition). During the installation Grub will recognize Vista and install it as one of the choices that will pop up during the initial bootup. When I installed Ubuntu 7.06 I was pleasantly surprised to find almost everything worked right away. The wireless could recognize available networks, and the graphics card only needed clicking the "Restricted Drivers" icon to get that up and running. While Vista brought my 1501 down to it's knees with slow unreliable performance, Ubuntu works like a charm, even with all the eye candy from Compiz turned on. 

The only problems I have run into have been associated with getting Flash videos to work properly without constantly crashing Firefox. However there are a lot of advice on these forums on how to fix this problem.
