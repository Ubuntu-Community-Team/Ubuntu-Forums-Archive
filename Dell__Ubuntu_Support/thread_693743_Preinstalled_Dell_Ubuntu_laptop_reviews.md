### Post by notwen on 2008-02-11
I ordered my Dell Inspiron 1420n as soon as they hit the website.  I have been needing a laptop for a while and when these came out I quickly supported Dell in offering Linux to the consumer.

1.  Everything worked out of the box, no issues what so ever.  Compiz has the Intel X3100 graphics chipset blacklisted, you may want to consider the Nvidia card offered.  Hibernate/Suspend were iffy a times depending on the current kernel.

2. > Intel® Core™ 2 Duo T5450 (1.66GHz/667Mhz FSB/2MB cache)
Jet Black
2GB Shared Dual Channel DDR2 at 667MHz
High Resolution, glossy widescreen 14.1 inch display (1440x900)
Intel Graphics Media Accelerator X3100
Size: 160GB SATA Hard Drive (5400RPM)
Ubuntu version 7.04
Integrated 10/100 Network Card and Modem
8X CD/DVD Burner (DVD+/-RW) with double-layer DVD+R write capability
Integrated High Definition Audio
Intel® 3945 802.11a/g Mini-card
No Camera
85Whr Lithium Ion Battery (9 cell)
1Yr Ltd Warranty and Mail-In Service
Built-in Bluetooth capability (2.0 EDR)
Free Recycling Kit
Intel Centrino Core Duo Processor

3.  I did not purchase any support as I have been using Linux on and off since '98 and am familiar enough to support myself.

4.  Consider what you're wanting out of this laptop.  Double check the compatibility of your hardware.  Make sure your needed applications will run on said hardware.  If you're not comfortable within Linux too much, I highly suggest getting paid support.  The internet/ubuntuforums.org are a great source of Linux/Ubuntu knowledge, but when you're a paying customer and you find yourself unable to use your new laptop after something goes wrong, the paid support sure beats the copy/paste guides available here and everywhere else on the web.  

That said, I would highly recommend a System76(terrific support) or Dell laptop.  I chose Dell over System76 simply due to the fact it was cheaper and in my situation money is very tight.  Best of luck w/ your future purchase. =]

Should you choose a Dell, you may wish to look at some issues on their Linux wiki page [here]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.10").
