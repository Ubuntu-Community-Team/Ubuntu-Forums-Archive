### Post by PurplePenguin on 2007-07-06
Dell Canada doesn't offer systems preinstalled with Linux yet, but I wanted a Dell Inspiron 1501 because they're VERY inexpensive and I've read a ton of good reviews with it (running Linux).  So I ordered one with Windows Vista Premium (didn't even boot it - just wiped the drive and installed Feisty the second I got it).

It works PERFECTLY.  The only thing is that the wireless needs to be installed with a HOW-TO (there are multiple ways to do this... I found that the ndiswrapper method worked the first time, so I haven't investigated anything further), which you can find on this forum.

The good thing about the laptop is that it seems to use pretty standard hardware.  Linux loves it - except for the wireless, as I mentioned.  But everything else, from sound to video to the screen turning off when you close the lid, works like a dream. 

If anybody out there hasn't bought one yet, but is looking for a good linux laptop, here's some advice:  Dell does not have a single price for their computers.  Search "dell coupons" in google and you'll easily find a bunch of different offers.  I got mine with a $100 off, 1 year of complete care, and free shipping (is shipping free on all laptops? I don't know).

The only thing I don't really like is that no distro I've found will pick up the wireless card.  I love playing with live discs. :D  Oh well.
