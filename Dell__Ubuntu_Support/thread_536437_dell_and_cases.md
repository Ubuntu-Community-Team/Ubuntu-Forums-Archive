### Post by fallencyi on 2007-08-27
It's called "BTX" style as opposed to "ATX" which is what you are probably used to seeing.

BTX is actually supposed to be better for Cooling, & overall motherboard layout.
(it actually makes more sense from an engineering standpoint)

This was supposed to be "THE NEW STANDARD" as pushed by intel, but it went nowhere. People just didnt see 
enough of an advantage to switch.

I don't want to sound anti Dell, I just bought an XPS-410N because I support the fact that they sell systems with Ubuntu pre-installed, but the fact is,  I really don't know if they use BTX for the advantages, or is it just a chance for propriatary lock in?

Either way, it works & is better at keeping your hardware cool, even if it looks funny.
