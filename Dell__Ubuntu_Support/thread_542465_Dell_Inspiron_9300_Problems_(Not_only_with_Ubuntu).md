### Post by packrat1 on 2007-09-25
Just a newbie here, but I am running Ubuntu 7.04 on a Dell Dimension 8300 desktop. (BTW, because Dell now sells PCs with Ubuntu preinstalled there are lots more people with them, therefore... own forum).

Option one (laugh if you want).....

Start off with laptop closed using external keyboard, 
keep your hands on the keyboard connector, 
then as soon as you hear the "post" (first) beep, 
pull the external keyboard connection and open the laptop, 
start hitting your F2 to boot the BIOS. 
Doesn't matter if you can see the screen, 
but when you think it has reached the BIOS, 
close the case and plug your keyboard back in. 
If you reached the BIOS, the monitor should show it at that point.

LOL Not sure if all of that can be done fast enough (get a helper to pull and open?)
-------------------

Option Two (more reasonable)....

Try to find the "switch" which tells your laptop that it is closed. I don't have a laptop, but on electronics usually things like that are a VERY small hole OR similar to the interior light of the refrigerator. Try taping it down, or gently inserting a paper clip (BE CAREFUL!).

Your laptop would THINK it was closed, but maybe you could use the keyboard  like I talked about above (pull the cord AND the paperclip, press F2, plug keyboard back in and close laptop).

Hope you find your answers.
