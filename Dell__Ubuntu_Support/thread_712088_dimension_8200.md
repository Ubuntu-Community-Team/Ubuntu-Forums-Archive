### Post by RedRat on 2008-03-02
first off, we had several Dimension 8200 and 8100 in our lab and they seem to be a good machine. 

I have since retired and I can't remember the size of the power supply in the machines. But you can always buy a fairly good power supply from outfits like Antec and some others. A good one in the 400-500watt ranges runs about $100 to 150.

Hard drives are relatively cheap now, $100 gets you at least 300GB. I think the Dimension supports an IDE drive, depends on how old your machine is.

Would suggest an nVidia card, and this will depend on your budget. I am running GeForce 8600 series now, also have a 7000 series in another machine. Check around.

Might want to stick in 2GB of memory.

You know after looking my reply over, it dawned on me that for about $700, you can get a Dell 530n preloaded with Ubuntu 7.10 and you are good to go. Upgrading can be useful but you can get trapped in dropping a $100 here and $100 there and then another $100 and before you know it, you could have just bought a pre-made Dell. Take a look at the Dell site under the Linux support and see if there is a machine for you.

Depends on what you want to do with the machine and that will also determine your graphics card.
