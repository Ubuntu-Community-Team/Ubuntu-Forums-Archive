### Post by dr_dimento on 2008-05-25
I have been in the same boat. What I did was call Dell directly. I was able to get a really good person on the phone and explained that back home in Canada, there is a model Inspiron 1525 that I really liked that you could get pre-installed with Unbuntu but that I wanted one here in Australia. This guy went through all of the hardware specs to make sure that the 1525 you can get here in Oz was the same model to a 'T', and it was. 

I ordered it, paid over the phone and within 1 week, got my new notebook. I downloaded the Ubuntu ISO, made my own CD and within 20 minutes had a working Ubuntu notebook (which I am using right now). In fact, I am a newbie to linux, but was tired of the whole Microsoft thing. 

Almost everything worked right out of the box. The only thing that has not worked is the 'home' button. I am still trying to find a work around but other than that, this has been a great experience. I highly recommend this notebook. It is a work-hoarse and basically everything works right out of the box.
