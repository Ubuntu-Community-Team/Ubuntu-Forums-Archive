### Post by rmullins on 2007-08-02
The Dell Latitudes are not working well at all.  Particularly with the D630 and D830 series.  Both are using either an Nvidea or Intel 3100 graphics card chipsets which is preventing installation of Ubuntu.  There are some hacks and work-arounds that are a bit tricky to make them work.

The average user should probably not attempt an Ubuntu install if they are running either of the new graphics cards with the 3-D acceleration.  In fact, the video drivers are so bad, That people are having to go out to the Gutzy repositories and pull off the Intel drivers there just to get the gui clean enough to read.

In addition, on the sound card.  I am currently researching a problem with Intel Corporation 82801H (ICH8 Family) HD Audio Controller (rev 02) cards.

My sound is not working, and I had to download and compile the entire ALSA, drivers, lib, and utils.  Upon reboot, my machine can't even SEE a sound card now, so I am trying a few things to make it fly.

If you are thinking about a NEW Dell, I would hold off on Ubuntu until they get Gutsy stable, or something.  This has become quite a project for me personally.

EDIT: Incidentally, you can track this sound-thread problem here;
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=515739](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=515739)

Thanks

Touchpad scrolling is not an issue. If it doesn't work you just have to add the following lines to the synaptics touchpad section of your /etc/X11/xorg.conf:```
	Option	    "VertEdgeScroll" "1"
	Option	    "HorizEdgeScroll" "1"
	Option	    "VertScrollDelta" "300"
	Option	    "HorizScrollDelta" "250"
```

Suspend and hibernate work great for me, and I even have an ATI card.

**The most important thing to remember when getting a new laptop for linux is to not get an ATI card.** I am not saying this out of personal bias but because their linux drivers are unbelievably bad.

### Post by redseventyseven on 2008-04-02
Hello,

I also got an Inspiron 1525 with Ubuntu pre-installed (March 2008). I'm pretty impressed - it was definitely "working" out of the box, just without some of the things I wanted.

Compiz was probably the biggest headache. I'd just about given up on it until suddenly it started to work and unfortunately I have no idea what it was that caused it to spring into life, apart from "turning it off and on again". I did some hacking of the blacklisting settings as per this advice: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=601711](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=601711)

I understand that the reason Compiz blacklists the Intel GM965 driver is because it has issues with the video output. It *is* possible to see video with Compiz switched on. I did it by setting VLC to send the video output to the X11 server. Here are instructions for how to do this:

> o Start VLC and click on Settings, then Preferences.
o Expand Video and then expand Output modules. You will notice several options for output device.
o Select the item Output modules, and notice the checkbox at the bottom right that says Advanced options. Check the box, and now you have the option to select a different output device.
o Pick X11 video output
o Click on Save and you are set! 

It seems from the forums that some people have found the X11 video output to be slow and choppy. I haven't found that to be the case.

Other issues: The touchpad was ok. It looks like Dell have responded to the criticisms that it was too sensitive and responded to accidental "taps" too readily. I have not had this problem. If anything the touchpad hasn't been sensitive enough, taking two or three "sweeps" to get the pointer all the way across the screen. I changed this by editing the xorg.conf settings rather than installing software to control it, because (counter-intuitively) editing the settings by hand was much simpler than trying to decipher the rather obscure gsynaptics program.

The keyboard settings took a little while to stick. I was forever typing at-signs instead of double quotation marks for a couple of days.

The presence of the "mystery" ubuntu-dell-reinstall.iso just sitting on the desktop is a little unnerving, with no indication as to what it does, or why it's taking up 5 whole gigabytes of my hard drive.

Wireless: No problems at all.
Suspend on shutting/opening the lid: failed to restart one time but only one time. Don't know why, hasn't ever recurred so I'm ignoring it.

Overall: Very happy. Still not sure I'd use Ubuntu if I hadn't been brought up on BBC BASIC and MS-DOS or if I wasn't somewhat mathematically minded, but it serves my purposes very well. I expect this computer to last me a while, and it was less than £400.
