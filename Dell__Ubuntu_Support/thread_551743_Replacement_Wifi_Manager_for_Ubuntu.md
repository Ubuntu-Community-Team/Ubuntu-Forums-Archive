### Post by deserthowler on 2007-09-15
I wasn't happy with the Ubuntu 7,04 wifi management on my 1420n.  I didn't like the way it always automatically signed me in, and most of the time on a neighbor's network.  I spent quite a bit of time looking for a simple and clean way to solve this problem.  I got tired of working from the CL.  Although it is an easy CL fix, wanted something better.

At the suggestion of someone on my local User's group, I went to [http://wicd.sourceforge.net/](http://wicd.sourceforge.net/) and downloaded the wifi manager they have.  It's a group of Python scripts that seems (from my limited knowledge of Python) to be an interface for wireless-tools.

I had to uninstall the GNOME network manager but this works great.  Simple and configurable.  Is worth a look.
