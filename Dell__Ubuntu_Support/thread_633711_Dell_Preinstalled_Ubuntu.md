### Post by eolson on 2007-12-06
Yes.  I bought a 630 desktop just simply as a vote of support for Linux.  Sure wasn't because of the deal I got.  Not a bad box or really a bad price (not real good either).   Performance for everyday stuff is more than adequate.  Have no idea how it worked with Feisty because the first thing I did was install Gutsy.  Which incidently seems to work fine.  Would I recommend anybody else to do it?   Don't know.  I'm happy with what I got.  Not overjoyed, but happy.  You can do better dollar wise if you shop around or build yourself.  Not sure about quality wise.  I've been running SETI@HOME continuously for almost a month.  System monitor shows alsmot 100 percent usage of both cores, so the machine is certainly robust.  My 5 year old home built system is as reliable and was about 200 dollars cheaper (less the monitor),  but don't think, even in it's time, that the performance was quite as good.  Close, but the dual cores and ddr ram give the newer machine the clear edge.  It's hard comparing perfomance from then to now because everything is relative.  What was super duper recapable snow grip then is blah now.  But if you want to buy something and have confidience that everything will work out of the box,  the Dell isn't such a bad choice.

### Post by natehall on 2007-12-07
I paid retail for my 1420N. I've been wanting to go Linux for quite some time but never could get it to work right so I figure a preinstall was the way to go. Broadcom is not supported by Linux but Intel is and that's why the 1420N is the price it is: It has Intel wireless. It definately draws in a better signal that my IBM Thinkpad with its Treadnet card. The Dell pre-install has the advantage that if you ever mess up your system you can reboot up everything all over again from the BIOS screen. For a newby like me that was a big help.
