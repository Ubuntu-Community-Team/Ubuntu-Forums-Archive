### Post by AdrianVeidt on 2007-06-23
The key combination to change monitor brightness is FN+UP and FN+DOWN. Obviously, UP increases the brightness, while DOWN reduces it. The monitor is the main  consumer or power, so by default, on battery alone, the screen brightness is reduced, but it can be increased if so desired.

### Post by NilsE on 2007-06-23
That is not normal but should be fixable.

Go into Applications/System Tools/Configuration Editor and under APPS look for gnome-power-manager and look for all the brightness keys and adjust them to your liking.  

AC brightness, battery brightness etc

They are on a scale of 0 to 100%

### Post by nine01a on 2007-06-23
Try plugging the machine into A/C power and setting the brightness to the highest setting using the fuction-key shortcut.

Then, unplug the A/C power and set the brightness to a darker setting using the function-key combination.

It's supposed to remember these settings and automatically change them as the A/C is plugged-in/unplugged.

Hope that works!

btw, the shortcuts on your 700m to increase/decrease your brightness are fn+up_arrow and fn+down_arrow, respectively.
