### Post by RobertGloverJr on 2007-11-09
Don't accept the lack of an option on the internet purchase form to include bluetooth for $20.  Insist on bluetooth being pre-installed on your 1420.  They can do it but you will have to make a phone call to Dell or chat with them online about it.  Make sure the bluetooth is  installed on the laptop and not sent separately .  Bluetooth is well worth the $20.

   The 1420 has an SD reader female opening on the front on the left.  It comes with a blank inserted and the user manual says to always keep the plastic blank in the socket except when you are actually putting a real Sd card into it.  It seems a little hokey to always have that plastic blank in the slot since it sticks out a little.  I have not put an SD card into it yet, but it looks like it should work fine.

### Post by Dellfan on 2007-11-09
Currently shipping 7.04. They said they would be releasing 7.10 "soon". Someone from their Linux group (John Hull I think) definitely stated they would be releasing with 7.10. He also said current users could upgrade from 7.04 to 7.10.

You can see more information here:

[http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.10]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.10")

including the current known issues and fixes (if any) for 7.10

Current machines do not have all codecs loaded due to legal issues. They said they were working on this but did not say what the solution will be. This is easily (but potentially illegally) fixed by loading the appropriate codecs yourself. It is a point and click solution.

It will read SD cards. Not sure if all card formats work but can't see why not.

It has Stereo in, headphone/speaker out & Dual digital mics. Standard jacks... so I think the answer is no.
