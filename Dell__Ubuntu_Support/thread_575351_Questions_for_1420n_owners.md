### Post by ÜbuntuMensch on 2007-10-14
Gutsy does great on the 1420. 64 bit, even. I mean, I was waiting to at least configure the flash player manually, but everything either worked out of the box or prompted for an easy automatic install. 

I've been running it on/off on a 1420 since Tribe 4. Little issues here and there, but overall it has been a very enjoyable experience. Lots of problems with Fesity, and this is why the options on a 1420n are so limited. Not to worry with Gutsy.

I think I have a similar reason for buying:

I got bumped off the home desktop machine when my wife started doing a lot of photography this summer and she needs her photoshop, etc. so only a small Ubuntu partition on that machine. So I got a Dell laptop for my own use.

Don't limit yourself to the 1420n!

1420 even with Vista tax was less for me than a lower spec 1420n.

Like you said, bide your time for price, but you shouldn't regret going with it.

oh, uhm, re: your Qs:

1) paint is fine. I have the espresso brown (matches the Gutsy wallpapers). :wink: It is not flawless. There is 1 little spot where, like, something got under the paint in the process and it's a little bump. I never notice it. The paint wears well and although oily fingerprints show up on it, they clean well. It's really very durable. I carry my laptop around in a courier bag and am not all that careful with what else gets dropped in with it. It has been more durable than I had expected. It has a nice grip to it. The brown is not boring black, but is not anything flashy.

2) I have the 1440x900 and it is beautiful. There is some glare, so I'm careful about where I use it and what light levels are around me. It would suck outside. But I don't use it outside, so it's fine for me. Also, it means I stick to lighter colored wallpapers (so I've actually switched over to the light-colored lion skin). Lots of pixels, and it seems that the text gets rather small. This could be a problem if you have eye issues, but I gather you cold adjust the fonts and display easily enough. If you're working with video or photos, definitely go with the better monitor. Although, now I do have to worry about my wife wanting to use my laptop for photo editing...this subverts the whole reason I got it, but it also gets her using Ubuntu...
