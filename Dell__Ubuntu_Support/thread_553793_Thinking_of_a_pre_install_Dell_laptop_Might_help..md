### Post by b3nr on 2007-09-18
Umming and arring? This is just my humble experience with my new laptop. On forums I often think you don't really get a feeling for the 90% of people for whom everything has gone swimmingly. And this story has a happy ending ...

Well, I was searching for a very cheap Laptop for quite a while; all I really wanted in terms of functionality were Music, DVDs, OOffice, and Wireless web. The wireless web was essential. I have had Linux in the past with varying degrees of success on desktop systems but my awkward addiction to many games requires windows. But I remembered my time with a SuSe system especially very fondly. 

As there&#8217;s no way I could afford a laptop with high enough specs for games anyway, the thought hit me&#8230; what about a Linux Laptop? Ludicrous I told myself, what about drivers? Plus I had done some reading and noticed a tendency for wireless networking to not be very Linux friendly. 

So I found a cheap Asus, lovely machine, and set about working out if I could get Linux to work on it, forums and websites suggested it may be pain, and as I was using a &#8216;well known search engine&#8217;, something popped up, DELL, the vast computing corporation, were doing extremely reasonable priced pre-loaded Linux Laptops&#8230; 

What can I say? I&#8217;m weak. It was payday and I&#8217;d had a few beers, i'd got to the 'confirm' many many times over the course of a week or two and finally cracked. The thought of a cheap laptop of my very own and one with such a trendy Linux distro pre loaded was too much to resist. Lazy? Perhaps. But seriously, it was pretty much the highest spec I&#8217;d seen in my searching for that price with a pre-install of Linux, which I really wanted to part of again. I parted with £350 and prayed it would all work well.

In the UK we only have one choice, the Inspiron 6400. The Inspiron 6400 is not a pretty machine. I had one at work so I knew it was a bit of a beast. But it&#8217;s the sturdy workhorse of thousands of companies across the globe. Its like a Vauxhall car, its not pretty, but the parts are cheap and it runs forever. DVD drive breaks out of warranty? No problem! Your local PC shop is likely to have a stack of them ready to slot in&#8230; Its big and clunky but has a beauty of its own... well kind of.

I plugged it in and did the usual rigmarole. Up popped a loading screen and *bling* I was in. Most of the software I wanted was pre-installed and I was ready to go, it detected wireless without being prompted in about a second. I&#8217;m in love with the dam thing now and take it everywhere I go. Linux is slowly coming back to me, although it was a baptism of fire getting the DVD drive to play DVDs I had paid good money for (I understand this is not Ubuntu or Dell's fault, its to do with proprietary decoding).

Basically it does exactly what I wanted in a budget Laptop. The media buttons on the front work, the standby function works, the wireless works, the CD RW works. It may be the 'easy' way to do it, but i can't say I feel bad about that. It just works and that&#8217;s what I wanted. 

Now I can get to work breaking it as I have fun with Linux again =P
