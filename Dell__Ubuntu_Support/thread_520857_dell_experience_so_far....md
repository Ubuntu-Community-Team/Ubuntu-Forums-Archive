### Post by syssyphus on 2007-08-08
so I have been wanting a laptop that is current and runs linux flawlessly for some time now.

the news that dell was going to ship the inspiron 1420 n from the factory was good news indeed, so I ordered one, getting the 2GB RAM upgrade, and the 160 GB HDD upgrades for free.

After ordering the laptop, (with an estimated 17 day shipping time) I started having second thoughts about the 5400 rpm hard drive and decided I needed the 7200 rpm hard drive instead.

so I called the "order modification team" and after 2 x 30 min hold times on the phone that got disconnected, I called a 3rd time and spent over an hour on hold.

the indian fellow on the other end asked me in broken english what I needed, I explained to him that I needed to upgrade the hard drive on my order, after 15 min of addtional holding, he told me that he could not change the order, that it (the system) was not allowing him to do the configuration because it was complaining that the os (linux) did not support the hardware.

so I asked him to cancel the order and I would place it again online.

sigh...

so order was canceled, I then reordered it with the 7200rpm drive.

then I did some more reading online about the "discount codes" that are frequently availible, and this was starting to irritate me, I really hate this crap where the ordering and discounting is so freeking complicated.

it's like the container stores bi-annual sale, it makes people not want to order anything execept during those 2 times a year.

I decided to cancel again so I could do some digging and see if I could find a valid coupon code...

so i tried calling in to cancel my 2nd order (16 hours after I submitted it) and it cannot be canceled because it isn't in the system yet, evidently it takes up to 48 hours..... (yes i know electricity moves near the speed of light, but evidently dell adds some latency in there some how, perhaps because they are a huge bureaucratic #$%@#$@, but i digress...)

btw, i called my company's rep to see if I could get a discount on the laptop, and he said he cannot give discounts on the inspiron line, only the Latitude or the vostro... 

anyways this crap is a mess, it is too complicated and I really hope dell gets its act together, cause all I really want is:

A decent deal on a decent laptop with a decent os.

for the record, dell support on the phone is horrid, and their complicated pricing and discounts are insane.

dell, if you are listening, please allow us to cancel or change our order online, it should be just as easy as making the order in the first place.

I am going to consider system76 or linuxcertified.com now, i have not given up on dell yet, but I am close.

just wanted to vent, thanks for reading.
