### Post by packrat1 on 2007-09-25
I'm very much a newbie at gnu/linux (using Ubuntu 7.04), but have you tried using an Ubuntu liveCD to boot? If you strill have the same problems, perhaps it is a 
hardware issue. You could try checking if all your ram is working etc.

Last resort, I also wonder if, since your pc boots up, you can copy your data files over to an external HDD (or your laptop?). Then simply wipe your HDD clean and do a clean install of your OS.

Use "Darik's Boot and Nuke" at [http://dban.sourceforge.net/](http://dban.sourceforge.net/)     
PLEASE NOTE THAT IT WILL COMPLETELY ERASE YOUR INSTALLED HARDDRIVES.

Remind yourself which software you want to tweak or add after a fresh install by writing it down before you erase everything.

Anyway, hope you find your answers.
