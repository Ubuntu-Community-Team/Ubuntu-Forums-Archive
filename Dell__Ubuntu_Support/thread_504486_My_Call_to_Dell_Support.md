### Post by WrathofthePenguin on 2007-07-19
Last night I had to call Dell to resolve my issue with being unable to enable the NVIDIA restricted driver. I first went to the "Chat with Dell" choice on their support webpage. I was quickly connected with somebody. When asked what they could help me with, I told them my issue. Then nothing. For several minutes I waited, then added a little to it (mainly to see if they'd respond). Nothing. After almost 15 minutes of nothing I gave up and called the 800 number listed on the website.

In a very short time I was connected with somebody. I told him what my issue was and could hear him typing. He asked me if I was using Windows Vista or Windows XP. I told him "Neither. I'm using Ubuntu".  He replied "So you are using Windows Vista?"  Not sure how he got that out of what I said, but I explained again. He asked me if he could put me on hold while he researched. Sure. A couple of minutes later (not long) he came back and said that he had spoken to his supervisor and asked me if I was running Linux. I confirmed this.

Now at this point I was half expecting to be told that Dell did not support Linux. What he told me, however, was that he wanted to transfer me to the correct technical support number, as he and his coworkers had no training in Linux. He first gave me the number, than realized that he couldn't transfer me. He apologized for not being able to, and I went on to dial in to the correct number.

Once there I was (again) quickly routed to somebody. The problem was resolved in less than two minutes.

Overall it took me about 20 minutes or so to get the problem resolved, but only because there's nothing telling you that there's a different number to call for Ubuntu support. I was routed to somebody very quickly on both calls and when I got the correct person he quickly resolved the issue.

Nice.

For anybody who's interested, the first level Ubunutu Support (at least part of it) is located in Ottawa, Canada.

### Post by gbrainin on 2007-12-02
The line between "hardware support" and "software support" can be a little fuzzy.  When I first got my 530N, I discovered that I could not mount floppy disks (yes, I know, who gets a computer with a floppy drive anyway).  I spent several hours searching the internet for what driver I was missing or what-have-you, without success.

Eventually, I tried booting into the Dell system utility partition (which, I presume, is the same for Windows or Ubuntu machines), and the floppy drive failed the hardware test.  Aha! I thought, it's a hardware problem.  Then, I made the mistake of calling the regular support number.  They couldn't get over the fact that I didn't have Windows installed, despite the fact that I told them I had used their own utilities (which boot up separately, before Ubuntu or Windows is touched) & the hardware failed.

Long story short, I eventually found my way to the Dell Linux hardware support line, who double-checked my results, then shipped me out a new drive, which worked perfectly without any new drivers, etc.  He even asked whether I was comfortable installing it myself, which was nice as it saved me having to wait around the house for someone to do it for me.

My rule since then: Look for software solutions on the internet (here, in particular), and if there's a hardware problem, make sure you're calling the Dell Linux line.
