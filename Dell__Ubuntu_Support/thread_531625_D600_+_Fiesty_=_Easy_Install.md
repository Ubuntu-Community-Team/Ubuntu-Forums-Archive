### Post by R_U_Q_R_U on 2007-08-21
Just a quick note to say that installing 7.04 on an old Dell D600 (1.6 GHz) works great. The system was bought on eBay with XP installed. I booted XP to verify it worked. Then added 512 MB of RAM to bring it to 768. Next booted from the DVD that came with the Official Ubuntu book (2nd edition) and tested Ubuntu using the Live DVD. No partition - used entire 40 GB disk for Ubuntu! All good so I installed and used a wired connection to do the 115 updates needed.

Next I plugged in the Cisco CB21AG-A-K9 cardbus wireless card and rebooted. Got a warning about using "restricted drivers" and it found my wireless network and correctly saw I was using WPA security. Entered the router password and BINGO -- Instant wireless.

After reading the many problems folks post on this board about wireless issues, I am really happy the Cisco card works "out of the box"! Best of all the card was free. My wife's company gave it her years ago when she had a company D600! 

So, even though Dell is selling hot new Inspirons with Ubuntu installed, you can still get a pretty sweet system going for many hundreds less using 4 year old hardware!
