### Post by fondle-em on 2007-11-03
Pretty much any USB mouse ought to work in at least a basic capacity.    I'm using a spare Apple Mighty Mouse at the moment with my 1420n running 7.10 Gutsy 64-bit, which seems to work fine.  Other mice and trackballs have also worked flawlessly for me under both 7.04 32-bit and 7.10 64-bit.

---

### Post by RobertGloverJr on 2007-11-03
Thanks.  Encouraged by your experience, I drove to Staples and bought a Logitech USB optimal mouse for $17.  I plugged it in to the Inspiron 1420  and it worked perfectly with no configuation necessary other than to adjust the motion speed in System.Preferences.Mouse.
   I would advise anyone not using a mouse to buy one with a wire that attaches it to the USB port.  It's dirt cheap, never runs out of power, and works great.  It's nearly impossible to get any real work done without a real mouse.  That awful tap-thing on the inspiron takes forever to use.
   Thanks again for your advice.
