### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-12-23
To a Windows user.  Then when the drive fails, they will say:  "Oh well, Windows nuked itself again, I need a new computer anyway, this one was too slow."

Then maybe you will get the old computer to put Ubuntu on it.

If you want better quality, you have to build it yourself and hand-pick the components.  All the manufacturers today have pushed the limits of "decontenting" or taking quality out of the parts they sell to maximize profit.  OEM drives, RAM, motherboards, there's a lot of crap out there.

I would build a desktop system consisting of an Intel Dual Core 2 processor (2.66 GHz) ~$200, an Intel P35 motherboard ~$105, an Antek 500 watt power supply $50 (on sale), a decent case Antek, Zalman, there are several $100 to $250.  2GB of Crucial, Corsair, Kingston, Patriot RAM, $60 DDR2 800 4-4-4-12.  nVidia 8600 graphics card with 256 or 512 MB of video RAM ($125-$170).  This will get you a fast Linux system.  It will also play most windows games if you want dual-boot with Win XP.  Don't waste your time with Vista.

For a serious gaming machine, there are websites devoted to reviewing gaming hardware.  The system I proposed is strickly for Linux desktop reliability and overall usability.

The best Dell is the free, discarded one you get from a business that just went through an upgrade cycle.  The business-grade machines (like Optiplex) are built a little better than the consumer-grade ones.  Add more RAM, a larger (and better disk drive), perhaps a better graphics card and that is a cheaper avenue to a decent Linux desktop machine.

Just my 2 cents.
