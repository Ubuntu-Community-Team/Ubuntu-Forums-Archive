### Post by gfahey on 2008-02-16
Don't give up!

I've used Linux since Mandrake 7.1. Now I am using Ubuntu 7.10 via Parallels on my MacBook Pro and also on my new ($400 beater) Dell Vostro 1000.

I will say this: This is the best Linux install and running experience I have ever had. Hassles? Sure. The wifi issue is without doubt the most frustrating. However, there is a how-to here in the Dell forums about how to get this done. Follow it to a "T" and take your time. It's worth it.

I have an Apple Airport Extreme and a Airport Express in my 200 year old farm house. I now get wifi in every room at no less then 90%. Every app is working. No freeze ups or unexpected problems (knock on wood). 

It took a little time. Sure. I'd say I spent a good 4 nights (from 7PM to 3AM) working on it. I get an immense amount of satisfaction "conquering" a Linux install. I gave up at one point with the wifi and installed Sabayon 3.4. It ran OK and did well with the wifi but, it was like a one night stand. I felt cheap and used. OK...you get it.

I just want to encourage people here that are ready to give up. DON'T!! When you get it right and show your friends what a rockin' system you have it'll all be worth it. I've actually been using this Vostro $400 beater more than my MBP lately it's been so much fun. So, stick with it. Don't give up and ...participate here! I applaud Dell and Ubuntu for their efforts and have made my mind up...it's Ubuntu forever now. I proudly have my Vostro covered in Ubuntu stickers and get a charge out of bringing it into a public wifi spot. They look and they always are curious. "I'm different" and love Linux/Ubuntu for giving me the ability to be different......and satisfied too.

### Post by gavintlgold on 2008-02-16
I'm glad you enjoy it. I know I enjoy my linux system :) I don't own a laptop, so I can't show it off in public places, but it's still fun. Linux is loads better than Windows or OS X just because of the feeling of being able to trust that you will continue to get good quality updates and new software with no cost to you! No worries about buying the hot new OS, you can check it out and see if it fits you. Or keep it on a separate partition for whenever you want.

Another part I love the most is that you can see some super-cool bleeding edge app (or compiz fusion plugin!) and compile it, try it out. I used to be an Apple fancier, but looking back, it was always annoying, you waited until they announced the new stuff at the expos, and then you never were able to get it right away (and we would never buy the new OS until we really needed it). With Ubuntu, I can see something on Digg or somewhere and immediately play with it, bugs and all. I don't mind the bugs. They'll go away sooner or later. And Ubuntu's default install is certainly quite free of bugs :)
