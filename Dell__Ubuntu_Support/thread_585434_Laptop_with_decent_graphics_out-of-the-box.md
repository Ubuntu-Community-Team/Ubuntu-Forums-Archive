### Post by SnappyCrunch on 2007-10-21
From what I understand, the X3100 is a 3D accelerated chipset, but not a very good one compared to the latest cards, and it uses system memory instead of having dedicated video memory. Still, StarCraft, Counter-Strike, and Marathon are all easily run these days even *without* 3D acceleration, so you don't need to worry about that. 

You didn't specify which Dell model you were talking about, so I'm going to assume you meant the Inspiron 1420N, which is the only notebook Dell currently sells with Ubuntu. Dell is still selling their computers with Ubuntu 7.04, which doesn't come with Compiz-Fusion enabled, so it certainly won't work "out of the box".

The odds of you being able to purchase a new laptop and having everything working in Ubuntu is slim. Currently, Dell officially supports one laptop, and one version of one Linux distribution, and even then not everything works out of the box (from what I understand). Either resign yourself to have one or two things be broken for a year or more; or buy a used laptop where you can verify that everything already works.
