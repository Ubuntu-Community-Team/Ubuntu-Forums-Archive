### Post by notwen on 2007-11-01
I have a Inspiron 1420n, had it almost 4 months now and I am very pleased w/ it.  That said the majority of everything worked out of the box, and what did not work, I was able to get working after some work.   

1.  The Intel X3100 chipset did not allow 3d effects/screensavers w/ it's installed driver.  -FIX-  Simply enable Feisty backports and upgrade/update to install the more current intel driver.

2.  The touchpad's scrollbars did not work. -FIX- [Here]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.04/Issues/Scrolling_feature_on_touchpad_does_not_work").

3.  This doesn't appear to be affecting every 1420n owner on the forums here, but my Hibernate function does not work what so ever, Suspend works flawlessly.  -FIX-  There are multiple threads which you could try, but I tried so many that I lost track of what may have enabled/disabled my Hibernate/Suspend.

Upgrading to Gutsy does not look to be completely safe yet, I'll be waiting for Dell to release a custom Gutsy image like they did for Feisty.  You can browse this Dell Ubuntu sub-forum for more information on what issues the 1420n owners are having while upgrading to Gutsy.  Be sure to browse [Dell's Linux Wiki]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Wiki_Main_Page") for all issues w/ their N-Series PCs.  Feisty page [here]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.04") and Gutsy [here]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.10").  Their support is getting better, but in no way what I would recommend to someone new to Linux.  

Hope all of this info helps you in your decision to purchase a pre-loaded Linux machine.  If you would like flawless out-of-the-box results you wish to look into [System76]("http://www.system76.com/") or [LinuxCert]("http://www.linuxcertified.com/linux_laptops.html") systems.  Both promise full functionality out of their pre-installed Linux machines.  I can't speak for either's suport, but System76 is said to have top-notch support for all of their systems.  Good luck.  =]

### Post by ÜbuntuMensch on 2007-11-03
I have a 1420 that I purchased in August. Even with the Vista tax it was more machine for less money than the 1420N. In the beginning it was tricky, but  I've had no problems since moving to Gutsy, The problems with Feisty were mostly LAN issues.  I ran it dual-boot for a while, but I did a clean install of Gutsy to remove the Vista partition. I didn't know how to do any of this when I started.

The only thing that is still non-functional is the integrated webcam. Well, it functions, just not in connection with any applications like Skype or Ekiga. So...that  is certainly an issue if you care about webcams, be sure to do some research and probably avoid the integrated.


But overall the experience has been very good. I feel like if you are technically proficient enough to write an email, you can probably install Gutsy on a laptop. If you can read the forums, then you can install it to work reliably. 

backup software: you can get all the software you would ever need with Synaptic. It's pretty amazing, you just type into a search engine what keywords you want and a list of software come up for you to choose from. The only problem is having too many choices of good free software. It's not so bad.


Oh, and on your widescreen config, I'm assuming you've got the right drivers for your video card...this did it for me in terms of getting the right monitor:

System > Administration >  Screens and Graphics

click on the selected Model to find your ideal setup, then you get a Choose Screen window with manufacturers on the left and models on the right. In the lower left corner there is a check box for Widescreen Monitor. It took me at least a week to even see this box.



Good luck, and please don't be so sure you can't install Gutsy on a laptop.
