### Post by T-MAN3000 on 2007-08-06
Here's a quote from [Acronis.com]("http://www.acronis.com/homecomputing/resource/tips-tricks/2004/missing-megabytes.html"). For the full article, follow the link. It's Microsoft focused, but it gets the message across I think.

[I]
If you want to get technical about it, a KB should be 1024 bytes, not 1,000, because you're dealing with binary (base 2) notation. But when most people, including marketers, say "Kb," they mean 1,000 bytes. Similarly, a megabyte (MB) should be 1,024 KB, not 1,000, and so on. That terminological leakage adds up by the time you get into multiple gigabytes. However your computer reckons such things as disk capacity in binary and to a computer, a KB is 1,024 bytes. This is the standard practice in the industry and even though there has been a lawsuit filed challenging the "truth in advertising" over the 1,024 vs. 1,000 byte debate, it's not likely to change.[/I]

So the 20 GB are not missing, they weren't even there, it was just false advertisement.
