### Post by notwen on 2008-04-08
Ordered my Inspiron 1420n pretty soon after they started offering pre-installed Ubuntu systems.  I experienced all of the issues dealing w/ the Intel X3100 chipset in both Feisty and Gutsy, but their Linux Wiki has good documentation on fixes/workarounds for said issues.  

As far as ordering went I ordered online just to avoid purchasing over the telephone.  Haven't had to phone support, I'd much rather figure it out on my own, than deal w/ support from a book being read to me by someone over the phone.  From what's been said her eon the forums, Dell support is somewhat baffled when you bring up Ubuntu Linux unless you specifically dial in to their Linux support line.  I understand once you've got the Linux support team they were very helpful in resolving the user's issues.  

In my experience w/ my Dell Inspiron 1420n, I have been nothing but satisfied and am happy I supported Dell's offering of Linux on their machines, hopefully other OEMs will follow suit.  I only have mediocre experience and would still recommend it to anyone **willing to learn** something new.  For the most part, you can solve lots of problems by copying/pasting commands into a terminal.  That is the case for Dell's Ubuntu systems, most workaround involve a couple of  commands(provided) in term or a simple .deb(double-click) to install.  
  =]
+1 for Dell-buntu.

### Post by natehall on 2008-04-09
I ordered my 1420N back in October. At the time the graphics card had issues so some of the screensavers would lock up the machine. I figured everything should work out of the box so I called tech support about it. Sad to say the guy I got knew nothing about it and he could not find anybody that did! 
    The experiance was not all bad. I posted on the Dell Linux site and they gave me a specific number to call and were much more helpful. By this time I'd figured out a workaround from this site and didn't need the help anymore.
   I'm glad I bought a Linux only machine. If I had bought a Windows machine I would have tried to set up a dual boot machine, just because I hate to buy something and just have to throw it away. Its made me dive into Ubuntu and I'm loving it!
