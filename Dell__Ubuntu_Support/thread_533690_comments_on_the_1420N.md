### Post by purplenite on 2007-08-24
I have a 1420n, and it works pretty well. The issues you will run into:

1. It comes preloaded with the incorrect video card driver. DO NOT enable desktop effects or a screensaver until you have fixed this problem. There is a fix somewhere on the forums; unfortunately, I can't find it back at the moment.

2. The touchpad doesn't scroll at first. Fix: [here]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.04/Issues/Scrolling_feature_on_touchpad_does_not_work")

3. Problems returning from suspend. I still haven't been able to fix this one :(

So basically, you have to do a little work to get everything working fine. And if anything gets messed up, Dell provides a nice system restore feature.

### Post by teich on 2007-08-24
Hi,

I bought a 1420N within the last month and have had the following problems, from mild to serious:

* screen and slocate had to be reinstalled for them to work without using sudo to run them.
* Occasional keyboard flukes where the last letter I pressed will think it is still being held down.  Minor.
* Occasional spontaneous shutdown on booting.  When this happens, the fan is running like crazy.  It's never happened twice in a row, and if I hear the fan on bootup I can shutdown and restart without a problem.  Very concerning.  I may be sending it back.  (Has anyone else seen this?)

Other than that, it's great.

### Post by notwen on 2007-08-28
I've had my 1420n for nearly a month and overall I'm pleased.  Flawless wireless and a full range of screen resolutions for my hi-res glossy screen. =]  To have those 2 work out of the box was worth it alone for me, as for the touchpad scrolling, it is easily fixed.  The only issues I'm having are hibernate and suspending.  Hibernate will sometime shut my system down completely or start hibernating and simply refuse to wake up.  My suspend works fine, although there's a 50/50 chance I will not have sounds when I wake the machine up and sometimes it does not go into suspend upon closing the lid.  I'm hoping Gutsy will cover all the drivers added into this pre-installed Feisty and also include some fixes for the issues that are still on-going.  Depending on how much you like tinkering, if at all, the 1420 may or may not be for you.  Good luck w/ whatever you decide to purchase. =]
