### Post by aldenhg on 2007-08-17
The laptop itself shouldn't wear out too quickly. There are a whole host of little things that can go wrong - your hard drive could die, your memory could go bad, the backlight might go out - but these are all standard problems with any laptop (or desktop, for that matter). The biggest thing that you'll notice is that after a year your batter probably won't last as long as it used to. The main reason for this is that the batter loses capacity when it's warm. An easy fix to keep it lasting long is to remove the battery once it is fully charged if you're going to be working on the computer with it plugged in.

### Post by r_l on 2007-08-20
I have a Dell Inspiron 6000.  I don't keep it runing continuously - I switch it off or standby it when I am not using it (except my Feisty standby is not working).  However, I would not deliberately stop using it when I need to work on it (for example, mine has been on for at least 6 hours now since this morning and I won't switch it off at least for another 3 hours).

The big thing about leaving a laptop on for a long time is the heat (and then also worn out the fan).  In this sense Dell is a good choice as they run relatively cool (as least Inspiron does) - good design.  High heat also will kill your battery faster (keeping your battery cool lengthen its life).

My suggestion is that if you want to run a laptop for long hours, use some cooling pad so that it has enough ventilation underneath the drive (and dim down the monitor perhaps).  I set it on a "rack" so the harddisk often stay at about 33 - 38 C when inactive. When I am using it on my lap, I always set it at a slight angle to allow ventilation underneath (and it rarely goes beyond 50C).  I have had this laptop for 19 months now and it still running like a horse.  Finally, I do not put it on any soft surface directly when it is on or when it is not entirely cool down yet (e.g., neither your sofa or your bed).
