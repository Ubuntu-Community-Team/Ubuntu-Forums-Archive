### Post by jamesbw on 2008-02-10
I just made a very expensive mistake. I bought a Dell Insiprion 530N desktop pre-loaded with Ubuntu 7.10. 

Nothing wrong with Ubuntu, I use it on 3 of my 8 computers (yeah I've gotten carried away with my hobby) and actually use it on two servers at work. 

The problem is Dell refuses to support the systems they sell. I have to send the system back at my expense and pay a 15% restocking fee ($97 by itself!) to get them to take back defective hardware. 

After spending 3 hours on the phone over several days with Dell "tech support" explaining to them that I do not have Windows XP or Vista, they tell me that I don't have software support. (I chose not to buy the 90 day installation support) so they can not help me. I explain to them that I am a computer tech myself and that I have isolated the problem to hardware. 

The system keeps locking up, anywhere from 0 to 90 minutes - usually about 12 minutes after boot. It will actually freeze in the BIOS setup screen sometimes. I tell them I have booted from a known good Ubuntu 7.10 Live CD and the system will still hang after random intervals. I tell them I have also booted the system with Bart's PE (for those that don't know this is a Windows XP standalone CD boot similar to the Ubuntu live  CD). "It could be a corrupted file", "You probably have a virus" . I patiently explain to them that neither is possible as I am booting from known good media using two different operating systems, and furthermore if I enter BIOS setup (no OS loaded) and hang around there for a while (I don't have to do anything) the system will lock up. They insist this is a software problem!

The Dell tech (who didn't know what Ubuntu was just a few minutes ago) offers me a software support contract - then he can help me with this issue. I ask him "If I buy this support, and it turns out to be hardware, do I get a refund?" for the first time he's sure of something - "No". I decline and he says if I'm sure it's hardware - he can transfer me to the hardware support folks. He gives me the number and then transfers me - unfortunately back to the same guys I talked to for the first hour! 

I decide I've had enough and call customer support to find out how to return the system. I get referred to the web site and the rules about the 15% restocking fee. It's boxed up and will be on it's way  with Mondays UPS shipment. A $134 mistake. 

I'm glad everyone else seems to have nice healthy machines from Dell. By the way - when the thing runs - it's great. Very fast. Graphics rendering is incredibly fast. I have an older Dell that I bought refurbished and loaded Ubuntu on - it works great. They just sold me a turd and won't stand by it. Thank goodness I was already familiar with Linux/Ubuntu or I would blame it all on Ubuntu.

[UPDATE] 02-18-2008 Banktec (the company Dell uses for hardware support in my area) sent out a tech an replaced my motherboard and memory. I'm happy to report so far, so good! I finally got to use my new computer for more than a few minutes three weeks after I first received it. The thread should probably be closed, except it does serve as a good "buyer beware" statement. I dread having another hardware issue, although now that I have the direct number to Dell Ubuntu support I won't have to go through hours of explaining what Ubuntu is to Dell's Windows support staff. Although I'm happy with the machine, I don't think I'd go through it again. I would not recommend Dell for any inexperienced Ubuntu users, or for that matter the non-technically inclined.

### Post by jamesbw on 2008-02-12
UPDATE 02/12/2008

Thanks to gbrainin  I got the Dell Ubuntu support number.  Why they don't enclose that number anywhere in your documentation or packing slip is a mystery. 

They scheduled a support call back with me and had me run through all the F12 diagnostics (actually the F12 on boot takes you to "boot menu" but in there is an option to boot from alternate partition. That partition boots you to a suite of diagnostic tests.) They acted like I should know that - my previous Dell didn't have that but it's pretty old.

**To make a long story short we started at 6:20 PM and by 8:40 PM they determined I had 2 bad memory chips and a bad motherboard!** I got a call today that the items had shipped and a tech would be calling me to setup a date & time to install. We'll see how it goes.

The tech at Dell Ubuntu (Ian) was very apologetic and actually quite helpful. He explained that there are only 20 Ubuntu techs at Dell (They are all U.S. based and typically Ubuntu fans) and they are spread across 3 shifts, so only about 4 on at any time.  I told him that's a testament to how reliable Ubuntu is. He said yeah most of the time they are like Maytag repairmen - waiting for something to break. From what I gathered most of the failures are "infant mortality" stuff like my unit is experiencing.

Guitar John - FYI - Ian said the XPS unit is a smaller form factor and runs hotter and noisier. 

So, I guess I got a lemon, but it sounds like they are going to turn it into lemonade for me. I'll keep everyone posted. Thanks again gbrainin for that number. (I better clarify - that number is for the support of the Dell installed Ubuntu).
