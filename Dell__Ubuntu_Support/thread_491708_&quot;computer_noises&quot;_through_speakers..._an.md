### Post by neptho on 2007-07-04
If it's a laptop, this is common.  If it's a desktop, again, this is common, unless you move the adapter for the speakers to a different outlet, or have a power conditioner between them.

It's a side effect of everything getting squished down so small, and various 'line noise' that jumps, to put it in less-than-technical terms.  If you have the volume fairly low, you'll likely never even notice it - and you're not supposed to sleep on your computer - it's a wrist rest, not a head rest!  :)

### Post by MindFlayer on 2007-07-04
I had a similar problem when I plugged my headphones directly to my computer. It turned out that the "noises" were coming from some sound inputs that weren't being used (line-in, microphone-input and so on...). I simply disabled all of them in the alsa-mixer and it's working fine now.
