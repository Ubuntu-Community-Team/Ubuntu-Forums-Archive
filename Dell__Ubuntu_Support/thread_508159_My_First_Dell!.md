### Post by randys on 2007-07-23
I took the plunge about a month ago and bought a Dell laptop preinstalled with Ubuntu Fiesty Fawn.  Little did I know that it would take about a month for it to be delivered. Like many computer buyers I selected all the extras and did not realize until after I had authorized my credit card that the delivery date would be weeks in the future. This experience has been practice in delayed gratification for me. My new computer was finally delivered today and so far I am pleased with what Dell has provided.  Here are the specifications for my new system:

Inspiron E1505N,Intel Core Duo processor T2350 (2MB/1.86GHz/533MHz)
15.4 Inch TrueLife Wide-screenWXGA, for Inspiron 6400/E1505
1GB, DDR2, 533MHz 2 Dimm for Inspiron 6400/E1505
256MB NVIDIA GeForce Go 7300 TurboCache, for Inspiron E1505N
80GB 5400RPM SATA Hard Drive for Inspiron 6400/E1505
Ubuntu Edition version 7.04
8X DVD+/-RW Drive for Inspiron 6400/E1505
Integrated High Definition Audio, Inspiron
Intel PRO/Wireless 3945 802.11a/g Mini Card (54Mbps) for Inspiron 6400/E1505
53 WHr 6-cell Lithium Ion Primary Battery, for Inspiron 6400/E1505

I am a complete newby to linux, but this new laptop has been a easy to configure to my needs and I was able to effortlessly connect it to my wireless network. I am bragging a little, but I am just happy to have a computer that just works, unlike all the windows systems I have owned in the past
