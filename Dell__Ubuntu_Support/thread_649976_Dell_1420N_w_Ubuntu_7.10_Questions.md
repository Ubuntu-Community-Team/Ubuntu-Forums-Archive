### Post by dnvikram on 2007-12-26
I have Gutsy Gibbon (7.10 Final) installed on My inspiron 1420N.I have been using it since last 3months and I havent faced any problems till now with it.Coming to the heat issue,I havent noticed any considerable heat while using gutsy on this machine.At times I leave it on with some heavy downloads overnite and the machine doesnt get heated up out of its limits.Yes,gutsy runs literally out of the box on Inspiron 1420N except the microphone problem.This is a known issue and a bug has been registered in launchpad.Hopefully it should be fixed in the next release or a fix will be provided.Everything else works flawlessly on it.

Now,if you are also considering notebooks from other manufacturers apart from Dell,then go for Lenovo .Lenovo is famous for its compatibilty with Linux distros and as you know Lenovo comes from the IBM stable,its hardware is extremely rock solid and the best in the market.I dont know much about the compaitbility of HP Compaq laptops with all the linux distros out there,but I believe that is also a good choice.The thing is there is no hard and fast thumb rule for choosing a laptop for linux.Some distros run out of the box on one hardware and the very same distros may not be out of the box on other hardware.So,you have to first decide which distro you intend to use primarily for all your internet and desktop computing needs and then go about choosing a laptop which is almost 90% out of the box if not 100%.The remaining 8-10% can be fixed using fixes or work arounds or some special packages.

### Post by notwen on 2007-12-27
I have a Inspiron 1420n(9 cell battery) running Dell's Feisty image and I avg 3.5-4 hrs on a full charge.  Reducing screen brightness can stretch it further as well, depending on the usage while unplugged.  The heat is a non-existent issue, I leave my laptop on all the time, watch videos on trips, constantly move files over NFS, nothing too strenuous, but I have never found it to be very hot at all.  As far as prices go for Ubuntu-compatible laptops, all you can do is research the hardware and other users' experiences.  I fortunately bought my 1420n during their free upgrade to 2GB ram and a 160GB HDD.  I used the saved money to get the glossy 1280x900 screen.  I highly recommend purchasing a Dell-buntu, it supports the OEM Linux cause and also it's more than likely going to be your best financial bet, but shopping around never hurt.  You should build a similar machine at [System76]("http://www.system76.com/") or [Emp Linux]("http://www.emperorlinux.com/").  Good luck w/ your future purchase. =]
