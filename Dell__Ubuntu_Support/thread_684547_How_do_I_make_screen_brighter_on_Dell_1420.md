### Post by RobertGloverJr on 2008-02-01
I am running Ubuntu 7.10 on a DELL inspiron 1420.  It has an  awful laptop screen that Dell should be ashamed of selling.  The reason it's bad is  because the glare is totally awful.   I would never have bought this laptop if I had known the screen glare is so extreme.  It's practically a mirror-- it reflects every particle of light in the room back at me.

   Well, sigh, since I am stuck with this laptop which I would never have bought if I had seen the screen in person rather than buying over the internet, can anyone please tell me how to make the screen brighter?  The screen not only glares,  it is a very dim screen.  I cannot find any option, either in Ubuntu or on the physical laptop itself, to make the screen brighter.

    Finally, can anyone recommend an anti-glare cover  to put over this awful DELL 1420 screen so I can see it better?

Thanks,
Robert

postscript--  Once I learned how to maximize the brightness (see the other posts later in this thread), the glare problem became a non-issue.  I had not realized the power saver was dimming the screen after 30 seconds of inactivity-- when the screen was dim the screen had terrible glare, but now that I turned off the automatic dim the glare  is no longer a problem;  the screen looks pretty nice all brightened up.

### Post by RobertGloverJr on 2008-02-01
Your suggestion to hold Fn key and then hit the Up arrow worked perfectly for 30 seconds... but after 30 seconds the screen mysteriously got dim and hard to read again!!!!   

     So, I went to System...preferences... Power Management and unchecked the "dim display when idle" checkbox.

     Now, all is well.  The screen is a LOT easier to read.

     Thanks you so much!!!
