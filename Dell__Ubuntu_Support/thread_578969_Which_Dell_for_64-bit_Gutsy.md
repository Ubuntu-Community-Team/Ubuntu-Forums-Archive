### Post by John Jason Jordan on 2007-10-17
My two and a half year old laptop has problems and I've decided it's time to donate it to a local Linux group and buy a new one. Because Dell is cool about supporting Linux, I want it to be a Dell. However, the current offerings that come with Ubuntu preloaded are not adequate for my needs. But that doesn't matter -  I've been using 64-bit Ubuntu on my laptop since Hoary, so I don't need support or a preloaded OS. I'm hoping someone here has experience with other Dell models and Gutsy.

Here is what I need (without PCMCIA cards, please), in order of importance:

2 GB RAM
Large, fast SATA HD, as big and as fast  as I can get (120 GB?)
Gigabit ethernet
Dual layer DVD+-

The above are pretty essential. But in addition -

An e-SATA port would be awesome
At least one USB port on the back, for my mouse receiver
Bluetooth
Wireless
A physical on-off switch for the touchpad, 'cause they suck

Money is not a problem, although always a consideration.

And, of course, everything working under Gutsy amd64. Note that I don't mind if I have to tweak something - ndiswrapper for the wireless, for example. Just so all of the above can be made to work.

There must be someone in this forum who has such a Dell laptop and can steer me toward which model I should be looking at.
