### Post by ÜbuntuMensch on 2008-01-08
There are hardware differences between the two, and I also found that the hardware on the standard issue was a better buy.  I've been running Ubuntu on a standard 1420 since September. No problems that cannot be overcome by noodling the forums a bit. Actually it's great.

I would advise you to avoid the 1390 wireless card, though. The 3945 works out of the box. Integrated webcam works fine with a little tweaking.

I recently re-installed Vista for kicks (and the odd app that doesn't work under WINE) and found it to be a major pain. I mean really. First I thought 10GB would be enough, but it really requires 20. And I'm not really even using it. And the drivers! It's a nightmare. If you plan to keep Vista, I recommend you keep the factory install and just shrink the Vista partition down as much as possible.
