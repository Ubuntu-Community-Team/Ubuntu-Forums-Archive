### Post by kuja on 2007-08-17
osx424242, It should run well with any of those to be perfectly honest. Apart from that it's just a matter of how much you want to spend. 1GB of RAM is probably plenty for most people (though I know they had a promotion going on th 2GB upgrade there for a while, maybe still?) Required installation space is more like ~2GB. You'll of course need more for installing more things (probably not more than about 10GB in an extreme scenario though), the rest is mostly for personal files. As per games, I really wouldn't count on this laptop for heavy gaming anyway, as the video card (integrated intel) really won't make the cut anyway, and there aren't any options for that. The Core2 Duo processors will handle pretty much anything nicely, even the slower ones (unless it's a Celeron ... those have always been low budget inferior processors) 

Anyhow, have fun.

### Post by jmnormand on 2007-08-17
in the 1420 go with the lowest processor unless your big into video conversion.  games do not matter as the video card will be obsolete long before the processor and as stated its better on heat and power.

Ubuntu will eat up at most 12gb even after installing your programs.  ive always considered the 80gb more than enough for the primary hard drive.  if your using a lot for video and music you should invest in an external anyway.

as for ram 1gb is enough 2gb is better.  no need for more unless your big into video editing.

graphics both the intel and nvida are running fine on ubuntu.  the nvida will be better for 3d gaming, other than that there isnt much difference.
