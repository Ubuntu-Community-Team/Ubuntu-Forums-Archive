### Post by mojorising on 2007-07-23
You might want to check the BIOS. I have seen issue similar to this on new Dell desktops before (can't remember exactly what it was, darn it) and it was remedied by tinkering around in the BIOS. Maybe you can start with any settings pertaining to the drives. 

I did a little checking and some other people have had the same problem. 

Your best bet might be to use the noapic option in GRUB. Search for noapic on this page ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=12563](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=12563)) for instructions on that of you need them. 

The good news is that there are people running Linux on your model PC successfully. You just need to do a little work to find that one detail that's tripping it up. You can do this by searching these forums (there's a bunch of threads on your PC) and doing the same on Google. Someone has most definitely figured it out for you and posted it on-line.

That should be a decent push in the right direction. If you have more questions, post them and we'll try to help more.

Good luck,

Mike
