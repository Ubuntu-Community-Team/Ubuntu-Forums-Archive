### Post by ph1 on 2008-03-28
I would say buy the Windows box--it's a better machine, a better deal, and it also has a longer warranty--and just wipe out Windows with Linux.  (Or you can dual boot, if that's your style.)  It's pretty easy to (even accidentally) wipe out Windows.  In fact, it's merely a couple of clicks, and Linux does the work for you.

Ironically, you're paying -$600 more for a system with an operating system that itself costs a couple hundred.

Also, Dell XPS's service ROCKS, speaking as a current owner.  So I would definitely go for the extended warranty of 2 years, because it'll actually be worth using if you ever have a problem or question.  And Dells are great for Ubuntu because much of Dell hardware is supported by Ubuntu.

### Post by arkmundi on 2008-05-14
There's a lot of in's & out's of a purchase decision and the research is essential.  I just bought a Dell Inspiron 1525 N - with Ubuntu pre-installed. I took the pre-installed route, so as to have Canonical, supporting Dell, for my machine.  Things go wrong, you really want your hardward and OS folks working together and not pointing fingers.
