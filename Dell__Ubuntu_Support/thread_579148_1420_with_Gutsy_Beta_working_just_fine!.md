### Post by sebald on 2007-10-18
OK, now I'm getting excited... Gutsy beta is working pretty well on this Dell 1420 (not the one with Ubuntu pre-installed) - got the nVidia card and it fully supports the fancy Compiz 3D effects, installed quite smoothly from the live CD, and each successive huge set of updates removed more and more glitches (like the wireless network dying out needing a reboot). If I read that system monitor right, it's using only 368 MB of my 2GB ram with just Firefox, a few terminals, home folder, OpenOffice word processor, and system monitor open. Compare that with about 700MB of RAM used when booted in Vista, and the feel is far less sluggish... I think we have a Windows killer here! I guess what I have is pretty much the final product - Ubuntu page says "0 days to go" at midnight US central time... Good work, Ubuntu!!!!!!!!:popcorn:

### Post by sebald on 2007-10-18
The video is the discrete option they offer,for the 1420,  nVidia GeForce Go 8400M GS with 128MB dedicated graphic memory - the proprietary driver didn't work in the Tribe 5 pre-releases, but fine now on the beta. wireless worked (the 802.11 b and g card) right away, haven't actually tried the LAN port ... even just did WPA just fine when I just changed it over. Basic audio playback working fine, haven't tried record functions with the built-in yet. Firefox offers a built-in plugin installer for Flash and for Xine - with all the codecs installed, and Xine installed, it plays Real and Windows Media just fine by clicking on links, YouTube working just fine... all the basic important stuff :lolflag:

Yes, 7.04 was a disaster on newer Dell laptops, lots of twiddling... even those who bought the ones with Ubuntu pre-installed were a bit disappointed, they even had to do some work themselves... Then somebody said Gutsy Tribe 5 was working well, so I tried that and had reasonable results. And now the beta is just hummin' along even better.
