### Post by jpl80 on 2007-11-28
Remember Nike: "Just Do It"

Ubuntu will perform hardware detection on install and hopefully will detect all of your hardware perfectly. You will not have to go in afterwards and perform tons of driver installs. Most of your hardware should be recognized. Notice I said "most."

If something on your computer is not working after the install, there is probably a workaround. Just search the forum. If you are unable to find anything, report it on this forum and someone should be able to help you. You may have to install additional packages/drivers. There isn't anything that I haven't been able to get to work.

What do you install next? That is entirely up to you. Explore the Synaptic package manager for thousands of applications. 

It's a whole different ball game with Linux/Ubuntu. Get ready to have complete control of your operating system. The caveat: there's a learning curve.

Note: I recommend against doing a dual-boot setup. Just wipe the hard drive clean and run a totally Ubuntu machine.
