### Post by vwbeamer on 2007-07-05
Hi, I'm a newbie, but I can help with this one.

On your dell, restart the computer. You will see a screen with the dell logo. On my Dell, I hit F12 for the boot menu. You have to act fast, because it will flash up there fast and have to hit the right funtion key before it starts booting from the hard drive. You function key may be different, but it will flash on the screen when it's first booting up.

Once you hit f12 or what ever key it is for your dell, you will get a menu of boot devices, hard drive, cd-rom, floppy. USB maybe on there also. no matter, highlight cd-rom (or whatever optical drive it shows, might say DVD-rom, or whatever depending,) install your disk, then hit enter.

It's all automatic from there.  Now, it will take longer to boot from the CD-Rom, because it's moving stuff back and forth in the memory from the CD.So just wait.

 Once you install Ubuntu, it loads much faster, faster then Windows XP also.

### Post by Medieval Cow on 2007-07-06
Setting up a dual-boot system is a great idea, you don't want to be too hasty in wiping out your current OS. There's a really helpful guide to planning your partitions [here]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partitioning"). As far as the wireless goes, definitely try some searches on the forum. The 1505 wireless should just work out of the box. Did you click on the network manager in the system tray area and pick a network to connect to? Ubuntu will see the networks automatically if your wireless is working, but it won't connect to the wireless ones automatically until you tell it to do so.
