### Post by bigfox on 2007-12-22
You will need to go into your BIOS to change the boot order.
Unfortunately, methods for doing this very widely from system to system.

Usually you must press one of the fallowing keys before the POST beep to get into the BIOS

I have seen: F1, F2, F10, Del.

Once in the BIOS, you need to find the boot order or startup device priority or something to that effect.  Again, BIOS's very widely on where this is and what it is called.


Also, I like installing off the Alternate CD.  It often does a better job installing successfully.
