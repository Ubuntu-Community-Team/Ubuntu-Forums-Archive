### Post by kidcharles on 2007-06-12
I just received my new Ubuntu-loaded Inspiron notebook computer yesterday and have had a chance to try it out a bit. Here's a few thoughts so far:

Cons:

The design of the laptop case itself is atrocious. There is no unified concept or color: the base is black, the top is silver, the sides are glossy white, the indicator lights for power, wifi, etc. are green but the front A/V controls are backlit with blue. It literally offends me to look at it. I'm sure I'll get used to it, but it will take time, a lot of time. Luckily I have complete control over what I see on the screen itself.

The audio signal coming out of the headphone jack has enough electronic pickup noise to be highly noticeable when I use my in-ear headphones (Shure e2c). These particular headphones are highly sensitive and tend to accentuate higher frequencies. Hooking it up to my hi-fi stereo however sounds very good, so the audio output is great except for the noise floor. It's not something most people are going to notice, but just a word of warning for those with high-sensitivity headphones and/or excellent hearing.

The backlight brightness settings are too far apart. The maximum brightness is a little too bright for a dark room, but the next notch down is a little too dim, in my experience. Overall the screen is quite nice though (WXGA+).

The base gets mighty warm, don't bother trying to actually use it on your lap. I think this is pretty typical of "laptops" these days though.

Pros:

Ubuntu just works on this thing. Wifi worked perfectly once I told my wireless router that it was okay for it to connect. The battery indicator works properly. The laptop goes into sleep mode when the shell is closed. I did try to "hibernate" once and didn't really get my desktop back when I booted again, but I think that might be beryl, which I have been playing with and is a bit buggy. I did a full update and nothing broke. It took me awhile to get everything I wanted going (DVD playback, Amarok, xine) but it wasn't too difficult.

I bought 2 GB of cheap RAM from newegg.com and opted for the minimum 512 MB option from Dell. Installing the RAM was pretty painless, there is easy access to it on the base of the laptop. When I booted it took awhile because the system detected a change in the amount of RAM and thought one of the sticks had become unseated from the socket. How this would cause the RAM to jump from 512 MB to 2 GB I have no idea, if only it were that easy to upgrade your RAM.

I really like the feel of the keyboard, the keys have nice "action" i.e. they have the right amount of spring. The touchpad has just the right speed setting too. I turned off the tap function though as soon as I could, way too much accidental clicking. I've experienced that on every touchpad I've ever used.

Overall I'm pretty happy with it. That's all I can think of for now, feel free to ask any questions you have, I'll try to check the thread on a regular basis.
