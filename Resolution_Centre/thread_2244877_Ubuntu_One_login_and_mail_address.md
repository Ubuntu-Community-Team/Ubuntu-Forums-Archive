### Post by coffeecat on 2014-09-19
At the Ubuntu One SSO Personal Data Request page, you will see two or three fields depending on the age of your Ubuntu One account. For Ubuntu One accounts created within the last couple of years or so, you will see only Full name and Email address fields. If your Ubuntu One account is somehwat older, you will see three fields: Full name, Username, and Email address. Only the Full name field can be unticked. 

When you first create your forum account by logging in from Ubuntu One SSO, if you don't untick the Full Name tickbox, your forum username will be based on your Ubuntu One Full name, with or without numerals to distingush it from other accounts. If you untick the Full name box, the system will use the username field (if present) or local part of the email (if not) to create the forum username. All explained here (as linked from the home page "Please read before SSO login" link).

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2230004](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2230004)

The only sharing of your email is that it is recorded in the forum database. Only forum admins and moderators can see this and it is considered confidential information. You can change your personal settings so that you can "Receive Email from Other Members" and/or "Receive Friendship Request Email" but I'm fairly sure that this does not make your email publicly visible.

It is a matter of regret to the forum admins that the process for creating forum usernames is not more flexible. After the security breach in 2013, those who pay the bills in the Ubuntu ecosphere deemed it necessary that Ubuntu One SSO login be instituted and this directly affects forum account creation. Vbulletin - on which the forum runs - was never designed with SSO in mind, hence the less than ideal setup. There is nothing we can do about this.

I haven't a clue what your malware comment is about.
