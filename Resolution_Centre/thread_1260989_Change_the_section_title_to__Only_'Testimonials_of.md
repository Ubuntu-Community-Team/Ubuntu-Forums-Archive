It is a balancing act, how to manage these threads. The outcomes are varied, and often the OP does not post a second time. Not every OS is for everybody and sometimes people move on.

Some threads are closed as they seem to have run their course and degenerate into trolling / flame wars and the OP is no longer participating in the discussion. If the OP is no longer seeking assistance it is perfectly reasonable to close it. Keep in mind these are support forums.

To be honest, calling such thread closure 'censorship' is a bit much on your part. Censorship would be if we say jailed these threads. In the case of closure, the OP feedback has been heard and remains on record. The OP is also free to return and seek assistance if he or she so desires.

Some threads fade away.

Some threads the OP gets the help they need and they stay with Ubuntu.


What can or should be done ?

1. First, we must recognize these threads for what they are, honest feedback. Sometimes there is a mismatch and Ubuntu will not meet a users experience, sometimes the OP is frustrated and just needs to express him or herself. Sometimes these threads are their way of saying "goodbye" and sometimes they are tolling.


2. We should direct people to proper channels. These forums are for support and not for feedback to developers. As such people should be directed to things such as Launchpad , IRC,  or [Ubuntu Brainstorm]("http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/") .

Part of this also needs to include education. The problem may need to be directed upstream or may not have anything to do with Ubuntu. There are also ways to effect change, and ranting, or the appearance of ranting, is not the best approach.


3. We have in fact started an effort to addressees these threads and will see if anything positive can come of it.

See : [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Forum/Spec/TestimonialTeam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Forum/Spec/TestimonialTeam)

I would encourage you to work with the Testimonial Team if you are interested.


4. If you feel the OP of such threads is mistreated, use the report button and the staff will review the posts. Likewise if you feel a thread should remain open, please report the thread and the staff will review it's closure. Be sure to justify why you feel the thread should be re-opened.
