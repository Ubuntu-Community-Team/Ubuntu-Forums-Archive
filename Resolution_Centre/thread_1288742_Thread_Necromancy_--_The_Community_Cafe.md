### Post by starcannon on 2009-10-11
I have seen a great deal of "Thread Necromancy" thread closures in The Community Cafe lately. I can understand thread closures with "Necromancy" as a consideration, but as a single reason, this makes no sense. If the discussion picks up where it left off yesterday, or 5 years ago, and if people are enjoying the thread again, it is The Community Cafe, why the desire to stifle discourse with wanton "Thread Necromancy" closures?

These forums do not belong to me, nor do I pay anything for them; so, in the end, it is the admin's and the forum owner's right to do as they will regarding this issue, but in the interest of giving my opinion on the matter, I think that the current way of dealing with "Thread Ressurection" is destructive to the Family/Community feel that makes the Cafe such an enjoyable place to "hang out". I do hope that other qualifiers will be added to the process of weeding out threads, and that "age" will no longer be a sole reason. It really is taking the fun outta the Cafe for some of us.

Sinerely,
Rob aka ~starcannon

### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2009-10-12
I understand what you are saying, but at some point if a thread is beyond a certain age it is likely the origional participants are no longer active. In addition, depending on the length of the thread, it is unlikely people are going to read the entire thread.

So , yes, you are essentially starting a new conversation when you post in such an old thread.

I agree it is not always clear when to start a new thread and when to continue the old, but , in general, our preference would be to (re)start up the conversation with a new thread. 

As with all things, if you disagree with a thread closure, please start your reasons why you would like a specific thread re opened and we will consider it.
