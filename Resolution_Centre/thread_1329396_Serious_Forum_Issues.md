The staff really does not read each and every post on these forums. Although there are times when we come across posts  or find spam, the vast majority of the time we are responding to an incident or post that has been reported by the community. I think it is mistake on your part to assume that the staff reviews, let alone condones, each and every post you see on these forums.

It is our philosophy and goal to provide support for Ubuntu, but also to keep these forums enjoyable to as many as possible.

I think you misunderstand the intent of the staff. The infraction point system is a feedback mechanism where we, as staff, can give feedback. It is not a punitive system and in the overwhelming majority of cases results in improvements. The vast majority of people appreciate the feedback and modify their behavior.

As I stated in my post [here](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=8329643&postcount=6) I think you are taking the interactions out of context and blowing this entire incident out of proportion.

Please, lets not be prejudiced against members of Linsux. They are welcome to our community, thier opinions, and their preferred communication strategies and we apply the Code of Conduce to them as equally as any other community member.

If you see an inappropriate post please report it and allow the staff to review the situation.

You are of course free to come and go as you please and if the forums are not enjoyable for you, I encourage you to take a break.
