### Post by coffeecat on 2015-09-08
I will address this point only for now:

> **este.el.paz said:**
> And, second, to request review of the decision to move the OPs thread.

The move of the thread in question was done in accordance with forum policy debated and then voted upon by the Forum Council, and not based upon some whim. Let me give you some background to that Forum Council decision.

This is the Ubuntu forum. Not the Linux forum. Not the any old distro that happens to be based on Ubuntu or Debian forum. It's the official Ubuntu forum, funded by Canonical and hosted on Canonical servers. There are 61 distros currently listed in Distrowatch which are based on Ubuntu and which are not so-called official flavours. Linux Mint is just one of those 61. Including Ubuntu itself, there are also [10 official flavours of Ubuntu](http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu/flavours): Ubuntu itself, Edubuntu, Ubuntu GNOME, Kubuntu, Ubuntu Kylin, Lubuntu, Mythbuntu, Ubuntu Studio, Xubuntu, and Ubuntu MATE. Support of the official flavours of Ubuntu is the primary purpose of this forum. Linux Mint is not an official flavour of Ubuntu, and it has its own [excellent and thriving forum](http://forums.linuxmint.com/). 

When faced with an increasing number of non-Ubuntu threads being posted to the main support sections of this forum a year or more ago, the Forum Council debated what to do about them in the best interests of the majority of members here. The debate was careful and detailed and informed by the principles I have described in the previous paragraph, the fact that most of the other 61 non-official derivatives have their own forums or support venues, and the fact that many newcomers to Linux have only a vague idea of the differences between the various distros. We needed to maintain our focus but not to be unwelcoming of those new to Linux who happen to be using non-Ubuntu systems. Our compromise was to allow only Ubuntu and official flavour questions in the first two main sections, and to have a comprehensive set of specific support sub-fora in the [Other OS and Support Projects](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=446) area. This area was opened in its current state as a Forum tenth birthday present to the membership on 10th October last year. The assumption implicit in the opening of this area is that it is a convenience for Ubuntu using forum members who may wish to seek support here for other operating systems that they may be using in addition to Ubuntu. This is clearly stated in the section tagline:

> This forum is for the support of other operating systems and other Linux distros. No official Ubuntu supported distro questions should be posted here. Please be aware this is not the best place to receive tech support for these OSs/Distros and users should look for official support channels for those systems.

Whilst your contention that the OP might get more attention for the particular thread in the Apple Hardware sub-forum has some validity, we are not in the business of formulating policy and then making arbitrary exceptions. If we allow one Mint question in the Ubuntu sections, then we have to allow them all. If we allow Mint questions, then we have to allow Elementary OS questions, then Zorin, then whatever comes next, and so on and on until our main purpose becomes diluted. 

If you wish to suggest a change to properly agreed forum policy, then the correct course of action is to prepare a carefully argued and coherent case, add your proposal to the next Forum Council meeting agenda, and turn up to the meeting (on irc) to support your case.

A final point for you to consider. I have already referred to Linux Mint's own excellent forum. We wish both the Mint project and their forum well. If we were to allow untrammelled access here to Mint questions, we would be guilty of poaching business from our friends there, and possibly even sucking the life blood out of their forum. That is just not right.

I close by quoting some of the helpful taglines we have included in various sections or sub-fora, and which you appear not to have noticed and a couple of which I know you have already been pointed to.

[Ubuntu Official Flavours Support](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=327):

> Choose the most appropriate category for your questions regarding Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, Edubuntu, Lubuntu, UbuntuGnome, Ubuntu Studio, Mythbuntu and Ubuntu Mate.

[Ubuntu Specialised Support](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=125) – which includes the Apple Hardware sub-forum:

> Specialised categories of support for the recognised Ubuntu flavours: Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, Edubuntu, Lubuntu, UbuntuGnome, Ubuntu Studio, Mythbuntu and Ubuntu Mate.

[Apple Hardware Users](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=328):

> Support for users who are using Apple Intel or PPC based systems with Ubuntu.

[MINT](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=453):

> For all your Linux Mint questions.
