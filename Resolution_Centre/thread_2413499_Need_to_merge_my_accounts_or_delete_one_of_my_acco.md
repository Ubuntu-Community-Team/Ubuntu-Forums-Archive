3 is wrong. So that you don&#8217;t have  to speculate any further, let me describe what appears to have gone on:

In 2007, before the introduction of SSO you created a forum account named wnmnkh (if that was you who created the wnmnkh account) using what I shall refer to as email address A. Since then you have not changed that email address on the wnmnkh account.

In September 2013, you logged into the forum from Ubuntu One. At the moment you logged in, your Ubuntu One preferred email address was what I shall refer to as email address B. I don&#8217;t know whether you have used any different email addresses in your Ubuntu One account before or since then &#8211; as I said, we do not administer Ubuntu One. Since the forum software could not find a match in the forum database for email address B, the system created a new forum account named wnmnkh2. (For an explanation of the affixed 2 in the username, see this sticky: [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2230004](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2230004) .

Please be aware that although the initial link between the Ubuntu One account and forum account is made on the basis of the email address, the actual link is your Ubuntu One openID, which is held in your profile in a way that is only accessible by forum admins. Once that link &#8211; by openID &#8211; is made between a forum account and an Ubuntu One account, it can only be unlinked by a forum admin, or by a change of openID. That means that you can change your email in either or both of your forum and Ubuntu One accounts so that the emails no longer match, and that will not break the link between the two accounts.

Be that as it all may if, as I suspect, you have now recalled the email address used to create the wnmnkh account and if it is still active, I have already outlined the best course of action in post #2. Can you do that?
