### Post by coffeecat on 2015-09-11
You missed this bit from the [SSO registration guide:]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2230004")

> If you un-tick your name in your Ubuntu One Personal Data Request page, the system uses the local part of the email to create a forum username.

That's what you did. You unticked your name in the personal data request page and the system used the local part of your email to create a forum username.

There is nothing more that "we guys" can do. It is out of our hands. Canonical IS have done their best to integrate a forum software (vbulletin - which is what the forum runs on) into an SSO process. Vbulletin was not originally designed to work with any form of SSO. What we have done is to add a link at the top of every page, helpfully labelled "PLease read before SSO login", which takes you to the SSO registration guide sticky. If people miss that, we are here to help in this section of the forum.

### Post by coffeecat on 2015-09-13
If you are seeing three fields in the Personal Data Request page, then you have an older Ubuntu One account. People who make new Ubuntu One accounts see only the Full Name and email fields. I have one of the old-style Ubuntu One accounts and I haven't, to this day, found a way of changing the "username" field, not that I need to anyway. Quite what the rationale for changing from two to one name fields in Ubuntu One was, I have no idea - we do not administer Ubuntu One.

I think the system used the local part of your email to create your forum username anyway. The greyed username field in Ubuntu One simply appears to be an echo from a few years ago. You must have specified that Ubuntu One username when you set your Ubuntu One account up some years ago.
