### Post by matthew on 2009-03-24
Sometimes it is difficult to tell whether something is spam or a legitimate personal site and request for assistance. 

I'm not sure how easy it would be to encode our preferences better than this quote from the Forum CoC, which is directed at user signatures, but gives a feel for what we think of when evaluating whether something is spam or not.

> No images or advertising in the signatures unless the advertising is directly related to linux in some way or is a user's personal site which does not violate the guidelines stated above.

Rather than try to write a definitive set of rules, here is a list of things I would use to make the determination:

- Is the poster someone who is already a positive and regular participant in the forums at large? Obviously, if this is their only post, or one of the first few, I would be far more likely to call it spam.

- Is the link only posted once, in one place? Most spammers can't stop with one post.

- Is the content on the site appropriate? If it is outside the boundaries of the FCoC, the choice is obvious. Most of our members' personal sites will contain content that is benign or better, not porn/sales/or other garbage.

In this specific case, I think the post and thread are fine and I am happy to reopen the thread.

### Post by matthew on 2009-03-24
There is no specific mechanism in place for doing this, but what some members have done occasionally when they have posted something they are not sure about is they report the post themselves. In the post report, they outline their thinking and usually say they would understand why the post might not be appropriate. 

I can only think of two cases where this ended up causing a problem; both times it was a spammer who posted blatant advertising for inappropriate material and thought that asking us whether his obvious violation of the FCoC would be permitted because he asked nicely. :)

No one else has ever received a warning nor an infraction for doing something like this, although sometimes the posts are removed.

This would be my suggestion if you would like a post to be looked at and considered. :)
