### Post by coffeecat on 2014-06-23
This isn't the place to continue this discussion as we have already dealt with your immediate problem, so I'll respond to a few important points and then close the thread. Please be assured this is not to silence you. Rather, we welcome constructive suggestions for improvements to the forum, improvements to any stickies that are posted, or anything that aids the user experience on this site. If you have suggestions or questions about the use of the forum, you are welcome to start a thread in Forum Feedback & Help here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=48](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=48)

First – we do not administer Ubuntu One SSO. It was introduced at the insistence of Canonical to improve security after a security breach last year. I can assure you that forum admins are not the greatest fans of SSO login, but we do our best to help those who experience difficulties with it. 

The Ubuntu One that is being closed down is the Ubuntu One Cloud Service. Not to be confused with Ubuntu One SSO which continues and which is the issue here.

**Changing the Ubuntu One name**. It is very straightforward – I have tested this. Go to login.ubuntu.com and log into your Ubuntu One SSO account if you are not already logged in. In the “Personal Details” page, edit your name in the name field, and then click on the “Save Changes” field. That's it. I give you that in case you wish to change your real name to a nickname in Ubuntu One, but this will make no difference to your forum account now. Ubuntu One and the forum are on different servers, and once the link between an Ubuntu One account and a forum account is made, changes to name, email, etc in either account do not break the link, or change details in the other account. Only a forum admin can disassociate the two accounts. 

**Making the “read this first” more obvious.** Believe me we've tried. This is the banner that people were greeted with during the latter half of last year (click on the thumbnail):

[ATTACH=CONFIG]254175[/ATTACH]

You would think that people would notice “Please read BEFORE logging in!”, in extremely large red letters, wouldn't you? So did we. A depressingly large number did not notice it. I really don't know what else we could have done. Eventually, we took it down because people complained about the large amount of space it consumed, even though it was only visible to those not logged in.

So I'll close this thread now and close this post by wishing you a belated welcome to the forum.
