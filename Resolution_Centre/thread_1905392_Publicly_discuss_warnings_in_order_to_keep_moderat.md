The warnings / infractions are sent as personal message for the benefit  of the individual concerned. It may be perceived to them as  embarrassing. We do not publicly name and shame.

> **spielball said:**
>  I also suggest to introduce a  vote system, so that the community can  vote against staff members who abuse their moderator role.

The forum staff simply do not have time to review every post made, we  rely on the community to report problematic posts. Usually several forum  members report the same post. The staff act of these reports and  discuss further if needed amongst themselves. The forum staff do not  actively seek problematic user posts, they have better uses of their  time.

The actions of the forum staff are reviewed by their peers. It is not  unknown for some actions to be undone.  I would add that any member is  welcomed to challenge a decision made by any member of the forum staff  (just as you are now with your warning) in the [Resolution Center]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=123"). It is why this section exists.

Regarding your warning, the comment was not appropriate and the warning warranted.

Please review the [Code of Conduct]("http://ubuntuforums.org/index.php?page=policy") for more information regarding  warnings and infractions, which you agreed to follow when you registered  on this forum.
