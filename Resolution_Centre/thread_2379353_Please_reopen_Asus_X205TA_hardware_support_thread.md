### Post by ZannaStar on 2017-12-04
I just noticed that this thread [https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2254322&page=185&p=13717710#post13717710](https://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2254322&page=185&p=13717710#post13717710) has been closed due to off-topic posting. I am writing to ask that the thread be reopened, because I believe it is extremely useful to X205TA + Ubuntu users, not only for the thread to exist, but for it to be kept up to date. 

As the thread's length and contents testify, the X205TA is a basketcase and since I first installed Ubuntu 14.04 I have been reading it and occasionally contributing to it. In those days it was necessary to compile GRUB to install Ubuntu on this device, wifi support required the installation of Android firmware (and the device has no ethernet port), there was no multitouch and nothing would make the audio work. Thanks in part to X205TA users on the thread collaborating with devs, there's now a patch for audio (which might even get merged some day). Functionality has come one small step after another. Every kernel release brings us something, and the awesome folks on the thread, most especially harryharryharry, keep on compiling and patching and providing us with new bits that work.

I spend (an inordinate amount of) time on Ask Ubuntu, where we close questions about other distros (generally this leads to their deletion, or we delete them by vote). Doing so doesn't have any negative effect on Ubuntu users; hopefully it helps them, by preventing the site being cluttered with irrelevant stuff and ensuring community resources of time and effort are given to on-topic things.

I am not very familiar with Ubuntu Forums, but it seems strange to me to close the thread (so Ubuntu users can no longer get help) rather than simply to delete off-topic posts. If I am meant to be flagging off-topic posts, I am happy to do so, as long as that is not going to harm me and other Ubuntu users by preventing us from accessing support.
