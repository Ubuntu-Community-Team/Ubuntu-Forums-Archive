You may not be aware of this, but I have to tell you that your posts, particularly the second, display an undercurrent of hostility and aggression, which is unfortunate, contrary to the spirit of the second paragraph of the forum Code of Conduct which howefield has linked for you, and unwelcome here. I'll now concentrate on a couple of points that you raise in your second post. Your suggestion that any staff member would move a thread based on alleged dislike of a particular desktop environment is quite bizarre, and entirely without any rational foundation. As howefield pointed out, it was not clear from your first post which version of Ubuntu and which desktop environment you are using. You refer to Ubuntu, xfce-4 terminal and xfce4 mouse settings. You are new here. Some newcomers are confused about the different flavours of Ubuntu and the different DEs. We are not to know whether you understand the differences, and how experienced you are unless you tell us.  Howefield merely wished you to clarify which version of Ubuntu and/or DE you are running as it would help others to help you. If you don't want to help yourself by being clear, that is your choice, but don't expect others to be helpful if you do not.

The other point is the move of the thread to the Virtualisation sub-forum. Despite what you say, your problem could very well be related to virtualisation. You are running Ubuntu as a guest OS and (probably) with a mouse driver specific to a guest OS. The move was correct, in my view. And there are better ways than you employed to query whether a thread move was appropriate.

So I will return to the tone of your posts. The abrupt, argumentative manner of your second post, the fact that you went into complaining mode immediately, and that you made unwarranted assumptions, all make howefield's action in closing your thread reasonable, in my opinion. I think I would have done the same. If you want the thread re-opened, or if you wish to make a fresh start so that you don't alienate other users here, I for one would want to see a more conciliatory tone. We are all, staff and the wider membership alike, unpaid volunteers here. Carrots are better than sticks.
