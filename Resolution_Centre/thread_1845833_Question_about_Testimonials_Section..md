### Post by Dangertux on 2011-09-17
First , I would like to say thank you to all the staff who work hard to make this place a helpful, fun environment for everyone here.

That being said, I do have a question which I think should be addressed. Maybe not publicly, but at least at whatever type of staff meetings you guys/girls do.

I have noticed several threads in the testimonials section that were closed for no apparent reason, by various staff. Each of the threads in question had two things in common, they were both new, and they were both negative in content about Ubuntu. None were false, just that users' opinion of Ubuntu. 

My question is this, why not leave them open? I think they should be left open for two reasons. One, they may contain positive suggestions for Ubuntu development. Two, closing them (especially after they were just created) gives the appearance that Canonical doesn't actually WANT user feedback. Now I'm sure this isn't the case, that is why I am making the suggestion. From an uninvolved third party perspective it could be taken as "we don't want negative feedback only the good stuff". Of course, that's not what the staff here are about, I know this from the brief time I've been here.

I just think that all opinions should be allowed to be shared unless they are outright flaming or false in nature. That way, users making the switch to Ubuntu from whatever platform have more information to aid them in their transition. I mean if something they want doesn't work, they'll be happier to know before they get there, and at very least will have more respect for the company and the product even if they choose not to use it.

This is just my opinion and something to consider. 

Thanks :-)
