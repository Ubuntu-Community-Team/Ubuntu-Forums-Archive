### Post by coffeecat on 2013-10-25
Several of your posts are now jailed, where they no longer count towards your bean count, which is now 6. You were pm'd by a forum moderator telling you why one post was jailed and also telling you that what we refer to as bean-mining is frowned on here. Bean-mining is the repetitive posting of posts of nil or valueless content simply to increase your bean count. Beans, by the way, are posts in support areas which count towards your post count. Posts in certain discussion areas such as the Cafe do not count.  

I have reviewed the posts that were jailed - I agree with the jailing. One such post would have been OK. The cumulative impression of the six jailed posts is of a deliberate attempt at increasing your bean count in order to get above the 10 post threshold quickly. Please take note of [this sticky]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2034393"):

> If the posts are helpful and have useful content then we have no problem with that. Indeed, that is to be encouraged. But if anyone posts a number of posts without useful content in an effort to circumvent this restriction, they will find that staff action will be swift and uncompromising. Persistent pointless posting is no better than spam and will be dealt with as such. Reporting spam does increase your post count - 1 bean for each report - so you can increase your post count and help the forum in its battle against the spammers by reporting any spam post you come across.

As far as "privacy settings" are concerned, I think you may be labouring under a misapprehension. Access to your profile settings allows you add information to your profile, and adjust certain other settings such as whether members' details are displayed above or on the left in posts and your default message editor. The default profile settings do not permit personal information to be displayed.
