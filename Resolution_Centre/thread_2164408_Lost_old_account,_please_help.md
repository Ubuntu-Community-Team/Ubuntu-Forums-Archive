### Post by coffeecat on 2013-08-02
> **darfsten said:**
> BUT now I am trying to solve my issue so that I don't have 3 different SOS accounts.

I think it would be best if you rationalised your Ubuntu One SSO accounts first otherwise we're all going to get confused! :) Either abandon a couple or contact the SSO admins to delete the unwanted accounts - see [here]("https://one.ubuntu.com/help/faq/how-can-i-delete-my-account/") under "Ubuntu One Single Sign-On" for contacting the SSO admins. I *believe* some people are using multiple Ubuntu One SSO accounts for different purposes but that does rather defeat the object of **Single** Sign On!

Once you've decided on which Ubuntu One SSO account you are going to use, contact us again because...

> **darfsten said:**
> Can you suggest how I would fix this? Can you change the email that's associated to my ubuntu forums account so that it's something else?

Only you can do the initial account association by logging in with your Ubuntu One SSO preferred email the same as the forum account you want to associate with, and with no other forum account already associated with your Ubuntu One account. You see - once a forum account is associated with an Ubuntu One account, you can change email in either or both without breaking the association. To associate your Ubuntu One account with a different forum account, a forum admin has to disassociate the unwanted forum account. We can help you with the email addresses in your forum accounts if need be, but we need to be both agreed on a plan of action, otherwise you might end up in the wrong forum account again.
