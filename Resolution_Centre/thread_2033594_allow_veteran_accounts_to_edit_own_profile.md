### Post by coffeecat on 2012-07-27
> **idallen said:**
> I did not create my account back in 2007 to be able to post spam here in 2012.

But some spammers do. The longevity of spammer sleeper accounts can be surprising, which is why older accounts were not grandfathered in when these arrangements were put into place.

You call your account a "veteran" one, yet you have managed to average only 2 posts per year since you created it. I would not employ the word veteran to describe your account, but rather "little used". I am going to suggest to the Forum Council (the seven admins) that we have a fresh look at the wording of the [sticky](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1836816) (not policy) that you refer to. My personal feelings - which may or may not be shared by the others admins - would be summed up as follows: 

[indent]*"Consider being able to access your user profile as a privilege, not a right, a privilege earned by being a contributing member of the forum community. Contributing means giving help to others in the support sections. A forum member who truly intends to be a contributing member of this community would have posted more than 50 times in support areas if they have been a member for more than about a year."*[/indent]

You have been using Ubuntu for more than 5 years. You have much experience to offer others. Have you considered being more active in the support areas? The [Beginner's section](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=326) is a good place to start.

Another thing to consider is that all staff, including admins, are volunteers who freely give of their time to help keep the forum running smoothly. Although it would not take long to make the change that you request to your profile, it would set a precedent and experience shows that this feeds demand, and the demand eventually becomes unsustainable.

This thread is still open, and it is possible that another admin may take a different view.
