### Post by carusoswi on 2009-02-10
What exactly has happened to the moderation here in the Ubuntu forum?  A thread with a title similar to this was started questioning what the OP of that thread considered excessive action on the part of moderators who had closed a couple of his threads.

That poster, in the course of his discussion, used a term referring to WWII police.  That reference notwithstanding, the thread had developed into, what I believe, was an evenhanded weighing of the issues pro an con for closing threads.

In comes a moderator who takes personal exception to the use of that single WWII reference, pronounces his/her disgust, and promptly closes the thread.

Is this moderator following that poster around the forum, or was that moderator having a bad day?  There are other means of pointing out inappropriate language (if you can call the poster's language inappropriate) other than shutting off the healthy exchange that ensued.

Is the use of that term so much more offensive than all of the disguised use of curse words that goes unchallenged on the forum?  Does the language in question violate forum guidelines, or, have all moderators been empowered to wield their thread-closing powers to impose their own arbitrary language standards upon any thread they happen to review?

Why is it ok to curse (by masking the words with one or more *'s), but a reference to a historical reality is met by a moderator who abruptly closes the thread?

. . . Or, was this action taken because the community has become so thin-skinned that it cannot tolerate criticism from fellow forum participants, even when that criticism occurs in the context of a thread that examines and makes defense for both sides of the issue?

The balance of the "offending" poster's words were not pointed in any nationalistic or ethnocentric direction.

My family includes members from both sides of that terrible WWII conflict, so my sensitivities are not immune to bruising from reminders of that terrible time in world history, and, while my personal feelings to not refute or justify those of the moderator, there are other ways of pointing out language to which you take exception other than stifling the exchange of ideas.

I, personally, find the actions of this moderator reprehensible and self-serving.  Closing that thread did nothing to improve this online community.
