### Post by QIII on 2021-01-16
> **fanmaker said:**
> Before I make that decision, could you please tell me if, in returning to my original user name of grey1beard, I shall also regain my original profile settings, including the avatar picture I used ?

If you tell me which of the usernames you want to have and I associate your SSO login (I will explain "SSO" in a moment) with that Ubuntu Forums user, when you sign in via SSO you will be associated with that Ubuntu Forums user and all profile settings.

> Likewise, will I be able to edit my email address back to my original one that was associated with my original user name ?

You may if you choose.  However, the SSO email and Ubuntu Forums email are not linked in any way.  The Ubuntu Forums email is no longer used in the login process and has not been for many years.  The UF email is like your appendix:  it's not important and really doesn't have to be there.

> I'm afraid I have no idea what the reference to SSO means, so you not administering it doesn't add anything to my understanding of how the problem started, nor how to avoid it in the future.

SSO is "Single Sign On".  Canonical, Ltd, the company that distributes the Ubuntu operating system and provides us with the server and software for us to run these forums, requires that method of signing in to any of their online resources.  The Ubuntu Forums are just one of many of those resources.  SSO is the gate to the playground, but we don't control it.  Your username on the Forums, however, is associated with an SSO account.  Once associated, changes to the SSO account won't affect your Ubuntu Forums account.  Creating a new SSO account, however, will not associate you with a current Ubuntu Forums username.  That new SSO account will generate a new Ubuntu Forums user account.  That may be what has happened here.
