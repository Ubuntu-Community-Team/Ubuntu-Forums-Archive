### Post by pparks1 on 2008-08-17
For production/QA/Dev systems at work, it's either RedHat Enterprise....but most likely CentOS (a RedHat Enterprise clone).  I grew up and cut my teeth on RedHat.  I've gotten certifications in RedHat.  All of my books and work documentation that I maintain is based on RedHat.   

For home workstation use, it used to be Fedora.  And I still use Fedora from time to time...but I've honestly found a happy spot with Ubuntu.   I like the long term releases, the multimedia stuff and eye-candy seems to work relatively easily and the ease of adding the restricted nvidia/ATI drivers is something that I choose to take advantage of.

At home, I'm just getting ready to setup a Ubuntu 8.04 Server for a home file,print,dns,dhcp server.   I've been doing testing in a virtual environment getting DHCP and DNS working with dynamic updates and documenting my learning experiences along the way.  Got a few things to iron out with Samba and I think i'm ready to go.
