### Post by Dragonbite on 2010-12-16
Ok, yes I understand that UbuntuOne is fairly new and may have some bugs, but for the past few weeks I have been using it and love it! \\:D/

I set up a couple folders on my desktop and uploaded files into it.  It took a long time, but that is because I have a slow DSL connection (download speeds maybe at 80 kbps if I'm lucky) and I was dumping 180+ MB of files.  This system is running Ubunut 10.04.

Then I had an Ubuntu 10.10 installation on my laptop and I pointed it to my UbuntuOne account.  *Blam!* My folders were marked for synchronizing and it started downloading all of the files I had uploaded before.

Yes, I have a problem with my 10.04 on the laptop not synchronizing the ~/Documents folder only. So I am focusing on 10.10 and future versions and since it works on the desktop's 10.04 (which is going to stay 10.04 for a while) then I am good.

Since then I have been editing files (articles usually) sitting in my synchronized folders (~/Documents mostly) and seamlessly UbuntuOne has been keeping things up to date between my two systems!

I used the web interface to jot down some notes in U1 for a presentation I was doing at the Linux SIG meeting while I was at work, and when I started up my computer at the meeting they were there.  The formatting, however, was really off (bullets) but the text was there.

While I use Gmail via IMAP, I am putting contacts and their "last known address/email", since things change and some people go through email like underwear (eww!).  I had a few minutes while at work so I put down some of my most common contacts and they showed up in Evolution with no problems.  

Be really cool if Evolution includes a link or button that opens Google Maps to the address in the contact's fields'

I don't have a smart phone (Android or iPhone) so I have no use for UbuntuOne Mobile (at this time) but one of the people at the SIG meeting has a wife who loves her Android. So he's going to see about possibly connecting the two.

With 10.10 I have been very pleased with UbuntuOne.  I also see that it is pretty busy with coming updates (Mobile Photo sync, Windows version, etc.).  

Keep up the good work!  =D>
