### Post by sanderd17 on 2012-01-04
A lot of free ([as in speech]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gratis_versus_libre#.22Free_beer.22_vs_.22free_speech.22_distinction")) software works on a software-as-a-service (SAAS) way.

That means you don't pay for the software, but you do pay for support (companies often buy support from canonical), or you pay for web services (like Ubuntu Music or U1).

Since the software is free, nobody forces you to buy music in the Ubuntu music store (you can also buy it in a regular CD store) or to use the U1 space (you can also use alternatives like Dropbox).
