### Post by samden on 2010-01-14
Doubt it will happen. There's a lot of collaboration between Mac and Windows, but neither likes to acknowledge the existence of Linux.

SkyDrive has a lot of free storage, but it's a nuisance to access, just uploading through a browser. Office Live sounds nice - but you can only access it after installing the Office 07 demo, which is Windows only, so that's little use on Linux either.

Google Docs is very limited as it's stuck in the cloud. Office will have a strong edge on it when that is both online and on the desktop, both synced. That will be actually useful, unlike Google Docs which is a gimmick at the moment for most users who have any need to access files offline.

If Google or Zoho teamed up with Oracle and integrated OpenOffice with Google Docs or Zoho web apps, that would give Office some serious competition. I haven't seen any signs of that though.

I see Office 07 being a great product with no serious competitors that integrate both the online and offline functions. And 07 is only available on Windows and Mac. If a competing service does not arise that works on all three platforms, this will be a major blow for any attempt to increase the market penetration of Linux.
