### Post by Wise Ferret on 2010-11-30
I work on Maverick with many large documents open, simultaneously.
When ubuntu one was working I experienced two unacceptable bugs:

1. The ubuntu one syncing daemon climbed to 200% cpu. When I killed it, one of my files DISAPPEARED. I lost my day's work thanks to this. I have old .u1conflict files to compensate for this.

2. Another large document (and extremely important one I might add) became corrupted. I used ubuntu one web access to retrieve a several-days-old version of it.

3. During my work I received several openoffice warnings about my files being edited by another process after I last saved them.

What kind of design is this?! Ubuntu one should read my files, not mess with their content! It should not be writing into my folders when they do not contain newer versions of my files in the cloud. In fact I have only one computer defined for ubuntu one, so it should not write anything at all! Folks, wake up, we are talking production here!
