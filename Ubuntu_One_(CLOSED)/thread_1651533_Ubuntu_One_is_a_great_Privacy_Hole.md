### Post by olivier.cailloux on 2011-01-08
Just a quick post to say thanks to [WthIteh]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=1210927") for having raised this (IMHO) entirely reasonable point about U1. The service would be much better if it encrypted the data before sending it to third party servers, with a private key kept on the user's HD.

Naturally this can be done manually but it is difficult, most users not so knowledgeable (or who don't have so much time) won't do it, then we will have a world where govs may have access to many people's private data on request. Scary, IMHO. (Let us think about what some past (democratically elected) governments would have done with this.)

Some similar cloud services already provide transparent encryption for their users. Why not U1? After all, that's what a good tool is there for: do the job instead of the user, not require him to work to ensure his and others safety.
