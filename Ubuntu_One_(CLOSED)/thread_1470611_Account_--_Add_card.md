Yes, this is correct. Music will count against your free 2 GB of storage. So, in your example, you can't be on the free plan, purchase 4 GB of music and then add an additional 2 GB of other files in your cloud storage. I see your concern about how this could be confusing given that type of scenario. We don't provide 2GB free storage plus all music purchased via the Ubuntu One Music Store is free on top of that 2 GB. We only provide that you won't have to pay additional money for storage of music purchased via the Ubuntu One Music Store. I don't see this changing for the free plan but we can probably do something different for paid plans.
