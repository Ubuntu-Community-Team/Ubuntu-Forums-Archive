Bug filed a *long* time ago: [Bug #375145 in Ubuntu One Client: &#8220;Ubuntu One should have a KDE client&#8221;](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntuone-client/+bug/375145)

A client was started and a PPA was put up and taken down. It looked really good. Unfortunately, it looks like developer Harald Sitter (@apachelogger) gave up due to either lack of time or lack of help from ... well, y'know .. *some*body.

[https://launchpad.net/ubuntuone-client-kde](https://launchpad.net/ubuntuone-client-kde)
[https://apachelog.wordpress.com/2010/08/19/ubuntu-one-the-kde-way/](https://apachelog.wordpress.com/2010/08/19/ubuntu-one-the-kde-way/)
[http://maketecheasier.com/how-to-install-and-setup-ubuntu-one-in-kubuntu/2010/03/15](http://maketecheasier.com/how-to-install-and-setup-ubuntu-one-in-kubuntu/2010/03/15)

Kubuntu forums discussion here: [http://kubuntuforums.net/forums/index.php?topic=3116547.0](http://kubuntuforums.net/forums/index.php?topic=3116547.0)

I'd really love to see a KDE native client. Unfortunately, nobody would accuse me of being a good enough coder to take a run at it myself.

Edit:

I was pointed to some further information on this topic.

[http://tech-foo.blogspot.com/2011/01/ubuntu-one-madness.html](http://tech-foo.blogspot.com/2011/01/ubuntu-one-madness.html)

Quoting from the bug report:

> Well, Google Summer of Code is over.

And after having spent more than a week shaking a new graphical frontend for the now third version of authentication handling out my sleeve, I have learned but one thing. While you work on building something, something else will surely break in a way that will requrie half a work day to track down.
It is somewhat impossible to develop a KDE frontend while sitting outside of canonical and being in a completely different time zone than the Ubuntu One team.
On top of that I have seen crappy code design, crappy packaging, inexistance of cross-desktop awareness and cross-operatingsystem awareness and unavailability of a stable working target to develop against...

So I would put it as "I am giving up".

Honestly, Canonical ... sometimes I hate you people.

Maybe if enough people answer affirmatively to the question "Does this problem affect you?" in Launchpad it will get their attention and they'll do some actual work.
