### Post by ndefontenay on 2010-03-16
Hi.

This has nothing to do with Ubuntu One I believe.
Evolution supports Gmail. There's plenty of how-tos to configure gmail. I got it working on my desktop.

Also, for the mail client accessible anywhere, you can again use gmail with Ubuntu and use the imap protocole. With imap mails can be read from the client but are not downloaded. That means that if you access it somewhere else, it's still available.

There's plenty of way to achieve this and it's not necessary to use Ubuntu One for that.

### Post by dwarfolo on 2010-03-23
Any client which supports mbox files will do the trick.....technically.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mbox](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mbox)

Anyway I think that this isn't a reliable solution to share your e-mail archives. Growing in size this kind of files tends to become a slow medium when accessed for reading and tends to get corrupted as well. Not to mention that, being no more and no less then a simple plain text file, this kind of archives do not have an indexing system suitable for search purpose.
