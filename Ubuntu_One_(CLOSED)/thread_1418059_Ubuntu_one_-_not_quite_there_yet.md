### Post by plaurits on 2010-02-28
Hi,

After having shared a few files with a friend online using Dropbox, I could really see myself storing and syncing most of my files online, and since Canonical is offering just that with Ubuntu One why not give that a try?

But now after being a Ubuntu One user for a few days, I have to say that I feel very disappointed to say the least.

Sometimes when I start up Ubuntu One the applet claims to be connected and that all files are up to date, though in reality they are not - it isn't even connected to the server at all! It also seems to be crashing a lot, and it is painfully slow to update my files when it actually does work.

I know it is in beta and I'm sure the developers are working hard on fixing the issues, but there is clearly a very long way still to go before they really have a usable product.

I've been a happy Ubuntu user for years and I will still keep on paying for my 50BG each month, if nothing else at least as a way to say thank you for Ubuntu. I really can't think of any other company than Canonical that I would really trust my files to, so I do hope it will not be too long until Ubuntu One is a viable alternative to Dropbox.
