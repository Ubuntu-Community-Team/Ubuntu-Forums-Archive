### Post by hackertarget on 2012-01-12
Like any "cloud" service you are entrusting the operator (Ubuntu / Canonical) uses industry best practice to ensure the servers and systems are secure.

Whether its gmail, dropbox, amazon or ubuntu you are putting your data into a companies hands (and its systems administrators). Depending on the nature of your data this may or may not be a problem. 

It is a matter of weighing the risk, your data may be lost through system failures or your data may be compromised by an attacker.

If you have all your data on a server in your office with no internet connectivity there is a still a risk of fire or burgulary.


In respect to the uploading and downloading of your data, all communication goes over HTTPS which is for the most part secure.


My advice is to weigh the risk and to always have a backup.  :D
