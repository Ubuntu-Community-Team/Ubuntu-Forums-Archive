Will Ubuntu one overwrite my local files?

Say I have Folder1 in my Home folder.  It WAS synced to my ubuntuone account.  Now that I have changed much of File-A inside of Folder1 without it being synced, will ubuntuone now overwrite File-A with the online data?

### Post by egeezer on 2012-06-01
No, I believe ubuntuone will upload your File A in its new form the next time you connect.  But be careful, because you are at the mercy of any interruption in your internet service.  One glitch cost me a lot of files.

### Post by jingaling on 2012-06-01
That's incorrect. What will happen is that when your machine reconnects to U1, it will check the time stamp on the files and the newest version will be synced to your machine.

If you want both versions, you could rename you file to filename-old for example, then connect to U1. U1 should then download the newer file so you have both versions available.
