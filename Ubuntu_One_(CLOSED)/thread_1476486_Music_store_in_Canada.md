### Post by joshuahoover on 2010-05-11
> **jturner said:**
> I too have to express my disappointment that Canonical only offers the "World" music store to Canadians... I understand that there are licensing issues in play, but surely there must be a partner company *somewhere* in the country that Canonical would be willing to do business with, no?

To take a quote from Matt Griffin:

> There's a fair amount of manual maintenance for each regional store we provide and in order  for us to run lots and lots of country-specific regional stores, we  would need quite a large staff. We will build some automated tools  to help us manage multiple stores easier and expand the number of  country-specific regional stores for the Ubuntu One Music Store. 

In the meantime, we have grouped some countries together into larger  regions (most of the EU and World-wide). This helps us give more people  access to the store but the trade-off is it reduces the selection of  music available from the major labels. Most major artists license their  music on a country-by-country basis. The more countries we add to a  region (EU or World),  the less of a chance of having the same artist  terms apply to all of those countries and fewer major artists are  available. There is still lots of fantastic music available from great  artists in both the EU and World stores and we are working with 7digital  to figure out ways to highlight them for customers.Thank you,

Joshua
