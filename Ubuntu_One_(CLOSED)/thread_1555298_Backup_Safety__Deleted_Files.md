Here's my question: What happens if my data gets deleted from the web server?  When the folders get synced back from the web interface, the data is gone, but it's not in the Trash.

Worst-case scenario:  I sync my most important stuff for "safe keeping", but somehow it gets deleted online.  For example: I accidentally click "delete", somebody else accesses the account, Ubuntu server gets fried, etc.  Now when the folders sync, all my most important stuff is gone.

I could leave the original data as is and copy it into the Ubuntu One folder, but that kind of misses the point.  I would double my disk usage with two copies on my local drive.

I wonder if anyone has any thought on this?  Reassurance about why it's OK to backup my data this way?  Maybe I shouldn't and it's more suited for sharing between devices?  What do you think?

### Post by afrowildo on 2010-08-26
Ubuntu One isn't really intended for use with sensitive and mission critical data. Your best option would be to back up your "most important" files to some sort of external storage device, rather than Ubuntu One. If you do wish to use such a service then Dropbox offers the ability to recover deleted files from their servers, although you have to pay an additional subscription fee for that feature.

Also, if this data is of a sensitive nature, your should be aware that Ubuntu One doesn't encrypt any data it receives, so there's the risk that a man-in-the-middle could view your data.
