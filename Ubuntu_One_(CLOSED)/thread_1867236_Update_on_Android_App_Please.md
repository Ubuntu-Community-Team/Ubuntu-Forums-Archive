### Post by pete04 on 2011-10-24
msp,

I have a galaxy s and have found a few pretty good ways to sync music... 

You can tether the phone and manage music via Banshee or Miro and other music apps. just give 'em a try. Miro is practically a Linux version of iTunes -- organizes all sorts of media and works with the Amazon mp3 store, etc. 

Also, Google Music woorks really well (after you wait a day or two to upload all your music). Google Music lets you cache songs to your phone as you like. Before you go and download 50 albums to your phone, though, test it out.  My phone has a weird bug in which whenever I connect to a computer via USB, it wipes out the Google Music cache. It's definitely a phone thing for me.

fwiw, I have ubuntu one and like it a lot for syncing photos to my desktop. It was having hiccups last week, but since the servers cleaned up, it's been awesome. I may get around to trying the music offering in the future.
