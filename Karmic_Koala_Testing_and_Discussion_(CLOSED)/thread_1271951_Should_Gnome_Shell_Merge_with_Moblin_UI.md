### Post by tretle on 2009-09-23
> **bela42 said:**
> Did you bother reading about the goals of both Moblin and gs?

There is absolutely no point in this suggestion...

I would have to disagree with you there, I wrote a blog post about gnome-shell and my opinion that it was taking the wrong approach some time before Moblin's 2.0 user interface was announced and I would just like to highlight something from it. 
The current HIG's are designed around the keyboard and mouse. Hardware manufacturers are starting to push new form factors which no longer use the keyboard and mouse. 
Gnome3.0 is a major step in terms of UI yet gnome-shell is designed around the same old principals. Its not going to be intuitive on touch based anything. 
Although the moblin team say that moblin is focused towards netbook's this is does not mean the toolbar only works well on netbooks. 
They are talking about the entire experience including their own hig for applications, kernal and drivers focused at the atom architecture.
I am talking about the clutter moblin toolbar.
Sorry but I just cant accept that gnome-shell is a user centric approach or that in order to have a user centric desktop we need use netbooks.
There is too much focus on the workspaces and not enough focus on the individual with gnome shell.
Packing everything into an activity overlay doesn't make things simpler, I was kind of shocked when I first saw it as it was gnome that first thought me this lesson with applications/places/system.
I cant stand it when I get a response like "Moblin is designed for netbooks and Gnome-Shell is designed for desktops". Give me some actual valid reasons why Gnome-Shell is better for the desktop over moblin rather than some broad assumptions.

What I want this thread to achieve is to have users think about what they do on their desktops, look at the two solutions and determine which one is helping them be more productive and organized. Neither Gnome-Shell or Moblin 100% fit this goal yet for desktop use but I feel Moblin is at least in the right direction. 
Gnome-Shell as you see it today is pretty much how it will ship, there might be minor differences but the underlying principal of overlay workspace driven design will remain. 
There was no alternatives discussed to gnome-shell when the board decided to use it in Gnome3.0 and this in my view is troubling. 
They say its great for prototyping yet I have not seen any other ui principals brainstormed since its 3 day inception.  

If you want to see what changes I was talking about in the hardware industry then you can view the blog post here ->
[http://tretle.co.cc/2009/01/30/gnome-30-and-user-interface-design/](http://tretle.co.cc/2009/01/30/gnome-30-and-user-interface-design/)

Touch based ui will not only be for mobile devices or the rich and we need to prepare. Even Microsoft and Apple have done some incremental changes to prepare for this which in my view means its coming even sooner.
