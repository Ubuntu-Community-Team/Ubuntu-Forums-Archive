### Post by steev182 on 2009-09-25
Ok, I know what I was doing wrong. I'm unable to disable the inboard radeon es1000 in bios, but I found that I can blacklist it from the kernel. Just by adding 'blacklist radeon' in blacklist.conf, reverting the xorg.conf file to the one made by nvidia and a reboot, I booted back with the nvidia driver, I'm able to use desktop effects, video looks good and I'm going to be able to test gnome-shell. Woop :)
