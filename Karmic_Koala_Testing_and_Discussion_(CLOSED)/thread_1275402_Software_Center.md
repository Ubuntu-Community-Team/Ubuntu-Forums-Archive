### Post by issih on 2009-09-27
They actually do plan to introduce paid for software later, but that is an irrelevance.

As I said several times during the original argument, store is clearly somewhere to go for new things, new users who have never even heard of a repository will know what it is for precisely because of the more and more pervasive "app store" model that various mobile phones are now adopting. New users will realise that they can get software from there, despite the fact that their current experience of a desktop computer has no such thing as a software repository, and they are used to having to hunt on the internet for an install file.

The "store" means paid argument does not wash, as Apples app store has tons of free software and there is no confusion at all, if the price says free it is free, its not complicated. In fact people are delighted that their expectations are exceeded, they go to a store and get stuff free, how great is that?

Store was the right choice in terms of making it easy for new users to get up and running, centre is more ambiguous and does not match what people with no knowledge of linux expect. Ubuntu is meant to be about being easy to use, and driving adoption of linux, this decision was made because those who are already part of the community found any link to the concept of commercialism distasteful, it is a bad decision.

Anyway, as I said, centre will do, and that is the last I plan to say on it.
