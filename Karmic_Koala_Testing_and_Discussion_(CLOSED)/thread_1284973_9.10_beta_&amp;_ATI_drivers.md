### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-10-07
Unfortunately, I have one of the infamous "legacy" ATI cards (no longer supported since Catalyst 9.3), so when I loaded Ubuntu 9.10 beta, I was hoping to find ATI restricted drivers available again.  I had seen various posts about supposedly "new" ATI drivers coming with 9.10.  

No such luck.

Apparently, the video driver improvements in 9.10 are for Intel chipsets, not ATI.

I had read a couple of posts in another forum about the "new" legacy Catalyst 9.8 drivers.  But in actually downloading them and installing them in MS Windows, I discovered that they're just a rebranding of the 9.3 drivers.

Also, I checked the AMD site, and they had no "new" legacy Catalyst 9.8 drivers for Linux.

I didn't do any extensive testing of the open source drivers, sorry.  But, I can confirm that they work with legacy cards.

### Post by markbuntu on 2009-10-09
> **ssri said:**

As far as windows support for old cards, ATI will not be providing new drivers for windows either, just some bug periodic fixes, no new features. And no new drivers for any operating system released after Feb 09 so Windows 7 will also be using old drivers for those cards.

While they may have been a little premature about dropping support for all those gpus they are seriously committed to getting the open source drivers up to snuff. They have released all the documents they can and have 3 or 4 people working full time on the open source drivers alongside the community developers. It is a shift of resources that avoids a lot of duplication of effort and allows ATI to spend more resources on supporting newer cards. 

AMD/ATI's stated goal is to have their linux drivers equivalent with their windows drivers by next spring, and keep them there. It is a huge amount of work considering where they started from when AMD bought them two years ago. AMD has always been a big supporter of open source.

I expect that my 36xx cards will be dropped from the proprietary driver soon after the open source driver has full 3D for it. That is OK with me.
