### Post by Peter09 on 2009-09-24
I know there is an on-going debate about gnome-shell, but I just thought, having played with it for a short time today that I give an impression and ask a question.

First off I quite like it, although it is obviously still a work in-progress. 

One of the things I have always though is that the multiple desktop functionality of Linux is underused. My guess is that most users know little of it and use it even less, apart from the eye candy cube. I rarely use it because, for me, it breaks the 'normal' operations of the standard gnome GUI. I like the way that Gnome-Shell actually concentrates more on the usage of the multiple desktop. This is a strength of Linux. 


I also think that the standard panels and menus GUI has been around for a long time now, all O/S seem to use it. Its obviously a successful format, but perhaps it is time to look again. Providing a successful alternative would be a great differentiator.  

Query..

I had opened Openoffice in one of the desktops and then went to another desktop and, through the file manager, opened a document. I was somewhat confused as nothing seemed to hapeen. I later discovered that the document had been opened by the Application (which was already active) in the other workspace. There was no notification of this. Does anyone know how this is to be handled?

Just some thoughts and opinions :)
