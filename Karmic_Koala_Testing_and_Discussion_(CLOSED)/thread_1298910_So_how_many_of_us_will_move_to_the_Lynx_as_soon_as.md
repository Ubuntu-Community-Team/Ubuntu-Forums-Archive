### Post by ranch hand on 2009-10-24
The linux kernel and Ubuntu seem, to me to be pretty easy on hardware.  I could be wrong but it is a chance I am willing to take.

As for possible damage to data.  Say there is a bug in the installer on the CD and you choose to use custom partitions, you go through all that, looks great and click on install.  The partitioner is broken and  wipes your drive and installs on the whole drive.

There are all kinds of things that could go wrong.  The only problem I have had, and it was when grub2 was being updated every other day, was that, some how, os-prober really did a number on the /boot/grub directory on a Mandriva install.  Seeing that it was there to be part of testing 9.10 I just reinstalled the root partition for Mandriva.  No biggy but I do not wnat to take that kind of chance with my Main OS and its companions on the main drive.

As for turning it off, my bios lists all drives with the option of having them on or off.

I would not use anything less than a 40Gb drive.  You could put 2 installs on it even if both are on 2 partitions.  Two installs would be tight on it.  I like to have about 10Gb for / and about 20Gb for /home.

I do have 8.10 installed on am old computer on a 10Gb drive.  It is pretty full if you want music files or good picture files.

There is no sense in trying to test on a drive that is too small to really use because you need to use the bugger to break it.  I like to have a couple other OS' on to see what affect they have on booting and how file transfer goes.  It also gives you a place to boot to when the Alpha is really hosed and you can't boot to it.  You can get in and edit files and if need be chroot in and run the bugger remotely for updating and such.

I would say that I would like at least a 100Gb drive.  That is just me.

All my drive, simply because I like the size, are 320Gb.
