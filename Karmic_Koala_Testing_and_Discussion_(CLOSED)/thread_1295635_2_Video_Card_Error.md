### Post by bsupreme on 2009-10-20
I looked over my X logs and my Xorg.conf's again and discovered something interesting.

xorg.conf files generated by the current aticonfig **do not include glx in the module section**. So that is why the driver was failing whenever I tried to generate one using aticonfig.

Anyway I have it fixed and all 4 monitors with xinearama running.

To recap if you're trying to setup 2 ati cards with 4 monitors.

-Install 9.10 with only one video card installed.
-Install the ATI proprietary driver
-Install your 2nd video card
-Xserver will fail, use "aticonfig --adapter=all --initial=dual-head" to setup your video cards/screens
-edit/check your xorg.conf "nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf"
-find the Modules section and add glx if it is not present
-you can then use ATI catalyst control center from the desktop to edit your display settings "sudo amdcccle"

My working xorg.conf is below: I'm using 3 24 inch widescreen and one 17 inch (0-1)
