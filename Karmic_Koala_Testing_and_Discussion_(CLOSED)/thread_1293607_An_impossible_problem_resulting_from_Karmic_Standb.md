This one has me totally stumped.

I am testing Karmic on my Dell Inspiron E1705, and wanted to see if the suspend problems had been addressed/fixed.

I suspended once and woke the laptop; so far so good.

I suspended a second time, and the regular "hang on system suspend" occurred.  When I experienced this on my desktop, a hard reset would get the system back up and running.

I tried removing the battery and leaving it overnight (about 9 hours) and the system still wakes into the blank screen.

All I want is to get the system to back through normal boot up so I can get to grub and restart Ubuntu.

Has anyone heard of this / had experience hard resetting a laptop other than what is mentioned above?

### Post by P4man on 2009-10-17
Sounds like something is wrong with the computer (hardware). After 9 hours with no battery (or in fact 9 seconds), there is no way it could still be in S3/4 sleep mode, so if you power it on, its a cold boot. If its not booting in to your bios, then you got a hardware problem. You could try resetting the bios, you could even try removing the harddrive (perhaps its trying to resume from hibernate, bypassing the bios?) otherwise, I'd say its RMA time.
