### Post by ModdTaco on 2009-10-03
Well after going through the 9.04 NBR is wasn't happy with what I was using. So I decided to upgrade to the beta of Karmic Koala. Going through this build I have a few suggestions.

**Flip Launch:** In 9.04 whenever you opened an app the icon flipped around. I found this quite appealing. It has been removed from 9.10. It would be to see this again.

**Longer Default Delay in Moving App Icon:** So when ever using a mouse in NBR you end up attempting to double click an app but it continues wanting to move instead leaving you to have to right click to open the app.

**System Speed:** I notice 9.10 is a bit slower at launching applications and booting. This should be improved, although I'm sure it is being resolved as it is noticeable on most netbooks.

Thats it for now. I'll add if I notice anything later.

### Post by TunaCanTommy on 2009-10-06
I have Karmic (9.10 UNR) running on a Asus EeePC 4G/701 netbook, 4B SSD, Celeron Processor (not over clocked), Intel Graphics. I have 1MB of RAM installed, I plan to bump that up to 2MB.  I installed a 8-GB SDHC and run my /home directory from it. The initial install (Alpha4) put a swap partition on the SSD - I took it off.  It gave me more room for the OS. I watch to see if not having a swap dive causes and "drag" on the system.  So far I haven't noticed any.  It keeps up with me no matter how fast I type.  :)  I can watch movies (.avi), play music, streaming audio and video - including You-Tube.

All the hardware components of the EeePC are working - web-cam, audio, all the "f" keys.  I didn't have to do any special configuration.

As of the latest update, I have about 1GB left on the SSD, and I've used about 15% of the 8GB SDHC.

So far . . a few problems when I update, and I had to reload the OS once.  The reload was easier because I had /home on the SDHC.  But other than that it's been a good experience.  I'm very happy with it.
