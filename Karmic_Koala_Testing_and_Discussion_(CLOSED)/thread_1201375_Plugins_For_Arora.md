### Post by nightfrost on 2009-07-25
Since I'm migrating to KDE, I've been playing around with a bunch of different browsers lately to complement my usage of firefox. The most annoying thing with every single browser I have tried was precisely the lack of an ad blocker. At least it was until a few days ago. 

The answer to your question is privoxy. Just apt-get install privoxy, and add 127.0.0.1:8118 to your browser's proxy settings. That's it, an ad blocker for every browser you'd like to try.

### Post by taavikko on 2009-07-25
Or you can download .css stylesheet for adblocking and set it in use with arora's preferences, example this one: [http://www.gozer.org/mozilla/ad_blocking/css/ad_blocking.css](http://www.gozer.org/mozilla/ad_blocking/css/ad_blocking.css)

download it, place it in wherever you desire, and set the path in arora's settings.
Restart, and voilá. Much of the ad's are now gone.
