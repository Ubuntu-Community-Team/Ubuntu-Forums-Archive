I tend to look at it a bit differently. The only way I can explain is using history:

I began with Gutsy in about February 2008 and when Hardy rolled out I jumped in head first with a distro upgrade!

Pulse audio drove me insane and at about the four month mark I decided to go back to Gutsy. But that's when I decided to explore multi-booting.

So for a couple of months I used Gutsy and Hardy in a multi-boot (in the meanwhile I explored other distros and threw Linux Mint in too).

Then came Intrepid so I started by upgrading Hardy to Intrepid which left me sad. It just didn't work good for me.

But I upgraded the Gutsy partition to Hardy and I loved it! By then the pulse audio problems had been pretty well worked out in Hardy and I was happy.

Then came Jaunty so I upgraded the Intrepid partition to Jaunty and it just totally rocked! Jaunty was my best Ubuntu ever!

So far Karmic isn't so good, but look what's going on: deprecation of hal (that's huge but overshadowed by other changes), a new boot-loader, major changes in usplash (xsplash), Xorg changes in general, etc, etc.

This is a huge project! Now one might argue that Ubuntu bit off too much and there are always those who argue that the dev cycle should change. To that I say, "nonsense"! My Jaunty is rock solid and it's supported for another year, Hardy is also still supported so I'm good to go.
