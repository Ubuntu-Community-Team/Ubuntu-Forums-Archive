### Post by alvevind on 2009-09-25
I definitely think the chocolate theme is an improvement over the strong orange.

Brown looks both modern and exclusive when done correctly, and I think the designers do show they are on to something here. Still some way to go but the direction is good.

From a color psychology perspective and usability perspective a subdued brown as the base color is immensely more usable than a hectic vibrant in-your-face orange. The base color of a theme should be stable and relaxed, which pretty much excludes red/orange/yellow as sensible choices. That is not to say you can't use those colors for other things (such as folders) but they are unsuitable for use as the base, unless what you want to achieve is to raise peoples blood pressure or prevent them from relaxing too much.

The Ubuntu future looks brighter, and probably browner.
