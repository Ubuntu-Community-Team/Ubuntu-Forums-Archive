### Post by Merk42 on 2009-04-27
Another 6 months, another "new theme" thread.

Supposedly a new look is [one of the things in store for Karmic](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel-announce/2009-February/000536.html).

[quote="Mark Shuttleworth"]First impressions count[/quote]
[quote="Mark Shuttleworth"]The desktop will have a designer's fingerprints all over it - we're now beginning the serious push to a new look. Brown has served us well but the Koala is considering other options. Come to UDS for a preview of the whole new look.[/quote]


Of course, we've all heard this before:

Hey we'll deliver a new theme with the new LTS, 8.04 !
Uh, well we spent so much time on securing 8.04, that we didn't have time, so it'll be in 8.10 
Now that it got pushed again, don't worry, it'll be in 9.04...
(personally I predict it'll be canceled in Karmic due to the upcoming GNOME 3)

Most recently, on the [28th of April in a chatlog](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MeetingLogs/openweekJaunty/AskMark):
[quote="Mark Shuttleworth"]: it has taken a long, long time to pull together a design team i had hoped to have that team in place six months ago, but it's still forming i think we will make good progress in the next cycle you can already see a few things that have borne fruit from that team notifications, time zone selector in installer, etc but it's fragmentary i'm pretty darn confident we'll have a new look for 10.04 but i think only pieces of that will emerge for 9.10
[/quote]

Wow less than one week after Jaunty and already excuses for not having it in Koala

Now to the frequent responses.

*Don't waste time on a new theme, get rid of the bugs first!*
The people working on theme, aren't the people working on the bugs.  This is a good thing, since overall a person who knows programming doesn't know graphic/interface design and vice versa.

*It's so easy to change, what's the point?*
That may be true, but we shouldn't need to.  If we want to fix [bug #1](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/1), I'm sorry, but it has to look appealing. First impressions make a big deal, and if Ubuntu is in stores, it will be shown with the default theme.

Hopefully this thread can be used for a discussion on what sort of "new look" we'd like in Karmic Koala.  I myself would like for whatever the look is, to be also in the other *buntus.  Distros like Fedora and openSUSE have a similar look across their DEs.

If Ubuntu plans on doing more than a theme, I hope they do usability testing.  If they do usability testing, they need to test it with "Joe User", because if Ubuntu wants to fix bug #1, that's who they need to appeal to.
