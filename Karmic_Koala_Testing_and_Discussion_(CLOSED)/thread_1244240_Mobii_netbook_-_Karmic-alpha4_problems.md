Ubuntu 9.10 netbook remix karmic alpha 4, classic desktop.   Easypeasy (Ubuntu 8.10).   Tried also via live-usb (iso to usb with unetbootin) Ubuntu 8.10.   Everyone works great, but I have some annoying problems:    PROBLEM-1: When inserting the headphone, the speakers don't mute.   This problem is most important for my friend!  Googling around I found dozens of sites about this, this is a classic netbook issue (intel hda alc66 codec). I tested some posted solutions, failed all.    Clues: When I just installed 9.10 UNR alpha4, this problem don't exists, but at the first update it appeared. (Help here welcome!)    Another option that I have for this netbook (opinions welcome!)  is to leave the newly just installed 9.10 UNR alpha4, that solved the P1 problem, and that works nice and seems stable, and that I have read that it works well (even at its alpha4 stage) in netbooks, without upgrading from the newly iso Install.    As my friend lives very far from me and I cannot help her easily if there are problems, I can (temporarily) disable the automatic updates (except security ones), until November when Ubuntu launches the release version, supposing that the problem has been fixed then.     PROBLEM-2: Wifi internal does not work, keys FNF1 don't enable-disable internal wifi, and the wifi led is always on.    Wifi with an USB adapter goes perfect, and Ethernet also.  There are linux drivers at the pointofview-online.com site (the NL company of the (China made) Mobii), but I failed to install them, configure error. (?)   I think that the driver seems inappropriate for the kernels of the 9.04-9.10.  [http://www.ralinktech.com/](http://www.ralinktech.com/) seems to have newer drivers, probably OK for the newest linux kernels, I would try them if I find an easy newbie step by step installing instructions.    PROBLEM-3: I think that the video does not run at its best (INTEL945GME), it lags a little. In Karmic 9.10 alpha4, desktop effects don't work, and in 9.04 yes, but I feel that it slows down all a little more what it should be.    The netbook is from Point Of View, Holland, [http://www.pointofview-online.com](http://www.pointofview-online.com) Point of View (Holland) says they can give support for Ubuntu 8.10 only, but I want to install the 9.04 or the 9.10.   I tested the 8.10 (in live usb) and easyPeasy (that this based on 8.10) and they don't solved above mentioned problems.    Someone out there with a Mobii netbook?     Any tips, opinions, clues and solutions, very welcome, have a (cold) beer or two!  Thanks to all in advance.    Overall I am very happy with this little machine, fantastic bright screen, a sorry I have to give it back to her owner soon.   banjo.
