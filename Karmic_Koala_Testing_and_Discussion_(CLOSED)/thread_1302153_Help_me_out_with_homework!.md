### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2009-10-26
If you go to the Microsoft directory folders "Windows\System" or "Windows\System32" and delete random files, you can destroy your Microsoft Windows installation very easily. 

You can do the same thing in Linux by deleting random files in the folders "/bin", "/var", "/usr", and so forth.

Many Linux distributions have what are called "Package Managers" that allow you to make modifications to your installation. The Package Managers check for dependencies between the software installed on your computer, and warn you if you try to delete something another program needs. They are not perfect, and you can always ignore what they tell you at your own peril. 
[B]
You can read about Package Managers at [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Package_management_system](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Package_management_system).[/B] Hint! Hint! ;)
