### Post by drs305 on 2009-07-25
It depends on your needs and your personality. If what you have works for you and you don't need any new apps or upgrades, sticking with a LTS release makes sense. Just be sure to upgrade before the End of Life (EOL) of the version you are running. Here is the link for that:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Releases]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Releases")

If you want the latest versions of apps, have hardware that isn't working properly, or just like being at the head of the herd, upgrade with each release.

Karmic is still in Alpha, not even Beta, so I'd stick to more stable versions for production machines.

### Post by CatKiller on 2009-07-25
> **Wataru8675 said:**
> Is it common practice to always be at the front end of the Ubuntu OS'es, or should I just stay with Jaunty for 3-4 updates, then upgrade?

Both of these options are flawed. Karmic is not released until October. That's what the 10 in 9.10 signifies. It is currently in Alpha testing. You should not upgrade to it unless you wish to become an Alpha tester. Most people do not.

The other option you have given is also flawed. Jaunty is not a LTS release. That means that if you wish to upgrade in the future you will have to go through each release in turn. It is only the LTS releases that have been tested for missing out the intermediate releases, so that you can upgrade from one LTS to the next LTS. The last LTS release was Hardy (8.04 LTS).

Stick with Jaunty until Karmic has been released (currently due 29 October 2009) and then wait until such time as you are comfortable that any problems with the upgrade process have been ironed out. Then upgrade to Karmic.

Some people prefer to always use LTS releases. Others prefer to always be using the latest release. People in-between are just waiting to upgrade to the latest release. Only participate in Alpha testing if you understand what that means.
