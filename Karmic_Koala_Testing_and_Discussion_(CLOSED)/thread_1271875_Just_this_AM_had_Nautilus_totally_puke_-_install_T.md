### Post by Jim March on 2009-09-21
Folks,

Nautilus has been having minor glitchies of late, and has now totally puked on me - can't even see the desktop contents on the desktop.

I'm surviving OK on Thunar (even under Gnome), picking it out of Applications>Accessories - from there I can navigate to the desktop and my rig ain't a doorstop.

It's a good idea to have a whole spare desktop around with a command like:

sudo apt-get install xubuntu-desktop

...which will give you Thunar and more.  That way if all of Gnome takes a dump you can boot into an XFCE session instead and keep going.

You could also use KDE/Kubuntu as your backup but in my mind XFCE has a smaller footprint and is overall more likely to boot even if something shaky is going on in X.

(And yeah, despite the dire warnings Karmic is on my sole laptop because Jaunty's Intel video support blows chunks.  "Alpha" or not, Karmic is more stable than Jaunty on my machine.)
