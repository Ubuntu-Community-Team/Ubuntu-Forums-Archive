### Post by emarkay on 2009-10-17
I have been unemployed for a while, and am a former technical writer and editor, and therefore, have been spending multiple hours daily, at times, both debugging my Karmic issues and assisting others with theirs, to the best of my abilities. FWIW, I have been using PC's since the IBM in 1984, and have been using Ubuntu constantly since "Dapper" about 3 years ago.

Recently I noticed the official Wiki for Karmic:
[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Karmic](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Karmic)
was outdated, as is indicated as such: "Note: Karmic Koala has not been released yet. The currently available version still has some bugs. It is not recommended to use this version yet. In addition, this version of the guide is currently undergoing revisions and is not accurate.", so I decided to assist.

I have much familiarity with Kompozer, VLC, K3b and Audacity, and noticed that these items were either missing or were outdated. I spent an hour or more adding information from both the official websites of these projects and the Karmic repositories, and included data on available options in the Ubuntu Repositories and separate downloads that may be relevant from the official sources, such as the URL's or downloads to official documentation and the like. I also edited and added descriptions about these products to correspond to the relevant information the authors included on their  pages.

I was going to post a note here on the Message Boards for others, who are experts in the use of Karmic applications to assist in correcting information there that may be incorrect. Imagine my shock when I saw this note sent to me a few days ago:


"Please do not place personal commentaries in the guide, such as
"EDIT: October 14, 2009 - The above (Playing VCD on VLC) needs to be
confirmed (I do not have a VCD to test). MRK"
No one cares about your personal equipment.
Also, this guide is not meant to replicate other guides. There is no
reason to point to internal help manuals, such as Video LAN docs.
Lastly, do not include exhaustive lists of plugins. A user that starts
to use VLC will find those pretty quickly.
This guide is not meant, in general, to document development status of
every package. Please don't comment on each beta stage. The only
exceptions are packages (such as Moonlight) which have been discussed
thousands of times in forums.
While I appreciate contributions, yours are veering towards clutter. I
was forced to rewrite every entry of yours, most of which were
inaccurate. You ignored package dependencies, added duplicate entries,
gave incorrect links and incorrect descriptors. It takes a lot of work
to correct such inaccuracies. Please be more diligent about your
entries. If you are not a regular Ubuntu user, please don't add entries."

As a stated "in-process" Wiki I left the "VCD" note for others to address.  I validated every link, and ensured that I included all relevant plugins and options from the official authors, especially those that have assisted in my productivity (and, of course, labeled them as options). Upon seeing this note, I asked this person for some validation of my transgressions, but as of this afternoon, I have heard nothing.

I will leave it up to you, my peers, to decide if this is both out-of-line, condescending, a disservice to "noobs" and offensive as stated. As for the official Wiki, I would be embarrassed to recommend that my fellow experts attend to it, being that it is being dictated in this manner.
