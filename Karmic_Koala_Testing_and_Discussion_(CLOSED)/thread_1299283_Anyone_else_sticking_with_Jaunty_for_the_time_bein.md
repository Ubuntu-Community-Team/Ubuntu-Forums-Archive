### Post by CbrPad on 2009-10-23
I've been using Ubuntu since the very first release but I think this is the first time I'm going to be skipping an upgrade.  I know some of the issues aren't Ubuntu's fault, but here are my reasons for giving this one a miss..

A lot of 3g modems don't appear to work - an absolute killer for me, but I think there may be a fix in so fingers crossed.

No speedup when booting - Jaunty was an incredible improvement on previous releases, but I find Karmic (so far) to be no faster and in fact I think it may even be slower.

Bootup looks awful - Nowhere near as streamlined, integrated and smooth as Jaunty.

Lack of customisation - Can't easily change gdm themes etc, not good.

Nannying - Stuff like clicking to shutdown and having it ask me a second time to make sure or waiting 60 seconds, drives me insane.  I know these things can be changed but that's not the point.

Basically I can't find any compelling reasons to upgrade, and it seems to have actually gone backwards in many ways.  
This of course may not be the case for others, and indeed probably isn't, but I'm just curious as to whether there are many more who feel the same as myself ?
