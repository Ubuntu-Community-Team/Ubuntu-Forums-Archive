### Post by shiny11100 on 2009-10-06
open synaptic (under system>administration>synaptic packet manager)

click the reload button and wait for it to finish updating the list

click mark all upgrades and click accept or okay or whatever it says

click apply and it will update

that's an alternate way of updating... if that doesn't work try restarting and when grub comes up choose the recovery option then in the recovery use the option having to do with repairing broken packages... i can't remember what it says specifically but if you need me to i can look for you, after using that choose the continue normal boot option (not sure of actual wording) and use the alternate update method i have above...

hope that helps =)
