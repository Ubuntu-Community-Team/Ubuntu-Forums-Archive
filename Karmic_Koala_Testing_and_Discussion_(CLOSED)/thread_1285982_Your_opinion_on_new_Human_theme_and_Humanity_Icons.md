### Post by Squonk07 on 2009-10-08
I can only echo the sentiments of many in that I love the Humanity icons (I was an instant convert the moment I saw them, a week before they were accepted as default) but feel that the Human theme is unworkable. This is always highly subjective, and the cry is usually, "Then what do you want, then? You can't please everybody!"

My main problems with the Human theme are the color (yes, I know, the brown debate is long drawn out), the shape and prominence of the title bar button controls, and the rounded corners. I don't mind a slight curve, and I recognize that it's difficult with the DPI of most monitors, but to me the corner arcs in Human look imprecise--probably because they're outlined in black but are filled in with another color. I would rather the bottom at least not be rounded, lest we enter Apple's Roundrects folklore.

Personally, I like the Shiki-Colors window shape and color. A dark gray with warm overtones captures some of the brown without making it look like something the cat left on the stair, and--as I used to do with the New Wave window decoration--it should be possible to produce a style based on these design cues without necessitating a "dark" theme. I'll hold out some hope for something like this happening using GTK+3.
