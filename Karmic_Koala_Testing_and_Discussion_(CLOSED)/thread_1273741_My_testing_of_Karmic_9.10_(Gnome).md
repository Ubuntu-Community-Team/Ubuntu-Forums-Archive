### Post by vrkalak on 2009-09-23
I had Ubuntu 9.04 and LinuxMint-7 on a dual-boot on my PC.

The PC is a custom built computer with 4 Gb RAM and a 1 Terabyte hard-drive.
I use it for working (architect) and I apply a lot of graphics and CAD in what I do.

I recently decided to do some 'testing' of the new release of Karmic 9.10, Alpha 6 ... this is how I did it.

I, first, of course, backed up all my files and bookmarks to an external hard-drive.
Next, I downloaded and made a LiveCD / ISO disk of Ubuntu 9.10, Alpha 6 from a link in DistroWatch.  (as a back-up plan)

I then, did a system _upgrade_ using a command in terminal -- "update-manager -d"

It took me 23 minutes to download the 9.10 ISO files on my older laptop. (using a Xubuntu 9.04 OS)
It took the new PC over 5 hours to do a complete distro and kernel upgrade.

I have re-booted several times ... no problems.
The desktop settings and bookmarks are all intact.
Wireless 3G USB connection works.

The Conky seems to be the only thing that isn't working up to 'par'.
1.  The Conky does not start-up on a re-boot, like it did before.
2.  Every 10-15 seconds the Conky 'blinks'

I am checking out the Karmic repositories and the new Ubuntu Software Store.
I don't need to try and over-load the system, as I already know my computer is good, but I will 'work' the Linux kernel and Karmic desktop.

I shall continue with my testing over the next few weeks and keep you updated.
