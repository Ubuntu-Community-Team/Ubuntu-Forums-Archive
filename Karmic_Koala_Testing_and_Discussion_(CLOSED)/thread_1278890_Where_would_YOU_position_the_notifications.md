If the bubbles were positioned relative to a positional coordinate this wouldn't be a problem. For example, say the bubbles are positioned around a local coordinate system where (0, 0) is the centre of the combined area of the bubbles. Repositioning the bubbles as a whole would just mean adding a translation. The translation can be limited so that the distance to the left/right edges are never less than bubble width / 2, and the same for the top and bottom edges of the screen. 

Basically, it's easy to make the bubbles positional without creating any more complexity (besides the addition of the X, Y translation) if they are programmed that way from the outset, instead now any new positions are being retro-fitted to it.
