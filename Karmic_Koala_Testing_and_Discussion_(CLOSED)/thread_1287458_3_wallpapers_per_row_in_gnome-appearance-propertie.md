It used to be 4 wallpaper per row. Having 3 wallpapers per row is not the big deal, the issue here is that we're leaving almost 25% of the window empty. You've got two options: reducing the window so that just 3 wallpapers are shown (or until three themes just fit) or increase it up to 4 wallpapers.

It's also ironic that such an empty space occurs in Gnome 2.28, where many changes have been commited so that we have less wasted space on screen (compare nautilus in 2.26 and 2.28 ).

On the other hand, I do agree that it's a very small issue, and I'd certainly rather have my mute key working than a perfect-sized gnome-appearance-properties. But the developers here aren't the same, are they?
