### Post by Islington on 2009-06-19
[ Pinstack](http://pinstack.blogspot.com/) recently posted a list of 13 things to be excited about for karmic. But looking through the list, I think that karmic is going to be a stabilization of jaunty and not much more.

Looking though this [list]("http://pinstack.blogspot.com/2009/06/13-things-to-get-excited-for-in-ubuntu.html"):
1. New Theme -- don't care very much, I change it anyway. I haven't read anything more/new from Project Atayana.

2. Flawless PulseAudio -- [quote=Daniel Chen (writes a lot of Ubuntu's audio-related code)]
Glitch-free PulseAudio likely will not be enabled in Karmic. There is insufficient traction in changing kernel configuration options, and we will all need to evaluate how those configurations affect other objectives (e.g., improved battery life).[/quote][(source)]("http://drowninginbugs.blogspot.com/2009/06/action-items-from-uds-barcelona.html")

3. Firefox 3.5 -- Firefox on Linux still lags behind windows, 3.5 is fast, but a new version of the browser can be downloaded regardless of your distro.

4. Faster Boot Times --extension of the jaunty push for faster boot times, nice but really anything under 20 seconds is unnoticeable for me.

5. Ext4 now Default --I am excited by this, however shouldn't I still be running ext2 on an ssd?

6. GNU GRUB 2 --current grub works fine, dont really see the advantage.

7. Plymouth -- dropped due to 4.

8. New Linux Kernel- --extremely cool, but basically under the hood, nothing to excite the average user.

9. New Intel Drivers --about time no? damn the current intel drivers.

10. New NVIDIA Drivers --cool, but under the hood. Will users really see a massive shift in performance?

11. GNOME 2.28 (and Testing GNOME 3) -- cool, has the different wallpaper per workspace code been accepted yet? gnome 3.. clutter is interesting I will give you that.

12. PackageKit -- dropped [(see last comment)]("http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/64/")

13. Empathy -- sigh may or may not be a problem, I haven't ever tried it. Reports are saying that this may be a problem.


Looks to me like karmic is really going to be a rock solid LTS, not exciting, but rock solid.
