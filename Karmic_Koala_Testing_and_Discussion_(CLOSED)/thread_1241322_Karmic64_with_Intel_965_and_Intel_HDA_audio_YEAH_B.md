### Post by Jim March on 2009-08-15
Jaunty sucked for us Intel-equipped folk.  Karmic is looking absolutely perfect on the video side and while there's minor glitches, the audio looks damned promising too.

On video, there's basically nothing to report except one tiny glitch so far: desktop placement issues when you have double monitors.  My laptop's screen is 1280x800 and I'm doing an extended desktop to an external 1280x1024 screen (to the right).  If I download a new file to the desktop, it's sometimes placed in the "missing area" just below the display area for the laptop screen.  In other words, the file is there but down in the area below 800 pixels deep and above 1024.

Ummm...yeah...in other words, my total desktop area should be shaped a bit like the state of Oklahoma, except the "panhandle" area (the laptop screen" is almost as tall as it's buddy to the right.  But since the laptop screen isn't as tall, and since the "virtual desktop area" is a huge rectangle of 1024 pixels tall by 2560 wide, there's an unviewable chunk.

It happens with or without Compiz on.  If Compiz IS on, one "solution" is to rotate the desktop and then you can see the missing area during the rotation and see if any files are down there!

Regarding Intel HDA audio: by selecting different "hardware profiles" I've found I can seriously boost the volume from my laptop speakers.  Up until now the only way I could play a movie was to use VLC and it's "400%" available volume to get anything usable.  That's a thing of the past.

I'm getting odd "mutings" of the volume whenever I do anything Nautilus-related...file copying, opening an archive, etc.  I'm sure it's something that'll be sorted out soon.  Even then it's not a hard glitch to live with and if it's the only one, even if it stays into the release code, this release will kick Jaunty's butt.
