### Post by TheFu on 2017-01-28
Connect to it via ssh.  This is a common Unix thing.  Forget the desktop. Learn to manage it like a server, without a GUI. There are lots and lots of books for this.  LPI has a certification program and free, step-by-step, training materials. These cover what you ask and much more.  EdX has free training for beginners to prepare to move onto the beginning admin series. Amazon Prime has beginning Ubuntu videos too. There are lots and lots of other training methods.  Search for "Ubuntu Desktop Guide" and "Ubuntu Server Guide" to get started.

For my LUG, I'm in the process of helping about 25 students learn the skills needed to pass the LPIC-101 Administration test at the local University for IEEE members. We are using free materials that are linked on the LPI website, covering about 20 pgs every week, so in about 15 weeks, we should be through it all. That's the theory.  We shall see.  It covers Ubuntu, RHEL, and SuSE distros.  There are 2 halves necessary to get the LPI cert.
