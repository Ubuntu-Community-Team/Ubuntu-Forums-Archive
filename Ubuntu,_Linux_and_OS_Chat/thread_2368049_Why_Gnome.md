It was business decision by Canonical.

See [https://arstechnica.co.uk/information-technology/2017/04/ubuntu-unity-is-dead-back-to-gnome/](https://arstechnica.co.uk/information-technology/2017/04/ubuntu-unity-is-dead-back-to-gnome/) for further information about the reasons why Canonical took this decision.  You'll find many other posts on the web that go into much more detail than we can here but to sum up Canonical needed Unity for convergence across various form factors. Canonical is now concentrating on IOT (Internet of Things) and Ubuntu for the Cloud.

Convergence is dead and so is Canonical's version of Unity. Others may decide to fork and develop Unity themselves.
