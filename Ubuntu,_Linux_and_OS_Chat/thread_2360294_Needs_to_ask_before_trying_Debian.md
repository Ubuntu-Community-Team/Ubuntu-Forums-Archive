### Post by ian-weisser on 2017-05-03
Hmmmm.

I've never encountered hostility from Debian folks.
Quite the opposite: warm, thoughtful, kind.

As with any group: Use their etiquette. Be clear. Be patient and respectful. Avoid idioms. Don't cast aspersions. Let others have the final word.

Also, whenever I meet a Debian package maintainer, I let them know that I use their package and that I appreciate their hard work. Having maintained packages, I know just how little recognition they get!

### Post by poorguy on 2017-05-03
Debian forums expect the users to know more than the average novice user in my experience.

As with any forum one must consider the source and not let the bad forum help / members get to them.

Some Debian forum help / members are just are plain out right rude and others will go the full distance to give the needed help requested.

As on any forum a lot depends on the forum person who gives the  advice as sometimes forum help / members forget that they  were once a novice also.

Lots of good people on all forums although one has to ignore the not so friendly types on forums also.

The most important thing to remember about forums is that all forum help is on a voluntary bases and sometimes people seeking help can be difficult.

I base all of this on my own experience from several different forums where I participate.
