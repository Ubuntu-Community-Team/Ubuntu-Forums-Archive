### Post by Roasted on 2014-04-22
I second the above. At just shy of 28 years old, I feel as though my "IT maturity" has entered old man syndrome like you cannot believe. In my mid/late teens, I tinkered with Linux extensively. I got such a kick out of making it work, but it required work, reading, and know-how. These days, I just fire it up and go. At most I may toggle a handful of minor settings to fit my personal preference, but by no stretch of the imagination do I heavily tinker like I once did. For an idea of how things have changed, Google "things to do after installing Ubuntu 14.04" and "things to do after installing Ubuntu 6.06". My my, times have changed.

These days, my life is chaotic. I'm continually doing DIY projects on the house, I have a baby now, side jobs are stacking up as people come to me for more assistance with different things (anything from running a sound system for a wedding to creating an event web site for an animal rights group; both are current projects on the radar). These days, I just need things to work. These days, Ubuntu provides me with that. I find myself fussing with my systems far less than I ever have before.

It also helps that my employer (public school district of around 5,000 computers) is over 80% Ubuntu. My one time side hobby turned into a full time career. It works for me. And dangit, am I happy it does.

As a result, I mirror the above. Thank you: developers (both Ubuntu developers and the extensive amount of developers outside of Ubuntu), bug reporters, users who have submitted patches, the forum members who assist one another, and the forum mods for putting up with us. Way to be a community.
