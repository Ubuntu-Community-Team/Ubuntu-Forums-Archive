### Post by mjpatey on 2014-05-15
I haven't done a clean install of Ubuntu on any device since 12.04; I've just been upgrading since then (which speaks volumes for the stability of Ubuntu). So just now when I went to download the latest version to revive an old laptop, I encountered for the first time... the donation page. Each category is filled in with $2 by default, and I was in a hurry to download it so I set them all to $0 and prepared to continue.

Then it got me. There was a little graphic at the bottom of the list of donation categories that would change as the donation amount changed, showing what you could buy with your $xx.00... When I changed it to zero, the graphic became what subtly looked like an Ubuntu-styled sad face. And I couldn't do that to my Ubuntu, after all it's done for me. I've been using it for free since 2006 (Dapper Drake), and it's never asked me for a dime until now. So I broke down and gave it a few dollars.

I just want to congratulate the web designers and graphic artists at Canonical for successfully tugging at my geeky heartstrings like that. Well done! And it's a win-win, really. If Ubuntu had an actual price tag, I'd willingly pay a lot more than I did just now. Ubuntu is definitely worth it. That said, I'm glad it's free for all, and hope it stays that way!

-Mark
