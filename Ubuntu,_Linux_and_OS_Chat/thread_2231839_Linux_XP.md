### Post by squakie on 2014-06-28
The main reason people want to do this - making the desktop Windows-like - is to keep a sense of familiarity in use.  However,  as has been said so many times in so many places "Ubuntu ain't Windows".  You can't expect to see the same options, etc., as you would see in Windows.  Ubuntu has all kinds of tools and programs available for free that provide the functionaliy in Ubuntu.

So what is everyone, including myself, saying?  You may get it close to the APPEARANCE of Windows, but not that functionality.  That functionality is in other tools used in other ways with Ubuntu.  Amongst those are the various desktops that are available.  The really is a different look and feel to Ubuntu and to Linux in general.  It would be best not to carry over Windows expectations, including the look and feel, and then get frustrated.  There is a learning curve, but I would strongly suggest to get those frustrations at least doing something more useful and for which there is a way of success, by using one of the native Ubuntu desktops.  After you get some experience with Ubuntu in general, then you can try one of the "looks like" if you want, knowing in advance that you have the linux background to find your way through things.  No use throwing in an additional item to learn until you learn linux in general and Ubuntu in particular.

That's just my suggestions - just me.  I really do believe you'd find it much easier in the long run to not bring a Windows look and expectations to linux/Ubuntu.

It really is great, so jump in and just ask all the questions you want here on the forums.
