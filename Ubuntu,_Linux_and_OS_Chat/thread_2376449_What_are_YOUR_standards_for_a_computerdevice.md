### Post by ulysses77 on 2017-11-02
The only reality with computing is that things are getting better all of the time really, really quickly and with upgrades and better products... you could go as far as to spend as much as a car on them. So realistically, where do YOU draw the line? What is it that you need or think it necessary on a computer or smartphone or other devices?

For me, it's unique upon the form factor. 

A smartphone? Android, waterproof, good camera, good storage and expandable storage, good battery life, and a good display. Software updates are a must, obviously.
A laptop? Nothing less than an i5, 8 or more GB of RAM, FHD display or greater, a solid build quality, a good hard drive, and if I can get it, a backlit keyboard. Although my current doesn't have it, haven't had a problem without it. 
A desktop? Basically better than any game console and I call it good enough.

### Post by kansasnoob on 2017-11-02
Must be affordable! That's always number one for me. Of course it must be able to do what I want it to do. 

I've recently fixed up a few Dell Latitude E6400 14" laptops for under $50 each and they run amazingly well both with Windows 10 and Ubuntu. There are two basic variations - one with Intel graphics and the other with nVidia graphics. Those with Intel graphics have a 1280x800 resolution while those with nVidia chips have 1440x900. There's also the possibility of using either the T or P series C2D Intel CPU's - the smart choice is the P series because they use less power so battery life is better. Anything less than 3GB of RAM is a waste of time and energy (realistically just go for 4GB).

OTOH I recently wasted a couple weeks of my life bringing back to life a couple dozen Dell Latitude 2100 10" series netbooks which max out at 2GB of RAM. For some reason (probably CPU) the 2110's were better than either the 2100's or the 2120's, but the sound is really awful on all three models and forget about streaming anything. For just light browsing they're better than nothing but I could not recommend them. If only they were capable of running 4GB of RAM they wouldn't be bad other than the horrific sound.

At any rate a person can bring a 7 or 8 year old laptop back to life if you have moderate skills and a lot of patience. It requires some research and you must know what your actual needs are. For general use look for something with at least a 2.2ghz CPU and 4GB of RAM, and check out some video tutorials for basic stuff like upgrading the CPU and cleaning the fan because the weakest link in any mechanically cooled older device is filth and petrified thermal compound. Also check out the affordability of parts.

### Post by Tadaen_Sylvermane on 2017-12-04
For me power usage is the first thing I look at. When I first built my htpc / server I figured I needed a monster. Dual cpu, 12 cores, 64 gigs of ram, 30+tb of storage. Would have cost a bunch on the power bill. In the end though I found that I didn't need even a fraction of that muscle. Went with a AM1 socket AMD processor with a 25w tdp, 8 gigs of ram, 2.4 tb total storage on my snapraid pool. 540 movies and 1100 episodes of tv shows later with a few other things thrown in and I'm barely using 43% of my total storage space. The server costs me $1 US a month to run on the power bill. It barely idles at 1% cpu usage, Worst I've seen was 60% when we set off a good sized tnt explosion in minecraft once. 

I like having a low power bill while having all of my services available 24/7.
