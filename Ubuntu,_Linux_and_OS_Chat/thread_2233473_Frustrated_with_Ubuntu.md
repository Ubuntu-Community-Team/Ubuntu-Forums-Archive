### Post by mooreted on 2014-07-08
No, it's not normal. I've been using Ubuntu for years and 99% of the releases of installed without a single problem. The two I remember having trouble with were 9.10 and 13.10. Nowadays I just stick to LTS releases.

The first thing I would suspect is a bad download. I have had that happen before and I fixed it by downloading a new ISO and creating new install media.

I have never tried to install to a flash drive, I'm not sure that's supported. You might want to setup a partition on a local hard drive and try again.

I have never had the language switch while installing; part of what makes me think it's a bad ISO.

Video corruption would probably be related to not having the right video driver installed. We would need to know your hadware specs to troubleshoot that.

Basically, take a deep breath, slow down and try again. Maybe load up a live CD and make sure it runs on your hardware.
