### Post by d-cosner on 2014-07-09
I have been distro hopping like crazy lately.... I wanted the latest Gnome Shell, then the latest version of Mate and just wasn't getting what I really wanted. I tried rolling releases like Arch, Antergos, Sabayon and even tried building my own Mate remix out of Ubuntu 14.04. Everything I tried I kept finding bugs or things that just did not work the way I wanted. I could give examples but anyone that has gone this route already knows what I am talking about.

I had burned a copy of Xubuntu to put on a friends older computer, I liked it but at the time I thought I wanted something more modern. I finally realized though that all of the features I really was looking for were right there in front of my face in Xubuntu! The Xubuntu Team did a great job making the LTS look great and the default applications were for the most part just what I really needed and they were light on resources.

The only apps I removed were two games that I did not want. I added some software to work with my music and alacarte to take care of the few apps that were not showing up in the menu. After all this I was finally getting some work done and actually enjoying my computer again! I had several apps open and was working away and the system was quick and responsive.

All the little things that were bugging me in other desktop environments were no longer a problem! My network applet was working, all multimedia files were displaying exactly like they should, every single thing just worked the way I expected it to!  All of the things that the newer desktop environments keep taking away and putting back were already in Xfce. 

No single desktop environment is a one size fits all but if you are going through the frustration I was then you might want to take a good look at xubuntu. Xfce does not change much from version to version and it doesn't really need to because it already does everything right. I have read so many peoples post about Xfce and now I understand why they like it so well.

I hope my distro hopping is over! I might customize my desktop a little but I am really satisfied with how well it works!

### Post by shadesofoctober on 2014-07-10
I often jump around, especially when I get extra annoyed at a particular issue with my current environment.

It's honestly not a good habit, especially when I have to re-set-up a development environment, which takes away time from programming.
I often get an urge to see if another desktop environment has a better feel, and definitely go back-and-forth between Ubuntu and Fedora. Fedora's the only other distro that I've really had good luck with.

I find that I like the simple dialogs of Gnome, the dock of Unity, and the widgets/windows of KDE. Honestly, I like a little eye-candy too. Smooth window transitions, fade-in/out login effects, etc. Vsync when dragging windows. I love the overall design of Elementary OS, but their older packages cause issues with my wireless devices and video cards on each computer of mine. And I don't want to use their unstable repos. Maybe with a newer stable release, I'll try it.

Overall I've realized that the two I jive with the most are vanilla Ubuntu and Xubuntu. When I get that urge to try another distro/DE, I boot the image from a USB, and play around with it for a second to remind myself what annoys me about it and why I should not re-install.

It's very easy with my Macbook, seeing as Ubuntu has far better support for it's hardware than other distros (Not worth the trouble getting them to work). The tricky ones are my desktop and Lenovo laptop, where any distro usually runs without issue.

I just have to remind myself that what I'm using currently works great. It may not be perfect, but using another DE or distro for one perceived superior attribute usually brings with it several not-so-desired features.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2014-07-11
See here:

Minimal Install page:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD)

Basic example:
[http://www.maketecheasier.com/install-a-minimal-ubuntu-on-old-laptop/](http://www.maketecheasier.com/install-a-minimal-ubuntu-on-old-laptop/)

Older, but info still relevant while screenshots might be a bit different now:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/minimal)

If you go that way, don't make the mistake of doing 'sudo apt-get install *buntu-desktop' as many advise. That is equivalent to installing a full desktop version so no point in a minimal. You only want the desktop environment, for example, for an Xubuntu feel without all the default apps, install xfce4, NOT xubuntu-desktop. 

```
sudo apt-get lightdm xfce4 synaptic network-manager xorg
```

... might be a start. It's a good way to learn about Ubuntu. ;)
