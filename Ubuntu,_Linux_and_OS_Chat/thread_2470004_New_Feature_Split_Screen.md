### Post by grahammechanical on 2021-12-17
It is my understanding that Compiz Config Settings Manager (CCSM) works on the X-Server windowing system but not on Wayland. I also believe that Ubuntu 20.04 defaults to the X-Server with a login option to use a Wayland compositor. Whilst 21.10 and newer versions of Ubuntu default to Wayland with a login option to use the X-Server. It all adds complexity. Eventually, Ubuntu will do away with the X-Server entirely. Yes, there is such a thing as X-Wayland for those applications that have not been ported to Wayland. But the X Windowing system is the past and not the future.

### Post by ard+sur on 2021-12-19
thanks all for your suggestions.

I have now found an easy way to fix 2 Apps, next to each other on one screen. 
see here [https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/shell-windows-tiled.html.en](https://help.ubuntu.com/stable/ubuntu-help/shell-windows-tiled.html.en)

- have 2 Apps open
- in the active App, press "Super" key and left arrow to position on left 1/2 side of your screen
- active a 2nd App, and press "Super" key and right arrow to position it on right 1/2 side of your screen 
will do .
