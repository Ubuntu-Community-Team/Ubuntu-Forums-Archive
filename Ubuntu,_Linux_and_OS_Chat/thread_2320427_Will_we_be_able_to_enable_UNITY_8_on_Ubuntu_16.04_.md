### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-04-13
If you ask Canonical engineers when Mir & Unity 8 will be the default for Ubuntu desktop they will answer: "When it is ready." And nobody is predicting when it will be ready.

There is unity8-desktop-session-mir & unity8 in LXC. I have tried both on 16.04 and they never get past the log in screen. Besides, they are not meant to be much more than a curiosity. Another thing: If you use a proprietary video driver then Unity 8 most definitely will not run.

From the start of the 16.04 26 week development period it was always the intention that Unity 7 would be the default. Do not expect anything different. Not for many months if at all on an LTS.
