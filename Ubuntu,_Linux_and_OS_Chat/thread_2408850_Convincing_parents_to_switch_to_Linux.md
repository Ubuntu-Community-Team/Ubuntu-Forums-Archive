### Post by 1clue on 2019-01-02
Don't even try. I've converted plenty of people to Linux, but not by trying or suggesting or recommending, unless I was asked directly. The best way to sell Linux is to not sell it. Just use it.

I've been around Linux since 1996. I've been using it almost exclusively for much of that time. I used to "demo" Linux for everyone I could drag over to my workstation. Very, very few people listened to my whole demo.

The thing is, when you do that you sound like (are, actually) a crazed fanatic. The same as the crazy church people who walk up to your door and want to talk about religion. Or the guy trying to sell you a car. Or the guy who saw Bigfoot. You are exactly the same.

Let it be. Set up your Linux box the way you like it, to actually get work done. Change settings, develop habits and schemes to make best use of your time. Make a nice, personalized desktop picture that matters to you, maybe add one or two bells and whistles that you ACTUALLY find handy.

Do NOT load your system down with tons of animations and sounds and clicks. Years back it was the desktop cube. While that gets attention it has absolutely no practical use, nor is it tolerable on a practical system.

So configure your box so it's nice and comfortable for your personal use, and then use it. Don't conspicuously use it (LOOK AT ME!  I'M USING LINUX!!!!), just get things done.


When I met my wife, I tried to convert her to Linux. I actually installed it on her laptop, and about a month later she made me change it back. Because it was something I did to her, and she really didn't have a choice.

Some time later, when she actually WAS my wife, she saw me working on my system and started asking questions. I made an account on my system, and she started using it. Mostly when I was gone. After awhile she wanted Linux back on her laptop again.


People are not stupid. People don't want something "just like Windows" or "just like Mac." They see computers mostly as an appliance. If they're interested they'll figure it out. They figured out smart phones and android vs apple, and whatever ipad or tablet they had, and they figure out where the windshield wipers are when they buy a different car. What matters to them is that what they have is useful and not too ugly. 

When they see you doing your job quietly and effectively, and they see that what you use is not Windows or Mac OS X, then if they're curious they'll ask questions. Answer JUST THOSE QUESTIONS. If they ask a few questions on a topic, you can expand on it a bit. Remember that people are not stupid. If they use a computer at all, they'll be able to figure some stuff out on their own. If you push, they'll get information overload and you'll make them angry.
