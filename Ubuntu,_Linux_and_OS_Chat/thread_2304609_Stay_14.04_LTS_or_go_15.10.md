### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-11-28
One reason for installing the latest version of Ubuntu is to get the  latest Linux driver modules that are more up to date with the latest  hardware. If 14.04 is running fine on that hardware then, from a  hardware point of view there is no reason to upgrade to 15.10. Which you  cannot do directly. You have have to first upgrade to 15.04 than  upgrade again to 15.10 and then upgrade again to 16.04 before 15.10  reaches end of life.

That being said, my advice would be to stick  with 14.04 and then do a direct upgrade to 16.04 some time after it is  released at the end of next April. You might want to test 16.04 in a  dual boot set up and then experiment with getting the OS working just as  you like before you upgrade the 14.04 install.

Ubuntu 14.04 uses  Unity 7 as the UI and so does 15.10 and so will 16.04. There is not a  great deal of change happening in the area of the UI.

Regards.

If it's not broke, don't fix it. If 14.04 is working fine, why upgrade??? They both go to 16.04 LTS via a net upgrade in the latter half of next year and, from what you've posted, you gain nothing from an upgrade (except perhaps a bunch of issues to fix).

My 2c: Stay right where you are. 14.04 LTS *is* rock solid and from what I see you have no reason to change anything. A better use of time might be to install 16.04 LTS on another partition and use that as a plaything to test and report bugs (post in Ubuntu Development Version here). That way you have 14.04 LTS as your daily, stable squeeze and when officially released and stable sometime after next April, you can 'migrate' over to your fully tweaked 16.04 LTS. :)
