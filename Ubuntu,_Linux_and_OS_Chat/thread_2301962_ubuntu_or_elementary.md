### Post by mystics on 2015-11-06
I personally haven't tried out Elementary. I read that the developers weren't too fond of people using the distro for free and even set up their website to hide that you could get it for free. They even said people who want to use free software for free are "cheating". Basically, I decided that I wouldn't disappoint them by using it for free, and since I'm not about to pay money to try something out I may not use beyond trying it out, I never tried it out. 

But beyond that, from what I know of Elementary, it is Ubuntu-based (so you're still getting a solid foundation) that has been largely sold on its aesthetics, which can be reasonably well-emulated in Ubuntu anyways, and on its ease-of-use. I've heard that it is less flexible than Ubuntu, but I can't confirm or even know if it will affect the average user (every complaint I've heard has come from power users). Overall, it sounds like a solid distro that people find nice to use to pleasing to look at. But again, I don't want to disappoint the developers, and I don't want to pay to try it out, so I'm left personally choosing Ubuntu by default.

### Post by mystics on 2015-11-07
> **qamelian said:**
> They don't hide the free option. If you don't want to contribute, click on the price box to the right labelled "Custom Option" and enter a $0. It's no more hidden than on the Ubuntu download page, which also suggests a contribution. :)

To me, the difference is that Ubuntu gives an option on their page that says, "Not now, take me to the download." That very clearly states that you don't have to pay anything at the moment. Sure, I think it could be done better by being at the top, since users are liable to waste time moving sliders to zero (I can't see the option by default even with fullscreen), but at least it is there.

Comparatively, Elementary OS obscures that fact as much as possible. In fact, they even acknowledged that this was a problem on their old website in the blog they used to explain this design: [http://blog.elementary.io/post/110645528530/payments](http://blog.elementary.io/post/110645528530/payments)

> we&#8217;ve received feedback that this flow is deceiving. It doesn&#8217;t make it clear enough that there are multiple payment options and it includes a payments step even if you&#8217;ve chosen to enter $0.

Now, to be fair, they did address the latter problem in the new design, but they chose to make the other problem as the base for accessing the free option. I know they claim it wasn't meant to be deceptive, but if they're going to use a feature people have said is confusing, then "but we really aren't trying to deceive" isn't going to be convincing.
