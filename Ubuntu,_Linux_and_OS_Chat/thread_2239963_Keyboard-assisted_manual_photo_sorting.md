### Post by myname5 on 2014-08-16

### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-08-16
There is an expression:  "Do the work in the camera."

By using a tripod, understanding light, depth-of-field, exposure, tonal curve, and a dozen other photographic concepts, you will take better pictures and have less work to do in the back end.  Try searching for "digital photographic workflow" and see how others handle their workflow.  If you do the work in the camera, then there are less pictures to sort.  In fact the sorting goes away.

To see what is available in the repos:

```
apt-cache search photo
```

For me, I use an HDMI cable and connect my Canon directly to a 60" Samsung TV.  I use the tagging within the camera and delete them before importing into one of several photo managers.  There is some risk to this method, since you are deleting files from an SD or CF card, which could result in corruption of the entire card, so backups are important.  There are dedicated backup devices for this or you can use a card reader and your computer to mass copy files for backup protection.
