### Post by ian-weisser on 2014-11-07
It seems unrealistic to expect Ubuntu developers and the myriad of volunteers to support more than the current two upgrade paths. They are difficult enough to maintain already.

A four year skip in versions seems like a tremendous amount of work to try to manage the upgrade path for. Default applications change, applications come and go from the repositories, networking and graphics and audio and printing stacks change. Backup-and-install a fresh copy of the new version seems simpler and safer - avoid a lot of corner cases that way.

Plus lots of users will have five-year-old PPAs and other non-Ubuntu sources that will break an upgrade anyway....
