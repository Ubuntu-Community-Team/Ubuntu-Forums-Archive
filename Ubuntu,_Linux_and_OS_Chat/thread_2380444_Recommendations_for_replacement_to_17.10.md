### Post by pauljw on 2017-12-18
I recommend 16.04LTS.  At this point in time it is the newest of the long term support releases and it is very stable.  I just upgraded my primary laptop from 14.04 to 16.04 and I'm very pleased with it.  It's supported until 2021.  This spring 18.04LTS will be released and it's bringing many changes including replacing Unity with Gnome for the desktop.  If you choose to upgrade to 18.04, I recommend waiting for the 18.04.1 release which is typically a few months after initial release.  That gives some time for bugs to get worked out and you'll have a more stable OS.  Since you say you're using your laptop for productive work, I really think you should get on the LTS track and stay there.  They don't typically have the newest versions of all the software, but they're built for stability and you don't get that with "the latest and greatest."

Good luck, there's a world of choices out there, each with it's own pro's and con's.  Study and maybe consider setting up virtualbox on a stable system and create virtual machines of the distros that you're interested in to see how they run for you.
