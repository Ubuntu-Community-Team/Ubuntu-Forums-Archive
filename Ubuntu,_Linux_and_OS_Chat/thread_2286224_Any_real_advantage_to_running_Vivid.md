### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-07-11
Keep in mind that I always use the development release.

I would say that it comes down to how often you want to upgrade/fresh install. If you are happy to upgrade every six months and ready to fresh install if the upgrade fails in some way, then go ahead. If not, stick with 14.04 and upgrade to 16.04 when it is released.

You can upgrade directly from 14.04 to 16.04. But once you move to 15.04 you will have to upgrade to 15.10 and then to 16.04 before you get to another LTS release.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-07-11
When what will be released in 2019? 19.04 or 19.10, neither of which will be LTS releases? (Presuming Canonical sticks with their current practice: LTS every second, even year.) LTS releases, consequently, will be 16.04 LTS then 18.04 LTS. 2019 will have the two interim releases mentioned above.

See [here]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Releases") for further info. The interim releases come out in April and October, the LTS every second April.
