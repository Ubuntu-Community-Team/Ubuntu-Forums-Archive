### Post by ardouronerous on 2017-07-26
I just wanted to share my story on how I converted my friend into Linux.

This happened around middle of April, my friend lives somewhere in Europe, and anyway, she calls me out of the blue, asking if we could video chat and we did.

She tells me that her 10-year-old laptop was having trouble, it runs Windows Vista and she received a warning from Microsoft that Vista is EOL and wasn't receiving any updates, and according to her, all the web forums that she visited suggested that she trash her laptop and buy a new laptop or update to Windows 10, but according to her, her laptop cannot run Window 10, leaving her with one option to throw out her laptop and purchase a new laptop, well, I gave her a alternative option, Linux.

Since her laptop was 10-years-old and wasn't sure if it could handle Ubuntu or even Xubuntu, I told her to download Lubuntu and burn it into a DVD and I walked her through the install process.

She likes it, one of the first things she noticed after applying updates and rebooting is that rebooting and startup was blazing fast, because on Vista, it usually takes her about 2 minutes to reboot/shutdown her laptop, and she thanked me because I saved her money instead of wasting her money to buy a new laptop, I breathed new life into her laptop.

It felt really good introducing her to Linux and I find it hard to believe that no one on those forums suggested to her to install Linux.

### Post by lammert-nijhof on 2017-07-27
I wanted to do the same. I have a 10 years old Inspiron 1521. It had a lot of overheating problems and then my GPU died. I made the error to have the motherboard replaced for $60 locally or 15 bottles of very cold local beer. 2 weeks later the fan died and I got another one free of charge. The airflow of that other fan seemed weaker. I still had serious problems with the temperature of my AMD Athlon X2 at 1.8GHz. So I ordered a $30 AMD Turion X2 tl-66 at 2.3GHz for $25. That temperature problem really improved with the new CPU. Blocking the CPU at 2.0GHz the temp problem disappeared even in the sub-tropic Dominican Republic. I was happy and while on holiday in Belgium, it completely disappeared due to lower ambient temps and with Ubuntu 16.04 and a fast 320GB disk (90MB/s) it was really responsive and working good. So to be completely sure I ordered a new fan at Ebay. 

I wanted to leave the laptop there for my 14-year old grandchild. I wanted for myself a laptop with 8GB and support for virtualization, so I ordered the i5. Two hours before that I5 arrived, my old Dell died. Investigating the problem I found, it is the motherboard again. Probably all remaining second hand Inspiron 1521 motherboards are close to end of life due to years of overheating. 

Don't try to prolong the life of an old laptop, when it is over, accept it and let it die in peace.
