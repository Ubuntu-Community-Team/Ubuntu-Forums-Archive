### Post by deimios on 2014-02-19
Greetings,

As someone intimately familiar with this story I'd like to clarify a few things:

The Romanian Ministry of Education did not order the wiping of the Edubuntu laptops. Even if the schools do wipe them, it is their right (decentralization did give them a lot of freedom), if they possess the adequate number of Microsoft licenses to do so. The Romanian Ministry of Education does not provide any Microsoft licenses for these devices. In fact all the Microsoft agreements were for upgrade licenses, as such it's illegal to use them if you don't have an existing valid Microsoft license.

I applaud your enthusiasm regarding this matter, but please keep it constructive. We are dealing with under-payed and often times stubborn teachers dead-set in their ways, who already view every change, every new "toy" with an unhealthy amount of skepticism and dismissal. To be fair they have their reasons (office 2003->2007, windows 8).

The Romanian Ministry of Education is not the enemy here, as you will find out soon enough ;)

### Post by deimios on 2014-02-20
I come bearing good news. As of yesterday 21.02.2014 the Romanian Ministry of Education gave the order to **uninstall all microsoft upgrade licenses until 28.02.2014** and gave the schools 3 alternatives.
Either:
1. Return to the original Microsoft license with which the computers originally shipped (XP and Vista)
2. Acquire Microsoft academic licenses on their own(considering how severly underfunded most schools are this is the least likely scenario)
3. Use free and open source alternatives

In the open source section of the licensing portal Edubuntu is specifically named with links to download edubuntu ISOs.

If you are commited to bringing open source software to schools, the time is right. Especially in rural areas there is a definite lack of personell that could perform the uninstallation of windows/ installation of linux so any community help is appreciated. If you got the time, drop by your local school and ask them if they need some help or try contacting the county school inspectorate to see if there are schools that need help.

Since the order was sent on the 21st, wait for it to propagate to the individual schools. They might not get the message until monday.
