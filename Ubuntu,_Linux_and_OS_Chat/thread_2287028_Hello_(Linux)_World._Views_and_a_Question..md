### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-07-16
*Thread moved to **Ubuntu, Linux and OS Chat**.*

Better here as you are about to receive a barrage of opinions to your question. 

Another staff member may move this again to Recurring Discussions as we get asked this all the time, but the answer is simple: Try as many as you can and go with what suits you and your workflow. Regardless of what others recommend, it may stink for you.

Good luck.

PS: There is an [introductions thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1451097") for introducing your self to the community. You may want to post on it. :)

PPS: Do a search for an have a look on Distrowatch. You can easily spend the rest of your life trying out all kinds of exotic Linux spins there. Keep in mind that 'after sales support' is very important. The more exotic you go with a distro, the less likely you are to have support. One of the reasons Ubuntu is popular: the support community is second to none. :)

### Post by jeff127 on 2015-07-17
For me, the most important feature is support. For that reason I almost **never** go outside of Ubuntu and [Fedora]("https://getfedora.org/en/workstation/"). Things like OpenSUSE look okay, until you see that they're adding borderline-experimental software like reiser filesystem, instead of EXT4 (I never heard of anybody losing data but still, there's no benefit in reiser as far as I can tell). I'm sticking with Fedora GNOME for sake of predictability. KDE is a stupid idea for me - it likes to have a video card, but there's no driver for my AMD APU chip. If you find yourself distro hopping then you need to ask yourself "what is the end goal".
