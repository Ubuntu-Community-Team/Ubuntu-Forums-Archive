### Post by saiyon on 2017-03-17
I have basically been on a month of distro hopping and been on quite a journey working out my needs and wants.

I basically started with Linux Mint 18.1 Cinnamon which is a very solid distro but there are two reasons I choose not to stick with it:
1. I want a slightly faster update frequency than the LTS version that mint is based off
2. I had the odd flickr and graphics issue with cinnamon, not major but just enought to be annoying

Overall Mint 18.1 is very good just two niggles that kept me away.

Arch - I did build arch from scratch (rather than using an installer based distro or an install script)
1. I did get satisfaction from completing the install (95% of it is easy), it is the bootloader section that will catch you out
2. I like the idea of being on arch but for my needs I know I am going to be blowing away my OS install regularly as I tweak and build things that will most likely break packages, therefore I needed something that is less of a pain to get up and running
3. Arch is supposedly reasonably stabe but I wanted more confidence that my work has cause instability rather than an update -with arch the updates come quick and fast

Debian Testing:
1. this solved the quicker to install issues but...
2. I realised that I will pretty much always want the proprietary tools / drivers made available via ubuntu based dsitros

openSuse:
1. one last attempt at a rolling release
2. quite liked it but basically not a large enough pool of compiled packages
3. for those interested in a rolling distro I would really look at this one - very impressed

Back to Ubuntu:
1. I realised I should go with two ubuntu based installs on my machine one on each drive, one install being the LTS release and my fallback for when I break the main 16.10 install due to all my tinkering
2. I now decided to try some of the flavours, kubuntu, Xubuntu, Budgie Remix. Each of them had the odd bug or was missing something I personally wanted
3. I thought to mysef "to hell with it" lets try Unity regarless of all the whining I see on the web about it
4. I had a surprising moment when I assessed Unity without pre-imposed bias and realised that actually I quite liked it 
5. I installed moka/numix icons/themes via PPA's and like it even more!
6. Nothing has graphically bugged (yet) and actually you remember what it is like to have nice fonts again! I suppose it probably has the most wo/man hours of integration effort due to being professionally supported?
7. More time spent in self wonder that I am growing to like unity
8. The big moment when i re-rolled my laptop onto vanilla ubuntu... :) :)

So maybe I will save some poor soul a month of distro hopping but in the end the reasons for Ubuntu for me are:

1. Quick installs for when I break my machine (because of my fault tinkering on projects)
2. A nice balance between release based and rolling
3. An LTS version available and installed as a fallback
4. A Desktop DE that runs smoothly on my hardware
5. Minimal effort to make it look more uniqu and to my tastes
5. A great support community and wide ranging wiki
6. Issues on privacy seem to be in the past and the privacy control panel makes things simple

I was all ready to be an elitist bespoke built rolling distro and be meta hispster in the linux world but realised that actually the most sensible choice for me personally was ubuntu vanilla. Now I am back ot my projects.

Don't get broken by the "[Paradox of Choice]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Paradox_of_Choice")"!
