### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-01-25
Twenty years? Who knows? A lot would depend on whether they continue to support your graphics and wireless as much as anything else. I've had fifteen year old machines and older working on Xubuntu.

2011 is not an old machine when you're talking Xubuntu, or Ubuntu really. You have a 64bit, 4Gb RAM machine. It will be awhile before they disappear. Years. When machines like that are rare as hen's teeth, it probably won't be supported simply because 128bit processors and 64+Gb of RAM will be the norm. 

Not something I'd be losing any sleep over for a decade or so. Just in case you aren't aware, LTS for Xubuntu is three, not five, years. ;)

Maybe, or maybe not.

I'm not sure what it's like on Chromium and other Blink-based browsers.  But for Firefox/SeaMonkey/etc, you have the choice of the NPAPI version of Flash, which has the sound delayed by over half a second, or the PPAPI version through freshplayer, which has occasional graphics hangs.
