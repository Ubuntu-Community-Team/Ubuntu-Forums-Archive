### Post by montag dp on 2016-02-02
It's no different from whatever other OS you used before.  Organize your files in whichever way makes the most sense to you.

In general, on Linux (as most other OSes), you can do whatever you want to your home directory without affecting your system.  There is an exception to that, however, which is the hidden files and directories in your home directory.  These typically are used for storing user configurations for different programs, so deleting or changing them may cause you to lose settings (including your desktop environment settings).  As they are hidden, typically you will not see them in a file manager unless you tell it to show hidden files.  You can also show them in a terminal by typing ls -la in your home directory.
