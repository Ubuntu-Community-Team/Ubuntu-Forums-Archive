### Post by sudodus on 2015-10-10
Lubuntu with LXDE is definitely lighter than Xubuntu with XFCE. You notice the difference if you try to run video, particularly high-definition video for example 720p (1280x720-50p) or 1080p (1920x1080-50p), in a middle-aged or old computer.

If the computer is powerful enough (CPU, GPU cache and RAM), both Lubuntu and Xubuntu perform well. I think Ubuntu MATE performs at the same level as Xubuntu, and with even more 'horsepower' and memory, also standard Ubuntu with Unity and Kubuntu with KDE perform well.

So [try and select](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2230389) what works well for ***you*** :-)

### Post by night_sky2 on 2015-10-10
I don't find Lubuntu (LXDE) to be much more lighter than Xubuntu (Xfce). On my 32 bit system, the difference is around 90-100MB~ of RAM at idle.

Xfce has always been more stable and less buggy for me so I am willing to sacrifice that ~90 MB for a better overall experience.

Unless you are very tight on RAM (1GB or below) than Xfce will work perfectly fine on your computer. It's still a lightweight DE, especially compared with Unity, Gnome or KDE.
