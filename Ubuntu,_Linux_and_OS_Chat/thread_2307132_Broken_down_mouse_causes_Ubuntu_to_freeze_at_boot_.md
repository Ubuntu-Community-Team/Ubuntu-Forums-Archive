### Post by Maxei on 2015-12-22
Hello,
I am running Ubuntu 10.04.4 LTS, (yes, it is 22nd. December 2015!).

Yesterday, I started my PC as usual, pushing the power button. What a surprise: the screen that allows me to choose which kernel/OS to load was unresponsive, meaning that I could not switch it by using the up or down arrow keys. Anyways, the OS continued automatically (deaf to my request) after a few seconds to boot, until the splash login user screen. But I could not move the arrow pointer with the mouse, nor type anything with the keyboard; any key combination push did nothing.

I panicked. My systems is broken, I thought. I searched over internet for hints.

Next day, something, from the back of my mind (really, I don't know why), decided to unplug both the keyboard, and to plug another one, and also unplugged the mouse. Surprise. No more freezes! The problem was not the keyboard. It was the mouse, that somehow gave op its tiny ghost, and was causing the whole OS to freeze. Incredible! How lame is that! Such is the power of a tiny, broken down, peripheral David, causing a Goliath OS to become unusable.

I mean, I could accept that a memory going bad, or the CPU, or the graphics card, or the hard drive, could stop the OS from working, but, a broken down mouse????

Does Windows could be also frozen down by a non-working mouse? I doubt it.

Has this been reported before in Ubuntu? I searched the internet; nothing of the sort. So I post this as information hoping that it can be of some use.

Max DeVraie
