### Post by calzakk on 2015-02-21
I've been patiently waiting for an official Google Drive client for Linux, but I'm starting to think that one will never appear.

There's the [unofficial command line client]("https://github.com/odeke-em/drive") [initially developed by a Google employee]("https://github.com/rakyll/drive") who thinks that "Background sync is not just hard, it is stupid".

But then there are viable cross-platform alternatives such as SpiderOak and Dropbox, which actually work pretty well on Linux.

Google seriously need to get a move on, because by not prioritizing this they're losing ground to their competitors.  I, for one, won't be waiting much longer ;)
