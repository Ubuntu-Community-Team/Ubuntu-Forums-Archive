"Over the past 18 months, we've watched modern SSDs easily write far more  data than most consumers will ever need. Errors didn't strike the  Samsung 840 Series until after 300TB of writes, and it took over 700TB  to induce the first failures. The fact that the 840 Pro exceeded 2.4PB  is nothing short of amazing, even if that achievement is also kind of  academic."

"The important takeaway is that all of the drives wrote hundreds of terabytes without any problems."

### Post by 1clue on 2017-06-13
I used to use WD all the time.

I bought a dozen WD Green drives from a single order some years ago. All but one have had catastrophic failures.  I'm not talking about a plain old bad sector, I'm talking about you-better-have-a-backup failures.

Within the first year, 3 drives failed. I sent them back for an RMA. The second year the total of failed drives in the original batch were up to 7, and the 3 drives replaced had 2 fails. By the end of the first year I had decided to use the drives until failure but not replace other failures.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-06-14
> **1clue said:**
>  I'll give them maybe another 15 or 20 years to get their quality back up to standards before I try again.

Not sure it was flawed technology *exactly*, but I could be wrong. Had a ton of experience with the WD greens. Didn't use anything else for years (I still use a couple for old backups). The problem is, they aren't designed for some things. The 'green power technology' is kinda confusing and using in a media server or streaming server, using for heavy tasks that require a lot of read/write, they ain't good and don't seem to last long. The 'green power tech' wants to start/stop the drive spinning the whole time and it just wears 'em out.

I learnt my lesson with them when one died, then another (I will add that this took some years, not six months). I do audio/video production stuff and one was in that box, the other was connected to a Raspberry Pi and the TV and used for recording and streaming vid to TV and other computers in house. That is not what the greens are designed for. 

I switched to the blacks which *don't* try to park the whole time. Way more robust, no green power technology and they rock. They are designed for the purpose, and the almost double price gives you an idea! My AV box has four blacks and an Intel SSD. Also have a black for AV external backups.

So curious, 1clue. What were you using the greens for? Was it heavy duty or just surfing the net?

WD, in my experience, have been great, worked as they have been designed to, and don't see myself changing the formula anytime soon. Just throwing in another experience. :)

* You can have a look at [what they're designed for here]("https://www.wdc.com/products/internal-storage.html"). Interestingly, the greens are gone. Perhaps a good thing as it took me awhile to realise they didn't suit my purposes and never had. ;)
