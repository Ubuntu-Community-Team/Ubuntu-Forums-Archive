### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2013-10-26
Been using various flavors of Ubuntu since 7.10, on about a half dozen machines (average over the years).  I'd say my assessment of upgrades is:
 - There's always a smattering of little problems each upgrade on at least one machine.  Usually easily dealt with by reinstalling the problematic package or defaulting configs.
 - Maybe once every 3 releases or so, one of my machines will have a major driver regression that will require me to hold it back.  That's become rarer.
 - Maybe once every 5 releases or so, there's a major bug in a program I use all the time that holds me back from upgrading.
 - I really don't notice a difference in stability between any of the releases when they're released.  Even LTS releases are just as buggy the first few months (eventually they have time to mature, unlike regular releases).
 - Consistently, every release, some group of users claims that the current release is the buggiest they've ever encountered; someone will declare it "ubuntu's Vista" or "ubuntu's Windows 8" (i.e. monumental flop release).
 - Consistently, every release, some group of users will claim that the release feels snappier and is really solid.  They'll say it's the best Ubuntu they've ever used.

13.10 for me has been business as usual.  No major hiccups so far, which is nice since I had to hold up my 13.04 upgrades several months because of some bugs.  YMMV.
