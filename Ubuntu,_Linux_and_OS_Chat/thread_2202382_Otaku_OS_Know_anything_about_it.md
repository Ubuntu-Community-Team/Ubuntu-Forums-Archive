### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2014-01-28
There are some interesting remixes come along now and then; some of them are quite useful for demonstrating the possibilities of open source to different subgroups of people.  Then there are those that are obviously someone putting his favorite desktop background and applications on a random desktop environment and called it a distro.  I have to remind myself that these are probably just wide-eyed linux-loving youngsters much like my own children, stretching their distro-hacking wings for the first time.  Maybe this is the next Texstar or Clement Lefebvre at work. :)

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2014-01-30
I have no problem downloading or watching anime on stock Ubuntu platforms from any standard source.  Other than wallpapers with *kawaii* girls, I'm not sure what other advantages this derivative offers.  Distributing the wallpapers themselves probably treads closely on trademark and copyright infringement as the developer hints on the site.

In Japan the word "otaku" is [not limited]("http://www.japan-talk.com/jt/new/12-types-of-otaku") to fans of anime and manga.  It refers to someone who is intensely involved in a hobby often to the point of obsession.  There are "train otaku," for instance, who immerse themselves in schedules and build model railroading layouts.
