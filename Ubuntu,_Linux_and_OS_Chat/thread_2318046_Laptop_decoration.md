### Post by t0p on 2016-03-22
Have you decorated your laptop/PC/phone/whatever device you use to access the web so anyone around can gaze wistfully at your machine?  I have.  Here's a photo of my laptop lid. **Double-click or whatever on the image to see a larger version.**

[ATTACH=CONFIG]267932[/ATTACH]

The pictures are all meaningful.  The 3-eyed smiley-face is the emblam of the Transient movement (folk who blend their genome with that of an alien species just cos they could (from the comic book series *Transmetropolitan*).  Spider Jerusalem, the gonzo journalist protagonist of the comic says its meaning is "All people are scum.  No matter what they look like."  Which strikes a chord deep in me.

Also from *Transmetropolitan* is "Anthrax Cat", a popular tv cartoon character who slays dogs and mice in imaginatively gruesome fashion Llike Tom & Jerry with napalm and high explosives.  Itchy and Scratchy, eat your own blackened, barely-functioning hearts.

The "ubiquitous surveillance" sign is just that.  Big Brother is Watching You, and not in a good way.  

The little apple with the letter "K" is from the ha-ha religion Discordianism.  If you are not familiar with Discordianism, look it up.  Disco is a the most wonderful ha-ha religion ever.  I'm a pope *and* a saint.  As official as can be.

The sticker that reads "This machine kills fascists" is based on the label Country musician Woody Guthrie stuck to his guitar.  Guthrie's the nearest thing to a Country protest musician!

The golden sticker bears an image of the goddess Eris, holding the apple that started the Trojan War.  In Disco, we worship a Bitch Goddess.  She doesn't mind that description, what with it being true and all.

The evil, smiling loon is a loon I picked up somewhere along the line.  He seemed to fit in, so I fitted him in.

The "stickers" were not stickers to begin with.  They were images I found online or in e-comics etc, which I used Screenshot to copy, put em on a usb stick or sd card, then went to the local chemist shop (where they have a printer) and printed them out.  I could have used a glue, like Pritt Stick, to stick em to my laptop, but went for double-sided sticky tape instead (I've got rolls of the stuff, which I use framing photographs (I'm an amateur tog).  The sticky tape is very adhesive, I haven't had a single sticker come loose on me yet.  So there's no need to keep looking for suitable stickers to decorate your device - the web is full of images, pretty much no matter what your tastes and interests are.  I like to dig at Big Bro and fascism, plus comics are probably my first love.  And Anthrax Cat is grreat - if you can find back issues or collections of *Transmetropolitan*, and like comics/Warren Ellis/Darick Robertson/sf/political comment/gonzo journalism (like Hunter S Thompson)/future tech/grim humour/whatever - grab em!  If anyone's interested, I can make the entire 60 issue run, in .cbr format, available for you to download (.cbr is a document format which is readable with pdf formats but is better with the **COMIX** app - available in the repos, via apt-get).  PM me for a link to download *Transmetropolitan* if that's what you want...).  

Share *your* decorations with us, so I don't feel like a one-off nut-job.  Thanks!!
