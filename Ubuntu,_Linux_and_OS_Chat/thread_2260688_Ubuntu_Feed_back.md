hopefully this is just some good feed, I i didnt see anywhere to log this, and no i didn't look very hard because I've got better things to do with my time. 

I am pretty new to Linux i guess, I started on Ubuntu 12.04, really liked it, but because i like PC games you can imagine it was pretty frustrating, and to be fair i felt that it was quite unstable. 

This was probably down to me being used to windows and not being used to the freedom that Linux gives you, and, although things crash from time to time, you have to agree that its pretty solid, i then tried mint, they have great ideas and scope, but don't have the guts to go out on there own, i think this is why the OS will not be completely stable until they break away from Ubuntu. 

Finally i installed Ubuntu on a media PC, 14.04, and i realized that it had become a monster, good looking, stable and fast as F____ruit Cakes. I'm now using 14.10 and its solid, you can feel the hard work that's gone into this OS, its surpasses windows as a Desktop, and it almost feels as tight as OS-X, with the right programs.

I just wish, and I know that this is not only the fault of Ubuntu, that we could get latest AMD drivers to work without having to re-install 10 times. 

Apart from that, Good work.
