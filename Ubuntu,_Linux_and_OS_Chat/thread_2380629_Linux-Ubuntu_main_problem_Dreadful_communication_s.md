### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2017-12-23
Well, . . . before about 2010 or thereabouts, it was considerably easier to install Linux on most PC's using BIOS & MBR systems (master boot record).   Then with UEFI, these installs did become more challenging even for the experienced.

I have done two or three  UEFI machines and those went well, but, these days it's just much simpler and straight-forward to buy your PC with Ubuntu (or other distro) "pre-installed" . . . just like you did for Windows.   The work of checking out compatibility has all been done for you and the actual set-up takes about 10 minutes.   Similar in complexity to setting up a smart-phone or tablet (e.g., not zactly Rocket Science . . )

Still, there will be some Windows habits to unlearn.   Remember, Unix was around when William the Younger (Gates) was still in Junior High School . . . See my signature link for more info.

PS:   the main reason why "Idiot Proof" instructions cannot be added to the install process is there is too much variation of motherboards, chipsets, and so on by the OEM's that a standard instruction set won't apply to all these different manufacturer variations.   Some component OEM's won't engineer or provide specs for Linux devs to write driver modules - - other OEM's won't comply with the UEFI specifications.   So, other than ALL Intel machines (cpu, wireless, graphics) it is up to the user to do the preliminary checking OR buy a pre-loaded system.
