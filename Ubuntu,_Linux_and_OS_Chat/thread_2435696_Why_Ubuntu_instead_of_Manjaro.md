### Post by yetimon_64 on 2020-01-25
> Can you argue in favor of Ubuntu, in what ways it's better than Manjaro?No. Not until I learn a lot more about Manjaro.

> Why Ubuntu instead of Manjaro?
For me it is my familiarity with the Debian packaging system (.deb) and management tools etc.

Late last year, after 12 years on Ubuntu, I installed Manjaro for the first time. I liked the system generally and could keep it updated with the GUI tool provided but never "felt at home" using it that way. I have become too used to updating in terminal on Ubuntu; a habit left over from the days when Update Manager on Ubuntu was often nicknamed Update Mangler (about the time of Jaunty/Karmic/Lucid, ~ 2009 or 2010).

After recent problems with my Xubuntu install and a reconfiguration of my partitioning layout I have only 3 of my original 5 installations left; Manjaro and Xubuntu are gone. I still intend to reinstall Manjaro and Xubuntu but I'll likely leave Manjaro till last and only after I put in a bit more effort to research/learn more about pacman commands and updating of rolling releases. Basically it takes time/energy to learn a new system; two things that are sorely lacking for me at the moment so Manjaro is "on the back burner" at this time for me.

One comment I can make about Manjaro was it was very easy to install and looks very good. From my recent attempt I can see myself using both systems in the near future. Not a switch over but more or less running both in dual boot and booting into whichever appeals to me on any particular day.

Cheers, yeti.

Debian is not very difficult to use but Ubuntu made it even easier by adding such things as the easier installation of proprietary drivers if they were needed.
