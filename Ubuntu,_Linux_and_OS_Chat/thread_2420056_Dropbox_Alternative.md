### Post by TheFu on 2019-05-29
owncloud, nextcloud, seafile, retroshare
[https://opensource.com/alternatives/dropbox](https://opensource.com/alternatives/dropbox)
[https://retroshare.cc/](https://retroshare.cc/) - is for security.

I've used retroshare with friends a few times. It is good for a tight family wanting a way to communicate with ZERO chance of others snooping.

I use nextcloud, but not really for the file aspects.  NextCloud has 50+ addons that do all sorts of extra things, often with a 1 click install.  Tasks, Contacts, Calendars, Chat (text, audio, video), plus I use some GPS and photo addons.

Live by the cloud, die by the cloud.
* [https://www.cnet.com/news/gmail-is-down-outage-around-the-world-for-some-users/](https://www.cnet.com/news/gmail-is-down-outage-around-the-world-for-some-users/)
* [https://www.newsweek.com/microsoft-outage-azure-down-offline-sharepoint-office-365-one-drive-server-1413499](https://www.newsweek.com/microsoft-outage-azure-down-offline-sharepoint-office-365-one-drive-server-1413499)
* [https://www.it.ucla.edu/news/box-outage-resolved-0](https://www.it.ucla.edu/news/box-outage-resolved-0)

Self hosting has liabilities, so I don't want to make it seem all roses. Still, I much prefer knowing where my data is physically located and having fast access from where I spend most of my time.
