### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2017-05-20
I think it's a mistake if Canonical releases future Ubuntu iso's that are vanilla Gnome3.   Remember, that Classic Gnome (Gnome2) was actually very functional without major modifications.   And that helped Canonical/Ubuntu tremendously.

Gnome3 without key tweaks and extensions is just a "non-starter" . . . and will quickly give Canonical/Ubuntu a bad reputation from the average home AND institutional users (educ, gov, companies, etc.).

Canonical got burned by trying to force the square peg into the round hole (convergence of phone/mir/unity8 et all) and was careening on the "right guardrail".    Now it's overcompensating and has swung the car to the other side of the road so hard it's careening on the opposite guardrail.

In other words, it doesn't take anywhere near the resources* to produce a slightly modified Gnome3 (similar to Antergos for example) that immediately is user friendly enough to put to work.     A couple of system folks can do it part time.    Or an "enthusiast" like me and millions of other can make the mods with 2-3 hours of research and testing.   BUT . . . millions of casual and non-techie, non-enthusiasts may not decide it's worth it and word of mouth will be negative.   Something Linux & Ubuntu doesn't need.

So, hopefully between now and then other alternatives will be available.   Maybe the System76 mods will become a download option on the Ubuntu website, along with the PPA info???   Or other semi-official iso's will become available???  While I also use and like the Budgie and Mate desktops, from a business perspective, a standard and well supported DE is still required.   Hopefully that will be a customized Gnome3 image.

* compared to original "convergence teams staffing" . .
