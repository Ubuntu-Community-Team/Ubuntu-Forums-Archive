### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-03-02
This is the correct method of working in the latest version of Ubuntu. You do not say which version of Ubuntu you are using.

When we launch a application from the Dash an icon will be put into the launcher (on the left side of the screen).

A solid triangle pointing to the the right ( > ) will appear on the left side of the icon and a solid triangle pointing to the left ( < ) will appear on the right side of the icon. When we minimise the application the solid triangle on the right side of the icon will disappear.

So, an icon on the launcher with a solid triangle on the left side indicates an opened application that is minimised. We can restore applications three ways

1) With a single click on its icon
2) by right clicking the icon and selecting the application name.
3) by pressing and holding the Super key. The first 9 icons in the launcher will be numbered. Whilst still holding the Super key press a number from 1 - 9 that is relevant to the application we wish to restore or launch.

If VLC media player is installed we first launch it from the Dash and its icon will appear in the launcher which we can lock to the launcher with a right click of the icon. VLC will also now appear in the menu of the Sound app-indicator which can be used to launch VLC. When VLC is running an app-indicator icon for VLC will also appear in the top panel.

To get VLC to restore or appear on the desktop click its icon in the launcher. The Hide VLC in the task bar menu option will minimise the VLC opened window and prevent a click on its launcher icon from restoring the application window. Clicking the Hide option again will restore or reveal the application window. Which can then be minimised to the launcher and restored by a click on the launcher icon. 

If we launch VLC from the sound app-indicator menu and then select Hide VLC from the VLC app-indicator menu, then the VLC icon will not appear in the launcher. Selecting the Hide option again and the VLC icon will appear in the launcher.

This is my experience from a very brief examination of VLC. I can give no advice about Qbittorrent as I do not have it installed.
