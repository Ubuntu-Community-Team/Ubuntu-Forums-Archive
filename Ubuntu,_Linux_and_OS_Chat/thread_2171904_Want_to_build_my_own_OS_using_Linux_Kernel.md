### Post by MG&amp;TL on 2013-09-02
You won't really be writing an OS from scratch if you use the Linux kernel, you'll be making a *distribution*.

Minor terminology slips aside, I suggest you look at [http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/](http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/) and follow their tutorials. Brush up on your programming, scripting, and Linux knowledge first though. Have fun!

### Post by King Dude on 2013-09-03
As many have said, [Linux From Scratch]("http://www.linuxfromscratch.org/") is the way to go. However, you can only go so far with LFS without any kind of a programming background. To truly create your own OS, you should know a few programming languages, such as C, C++, Python, Perl, Bash, and perhaps even Assembly.
