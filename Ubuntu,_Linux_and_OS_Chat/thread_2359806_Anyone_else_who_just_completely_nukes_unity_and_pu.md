Obviously you don't use unity. ;) Any desktop can have a dock and there are a few good ones to choose from. But Unity is much more than just a dock. The dash and its search function for apps and files (so that, among other things, you don't have to dump launcher icons all over your desktop, when you need to see your apps and document displayed in the desktop just press the super key),  the hud, the way it uses all the top space,  the global menu (I started to appreciate it when I switched to a small screen, on big screen it can be turned off) the smooth way of switching between virtual desktops (thanks to compiz)  which in gnome shell is a pain, shortcuts etc. To name just a few features.

Sorry anyone who says that unity is not for  production and that it is a phone interface obviously has not used it. It is no more a phone interface than the Mac OS but is more intuitive and straight forward to navigate. I find it much more handy for multi-tasking than traditional DEs like mate and xfce. For examples, the hud really kills it when you want to easily switch between like 20 open pdf files using a tabbed pdf viewer like qpdfview; locating applications and files with the dash is much faster and more elegant than using the menus or dumping desktop files randomly all over the desktop like all users of "traditional desktop" do (which proves that everyone thinks that those classic menus are inefficient even though they scream when they are gone).

So far I find KDE's plasma 5 most promising in reproducing many of unity's features, but KDE has its own quirks like icons kind of move all over the places on the "dock" even when they are pinned.
