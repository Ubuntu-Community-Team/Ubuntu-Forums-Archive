### Post by 1clue on 2016-03-15
While I can understand your mom not wanting something bad to happen to a computer she bought, I get a little bent when someone says it will trash the computer.

I'm a 50 year old software developer, and have never bought Windows except for one time when I bought a laptop for my wife. I've been using Linux as my workstation since 1996. I run Windows, but in a VM provided by my employer, for testing purposes, on hardware my employer provided (which runs Mac OS).  Most of my development work is done on Linux. The rest is done on a Mac. The only thing Windows is for is for testing, since our clients use Windows we must test there.

Go to [http://top500.org](http://top500.org), go to the Statistics menu and click Sublist generator.  Top500.org is a site that keeps track of the fastest publicly-acknowledged computers on the planet.  It's a given that there will be some secret government and private organizations who have faster computers, but chances are at any time they will resemble computers on the public list.

So back to the web site. Go down to "OS Family" and choose Linux.  Note that this month there are two options in this list:  UNIX or Linux.  Click search with OS Family=Linux and you will see 494 results.  That means of the 500 fastest computers on the planet, 6 of them do NOT run Linux.

Go back up and change OS Family to UNIX, and submit.  You'll see that the top entry is 210.  That means the top 209 fastest computers on the planet are Linux.  Note that Windows is not on the list at all. The times I've seen it show up, it's near the bottom of the list.

While this list has very little to do with your home computer, it does have to do with the stability, reliability, adaptability and scalability of Linux. You already mentioned Android. Linux runs on phones, watches, rings (!!?!) and everything else all the way up to supercomputers. It's used on supercomputers not because it's cheap, but because it can be tailored to whatever purpose they need the computer for. Anything from a smart ring to a supercomputer.
