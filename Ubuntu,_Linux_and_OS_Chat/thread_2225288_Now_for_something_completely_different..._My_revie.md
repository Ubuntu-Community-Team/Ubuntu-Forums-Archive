### Post by estamets on 2014-05-20
Some of you may be interested, others not. I just thought some might like to hear a linux user talking about a Windows phone. Don't flame me too hard for at least exposing this a option. I know we all "hate" Microsoft to some extent. But at least hear me out...

Quick specs:

5.3 Inch display
2.2ghz Snapdragon Processor
2gb of Ram
32gb of storage

First things first. It's a Windows phone. Oddly and similarly to Linux in the computing space.. We're fighting for apps that giants like Android and IOS have. The odd thing that I find amusing though, we're talking about Microsoft being a underdog with a computing device. I'm guessing Ubuntu Phone will have the same issues when it goes live also.

The phone shipped with regular Windows 8 mobile. I didn't hate it, in fact, I never really "hated" Windows 8 in general on either a desktop or a tablet device. I just didn't think it was fully baked when they released it. So, about 3 days in with the phone, I upgraded to Windows 8.1 developer release. This process is dead simple.

Much like upgrading from XP service pack 1 to XP service pack 2, it's a totally different OS. Point releases in this case make the whole phone experience totally different.

The inclusion of Cortana (Microsoft's answer to Google Now and Siri) makes a world of difference. I know it's dangerous to upgrade to a OS that isn't released yet but I've done it with Ubuntu for years. I was running 14.04 since last Thanksgiving. This seems to be the same way. Slight hiccups but nothing serious. I didn't brick my phone or anything. Just about the only issue I've had is unlocking the phone seems to induce a slight delay when you're on Wifi.

The battery life on this phone is roughly 2 days and at the end of 48 hours, I'm normally at somewhere between 20-30 percent. Playing my drag racing game, texting, talk time about 1 to 3.5 hours in total a day. Cortana usage every couple of hours because I want to play with it. I really don't stream music or anything like that so I can't answer for that. I just use my phone as a information device, not a music player.

New in Windows 8.1. The "Action Center" and obviously Cortana. The Action Center would be a notification center in IOS and Android. The neat thing is, I can access the action center while the phone is still locked. I can read the first line of my texts, who's calls I missed and anything else that's integrated with it. I don't do facebook or whatsapp or anything. In fact, I'm not totally sure what Whatsapp is.

To access Cortana, you can press the magnifying glass on the bottom right of the phone or use the live tile. You can text it, it will text back or you can talk to it and it will talk back. I normally use it to text my wife and the speech recognition is as good as anything else. I also use it to turn Wifi on and off, check the weather.. yada yada.. Nothing special. You can tell Cortana specific things that interest you and it will use Bing (Yeah, it's all bing'ed out.. hate to say that.) I said Live tile.. Well, if you tell it what you want it to "know" about specific things, it'll flash headlines based on your interests. This is very simple to set up.

Live Tiles. Well, this is where things get interesting. Obviously Microsoft wants the same interface across platforms. Live tiles didn't seem that great in desktop form but they make perfect sense for mobile. I don't have to click anything to see what the temp is, who texted me, or see the latest headlines or how much money I lost in the stock market. . I just look at the tile. Literally, unlock the phone and look. In fact, you can even program your lock screen to give you the weather, news, facebook whatever.. You don't even have to unlock the phone to get to the information you want.

Customization. Well, here's another interesting thing. With Windows Phone 8.1, it's relatively easy to customize. I'd say as accessible as Unity. I say that because you CANNOT do certain things in Unity. The OS won't allow you too. You can change (or remove) whatever you want from the main screen. Just unpin it. You can also make a live tile as big or small as you want. The attached image is a simple black/white scheme but I like simple and that's a simple as it gets. You can pin or unpin or even UNINSTALL any app that came with the phone barring a few exceptions. If I don't like any of the Nokia stuff? Well, I just point, hold and uninstall.

Yes, it does have IE 11. Ugg.. It works well enough for what I do.. The interesting thing is, I managed to get a adblocker installed using a smart screen filter plugin to block ads in IE. Imagine that. 


Like I said, this is a loyal Ubuntu user since Badger. I'm not intending to start a flame war or anything like that. I really can't explain why I chose a Windows phone either. All I know is that I hook it up and it will detect my phone as a Windows Phone in Ubuntu 14.04 unlike my Motorola Droid M. I can swap photo's and all that stuff.. 

Ask me whatever you want and I'll do the best to answer and or elaborate if necessary.

### Post by estamets on 2014-05-21
@tgalati I've had the phone for about a week and half now and the only time it rebooted was when it was updating to Windows 8.1, other than that, It's been fine.

As far as apps locking up.. I haven't really experienced that. My drag racing game by creative mobile can be laggy at times. I don't know if it's the game or the phone though. Mind you, Nokia hasn't released the firmware for the Lumia 929 for Windows 8.1 so the slight delays with my lockscreen on wifi and my game being laggy might be fixed up when Nokia rolls out the firmware update. It's a gray area though; I've heard others have major issues with apps, mainly Whatsapp. 

@Nobugs  Of the three OS's you listed, I've only used Android. I do like the fact that I don't have to open any apps to glance at the weather or the stock market. I know you can do this in Android too, but with ads. Cortana is pretty good about calling/texting people and setting reminders and such. Cortana is also surprisingly good at settings. I can tell it to turn on/off Wifi, change my background etc.. I also like that I can access my notifications without unlocking the phone.

What I don't like and/or getting used to. The lock button is in a weird spot. You have the volume buttons on the top corner, the lock button in the middle and a dedicated camera button on the bottom. You can't use the volume keys to lock or unlock the phone. You can however go directly into the camera by hitting the camera button. 

Also, I miss the fact that I could generate rules on the phone. Like, on my android, I could tell it to turn on Wifi when I'm near home and turn it off when I leave. If there is a way to do this, I have yet to find it. 

Obviously comparing Android's app store to Windows App store, you're gonna be disappointed. Standard things like reddit, twitter, facebook and youtube are there but custom apps for fitness tracking (fitbit?) aren't there yet. I've never used Bing fitness so I have no idea if that compares.
