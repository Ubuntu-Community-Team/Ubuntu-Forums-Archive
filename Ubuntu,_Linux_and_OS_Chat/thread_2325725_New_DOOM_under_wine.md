### Post by ZoiaGuyver on 2016-06-01
Hmm "Linux is not for gaming", I think Valve/Steam, Epic Games/UE4, Crytek/CryEngine and most the "gaming servers" people are playing on would disagree with that.

At the moment Linux has a massive potential for development and gaming, it's just not reached a point where it has snowballed. New tech like Vulkan in the future will push the envelope a lot further, but for most at the mo it's a waiting game, we are seeing ports for Linux and a general direction/attitude change in the games industry toward Linux support. Valve getting involved in development of a Linux based gaming OS has pushed a lot of the manufacturers to improve hand over fist with drivers for Linux. 

The problem at the moment Is it's really a new target for Linux (yes people have been playing games for years on Linux, but Linux was never aimed at "Gamers", Windows did). Having companies like Valve, Epic and Crytek backing Linux is what Linux needs, but this isn't going to change overnight you have over 20 years of a "nothing more than a basement devs system" mindset to overcome.

Getting back to the topic though, there have been some advances with the latest wine in running Doom 2016, at the moment the stickler is the DRM it seems and they are only on the first steps of being able to work with it. I think we will see it in Wine before we see a "native" port (mainly as id was bought by Zenimax) if we ever see it native. But I'm pretty sure the guys at the Wine Project are hard at work on getting it going.
