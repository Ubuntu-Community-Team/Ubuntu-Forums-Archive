### Post by mystics on 2015-09-16
From the sounds of it, your love is Mate, not any particular distro. It  is just that Arch was used by someone that showed you how great Mate  could be, but I'd imagine you could make it look just as nice in an  easier distro like Ubuntu Mate or Linux Mint Mate.

> **Higgins909 said:**
> It's also said arch will make you smarter with linux or something?

Things  like Arch and Gentoo force you to learn Linux, so their average,  every-day user is likely more Linux-smart by necessity. I'm pretty sure  Ubuntu could give you the same amount of knowledge if you put in the  effort. 

But I guess if you want strong motivation, going with  Arch is one way to force it on yourself. With Ubuntu, you'd run the risk  of getting so use the ease-of-use you might loose interest in breaking  stuff. 

Still, best not to think of Arch as magically making your  smarter. That will lead to frustration, and your ego will take a beating  when you eventually meet an Ubuntu user that knows more than you.

> performance?

Most benchmark comparisons I've seen are old (as in five years old) , but tend to put Arch and Ubuntu on equal footing.

> customization?

Like I mentioned above, Arch forces  you to learn Linux, so the amount of customization you realize you have  access to will likely be greater, but I believe Ubuntu will allow you  to get down in to most of that yourself. I guess in the long run, Arch  might have more customization once you get really in to things (not  sure, as I've never gotten that deep in to Arch), but unless you want to  get deep down into the system, this may be more an issue of things like  desktop environment than distro.

> reliability?

Ubuntu probably has the edge here.  Most people I know who use Arch will openly admit that it is an  adventure in fixing stuff, but that's part of the fun of the distro.  Ubuntu is based around being easy-to-use for the average consumer. Arch  is for people embracing their inner computer nerd.
