### Post by mantaboo on 2013-12-11
I've been using Arch for quite some time now. I love how fast and responsive my system is with it. Even my aging Acer Aspire 7541 laptop seems to zing with Arch installed on it. Lately I've been growing tired of all of the tweaking and configuring that you need to with Arch though. Over the last several days I've been installing different distros in VirtualBox to try to find a good replacement candidate. I wasn't really planning on even giving Ubuntu chance at first but I installed several different distros, i.e. OpenSuSe, Fedora, Debian, Mint(Cinnamon and Mate), Kubuntu and Mageia which is actually currently installed on my laptop at the moment. Anyhow, none of them felt like a good fit. On a whim I went ahead and downloaded Ubuntu and installed it in VirtualBox and played with it for a bit. I wasn't quite feeling at home with Unity at first as I've predominately used KDE since the beginning of my adventures in Linux which date back to the mid 90's with Slackware. Well I've been having a horrible time trying to get rid of the awful screen tears that seem to plague KDE with the proprietary NVidia drivers installed and instead of installing Gnome and going through all of the configuration and tweaking to get everything running nice and smooth, I decided to go ahead and install Ubuntu. It didn't take too much time to get a feel for things and I am very pleased with it so far. It runs very smooth on my system and the screen tearing is non-existent, which makes me very happy! :) So after a few years hiatus from Ubuntu, I'm actually glad I gave it another chance. In fact, I'm going to install it on my laptop too.
