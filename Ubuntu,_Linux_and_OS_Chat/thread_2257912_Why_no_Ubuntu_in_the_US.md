### Post by ajgreeny on 2014-12-23
When Netbooks first arrived on the scene here in UK there were several that were available with the Netbook edition of Ubuntu 10.04 that could be bought from retailers.

"Great!" I thought,"That will make life easy when I need replacement machines" as Ubuntu was then, and had been for some time, the only OS in my house, on both my desktop and my wife's laptop.

However the Ubuntu systems available then from Dell and Samsung, and possibly others, (but they are the two I was aware of), disappeared very quickly, not because they were sold but because too many buyers took them home and then took them straight back to the shop as they could not get any of their software to install on them, and did not understand how to use Linux.
So before very long, the machines with Ubuntu were removed from sale in those retailers that had sold them and replaced with the same machines running WinXP or another Windows starter edition.  Interestingly there was no difference in price between the Ubuntu and Windows machines, as far as I can remember.

All this proves is that MS has such a hold on the market, that making inroads with a new OS without more information being made available to the purchasers by retailers is totally wasted, and I know from personal experience that the vast majority of retail staff in UK have no knowledge of Linux as an OS, and in many cases do not even know it exists, which is not too far removed from the gist of your conversation with the Dell tech person.

I have now stopped adding to the MS coffers by buying from only those companies in UK that will sell a machine without an OS, built to a spec that I know will run Ubuntu, and I then install my own OS.  Even with the intricacies of UEFI that is still a quick simple job when there is no dual boot to worry about.

### Post by DuckHook on 2014-12-23
> **vtpoet2 said:**
> In my continuing, but seemingly fruitless search for a netbook/or hybrid...Does [this]("http://www.linux-netbook.com/") site help?> ...Is there a conspiracy between MS and DELL to keep Ubuntu out of the US market? Do I have to order it from Germany?I wouldn't go so far as to call it a conspiracy, but is MS continuing to leverage their desktop dominance to keep Linux out? As your Dell rep might say, "You betcha!" *We* might have to pay hundreds of dollars for an MS license, but when a company like Dell buys them by the hundreds every day, you can bet that their volume discount will be pennies to our dollars. Moreover, MS is not stupid: like all drug dealers, they know that the first taste has to be free. It's the subsequent addiction that's the real cash cow. So, there's no reason for Dell to continue sustaining the expense of supporting a marginal OS when the monopolist leader is willing to basically throw licenses at them. There are further dynamics at work too. MS is fully aware that Win 8 was not received well. In fact, they are terrified of peeved customers experimenting with alternatives (not just Linux). This is a further impetus for them to do whatever they must to ensure that all computers are preloaded with Windows, and that those boxes are very difficult to run anything else on. Last but not least, the success of Linux/Android in the mobile space, which is the fastest growing segment of IT by far, has them soiling their shorts. MS didn't get where they are by ignoring existential threats to their business.

This is just a long-winded way of saying: I doubt you will ever again see Dell offering Linux preloaded on consumer-grade machines. I wouldn't be surprised if MS paid Dell to keep Linux off anything except so-called "developer" models which are price-hiked to the point of absurdity.

I encourage you to buy from Linux-friendly vendors or just load Ubuntu onto a compatible machine. I tend to avoid this problem by using older laptops that will install Ubuntu without any hitches at all, but I realize this is not an appropriate solution for everyone.
