### Post by speedwell68 on 2014-09-06
The expression hacker has been misused by the public and media at large since the mid 80s and the advent of films like War Games.  This is never going to change.  This will not paint Ubuntu or Linux as a whole in a bad light.  The concept of basic online/computer/phone security passes most users by.

There is a certain brand of ISP router that is used widely in the UK.  It comes with a default SSID and Wifi key.  I have seen one placed in a shop window with the sticker for all the world to see, seriously.  There is a well know exploit with these routers that means anyone with a laptop can get the Wifi key in seconds.  The amount of people that still won't take security seriously after I show them that one is amazing.  They just believe that it won't happen to them.

Where I used to work a admin manager gave every member of staff the Wifi key, that we weren't really allowed to have.  I publicly made a point of refusing the offer and made a point voicing my opinion as to why it wasn't a very good idea.  Anyway months pass and it became apparent that someone had been into some 'secure' files stored on the only PC on that particular network.  I checked the CCTV and no one had used that computer other than this particular admin manager.  I had enough at this point.  Borrowed a colleague's phone and that yielded me the Wifi key.  I then connected to the Wifi and then showed this woman the files on her PC, using a cheap android tablet.  Needless to say the IT dept had her shot.
