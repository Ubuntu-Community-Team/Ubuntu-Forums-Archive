### Post by lads on 2023-10-27
Hi all.

The tripartite dialogue is reportedly going in haste. A final document is expected in the next few weeks, in time for approval still during the Spanish presidency. In all likelihood this means few modifications to the draft(s), i.e. no thorough exemption to open source software.

In [one of its latest documents]("https://www.linuxfoundation.org/blog/open-source-and-the-cra-will-not-work"), the Linux Foundation is making it clear that without the exemption an expunge from the European Union will result: 

> To be clear, global open source software projects will be unable to strictly comply with provisions contained in current drafts of the CRA. Much of the world's open source software will likely soon be either 1) blocked for distribution in the EU or 2) publicly accessible with caveats similar to *“this software is not appropriate or approved for use or distribution in the European Union market.”* 

Has Canonical expressed itself on the CRA? Is it even realistic to attempt at certifying Ubuntu? I guess without the Linux Kernel there isn't much hope, but any insights would be welcome.
