### Post by QIII on 2017-08-20
Tuxguitar is in the Universe repository.  The Universe repository is gathered from external sources (that is, it is not part of the core Ubuntu repos).  What is in the Universe repository are packages that are maintained by volunteers know as MOTUs (Masters of the Universe).

You are welcome to contact the package maintainer and ask why the package has not been updated.  

Please use this email address:  [email]ubuntu-motu@lists.ubuntu.com[/email]

### Post by ardouronerous on 2017-08-20
Based off my experiences with Xubuntu and Lubuntu LTS releases, I stick to LTS because I don't want upgrade every 6 months, Ubuntu just works.

The installer doesn't give you headaches unlike other Linux distros and Linus Torvalds agrees. In 2008, Linus tried and failed to install Debian because he found the installer too difficult.

> I&#8217;m sorry, you may want to close your ears now, I want a distribution to  be easy to install, so that I can just get on with my life, which is  mostly kernel.

I&#8217;ve tried Ubuntu a couple of times over the years, mainly because the thing Ubuntu did so well was make Debian usable.

Ubuntu just works out of the box, almost everything you need in already pre-installed and you don't need to live in the Terminal to enjoy Ubuntu, and this really bodes well and is a great selling point for Windows users migrating into Linux who had very little to no experience using the command prompt.

And finally, the Ubuntu Forums community is the friendliest community I've come across, I've been to other Linux forums and they are very hostile to newbies for some reason, while here at Ubuntu Forums, I've met some of the most patient and understanding users I've ever met, they made my migration from Windows to Linux a breeze.

To sum it up, why Ubuntu is popular.

1) Easy to install.
2) Works out of the box.
3) Friendliest user forum ever.
