### Post by QIII on 2017-06-01
There is still a Red Hat desktop and a SUSE desktop.  For each there is also a community supported open source version and an open source "enterprise" version.  In Red Hat's case, Fedora is effectively the test-bed for what will be incorporated into RHEL.

What would spell the demise of Canonical is a sudden collapse of this new business model (the jumped off of conversion just in time).  That would be the end of Ubuntu on the desktop unless it was taken up by the community.
