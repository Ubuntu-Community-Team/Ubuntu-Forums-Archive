### Post by TheFu on 2022-04-30
**Nobody here works for Canonical.**

You probably know more about where to look for certs that we do. I'd watch the blog RSS feed for any announcement.

I'm not certified in any OS.  Took plenty of classes (free and paid) over the decades, but always decided that for what I wanted in a career, certification would be more of a detriment than aid.  But I'm older. People expect knowledge. I get the feeling that certs are the main way that HR drones use to filter younger, unqualified, candidates from positions.  

For some time, I was part of the interview process in a few companies. I saw plenty of "certified" people who lacked the knowledge their certs claimed. At least, from what I could tell by asking questions in areas that I know.  I've also been on the other side. Interview went great, but they asked me to take a test in a language that was on my resume, but that I hadn't used in about 2 yrs, on a platform I hadn't used in 4 yrs, with a compiler that I'd never used. About 15 minutes in, I decided this wasn't the job for me, thanked the test proctor and left. Wrote a thank you to the interviewer, explained why I'd left, and never heard back from them again.

In my country, the RHCE pays significantly more than any other OS cert and is the minimum required, except for govt jobs.
