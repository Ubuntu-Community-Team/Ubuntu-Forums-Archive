### Post by 3rdalbum on 2014-03-06
I would disagree with your assertions:

1. If people are still on XP, are they really going to suddenly switch to Linux? Don't you think most of them will stay on XP anyway, and when their computer dies switch to a later preinstalled Windows OS? They are still on XP because they don't like change. A change to a completely foreign OS is definitely NOT on the cards.

2. A distro that mimics XP could be more confusing than one that doesn't. Users will feel that this is familiar when in fact it works nothing like Windows. They will try installing Windows programs and find they don't work. The mimickery will not be perfect and whenever the alien system underneath is exposed this will be more confusing for someone who thought this system worked like XP.

It could also be really negative for perceptions of Linux. If people start thinking that Linux "looks like XP" they might believe it's a cheap knock-off of Windows, or that Linux developers are so unoriginal that they didn't design a desktop by themselves.
