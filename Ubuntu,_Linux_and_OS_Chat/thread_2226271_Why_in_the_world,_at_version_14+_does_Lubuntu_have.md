### Post by gtravis3 on 2014-05-26
I was looking around in my and accessories and found three terminals. LXTerminal, UXTerm, and Xterm. I did not check around for this when I was using the ubuntu DE. I mean really, why at this point? I would have thought that the developers, etc. would have decided which was the best and just had it in the software package. Lubuntu in particular because it is my impression this is supposed to a small, light package. Evidently I'm wrong.

 I can't remember which one that I was using the ubuntu DE, but it evidently was not UX or X term because the fonts size and styles were pretty bad as compaired to what was on the ubuntu DE and even LXTerm on Lubuntu. I discovered that on the others, one can ctrl click and get truetype which is somewhat better.

So, for now I am using LXTerm, which has a really nice preferences option, almost Mac Terminal like.  I have noticed one interesting thing; the white text colors will start out nice and bright then 'fade' down a noticable bit. There is no dimming on the computer screen nor is there a dimming of the ls utility return. So my vote would be to get ride of the other terminals and just include the LXTerminal.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2014-05-26
All Linux distros come with Xterm, although normally it doesn't have an icon. If your system ever gets so broken that LXTerminal doesn't work, well, then XTerm will work. Actually I think this happened to me once, so it's not a hypothetical situation.

I do broadly agree with you, though. Assuming all three terminals are indeed in a default Lubuntu install, that's slightly wasteful. Ubuntu originally made its name by only having one program per task. Before Ubuntu, a default install of a Linux distribution would leave you with Openoffice and Gnome Office, two image editors, three web browsers, an icon editor, two speech synthesis engines, three email clients, four music players and a partridge in a pear tree. Downloading a whole DVD image on the internet connections we had in 2003 really stank. Thank god for Ubuntu.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-05-26
Ubuntu also has UXterm and Xterm as well as Gnome Terminal. When did "light" come to mean "not taking up much space on the hard disk"? We can always uninstall. 

Both UXterm and Xterm work with or on the X window system. When Linux distributions replace X then UXterm and Xterm will have to be removed from the distributions. It makes no difference whether the replacement for X is Mir or some Wayland compositor. These two utilities do not have a future.
