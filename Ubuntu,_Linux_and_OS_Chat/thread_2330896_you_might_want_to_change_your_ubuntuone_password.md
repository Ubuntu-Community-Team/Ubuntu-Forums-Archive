It's best if you don't rely on third-hand information written by journalists who do not have all the facts to hand.

The 2 million or so forum (vbulletin) passwords that the hackers obtained were completely and utterly useless. After the hack in 2013, the use of forum (vbulletin) passwords was discontinued in favour of Ubuntu One SSO login, relying on Ubuntu One account passwords which have not been compromised. In 2013, all the vbulletin passwords were randomised - that means that they were replaced with random strings - as an added precaution. That is what the latest hacker has got hold of: 2 million meaningless random strings, in lieu of valid passwords!
