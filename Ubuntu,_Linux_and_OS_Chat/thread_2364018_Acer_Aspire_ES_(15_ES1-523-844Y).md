### Post by jt4u on 2017-06-17
So I received my son's new laptop yesterday and proceeded with installing 17.04 on it after finding out what to do to enable the dual boot. It's been a bit of a pain as you first need to set a bios administrator password to access the "select an uefi file as trusted", then you must select the "shimx64.efi". But it works fine then.

I struggled with wifi as, for unknown reasons, I couldn't get the MAC address from the wifi adapter until I disabled the MAC filtering and used the network informations. ifconfig reported some strange unreliable MAC address! It looks,like it works now. 

The touchpad also required to change the BIOS from Advanced to Basic to work. 

One thing I haven't been able to make work yet is bluetooth. It's not detecting my audio headset. But it's not high on the list so, I'll have another look if I have time.

Now, I need to hand it over to my son and see what's working and what's not. My initial tests were positive, so I keep my fingers crossed. 

I'll post more if I run into other things and I'll report back on how well it works if it does. 

I was looking for an affordable laptop for a teenager. I have not been too pleased with my previous purchase of a Lenovo G50 a couple of years ago for my older son. Audio is a real pain and wifi required some tweaking to work properly. Right or wrong it put me off Lenovo for a while.
