### Post by Juan_Jess_Parede on 2016-01-30
Every music recording engineer can explain, better than me, that the best stereo sound is reachable only when it is full mono sound systems compatible.

But... Somebody can tell that all the hardware (and software) available today works on stereo. And, it is absolutely true!!!

Whatever, the first premise is still valid: The best stereo sound is when, and only when, it is full mono compatible.

So... How can we hear an stereo sound recording and to know if it is (or not) full compatible with a mono sound system?

In this line, I wonder... Why there is no an [Stereo/Mono] switch into the sound mixer we have on Linux? Would it be so hard to add this feature? ???

Of course, I'm talking about an absolutely non-destructive processing: The switch has to affect ONLY the final sound stage part of the sound system (hardware sound amplifier), not the original sound source (audio file).

Just an idea.
