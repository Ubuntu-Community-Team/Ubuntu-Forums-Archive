### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-06-26
In my opinion, no. Not sure what you mean by Lubuntu not receiving the love of Ubuntu. Not the case. You are using a supported release? LTS releases don't get the updates the most recent releases get, but that is the nature of the beast.

I have Xubuntu-core, even lighter than Lubuntu I'd imagine, on all my machines, including my AV desktop with i7, 16Gb RAM and GTX 970. Flies.

You will note that AV specific distros tend to use xfce4 or lxde as the desktop environment rather than Unity and/or are based on a light version of Xubuntu (Ubuntu Studio, for instance). Unity is the default DE for Ubuntu (at the moment, but believe that's switching back to Gnome in the next release or the one after). The idea of using a lighter OS is to keep resources use as low as possible so they can be used for actual AV work!

Anyhow, if you want the fastest and least 'bloated' experience you can get, you're pretty close already with what you know and like. Why change? The only thing achieved by installing Ubuntu may be a slowing of the system and the addition of a bunch of stuff you will never use.

Good luck.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2017-06-27
I totally agree with BB about this.

All the *ubuntu family of OSs use the same repositories and can therefore have as many or as few of the packages that are available in the repos according to your personal choice.

The unity DE that is about to disappear as the default DE of Ubuntu and be replaced by gnome-shell, is pretty heavy, and some users have found it difficult to navigate around.  I used to be one of those who found it confusing, but continued use of it in a virtual installation has shown me that it is actually quite logical it its use; it is however, very different from the more normal menu driven DEs such as XFCE or LXDE.

Like you I also have a fairly powerful machine but have not used Ubuntu with unity as my main installation other than in a virtual install as I also prefer the menu driven DEs, and in my case, Xubuntu is my choice.

Use whatever DE you prefer to use and don't worry about the visual side of the GUI; they can all do the same work but simply do it, or more correctly, let you do it, in different ways.
