### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-11-16
It's my impression that most problems of the number and severity you describe are self-inflicted, often by the use of poorly supported hardware or by mixing and matching large and complex software collections -- like desktop environments -- on the same system. While you can install Gnome on KDE, or XFCE on Unity, etc., I think you're just increasing the risk of eventual breakage. That's a risk that increases when we try to delete one of those DE's and don't notice everything that's being removed.

Or just by undisciplined use of PPA's.

Many sites I've come across offer bad, if not bogus, advice.

Users also often fail to remove all the detritus from a failed proprietary driver install before trying again. 

I find that distributions backed by responsible committed organizations -- Canonical, Fedora/RedHat, OpenSuse, Debian, Mint, for example -- do what they say they will do on the rather mainstream hardware I use. I avoid distributions that are obviously the products of one or just a few enthusiasts.
