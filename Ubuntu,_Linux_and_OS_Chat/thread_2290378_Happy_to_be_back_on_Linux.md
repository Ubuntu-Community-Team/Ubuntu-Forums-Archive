### Post by paul207 on 2015-08-11
Long time Linux user here (17 years). Share a birthday with Gnome and started in 1998. I have used Linux as my sole desktop since and had a brief (6 year) hiatus with OSX, until I tired of being shoved down a path I didn't want to walk. So, on this monster Mac Pro of mine, I am running Ubuntu Gnome. After many trials and tribulations, (read that as distro installs), I have settled on Ubuntu Gnome. I was looking for a Gnome based distro and found a few, but none worked as well as Ubuntu. I could not, as much as I tried, to get used to Unity, so when I discovered there was a Gnome Ubuntu flavour, I dove in. I am a happy camper: everything works, system is fast and stable, what more can you ask for?!

Selling the Mac Pro beast in a few months and I'll be back on "real" Intel hardware in the form of an HP z800 workstation. Gotten so used to having a bunch of Xenon's and a bunch of ram, I am spoiled...

Be careful with HP workstations. HP often makes their UEFI hard-coded to support Windows-only. Beware.  I don't know if this applies to the specific machine you've selected - definitely look around for install issues before jumping in.
[https://askubuntu.com/questions/486752/dual-boot-win-8-ubuntu-loads-only-win](https://askubuntu.com/questions/486752/dual-boot-win-8-ubuntu-loads-only-win)

It is just easier to spend your money from vendors that aren't out to screw Linux users, IMHO.
