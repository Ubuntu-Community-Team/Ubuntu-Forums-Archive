That's the greatest advantage of the cloud and why it's the future of computing. If a developper writte a web application, he can be sure that it will be cross-platform, that the users will be able to use it regardless of the device and the OS. The web is agnostic as a platform and everyone can have access to it whether they are on Linux, Windows, Android ect. That's not the case with native apps. The developpers have to adapt their software for every single OS and as result Linux is often marginalized.

Wouldn't be interesting to use Adobe Photoshop in a powerful web app on my Ubuntu install? Or to use Netflix or Amazon Prime in a convenient way through my browser without always having to find workarounds? This reason alone is enough to dissuade many people to switch over Linux.
