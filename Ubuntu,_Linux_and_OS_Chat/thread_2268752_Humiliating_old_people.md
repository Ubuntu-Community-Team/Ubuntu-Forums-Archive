### Post by DuckHook on 2015-03-22
> **Seppo_Jyrkinen said:**
> Permanent OS until the eternity... perhaps not so long time, but several decades anyhow.There is a solution to your problem. And since you are posting on a Linux forum, the answer is actually an obvious one: all you have to do is create your own distribution based on the principle that the user interface will never change. For example, you can keep Firefox in it's current form "forever"&#8213;as you put it. You can do the same with Ubuntu&#8213;freeze it in its current form "forever". These are all open source projects. No secrets. No restrictions. Nothing hidden. Nothing to stop you from creating a different flavour, or even forking it if you wish. It's not like MS or Apple jailware where you have to swallow whatever they force-feed you. It's Linux. Go custom. Go retro. Go medieval. Go nuts.

But, upon reflection, it's not that easy, is it? You would have to maintain it. You would have to patch security holes. If something better than HTTPS comes along, and your bank decides that it wants to make use of that better security, you would have to add that feature to your "frozen" fork of Firefox without changing its look and feel. You might have to do the same thing for your "permanent" OS itself. And while the mainline distro is adapting to these changes, evolving its guts and its looks, you would be duplicating those efforts (in order to stay functional), reinventing the wheel every time, but with the added burden of maintaining the delusion that nothing has changed.

To the extent that you are warning against change merely for change's sake&#8213;change without any rhyme or reason&#8213;you have my ear. I can agree with that. But if what you want is to obligate *others* to create for you a changeless bubble world, one frozen in time, so that you never have to deal with the inconvenience of learning something new, then, like many others on this thread, I strongly and fundamentally disagree with you. If you insist upon such a world, then go make it yourself. See how many others you get to sign on.

I'm an old guy. Change does not humiliate us old guys. We old guys are a lot tougher than that. But I will tell you who it does humiliate: it humiliates the self-satisfied, the bloody-minded, the already-know-it-alls, the ones who think that the world ought to fit itself to their limitations. And that crowd comes in all ages, in all shapes and sizes. For those people, I will go so far as to say that change *ought* to humiliate them. Because it's wrong to hold back betterment of our tools and of our world to cater to such people. In fact, it would be an awful world in which they were *not* humiliated.
