### Post by udippel on 2019-02-16
With the Helix 2 being my favourite laptop/tablet for a number of reasons, I have tried to get 18.04 installed. The begin was fantastic, booted, I could use my finger as well as the pen (ThinkPad), and the rubber tip of the cheapo-stylos also worked out of the box. WiFi also no problem. Both cameras work fine out of the box. Sound emanates from the speakers.

The most major annoyance to me is the inability to get proper screen clicks, left, right at least. I tried quite a lot, including resting the finger or pen for some time at the location for the right click. Only left works. I also tried the System Settings, but what could have served me, was grayed out. I tried some other software that was suggested by Dr. Google, but to no avail so far.
The microphone is not working, though pavucontrol sees it. 
The CPUs seem to consume comparatively more power than on the (double boot on internal hard disk) W10. At least, Core Temp there usually shows around 50°, here I get usually close to 60°. 

There are some posts for this machine on the Archos-site, but it does look that both distributions are by now dissimilar. So the help over there is rather different from what one might have to do here. 
Items like screen rotation do not bother me as of now, as long as I need to get more basic stuff still resolved. 
The LTE-modem is detected, I haven't yet had the chance to insert a SIM-card. 

Since I have been on *nix for more than 20 years now, I would really want to get everything working. Instead of going back to Windows. I'll report here, whenever I encounter success. Promised!

This actually being a chat -as is indicated further up ("this sub-forum is for chat, not support – hence the name") I dare to also express some disappointment. Not explicitly with *buntu or *nix. And yet, haven't we been told when we were weaned off KDE 3 (and later KDE 4) that all this was for the better of the project, since touch screens and mobiles were coming and that we needed a proper framework for these upcoming interfaces?  </rant>
