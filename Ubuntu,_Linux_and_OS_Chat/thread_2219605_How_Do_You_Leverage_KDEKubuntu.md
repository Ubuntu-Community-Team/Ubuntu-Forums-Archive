### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-04-24
Every so often, I succumb to some article or post extolling the wonders of KDE.  I install the thing, use it for a few days, decide it isn't really for me, and move on.

So, here I am on Kubuntu 14.04 and, I have to say, it's the slickest KDE install I've ever used.  Even the font rendering -- my Number One priority -- is *almost* as good as in Unity 14.04. (Pretty close, but still different.  A QT vs GTK thing?) I could live with it. I'm thinking about it. Maybe I won't move on this time.

My usual routine is to open a minimum of three apps on three workspaces and go from there. (Those three are Firefox, Thunderbird, and Liferea, the RSS reader.  They are always open. They are also all GTK apps.) I want to switch between workspaces with one click, and I want minimized windows to open in their workspace, not my current workspace.

All that's not a problem in KDE.  Neither is it a problem in Gnome, Mate, Unity, XFCE, etc.

So, what's KDE got to offer beyond that?

If you're an experienced KDE user,  define the "KDE edge" for me.  What's the best -- specific -- way for me to leverage this obviously very capable piece of work? What habits should I change?

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2014-04-25
I spend so little time on the desktop that I find the default Kubuntu desktop fine for my purposes.  I've spruced it up a bit with different background wallpaper on the two virtual desktops, and I've modified my panel a bit.  Most of my time is spent in applications.  Right now Firefox is full screen.  The KDE desktop is irrelevant.

I have noticed over the years that little annoyances I had with the desktop environment were ultimately fixed by the developers.  "Activities" were originally pretty clunky but have undergone a couple of revisions and become much more useful.  Having separate desktop wallpapers was once not possible as well, now it is.  I also like that I can restrict the task manager to displaying only the applications on the current desktop.  I find that when I want to tweak something in KDE to suit my tastes, the option is nearly always available.  If it's not, it often shows up soon thereafter.

The one thing I would like is a mixer that works like pavucontrol but looks like kmix.  I've removed the latter from the system tray and added an icon to launch pavucontrol instead.  With both analog and HDMI audio outputs, I find it easier to switch between them with pavucontrol.

I still have problems managing my Android phone from Dolphin.  I'll lose the connection between it and the computer and often have to reconnect the USB cable to get the phone and computer to sync up once again.  I don't know whether this is another example of MTP problems in Ubuntu Linux, or whether it is unique to KDE's kio-slaves method of handling media.  I do think that kio-slaves works great with other media sources, particularly CDs.  If you insert a CD into the drive with KDE, it will open a Dolphin window with the music arranged in folders using various common codecs.  You have the option of ripping individual tracks or the entire CD into FLAC, ogg, and, if you have restricted-extras installed, mp3.  You don't need any separate ripping application whatsoever; you just drag-and-drop the objects you want into another location.
