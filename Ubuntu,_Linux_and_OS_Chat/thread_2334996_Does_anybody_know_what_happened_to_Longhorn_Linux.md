### Post by fromwin2lin on 2016-08-24
Nope. When Microsoft started the Longhorn project, it was based on XP. Longhorn was intended to be a small release, but as the technologies WinFS, Avalon and other became very promising, the project became a larger release. But when Longhorn hit the 409x range, Microsoft got lazy and decided to reset Longhorn. This was when Bill Gates turned off his brain. At this point, Microsoft was back to square one on making the latest and greatest Windows OS. They decided that the new Longhorn would be based on the more secure Windows Server 2003 codebase, even though the newly reset project was still codenamed Longhorn, it was not the Longhorn that millions were hoping for. Longhorn was probably be the OS of the century, but instead, we user received the not-so-great Vista. All of that hype, all for just an OS that MS easily gave up on. I still remember the 2003 PCD conference. Oh Microsoft! Why did you ditch Longhorn?!

### Post by jman64952 on 2016-11-12
Yo there, jman6945, original dev of the project. First of all thanks so much for your interest
Sadly the project was abandoned. There were multiple reasons for this, First of all developing an entire distro just to look like longhorn was a bad move, we did develop a transformation pack at a later date that worked for a considerable amount of time, however with the advent of GTK3 there were barriers that we simply couldn't break, especially relating to transparency in the early days. Not to mention some members of the team being unable to continue working on the project. Now things have massively improved in that domain however I don't really think that many people want something like Longhorn Linux anymore, as the hype for Longhorn and shiny stuff is gone now, most people are more interested in material and flat design. One of the other issues is that Emerald is no longer what it was and nore is compiz, hence effects like Blur are no longer as availiable as they used to be. I may look into redeveloping the pack in the future if there is interest, It warms my heart to see that even in 2016 people still remember our little project

Thankyou  for your message and all the best in the future

Jordan Maris (jman6495), original Lead Dev, Longhorn Linux.
