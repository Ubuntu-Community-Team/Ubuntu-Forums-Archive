### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-06-18
There is one simple way to change Canonical policy - buy the company! Otherwise you should work hard helping to develop Ubuntu and after many years you will get respect from the Ubuntu community and your opinions will be accepted as valuable. But that will not mean your opinions will be adopted as the path for Ubuntu to follow. There is a good reason why people on this forum used to say, "Ubuntu is not a democracy."

Canonical is not trying to make Ubuntu the most installed operating system on desktop machines. Microsoft already has that position and sales of desktop machines are falling. So, there is little commercial future for Canonical in that direction.

Nor, is Canonical trying to compete with Apple and Google on mobile devices. That is a battle that Canonical will not win because Canonical does not have the spending power of those two companies. Further more, Canonical is a software company. It is not going to become a hardware company, which both Apple and Google are.

You have failed to mention the direction that Canonical is taking Ubuntu in. And you fail to mention the good situation that Canonical/Ubuntu are in as regards the authorities in China. Ubuntu Kylin is part of that. Now that it is accepted as part of the Ubuntu family there will be greater cooperation between Canonical and the developers of Kylin which will bring many improvements to the distribution. It will also help Ubuntu become a recognised brand name among Chinese people.

[http://www.canonical.com/content/canonical-and-chinese-standards-body-announce-ubuntu-collaboration](http://www.canonical.com/content/canonical-and-chinese-standards-body-announce-ubuntu-collaboration)

In one sense all this is none of my business - I am not a Canonical shareholder. I am a Ubuntu desktop user. I would like a Ubuntu Touch device. I wait and see how things turn out. In the mean time I have an excellent distribution of Free and Open Source Software.
