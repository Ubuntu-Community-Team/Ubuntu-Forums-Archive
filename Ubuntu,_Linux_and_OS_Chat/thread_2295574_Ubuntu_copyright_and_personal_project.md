### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-09-20
I would be surprised if anyone here has the authority to give or deny permission to what you are doing. It would be best for you to consider the Canonical trademark policy.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/legal/terms-and-policies/intellectual-property-policy](http://www.ubuntu.com/legal/terms-and-policies/intellectual-property-policy)

See the section on Trademarks

> 
[LIST]
[*]You can use the Trademarks, in accordance with Canonical’s brand guidelines, **with Canonical’s permission in writing**. If you require a Trademark licence, please contact us (as set out below).
[/LIST]

> 
[LIST]
[*]You can write articles, create websites, blogs or talk about Ubuntu,** provided that it is clear** that you are in no way speaking for or on behalf of Canonical and **that you do not imply endorsement by Canonical**.
[/LIST]


My advice would be: If in doubt, don't. At the moment there is no intention of moving this project beyond an intellectual exercise. But who knows what the future may bring. It is best to start out as you mean to go on. You do not want to end up with a viable and useful project that has to be re-written because you have forgotten about "Powered by Ubuntu Canonical." You have not even included the standard trademark symbol.

You never know. In future you might want to seek some kind of official recognition and that will require meeting the brand guidelines. Which you seem aware of. So, you are starting off in the right way. I understand that this forum started out as someone's personal project. It had its own look that persisted even after the forum was granted official status. And then the other year it was redesigned to compy with the brand guidelines.

This is an idea that someone else has had. Notice how they are going about this issue.

[https://uappexplorer.com/](https://uappexplorer.com/)

And no one has forced OMGUbuntu off the web

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/)

Regards.

P.S. I have seen web sites that use other people's trademarks with a mouseover that produces information about the trademark owner. That seems to be acceptable.
