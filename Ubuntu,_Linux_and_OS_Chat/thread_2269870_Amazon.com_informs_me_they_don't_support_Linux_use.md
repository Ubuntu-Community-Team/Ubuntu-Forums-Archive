### Post by SeijiSensei on 2015-03-18
Compared to users of Windows and OSX, the Linux market is trivially small.  Sure you might cite "millions" of users, but in reality, desktop usage of Linux is perhaps a couple percent of all computer users.  Since most of Amazon's licenses have geographic limitations, we're talking about Linux users in North America who want to download songs and are willing to pay for them.  In practice, that's not a large number of people.

Amazon's Instant Video service also won't run on Ubuntu unless you install the long-deprecated HAL package.  There's a [long-running complaint thread]("http://www.amazon.com/forum/amazon%20video%20on%20demand/ref=cm_cd_rvt_np?_encoding=UTF8&cdForum=Fx3EQAX98ED5WQ3&cdPage=1&cdThread=Tx167YET6CH0PQI") about this fact that Amazon ignores.

### Post by speartip on 2015-03-20
Hi.
I purchased an 11 track Album from Amazon only yesterday.
You can download all the tracks at once using clamz which is in the repos.
Follow the usual options, except when it comes to the cloud player part, click "download your music now".
This will download a .amz file to your computer. Right click it and open with clamz. A terminal will open & download all your music at once, to your music folder.
Hope this info is useful.
