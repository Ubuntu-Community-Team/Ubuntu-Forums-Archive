### Post by #&amp;thj^% on 2019-09-25
Smartphones are inherently bad for privacy. You basically have a tracking device in your pocket, pinging off cell towers and locking onto GPS satellites. All the while, the handset's data connection ensures that tracking cookies, advertising IDs, and usage stats follow you around the internet.
So no, there's no such thing as a perfectly secure and truly private smartphone, let's get that out of the way now.(IMHO)
Trusted VPN's help, but still....

Always-On VPN: A virtual private network, or VPN, allows you to reroute internet traffic through an external server. A good VPN service will even let you encrypt all data traffic for increased anonymity. With Android devices, you can funnel all types of internet traffic through a VPN. With an iPhone, however, you can only use a VPN over Wi-Fi, unless you're willing to reset your device and enable "Supervised Mode" to get the VPN working on your mobile data connection.

### Post by TheFu on 2019-09-26
What I do for cell privacy is beyond what most sane people would do. I've lost cell phones in the house for weeks at a time and didn't notice. I have 3 phones and move the SIM between them as needed.
* Motorola flip-phone - $20 new. It has a Copter game. ;)
* Nexus 4 - company bought this and didn't want it back when I left. Travel phone these days.
* Huawei something - $99. 

I avoid google services, but the Huawei has google apps. The others do not.
I side-load apps, F/LOSS.
Airplane mode almost always.
No financial information touches a phone, never. NONE.
No data plan most of the time. When I travel, I'll add a 1GB data plan or pick up a new SIM in different countries.  I have no issue being tracked outside my home area.  When I'm in a different state or country, being tracked is probably helpful to me. It is the ability to capture our patterns in our normal environments, which I find the most scary.  Does anyone need to know where I go most Tuesday evenings?  Looking at a few weeks of GPS tracks, I bet they could figure out where I'm likely to be next Tuesday evening.  [https://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-08-16/metadata-retention-privacy-phone-will-ockenden/6694152](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-08-16/metadata-retention-privacy-phone-will-ockenden/6694152) is an interesting story when a reporter lets the public see his tracking data. What they learned could be frightening. That was Australia.  In the USA, I've never heard of any cell metadata captured by service providers being deleted - EVER. There is no law to mandate deletion.

Data is pre-loaded over USB/wifi when at home by connecting to my servers. Mostly plex, nextcloud, wallabag.  My phone isn't connected to email.  email can wait and I don't believe any Android/iOS device can be secured due to the hardware involved.

The device is mainly used for off-line GPS, listening to music/audiobooks and for emergency voice calls out. If I'm desperate to read something while in a waiting room, some wallabag articles are always sync'ed to read.

I will connect to wifi, then to my openvpn server if I want access to data I forgot to load. Have a paid VPN provider too, but seldom use it from a phone.

I have reminders to change my "tracking advertising" identifier every month.
Everything that can be toggled in favor of privacy is toggled.  I don't load many apps to try them out, just like I don't load many Linux GUI apps to try them out. The maker of the app matters to me.

A little inconvenience is worth it to me to have a tiny bit more privacy.  Between cell tracking, traffic cameras, public safety vehicle license plate scanners at highway entrances and on random vehicles, RF device scanners, my metro area places these on utility polls, I have little belief in actually having much privacy.  Forget about all the video cameras that every shop/cafe/restaurant and all my neighbors have hooked up so police have direct access.

If you leave BT, wifi, or cell services enabled, you are being tracked in and around almost every modern city.

I'm not so afraid of this tracking, that it prevents a trip to get some tacos.  But I probably won't remember to take a cell phone.  ;)
