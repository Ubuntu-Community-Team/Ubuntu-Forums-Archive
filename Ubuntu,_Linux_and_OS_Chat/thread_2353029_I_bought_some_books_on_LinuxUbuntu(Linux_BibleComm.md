### Post by Habitual on 2017-02-18
Buy as many as you can afford and want.
The info may become dated, but *technique* is priceless.
B&N has some decent periodicals in the Magazine format that cover ranging topics from more the desktop angle than "under the hood".

I miss my MS-DOS 5.0 Installation and User Guide. It was my "bible" when I cut teeth in IT back in the mid '90s.
If I ever see another one, I'd buy it.

When I installed Linux, I decided that "Linux is DOS on steroids" and I have never looked back.
It got easier. It made more sense, for me. It just clicked.

I have Computer Books I may never read!
My point is don't dismiss any publication based on Published Date or Version numbering nonsense.
Those criteria, in my opinion, are secondary.

Peace.
