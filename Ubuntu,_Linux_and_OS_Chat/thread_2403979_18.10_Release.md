### Post by TheFu on 2018-10-18
I would urge people to carefully consider what you are signing up for by moving from 18.04, an LTS release, to a non-LTS release, like 18.10.

18.10 gets 9 months of support, which means if you install 18.10 and plan to keep the system more than 6-9 months, then you ****Must**** migrate to 19.04, then 19.10, then 20.04 to get to the next LTS.  All the 19.xx releases will also have 6-9 months of support.  18.04 and 16.04 have 5 yrs of support from their respective release dates in contrast.

**New** is the enemy of **stable**. Seriously consider that.

Production servers should NOT be deployed on 18.10 or any of the 19.xx releases. 

Who is 18.10 for?
* people who care more about new than anything else.
* people with bleeding edge hardware that doesn't currently have support in 18.04.1.
* people writing books about 18.10
* developers who need to be on the latest versions of libraries for Ubuntu.
* people willing to have system and application issues and reporting those through the bug tracking system. Thank you.

Everyone else should be cautious about using any non-LTS release.

IMHO.


And why not let the official mirrors get the release, so the load can be shared?   Also, if you do grab it, be certain to seed a torrent to help others. Thanks!
