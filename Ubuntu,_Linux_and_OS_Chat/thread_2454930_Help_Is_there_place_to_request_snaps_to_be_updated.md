**Top 5 best baby monitors**


**Parenting has never been an easy task, especially at the first few periods of your baby&#8217;s life when they need the most care and attention. Fortunately, the rise of modern technology has taken away much of the burdens that fall on parents&#8217; shoulders. Among others, baby monitors have come in handy in assisting parents to watch over their babies, especially during night time, saving you that precious good-night sleep that we all in (dire) need of.**


Out of the thousands models on the market, [**what is the best baby monitor**](https://forums.servethehome.com/index.php?threads/how-to-choose-the-best-baby-monitor.24897/)? This article helps you tackle that question by giving a list of the 5 best models that have gained the trust of many parents.


**1. Infant Optics DXR-8**


This one has been the top pick of multiple **baby video monitor reviews** for years. The reasons are that it is fair in price, high in quality, and simple to use.


The Infant Optics is a non-wifi baby monitor, allowing you to see your baby even in the absence of wifi. It comes with a 3.5-inch, 320x240 screen that displays a clear vision of your baby, even in darkness. This monitor is able to spin smoothly and in small increments, allowing you that flexibility to take a good look at your little one.


The producer of the Infant Optics claims its range to be 700 feet, a fairly wide range compared to the average baby monitor. Within this range, you can be sure that the monitor maintains a stable connection, whether it&#8217;s between floors or through thick walls. This monitor also has an intuitive interface, making it easy for users to control. The Infant Optics comes with 2 lenses from the box: one for the standard angle, and the other for zooming. This feature makes it convenient for parents to zoom in on their child or keep an overall guard of the nursery, depending on their needs.


Battery is another important issue, because you wouldn't want to get a monitor that can&#8217;t even last for the entirety of your sleep. The Infant Optics lasts for 10 hours straight with any mode of viewing-- be it intermittent check or with the screen off. With these well-rounded features, it is not surprising that the Infant Optics has remained the **best baby monitors** for many users.

**2. Eufy Spaceview Baby Monitor**


The Eufy is another non-wifi monitor, saving you that battery on your phone. Indeed, it has been said that wifi baby monitors that can connect to your smartphone take a toll on the phone&#8217;s battery. The Eufy has a longer pan capacity than the Infant Optics: at 330 degrees, whereas the Infant Optics is only at 135 degrees. This allows for a larger view of the room, especially convenient if you have a large nursery or have two babies in the same room.


The Eufy Spaceview claimed only 460 feet in range, but many users confirmed that it is actually larger than that. The connection remained stable even when they left the house and walked onto the street, and that is without any wifi connection! The display of this monitor is 5-inch in size and a 720 LCD screen that gives you a perfect vision, day or night. The large screen resembles a smartphone, giving you the optimal space to observe your baby. The interface of the monitor is another plus point: it is easy to control with colored buttons, allowing for neat and simple navigation.


What&#8217;s more, the battery of this one is hard-core, lasting for up to 15 hours. It comes with a USB connector that is more simple to charge than the standard DC charger. Because of all the reasons above, the Eufy Spaceview is among the [**best rated baby monitors - top rated baby monitors**](https://thekinglive.com/the-ultimate-guide-to-getting-the-best-baby-monitor.html).


**3. Nest Cam Indoor Security Camera**


Rather than being just a baby monitor, the Nest Cam is a security camera. This has the benefit of allowing multiple phone connections at once. Every member of the family can then have a view to their little one, ensuring extra security and spreading the responsibility to more than just one person.


The Nest Cam is a wifi monitor, so you would need wifi connection in order to get it working. While wifi connection, as mentioned before, can burn your battery, it is absolutely necessary if you are a working parent and need to keep an eye on your child from far away. Wifi baby monitors are also more prone to security issues such as someone hacking into the monitor and gaining access to all the information about your family, but the Nest Cam has an extra layer of encryption, making it harder for outsiders to break in.


In addition, Nest Cam offers various features such as two-way audio, allowing you to talk to your baby and hearing their response. There is no better solution than hearing their mommy&#8217;s voice when the baby wakes up in the middle of the night, and no better reassurance than hearing your baby constant breathing, knowing that they are safe and sound in their sleep. Despite their pros and cons, **top rated baby monitors** are the ones that suit your needs.


**4. Motorola Halo and Over-The-Crib Baby Monitor**


The Motorola Halo is the perfect blend between tradition and modernity. It is both a wifi, phone-connecting baby monitor, and a classic parent unit. This monitor is the suitable option if you don&#8217;t have a stable wifi connection because once your wifi gets cut off, you can immediately switch to the parent unit. The smartphone connection comes in handy when you have to travel to work or be far from your baby.


The Motorola Halo bends over the crib to give you the best and closest view. It also comes with an assisting monitor that offers various added functions. Among which, the display of room temperature helps you determine if the room is too hot or too cold, allowing for timely adjustment. You can also see sleep analytics that give you insight on the quality of your baby&#8217;s sleep and their sleep pattern. With this useful information, you can understand your baby&#8217;s sleeping habit and develop an even better schedule that fits their habits.

**5. Arlo Baby**


The last item on our list is another wifi-enabled baby monitor. Arlo Baby, compared to its competitors, has a more convenient app with better customer support. Unlike many others, you can actually hear your baby on the app even with your phone turned off, instead of having the app open at the foreground all the time. With this feature, you can listen to your baby all night while sleeping, as long as the app is open in the background. This could be a burden to your battery, but you can always recharge it in the morning.

The Arlo has a narrower angle than the Eufy or the Infant Optics, but it is wide enough if you have a small nursery or if you only need a fixed view of your baby. The Arlo offers a convenient feature of sending a sound notification when the room temperature deviates from the accepted range, thereby allowing parents to adjust in time.
