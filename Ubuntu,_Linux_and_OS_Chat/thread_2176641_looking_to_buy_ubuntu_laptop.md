### Post by mastablasta on 2013-09-26
Ubuntu certification list mostly has old laptops that are no longer available.

Dell and HP sell Ubuntu. Dell has a nice XPS developers version ultrabook, but it's pricey.

HP also sells probooks here with suse linux preinstalled (ubuntu works out of the box on them). 

Lenovo sells plenty here with no OS that are Ubuntu compatibel (out of the box).

as mentioned why do you need Ubutnu preinstalled if you will replace the disk? When you replace the disk you will have to redo the whole OS install anyway.

system76 has good builds. they are not big but users here report that their products are of very good quality. 

Problem with big ones (dell, HP, lenovo) is that they will make some good models and plenty of crappy ones. it's hard to say which ones are good. sometimes even their "Improved" model is worse that original one and vice versa. business laptops tend to be a bit more sturdy/durable, but they are also usually 15" (unless you go with ultrabook which have windows preloaded).
