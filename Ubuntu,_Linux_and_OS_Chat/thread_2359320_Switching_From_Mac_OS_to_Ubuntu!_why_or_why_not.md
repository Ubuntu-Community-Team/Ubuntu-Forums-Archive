### Post by sp40140 on 2017-04-22
If I had your machine, I would keep that set-up as you currently have. As good as Ubuntu (many other distro) is, the hardware/drivers/software combination of apple can't be beat. And you must have paid premium for it as apple stuff isn't cheap.
Now, if you had a generic / standard PC, I would suggest you nuke it and install Ubuntu.
Also, if you use iphone/ipads, then Ubuntu will not work with it. And you will have to adapt to new software depending on what you currently use.
I should really draw your attention to drivers situation. I think your current set-up will give you better performance (as Ubuntu guest os) than native install on same hardware as you won't same quality of drivers for Ubuntu. This is because Apple closely controls their system. And it's one of the perks. That's the reason iPhones beat Android phones with better cpus in real life tests.

### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2017-04-27
Very easy to have your cake and eat it too. Install Ubuntu in an external drive or a spare usb drive and than boot from it. To create an installation in  an external media that is bootable in a Mac is a bit complicated and apparently you need some software that runs in Mac to do it. But it could be done and I had done it with some tutorial online. Just google or ask in the forum (unfortunately I don't remember how to do it, I don't have a Mac and was experimenting with my friends' since he needed me to reinstall his OSX ;)) If you install in a usb (not running it live, but a full install) you should use a usb3 flash drive or it will be very slow, but if just as a proof of concept and check hardware compatibility speed may not matter. You need two flash drives, one for the installer (live session) one as the target to install into and make you install in the correct drive and not your internal and also be careful about where you install the bootloader (in the external drive)

In my case I booted Ubuntu 14.04 off an older Macbook that ran El Captain, everything worked and smooth like butter except for the webcam which required special driver. There was a hack to get it to work but I didn't bother.
