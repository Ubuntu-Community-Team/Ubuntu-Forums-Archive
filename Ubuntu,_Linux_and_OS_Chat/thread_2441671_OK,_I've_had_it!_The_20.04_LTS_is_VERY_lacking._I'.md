### Post by DuckHook on 2020-04-26
[LIST]
[*]Every initial release since, oh, the first version of DOS has had bugs, regressions, holes and faults. That's the nature of .0 releases.
[*]This is why Bionic won't upgrade to Focal till the first point release, sometime in July.
[*]On the other hand, I've installed Focal on six machines so far, both bare metal and VMs/containers. They have all gone smoothly, are stable, fast, minimal problems and I like most of the new features.
[*]It makes no more sense for me to judge the entirety of Focal based on my personal anecdotes than it does for you to base such judgment on yours.
[/LIST]
If you have specific problems, please post them in the appropriate forum with specific details and context. You might just receive good help.

Or, as you've already concluded, you can wait for the first point release.

It is worth noting that if you want stability and no surprises, you shouldn't be on a standard release like Eoan either. Go from LTS to LTS only. So, install Bionic and don't *do-release-upgrade* to Focal until the system allows you. Thereafter, stick with Focal until the first point release of 22.04. This will save you much wailing and gnashing of teeth.
