### Post by buzzingrobot on 2013-11-19
Good question.

Many Linux "enthusiasts" seem to get a vicarious thrill out of painting Linux as the underdog in some kind of heroic struggle against Evil Microsoft and Evil Apple, with themselves as loyal footsoldiers.

There's much ignorance of Apple and OS X in Linuxland because few Linux users have actually bought and used a Mac. You see this when people falsely assert that OS X is simply BSD with a spiffy window manager. There are even claims OS X is built on Linux.

A good part of the hostility is likely due to ideologically-fueled animosity toward anyone trying to make money.

Like Canonical, Apple abides by the licensing requirements of FOSS, but it isn't in lockstep with the cultural demands many in FOSS try to impose, beyond adherence to licenses.  That offends those people.

And, Apple sells quality hardware at a healthy markup.
