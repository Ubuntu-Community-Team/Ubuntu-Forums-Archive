### Post by JoshKlint on 2013-07-30
Hi guys, I am the creator of the game engine Leadwerks.  Six weeks ago I had a lot of people asking for Linux support, so I decided to give it a chance with a Kickstarter campaign.  The project ends tomorrow, and it's almost 180% funded, so we're in really good shape.  Canonical even gave us a [shout out]("https://plus.google.com/u/0/+Ubuntu/posts/S6vy5nqfGC2") on Google+, and I really appreciate the acknowledgement.

I'm coming to you because I have a different kind of goal in mind.  I'm going to the [IGDA Summit]("http://www.igda.org/summit/") tomorrow, and I think it would just blow away everyone's concept of Linux gaming if I was able to tell them we had a thousand backers for a Linux-specific gaming project.  It wouldn't take much money, it just requires 336 individuals who can spare $1 to promote Linux gaming.

"1000 backers" is a quotable statistic I can use to convince game developers to target Linux.  This means more PC games will come to Linux, and can even lead to the development of Linux-exclusive titles.

If we can do it, I think it would be a really good reference we can use when we're talking about the strength of Linux gaming.  So if you can spend a couple of minutes on Kickstarter, I'd appreciate it.  The campaign has already exceeded my expectations:
[http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1937035674/leadwerks-build-linux-games-on-linux/posts/554569](http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1937035674/leadwerks-build-linux-games-on-linux/posts/554569)

Welcome to the forums, I see you joined in June.

I've been watching a lot of Leadwerks stuff and was very moved to hear that you guys want to start working on a Linux version of your engine (understandably with a kickstarter backing). Not too long ago, Unity3d has released its version with export functionality to Linux. The more companies we can get motivated to start developing games for Linux, the better. We have a lot of requests come from the community for game ports to Linux itself. I'm not sure if you've watched the Humble Indie Bundle sales where they let you choose how much you want to pay for the 4 or 5 games they offer, but in general, Linux users pay more than Windows or Mac users. Naturally, the reason is because we want more games for Linux. You're reaching out to a market that has wanted larger support for companies like yours, and we greatly appreciate it.

Thank you for your time and you and your companies efforts. Personally if I had $1 right now I would go donate.
