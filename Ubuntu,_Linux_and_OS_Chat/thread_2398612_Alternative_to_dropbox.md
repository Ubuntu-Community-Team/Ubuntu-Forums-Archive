### Post by DuckHook on 2018-08-14
[This]("https://www.cloudwards.net/cloud-storage-outside-the-us/") site may interest you. The two biggest names in the outside-the-US cloud-space (you are not alone in your wishes) are MEGA and Sync.com, but others also exist. BTW, Sync.com is Canadian, which may interest you as a Canadian.

You might also wish to consider running your personal cloud server offshore. You would need to research the offshore locations, but many will set up an ownCloud server for you. Wikipedia has a decent summary on [ownCloud]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OwnCloud").

[NextCloud]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nextcloud") is a further alternative. They sell a self contained NextCloud box that is preconfigured to act as your own cloud storage. I believe that some company in Switzerland or Germany will also host such boxes so long as they are preconfigured and only need to be plugged into the net (they do not maintain). I don't remember the company's name.

### Post by mail-2o on 2018-08-26
I'm using Nextcloud on an old raspberry Pi and old 100GB HDD as a server. It has worked very well for several months, holding 60gb of data. However, I am only using it internally as am a bit cautious about releasing it onto the WWW until I'm completely satisfied with it. It is so useful to be able to synchronise large amounts of data between several machines, plus the cost and power usage of a RPi is minimal.
