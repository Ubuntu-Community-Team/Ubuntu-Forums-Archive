### Post by cariboo on 2013-10-03
Your home directory isn't encrypted by default, you have to specifically choose it during the install process, or add it later. It looks to me that you didn't read any of the on screen notices on first boot after setting up ecryptfs. Dustin Kirkland, the author of ecryptfs, has several blog posts [here]("http://blog.dustinkirkland.com/"), on how to restore your data if and when you run into problems.
