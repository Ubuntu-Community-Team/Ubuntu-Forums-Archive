### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-11-08
I have no experience of Dell products, though they are often good for Linux distros, and even though they made some changes to the operating system, they used to, and may still, provide some machines pre-installed with Ubuntu.  There is nothing in the specs hardware that worries me too much.

It has Win 8 installed so you will need to be aware of the potential problems of UEFI etc etc, so have a good long look at [UEFI - Ubuntu Documentation]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UEFI") for all the potential difficulties you may have with that.  Don't be too afraid, however, as many users have managed to install on a UEFI system both in single OS and dual boot situations, so it can be done.
