### Post by fragglebliss on 2019-07-07
You will generally find that important updates and more updated packages find their way into Ubuntu before Debian. Debian is developed at a much slower rate than Ubuntu. Debian is relied on as a base only and provides the foundation for which Ubuntu build their own opersting system. Ubuntu veterans will remember a time when you could see obvious signs that Ubuntu was Debian-derived. Nowadays, it's not so obvious and Ubuntu really has matured into a system of its own. But that's not to say that its appreciation of its reliance on Debian should be forgotten or ignored.
