### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-01-07
Just as info . . . System76 and Zareason are value added PC integrators.   Their suppliers (90%) include Clevo, Compal & Quanta Computer Companies.    These are largely the same companies (hq'd in Taiwan - manufactured in China mainland), that also supply to Dell, Toshiba, HP, Acer, and many others.

So, what you're actually looking for are the system components, including what generation of cpu,ram, etc.   Also, do they use the top chipset manufacturers?  (like Samsung, Intel, etc.).

Right now, in the pre-configured Linux PC world, System 76 offers the latest chipsets (Skylake g6), and has formal, ticket-based free "lifetime" (OS) support.    The best 2 laptop I've ever owned were/are by IBM/Lenovo and System76.

Your mileage may vary (depends on how you care for and manage your device).

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2016-01-12
+1 for Kale's post.

Consumer PC's running Linux are just not profitable at large scale (hence no mass produced Dells, HP's, Lenovo's for the "_consumer_" market).    

Canonical, Redhat and Suse (the big 3 commercial Linux providers) are not in a position to modify Linux to run on thousands of different PC/Chipset configurations.   That engineering is done by others for other reasons.

That we even have any kernel engineering for i386, amd64, arm etc. is because a large proportion of business, education, govt, science, military, etc. are running mass quantities of servers and higher end devices (mainframes) that require the Linux arch. to run . . . . (no Windows or MacOS's here).    These _orgs/companies_ demand a minimum level of compatibility for PC's, printers and related devices.    

So, if the "enthusiast" Linux community wants Linux on new PC hardware - - they had best be prepared to do some serious hacking on many or most new PC's . . . OR, buy pre-installed from the smaller vendors that do the engineering spec'ing up front, and provide the tech help/assistance post sale (like Sys76, Zareason and similar).

PS:   thank goodness for "Intel".
