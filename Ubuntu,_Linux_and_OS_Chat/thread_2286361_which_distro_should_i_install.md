### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2015-07-11
Any Linux distro would fly with that kind of specs, so resources should not be your consideration at all.  I would definitely run Ubuntu with its full gory if I were you. You may want Xubuntu because you like it, but it should be an aesthetic consideration rather than resources.

The only thing to test is the graphic driver. Depending on how new your graphic card is, I think unless the card is very new or if you are a gamer who wants to squeeze a few extra drops of fps more from the card,--but gamers probably go for Nvidia anyway,-- the open driver is actually better than Catalyst for most usage, much smoother and no problem upgrading kernels. But unless you have 15.04 I would recommend getting up to date driver from ppas like xorg-edgers or oibaf A lot of work has gone into AMD cards upstream and Ubuntu's stock drivers before 15.04 are too old to reflect that.
