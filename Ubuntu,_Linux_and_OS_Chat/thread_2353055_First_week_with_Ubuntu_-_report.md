### Post by lysander6662 on 2017-02-18
I will use this thread to make a small list of the things that I liked [or not] in my first week of using the OS. Overall the experience was very positive and the OS is excellent. Especially since I have been wanting to move to Linux for years [particularly Ubuntu], I'm pleased to be making this thread. I'd be interested to see other's thoughts on these items.

**Things I liked:**

*Ease/speed of install/live USB* - Being able to try the OS on a live USB was a lot easier than on the cover disks/CDRs I'd used in the 2000s. Back then I spent a lot of time downloading large files, burning them to disk and then waiting for them to load up. Conversely, torrenting Ubuntu now takes about 20 mins and it can be written to USB in about one min. Loading it from USB similarly takes me a minute or less, and it's beautifully smooth to run. Writing it to the hard drive was also very straightforward. I had to do it on three occasions [for various reasons], and each install took about ten minutes [it may help that I'm using an SSD].

*Smoothness/speed of OS: *I'm using 5GB of RAM, so that may assist Ubuntu in being beautifully quick and smooth to use, and it's a joy to do so. I'm sure that loading programs like Opera, for instance, is a lot faster than on Windows 7. It takes about two seconds.

*Terminal/Synaptic installations: *This was my biggest deterrent from using Linux before. I don't know how long Synaptic has been around for but it makes things very easy indeed to install. On top of this, installing via the command line isn't quite as daunting as I'd believed before and now it's my preferred method. It's great to see the detail of things working inside the terminal rather than graphically. Also my understanding of the command line could not be done without the...

*Ubuntu community: *The people on these forums are extremely helpful and I very pleased and happy with the outstanding community support here. People don't only want to solve problems but they seem to care about making it work for you, and everyone else who may come across the topic. I should also say the website OMG Ubuntu has been very helpful, as has YouTube and those who chose to make instructional videos. The expansion of the community, I'm sure, has really helped an overall greater understanding of the OS.

*Out-of-the-box functionality: *In spite of the fact that I installed no drivers directly, certain hardware, e.g. my X Fi audio card, sounds fantastic. Music is impressively full and textured, which I didn't expect. I was a little worried about it, but it's great that something simple yet so important worked so well.

**Things that could be improved:**

*[Certain] Software: *There are certain programs that, though they run generally well, have negative aspects. Even though Steam has hundreds of Linux games, less than half my collection is Linux compatible. Fortunately most of the games I care about are there. This is not the fault of Canonical or the OS, but it's still a mild irritant. I find it ironic that Ubuntu is the only officially supported Linux distro and yet there are hundreds of games [probably] that don't work on it, and that only 12.04-14.04 are advisable distros. Secondly, though not quite as drastically, Opera and Spotify have minor glitches. It's great that they work, and work as well as they do, but the fact that I still haven't got Opera to work with Bandcamp [for reasons that were explained to me in an earlier thread] seems slightly ludicrous.

**Things I don't like:**

One thing here: *ATI drivers*. This was the second thing that stopped me moving over years ago. I don't game now as much as I used to but really, the fact that there are no ATI drivers for 16.04 [and the available ones aren't advisable to use] is quite a disappointment, especially since I paid a respectable sum for my gfx card back in the day. This isn't a major problem for me [not at the moment], but the fact that I may not be able to play Left 4 Dead 2 or the new Tomb Raider [if they make it compatible] without rolling back to 14.04 feels not just annoying, but slightly wrong. Additionally, with no integrated support on the horizon, a question mark hangs over how long one would have to hold onto such a distro version [e.g. 14.04] for. Until after the end of LTS? Alternatively, get an nvidia?

[B]Overall:

[/B]It's been a great experience so far. In spite of some, admittedly, minor shortcomings, I'm very pleased to have come over to the Linux side and Ubuntu is actually better than I imagined it would be. I first attempted to move to Linux back in the mid 2000s but was unsuccessful many times, so I'm very content to have been able to do so now.
