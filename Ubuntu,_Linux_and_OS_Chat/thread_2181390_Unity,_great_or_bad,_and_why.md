### Post by buzzingrobot on 2013-10-17
Unity's really distinctive feature is the use of search in the Scopes?Lenses and in the HUD.  You may like that, you may not.

The Launcher is just a reworked dock.  Nothing really revolutionary there.

Today's desktop environments are divided into two broad categories:  Panel-based and dock-based.  KDE, XFCE, Mate, and Windows 7 are examples of the former.  Unity, Gnome Shell and OS X are examples of the latter.   At heart, the difference between clicking an icon in a dock or an icon in a panel is pretty minimal, which gets us back to the reliance on search in Unity.

Try a live image and see what you think.  It's no more important than the difference between waffles and pancakes.

### Post by QIII on 2013-10-19
Of your cons...

1, 4 and the first item in 5 sound like environmental issues you have encountered that are probably things that can be fixed.
3 and the second part of 5 are matters of personal taste.

As an example of Item 1:  The Phenom II x4 on the machine where I am running Unity runs at about 5% at idle -- the largest usage coming from conky.  Compare that to the Phenom II x6 on the machine I am using right now running at about 6% with KDE.  Xorg and conky are swapping positions 1 and 2 in top.

2 is most certainly true and is well known.  Unity does not work well on older hardware.

It is important to resist the urge to assume that what you are experiencing is common to everyone else.
