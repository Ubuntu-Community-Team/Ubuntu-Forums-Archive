### Post by yoshii on 2017-09-16
Really a poll should be up for the duration of the lifespan of the distro version since a lot of people will jump onboard or offboard during that time.  
21 responses just isn't enough in my educated opinion.    

But anyways, back on topic...

Installed Xubuntu 64-bit Zesty recently.  
I had some severe installer problems; slowness, crashing, the installer installed to the wrong partition, erasing my data!!!!  
But I didn't give up... it turned out there was some kind of issue with whether or not the BIOS was in MBR or UEFI mode.  
Nevertheless, it was odd because I turned MBR mode off, then on again to exactly how it was before, yet Xubuntu LiveDVD exhibited about 5 or 6 different behaviors (not 2) during the various times I tried to run it even though I hardly changed anything at all.  

Well, during the final run, the installer didn't fail and wasn't slow and it worked.  
After that, it's been smooth sailing.  Downloading upgrades worked fine, the interface seems pretty good, no significant issues.  
I was able to remove most of what I don't use (whoopsie, apport, bluetooth*, mugshot, parole, etc).  

Then I installed a low-latency kernel with ease.  Removed some fonts, installed more nice stuff with synaptic after I installed that and gdebi.  
Installed wine-staging version of WINE and it's running really well.  Only bug I've run into is emblems can't be set at this point for some odd reason.  

Also, I was annoyed that when installing VLC, it installed some kind of email service program??!  I had the suggests/recommends options set I think.  But what does email have to do with VLC????  They aren't related.  Annoying.  Oh well, I removed it.  

Overall, I'm liking Xubuntu better than Lubuntu and Ubuntu Studio.  I did the DAW/audio optimizations to get it like Ubuntu Studio and didn't have to remove as much stuff that I don't use.  It's missing a lot of software synths, but I don't yet use those anyhow.  I didn't have to remove 500+ fonts.  After I did remove some fonts, I have about 60 which is much much more manageable.  

I'd give Xubuntu 9/10 stars these days.
