### Post by &amp;KyT$0P# on 2017-08-10
> **doru001 said:**
> I believe that using TOR is safer than not using it, 
Some people also believe that the Earth is flat, Michael Jackson is alive, and lemmings commit mass suicide.

This is an example where privacy vs security becomes a trade-off.  Using Tor is great for privacy.  But yes you are opening yourself to potential hacking, though - as far as I know - not exactly in the way ardouronerous is thinking.  The big risk I've heard about is malicious Tor nodes compromising everything you do on the Net.  Web-based attacks such as man-in-the-middle (MITM) are pretty rare on a typical home connection, but through Tor it's like Russian roulette -
[https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2014/11/for-a-year-one-rogue-tor-node-added-malware-to-windows-executables/]("https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2014/11/for-a-year-one-rogue-tor-node-added-malware-to-windows-executables/")
(also see the part about Tor [here]("https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2016/06/aiming-for-anonymity-ars-assesses-the-state-of-vpns-in-2016/"))

Not that you're inevitably screwed if you really need the privacy.  The link 1fallen posted contains good advice for mitigating the risk.  Also, firejail sandbox might help protect your local computer.

So it all boils down to, which matters more to you, privacy or security?
