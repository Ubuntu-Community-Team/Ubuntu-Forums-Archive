### Post by varunendra on 2014-03-06
Most of such Tech Support is set up and clearly instructed to AVOID as much trouble as possible. So don't expect much from them if they have denied the service once.

In my personal experience, a written communication (i.e., email with CCs to all concerned addresses) is much better than telephonic conversation. It is a proof of your report and their response. Just keep it concise and clear, with all relevant references included.

From the experience of others, I've seen that the Consumer Court (if exists in your country) is a perfect deterrence for them to act favourably, although you should not start the communication in 'threatening' mode. Keep it formal and as a rightful request, and mention going to consumer court (with all evidence of correspondence) only as a last resort (when initial attempts to claim the warranty fail).

If your country has Consumer Rights protection laws, it will be an immediate hands down win if you are using Xubuntu only in Live mode and haven't actually installed it. And even if you HAVE installed it, you should easily win the case no matter what their Warranty Terms say. This is something I am saying from what I have read about such cases on the forums and have seen happening with some friends. Rarely does a case go against the consumer in such courts and that happens in cases when the consumer has done something clearly and terribly wrong to void the warranty. Using a "Not-Recommended" program or OS is a ridiculous excuse to deny the warranty.
