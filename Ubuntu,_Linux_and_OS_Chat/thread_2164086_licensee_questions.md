No, they won't. There's a thousand and one Ku/Xu/Lu/Ubuntu respins, all about the same. No-one has been sued yet. To my knowledge, you're allowed to freely distribute respins, or even charge for them. 

Most of the software in Ubuntu is BSD or GNU (L)GPL-licensed, so I suggest you read those licenses. There's a page here for more information: [URL="http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu/licensing"]http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu/licensing 
[/URL]
EDIT: one thing I might be concerned about, as mentioned by grahammechanical below, is the distribution logos. I would look carefully for those, as the copyright on those is variable.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-07-31
As I understand it the issue is more to do with copyrighted names and logos. Consider the two distributions that you mentioned - Linux Mint and Xubuntu. One distribution is close enough to Ubuntu to be allowed to use the word 'Ubuntu' or a variation of it in its name. The other is far enough away from Ubuntu to not be allowed to use the word 'Ubuntu' in its name.

What are you calling this remix? Could someone think that they are downloading Xubuntu or Linux Mint? That would be an issue with the developers of those distributions.
