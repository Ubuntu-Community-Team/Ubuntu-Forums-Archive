### Post by Z80A on 2015-06-30
Some end-user perspective: While elementaryOS is an absolutely wonderfully good-looking OS, with a very responsive DE and great usability, it does also have some drawbacks.


To many, it is a good strategy from the team to rely on LTS releases, but development is slow or poorly aligned and after Ubuntu 14.04 was released, it took a year before elementaryOS 0.3 Freya was released. During such phases you are stuck with old applications in the repositories (e.g. Ubuntu 12.04 based until recently). And while we soon can start to look forward to Ubuntu 16.04, elementary lack firm (published)planning of releases - but to their credit, they don't let go of buggy software (my experience).


Another ting that I have not been to happy about, is the "Not Invented Here" approach to included applications. I would be perfectly happy with a plain Shotwell photo app and a mainstream music app (maybe even of my choice), instead of elementaryOS specific Photos and Music. Could resources be better spend on maintaining the DE and release planing?


Anyway, likely I will always have elementaryOS running on one of my machines, but my primary machine will be a real Ubuntu OS machine of some sort for now.

### Post by Stinger on 2015-06-30
I have used both elementary os Luna and Freya.
Although Freya has a lot of improvements **I still would recommend Luna**, I never got Freya working with my locale language , Audience-mediaplayer is in it's current state not good enough for replacing Totem, connecting to network shares with files ( marlin ) is still a work in progress.
When Freya was released there still was a lot of bugs remaining but those bugs where just pushed to the next milestone [freya-0.3.1  ]("https://launchpad.net/elementary/+milestone/freya-0.3.1") or even further back.

I like the philosophy behind the project about design, speed and multitasking  :) and will follow the project.

But why settle for less when you can have it all ?
For me the most important factor with elementary was the user interface. 
Plank, the app-launcher at the bottom, Slingshot the application menu at the upper left corner, and a panel with all the indicators at the top.
So when Linux Mint released 17.1 Rebecca I decided to see how far I could get with that as a base. ( [17.2 Rafaela, just came out today]("http://blog.linuxmint.com/?p=2863") )
Now I got the design of elementary with all the benefits from Mint Cinnamon and Ubuntu. I can have files on my desktop too thanks to Nemo and the memory use and speed is comparable with that from elementary os Freya.
Have a look ;)
