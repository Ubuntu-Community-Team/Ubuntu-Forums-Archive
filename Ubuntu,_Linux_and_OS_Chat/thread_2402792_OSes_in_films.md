### Post by SagaciousKJB on 2018-10-05
Most of the times you see a screen, like a television or a computer monitor, it's actually a green screen with a computer-generated image over-layed onto it.  They do that to avoid things like screen glare, and refresh rates causing scroll lines.  Even when there is a real genuine OS that they're showing, it's going to be computer-generated over a green-screen.

That's been standard practice for a long time, so actors are pretty use to looking at blank green screens and pretending stuff is there.  Meanwhile, having a graphics artist just render a fake windowed environment that looks futuristic and high-tech and choreograph certain movements works fine and is very cheap and straight-forward.  Basically, they just have to mimmick and actual OS versus actually writing one.

There are a lot of different widow managers and stuff with user generated themes that look quite high tech and futuristic.  That all takes quite a lot of time to setup, even though most of it is just window dressings on top of already-established window managers.  A few years ago things like compiz use to be all the rage, and people were making some pretty slick designs with that, but then also had to figure out how to make configurations for task managers like 'conky' that matched, so it wasn't as if there was any distribution specifically rolled to look futuristic.

So you can accomplish a lot of that kind of futuristic look for yourself if you look around enough for different themes, or look up how to make your own.  A lot of it depends on the window manager and desktop environment you're using, and I'm sure that there are dozens in development out there that can do a lot more stuff than some of the more standard ones.

You can pretty much google, "How do I make Linux look futuristic" and get started.  I've seen some pretty sleek looks that were cobbled together by just a few different themes for a user's window manager, and then the use of something like "conky" which is a very robust and customizable resources manager.
