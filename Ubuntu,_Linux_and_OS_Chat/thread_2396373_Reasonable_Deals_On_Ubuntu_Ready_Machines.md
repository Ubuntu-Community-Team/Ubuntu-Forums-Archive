### Post by TheFu on 2018-07-14
You can build your own PC. It isn't hard and there are lots of guides and videos these days.  pcpartpicker will show compatible components and has pre-build lists of components.  That way, you can set a budget and optimize where the money is spent, especially if you reuse the HDD, monitor, case, PSU, and a few other things.

I'll never buy a pre-built desktop again.  Every 5-10 yrs, I upgrade my desktop for $300-$450 and get a system that is usually 5-10x faster.

BTW, saying "Core i5" isn't really sufficient, since there are many different i5 processors out on the market over the last 8+ yrs. You could get a used i5 that is slower than a current Celeron, for example.  And the new processors have a price premium, so being flexible is how to get the best deal.  I use passmark to get apples-to-apples comparisons.  Benchmarks suck, but they are better than nothing.
Core i5-4400e has a passmark around 1,300.
Core i5-8600 has a passmark around 12,200 ($270)
AMD Ryzen 5 1600 has a passmark around 12,200 ($172)

So you can see that great performance is possible for $100 less if you are aware of the options.

And there is the pre-owned option.  Lots of gamers constantly upgrade their systems, so those show up on ebay.  Being a knowledgeable shopper is necessary, so you don't pay more than something is worth.  Details matter. Greatly.  

If you have some time, you can look around for the best deals available in your part of the world. Some deals show up every 2-3 months around here. I suspect that happens everywhere, but you have to be looking and be flexible.

If you post what your current system has (details matter), then we can compare that performance to what a new system would be predicted to provide.  Would also help to know the sorts of workloads you intend to run so you don't overspend for things that just don't matter.
