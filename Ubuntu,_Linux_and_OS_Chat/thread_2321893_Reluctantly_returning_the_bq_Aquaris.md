### Post by Terence_Eden on 2016-04-25
Spent the weekend trying to use the tablet. Sad to say I'll be returning it.

I've [reported a bunch of bugs]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/~edent") which basically make it unusable for day-to-day work.

This is really only usable as a web-browser.  And, to be honest, you might as well buy a cheaper Android tablet if that's what you want to do.

It's light and the battery lasts a good 6 hours of use.  The UI looks pretty good - until you launch apps like FF or LibreOffice, then it's back to Windows 95 style themes.

In brief:

[LIST]
[*]LibreOffice doesn't work without a keyboard & mouse. Worse - there are no language packs installed so you can't do something as basic as spell checking. 
[*]Browser struggles to keep more than a couple of tabs open at a time. 
[*]FireFox isn't touch compatible. If you alt-tab out of it and then back again, the keyboard doesn't work any more. 
[*]FireFox doesn't respect your keyboard's language settings. Meaning I have to remember to swap the @ and " keys. 
[*]The task switcher is bizarre - especially if used with a keyboard. I've no idea how it works.  It also leaves screenshots rotated - meaning you have to twist your head to see what they are. 
[*]No Python, Ruby, or PHP.  I wasn't expecting to do full stack development - but I *did* expect to run basic Ubuntu programs. 
[/LIST]

I could wait for an upgrade to see if they can fix all of this, but I didn't spend €300 to beta test a half-finished product.  I wanted Ubuntu in a tablet form - this isn't it.
