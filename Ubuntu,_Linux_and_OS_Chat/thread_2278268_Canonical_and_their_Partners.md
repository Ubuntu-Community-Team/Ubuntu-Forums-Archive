### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-05-15
The person who asked that question, did they give their reasons for thinking that there has been a failing out? If not then I would mark such a person as a trouble maker.

We believe in freedom do we not? Then the managers of commercial enterprises have the freedom to make business decisions based on what they think is best for their business. If that means that they do not release hardware running Ubuntu, then they are only exercising their responsibility to their shareholders to make profit. It does not mean that there is a falling out between them and Canonical. All these kinds of business dealings would be seen as confidential and not something to be a subject for gossip.
