This is not a "which is better for games" thread, or even as thorough as some of the Phoronix threads on the subject.  My question is a little bit different.

I installed Tomb Raider (2013) in all three of the main Ubuntu OS's, namely Ubuntu, Kubuntu, and Xubuntu.  I ran the basic game benchmark on my computer, which is a i7-3440 with a GTX 780ti.  These are running the Nvidia 415 drivers from the Nvidia PPA

On Ubuntu, my benchmark comes back at 90.5 fps.
On Kubuntu, my benchmark comes back at 89.8 fps.
On Xubuntu, my benchmark comes back at 91.3 fps.

All of which are incredibly acceptable for me.  I like 60fps for my games.  If I need more than that, I will have to look at new hardware.

But my question is, what causes these subtle differences?
