### Post by Sinixstar on 2013-10-12
> **lordbills said:**
> There are few models like waterfall , spiral etc ...Why agile process  was adopted and why it fits the software produced by Microsoft?

Microsoft doesn't just use one model. Different groups within microsoft use different models depending on their needs from what I understand. 

It makes sense, as for the last several years they've been trying to push out more frequent feature updates. I mean look at the number of years between SQL 7 and 2000. Then along came Sql 2005, then 2008, then '12, now '14? Same thing with visual studio. 

Hardware is developing at a blistering pace, the way people use computers and software changes at a blistering place. In order to keep up they can't have these 3-5 year top down programming methodologies and expect to stay in the game. They need something closer to continuous integration with regular releases. That means an Agile way of managing things.

Another big part of it - is it helps them understand their customers and their customers needs better. More and more organizations are going the agile route. If Microsoft is going to support those organizations, they can't do that without understanding what that means. The best way to have that understanding is through experience - so they do it themselves. Visual Studio is one of the best IDEs on the planet because it's developers writing tools for other developers. The people designing and creating Visual Studio know EXACTLY what developers need. It's the same concept.
