### Post by Copper Bezel on 2015-05-10
Unity's controls *can *be moved to the right of the titlebar, but they'll jump back to the left when the window is maximized and the titlebar merges with the menu panel. It's not particularly ergonomic. 

On the other hand, with mouse or trackpad and keyboard, left handed and right handed aren't directly applicable. Your dominant hand is doing the work no matter which side of the screen you're on. Left and right handed accessibility applies primarily to 1) touch interfaces and 2) keyboard-mouse combos, like in gaming, where the app might use the left half of the keyboard and the mouse - very awkward for a left-handed person.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-05-12

> ...one major thing that absolutely bugs me-- control buttons...located on the left.  I'm right-handed, and this is incredibly obnoxious.  And, no, I'm not going to switch to left-handed mouse use.:mad:


I don't get that. How would a left-handed mouse make any difference? I'm left-handed and don't find control buttons on the right side to be obnoxious. ;)


More seriously, Unity's Launcher is a dock. Several good docks are available that can be used in other desktop environments and window managers.  Docky and Cairo have been around for years, and Plank is currently popular (used in Elementary). Folks use docks in Cinnamon, too.

Panels in KDE and XFCE can be placed and tweaked to effectively become docks, as well. 

Gnome Shell uses a dock by default, and the Dash To Dock extension transforms it into a very useful tool.
