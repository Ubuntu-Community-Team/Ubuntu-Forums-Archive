> Sorry if I wasn't clear, the meaning of the title is both, about users from different forums being unhelpful and being rude to newbies and making their OSes hard to install.
Those would seem to be two very different subjects.

Re: the first part, I just avoid forums where that sort of behavior is rampant and/or tolerated.  Problem solved.

As for the second part - while I personally prefer Ubuntu, I do understand why some OS are "hard to install" - and why that can be a good thing.

Like oldos2er said, it's about setting your own choices and having more control over your system.  The more difficult installers give you more options.

Some distros, e.g. Slackware, let you pick which individual packages to install, leaving you on your own to sort out dependencies.  How is that helpful, you ask?  Well, if you think some of a program's functionality is bloat, you might be able to just not install the dependencies you don't need.  For example, I've successfully run KDE without Akonadi this way.

Some distros are designed to let you pick compile-time options for the kernel and individual programs.  Definitely not my idea of easy.  But if you and your hardware can handle it, you gain a whole world more control.

I don't think that "making it hard" is really the main point of these systems.  Having that level of control just plain does require complexity.
