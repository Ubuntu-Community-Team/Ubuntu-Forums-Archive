### Post by coldraven on 2015-11-29
I don't think that this idea is workable for a couple of reasons.
Identical machines will use different amounts of power depending on what they're being used for.
Do you expect us all to buy a power meter? 
Many people use Ubuntu because they don't have the money to waste on state of the art equipment. My laptop is over five years old but runs perfectly well with Ubuntu. 
Any power savings are offset by not putting this into landfill and buying a shiny new one. 
If I suddenly have the need to render HD video then is it better to have a power hungry fast machine that will do the job quickly or an economical machine that will take 10 times longer?
