### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-01-11
I use a HP Stream 11 running Xubuntu for pretty much exactly what you're doing. Xubuntu, Lubuntu are both officially supported here. wattOS looks interesting, but developing. Promising signs that they have a forum and are developing a wiki. There is also LXDE which I believe is lightweight. As you are new to Linux, probably best to stick with something that has a fairly strong support community to start with, whichever distro you end up with. 

My personal preference is to go for a [minimal install]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD"), the lightweight xfce4 desktop environment (as used by Xubuntu) then add only the apps I use. If you only need email and a web browser, you laptop would fly and probably use little power.

PS: There is Xubuntu-core available for 15.10. That would be lighter than a regular Xubuntu and upgradeable to 16.04 LTS, with five years support, in the latter part of next year.

Whichever distro you choose, I'd recommend TLP to improve battery life:

[http://linrunner.de/en/tlp/tlp.html](http://linrunner.de/en/tlp/tlp.html)

I'll also say that I've recently started using Manjaro as my distro, and it installs TLP by default, so that might be a good choice if you are looking for a distro.  Of the official releases, the Xfce spin is most likely less power hungry than the KDE one.
