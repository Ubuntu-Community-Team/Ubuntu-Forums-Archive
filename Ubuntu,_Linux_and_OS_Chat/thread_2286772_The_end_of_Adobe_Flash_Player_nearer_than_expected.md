### Post by night_sky2 on 2015-07-14
Looks like Adobe flash Player received yet another blow after 2 critical vulnaribilities were found recently and left the compagny scrambling to issue security patches.

At the same time, Facebook's Head of Security, Alex Stamos, calls for Adobe to set an end-of-life date to the plugin. The wardrums are beating.

Google and Mozilla both blocked the flash plugin in their respective browsers, pending an update to adress the vulnaribilities.

This is getting serious, folks. Adobe Flash Player has become a liability to the web and a threat to it's users.


[I]''The mob is turning against Flash. Mozilla has blocked every version of Adobe&#8217;s Flash plugin from running within its [Firefox]("http://www.theguardian.com/technology/firefox") browser, while Facebook&#8217;s head of security has called for Adobe to kill it off.

[/I]
[I]The moves come following a series of vulnerabilities in Flash being actively exploited, including [those exposed]("http://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/jul/08/warning-adobe-flash-vulnerability-hacking-team-leak") by [the Hacking Team compromise]("http://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/jul/13/hacking-team-ethiopia-attack-data").''
