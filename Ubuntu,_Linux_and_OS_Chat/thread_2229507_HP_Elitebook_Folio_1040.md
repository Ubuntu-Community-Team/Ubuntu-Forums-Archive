### Post by jagosta on 2014-06-13
Posting my experience with the HP Folio Elitebook 1040 so others may understand what is working and not working. 

I purchased the system in 2014. It is an i7, 8GB, 256GB SSD, and has a high res screen of 1920 x 1080.
I also have a folio docking station. 
I installed 64 bit 14.04 (trusty) with duel boot Windows 7 Pro. 

In general, I *love* this system. 

Behaviors are very similar to the existing report on the Folio 9470m found here:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2181673](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2181673) 

Everything seems to work with exception of:

- hardware wireless on/off button
- limited multitouch gesture. You can two finger scroll, but no zoom in/out
- no right mouse button click on pad (you can get to right mouse with two finger tap - which generally has been working well for me)
- Bluetooth always turned on at start-up (actaully, I have not fully tested this yet, but suspect it is the same as the 9470m - the link above has a workaround). 


I have done a lot of playing around trying to get the Synaptics pad to work with a single finger right mouse button, but have not been successful.  I also assume no kernel driver support yet for the zoom in/out.  
One additional note on the Synaptics, this pad is pressure sensitive, which I found to be a really nice feature when I played with it on Windows.  Again, I could not find a way to activiate that on Ubuntu/Linux and suspect no kernel driver support here either.
