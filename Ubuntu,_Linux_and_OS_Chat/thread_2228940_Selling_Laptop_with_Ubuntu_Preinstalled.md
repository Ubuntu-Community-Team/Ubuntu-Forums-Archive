### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-06-10
Canonical does not charge for Ubuntu and you cannot charge for Ubuntu. Canonical charges for various services and you can charge for various services. In my opinion, modification refers to modification of the open source code and not to removing default applications after installation or the installation of other applications that are not part of the default distribution.

Here are the official documents.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/intellectual-property-policy](http://www.ubuntu.com/intellectual-property-policy)

### Post by HermanAB on 2014-06-11
If you install one or two additional programs from the official repos, no-one at Canonical will care, but if you change the look and feel to something different (e.g. Mint) then you need to make your own distribution with your own name.

The argument is simple: If you do something that will alter the number and type of support questions going to Canonical, then they will care and then you should make your own distro.
