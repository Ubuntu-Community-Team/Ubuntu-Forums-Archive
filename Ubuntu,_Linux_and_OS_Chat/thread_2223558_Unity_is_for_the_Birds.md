### Post by buzzingrobot on 2014-05-11
The Gnome you used in 10.04 was not a Canonical product.  It was a product of the Gnome project, which ceased development of Gnome 2 at least three years ago. If you want to use the same interface, investigate the Mate project, which is a continuation of Gnome 2, with bug fixes and other changes to keep it working in 2014.  

If you expect Unity to work as 10.04 worked, you will be frustrated.  What you did in 10.04 you can do in Unity.  You just may need to do it differently.
