### Post by DuckHook on 2019-04-30
Welcome to the forums, aughey.

Might I risk a bit of presumption and impart some well-meaning advice?

Based on your other posts, it appears to me that you may be dragging in some old Windows habits with you as you start on your new Ubuntu adventures. This is not a good thing for the following reasons:

[LIST]
[*]The Ubuntu ecosystem does not depend on proprietary secrecy to monetize software. Therefore, developers gain nothing from hiding their source code and dispensing only binaries from their own websites. In fact, they will lose out by building walls around their software because they will fail to take advantage of the most effective method of dissemination—inclusion in the repos.
[*]This dynamic is so much opposed to the proprietary world that, in the Linux-sphere, it is a very bad idea to download software from outside the official repos. After all, if it's good code with nothing to hide, why ***isn't*** it in the repos? I would go so far as to say that anything from outside should be approached with suspicion by default.
[*]It's a good idea to explore native Linux equivalents for all of the Windows apps that you are now using. The initial transition may be challenging, but once you are past the hump, you will not look back. (Example: do you really need Team Viewer? Remmina is part of a default Ubuntu install.)
[*]Of course, it is neither necessary nor advisable to get fanatical about the above: if there is a Windows app that you simply cannot live without, then it may be useful to run it from within a Windows Virtual Machine.
[*]You may find the link in my sig a useful primer to understanding how Linux Is Not Windows.
[/LIST]
Good Luck and Happy Ubuntu-ing!
