### Post by CatKiller on 2021-01-04
To answer your question, I *try* to use the Ubuntu forums to solve problems, but I've been using Ubuntu for 15 years and I like to tinker so, if there's a problem I can't work out how to solve, the chances of there being someone else here who can are pretty slim. I still try, though, as much for the moral support as the technical support. 

> **linuxyogi said:**
> I refuse to believe that people have stopped using Ubuntu in favor of some other distro so I am curious. What's the reason then ?

You can't tell anything about Ubuntu usage from Ubuntu forum usage. People almost never sign up so that they can say, "I wasn't confused by anything; everything worked straight away; and everything is exactly to my liking." We have the Solved tag specifically so that people don't have to make new threads: Google can find the solution for them without them having to post anything. 

Usage of the forum is down compared to the past, but we have no way of knowing whether that's because it's all easier than it used to be or because of the trove of existing knowledge. There isn't any indication I've seen anywhere that Ubuntu usage has fallen significantly relative to other distros: all distros are just getting more users.
