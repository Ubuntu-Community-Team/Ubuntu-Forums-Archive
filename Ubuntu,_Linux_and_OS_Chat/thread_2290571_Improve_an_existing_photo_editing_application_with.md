### Post by wirywolf on 2015-08-13
A lot of individuals who want to use a Linux distro as their primary environment will not due to a lack certain tools. We have some great freeware options. Corel AfterShot Pro 2 might be a good Lightroom alternative. Want a complete replacement for Photoshop? Start installing a Windows VM. GIMP is an excellent tool itself. I think it could be a lot better with an increase in financial support. Volunteer developers can only contribute small portions of their time. They have their family to care for first. Krita took some steps to hasten its development. [SUP][1]("https://krita.org/support-us/donations/") [2]("https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/krita/krita-open-source-digital-painting-accelerate-deve") [3]("http://libregraphicsworld.org/blog/entry/krita-launches-second-kickstarter-campaign-to-fund-development")[/SUP] Perhaps crowdfunding along with [donations]("http://www.gimp.org/donating/") to could speed up GIMP development or support a new project altogether.


I am curious to know just how hard it would be start a new software development project with participants from the Linux community. There are those who have said, &#8220;You are wasting your of time.&#8221; I could not help but to think of the many people who changed our world while ignoring similar statements. Would we have Linux if Linus decided it was a waste of time? What about Steam OS? Plenty of examples could be used.

What are your thoughts?
