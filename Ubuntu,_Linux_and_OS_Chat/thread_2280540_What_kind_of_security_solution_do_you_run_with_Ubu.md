### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-06-01
Here it's ethernet to the cable box.  There's a firewall, but ports are closed in Ubuntu by default.  I've never used any antivirus or antimalware on Linux.

I don't click on random links in random mail.  I have very good spam filtering from the mail provider I pay for ($40 per year). I have Firefox configured to prompt me before opening PDF's, podcasts, videos, etc. I usually visit the same, known to be trustworthy, sites.

I back up locally every 4 hours and anything I need to survive a house fire is off site, and must-retain-no-matter-what document files are off-site in a deposit box as hardcopy and on a DVD.

I do all online financial transactions on another device, and only on that device, and only with one app that uses two-factor authentication on that device.

### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-06-01
I regularly run netstat -tulpn to see what services are listening on which ports, and uninstall services I don't use.
I randomly use wireshark/etherape/tcpdump to see what's communicating on the LAN, and who they are communicating to.
I regularly erase/reset my browser profile, and stay off dodgy websites. Very few cookies accepted.
I don't do financial transactions on the same OS as e-mail or social networking. Separate bootable-USB-OS stick for the money stuff.  
I do regular backups.

And for physical security, my dog sleeps next to the machine.
