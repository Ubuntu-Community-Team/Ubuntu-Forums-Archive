### Post by AJ_Wasek on 2015-10-18
So I was having trouble instaling Ubuntu from a USB stick.  What I did is I found a pretty nifty alternative that actually works.  With my Android based phone (Galaxy S6), I downloaded a free appliction called DriveDroid.  It pops up a wizard that lets you setup your phone to become recognized by your computer as a USB drive.  It even has an option where you can download multiple ISO (ubuntu rocks! why would you want anything else? :)).  Load the said image, click on it to change seting to USB mass store read/write access, reboot, and voila you are able to install ubuntu, or any other OS just by using your phone and a USB cable.  At first this didn't work for me because I had my USB cable plugged into USB 3.0, but switching ports it worked like a charm. It's pretty convenient because I always have my phone on me, therefore I always have the iso images on me whenever I need them!


Edit:  Phone needs to be rooted!
