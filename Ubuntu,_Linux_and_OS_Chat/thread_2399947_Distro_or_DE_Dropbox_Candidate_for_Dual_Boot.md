Distro or DE Dropbox Candidate for Dual Boot??

I’m a senior citizen, so I like to reminisce!

I began Ubuntu with Dapper Drake after a segue from Red Hat which I purchased retail box with phone support (expensive!). I paid a lot of money for the one phone call I needed for an install hiccup. 
In that seque I switched from the linuxquestions.org forum to the Ubuntu forum.

DE: I cut my teeth on Gnome 2. I remember reading the entire tutorial that came with the DE. Wow on the granularity! Up to four panels with ability to change width, transparency, attach applets, etc. 

Meanwhile, my digital life revolves around Dropbox. From my first free account I garnered around 20 GB’s from invites. Now I have two 1 TB accounts (one is a backup).  My digital set is around 450 GB and consists of about 500 folders. With Dropbox’s powerful “selective sync” feature, regardless of where I am in the world, or which device or platform I am using, I can always download the exact subset of data I need. 

In fact, one of these subsets, includes a complete Linux re-install—screenshots, CLI commands, etc. should I experience a lost device or irrecoverable crash (almost never) or just want to do a clean install of the next iteration. 

Here comes “the problem.” I love MATE and this would be an excellent candiate for my second OS in a dual boot config. Unfortunately, MATE is a dealbreaker for Dropbox. In fact, nearly all Linux distros and DE’s that do not have a fixed property-less taskbar are dealbreakers for Dropbox. The problem is the failure of the Dropbox icon. There are a million workarounds with negligible results. If my Dropbox does not work “out of the box” I am forced to kick that distro/DE to the curb. In fact, if you want to know, in the clearest way, which Linux distros Dropbox supports, just go to dropbox.com. There you will find that the answer is either Ubuntu (Gnome 3) and Fedora (ditto). 

Fedora is very clean, like Ubuntu “BB” but almost identical, so why dual boot it?

For those of you who use Dropbox, can you tell me which distros and DE’s you have success with--”success” = “works out of the box” without workarounds???

### Post by Geoffrey_Arndt on 2018-08-31
I think you are referring to the "Raven" panel manager.    

There are two "settings" apps in Solus.   One is similar to Gnome Settings (maybe the same even), and the other is the "Budgie Settings" app.

This is where you control the specific Budgie components like the panel, budgie icons, themes etc.   The true "rolling release" capability of Solus is very good (fast & stable).    Some downsides are incompatibility with the prevalent package systems in Linux (no Deb or RPM support).   That can be a challenge if you have a non-standard hardware peripheral such as a Brother printer.   Ubuntu Budgie edition fixes that, but is not as streamlined as Solus.
