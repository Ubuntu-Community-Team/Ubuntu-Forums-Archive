I wouldn't be opposed to that, and Umail is definitely the obvious choice to go, haha.

I know it's probably not a big deal to a lot of people, but I personally am one of the individuals who would appreciate the alternative email. I know that Ubuntu contributors get the nifty @Ubuntu.com email address, but having a solution for every-day users would be excellent, and the foundation is essentially there already.

Just have an option that any Ubuntu One account created gets a limited amount of storage, and an @umail.com email address along with their Ubuntu One services. Hell, I'd even sacrifice some of my free Ubuntu One storage space to make room for the email account. And if you wanted to eliminate Windows/Mac users who just use the cloud services, then only offer the email for accounts registered on the Ubuntu OS platform itself. Something along the lines of being prompted to register for Ubuntu One and information during the first boot operating system.

I suppose the details don't matter too much, but having the additional service would definitely be appreciated by a lot of people. Plus, it adds an extra form of user-to-system attachment and community building, similar to Apple and the @me/@mac/@icloud addresses.
