### Post by TheFu on 2022-01-13
I'm thinking about getting a $20 flip phone with 20 day battery.  I have a $550 smart phone from 2014 and a $99 smartphone from 2017.  I was just as happen (not very) with the $99 phone.  But most people think my use case for phones isn't normal.  For example, I haven't had a cell data or calling plan for almost 2 yrs now. We have a home phone ($5/month VoIP) and have been homebodies the last 2 yrs except 1 trip out of state. A cell phone wasn't needed and isn't needed.

Sadly, raspberry pi v4 computers aren't available near $50 anymore.  They are out of stock or $120 in a "kit" around here. There are "better" used computers for similar prices which are much faster ... but that depends on our definition of "better".  A pi v4 is impressive for many uses, but you won't see me trying to transcode 4K video using any cheap ARM computer.

>     Like a new desktop computer it's price 300 dollars and above While a price of a smartphone is 100 dollars and above 

There are new computers for $99.  Walmart has them from time to time. There are usually $130 new computers available all the time, but if you want something faster and more capable, perhaps getting a used $300 computer would be smarter?  Or if you can reuse some parts from your current desktop and are a little bit of a deal hunter, then you can upgrade to a 20K passmark system for about $300 now. That's a new MB + Ryzen CPU. If you need a GPU, you can use a Ryzen APU for about $80 more than the CPU alone.  It will work for average games and normal desktop productivity apps, but don't expect autocad or high frame rate + high resolution gaming like a nvidia 3080 provides.
