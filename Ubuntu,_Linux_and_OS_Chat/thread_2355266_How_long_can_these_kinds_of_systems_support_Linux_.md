### Post by Bucky Ball on 2017-03-10
Similar to your question about 'how stable is LTS'. That would depend on a lot of things about your hardware, but how long your hardware will last is impossible to answer. Presuming you're talking about your hardware, it will last until it dies. The OS is irrelevant. As for Lubuntu supporting old hardware, that is one of the reasons it exists. 

Will Lubuntu stop supporting machines with 6Gb or RAM or less any time soon? Not for years one wouldn't think. Intel i* processors? Ditto.

The system you have has much better specs than many computers currently running Lubuntu. It would really pay to have a dig around prior to posting and some of your questions may be answered saving you some time. You might start with the Lubuntu webpage and do some digging there if you want to know more about Lubuntu and hardware support. You could even try contacting the Lubuntu folk directly and asking them.

As for hardware, an SSD can last for a year, it can last for five or more. Who knows?

Longevity of hardware depends on lots of factors. Workload (basically running temp of the cpu and entire system). Quality of hardware. Maintenance (cleaning the dust and such). Software doesn't play any roles (unless in extreme cases where some badly designed or buggy software causes the hardware to go bang). Just last week I recycled perfectly working thinkpad with P3. and I have had HP Pav loptop mobo go out in year and half.
But all things being equal, nobody can tell you how long the hardware will last.

Or it might be 'how long will a Linux OS support the hardware,' which may be filtered down to 'how long will a Linux OS *release* support the hardware.' 

The release you're using at the moment will support the hardware you are using it on forever, although the former will become EOL and unsupported while the latter will, eventually, become obsolete. Doesn't mean they both won't still work *together* twenty years from now.
