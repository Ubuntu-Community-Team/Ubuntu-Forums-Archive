### Post by kw_martin on 2015-10-29
I have spent numerous days over almost two years to get it working, unsuccessfully. In the meantime, have been using at TP-LINK TL-WN722N USB adapter. Just upgraded Ubuntu from 15.04 to 15.10 with kernel 4.2.0-16-generic, and lo and behold, the wireless worked at first boot after upgrade. Finally! Still good going whomever I have to thank. With the wireless now working, this laptop (and probably newer versions of it) now becomes a good Linux machine; I've been very happy with everything else (I use a mouse with the touch pad turned off as I otherwise touch it all the time during operation). Excellent keyboard. I have 500Gbyte for windows and 500Gbytes for Linux (dual boot) and having the DVD with still a very slim height is nice (for example watching videos on the plane - the 14" laptop is small enough to fit on top of the fold-down table). (Also for info i7 46000U 2.1GHz x 4 and 12 Gbytes of RAM make it snappy). Overall, highly recommended.
