### Post by linux-tr on 2015-09-11
Hi,

After reading a lot about Windows 10 privacy issues I decided to switch my small business IT environment to Linux. I tried Gnome and KDE desktops and a few other distos for some time. 
Contact Database is most important tool for my business. Nearly I try to collect all of personal/business information of my vendors, service providers and customers into Contacts App.

Here just I try to tell my opinions about Gnome Contacts which has made me a happy KDE Address Book user. Otherwise Gnome has a great set of Tools for Small Businesses (Map, Calendar etc.)
[B]
Gnome Contacts' Limitations

Evolution Dependency:[/B] It's backed/tightly integrated with Evolution PIM. This prevents it to be used as a system wide Contacts Database even for some distros like Elementary OS. Elementary OS still doesn't have a native Contact Database because it uses Geary as Mail App.

**Outlook Compatibility:** It's built on the idea of Outlook Replacement thus it offers some limited fields for business use. (Ok, it's better than Outlook Contacts but..) 
- Three Addresses per Contact. Imagine I have a customer who has one general phone number (800.. etc) with multiple shops..
- No customs labels (aliases) for Address Information. Warehouse, Meeting Point etc.. 
- Only one Skype Account per Contact. I need to log people's business/personal or more skype (or other messaging) accounts
- Limited Phone Number Fields (Labels)
- No custom fields

**No ability to enter Contacts' Online Accounts as free form database: **Doesn't let enter/see Contacts' some online accounts as free text database fields. Just You need log in to such services as Facebook, Jabber etc. and connect/bind them.

If I could play more I would tell more about it. From my (business) point of view, simply Gnome's biggest failure is "Gnome Contacts".
In this "social age" a Contact Database App is the center of basic usage of computing together with a good Calendar / Task Manager.

**My recommendation for Gnome Team: **Just implement / port KDE Address Book into Gnome Desktop and make it available as a centralized Contact Database.

Thanks for reading..
