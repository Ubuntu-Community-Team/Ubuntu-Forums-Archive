### Post by QIII on 2018-08-31
Like so many things Linux, what is easiest depends on what any given user finds easiest.

Of the distros which come by default with desktop environments, the easiest one would the that which suits any person's workflow and personal preference.  There are a surprisingly large number of DEs, but there are four or five that are most common.  

The build-from-scratch or nearly-scratch are best suited to those who know what they are doing, but because they are highly configurable they can be exceptionally easy to use for the person who sets them up.

Some server versions may be better for particular applications than others, but the others may, in turn, be better for something else.

When one asks a question like "what is the easiest", they invite the same sort of cacophony of opinions as one who asks "which flavor of ice cream is the best."  The answers may cluster around a few, but one is bound to get answers from the mundane to the exotic.

It's chocolate, by the way.  Anyone who thinks otherwise is simply not fit to be allowed in public without adult supervision.
