### Post by papibe on 2014-03-08
Hi adnaan404. Welcome to the forum ):P

If you go to the MATE website, and go the [screenshots page]("http://mate-desktop.org/gallery/1.8/"), there's one them that is pretty close to Ubuntu classic (this [one]("http://mate-desktop.org/gallery/1.8/ubuntu.png")). It looks like a very good place to start. 

You could easily install MATE on Ubuntu by adding a repository to your sources (info [here]("http://askubuntu.com/questions/87040/how-to-install-mate"), and [here]("http://wiki.mate-desktop.org/download")).

With all that in mind, and IMHO, I don't see the much value on a new re-spin. However, it may be add value to some people to make it easier, almost like a transparent experience. I'm thinking something like a ppa that that do all the work for them (add repositories, set correct theme, and further tweaks).

You also have to consider that MATE will be available for 14.04 (read [here]("http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2014/01/mate-desktop-ubuntu-1404")).

If you are really driven to do this, it looks to me that joining forces with the 14.04 MATE maintainer, or that team, would be the best place to bring that experience to Ubuntu.

Just my thoughts.
