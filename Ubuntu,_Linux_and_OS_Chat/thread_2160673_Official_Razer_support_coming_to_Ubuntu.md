### Post by Gnawnsense on 2013-07-07
Well, I know it wouldn't stop at supporting just Ubuntu. But, since it's the distribution that gets the most mainstream attention, you'll have to forgive my title.

I've been a long time Razer user for all my peripherals and most accessories for my PC and XBOX. When Steam made the jump to Ubuntu, I was excited, because my AMD woes were solved and I could finally make the jump. 

But what I found was kind of frustrating. With the recent focus to improve gaming on Ubuntu, I was disappointed to find that my software needs were covered with content and display drivers making their way over, but my Razer peripherals lacked every feature they ship with due to lack of drivers, other than basic use. No sensitivity control, additional buttons and macros not working. LED light issues, specialized keyboard keys not working properly.

I sent Razer an email explaining the situation and that I was surprised they have not announced any plans to begin native Linux support with all of the display hardware manufacturers beginning to support it and more and more proprietary software making it's way over. Below is the response I got. And while it might not be much, many, many gamers I know will be extremely happy to know that official support **is **coming.

Didn't think the Linux adoption would happen so fast. I'm super glad it is, though.
