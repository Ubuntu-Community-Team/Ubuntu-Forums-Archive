### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2018-06-04
Why? Because it is a challenge. I have set up arch just for the hack of it, but if I want an arch based distro for actual use rather than spending my time installing 10000 things to get a functioning desktop and reading through Arch wiki and hunting down updated info to solve a problem (a lot of documentations are outdated) I would use Manjaro, easy to setup and ready for use in 10 minutes. It is always easier to delete stuffs that you don't need than to figure out what are missing in a "minimalist" install.

Resource is never a problem, I don't run 10+ years old antiques. Most modern machines come with minimum 4 G of rams and it is more than enough even for the most extravagant Linux DE.
