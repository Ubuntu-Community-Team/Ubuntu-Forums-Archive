### Post by robin7 on 2014-02-27
**"User friendly"** is such a subjective term!  Too often confused with **familiarity**.  In my distro-hopping days I found some distros more "friendly," but in fact they were simply more *familiar*.  Since I started on Ubuntu, naturally Debian and Ubuntu-based distros seemed "friendlier" to me than most, unless they were made to be Debian/Ubuntu-like.  PCLinuxOS, for example, uses Synaptic just like Ubuntu and Debian!  It's unique combination of apt-get with RPM packaging makes it exceptionally "friendly" for folks coming from the Ubuntu family.

It took me over a year to pick a favorite dektop environment (Xfce), so now *any* Xfce distro seems "friendly" to me no matter what distro  it's on, because it's *familiar*.  But in my opinion Xubuntu has made the Xfce desktop even "friendlier" with the really smart way they have it configured.  It's somewhat "familiar" to users of most *other* desktop environments, yet still as fast, configurable, and elegant as ever.  I've quit my distro-hopping though, because for me it's better to be very well acquainted with one distro than to keep track of how to do this-or-that on multiple platforms.
