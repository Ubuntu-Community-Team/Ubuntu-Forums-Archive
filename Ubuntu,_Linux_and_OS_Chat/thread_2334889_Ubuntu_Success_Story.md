### Post by Tadaen_Sylvermane on 2016-08-23
I'm not new to linux by any means, been running it exclusively in my home for 4-5 years now. Still learning. I recently upgraded my server to 16.04. This server is doing dhcp, dns caching, apt-cache proxy, backups for all other laptops / desktops in house, direct boot to kodi for media center, mythtv backend, ltsp server for my 2 other satellite media centers I am planning on in the near future, print server, and torrent server.

I love to tinker, always looking to improve my setup. I've distro hopped a great deal over the years always looking for the perfect setup. For my server I have tried in Virtualbox no less than  4 distros just in the last couple weeks, all with various strengths. Going for stability and duration of support > all else. I always give up after a short while on what I try. Ubuntu has been flawless. Once I ironed out a few little niggles that were bugging me it is literally as perfect as it gets. No other distro comes close to the ease, package availability, and "just works" that I've had with Ubuntu on my server. I upgraded my personal laptop to Server 16.04 the other day with i3 (slim trim install) and it is going wonderful. Upgrading wifes laptop in the coming few days most likely to vanilla Ubuntu 16.04 desktop. 16.04 also seems to have eliminated the Unity bugs that plagued me in 14.04 as well. It's as good as it gets so far.

I think it's fair to say I have tried every major distro under the sun. Arch, Debian, Centos, Suse, Fedora, and derivatives of the above. Always come back to Ubuntu. Gives me little to no hassle while at the same time giving me rock solid stability, a good support cycle, and is very well documented... albeit scattered around on the internet a bit. Love it, staying with it for the foreseeable future.
