### Post by PondPuppy on 2014-11-21
Short answer: probably not. Long answer: read [http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/](http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/installing-ubuntu-for-devices/) and look at the device list on [http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/devices/](http://developer.ubuntu.com/start/ubuntu-for-devices/devices/) and see what you think.

The problem is that chipsets and processors are not necessarily standardized across all tablets. Images of Ubuntu Touch have not been built for all tablet hardware... not by a long shot. (Ubuntu-plain-vanilla runs on PC-laptop architecture.)

You might look into optimizing your tablet instead. Enable developer mode, see what's running and what resources are being used. If you want to put another OS on it, look into CyanogenMod or something similar, I guess.
