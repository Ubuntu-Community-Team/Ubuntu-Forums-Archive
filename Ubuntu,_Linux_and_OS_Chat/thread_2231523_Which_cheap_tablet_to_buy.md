### Post by Bucky Ball on 2014-06-26
A dual-boot is not out of the question as both operating systems don't run at the same time. You have one or the other, not both. Perhaps you have a different idea of a dual-boot, but it is one OS on one partition, one on another. They are totally independent. When one is booted, it uses 1Gb of RAM. The other uses none. It is not active.

Ok. It is November. 
I ended up buying a handfull of Used Asus HD7's. The 1st generation had mostly bad reviews, and the 3rd generation lacked multiple wifi frequencies. My objective was inexpensive, mostly for reading WITHOUT being locked into Amazon or Google. Not be stuck with only WiFi transfer and backups. to have an external memory card access.
So
-  1MB or ram has been more than enough.
 - Although 16GB hard drive(flash memory) may seem small. I bought (2) 32GB micro SD cards, and I can buy more if I need them. It is also much easier to decide what I want to load onto the tablet or transfer to the desktop computer
- Speed, processor stuff. For reading??? in fact, I find myself playing with Spreaker and some web TV app. I installed a utility program to kill Google Paly Store, Gmail,   Email, and other services that are unnecessary  background apps. (they will startup if I activate them by simply launching the app).
-YouTube and my videos work fine. I transferred most of the videos i had on an iTouch to the tablet.
- I love the fact that I have multiple versions of Readers (EReaders, PDF, Dejvu,etc) and can keep open a dozen different titles to switch between. (really helps when researching, and cross referencing stuff)
- I have given up on installing Ubuntu. It seems Ubuntu has given up developing a non-beta tablet version.
Since I have multiple tablets, I can experiment with the others, try out different jail-broken, er, rooted OS versions.

I have learned that the most common complaints are 1.) breaking the screen and 2.) doesn't work at all or fails after a short while.

My solution has been.
 1.) just don't sit on it, and provide each with some type of re-inforcing case (again, with multiple tablets,
-I can experiment with various inexpensive Chinese versions on Ebay. 
 2.) Not working..I learned  through opening up the units, some idiot..(I am an idiot too many times, too) in assembly decided not to fully connect the battery ribbon cable. Each defective unit worked fine as soon as I firmly re-inserted the battery ribbon.

One seller, since I had bought a set of 3 (for under $100) threw in two more with broken screens and both would not startup. I have found replacement touch screens on Amazon for only $30 ! However since I have extras to play around with, I simply laid a screen protector over the cracked glass and use it without any problem.

All, in all, if you can open up the case.  and there are plenty of YouTube videos out there to show you how. I feel I have gotten a really good deal that I am very happy with, that is very useful.
