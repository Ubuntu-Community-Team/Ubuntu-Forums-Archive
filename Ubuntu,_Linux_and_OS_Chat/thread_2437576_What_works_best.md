### Post by DuckHook on 2020-03-02
+1 for both Bodhi and Bunsenlabs: they perform a valuable service for those of us still using 32-bit HW.

@OP

Though the recommendations for ultra-skinny OSes are all spot on, it must be noted that the big resource hogs these days are not the OS, but the apps that most users take for granted. The mainline web browsers are all pigs and no matter how much you save with a skinny OS, it will all come to naught if you insist on running Chrome, FF or even Opera. A skinny OS is pointless without correspondingly skinny apps.

So, on the browser front, consider Epiphany, Dillo, Midori (which is being maintained again) or even the text-based browsers like Lynx/Links/Links2.

Replace T-Bird with Sylpheed, LibreOffice with lightweight office alternatives and GIMP with Shotwell, etc. You get the idea.

Given that you have 8G of RAM, memory is not a concern. But your CPU is on the slow side, so lightweight apps will help.
