### Post by Mud Pie on 2014-09-07
Went to Dell's web site just to see what's there for this little machine.  Found a BIOS update, A15, mine is at A05.  I was curious.  I'm from the old school of computing (started in 1983 or so..time flies !!) and I'm fully aware that a faulty BIOS update can create a mighty purty expensive brick if it fails.  I figured "eh", it's a free PC, let's play.

Dell expects you to be in Windows, so I had to create a DOS bootable USB drive to install the update.  Having to jump in my Way Back Machine and remember HOW to create one, I did.  Loaded the BIOS update on it and restarted my machine with the USB drive inserted.  It booted to DOS, executed the file and is says it takes up to 10 minutes.  I sit back and sip my tea.

Outside I hear thunder rumbling.  I look out my window and it's raining.  Hard.  Sometimes during a hard rain/thnder storm, I briefly lose power.  I look under my desk, the PC is plugged into a surge protector, not a UPS.  Oh, the UPS is there, I'm just not plugged into it. I begin to consider my folly in doing a BIOS update for gee-whiz purposes.

7 minutes later I get "update successful" notfication onscreen.  Not wanting to have a premature exclaimation, I reboot before I claim success.  I reboot, everythng comes back peachy.  I shut everything down.

15 minutes later, the power goes off for a few seconds.

Whew !!
