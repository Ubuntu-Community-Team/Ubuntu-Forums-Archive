### Post by Bucky Ball on 2014-10-02
Agreed. Not just for this, but for all incompatible hardware. Notify the manufacturers of that hardware rather than ask for support here when the community can't give it. We have nothing to do with whether a hardware manufacturer provides Linux drivers for their hardware or not. Mostly, somebody who is wholly and solely volunteering their time needs to spend a lot of time reverse engineering any drivers, or taking apart not always inexpensive hardware, to see how it works and make life better for the rest of us.

Please, email, phone, visit, contact hardware manufacturers if your hardware doesn't work with Linux. Might not do much, but, least they'll know we're here.

(PS: What peeves me more is buying hardware I know works with Linux "out of the box" and there is a 'Windows/Mac compatible' sticker or icon on the box. No mention that it works with Ubuntu or Linux! Buy a hard drive or DVD. Compatible with Win/Mac. In fact, compatible with Ubuntu, too!!! Make hardware manufacturers aware of this, too, and ask why it is not made clear when their hardware DOES work with Ubuntu. Stick it on the box.)
