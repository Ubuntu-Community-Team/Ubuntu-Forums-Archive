### Post by TheFu on 2019-09-24
Yes, enabling 3rd party support for logins is required, but it isn't new.  Think it was forced about 2 yrs ago.  Google wants people to use their 'more secure' methods.  Can't say that I blame them, but since I don't use gmail for anything important, definitely never connected to any banking or other financial accounts, and my gmail logins are 66+ random characters, I'm not exactly worried.
I can't remember the last time I  use a web browser to login to google-anything.

I do have a few other gmail accounts. Those aren't connected to my real name in any way. I've never sent/received emails with my real name and don't use the same OS userid when accessing them.  No connection.  Plus they are old enough not to have any phone numbers connected to them.

For at least 2 yrs, google's security checkup has been incorrectly claiming that direct access like this isn't secure.  Allowing some other party access to your gmail account definitely isn't secure, but thunderbird is under your control, presumably.  You manage the password in the same way you would manage the webmail login password.  If users can't be trusted with their own password security, nothing anyone does will fix that.

I find it easier just to never trust google.  I don't regret that decision. Never have.
