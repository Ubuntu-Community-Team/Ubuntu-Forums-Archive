### Post by DuckHook on 2015-06-02
You don't have to program to be knowledgeable enough to work in an IT field. You can be a network admin, or you can get a job on the sales and marketing side, as only two of many examples. With your undergrad degree, you would be a natural in the latter. However, programming will clearly open up additional options. But it isn't a necessity. The editor of my favourite Linux e-zine (Linux Journal) is constantly reminding readers that he is not a programmer, yet he is well known and influential in the Linux community.

Or, like me, you can always take it up as a hobby more than a career.

Life is not a railroad track that constrains you to just one path. You have many options and the freedom to define your own way.

### Post by vellon on 2015-06-02
You can definitely use Linux in non-programmer roles. There are various support jobs in IT which involve building, fixing and upgrading servers, and Linux has a good presence in the server world. When a production Linux server goes down, the support guys are in big demand, I can tell you. What you will need to concentrate on learning, though, is the back-end knowledge, like networking, logs and networked storage.
