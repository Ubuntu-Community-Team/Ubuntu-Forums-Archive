### Post by LastDino on 2014-05-08
It's certainly possible, there are many software which allow people to clone their windows HD. Most people suggest dual boot or VM because with newbies it's generally observed that they are expecting things which are not there, or they are truly not yet prepared for that learning curve required to make that jump. In either case, most of the times it only translates into ''Linux  sux!'' after mere day or two. People here are not into marketing and we by no means benefit if you make that shift or not (Apart from maybe growing community of enthusiastic people and maybe some really really long term benefits to the Linux community itself). So, yeah, we like to take safer approach in way that those same newbies can come back in future more prepared and more aware. And most importantly, we like to make sure this does not effect anyone’s professional/personal life which depends on certain level of working computerized systems to those users expectations.
