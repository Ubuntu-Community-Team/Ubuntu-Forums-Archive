### Post by d-cosner on 2015-05-06
The specs of that HP are similar to my HP 655 other than mine has 8 GB of RAM and a 1.2 GHz E1 processor. That's not a bad price for a new laptop with a warranty. The thing is though that mine does not run all that fast with Ubuntu, it's usable but definitely slower than my 8 year old desktop. I recently installed Ubuntu 15.04 x 64 on the laptop and it runs better than 14.04 did after installing the proprietary ATI graphics divers and the AMD micro code drivers.

I bought mine at a pawn shop for $130.00, it was only a few months old. The laptop originally had Windows 8 installed. I suspect the former owner sold it because it was painfully slow. I saw many reviews on the HP 655 where people complained about it being slow. My guess is that this is why HP is offering this new model with Ubuntu installed. If you purchase one of these laptops I highly recommend installing 15.04 and the proprietary drivers if you want to run Unity.

I should mention that the processor is soldered to the board so you can not upgrade the chip. The display is not the greatest too but with the proprietary drivers and some minor adjustments it can be made to look pretty crisp and sharp. As for gaming on this laptop, maybe on-line games would be alright but any serious gaming is out of the question, it just isn't powerful enough.

Edit: Battery life is about 3 hours and 25 minutes without the annoying screen dimming on.
