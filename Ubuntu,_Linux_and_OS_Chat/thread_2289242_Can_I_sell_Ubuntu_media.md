### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-08-03
The Ubuntu license gives us certain freedoms and those same freedoms apply to anyone we distribute Ubuntu to whether we charge for a DVD or USB stick with a Ubuntu ISO image on or for the action of installing Ubuntu or for just giving advice. Or not.

I found this blog post interesting. It is on a related subject. I quote from it:

> Our policy exists to ensure that when you receive something that claims  to be Ubuntu, you can trust that it will work to the same standard,  regardless of where you got it from. And people everywhere tell us they  appreciate that - when they get Ubuntu on a cloud or as a VM, it works,  and they can trust it.  

> Third party redistribution is more complicated because, when things go  wrong, there&#8217;s a real question as to who is responsible for it. Here&#8217;s a  real example: a school district buys laptops for all their students  with free software. A local supplier takes their preferred Linux  distribution and modifies parts of it (like the kernel) to work on their  hardware, and sells them all the PCs. A month later, a distro kernel  update breaks all the school laptops. In this case, the Linux distro who  was not involved gets all the bad headlines, and the free software  advocates who promoted the whole idea end up with egg on their faces.

[http://blog.dustinkirkland.com/2015/07/appellation-of-origin-from-ubuntu.html](http://blog.dustinkirkland.com/2015/07/appellation-of-origin-from-ubuntu.html)

### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-08-03
Can you sell an unmodified Ubuntu ISO burned to DVD while falsely representing yourself as a representative or partner of Canonical? No. 

Can you sell an unmodified Ubuntu ISO burned to DVD while truly representing yourself as an independent vendor? Yes.

Can you sell a modified or remastered Ubuntu ISO burned to DVD? No. An agreement with Canonical is required to use the *buntu name if you have made any alterations.

Can you sell a modified or remastered Ubuntu ISO with all the Ubuntu branding replaced by your own and burned to DVD? Yes. The upstream software licenses still apply: As the vendor, you must make source code available.
