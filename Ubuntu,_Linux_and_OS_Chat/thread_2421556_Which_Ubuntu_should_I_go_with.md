### Post by Artim on 2019-06-24
Try them both!

The Xfce desktop is deliberately slow to develop, so there won't be these huge major radical changes to the desktop like both Gnome and KDE have done.  The LXDE desktop is also undergoing a major change, from GTK to the Qt base.  A smart move, perhaps, but big changes like that also haven't had time to work any bugs out.  Xfce has a really slow and deliberate way of making changes, a little at a time, that don't upset their users and make the desktop buggy.  And in spite of it's younger sibling's rapid growth, Xfce doesn't need that much improving to begin with!  It's infinitely configurable, wonderfully simple, and a speedy, nimble desktop.  Xubuntu is the way to go, especially for newbies!
