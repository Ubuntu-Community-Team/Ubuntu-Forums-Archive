### Post by grahammechanical on 2020-08-03
Ubuntu is sponsored by a company called Canonical which is a privately owned company registered with the United Kingdom's Companies House department. It is within the bounds of possibility that the owner of Canonical or his heirs (upon his death) may sell Canonical one day. On the other hand Ubuntu is Open Source software. Even if Canonical decided to stop developing Ubuntu anyone interested in continuing development would be free to take the source code and continue development.

[https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/05055134/persons-with-significant-control](https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/05055134/persons-with-significant-control)

This is what has happened to the desktop environment and user interface that Ubuntu was developing for smart phones and tablet PCs. Ubuntu Touch is now developed by a group of developers called Ubports.

[https://ubports.com/](https://ubports.com/)

Whether a completely community developed Ubuntu will still be called Ubuntu is anybody's guess.
