### Post by Roland_Msl on 2017-05-20
I started my change to Linux May 2016 with an Acer ES1-331-C0YK for 269 EUR.
In November 2016 also my wife got an Acer ES1-331-P498 for 299 EUR.
I watched the market on the German price compare platform "Geizhals".
In January 2017 had been offers for economic 13" - 14" notebooks from all major brands like Asus, Acer, hp and Lenovo.

In April remained not a single offer. I wrote to Acer Austria, they confirmed, that they offer the successor of the ES1-331 series, the ES1-332 series only with Windows.
Only Linux? Yes, the 11" B117, the 15,6" ES1-531 series, but no 13" - 14" notebook.

When I can choose between Asus, Acer, hp and Lenovo for a modern 6 W DTP CPU, 4 GB RAM and 500 MB harddisk for less than 320 EUR,
I would really not come on the idea to search in AliExpress for notebooks. But so, I had no other choice.

I have identified some major 14" notebook lines offered on AliExpress. But oder from China and have some nasty surprises when installing Ubuntu?
Also just right now, they have a NO GO KO criteria: glare display.
I have ordered from China until now 13 smartphones for my family and friends, but as an only Linux user, changing a complete unknown notebook to Linux is an other dimension.

What's about a cooperation with Chinese vendors?

The Chinese vendor should:
1.) offer with non-glare display
2.) RAM not soldered, to be able to upgrade to 8 GB
3.) Can be direct ordered with Ubuntu preinstalled

The community should
1.) Make clear why this is a great business idea
For example, they write "Is with Windows 10, if You want an official licensed, You should install it..."
No Problem in China or Russia, but here is the danger of an control of the BSA - Business Software Alliance
This would be my part.
3.) Technical support to make their notebooks perfectly compatible with Linux Ubuntu.

Would it not be great to have an 225 EUR or US$ 250 laptop (delivered with customs and VAT) with Celeron N3150, 4 GB RAM, 32 GB eMMC and place for any 7mm high 2,5" harddisk?

When this project is successful, it makes also pressure to the big brands to offer again notebooks without Windows.
