### Post by TheFu on 2016-10-16
Welcome to the forums.

None.

Run your own on equipment that you physically control.

"cloud" is code for "someone else's computer" - ZERO privacy with that. Ain't nuthin' you can do to make it different. Don't buy the marketing hype - they aren't telling the truth, even the best of them. When people with guns show up to a business, they get what they want and the clients/customer may never know.  At least with it under your physical control, either you will be there or the door will be broken.  They will still get the information, but at least you will know.

Heck, even a raspberry-pi v2 can handle all this stuff (well, not the enterprise calendaring stuff in a nice way, but everything else).  Bad calendaring can be handled on the r-pi.  That's $24 (on sale here now) + an old SDHC card and old USB power supply.  Anyway - under $50 and a broadband connection solves this.  

Common sense.  How can anything owned by another organization provide real privacy to you when they are trying to service as many other people as possible? It can't be done.  Software has bugs. DBAs make mistake. Admins make mistakes. Marketing demands usability over security **every time**.  If you are willing to relax your idea of privacy, there are probably many options, but all of them have issues.

I'd probably start looking for providers in northern Europe with string privacy laws which are outside the EU.  Things are beginning to look better in the USA, but it will take years before I'd trust them.

BTW, HTTPS is NOT secure enough if you care about privacy.
