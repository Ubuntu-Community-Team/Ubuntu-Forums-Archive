### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-01-26
*Thread moved to **Ubuntu, Linux and OS Chat**.*

Welcome. Nice words and thoughts. Thanks for sharing. Got me a little emotional. ;)

You mirror my feelings. I have had a great ride with Ubuntu over the past seven or so years and the folks here are a terrific, friendly and helpful bunch. I was once a Windows 'power' user. Now I just find Windows clunky and confusing. 

Have you heard of [Edubuntu]("http://edubuntu.org/about")? Might be perfect for the kids computer. It comes with four 'levels':

* Preschool application bundle
 * Primary level application bundle
 * Secondary level application bundle
 * Tertiary level application bundle

If you have Ubuntu on their computer already you can install the bundle you want directly from the repositories, or 'edubuntu-desktop' for the whole lot. For instance, if you just want the primary level bundle, then software centre and search for 'ubuntu-edu-primary'. 

Good luck with it all. Enjoy the OS, the learning curve and the forums.

PS: My wife has been using Ubuntu for almost as long as I have and I built a Ubuntu machine for my mother-in-law who was a diehard Windows gal. She now hates Windows and loves her Kubuntu machine. Apart from this, various builds and installs for folks along the way. ;)
