### Post by mastablasta on 2020-05-19
well the ones with largest usage are KDE and Gnome and they are not going anywhere. Similarly there are relatively large teams working on LXQT and XFCE. There were even some discussions to merge KDE and Gnome, but i think the philosophies behind these DE are so very different that it probably won't happen. when choosing a DE it is also important to know what you want form it and what their aim is. the big difference between KDE and Gnome is that Gnome DE hides certain advanced functions, so as to not overwhelm the users. so the design of menus, icons and functions is simplified. the advanced settings are often found via command line. KDE on the other hand has all possible settings available in GUI (icons, menus, buttons, tick boxes...).
