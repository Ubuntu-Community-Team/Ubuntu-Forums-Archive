### Post by Trandok on 2016-02-02
Open source is socialism in a kind of way, as the code from the developers at the top is freely available for everyone. It has definitely helped with the development of IT in places which could not afford proprietary software. No one can claim exclusive rights for their code, which does go against one of the fundamental tenets of capitalism, while it still espouses meritocracy (which one would associate more perhaps with capitalism).

Looks like Linus himself is more focused on the practical aspects of open source then. There are similarly people who place importance on the ethical aspects in the free software movement.
