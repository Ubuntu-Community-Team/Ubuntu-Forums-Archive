### Post by vasa1 on 2014-12-16
> ... Even with this new import support, it's not clear there have been improvements to ODF export.

Google, like Microsoft, does not make it easy to use ODF as part of a workflow. Change tracking information, annotations, and other metadata gets lost in the import process and doesn't get exported, so for both companies, ODF is seen as a migration format rather than as a working format.

That will have to change, because there's no doubt official interest in ODF around the world is growing. Google wants to sell Drive and Chromebooks into government-controlled markets, and ODF is becoming a gating factor. Perhaps as a result, Google will continue to improve its ODF support as it pushes ahead to capture future business.[http://www.infoworld.com/article/2860082/open-source-software/googles-odf-gift-surprise.html](http://www.infoworld.com/article/2860082/open-source-software/googles-odf-gift-surprise.html)

### Post by DuckHook on 2014-12-18
> **Bucky Ball said:**
> Any move in an open-source direction is a good thing for all governments... If they swapped to 95% open-source OSs on the machines they've already got now it would save considerably more...You only have to scan through my old postings to see what kind of Linux fanatic I am. But granting that, I must still say that it isn't possible for large organizations to just drop MSOffice. Back in the Dark Ages when I was still working, there were MS-specific macros that I relied on to get real work done. Countless man-months of work went into those macros, and countless docs would break if they ran on anything other than MS apps. Yes, in hindsight, it was stupid to get so irreversibly addicted to one drug, but there was nothing else in the late '80s/early '90s. Linux was simply not ready for prime time and WordPerfect/Lotus was just as much jailware and even then clearly on the way to marginal adoption. Any organization dealing with legacy docs that are more than of Mickey Mouse complexity cannot just give up MS products, no matter how fanatical about Linux they may be.

It *is* possible to wean yourself off the MS hamster mill by insisting on open file formats first. Hopefully, the cure then proceeds slowly and in stages. But you can't just go cold turkey. And I doubt that any organization can ever go as far as 95% FOSS.

Kudos to the UK govt for taking the right steps to end its addiction. But it's going to be a long and often painful process. Better to know that now and prepare for it than to get discouraged when the inevitable pain hits.
