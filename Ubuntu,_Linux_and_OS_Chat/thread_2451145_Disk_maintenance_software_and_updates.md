### Post by mastablasta on 2020-09-28
underpowered laptop with HDD (5400 rpm), 2 Gb ram, low powered, low performance AMD E-450. disk is split between two OS.

I did an update of windows 7 starter (security essentials+malware removal tool+ new Edge browser < 300 MB, then manual update of Firefox and Oracle Java JRE). and i used the tools for disk maintenance provided by MS. So the updates stalled, PC got stuck at boot and i finally resolved it next day (0:15). the update session lasted....................   ...... 11 hours

before that same PC had (K)Ubuntu update. apply all updates since June and to perform auto remove. in total about 380 MB updates were downloaded, kernel patched... it took.... slightly less than 20 mins

i though something went wrong and my PC was too slow for latest update, but i see the same issue i still present in 2020 on windows 10. and even when you apply the solution - renaming software distribution folder (which was easy to do since i had linux on the other side of the disk) the updates resolve within 2 hours on Core i5 and SSD drive (is what people report, osme reported even 3 hours). 

i mean - come on!!! i can install linux at least 4 times in that period. or restore OS image like 6 times. but that is not the funny part. the funny part is users giving the patience is a virtue advice. and some questioning why people are in such a hurry when the do the updates.

the update system on window is seriously messed up. i use business version at work so updates are vetted by IT before i get them and i have an older version so mostly i would now receive just security updates & patches. normal monthly update takes about 1.5 hours to complete (updates+reboot which you "*may need to do*") and they take over all of the system resources, so i can't do much when they are running. so it got me thinking - if you have 1.5 hours per pc of work time lost, and you have 2000 pcs that get this patch and the get it 12 times a year (at minimum) then about 36.000 work hours are lost  per year on updating the PC. and these are just security patches. so let's say the average gross for skilled workers using the PC is 20€ per hour (i am sure it is more, but then again some of us are maybe undervalued and cheap), the company is paying 720.000 EUR/year for people just to watch their PC being updated. very efficient indeed.

in Linux you don't even notice the updates in the background, reboot is unnecessary (you can also use Ubuntu Live). the only update i notice (with regarding OS resources) is kernel update, but even they are fast. and my PC is old.
