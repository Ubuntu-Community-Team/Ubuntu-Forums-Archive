### Post by albefm84 on 2013-12-29
According i'm not very new to Linux, i tried a lot of distros: OpenSuse, Chromium, Knoppix, and Geexbox.

I wanted to use my old pc as a HTPC, here its specs

Motherboard MS6390;
CPU: Athlon XP+ 2400 (recognised as 1666Mhz, overclocked to its 1800 real clock and no trouble);
RAM: 1,5 GB (1 row of 512 MB, 1 row of 1GB);
GPU: Geforce FX5200, 128MB;

ADDONS:
Hamlet USB 2,0 with 5 USB 2.0 doors, 3 front, 2 rear;
Hauppauge WIN-TV HVR 1100;
Sound Card: Creative Audigy Platinum including Audigy Drive and remote.
Wi-FI: USB D-LINK DWA140 Rangebooster adapter.

Tried the tv card as well in Windows XP and in Seven and worked good, as well with Windows Media Center as with ProgDvb.
But I wanted a dedicated and alternative OS for having an HTPC of my "junkytower", so I tried different Linux distros.

Here are the facts:

Opensuse:
- Although I found help in forum, something by installing tv tuner driver went wrong (working by xterm);
- WiFi dongle requested extra firmware, found but key still not working;
- Sound worked fine:

Knoppix:
- Used just in live mode, didn't recognize Sound Card, worked with wifi after firmware install, didnt' use TV Tuner;

Chromium (live mode);
- No matter how many firmware I tried to install, DWA140 still not working;
- Sound card not recognized;
- Didn't try TV-Tuner;

Geexbox (installed)
- Didn't try Wi-FI, after seeing the next two points;
- No matter how many front-end and back-end I installed, TV-Tuner didn't work;
- Sound Card NOT WORKING, although various setting: I ABSOLUTELY didn't want to be forced using my onboard audio and I decided to format all the geexbox partition;
- Installation: poor, there is no partition choice, just choose a disk and erase all; 

Then I tried Mythbuntu (installed 29 December, yesterday, 22.30, choosing Opensource NVIDIA driver after a previous install with dedicated NVIDIA driver left me with only a blank screen) and it worked.
After a little tweaking in the Control Center, i got
+ Hauppauge WIN-TV HVR1100 working, catching all channels I found under Windows and maybe a little more; didn't try the Composite source cause not interested at the time; :-)
+ Sound Blaster Audigy working :-) ;
+ Wifi DWA140 working with no extra firmware needed;

I want to thanks everybody a lot for making a REAL Htpc Operating System.

Although there are people working hard on every Linux's distro, I think Ubuntu und Mythubuntu workers did the best job.

I was encorauged trying Mythubuntu after trying successfully Ubuntu on a MacBook pro, everything worked fine, included keyboard light and Broadcom Wireless Airport.
Just thought: if it works fully on a Mac, why not with an ordinary X86 old pc?
I thought well: now my ex-junk pc is a fully working HTPC.
