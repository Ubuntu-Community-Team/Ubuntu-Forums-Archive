### Post by pfeiffep on 2016-02-19
Ever since I started using Ubuntu for the majority of time I've been really happy with the results. There was an obtuse learning curve involved because my professional background in computing started with super-mini computers, then to Apple, onto DOS and Windows and landing onto Sun Solaris. 

I don't use Ubuntu exclusively and rely on Windows 7 for some of my tasks. When Microsoft Money curtailed support I switched to using Intuit's [Mint]("https://www.mint.com/") online version. 

I noticed that many financial institutions adopted multifactor authentication so I queried Mint support in the beginning of Feb. about how to enable this on their site. I was informed that this was already in place and had been tested thoroughly. So I informed them that I had not seen evidence of this on my end. Their support provided me with detailed instructions which I followed to the letter - still no multifactor authentication. Up to this point OS was not mentioned, but they did provide a list of suggested browsers. 

Well I responded with my chosen browser AND operating system. Additionally I revisited their instruction while using Windows 7 - I was immediately prompted for a second form of id. 

As of this morning (Feb 19) while using Ubuntu LTS I was prompted for a second form of authentication.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2016-02-19
Linux will almost always acquire those kinds of capabilities after commercial products like Windows, if at all, because banks, in this case, are going to pay to implement and maintain something that works for almost all their customers, i.e. people on Windows.  They may or may not implement it for OS X.  And they will probably never pay to implement it on Linux.  

So, things like this usually don't get implemented on Linux until FOSS developers manage to do it. 

I've taken to doing banking and all bill paying only from my Android tablet, from that bank's app.  I can pay established bills without 2FA, but creating a new bill pay account or anything else that sends money outside the accounts requires 2FA,  and has for several years.
