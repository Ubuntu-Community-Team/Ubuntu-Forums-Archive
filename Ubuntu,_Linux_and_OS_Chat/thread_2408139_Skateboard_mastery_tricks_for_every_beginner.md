### Post by lindapeterson on 2018-12-14
As you begin your journey to master the skateboard, you need to have first learned the basics. First, you need to ensure that you have the right balancing, rolling, pushing and stopping. Falling is inevitable, however, you need to ensure your safety to avoid adverse risks. The journey of becoming a skateboarding master is not a walk in the park as it requires a lot of commitment and passion with a lot of practice leading to perfection. Skateboarding tricks and tweaks are movements you would like to achieve as you skateboard. To be able to perform good and safe tricks, you need to have a good skateboard, see the best skateboard for beginnes here: **[https://skateadvisors.com/best-skateboard-for-beginners.html](https://skateadvisors.com/best-skateboard-for-beginners.html)**

The tricks which you are about to learn here have varying difficulty level and each trick will require dedicated practice. You need to ensure that you are using the right kind of skateboarding wheels for each trick.

The tricks shown below are the general basic skateboarding tricks and are a reliable starting point for you as a beginner:
[h=1]Manual[/h]This trick can be compared with a wheelie on the bike. The aim is for you to lift the front wheels of the skateboard off the ground as you lean back while rolling. The bottom line of achieving a manual is achieving balance by placing your back foot on the tail of the skateboard and the front foot just behind the front trucks. You then lean your weight backward till the front wheels come off the ground trying to maintain this position while still rolling.
While learning these tricks, it is common that you might fall and hurt your head. To avoid any adverse risks, it is always advisable to wear a helmet while skating. 
[h=1]Ollie[/h]This is the first and most outstanding trick where you kick the tail of the skateboard downward while jumping which makes the skateboard pop up in the air. Ollie is the most vital trick as it is the starting point of most of the other advanced tricks. Basically, an ollie is a jump with the board sticking to your feet. To achieve it, place the front foot in the middle of the board and the back foot at the very edge of the board. With the back foot, kick the tail down to lift it up and simultaneously slide the front foot to the front of the skateboard. Ensure your knees are bent in a crouched position to help absorb the shock as you land. As you advance, you can try longer and higher ollies.

[IMG]https://skateadvisors.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/how-to-ollie-higher-1020x510.jpg[/IMG]
[h=1]Kickflip[/h]A kickflip is just a more advanced Ollie. With this trick, you need to kick the heel side edge of the skateboard as you jump. This is meant to achieve a flip of the board in the air before landing. You can practice more kickflip variations like the double kickflip after mastering the basic kickflip.
[h=1]Nollie[/h]A Nollie is an Ollie variant, where you need to pop the front of the skateboard as you just unlike in the Ollie where you pop the backside. Ease to learn a Nollie is dependent on the mastery you achieved in the first trick. Unlike in an ollie, shift the back foot to the middle of the skateboard and the front foot to the nose of the skateboard. Obtain a crouched position before jumping after which you need to pop hard the nose of the board of the ground. While on mid-air, level the board and bend your knees to ensure they absorb the shock as you land just like in an ollie.
[h=1]Heelflip[/h]This is the opposite of kickflip. You use your back foot to flip the board toe-side instead of using your front foot. You start in an ollie position after which you pop the board of the ground using your back foot. You need to slide your front foot in a diagonal manner towards the board’s toe-side edge using your heel to flick the board.
