### Post by d-cosner on 2014-12-24
I installed Ubuntu 14.04 again a couple of days ago. I have installed Ubuntu for so many people this week that I needed it to be able to help walk them through some tasks they needed help with. I have noticed less bugs than the last time I had it installed. I had issues with some thumbnails not displaying right but I just stopped some of the problem ones from displaying in the home lens, they display fine in the other lenses and I am happy with how things turned out.

I had installed Ubuntu for a couple of people and the word spread through the factory! I chose Ubuntu because it was easy for beginners and it looks really nice without looking like Windows. I have not had a single user that has not been happy with it! People love the Guest account because they can let their kids and grandchildren play on the computer without having to worry about anything getting messed up. Believe it or not people really want this feature.

People seem to realize that Ubuntu does not work like Windows too. When I used to give people KDE thinking they would be more at home with it they thought it should work like a free version of Windows. I think the visual difference with Unity makes them understand that they are working with something different and it makes my life so much easier. People seem to be able to adapt to Ubuntu pretty quick and so far everyone has said that they like it more than Windows after using it for a while.

No software is perfect but it's nice to see the bug count going in the right direction for a change.

### Post by d-cosner on 2014-12-26
I saw the same thing where my wife used to work. A guy put Ubuntu on his laptop and everyone seemed to want it. I think Ubuntu has a lot more appeal since they went with Unity and changed the color scheme. I installed Ubuntu on a laptop for a neighbor, she said it looked better than OS X and she loved the selection of applications that were available. One of my friends loved the guest account because his girlfriends grandchildren kept messing up his Windows installs.

Offering a nice looking desktop with interesting features sure goes a long way! Ubuntu does have it's share of bugs but at least the whole system does not fall apart and go down. Ubuntu is more tolerant of things like a little kid pushing the power button over and over. People seem to get the hang of using Ubuntu real quick too because there is not really any manual configuration they need to do, they can just use their computer.

The things long time users complain about do not bother new users the least bit. You see complaints about thing like the software center being slow, new users love it! When Canonical developed Unity it was probably the best thing they ever did. They made the desktop look more appealing and they took responsibility for the bugs it might have. Giving LTS releases 5 years of support made Ubuntu one of the best choices for new users too. 

I ended up putting Ubuntu back on my main computer because I needed it to be able to walk people through doing things over the phone if they needed some help. People seem to want to do more with their computers after switching to Ubuntu! It is really satisfying seeing people learning to do more with their computers. I think people are less afraid of messing things up with Ubuntu. I switched my father-in-law over to Ubuntu 3 years ago. I used to have to fix his computer every few months, now I never see it because it always works. He loves it now that he has gotten used to it.

Most people get used to Ubuntu in a few days, then they ask why aren't more people using it? Ubuntu is the only distro I use on new users computers these days because I do not have to explain much to them. Ubuntu lets them know when updates are available, has a good length of support and the software center makes it simple for them to add the things they want or need. All the things the average user wants are included by default.

Also, I charge very little to install Ubuntu on peoples computers, sometimes I do it for free. Yes, I need the money because work has been so scarce this year... Windows takes all day to install, update and put everything on it to make it useful. Ubuntu takes less than an hour! I can even give anyone a Ubuntu DVD and be pretty certain they will be able to install it themselves if they want. So far I have not had any complaints from any users. One person asked me how to create shortcuts in the launcher, that took all of 5 seconds to answer. I did walk a user through creating mp3 files the other day, he had never done it and wanted to make his wife a mix CD for Christmas.

Ubuntu has become my first choice for new users and really once someone tries it they tell their friends and the next thing I know I am installing it on more computers. Ubuntu really has gotten much better with fixing bugs.
