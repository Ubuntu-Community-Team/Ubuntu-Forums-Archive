### Post by d-cosner on 2014-07-11
I have never seen Easy TAG mentioned here so I thought I would share my experience with it. I used to just drag and drop my album art into Rhythmbox to have it display but that was not a permanent solution. With Easy TAG I can drag and drop my album art and save it permanently. Also, I can edit everything else about it  and save it.

I always back up all my music and this makes things easy in the event I reinstall my OS or want to have my music in other operating systems I have loaded. It's nice to open a music app and have everything there!

Another application I have been using is OggConvert, it converts mp3's, mp4's etc to ogg, this format takes up less space and is supposed to be higher quality. I honestly can not notice the difference in quality but it is nice having my music collection take up less space.

You do have to have the ubuntu restricted extras installed for these to work but I am sure most people install this anyway. I get a lot of album art and information from Wikipedia. To get the album art you do need to click on the image in the article and bring up the image itself to be able to drag and drop it in. 

These two applications have really helped refine my music collection. No more missing bits and pieces displaying in music players and my backups retain all the data.
