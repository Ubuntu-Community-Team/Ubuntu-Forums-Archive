### Post by symphony0flife on 2016-11-20
I've been back and forth between Windows and Ubuntu for about a decade now. It's not really possible to sell you on it because we're all so different. There are some major differences (a lot really) and the entire way you go about using Ubuntu is different. You have to think different and you have to be open to learning. It's not a drop in replacement as Ian said, though you can do just about anything in Ubuntu that you can in Windows. There are alternatives to almost every application you can imagine, and in many cases the alternatives are superior. It takes a lot of reading and tweaking to get things just how you like it, but that's one of the things I like most. You can actually get it set up how ***you*** like it. Windows is extremely restrictive. From the appearance to the apps you use and the functionality of the OS, you can mold Ubuntu around you, rather than having to learn to work around it like you have to do with Windows.

A few of the things I like about it that always bring me back, this time for good:
1. It is much less resource intensive.

2. You can mold it around you, as I said, and find an alternative to almost anything you can get in Windows for free. There are also a lot of great paid options, and every year more and more previously Windows/Mac exclusive stuff becomes available for Linux.

3. More secure. Naturally, security always ultimately comes down to the brains behind the keyboard, but overall your privacy and data is far more safe with Ubuntu/Linux.

4. It's fun. Honestly, learning how the operating system works is fun. It can be frustrating for some at first but once you learn HOW things work, and not just how to use them, you find yourself more educated and capable. It's very satisfying in that way.

5. Honestly it's better for light use activities than Windows (or OS X/mac OS). It's faster, better, and smoother for things like email, browsing, research and word processing, watching videos... Basically everything you stated that you do. However I use Google Docs rather than LibreOffice, but it's very capable as well.

Everyone is different. It may not be for you at all. In fact, out of the box you'll probably find it leaves a lot to be desired. But that's where the fun begins. Because there's a lot out there to do and again you can literally mold it around you. From the appearance to the functionality. The community has always been friendly in the past and there are tons of people ready to help you learn. Heck, you can even game on it these days. Steam, Humble Bundle and other sources are putting out more and more games all the time.
