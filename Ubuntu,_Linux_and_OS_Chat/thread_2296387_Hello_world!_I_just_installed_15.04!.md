### Post by superkid333 on 2015-09-28
Steam is an application owned and operated by a gaming company Valve. They are one of the largest providers of online games. The Steam application is a portal to play these games. They took a huge step forward (for Linux) by producing games which were playable on Linux, natively, without requiring any work around. 

Games like Counter-Strike, Team Fortress, and Left 4 Dead, are some of their biggest names. All are excellent and playable on Linux.

I hope that explains the point of it. Why to Linux users care? Well, I believe gaming to be an important aspect of computers - if not an important aspect, it is certainly a popular one! For years Linux could not cross the barrier into the realm of gaming. Valve decided that Steam would work well for it, and said "Hey Linux users, you deserve entertainment in addition to your excellent open source computing model!"

GPL Licensed? Steam is not GPL licensed but a few (very few) games available through Steam are GPL licensed.
