### Post by mystics on 2016-01-28
I'm not sure that there's enough to say one way or another. There's maybe one chart that shows benchmarks for Linux distros that I've been able to find, and it was more comparing Steam OS to Windows 10 rather than trying to find the perfect distro. As a result, everything beyond this point is speculation.

 Steam OS is designed around gaming, but it isn't so great for general use (from what I hear), and it's also meant to build off of a Steam, which is a rather restrictive platform for gaming. (I use it, but it was designed so that Valve could more easily control their games.) If you're looking beyond Steam, though, other platforms tend to target Ubuntu specifically, and GOG in particular doesn't officially support anything but Ubuntu and Linux Mint with its installers. Still, if you primarily focus on Steam and want a gaming-centered distro, then Steam OS would probably be the best for you.

As for flavors of Ubuntu: Xubuntu and Lubuntu would probably be the best. Ideally you'd want to get a desktop environment that doesn't use a lot of resources, and both Xfce and LXDE do a good job of that. I know it is possible to login directly to Steam without any window manager coming up, but I don't know the process of doing that. I just have a friend who once mentioned that he did it. Anyways, if you can figure it out, that would make the resource usage of different desktop environments a non-issue.
