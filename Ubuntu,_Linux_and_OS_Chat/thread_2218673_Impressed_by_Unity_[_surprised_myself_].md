### Post by craig10x on 2014-04-21
@Ian: the best way to approach it, is to see unity as what it is..a DOCK...just like say, a mac has (except it's on the left)...but it's used in the same manner...and the most efficient way to use it, is to put your favorite and most used apps on the dock for easy "1 click" access...efficient, right???  Then for apps you rarely use, just bring them up using the dash search...Also, you can make the unity icons smaller (pixel size) in the settings (i do) and then you can put more apps on that dock...also on 14.04 (in system settings) you can non-global the global menus and put them back in the app windows again...but it doesn't affect the top panel, so you still save screen space when web surfing (a real "win win")...

Work with it for about 2 or 3 weeks and see if after a while, you realize how nice it actually is to work with ;)
