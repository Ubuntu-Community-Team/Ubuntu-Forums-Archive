most features you seek are available on wordpress by adding plugins. this would seem to be the easiest solution to use in your case there are others that offer these features but might no be as easy to use. as for the webshop - i suggest you use opencart, though if you don't have too many items then wordpress with plugins will also do. 

frontpage is bad because it adds to much code. this results in slower website. google & other search engines will/could rank you lower because of that. Wordpress and Apache web server (probably others as well) have plugins for auto optimisation.

we used wysiwig normal HTML page for my wife's site. then we decided to move as previous hosting was just too expencive. it was ok for the first year when they provided basic SEO and website learning material. anyway after some research we decided to move it all to wordpress. she maintains the site now and has static websites (galeries, embeded videos and pics are really easy on wordpress), news and a digital material (e-book) webshop all on wordpress. she doesn't know how to code and neither do i. we followed manuals and instructions to optimise the page load speed. during this time some awesome free plugins were abandoned, some new ones were made. this is usually not the case if you pay for the plugins. overall i would give the wordpress tool 8/10. not perfect, but it is easy to use and maintain and also very powerful at the same time. which is why i would also recomend it to you. 

if you go with it:
 the platform is one of the most popular (if not THE MOST) among users. as a result it is often (like windows) targeted by hackers. you are 99 % safe if you do regular updates and watch for abandoned plugins. luckily there are free plugins that will manage this for you (i highly recomend installing both Wordfence and Sucuri plugins). Both actively protect the owners and users of the wordpress website. They will also block plugins that suddenly grant themselves to much powers. i got hacked once (via a newsletter plugin i didn't even use) and the site sent out malicious emails, so don't be me and learn form my mistakes. next important plugin is backup plugin that does regular scheduled backups. i recommend UpDraft plugin. backups are best antihacking "defence" and they saved me after the hack and i had my website up and running in about 3 hours. i had a system error on one upgrade & site move and because of updraft (which made better version that my manual backups) the site was up and running in less than 15 mins. and finally don't install too many plugins an uninstall those you do not use.
