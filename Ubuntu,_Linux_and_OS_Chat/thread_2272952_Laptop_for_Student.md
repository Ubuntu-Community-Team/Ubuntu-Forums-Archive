### Post by branau on 2015-04-11

### Post by coldraven on 2015-04-12
I would buy any second-hand laptop that is dual core and has at least 2GB of memory. Then replace the hard drive with a 128GB SSD for about $80. Install Ubuntu 14.04 and It will boot up in about 12 seconds! Applications will load so fast that your friends will not believe it. (Award yourself Geek points)
You can then buy a $5 USB enclosure for the old hard drive and have lots of space for backups etc.

### Post by Kpenguin on 2015-04-13
I bought a refurbished HP TouchSmart 15 for college off of [Newegg]("http://newegg.com") in January. It's a dual core Intel i3, 15" laptop with 6gb of memory. I bought it for around $400 I believe. I installed Ubuntu on it in March and haven't had any trouble yet. It came with Windows 8.1 on it, so I installed Ubuntu alongside it for compatibility.
By the way, I **highly** recommend getting a refurbished laptop because they are so much cheaper than new, but have the same quality. I've bought 4 refurbished electronics so far and couldn't even tell the difference between them and a brand new one.
