### Post by mystics on 2015-09-26
> **night_sky2 said:**

Well, to give a further explanation of what I said above, there are a lot of other things to consider about Mac beyond a top panel:
-It puts Window buttons on the left. Xubuntu defaults to the right.
-It keeps a menu on the aforementioned panel similar to Unity. Xubuntu requires a plugin to do this that is universal to anyone running Xfce.
-It doesn't have a traditional start menu. Xubuntu does.
-It has a dock. Xubuntu requires you to install a third-party one. Well, I guess you could add a second panel, but that's just an Xfce feature that will go across all distros.
-It uses a spotlight to search for everything from the panel. Xubuntu requires you to install a plugin.
-Launchpad on Mac doesn't have equivalent on Xubuntu that I'm away. Come to think of it, I don't think Xfce has anything like that.

Those are just the non-theming ones I can think of off the top of my head. And yes, I know it is possible to get Xubuntu to look and behave very similarly to a Mac, but by default the overall look and behavior is Windows-inspired. The panel is the only real thing they've taken from Mac, and personally I don't see a point in trying to pull that one comparison out when moving it to the bottom requires less than 30 seconds of effort.

And trust me, I spent a lot of time trying to get Xubuntu to look and behave like a Mac when I first installed it. The only thing I didn't change was the top panel. So yes, I guess you could say it is more "Mac-like" in that regard, but I doubt anyone grabbing Xfce over more lightweight DEs isn't at least already ready to put in at least some effort to customize things, so I personally wouldn't count the easily-movable top panel as a worthwhile difference.

It certainly took longer than moving the Xfce panel to the bottom in Xubuntu. :p (OK, OK, I keep it at the top. I have separate panels at the bottom for other things, though.)

And I do want to point out that I exaggerate just how ticked off I was by that. Was I angry when I saw that? Yes. Did it taint my experience of Mint?
