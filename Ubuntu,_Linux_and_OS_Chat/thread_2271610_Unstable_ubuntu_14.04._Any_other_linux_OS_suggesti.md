### Post by monkeybrain20122 on 2015-04-06
Why don't you try with a different kernel first?
[http://kernel.ubuntu.com/~kernel-ppa/mainline/](http://kernel.ubuntu.com/~kernel-ppa/mainline/)

Pick your version, then you need to download three .deb files into the same folder. E.g create a folder in ~/Downloads  and call it ker. Then download the three files into that.
The three files are Linux-headers-generic (amd64 or i386 depending on 64 or 32 bits) Linux-headers-all and Linux-image -generic (amd64 or i386 depending on 64 or 32 bit)

Then open a terminal
```
cd Downloads/ker
sudo dpkg -i *.deb
```

Reboot when done. I suggest choosing a newer rather than an older kernel version than 14.04's. Instead of "downgrading" I would try upgrading first.
