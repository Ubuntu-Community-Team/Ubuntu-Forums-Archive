### Post by Dlambert on 2014-01-15
Hello all,

**[SIZE=3]Background[/SIZE]:**

I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Michigan, and it was my plan to have the best laptop for me as I'm OCD and a bit of a perfectionist. My first instict was to go for a mac, so I bought a maxed out (Not Storage) 11" Macbook air. The Build Quality was amazing, but at close to $1500 the performance left me wanting more. I kept that laptop for two thirds of my first semester and it served me quite well, but I grew tired of Mac OS X and it's limitations as well as the performance of the hardware itself. So I decided to sell it on Craigslist. Then my journey for the perfect college laptop came to the Lenovo y510p, a SLi gaming notebook. The Build quality was great (not as good as the macbook), but the performance was top notch abit a < four hour battery life. The problem with having a gaming laptop is that I have a high end desktop and found myself rarely used the lenovo for gaming. So I was carrying around all this gaming hardware with a giant power brick only to have the battery die halfway through a school day. This happen until one day when windows secureboot/windows 8 decided to stop working. After fighting with amazon about my return, I had originally planned to by a refurbished Alienware 14 (2013). But that's when it happen...

[B]
[SIZE=3]The Prize:[/SIZE][/B]

I think I won the ebay lottery. I was dead set on buying an Alienware 14 when I happened to search "system76" on ebay. I saw a brand new upgraded Kudo Professional (Retails $1295 as configured) sitting at $200 something with a little under a day left. I immediatly thought, "well if I can get it for a good price, why not?" So I put in a bid of 900 bucks, and immediatly the automatic bids from the other bidders brought the price up to $768 with me as the highest bidder. Then the seconds ticked by, and I...won. I was the proud owner of a Brand-New-in-the-Box System76 Kudo Professional for literally a fraction of the cost of retail and after airmail from canada, it was finally in my hands.
[B]
[SIZE=3]The Specs:[/SIZE][/B]

[COLOR=#0000ff]Intel Core i7 4700MQ (4 Cores with Hyperthreading and Iris Pro Graphics) [/COLOR][COLOR=#000000]*This is the same CPU as is in the Alienware 14 and the Lenovo y510p[/COLOR][COLOR=#0000ff]
8GB Ram
Intel 530 120GB SSD
DVD Drive
Intel AC Wireless[/COLOR]

I had always wanted a system76 computer, but being OCD and perfectionalistic, I was frankly scared. It **IS** a clevo afterall. I had heard the horror stories, and inorder to calm my nerves used my friend's Sager (clevo-2011/20012) gaming laptop only to find the build quality was sub-par with a lot of flex and tinny and very low quality speakers. So I was all but reassured.
[B]
[SIZE=3]Then it arrived...[/SIZE][/B]

My first impression was that of...dissapointment, as the laptop came in a cheap looking cardboard box that said "notebook computer" and "Enjoy with me." Once I oppened the box I was struck by how beautiful the system really is. The Brushed/striped almost carbon fiber like finish (non-textured) looks amazing. The laptop is heavy, but lighter than the lenovo, so it felt nice in my hands.

[SIZE=3]**Pros:**[/SIZE]


[LIST]
[*]The build quality is **GREAT**, very comparable to the lenovo despite being made of all plastic. 
[*]There is little to no flex, **and the machine feels solid**. 
[*]**The screen is amazing**. Way better than the 1366x768 Macbook Screen and better color representation than the lenovo's 1080p screen. 
[*]**The trackpad is on par with Apple's**. I loved the MBA's touchpad, and the lenovo's was a major step back, so** it's great to see natural two fingering scrolling work so smoothly as well as the two finger right click** (something I missed on the lenovo). I just wished there were more jestures that could be used in Ubuntu...:confused: 
[*]**The Keyboard feels great**. I would say it's halfway inbetween an Apple Chicklet style and a mechanical keyboard. The "slight" curving of each key allows your fingers to easily sit snug on the keys allowing for easy typing. There is no noticble flex when typing. The Backlight (2 settings) is also a very nice touch, I just wish it didn't "bleed" as much (as in the area around the key being lit more than the letter on the key). The spacing is perfect with no accidental key presses. 
[*]SPEAKERS. Against my fears,** these speakers a really good**. A little quiet I'll be honest, and not up to par with the JBL speakers on the lenovo but still loud enough that I can watch a movie by myself just fine without any "tinny" sounds experienced on my friend's clevo. 
[*]**Ubuntu flies on this machine**.* The "eight" core cpu tears through ubuntu booting in under 3 seconds 
[*]The webcam looks sharp, but seems a little washed out ("too white"), I'll try in a darker room and report back. **Note**: **The [COLOR=#0000cd]Blue [/COLOR]Function key that looks like a camera turns on/off the webcam.** 
[*]**BATTERY LIFE! **Going from <4 hours to this has been a dream. THe indicator shows up to 8 hours remaining at some moments, but I'm experiencing [B]6+ hours of battery life almost everyday with screen turned down a bit, no bluetooth, and the keyboard lights off.
[/B] 
[/LIST]
[B]
*Before botting the computer for the first time I took out the Intel SSD and replaced it with a 120GB Samsung 840 EVO SSD (some benchmarks so it being more than twice as fast)[/B] **and fresh installed Ubuntu**. **At a later date I plan to add a mSATA ssd for more storage.**

[SIZE=3]**Cons (for me)**:

[/SIZE]
[LIST]
[*]The system76 silver sticker on the outside is scratched (I'm OCD), but it can be easily overlooked because the rest of the laptop is flawless. I guess I could just remove this if I wanted to, but I'll pass for now. 
[*]There's no "dummy" sd card to fit the slot when the card reader isn't in use, something that most modern laptops come with. (I blame clevo) 
[*]One piece of the platic "pops" near the card reader when pressed and depressed near that exact spot. (again, I'm OCD) 
[*]The "Powered by Ubuntu" stickers included were not of usual system76 quality. Flat great and dark orange? You can do better than that system76. 
[/LIST]

[SIZE=3]**Gripes Against Ubuntu (if you know how to fix any of them, please let me know!)**:

[/SIZE]
[LIST=1]
[*][COLOR=#000000]The screen brightness and keyboard backlit get reset (to 100%) every boot. [/COLOR]**Fixed by installing the system76 driver.** 
[/LIST]


*[SIZE=2]I'm able to enjoy any programs I need for college including remote  desktop connections (if I need to use windows), word processing, and  entertainment. The looks I get running Ubuntu in a world dominated by  Macs is amazing.[/SIZE]*


[COLOR=#ff0000]*[SIZE=4]**Overall, System76 has made me a customer for life. I would easily recommend this laptop, and well as any from system76 if someone was looking for an excellent laptop. Well done system76, and thank you!**[/SIZE]*[/COLOR][SIZE=4][B]

UPDATE Jan 17th 2014: I've since replaced the stock (crappy brand in my opinion) Super Talent 8GBs Ram with 16GBs of Kinston HyperX CL9 Ram.[/B][/SIZE]


[SIZE=3]***I will update this review later with pictures, more details, and error correcting. But now I have to go to bed. Thank you for reading and feel free to ask any questions about the laptop or my experiences.**[/SIZE]*

### Post by fataddict on 2014-07-08
It is a bit large to lug around all day setting up and tearing down. Plus, as a college student, I don't think you are going to get the battery life out of it that you will need. If you are just going to take notes in class you might consider carrying a tablet with keyboard or a Chromebook and keeping the System76 laptop in your dorm.

Overall I am really pleased with the laptop. I had to return mine for maintenance; the video ribbon went bad and had to be replaced, but they took care of it pretty quickly. It is supposed to arrive tomorrow with the repairs complete so I can post later about how well they did. I am assuming they took the screen apart to replace the cable so I will check to see if I have any nicks or abrasions around the screen and lid.

Just so you know about my setup, I use the Kudu as a desktop replacement because of where I live. Power is not dependable and I am mobile, so that rules out the traditional desktop. I also carry an 11" MacBook Air for all my mobile stuffs, which is much more convenient than lugging a 17" 12# monster with me.

_**Update**_: Got the laptop back and it works much better. I only found one abrasion around the lid to the acces the screen. It's very small, about the size of a ball point pen head: Nothing to complain about though.

Overall very satisfied with the laptop and the company.
