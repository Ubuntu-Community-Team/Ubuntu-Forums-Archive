### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-11-01
Listen and watch all the secret Canonical discussions. :)

[http://summit.ubuntu.com/](http://summit.ubuntu.com/)

As for Ubuntu Phones, I am glad that you are not the owner of Canonical and Ubuntu. You cannot see the vision. Ubuntu Phone is not meant to be a direct competitor of Apple and Google products. Canonical could never compete with those mega corporations. And it is not trying to.

Stop thinking of world domination. Start thinking in terms of a "nice little earner." Products that get a small market share but make enough profit for the OEM and Canonical to keep the product line going. Samsung could never bring out a Ubuntu phone. It would need to sell millions of units or its business reputation will suffer. But there are manufacturers out there who seem content to make and sell a few/several hundred thousand units, especially if someone else takes responsibility for the maintenance of the software.

Apple keeps the money in rolling by regularly bringing out new models. The OS on a Ubuntu phone purchased this year will be kept up to date with the OS on a Ubuntu phone purchased next year and the year after. Planned Obsolescence is not the business model for Ubuntu Phone.
