Privacy and security are not synonymous. The internet is a public space.  You are not anonymous there. 

An entire section of Win10's Settings app is devoted to configuring privacy settings, where the defaults can be changed.

This statement:

> We will access, disclose and preserve personal data, including your content (such as the content of your emails, other private communications or files in private folders), when we have a good faith belief that doing so is necessary to protect our customers or enforce the terms governing the use of the services.

seems to me the kind of statement anyone running any kind of online service would need to include in their Terms and Services to demonstrate due diligence in informing customers. That statement represents proof that customers -- and their lawyers -- were informed. 

It's silly to imagine Microsoft is paying people to read your mail or my mail. But, Microsoft would certainly have a legitimate reason to target your email if it found your were distributing malware via its mail servers, or if it becomes involved in a legal action that requires that. The same applies to any other online business, service providers, etc. Putting that statement in the T&S means customers can't tell a court, for example, the evidence of criminal activity Microsoft delivered under court order can't be used because Microsoft failed to tell the customer they would do that.
