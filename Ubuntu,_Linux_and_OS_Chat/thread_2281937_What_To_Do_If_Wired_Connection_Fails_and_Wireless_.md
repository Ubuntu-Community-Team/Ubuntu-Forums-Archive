### Post by oldefoxx on 2015-06-10
This happens to me, znd it could happen simply because you need to move a Laptop to another point in the house or at work.

Advice found elsewhere was to save all your work and shut down the Laptopp.  Some have advised using the command line to remove the wireless driver then reinstall it.  This works, but there is a better way.

Just enter the Suspend mode.  Whrn you press the power button to recover, the OS will re=enable the wireless connection and there is no need to interrupt what you were doing, as you will start again just where you were when you entered the Suspend state.

If you get back to the point where you had the wired connection and plug that back into the Laptop, it makes for a faster and more secure connection, and the OS will return to using it instead of using the wireless capability,

### Post by oldefoxx on 2015-06-16
Wireless is not as secure as wired, nor is it as fast.  I sit all day, so being cabled up is not an issue for me.  Wireless speed sepends in part on the distance between you and the access point, whereas being wired up means the same speed regardless.  And I don't need to ask the person with the network to let me have access, which he/she gives me by entering or giving me the password.  Once in on wireless, that connectivity does not go away just because the current need has been dealt with.

How many people do you know that can get to the internet through your wireless connectivity?  And that is just the ones you know about.  You can just go in and change your access password, but most people probably find that too much of a hassle.  Heck, they probably still have the default Admin password unchanged.

The courts have held that what goes through the air is subject to legal snooping without a warrant, while what goes through wiring required probable cause.  I'm not at risk, in that what I do is perfectly legal, but this might be good advice for some others.

### Post by oldefoxx on 2015-06-20
Suspend does not always work as expected.  I've had it wake up again just by lifting the laptop screen-cover, and I've most often had to use the power up button to bring it to life again.  But either way, it recovers wirelessly if I did not reattach the cable.  If I belatedly plug in the cable, it takes over from the wireless, which is what I want.

And if you are going to be moving about with a netebook, it seems to make sense that you suspend/unsuspend it while in progress.  But not everybody does this, something that the laptop community probably takes for granted.
