### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-07-25
You are trying to make a case to go back to Windows.  What is preventing you from dual-booting?  Use Windows when you absolutely have to and use Linux for everything else.

I make my technical drawings by hand because it is easier than dual-booting into Windows to use Visio.  The drawings are for my use on projects and not for publication and rubber-stamping by several layers of upper management.  The drawing tools in Linux are OK, but Visio has a lot of stuff to handle complicated network drawings.  I can still draw by hand faster than using Linux drawing tools or Visio.  So one needs to step back and assess:  "What is most important?"

It's interesting how some doctoral thesis students still use LaTex for formatting because Office (or LibreOffice) can't display equations the way they want them on the page.

From a legal standpoint, you have several options--and you want to keep those options open.  I'm not sure they teach that in law school.

Personally, I don't understand why this question is always framed as "switch". 

You can own both a Ford and a Chevy and have each parked side by side in your garage.  Why is an operating system any different?
