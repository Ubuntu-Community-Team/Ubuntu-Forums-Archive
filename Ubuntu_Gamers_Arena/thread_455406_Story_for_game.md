This is a short story I wrote sometime ago I am adding as an example, although this particular story might not be suitable for a game format it should give you an idea of how to modify a story to better suit the game format. So here it is :

A mercy mist of a scale indeterminable swept over the battalion,
The heat and excitement of it created a sweat which was quickly swept from our skin,
I heard a deep shudder next to me.
"GET DOWN!!!"
I saw a sight which seemed so beautiful at the time. Time seemed to lapse as the events occurred . A fiery explosion blasted me back onto the silky ground. I got up.. A sharp sting rung repetitively in my ears. I could hear only the muffled bloodshed and hatred around me. My excitement soon transformed to fear. A sudden moment of clarity overcame me.
I arose looking at blood on a rock. Blackout I thought to myself?


I looked upwards towards the sky where I could barely make out the stars through the ripping waves of the sand upon the air. Stretching across the edge of the trench I saw a hand. The sand seemed to be piercing through the skin, soaking up the blood into a grainy substance. I placed my hand into my left pocket to retrieve a cigarette , well deserved moment in the hell which was unfolding around me. As I inhaled the ringing began to become unnoticeable. The war began to become unnoticeable.

I covered my head when the blast occurred, opened my eyes and saw the hand staring back at me. Exhaled a cloud of smoke and stared back in shock. The hand was now severed from its body and withered on the ground. I finished my cigarette, stamped my foot on it and proceeded to crawl through the trenches. I saw a dark figure running towards me "wait....wait.." I said cautiously to myself. I saw a gun so I fired.

I stopped yet again to regain my bearings. Took a shot of J.D and opened my eyes once more. They were not closed but the clarity felt like an awakening. I stumbled forward to see the face of the man I had killed only to see a child's face staring back at me. The child was in military uniform with a machine gun in his cold lifeless hands. A tear rolled down my face. The sand storm took it away

I took my pistol and placed it in my mouth, for my country and my government had already killed me.

THE END
