### Post by donkyhotay on 2008-10-17
Most open source projects are done either because a company needs it for it's business or because some geek does it in his spare time. Because games are generally not business crucial we get stuck with games done in peoples spare time. Admittedly if you get enough people together amazing things can happen (just look at bzflag or battle for wesnoth as an example) but this requires enough people with the same vision to bring it forth. I'm currently working on developing an open source remake of Moonbase Commander (best strategy game ever! IMHO) however I'm not a good programmer, a horrible artist and although the game is (technically) playable it looks absolutely horrible (especially compared to the original game). But as I mentioned this project is being done by pretty much me by myself solely because I absolutely love Moonbase Commander and want to be able to play it natively and without the limitations the developers put into the original. It's one of the strengths/weaknesses of open source. If you don't like the way a program does something you can just change it yourself, on the other hand if you don't like the way a program does something you generally have to change it yourself (unless you want to pay someone of course).

### Post by Bateluer on 2008-12-04
A few Windows games do have Linux clients, such as Bioware's Neverwinter Nights. Pity Atari and Obsidian chose not to continue with a Linux client for NWN2. 

Most Quake engine based games also run very well in Linux, and the UT games usually have native Linux clients. 

Otherwise, you'll be using Wine, Crossover, or Cedega for your gaming fix.
