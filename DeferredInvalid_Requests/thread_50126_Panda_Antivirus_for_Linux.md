### Post by linbetwin on 2005-12-06
Panda is a good AV on the Windows platform. I've been using it for the past two years and never got infected with malware.

On the other hand, Panda for Linux (as you could have read in the [Ubuntu Wiki]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PandaAntivirus")) is mostly used in servers to scan for files that could infect Windows computers connected to those servers. And although it is freeware, **you will not get virus definitions updates unless you buy the product**.
